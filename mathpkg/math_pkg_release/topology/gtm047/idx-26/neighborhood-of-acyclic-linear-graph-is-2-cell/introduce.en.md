In a triangulated compact connected $2$-manifold, the regular neighborhood $N(L)$ of any connected acyclic linear graph $L$ (i.e., a subtree of the $1$-skeleton) is homeomorphic to a $2$-disk. The proof proceeds by induction on the number of edges: removing an edge splits $L$ into two smaller acyclic components whose neighborhoods are $2$-cells by induction, and $N(L)$ is the union of these two $2$-cells joined by the edge interior neighborhood $N'(e)$, forming a single $2$-cell.
