For each edge $e$ in a triangulation $K$ of a $2$-manifold, the edge interior neighborhood $N'(e)$ is the closure of the regular neighborhood $N(e)$ minus the neighborhoods of the endpoints $\operatorname{Bd} e$. Together with vertex neighborhoods $N(v)$, these form a decomposition of the edge neighborhood $N(e)$ into three $2$-cells laid end-to-end. This construction is a technical tool used to prove that the neighborhood $N(L)$ of an acyclic linear graph $L$ is a $2$-cell.
