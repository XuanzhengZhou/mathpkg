A linear graph (a $1$-dimensional simplicial complex) is called acyclic if its geometric realization contains no polygon, i.e., no simple closed curve composed of edges. Equivalently, an acyclic linear graph is a tree — a connected graph without cycles. This notion is used in the classification of $2$-manifolds to ensure that the regular neighborhood $N(L)$ of a connected acyclic linear graph is a $2$-cell.
