Let $S_i$ be a twisted strip with $J = \operatorname{Bd}(S_i \cup D)$ a polygon. Let $P, Q, R, T$ be points on $J$ not lying in any other strip $S_j$, with $PT$ the arc of $J$ between $P$ and $T$ intersecting $\operatorname{Bd} S_i$, and suppose $P, Q, R, T$ appear in cyclic order on $J$ with the arcs $PQ \subset PT$ and $RT \subset PT$ intersecting no $S_j$. Then there exists a piecewise-linear homeomorphism
$$h: M \leftrightarrow M, \quad J \leftrightarrow J, \quad D \cup S_i \leftrightarrow D \cup S_i,$$
$$P \mapsto P, \quad T \mapsto T, \quad QT \leftrightarrow RT,$$
such that $h|(J - PT)$ is the identity. \emph{Operation $\alpha$} is the replacement of the old system of $2$-cells $\{D, D', S_i, S_j\}$ by the new system $\{D, h(D'), S_i, h(S_j)\}$.
