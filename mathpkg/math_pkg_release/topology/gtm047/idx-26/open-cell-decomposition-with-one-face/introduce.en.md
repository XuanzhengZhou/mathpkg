Given any triangulation $K$ of a compact connected $2$-manifold $M$, one can reorganize the $2$-faces of $K$ into a single open $2$-cell $C^2$ while retaining the edges of $K$ as the edges of the new cell-decomposition. This structural result is the foundation for the classification of compact connected $2$-manifolds, as it reduces the manifold to a single polyhedral disk with identifications on its boundary.
