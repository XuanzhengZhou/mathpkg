In the decomposition of a compact connected $2$-manifold into a $2$-cell with strips, each strip $S_i$ is classified by the topology of $S_i \cup D$: it is annular if the union is an annulus (orientable, two boundary components), and twisted if the union is a M\"obius band (non-orientable, one boundary component). This distinction is crucial because annular strips contribute handles after further manipulation, while twisted strips contribute cross-caps, and the number of twisted strips determines orientability.
