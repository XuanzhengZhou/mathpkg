The first Betti number and torsion group depend only on the number of handles $h$ and cross-caps $m$. For orientable surfaces ($m=0$), $p^1 = 2h$ (twice the genus) and there is no torsion. Adding one cross-cap ($m=1$) does not change the Betti number but introduces $\mathbf{Z}_2$ torsion. Adding a second cross-cap ($m=2$) increases the Betti number by one while the torsion remains $\mathbf{Z}_2$. These formulas reflect the geometric fact that cross-caps contribute torsion from the non-orientable homology cycle.
