This theorem provides a fundamental structural representation for triangulated 3-manifolds with boundary. By taking the "doubling" of $M = |K|$ (gluing two copies along their boundary via Theorem 5) and triangulating the result, $K$ becomes isomorphic to a subcomplex $L$ of a closed triangulated 3-manifold $K'$. The theorem then asserts that $K$ is combinatorially equivalent to the regular neighborhood $N(L)$ of this subcomplex in $K'$.
