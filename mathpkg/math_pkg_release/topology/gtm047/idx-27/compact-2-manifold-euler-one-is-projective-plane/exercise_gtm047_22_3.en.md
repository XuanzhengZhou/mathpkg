Let $N$ be a space which is the union of two 2-cells $D_1$ and $D_2$, such that $D_1 \cap D_2$ is the union of a finite collection of disjoint arcs lying in $\operatorname{Bd} D_1 \cap \operatorname{Bd} D_2$. Can we infer (as at the end of the proof of Theorem 4) that $D_1 \cup D_2$ is a 2-sphere with holes? If not, what are the other possibilities for $N$?
