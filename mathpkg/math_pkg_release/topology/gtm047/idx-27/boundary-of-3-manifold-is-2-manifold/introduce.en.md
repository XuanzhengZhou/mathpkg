This theorem states that the topological boundary of any 3-manifold with boundary is always a 2-dimensional manifold (without boundary). This is a corollary of Theorem 1 (every triangulated 3-manifold is combinatorial), which implies that every point in the boundary has a local neighborhood structure modeled on a half-space of $\mathbf{R}^3$, and therefore the boundary locally looks like $\mathbf{R}^2$.
