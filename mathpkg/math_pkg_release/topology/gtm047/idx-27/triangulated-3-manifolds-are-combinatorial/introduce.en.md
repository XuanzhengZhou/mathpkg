This theorem establishes that the defining local condition of a combinatorial 3-manifold — that every vertex star is combinatorially equivalent to a 3-simplex — is automatically satisfied by any triangulated 3-manifold. The proof relies on the invariance of domain and a detailed analysis of the simplicial structure, showing that every point has a neighborhood homeomorphic to $\mathbf{R}^3$, that every 2-simplex lies in at least two 3-simplexes, and that the link of each vertex is a simply connected 2-sphere.
