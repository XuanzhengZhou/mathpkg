Let $M^3 = |K|$ be a triangulated 3-manifold with boundary, let $M^2$ be a compact polyhedral 2-manifold in $\text{Int } M^3$, let $i$ be the inclusion $M^2 \rightarrow M^3$, and let $i^*$ be the induced homomorphism $\pi(M^2) \rightarrow \pi(M^3)$. Suppose that (1) $M^2$ is two sided in $\text{Int } M^3$ and (2) $\ker i^*$ is nontrivial. Then there is a polyhedral 2-cell $\Delta$ in $\text{Int } M^3$, with $\text{Bd } \Delta = C$, such that $C = \Delta \cap M^2$ and $C$ is not contractible in $M^2$.
