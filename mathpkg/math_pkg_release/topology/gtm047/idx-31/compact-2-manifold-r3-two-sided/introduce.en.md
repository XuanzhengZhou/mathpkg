This theorem establishes that every compact connected polyhedral 2-manifold in $\mathbf{R}^3$ is automatically two-sided. Moreover, the complement $\mathbf{R}^3 - M^2$ consists of exactly two connected components -- a bounded interior $I$ and an unbounded exterior $E$ -- with $M^2$ serving as the common frontier of both. This generalizes the Jordan curve theorem to surfaces in $\mathbf{R}^3$.
