This theorem establishes that any polyhedral 2-manifold formed by boundary components of a 3-submanifold $N$ embedded in the interior of a triangulated 3-manifold $M^3$ is automatically two-sided. The proof decomposes a connected neighborhood $W$ of each component $B$ into the disjoint union of the part inside $N$ and the part outside $N$, which are nonempty and separated, verifying the two-sidedness condition.
