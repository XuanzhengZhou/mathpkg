Let $K$ be a complex (finite or infinite) and let $P_0$ be a vertex of $K$. There is a natural homomorphism

$$h: \pi(|K|, P_0) \to H_1(K) = H_1(K, \mathbb{Z}),$$

called the Hurewicz homomorphism, defined as follows:

(1) In each equivalence class $\bar{p} \in \pi(|K|, P_0)$ there is a representative $p: [0,1] \to |K^1|$ which is simplicial relative to $K^1$ and a subdivision $L$ of $[0,1]$.

(2) Suppose that the simplexes of $K$ are oriented. To each simplicial path $p$ there corresponds a $1$-cycle
$$Z^1(p) = \sum \alpha_i \sigma_i^1,$$
where $\alpha_i$ is the net number of times $\sigma_i^1$ is traversed (counting orientation). Then

$$p \cong p' \text{ in } \pi(|K|, P_0) \Rightarrow Z^1(p) \sim Z^1(p') \text{ in } H_1(K).$$

The homomorphism $h$ sends $\bar{p}$ to the homology class of $Z^1(p)$. The kernel of $h$ is the commutator subgroup of $\pi(|K|, P_0)$, so $h$ induces an isomorphism $\pi(|K|, P_0)^{\text{ab}} \cong H_1(K)$.
