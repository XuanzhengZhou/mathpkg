**Proof.** (Outline, as indicated by Problems 4-13.)

The proof proceeds in several steps:

1. If $\bar{p}$ is a commutator $\bar{p}_1 \bar{p}_2 \bar{p}_1^{-1} \bar{p}_2^{-1}$, then $\bar{p} \in \ker h$ (Problem 4).

2. The commutator subgroup $C$ of $\pi(|K|, P_0)$ is contained in $\ker h$ (Problem 5).

3. For each vertex $v_i$ of $K$, choose a simplicial path $b_i$ from $P_0$ to $v_i$. For each $2$-simplex $\sigma^2 = v_i v_j v_k$, construct a path $q(\sigma^2)$ that traverses the boundary. These paths are commutators in $\pi(|K|, P_0)$ (Problems 6-8).

4. Every relation among $1$-cycles in $H_1(K)$ arises from $2$-simplex boundaries. The corresponding loops are products of paths $q(\sigma^2)$, hence lie in the commutator subgroup (Problems 9-11).

5. This shows $\ker h = C$, establishing the isomorphism $\pi(|K|, P_0)/C \cong H_1(K)$ (Problems 12-13).
