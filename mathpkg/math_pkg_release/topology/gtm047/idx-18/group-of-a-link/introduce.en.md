The group of a link $L$ is defined as the fundamental group of its complement $\pi(\mathbb{R}^3 \setminus L)$. This group is a powerful invariant: it is always finitely generated and can be obtained from a free group by imposing a finite number of four-letter relations (the Wirtinger presentation). The group of a link captures essential information about how the link is knotted and linked in space.
