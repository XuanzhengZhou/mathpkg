A link diagram is the planar projection of a link onto $\mathbb{R}^2$ under a general position assumption on the projection direction. The conditions ensure that the only singularities in the projection are transverse double points (crossings), ruling out triple intersections and tangential "almost-crossings." At each crossing, an orientation is assigned to each component, and the over/under information is recorded to faithfully represent the embedding of the link in $\mathbb{R}^3$.
