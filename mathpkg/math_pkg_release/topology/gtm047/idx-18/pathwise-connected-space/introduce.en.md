A topological space is pathwise connected if every pair of points can be joined by a continuous path. This notion, defined at the beginning of Section 1, is the natural setting for defining the fundamental group. In the sequel, $X$ will typically be a polyhedron in a Cartesian space, an open subset thereof, or a space homeomorphic to one of these.
