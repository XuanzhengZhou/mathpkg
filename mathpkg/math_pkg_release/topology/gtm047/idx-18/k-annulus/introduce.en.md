A $k$-annulus generalizes the classical annulus (a $1$-annulus) to a compact connected $2$-manifold with boundary that can be embedded in the plane and whose boundary consists of $k+1$ components. Thus a $1$-annulus is an ordinary annulus, while a $k$-annulus can be visualized as a $2$-cell with $k$ holes removed.
