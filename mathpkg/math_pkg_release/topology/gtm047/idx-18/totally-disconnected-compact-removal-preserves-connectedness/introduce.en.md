Theorem 4 is a crucial lemma: removing a totally disconnected compact set from a connected open set in the plane does not disconnect it. The proof uses a $2$-cell decomposition of a broken line joining two arbitrary points in $U-M$, applying Theorem 3 to show that the endpoints remain connected after removing the compact set. This generalizes Theorem 4.4 and is essential for the main extension theorem.
