**Proof.** Given a continuous homotopy $f: [0,1]^2 \to U$ witnessing $p \cong p'$, one applies a simplicial approximation theorem to triangulate $[0,1]^2$ finely enough. By the same compactness argument as in Theorem 6, the image of $f$ has positive distance from $\mathbb{R}^3 \setminus U$. A PL map $f'$ is constructed by simplicial approximation, agreeing with $f$ on the boundary of $[0,1]^2$ (where $p$ and $p'$ are already PL). The resulting PL map provides the required PL based homotopy.
