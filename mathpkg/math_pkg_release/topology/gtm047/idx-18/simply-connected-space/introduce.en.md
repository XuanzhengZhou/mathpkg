A space is simply connected if it is pathwise connected and every loop can be continuously shrunk to a point (i.e., the fundamental group is trivial). Examples include $\mathbb{R}^n$ for $n \geq 2$, convex sets, and $n$-spheres for $n \geq 2$. Simply connected spaces are precisely those with no $1$-dimensional holes.
