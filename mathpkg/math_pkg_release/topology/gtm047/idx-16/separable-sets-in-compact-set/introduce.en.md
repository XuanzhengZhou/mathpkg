In a metrizable space, two disjoint closed sets $A$ and $B$ are called separable in a closed set $M$ if $M$ can be partitioned into two disjoint closed subsets that respectively contain $M \cap A$ and $M \cap B$. If no such partition exists, $A$ and $B$ are inseparable in $M$. This notion is used to characterize when a compact set must contain a connected subset intersecting both $A$ and $B$.
