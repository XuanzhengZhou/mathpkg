An isotopy is a homotopy through homeomorphisms. Given two homeomorphisms $f_0, f_1: A \to B$, an isotopy $\phi: A \times [0, 1] \to B$ is a continuous family of homeomorphisms $f_t$ connecting them. Isotopy is the appropriate equivalence relation for studying embeddings and homeomorphisms in geometric topology, stricter than mere homotopy.
