Let $M$ be a compact set, in a metrizable space $[X, \emptyset]$, and let $A$ and $B$ be disjoint closed sets in $X$, such that $A$ and $B$ are inseparable in $M$. Then $M$ contains a closed subset $M'$ in which $A$ and $B$ are still inseparable, and which is irreducible with respect to this property, in the sense that every proper closed subset of $M'$ fails to have this property.
