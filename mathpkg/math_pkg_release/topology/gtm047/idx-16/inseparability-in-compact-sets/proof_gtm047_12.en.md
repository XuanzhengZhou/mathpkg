The proof proceeds by Zorn's lemma (or by considering the intersection of a maximal chain of closed subsets of $M$ in which $A$ and $B$ remain inseparable). Let $\mathcal{F}$ be the family of all closed subsets of $M$ in which $A$ and $B$ are inseparable. $\mathcal{F}$ is nonempty (it contains $M$). Order $\mathcal{F}$ by reverse inclusion. By the preceding theorem (Theorem 1), every descending chain in $\mathcal{F}$ has a lower bound in $\mathcal{F}$ (their intersection). By Zorn's lemma, $\mathcal{F}$ has a minimal element $M'$. This $M'$ is irreducible with respect to the inseparability property.
