This theorem (Theorem 2 of the section) states that if two disjoint closed sets $A$ and $B$ are inseparable in a compact set $M$, then $M$ contains an irreducible closed subset $M'$ that still makes $A$ and $B$ inseparable. The subset $M'$ is minimal with respect to this inseparability property, providing a key step toward proving that $M$ must contain a connected set intersecting both $A$ and $B$.
