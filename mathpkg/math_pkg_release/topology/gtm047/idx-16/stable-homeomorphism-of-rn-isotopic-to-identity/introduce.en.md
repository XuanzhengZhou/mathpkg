This theorem states that any stable homeomorphism of Euclidean space $\mathbf{R}^n$ is isotopic to the identity. The proof reduces to Alexander's theorem on the ball by using the inversion map $\operatorname{inv}(P) = P/\|P\|^2$ to conjugate the given homeomorphism to one defined on the closed unit ball $\mathbf{B}^n$ that fixes the boundary sphere $\mathbf{S}^{n-1}$.
