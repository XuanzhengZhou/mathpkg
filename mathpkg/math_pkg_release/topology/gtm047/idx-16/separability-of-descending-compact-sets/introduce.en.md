This theorem (Theorem 1 of the section) states that inseparability of two disjoint closed sets $A$ and $B$ persists under countable descending intersections of compact sets. If $A$ and $B$ are inseparable in each compact set $M_i$ of a descending sequence, then they remain inseparable in the intersection $M_{\infty}$. The proof uses a metric argument with $\varepsilon$-neighborhoods and the finite intersection property of compact sets.
