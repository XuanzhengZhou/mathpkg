Suppose that (2) is false. We shall show that (1) is true. Let $M'$ be as in the preceding theorem. Suppose that $M'$ is not connected. Then $M'$ is the union of two disjoint nonempty sets $H$ and $K$. Since $M'$ is irreducible, $A$ and $B$ are separable in each of the sets $H$ and $K$. Thus we have
$$H = H_A \cup H_B, \quad K = K_A \cup K_B,$$
as in the definition of separable. Therefore
$$M' = (H_A \cup K_A) \cup (H_B \cup K_B),$$
where the sets in parentheses are disjoint and closed. Therefore $A$ and $B$ are separable in $M'$, which is false.
