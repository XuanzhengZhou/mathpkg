Theorem 3 gives a fundamental dichotomy for compact sets: either two disjoint closed sets $A$ and $B$ are separable in $M$, or $M$ contains a connected set intersecting both. The proof uses the irreducible set $M'$ from Theorem 2: if $M'$ were disconnected, it could be partitioned, contradicting its irreducibility. Therefore $M'$ is connected and intersects both $A$ and $B$.
