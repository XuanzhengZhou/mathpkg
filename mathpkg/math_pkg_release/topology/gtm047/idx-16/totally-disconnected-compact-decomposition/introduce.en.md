Theorem 4 states that any totally disconnected compact metric space can be decomposed into finitely many disjoint closed subsets of arbitrarily small diameter. This provides a fundamental structural characterization: the space is covered by a finite clopen partition where each piece has diameter less than any prescribed $\varepsilon > 0$. This property is used to construct finite coverings that approximate Cantor sets.
