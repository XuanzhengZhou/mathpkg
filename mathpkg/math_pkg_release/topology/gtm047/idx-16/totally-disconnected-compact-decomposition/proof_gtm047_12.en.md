$C$ is covered by the set of all neighborhoods of the form
$$N\!\left(P, \frac{\varepsilon}{4}\right) \quad (P \in C).$$
(Hereafter, we regard $C$ as a space.) Therefore $C$ is covered by a finite collection of such neighborhoods. By Theorem 3, each pair of points lying in distinct components of $C$ can be separated by a partition into disjoint closed sets. Using the finite cover, we construct a finite collection $G_\varepsilon = \{g_1, g_2, \ldots, g_n\}$ of disjoint nonempty closed (and therefore open in $C$) sets covering $C$, with $\delta g_i < \varepsilon$ for each $i$. Finally, if any set $g_i$ is empty, we simply delete it from $G_\varepsilon$.
