This theorem asserts that any homeomorphism from the polyhedron of a combinatorial 2-manifold with boundary into the Euclidean plane can be approximated arbitrarily closely by a piecewise linear homeomorphism. The approximation is controlled by a strongly positive function $\phi$ defined on the polyhedron, meaning that for every point $P$, the distance between $h(P)$ and $f(P)$ is less than $\phi(P)$. The proof proceeds by first obtaining a PL approximation on the 1-skeleton using a preceding theorem on 1-dimensional complexes, and then extending this to the 2-simplices via the combinatorial Schoenflies theorem. Careful choices of subdivision and auxiliary positive functions ensure that the extensions on individual 2-simplices fit together to form a global homeomorphism.
