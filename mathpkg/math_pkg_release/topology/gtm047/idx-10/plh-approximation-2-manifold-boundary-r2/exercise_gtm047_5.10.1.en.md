Prove or disprove:

Let $K$ be a combinatorial $2$-manifold with boundary, such that there is a homeomorphism $f: |K| \to \mathbb{R}^2$. Then there is a subdivision $L$ of $K$, and a PL homeomorphism $g: |L^1| \to \mathbb{R}^2$, such that $g$ cannot be extended to give a PL homeomorphism $g': |L| \to \mathbb{R}^2$.
