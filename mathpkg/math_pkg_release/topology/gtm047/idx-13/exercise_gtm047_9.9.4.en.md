Let $\mathbf{B}^2$ and $J$ be as in Problem 3, and let $M$ be a set which is the union of three arcs $A_i = P_0P_i$ ($1 \leq i \leq 3$), where $P_0 \in \text{Int } \mathbf{B}^2$, $P_i \in J$ for $i \neq 0$, $\text{Int } A_i \subset \text{Int } \mathbf{B}^2$, and $A_i \cap A_j = \{P_0\}$ for $i \neq j$. Prove or disprove: $\mathbf{B}^2 - M$ has exactly three components, and each of these contains a component of $J - \{P_1, P_2, P_3\}$.
