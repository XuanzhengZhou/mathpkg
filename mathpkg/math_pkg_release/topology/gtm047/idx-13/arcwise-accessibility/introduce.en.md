A point $P$ is arcwise accessible from a set $U$ if there exists an arc $A$ containing $P$ such that the entire arc except possibly $P$ itself lies inside $U$. This generalizes the notion of linear accessibility to arbitrary arcs and is used in the study of boundary behavior of planar sets. In the context of Jordan curves, arcwise accessibility describes which points on the curve can be reached by an arc from the interior or exterior.
