Given two triangulated 2-manifolds $K_1$ and $K_2$, any homeomorphism from an open subset $U$ of $|K_1|$ into $|K_2|$ can be approximated arbitrarily closely (in the sense of a strongly positive function $\phi$) by a piecewise-linear homeomorphism $f$, with the additional property that $f$ has exactly the same image as the original homeomorphism. This theorem bridges the gap between topological and combinatorial structures on 2-manifolds, showing that topological homeomorphisms can be replaced by PL homeomorphisms without changing the image.
