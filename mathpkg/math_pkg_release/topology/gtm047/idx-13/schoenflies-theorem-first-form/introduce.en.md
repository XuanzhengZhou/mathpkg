The Schönflies theorem in its first form states that the closure of the interior of any 1-sphere (simple closed curve) in the plane is homeomorphic to the standard 2-cell (closed unit disk). This strengthens the Jordan curve theorem by characterizing not just the separation properties but the actual topological type of the region bounded by a simple closed curve. The proof proceeds by constructing a sequence of polygonal approximations converging to the curve from the interior, then using the polygonal Schönflies theorem and extending the homeomorphism via a cellular decomposition argument.
