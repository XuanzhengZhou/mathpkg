Let $J$ be a 1-sphere in $\mathbf{R}^2$, with interior $I$, and let $G_1, G_2, \ldots$ be a sequence of finite collections of arcs in $J$ (as in Theorem 3). Then there is a sequence $H_1, H_2, \ldots$ of collections of linear intervals $vv'$ ($v \in J$), such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item If $vv' \in H_i$, then $vv' - \{v\} \subset I$, and $v$ is an end-point of some $g \in G_i$,
\item Each end-point of each $g \in G_i$ lies in one and only one interval $vv' \in H_i$,
\item For each $i$, the elements of $H_i$ are disjoint, and
\item If $vv' \in H_i$, and $ww' \in H_j$ ($i < j$), and $vv'$ intersects $ww'$, then $v = w$ and $ww' \subset vv'$.
\end{enumerate}
