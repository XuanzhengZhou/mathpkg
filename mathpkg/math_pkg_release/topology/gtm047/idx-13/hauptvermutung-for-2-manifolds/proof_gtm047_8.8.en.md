This follows directly from Theorem 4. Take $U = |K_1|$ and $h$ the given homeomorphism $|K_1| \leftrightarrow |K_2|$. By Theorem 4, there exists a PLH $f: |K_1| \rightarrow |K_2|$ such that $f(|K_1|) = h(|K_1|) = |K_2|$. Thus $f$ is a PLH between the two triangulated 2-manifolds, establishing combinatorial equivalence.

Note that in the proof of Theorem 4 no use was made of the fact that the manifolds are 2-dimensional. Thus, a 3-dimensional version of Theorem 6.4 would yield 3-dimensional versions of Theorems 4 and 5.
