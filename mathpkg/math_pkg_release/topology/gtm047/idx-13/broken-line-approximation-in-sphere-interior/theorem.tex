Let $J$ be a 1-sphere in $\mathbf{R}^2$, let $I$ be its interior, and let $A$ be an arc in $J$, with end-points $v_0$ and $v_1$. Let $v_0v'_0$ and $v_1v'_1$ be linear intervals such that $v_i v'_i - \{v_i\} \subset I$ for $i = 0, 1$. Let $\varepsilon > 0$ be given. Then there is a broken line $b_A$ joining a point $w_0$ of $v_0 v'_0$ to a point $w_1$ of $v_1 v'_1$ such that $b_A - \{w_0, w_1\} \subset I$ and $b_A$ lies in the $\varepsilon$-neighborhood of $A$.
