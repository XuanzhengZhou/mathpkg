Given an arc $A$ on a 1-sphere $J$ and two linear intervals $v_0 v_0'$, $v_1 v_1'$ extending from its endpoints into the interior $I$, one can construct a broken line $b_A$ that approximates $A$ arbitrarily closely (within any prescribed $\varepsilon$-neighborhood) while lying in the closure of $I$ and joining points on the two radial intervals. This broken line serves as a "polygonal replacement" for the arc $A$, and is a key technical tool in the proof of the Schönflies theorem where arcs of $J$ are systematically replaced by polygonal arcs inside $\bar{I}$.
