In an abstract complex $\Phi$, an element $\phi$ with $k+1$ members is called an abstract $k$-simplex. The dimension of the simplex equals its cardinality minus one, mirroring the geometric intuition that a $k$-simplex has $k+1$ vertices. This terminology unifies the combinatorial and geometric viewpoints.
