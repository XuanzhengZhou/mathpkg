A face of an abstract simplex $\phi$ is any nonempty subset $\phi' \subset \phi$ that also belongs to the abstract complex. By property (2) of an abstract complex, every nonempty subset of a simplex is itself a simplex of the complex. This generalizes the geometric notion that the vertices of any face of a Euclidean simplex determine that face.
