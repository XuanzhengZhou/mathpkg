Let $K$ be a Euclidean complex in $\mathbf{R}^m$. The \textit{diagram} $\Phi = \Phi(K)$ of $K$ is the set of all sets of the type $\{v_0, v_1, \ldots, v_k\}$, where $v_0 v_1 \ldots v_k \in K$. Then $\Phi$ has the following properties:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $\Phi$ is a collection of nonempty finite sets.
\item[(2)] If $\phi \in \Phi$, and $\phi'$ is a nonempty subset of $\phi$, then $\phi' \in \Phi$.
\item[(3)] Every element of $\Phi$ intersects only a finite number of elements of $\Phi$.
\item[(4)] The union of the elements of $\Phi$ is countable.
\item[(5)] There exists an integer $n$ such that every element of $\Phi$ has at most $n+1$ elements.
\end{enumerate}
