This theorem asserts that any finite collection of disjoint polygons in the boundary of a combinatorial solid torus can be simultaneously isotoped into standard position relative to a fixed reference polygon $J_x$ (typically a longitude or meridian). The isotopy is realized by a PL homeomorphism $h$ of the ambient PL 3-manifold $M^3$ that is supported within an arbitrarily small neighborhood of the boundary torus $T$, meaning $h$ can be taken to be the identity outside any given neighborhood $W$ of $T$.
