Form a strip-neighborhood $N$ of $B$. As in the proof of Theorem 1, for a broken line one endpoint serves as the analog: starting at any point $P$ of $N - B$, we can work our way along the broken line until we reach the neighborhood of the endpoint. Then $P_1$ can be joined to $P_2$ by a path in $N - B$. Therefore $N - B$ is connected. Consequently, as in the proof of Theorem 1, $\mathbf{R}^2 - B$ is connected. $\square$
