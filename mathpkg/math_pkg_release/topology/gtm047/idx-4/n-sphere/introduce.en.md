An $n$-sphere is any topological space that is homeomorphic to the standard $n$-sphere. In geometric topology, the standard model is the unit sphere in $\mathbf{R}^n$, and any space sharing its topological properties qualifies as an $n$-sphere. Polygons, being polyhedral 1-spheres, are the primary $1$-dimensional examples studied in plane separation theory.
