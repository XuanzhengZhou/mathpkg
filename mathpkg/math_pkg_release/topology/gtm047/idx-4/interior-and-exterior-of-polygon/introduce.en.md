By the Jordan Curve Theorem for polygons, the complement of a polygon $J$ in the plane consists of exactly two connected components. The bounded component is called the interior $I$ of $J$, and the unbounded component is called the exterior $E$ of $J$. These definitions are fundamental to all subsequent geometric topology results regarding polygonal separation, polyhedral triangulation, and limit-point properties.
