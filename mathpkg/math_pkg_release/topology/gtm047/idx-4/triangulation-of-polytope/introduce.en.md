A triangulation of a polytope $|K|$ is a simplicial complex $K$ whose underlying space (the union of its simplices) equals $|K|$. In geometric topology, a triangulation provides a combinatorial description of a polyhedron, decomposing it into vertices, edges, triangles, and higher-dimensional simplices that meet face-to-face.
