A broken line is a polyhedral arc -- a piecewise-linear curve consisting of finitely many straight line segments joined end-to-end, with two distinct endpoints. Unlike a polygon, a broken line does not close back on itself. A key theorem in plane separation theory states that broken lines do not separate the plane: the complement of a broken line in $\mathbf{R}^2$ is connected.
