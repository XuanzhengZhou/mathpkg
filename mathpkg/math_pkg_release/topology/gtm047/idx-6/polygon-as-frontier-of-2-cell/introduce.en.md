Every polygon in the plane bounds a topological 2-cell (a space homeomorphic to the closed 2-ball). This follows directly from Theorem 4, which provides a homeomorphism of the plane sending the given polygon to the frontier of a 2-simplex; the interior of that simplex is a 2-cell whose frontier is the image of the polygon. This result establishes the fundamental fact that polygons are topologically equivalent to the boundary of a disk.
