The mapping $v \mapsto v - v_0$ is a homeomorphism $\mathbb{R}^n \leftrightarrow \mathbb{R}^n$, and maps every simplex simplicially onto a simplex. The composition of two such mappings has the same properties. Therefore we may assume, with no loss of generality, that $v_0$ is the origin in $\mathbb{R}^n$. Similarly for $w_0$. We define

$$g(v) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \alpha_i w_i.$$

Now $g|\sigma^n$ is the $f$ of Theorem 1.
