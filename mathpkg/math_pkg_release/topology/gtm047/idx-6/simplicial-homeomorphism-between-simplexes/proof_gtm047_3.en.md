For each $v = \sum \alpha_i v_i$ ($\alpha_i \geqslant 0, \sum \alpha_i = 1$), define $f(v) = \sum \alpha_i w_i$. Then $f$ is bijective, and $f$ and $f^{-1}$ are continuous. Since $f$ and $f^{-1}$ are linear relative to barycentric coordinates, they are linear relative to Cartesian coordinates, and so both are continuous relative to the subspace topology, which we are using, as always.
