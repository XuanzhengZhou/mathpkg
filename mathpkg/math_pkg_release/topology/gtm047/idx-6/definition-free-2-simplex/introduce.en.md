In a triangulation of a closed polygonal disk, a 2-simplex is called "free" if it meets the boundary of the polygon in either one or two of its edges. Free simplexes are those that can be "peeled off" from the triangulation by a homeomorphism of the plane without disturbing the rest of the complex. The existence of free 2-simplexes in any non-trivial triangulation of a polygonal disk (Theorem 3) is the combinatorial engine behind the proof of the Schönflies theorem for polygons.
