Every triangulation of a closed polygonal disk with more than one 2-simplex contains at least two free 2-simplexes. A 2-simplex is free if its intersection with the boundary of the polygon is one or two of its edges. This combinatorial fact about triangulations is crucial for proving the Schönflies theorem for polygons, as free simplexes can be removed one by one via homeomorphisms of the plane, eventually reducing the polygon to the frontier of a single 2-simplex.
