The Jordan curve theorem states that every simple closed curve (topological 1-sphere) in the plane separates the plane into exactly two connected components: an interior region and an exterior region, with the curve itself being the common boundary of both. This foundational result in planar topology, though intuitively obvious, requires careful proof. The interior $I$ is bounded and simply connected, while the exterior $E$ is unbounded; both are open and connected, and their frontiers equal the curve $J$.
