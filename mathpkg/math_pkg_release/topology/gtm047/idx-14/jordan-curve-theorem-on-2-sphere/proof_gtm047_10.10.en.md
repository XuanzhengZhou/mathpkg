Delete any point $P_0$ of $S^2 - J$. Then $S^2 - P_0$ is homeomorphic to $\mathbf{R}^2$. Apply the classical Jordan curve theorem in this copy of $\mathbf{R}^2$ to obtain two disjoint connected open sets whose common frontier is $J$. Reinstate the deleted point $P_0$; it lies in one of the two open sets, and the topological separation is preserved. Thus $S^2 - J$ decomposes into two disjoint connected open sets $U$ and $V$ with $J = \operatorname{Fr} U = \operatorname{Fr} V$.
