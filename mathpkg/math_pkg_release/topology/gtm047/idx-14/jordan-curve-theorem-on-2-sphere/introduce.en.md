This theorem extends the classical Jordan curve theorem from the plane $\mathbf{R}^2$ to an arbitrary $2$-sphere $S^2$. The key observation is that $S^2$ with one point removed is homeomorphic to $\mathbf{R}^2$, so the classical result lifts to the sphere. The frontier characterization $J = \operatorname{Fr} U = \operatorname{Fr} V$ strengthens the separation statement by identifying the $1$-sphere as the common boundary of the two complementary regions.
