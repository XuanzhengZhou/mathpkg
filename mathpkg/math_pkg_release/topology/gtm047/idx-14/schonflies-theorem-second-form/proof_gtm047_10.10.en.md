Identify $\mathbf{R}^2$ with $S^2 - P_0$ for a point $P_0$ not on $J$. Then $J$ is a $1$-sphere in the $2$-sphere $S^2$, and the given homeomorphism $h: J \to J'$ is a homeomorphism between $1$-spheres in $S^2$. By Theorem 3, $h$ extends to a homeomorphism $H: S^2 \leftrightarrow S^2$. Since $H$ can be chosen to fix $P_0$ (or by composing with a suitable rotation of $S^2$), $H$ restricts to a homeomorphism of $\mathbf{R}^2 = S^2 - P_0$ onto $\mathbf{R}^2 = S^2 - H(P_0)$, which equals $\mathbf{R}^2$ after a suitable adjustment.
