This second form of the Schönflies theorem states that any homeomorphism defined on a $1$-sphere in the plane can be extended to a self-homeomorphism of the entire plane. It is a direct consequence of the corresponding theorem for $2$-spheres (Theorem 3), obtained by identifying $\mathbf{R}^2$ with $S^2$ minus a point. This formulation provides the key step toward proving that every $1$-sphere in $\mathbf{R}^2$ is tame.
