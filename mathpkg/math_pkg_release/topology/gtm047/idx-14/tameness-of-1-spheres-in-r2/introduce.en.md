This theorem states that the tameness question for $1$-spheres in the plane is trivial: every $1$-sphere in $\mathbf{R}^2$ is tamely imbedded. It follows immediately from the second form of the Schönflies theorem (Theorem 4), which provides the required homeomorphism of $\mathbf{R}^2$ that sends the given $1$-sphere to a polygonal (hence polyhedral) simple closed curve. In contrast, $1$-spheres in $\mathbf{R}^3$ may be wild.
