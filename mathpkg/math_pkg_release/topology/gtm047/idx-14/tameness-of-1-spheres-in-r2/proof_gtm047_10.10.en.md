Let $J$ be a $1$-sphere in $\mathbf{R}^2$. Take a standard polygonal $1$-sphere $P \subset \mathbf{R}^2$ (for instance, a triangle). There exists a homeomorphism $f: J \leftrightarrow P$. By Theorem 4 (the Schönflies theorem, second form), $f$ extends to a homeomorphism $h: \mathbf{R}^2 \leftrightarrow \mathbf{R}^2$. Then $h(J) = P$ is a polyhedron, so by definition $J$ is tame.
