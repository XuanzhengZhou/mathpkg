A coordinate mapping $h \in C(X)$ is defined on a Euclidean simplex $\sigma_h$. For any face $\tau$ of the domain simplex $\sigma_h$, the restriction $h|_\tau$ is called a face of $h$. The image $S = h(\tau)$ is then said to form a face of the geometric realization $|h|$. This definition extends the combinatorial face structure from the domain simplex to the PL mapping, and is essential for stating the face-closure condition (K.1) in the definition of a PL complex.
