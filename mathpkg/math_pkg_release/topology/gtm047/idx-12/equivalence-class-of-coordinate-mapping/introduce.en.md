For each coordinate mapping $h \in C(X)$, its equivalence class $[h]$ under the relation $\sim$ is defined as the set of all coordinate mappings equivalent to $h$. These equivalence classes are precisely the PL simplexes — they are the fundamental building blocks of PL complexes, analogous to how Euclidean simplexes are the building blocks of Euclidean complexes. The notation $|[h]|$ denotes the common image set of all members of the equivalence class, which is well-defined since equivalent coordinate mappings have the same image.
