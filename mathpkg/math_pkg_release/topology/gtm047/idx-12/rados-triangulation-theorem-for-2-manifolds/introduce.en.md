Radó's triangulation theorem states that every 2-manifold (topological surface) admits a triangulation — it is homeomorphic to the geometric realization of some Euclidean complex, or equivalently, it can be given the structure of a PL complex. This is a fundamental result in geometric topology, establishing that topological surfaces can be studied using combinatorial and PL methods. The proof uses a recursive construction: starting from a countable cover by Euclidean ball pairs and using the subdivision theorem to inductively build a PL complex that exhausts the entire manifold while remaining a combinatorial 2-manifold with boundary at each finite stage.
