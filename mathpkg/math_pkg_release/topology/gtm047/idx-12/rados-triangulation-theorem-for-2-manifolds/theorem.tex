Every 2-manifold is triangulable. That is, for every 2-manifold $M$:

(1) There is a (Euclidean) complex $K$ such that $M$ and $|K|$ are homeomorphic.

(2) There is a PL complex $\mathcal{K}$ in $M$ such that $|\mathcal{K}| = M$.

By Theorem 7.5, (2) $\Rightarrow$ (1), and the converse is obvious: given a homeomorphism $f: |K| \leftrightarrow M$, define $\mathcal{K}$ via the PL structure induced by $f$ as in Theorem 7.5.
