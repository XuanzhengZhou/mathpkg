This theorem ensures that the property of being a face is preserved under the equivalence relation $\sim$ on coordinate mappings. Given two equivalent coordinate mappings $g \sim h$, if a subset $S$ of their common image forms a face of $g$ (i.e., $S = g(\tau)$ for some face $\tau$ of $\sigma_g$), then $S$ also forms a face of $h$. This is a key consistency condition that allows the definition of faces for PL simplexes (equivalence classes $[h]$), which is required for the face-closure axiom (K.1) in the definition of a PL complex.
