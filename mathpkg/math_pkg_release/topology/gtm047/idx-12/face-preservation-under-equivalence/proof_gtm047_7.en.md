Given $S = g(\tau)$ where $\tau$ is a face of $\sigma_g$, let $\rho = h^{-1}(g(\tau)) = h^{-1}(S)$. Then $\rho$ is a face of $\sigma_h$, because $h^{-1} \circ g$ is a simplicial isomorphism from $\sigma_g$ to $\sigma_h$ (since $g \sim h$), and the image of a face under a simplicial isomorphism is a face. Thus $S = h(\rho)$, so $S$ forms a face of $h$.
