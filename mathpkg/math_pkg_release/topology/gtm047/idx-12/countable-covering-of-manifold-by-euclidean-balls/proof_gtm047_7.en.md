Since $M$ is locally Euclidean, for each point $P \in M$ there exist open sets $N_P, N_P'$ with $P \in N_P'$ and a homeomorphism $h_P: \overline{N}_P \leftrightarrow \overline{D}, \overline{N}_P' \leftrightarrow \overline{D}'$. Thus the collection $\{N_P'\}$ covers $M$.

Since $M$ is a separable metric space, there is a countable neighborhood system $\mathscr{N} = \{U_1, U_2, \dots\}$ such that the topology generated by $\mathscr{N}$ is the given topology of $M$. Thus for each point $Q \in M$ there exists a $U_i$ such that $Q \in U_i$ and $U_i$ lies in some set $N_P'$. Therefore some countable subset $\{N_i'\}$ of $\{N_P'\}$ covers $M$, which was to be proved.
