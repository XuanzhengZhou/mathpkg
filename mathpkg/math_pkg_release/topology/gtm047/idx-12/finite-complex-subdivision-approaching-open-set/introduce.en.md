This technical theorem provides a crucial tool for the proof of Radó's triangulation theorem. Given a finite complex $K$ and an open subset $U$ of its geometric realization $|K|$, it constructs a complex $K_U$ whose underlying space equals $U$ and whose simplexes are rectilinear subsimplexes of simplexes of $K$. The construction uses a sequence of subdivisions with mesh tending to zero and recursively builds an expanding family of subcomplexes $L_i$ that exhaust $U$. This theorem is used in the inductive step of the triangulation proof to incorporate new chart neighborhoods into an existing PL structure.
