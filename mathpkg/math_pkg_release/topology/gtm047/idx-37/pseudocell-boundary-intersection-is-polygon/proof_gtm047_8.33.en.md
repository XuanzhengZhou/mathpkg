The components of $E \cap \operatorname{Bd} X$ appear in $E$ in topologically the same way as concentric circles in a circular disk. Therefore, if some set $E \cap \operatorname{Bd} X$ contains more than one polygon, it must contain two polygons $J_1$ and $J_2$ such that $J_1 \cup J_2$ is the boundary of an annulus $A$, with $\operatorname{Int} A \subset E - X$. We then split $A \cup \operatorname{Bd} X$ apart at $A$, leaving $E$ fixed. (This operation replaces $X$ by a larger set.) This reduces the total number of components of all sets $E \cap \operatorname{Bd} X$. Therefore, in a finite number of such steps, we get an $X$ which satisfies the required condition.
