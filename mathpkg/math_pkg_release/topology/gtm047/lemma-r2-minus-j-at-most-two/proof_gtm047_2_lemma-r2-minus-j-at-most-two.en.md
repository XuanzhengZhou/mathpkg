Starting at any point $P$ of $N - J$, we can work our way around the polygon, along a path in $N - J$, until we get to either $P_1$ or $P_2$. From this the lemma follows, because every point $Q$ of $\mathbb{R}^2 - J$ can be joined to some point $P$ of $N - J$ by a linear segment in $\mathbb{R}^2 - J$.
