Let $g: \tilde{M} \rightarrow M$ be a covering, let $P_0 \in M$, and let $\tilde{P}_0 \in g^{-1}(P_0)$. Define $g_0^*: \pi(\tilde{M}, \tilde{P}_0) \rightarrow \pi(M, P_0)$ by
$$g_0^*(\overline{\tilde{p}}) = \overline{g \circ \tilde{p}}.$$
The subgroup $\pi_0 = g_0^*(\pi(\tilde{M}, \tilde{P}_0)) \subset \pi(M, P_0)$ is called the \emph{group associated with $g$ and $P_0$}.
