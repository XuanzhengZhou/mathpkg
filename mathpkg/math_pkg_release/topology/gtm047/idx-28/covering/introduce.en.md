A covering (or covering space) is a surjective mapping $g: \tilde{M} \to M$ between connected spaces where $M$ is a polyhedron, such that every point $P \in M$ has an open neighborhood $U$ whose preimage $g^{-1}(U)$ decomposes as a disjoint union of open sets $\tilde{U}_i$, each mapped homeomorphically onto $U$ by $g$. The preimage sets $g^{-1}(P)$ are required to be at most countable with no limit points. Covering spaces are fundamental objects in algebraic topology, providing a way to "unfold" the fundamental group of a space.
