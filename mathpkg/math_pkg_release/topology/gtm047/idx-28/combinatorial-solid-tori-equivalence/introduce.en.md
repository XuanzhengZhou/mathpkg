Any two combinatorial solid tori (CSTs) are combinatorially equivalent; that is, there is a piecewise-linear homeomorphism between them. This theorem establishes that the CST is a uniquely determined combinatorial object. The proof uses the cylindrical diagram apparatus from Theorem 9, which represents a CST as a union of combinatorial 3-cells meeting face-to-face along 2-cells, providing a canonical decomposition that makes the combinatorial equivalence straightforward.
