**Proof.** Use the apparatus of the preceding proof (of Theorem 9). Given two combinatorial solid tori $S_1$ and $S_2$, each has a cylindrical diagram $\phi_i: \sigma^2 \times [0,1] \to S_i$. The decompositions into sequences of combinatorial 3-cells given by these diagrams provide the combinatorial structures, and since any two such decompositions with the same gluing pattern are combinatorially equivalent, the result follows.
