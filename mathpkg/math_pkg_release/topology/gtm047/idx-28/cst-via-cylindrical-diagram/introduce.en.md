In a triangulated 3-manifold, a polyhedral solid torus $S$ is a combinatorial solid torus (CST) if and only if there exists a piecewise-linear mapping $\phi: \sigma^2 \times [0,1] \to S$ from a prism such that the two ends $\sigma^2 \times \{0\}$ and $\sigma^2 \times \{1\}$ are mapped onto the same 2-cell in $S$, and $\phi$ is a homeomorphism elsewhere. Such a mapping $\phi$ provides a "cylindrical diagram" that decomposes $S$ into a sequence of combinatorial 3-cells meeting face-to-face, effectively representing $S$ as a twisted product of a 2-cell with a circle.
