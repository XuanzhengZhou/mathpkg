**Proof.** Under the conditions of Theorem 2, let $p \in \text{CP}(M, P_0)$. By Theorem 1, $p$ can always be lifted so as to give a path $\tilde{p}$, with initial point $\tilde{P}_0 = \tilde{p}(0)$. If $\overline{p} \in \pi_0 = g_0^*(\pi(\tilde{M}, \tilde{P}_0))$, then by definition there exists a closed path $\tilde{q} \in \text{CP}(\tilde{M}, \tilde{P}_0)$ such that $g_0^*(\overline{\tilde{q}}) = \overline{g \circ \tilde{q}} = \overline{p}$. By the uniqueness of path lifting (Theorem 1), the lift $\tilde{p}$ of $p$ starting at $\tilde{P}_0$ must coincide with $\tilde{q}$, hence $\tilde{p}(1) = \tilde{P}_0$, so $\tilde{p} \in \text{CP}(\tilde{M}, \tilde{P}_0)$. Conversely, if $p$ has a lifting $\tilde{p} \in \text{CP}(\tilde{M}, \tilde{P}_0)$, then $\overline{p} = \overline{g \circ \tilde{p}} = g_0^*(\overline{\tilde{p}}) \in \pi_0$.
