The proof is by Theorem 33.1. Conditions (2)–(5) hold whenever $f_1$ is a sufficiently close approximation of $h|_N$. Note that in Lemma 1, $N$ can be chosen so as to lie in any given neighborhood of $K^1$, and so $N$ and $f_1$ can be chosen so that $N''$ lies in any given neighborhood of $h(K^1)$.

By Theorem 33.1, given the homeomorphism $h$ extended to $U$, there exists a PLH $f_1$ defined on a regular neighborhood $N$ of $K^1$ such that $f_1$ is an $(\varepsilon/3)$-approximation of $h|_N$. Since the conditions (2)–(4) are open conditions (they involve nonempty intersection being preserved or avoided), they hold automatically whenever $f_1$ is sufficiently close to $h|_N$. Condition (1) follows because $N'' = f_1(N)$ is a neighborhood of $f_1(K^1)$, and $f_1(K^1)$ is close to $h(K^1)$, so $N''$ is a neighborhood of $h(K^1)$ when $N$ is chosen sufficiently small and $f_1$ sufficiently close.
