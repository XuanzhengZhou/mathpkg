This theorem is a central result in the piecewise linear topology of 3-manifolds, establishing that any homeomorphism from a polyhedral 3-cell into $\mathbb{R}^3$ can be approximated arbitrarily closely by a piecewise linear homeomorphism (PLH). The result is a 3-dimensional version of the approximation theorems that underpin the triangulation and Hauptvermutung theory for 3-manifolds. The proof proceeds by constructing a regular neighborhood of the 1-skeleton, applying a prior theorem (33.1) to get a PLH on that neighborhood, and then inductively extending the PLH to the entire 3-cell using properties of solid tori and polyhedral disk splittings.
