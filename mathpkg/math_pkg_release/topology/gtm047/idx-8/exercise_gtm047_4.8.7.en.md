Let $D$ be a polyhedral disk in $\mathbf{R}^2$, let $P, Q \in \operatorname{Bd} D$, and let $B_1$ and $B_2$ be broken lines $PQ$ such that $\operatorname{Bd} D = B_1 \cup B_2$. Let $M$ be a set which is the union of a finite collection of disjoint broken lines, each of which joins two points of $\operatorname{Int} B_1$ or two points of $\operatorname{Int} B_2$. Then $P$ and $Q$ lie in the same component of $D - M$.
