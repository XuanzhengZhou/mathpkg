Suppose that $e$ lies in only one $\sigma^2 \in K$; and let $v$ and $U$ be as in the proof of Lemma 2. Then $\sigma^2$ contains a semicircle which lies in $U$ and separates $U$, and this is impossible, because no arc separates $\mathbf{R}^2$.
