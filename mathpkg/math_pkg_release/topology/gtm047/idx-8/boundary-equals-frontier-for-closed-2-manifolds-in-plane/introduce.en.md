This theorem establishes that for a closed 2-manifold with boundary embedded in the plane, the manifold boundary $\operatorname{Bd} M$ coincides with the topological frontier $\operatorname{Fr} M$ of $M$ in $\mathbf{R}^2$. The proof uses Invariance of Domain to show that frontier points cannot have locally Euclidean neighborhoods. When $M$ is realized as the polyhedron of a complex $K$, this implies $\operatorname{Fr} M = |\partial K|$, connecting the combinatorial and topological notions of boundary.
