This theorem states that for any 1-sphere $J$ (a topological circle) in the plane, the frontier of any component $U$ of its complement is exactly the entire 1-sphere $J$. In other words, every point of $J$ is a limit point of $U$, so the 1-sphere cannot be partitioned among frontiers of different components. This is a fundamental consequence of the Jordan Curve Theorem and is used to establish properties of triangulated 2-manifolds in the plane.
