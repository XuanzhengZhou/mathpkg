This theorem states that in any triangulation of a 2-manifold without boundary, every edge must belong to at least one 2-simplex. The proof follows by contradiction: if an edge belonged to no 2-simplex, an interior point of that edge would have a neighborhood homeomorphic to a line, contradicting the fact that every point of a 2-manifold has a neighborhood homeomorphic to the plane.
