An abstract complex is a collection $\Phi$ of nonempty finite sets satisfying:
(1) If $\phi \in \Phi$, and $\phi'$ is a nonempty subset of $\phi$, then $\phi' \in \Phi$.
(2) Every element of $\Phi$ intersects only a finite number of elements of $\Phi$.
(3) The union of the elements of $\Phi$ is countable.

The elements of $\Phi$ are called (abstract) simplexes.
