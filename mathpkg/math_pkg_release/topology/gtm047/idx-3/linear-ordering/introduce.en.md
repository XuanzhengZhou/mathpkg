A linear ordering (also called a total ordering) is an irreflexive, transitive, and trichotomous binary relation on a set. This structure generalizes the usual ordering of the real numbers and allows one to define open intervals and the order topology on any linearly ordered set, extending many connectivity results from the real line to more general ordered spaces.
