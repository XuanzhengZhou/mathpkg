**Theorem 6.** Let $\mathcal{K}_1$ and $\mathcal{K}_2$ be PL complexes, in the same space $[X, \emptyset]$. Suppose that if $[g] \in \mathcal{K}_1$, $[h] \in \mathcal{K}_2$, and $S = |[g]| \cap |[h]| \neq \varnothing$, then there are faces $\tau_g, \tau_h$ of $\sigma_g$ and $\sigma_h$ respectively, such that $g(\tau_g) = h(\tau_h) = S$ and $[g \mid \tau_g] = [h \mid \tau_h]$. Then $\mathcal{K}_1 \cup \mathcal{K}_2$ is a PL complex.
