The barycenter $v$ of $\sigma^n$ is the point all of whose barycentric coordinates are equal ($= 1/(n+1)$). The (first) barycentric subdivision $bK$ of a complex $K$ is defined inductively: $bK^0 = K^0$, and $bK^{i+1}$ is the union of $bK^i$ and the set of all joins $v\sigma^i$, where $v$ is the barycenter of a simplex $\sigma^{i+1}$ of $K$ and $\sigma^i \in bK^i$, $\sigma^i \subset \sigma^{i+1}$.
