Let $C^2$ be a face of $\mathcal{C}$. Since $C^2$ is homeomorphic to the interior of a $2$-simplex, its closure $\overline{C^2}$ is a finite polyhedron. The frontier $\operatorname{Fr} C^2 = \overline{C^2} - C^2$ is a $1$-dimensional subcomplex. By condition (3) of the definition of an open cell-complex, $\operatorname{Fr} C^2$ is a union of edges and vertices of $\mathcal{C}$. In a $2$-simplex, the boundary is a simple closed curve formed by three edges and three vertices, which is connected. Even after identifications, the frontier remains the continuous image of a connected set, hence connected.
