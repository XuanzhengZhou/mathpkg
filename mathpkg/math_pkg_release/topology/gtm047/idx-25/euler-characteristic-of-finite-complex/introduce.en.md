The Euler characteristic $\chi(K) = V - E + F$ is a fundamental combinatorial invariant for finite $2$-dimensional complexes. It counts vertices, edges, and $2$-faces with alternating signs. Although initially defined combinatorially, the Euler characteristic is invariant under subdivision and, for $2$-manifolds, is a topological invariant.
