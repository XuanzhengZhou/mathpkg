An open cell-complex generalizes the notion of a simplicial complex by decomposing a polyhedron into disjoint open cells --- points, open $1$-cells (edges), and open $2$-cells (faces). Each cell is homeomorphic to the interior of a Euclidean simplex, their closures are finite polyhedra, and the frontier of each cell is a union of lower-dimensional cells. This concept provides a flexible framework for computing Euler characteristics without requiring a full triangulation.
