By Theorem 4 there is a homeomorphism $h: \mathbf{R}^2 \leftrightarrow \mathbf{R}^2$, mapping $\operatorname{Bd} C^2$ onto the unit circle $\mathbf{S}^1$. Now $h(\operatorname{Int} C^2) = \operatorname{Int} h(C^2)$, and the interior of $h(\operatorname{Bd} C^2)$ (that is, the bounded component of $\mathbf{R}^2 - h(\operatorname{Bd} C^2)$) is the image of the interior of $\operatorname{Bd} C^2$. Thus Theorem 12 is a consequence of Theorem 11.
