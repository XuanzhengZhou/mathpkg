Let $K$ be a linear graph with no end-points, and let $f$ be a homeomorphism $|K| \leftrightarrow M \subset \mathbf{R}^2$. Then $M$ is tame. In fact, for every open set $U$ containing $M$ and every strongly positive function $\phi: U \to \mathbf{R}$, there is a homeomorphism $h: \mathbf{R}^2 \leftrightarrow \mathbf{R}^2$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $h(M)$ is a polyhedron,
\item $h | (\mathbf{R}^2 - U)$ is the identity, and
\item $h | U$ is a $\phi$-approximation of the identity.
\end{enumerate}
