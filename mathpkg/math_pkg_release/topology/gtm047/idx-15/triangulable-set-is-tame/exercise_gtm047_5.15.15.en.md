In a topological space, a set $M$ is \textit{locally compact} if every point of $M$ has a (closed) neighborhood $N$ such that $N \cap M$ is compact. Let $M$ be a locally compact set in $\mathbf{R}^2$, and suppose that $M \cap \operatorname{Fr} M$ is a polyhedron. Then $M$ is a polyhedron.
