Let $N_v$ be a convex polyhedral 2-cell neighborhood of a vertex $v$ of $M$, with $\operatorname{Bd} N_v = J_v$. Let $w_1, w_2, \ldots, w_n$ be the points at which edges of $M$ intersect $J_v$, and let $e'_j$ be the arcs into which the points $w_i$ decompose $J_v$. For each arc $a_i$ of $\operatorname{Int} N_v \cap M$ with endpoints $w_j, w_k$, define
$$J_i = a_i \cup e'_j \cup e'_k$$
which is a $1$-sphere with interior $I_i$ lying in $N_v$. Then the sets $I_i$ are pairwise disjoint.
