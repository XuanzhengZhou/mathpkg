Let $K$ be a complex and let $f: |K| \hookrightarrow \mathbf{R}^2$ be a homeomorphism. Then there exists a complex $K'$ and a homeomorphism $f': |K'| \hookrightarrow \mathbf{R}^2$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is a subcomplex of $K'$,
\item $K'$ has no end-points, and
\item $f = f' \big| |K|$.
\end{enumerate}
