This lemma provides the bridge from Theorem 7 (which only applies to linear graphs without end-points) to the general case. By attaching half-open intervals to each end-point of $K$ and triangulating the result, we obtain a larger complex $K'$ without end-points. The homeomorphism $f'$ extends $f$ to $K'$. Since $K'$ has no end-points, Theorem 7 applies to $f'$, and by restriction we obtain the taming of $f(|K|)$.
