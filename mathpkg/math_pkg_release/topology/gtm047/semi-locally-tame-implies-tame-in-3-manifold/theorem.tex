**Theorem 3.** In $\mathbb{R}^3$, or in a PL 3-manifold $M$, every semi-locally tame set is tame. In fact, if $L$ is semi-locally tame, then for every open set $V$ containing $L$, and for every $\psi \gg 0$ on $V$, there is a homeomorphism $g': M \leftrightarrow M$ such that (1) $g'(L)$ is a polyhedron, (2) $g' \mid (M-V)$ is the identity, and (3) $g' \mid V$ is a $\psi$-approximation of the identity.
