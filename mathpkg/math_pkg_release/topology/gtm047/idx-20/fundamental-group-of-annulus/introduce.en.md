The fundamental group of an annulus is infinite cyclic. This is one of the simplest nontrivial fundamental group computations. The proof uses the polyhedral structure of the annulus as a subset of $\mathbb{R}^2$: a single generator $g_1$ (a loop going around the hole) suffices to generate the group, and there are no relations because moving a path across a triangle only inserts or deletes canceling pairs of the form $g_1^{\pm 1} g_1^{\mp 1}$.
