Let $S$ be a polyhedral 2-sphere in $\mathbf{R}^3$, let $I$ be the bounded component of $\mathbf{R}^3 - S$, and let $A$ be a linear segment, lying in $I$, except for its end-points, which lie in $S$. Prove or disprove: there is a homeomorphism $h: \mathbf{R}^3 \leftrightarrow \mathbf{R}^3$, such that $h(S)$ is the surface of the unit ball $\mathbf{B}^3$ and $h(A)$ is a linear segment joining two antipodal points of $h(S)$.
