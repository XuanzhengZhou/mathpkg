When the alphabet $A$ has finite cardinality $n$, the free group $F(A)$ is called a free group on $n$ generators. The equivalence classes $[a]$ of the individual alphabet elements $a \in A$ provide a natural generating set. This finite-rank free group is the fundamental object appearing in knot theory: the group of an $n$-component link is presented as a quotient of a free group on $n$ generators.
