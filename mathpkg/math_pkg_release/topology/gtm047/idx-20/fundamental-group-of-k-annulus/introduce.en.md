The $k$-annulus (a disk with $k$ holes) has fundamental group that is free on $k$ generators. Each generator corresponds to a loop going around one of the $k$ inner boundary components. The proof uses the Tame Imbedding Theorem to arrange the annulus in $\mathbb{R}^2$ so that the inner boundary components are connected to the outer boundary by disjoint linear intervals, each giving a generator. There are no relations because paths between different holes do not interact.
