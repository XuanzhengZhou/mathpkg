A word is a finite sequence of syllables over a given alphabet $A$. The collection of all such words, together with the empty word $e$, forms the set $W(A)$. Words are multiplied by concatenating their syllable sequences end to end, giving $W(A)$ the structure of a monoid with identity $e$. The free group $F(A)$ will be obtained as equivalence classes of words under certain elementary operations.
