A normal system $[M, K, D, K(\Delta), B, N]$ consists of a triangulated 3-manifold $M$, a triangulation $K$, a PL singular 2-cell $D: \Delta \to M$, a triangulation $K(\Delta)$ of $\Delta$, a subcomplex $B$ of $K$ forming a neighborhood of $|\operatorname{Bd} D|$ in $\operatorname{Bd} M$, and a normal subgroup $N$ of $\pi(B, P_0)$ such that $L(B) \cap N = \varnothing$.
