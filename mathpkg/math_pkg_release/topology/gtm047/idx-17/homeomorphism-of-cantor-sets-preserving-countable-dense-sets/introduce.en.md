Theorem 9 strengthens Theorem 8 by showing that the homeomorphism between two Cantor sets can be chosen to map a prescribed countable dense subset of the first onto a prescribed countable dense subset of the second. This is a refinement of the proof of Theorem 8, where the inductive construction of the bijections between coverings is modified with additional provisos to ensure the limit homeomorphism sends each point of $D$ to a corresponding point of $D'$ and is surjective onto $D'$.
