By a brick-decomposition of the plane we mean a collection $G = \{g_i\}$ of polyhedral disks ($= 2$-cells) such that (1) $\bigcup g_i = \mathbb{R}^2$, (2) if two sets $g_i$ and $g_j$ intersect, then their intersection is a broken line lying in the frontier of each, and (3) every point has a neighborhood which intersects at most three of the sets $g_i$.
