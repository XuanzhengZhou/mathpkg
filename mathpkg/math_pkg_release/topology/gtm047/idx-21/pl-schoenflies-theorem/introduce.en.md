The PL Schönflies theorem states that every polyhedral 2-sphere $S$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$ is simply imbedded: there exists a piecewise-linear homeomorphism of $\mathbb{R}^3$ sending $S$ onto the boundary of a 3-simplex, acting as the identity outside any prescribed convex open neighborhood of $S$. This is the piecewise-linear analogue of Alexander's theorem that every polyhedral 2-sphere in $\mathbb{R}^3$ bounds a 3-cell, and strengthens it by showing the 3-cell is combinatorial. The proof proceeds by induction on a combinatorial index.
