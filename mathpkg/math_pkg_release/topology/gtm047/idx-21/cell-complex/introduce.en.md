A cell-complex is a finite collection of topological cells satisfying three conditions: distinct cells have disjoint interiors, the boundary of each cell is a union of cells from the complex, and the intersection of any two overlapping cells is itself a cell that decomposes into elements of the complex. The cell-complex provides a combinatorial decomposition of a space $|K|$ into cells, analogous to simplicial complexes but allowing more general building blocks.
