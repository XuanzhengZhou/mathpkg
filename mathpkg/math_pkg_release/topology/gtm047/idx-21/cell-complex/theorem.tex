A **cell-complex** is a finite collection $K$ of topological cells, such that:

(1) different elements of $K$ have disjoint interiors;

(2) for each $C \in K$, $\operatorname{Bd} C$ is a union of elements of $K$;

(3) if $C, C' \in K$ and $C \cap C' \neq \emptyset$, then $C \cap C'$ is a cell, and is a union of elements of $K$.

The union of all elements of $K$ is denoted by $|K|$, and $K$ is called a **cell-decomposition** of $|K|$.
