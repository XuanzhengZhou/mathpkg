An open set $U$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$ is locally simply connected at a boundary point $Q$ if every neighborhood of $Q$ contains a smaller neighborhood $W$ such that every closed path in $W \cap U$ can be contracted to a point within $V \cap U$. This property characterizes tame embeddings: the complement of a tame arc or tame 2-sphere is locally simply connected at each point of the arc or sphere, while wild embeddings fail to have this property.
