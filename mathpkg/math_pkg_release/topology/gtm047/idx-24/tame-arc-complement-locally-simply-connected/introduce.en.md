For a tame arc $A$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$, the complement $\mathbb{R}^3 - A$ is locally simply connected at every point $Q$ of the boundary of $A$. The same holds for tame 2-spheres: each component of the complement of a tame 2-sphere $S$ is locally simply connected at every point of $S$. This property holds trivially for polyhedral arcs and 2-spheres and is preserved by homeomorphisms of $\mathbb{R}^3$, making it a topological invariant that can detect wildness.
