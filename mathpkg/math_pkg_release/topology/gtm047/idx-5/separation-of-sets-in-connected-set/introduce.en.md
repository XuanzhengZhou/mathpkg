This definition generalizes the separation of points to the separation of arbitrary disjoint subsets $H_1$ and $H_2$. A subset $D$ of a connected set $C$ separates $H_1$ from $H_2$ if removing $D$ disconnects $C$ in such a way that $H_1$ and $H_2$ lie in different separated components of $C - D$. This concept is essential for describing how curves and surfaces partition connected spaces in geometric topology.
