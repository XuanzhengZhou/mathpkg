In a connected set $C$, a subset $D$ separates two points $P$ and $Q$ if the removal of $D$ splits $C$ into two separated pieces, each containing one of the points. This formalizes the intuitive notion of a "cut" disconnecting two specific locations within a connected space, and is a fundamental concept used throughout the study of planar separation by polygons and other curves.
