Let $E_{13}$ be the exterior of $B_1 \cup B_3$. Then the bounded components of $\mathbf{R}^2 - M = \mathbf{R}^2 - \bigcup_r B_r$ lie in $I_{13}$, and each of them has a polygon in $\bigcup_r B_r$ as its frontier. Again consider a small circular neighborhood of $P$. (See Figure 2.8.) The circular sectors $A_1$ and $A_2$ lie in different components of $\mathbf{R}^2 - \bigcup_r B_r$, because they lie in different components of the larger set $\mathbf{R}^2 - (B_1 \cup B_2)$. Therefore no bounded component $U$ of $\mathbf{R}^2 - \bigcup_r B_r$ has $B_1 \cup B_3$ as its frontier.

Thus the remaining possibilities are $\operatorname{Fr} U = B_1 \cup B_2$ and $\operatorname{Fr} U = B_2 \cup B_3$. These give the bounded components $I_{12}$ and $I_{23}$, so that (1) holds.

We now have
$$I_{13} = I_{12} \cup \operatorname{Int} B_2 \cup I_{23}$$
and
$$\overline{I}_{13} = \overline{I}_{12} \cup \overline{I}_{23},$$
so that (2) holds. From this we easily get (3). (See Theorem 1.15.)
