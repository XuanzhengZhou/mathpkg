This lemma is the crucial final step in the proof that a topological 1-sphere separates the plane. It shows that the point $Z$, located on the broken line $B$ between the last intersection with $A_1$ and the first intersection with $A_2$, must lie in a bounded component of $\mathbb{R}^2 - J$. The proof proceeds by contradiction, considering two cases depending on which side of the frontier $W$ lies, each leading to a contradiction with the preceding separation theorem.
