The diameter of a set in a metric space is the supremum of distances between any two points in the set. It provides a measure of the "size" of the set and generalizes the usual notion of diameter from Euclidean geometry. A set is bounded precisely when its diameter is finite. This concept is fundamental in metric topology and is used here to define the mesh of a brick-decomposition.
