Suppose not. Let $B_1$ be the arc $ST$ in $B$; let $B_2$ be the arc $TX$ in $A_1$; and let $B_3$ be the arc $XQ$ in $B$. Then $B_1 \cup B_2 \cup B_3$ is an arc $SQ$, in $\bar{I} - A_2$. Therefore $S$ and $Q$ lie in the frontier of the same component of $I - A_2$; and this contradicts the preceding theorem.
