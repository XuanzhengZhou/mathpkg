A brick-decomposition of the plane is a tiling by polyhedral 2-cells (disks) satisfying three conditions: the tiles cover the entire plane, any intersection of two tiles is a broken line contained in both frontiers, and every point has a neighborhood meeting at most three tiles. This decomposition is used in geometric topology to construct controlled neighborhoods and 2-manifolds with boundary. One concrete example is the "infinite brick wall" formed by cutting the plane with horizontal lines and vertical segments.
