By a brick-decomposition of the plane we mean a collection $G = \{g_i\}$ of polyhedral disks ($=2$-cells) such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\bigcup_{i=1}^{\infty} g_i = \mathbf{R}^2$,
\item if two sets $g_i$ and $g_j$ intersect, then their intersection is a broken line lying in the frontier of each of them, and
\item every point has a neighborhood which intersects at most three of the sets $g_i$.
\end{enumerate}
