The mesh of a collection of subsets of a metric space is the supremum of the diameters of its members. It measures the maximum "size" of sets in the collection. A collection with small mesh consists of sets that are all small in diameter. This concept is essential when constructing brick-decompositions of the plane with arbitrarily fine control, as needed in proofs that rely on the brick-decomposition technique.
