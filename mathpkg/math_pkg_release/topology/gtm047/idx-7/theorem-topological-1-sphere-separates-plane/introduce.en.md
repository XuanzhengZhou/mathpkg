This theorem states that any topological 1-sphere (a continuous image of the standard circle) embedded in the plane separates the plane into at least two components. It is a crucial step in the proof of the full Jordan Curve Theorem, showing that the complement of such a curve cannot be connected. The proof constructs arcs and uses the preceding theorem about 2-cells and their frontiers to derive a contradiction from the assumption of connectedness.
