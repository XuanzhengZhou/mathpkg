This theorem describes a fundamental configuration in the plane: given a polyhedral 2-cell with four distinguished frontier points and two disjoint arcs each connecting one of these points to the interior, the remaining two frontier points must lie in the frontier of the same complementary component. The proof uses a brick-decomposition of the plane to construct a separating 1-sphere from the frontier of a neighborhood of one arc, then analyzes how the broken line $B$ interacts with this structure.
