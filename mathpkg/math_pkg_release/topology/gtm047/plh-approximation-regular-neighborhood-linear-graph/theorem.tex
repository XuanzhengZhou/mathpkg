**Theorem 33.1.** Let $K$ be a finite linear graph with no end-points, let $N$ be a small regular neighborhood of $K$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$, and let $h: N \to \mathbb{R}^3$ be a homeomorphism. Then there is a PLH $f: N \to \mathbb{R}^3$ which is an $\varepsilon$-approximation of $h$, for any prescribed $\varepsilon > 0$.
