A nonsingular loop is a loop $L: S^1 \to X$ in a topological space $X$ that is a homeomorphism onto its image. Equivalently, it is an embedded circle in $X$, also known as a simple closed curve. In the context of the loop theorem for 3-manifolds, the goal is to replace a singular loop that is null-homotopic in the manifold but essential in the boundary with a nonsingular loop having the same properties.
