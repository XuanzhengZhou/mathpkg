A singular 2-cell in a topological space $X$ is a PL mapping $D: \Delta \to X$ from a polyhedral 2-cell $\Delta$ into $X$. This is the 2-dimensional analogue of a singular simplex and serves as the domain for the maps whose boundaries are loops considered in the loop theorem. In the Stallings proof, the goal is to show that a singular 2-cell with certain properties can be replaced by a nonsingular one.
