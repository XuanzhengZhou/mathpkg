Let $K$ be an orientable triangulated 3-manifold with boundary, and let $M = |K|$. Let $B$ be a component of $\operatorname{Bd} M$. Suppose there is a loop $L$ in $B$ that is null-homotopic in $M$ but not null-homotopic in $B$. Then $L$ bounds a nonsingular PL 2-cell in $M$.

More generally, in the notation of Theorem 1 with $N = \{1\}$: if there is a PL singular 2-cell $D: \Delta \rightarrow M$ such that $L = \operatorname{Bd} D$ is a loop in $B$ and $L$ is essential in $B$, then there is a nonsingular PL 2-cell $D_1: \Delta \rightarrow M$ such that $L_1 = \operatorname{Bd} D_1$ is a loop in $B$ and $L_1$ is essential in $B$.
