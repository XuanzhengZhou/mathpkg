The loop theorem of Papakyriakopoulos is a foundational result in 3-manifold topology. It states that if a loop in the boundary of an orientable 3-manifold is null-homotopic in the interior but essential in the boundary, then it bounds an embedded (nonsingular) disk in the manifold. Together with Dehn's lemma, this theorem was instrumental in proving the sphere theorem and establishing key structural results for 3-manifolds. The theorem follows from the more general Stallings loop theorem (Theorem 1 of the section) by taking the normal subgroup $N$ to be trivial.
