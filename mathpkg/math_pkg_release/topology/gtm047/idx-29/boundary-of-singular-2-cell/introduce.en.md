The boundary of a singular 2-cell $D: \Delta \to X$ is defined as the restriction of $D$ to the boundary of the polyhedral 2-cell $\Delta$, denoted $\operatorname{Bd} D = D|_{\operatorname{Bd} \Delta}: \operatorname{Bd} \Delta \to X$. Since $\operatorname{Bd} \Delta$ is homeomorphic to $S^1$, the boundary $\operatorname{Bd} D$ is a loop in $X$. This construction connects singular 2-cells to the loops that appear as their boundaries, which is central to the statement and proof of the loop theorem.
