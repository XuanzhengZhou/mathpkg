This definition captures the geometric idea that a broken line is unknotted when it lies essentially as a straight segment within a polyhedral 3-cell. Specifically, the broken line's interior is contained in the interior of the cell, its endpoints lie on the boundary, and there exists a piecewise-linear homeomorphism sending the cell to a product $\sigma^2 \times [0,1]$ (where $\sigma^2$ is a 2-simplex) that maps the broken line onto a product of a point in the interior of the base simplex with the interval. This formalizes the intuition that all broken lines in $\mathbf{R}^3$ are tame.
