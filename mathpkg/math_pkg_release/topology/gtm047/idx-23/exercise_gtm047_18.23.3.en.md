Let $M$ be a compact set in $\mathbf{R}^2$. Prove or disprove: There is a 2-sphere $S$ in $\mathbf{R}^3$ such that $S \cap \mathbf{R}^2 = M$. (This suggests one of the difficulties in generalizing the result of Problem 2.)
