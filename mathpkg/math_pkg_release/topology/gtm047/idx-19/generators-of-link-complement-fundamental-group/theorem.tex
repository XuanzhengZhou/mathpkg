Let $L$ be a link in $\mathbf{R}^3$ with a regular projection onto the $xy$-plane, producing a diagram consisting of $n$ disjoint arcs $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n$. Choose a base point $P_0$ far above the link, separated from $L$ by a horizontal plane. For each arc $a_i$, choose a closed path $g_i$ which forms a geometric triangle looping around $a_i$ with a right-handed crossing. Then

$$\pi(\mathbf{R}^3 - L, P_0)$$

is generated by the homotopy classes $\{\bar{g}_1, \bar{g}_2, \ldots, \bar{g}_n\}$. That is, every $\bar{p} \in \pi(\mathbf{R}^3 - L, P_0)$ is equal to a product

$$\bar{p} = \prod_{i=1}^{m} \bar{g}_{j_i}^{\alpha_i} \quad (\alpha_i = \pm 1).$$
