Let $\phi$ be a function $X \rightarrow \mathbf{R}^+$. Suppose that $\phi$ is bounded away from 0 on every compact set, i.e., for every compact set $M$ there is an $\varepsilon_M > 0$ such that $\phi(P) \geqslant \varepsilon_M$ for each $P \in M$. Then $\phi$ is called strongly positive, and we write $\phi \gg 0$ on $X$.

Let $\phi \gg 0$ on $X$, and let $f$ and $g$ be mappings $X \rightarrow Y$. Suppose that for each $P \in X$, $d'(f(P), g(P)) < \phi(P)$. Then $g$ is a $\phi$-approximation of $f$.
