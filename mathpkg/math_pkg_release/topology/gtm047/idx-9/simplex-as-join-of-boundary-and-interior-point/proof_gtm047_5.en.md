For any $v \in \sigma^n - |\partial \sigma^n|$, $v$ is an interior point of $\sigma^n$. The join $J(|\partial \sigma^n|, v)$ consists of all segments from $v$ to points of the boundary $|\partial \sigma^n|$. Since $\sigma^n$ is a convex set and $v$ lies in its interior, every point $x \in \sigma^n$ lies on a ray from $v$ through some boundary point: the ray $v + t(x - v)$ for $t \geqslant 0$ intersects $|\partial \sigma^n|$ at exactly one point (when $t$ is maximal such that the point remains in $\sigma^n$). Thus $x$ lies on the segment from $v$ to that boundary point, proving $\sigma^n = J(|\partial \sigma^n|, v)$.
