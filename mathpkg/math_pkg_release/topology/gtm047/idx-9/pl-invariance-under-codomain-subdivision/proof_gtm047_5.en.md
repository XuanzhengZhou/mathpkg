Here "if" is trivial. To prove "only if," let $K_1$ be such that $K_1 < K$, and such that for each $\sigma \in K_1$, $f|\sigma$ maps $\sigma$ linearly into a simplex of $L$. Let $f(K_1)$ be the set of all images $f(\sigma)$ ($\sigma \in K_1$). These form a subdivision of the image complex in $L$, and since each $f|\sigma$ is linear on $\sigma$, $f$ is PL relative to $K$ and $L_1$. $\square$
