Let $K$ be a finite complex, of dimension $\leqslant 2$, and let $\mathcal{C}$ be a finite collection of subsets of $|K|$ such that: (1) the elements of $\mathcal{C}$ are disjoint, (2) each $\overline{C}$ is a finite polyhedron, (3) each $\operatorname{Fr} C$ is a union of elements of $\mathcal{C}$, and (4) each $C \in \mathcal{C}$ is either a point or homeomorphic to the interior of a Euclidean simplex. Then $\mathcal{C}$ is an open cell-complex.
