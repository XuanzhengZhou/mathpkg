Let $M$ be a Riemannian manifold with inner product $\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle$ on each tangent space. For a $C^1$ function $f: M \to \mathbb{R}$, the gradient of $f$, denoted $\operatorname{grad} f$, is the unique vector field on $M$ such that for all $x \in M$ and all $Y \in M_x$,
$$\langle \operatorname{grad} f(x), Y \rangle = T_x f(Y).$$
In local coordinates, $\operatorname{grad} f$ is the vector field dual to the differential $Df$ via the Riemannian metric.
