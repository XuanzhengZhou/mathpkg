The Jet Transversality Theorem (also known as the Thom Transversality Theorem) is a powerful generalization of ordinary transversality. Instead of requiring the map itself to be transverse to a submanifold, it asks that the $r$-jet prolongation $j^r f: M \to J^r(M,N)$ be transverse to a given submanifold $A$ of the jet space. Such maps form a residual set in the strong $C^s$ topology (for $s > r$). This theorem unifies and vastly generalizes results about immersions, submersions, and other singularity types.
