# Exercise 3.2.7

A $k$-fold point of a map $f: M \to N$ is the set of $x \in M$ such that there are $k$ distinct points $x = x_1, \ldots, x_k$ with $f(x_1) = \cdots = f(x_k)$. Let $M$ and $N$ be manifolds such that

$$\frac{k + 1}{k} < \frac{\dim N}{\dim M} \leqslant \frac{k}{k - 1}, \quad k \geqslant 2.$$
