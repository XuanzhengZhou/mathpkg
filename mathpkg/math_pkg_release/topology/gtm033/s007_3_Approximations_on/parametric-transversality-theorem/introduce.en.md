The Parametric Transversality Theorem states that if a family of maps $F: V \times M \to N$ parametrized by a manifold $V$ is transverse to a submanifold $A \subset N$, then "almost all" members $F_v$ of the family are transverse to $A$---the set of such $v$ is residual (hence dense) in $V$. The proof reduces to the Morse-Sard theorem applied to the projection of the preimage submanifold $F^{-1}(A)$. This theorem is the workhorse for proving that generic maps have specified properties.
