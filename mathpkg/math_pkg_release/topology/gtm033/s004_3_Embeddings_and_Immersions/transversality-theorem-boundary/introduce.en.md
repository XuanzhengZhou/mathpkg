Theorem 4.2 generalizes the transversality theorem to manifolds with boundary. If a $C^r$ map $f: M \to N$ and its restriction to $\partial M$ are both transverse to a submanifold $A \subset N$, then the preimage $f^{-1}(A)$ is a $C^r$ submanifold whose boundary is exactly the preimage of $\partial A$. The theorem requires $A$ to be either neat, or contained entirely in the interior or entirely in the boundary of $N$.
