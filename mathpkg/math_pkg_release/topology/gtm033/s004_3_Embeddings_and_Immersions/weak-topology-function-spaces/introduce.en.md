The weak topology on $C^r(M,N)$, also called the $C^r$ compact-open topology, controls the closeness of maps and their derivatives up to order $r$ only over compact subsets of the domain $M$. A neighborhood of $f$ consists of maps $g$ whose local coordinate representations and their derivatives are within $\varepsilon$ of those of $f$ on a specified compact set. For non-compact $M$, the weak topology does not control behavior at infinity well, motivating the strong topology.
