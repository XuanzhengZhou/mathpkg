# Exercise 7

A $C^1$ immersion $f: M \to N$ which is injective on a closed subset $K \subset M$ is injective on a neighborhood of $K$. In fact $f$ has a neighborhood $\mathcal{N} \subset C_S^1(M,N)$ and $K$ has a neighborhood $U \subset M$ such that every $g \in \mathcal{N}$ is injective on $U$. If $K$ is compact $\mathcal{N}$ can be taken in $C_W^1(M,N)$.
