A manifold with boundary generalizes the notion of a manifold by allowing charts into halfspaces of $\mathbb{R}^n$. The boundary $\partial M$ consists of points that map to the boundary of the halfspace, and it is itself a manifold of one lower dimension without boundary. The concepts of smooth maps, tangent bundles, immersions, submersions, diffeomorphisms, and embeddings extend naturally to $\partial$-manifolds.
