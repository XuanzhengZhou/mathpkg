The **strong topology** (also called the **fine** or **Whitney topology**) on $C^r(M,N)$ is generated by neighborhoods of the form $\mathcal{N}^r(f; \Phi, \Psi, K, \varepsilon)$ where:
- $\Phi = \{\varphi_i, U_i\}_{i \in \Lambda}$ is a locally finite atlas for $M$,
- $\Psi = \{\psi_i, V_i\}_{i \in \Lambda}$ is a family of charts for $N$,
- $K = \{K_i\}_{i \in \Lambda}$ is a family of compact sets with $K_i \subset U_i$ and $f(K_i) \subset V_i$,
- $\varepsilon = \{\varepsilon_i\}_{i \in \Lambda}$ is a family of positive numbers (possibly $\infty$).

A map $g$ belongs to $\mathcal{N}^r(f; \Phi, \Psi, K, \varepsilon)$ if $g(K_i) \subset V_i$ and
$$\| D^k(\psi_i \circ f \circ \varphi_i^{-1})(x) - D^k(\psi_i \circ g \circ \varphi_i^{-1})(x) \| < \varepsilon_i$$
for all $x \in \varphi_i(K_i)$, $k = 0, \ldots, r$, $i \in \Lambda$.

The resulting topological space is denoted $C^r_S(M,N)$. When $M$ is compact, the strong topology coincides with the weak topology.
