# Exercise 10

Every $C^2$ map $f: T^2 \to \mathbb{R}$ has at least 3 critical points. $[T^2 = S^1 \times S^1$ is the torus.]

If $f$ has only a maximum $p_+$ and a minimum $p_-$, let $U$ be a simply connected neighborhood of $p_-$. Let $\varphi_t: T^2 \to T^2$, $t \in \mathbb{R}$, be the gradient flow of $f$. Then one can show that $T^2 - p_+ = \bigcup_{t > 0} \varphi_t(U)$. This makes $T^2 - p_+$ simply connected.
