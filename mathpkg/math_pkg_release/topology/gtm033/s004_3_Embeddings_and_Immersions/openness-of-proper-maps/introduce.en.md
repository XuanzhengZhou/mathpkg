Theorem 1.5 states that proper maps form an open subset of $C^r_S(M,N)$. The proof uses the fact that for any map $f$ there exist a compact cover $\{K_i\}$ of $M$ and a locally finite open cover $\{V_i\}$ of $N$ with $f(K_i) \subset V_i$. If $f$ is proper, the $V_i$ can be chosen locally finite. Any $g$ sufficiently close to $f$ in the strong topology then satisfies $g(K_i) \subset V_i$, and a compactness argument shows $g$ is proper.
