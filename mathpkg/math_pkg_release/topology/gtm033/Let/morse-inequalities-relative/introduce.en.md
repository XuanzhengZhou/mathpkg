The Morse inequalities (Theorem 3.4) relate the type numbers $v_k$ (counts of critical points of each index) to the relative Betti numbers $\beta_k$ (dimensions of relative homology groups). The alternating sum of type numbers equals the homological Euler characteristic, while the partial alternating sums bound the corresponding partial sums of Betti numbers from below. Setting $m = 0$ gives $v_0 \geq \beta_0$, and more generally $v_k \geq \beta_k$ for all $k$.
