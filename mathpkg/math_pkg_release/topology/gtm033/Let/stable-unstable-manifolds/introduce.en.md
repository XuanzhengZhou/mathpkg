At each critical point of a Morse function, the negative gradient flow partitions nearby points into those that flow into the critical point forward in time (the stable manifold) and those that flow into it backward in time (the unstable manifold). The stable manifold has dimension $n - k$ and the unstable manifold has dimension $k$, where $k$ is the Morse index. These submanifolds intersect transversely at the critical point and provide a dynamical decomposition of the manifold that is dual to the cell decomposition of Morse theory.
