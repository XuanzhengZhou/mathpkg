The homological Euler characteristic of a pair $(X,A)$ is the alternating sum of the Betti numbers (the dimensions of the singular homology groups with field coefficients). For compact manifolds and compact submanifolds, this number is finite and independent of the choice of coefficient field. The celebrated Hopf theorem equates this homological Euler characteristic with the geometric Euler characteristic defined via indices of vector fields.
