A Morse function is a smooth real-valued function on a manifold whose critical points are all nondegenerate. Morse functions are generic (they form an open dense set in appropriate function spaces) and provide a powerful tool for analyzing the topology of manifolds through the study of critical points and their indices. The local structure near each critical point is completely described by Morse's Lemma.
