Let $M$ be a compact $\partial$-manifold. The \textbf{Euler characteristic} $\chi(M)$ is defined as follows. Choose a section $f \colon M \to TM$ which is transverse to the zero section $M \subset TM$ and such that $f$ points outward at all points of $\partial M$. Then set
$$
\chi(M) = \sum_{x \in f^{-1}(M)} \operatorname{Ind}_{x} f.
$$
Such an outward-pointing section always exists (e.g., obtained from a collar and extended by a partition of unity).
