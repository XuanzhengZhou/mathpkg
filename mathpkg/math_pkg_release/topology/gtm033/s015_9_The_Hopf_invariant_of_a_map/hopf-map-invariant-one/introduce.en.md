The Hopf map $\varphi(z,w) = [z,w]$ is the canonical projection from the unit 3-sphere in $\mathbb{C}^2$ to the complex projective line $\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^1 \cong S^2$. Its Hopf invariant equals 1, proving that $\varphi$ represents a nontrivial element of $\pi_3(S^2)$. This is historically the first example of a map between spheres of different dimensions that is not null homotopic, discovered by Heinz Hopf in 1931.
