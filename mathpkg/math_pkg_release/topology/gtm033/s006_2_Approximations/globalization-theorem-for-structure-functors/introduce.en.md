The Globalization Theorem (Theorem 2.11) formalizes the local-to-global passage that recurs throughout differential topology. Given a nontrivial, continuous, and locally extendable structure functor $(\mathcal{F}, \mathfrak{Y})$ on a set $X$, global sections always exist: $\mathcal{F}(X) \neq \emptyset$. Moreover, any local section on $Y_0$ extends to a global section. The proof uses Zorn's Lemma on the partially ordered set of pairs $(Y, a)$ with $a \in \mathcal{F}(Y)$ extending a fixed $a_0 \in \mathcal{F}(Y_0)$. This theorem underpins the construction of compatible $C^s$ structures (Theorem 2.9) and the density of immersions and embeddings.
