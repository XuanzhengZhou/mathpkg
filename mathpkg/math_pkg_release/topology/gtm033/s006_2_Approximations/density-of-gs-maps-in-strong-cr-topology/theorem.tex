Let $M$ and $N$ be $C^s$ manifolds, $1 \leqslant s \leqslant \infty$, with $\dim M < \infty$. Let $G^k(M,N) \subset C^k(M,N)$, $k \geqslant 1$, denote any one of the following subsets: diffeomorphisms, embeddings, closed embeddings, immersions, submersions, or proper maps. Then $G^s(M,N)$ is dense in $G^r(M,N)$ in the strong $C^r$ topology, $1 \leqslant r < s$. In particular, $M$ and $N$ are $C^s$ diffeomorphic if and only if they are $C^r$ diffeomorphic.
