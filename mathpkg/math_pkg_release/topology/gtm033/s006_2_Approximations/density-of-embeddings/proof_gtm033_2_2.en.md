# Proof of Theorem 2.13 (Density of embeddings)

Let $M, N$ be $C^r$ manifolds, $1 \leqslant r \leqslant \infty$, with $\dim N \geqslant 2 \dim M + 1$.

**Sketch of proof.** The structure functor method used for Theorem 2.12 (density of immersions) encounters a difficulty for embeddings: if $M$ is not compact, the structure functor defined by $A$-germs of embeddings is not continuous. It is continuous, however, when $M$ is compact; and if $\dim N \geqslant 2 \dim M + 1$ it can be proved locally extendible.

More generally, if the neighborhood $\mathcal{N} \subset C_S^r(M,N)$ consists of proper maps, then the structure functor of $A$-germs of maps in $\mathcal{N}$ which are embeddings in a neighborhood of $A$ is continuous, and locally extendable if $\dim N \geqslant 2 \dim M + 1$.

One then applies the Globalization Theorem (Theorem 2.11) to this structure functor. When $M$ is compact, every continuous map is proper, so the structure functor is defined on the family $\mathfrak{Y}$ generated by finite unions of compact disks covering $M$, and the globalization argument goes through. When $M$ is not compact, one restricts to the subspace $\operatorname{Prop}_S^r(M,N)$ of proper maps, within which the structure functor on $A$-germs of embeddings is continuous and locally extendable, yielding density of embeddings in $\operatorname{Prop}_S^r(M,N)$.

The details are left as an exercise.

QED
