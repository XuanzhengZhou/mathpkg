A disk with $k$ holes is the basic example of a planar surface. It is obtained by removing the interiors of $k$ disjoint subdisks from a 2-disk. Such a surface has genus 0 and $k+1$ boundary components. For example, $H_1$ is diffeomorphic to the cylinder $S^1 \times I$.
