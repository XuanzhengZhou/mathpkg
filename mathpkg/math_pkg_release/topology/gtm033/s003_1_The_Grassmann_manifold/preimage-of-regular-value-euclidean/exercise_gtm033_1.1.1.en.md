# Exercise 1

The Grassmann manifold $G_{n,k}$ of $k$-dimensional linear subspaces or $k$-planes of $\mathbb{R}^n$ is given an atlas as follows. Let $E \subset \mathbb{R}^n$ be a $k$-plane and $E^\perp$ its orthogonal complement. Identify $\mathbb{R}^n$ with $E \times E^\perp$. Every $k$-plane near enough to $E$ is the graph of a unique linear map $E \to E^\perp$. In this way a neighborhood of $E \in G_{n,k}$ is mapped homeomorphically onto an open set in the vector space of linear maps $E \to E^\perp$. This makes $G_{n,k}$ an analytic manifold of dimension $k(n-k)$.
