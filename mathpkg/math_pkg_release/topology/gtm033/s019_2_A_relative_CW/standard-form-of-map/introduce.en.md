A map into a Thom space is in standard form if it is a vector bundle map on a tubular neighborhood of the inverse image of the base space, mapping the complement to the point at infinity. This representation simplifies the analysis of the Thom homomorphism: cobordism classes correspond precisely to the inverse images of the base space under maps in standard form, making the connection between geometry (submanifolds) and homotopy theory explicit.
