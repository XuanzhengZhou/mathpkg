Let $M, N$ be $C^r$ manifolds. A \textbf{weak subbasic neighborhood} of $f \in C^r(M,N)$ is defined as $\mathcal{N}^r(f; (\varphi, U), (\psi, V), K, \varepsilon)$: the set of $C^r$ maps $g: M \to N$ such that $g(K) \subset V$ and

$$\| D^k(\psi f \varphi^{-1})(x) - D^k(\psi g \varphi^{-1})(x) \| < \varepsilon$$

for all $x \in \varphi(K)$, $k = 0, \ldots, r$. Here $(\varphi, U)$ and $(\psi, V)$ are charts on $M, N$; $K \subset U$ is compact with $f(K) \subset V$; $0 < \varepsilon \leqslant \infty$.

The \textbf{weak topology} on $C^r(M, N)$ is generated by these subbasic neighborhoods; it defines the topological space $C^r_W(M, N)$.
