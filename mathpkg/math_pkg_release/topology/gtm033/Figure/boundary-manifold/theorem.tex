If $\partial M \neq \varnothing$ we call $M$ a $\partial$-manifold (manifold with boundary). If $\partial M = \varnothing$ we call $M$ a manifold without boundary. The definition of $C^r$ map between $C^r$ manifolds is unchanged, as are the definitions of tangent vectors and the tangent bundle (if $r \geqslant 1$). The concepts of immersion, submersion, diffeomorphism, and embedding go through as before.
