A $\partial$-manifold, or manifold with boundary, is a $C^r$ manifold whose boundary $\partial M$ is nonempty. Most of the standard differential topology concepts (maps, tangent bundles, immersions, submersions, diffeomorphisms, embeddings) extend without modification to the boundary case. The distinction matters when dealing with regular values and transversality theorems.
