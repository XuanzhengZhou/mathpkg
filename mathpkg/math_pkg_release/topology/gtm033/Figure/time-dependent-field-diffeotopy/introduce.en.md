Theorem 1.1 is the fundamental existence theorem for isotopies: a time-dependent vector field with bounded velocity on a manifold $M$ generates a unique diffeotopy — a smooth family of diffeomorphisms starting at the identity. The proof converts the time-dependent field into a vector field on $M \times I$ and uses completeness from the bounded velocity condition to ensure solution curves exist on the full interval.
