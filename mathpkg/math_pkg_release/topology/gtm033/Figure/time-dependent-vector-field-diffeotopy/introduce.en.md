A central result in the theory of isotopies: a time-dependent vector field with bounded velocity on a manifold $M$ gives rise to a unique diffeotopy — a smooth family of diffeomorphisms $F_t: M 	o M$ parameterized by $t \in I = [0,1]$, satisfying the ordinary differential equation $\partial_t F(x,t) = G(F(x,t),t)$. The proof lifts the field to $M 	imes I$ and uses completeness of a suitable Riemannian metric to ensure solution curves exist on the whole interval. This theorem is the engine behind isotopy extension results.
