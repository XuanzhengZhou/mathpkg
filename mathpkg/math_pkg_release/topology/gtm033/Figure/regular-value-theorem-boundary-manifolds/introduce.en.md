This theorem extends the classical regular value theorem to the setting of manifolds with boundary. It requires that the regular value $y$ not lie in the boundary of the target $N$, and that $y$ be regular for both the map itself and its restriction to the boundary of the domain. Under these conditions, the preimage $f^{-1}(y)$ is a neat submanifold — meaning its boundary lies precisely where it meets $\partial M$, and it is not tangent to $\partial M$.
