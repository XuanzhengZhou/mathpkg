This theorem states that a vector bundle monomorphism defined over a neighborhood of a closed subset can be extended to a global monomorphism into a trivial bundle, provided the target fiber dimension $s$ is at least $k + \dim M$. The proof reduces to the special case of a trivial bundle and invokes the fact that $\dim M \leqslant s - k$ to apply Theorem 3.2.5 on extending maps into the Stiefel manifold $V_{s,k}$. This extension result is the key technical step toward the homotopy classification of vector bundles.
