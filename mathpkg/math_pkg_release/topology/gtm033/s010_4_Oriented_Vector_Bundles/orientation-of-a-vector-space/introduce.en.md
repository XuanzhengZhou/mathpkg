An orientation of a finite-dimensional real vector space is an equivalence class of ordered bases, where two bases are equivalent if the linear automorphism sending one to the other has positive determinant. For positive dimension there are exactly two orientations, conventionally denoted $\omega$ and $-\omega$; for dimension zero an orientation is simply the choice $+1$ or $-1$. Isomorphisms of vector spaces carry orientations forward in a natural way, and orientations of direct summands determine an orientation of the direct sum.
