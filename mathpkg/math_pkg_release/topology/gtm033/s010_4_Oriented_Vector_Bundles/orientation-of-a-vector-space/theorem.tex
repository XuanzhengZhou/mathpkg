Let $V$ be a finite dimensional real vector space of dimension $n > 0$. Two bases $(e_1, \ldots, e_n)$ and $(f_1, \ldots, f_n)$ of $V$ are \text{equivalent} if the automorphism $A: V \to V$ such that $A e_i = f_i$ has positive determinant. An \text{orientation} of $V$ is an equivalence class $[e_1, \ldots, e_n]$ of bases. If $\dim V > 0$ there are exactly two orientations. If one of them is denoted by $\omega$, then $-\omega$ denotes the other. If $\dim V = 0$, an orientation of $V$ means one of the numbers $+1$ or $-1$.

If $L: V \to W$ is an isomorphism of vector spaces and $\omega = [e_1, \ldots, e_n]$ is an orientation of $V$, then $L(\omega) = [L e_1, \ldots, L e_n]$ is the induced orientation of $W$.

Given orientations $\omega'$ of $V'$ and $\omega''$ of $V''$, a direct sum orientation $\omega' \oplus \omega''$ of $V' \oplus V''$ is defined by taking the concatenation of oriented bases. Any two of $\omega', \omega, \omega''$ determine the third uniquely when $V = V' \oplus V''$.
