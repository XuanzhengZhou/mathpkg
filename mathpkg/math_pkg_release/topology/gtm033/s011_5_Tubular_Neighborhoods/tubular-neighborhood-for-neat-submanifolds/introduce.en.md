Theorem 6.3 generalizes Theorem 5.2 to manifolds with boundary by showing that every neat submanifold admits a tubular neighborhood. The proof uses Theorem 6.2 to equip the ambient manifold $V$ with a collar compatible with the submanifold $M$, embeds $V$ as a neat submanifold of a half-space $\mathbb{R}_+^q$, and constructs a normal tubular neighborhood there. This result ensures that tubular neighborhoods are available in the full generality needed for transversality theory and other constructions involving manifolds with boundary.
