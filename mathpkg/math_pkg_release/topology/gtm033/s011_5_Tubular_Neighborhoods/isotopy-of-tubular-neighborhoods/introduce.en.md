An isotopy of tubular neighborhoods is a special kind of isotopy that deforms one tubular neighborhood into another while respecting the vector bundle structure. The key requirement is that the final map $F_1$, when pulled back via $f_1^{-1}$, is a vector bundle isomorphism between the two bundles. This ensures that the deformation maps fibres linearly onto fibres. The family $\{F_t(E_0)\}_{t \in I}$ can be thought of as a one-parameter family of tubular neighborhoods of $M$, and the track $\hat{F}$ itself defines a tubular neighborhood of $M \times I$ in $V \times I$.
