This definition introduces the notion of support for a time-dependent vector field on a smooth manifold. Unlike the support of an ordinary vector field (which is a subset of the manifold), the support of a time-dependent vector field is defined as the closure of all points in $M$ where the field is nonzero at some time $t \in I$.

This concept is crucial for isotopy extension theory because time-dependent vector fields with compact support automatically have bounded velocity, making them applicable to the generation of isotopies via Theorem 1.1. The compact support condition provides a practical sufficient condition for the existence of globally defined flows and isotopies.
