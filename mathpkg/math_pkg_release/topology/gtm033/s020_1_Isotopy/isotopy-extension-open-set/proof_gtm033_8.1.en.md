# Proof of Isotopy Extension Theorem for Open Sets

**Theorem 1.4.** Let $U \subset M$ be an open set and $A \subset U$ a compact set. Let $F: U \times I \rightarrow M$ be an isotopy of $U$ such that $\hat{F}(U \times I) \subset M \times I$ is open. Then there is a diffeotopy of $M$ having compact support, which agrees with $F$ on a neighborhood of $A \times I$.

*Proof.* The tangent vectors to the curves

$$\hat{F}: x \times I \rightarrow M \times I \quad (x \in U)$$

define a vector field $X$ on $\hat{F}(U \times I)$ of the form $X(y,t) = (H(y,t),1)$. Here $H: \hat{F}(U \times I) \rightarrow TM$ is a time-dependent vector field with $H(y,t) \in T_y M$.

By means of a partition of unity, we construct a time-dependent vector field $G: M \times I \rightarrow TM$ which agrees with $H$ on a neighborhood of $A \times I$. This construction requires $\hat{F}(U \times I)$ to be open, so that we can extend $H$ smoothly from the open set $\hat{F}(U \times I)$ to all of $M \times I$.

Since $A \times I$ is compact, we can make $G$ have compact support (by multiplying by a bump function supported in a compact neighborhood of $A \times I$). The required diffeotopy of $M$ is that generated by the compactly supported time-dependent vector field $G$, via Theorem 1.2. This diffeotopy has compact support and agrees with $F$ on a neighborhood of $A \times I$ because $G$ agrees with $H$ there. $\square$
