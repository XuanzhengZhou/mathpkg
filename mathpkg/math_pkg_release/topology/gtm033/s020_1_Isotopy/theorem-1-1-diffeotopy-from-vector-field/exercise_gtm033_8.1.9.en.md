# Exercise *9

Let $L \subset \mathbb{R}^3$ be obtained from a straight line by putting a small knot in it.

(a) There is an isotopy $F_t$ of $L$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$ such that $F_1(L)$ is a straight line $R$. ["Roll" the knot to infinity.]

(b) Such an isotopy cannot be ambient because $\mathbb{R}^3 - L$ and $\mathbb{R}^3 - R$ have different fundamental groups.
