Theorem 1.3 is the first of the isotopy extension theorems in Hirsch's treatment of differential topology. It states that an isotopy defined on a compact submanifold $V$ of $M$ can be extended to a global diffeotopy of the ambient manifold $M$, provided the isotopy stays either entirely within the boundary $\partial M$ or entirely within the interior $M - \partial M$.

The compactness of $V$ is essential for the proof, which proceeds by constructing a time-dependent vector field on the image of $V \times I$ under the graph map and extending it via a tubular neighborhood and partition of unity. This theorem is fundamental for the study of embeddings and submanifolds up to isotopy.
