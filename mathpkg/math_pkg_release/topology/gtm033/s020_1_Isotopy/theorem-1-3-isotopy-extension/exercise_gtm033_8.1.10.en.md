# Exercise 10

Let $f, g: M \hookrightarrow V$ be homotopic embeddings. If $1 + \dim V > 2(1 + \dim M)$, then $f$ and $g$ are isotopic.

[*Hint:* Let $F: M \times I \to V$ be a homotopy from $f$ to $g$. Approximate the map $M \times I \to V \times I$, $(x,t) \mapsto (F(x,t),t)$ by an embedding $H$. Write $H(x,t) = (G(x,t),K(x,t)) \in V \times I$. Then, assuming $H(x,i) = F(x,i)$ for $i = 0, 1$, the map $G: M \times I \to V$ is an isotopy from $f$ to $g$.]
