A compact 2-manifold without boundary that admits a Morse function with exactly two critical points must be diffeomorphic to the 2-sphere. By Morse theory, such a manifold decomposes as the union of two 2-disks glued along their boundaries. The isotopy classification of circle diffeomorphisms then implies the gluing map can be taken as the identity, yielding the sphere.
