Every diffeomorphism of the circle is isotopic to either the identity map (if it has degree 1) or complex conjugation (if degree -1). The proof proceeds by first isotoping the map to be the identity on some open interval, then using a straight-line homotopy on the complement. This gives a complete classification of circle diffeomorphisms up to isotopy.
