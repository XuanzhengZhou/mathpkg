# Proof of Theorem 1.4 (Conditions for Equivalent Handle Attachments)

Let $M$ be a connected surface and $f, g: S^0 \times D^2 \to M - \partial M$ embeddings. Then $M[f] \approx M[g]$ in the following cases:

(a) $M$ is nonorientable;
(b) $M$ is oriented and $f$ and $g$ both preserve or both reverse orientation;
(c) $M$ is reversible and both $f$ and $g$ are orientable.

**Proof.** Part (a) has already been proved (Corollary 1.2). Part (b) follows from the isotopy classification of disks (Theorem 8.3.2): when $M$ is oriented and $f, g$ both preserve (or both reverse) orientation, the embeddings are isotopic, so Theorem 1.1 applies. Part (c): since $M$ is reversible, there exists an orientation-reversing diffeomorphism of $M$. If $f, g$ are both orientable, one can compose with this diffeomorphism if needed to put them in the same orientation class, then apply Theorem 8.3.2 and Theorem 1.1. $\square$
