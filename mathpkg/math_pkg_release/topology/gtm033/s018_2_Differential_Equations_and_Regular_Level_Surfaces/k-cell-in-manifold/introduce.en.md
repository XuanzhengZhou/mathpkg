A $k$-cell in a manifold $M$ is the image of an embedding of the $k$-dimensional closed disk $D^k$ into $M$. In Morse theory, $k$-cells arise naturally from critical points of index $k$ as the descending manifolds. The cellular decomposition obtained from a Morse function expresses a compact manifold as built from finitely many cells.
