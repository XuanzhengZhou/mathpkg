A CW-complex is a space built inductively by attaching cells of increasing dimension, starting from a discrete set. The topology is the weak topology: a subset is closed if and only if its intersection with each closed cell is closed. CW-complexes provide a combinatorial framework for homotopy theory, and every compact manifold has the homotopy type of a finite CW-complex via Morse theory.
