Let $U \subset \mathbb{R}^m$ and $V \subset \mathbb{R}^n$ be open subsets, $f: U \to V$ be a $C^r$ map, $K \subset U$ be a closed subset, and $W \subset U$ be an open subset. Suppose $0 \leqslant r < s \leqslant \infty$ and $f$ is $C^s$ on a neighborhood of $K \setminus W$. Then $f$ can be $C^r$-approximated by maps $h: U \to V$ such that $h$ is $C^s$ on a neighborhood of $K$ and $h = f$ on $U \setminus W$.
