The norm of a $k$-linear map $S$ from $(\mathbb{R}^m)^k$ to $\mathbb{R}^n$ is defined as the supremum of $|S(x_1,\ldots,x_k)|$ over unit vectors. This extends the operator norm to multilinear settings and satisfies the fundamental bound $|S(y_1,\ldots,y_k)| \leq \|S\| \cdot |y_1| \cdots |y_k|$. It is used to measure higher-order derivatives in the $C^r$ topology.
