A convolution kernel is a nonnegative, compactly supported function on $\mathbb{R}^m$ whose integral equals $1$. Such kernels serve as mollifiers: convolving a function with a smooth convolution kernel produces a smooth approximation of the original function. Smooth convolution kernels of any prescribed support radius exist.
