The notation $\|f\|_{r,K}$ measures the maximum size of a $C^r$ map $f$ and all its derivatives up to order $r$ over a subset $K$. This seminorm is the fundamental building block for the strong $C^r$ Whitney topology used throughout differential topology to define closeness of smooth maps.
