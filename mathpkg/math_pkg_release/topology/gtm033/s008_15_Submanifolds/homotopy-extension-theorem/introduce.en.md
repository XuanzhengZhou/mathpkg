The Homotopy Extension Theorem provides sufficient conditions for extending a homotopy defined on a closed subspace to the entire space. It states that if a homotopy of $f|Z$ can be extended to a neighborhood of $Z$, then it extends globally. This is a fundamental result in homotopy theory and is used extensively in the theory of vector bundles.
