An orthogonal structure on a vector bundle is a continuously varying family of inner products on each fibre. Over paracompact bases, orthogonal structures always exist and can be constructed via partitions of unity. The Gram-Schmidt process shows that every orthogonal bundle has an atlas with transition functions taking values in the orthogonal group $O(n)$, making the structure group of the bundle $O(n)$ rather than $GL(n)$.
