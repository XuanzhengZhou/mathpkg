A vector bundle morphism is a map between total spaces that preserves the fibre structure and is linear on each fibre. The hierarchy of morphisms — monomorphism, epimorphism, bimorphism, equivalence, isomorphism — mirrors the linear algebra hierarchy of injective, surjective, and bijective maps, adapted to the bundle setting. These morphisms form a category, with the tangent functor $T$ as a covariant functor from manifolds to vector bundles.
