# Exercise 4.4: Trivial Neighborhood of a Neat Disk

Let $D \subset M$ be a neat $p$-disk of codimension $k$. Then $D$ has a neighborhood $E \subset M$ such that

$$(E, D) \approx (D^p \times \mathbb{R}^k, D^p \times 0).$$

*Note: This exercise establishes that a neat $p$-disk submanifold of codimension $k$ has a product neighborhood of the standard form. This is a local triviality result: near a neat disk, the ambient manifold looks like a product of the disk with Euclidean space. The proof uses the tubular neighborhood theorem (since a disk is contractible, its normal bundle is trivial) combined with the fact that a tubular neighborhood of a compact submanifold with trivial normal bundle gives the desired product structure.*
