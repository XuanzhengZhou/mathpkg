For the sphere $S_{(n)}$ with $n \geq 1$ holes, stretch one hole to an equator and project onto a plane, obtaining a disc with $n-1$ holes. For $n > 1$, this disc contains an $(n-1)$-leafed rose as a deformation retract. Since the fundamental group of an $(n-1)$-leafed rose is free of rank $n-1$, we have $\pi(S_{(n)}) \cong F_{n-1}$. For $n=1$, the sphere with one hole is a disc, so $\pi(S_{(1)}) = 1$ (free of rank 0).
