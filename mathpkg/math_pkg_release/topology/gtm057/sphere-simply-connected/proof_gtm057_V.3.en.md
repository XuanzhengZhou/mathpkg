Let $X_0$ be an open equatorial band dividing the sphere $S^2$ into north and south polar caps. Set $X_1$ = $X_0$ $\cup$ north cap, $X_2$ = $X_0$ $\cup$ south cap. Each of $X_1, X_2, X_0$ is pathwise connected and open in $S^2$. Moreover, $X_1$ and $X_2$ are homeomorphic to open discs, hence simply-connected. By Corollary (3.2) of the van Kampen theorem, $S^2 = X_1 \cup X_2$ is simply-connected. Thus $\pi(S^2) = 1$.
