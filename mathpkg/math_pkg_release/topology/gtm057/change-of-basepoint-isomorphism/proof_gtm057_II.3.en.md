The product $\alpha^{-1} \cdot \beta \cdot \alpha$ is defined since $\beta$ is a $p$-based loop. For any $\beta_1, \beta_2 \in \pi(X,p)$:
$$\beta_1 \cdot \beta_2 \mapsto \alpha^{-1} \cdot (\beta_1 \cdot \beta_2) \cdot \alpha = (\alpha^{-1} \cdot \beta_1 \cdot \alpha) \cdot (\alpha^{-1} \cdot \beta_2 \cdot \alpha).$$
Thus the mapping is a homomorphism. It is invertible via conjugation by $\alpha^{-1}$, so it is an isomorphism.
