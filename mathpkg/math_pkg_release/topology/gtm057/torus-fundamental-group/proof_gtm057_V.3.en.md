Decompose the torus $X = X_1 \cup X_2$ where $X_1$ is the torus minus a closed disc (homotopy type of 2-leafed rose, so $\pi(X_1)$ free of rank 2 with basis $x = [a], y = [b]$) and $X_2$ is an open disc (simply-connected). $X_0 = X_1 \cap X_2$ is an open annulus with infinite cyclic fundamental group generated by a path $c$ whose image in $\pi(X_1)$ is $xyx^{-1}y^{-1}$. By (3.4), $\omega_1$ is onto and its kernel is the consequence of $xyx^{-1}y^{-1}$, giving $\pi(T^2) = (x,y : xyx^{-1}y^{-1})$.
