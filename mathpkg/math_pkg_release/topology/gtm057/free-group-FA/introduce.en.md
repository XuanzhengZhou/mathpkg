The free group $F[\mathcal{A}]$ on an alphabet $\mathcal{A}$ is constructed from formal words in the letters and their inverses. Two words are equivalent if one can be transformed into the other by removing or inserting $a^0$ syllables and by combining consecutive syllables on the same letter. Each equivalence class contains a unique reduced word. Free groups serve as coordinate systems for group presentations, analogous to coordinate axes in analytic geometry.
