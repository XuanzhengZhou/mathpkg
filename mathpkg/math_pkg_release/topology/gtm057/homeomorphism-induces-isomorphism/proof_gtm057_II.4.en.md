Consider $X \xrightarrow{f} Y \xrightarrow{f^{-1}} X$, inducing
$$\pi(X,p) \xrightarrow{f_*} \pi(Y, fp) \xrightarrow{(f^{-1})_*} \pi(X,p).$$
Since $f^{-1}f = \mathrm{id}_X$ and $ff^{-1} = \mathrm{id}_Y$, we have $(f^{-1}f)_* = f^{-1}_* f_* = \mathrm{id}$ and $(ff^{-1})_* = f_* f^{-1}_* = \mathrm{id}$. Thus $f_*$ has a two-sided inverse and is an isomorphism.
