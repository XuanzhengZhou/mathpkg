The over presentation of the clover-leaf knot simplifies to $(x,y : xyx = yxy)$. Define $\theta: F(x,y) \to S_3$ by $\theta(x) = (12)$, $\theta(y) = (23)$. Then $\theta(xyx) = (12)(23)(12) = (13)$ and $\theta(yxy) = (23)(12)(23) = (13)$, so $\theta(xyx) = \theta(yxy)$. Thus $\theta$ induces a homomorphism from the knot group onto $S_3$. Since $S_3$ is nonabelian, the knot group is nonabelian. The trivial knot's group is infinite cyclic (abelian), so the clover-leaf and trivial knot have non-isomorphic groups and thus distinct knot types.
