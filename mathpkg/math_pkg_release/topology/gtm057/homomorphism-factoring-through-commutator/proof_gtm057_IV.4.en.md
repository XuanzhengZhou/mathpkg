Let $\theta: G \to A$ where $A$ is abelian. Define $\theta': G/[G,G] \to A$ by $\theta'([G,G]g) = \theta(g)$. This is well-defined because for any commutator $[g,h]$, $\theta([g,h]) = [\theta(g),\theta(h)] = 1$ since $A$ is abelian. Uniqueness follows since the canonical map $G \to G/[G,G]$ is onto.
