Theorem 4.5 establishes that elementary ideals are invariants of the group, independent of the particular presentation. The proof reduces via the Tietze theorem to checking invariance under Tietze operations I and II. Under Tietze I (adding a consequence relator), the new row in the Alexander matrix is a linear combination of existing rows, preserving the row space. Under Tietze II (adding a generator $y = \xi$), the Alexander matrix transforms by an equivalence operation.
