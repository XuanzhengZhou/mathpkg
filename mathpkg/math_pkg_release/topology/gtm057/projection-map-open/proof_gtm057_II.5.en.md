For any subset $B$ of $\mathbb{R}/3\mathbb{Z}$, $B$ is open if and only if $\phi^{-1}(B)$ is open. For any subset $X$ of $\mathbb{R}$,
$$\phi^{-1}\phi(X) = \bigcup_{n \in \mathbb{Z}} (3n + X),$$
where $3n + X = \{3n + x : x \in X\}$. Since translation by a fixed amount is a homeomorphism and the union of open sets is open, if $X$ is open then $\phi^{-1}\phi(X)$ is open, hence $\phi(X)$ is open.
