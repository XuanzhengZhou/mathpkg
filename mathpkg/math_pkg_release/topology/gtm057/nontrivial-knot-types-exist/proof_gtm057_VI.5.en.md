For the clover-leaf knot $K$, $\pi(\mathbb{R}^3 - K) = |x, y : xyx = yxy|$. Define $\theta: F(x,y) \to S_3$ by $\theta(x) = (12), \theta(y) = (23)$. Then $\theta(xyx) = (12)(23)(12) = (13)$ and $\theta(yxy) = (23)(12)(23) = (13)$. Thus $\theta$ kills the relator and induces a homomorphism from the knot group onto $S_3$, a nonabelian group. Since the trivial knot group is infinite cyclic (abelian), the groups cannot be isomorphic, so the knots are of different types.
