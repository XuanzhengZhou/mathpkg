Let $K_G$ be the consequence of $\{g_i\}$ and $K_H$ the consequence of $\{h_i = \phi g_i\}$. Since $\phi K_G$ contains all $h_i$ and is a normal subgroup (because $\phi$ is onto), $K_H \subset \phi K_G$. Conversely, any element of $\phi K_G$ is $\phi$ of a product of conjugates of powers of the $g_i$, which maps to a product of conjugates of powers of the $h_i$, i.e., an element of $K_H$.
