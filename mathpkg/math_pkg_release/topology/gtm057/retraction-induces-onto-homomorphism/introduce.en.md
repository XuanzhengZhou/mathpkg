Theorem 1.1 shows that a retraction induces a surjective homomorphism on fundamental groups. This provides a powerful tool for proving that certain subspaces are not retracts: if $\pi(X)$ is trivial but $\pi(Y)$ is nontrivial, there cannot be a retraction $X \to Y$. For instance, a disc's boundary is not a retract of the disc.
