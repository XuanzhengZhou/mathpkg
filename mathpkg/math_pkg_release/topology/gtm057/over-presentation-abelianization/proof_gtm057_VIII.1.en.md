In any over presentation $(x_1, \dots, x_n : r_1, \dots, r_n)_\phi$, each relator $r_i$ corresponds to a crossing where underpass $B_i$ meets overpasses $A_{\kappa(i)}$ and $A_{\lambda(i)}$. The exponent sum of $r_i$ with respect to generator $x_j$ equals $t(\partial r_i/\partial x_j) = \delta_{j,\kappa(i)} - \delta_{j,\lambda(i)}$. Hence the total exponent sum $\sum_j t(\partial r_i/\partial x_j) = 0$ for each $i$. It follows that mapping every generator to the same element $t$ in an abelian group is consistent with all relations, so $\alpha\gamma x_i = \alpha\gamma x_j$ for all $i,j$. Thus condition (3.6) of Chapter VIII is always satisfied for over presentations.
