The geometric idea is to hold $K$ fixed and move the projection. Consider $\mathbb{R}^3$ as a subset of real projective 3-space $P^3$. To every parallel projection we associate the point of intersection of any line parallel to the direction of projection with the projective plane $P^2$ at infinity.

Let $Q$ be the set of all points of $P^2$ corresponding to projections with respect to which $K$ is not in regular position. We show that $Q$ is nowhere dense in $P^2$. Consider the set of all straight lines which join a vertex of $K$ to an edge of $K$. These intersect $P^2$ in a finite number of straight-line segments whose union we denote by $Q_1$. Any projection corresponding to a point of $P^2 - Q_1$ must satisfy condition (ii) of regular position.

The remaining points of $Q$ correspond to projections producing triple or higher-order multiple points. These also form a nowhere-dense subset. Since $Q$ is nowhere dense, there exists a projection $\mathcal{P}_0$ arbitrarily close to the original projection with respect to which $K$ is in regular position. Any rotation of $\mathbb{R}^3$ transforming the line $\mathcal{P}_0^{-1}(0,0,0)$ into the $z$-axis completes the proof.
