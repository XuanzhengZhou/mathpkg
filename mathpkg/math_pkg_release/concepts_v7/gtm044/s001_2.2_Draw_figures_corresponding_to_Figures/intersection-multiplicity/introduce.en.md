Intersection multiplicity assigns a positive integer $n_P$ to each isolated intersection point $P$ of two algebraic curves, quantifying how many distinct intersection points coalesce at $P$ under a generic small perturbation of one curve. Intuitively, if one curve is translated slightly in a generic direction, a single intersection of multiplicity $n_P$ separates into $n_P$ distinct simple intersections clustered near $P$. This notion generalizes the multiplicity of a root of a polynomial and is essential for the precise formulation of Bézout's theorem.
