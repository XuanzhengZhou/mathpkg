Let $G^*$ and $J^*$ be the maps between $\mathcal{J}(R_{\mathfrak{p}})$ (closed ideals of the local ring) and $\mathcal{G}(R_{\mathfrak{p}})$ (germs of subvarieties at $W$) defined analogously to the global $V$ and $J$ correspondences. For any ideals $\mathfrak{a}^*, \mathfrak{b}^* \in \mathcal{J}(R_{\mathfrak{p}})$ and any germs $X_W, Y_W \in \mathcal{G}(R_{\mathfrak{p}})$:

\begin{align}
G^*(\mathfrak{a}^* \cap \mathfrak{b}^*) &= G^*(\mathfrak{a}^*) \cup G^*(\mathfrak{b}^*), \\
G^*(\mathfrak{a}^* + \mathfrak{b}^*) &= G^*(\mathfrak{a}^*) \cap G^*(\mathfrak{b}^*), \\
J^*(X_W \cup Y_W) &= J^*(X_W) \cap J^*(Y_W), \\
J^*(X_W \cap Y_W) &= J^*(X_W) + J^*(Y_W).
\end{align}

Thus $G^*$ and $J^*$ are mutually inverse lattice-reversing isomorphisms, serving as the local analogues of the global $V$ and $J$ correspondences.
