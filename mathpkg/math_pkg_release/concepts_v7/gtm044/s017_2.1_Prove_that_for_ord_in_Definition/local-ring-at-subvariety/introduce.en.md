The local ring $\mathfrak{o}(W; V)$ of a projective variety $V$ at an irreducible subvariety $W$ consists of rational functions representable as quotients of same-degree forms whose denominators do not vanish identically on $W$. Through dehomogenization, $\mathfrak{o}(W; V)$ is isomorphic to the localization $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ of the affine coordinate ring at the prime ideal corresponding to $W$. This ring encodes the local algebraic geometry of $V$ near $W$.
