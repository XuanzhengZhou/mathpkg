# Exercise 3.3

Show that for any ideals $\mathfrak{a}$ and $\mathfrak{b}$ in any ring $R$, we have $\sqrt{\mathfrak{a} + \mathfrak{b}} = \sqrt{\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} + \sqrt{\mathfrak{b}}}$.

*Hint:* Observe that $\mathfrak{a} + \mathfrak{b} \subset \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} + \sqrt{\mathfrak{b}}$, that $\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} + \sqrt{\mathfrak{b}} \subset \sqrt{\mathfrak{a} + \mathfrak{b}}$, and that $\sqrt{\ \cdot\ }$ is a closure map.
