The localization $R_{\mathfrak{m}}$ of the coordinate ring $R$ at a maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$ corresponding to a point $P$ captures the behavior of rational functions near $P$. An element $p/q \in \mathbb{C}(x_1,\ldots,x_n)$ belongs to $R_{\mathfrak{m}}$ precisely when the denominator $q$ does not vanish at $P$, ensuring the rational function is well-defined and finite there. Like valuation rings, $R_{\mathfrak{m}}$ possesses a unique maximal ideal.
