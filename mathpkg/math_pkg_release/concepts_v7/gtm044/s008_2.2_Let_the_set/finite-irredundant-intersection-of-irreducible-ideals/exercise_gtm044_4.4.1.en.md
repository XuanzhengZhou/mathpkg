# Exercise 4.4

Show that decompositions in Theorem 4.2 may not be unique by showing that $(X^2, XY)$ has two different irredundant decompositions into irreducibles.

**Hint:** Show that

$$(X^2, XY) = (X) \cap (X^2, Y) = (X) \cap (X^2, X + Y).$$

Then show that $(X)$, $(X^2, Y)$ and $(X^2, X + Y)$ are irreducible, and that there are no containment relations between any of these irreducibles.
