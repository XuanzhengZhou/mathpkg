Theorem 4.2 states that every ideal in the polynomial ring $\mathbb{C}[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$ admits a decomposition as a finite irredundant intersection of irreducible ideals. This is an algebraic analog of the geometric fact that every variety can be expressed as a finite union of irreducible varieties. The result follows from the Hilbert basis theorem — which guarantees the a.c.c. on the lattice of ideals — combined with general lattice-theoretic principles (Theorem 2.11). The decomposition is not necessarily unique.
