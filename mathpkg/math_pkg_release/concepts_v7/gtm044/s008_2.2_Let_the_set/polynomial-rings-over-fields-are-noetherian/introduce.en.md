Corollary 3.6 follows directly from the Hilbert basis theorem by induction on the number of variables. A field $k$ is trivially Noetherian since it has only two ideals ($\{0\}$ and $k$). Applying Theorem 3.5 repeatedly yields that $k[X_1], k[X_1, X_2], \ldots, k[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$ are all Noetherian. This result is foundational for algebraic geometry: the coordinate ring of affine $n$-space over a field is Noetherian, ensuring that every algebraic variety can be decomposed into finitely many irreducible components.
