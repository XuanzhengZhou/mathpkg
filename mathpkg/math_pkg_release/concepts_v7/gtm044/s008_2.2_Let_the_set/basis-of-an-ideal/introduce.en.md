A basis of an ideal is a generating set — every element of the ideal can be expressed as a finite linear combination of basis elements with coefficients from the ring. When the indexing set is finite, the ideal is said to have a finite basis (equivalently, to be finitely generated). The notion of a finite basis is the key stepping stone to the Hilbert basis theorem: the a.c.c. is equivalent to every ideal possessing a finite basis.
