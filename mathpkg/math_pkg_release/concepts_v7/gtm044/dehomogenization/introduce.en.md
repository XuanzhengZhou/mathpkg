Dehomogenization is the operation of setting one variable to $1$ in a homogeneous polynomial, producing an affine polynomial in one fewer variable. This corresponds geometrically to taking an affine representative of a projective variety by intersecting it with the affine chart $X_i = 1$. Together with homogenization, dehomogenization provides the essential bridge between affine and projective algebraic geometry.
