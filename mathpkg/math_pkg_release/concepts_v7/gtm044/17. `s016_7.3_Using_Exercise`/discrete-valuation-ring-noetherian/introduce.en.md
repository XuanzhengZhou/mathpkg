Corollary 2.22 follows immediately from Corollary 2.21: every principal ideal ring is Noetherian, since the ascending chain condition is trivially satisfied when every ideal is finitely generated (indeed, singly generated). For a discrete rank one valuation ring, any ascending chain of ideals corresponds to a non-increasing sequence of integers (the minimal orders), which must stabilize. This Noetherian property is essential for the algebraic geometry of curves.
