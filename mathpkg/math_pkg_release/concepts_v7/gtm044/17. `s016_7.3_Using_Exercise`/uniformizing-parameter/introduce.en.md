A uniformizing parameter at a point $P$ on an irreducible curve $C$ is a function field element that serves as a local coordinate near $P$: every coordinate function of $C$ can be expressed as a convergent power series in $U$ near $P$, with $U = 0$ corresponding to $P$. This is analogous to the complex variable $z$ near the origin in $\mathbb{C}$. Uniformizing parameters play a central role in the local study of algebraic curves, allowing the transfer of analytic methods to the algebraic setting.
