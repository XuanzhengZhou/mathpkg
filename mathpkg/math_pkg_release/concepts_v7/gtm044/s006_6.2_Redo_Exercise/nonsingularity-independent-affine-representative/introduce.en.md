This remark establishes that the property of being a nonsingular (or singular) point on a projective curve is well-defined with respect to the choice of affine coordinate chart. If a point $P$ on a projective curve $C$ is nonsingular when $C$ is viewed in one affine open set containing $P$, then it remains nonsingular in every other affine open set that also contains $P$. This ensures that smoothness and singularity are genuinely geometric (coordinate-independent) properties of the projective curve.
