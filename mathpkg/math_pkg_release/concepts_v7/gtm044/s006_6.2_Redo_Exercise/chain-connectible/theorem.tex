Let $S$ be a topological space.

Two connected open sets $\mathcal{O}, \mathcal{O}'$ in $S$ are **chain connectible** if there is a chain from $\mathcal{O}$ to $\mathcal{O}'$.

Two points $P, P' \in S$ are **chain connectible** if there is a chain from $P$ to $P'$.

If every two points $P, P' \in S$ are chain connectible, then $S$ is **chainwise connected**.
