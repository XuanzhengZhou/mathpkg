This theorem upgrades the relative local degree (Theorem 6.6) to an absolute invariant: for almost every complementary-dimensional linear subspace passing through $P$, the intersection multiplicity is the same constant. This constant is the absolute local order of $V$ at $P$. The proof uses an algebraic-geometric fiber-dimension argument: the relevant incidence variety over the parameter space of $P$-containing transforms has a component that is simply the translate $S \times \{P\}$, and applying Theorem 6.11 yields the constancy.
