With respect to appropriate coordinates, any pure-dimensional variety $V \subset \mathbb{P}^n(\mathbb{C})$ can be reduced to a union of $\deg V$ distinct projective subspaces of dimension $r = \dim V$ by means of a projective transformation defined by an $(n+1) \times (n+1)$ matrix in which $r$ of the entries on the main diagonal are $0$ and $n-r+1$ entries are $1$.
