This lemma provides the geometric heart of the proof of Bezout's theorem. It shows that when $V$ is degenerated (via Lemma 7.3) to a union of $\deg V$ distinct linear subspaces $V'$, the intersection $V' \cap W \cap L$ consists of exactly $\deg V \cdot \deg W$ distinct points, and this configuration is stable under small perturbations. The proof uses the Implicit Mapping Theorem to show that $V^{\dagger\dagger}$ is locally the graph of an analytic map over the parameter space near the chosen transversal configuration.
