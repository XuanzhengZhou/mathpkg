The order of a polynomial at a point, relative to a linear subspace through that point, generalizes the notion of multiplicity of a root from one-variable polynomials to several variables. It is defined by expanding the polynomial in a Taylor series about the point and examining the lowest-degree homogeneous component that does not vanish identically when restricted to the given subspace. This algebraic concept underpins the geometric definitions of local intersection multiplicity and local degree of a variety.
