Bezout's theorem is the fundamental result of intersection theory for projective varieties: the degree of the intersection product of two properly intersecting pure-dimensional varieties equals the product of their individual degrees. The proof strategy is to reduce $V$ to a union of $\deg V$ distinct linear subspaces (via Lemma 7.3), then apply the definition of degree via generic linear section to count intersection points. The theorem generalizes the classical Bezout theorem for plane curves to varieties of arbitrary dimension.
