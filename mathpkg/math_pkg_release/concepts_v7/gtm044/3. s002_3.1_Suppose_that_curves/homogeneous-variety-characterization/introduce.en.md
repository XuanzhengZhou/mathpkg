This theorem characterizes when an affine variety $V \subseteq k^n$ is a homogeneous set (a cone over a projective variety). The forward direction is immediate: if $V$ is defined by homogeneous polynomials, scaling any point by $c \in k$ preserves membership. The converse is more subtle: even if the original defining polynomials are not homogeneous, one can replace them with the collection of all their homogeneous components and obtain the same zero set. This shows that the property of being a projective variety is intrinsic and can always be realized by homogeneous equations.
