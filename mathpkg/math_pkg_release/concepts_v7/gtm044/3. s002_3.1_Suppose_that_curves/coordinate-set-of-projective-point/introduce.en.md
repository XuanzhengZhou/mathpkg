A coordinate set of a projective point $P \in \mathbb{P}^n(k)$ is simply the $(n+1)$-tuple of coordinates of any nonzero vector lying in the $1$-subspace $L_P \subset k^{n+1}$ corresponding to $P$. These coordinates are not unique: any two coordinate sets for the same point differ by multiplication by a nonzero scalar in $k$. This is the origin of the term "homogeneous coordinates" in projective geometry.
