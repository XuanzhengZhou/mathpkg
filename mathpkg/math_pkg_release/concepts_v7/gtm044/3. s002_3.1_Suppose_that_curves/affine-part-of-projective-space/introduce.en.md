Given a choice of projective hyperplane at infinity $\mathbb{P}_{\infty}^{n-1}(k) \subset \mathbb{P}^n(k)$, the complement is an affine $n$-space. Concretely, by parallel-translating the corresponding $n$-dimensional subspace $W \subset k^{n+1}$, each $1$-subspace not lying in $W$ intersects the affine translate in exactly one point, giving a bijection with $k^n$. Any affine $n$-space can be realized as the affine part of some $\mathbb{P}^n(k)$ by an appropriate choice of hyperplane at infinity, allowing one to move between affine and projective viewpoints freely.
