Definition 2.22 defines a hypersurface as a variety cut out by a single nonconstant equation. In $\mathbb{P}^n(\mathbb{C})$ the defining polynomial must be homogeneous; in $\mathbb{C}^n$ any nonconstant polynomial suffices. Hypersurfaces are the simplest varieties beyond linear subspaces, and Theorem 2.23 characterizes them as exactly the varieties of pure codimension 1.
