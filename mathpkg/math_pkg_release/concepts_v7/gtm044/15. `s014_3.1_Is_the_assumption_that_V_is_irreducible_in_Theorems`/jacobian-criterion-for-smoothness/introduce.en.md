Theorem 4.1 gives the Jacobian criterion for smoothness of an irreducible affine variety $V \subset \mathbb{C}^n$. It states that $V$ is smooth at a point $P$ if and only if the rank of its Jacobian matrix $J(V)_P$ equals the codimension of $V$ in $\mathbb{C}^n$. This criterion connects the algebraic notion of the Jacobian rank—measuring how many independent defining equations are effective at $P$—with the geometric notion of smoothness. The theorem is fundamental in algebraic geometry because it provides a computable test for singularities using partial derivatives of the defining polynomials.
