A polynomial $p$ is homogeneous of degree $m$ precisely when scaling all variables by $T$ multiplies the polynomial by $T^m$. This criterion follows from comparing coefficients: writing $p = \sum p_i$ as a sum of homogeneous components of different degrees, the identity $p(TX) = T^m p(X)$ forces all components except the degree-$m$ part to vanish. This lemma provides the key algebraic tool used in the proof of Theorem 6.2 on resultant homogeneity.
