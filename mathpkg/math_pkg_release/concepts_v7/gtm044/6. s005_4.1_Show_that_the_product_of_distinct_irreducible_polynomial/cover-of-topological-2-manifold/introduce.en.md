A cover formalizes the idea of one space lying "above" another in a locally trivial way. Given a topological 2-manifold $M$ and a locally compact space $A$, a continuous surjection $\pi : A \to M$ is a cover if each point of $M$ has a neighborhood whose preimage splits into disjoint open subsets of $A$, each homeomorphic to that neighborhood via $\pi$. When this local number of sheets is constant and equal to a positive integer $s$, the cover is called $s$-sheeted.
