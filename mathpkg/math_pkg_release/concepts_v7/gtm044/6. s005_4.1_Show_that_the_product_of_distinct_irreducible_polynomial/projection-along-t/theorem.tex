Let $S$ and $T$ be sets. The map $\pi_T \colon S \times T \to S$ is defined by

$$
\pi_T((s, t)) = s \quad \text{for each } (s, t) \in S \times T.
$$

It is called the \text{projection of $S \times T$ on $S$ along $T$}. Similarly we define $\pi_S$ by

$$
\pi_S((s, t)) = t.
$$

If $A$ is a subset of $S \times T$, and if no confusion can arise, we also denote the restrictions $\pi_S|_{A}$ and $\pi_T|_{A}$ by $\pi_S$ and $\pi_T$ respectively. The map $\pi_{\mathbb{C}_X} \colon \mathbb{C}_{XY} \to \mathbb{C}_Y$ is denoted by $\pi_X$, and $\pi_{\mathbb{C}_Y} \colon \mathbb{C}_{XY} \to \mathbb{C}_X$, by $\pi_Y$, etc.
