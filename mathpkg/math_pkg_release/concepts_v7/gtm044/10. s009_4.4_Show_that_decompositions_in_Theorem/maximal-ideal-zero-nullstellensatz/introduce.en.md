This theorem is an equivalent formulation of the Hilbert Nullstellensatz. Since the polynomial ring $\mathbb{C}[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$ is Noetherian, every proper ideal is contained in a maximal ideal, so the statement that every maximal ideal has a zero immediately implies the weak Nullstellensatz. This form highlights that maximal ideals in $\mathbb{C}[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$ correspond precisely to points in $\mathbb{C}^n$.
