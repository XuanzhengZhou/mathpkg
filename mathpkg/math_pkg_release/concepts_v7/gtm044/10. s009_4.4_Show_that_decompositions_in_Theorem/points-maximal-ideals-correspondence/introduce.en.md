This corollary of the Nullstellensatz establishes the foundational dictionary of algebraic geometry: points in affine space $\mathbb{C}^n$ correspond bijectively to maximal ideals in the polynomial ring. Concretely, a point $(c_1, \ldots, c_n)$ maps to the maximal ideal generated by $(X_1 - c_1), \ldots, (X_n - c_n)$. This correspondence allows geometric questions to be translated into algebraic ones and vice versa.
