Every proper ideal of $\mathbb{C}[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$ has a zero in $\mathbb{C}^n$.

More precisely, if $\mathfrak{a} \subsetneq \mathbb{C}[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$ is a proper ideal, then there exists a point $(a_1, \ldots, a_n) \in \mathbb{C}^n$ such that $p(a_1, \ldots, a_n) = 0$ for all $p \in \mathfrak{a}$.

The theorem holds when $\mathbb{C}$ is replaced by any algebraically closed field.
