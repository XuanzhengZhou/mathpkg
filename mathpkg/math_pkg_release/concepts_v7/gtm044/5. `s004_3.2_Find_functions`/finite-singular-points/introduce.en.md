Lemma 4.8 establishes that for a plane curve defined by $p(X,Y)$ with no repeated irreducible factors, the set of points where both $p$ and $\partial p / \partial Y$ vanish is finite. The proof proceeds in two steps: first, that the discriminant $\mathcal{D}_Y(p)$ is not the zero polynomial (by Lemma 4.7, since $p$ has no repeated factors); second, that at any zero $x_0$ of the discriminant, the polynomial $p(x_0, Y)$ has at most $n$ roots. This lemma is crucial for proving that all but finitely many points of a plane curve are nonsingular.
