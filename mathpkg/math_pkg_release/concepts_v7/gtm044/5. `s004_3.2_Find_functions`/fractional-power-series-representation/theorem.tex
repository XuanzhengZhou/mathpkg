Let $p(X, Y) = Y^n + a_1(X)Y^{n-1} + \ldots + a_n(X) \in \mathbb{C}[X, Y]$ ($n > 0$) have no repeated nonconstant factors. Let $(x_0, y_0)$ be a point of $C = V(p(X, Y)) \subset \mathbb{C}_{XY}$. Then the set of points of $C$ lying in a sufficiently small open neighborhood $U$ of $(x_0, y_0)$ is the union of $N$ different point sets $S_j$, where $S_j$ is the set of points in $U$ satisfying
$$Y = y_0 + \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} a_{jk} (X - x_0)^{k/m_j}$$
for some positive integer $m_j$ and coefficients $a_{jk} \in \mathbb{C}$. Each such series converges for $|X - x_0|$ sufficiently small. The composition $h = h_2 \circ h_1$ is given by
$$h_1(X) = T = (X - x_0)^{1/m},$$
$$h_2(T) = Y = y_0 + a_1 T + a_2 T^2 + \ldots.$$
The positive integers $m_j$ satisfy $m_1 + m_2 + \ldots + m_N = r$, where $r$ is the multiplicity of $y_0$ as a root of $p(x_0, Y)$.
