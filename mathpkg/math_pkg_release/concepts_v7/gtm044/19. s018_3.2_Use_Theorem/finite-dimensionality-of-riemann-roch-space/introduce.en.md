This lemma establishes that for any divisor $D$ on an algebraic curve, the vector space $L(D)$ of rational functions whose polar divisor is bounded by $D$ is always finite-dimensional. The proof first notes that if $\deg D < 0$ then $L(D) = \{0\}$. For $\deg D \geq 0$, one reduces to the case $D = P_1 + \cdots + P_n$ (all coefficients positive) and considers the filtration $L(0) \subset L(P_1) \subset \cdots \subset L(D)$. Each successive quotient has dimension at most 1, as shown by the evaluation map $f \mapsto (u^m f)(P)$ where $u$ is a uniformizing variable.
