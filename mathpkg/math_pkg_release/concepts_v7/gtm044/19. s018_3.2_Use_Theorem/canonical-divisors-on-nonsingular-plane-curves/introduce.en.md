This theorem describes the precise structure of canonical divisors on a nonsingular plane curve $C$ of degree $d \geq 3$. Every canonical divisor (the divisor of a differential form) can be expressed as the sum of the divisor of a homogeneous polynomial $F$ of degree $d-3$ and a principal divisor $\operatorname{div}(f)$. Conversely, every such sum yields a canonical divisor. A corollary is that the degree of every canonical divisor is $d(d-3) = 2g-2$, where $g$ is the genus of $C$.
