A domain $R$ is integrally closed (or normal) if every element of its quotient field that satisfies a monic polynomial equation with coefficients in $R$ already belongs to $R$. This property is one of the two defining axioms of a Dedekind domain (together with every nonzero prime ideal being maximal). Examples include $\mathbb{Z}$ and $\mathbb{C}[X]$. A key fact used in the book is that the intersection of integrally closed subrings is integrally closed, and that for a domain where every prime ideal is maximal, the localization at each maximal ideal being regular implies the domain equals the intersection of these localizations and is therefore integrally closed.
