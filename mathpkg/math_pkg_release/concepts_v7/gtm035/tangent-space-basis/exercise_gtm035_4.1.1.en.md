# Exercise 4.1

Prove Lemma 4.1: The operators $\partial/\partial x_1|_x, \ldots, \partial/\partial x_N|_x$ form a basis for the tangent space $T_x$.

Specifically, show that:
1. Each $\partial/\partial x_j|_x$ is a tangent vector at $x$ (i.e., satisfies properties (a) and (b) of Definition 4.2).
2. The $\partial/\partial x_j|_x$ are linearly independent over $\mathbb{C}$.
3. Every tangent vector $v \in T_x$ can be expressed as a linear combination $v = \sum_{j=1}^{N} c_j \, \partial/\partial x_j|_x$.

**Hint:** For linear independence, apply a linear combination to the coordinate function $x_k$. For spanning, use a first-order Taylor expansion of $f \in C^\infty$ about $x$, and use properties (a) and (b) of tangent vectors.
