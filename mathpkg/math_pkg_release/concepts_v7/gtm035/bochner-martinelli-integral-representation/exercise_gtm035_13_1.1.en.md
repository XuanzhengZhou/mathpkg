# Exercise 13.1

Verify the three properties of the Cauchy kernel $C(\zeta, z) = d\zeta / (\zeta - z)$ on $\mathbb{C}$:

(i) $dC = 0$ on $\mathbb{C} \setminus \{z\}$.

(ii) $\int_{|\zeta - z| = \epsilon} C(\zeta, z) = 2\pi i$, independent of $z$ and $\epsilon > 0$.

(iii) For every continuous function $g$ on $\mathbb{C}$,

$$\left| \int_{|\zeta - z| = \epsilon} g(\zeta) C(\zeta, z) \right| \leq 2\pi \max_{|\zeta - z| = \epsilon} |g(\zeta)|,$$

valid for all $z \in \mathbb{C}, \epsilon > 0$.
