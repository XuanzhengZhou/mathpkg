# Proof of Pseudoconcave Sets Give Maximum Modulus Algebras

## Theorem Statement (Theorem 25.2)

Let $X$ be a pseudoconcave set contained in the open bidisk $\{|z| < 1, |w| < 1\} \subseteq \mathbb{C}^2$. Denote by $A$ the algebra of polynomials in $z$ and $w$ restricted to $X$. Putting $D = \{|z| < 1\}$ and $\pi : (z, w) \mapsto z$, then $(A, X, D, \pi)$ is a maximum modulus algebra.

## Source of the Proof

This theorem was proved by John Wermer in 1980 and published in reference [We12]. The book *Several Complex Variables and Banach Algebras* (GTM 35, 3rd Edition), Chapter 25, presents this result as part of the historical survey "Historical Comments and Recent Developments" and does not reproduce the full proof. The reader is referred to [We12] for the complete argument.

## Context and Key Ideas

The proof builds on the theory developed in Chapters 9--11 of the same book:

1. **Maximum modulus algebra** (Chapter 11): A quadruple $(A, X, D, \pi)$ where $X \subseteq \mathbb{C}^n$ is compact, $D \subseteq \mathbb{C}$ is a domain, $\pi: X \to D$ is a continuous projection, and $A$ is a uniform algebra on $X$ such that for every $f \in A$, the function $\lambda \mapsto \max_{\pi^{-1}(\lambda)} |f|$ is subharmonic on $D$.

2. **Pseudoconcave set** (Definition 25.1): A relatively closed subset $E$ of a domain $\Omega \subseteq \mathbb{C}^n$ is called *pseudoconcave* in $\Omega$ if $\Omega \setminus E$ is pseudoconvex. Equivalently, the complement admits a plurisubharmonic exhaustion function.

3. **Local Maximum Modulus Principle** (Theorem 9.3, Rossi): This principle provides a mechanism to generate examples of maximum modulus algebras from pseudoconvex/pseudoconcave configurations.

4. **Slodkowski's generalization** (1981, [Sl1]): Z. Slodkowski later proved the stronger result: for $X$ a relatively closed set in a cylinder domain $G \times \mathbb{C} \subseteq \mathbb{C}^2$, with $A$ the restriction of polynomials and $\pi: (z,w) \mapsto z$, the triple $(A, X, G, \pi)$ is a maximum modulus algebra **if and only if** $X$ is pseudoconcave in $G \times \mathbb{C}$.

## Proof Sketch

While the complete proof appears in [We12], the essential strategy is as follows:

1. **Exhaustion function**: Since $X$ is pseudoconcave in the bidisk, the complement $\{|z|<1, |w|<1\} \setminus X$ is pseudoconvex. Consequently, there exists a plurisubharmonic exhaustion function $\psi(z,w)$ for this complement.

2. **Subharmonicity of fiber-maxima**: For any polynomial $P(z,w) \in A$, consider the function
   $$M_P(\lambda) = \max\{|P(z,w)| : (z,w) \in X,\ z = \lambda\}, \quad \lambda \in D.$$
   The pseudoconcavity of $X$ ensures that $\log M_P(\lambda)$ is subharmonic on $D$, which is precisely the defining condition for $(A, X, D, \pi)$ to be a maximum modulus algebra.

3. **Boundary behavior**: The pseudoconcavity condition controls the behavior of $M_P(\lambda)$ as $\lambda$ approaches $\partial D$, guaranteeing that the maximum modulus property holds globally on $D$.
