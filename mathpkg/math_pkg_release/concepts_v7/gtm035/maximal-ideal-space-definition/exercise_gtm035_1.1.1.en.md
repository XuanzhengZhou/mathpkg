# Exercise 1.1

Let $X$ be a compact Hausdorff space. Show that to every maximal ideal $M$ of $C(X)$ corresponds a point $x_0 \in X$ such that

$$M = \{ f \in C(X) \mid f(x_0) = 0 \}.$$

Thus $\mathcal{M}(C(X)) = X$, where $\mathcal{M}(C(X))$ denotes the maximal ideal space of the Banach algebra $C(X)$.

**Hint:** If $M$ is a maximal ideal not of this form, then for each $x \in X$ there exists $f_x \in M$ with $f_x(x) \neq 0$. Use compactness and the fact that $M$ is a proper ideal to derive a contradiction.
