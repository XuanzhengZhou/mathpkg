# Exercise 15.2

Let $\mathfrak{A} = C(I)$, where $I$ is the unit interval $[0, 1]$. Verify Theorem 15.3 (the Arens-Royden Theorem) in this case.

That is, show that

$$\mathfrak{A}^{-1} / \exp \mathfrak{A} \cong H^1(I, \mathbb{Z}).$$

You may take as given that $H^1(I, \mathbb{Z}) \cong \{0\}$.

*Hint:* Show that every nonvanishing continuous function on $I$ has a continuous logarithm. Use the fact that $I$ is contractible (simply connected) to lift any $f \in C(I)^{-1}$ to its logarithm via the covering map $\exp : \mathbb{C} \to \mathbb{C}^*$. Conclude that $\mathfrak{A}^{-1} = \exp \mathfrak{A}$, hence the quotient is trivial.
