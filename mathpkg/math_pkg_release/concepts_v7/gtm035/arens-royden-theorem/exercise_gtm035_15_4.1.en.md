# Exercise 15.1

Let $\mathfrak{A} = C(\Gamma)$, where $\Gamma$ is the unit circle. Verify Theorem 15.3 (the Arens-Royden Theorem) in this case.

That is, show that

$$\mathfrak{A}^{-1} / \exp \mathfrak{A} \cong H^1(\Gamma, \mathbb{Z}).$$

You may take as given that $H^1(\Gamma, \mathbb{Z}) \cong \mathbb{Z}$.

*Hint:* An element $f \in C(\Gamma)^{-1}$ is a nonvanishing continuous function on the circle. Consider its winding number. Show that two functions have the same winding number if and only if they differ by an exponential. Map $f$ to its winding number to obtain the isomorphism.
