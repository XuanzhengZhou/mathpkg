# Exercise 4.4

Prove Lemma 4.4: Under the wedge product $\wedge$, $\mathcal{G}(V)$ is an associative algebra with identity.

Specifically, show that:
1. For $\tau \in \wedge^k(V)$, $\sigma \in \wedge^l(V)$, $\rho \in \wedge^m(V)$: $(\tau \wedge \sigma) \wedge \rho = \tau \wedge (\sigma \wedge \rho)$.
2. The element $1 \in \wedge^0(V) = \mathbb{C}$ satisfies $1 \wedge \tau = \tau \wedge 1 = \tau$ for all $\tau \in \mathcal{G}(V)$.

**Hint:** It suffices to verify associativity on basis elements $e_{i_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge e_{i_k}$. The definition of the wedge product as an alternating multilinear form ensures that the iterated product is well-defined regardless of parenthesization.
