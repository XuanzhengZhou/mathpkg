# Proof of the Local Maximum Modulus Principle for Banach Algebras

**Theorem 9.3 (Local Maximum Modulus Principle).** Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a Banach algebra and fix $x \in \mathcal{M} \setminus \check{S}(\mathfrak{A})$. Let $U$ be a neighborhood of $x$ with $U \subset \mathcal{M} \setminus \check{S}(\mathfrak{A})$. Then for all $f \in \mathfrak{A}$,

$$|\hat{f}(x)| \leq \max_{\partial U} |\hat{f}|.$$

*Proof.* This is a deep theorem whose proof occupies the remainder of Chapter 9 (Sections 9.3--9.7). The proof proceeds by reducing to the case of finitely generated algebras and using the Oka extension theorem together with several analytic lemmas.

**Outline of the proof.** Without loss of generality, we may assume $\mathfrak{A}$ is generated by finitely many elements $g_1, \ldots, g_n$. Put $\sigma = \sigma(g_1, \ldots, g_n) \subset \mathbb{C}^n$. Let $G: \mathcal{M} \to \mathbb{C}^n$ be the Gelfand map $G(M) = (\hat{g}_1(M), \ldots, \hat{g}_n(M))$.

The key idea is to show that for each $f \in \mathfrak{A}$, the function $\hat{f} \circ G^{-1}$ is holomorphic on the interior of the polynomial hull $\hat{\sigma}$ in a certain sense. This "analytic structure" in $\mathcal{M} \setminus \check{S}(\mathfrak{A})$ is established through a series of lemmas:

- **Lemma 9.4** ($\bar{\partial}$-Solution on Union of Two Open Sets): Given a decomposition problem on $U_1 \cup U_2$, one can solve a $\bar{\partial}$-equation to obtain holomorphic functions whose difference matches a given holomorphic function on $U_1 \cap U_2$.

- **Lemma 9.5** (Holomorphic Separation via $\log z_1/z_1$): Under a separation condition involving $\operatorname{Re} z_1 < 0$ on the overlap, the function $\log z_1/z_1$ can be represented as a difference of functions holomorphic on the respective open sets.

- **Lemma 9.6** (Extension of Peak Functions): If a function peaks on a closed set $T$ (value 1 on $T$, modulus $< 1$ on a neighborhood minus $T$), then it can be extended to a global peak function.

Using these lemmas together with the Oka Extension Theorem (Theorem 7.6), one constructs, for any point $x \in \mathcal{M} \setminus \check{S}(\mathfrak{A})$, a holomorphic function $\Phi$ defined on a neighborhood of $\hat{\sigma}$ such that for each $f \in \mathfrak{A}$, $\hat{f} = \Phi_f \circ G$ on $\mathcal{M}$, where $\Phi_f$ is holomorphic on a neighborhood of $\hat{\sigma}$. The classical maximum modulus principle for holomorphic functions on $\mathbb{C}^n$ then yields the local maximum modulus inequality (3).

The full details can be found in Sections 9.4--9.7 of the book. $\square$
