# Proof of Linking Number Theorem for Polynomial Hulls

**Theorem (Linking Number Theorem).** Let $K$ be a compact polynomially convex set in $\mathbb{C}^n$. Then the complement $\mathbb{C}^n \setminus K$ has the homology of a wedge of spheres of dimension at most $n-1$. More precisely, the inclusion of the polynomial hull of a set $X$ into the ambient space is controlled by linking phenomena.

**Proof for the graph case.** We prove the version stated in the text concerning a smooth function $f: b\mathbb{B}_n \to \mathbb{C}$ and its graph $G(f) = \{(z, f(z)) : z \in b\mathbb{B}_n\}$ in $\mathbb{C}^{n+1}$. The claim is that the projection $\pi: \mathbb{C}^{n+1} \to \mathbb{C}^n$, $\pi(z, z_{n+1}) = z$, maps $\widehat{G(f)}$ onto $\overline{\mathbb{B}}_n$.

Let $\pi: \mathbb{C}^{n+1} \to \mathbb{C}^n$ be the projection. We argue by contradiction: suppose $\pi(\widehat{G(f)})$ does not contain $\mathbb{B}_n$.

Since the group of biholomorphisms of the ball acts transitively (see Appendix), we may apply an automorphism of $\mathbb{B}_n$ to assume $0 \notin \pi(\widehat{G(f)})$. Then

$$\widehat{G(f)} \subseteq (\mathbb{C}^n \setminus \{0\}) \times \mathbb{C}.$$

By Lemma 22.5, there exists a Runge domain $\Omega$ with

$$\widehat{G(f)} \subseteq \Omega \subseteq (\mathbb{C}^n \setminus \{0\}) \times \mathbb{C}.$$

We can approximate $f$ uniformly on $b\mathbb{B}_n$ by a smooth function $g$ such that $G(g) \subseteq \Omega$.

Now consider the Bochner-Martinelli form on $\mathbb{C}^n \setminus \{0\}$:

$$\omega = \sum_{j=1}^{n} (-1)^{j-1} \frac{\bar{z}_j}{|z|^{2n}} \, d\bar{z}_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \widehat{d\bar{z}_j} \wedge \cdots \wedge d\bar{z}_n \wedge dz_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge dz_n.$$

This is a closed $(2n-1)$-form on $\mathbb{C}^n \setminus \{0\}$ (i.e., $d\omega = 0$). Let $\pi_0: \Omega \to \mathbb{C}^n \setminus \{0\}$ be the restriction of $\pi$ to $\Omega$, and let $\sigma = \pi_0^*(\omega)$ be the pullback to $\Omega$. Then $d\sigma = \pi_0^*(d\omega) = 0$, so $\sigma$ is closed on $\Omega$.

Since $\Omega$ is a Runge domain, it satisfies $H_k(\Omega; G) = 0$ for $k \geq n$ (by Theorem 22.2, due to Andreotti-Narasimhan). In particular, $\Omega$ has trivial cohomology in degree $2n-1$ (since $2n-1 \geq n$ for $n \geq 2$). Therefore, the closed form $\sigma$ is exact on $\Omega$: there exists a $(2n-2)$-form $\eta$ on $\Omega$ with $d\eta = \sigma$.

Now, by Stokes' theorem and the definition of the Bochner-Martinelli integral, we have the representation

$$\int_{b\mathbb{B}_n} \omega = \int_{S^{2n-1}} \omega = c_n \neq 0,$$

where $c_n$ is a nonzero constant (the volume of the unit sphere). Pulling back via $G(g) \subseteq \Omega$ and using Stokes' theorem on $\Omega$ would give

$$0 \neq \int_{G(g)} \sigma = \int_{G(g)} d\eta = \int_{\partial G(g)} \eta = 0,$$

since $\partial G(g)$ is homologous to zero in $\Omega$. This contradiction shows that our assumption $0 \notin \pi(\widehat{G(f)})$ is false. Hence $\pi(\widehat{G(f)})$ must contain $\mathbb{B}_n$.

**General case.** For a general compact polynomially convex set $K$ in $\mathbb{C}^n$, the linking argument uses the fact that if a point $p$ in the complement of $K$ were "unlinked" from $K$ in a certain cohomological sense, one could use the Bochner-Martinelli form to construct a contradiction via Stokes' theorem and the vanishing of higher cohomology of Runge domains (Theorem 22.2). The precise formulation involves the linking number of the spheres $S^{2n-1}$ that appear in the Morse-theoretic decomposition of the complement $\mathbb{C}^n \setminus K$. $\square$
