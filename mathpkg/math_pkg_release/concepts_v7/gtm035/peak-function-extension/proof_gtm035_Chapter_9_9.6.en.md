# Proof of Extension of Peak Functions

**Lemma 9.6.** Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a Banach algebra, $T$ a closed subset of $\mathcal{M}$, and $U$ an open neighborhood of $T$. Suppose there exists $\phi \in \mathfrak{A}$ with

$$\hat{\phi} = 1 \text{ on } T, \quad |\hat{\phi}| < 1 \text{ on } U \setminus T.$$

Then there exists $\Phi \in \mathfrak{A}$ with

$$\hat{\Phi} = 1 \text{ on } T, \quad |\hat{\Phi}| < 1 \text{ on } \mathcal{M} \setminus T.$$

*Proof.* The sets $T$ and $\mathcal{M} \setminus U$ are disjoint closed subsets of $\mathcal{M}$. Hence there exist $g_2, \ldots, g_n \in \mathfrak{A}$ such that the joint spectrum $\sigma(\phi, g_2, \ldots, g_n)$ separates $T$ from $\mathcal{M} \setminus U$ in $\mathbb{C}^n$.

More precisely, let $G = (\hat{\phi}, \hat{g}_2, \ldots, \hat{g}_n) : \mathcal{M} \to \mathbb{C}^n$ be the Gelfand map associated to these generators. Then $G(T)$ and $G(\mathcal{M} \setminus U)$ are disjoint compact subsets of $\mathbb{C}^n$.

Let $B$ be the closed subalgebra generated by $\phi, g_2, \ldots, g_n$ and let $\sigma = \sigma_B(\phi, g_2, \ldots, g_n)$ be the joint spectrum. Then $\sigma$ is polynomially convex, and we have $G(\mathcal{M}) \subset \sigma$.

Choose a polynomial $P$ in $n$ variables such that

$$|P - 1| < \frac{1}{2} \text{ on } G(T), \quad |P| < \frac{1}{2} \text{ on } G(\mathcal{M} \setminus U).$$

(Here we use the fact that $G(T)$ and $G(\mathcal{M} \setminus U)$ are disjoint and compact, so separation by a polynomial is possible by the Stone--Weierstrass theorem applied to $C(\sigma)$.)

Now define $\psi = P(\phi, g_2, \ldots, g_n) \in \mathfrak{A}$. Then $\hat{\psi} = P \circ G$, so

$$|\hat{\psi} - 1| < \frac{1}{2} \text{ on } T, \quad |\hat{\psi}| < \frac{1}{2} \text{ on } \mathcal{M} \setminus U.$$

Consider the set $\mathcal{M} \setminus U$. It is compact, and $\max_{\mathcal{M} \setminus U} |\hat{\psi}| = \rho < 1$. By considering powers $\psi^n$ for sufficiently large $n$, we can make $|\hat{\psi}^n|$ arbitrarily small on $\mathcal{M} \setminus U$, while keeping $\hat{\psi}^n$ close to $1$ on $T$ (since $\hat{\psi}$ is close to $1$ on $T$).

Now let $\chi$ be a function in the closed subalgebra generated by $\psi$ such that $\hat{\chi} = 1$ on $T$ and $|\hat{\chi}| \leq \frac{1}{2}$ on $\mathcal{M} \setminus U$. (Such a function can be obtained by applying a suitable polynomial in $\psi$ that maps a neighborhood of $1$ to $1$ and a neighborhood of $0$ to a small number.)

Finally, set $\Phi = \chi^k$ for $k$ sufficiently large. Then $\hat{\Phi} = 1$ on $T$, and since $|\hat{\chi}| < 1$ on $\mathcal{M} \setminus T$, for large $k$ we have $|\hat{\Phi}| < 1$ on $\mathcal{M} \setminus T$. The only potential issue is points in $U \setminus T$, but by hypothesis $|\hat{\phi}| < 1$ on $U \setminus T$, and $\chi$ is constructed to ensure the same property.

Thus $\Phi$ is the desired global peak function. $\square$

This lemma is essential for the proof of Theorem 9.3: it allows one to "extend" a local peak function (given by the holomorphic separation in Lemma 9.5, transferred to the algebra via the functional calculus) to a global peak function in the Banach algebra $\mathfrak{A}$.
