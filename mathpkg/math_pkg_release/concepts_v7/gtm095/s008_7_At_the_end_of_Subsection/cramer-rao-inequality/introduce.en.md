The Cramér–Rao inequality (also called the information inequality) establishes a fundamental lower bound on the variance of any unbiased estimator. The bound is the reciprocal of the Fisher information $I_n(\theta)$. An unbiased estimator whose variance attains this lower bound is called efficient. This theorem provides a precise quantitative meaning to the notion of estimator optimality and is one of the cornerstones of mathematical statistics. The proof elegantly combines the Cauchy–Bunyakovskii (Cauchy–Schwarz) inequality with differentiation of the unbiasedness identity.
