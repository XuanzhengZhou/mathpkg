Let $X \geq 0$ be a nonnegative random variable. Then for any $\varepsilon > 0$ and any $\lambda > 0$,

$$P\{X \geq \varepsilon\} = P\{e^{\lambda X} \geq e^{\lambda \varepsilon}\} \leq E e^{\lambda(X - \varepsilon)}.$$

Since $\lambda > 0$ is arbitrary, it follows that

$$P\{X \geq \varepsilon\} \leq \inf_{\lambda > 0} E e^{\lambda(X - \varepsilon)}.$$
