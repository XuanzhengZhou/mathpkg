In the Bernoulli scheme $(\Omega, \mathcal{A}, \mathrm{P})$ with sample space
$$
\Omega = \{\omega : \omega = (x_1, \ldots, x_n), \; x_i = 0, 1\},
$$
and $\mathcal{A}$ the algebra of all subsets of $\Omega$, the family of probabilities $\{P_\theta, \theta \in \Theta\}$ with $\Theta = [0,1]$ and
$$
P_\theta(\omega) = \theta^{\sum x_i} (1-\theta)^{n - \sum x_i}
$$
is called a probabilistic-statistical model corresponding to $n$ independent trials with probability of success $\theta \in \Theta$. Any function $T_n = T_n(\omega)$ with values in $\Theta$ is called an estimator.
