The characteristic function of a probability distribution is its Fourier-Stieltjes transform. It is one of the principal tools of the analytic theory of probability, especially for proving limit theorems such as the central limit theorem. For distributions with a density, it reduces to the ordinary Fourier transform. The characteristic function uniquely determines the distribution, and its derivatives at zero yield the moments of the distribution.
