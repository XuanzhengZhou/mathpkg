Given an orthonormal system of random variables, the best mean-square linear estimator of a target variable is obtained by projecting it onto the span of the system. The optimal coefficients are exactly the inner products of the target with each basis element, analogous to Fourier coefficients. The residual error is the squared norm of the target minus the sum of squared coefficients. This is the finite-dimensional counterpart of the general projection theorem in Hilbert space.
