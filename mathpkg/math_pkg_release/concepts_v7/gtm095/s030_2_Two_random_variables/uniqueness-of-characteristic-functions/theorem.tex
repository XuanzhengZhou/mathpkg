Let $F$ and $G$ be distribution functions on $\mathbb{R}$ having the same characteristic function, i.e.,

$$\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{itx} \, dF(x) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{itx} \, dG(x)$$

for all $t \in \mathbb{R}$. Then $F(x) \equiv G(x)$ for all $x \in \mathbb{R}$.

In other words, the characteristic function uniquely determines the distribution function. Equivalently, the Fourier-Stieltjes transform is injective on the class of probability distribution functions.
