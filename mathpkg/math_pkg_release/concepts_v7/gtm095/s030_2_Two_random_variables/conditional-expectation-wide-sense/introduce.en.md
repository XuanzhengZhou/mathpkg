The wide-sense conditional expectation is the best linear mean-square estimator of one random variable in terms of others. It is computed as the orthogonal projection onto the linear span of the conditioning variables. In the finite case it reduces to a finite Fourier sum; for countable systems it is defined as an $L^2$ limit. The terminology reflects its relationship with the usual conditional expectation: when all Borel-measurable estimators are allowed (not just linear ones), the optimal estimator is the ordinary conditional expectation $\mathsf{E}(\xi \mid \eta_1, \ldots, \eta_n)$.
