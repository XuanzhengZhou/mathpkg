A countable orthonormal basis is a complete orthonormal system: every element of the closed linear manifold can be expressed as a convergent Fourier series. Completeness is equivalent to Parseval's identity. A separable Hilbert space admits a countable orthonormal basis. Important examples in probability include normalized Hermite polynomials for Gaussian measure, Poisson-Charlier polynomials for Poisson measure, Rademacher functions, and the Haar system for Lebesgue measure on $[0,1)$.
