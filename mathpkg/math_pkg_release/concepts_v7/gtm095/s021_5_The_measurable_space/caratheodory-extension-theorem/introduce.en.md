Caratheodory's Extension Theorem is one of the most important results in measure theory. It guarantees that a $\sigma$-finite, $\sigma$-additive set function defined on an algebra of sets can be uniquely extended to a measure on the $\sigma$-algebra generated by that algebra. This theorem is the primary tool for constructing probability measures: one defines a candidate measure on a convenient algebra (e.g., finite unions of intervals) and then extends it to the full Borel $\sigma$-algebra. The proof uses outer measure techniques and the notion of Caratheodory measurability.
