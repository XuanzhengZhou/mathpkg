# Exercise 6

Let $\mu$ be a finitely additive measure on an algebra $\mathcal{A}$, and let the sets $A_1, A_2, \ldots \in \mathcal{A}$ be pairwise disjoint and satisfy $A = \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} A_i \in \mathcal{A}$. Show that

$$\mu(A) \geq \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} \mu(A_i).$$

That is, a finitely additive measure is countably superadditive.
