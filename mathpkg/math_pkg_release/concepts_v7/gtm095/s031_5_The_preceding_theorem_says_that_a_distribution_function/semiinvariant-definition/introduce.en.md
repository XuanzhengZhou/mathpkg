Semi-invariants (also called cumulants) are the coefficients in the power series expansion of $\log \varphi_\xi(t)$, where $\varphi_\xi$ is the characteristic function. They are named for their key property: for independent random vectors $\xi$ and $\eta$, the semi-invariants are additive: $s_{\xi+\eta}^{(\nu)} = s_\xi^{(\nu)} + s_\eta^{(\nu)}$. In the one-dimensional case, the first semi-invariant is the mean, the second is the variance, and all higher semi-invariants of a Gaussian distribution vanish.
