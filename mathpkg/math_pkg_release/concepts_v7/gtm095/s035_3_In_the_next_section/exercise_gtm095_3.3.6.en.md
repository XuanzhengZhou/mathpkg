# Exercise 6

Let $\xi_1, \xi_2, \ldots$ and $\eta_1, \eta_2, \ldots$ be sequences of random variables such that $\xi_n$ and $\eta_n$ are independent for each $n$. Suppose that $\xi_n \xrightarrow{d} \xi$, $\eta_n \xrightarrow{d} \eta$ as $n \to \infty$, where $\xi$ and $\eta$ are independent. Prove that the sequence of two-dimensional random variables $(\xi_n, \eta_n)$ converges in distribution to $(\xi, \eta)$.

Let $f = f(x, y)$ be a continuous function. Verify that the sequence $f(\xi_n, \eta_n)$ converges in distribution to $f(\xi, \eta)$.
