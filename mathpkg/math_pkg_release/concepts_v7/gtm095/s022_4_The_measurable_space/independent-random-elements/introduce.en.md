This definition extends independence from events and random variables to arbitrary random elements taking values in measurable spaces. The defining property — that the joint distribution factorizes as the product of marginals — captures the probabilistic meaning of "no information transfer." Mutual independence of an arbitrary family reduces to independence of every finite subfamily, which is sufficient for all practical constructions in probability theory.
