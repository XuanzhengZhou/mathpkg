When random variables possess a joint density, independence is equivalent to the factorization of the joint density as the product of the marginal densities. The proof uses Fubini's theorem to show that factorization of densities implies factorization of the joint distribution function, which by the theorem of Section 5 is necessary and sufficient for independence. The converse also holds: independence implies the joint distribution function factorizes, and differentiating yields the density factorization.
