Fubini's Theorem justifies the interchange of the order of integration for product measures. When the absolute integral of a jointly measurable function with respect to the product measure is finite, the iterated integrals exist almost everywhere, are measurable, and equal the double integral. The proof proceeds by verifying the result for indicator functions of measurable rectangles, extending to general $\mathcal{F}_1 \otimes \mathcal{F}_2$-measurable functions via monotone approximation, and then handling the signed case via decomposition into positive and negative parts.
