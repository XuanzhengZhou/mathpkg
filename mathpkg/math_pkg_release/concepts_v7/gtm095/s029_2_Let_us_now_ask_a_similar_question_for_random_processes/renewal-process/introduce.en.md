A renewal process is a counting process $N_t$ that records the number of events occurring up to time $t$, where inter-arrival times $\sigma_n$ are i.i.d. positive random variables. The key relation $\{N_t \geq n\} = \{T_n \leq t\}$ reduces the study of $N$ to the study of partial sums $T_n = \sigma_1 + \cdots + \sigma_n$. The Poisson process is the special case where $\sigma_n$ are exponentially distributed. Renewal processes are constructed constructively from a sequence of i.i.d. random variables with minimal use of abstract probabilistic structures.
