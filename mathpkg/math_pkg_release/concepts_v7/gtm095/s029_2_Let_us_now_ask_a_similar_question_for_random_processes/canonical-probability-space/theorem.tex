The probability space $(\mathbb{R}^T, \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R}^T), P)$ constructed in Kolmogorov's existence theorem, where $P$ is the unique probability measure on the product $\sigma$-algebra $\mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R}^T)$ extending the consistent family of finite-dimensional distributions, is called the \textbf{canonical probability space} for the random process.
