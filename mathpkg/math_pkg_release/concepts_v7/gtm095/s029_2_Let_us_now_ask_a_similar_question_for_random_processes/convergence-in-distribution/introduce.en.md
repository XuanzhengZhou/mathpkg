Convergence in distribution (weak convergence / convergence in law) is the weakest mode of convergence for random variables. Unlike the other modes, it concerns only the distributions, not values on a common probability space. The definition requires $E f(\xi_n) \to E f(\xi)$ for all bounded continuous $f$; equivalently, $F_{\xi_n}(x) \to F_\xi(x)$ at continuity points of $F_\xi$. Convergence in distribution is the natural mode for the central limit theorem.
