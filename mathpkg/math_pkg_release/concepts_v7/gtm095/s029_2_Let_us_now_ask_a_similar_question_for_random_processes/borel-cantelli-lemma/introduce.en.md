The Borel–Cantelli lemma is a fundamental result linking probability summability to the occurrence of infinitely many events. Part (a): if $\sum P(A_n) < \infty$, then with probability 1 only finitely many $A_n$ occur. Part (b) requires independence: if $\sum P(A_n) = \infty$, then infinitely many occur with probability 1. The lemma is essential for proving almost sure convergence results including the strong law of large numbers, and underpins many zero-one laws.
