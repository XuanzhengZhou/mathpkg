The dual bounded-Lipschitz metric $\|\cdot\|_{BL}^*$ provides a second metrization of weak convergence on the space of probability measures. Unlike the Lévy–Prokhorov metric, which is defined geometrically via $\varepsilon$-neighborhoods, the BL* metric is defined analytically as the supremum of integral differences over the unit ball of the $BL$ function space. Lemma 2 (that weak convergence needs only be checked against $BL$ functions) is the key ingredient for proving the equivalence.
