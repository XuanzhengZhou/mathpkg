The capture-recapture method, introduced by Laplace in 1786 to estimate the population of France, is a classic statistical technique for estimating an unknown population size. By marking a sample, releasing it, and then observing how many marked individuals appear in a second sample, the hypergeometric distribution provides a likelihood function whose maximizer yields the simple estimator $\hat{N} = \lfloor Mn/m \rfloor$. This method remains widely used in ecology and epidemiology.
