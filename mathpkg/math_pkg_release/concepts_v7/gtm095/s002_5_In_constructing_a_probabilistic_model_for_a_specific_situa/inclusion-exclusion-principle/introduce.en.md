The inclusion-exclusion principle provides an exact formula for the probability of a union of events in terms of probabilities of intersections. By alternating between adding and subtracting probabilities of $k$-fold intersections, it corrects for overcounting. This principle is fundamental in both probability theory and combinatorics, underpinning formulas for the matching problem, derangements, and Bonferroni inequalities.
