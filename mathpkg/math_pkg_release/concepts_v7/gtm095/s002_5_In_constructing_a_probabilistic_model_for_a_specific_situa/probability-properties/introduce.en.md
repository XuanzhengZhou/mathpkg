These elementary properties follow directly from the definition of a probability measure. They formalize the intuitive ideas that the impossible event has probability zero, the certain event has probability one, probabilities are additive for disjoint events, and the probability of a complement is one minus the original. The general union formula $\text{P}(A \cup B) = \text{P}(A) + \text{P}(B) - \text{P}(A \cap B)$ corrects for double-counting the intersection.
