Euler numbers arise in the series expansions of $\sec x$ and $\text{sech}\, x$, and play a role analogous to Bernoulli numbers in alternating sums. Like Bernoulli numbers, all odd-indexed Euler numbers are zero. Their exponential generating function $2e^x/(e^{2x}+1)$ is related to the hyperbolic secant, reflecting their appearance in the Maclaurin series $\text{sech}\, x = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} e_{2n} x^{2n}/(2n)!$.
