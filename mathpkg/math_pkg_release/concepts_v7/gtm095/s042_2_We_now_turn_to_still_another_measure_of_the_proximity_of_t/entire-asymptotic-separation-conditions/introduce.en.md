This theorem provides the dual characterization of entire asymptotic separation $(\tilde{P}^n) \triangle (P^n)$ in terms of Hellinger integrals, paralleling Theorem 1 for contiguity. Whereas contiguity corresponds to $\liminf_n H(\alpha; P^n, \tilde{P}^n) = 0$, entire asymptotic separation corresponds to the stronger condition $\limsup_n H(\alpha; P^n, \tilde{P}^n) = 0$. The proof for (a) $\Rightarrow$ (b) uses the existence of a subsequence $n_k$ and sets $A^{n_k}$ with $P^{n_k}(A^{n_k}) \to 1$, $\tilde{P}^{n_k}(A^{n_k}) \to 0$, together with the identity $z^n + \tilde{z}^n = 2$, to bound $\tilde{P}^{n_k}(z^n_k \geq \varepsilon) \leq \tilde{P}^{n_k}(A^{n_k}) + (2/\varepsilon)P^{n_k}(\overline{A}^{n_k})$. For product measures, separation is equivalent to divergence of the series $\sum [1 - H(\alpha; P_k, \tilde{P}_k)]$, which is the natural counterpart to Kakutani's dichotomy for infinite product measures.
