# Exercise 6

Let $P$, $\tilde{P}$, and $Q$ be probability measures on $(\mathbb{R}, \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R}))$, and let $P * Q$ and $\tilde{P} * Q$ denote their convolutions (see Subsection 4 in Sect. 8, Chap. 2). Prove that

$$\|P * Q - \tilde{P} * Q\| \leq \|P - \tilde{P}\|.$$
