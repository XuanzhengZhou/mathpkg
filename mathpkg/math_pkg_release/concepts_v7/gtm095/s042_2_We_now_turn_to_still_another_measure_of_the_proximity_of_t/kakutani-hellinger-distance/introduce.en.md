The Kakutani--Hellinger distance is a measure of proximity between two probability measures, closely related to the total variation distance. It is defined via the square roots of Radon--Nikodym derivatives with respect to any dominating measure. The Hellinger integral $H(P,\tilde{P}) = E_Q\sqrt{z\tilde{z}}$ captures the overlap between the two measures, and the distance is given by $\rho^2(P,\tilde{P}) = 1 - H(P,\tilde{P})$. This distance plays a fundamental role in asymptotic statistics, particularly in contiguity theory and the study of absolute continuity and singularity of product measures.
