Kolmogorov's axioms, published in his 1933 "Foundations of the Theory of Probability," established probability theory as a rigorous mathematical discipline on par with geometry and algebra. The key insight was to treat probability as a normalized measure, enabling the use of Lebesgue's integration theory. This framework handles both finite and infinite sample spaces uniformly and provides the foundation for all modern probability theory, from stochastic processes to statistical inference.
