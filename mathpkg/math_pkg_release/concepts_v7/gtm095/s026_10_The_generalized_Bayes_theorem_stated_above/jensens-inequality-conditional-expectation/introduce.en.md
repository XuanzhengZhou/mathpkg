Jensen's inequality for conditional expectations states that a convex function of a conditional expectation is bounded above by the conditional expectation of the convex function. This is a conditional analogue of the classical Jensen inequality and is a fundamental tool for proving inequalities involving conditional moments. It is used, for instance, in the proof of the Rao-Blackwell theorem to show that conditioning an estimator on a sufficient sigma-algebra cannot increase its mean squared error.
