Conditional variance measures the residual variability of a random variable after accounting for information contained in another variable. The law of total variance decomposes the unconditional variance into the expectation of the conditional variance plus the variance of the conditional expectation, corresponding to within-group and between-group variation respectively. This decomposition is a cornerstone of variance analysis in hierarchical models.
