Let $\chi_n = +\sqrt{\chi_n^2}$ where $\chi_n^2$ has the chi-square distribution with $n$ degrees of freedom. Then $\chi_n$ has the chi distribution with $n$ degrees of freedom, with density

$$f_{\chi_n}(x) = \begin{cases} \dfrac{2 x^{n-1} e^{-x^2/2}}{2^{n/2} \Gamma(n/2)}, & x \geq 0, \\[8pt] 0, & x < 0. \end{cases}$$

When $n = 2$, this is called the Rayleigh distribution.
