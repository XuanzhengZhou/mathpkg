Order statistics are the sorted values of a random sample. The joint density formula $n! \prod f(x^i)$ on the ordered region reflects that any of the $n!$ permutations of the sample is equally likely to produce a given ordering. The marginal density of the $k$th order statistic involves the binomial coefficient and powers of $F$ and $1-F$, reflecting the combinatorial fact that $k-1$ observations must fall below $x$, one at $x$, and $n-k$ above $x$. Order statistics are fundamental to nonparametric statistics, robust estimation, and extreme value theory.
