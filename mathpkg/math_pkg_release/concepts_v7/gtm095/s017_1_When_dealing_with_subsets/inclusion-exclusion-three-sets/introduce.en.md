The inclusion–exclusion principle for three sets extends the two-set version by alternately adding and subtracting the cardinalities of intersections of increasing order. For three sets, this means adding all single-set sizes, subtracting all pairwise intersections, and adding back the triple intersection. The alternating pattern (inclusion–exclusion–inclusion) generalizes to any number of sets.
