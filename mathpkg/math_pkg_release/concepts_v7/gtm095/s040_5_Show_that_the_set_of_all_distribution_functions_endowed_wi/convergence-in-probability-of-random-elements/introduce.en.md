Convergence in probability of random elements generalizes the classical notion to random elements taking values in a general metric space $(E, \mathcal{E}, \rho)$. A sequence $X_n$ converges in probability to $X$ if, for every $\varepsilon > 0$, the probability that the distance between $X_n$ and $X$ exceeds $\varepsilon$ tends to zero as $n \to \infty$. Unlike convergence in distribution, convergence in probability requires all random elements to be defined on the same underlying probability space $(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \mathbb{P})$.
