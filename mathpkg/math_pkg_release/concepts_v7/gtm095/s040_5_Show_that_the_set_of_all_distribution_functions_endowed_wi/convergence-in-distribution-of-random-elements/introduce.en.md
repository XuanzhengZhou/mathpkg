Convergence in distribution (also called convergence in law) of random elements generalizes the classical notion of convergence in distribution of random variables to random elements taking values in a general metric space $E$. A sequence of random elements $X_n$ converges in distribution to $X$ if the sequence of their induced probability measures $P_n$ on $(E, \mathcal{E})$ converges weakly to the probability measure $P$ induced by $X$. Unlike convergence in probability or almost sure convergence, convergence in distribution does not require the random elements to be defined on the same probability space; it concerns only the behavior of their distributions.
