A cyclic group is one generated by a single element — all its elements are powers of one generator. Theorem 28 gives a crisp characterization: a finite Abelian group is cyclic iff for each prime divisor $p$ of its order, there are exactly $p$ elements with $A^p = E$ (equivalently, each Sylow $p$-subgroup is cyclic). This is equivalent to the basis number $e(p)$ being $1$ for every prime $p$. Cyclic groups are the simplest and most fundamental class of groups.
