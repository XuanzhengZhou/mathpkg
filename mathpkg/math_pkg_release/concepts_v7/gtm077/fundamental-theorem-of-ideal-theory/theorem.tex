Every ideal in $K$ different from $(0)$ and $(1)$ can be written in one and only one way (except for order) as a product of prime ideals:
$$\mathfrak{a} = \mathfrak{p}_1 \mathfrak{p}_2 \cdots \mathfrak{p}_r.$$
A prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ is an ideal different from $(1)$ with the property: if a product $\alpha\beta$ belongs to $\mathfrak{p}$, then at least one factor belongs to $\mathfrak{p}$.
