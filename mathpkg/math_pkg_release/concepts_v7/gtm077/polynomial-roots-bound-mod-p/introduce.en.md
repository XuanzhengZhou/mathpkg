Theorem 12, often called Lagrange's theorem for polynomial congruences, states that a non-zero polynomial of degree $k$ over the field $\mathbb{F}_p = \mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z}$ has at most $k$ distinct roots. The proof uses induction on the degree and the factor theorem (Theorems 10 and 11). The result fails for composite moduli, and is a special case of the fact that a polynomial over a field has at most its degree many roots.
