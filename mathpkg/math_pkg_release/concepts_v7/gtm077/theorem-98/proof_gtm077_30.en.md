# Proof of Theorem 98

By Theorem 97, $\mathfrak{a}^h$ is equal to a principal ideal $(\omega)$. The number $A = \sqrt[h]{\omega}$ has the asserted property.

For if $\alpha$ is a number in $\mathfrak{a}$, then $\alpha^h$ belongs to $\mathfrak{a}^h$ and therefore $\alpha^h/\omega$ is an integer. Hence $\alpha/\sqrt[h]{\omega} = \alpha/A$ is also an integer.

Conversely, if $\alpha$ is a number of the field such that $\alpha/A$ is an integer, then $\alpha^h/\omega$ is an integer, that is, $\alpha^h \in \mathfrak{a}^h$, which implies $\alpha \in \mathfrak{a}$ (by the unique factorization into prime ideals).

Thus the numbers of $\mathfrak{a}$ are identical with those numbers of the field $K$ which are divisible by $A$.
