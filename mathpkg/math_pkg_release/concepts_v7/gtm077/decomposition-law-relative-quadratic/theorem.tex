Let $K = k(\sqrt{\mu})$ be a relative quadratic extension, where $\mu$ is an integer of $k$. For a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ of $k$ not dividing $2\mu$, the decomposition in $K$ is determined by the Legendre symbol in $k$: $\mathfrak{p}$ splits iff $\mu$ is a quadratic residue modulo $\mathfrak{p}$, remains prime (inert) iff $\mu$ is a nonresidue, and ramifies iff $\mathfrak{p} \mid \mu$. The general case includes detailed treatment of prime ideals dividing $2$.
