This is Theorem 126 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke.

If $\mathfrak{p}_1$ runs through the distinct prime ideals of degree one in $k$, then, for $s > 1$,

$$\sum_{\mathfrak{p}_1} \frac{1}{N(\mathfrak{p}_1)^s} = \log \frac{1}{s-1} + g_1(s),$$
