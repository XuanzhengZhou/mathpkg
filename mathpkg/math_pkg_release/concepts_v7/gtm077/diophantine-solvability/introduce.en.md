Theorem 3 generalizes the Bezout identity to linear forms in arbitrarily many variables. It states that the set of all integral values taken by a linear form $a_1 x_1 + \cdots + a_n x_n$ is exactly the set of multiples of the greatest common divisor of its coefficients. This provides a complete criterion for the solvability of linear Diophantine equations in integers.
