The generalized associativity lemma extends the binary associative law to products of arbitrarily many elements: in a group, all ways of parenthesizing a product of $k$ elements yield the same result. This justifies writing products like $A_1 A_2 \cdots A_k$ without parentheses. The proof is a straightforward induction on the number of factors.
