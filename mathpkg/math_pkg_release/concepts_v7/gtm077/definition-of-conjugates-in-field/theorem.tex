If $n$ is the relative degree of $K(\theta)$ with respect to $k$ and $\alpha = g(\theta)$ is a number of $K(\theta)$ of degree $n/q$, then the system of $n$ numbers $\alpha_i = g(\theta_i)$ ($i = 1, 2, \ldots, n$) is called the conjugates of $\alpha$ in the field $K(\theta)$ with respect to $k$. These are the conjugates of $\alpha$ with respect to $k$, each taken $q$ times.
