The Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic states that every integer greater than $1$ factors uniquely into primes. This is the central structural theorem of elementary number theory, making primes the irreducible building blocks of the multiplicative structure of $\mathbb{Z}$. The proof uses the prime divisor property ($p \mid ab \implies p \mid a$ or $p \mid b$), which itself follows from Bezout's identity. The theorem immediately yields formulas for GCD and LCM in terms of prime exponents.
