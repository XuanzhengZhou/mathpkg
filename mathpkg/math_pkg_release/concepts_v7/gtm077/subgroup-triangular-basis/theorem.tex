If $\mathfrak{G}$ is a torsion-free Abelian group with finite basis $B_1, \ldots, B_n$, then every subgroup $\mathfrak{U}$ of $\mathfrak{G}$ with finite index has a basis $U_1, \ldots, U_n$ of the triangular form:
$$U_1 = B_1^{r_{11}} B_2^{r_{12}} \cdots B_n^{r_{1n}},$$
$$U_2 = B_2^{r_{22}} \cdots B_n^{r_{2n}},$$
$$\vdots$$
$$U_n = B_n^{r_{nn}},$$
with $r_{ii} \neq 0$ for all $i$. The index $[\mathfrak{G} : \mathfrak{U}] = |r_{11} r_{22} \cdots r_{nn}|$.
