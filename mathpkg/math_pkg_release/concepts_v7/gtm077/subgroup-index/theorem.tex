The index of a subgroup $\mathfrak{U}$ in a group $\mathfrak{G}$, denoted $[\mathfrak{G} : \mathfrak{U}]$, is the number of distinct cosets of $\mathfrak{U}$ in $\mathfrak{G}$. For finite groups,
$$[\mathfrak{G} : \mathfrak{U}] = \frac{|\mathfrak{G}|}{|\mathfrak{U}|}.$$
The index may be finite even when both $\mathfrak{G}$ and $\mathfrak{U}$ are infinite.
