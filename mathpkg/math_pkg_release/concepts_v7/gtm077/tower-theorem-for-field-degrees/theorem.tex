If $\alpha$ is a number of degree $m$ over $k$ and $\beta$ is a number of degree $q$ over $K(\alpha; k)$, then the field $K(\alpha, \beta; k)$ has degree $mq$ over $k$. Furthermore, the $mq$ conjugates of a generating number decompose into $m$ sequences of $q$ elements each; the $q$ numbers of a sequence are the conjugates with respect to $K(\alpha_i; k)$, where $\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_m$ are the conjugates of $\alpha$.
