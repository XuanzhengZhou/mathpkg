In a Galois extension, all prime ideals dividing a given rational prime are conjugate. The field degree factorizes as $n = r \cdot f$ where $r$ is the number of prime ideal factors and $f$ is their common residue degree. The Galois group acts transitively on the prime ideals above $p$.
