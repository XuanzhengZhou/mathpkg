If $\mathfrak{a} = (\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_r)$ and $\mathfrak{b} = (\beta_1, \ldots, \beta_s)$, their product is defined as
$$\mathfrak{a}\mathfrak{b} = (\alpha_1\beta_1, \ldots, \alpha_i\beta_k, \ldots, \alpha_r\beta_s),$$
the ideal generated by all products $\alpha_i\beta_k$. Multiplication is commutative, associative, and independent of the choice of generating sets.
