# Proof of Theorem 92

By the definition of $f$, the equation $f_1 = f$ follows from $p^{f_1} \equiv 1 \pmod{m}$ and $f_1 \leq f$.

Since, by Theorem 91, the conjugate prime ideals divide $p$ only to the first power, then, by the remark in §29, $p$ splits into exactly $h/f$ factors and everything is thereby proved.

Accordingly, the field $K(\zeta)$ is closely related to the group of residue classes mod $m$ in the field $k(1)$. Primes which belong to the same residue class mod $m$ split in $K(\zeta)$ in exactly the same way—except for finitely many exceptions. Later, in §43, we will also show that the field $K(\zeta)$ has degree $\varphi(m)$, thus the same degree as the group $\Re(m)$ in $k(1)$.
