This is Theorem 150 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke. For each rational integer $n$  $$G(n, d) = \chi(n)G(1, d), \quad c_n = \chi(n) \frac{G(1, d)}{d}.$$  For if $n$ and $d$ are not coprime, then by (154) both sides of the first equation are equal to 0.... This result appears in the section §52 of the book and is part of the foundational development of algebraic number theory.
