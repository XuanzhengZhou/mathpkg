The Dedekind zeta-function of a number field $K$ is defined for $\Re(s) > 1$ by the Dirichlet series
$$\zeta_K(s) = \sum_{\mathfrak{a} \neq (0)} \frac{1}{N(\mathfrak{a})^s},$$
where $\mathfrak{a}$ runs through all nonzero ideals of $K$. It admits an Euler product over all prime ideals:
$$\zeta_K(s) = \prod_{\mathfrak{p}} \frac{1}{1 - N(\mathfrak{p})^{-s}}.$$
$\zeta_K(s)$ can be analytically continued to a meromorphic function on $\mathbb{C}$ with a simple pole at $s=1$.
