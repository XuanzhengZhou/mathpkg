If $f(x) \equiv g(x)g_1(x) \pmod{p}$, where $p$ is a prime, then each root of $f(x) \pmod{p}$ is a root of at least one of the two polynomials $g(x), g_1(x) \pmod{p}$.

If for the integer $a, f(a) \equiv 0 \pmod{p}$, then

$$g(a) \cdot g_1(a) \equiv f(a) \equiv 0 \pmod{p}.$$

If the prime $p$ divides the product $g(a) \cdot g_1(a)$, then it divides one of the two factors.
