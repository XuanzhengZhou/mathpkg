# Proof of Theorem 173

**Theorem 173.** In order that an odd ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ can be made into a primary number of the field by multiplication by a square of an ideal, it is necessary and sufficient that the conditions

$$Q(\varepsilon, \mathfrak{a}) = +1$$

are satisfied for each totally positive singular number $\varepsilon$.

**Proof.** The proof follows from Theorem 172 and the properties of the characters $Q(\varepsilon, \mathfrak{a})$.

**Necessity.** Suppose $\mathfrak{a} \mathfrak{m}^2 = \alpha$ with $\alpha$ a primary number. Then for any totally positive singular number $\varepsilon$, we have

$$Q(\varepsilon, \mathfrak{a}) = Q(\varepsilon, \alpha \mathfrak{m}^{-2}) = \left(\frac{\varepsilon}{\alpha}\right).$$

By the reciprocity law (Theorem 165 or its consequence), since $\varepsilon$ is totally positive and singular, $\left(\frac{\varepsilon}{\alpha}\right) = +1$ for primary $\alpha$. Hence $Q(\varepsilon, \mathfrak{a}) = +1$.

**Sufficiency.** Suppose $Q(\varepsilon, \mathfrak{a}) = +1$ for all totally positive singular numbers $\varepsilon$. By Theorem 172, the $e_0$ characters $Q(\omega_i, \cdot)$ form a complete system of characters of the group of strict class complexes. The condition $Q(\varepsilon, \mathfrak{a}) = +1$ for all totally positive singular $\varepsilon$ (which include the $p = e_0 + m - r_1$ independent such numbers) implies that $\mathfrak{a}$ belongs to the principal class complex in the group $\mathfrak{B}$. This means there exists a square of an ideal $\mathfrak{m}^2$ such that $\mathfrak{a} \mathfrak{m}^2$ is a principal ideal generated by a primary number. Thus $\mathfrak{a}$ can be made into a primary number by multiplication by a square of an ideal. ∎

Theorem 173, together with Theorem 171, is referred to as the *first supplementary theorem* of the reciprocity law.
