For two nonzero ideals $\mathfrak{a}$ and $\mathfrak{c}$, the divisibility $\mathfrak{c} \mid \mathfrak{a}$ (i.e., $\mathfrak{a} = \mathfrak{c}\mathfrak{b}$ for some ideal $\mathfrak{b}$) is equivalent to the set-theoretic inclusion $\mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathfrak{c}$. In particular, an ideal divides another iff it contains it.
