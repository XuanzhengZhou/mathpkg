Let $K$ be an algebraic number field of degree $n$. Among the $n$ conjugates, let $r_1$ be the number of real embeddings and $2r_2$ the number of complex embeddings (so $n = r_1 + 2r_2$). Then the group of units of $K$ is the direct product of the finite cyclic group of roots of unity in $K$ and a free Abelian group of rank
$$r = r_1 + r_2 - 1.$$
That is, there exist $r$ fundamental units $\varepsilon_1, \ldots, \varepsilon_r$ such that every unit can be written uniquely as
$$\varepsilon = \zeta \varepsilon_1^{a_1} \cdots \varepsilon_r^{a_r},$$
where $\zeta$ is a root of unity and $a_i \in \mathbb{Z}$.
