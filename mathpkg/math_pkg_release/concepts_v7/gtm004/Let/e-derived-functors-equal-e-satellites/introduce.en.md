This theorem identifies $\mathcal{E}$-derived functors with $\mathcal{E}$-satellites, establishing their universal property. The left satellites of a right $\mathcal{E}$-exact functor are computed by the same recipe as derived functors: resolve by $\mathcal{E}$-projectives, apply $H$, and take homology. This unifies the satellite approach (due to Buchsbaum and Cartan-Eilenberg) with the derived functor approach.
