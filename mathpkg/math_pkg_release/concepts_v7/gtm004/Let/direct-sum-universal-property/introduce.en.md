Proposition 3.2 states the universal property of the direct sum in the category of $\Lambda$-modules: given any family of homomorphisms $\psi_j$ from each summand $A_j$ to a fixed module $M$, there exists a unique homomorphism $\psi$ from the direct sum to $M$ making each triangle commute with the canonical injections $\iota_k$. This property characterizes the direct sum as a coproduct, meaning the direct sum construction is uniquely determined up to isomorphism by this mapping property.
