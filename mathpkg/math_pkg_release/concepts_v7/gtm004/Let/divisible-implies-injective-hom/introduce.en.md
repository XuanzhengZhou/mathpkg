Theorem 8.2 states that if $G$ is a divisible abelian group (such as $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$), then the coinduced module $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\Lambda, G)$ is an injective $\Lambda$-module. The proof uses Proposition 8.1 to convert a $\Lambda$-module homomorphism into $G$ into an abelian group homomorphism, then lifts using the injectivity of $G$ as an abelian group. This theorem guarantees the existence of sufficiently many injective modules in any module category.
