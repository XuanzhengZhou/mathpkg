Theorem 2.1 establishes the left exactness of the covariant Hom functor $\text{Hom}_\Lambda(A, -)$ in the category of $\Lambda$-modules. Given an exact sequence $B' \to B \to B''$ (with $\mu$ injective and $\varepsilon$ surjective onto the image of $\mu$), the induced sequence of abelian groups $0 \to \text{Hom}_\Lambda(A, B') \to \text{Hom}_\Lambda(A, B) \to \text{Hom}_\Lambda(A, B'')$ is exact. This means $\mu_*$ is injective and the kernel of $\varepsilon_*$ equals the image of $\mu_*$. The theorem is fundamental: it shows that Hom is always left exact, though the map $\varepsilon_*$ is not surjective in general even when $\varepsilon$ is.
