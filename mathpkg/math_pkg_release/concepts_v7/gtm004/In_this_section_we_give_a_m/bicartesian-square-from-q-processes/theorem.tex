In the notation of the Q-process construction, the square

$$\begin{array}{ccc}
B_{0,1} & \xrightarrow{\sigma_{0,1}} & B_1 \\
\varrho_{0,1} \downarrow & & \downarrow \\
B & \xrightarrow{\sigma_{1,0}} & B_{1,0}
\end{array}$$

is bicartesian, i.e., simultaneously a pull-back and a push-out. Concretely, this square may be written as

$$\begin{array}{ccc}
\gamma^{-1}(C_1) & \longrightarrow & \gamma^{-1}(C_1) / \beta\sigma^{-1}(0) \\
\downarrow & & \downarrow \\
B & \longrightarrow & B / \beta\sigma^{-1}(0)
\end{array}$$

with the obvious morphisms.
