The Krull dimension of the cohomology ring $H^*(G,k)$ is defined through the polynomial growth rate of the sequence $\dim_k H^n(G,k)$. This homological definition coincides with the commutative algebra notion of Krull dimension for the graded ring $H^*(G,k)$. The case $c(G)=1$ characterizes groups whose cohomology dimensions are uniformly bounded, which happens exactly when the $p$-Sylow subgroups are of a restricted type.
