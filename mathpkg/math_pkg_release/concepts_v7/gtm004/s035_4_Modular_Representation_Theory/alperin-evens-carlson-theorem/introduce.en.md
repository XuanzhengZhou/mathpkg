The Alperin-Evens-Carlson theorem reduces the computation of the complexity $c_G(M)$ to the restriction over elementary abelian $p$-subgroups. It generalizes Quillen's theorem from the trivial module to arbitrary indecomposable modules. This reduction principle is a key technical tool for studying the cohomological structure of $kG$-modules.
