The cohomology variety $X_G$ is the maximal ideal spectrum of the even cohomology ring $H^{\mathrm{ev}}(G,k)$, providing a geometric interpretation of the Krull dimension $c(G)$. For each $kG$-module $M$, its support variety $X_G(M)$ is a homogeneous subvariety whose dimension recovers the complexity $c_G(M)$. This geometric framework has proved invaluable in both the cohomology theory of finite groups and modular representation theory.
