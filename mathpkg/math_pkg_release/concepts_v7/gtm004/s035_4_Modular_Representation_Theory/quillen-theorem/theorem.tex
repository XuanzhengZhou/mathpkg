\begin{theorem}[Quillen]
\label{thm:quillen}
Let $G$ be a finite group and $k$ a field of characteristic $p$. The Krull dimension $c(G)$ of the cohomology ring $H^*(G, k)$ equals the maximum of the Krull dimensions $c(E)$ of $H^*(E, k)$, where $E$ runs through the elementary abelian $p$-subgroups of $G$.

Equivalently, $c(G)$ equals the $p$-rank of $G$, i.e., the maximal rank of an elementary abelian $p$-subgroup of $G$. For an elementary abelian $p$-group $E$ of order $p^n$, one has $c(E) = n$.
\end{theorem}
