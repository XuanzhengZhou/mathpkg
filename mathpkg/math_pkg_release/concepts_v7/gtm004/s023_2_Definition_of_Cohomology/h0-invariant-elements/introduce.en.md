The zeroth cohomology group $H^0(\mathfrak{g}, A)$ admits a simple interpretation: it is precisely the subspace $A^{\mathfrak{g}}$ of invariant elements — those $a \in A$ annihilated by the action of every element $x \in \mathfrak{g}$. This follows from the definition $H^0(\mathfrak{g}, A) = \text{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(K, A)$ and the observation that a $\mathfrak{g}$-module homomorphism from the trivial module $K$ into $A$ is determined by the image of $1 \in K$, which must be an invariant element.
