# Proof of Characterization of $H^1$: Derivations Modulo Inner Derivations

**Statement.** For any $\mathfrak{g}$-module $A$,
$$H^1(\mathfrak{g}, A) \cong \operatorname{Der}(\mathfrak{g}, A) / \operatorname{Inder}(\mathfrak{g}, A),$$
where $\operatorname{Der}(\mathfrak{g}, A)$ is the space of derivations and $\operatorname{Inder}(\mathfrak{g}, A)$ is the subspace of inner derivations.

---

## Proof

Take the obvious free presentation of the trivial module $K$,
$$I\mathfrak{g} \hookrightarrow U\mathfrak{g} \twoheadrightarrow K,$$
where $U\mathfrak{g}$ is the universal enveloping algebra, $I\mathfrak{g} = \ker(\varepsilon: U\mathfrak{g} \to K)$ is the augmentation ideal, and $K$ is the trivial module. Applying the functor $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(-, A)$ yields the exact sequence
$$0 \to \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(K, A) \to \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(U\mathfrak{g}, A) \to \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(I\mathfrak{g}, A) \to \operatorname{Ext}_{\mathfrak{g}}^1(K, A) \to \operatorname{Ext}_{\mathfrak{g}}^1(U\mathfrak{g}, A) \to \cdots.$$
Since $U\mathfrak{g}$ is a free $U\mathfrak{g}$-module, $\operatorname{Ext}_{\mathfrak{g}}^1(U\mathfrak{g}, A) = 0$. Hence
$$H^1(\mathfrak{g}, A) = \operatorname{Ext}_{\mathfrak{g}}^1(K, A) = \operatorname{coker}\bigl(\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(U\mathfrak{g}, A) \to \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(I\mathfrak{g}, A)\bigr). \tag{2.3}$$

It remains to identify $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(I\mathfrak{g}, A)$ with $\operatorname{Der}(\mathfrak{g}, A)$ and the image of $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(U\mathfrak{g}, A)$ with inner derivations.

### Step 1: From a derivation to a $\mathfrak{g}$-module homomorphism $I\mathfrak{g} \to A$

Let $d: \mathfrak{g} \to A$ be a derivation. Extend $d$ to a $K$-linear map $d': T\mathfrak{g} \to A$ on the tensor algebra $T\mathfrak{g} = K \oplus \mathfrak{g} \oplus (\mathfrak{g} \otimes \mathfrak{g}) \oplus \cdots$ by
$$d'(x_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes x_n) = \sum_{i=1}^n (x_1 \circ \cdots \circ x_{i-1}) \circ d(x_i) \circ (x_{i+1} \circ \cdots \circ x_n)$$
for $n \geq 1$ and $d'(1) = 0$, where $\circ$ denotes the $\mathfrak{g}$-module action on $A$.

The derivation property of $d$ implies that $d'$ vanishes on all elements of the form
$$t_1 \otimes (x \otimes y - y \otimes x - [x, y]) \otimes t_2,$$
with $x, y \in \mathfrak{g}$ and $t_1, t_2 \in T\mathfrak{g}$. Consequently, $d'$ factors through the universal enveloping algebra $U\mathfrak{g} = T\mathfrak{g} / J$, where $J$ is the two-sided ideal generated by $\{x \otimes y - y \otimes x - [x, y] : x, y \in \mathfrak{g}\}$.

Restricting the induced map to the augmentation ideal $I\mathfrak{g} \subset U\mathfrak{g}$ yields a $\mathfrak{g}$-module homomorphism
$$f_d: I\mathfrak{g} \to A.$$

### Step 2: From a $\mathfrak{g}$-module homomorphism $I\mathfrak{g} \to A$ to a derivation

Conversely, given a $\mathfrak{g}$-module homomorphism $f: I\mathfrak{g} \to A$, extend it to $U\mathfrak{g}$ by setting $f(K) = 0$ on the scalars $K \subset U\mathfrak{g}$. Then define
$$d_f: \mathfrak{g} \to A, \qquad d_f = f \circ i,$$
where $i: \mathfrak{g} \hookrightarrow U\mathfrak{g}$ is the canonical embedding (sending $x \in \mathfrak{g}$ to its image in $U\mathfrak{g}$). One verifies that $d_f$ is indeed a derivation.

### Step 3: The bijection

The two constructions are mutually inverse:
$$f_{(d_f)} = f, \qquad d_{(f_d)} = d,$$
and the map $f \mapsto d_f$ is $K$-linear. Hence $\operatorname{Der}(\mathfrak{g}, A) \cong \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(I\mathfrak{g}, A)$ as $K$-vector spaces.

### Step 4: Inner derivations

Under this isomorphism, a $\mathfrak{g}$-module homomorphism $h: U\mathfrak{g} \to A$ corresponds to an inner derivation. Indeed, given $h \in \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(U\mathfrak{g}, A)$, its image under the map $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(U\mathfrak{g}, A) \to \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(I\mathfrak{g}, A)$ is the restriction $h|_{I\mathfrak{g}}$. The associated derivation $d_{h|_{I\mathfrak{g}}}$ sends $x \in \mathfrak{g}$ to $h(i(x)) = x \circ h(1)$ (since $h$ is a $\mathfrak{g}$-module map and $i(x) = x \cdot 1$ in $U\mathfrak{g}$). Thus $d_{h|_{I\mathfrak{g}}}$ is the inner derivation determined by the element $h(1) \in A$.

Therefore,
$$H^1(\mathfrak{g}, A) \cong \operatorname{Der}(\mathfrak{g}, A) / \operatorname{Inder}(\mathfrak{g}, A). \quad \square$$
