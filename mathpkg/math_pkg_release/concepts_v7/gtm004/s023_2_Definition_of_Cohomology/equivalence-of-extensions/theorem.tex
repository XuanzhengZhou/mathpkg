Two extensions $A \xrightarrow{i} \mathfrak{g} \xrightarrow{p} \mathfrak{h}$ and $A \xrightarrow{i'} \mathfrak{g}' \xrightarrow{p'} \mathfrak{h}$ of $\mathfrak{h}$ by the $\mathfrak{h}$-module $A$ are called equivalent if there exists a Lie algebra homomorphism $f : \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{g}'$ such that the diagram

\[
\begin{CD}
A @>{i}>> \mathfrak{g} @>{p}>> \mathfrak{h} \\
@| @V{f}VV @| \\
A @>>{i'}> \mathfrak{g}' @>>{p'}> \mathfrak{h}
\end{CD}
\]

commutes. Such an $f$ is automatically an isomorphism. The set of equivalence classes is denoted $M(\mathfrak{h}, A)$ and always contains the class of the semidirect product $A \rtimes \mathfrak{h}$.
