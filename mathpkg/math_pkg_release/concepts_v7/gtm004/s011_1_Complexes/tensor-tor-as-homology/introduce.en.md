Starting from a projective presentation $R \to P \to B$ of a module $B$, one applies the covariant functor $-\otimes_\Lambda A$ to obtain a short chain complex. The homology of this complex recovers $H_0 \cong B \otimes_\Lambda A$ and $H_1 \cong \operatorname{Tor}_1^\Lambda(B, A)$, with all higher homology vanishing.

This example, together with its dual using injective presentations and the Hom functor, demonstrates that the classical functors $\operatorname{Hom}$, $\operatorname{Ext}$, $\otimes$, and $\operatorname{Tor}$ can all be expressed as (co)homology of appropriate 2-term complexes. These computations do not depend on the choice of projective or injective presentation, a fact that is clarified and generalized in subsequent sections on resolutions and derived functors.
