A \textit{chain complex} $C = \{C_n, \partial_n\}$ over $\Lambda$ is an object in $\mathfrak{M}_\Lambda^{\mathbb{Z}}$ together with an endomorphism $\partial: C \rightarrow C$ of degree $-1$ such that $\partial \partial = 0$. In other words, we are given a family $\{C_n\}_{n \in \mathbb{Z}}$ of $\Lambda$-modules, together with module homomorphisms $\partial_n: C_n \rightarrow C_{n-1}$, $n \in \mathbb{Z}$, such that $\partial_n \partial_{n+1} = 0$ for all $n \in \mathbb{Z}$.
