Let $M$ be a vector space over $K$. Set $T_0 M = K$ and $T_n M = M^{\otimes n}$ for $n \geq 1$. The tensor algebra $TM$ is

$$TM = \bigoplus_{n=0}^{\infty} T_n M,$$

with multiplication induced by

$$(m_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes m_p) \cdot (m'_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes m'_q) = m_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes m_p \otimes m'_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes m'_q,$$

where $m_i, m'_j \in M$. The tensor algebra $TM$ satisfies the universal property: for any $K$-algebra $\Lambda$ and any $K$-linear map $f: M \to \Lambda$, there exists a unique algebra homomorphism $f_0: TM \to \Lambda$ extending $f$. In other words, the functor $T$ is left adjoint to the forgetful functor from $K$-algebras to $K$-vector spaces.
