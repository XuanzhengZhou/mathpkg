The derived series $\mathfrak{g}_0 \supseteq \mathfrak{g}_1 \supseteq \cdots$ of a Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ is defined recursively by $\mathfrak{g}_0 = \mathfrak{g}$ and $\mathfrak{g}_n = [\mathfrak{g}, \mathfrak{g}_{n-1}]$, where the bracket of subsets denotes the Lie subalgebra generated by all commutators. Each term is an ideal in $\mathfrak{g}$, and the series measures how far $\mathfrak{g}$ is from being abelian. This construction is used to define solvable Lie algebras.
