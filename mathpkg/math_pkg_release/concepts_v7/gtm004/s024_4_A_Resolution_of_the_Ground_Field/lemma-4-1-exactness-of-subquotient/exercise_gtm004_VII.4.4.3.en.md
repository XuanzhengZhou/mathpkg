# Exercise 4.3

Let $A \to \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{h}$ be an extension of Lie algebras over $K$. Let $s : \mathfrak{h} \to \mathfrak{g}$ be a section, that is, a $K$-linear map with $p s = 1_{\mathfrak{h}}$, so that, as $K$-vector spaces, $\mathfrak{g} \cong A \oplus \mathfrak{h}$. Show that the Lie algebra structure of $\mathfrak{g}$ may be described by a $K$-bilinear function $h : \mathfrak{h} \times \mathfrak{h} \to A$ defined by

$$[sx, sy] = s[x, y] + h(x, y), \quad x, y \in \mathfrak{h}.$$

Show that $h$ is a 2-cocycle in $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(C, A)$ where $C$ is the resolution of Theorem 4.2 for the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{h}$. Also, show that two different sections $s_1, s_2$ yield two cohomologous cocycles $h_1, h_2$.
