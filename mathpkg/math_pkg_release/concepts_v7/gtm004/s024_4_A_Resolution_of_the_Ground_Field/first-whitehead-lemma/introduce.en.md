Proposition 6.1 (the First Whitehead Lemma) asserts the vanishing of the first cohomology group $H^1(\mathfrak{g}, A)$ for any finite-dimensional module $A$ over a semi-simple Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$. The proof proceeds by induction on $\dim_K A$: if a counterexample exists with minimal dimension, then $A$ must be simple, hence trivial by Proposition 5.6; but then $H^1(\mathfrak{g}, K) \cong \operatorname{Hom}_K(\mathfrak{g}_{ab}, K) = 0$ since $\mathfrak{g}_{ab} = 0$ for semi-simple $\mathfrak{g}$. This lemma is the key step in proving Weyl's complete reducibility theorem.
