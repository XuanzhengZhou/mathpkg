Let $\mathfrak{U} = \mathbf{1}$ be the category with one object and one morphism, so that $[\mathbf{1}, \mathfrak{A}]$ may be identified with $\mathfrak{A}$. Let $V \in \mathfrak{B}$ and let $J = K_V : \mathbf{1} \rightarrow \mathfrak{B}$ be the functor with $K_V(1) = V$. Then the functor $K_V^* : [\mathfrak{B}, \mathfrak{A}] \rightarrow [\mathbf{1}, \mathfrak{A}] = \mathfrak{A}$ is evaluation at $V$.

The Kan extension $\tilde{I}: [\mathfrak{B}_d, \mathfrak{A}] \rightarrow [\mathfrak{B}, \mathfrak{A}]$, where $\mathfrak{B}_d$ is the discrete category on the objects of $\mathfrak{B}$, is given by
$$(\tilde{I}\{A_V\})_{V'} = \coprod_{V \in \mathfrak{B}} (\tilde{K}_V A_V)_{V'} = \coprod_{V \in \mathfrak{B}} \coprod_{v \in \mathfrak{B}(V', V)} (A_V)_v,$$
where $(A_V)_v = A_V$, with the obvious values on morphisms.
