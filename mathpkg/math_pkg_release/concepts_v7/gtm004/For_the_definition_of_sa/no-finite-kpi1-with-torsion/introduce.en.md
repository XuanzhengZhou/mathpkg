This theorem establishes a fundamental obstruction in algebraic topology: a group with torsion cannot have a finite-dimensional Eilenberg-MacLane space $K(\pi, 1)$. The proof first handles the cyclic case $C_m$ by noting that cyclic groups have non-trivial homology in arbitrarily high odd dimensions, forcing $K(C_m, 1)$ to be infinite-dimensional. For a general group $\pi$ containing an element of order $m$, the subgroup inclusion $C_m \hookrightarrow \pi$ implies $C(\tilde{K}(\pi, 1))$ is a free $C_m$-resolution, transferring the obstruction.
