The homology of a small category $\mathfrak{U}$ with coefficients in a functor $T: \mathfrak{U} \rightarrow \mathfrak{A}$ is a special case of $J$-homology where the target category $\mathfrak{B}$ is the terminal category $\mathbf{1}$. This construction generalizes group homology: when $\mathfrak{U}$ is a group $G$ regarded as a category with one object, this recovers the usual group homology $H_n(G, T)$. It provides a unified framework for defining homology theories in arbitrary categories with a specified subcategory of models.
