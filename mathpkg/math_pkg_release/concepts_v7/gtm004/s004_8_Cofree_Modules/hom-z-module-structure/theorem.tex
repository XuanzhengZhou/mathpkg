Let $A$ be a right $\Lambda$-module and let $G$ be an abelian group. Regarding $A$ as an abelian group, one forms the abelian group $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, G)$ and endows it with a left $\Lambda$-module structure by
$$(\lambda \varphi)(a) = \varphi(a \lambda), \quad a \in A,\; \lambda \in \Lambda,\; \varphi \in \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, G).$$
Similarly, if $A$ is a left $\Lambda$-module, then $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, G)$ acquires the structure of a right $\Lambda$-module via $(\varphi \lambda)(a) = \varphi(\lambda a)$.
