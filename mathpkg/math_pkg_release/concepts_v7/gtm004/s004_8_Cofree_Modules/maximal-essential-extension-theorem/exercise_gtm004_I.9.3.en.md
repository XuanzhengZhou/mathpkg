# Exercise 9.3

Given any abelian group $A$, let $T(A)$ denote its torsion subgroup and $F(A) = A/T(A)$ its torsion-free quotient. Show that if $C$ is a divisible abelian group, then $T(C)$ and $F(C)$ are also divisible.

What does this tell us about the injective envelope of $T(A)$, $A$, and $F(A)$?

*Hint:* Recall that every divisible group splits as $C \cong T(C) \oplus F(C)$ where $T(C)$ is a direct sum of copies of $\mathbb{Z}_{p^\infty}$ (Pr\"ufer groups) and $F(C)$ is a direct sum of copies of $\mathbb{Q}$. Use Theorem 9.2.
