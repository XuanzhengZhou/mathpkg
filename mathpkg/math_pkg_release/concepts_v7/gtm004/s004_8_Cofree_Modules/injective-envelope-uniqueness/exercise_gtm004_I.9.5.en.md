# Exercise 9.5

Show that the dual of an injective envelope does not always exist. That is, given a $\Lambda$-module $A$, we cannot in general find $P \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} A$ with $P$ projective, such that given any $Q \xrightarrow{\eta} A$ with $Q$ projective, we may factor $\eta$ as $Q \rightarrow P \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} A$.

*Hint:* Take $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}$ and $A = \mathbb{Z}_5$. Show that no projective module can map essentially onto $\mathbb{Z}_5$. Where does the dual argument of Theorem 9.2 fail?

Recall that for $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}$, projective modules are exactly free abelian groups. Consider the epimorphism $\mathbb{Z} \twoheadrightarrow \mathbb{Z}_5$; the kernel is $5\mathbb{Z}$, and no proper submodule of $\mathbb{Z}$ maps onto $\mathbb{Z}_5$.
