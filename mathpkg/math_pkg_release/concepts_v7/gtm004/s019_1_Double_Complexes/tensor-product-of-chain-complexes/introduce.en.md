The tensor product of two chain complexes $C$ and $D$ is a chain complex obtained by first forming a double complex with components $C_p \otimes_\Lambda D_q$ and then taking the total complex. The sign $(-1)^p$ in the vertical differential $\partial''$ is the standard Koszul sign convention, ensuring that the two differentials anticommute.

This construction is fundamental to the Künneth theorem, which expresses the homology of $C \otimes_\Lambda D$ in terms of the homologies of $C$ and $D$. When one complex is concentrated in degree 0, this recovers the ordinary tensor product of a module with a chain complex.
