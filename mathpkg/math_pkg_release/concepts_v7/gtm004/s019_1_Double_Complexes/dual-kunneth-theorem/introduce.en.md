The dual Künneth theorem is the Hom-analogue of the ordinary Künneth theorem. It expresses the homology of the Hom complex in terms of $\operatorname{Hom}$ and $\operatorname{Ext}$ of the homology modules of the two complexes. The $\operatorname{Ext}^1$ term appears as an obstruction, analogous to the $\operatorname{Tor}$ term in the ordinary Künneth theorem.

A key difference is that the source complex $C$ must be free (not merely flat), because the functor $\operatorname{Hom}(C, -)$ is exact only when $C$ is projective. The splitting is unnatural in $C$ but natural in $D$.
