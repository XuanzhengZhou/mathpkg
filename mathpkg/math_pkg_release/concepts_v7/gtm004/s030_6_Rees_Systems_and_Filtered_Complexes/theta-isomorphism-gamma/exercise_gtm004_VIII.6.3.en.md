# Exercise 6.3

Establish the remark following Proposition 6.7: *There is an isomorphism $\Gamma^{-} \cong \Gamma^{+}$ in any Rees system, even if it is not special* (see Theorem 7.25 of [10]).

*Hint.* Even without a distinguished automorphism $\theta$, the exactness of the couples $\textcircled{1}$ and $\textcircled{2}$ together with the morphisms $\varphi$, $\bar{\varphi}$, $\xi$ provides a homomorphic relation (in the sense of Section 7) between $\Gamma^{-} = \ker(\bar{\alpha}\bar{\varphi})/\ker(\bar{\varphi})$ and $\Gamma^{+} = \varphi D / \varphi\alpha D$. Show that this relation is in fact an isomorphism of subquotients of $\Gamma$, without invoking $\theta$. The specialness merely provides a *canonical* choice of isomorphism via $\theta$.
