The dual Künneth theorem expresses the homology of the Hom complex $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(C, D)$ in terms of Hom and Ext of the homology groups of $C$ and $D$. It requires $C$ to be free (stronger than the flatness condition in the ordinary Künneth theorem). The sequence splits but not naturally. A special case where $C$ is a resolution and $D$ is concentrated in dimension 0 yields the universal coefficient theorem for cohomology: $H^n(C; G) \cong \operatorname{Hom}(H_n(C), G) \oplus \operatorname{Ext}(H_{n-1}(C), G)$.
