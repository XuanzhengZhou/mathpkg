# Proof of Proposition 3.4: $H_0(G, B) = B_G$

By definition (Section VI.2),

$$H_0(G, B) = \operatorname{Tor}_0^{\mathbb{Z}G}(B, \mathbb{Z}) = B \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}G} \mathbb{Z},$$

where $\mathbb{Z}$ is the trivial right $G$-module. Using the isomorphism $B \cong B \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}} \mathbb{Z}$ (as abelian groups), the tensor product over $\mathbb{Z}G$ imposes the relation that $b x \otimes n = b \otimes x n = b \otimes n$ for all $x \in G$, since $G$ acts trivially on $\mathbb{Z}$. Thus

$$B \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}G} \mathbb{Z} = B / \langle b x - b \mid b \in B,\; x \in G \rangle.$$

Now observe that $b x - b = b(x - 1)$ as elements of $B$ (or more precisely, as elements of $B \cdot IG$ inside $B \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}G} \mathbb{Z}G \cong B$). The augmentation ideal $IG$ is additively generated by $\{x - 1 \mid x \in G\}$, so the subgroup generated by $\{b(x-1) \mid b \in B, x \in G\}$ is precisely $B \circ IG$, the image of the action of $IG$ on $B$.

Therefore

$$H_0(G, B) = B / B \circ IG =: B_G,$$

the group of coinvariants (or orbits) of $B$ under the action of $G$.

Explicitly, the natural map $B \to B_G$ sends $b$ to its orbit under the $G$-action, where two elements $b, b'$ are identified if they differ by a sum of terms of the form $b''(x - 1) = b'' x - b''$.
