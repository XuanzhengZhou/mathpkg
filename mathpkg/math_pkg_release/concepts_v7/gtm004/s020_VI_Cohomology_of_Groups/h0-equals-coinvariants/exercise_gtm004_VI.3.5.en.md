# Exercise 3.5

Express the functorial dependence of $A^G$ (the subgroup of $G$-invariant elements of a $G$-module $A$) and $B_G$ (the group of $G$-coinvariants of a right $G$-module $B$) on the group $G$. That is, describe how a group homomorphism $f: G \to G'$ induces maps between the corresponding invariant and coinvariant subgroups.

*Hint:* For invariants, a $G'$-module $A'$ becomes a $G$-module via $f$, and there is a natural inclusion $(A')^{G'} \hookrightarrow (Uf A')^G$. For coinvariants, there is a natural surjection $(Uf B')_G \twoheadrightarrow B'_{G'}$.
