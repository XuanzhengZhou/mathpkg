A \emph{derivation} (or \emph{crossed homomorphism}) from a group $G$ into a left $G$-module $A$ is a map $\varphi: G \to A$ satisfying
$$\varphi(xy) = x \varphi(y) + \varphi(x) \quad \text{for all } x, y \in G.$$

Equivalently, a derivation corresponds to a $G$-module homomorphism $\tilde{\varphi}: IG \to A$ defined by $\tilde{\varphi}(x-1) = \varphi(x)$. The derivation $\varphi$ is called \emph{principal} if there exists $a \in A$ such that $\varphi(x) = xa - a$ for all $x \in G$. The group $H^1(G, A)$ is isomorphic to the group of derivations modulo principal derivations:
$$H^1(G, A) \cong \operatorname{Der}(G, A) / \operatorname{PDer}(G, A).$$
