A derivation (or crossed homomorphism) is a map from a group $G$ into a $G$-module $A$ satisfying a twisted Leibniz rule. Derivations correspond precisely to $G$-module homomorphisms from the augmentation ideal $IG$ to $A$, via $\varphi \mapsto \tilde{\varphi}$ where $\tilde{\varphi}(x-1) = \varphi(x)$. The first cohomology group $H^1(G, A)$ classifies derivations modulo those that are principal (i.e., of the form $x \mapsto xa - a$). This interpretation is fundamental for applications to group extensions and Galois cohomology.
