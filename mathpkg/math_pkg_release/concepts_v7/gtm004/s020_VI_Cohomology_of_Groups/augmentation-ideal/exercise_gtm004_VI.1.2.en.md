# Exercise 1.2

Interpret the augmentation $\varepsilon: \mathbb{Z}G \to \mathbb{Z}$ (i) as a $G$-module homomorphism, (ii) as a morphism in the image of the functor $\mathbb{Z}(\cdot)$.

*Note:* The augmentation ideal $IG$ is defined as $\ker \varepsilon$. Understanding $\varepsilon$ as a $G$-module homomorphism is essential for understanding the structure of $IG$ and its role in group (co)homology.
