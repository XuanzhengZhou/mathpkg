The universal property characterizes the limit as the final object in the category of cones over a diagram. Given any cone $X$ with maps $\varphi_i : X \rightarrow F(i)$ compatible with the diagram, there is a unique factorizing morphism $\varphi : X \rightarrow \varprojlim F$. This property is the working definition used throughout the theory of limits in homological algebra.
