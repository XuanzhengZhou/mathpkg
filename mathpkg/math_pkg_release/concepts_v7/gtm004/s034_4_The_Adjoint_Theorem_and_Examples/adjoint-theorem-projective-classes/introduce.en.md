The Adjoint Theorem describes how a projective class of epimorphisms in an abelian category can be transferred to another abelian category via a pair of adjoint functors. Given a projective class $\mathcal{E}$ in $\mathfrak{A}$ and adjoint functors $F \dashv U$ with $U$ faithful, the preimage $\mathcal{E}' = U^{-1}\mathcal{E}$ forms a projective class in $\mathfrak{A}'$. Moreover, the image under $F$ of $\mathcal{E}$-projective objects provides a sufficient supply of $\mathcal{E}'$-projective objects, and every $\mathcal{E}'$-projective is a direct summand of such an image. This theorem is fundamental for constructing derived functors in new categorical contexts.
