Let $J: \mathfrak{U} \rightarrow \mathfrak{B}$ be a full embedding of small categories. Then for any functor $T: \mathfrak{U} \rightarrow \mathfrak{A}$ where $\mathfrak{A}$ admits colimits, the left Kan extension $\tilde{J}T$ satisfies $\tilde{J}T \circ J \cong T$. More precisely, for $U \in \mathfrak{U}$, the category $\mathfrak{J}_{JU}$ has a terminal object $(U, 1_{JU})$, so that
$$
\tilde{J}T(JU) = \varinjlim_{(U_1, \psi_1) \in \mathfrak{J}_{JU}} T(U_1) = T(U),
$$
with the canonical morphism $\varrho_{JU}(U_1, \psi_1) = T(\varphi)$ where $\varphi: U_1 \rightarrow U$ is the unique morphism with $J\varphi = \psi_1$.
