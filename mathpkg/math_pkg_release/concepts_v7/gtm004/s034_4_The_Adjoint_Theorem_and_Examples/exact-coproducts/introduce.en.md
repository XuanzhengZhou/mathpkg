An abelian category has exact coproducts if the formation of coproducts preserves short exact sequences, or equivalently, preserves monomorphisms. This property ensures that coproducts of projective resolutions remain resolutions. It is a crucial hypothesis in comparing different homology theories: when $\mathfrak{A}$ has enough projectives and exact coproducts, the absolute $J$-homology (computed using all epimorphisms as the projective class) coincides with the relative $J$-homology (computed using a restricted projective class).
