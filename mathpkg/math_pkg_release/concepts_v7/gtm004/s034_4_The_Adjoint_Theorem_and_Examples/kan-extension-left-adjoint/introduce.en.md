Given a functor $J: \mathfrak{U} \rightarrow \mathfrak{B}$ between small categories, this theorem establishes the existence of a left adjoint $\tilde{J}$ to the precomposition functor $J^*$, provided the target category $\mathfrak{A}$ admits colimits. The left adjoint $\tilde{J}$ is the left Kan extension along $J$, and its value at a functor $T$ and object $V$ is computed as a colimit over the comma category $\mathfrak{J}_V$ of $J$-objects over $V$. This construction is fundamental in category theory and underlies many homological constructions, including the homology of small categories.
