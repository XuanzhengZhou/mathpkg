Let $\mathfrak{U}$ be a small category and let $\mathfrak{B} = \mathbf{1}$ be the category with one object and one morphism. Let $J: \mathfrak{U} \rightarrow \mathbf{1}$ be the unique functor. For a functor $T: \mathfrak{U} \rightarrow \mathfrak{A}$, define the \text{homology of the small category $\mathfrak{U}$ with coefficients in $T$} by
$$
H_n(\mathfrak{U}, T) = H_n(J, T), \quad n \geq 0,
$$
where $H_n(J, T)$ is the $J$-homology of Section 5, i.e., the $n$-th left derived functor of the Kan extension $\tilde{J}$.
