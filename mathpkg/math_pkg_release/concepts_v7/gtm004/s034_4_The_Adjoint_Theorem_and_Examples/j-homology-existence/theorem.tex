Let $\mathfrak{A}$ have enough projectives. Then the $J$-homology
$$
H_*(J, -): [\mathfrak{U}, \mathfrak{A}] \rightarrow [\mathfrak{B}, \mathfrak{A}]
$$
exists. It may be computed as the left $\mathcal{E}_1'$-derived functor of the Kan extension:
$$
H_n(J, T) = (L_n^{\mathcal{E}_1'} \tilde{J}) T, \quad n = 0, 1, \dots.
$$
Here $\mathcal{E}_1$ is the projective class of all epimorphisms in the functor category and $\mathcal{E}_1'$ is its transfer under the adjunction associated with the inclusion of the discrete subcategory. A functor $S: \mathfrak{U} \rightarrow \mathfrak{A}$ is $\mathcal{E}_1'$-projective if and only if it is a direct summand of a functor $\bar{S}$ of the form
$$
\bar{S}(U') = \coprod_{U \in \mathfrak{U}} \coprod_{v \in \mathfrak{U}(U, U')} (P_U)_v,
$$
where each $P_U$ is projective in $\mathfrak{A}$.
