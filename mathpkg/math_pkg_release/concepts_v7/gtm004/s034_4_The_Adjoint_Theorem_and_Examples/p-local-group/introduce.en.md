A $P$-local group is one in which exponentiation by any $P'$-number (an integer whose prime factors lie outside the chosen family $P$) is a bijection. For abelian groups, this is equivalent to being a module over the localized ring $\mathbb{Z}_P$. The notion generalizes to nilpotent groups through a localization functor $N \mapsto N_P$, constructed as a direct limit of central extensions. This $P$-localization satisfies exactness, has a homological characterization, and is a powerful tool for studying nilpotent groups by focusing on torsion at prescribed primes.
