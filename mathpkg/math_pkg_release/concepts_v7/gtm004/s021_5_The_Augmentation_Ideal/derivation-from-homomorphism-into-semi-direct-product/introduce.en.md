Corollary 5.4 is the converse to the universal property of the semi-direct product (Proposition 5.3). It states that any group homomorphism $h : X \to A \rtimes G$ automatically yields a homomorphism $f = p \circ h$ to $G$ and a derivation $d = q \circ h$ to $A$, with the derivation compatible with the $X$-module structure on $A$ induced by $f$. Together with Proposition 5.3, this establishes a bijective correspondence between homomorphisms $X \to A \rtimes G$ and pairs $(f : X \to G,\; d : X \to A)$ where $d$ is an $f$-derivation.
