# Exercise 5.1

Let $d: G \to A$ be a derivation. Interpret and prove the following relation

$$d(x^n) = \left( \frac{x^n - 1}{x - 1} \right) dx, \quad n \in \mathbb{Z}, \quad x \in G,$$

where the formal expression $\frac{x^n - 1}{x - 1}$ is to be interpreted as the element $1 + x + x^2 + \cdots + x^{n-1}$ in the group ring $\mathbb{Z}G$ (for $n \geq 0$), and the action is the $G$-module action on $A$.
