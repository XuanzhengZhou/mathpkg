The set $E(A,B)$ of equivalence classes of extensions is given a functorial structure by defining induced maps in both variables. Given $\alpha: A' \to A$, the map $\alpha^*: E(A,B) \to E(A',B)$ is defined via pullback of the extension along $\alpha$. Dually, given $\beta: B \to B'$, the map $\beta_*: E(A,B) \to E(A,B')$ is defined via pushout along $\beta$. These constructions make $E(-,B)$ a contravariant functor and $E(A,-)$ a covariant functor, and together they form the bifunctor structure established in Theorem 1.4.
