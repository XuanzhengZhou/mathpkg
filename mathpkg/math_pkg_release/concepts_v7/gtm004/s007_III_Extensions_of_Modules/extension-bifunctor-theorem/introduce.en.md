Theorem 1.4 is the central structural result of Section 1. It states that the assignment $(A,B) \mapsto E(A,B)$ of extension classes is a bifunctor: contravariant in the first argument (via pullback) and covariant in the second (via pushout). The compatibility condition $\beta_* \circ \alpha^* = \alpha^* \circ \beta_*$ means that these induced maps commute, which is the essential property needed to later endow $E(A,B)$ with an abelian group structure isomorphic to $\operatorname{Ext}_\Lambda^1(A,B)$. This theorem elevates the naive set of extension classes to a proper functorial framework, preparing the ground for the homological algebra of Ext.
