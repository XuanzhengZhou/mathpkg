Let $\Lambda$ be a ring and let $A, B$ be $\Lambda$-modules. An \text{extension of $A$ by $B$} is a short exact sequence
$$
B \xrightarrow{\kappa} E \xrightarrow{\nu} A
$$
of $\Lambda$-modules. Two such extensions $B \xrightarrow{\kappa_1} E_1 \xrightarrow{\nu_1} A$ and $B \xrightarrow{\kappa_2} E_2 \xrightarrow{\nu_2} A$ are called \text{equivalent} if there exists a homomorphism $\xi: E_1 \to E_2$ making the diagram
$$
\begin{CD}
B @>{\kappa_1}>> E_1 @>{\nu_1}>> A \\
@| @V{\xi}VV @| \\
B @>{\kappa_2}>> E_2 @>{\nu_2}>> A
\end{CD}
$$
commutative. The set of equivalence classes of extensions of $A$ by $B$ is denoted by $E(A, B)$.
