An extension of a module $A$ by a module $B$ is a short exact sequence $B \to E \to A$. Given modules $A$ and $B$, the extension problem asks which modules $E$ can be constructed having $B$ as a submodule and $A$ as the associated quotient. Two extensions are equivalent when there is a homomorphism between the middle terms $E_1 \to E_2$ inducing the identity on both $A$ and $B$. The set $E(A,B)$ of equivalence classes is the central object of study in the extension theory of modules and will later be identified with $\operatorname{Ext}_\Lambda^1(A,B)$.
