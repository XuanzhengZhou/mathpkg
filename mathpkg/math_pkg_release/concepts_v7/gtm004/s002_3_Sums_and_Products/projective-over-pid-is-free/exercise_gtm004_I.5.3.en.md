# Exercise 5.3

Let $A, B, C$ be finitely generated modules over the principal ideal domain $\Lambda$. Show that if $A \oplus C \cong B \oplus C$, then $A \cong B$. Give counterexamples if one drops (a) the condition that the modules be finitely generated, (b) the condition that $\Lambda$ is a principal ideal domain.
