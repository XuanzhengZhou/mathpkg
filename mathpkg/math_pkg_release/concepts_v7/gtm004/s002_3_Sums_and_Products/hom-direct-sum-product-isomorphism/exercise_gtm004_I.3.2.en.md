# Exercise 3.2

Show how a map from $\bigoplus_{i=1}^{m} A_i$ to $\bigoplus_{j=1}^{n} B_j$ may be represented by a matrix where $\varphi_{ij} : A_i \to B_j$. Show that, if we write the composite of $\varphi : A \to B$ and $\psi : B \to C$ as $\psi \varphi$ (not $\varphi \psi$), then the composite of

$$\Phi = (\varphi_{ij}) : \bigoplus_{i=1}^{m} A_i \to \bigoplus_{j=1}^{n} B_j$$

and

$$\Psi = (\psi_{jk}) : \bigoplus_{j=1}^{n} B_j \to \bigoplus_{k=1}^{q} C_k$$

is the matrix product $\Psi \Phi$.
