# Proof: Every Submodule of a Free Module over a PID is Free

Let $P = \bigoplus_{j \in J} \Lambda_j$, where $\Lambda_j = \Lambda$, be a free $\Lambda$-module and let $R$ be a submodule of $P$. We shall show that $R$ has a basis.

Assume $J$ is well-ordered and define for every $j \in J$ the modules

$$\bar{P}_{(j)} = \bigoplus_{i < j} \Lambda_i, \qquad P_{(j)} = \bigoplus_{i \leq j} \Lambda_i.$$

Every element $a \in P_{(j)} \cap R$ can be written uniquely in the form $(b, \lambda)$ where $b \in \bar{P}_{(j)}$ and $\lambda \in \Lambda_j$. Define a homomorphism $f_j : P_{(j)} \cap R \to \Lambda$ by $f_j(a) = \lambda$. Since $\ker f_j = \bar{P}_{(j)} \cap R$, we obtain an exact sequence

$$\bar{P}_{(j)} \cap R \hookrightarrow P_{(j)} \cap R \twoheadrightarrow \operatorname{im} f_j.$$

Clearly $\operatorname{im} f_j$ is an ideal in $\Lambda$. Since $\Lambda$ is a principal ideal domain, this ideal is generated by one element, say $\lambda_j$. For $\lambda_j \neq 0$, choose $c_j \in P_{(j)} \cap R$ such that $f_j(c_j) = \lambda_j$. Let $J' \subseteq J$ consist of those $j$ for which $\lambda_j \neq 0$. We claim that $\{c_j\}_{j \in J'}$ is a basis of $R$.

**Linear independence.** Let $\sum_{k=1}^{n} \mu_k c_{j_k} = 0$ with $j_1 < j_2 < \dots < j_n$. Applying the homomorphism $f_{j_n}$, we get $\mu_n f_{j_n}(c_{j_n}) = \mu_n \lambda_{j_n} = 0$. Since $\lambda_{j_n} \neq 0$, we have $\mu_n = 0$. The assertion then follows by induction on $n$.

**Spanning.** Assume the contrary, that $\{c_j\}_{j \in J'}$ does not generate $R$. Then there is a least $i \in J$ such that there exists $a \in P_{(i)} \cap R$ which cannot be written as a linear combination of $\{c_j\}_{j \in J'}$. If $i \notin J'$, then $a \in \bar{P}_{(i)} \cap R$, so there exists $k < i$ with $a \in P_{(k)} \cap R$, contradicting the minimality of $i$. Thus $i \in J'$.

Consider $f_i(a) = \mu \lambda_i$ and form $b = a - \mu c_i$. Then

$$f_i(b) = f_i(a) - f_i(\mu c_i) = \mu \lambda_i - \mu \lambda_i = 0.$$

Hence $b \in \bar{P}_{(i)} \cap R$, and $b$ cannot be written as a linear combination of $\{c_j\}_{j \in J'}$ (otherwise $a = \mu c_i + b$ would also be). But there exists $k < i$ with $b \in P_{(k)} \cap R$, contradicting the minimality of $i$.

Therefore $\{c_j\}_{j \in J'}$ is a basis of $R$, proving that $R$ is free.
