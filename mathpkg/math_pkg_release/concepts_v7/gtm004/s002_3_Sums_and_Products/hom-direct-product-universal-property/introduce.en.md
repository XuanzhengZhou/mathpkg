Let $A$ be a fixed $\Lambda$-module and $\{B_j\}_{j \in J}$ a family of $\Lambda$-modules. This proposition states that a homomorphism from $A$ into the direct product $\prod B_j$ is equivalent to a family of homomorphisms $A \to B_j$, one for each $j \in J$. The isomorphism $\zeta$ is induced by composing with the canonical projections $\pi_j: \prod B_j \to B_j$. This result is dual to Proposition 3.4 and reflects the universal property of the direct product.
