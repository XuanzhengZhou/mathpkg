A module homomorphism is a map between modules that respects both the additive group structure and the scalar multiplication by the ring. This is the natural notion of structure-preserving map in module theory, analogous to linear transformations between vector spaces. Module homomorphisms form the morphisms of the category of $\Lambda$-modules, which is the central setting for homological algebra.
