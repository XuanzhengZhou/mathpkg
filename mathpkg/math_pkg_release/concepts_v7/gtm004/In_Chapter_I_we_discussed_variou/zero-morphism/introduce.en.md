In a category with a zero object, the zero morphism between any two objects is defined as the unique composite through the zero object. Zero morphisms act as absorbing elements: pre-composing or post-composing any morphism with a zero morphism yields another zero morphism. Zero morphisms are essential for defining kernels, cokernels, and exact sequences in abelian categories.
