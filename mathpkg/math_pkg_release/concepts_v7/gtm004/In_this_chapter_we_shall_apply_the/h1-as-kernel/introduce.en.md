Formula (4.2) gives a concrete description of $H_1(G, B)$ as the kernel of the map $\iota_*: B \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}G} IG \to B$ induced by the inclusion $IG \hookrightarrow \mathbb{Z}G$. When $B$ is a trivial $G$-module, $\iota_*$ vanishes and $H_1(G, B) \cong B \otimes G_{\mathrm{ab}}$. In the special case $B = \mathbb{Z}$, this yields the classical identification $H_1(G, \mathbb{Z}) \cong G_{\mathrm{ab}}$, linking group homology to the abelianization.
