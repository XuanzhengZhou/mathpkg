An epimorphism in a category generalizes the notion of a surjective function. It is a morphism that is right-cancellative: if composing it before two parallel morphisms gives the same result, then those two morphisms must already be equal. In the category of modules, epimorphisms are precisely the surjective homomorphisms. An epimorphism need not be surjective in general categories (e.g., the inclusion $\mathbb{Z} \subset \mathbb{Q}$ is an epimorphism in the category of integral domains).
