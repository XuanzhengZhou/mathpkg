Given morphisms $\varphi : A \to X$ and $\psi : B \to X$ in a category $\mathfrak{C}$, a \textit{pull-back} of $(\varphi, \psi)$ is a pair of morphisms $\alpha : Y \to A$, $\beta : Y \to B$ such that $\varphi \alpha = \psi \beta$, with the universal property: given any $\gamma : Z \to A$, $\delta : Z \to B$ with $\varphi \gamma = \psi \delta$, there exists a unique $\zeta : Z \to Y$ such that $\gamma = \alpha \zeta$ and $\delta = \beta \zeta$. The resulting commutative square is called a \textit{pull-back square}.
