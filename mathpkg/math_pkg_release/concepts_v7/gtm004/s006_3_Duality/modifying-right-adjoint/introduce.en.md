When the unit of an adjunction is a natural equivalence, the right adjoint can be "strictified": there exists a naturally equivalent right adjoint such that the composition $GF = 1$ holds strictly and the unit is the identity. This construction is a precursor to the notion of reflective subcategories and is technically useful in many arguments in category theory.
