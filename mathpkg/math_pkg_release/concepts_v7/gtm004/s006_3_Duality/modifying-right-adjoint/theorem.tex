If $F \dashv G$ with unit $\varepsilon$ a natural equivalence, there exists a functor $G' : \mathfrak{D} \to \mathfrak{C}$ and a natural equivalence $\theta : G \to G'$ such that $F \dashv G'$ with $G'F = 1_{\mathfrak{C}}$ and unit $1$. The functor $G'$ is defined by:
$$
G'(Y) = G(Y) \text{ if } Y \notin \operatorname{Im} F, \qquad G'(FX) = X,
$$
with morphisms adjusted accordingly using the unit $\varepsilon$ and its inverse $\varrho$.
