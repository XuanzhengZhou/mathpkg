An abelian category is the natural setting for homological algebra. It is an additive category with kernels, cokernels, and the property that every morphism factors as an epimorphism followed by a monomorphism (image factorization). The axioms are self-dual. Key examples include module categories, sheaf categories, and the category of abelian groups. The Freyd-Mitchell embedding theorem guarantees that every small abelian category can be embedded in a module category.
