If a functor $F$ is left adjoint to $G$, and $G$ preserves epimorphisms, then $F$ preserves projective objects. The proof is a straightforward diagram chase using the adjunction: a lifting problem in $\mathfrak{D}$ is transported via the right adjoint to a lifting problem in $\mathfrak{C}$, solved using projectivity, and then transported back. This proposition explains why free objects are projective.
