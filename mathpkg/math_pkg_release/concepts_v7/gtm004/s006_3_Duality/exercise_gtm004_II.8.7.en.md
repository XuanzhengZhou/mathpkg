# Exercise 8.7

The Birkhoff-Witt Theorem asserts that every Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ over the field $K$ may be embedded in an associative $K$-algebra $U\mathfrak{g}$ in such a way that the Lie bracket $[x,y]$ coincides with $xy - yx$ in $U\mathfrak{g}$, $x,y \in \mathfrak{g}$, and such that to every associative $K$-algebra $A$ and every $K$-linear map $f: \mathfrak{g} \to A$ with

$$f[x,y] = f(x)f(y) - f(y)f(x), \quad x,y \in \mathfrak{g},$$

there exists a unique $K$-algebra homomorphism $f^*: U\mathfrak{g} \to A$ extending $f$. Express this theorem in the language of this section.
