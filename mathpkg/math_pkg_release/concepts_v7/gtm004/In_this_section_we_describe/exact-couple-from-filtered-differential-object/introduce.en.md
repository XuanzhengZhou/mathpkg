Given a filtered differential object $C$, one assembles the homology groups of the filtration layers $H(C^{(p)})$ and of the successive quotients $H(C^{(p)}/C^{(p-1)})$ into an exact couple. The morphisms $i$, $j$, $k$ of the exact couple are induced respectively by the filtration inclusions, the quotient projections, and the connecting homomorphisms from the homology exact triangles. This construction is the most common source of exact couples in homological algebra and provides a functor from filtered differential objects to exact couples, whose associated spectral sequence is the spectral sequence of the filtered differential object.
