A filtered object in an arbitrary abelian category generalizes the notion of a filtered module or chain complex. The category $(\mathfrak{B}, f)$ of filtered objects over $\mathfrak{B}$ has as objects pairs $(B, \{B^{(p)}\})$ where $\{B^{(p)}\}$ is a doubly-infinite increasing sequence of subobjects, and as morphisms those $\mathfrak{B}$-morphisms that preserve the filtration at each level. This category supports the associated graded functor $Gr : (\mathfrak{B}, f) \to \mathfrak{B}^{\mathbb{Z}}$, which extracts the successive quotient objects $B^{(p)}/B^{(p-1)}$.
