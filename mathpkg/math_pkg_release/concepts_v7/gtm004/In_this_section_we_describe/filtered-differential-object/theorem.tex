Let $(\mathfrak{A}, d)$ be a category of differential objects. An object $C$ of $(\mathfrak{A}, d)$ is a \textbf{filtered differential object} if $C$ is filtered by subobjects of $(\mathfrak{A}, d)$,
$$
\cdots \subseteq C^{(p-1)} \subseteq C^{(p)} \subseteq \cdots \subseteq C, \quad -\infty < p < \infty,
$$
where each $C^{(p)}$ is closed under the differential $d$ on $C$, i.e., $d C^{(p)} \subseteq C^{(p)}$. The category of filtered differential objects in $\mathfrak{A}$ is denoted by $(\mathfrak{A}, d, f)$; morphisms in $(\mathfrak{A}, d, f)$ respect filtration and commute with differentials.
