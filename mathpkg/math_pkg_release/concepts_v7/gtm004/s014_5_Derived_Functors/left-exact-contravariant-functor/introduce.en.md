A contravariant functor $S$ is left exact if it preserves the exactness of sequences that terminate in zero on the right, but reverses the direction of arrows: from an exact sequence $A' \rightarrow A \rightarrow A'' \rightarrow 0$, one obtains an exact sequence $0 \rightarrow SA'' \rightarrow SA \rightarrow SA'$. This is equivalent to $S$ being left exact when viewed as a covariant functor on the opposite category. The canonical example is the contravariant Hom functor $\operatorname{Hom}_A(-, B)$, whose right derived functors are the Ext groups.
