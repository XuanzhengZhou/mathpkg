Lambek's Lemma is a technical but powerful isomorphism theorem for commutative diagrams with exact rows. Given such a diagram, it identifies a canonical quotient of a kernel with a subquotient of an image: $\Phi : \ker(\theta\alpha_2)/(\ker\alpha_2 + \ker\varphi) \cong (\operatorname{im}\varphi \cap \operatorname{im}\beta_1)/\operatorname{im}(\varphi\alpha_1)$. In Hilton--Stammbach, this lemma is applied repeatedly to a large diagram (3.2) to prove that the Ext groups defined via projective presentations and via injective presentations are naturally isomorphic, establishing the symmetry of the two approaches.
