This theorem states that the two a priori different constructions of Ext — one via a projective presentation of the first argument, the other via an injective presentation of the second argument — yield naturally isomorphic abelian groups. The proof uses a large commutative diagram and Lambek's Lemma repeatedly to establish that successive kernels in the diagram are isomorphic, ultimately linking the projective-side Ext (Ker $\Sigma_5$) to the injective-side Ext (Ker $\Sigma_1$). This symmetry is a hallmark of homological algebra: Ext can be computed by resolving either argument, making it a balanced functor.
