A full subcategory contains all morphisms between its objects that exist in the ambient category. In other words, the restriction to a full subcategory only selects which objects to keep, but retains all structure-preserving maps between them. For example, the category of abelian groups is a full subcategory of the category of groups, while the category of rings-with-unity is a non-full subcategory of the category of rings.
