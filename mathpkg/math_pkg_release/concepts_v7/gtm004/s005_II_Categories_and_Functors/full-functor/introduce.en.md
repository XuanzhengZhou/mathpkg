A full functor is one that is surjective on morphism sets: every morphism between $FA$ and $FB$ in the target category comes from some morphism between $A$ and $B$ in the source category. Together with faithfulness, fullness characterizes when a functor gives an equivalence of categories. The inclusion of a full subcategory into the ambient category is the prototypical example of a full functor.
