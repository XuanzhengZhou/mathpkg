An extension of $A$ by $B$ captures the idea of building a module $E$ from a submodule $B$ and a quotient $A$. This is the natural module-theoretic analogue of the group extension problem: given two modules, classify all ways to fit them together into a larger module via a short exact sequence. The set of equivalence classes of such extensions, denoted $E(A, B)$, forms the central object of study in this chapter and is ultimately identified with the Ext functor.
