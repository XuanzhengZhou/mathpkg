# Exercise 2.3

Suppose $\Lambda$ is commutative, and $A$ and $B$ are two $\Lambda$-modules. Define for a $\Lambda$-module homomorphism $\varphi: A \to B$, $(\lambda \varphi)(a) = \varphi(\lambda a)$, for $a \in A$. Show that this definition makes $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(A, B)$ into a $\Lambda$-module. Also show that this definition does not work in case $\Lambda$ is not commutative.
