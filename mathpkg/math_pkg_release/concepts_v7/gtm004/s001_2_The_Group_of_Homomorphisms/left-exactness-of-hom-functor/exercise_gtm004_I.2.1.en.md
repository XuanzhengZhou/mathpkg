# Exercise 2.1

Show that in the setting of Theorem 2.1, $\varepsilon_* = \operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(A, \varepsilon)$ is not, in general, surjective even if $\varepsilon$ is. (Take $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}$, $A = \mathbb{Z}_n$, the integers mod $n$, and the short exact sequence $\mathbb{Z} \xrightarrow{\mu} \mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z}_n$ where $\mu$ is multiplication by $n$.)
