Let $\beta \colon B_1 \to B_2$ be a homomorphism of $\Lambda$-modules. To a homomorphism $\varphi \colon A \to B_1$ one assigns the homomorphism $\beta \circ \varphi \colon A \to B_2$, thus defining a map
$$
\beta_{*} = \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, \beta) \colon \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, B_1) \longrightarrow \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, B_2).
$$
This map $\beta_{*}$ is a homomorphism of abelian groups.
