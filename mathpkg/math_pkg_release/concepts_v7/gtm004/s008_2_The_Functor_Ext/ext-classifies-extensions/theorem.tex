There is a natural equivalence of set-valued bifunctors
$$\eta : E(-, -) \xrightarrow{\cong} \text{Ext}_\Lambda(-, -),$$
where $E(A, B)$ denotes the set of equivalence classes of extensions of $A$ by $B$. The map $\eta$ is defined as follows: given a projective presentation $R \xrightarrow{\mu} P \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} A$ and an extension $B \xrightarrow{\kappa} E \xrightarrow{\nu} A$, construct the pull-back $E^\alpha$ of $\nu$ and $\varepsilon$, then define $\psi : R \to B$ via the universal property of the pull-back; the class $[\psi] \in \text{Ext}_\Lambda^\varepsilon(A, B)$ is $\eta$ of the equivalence class of the extension.
