Let $R \xrightarrow{\mu} P \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} A$ be a projective presentation of the $\Lambda$-module $A$. It induces for a $\Lambda$-module $B$ an exact sequence
$$\text{Hom}_\Lambda(A, B) \xrightarrow{\varepsilon^*} \text{Hom}_\Lambda(P, B) \xrightarrow{\mu^*} \text{Hom}_\Lambda(R, B).$$
Define
$$\text{Ext}_\Lambda^\varepsilon(A, B) = \operatorname{coker}\!\big(\mu^* : \text{Hom}_\Lambda(P, B) \rightarrow \text{Hom}_\Lambda(R, B)\big).$$
The superscript $\varepsilon$ indicates that the group is defined via the particular projective presentation $R \to P \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} A$.
