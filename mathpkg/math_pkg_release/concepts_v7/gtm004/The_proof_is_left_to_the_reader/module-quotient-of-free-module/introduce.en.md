This proposition asserts that any module can be expressed as a quotient of a free module. By taking a set of generators and constructing the free module on that set, the universal property of the free module yields a canonical surjection onto the original module. This is the first step in constructing a free (or projective) resolution of a module, a central technique in homological algebra. The kernel of this surjection measures the relations among the generators.
