A spectral sequence is a sequence of differential objects $\{(E_n, d_n)\}$ such that the homology of each term computes the next term: $H(E_n, d_n) = E_{n+1}$. This iterative structure allows one to compute a desired homology group by successively approximating it through a series of pages. Spectral sequences are among the most powerful computational tools in homological algebra, used extensively in algebraic topology, algebraic geometry, and group cohomology.
