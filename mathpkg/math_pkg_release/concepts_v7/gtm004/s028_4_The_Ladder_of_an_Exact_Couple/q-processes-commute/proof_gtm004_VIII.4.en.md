# Proof of Commutativity of Q-processes

**Theorem 4.1.** $Q^{\varrho}Q_{\sigma} = Q_{\sigma}Q^{\varrho}$.

**Proof.** It is clear, in fact, that $B_1$ is obtained from $B$ by first cutting down to the subobject $\gamma^{-1}(C_1)$ and then factoring out $\beta\sigma^{-1}(0)$, whereas $\bar{B}_1$ is obtained by the opposite process, that is, first factoring out $\beta\sigma^{-1}(0)$ and then cutting down to the subobject corresponding to $\gamma^{-1}(C_1)$. Thus

$$B_1 = \bar{B}_1 = \gamma^{-1}(C_1)/\beta\sigma^{-1}(0).$$

Moreover, $\bar{\beta}_1$ and $\beta_1$ are induced on $A_1 = A/\sigma^{-1}(0)$ by $\beta$, and $\bar{\gamma}_1$ and $\gamma_1$ are induced on $C_1$ by $\gamma$. $\square$

(The reader requiring a more category-theoretical argument will find it in [10], where the categorical argument appears in the proof of Theorem III.1.4 in the case when $A \to B \to C$ is short exact.)
