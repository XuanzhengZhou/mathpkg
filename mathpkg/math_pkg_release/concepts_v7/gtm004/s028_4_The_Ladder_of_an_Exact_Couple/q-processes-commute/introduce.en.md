Theorem 4.1 establishes that the two fundamental operations used to construct the ladder of an exact couple — the $Q^{\varepsilon}$ pullback process and the $Q_{\sigma}$ pushout process — commute with each other. This commutativity is crucial because it means the order in which one "cuts down" (pulls back along a monomorphism) and "factors out" (pushes out along an epimorphism) does not affect the resulting object, which equals $\gamma^{-1}(C_1)/\beta\sigma^{-1}(0)$. The result justifies the introduction of the combined notation $Q_{\sigma}^{\theta} = Q^{\theta}Q_{\sigma} = Q_{\sigma}Q^{\theta}$ used throughout the ladder construction.
