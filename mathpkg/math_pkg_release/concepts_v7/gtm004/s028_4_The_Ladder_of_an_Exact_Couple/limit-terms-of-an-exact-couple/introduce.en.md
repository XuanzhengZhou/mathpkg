The limit terms $E_{m,\infty}$ and $E_{\infty}$ provide a concrete description of the $E_{\infty}$ page of a spectral sequence arising from an exact couple. The term $E_{m,\infty}$ is the limit as the column index $n$ tends to infinity (with fixed rung $m$), while $E_{\infty}$ is the double limit over both indices. In module categories, these limits admit the explicit quotient descriptions $E_{m,\infty} = \gamma^{-1}(\alpha^{\infty}D)/\beta\alpha^{-m}(0)$ and $E_{\infty} = \gamma^{-1}(\alpha^{\infty}D)/\beta\alpha^{-\infty}(0)$, which are fundamental for understanding the convergence of the spectral sequence to the associated graded object of a filtration on the abutment.
