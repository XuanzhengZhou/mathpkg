The $Q^{\varepsilon}$-process (also called the $Q^{\varrho}$-process) is a categorical pullback construction that "cuts down" the middle object of a two-step sequence $A \to B \to C$ along a monomorphism $C_1 \rightarrowtail C$. Given that the composite $\gamma\beta$ factors through the monomorphism, one forms the pullback of $\gamma$ along $\varrho$, yielding a new middle object $B_{0,1} = \gamma^{-1}(C_1)$ together with an induced morphism $\beta_{0,1} : A \to B_{0,1}$. This process is one half of the machinery used to construct the ladder of an exact couple, the other half being the dual push-out $Q_{\sigma}$-process.
