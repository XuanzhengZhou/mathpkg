Two abelian categories $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ are said to be \textbf{derived equivalent} if their derived categories are equivalent as triangulated categories:
$$
\mathfrak{D}(\mathfrak{A}) \simeq \mathfrak{D}(\mathfrak{B})
$$
(or similarly for the bounded variants $\mathfrak{D}^{+}$, $\mathfrak{D}^{-}$, $\mathfrak{D}^{b}$). A triangulated functor establishing such an equivalence is called a derived equivalence.

Example: Let $k$ be a field of characteristic $p$, $G$ a finite group with cyclic $p$-Sylow subgroup $P$, and $H = N_G(P)$ the normalizer of $P$ in $G$. Then the bounded derived categories of the principal blocks of $kG$ and $kH$ are equivalent:
$$
\mathfrak{D}^{b}(B_0(kG)) \simeq \mathfrak{D}^{b}(B_0(kH)).
$$
