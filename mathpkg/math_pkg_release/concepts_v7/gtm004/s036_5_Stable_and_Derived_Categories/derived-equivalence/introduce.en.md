Derived equivalence is a profound notion: two abelian categories may have equivalent derived categories even when the categories themselves are not equivalent. This means their homological algebra is essentially the same, despite appearing different at the abelian level. In modular representation theory, derived equivalences (such as those arising from Rickard's tilting theory) have become a powerful tool for relating the representation theory of different groups or different blocks of the same group. The example with cyclic Sylow subgroups shows how derived equivalences can reduce questions about $G$ to questions about a smaller subgroup $N_G(P)$.
