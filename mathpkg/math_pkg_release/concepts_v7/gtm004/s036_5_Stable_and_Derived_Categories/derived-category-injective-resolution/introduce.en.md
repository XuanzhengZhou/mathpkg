This theorem bridges the classical and modern approaches to homological algebra. It shows that when the abelian category has enough injectives, the bounded-below derived category $\mathfrak{D}^+(\mathfrak{A})$ is equivalent to the homotopy category of injective complexes. This means that the derived category framework faithfully generalizes the classical theory of injective resolutions: derived functors defined via the derived category (as $H^n(RF)$) coincide with the classical right derived functors when the starting functor is left exact. The theorem justifies the derived category as the proper generalization of classical homological algebra to settings without enough projectives or injectives.
