The Heller operator (Omega) assigns to each module the kernel of a projective presentation. While Omega is not a functor on the ordinary module category, it becomes an endofunctor on the stable module category. In many cases, such as for group algebras of finite groups in characteristic dividing the group order, Omega is invertible and thus establishes a nontrivial stable self-equivalence.
