For the ring of integers $\mathbb{Z}$, the group $\mathbb{Z}$ itself is a projective module. A basic property of the Ext functor is that $\text{Ext}(P, B) = 0$ whenever $P$ is projective (Proposition 2.6). Applying this to $P = \mathbb{Z}$ yields $\text{Ext}(\mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z}) = 0$ and $\text{Ext}(\mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z}_q) = 0$ for any $q$. These vanishing results simplify many computations involving Ext over the integers.
