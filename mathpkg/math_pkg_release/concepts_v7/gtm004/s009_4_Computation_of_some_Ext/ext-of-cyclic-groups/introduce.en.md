A general computation of Ext between two finite cyclic groups yields $\text{Ext}(\mathbb{Z}_r, \mathbb{Z}_q) \cong \mathbb{Z}_{(r,q)}$, where $(r,q)$ denotes the greatest common divisor of $r$ and $q$. The computation uses the same projective presentation $\mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z}_r$ as before, applying $\text{Hom}(-, \mathbb{Z}_q)$. The map $\mu^*$ on $\text{Hom}(\mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z}_q) \cong \mathbb{Z}_q$ is multiplication by $r$, so its cokernel is $\mathbb{Z}_q / r\mathbb{Z}_q \cong \mathbb{Z}_{(r,q)}$. When $r$ and $q$ are coprime, $\text{Ext}(\mathbb{Z}_r, \mathbb{Z}_q) = 0$; when $r$ divides $q$, $\text{Ext}(\mathbb{Z}_r, \mathbb{Z}_q) \cong \mathbb{Z}_r$.
