# Proof: $\operatorname{Ext}(\mathbb{Z}_r, \mathbb{Z}_q) \cong \mathbb{Z}_{(r,q)}$

Here $\operatorname{Ext}$ denotes $\operatorname{Ext}_{\mathbb{Z}}$ and $(r,q)$ denotes the greatest common divisor of $r$ and $q$.

## Statement

For positive integers $r, q$,

$$\operatorname{Ext}(\mathbb{Z}_r, \mathbb{Z}_q) \cong \mathbb{Z}_{(r,q)},$$

where $(r,q) = \gcd(r,q)$.

## Proof

We use the same projective presentation of $\mathbb{Z}_r$ as before:

$$\mathbb{Z} \xrightarrow{\;\mu\;} \mathbb{Z} \xrightarrow{\;\varepsilon\;} \mathbb{Z}_r \longrightarrow 0,$$

where $\mu$ is multiplication by $r$ and $\varepsilon$ is reduction modulo $r$.

Apply the contravariant functor $\operatorname{Hom}(-, \mathbb{Z}_q)$ to this presentation. By the definition of $\operatorname{Ext}$ (Proposition 2.6), we obtain the exact sequence

$$\operatorname{Hom}(\mathbb{Z}_r, \mathbb{Z}_q) \longrightarrow \operatorname{Hom}(\mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z}_q) \xrightarrow{\;\mu^*\;} \operatorname{Hom}(\mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z}_q) \longrightarrow \operatorname{Ext}(\mathbb{Z}_r, \mathbb{Z}_q) \longrightarrow 0.$$

Now evaluate each term:

- $\operatorname{Hom}(\mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z}_q) \cong \mathbb{Z}_q$, via the isomorphism $\varphi \mapsto \varphi(1)$.
- Under this identification, the induced map $\mu^*$ is again multiplication by $r$. Indeed, for $\varphi \in \operatorname{Hom}(\mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z}_q)$ corresponding to the element $a = \varphi(1) \in \mathbb{Z}_q$, we have

  $$\mu^*(\varphi)(1) = (\varphi \circ \mu)(1) = \varphi(r) = r \cdot \varphi(1) = r a \pmod{q},$$

  so $\mu^*$ corresponds to the map $\mathbb{Z}_q \xrightarrow{r} \mathbb{Z}_q$ given by multiplication by $r$ modulo $q$.

Thus the exact sequence takes the form

$$\operatorname{Hom}(\mathbb{Z}_r, \mathbb{Z}_q) \longrightarrow \mathbb{Z}_q \xrightarrow{\;r\;} \mathbb{Z}_q \longrightarrow \operatorname{Ext}(\mathbb{Z}_r, \mathbb{Z}_q) \longrightarrow 0,$$

from which we read off

$$\operatorname{Ext}(\mathbb{Z}_r, \mathbb{Z}_q) \cong \operatorname{coker}\!\big(\mathbb{Z}_q \xrightarrow{r} \mathbb{Z}_q\big).$$

The cokernel of multiplication by $r$ on $\mathbb{Z}_q$ is $\mathbb{Z}_q / r\mathbb{Z}_q$. In $\mathbb{Z}_q$, the ideal generated by $r$ equals the ideal generated by $\gcd(r,q) = (r,q)$, because in the ring $\mathbb{Z}_q$ we have

$$r\mathbb{Z}_q = (r,q)\,\mathbb{Z}_q.$$

(Indeed, write $(r,q) = ar + bq$ with $a,b \in \mathbb{Z}$ by the Euclidean algorithm; then $r \equiv (r,q) \cdot a^{-1} \pmod{q}$ when $\gcd(a,q) = 1$, and more generally the subgroup generated by $r$ in $\mathbb{Z}_q$ is the subgroup generated by $(r,q)$.)

Therefore,

$$\operatorname{Ext}(\mathbb{Z}_r, \mathbb{Z}_q) \;\cong\; \mathbb{Z}_q / (r,q)\mathbb{Z}_q \;\cong\; \mathbb{Z}_{(r,q)}.$$

This completes the computation.

## Special cases

- If $r$ and $q$ are coprime, i.e. $(r,q) = 1$, then $\operatorname{Ext}(\mathbb{Z}_r, \mathbb{Z}_q) \cong \mathbb{Z}_1 = 0$.
- If $r$ divides $q$, then $(r,q) = r$ and $\operatorname{Ext}(\mathbb{Z}_r, \mathbb{Z}_q) \cong \mathbb{Z}_r$.
- In particular, $\operatorname{Ext}(\mathbb{Z}_r, \mathbb{Z}_r) \cong \mathbb{Z}_r$.
