# Exercise 4.1

Show that there are $p$ nonequivalent extensions

$$Z_p \rightarrowtail E \twoheadrightarrow Z_p$$

for $p$ a prime, but only two nonisomorphic groups $E$, namely $Z_p \oplus Z_p$ and $Z_{p^2}$. How does this come about?
