Lemma 3.3 of Hilton--Stammbach states that the homotopy relation respects composition: if $\varphi \simeq \psi : C \to D$ and $\varphi' \simeq \psi' : D \to E$, then the composites satisfy $\varphi'\varphi \simeq \psi'\psi : C \to E$. The proof proceeds by first composing the homotopy for $\varphi,\psi$ with $\varphi'$, and then composing the homotopy for $\varphi',\psi'$ with $\psi$, adding the two resulting homotopies. This compatibility with composition is what makes the homotopy category well-defined.
