Lemma 2.2 is the relative analogue of the Horseshoe Lemma. It states that
any short $\mathcal{E}$-exact sequence can be embedded in a $3 	imes 3$
diagram whose middle column is built from $\mathcal{E}$-projective presentations.
The construction proceeds exactly as in the absolute case, setting
$P = P' \oplus P''$ and lifting through the closed class $\mathcal{E}$.
The key extra verifications are that the constructed epimorphism $arepsilon$
and the induced map $\kappa''$ belong to $\mathcal{E}$, which relies on
$\mathcal{E}$ being closed. This lemma is the crucial technical step for
establishing the long exact sequence of $\mathcal{E}$-derived functors.
