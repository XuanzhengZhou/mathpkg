Proposition 2.4 shows that the condition of right $\mathcal{E}$-exactness is
strong enough to force additivity. The proof uses the fact that the zero object
is characterized by an $\mathcal{E}$-exact sequence, yielding $T(0) = 0$, and
then proceeds as in the absolute case. This is the relative version of the
classical result that a right exact functor between abelian categories is
automatically additive.
