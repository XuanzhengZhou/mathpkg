Theorem 3.5 is the dual of Theorem 3.2, formulated for right $\mathcal{M}$-derived
functors with respect to an injective class $\mathcal{M}$ of monomorphisms.
The notational convention differs: for $\mathcal{M}$-connected sequences, the
connecting morphisms go from $T_j A''$ to $T_{j+1} A'$ (instead of to $T_{j-1} A'$).
This theorem shows that right satellites of left $\mathcal{M}$-exact functors
coincide with right $\mathcal{M}$-derived functors, completing the duality
between the projective and injective formulations of relative homological algebra.
