A class of epimorphisms is closed when it already contains every epimorphism
against which its $\mathcal{E}$-projective objects lift. In other words, the
class is a fixed point of the closure operator $C$. The theory of relative
homological algebra is developed primarily for closed classes, as they provide
the right setting for $\mathcal{E}$-projective resolutions and $\mathcal{E}$-derived functors.
