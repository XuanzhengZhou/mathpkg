Completeness of a filtration captures whether the filtered object can be reconstructed from its filtration pieces via limits and colimits. Left completeness ensures the filtration exhausts the object, while right completeness ensures the object is the limit of its quotients. These conditions generalize homological finiteness and provide the minimal hypotheses needed for Theorem 8.4, which allows recovering isomorphisms of objects from isomorphisms of their associated graded objects.
