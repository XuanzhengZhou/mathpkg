\textbf{Theorem 8.5.} The completion $Y = (X^\infty)_{-\infty}$ of a filtered object $X$ is a complete filtration of $Y$, and
$$
\operatorname{Gr}(Y) \cong \operatorname{Gr}(X).
$$

Moreover, the completion is universal in the sense that the diagram
$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
X^\infty & \xrightarrow{\lambda} X \\
\downarrow{\scriptstyle \kappa} && \\
Y & \xrightarrow{\lambda} X_{-\infty}
\end{array}
$$
is bicartesian. The original filtration is left complete precisely when $\lambda$ is an isomorphism, and right complete precisely when $\kappa$ is an isomorphism.
