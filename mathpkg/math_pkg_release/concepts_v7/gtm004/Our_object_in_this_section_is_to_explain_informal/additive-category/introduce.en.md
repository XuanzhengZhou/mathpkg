An additive category enriches the notion of a category by requiring each hom-set to be an abelian group and composition to be bilinear. This structure is precisely what is needed to do linear algebra in a categorical setting. Key examples include the category of modules over a ring, the category of abelian groups, and categories of graded modules (with degree-0 morphisms). While finite products are required, coproducts follow automatically — in fact, finite products and coproducts coincide (biproducts).
