Let $\varphi: A \to X$ and $\psi: B \to X$ be morphisms in $\mathfrak{C}$, and let $\Delta = \varphi \alpha = \psi \beta$ be the diagonal of the pull-back square. Then $(\Delta; \alpha, \beta)$ is the product of $\varphi$ and $\psi$ in the comma category $\mathfrak{C}/X$, where $\alpha: \Delta \to \varphi$ and $\beta: \Delta \to \psi$ play the roles of the projections in the definition of a product when interpreted as morphisms in $\mathfrak{C}/X$.
