Suppose $F \dashv G$. Then $F$ determines $G$ up to natural equivalence. Specifically, if $F \dashv G$ and $F \dashv G'$, then there exists a natural equivalence $\theta: G \to G'$ (and its inverse $\bar{\theta}: G' \to G$) such that the adjugants are related by $\eta'(\varphi) = \theta_Y \circ \eta(\varphi)$ for any $\varphi: FX \to Y$.
