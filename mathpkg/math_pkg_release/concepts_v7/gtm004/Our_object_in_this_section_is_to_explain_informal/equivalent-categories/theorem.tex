Let $F: \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{D}$, $G: \mathfrak{D} \to \mathfrak{C}$ be functors such that $GF \simeq I_{\mathfrak{C}}$ and $FG \simeq I_{\mathfrak{D}}$, where $I$ denotes the identity functor. Then $\mathfrak{C}$ and $\mathfrak{D}$ are called \text{equivalent categories}. Isomorphic categories are equivalent, but equivalent categories need not be isomorphic.
