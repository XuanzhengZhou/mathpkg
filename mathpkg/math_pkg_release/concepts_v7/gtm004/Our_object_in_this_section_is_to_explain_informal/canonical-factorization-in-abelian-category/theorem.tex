Given a morphism $\varphi: A \to B$ in an abelian category $\mathfrak{A}$, there exists an essentially unique sequence (called the \text{canonical factorization})
$$A \xrightarrow{\eta} I \xrightarrow{\nu} B$$
where $\eta$ is an epimorphism and $\nu$ is a monomorphism, such that $\varphi = \nu \eta$. Specifically, if $\mu$ is the kernel of $\varphi$ and $\eta$ is the cokernel of $\mu$, then $\varphi = \nu \eta$ with $\nu$ a monomorphism. The object $I$ is the image of $\varphi$. Moreover, if $\varphi = \nu_1 \eta_1$ is any other epi-mono factorization, then there exists an isomorphism $\omega$ with $\eta_1 = \omega \eta$ and $\nu = \nu_1 \omega$.
