Any two objects satisfying the universal property of a product for the same family are canonically isomorphic via a unique isomorphism that respects the projections. This essential uniqueness justifies speaking of "the" product. If $(X; p_i)$ is a product and $\eta: X' \to X$ is an isomorphism, then $(X'; p_i\eta)$ is also a product, so products are only defined up to compatible isomorphism.
