Let $\mathfrak{C}$ be a category and $I, J$ small index categories. There are canonical identifications
$$
\mathfrak{C}^{I \times J} \cong (\mathfrak{C}^J)^I \cong (\mathfrak{C}^I)^J.
$$
Let $P : \mathfrak{C} \rightarrow \mathfrak{C}^{I \times J}$, $P_1 : \mathfrak{C} \rightarrow \mathfrak{C}^I$, $P_2 : \mathfrak{C} \rightarrow \mathfrak{C}^J$ be the diagonal functors and suppose $P_i \dashv R_i$ for $i = 1, 2$. Then there is a commutative diagram of functors
$$
\begin{CD}
\mathfrak{C}^{I \times J} @>{R_I}>> \mathfrak{C}^J \\
@V{R_J}VV @VV{R_2}V \\
\mathfrak{C}^I @>>{R_1}> \mathfrak{C}
\end{CD}
$$
and the limit functors satisfy
$$
\lim_{I} \lim_{J} \cong \lim_{I \times J} \cong \lim_{J} \lim_{I}.
$$
In particular, limits commute with limits.
