# Exercise 5.1

Complete the details of the proof of Theorem 5.1.

Specifically, carry out the following steps in full detail:

1. Verify that the identifications $\mathfrak{C}^{I \times J} \cong (\mathfrak{C}^J)^I \cong (\mathfrak{C}^I)^J$ given by the exponential law for functor categories are indeed natural isomorphisms of categories.

2. Show explicitly that the diagonal functors $P_1: \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{C}^I$ and $P_2: \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{C}^J$ induce functors $\mathfrak{C}^J \to \mathfrak{C}^{I \times J}$ and $\mathfrak{C}^I \to \mathfrak{C}^{I \times J}$ respectively, and that these fit into the commutative diagram of Theorem 5.1.

3. Verify that the composition $R_1 \circ R_J$ (where $R_J: \mathfrak{C}^{I \times J} \to \mathfrak{C}^I$ is the right adjoint induced by $R_2$) is right adjoint to $P$, and similarly for $R_2 \circ R_I$. Conclude, by the uniqueness of right adjoints up to canonical isomorphism, that $\lim_I \lim_J \cong \lim_{I \times J} \cong \lim_J \lim_I$.

4. Provide a concrete description of the universal property of the iterated limit $\lim_I \lim_J F$ for a functor $F: I \times J \to \mathfrak{C}$, and show directly that it satisfies the same universal property as $\lim_{I \times J} F$.
