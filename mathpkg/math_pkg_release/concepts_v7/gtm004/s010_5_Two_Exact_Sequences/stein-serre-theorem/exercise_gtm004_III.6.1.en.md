# Exercise 6.1

Show that if $A$ is torsionfree, $\operatorname{Ext}(A, \mathbb{Z})$ is divisible, and that if $A$ is divisible, $\operatorname{Ext}(A, \mathbb{Z})$ is torsionfree. Show conversely that if $\operatorname{Ext}(A, \mathbb{Z})$ is divisible, $A$ is torsionfree and that if $\operatorname{Ext}(A, \mathbb{Z})$ is torsionfree and $\operatorname{Hom}(A, \mathbb{Z}) = 0$, then $A$ is divisible. (See Exercise 5.8.)
