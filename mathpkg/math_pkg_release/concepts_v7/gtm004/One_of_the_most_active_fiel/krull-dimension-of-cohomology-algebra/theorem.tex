Let $G$ be a finite group and let $k$ be a field of characteristic $p$ dividing $|G|$. Let
$$d_n = \dim_k H^n(G, k), \qquad n = 0, 1, 2, \ldots$$
be the dimensions of the cohomology groups of $G$ with coefficients in the trivial module $k$. The \textbf{Krull dimension} $c(G)$ of the cohomology algebra $H^*(G, k)$ is defined to be the growth of the sequence $\{d_n\}_{n \geq 0}$.

In particular, $c(G) = 1$ if and only if the dimensions $d_n$ are uniformly bounded. This occurs precisely when a $p$-Sylow subgroup of $G$ is cyclic, or when $p = 2$ and the $2$-Sylow subgroup is cyclic or generalized quaternion.
