Godel's completeness theorem (1930) is a foundational result stating that first-order logic is complete: every consistent set of formulas has a model, and every valid formula is provable. Together with the compactness theorem, it forms the basis of model theory. The proof constructs a model from constant terms of the language (the Henkin construction), establishing that semantic and syntactic consequence coincide for first-order logic.
