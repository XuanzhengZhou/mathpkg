A subset $R' \subset G' = \langle a_1, \ldots, a_n \mid \;\rangle$ (a free group) is called \text{bounded} if there exists $r > 1$ such that every element of $R'$ can be expressed as $a_{i_1}^{x_1} \cdots a_{i_r}^{x_r}$ with $x_i \in \mathbf{Z}$. That is, the word length of elements in $R'$ is uniformly bounded.
