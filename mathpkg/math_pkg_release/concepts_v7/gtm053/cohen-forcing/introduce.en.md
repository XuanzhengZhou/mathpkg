Cohen's forcing is the central technique for proving independence results in set theory. Starting from a countable transitive model M of ZFC, one adds a generic set G to obtain M[G], a larger model where desired properties hold. The forcing relation, definable in M, predicts truth in M[G]. Forcing established the independence of CH, the axiom of choice from ZF, and many other statements. It revolutionized set theory and led to the discovery of large cardinal axioms and descriptive set theory.
