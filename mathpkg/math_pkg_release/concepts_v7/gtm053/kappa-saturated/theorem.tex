Given an infinite cardinal $\kappa$, a structure $\mathbf{A}$ is called \text{$\kappa$-saturated} if for every cardinal $\lambda < \kappa$ and every expansion $\mathbf{A}_C$ of $\mathbf{A}$ by constant symbols $C = \{c_i : i \leq \lambda\}$, every $1$-type in $\operatorname{Th}(\mathbf{A}_C)$ is realized in $\mathbf{A}_C$. The structure is called \text{saturated} (without qualification) when $\kappa = \operatorname{card} A$.
