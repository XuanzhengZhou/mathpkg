A \text{differential field} is a field $K$ equipped with a derivation $D : K \to K$ satisfying $D(x+y) = Dx + Dy$ and $D(xy) = xDy + yDx$. The theory $\mathrm{DCF}_0$ of differentially closed fields of characteristic zero is the model completion of the theory of differential fields. It is $\aleph_0$-stable and admits quantifier elimination. The field of constants $C_K = \{y \in K : Dy = 0\}$ is an algebraically closed field of rank $1$.
