The compactness theorem is a foundational result in model theory: if every finite subset of a set of first-order sentences has a model, then the whole set has a model. It has profound consequences, including the existence of nonstandard models of arithmetic and analysis, and is equivalent to the ultrafilter principle. The proof uses the axiom of choice.
