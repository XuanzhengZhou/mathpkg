Church's thesis is not a formal theorem but an empirical claim identifying the informal notion of effective computability with the mathematically precise notion of recursive (Turing computable) function. All formal models of computation (Turing machines, lambda calculus, register machines, Markov algorithms) have been proved equivalent, providing strong evidence for the thesis. It underlies the theory of algorithmic undecidability and complexity theory.
