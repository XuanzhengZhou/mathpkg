Stability theory, developed by Shelah, classifies complete theories by the number of types over parameter sets. $\aleph_0$-stability is the strongest form, superstable is intermediate, and stable theories are the broadest class admitting a workable independence calculus (nonforking). Unstable theories include those with a definable linear order on an infinite set (e.g., RCF).
