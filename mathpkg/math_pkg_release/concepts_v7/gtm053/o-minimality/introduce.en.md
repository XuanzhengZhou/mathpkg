O-minimality is a tameness property of ordered structures generalizing the behavior of semialgebraic sets. Examples include RCF, the real field with exponentiation (Wilkie's theorem), and expansions by restricted analytic functions. O-minimal structures admit a well-behaved dimension theory, cell decomposition, and geometric structure resembling tame topology.
