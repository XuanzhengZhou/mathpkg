The downward Löwenheim-Skolem theorem states that every infinite first-order structure has elementary substructures of every smaller infinite cardinality (above the language size). Combined with the upward direction (from compactness), it shows first-order logic cannot control cardinality: any theory with infinite models has models of every infinite cardinality.
