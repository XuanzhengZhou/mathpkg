# Proof of Tarski's Theorem on ACF

**Theorem 4.2 (Tarski).** The theory $\mathrm{ACF}_p$ (algebraically closed fields of characteristic $p$, where $p$ is a prime or $0$) is complete and admits quantifier elimination.

*Proof.* The theorem follows from Steinitz's theorem in algebra together with the quantifier elimination criterion of Section 3.18.

**Steinitz's Theorem.** Given two algebraically closed fields $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ of the same characteristic $p$ and a common subfield $\mathbf{k}$,

$$\mathbf{A} \cong_{\mathbf{k}} \mathbf{B} \quad \text{if and only if} \quad \operatorname{trd}(\mathbf{A}/\mathbf{k}) = \operatorname{trd}(\mathbf{B}/\mathbf{k}),$$

where $\operatorname{trd}$ denotes the transcendence degree over $\mathbf{k}$, i.e., the cardinality of a maximal algebraically independent subset.

To prove quantifier elimination for $\mathrm{ACF}_p$, consider a sufficiently saturated model $\mathbf{A}$ of $\mathrm{ACF}_p$. By the criterion of Theorem 3.18, it suffices to show: for any two $n$-tuples $\bar{a}, \bar{b} \in A^n$,

$$\operatorname{qftp}(\bar{a}) = \operatorname{qftp}(\bar{b}) \implies \exists \pi \in \operatorname{Aut}(\mathbf{A}) : \pi(\bar{a}) = \bar{b}.$$

If $\operatorname{qftp}(\bar{a}) = \operatorname{qftp}(\bar{b})$, then the subfields $\mathbf{k}(\bar{a})$ and $\mathbf{k}(\bar{b})$ generated by the tuple entries over the prime field $\mathbf{k}$ (isomorphic to $\mathbb{Q}$ or $\mathbb{F}_p$) are isomorphic via $\bar{a} \mapsto \bar{b}$, since quantifier-free formulas express exactly polynomial equalities and inequalities. Both $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{A}$ (the same field) are algebraically closed extensions of these subfields. By Steinitz's theorem, the isomorphism $\mathbf{k}(\bar{a}) \to \mathbf{k}(\bar{b})$ extends to an automorphism of the algebraic closures. Since $\mathbf{A}$ is algebraically closed, these algebraic closures embed in $\mathbf{A}$. Using the saturation of $\mathbf{A}$ and the fact that transcendence bases can be matched (both have the same transcendence degree over the prime field, namely $|\mathbf{A}|$), the isomorphism extends to all of $\mathbf{A}$.

Completeness follows directly from quantifier elimination: the only quantifier-free sentences are Boolean combinations of polynomial equations over the prime field, whose truth values are determined by the characteristic alone. Hence all models of $\mathrm{ACF}_p$ satisfy the same sentences. $\square$
