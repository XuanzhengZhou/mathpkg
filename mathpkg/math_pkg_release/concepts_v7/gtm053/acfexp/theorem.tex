$\mathrm{ACFExp}$ is the theory of algebraically closed fields of characteristic zero equipped with a \text{pseudoexponentiation} $\operatorname{ex}$, a homomorphism from the additive group to the multiplicative group:
$$\operatorname{ex}(x_1 + x_2) = \operatorname{ex}(x_1) \cdot \operatorname{ex}(x_2).$$
The predimension for this class is
$$\delta(X) = \operatorname{trd}(X \cup \operatorname{ex} X) - \operatorname{ldim} X,$$
where $\operatorname{ldim} X$ is the $\mathbf{Q}$-linear dimension of $X$. The Hrushovski inequality $\delta(X) \geq 0$ is then equivalent to Schanuel's conjecture for the tuple $X$.
