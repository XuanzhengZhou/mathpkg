# Model-Theoretic Treatment of Schanuel's Conjecture

**Theorem 6.16 (B. Zilber).** There exists an axiomatization ACFExp (algebraically closed fields with pseudoexponentiation) in the language $\{+, \cdot, \mathrm{ex}, 0, 1\}$ such that:

(i) The axioms are modeled on Schanuel's conjecture: $\operatorname{trd}(x_1, \ldots, x_n, \mathrm{ex}(x_1), \ldots, \mathrm{ex}(x_n)) \geq \operatorname{ldim}_{\mathbb{Q}}(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$, together with an existential closedness condition stating that any system of $n$ independent exponential-polynomial equations in $n$ variables that does not directly contradict Schanuel's conjecture has a regular zero (at most countably many);

(ii) ACFExp is categorical in uncountable powers $\kappa$: for every uncountable $\kappa$ there is a unique, up to isomorphism, algebraically closed field with pseudoexponentiation of cardinality $\kappa$;

(iii) An algebraically closed field with pseudoexponentiation carries a homogeneous pregeometry: any bijection between two bases of the pregeometry extends to an automorphism of the field with pseudoexponentiation.

*Proof sketch.* The construction uses Hrushovski's method of free fusion of pregeometries (Section 6.11-6.14).

**Step 1: Defining the predimension.** Consider the class of algebraically closed fields $\mathbf{F}$ of characteristic $0$ extended by a new function $\mathrm{ex}$ satisfying the homomorphism condition:
$$\mathrm{ex}(x_1 + x_2) = \mathrm{ex}(x_1) \cdot \mathrm{ex}(x_2).$$

The free fusion principle uniquely determines the predimension $\delta$: for any finite $X \subseteq F$,
$$\delta(X) = \operatorname{trd}(X \cup \mathrm{ex}(X)) - \operatorname{ldim}_{\mathbb{Q}}(X),$$
where $\operatorname{ldim}_{\mathbb{Q}}(X)$ is the dimension of the $\mathbb{Q}$-vector space generated by $X$. 

**Step 2: The Hrushovski inequality.** One restricts to the class $\mathcal{H}_\delta$ of structures satisfying $\delta(X) \geq 0$ for all finite $X$. The Hrushovski inequality $\operatorname{trd}(x_1, \ldots, x_n, \mathrm{ex}(x_1), \ldots, \mathrm{ex}(x_n)) \geq \operatorname{ldim}_{\mathbb{Q}}(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ is precisely the statement of Schanuel's conjecture.

**Step 3: Existential closedness.** One constructs an algebraically closed object in the class $\mathcal{H}_\delta$ using the notion of strong embedding. This construction automatically yields the categoricity statement (ii) and the homogeneity statement (iii) by general properties of Hrushovski's construction.

**Consequences.** The theorem shows that Schanuel's conjecture is consistent with field-theoretic algebra. The categoricity statement strengthens this: Schanuel's conjecture together with the existential closedness axiom makes the algebra of the structure uniquely determined.

**Conjecture 6.17.** The unique algebraically closed field with pseudoexponentiation of cardinality continuum is isomorphic to $(\mathbb{C}, +, \cdot, \exp)$, the complex field with exponentiation. This conjecture implies Schanuel's conjecture and the statement that $(\mathbb{C}, +, \cdot, \exp)$ is algebraically closed as a field with exponentiation. $\square$
