# Proof of Theorem 2.9 (Existence and uniqueness of compatible $C^s$ differential structure)

Let $\alpha$ be a $C^r$ differential structure on a manifold $M$, $r \geqslant 1$. For every $s$, $r < s \leqslant \infty$, there exists a compatible $C^s$ differential structure $\beta \subset \alpha$, and $\beta$ is unique up to $C^s$ diffeomorphism.

**Existence.** For convenience we shall denote a differential structure and its restriction to an open set by the same letter. Let $\{U_i\}_{i \in \Lambda}$ be a locally finite cover of $M$ by coordinate domains of $\alpha$. For each $i$, the identity map $\operatorname{id}: U_i \to U_i$ is a $C^r$ diffeomorphism between the $C^r$ structure $\alpha|U_i$ and the standard structure on the image in $\mathbb{R}^m$. By applying Theorem 2.6 locally, there exists a $C^s$ differential structure on $U_i$ compatible with $\alpha|U_i$.

One now patches these local $C^s$ structures using Lemma 2.8 and a structure functor on the family $\mathfrak{Y}$ generated by finite unions of the $U_i$. Define $\mathcal{F}(Y)$ for $Y \in \mathfrak{Y}$ as the set of compatible $C^s$ differential structures on $Y$ (relative to $\alpha|Y$). This defines a structure functor $(\mathcal{F}, \mathfrak{Y})$ which is nontrivial (nonempty on each $U_i$), continuous, and locally extendable. The extendability follows from Lemma 2.8: given a $C^s$ structure on $A \in \mathfrak{Y}$ and an additional coordinate domain $U_k$, one uses Lemma 2.8 to extend the transition maps to $C^s$ on a neighborhood of $A \cap U_k$, thereby extending the $C^s$ structure to $A \cup U_k$.

By the Globalization Theorem (Theorem 2.11), $\mathcal{F}(M) \neq \emptyset$, yielding a compatible $C^s$ differential structure $\beta \subset \alpha$.

**Uniqueness up to $C^s$ diffeomorphism.** Let $\beta_1, \beta_2 \subset \alpha$ be two compatible $C^s$ structures. Consider the identity map $\operatorname{id}: M_{\beta_1} \to M_{\beta_2}$. By compatibility, $\operatorname{id}$ is $C^r$. By Theorem 2.6 there is a $C^s$ diffeomorphism $\theta_0$ in a suitable neighborhood $\mathcal{N}_0$; the required $C^s$ diffeomorphism $\theta: M_{\beta_1} \to M_{\beta_2}$ is then obtained via the map $T$ from Lemma 2.8.

It is amusing that neither paracompactness nor Hausdorffness of $M$ was used in the preceding proof; these properties were needed only for the coordinate domain $U$.

QED
