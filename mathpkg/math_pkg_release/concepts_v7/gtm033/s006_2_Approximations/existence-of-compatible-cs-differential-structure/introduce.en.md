Theorem 2.9 shows that any $C^r$ differential structure ($r \geqslant 1$) can be refined to a compatible $C^s$ structure for any $s > r$, and this refinement is unique up to $C^s$ diffeomorphism. The proof uses Lemma 2.8 to inductively upgrade charts, and the Globalization Theorem 2.11 to assemble local $C^s$ structures into a global one. Notably, neither paracompactness nor Hausdorffness of $M$ is required.
