Hopf's theorem establishes the equality of two a priori different definitions of the Euler characteristic for compact manifolds without boundary. The homological Euler characteristic is the alternating sum of Betti numbers, while the geometric Euler characteristic is the sum of indices of zeros of any vector field with isolated zeros. The proof uses a Morse function to construct a gradient vector field whose zeros correspond to critical points, with each critical point of index $k$ contributing $(-1)^k$ to the index sum, matching the alternating sum of the Morse type numbers.
