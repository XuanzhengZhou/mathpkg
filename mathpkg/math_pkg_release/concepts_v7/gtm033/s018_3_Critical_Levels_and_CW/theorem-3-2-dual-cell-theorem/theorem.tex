Let $f: M \to [a, b]$ be an admissible Morse function having a unique critical point $z$, of index $k$. Then there exists an $(n-k)$-cell $e_*^{n-k} \subset M - f^{-1}(a)$ such that $e_*^{n-k} \cap f^{-1}(b) = \partial e_*^{n-k}$, and there is a deformation retraction of $M$ onto $f^{-1}(b) \cup e_*^{n-k}$. Moreover $e_*^{n-k}$ can be chosen so that $e^k$ meets $e_*^{n-k}$ only at $z$, and transversely.
