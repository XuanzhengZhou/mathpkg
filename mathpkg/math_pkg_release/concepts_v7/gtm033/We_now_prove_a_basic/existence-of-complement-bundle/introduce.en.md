This theorem guarantees that every vector bundle $\xi$ over a manifold admits a complementary bundle $\eta$ such that their direct sum is trivial. The proof uses Theorem 3.1 to embed $\xi$ as a subbundle of a trivial bundle $M \times \mathbb{R}^{n+k}$, then takes the orthogonal complement with respect to the standard inner product. This result is fundamental for defining the Grothendieck group $K(M)$ of vector bundles over $M$.
