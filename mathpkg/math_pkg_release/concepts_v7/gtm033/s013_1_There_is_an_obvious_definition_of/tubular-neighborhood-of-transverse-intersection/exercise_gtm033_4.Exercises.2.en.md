# Exercise 4.2 (Starred): Tubular Neighborhood of Transverse Intersection

Let $M_0, M_1$ be neat submanifolds of $V$ in general position. Let $(f_i, \xi_i)$ be a tubular neighborhood of $M_0 \cap M_1$ in $M_i$, $i = 0, 1$. Then there is a tubular neighborhood $(f, \xi_0 \oplus \xi_1)$ of $M_0 \cap M_1$ in $V$ such that $f | \xi_i = f_i$.

*Note: This is a starred (more difficult) exercise. When $M_0$ and $M_1$ intersect transversely, their intersection $M_0 \cap M_1$ is a neat submanifold of both $M_0$ and $M_1$. The normal bundle of $M_0 \cap M_1$ in $V$ splits as the Whitney sum $\xi_0 \oplus \xi_1$ of its normal bundles in $M_0$ and $M_1$ respectively. The tubular neighborhoods $(f_i, \xi_i)$ provide the restriction of the desired tubular neighborhood to each summand.*
