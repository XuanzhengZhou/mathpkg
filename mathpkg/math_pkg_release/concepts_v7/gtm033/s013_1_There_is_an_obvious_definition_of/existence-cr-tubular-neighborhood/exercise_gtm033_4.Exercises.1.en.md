# Exercise 4.1: Existence and Uniqueness of $C^r$ Tubular Neighborhoods up to $C^r$ Isotopy

There is an obvious definition of $C^r$ tubular neighborhood and $C^r$ isotopy, $1 \leqslant r < \infty$. A neat $C^r$ submanifold has a $C^r$ tubular neighborhood, unique up to $C^r$ isotopy.

*Note: This exercise asks the reader to extend the tubular neighborhood theorem from the $C^\infty$ category to the $C^r$ category, for any finite differentiability class $r \geqslant 1$. The proof follows the same strategy as the smooth case, replacing smooth approximations with $C^r$ approximations (using the $C^r$ version of the approximation theorems in Chapter 2).*
