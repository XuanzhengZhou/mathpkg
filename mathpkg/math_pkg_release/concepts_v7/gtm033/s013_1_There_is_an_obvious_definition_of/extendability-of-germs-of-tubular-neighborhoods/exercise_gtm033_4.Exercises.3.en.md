# Exercise 4.3 (Starred): Extendability of Germs of Tubular Neighborhoods

Let $M \subset V$ be a neat submanifold and $U \subset M$ a neighborhood of a closed subset $A \subset M$. For every tubular neighborhood $E_0$ of $U$ in $V$, there is a tubular neighborhood $E$ of $M$ in $V$, and a neighborhood $W \subset U$ of $A$, such that $E | W = E_0 | W$.

*Note: This is a starred (more difficult) exercise. The statement asserts that a tubular neighborhood defined only near a closed subset $A \subset M$ can be extended to a global tubular neighborhood of $M$, possibly after shrinking the domain of the original neighborhood. This is a germ extension theorem and is a key technical tool for local-to-global arguments involving tubular neighborhoods. The proof typically uses a partition of unity to patch local extensions together.*
