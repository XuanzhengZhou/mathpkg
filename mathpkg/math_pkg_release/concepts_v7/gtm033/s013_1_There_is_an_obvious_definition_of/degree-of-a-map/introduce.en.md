The degree of a map $f: M \to N$ between compact $n$-manifolds (without boundary, with $N$ connected) is a fundamental invariant in algebraic topology that counts how many times $f$ wraps $M$ around $N$. In the oriented case, $\deg(f)$ is an integer computed by summing orientation signs $\pm 1$ over the preimages of any regular value; in the unoriented case, it is an integer mod 2. The degree provides the essential bridge from continuous geometry to discrete algebra.
