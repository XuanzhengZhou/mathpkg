The degree of a map between compact $n$-manifolds depends only on the homotopy class of the map. This homotopy invariance has two crucial consequences: it makes the degree of any given map easy to compute (by deforming it to a more convenient representative), and it provides a powerful method for distinguishing homotopy classes of maps. This theorem is the foundational result that makes the degree a practical topological invariant.
