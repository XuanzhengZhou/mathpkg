The norm of a $k$-linear map $S: (\mathbb{R}^m)^k \to \mathbb{R}^n$ is the maximum absolute value attained by $S$ when all arguments are restricted to the unit sphere in $\mathbb{R}^m$. This definition extends the usual operator norm from linear maps to multilinear maps and satisfies the multiplicative bound $|S(y_1,\ldots,y_k)| \leq \|S\| \cdot |y_1|\cdots|y_k|$. It is used to define the norm of higher-order derivatives $D^k f(x)$, which are $k$-linear maps from $\mathbb{R}^m$ to $\mathbb{R}^n$.
