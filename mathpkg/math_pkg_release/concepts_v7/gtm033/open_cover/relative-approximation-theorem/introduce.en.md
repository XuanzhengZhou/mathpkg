Theorem 2.5, the Relative Approximation Theorem, refines the density result of Theorem 2.4 by allowing one to increase the differentiability class from $C^r$ to $C^s$ on a neighborhood of a prescribed closed set $K$, while keeping the approximating map arbitrarily close to $f$ in the strong $C^r$ topology and unchanged outside a given open set $W$. This localization capability is essential for globalization arguments on manifolds, where one patches together locally improved approximations.
