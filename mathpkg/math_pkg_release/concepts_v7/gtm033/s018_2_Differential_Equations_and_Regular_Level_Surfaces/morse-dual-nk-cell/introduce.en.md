Theorem 3.2 is the dual of Theorem 3.1, obtained by considering $-f$ instead of $f$. It yields an $(n-k)$-cell attached to $f^{-1}(b)$ rather than a $k$-cell attached to $f^{-1}(a)$. The two cells $e^k$ and $e_*^{n-k}$ are called dual cells: they intersect transversely only at the critical point $z$.
