The Betti numbers of a pair $(X,A)$ with rational coefficients are the dimensions of the singular homology groups $H_k(X,A;\mathbb{Q})$. The homological Euler characteristic $\chi'(X,A)$ is the alternating sum of Betti numbers. For compact manifolds, it is independent of the field and agrees with the Euler characteristic defined via vector field indices (Hopf's theorem).
