A CW-pair $(X, A)$ consists of a CW-complex $X$ together with a subcomplex $A$. This is the CW analogue of a pair of spaces and provides the natural setting for relative cellular constructions. CW-pairs have the homotopy extension property, making them well-behaved for homotopy-theoretic arguments.
