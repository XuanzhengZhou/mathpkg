# Proof of Corollary 3.4 (Morse Function with Two Critical Points Yields Sphere)

Let $M$ be a compact $2$-manifold without boundary admitting a Morse function having only $2$ critical points. Then $M \approx S^2$.

**Proof.** By Theorem 5.2.5 (and its proof), $M$ is the union of two $2$-disks glued along their boundaries. We may take the disks to be the upper and lower hemispheres of $S^2$; by Theorem 3.3 (classification of circle diffeomorphisms) we may take the gluing map to be the identity (after an isotopy). The result now follows from Theorem 2.2 (uniqueness of gluing). $\square$
