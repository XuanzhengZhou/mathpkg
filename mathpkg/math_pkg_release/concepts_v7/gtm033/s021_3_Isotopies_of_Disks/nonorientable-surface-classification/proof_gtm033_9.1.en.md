# Proof of Theorem 1.6 (Classification of Nonorientable Surfaces by Genus)

Let $M, N$ be nonorientable surfaces of genus $p, q$ respectively. Then $M \approx N$ if and only if $p = q$.

**Proof.** Left as an exercise in the text. The forward direction follows from the fact that the Euler characteristic of a nonorientable surface of genus $p$ is $2 - p$, and the Euler characteristic is a diffeomorphism invariant. Hence $M \approx N$ implies $\chi(M) = \chi(N)$, so $2 - p = 2 - q$, giving $p = q$. The converse follows from the inductive construction of nonorientable surfaces: any two nonorientable surfaces of the same genus $p$ are built from $S^2$ by the same sequence of crosscap attachments, and are therefore diffeomorphic by the isotopy classification of embeddings. $\square$
