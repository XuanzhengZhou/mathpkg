# Proof of Theorem 2.3 (Isotopic Gluing Diffeomorphisms Yield Diffeomorphic Manifolds)

Let $f, g: \partial Q \approx \partial P$ be isotopic diffeomorphisms. Then $P \bigcup_f Q \approx P \bigcup_g Q$.

**Proof.** The composition $g^{-1}f$ is isotopic to the identity of $\partial Q$ (since $f$ and $g$ are isotopic). This isotopy can be spread out over a collar neighborhood of $\partial Q$ in $Q$, and then extended to a diffeomorphism of $Q$ which is the identity outside the collar. The result now follows from Theorem 2.2 (uniqueness of gluing). $\square$
