# Proof of Corollary 1.2 (Handle Attachment to Nonorientable Surfaces)

Let $M$ be a connected surface. If $M$ is nonorientable, all surfaces obtained by attaching a handle to $M$ are diffeomorphic.

**Proof.** Use Theorem 8.3.2 (isotopy classification of disjoint disk pairs in nonorientable manifolds). Since any two embeddings $S^0 \times D^2 \hookrightarrow M$ can be isotoped to one another when $M$ is nonorientable, Theorem 1.1 implies all resulting handle attachments are diffeomorphic. $\square$
