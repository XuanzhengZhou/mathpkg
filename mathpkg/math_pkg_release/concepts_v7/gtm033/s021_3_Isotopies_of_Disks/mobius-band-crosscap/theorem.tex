A \textbf{Möbius band} (denoted $B$) is any surface diffeomorphic to the quotient of $[0,1] \times [0,1]$ by identifying $(0, y) \sim (1, 1-y)$. Note that $\partial B \approx S^1$.

The real projective plane $P$ can be obtained by gluing a disk $D^2$ and a Möbius band $B$ along their boundaries. For any surface $M$ without boundary,
$$
M \# P \approx (M - \operatorname{Int} D) \bigcup_f B
$$
where $D \subset M$ is a disk and $f: \partial B \approx \partial D$ is arbitrary.

The image of $B$ in $M \# P$ is called a \textbf{crosscap}. A \textbf{nonorientable surface of genus} $p \geqslant 1$ is a sphere with $p$ crosscaps attached, i.e., the connected sum of $p$ copies of the projective plane. Its Euler characteristic is $2 - p$.

A nonorientable surface of genus 2 is called a \textbf{Klein bottle}. It can be obtained from a sphere by attaching a handle via any nonorientable embedding $S^0 \times D^2 \hookrightarrow S^2$.
