A handle is attached to a surface by removing the interiors of two disjoint embedded disks and gluing in a cylinder $D^1 \times S^1$ along the resulting boundary circles. This surgery operation is the fundamental building block for constructing all compact surfaces. By the uniqueness of gluing, the resulting differential structure is well-defined up to diffeomorphism.
