# Exercise 3.2.6

A map $f: \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}^2$ has a *cusp* at $x \in \mathbb{R}^2$ if (i) $Df_x$ has rank $1$, (ii) $j^1 f$ is transverse at $x$ to the $1$-jets of rank $1$, and (iii) $\operatorname{Ker} Df_x$ is tangent to $R(f,1)$ (see Exercise 4).

(a) $(0,0)$ is a cusp of the map $g(x,y) = (x^3 - xy, y)$.

(b) If $U \subset \mathbb{R}^2$ is any neighborhood of $(0,0)$, there is a weak $C^2$ neighborhood $\mathcal{N}$ of $g$ such that every map in $\mathcal{N}$ has a cusp in $U$.
