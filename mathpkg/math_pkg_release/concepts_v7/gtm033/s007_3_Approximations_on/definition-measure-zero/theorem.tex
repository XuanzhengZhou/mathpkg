An $n$-cube $C \subset \mathbb{R}^n$ of edge $\lambda > 0$ is a product
$$C = I_1 \times \cdots \times I_n \subset \mathbb{R}^n$$
of closed intervals of length $\lambda$, where $I_j = [a_j, a_j + \lambda] \subset \mathbb{R}$. The $n$-measure of $C$ is $\mu(C) = \mu_n(C) = \lambda^n$.

A subset $X \subset \mathbb{R}^n$ has measure zero if for every $\varepsilon > 0$ it can be covered by a family of $n$-cubes, the sum of whose measures is less than $\varepsilon$. A countable union of sets of measure zero has measure zero. Therefore $X$ has measure zero if every point of $X$ has a neighborhood in $X$ of measure zero (by Lindelöf's principle).
