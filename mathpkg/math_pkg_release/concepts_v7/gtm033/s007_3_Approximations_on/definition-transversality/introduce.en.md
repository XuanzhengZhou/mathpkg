Transversality is the fundamental geometric notion generalizing the concept of a regular value. A $C^1$ map $f: M \to N$ is transverse to a submanifold $A \subset N$ if at every preimage point the tangent space of $N$ is spanned by the tangent space of $A$ together with the image of the derivative of $f$. When this holds, $f^{-1}(A)$ is a submanifold of $M$ of codimension equal to $\operatorname{codim} A$. Two submanifolds are in general position if they intersect transversely, ensuring their intersection is again a submanifold.
