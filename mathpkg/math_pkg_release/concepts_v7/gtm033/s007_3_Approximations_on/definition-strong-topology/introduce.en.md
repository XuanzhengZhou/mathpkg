The strong topology (also called the Whitney topology or graph topology) on $C(X,Y)$ is defined by requiring that the graphs of nearby maps lie in a prescribed open neighborhood of the graph of the reference map. When $X$ is paracompact and $Y$ is metric, this topology admits a convenient description via $\varepsilon$-neighborhoods where the allowed deviation $\varepsilon(x)$ varies continuously with $x \in X$. The strong topology is finer than the weak (compact-open) topology and is essential for Baire category arguments in differential topology.
