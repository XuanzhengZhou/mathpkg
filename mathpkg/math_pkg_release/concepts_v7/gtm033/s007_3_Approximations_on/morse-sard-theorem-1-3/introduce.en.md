The Morse-Sard Theorem is a profound result in analysis stating that for a sufficiently differentiable map $f: M \to N$, the set of critical values has Lebesgue measure zero in the target. The required differentiability is $r > \max\{0, m - n\}$. In differential topology, the $C^\infty$ case ($r = \infty$) suffices and is considerably easier to prove. The theorem guarantees an abundance of regular values, which is the foundation for transversality theory: preimages of regular values are well-behaved submanifolds.
