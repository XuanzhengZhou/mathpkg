The injective form of the Implicit Function Theorem states that a $C^r$ map with injective derivative at a point can be straightened to the standard inclusion by a local coordinate change in the target. This is dual to the surjective (submersion) form and is fundamental for the local theory of immersions and embeddings in differential topology.
