Except perhaps for orientation, there is essentially only one way to embed a disk in a connected manifold. The proof proceeds by ambiently isotoping the centers to coincide, then radially isotoping both embeddings into a coordinate chart, and finally using linear isotopies and paths in $GL(n)$ to match the derivatives. For manifolds with boundary, the embeddings are first pushed into the interior using a collar.
