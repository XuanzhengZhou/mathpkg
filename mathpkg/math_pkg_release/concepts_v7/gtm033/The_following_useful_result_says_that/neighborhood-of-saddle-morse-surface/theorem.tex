Let $f$ be an admissible Morse function on a compact connected surface $M$ and let $p$ be a saddle (index 1 critical point) with $f(p) = 0$. Let $N = f^{-1}[-\varepsilon, \varepsilon]$ be a connected neighborhood containing only $p$ as critical point. Then either:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $N$ is orientable and $\partial N$ has three components; or
\item[(b)] $N$ is nonorientable and $\partial N$ has two components.
\end{enumerate}
