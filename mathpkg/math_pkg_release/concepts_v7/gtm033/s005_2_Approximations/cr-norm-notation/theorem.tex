Let $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^n$ be a $C^r$ map, where $U \subset \mathbb{R}^m$ is open and $K \subset U$ is any subset. Define
$$\|f\|_{r, K} = \sup \left\{ \|D^k f(x)\| : x \in K,\ 0 \leqslant k \leqslant r \right\},$$
where $\|D^0 f(x)\|$ means $|f(x)|$, and for $k > 0$ the $k$-th derivative at $x$ is a $k$-linear map from $\mathbb{R}^m$ to $\mathbb{R}^n$ whose norm $\|D^k f(x)\|$ is the norm of a $k$-linear map. The value $\infty$ is allowed. If $K$ is the entire domain of $f$ we write simply $\|f\|_r$.
