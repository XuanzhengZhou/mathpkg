A subset $M \subset \mathbb{R}^{n+k}$ is called an \textit{$n$-dimensional submanifold of $\mathbb{R}^{n+k}$} if each point of $M$ has a neighborhood $W \subset \mathbb{R}^{n+k}$ such that

$$W \cap M = f^{-1}(0)$$

for some $C^r$ map $f: W \to \mathbb{R}^k$ having rank $k$ at each point of $W \cap M$.
