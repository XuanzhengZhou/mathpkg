An atlas $\Phi$ on a topological space $M$ is called a \textit{$C^r$ atlas} if for every pair of its charts $(\varphi_i, U_i)$ and $(\varphi_j, U_j)$, the transition map

$$\varphi_j \circ \varphi_i^{-1}: \varphi_i(U_i \cap U_j) \to \varphi_j(U_i \cap U_j)$$

is of differentiability class $C^r$, and likewise $\varphi_i \circ \varphi_j^{-1}$ is $C^r$. Here $r$ can be a natural number, $\infty$, or $\omega$ (real analytic).
