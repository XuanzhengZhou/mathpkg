Differentiability of a map defined on $S^n$ is defined by pulling back to Euclidean space via the inverse chart maps. A map $f: S^n \to \mathbb{R}^k$ is $C^r$ precisely when each local representative $f \circ \varphi_i^{-1}$ on the open $n$-disk is $C^r$ in the usual Euclidean sense.
