Theorem 6.4 is the extension theorem for tubular neighborhoods: any tubular neighborhood of the boundary pair $(\partial V, \partial M)$ can be extended to a tubular neighborhood of the interior pair $(V, M)$. The proof proceeds by first handling the product case $V = W \times I$ using isotopy uniqueness (Theorem 5.3), then reduces the general case via a collar decomposition $V = V' \cup V''$ where $V' = \partial V \times [0,1]$ and $V'' = \partial V \times [1,\infty)$. This result is crucial for constructions that need to propagate boundary data into the interior.
