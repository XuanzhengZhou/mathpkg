This theorem states that the Hopf invariant depends only on the homotopy class of $f \colon S^3 \to S^2$, not on the choice of smooth approximation or regular values. In particular, any map homotopic to a constant map has Hopf invariant zero, making the invariant a useful tool for detecting nontrivial homotopy classes in $\pi_3(S^2)$.
