Cobordism is an equivalence relation on compact boundaryless manifolds: two manifolds are cobordant if their disjoint union forms the boundary of a compact manifold of one higher dimension. This relation, introduced by Rene Thom, partitions manifolds into cobordism classes. The oriented version accounts for orientations via the convention that the first boundary component carries the opposite orientation. Cobordism provides a powerful bridge between differential topology and homotopy theory.
