Under disjoint union, cobordism classes of n-manifolds form abelian groups denoted by N^n (unoriented) and Omega^n (oriented). Every manifold is its own inverse in the unoriented case, making N^n a group of exponent 2. In the oriented case, reversing orientation gives the inverse. Manifolds that are boundaries represent the zero element. These groups are the central objects of Thom's cobordism theory.
