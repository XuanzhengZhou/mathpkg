Let $f: M \to [a, b]$ be a $C^{r+1}$ map on a compact $\partial$-manifold, $1 \leqslant r \leqslant \omega$. Suppose $f$ has no critical points and $f(\partial M) = \{a, b\}$. Then there is a $C^r$ diffeomorphism $F: f^{-1}(a) \times [a, b] \to M$ such that the diagram commutes. In particular all level surfaces of $f$ are diffeomorphic.
