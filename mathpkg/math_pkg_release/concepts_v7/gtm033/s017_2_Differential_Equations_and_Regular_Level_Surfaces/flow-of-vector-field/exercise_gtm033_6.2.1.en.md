# Exercise 1

Consider a $C^r$ vector field $X$ on a manifold $M$ ($1 \leq r \leq \infty$). For the maximal flow $\eta: \Omega \to M$ to be defined on all of $\mathbb{R} \times M$, it is sufficient that the support of $X$ have compact closure. Give a more general sufficient condition.

In particular, show that if $M$ is compact and without boundary, then $\Omega = M \times \mathbb{R}$ and each $\eta_t$ is a $C^r$ diffeomorphism of $M$. Thus the maps $\{\eta_t\}_{t \in \mathbb{R}}$ form a one-parameter group of $C^r$ diffeomorphisms of $M$: the map

$$\mathbb{R} \rightarrow \operatorname{Diff}^r(M), \quad t \mapsto \eta_t$$

is a continuous homomorphism, and $\eta_t \eta_s = \eta_{t+s}$.
