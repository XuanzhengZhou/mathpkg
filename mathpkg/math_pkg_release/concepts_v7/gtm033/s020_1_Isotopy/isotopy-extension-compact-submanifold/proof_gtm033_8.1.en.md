# Proof of Isotopy Extension Theorem for Compact Submanifolds

**Theorem 1.3.** Let $V \subset M$ be a compact submanifold and $F: V \times I \rightarrow M$ an isotopy of $V$. If either $F(V \times I) \subset \partial M$ or $F(V \times I) \subset M - \partial M$, then $F$ extends to a diffeotopy of $M$ having compact support.

*Proof.* We start from the vector field $X$ on $\hat{F}(V \times I)$ that is tangent to the curves $\hat{F}(x \times I)$ for $x \in V$. The vector field $X$ has the form $X(y,t) = (H(y,t), 1)$ where $H: \hat{F}(V \times I) \rightarrow TM$ with $H(y,t) \in T_y M$.

By Theorem 1.4, we first note that the same reasoning applies: by means of a tubular neighborhood of $\hat{F}(V \times I)$ in $M \times I$ and a partition of unity, the horizontal part of $X$ can be extended to a time-dependent vector field $G$ on $M \times I$ having compact support, which agrees with $H$ on $\hat{F}(V \times I)$. Since $\hat{F}(V \times I)$ is compact (as the image of a compact set under a continuous map), the extension $G$ with compact support exists as in Theorem 1.4.

The diffeotopy of $M$ generated by $G$ (via Theorem 1.2) then extends the original isotopy $F$. $\square$
