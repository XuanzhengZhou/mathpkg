# Exercise 8

If $\partial M = \varnothing$ then $M$ is a boundary, i.e., $M = \partial N$ for some $\partial$-manifold $N$. However, if $M$ is compact, it may be impossible to choose $N$ compact. [Suppose $M$ has dimension 0.]
