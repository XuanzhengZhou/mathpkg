A $C^r$ submanifold $A \subset M$ of a $\partial$-manifold $M$ is called a **neat submanifold** if $A$ is a $C^r$ $\partial$-manifold and
$$\partial A = A \cap \partial M,$$
and (for $r \geq 1$) $A$ is not tangent to $\partial M$ at any point $x \in \partial A$; that is,
$$A_x \not\subset (\partial M)_x.$$
If $\partial A = \varnothing$, then $A$ is neat if and only if $A \cap \partial M = \varnothing$.
