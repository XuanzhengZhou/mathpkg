The secant map $\sigma$ sends a pair of distinct points on a submanifold $M \subset \mathbb{R}^q$ to the unit direction vector determined by the secant line between them. For a linear projection along direction $v$ to be injective on $M$, $v$ must avoid the image of $\sigma$. The dimension comparison $\dim(M \times M - \Delta) = 2n < q-1 = \dim S^{q-1}$ enables the use of the Nowhere Density Lemma.
