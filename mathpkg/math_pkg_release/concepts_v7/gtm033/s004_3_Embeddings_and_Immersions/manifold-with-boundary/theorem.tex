A $C^r$ manifold with boundary (or $\partial$-manifold) is a topological space $M$ together with a $C^r$ atlas $\Phi$ whose charts take values in open subsets of a halfspace $H \subset \mathbb{R}^n$. A point $x \in M$ is a boundary point if its image under some (hence any) chart lies in $\partial H$. The boundary of $M$ is denoted $\partial M$.

If $\partial M \neq \varnothing$, $M$ is called a $\partial$-manifold. If $\partial M = \varnothing$, $M$ is a manifold without boundary. The boundary $\partial M$ is a $C^r$ manifold of dimension $n-1$, with atlas given by restricting charts to $\partial H \cong \mathbb{R}^{n-1}$.
