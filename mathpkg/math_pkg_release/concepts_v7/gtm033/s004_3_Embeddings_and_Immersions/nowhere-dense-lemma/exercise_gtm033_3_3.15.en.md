# Exercise 15

$P^2$ embeds in $\mathbb{R}^4$. [Think of $P^2$ as the union of a Möbius band $M$ and a disk $D$. Embed $M$ and $D$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$ with a common boundary circle $S^1$; then push them out into opposite sides of $\mathbb{R}^3$ in $\mathbb{R}^4$ leaving $S^1$ fixed.]
