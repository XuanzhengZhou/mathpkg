# Exercise 9

Let $f: S^1 \to \mathbb{R}$ be a $C^1$ map and $y \in \mathbb{R}$ a regular value.
(a) $f^{-1}(y)$ has an even number of points.
(b) If $f^{-1}(y)$ has $2k$ points, $f$ has at least $2k$ critical points.
*(c) Let $g: S^2 \to \mathbb{R}$ be a $C^1$ map and $y \in g(S^2)$ a regular value. If $g^{-1}(y)$ has $k$ components then $f$ has at least $k + 1$ critical points. [Use the Jordan curve theorem.]
