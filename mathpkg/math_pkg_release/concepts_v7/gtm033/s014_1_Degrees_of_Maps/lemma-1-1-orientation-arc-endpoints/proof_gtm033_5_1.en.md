# Proof of Lemma 1.1 (Orientation Along an Arc Transverse to the Boundary)

Let $X_u, X_v$ be tangent vectors to $K$ at $u, v$ which belong to $\kappa_u, \kappa_v$ respectively. Then $X_u$ is inward pointing (at $u$) if and only if $X_v$ is outward pointing (at $v$); this is equivalent to the statement of the lemma. Indeed, with the quotient orientation $\omega/\kappa$ of the algebraic normal bundle, the inward condition at $u$ corresponds to $\omega_u/\kappa_u = (\partial\omega)_u$, while the outward condition at $v$ corresponds to $\omega_v/\kappa_v = -(\partial\omega)_v$. The equivalence follows from the fact that the two ends of an embedded arc transverse to the boundary have opposite orientation behavior with respect to the boundary orientation.
