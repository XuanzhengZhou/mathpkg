Let $N$ be a connected manifold and let $M \subset N$ be a connected closed submanifold of codimension $1$ with $\partial M = \partial N = \varnothing$. Suppose $M$ separates $N$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item The normal bundle $\nu$ of $M$ in $N$ is trivial.
\item $N \setminus M$ has exactly two components.
\item The topological boundary of each component of $N \setminus M$ equals $M$.
\end{enumerate}

Moreover, if $\bar{A}$ denotes the closure of a component $A$ of $N \setminus M$, then $\bar{A}$ is a submanifold of $N$ with $\partial \bar{A} = M$, and $\bar{A} \cup \bar{B} = N$ where $A$ and $B$ are the two components.
