An embedding is a $C^1$ immersion that is also a homeomorphism onto its image. This is the strongest "niceness" condition for a map between manifolds: it requires both the infinitesimal condition (injective derivative) and the global topological condition (the map is a homeomorphism onto $f(M)$). Theorem 3.1 shows that a subset of a manifold is a submanifold precisely when it is the image of an embedding, making embeddings the bridge between local and global descriptions of submanifolds.
