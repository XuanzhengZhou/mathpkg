M. H. Stone's representation theorem (1936) states that every Boolean algebra is isomorphic to a field of sets -- specifically, to the algebra of all clopen subsets of a compact Hausdorff totally disconnected space. The proof constructs the Stone space $S(B)$ whose points are the ultrafilters of $B$, with a topology generated by sets of ultrafilters containing a fixed element. This theorem is the gateway to Stone duality, which Kelley presents in the context of the Wallman compactification: the Wallman compactification of a discrete space is precisely the Stone-Cech compactification, and the Stone representation theorem emerges naturally from this topological construction.
