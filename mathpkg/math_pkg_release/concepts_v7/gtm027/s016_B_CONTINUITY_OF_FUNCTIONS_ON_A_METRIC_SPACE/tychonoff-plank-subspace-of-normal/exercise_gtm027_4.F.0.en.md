# Exercise F (Main) — A Subspace of a Normal Space May Fail to be Normal

Show that a subspace of a normal space need not be normal. Specifically, consider the Tychonoff Plank $T = (\Omega' \times \Omega') \setminus \{(\Omega, \Omega)\}$ as a subspace of the compact Hausdorff (hence normal) space $\Omega' \times \Omega'$. Prove that $T$ is not normal by exhibiting two disjoint closed subsets of $T$ that cannot be separated by disjoint open sets.
