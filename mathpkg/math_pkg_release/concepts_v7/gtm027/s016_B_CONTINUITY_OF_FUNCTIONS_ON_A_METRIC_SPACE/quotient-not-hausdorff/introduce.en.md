This proposition shows that the Hausdorff property is not always preserved under quotient maps. Even when the equivalence relation $S$ is a closed subset of the product space $X \times X$, the resulting quotient space $X/S$ may fail to be Hausdorff. Collapsing two disjoint closed sets $A$ and $B$ to distinct points can create a situation where those two points cannot be separated by disjoint open sets.
