# Exercise F.3 — The Product of Quotient Maps Need Not Be Open

Let $S$ be the equivalence relation defined in Exercise F.2. Consider the natural map
$$\varphi: X \times X \to (X/S) \times (X/S), \qquad \varphi(x, y) = (S[x], S[y]),$$
where $X/S$ is given the quotient topology and both products are given the product topologies. (This is equivalent to asking whether the product of quotients is topologically equivalent to the quotient of the product.)

Prove that $\varphi$ is not an open map. (Hint: Consider the open neighborhood $W = X \times X \setminus (A \times A \cup B \times B \cup \Delta)$ of a point in $A \times B$.)
