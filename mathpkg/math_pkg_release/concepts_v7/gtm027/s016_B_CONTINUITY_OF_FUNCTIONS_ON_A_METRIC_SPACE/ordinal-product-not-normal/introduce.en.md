This proposition provides a classic counterexample showing that the product of two normal spaces need not be normal. Although both $\Omega_0$ and $\Omega'$ are individually normal, their product $\Omega_0 \times \Omega'$ fails to be normal. The proof considers the diagonal $A = \{(x,x)\}$ and the top edge $B = \Omega_0 \times \{\Omega\}$, constructing an expanding function from any neighborhood of $A$ and applying the fixed-point accumulation theorem to derive a contradiction.
