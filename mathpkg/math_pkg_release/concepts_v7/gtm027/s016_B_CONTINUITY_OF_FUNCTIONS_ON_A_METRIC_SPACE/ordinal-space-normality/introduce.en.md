This theorem establishes that the ordinal spaces $\Omega_0$ (the set of countable ordinals) and $\Omega'$ (the set of ordinals up to and including the first uncountable ordinal) are both normal topological spaces under the order topology. The proof strategy involves constructing a finite interlacing sequence from the disjoint closed sets and using intervals of the form $(a_i, b_i]$, which are simultaneously open and closed, to separate them.
