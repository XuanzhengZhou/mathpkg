A divisible property is a topological property that is preserved under quotient maps. If a space has a divisible property, then every quotient space of that space also enjoys the same property. Divisibility is generally the strongest of the three preservation properties (hereditary, productive, divisible), and relatively few topological properties are divisible. For instance, connectedness and compactness are divisible, while most separation axioms are not.
