A map between uniform spaces is uniformly open if the forward image of every uniform neighborhood of a point contains a uniform neighborhood of the image point, in a way that is controlled uniformly across the domain. Specifically, for each entourage U in the domain uniformity there exists an entourage V in the codomain uniformity such that the image under f of the U-neighborhood of any point x contains the V-neighborhood of f(x). This is the uniform-space analogue of an open map in topology. Uniformly open maps are central to the completeness preservation theorem for metric spaces.
