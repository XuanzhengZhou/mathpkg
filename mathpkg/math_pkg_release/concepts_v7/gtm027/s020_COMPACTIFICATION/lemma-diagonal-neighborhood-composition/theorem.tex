Let $X$ be a topological space and let $\Delta = \{(x, x) : x \in X\}$ be the diagonal in $X \times X$. If $U$ is a neighborhood of $\Delta$, then there exists a symmetric neighborhood $V$ of $\Delta$ (i.e., $V = V^{-1}$) such that
\[
V \circ V \subset U,
\]
where $V \circ V = \{(x, z) : \exists y \in X \text{ with } (x, y) \in V \text{ and } (y, z) \in V\}$.
