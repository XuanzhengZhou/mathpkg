# Exercise E.1 — Countably Compact Spaces

A topological space is **countably compact** iff every countable open cover has a finite subcover.

(a) Prove that a space is countably compact iff each sequence has a cluster point. (A point $x$ is a cluster point of a sequence $\{x_n\}$ iff every neighborhood of $x$ contains $x_n$ for infinitely many $n$.)

(b) Prove that a $T_1$-space is countably compact iff every infinite subset has a limit point (i.e., the space is limit point compact).
