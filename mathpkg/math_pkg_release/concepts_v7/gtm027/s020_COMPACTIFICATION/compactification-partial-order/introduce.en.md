The collection of all compactifications of a space can be partially ordered by a natural relation: one compactification is larger than another if there is a continuous surjection between them that respects the embeddings. For Hausdorff compactifications, mutual comparability implies topological equivalence. This ordering provides a framework for comparing different compactification constructions.
