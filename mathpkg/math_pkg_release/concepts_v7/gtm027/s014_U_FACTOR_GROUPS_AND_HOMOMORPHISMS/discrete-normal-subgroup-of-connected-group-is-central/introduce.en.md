In a connected topological group, any discrete normal subgroup must lie in the center. The key idea is that for a fixed element $h$ of the discrete normal subgroup $H$, the conjugation map $x \mapsto x^{-1}hx$ is a continuous function from the connected group $G$ into the discrete space $H$. Since the continuous image of a connected space is connected, and the only connected subsets of a discrete space are singletons, this map must be constant. Evaluating at the identity shows the constant is $h$, hence $h$ commutes with every element of $G$.
