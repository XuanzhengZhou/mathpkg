A subset $A$ of the domain $X$ distinguishes members of a function family $F$ if no two distinct functions in $F$ agree on all of $A$. Equivalently, the restriction map from $F$ to $Y^A$ is injective. This condition is precisely what is needed for the topology $\wp_A$ of pointwise convergence on $A$ to be Hausdorff: since $Y^A$ is Hausdorff when $Y$ is, the relativized topology on $F$ via the restriction map is Hausdorff exactly when that map is one-to-one.
