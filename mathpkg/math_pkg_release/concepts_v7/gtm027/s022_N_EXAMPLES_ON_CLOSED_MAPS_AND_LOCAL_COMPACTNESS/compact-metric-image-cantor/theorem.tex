Let $X$ be a compact metric space. Then there exists a continuous surjection from the Cantor space $2^\omega$ onto $X$. Explicitly, let $\{U_n\}_{n \in \omega}$ be a countable base for the topology of $X$, and define $f_n(0) = \overline{U_n}$ and $f_n(1) = X \setminus U_n$ for each $n \in \omega$. Then the family $\{f_n\}_{n \in \omega}$ plays the role of the family $F$ in the general construction for compact Hausdorff spaces.
