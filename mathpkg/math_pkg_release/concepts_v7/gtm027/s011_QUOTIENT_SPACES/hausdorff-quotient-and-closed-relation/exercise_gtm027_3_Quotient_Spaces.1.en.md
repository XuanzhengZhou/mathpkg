# Exercise 3.R: Example on Quotient Spaces — an Open Map from a Hausdorff Space onto a Non-Hausdorff Space

**Problem (Kelley 3.R).** Let $X$ be the set of real numbers with the usual topology, and let $R$ be the equivalence relation consisting of all pairs $(x, y)$ such that $x - y$ is rational. Then:

1. The quotient space $X/R$ is indiscrete (the only open sets are $\varnothing$ and $X/R$).

2. The projection $P$ of $X$ onto $X/R$ is an open map.

3. Consequently, an open map may carry a Hausdorff space onto a non-Hausdorff space.

*Remark.* This example shows that the hypothesis in Theorem 11 (that $R$ is closed in $X \times X$) is essential for the converse direction: $P$ is open, but $X/R$ is not Hausdorff because $R$ is not closed in $X \times X$.

*Note: Problem 4.G constructs an example of a closed map carrying a Hausdorff space (respectively, a first-countable space) onto a non-Hausdorff space (respectively, a space failing the first axiom of countability).*
