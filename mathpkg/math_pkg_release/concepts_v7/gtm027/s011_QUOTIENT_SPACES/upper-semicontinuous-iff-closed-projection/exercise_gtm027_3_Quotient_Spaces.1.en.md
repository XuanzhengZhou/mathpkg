# Exercise 3.F(g): Upper Semi-continuous Decomposition of the Subgraph

**Problem (Kelley 3.F, part (g)).** Let $f$ be a non-negative real-valued function on a topological space $X$, let $\mathbb{R}$ have the usual topology, and let

$$G = \{(x, t) : 0 \leq t \leq f(x)\}$$

have the relativized product topology of $X \times \mathbb{R}$. (The set $G$ is the region under the graph of $f$.) Let $\mathfrak{D}$ be the decomposition of $G$ into "vertical slices"; that is, sets of the form $(\{x\} \times \mathbb{R}) \cap G$.

If the decomposition $\mathfrak{D}$ is upper semi-continuous, then $f$ is upper semi-continuous. (The converse is also true, but the simplest proof requires Theorem 5.12.)

*Note.* This problem provides the historical motivation for the terminology "upper semi-continuous decomposition": the decomposition of the subgraph into vertical slices is upper semi-continuous exactly when the function itself is upper semi-continuous.
