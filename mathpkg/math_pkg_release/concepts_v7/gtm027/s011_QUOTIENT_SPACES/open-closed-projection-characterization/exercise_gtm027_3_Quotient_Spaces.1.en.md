# Exercise 3.Q: Characterization of Open and Closed Quotient Projections via Closure and Interior

**Problem (Kelley 3.Q).** Let $X$ be a topological space, $R$ an equivalence relation on $X$, and $P: X \to X/R$ the quotient projection. Denote by $A^-$ the closure of $A$ and by $A^0$ the interior of $A$.

1. The projection $P$ is a closed map if and only if, for each subset $A$ of $X$,
   $$R[A]^- \subset R[A^-].$$

2. The projection $P$ is an open map if and only if, for each subset $A$ of $X$,
   $$R[A^0] \subset R[A]^0.$$

Recall that $R[A] = \bigcup\{D \in X/R : D \cap A \neq \varnothing\}$ is the saturation of $A$ with respect to the equivalence relation $R$.
