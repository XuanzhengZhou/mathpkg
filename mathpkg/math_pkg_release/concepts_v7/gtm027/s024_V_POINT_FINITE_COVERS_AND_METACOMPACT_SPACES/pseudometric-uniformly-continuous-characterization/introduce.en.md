A pseudo-metric d on a uniform space is uniformly continuous on the product if and only if every entourage V_{d,r} = {(x,y): d(x,y) < r} belongs to the uniformity. This provides the fundamental link between pseudo-metrics and uniformities: the uniformity generated by a pseudo-metric is the smallest uniformity containing all V_{d,r}. The result underpins the metrization theory of uniform spaces.
