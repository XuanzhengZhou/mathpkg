# Gage Dictionary for Uniform Spaces (Theorem 6.19)

**Theorem 6.19.** Let $(X, \mathcal{U})$ be a uniform space and let $P$ be the gage of $\mathcal{U}$. Then:

(a) The family of all sets $V_{p,r}$ for $p \in P$ and $r > 0$ is a base for $\mathcal{U}$.

(b) A net $\{x_n : n \in D\}$ converges to $x$ if and only if $\{p(x_n, x) : n \in D\}$ converges to zero for all $p \in P$.

(c) A net $\{x_n : n \in D\}$ is Cauchy if and only if $\{p(x_m, x_n) : (m,n) \in D \times D\}$ converges to zero for all $p \in P$.

(d) The closure of a set $A$ is $\{x : p\text{-dist}(x, A) = 0 \text{ for all } p \in P\}$, where $p\text{-dist}(x, A) = \inf\{p(x,y) : y \in A\}$.

(e) A function $f$ on $X$ to a uniform space $(Y, \mathcal{V})$ is uniformly continuous if and only if for each member $q$ of the gage $Q$ of $\mathcal{V}$ it is true that $q \circ f_2 \in P$, where $f_2(x,y) = (f(x), f(y))$. Equivalently, $f$ is uniformly continuous if and only if for each $q \in Q$ and each $s > 0$ there is $p \in P$ and $r > 0$ such that $p(x,y) < r$ implies $q(f(x), f(y)) < s$.

(f) If $(X_a, \mathcal{U}_a)$ is a uniform space for each $a \in A$ and $P_a$ is the gage of $\mathcal{U}_a$, then the gage of the product uniformity for $\prod_{a \in A} X_a$ is generated by all pseudo-metrics of the form $q(x,y) = p_a(x_a, y_a)$ for $a \in A$ and $p_a \in P_a$.

**Proof.** The proof is a straightforward application of earlier results and consists of translating each uniformity-based definition into the corresponding gage-based characterization.

(a) This is essentially the definition of the uniformity generated by $P$, combined with Theorem 6.15 which shows that $\mathcal{U}$ equals this uniformity. Since $P$ generates $\mathcal{U}$, the sets $V_{p,r}$ form a subbase; and since for $p, q \in P$ the maximum $\max(p, q)$ is also in $P$, finite intersections of such sets contain such a set, making the family a base.

(b)-(c) These follow directly from the fact that the $V_{p,r}$ form a base for $\mathcal{U}$ (by (a)), together with the definition of convergence and the Cauchy property in terms of the uniformity.

(d) This follows from (b): $x$ belongs to the closure of $A$ if and only if there is a net in $A$ converging to $x$, which by (b) is equivalent to $p\text{-dist}(x,A) = 0$ for all $p$.

(e) By Theorem 6.11, $f$ is uniformly continuous if and only if for each $V \in \mathcal{V}$, we have $f_2^{-1}[V] \in \mathcal{U}$. In gage terms: for each $q \in Q$ and $s > 0$, $V_{q,s} \in \mathcal{V}$, so we need $f_2^{-1}[V_{q,s}] \in \mathcal{U}$. But $f_2^{-1}[V_{q,s}] = \{(x,y) : q(f(x), f(y)) < s\} = V_{q \circ f_2, s}$. This belongs to $\mathcal{U}$ if and only if $q \circ f_2$ is a uniformly continuous pseudo-metric, i.e., $q \circ f_2 \in P$.

(f) This follows from Theorem 6.10 and the definition of the product uniformity: the subbase sets $\{(x,y) : (x_a, y_a) \in U\}$ correspond exactly to pseudo-metrics of the form $q(x,y) = p_a(x_a, y_a)$ when $U = V_{p_a, r}$.
