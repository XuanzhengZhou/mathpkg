In any uniform space, every convergent net is a Cauchy net. Conversely, if a Cauchy net has a cluster point, then the net converges to that cluster point. This generalizes the familiar metric space fact that convergent sequences are Cauchy, and that a Cauchy sequence with a convergent subsequence must itself converge. The result is fundamental to the theory of completeness in uniform spaces.
