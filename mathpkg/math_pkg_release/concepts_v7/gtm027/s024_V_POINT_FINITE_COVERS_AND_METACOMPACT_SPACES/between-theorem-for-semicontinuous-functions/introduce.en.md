Given a lower semi-continuous function g and an upper semi-continuous function h on a paracompact space with h < g everywhere, there exists a continuous function p lying strictly between them. The proof constructs p as a locally finite convex combination using a partition of unity subordinate to a suitable open cover. This result characterizes countable paracompactness in normal spaces: Dowker proved the equivalence of countable paracompactness plus normality, normality of the product with the closed unit interval, and the validity of the between theorem.
