For a symmetric relation V on a set X, the triple composition V∘U∘V equals the union of Cartesian products V[x]×V[y] over all pairs (x,y) belonging to U. This identity is a key technical lemma used repeatedly in the theory of uniform spaces, particularly in arguments about interiors and closures of members of a uniformity.
