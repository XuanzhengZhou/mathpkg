# Proof of the Metrization Theorem for Uniform Spaces (Theorem 6.13)

**Theorem 6.13 (Metrization Theorem).** A uniform space is pseudo-metrizable if and only if its uniformity has a countable base.

**Proof.** ($\Leftarrow$) If a uniformity $\mathcal{U}$ for $X$ has a countable base $V_0, V_1, \ldots, V_n, \ldots$, then it is possible to construct by induction a family $U_0, U_1, \ldots, U_n, \ldots$ such that each $U_n$ is symmetric, $U_{n+1} \circ U_{n+1} \circ U_{n+1} \subset U_n$, and $U_n \subset V_n$ for each positive integer $n$.

The construction proceeds as follows: set $U_0 = X \times X$. Given $U_n$, since $V_{n+1}$ is in the uniformity, there exists a symmetric $W \in \mathcal{U}$ with $W \circ W \circ W \subset V_{n+1}$. Since $\{V_k\}$ is a base, $W$ contains some $V_k$. Let $U_{n+1}$ be a symmetric member of $\mathcal{U}$ with $U_{n+1} \subset U_n \cap V_k$ and $U_{n+1} \circ U_{n+1} \circ U_{n+1} \subset V_{n+1}$. Then $U_{n+1} \subset V_{n+1}$ and $U_{n+1} \circ U_{n+1} \circ U_{n+1} \subset U_n$ (after appropriate reindexing).

The family $\{U_n\}$ is then a base for $\mathcal{U}$ (since each $U_n \subset V_n$ and $\{V_n\}$ is a base). By the Metrization Lemma (6.12), there exists a pseudo-metric $d$ on $X$ satisfying

$$U_{n+1} \subset \{(x,y) : d(x,y) < 2^{-n}\} \subset U_{n-1}.$$

Thus the uniform topology coincides with the pseudo-metric topology, and $(X, \mathcal{U})$ is pseudo-metrizable.

($\Rightarrow$) If $X$ is pseudo-metrizable with pseudo-metric $d$, then the family $\{V_{d,2^{-n}} : n \in \mathbb{N}\}$ is a countable base for the uniformity generated by $d$. Hence the uniformity has a countable base.

This theorem clearly implies that a uniform space is metrizable if and only if it is Hausdorff and its uniformity has a countable base.
