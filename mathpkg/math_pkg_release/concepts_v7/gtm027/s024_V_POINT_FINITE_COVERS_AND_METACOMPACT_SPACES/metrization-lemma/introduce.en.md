The Metrization Lemma constructs a pseudo-metric from a decreasing sequence of subsets of X×X satisfying a generalized triangle inequality. Starting from a "first approximation" function f defined on the differences U_{n-1}\U_n, the proof uses a chaining argument: d(x,y) is the infimum of sums of f-values along finite chains from x to y. This lemma is the crucial bridge between countable bases of a uniformity and pseudo-metrizability, underpinning the Metrization Theorem.
