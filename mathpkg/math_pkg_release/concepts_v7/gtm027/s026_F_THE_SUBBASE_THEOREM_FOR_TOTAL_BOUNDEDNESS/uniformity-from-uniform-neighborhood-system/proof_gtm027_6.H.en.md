# Proof of Uniformity from a Uniform Neighborhood System

Let $(V, \geq)$ be a uniform neighborhood system for $X$, indexed by the directed set $(A, \geq)$. Recall that a uniform neighborhood system satisfies:

(i) For each $a \in A$ and $x \in X$, $x \in V_a(x)$;

(ii) For each $a \in A$, there exists $b \in A$ such that $y \in V_b(x)$ whenever $z \in V_b(x)$ and $y \in V_b(z)$;

(iii) For each $a, b \in A$, there exists $c \in A$ such that $V_c(x) \subset V_a(x) \cap V_b(x)$ for all $x \in X$;

(iv) For each $a \in A$, there exists $b \in A$ such that for all $x \in X$: $y \in V_b(x)$ implies $x \in V_a(y)$;

(v) For each $a \in A$, there exists $b \in A$ such that $z \in V_a(x)$ whenever $y \in V_b(x)$ and $z \in V_b(y)$.

Define for each $a \in A$ the set
$$
U_a = \{(x, y) \in X \times X : y \in V_a(x)\}.
$$
Let $\mathcal{B} = \{U_a : a \in A\}$. We claim that $\mathcal{B}$ is a base for a uniformity $\mathfrak{u}$ on $X$.

**Base for a uniformity.** We verify that $\mathcal{B}$ satisfies the conditions for a uniformity base:

- *Diagonal:* For each $a \in A$, condition (i) gives $x \in V_a(x)$ for all $x$, so $(x, x) \in U_a$, i.e., $\Delta \subset U_a$.
- *Symmetry up to refinement:* For each $a \in A$, condition (iv) gives $b \in A$ with $y \in V_b(x) \Rightarrow x \in V_a(y)$. This means $U_b \subset U_a^{-1}$. Since this holds for all $a$, the system is symmetric up to refinement.
- *Intersection (filter base):* For $a, b \in A$, condition (iii) gives $c \in A$ with $V_c(x) \subset V_a(x) \cap V_b(x)$ for all $x$. Hence $U_c \subset U_a \cap U_b$. Combined with the directedness of $A$, $\mathcal{B}$ is a filter base.
- *Square-root property:* For each $a \in A$, condition (v) gives $b \in A$ such that $z \in V_a(x)$ whenever $y \in V_b(x)$ and $z \in V_b(y)$. This is equivalent to $U_b \circ U_b \subset U_a$.

Thus $\mathcal{B}$ generates a uniformity $\mathfrak{u}$ on $X$.

**Mutual approximation property.** We verify the two additional properties:

(1) For each $a \in A$, take $U = U_a \in \mathfrak{u}$. Then $U[x] = \{y : (x,y) \in U_a\} = V_a(x)$, so $U[x] \subset V_a(x)$ (in fact equality holds).

(2) For each entourage $U$ in the uniformity generated by $\mathcal{B}$, there exists $a \in A$ such that $U_a \subset U$. Then $V_a(x) = U_a[x] \subset U[x]$ for all $x \in X$.

The uniformity $\mathfrak{u}$ is called the *uniformity of the system* $(V, \geq)$. $\square$
