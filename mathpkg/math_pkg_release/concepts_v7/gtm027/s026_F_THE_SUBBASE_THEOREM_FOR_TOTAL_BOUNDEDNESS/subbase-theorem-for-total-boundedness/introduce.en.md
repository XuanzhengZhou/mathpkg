# Subbase Theorem for Total Boundedness

This theorem is the uniform space analogue of Alexander's theorem on compact subbases (Theorem 5.6). It provides a criterion for a uniform space to be totally bounded in terms of a subbase of the uniformity: if every member of some subbase admits a finite cover of the space by sets whose squares are contained in that member, then the uniform space is totally bounded. This generalizes the usual definition of total boundedness, which requires such covers for all members of the uniformity, down to merely a subbase.
