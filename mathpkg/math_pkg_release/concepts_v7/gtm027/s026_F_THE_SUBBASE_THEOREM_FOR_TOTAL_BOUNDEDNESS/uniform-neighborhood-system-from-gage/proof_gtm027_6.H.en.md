# Proof of Uniform Neighborhood System from a Gage

Let $\mathfrak{u}$ be a uniformity on $X$ and let $P$ be its gage --- the family of all pseudometrics $p$ on $X$ that are uniformly continuous with respect to the product uniformity $\mathfrak{u} \times \mathfrak{u}$ on $X \times X$. Recall that the uniformity $\mathfrak{u}$ is generated by the sets
$$
\{(x,y) \in X \times X : p(x,y) < \varepsilon\}
$$
for $p \in P$ and $\varepsilon > 0$.

Define the index set $A = P \times \mathbb{R}_{>0}$, the Cartesian product of the gage $P$ and the positive real numbers. Direct $A$ by:
$$
(p, r) \geq (q, s) \quad \text{iff} \quad r \leq s \;\text{and}\; p(x,y) \geq q(x,y) \;\text{for all}\; x,y \in X.
$$

For each $(p, r) \in A$ and $x \in X$, define
$$
V_{p,r}(x) = \{y \in X : p(x, y) < r\},
$$
the open ball of radius $r$ centered at $x$ with respect to the pseudometric $p$.

We verify that $(V, \geq)$ satisfies the axioms of a uniform neighborhood system.

**(i) Reflexivity.** For each $(p, r) \in A$, $p(x, x) = 0 < r$, so $x \in V_{p,r}(x)$ for all $x \in X$.

**(ii)-(v) Uniform neighborhood system axioms.** Let us verify each:

*Directedness of $A$:* Given $(p_1, r_1)$ and $(p_2, r_2)$, set $r = \min(r_1, r_2)$ and define $p(x,y) = \max(p_1(x,y), p_2(x,y))$. Then $p$ is a pseudometric and belongs to the gage $P$. Clearly $(p, r) \geq (p_1, r_1)$ and $(p, r) \geq (p_2, r_2)$: we have $r \leq r_1, r_2$ and $p \geq p_1, p_2$ pointwise. Thus $A$ is directed.

*Triangle inequality (axiom (v)):* Given $(p, r) \in A$, set $s = r/2$. Then $(p, s) \in A$ and $(p, s) \geq (p, r)$. If $y \in V_{p,s}(x)$ and $z \in V_{p,s}(y)$, then $p(x,y) < s$ and $p(y,z) < s$, so by the triangle inequality for $p$:
$$
p(x,z) \leq p(x,y) + p(y,z) < s + s = r,
$$
hence $z \in V_{p,r}(x)$. Thus $(p, s)$ satisfies condition (v).

*Intersection condition (axiom (iii)):* Given $(p_1, r_1)$ and $(p_2, r_2)$, let $r = \min(r_1, r_2)$ and $p = \max(p_1, p_2)$. Then $(p, r) \geq (p_1, r_1), (p_2, r_2)$ and $V_{p,r}(x) \subset V_{p_1, r_1}(x) \cap V_{p_2, r_2}(x)$ for all $x \in X$ (since $p(x,y) < r$ implies $p_i(x,y) \leq p(x,y) < r \leq r_i$ for each $i$).

*Symmetry condition (axiom (iv)):* Pseudometrics are symmetric ($p(x,y) = p(y,x)$), so $V_{p,r}(x)$ is symmetric in the sense that $y \in V_{p,r}(x)$ iff $x \in V_{p,r}(y)$. Thus $(p, r)$ itself serves as the required index.

**Associated uniformity recovers $\mathfrak{u}$.** The uniformity generated by the uniform neighborhood system $(V, \geq)$ has as a base the sets
$$
U_{p,r} = \{(x,y) \in X \times X : y \in V_{p,r}(x)\} = \{(x,y) \in X \times X : p(x,y) < r\}.
$$
But this is precisely the standard base for the uniformity $\mathfrak{u}$ from its gage $P$. Thus the uniformity of the system $(V, \geq)$ coincides with the original uniformity $\mathfrak{u}$. $\square$
