# Proof of Uniform Neighborhood System from a Uniformity

Let $\mathfrak{u}$ be a uniformity on $X$. For each entourage $U \in \mathfrak{u}$ and each $x \in X$, define
$$
V_U(x) = U[x] = \{y \in X : (x, y) \in U\}.
$$
The index set $A$ is taken to be $\mathfrak{u}$ itself, directed by *reverse inclusion*: $U \geq U'$ iff $U \subset U'$. (Note: the uniformity is directed by inclusion $\subset$, so reversing gives the appropriate direction.) We claim that $(V, \geq)$ is a uniform neighborhood system for $X$ and that its associated uniformity coincides with $\mathfrak{u}$.

**Verification of the uniform neighborhood system axioms.** We check conditions (i)-(v) using the uniformity axioms for $\mathfrak{u}$.

(i) For each $U \in \mathfrak{u}$ and $x \in X$, $(x, x) \in U$ (since every entourage contains the diagonal), so $x \in U[x] = V_U(x)$.

(ii) Given $U \in \mathfrak{u}$, choose $W \in \mathfrak{u}$ such that $W \circ W \subset U$. Then for any $x \in X$, if $z \in V_W(x) = W[x]$ and $y \in V_W(z) = W[z]$, we have $(x, z) \in W$, $(z, y) \in W$, hence $(x, y) \in W \circ W \subset U$, so $y \in U[x] = V_U(x)$. Thus $W$ serves as the index required.

(iii) Given $U_1, U_2 \in \mathfrak{u}$, set $U = U_1 \cap U_2 \in \mathfrak{u}$. Then $V_U(x) = U[x] \subset U_1[x] \cap U_2[x] = V_{U_1}(x) \cap V_{U_2}(x)$ for all $x \in X$. Moreover $U \subset U_1$ and $U \subset U_2$ means $U \geq U_1, U_2$ in the directed order, verifying the filter condition.

(iv) Given $U \in \mathfrak{u}$, let $U^{-1} = \{(x,y) : (y,x) \in U\} \in \mathfrak{u}$ be its symmetric inverse, and set $W = U \cap U^{-1} \in \mathfrak{u}$. Then $W$ is symmetric and $W \subset U$. If $y \in V_W(x) = W[x]$, then $(x,y) \in W = W^{-1}$, so $(y,x) \in W \subset U$, hence $x \in U[y] = V_U(y)$. Thus $W$ fulfills the symmetry condition.

(v) Given $U \in \mathfrak{u}$, choose $W \in \mathfrak{u}$ with $W \circ W \subset U$. Then $W \subset U$ so $W \geq U$. For any $x \in X$, if $y \in V_W(x) = W[x]$ and $z \in V_W(y) = W[y]$, then $(x,z) \in W \circ W \subset U$, so $z \in V_U(x)$. This verifies the composition/triangle condition.

**Directedness.** The uniformity $\mathfrak{u}$ is directed by inclusion $\subset$: for $U_1, U_2 \in \mathfrak{u}$, $U_1 \cap U_2 \in \mathfrak{u}$ and $U_1 \cap U_2 \subset U_1, U_2$. In the reverse order $\geq$, this means $U_1 \cap U_2 \geq U_1, U_2$, so $(A, \geq)$ is a directed set.

**Associated uniformity recovers $\mathfrak{u}$.** The uniformity generated by $(V, \geq)$ has as base the sets $W_U = \{(x,y) : y \in V_U(x)\} = \{(x,y) : (x,y) \in U\} = U$. Thus the base of the generated uniformity is precisely $\mathfrak{u}$ itself, and the associated uniformity coincides with $\mathfrak{u}$. $\square$
