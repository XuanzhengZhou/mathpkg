There is a natural isometric isomorphism between the Banach algebra $A$ of continuous left almost periodic functions on $G$ and the algebra $C(\alpha[G])$ of continuous real-valued functions on the compactification $\alpha[G]$. Every continuous function on $\alpha[G]$ pulls back via $L$ to an almost periodic function, and conversely every almost periodic function extends uniquely to a continuous function on $\alpha[G]$. This is the Gelfand-Naimark type duality for the Bohr compactification.
