This exercise asks for the construction of a subnet of $S$ that converges uniformly on all members of a directed cover $\alpha$, given that for each individual member $A \in \alpha$ there exists a subnet converging uniformly on $A$. The challenge is to diagonalize across the directed family of subnets to produce a single subnet that works simultaneously for all $A \in \alpha$, using the fact that $\alpha$ is directed by reverse inclusion.
