In the Banach algebra $C(X)$ of continuous real-valued functions on a compact Hausdorff space, both the order structure (when a function is non-negative) and the uniform norm can be expressed purely in terms of the algebraic operations of the algebra. A function is non-negative exactly when it is a square, and the sup-norm is recovered algebraically from the order relation. This means the entire topological structure of $C(X)$ is encoded in its algebraic structure.
