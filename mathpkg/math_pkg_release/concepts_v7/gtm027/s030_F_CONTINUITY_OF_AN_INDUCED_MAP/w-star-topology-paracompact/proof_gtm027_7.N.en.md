# Proof of Paracompactness of the Weak-Star Topology

The space $X^*$ with the $w^*$-topology is paracompact and hence topologically complete.

**Proof (sketch).** By problem 5.Y, every product of metric spaces is paracompact. The $w^*$-topology on $X^*$ is the relativization of the product topology on $\mathbb{R}^X$. Since $\mathbb{R}$ is a metric space, $\mathbb{R}^X$ is paracompact. Alternatively, by 6.L, paracompactness of $X^*$ with the $w^*$-topology follows from the fact that $X^*$ is a subspace of the product $\prod_{x \in X} \mathbb{R}$ with its natural uniformity.

A paracompact Hausdorff space is topologically complete (admits a complete uniformity compatible with its topology).
