A complete accumulation point is a stronger notion than an ordinary accumulation point. While an accumulation point merely requires every neighborhood to intersect $A$ in at least one point other than $x$, a complete accumulation point requires every neighborhood to intersect $A$ in a subset of the same cardinality as $A$ itself. This concept is used in the study of compactness for uncountable sets and appears in the characterization of compactness via the Bolzano-Weierstrass property generalized to arbitrary cardinalities. Kelley introduces it in Problem 5.I.
