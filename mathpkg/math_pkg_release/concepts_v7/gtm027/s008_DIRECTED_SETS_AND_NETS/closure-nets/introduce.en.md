A point belongs to the closure of a set exactly when some net lying entirely in that set converges to the point. This characterization unifies two cases: accumulation points (where a net in the set minus the point converges) and points of the set itself (served by the constant net). This result shows that the closure operation, and consequently the entire topology, can be recovered from the knowledge of which nets converge to which points, making net convergence a fully adequate substitute for the open-set definition of topology.
