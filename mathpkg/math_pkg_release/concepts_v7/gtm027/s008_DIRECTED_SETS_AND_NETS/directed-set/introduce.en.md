A directed set is a non-empty set equipped with a binary relation that is transitive, reflexive, and has the property that any two elements have a common successor. This generalizes the natural ordering of the positive integers, providing a flexible index domain for nets. Directed sets are the foundational structure of Moore-Smith convergence, enabling the study of limits in general topological spaces where sequences are insufficient. Common examples include the real numbers under the usual order, the family of neighborhoods of a point ordered by reverse inclusion, and the family of finite subsets of a set ordered by reverse inclusion.
