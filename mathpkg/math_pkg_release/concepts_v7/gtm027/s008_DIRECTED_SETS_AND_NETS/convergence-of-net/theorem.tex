A net $(S, \geq)$ in a topological space $(X, \mathfrak{I})$ converges to $s$ relative to $\mathfrak{I}$ if and only if it is eventually in each $\mathfrak{I}$-neighborhood of $s$.

If $X$ is a discrete space, a net $S$ converges to $s$ iff $S$ is eventually in $\{s\}$ (i.e., from some point on $S$ is constantly equal to $s$). If $X$ is indiscrete, every net in $X$ converges to every point of $X$.
