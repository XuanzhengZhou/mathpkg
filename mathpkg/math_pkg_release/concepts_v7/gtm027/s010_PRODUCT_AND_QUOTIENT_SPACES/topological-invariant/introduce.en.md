A topological invariant is a property preserved under homeomorphisms. These properties are the primary tools for proving that two topological spaces are \emph{not} homeomorphic: if one space possesses an invariant that the other lacks, they cannot be homeomorphic. Connectedness, the Hausdorff property, the $T_1$ axiom, having a countable base, and satisfying the first axiom of countability are all examples of topological invariants. Properties defined purely in terms of the topology and set membership are automatically invariant.
