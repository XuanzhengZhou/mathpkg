Let $X$ and $Y$ be topological spaces and let $\mathscr{R}$ be the family of all Cartesian products $U \times V$ where $U$ is an open subset of $X$ and $V$ is an open subset of $Y$. The intersection of two members of $\mathscr{R}$ is a member of $\mathscr{R}$, because
$$(U \times V) \cap (R \times S) = (U \cap R) \times (V \cap S),$$
and consequently $\mathscr{R}$ is a base for a topology for $X \times Y$. This topology is called the \textbf{product topology} for $X \times Y$.

A subset $W$ of $X \times Y$ is open relative to the product topology iff for each member $(x,y)$ of $W$ there are open neighborhoods $U$ of $x$ and $V$ of $y$ such that $U \times V \subset W$.

The product topology is the smallest topology for which the projections $P_0$ and $P_1$ into the coordinate spaces are continuous.
