The product topology on the Cartesian product $X \times Y$ of two topological spaces is defined by the base consisting of all products $U \times V$ of open sets. This topology is characterized as the smallest topology that makes both coordinate projections $P_0(x,y) = x$ and $P_1(x,y) = y$ continuous. The Euclidean plane $\mathbb{R}^2$ is the canonical example: it is the product space of $\mathbb{R}$ with itself under the product topology, which coincides with the usual Euclidean topology.
