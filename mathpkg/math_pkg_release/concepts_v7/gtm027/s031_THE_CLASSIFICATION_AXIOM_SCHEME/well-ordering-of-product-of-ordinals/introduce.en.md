The canonical well-ordering $\ll$ on $R \times R$ orders pairs first by the maximum of their coordinates, then by the first coordinate, then by the second. This is a generalization of the standard "square" ordering of $\omega \times \omega$ where all predecessors of a point are finite, a property crucial for cardinal arithmetic.
