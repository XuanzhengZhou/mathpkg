A function $f$ is an extension of a function $g$ when $f$ agrees with $g$ on the domain of $g$ and possibly assigns values to additional points. Formally, this means $g$ is a subset of $f$ when both are viewed as sets of ordered pairs. Extension and restriction are dual notions: $f$ extends $g$ precisely when $g$ restricts $f$ to a subset of its domain. Extensions are essential in analysis (e.g., extending continuous functions) and topology (e.g., Tietze extension theorem).
