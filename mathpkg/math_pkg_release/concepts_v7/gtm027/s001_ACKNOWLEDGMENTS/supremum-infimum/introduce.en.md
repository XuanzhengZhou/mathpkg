The supremum (least upper bound) of a subset is the smallest element that is greater than or equal to every member of the subset. Dually, the infimum (greatest lower bound) is the largest element that is less than or equal to every member. In chains, suprema and infima are unique when they exist. These concepts are fundamental in real analysis (completeness of the reals), lattice theory, and topology. Kelley defines them in the context of arbitrary orderings, noting that without antisymmetry they need not be unique.
