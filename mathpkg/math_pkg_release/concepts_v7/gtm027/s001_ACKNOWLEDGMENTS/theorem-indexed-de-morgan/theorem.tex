Let $A$ be an index set, and for each $a$ in $A$ let $X_a$ be a subset of a fixed set $Y$. Then:

(a) If $B$ is a subset of $A$, then
$$
\bigcup \{X_b: b \in B\} \subset \bigcup \{X_a: a \in A\}
\quad\text{and}\quad
\bigcap \{X_b: b \in B\} \supset \bigcap \{X_a: a \in A\}.
$$

(b) (De Morgan formulae)
$$
Y \sim \bigcup \{X_a: a \in A\} = \bigcap \{Y \sim X_a: a \in A\},
$$
$$
Y \sim \bigcap \{X_a: a \in A\} = \bigcup \{Y \sim X_a: a \in A\}.
$$
