Theorem 5 establishes the fundamental algebraic laws for relations: double-inverse cancellation, the anti-homomorphic behavior of inverse under composition, associativity of composition, and the interaction of images with unions and intersections. Note the subset (not equality) for images of intersections — a subtle point that also appears for forward images under functions. These identities are the relational analogues of function-theoretic computations used throughout the book.
