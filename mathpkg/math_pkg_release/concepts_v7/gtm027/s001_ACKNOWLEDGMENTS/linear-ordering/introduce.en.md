A linear ordering (also called total, complete, or simple ordering) is a transitive relation that is antisymmetric and total: any two distinct elements are comparable. This contrasts with partial orderings where some pairs may be incomparable. Linear orderings are the natural setting for chains, and suprema and infima are unique in linearly ordered sets. The real numbers under the usual order and the dictionary order on words are standard examples.
