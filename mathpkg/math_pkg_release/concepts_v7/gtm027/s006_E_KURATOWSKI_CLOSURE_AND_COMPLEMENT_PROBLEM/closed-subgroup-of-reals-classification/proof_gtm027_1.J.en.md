# Proof of Classification of Closed Subgroups of the Reals

**Theorem.** A closed subgroup $G$ of $\mathbb{R}$ is either countable or identical with $\mathbb{R}$.

*Proof.* Let $G$ be a closed additive subgroup of $\mathbb{R}$. By the classification of additive subgroups (Problem J(a)), either $G$ has a smallest positive element $a > 0$, or $G$ is dense in $\mathbb{R}$.

**Case 1:** $G$ has a smallest positive element $a > 0$. Then $G = a\mathbb{Z} = \{na : n \in \mathbb{Z}\}$, which is countable (isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}$).

**Case 2:** $G$ is dense in $\mathbb{R}$. Since $G$ is additionally assumed to be closed, and the only subset of $\mathbb{R}$ that is both dense and closed is $\mathbb{R}$ itself, we must have $G = \mathbb{R}$. $\square$
