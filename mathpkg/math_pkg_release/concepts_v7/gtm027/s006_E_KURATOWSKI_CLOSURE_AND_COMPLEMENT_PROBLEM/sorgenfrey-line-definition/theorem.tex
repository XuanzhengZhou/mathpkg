Let $X = \mathbb{R}$ be the set of real numbers and let $\mathfrak{J}$ be the topology on $X$ having as a base the family $\mathfrak{R}$ of all half-open intervals
$$[a, b) = \{x \in \mathbb{R} : a \leq x < b\}$$
where $a$ and $b$ are real numbers. The space $(X, \mathfrak{J})$ is called the Sorgenfrey line (or half-open interval space). An $\mathfrak{J}$-accumulation point of a set is called an accumulation point from the right, and accumulation points from the left are similarly defined.
