Problem 1.P provides an important counterexample about components and separation. The space $X$ consists of vertical segments at $x = 1/n$ for each positive integer $n$, together with the two limit points $(0,0)$ and $(0,1)$. These two points form distinct singleton components, yet every clopen subset of $X$ must contain either both or neither of them. This demonstrates that the converse of the statement "points in the same component always lie in the same half of a separation" is false: two points may always co-occur in separations while belonging to different components.
