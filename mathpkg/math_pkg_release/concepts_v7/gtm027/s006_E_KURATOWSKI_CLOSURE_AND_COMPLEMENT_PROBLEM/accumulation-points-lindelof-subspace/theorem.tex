Let $X$ be a topological space in which every subspace is Lindel\"of. Let $A$ be an uncountable subset of $X$, and let $B$ be the set of all points $x \in A$ such that every neighborhood of $x$ contains uncountably many points of $A$. Then $A \setminus B$ is countable, and consequently every neighborhood of a point of $B$ contains uncountably many points of $B$.
