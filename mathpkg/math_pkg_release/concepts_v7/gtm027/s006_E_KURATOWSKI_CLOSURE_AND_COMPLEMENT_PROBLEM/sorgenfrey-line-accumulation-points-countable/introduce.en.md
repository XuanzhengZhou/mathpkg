In the Sorgenfrey line, the notion of accumulation points splits into one-sided versions. A key structural result is that for any set $A$ of real numbers, only countably many points of $A$ can fail to be right-accumulation points. An even stronger statement holds for points that fail to be accumulation points from both sides. This result underpins the hereditary Lindelof property of the Sorgenfrey line.
