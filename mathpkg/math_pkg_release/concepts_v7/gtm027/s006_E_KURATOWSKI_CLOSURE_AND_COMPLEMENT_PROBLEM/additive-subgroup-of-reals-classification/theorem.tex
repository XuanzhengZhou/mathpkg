Let $G$ be an additive subgroup of the real numbers $\mathbb{R}$ (with the usual topology) that contains more than one element. Then either $G$ is dense in $\mathbb{R}$, or $G$ has a smallest positive element (in which case $G$ is infinite cyclic). In particular, the set $\mathbb{Q}$ of rational numbers is dense in $\mathbb{R}$.
