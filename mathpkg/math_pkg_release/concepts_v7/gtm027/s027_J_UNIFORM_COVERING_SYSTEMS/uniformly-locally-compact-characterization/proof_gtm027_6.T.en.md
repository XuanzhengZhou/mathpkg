# Proof: Characterization of Uniformly Locally Compact Topological Spaces

Let $(X, \mathfrak{J})$ be a topological space.

- If there exists a uniformity $\mathfrak{U}$ compatible with $\mathfrak{J}$ such that $(X, \mathfrak{U})$ is uniformly locally compact, then by part (d), $X$ is the union of a disjoint open family of $\sigma$-compact subspaces. Since each $\sigma$-compact space is paracompact and locally compact, $X$ is locally compact and paracompact (a disjoint union of paracompact spaces is paracompact).

Conversely, suppose $(X, \mathfrak{J})$ is locally compact and paracompact. By 5.28, every locally compact paracompact space admits a compatible uniformity in which it is uniformly locally compact. The construction uses a locally finite cover by relatively compact open sets and the associated uniform cover.

Thus $(X, \mathfrak{J})$ admits a compatible uniformly locally compact uniformity iff it is locally compact and paracompact.
