# Proof of the Completion of a Topological Group

Let $(G, \cdot, \mathcal{J})$ be a Hausdorff topological group. Let $(H, \mathcal{U})$ be a Hausdorff completion of the uniform space $(G, \mathcal{U})$, where $\mathcal{U}$ is the two-sided uniformity. Let $\mathcal{S}$ be the topology of $\mathcal{U}$.

We need to extend the group operation $\cdot$ uniquely to $H$ so that $(H, \cdot, \mathcal{S})$ becomes a topological group and $\mathcal{U}$ becomes its two-sided uniformity.

Since $(G, \mathcal{U})$ is a dense uniform subspace of its completion $(H, \mathcal{U})$, each $h \in H$ is the limit of a Cauchy net $\{x_\alpha\} \subset G$.

For $h_1, h_2 \in H$, choose Cauchy nets $\{x_\alpha\}, \{y_\beta\}$ in $G$ converging to $h_1, h_2$ respectively. Define $h_1 \cdot h_2 = \lim_{\alpha} (x_\alpha \cdot y_\alpha)$.

The multiplication on $G$ is uniformly continuous relative to $\mathcal{U}$, hence it extends uniquely to a uniformly continuous map $H \times H \to H$. Similarly, inversion $x \mapsto x^{-1}$ extends uniquely.

Since the group axioms are identities that hold on the dense subset $G$, they also hold on $H$ by continuity. The resulting structure is a topological group, and $\mathcal{U}$ is its two-sided uniformity (since $\mathcal{U}$ is generated by $\mathcal{L} \cup \mathcal{R}$, which extend uniquely to the completion).

The completion relative to the right uniformity $\mathcal{R}$ yields a topological group completion only when $\mathcal{L}$ and $\mathcal{R}$ have the same Cauchy nets (part (a)). This condition is necessary and is not always satisfied.
