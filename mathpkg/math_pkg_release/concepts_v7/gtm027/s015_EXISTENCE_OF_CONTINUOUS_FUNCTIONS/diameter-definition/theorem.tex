Let $(X,d)$ be a pseudo-metric space and $A \subset X$. The \textbf{diameter} of $A$ is
$$\operatorname{diam}(A) = \sup \{d(x,y) : x \in A,\; y \in A\}.$$
If the supremum does not exist (i.e., the set is unbounded above), the diameter is said to be infinite. The property of having finite diameter is not a topological invariant.
