A family $\mathcal{A}$ of subsets of a topological space is \textbf{$\sigma$-locally finite} (respectively \textbf{$\sigma$-discrete}) iff it is the union of a countable number of locally finite (respectively discrete) subfamilies: $\mathcal{A} = \bigcup_{n \in \omega} \mathcal{A}_n$ where each $\mathcal{A}_n$ is locally finite (respectively discrete).
