Let $(X,d)$ be a pseudo-metric space. Define an equivalence relation $R$ on $X$ by $x R y$ iff $d(x,y) = 0$, and let $\mathfrak{D}$ be the family of all equivalence classes $\overline{\{x\}}$. For $A, B \in \mathfrak{D}$, define $D(A,B) = \operatorname{dist}(A,B) = \inf \{d(x,y) : x \in A,\; y \in B\}$.

Then $(\mathfrak{D}, D)$ is a metric space, and the projection $X \to \mathfrak{D}$ is both open and closed. The metric topology on $\mathfrak{D}$ coincides with the quotient topology.
