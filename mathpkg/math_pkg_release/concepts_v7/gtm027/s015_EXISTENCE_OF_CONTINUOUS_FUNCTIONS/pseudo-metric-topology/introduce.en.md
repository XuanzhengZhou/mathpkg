The pseudo-metric topology is defined by taking all open spheres (sets of points within a given distance of a center point) as a base. Because the intersection of two open spheres contains an open sphere around each of its points (by the triangle inequality), this family indeed forms a base for a topology. This construction generalizes the standard metric topology to pseudo-metrics.
