Theorem 7.15 is a crucial result for the Ascoli theorem: on an equicontinuous family, the pointwise topology is jointly continuous and therefore coincides with the topology of uniform convergence on compacta. This means that for equicontinuous families, pointwise compactness implies compactness under compact convergence (and the compact-open topology). The theorem bridges the gap between the purely topological pointwise topology and the uniform notion of compact convergence via equicontinuity.
