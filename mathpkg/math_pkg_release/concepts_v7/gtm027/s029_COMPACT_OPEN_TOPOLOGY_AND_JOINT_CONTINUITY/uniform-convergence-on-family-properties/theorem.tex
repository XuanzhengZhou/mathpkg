Let $F$ be a family of functions on a set $X$ to a uniform space $(Y, \mathcal{V})$, let $\alpha$ be a family of subsets of $X$, and let $G$ be the family of all functions on $X$ to $Y$ which are continuous on each member of $\alpha$. Then for the uniformity $\mathfrak{U} \mid \alpha$ of uniform convergence on members of $\alpha$:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] The uniformity $\mathfrak{U} \mid \alpha$ is larger than the uniformity of pointwise convergence and smaller than that of uniform convergence on $X$.

\item[(b)] A net $\{f_n, n \in D\}$ converges to $g$ relative to the topology of $\mathfrak{U} \mid \alpha$ if and only if it is a Cauchy net (relative to $\mathfrak{U} \mid \alpha$) and converges to $g$ pointwise.

\item[(c)] If $(Y, \mathcal{V})$ is complete, then $G$ is complete relative to $\mathfrak{U} \mid \alpha$.

\item[(d)] The family $F$ is closed in $G$ relative to the topology of $\mathfrak{U} \mid \alpha$, and hence if $(Y, \mathcal{V})$ is complete so is $(F, \mathfrak{U} \mid \alpha)$.

\item[(e)] The topology of $\mathfrak{U} \mid \alpha$ for $F$ is jointly continuous on each member of $\alpha$.
\end{enumerate}
