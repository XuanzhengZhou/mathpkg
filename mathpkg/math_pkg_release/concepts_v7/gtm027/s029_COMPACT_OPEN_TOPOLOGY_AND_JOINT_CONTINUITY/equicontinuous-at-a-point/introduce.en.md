A family $F$ of functions to a uniform space is equicontinuous at a point $x$ if there is a neighborhood of $x$ whose image under every member of $F$ is arbitrarily small. This is a uniform "equal continuity" condition: given any uniformity entourage $V$, the same neighborhood $U$ of $x$ works for all $f \in F$ to ensure $f[U] \subset V[f(x)]$. Equicontinuity is the key hypothesis in the Ascoli theorem, which characterizes compactness in function spaces.
