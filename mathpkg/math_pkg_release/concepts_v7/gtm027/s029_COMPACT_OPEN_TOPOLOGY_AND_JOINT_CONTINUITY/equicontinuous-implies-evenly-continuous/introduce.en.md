Theorem 7.22 establishes that equicontinuity (a uniform notion) implies even continuity (a purely topological notion). The proof uses a pseudo-metric $d$ from the gage of the uniform space: given a neighborhood $U$ (an $r$-sphere about $y$), equicontinuity at $x$ yields a neighborhood $V$ of $x$ with $d(f(x),f(z)) < r/2$ for all $f \in F$. Then the $r/2$-sphere about $y$ serves as $W$, ensuring $f(z) \in U$ whenever $x \in V$ and $f(x) \in W$. Thus equicontinuity is the result of "uniformizing" even continuity with respect to the range space.
