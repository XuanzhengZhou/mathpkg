This corollary to Theorem 7.5 notes that when the domain $X$ is locally compact, the distinction between joint continuity and joint continuity on compacta collapses: the two are equivalent. This follows because every point has a compact neighborhood, so the global behavior is determined by behavior on compacta. This observation is key to understanding why the compact-open topology is the smallest jointly continuous topology for continuous functions on locally compact spaces.
