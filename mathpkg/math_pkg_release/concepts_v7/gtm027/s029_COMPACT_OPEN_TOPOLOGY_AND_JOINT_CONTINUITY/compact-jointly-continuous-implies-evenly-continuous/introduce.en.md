Theorem 7.20 is the purely topological counterpart of Theorem 7.16. If a family of continuous functions to a regular Hausdorff space is compact under a jointly continuous topology, then it is evenly continuous. The proof uses that the identity from the given compact topology to the pointwise topology is continuous, so the two topologies coincide (the pointwise topology is Hausdorff). Joint continuity of the pointwise topology then yields even continuity via a compactness argument applied to $K \times \{x\}$.
