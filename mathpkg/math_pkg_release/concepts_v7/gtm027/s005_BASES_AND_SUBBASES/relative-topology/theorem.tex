If $(X, \mathfrak{J})$ is a topological space and $Y \subset X$, the \textbf{relative topology} $\mathfrak{u}$ (or the relativization of $\mathfrak{J}$ to $Y$) is the family of all intersections of members of $\mathfrak{J}$ with $Y$; that is, $U$ belongs to the relative topology $\mathfrak{u}$ iff $U = V \cap Y$ for some $\mathfrak{J}$-open set $V$. Each member $U$ of $\mathfrak{u}$ is said to be \textbf{open in $Y$}, and its relative complement $Y \sim U$ is \textbf{closed in $Y$}.
