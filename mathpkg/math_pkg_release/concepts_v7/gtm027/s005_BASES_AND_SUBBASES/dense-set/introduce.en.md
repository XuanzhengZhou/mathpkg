A subset $A$ of a topological space $X$ is dense if its closure equals the whole space $X$. Equivalently, every non-empty open set in $X$ intersects $A$. Dense subsets are fundamental in topology and analysis: the rational numbers are dense in the real line, and the existence of a countable dense subset defines separability.
