Theorem 16 establishes the fundamental relationships between topological notions in a subspace and those in the ambient space. The closure of a set in a subspace is the intersection of the subspace with the closure in the original space. When the subspace itself is open (resp. closed) in the ambient space, openness (resp. closedness) in the subspace implies the same in the original space. These facts are essential for working with restrictions of topological properties.
