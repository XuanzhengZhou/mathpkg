A topological space satisfies the first axiom of countability if every point admits a countable local base. Every second countable space is first countable, but the converse is false: any uncountable discrete space is first countable (the singleton $\{x\}$ alone forms a local base) yet fails to be second countable. The first countability axiom is sufficient for sequential characterizations of closure and continuity.
