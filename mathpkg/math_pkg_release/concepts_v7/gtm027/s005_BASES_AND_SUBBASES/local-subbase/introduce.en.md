A local subbase at a point $x$ is a family of sets whose finite intersections form a local base at $x$. This localizes the global notion of a subbase to a single point. Local subbases are useful in defining initial topologies and product topologies, where the coordinate projections' preimages of open sets form a convenient local subbase.
