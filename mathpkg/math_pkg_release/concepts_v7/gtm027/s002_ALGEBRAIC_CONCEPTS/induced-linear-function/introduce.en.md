Given linear maps $f \colon X \to Y$ and $g \colon X \to Z$ with $g$ surjective and $\ker g \subseteq \ker f$, there exists a unique linear map $h \colon Z \to Y$ such that $f = h \circ g$. This is the universal property of the quotient: every linear map factors through the quotient by its null space as a projection followed by an injection. This factorization theorem is fundamental throughout algebra.
