The Axiom of Choice asserts the existence of a choice function for any family of non-empty sets. It is equivalent to the Hausdorff Maximal Principle, Zorn's Lemma, and the Well-Ordering Principle. The proof from the Hausdorff principle uses the fact that the family of partial choice functions (functions on subsets of $A$ choosing from each $X_a$) has finite character, hence by Tukey's Lemma has a maximal element, which must be defined on all of $A$.
