\textbf{Theorem 14.} Let $E$ be the set of $b$-adic expansions, let $R$ be the set of rational expansions, and for $a \in R$ let $r(a)$ be the corresponding $b$-adic rational. Then there is a unique order-preserving function $\bar{r}$ on $E$ to the real numbers such that $\bar{r}(a) = r(a)$ for $a \in R$. Moreover, $\bar{r}$ is one-to-one on $E \setminus R$, and its image is the entire set of real numbers.
