# Proof of Theorem 25(b): Minimal Principle

**Minimal Principle (25b).** There is a minimal member of a family $\alpha$, provided that for each nest in $\alpha$ there is a member of $\alpha$ which is contained in every member of the nest.

*Proof.* The minimal principle follows from the maximal principle (25a). Let $X = \bigcup \{A : A \in \alpha\}$, and let $\alpha'$ be the family of complements relative to $X$ of members of $\alpha$. Observing that, because of the De Morgan formulae, $\alpha'$ satisfies the hypothesis of (25a), $\alpha'$ hence has a maximal member $M$. Then $X \setminus M$ is surely a minimal member of $\alpha$.
