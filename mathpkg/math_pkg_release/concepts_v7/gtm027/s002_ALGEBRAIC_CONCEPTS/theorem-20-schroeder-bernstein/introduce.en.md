The Schroeder-Bernstein theorem states that if $|A| \leq |B|$ and $|B| \leq |A|$ (in the sense of injections), then $|A| = |B|$ (there is a bijection). Kelley gives a proof that decomposes $A$ and $B$ via "parthenogenesis" (ancestor tracking), constructing a bijection from the two given injections. The proof does not use the axiom of choice and produces a function with the same "pleasant properties" (e.g., Borel measurability) as the given maps.
