A group is an algebraic structure consisting of a non-empty set equipped with an associative binary operation, an identity element, and inverses for every element. Groups formalize the notion of symmetry and are fundamental throughout mathematics. The group operation is often written multiplicatively ($x \cdot y$) or additively ($x + y$), with the identity denoted $e$ or $0$ and inverses as $x^{-1}$ or $-x$ respectively. Kelley introduces this definition to support later algebraic discussions, particularly in the problems.
