Lower semi-continuity is the dual notion to upper semi-continuity, defined by negating the function. For a bounded real-valued function, the oscillation measures the gap between its upper and lower semi-continuous regularizations. The oscillation function is itself upper semi-continuous, and continuity at a point is precisely characterized by vanishing oscillation. This provides a quantitative measure of how far a function is from being continuous.
