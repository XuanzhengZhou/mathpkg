This theorem gives an axiomatic characterization of the family of closed sets, dual to the definition of a topology. Any family of sets closed under finite unions and arbitrary intersections, that covers $X$, uniquely determines a topology by taking complements. This shows that a topology can be equivalently specified by giving either the open sets or the closed sets.
