# Exercise 1.J

In the usual topology for the real numbers, prove that the only subsets which are both open and closed are the space $\mathbb{R}$ and the void set $\emptyset$.

*Hint.* Suppose $A \subseteq \mathbb{R}$ is both open and closed, and $A \neq \emptyset$, $A \neq \mathbb{R}$. Then both $A$ and $\mathbb{R} \setminus A$ are nonempty open sets whose union is $\mathbb{R}$. Pick $a \in A$ and $b \in \mathbb{R} \setminus A$, and consider the supremum or infimum of a suitable set to derive a contradiction with the openness of $A$ and $\mathbb{R} \setminus A$.
