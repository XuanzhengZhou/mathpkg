Unordered summation extends the notion of series to functions defined on arbitrary (possibly uncountable) index sets. The sum is defined as the limit of the net of finite partial sums, directed by inclusion. Historically, unordered summation was the forerunner of Moore-Smith convergence. It provides a foundation for measure theory that replaces absolutely convergent series and generalizes elegantly to vector-valued integration.
