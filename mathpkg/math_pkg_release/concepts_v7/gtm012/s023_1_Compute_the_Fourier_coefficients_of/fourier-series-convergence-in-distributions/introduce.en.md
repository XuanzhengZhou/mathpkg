Corollary 1.4 follows immediately from Theorem 1.3 (which characterizes the Fourier coefficients of a distribution) and states that the symmetric partial sums of a distribution's Fourier series converge back to the original distribution in the topology of $\mathcal{P}'$. More precisely, if $F$ is a periodic distribution with Fourier coefficients $(a_n)$, then the distributions $F_N$ defined by the trigonometric polynomials $\sum_{-N}^{N} a_n e^{inx}$ converge to $F$ in the sense of distributions. This result justifies the Fourier series representation of periodic distributions and is the distributional analogue of Fourier series convergence for smooth functions.
