# Exercise 2

Show that the limit in (17) exists. That is, if $u_n \to F$ and $v_n \to G$ in $\mathbf{L}^2$, show that $\lim_{n \to \infty} (u_n, v_n)$ exists.

*Hint.* Use the fact that $(u_n)$ and $(v_n)$ are $\|\cdot\|$-Cauchy, and apply the Schwarz inequality to bound $|(u_n, v_n) - (u_m, v_m)|$.
