Corollary 1.2 states that the quantity $\|u\| = (u,u)^{1/2}$ defined via the inner product satisfies the three axioms of a norm: positive definiteness, absolute homogeneity, and the triangle inequality. The triangle inequality follows from the Schwarz inequality (Lemma 1.1) by expanding $\|u+v\|^2$ and bounding the cross terms. Thus $\mathbb{C}$ is a normed vector space with respect to the $L^2$ norm.
