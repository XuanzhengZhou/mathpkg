# Exercise 3

Show that (18) is true: if $u_n \to F$ and $v_n \to G$ in $\mathbf{L}^2$, and also $u'_n \to F$ and $v'_n \to G$ in $\mathbf{L}^2$, then

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} (u_n, v_n) = \lim_{n \to \infty} (u'_n, v'_n).$$

Thus the inner product $(F, G)$ defined by (17) is independent of the choice of approximating sequences.

*Hint.* Apply Lemma 1.2 to the sequences $(u_n)$ and $(u'_n)$, and to $(v_n)$ and $(v'_n)$. Then use the Schwarz inequality to estimate the difference of the inner products.
