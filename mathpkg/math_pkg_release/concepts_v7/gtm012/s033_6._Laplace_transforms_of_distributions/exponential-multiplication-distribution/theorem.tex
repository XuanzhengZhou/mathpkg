Let $F \in \mathcal{L}'$ and let $w \in \mathbb{C}$. The distribution $e_w F$ is defined by
$$e_w F(u) = F(e_w u), \quad u \in \mathcal{L},$$
where $e_w(t) = e^{-wt}$. If $u_n \to u$ in $\mathcal{L}$, then $e_w u_n \to e_w u$ in $\mathcal{L}$, so $e_w F$ is indeed a continuous linear functional on $\mathcal{L}$.
