Proposition 6.1 collects the fundamental algebraic and operational properties of the Laplace transform on the space of distributions $\mathcal{L}'$. The transform is linear (identities 5--6) and compatible with translation: translating the distribution by $s$ multiplies the transform by $e^{-zs}$ (identity 7). Similar identities hold for differentiation, multiplication by exponentials, and multiplication by powers of $t$ (identities 8--10). The proposition also establishes that the distribution $e_w F$ defined by duality in (11) is well-defined, and that when the distribution comes from an ordinary function $f$, the distributional Laplace transform coincides with the classical one (identity 12).
