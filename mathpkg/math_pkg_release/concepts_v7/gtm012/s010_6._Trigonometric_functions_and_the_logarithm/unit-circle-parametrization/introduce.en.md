Theorem 6.3 connects the analytic definition of sine, cosine, and π with their classical geometric interpretation. It proves that the mapping t ↦ (cos t, sin t) is a bijection from [0, 2π) onto the unit circle, that the arc length measured from the starting point equals the parameter t, and that the total circumference of the unit circle is 2π. This justifies the use of the geometric vocabulary for these analytically defined objects.
