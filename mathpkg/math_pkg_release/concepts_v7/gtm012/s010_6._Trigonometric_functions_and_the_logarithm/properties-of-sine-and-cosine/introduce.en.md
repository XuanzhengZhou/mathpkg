Theorem 6.2 derives the fundamental algebraic and analytic properties of S and C from Theorem 6.1. The explicit formulas express S and C in terms of the complex exponential, establishing that they are real-valued C^∞ functions satisfying Euler's formula e^{ix} = C(x) + iS(x). The derivative relations S' = C and C' = -S and the Pythagorean identity S^2 + C^2 = 1 follow directly. Part (c) proves the existence of a first positive zero p of C, which is used to define π = 2p; part (d) then shows 4p-periodicity.
