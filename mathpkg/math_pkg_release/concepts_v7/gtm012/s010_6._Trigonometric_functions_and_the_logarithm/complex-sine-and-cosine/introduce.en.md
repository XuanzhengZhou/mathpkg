The sine and cosine functions are extended from real to complex arguments via the complex exponential function. For real x these definitions coincide with the functions S and C of Theorem 6.1, and the power series expansions converge for all complex z, making sine and cosine entire holomorphic functions. The series representation reveals the parity properties: sine is odd and cosine is even.
