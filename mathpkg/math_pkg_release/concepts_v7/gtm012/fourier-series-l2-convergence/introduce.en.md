This theorem establishes that the complex exponentials form a complete orthonormal system in $L^2$. The Parseval identity equates the $L^2$ norm of a distribution with the $\ell^2$ norm of its Fourier coefficient sequence. The Riesz-Fischer theorem is the converse: any $\ell^2$ sequence corresponds to an $L^2$ element.
