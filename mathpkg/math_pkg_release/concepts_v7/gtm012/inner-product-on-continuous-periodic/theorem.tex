Suppose $u, v \in \mathbb{C}$, the space of continuous complex-valued periodic functions. The \textbf{inner product} of $u$ and $v$ is
$$(u, v) = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} u(x) v(x)^* \, dx.$$
Properties: $(au, v) = a(u,v) = (u, a^*v)$, $(u_1+u_2, v) = (u_1,v)+(u_2,v)$, $(v,u) = (u,v)^*$, $(u,u) \geq 0$ and $(u,u)=0$ only if $u=0$.

The associated norm is $\|u\| = (u,u)^{1/2}$.

\textbf{Schwarz inequality:} $|(u,v)| \leq \|u\| \, \|v\|$, with equality iff $u$ and $v$ are linearly dependent.
