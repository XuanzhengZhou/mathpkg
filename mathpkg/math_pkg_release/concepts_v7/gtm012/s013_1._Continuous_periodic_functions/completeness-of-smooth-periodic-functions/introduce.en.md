Theorem 2.2 establishes that the metric $d'$ on $\mathcal{P}$ induces exactly the topology of $\mathcal{P}$-convergence (uniform convergence of all derivatives), and that $\mathcal{P}$ is complete under this metric. This makes $\mathcal{P}$ a Frechet space: a complete metrizable locally convex topological vector space whose topology is generated by the countable family of seminorms $p_k(u) = \|D^k u\|$. The completeness proof relies on the fact that uniform convergence of derivatives allows interchange of limits and differentiation.
