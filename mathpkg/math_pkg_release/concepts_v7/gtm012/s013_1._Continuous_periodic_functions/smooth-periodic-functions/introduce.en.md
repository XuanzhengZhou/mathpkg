The space $\mathcal{P}$ of smooth ($C^\infty$) $2\pi$-periodic functions is a dense subspace of $\mathbb{C}$. Unlike $\mathbb{C}$, $\mathcal{P}$ is not complete under the uniform norm, but it carries a natural Frechet space structure given by the countable family of seminorms $p_k(u) = \|D^k u\|$. The space $\mathcal{P}$ plays a central role as the space of test functions in the theory of periodic distributions, analogous to the role of $C_c^\infty$ in Schwartz distribution theory.
