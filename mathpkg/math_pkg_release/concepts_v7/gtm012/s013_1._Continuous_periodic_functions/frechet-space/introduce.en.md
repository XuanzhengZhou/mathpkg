A Frechet space is a complete metrizable locally convex topological vector space. It generalizes the concept of a Banach space by allowing the topology to be generated by a countable separating family of seminorms rather than a single norm. The space $\mathcal{P}$ of smooth periodic functions is the prototypical example in this chapter, with seminorms $p_k(u) = \|D^k u\|$. Frechet spaces arise naturally in analysis wherever infinite differentiability or other properties requiring control of infinitely many derivatives are studied.
