Theorem 6.2 establishes that every periodic distribution has some finite order. Although the definition of a distribution only requires continuity with respect to the full $\mathcal{P}$-topology (which involves infinitely many derivatives), the theorem shows that continuity is in fact equivalent to boundedness with respect to finitely many sup-norms of derivatives. The proof proceeds by contradiction: if a distribution were not of any finite order, one could construct a sequence violating continuity.
