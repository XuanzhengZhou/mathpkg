A \textit{periodic distribution} is a continuous linear functional on the space $\mathcal{P}$ of smooth periodic functions. Thus a periodic distribution is a mapping $F: \mathcal{P} \to \mathbb{C}$ such that

$$F(au) = aF(u), \quad a \in \mathbb{C}, \quad u \in \mathcal{P};$$

$$F(u + v) = F(u) + F(v), \quad u, v \in \mathcal{P};$$

$$F(u_n) \to F(u) \quad \text{if } u_n \to u \; (\mathcal{P}).$$
