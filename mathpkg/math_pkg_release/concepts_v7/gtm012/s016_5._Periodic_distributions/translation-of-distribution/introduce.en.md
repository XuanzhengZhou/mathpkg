The translation of a periodic distribution $F$ by $t$ is defined by $T_t F(u) = F(T_{-t}u)$, where $T_{-t}u(x) = u(x+t)$. This extends the classical translation of functions to distributions in a way that is consistent with the function embedding: $T_t(F_v) = F_{T_t v}$. The negative sign in the argument ensures that the distributional translation matches the natural action on test functions via change of variables.
