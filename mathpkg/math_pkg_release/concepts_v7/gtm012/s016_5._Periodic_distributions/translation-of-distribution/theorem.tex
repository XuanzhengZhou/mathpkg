If $v \in \mathcal{C}$ and $t \in \mathbb{R}$, the translate $T_t v$ is defined by $T_t v(x) = v(x - t)$. Then

$$\frac{1}{2\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} T_t v(x) u(x) \, dx = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} v(x - t) u(x) \, dx
= \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} v(x) u(x + t) \, dx = F_v(T_{-t}u).$$

We define $T_t F$, for $F \in \mathcal{P}'$, by

$$T_t F(u) = F(T_{-t}u), \quad u \in \mathcal{P}.$$
