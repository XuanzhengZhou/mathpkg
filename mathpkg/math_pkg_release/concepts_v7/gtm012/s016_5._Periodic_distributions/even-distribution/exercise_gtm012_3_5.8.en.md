# Exercise 8

Show that any $F \in \mathcal{P}'$ can be written uniquely as $F = G + H$, where $G, H \in \mathcal{P}'$ and $G$ is even, $H$ is odd.

Hint: Consider $G = \frac{1}{2}(F + F^\sim)$ and $H = \frac{1}{2}(F - F^\sim)$.
