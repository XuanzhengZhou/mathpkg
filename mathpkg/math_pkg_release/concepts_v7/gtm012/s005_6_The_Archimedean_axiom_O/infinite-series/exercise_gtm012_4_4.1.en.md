# Exercise 1

If $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} z_n$ converges with sum $s$ and $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} w_n$ converges with sum $t$, then $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (z_n + w_n)$ converges with sum $s + t$.

*Hint.* Let $s_n = \sum_{m=1}^{n} z_m$ and $t_n = \sum_{m=1}^{n} w_m$. Then the $n$-th partial sum of $\sum (z_n + w_n)$ is $s_n + t_n$. Apply the algebraic properties of limits (Proposition 3.1(a)) to the sequences of partial sums.
