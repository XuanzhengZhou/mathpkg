The root test examines the $n$th root of the absolute value of terms. If the limsup of $|z_n|^{1/n}$ is less than 1, eventual comparison with a convergent geometric series yields convergence. If the limsup exceeds 1, infinitely many terms have magnitude at least 1, forcing divergence. The root test is theoretically more powerful than the ratio test (it has a larger domain of applicability) and is the natural tool for determining the radius of convergence of power series via the Cauchy-Hadamard formula.
