Suppose $z_n \to z$ and also $z_n \to w$. Then for any $\varepsilon > 0$, we can take $n$ so large that $|z_n - z| < \varepsilon$ and also $|z_n - w| < \varepsilon$. Then
$$|z - w| \leq |z - z_n| + |z_n - w| < \varepsilon + \varepsilon = 2\varepsilon.$$
Since this holds for all $\varepsilon > 0$, necessarily $z = w$.
