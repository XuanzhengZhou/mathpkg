# Exercise 3

Show that

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n+1}{2n^2+1}$$

diverges, and that

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n+1}{2n^3+1}$$

converges.

*Hint.* Use Proposition 4.3 (comparison test) together with Exercise 2. Compare these series to $\sum 1/n$ and $\sum 1/n^2$, respectively. Note that

$$\frac{n+1}{2n^2+1} \sim \frac{1}{2n} \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{n+1}{2n^3+1} \sim \frac{1}{2n^2}$$

as $n \to \infty$.
