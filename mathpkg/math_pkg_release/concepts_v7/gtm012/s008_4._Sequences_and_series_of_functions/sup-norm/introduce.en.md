The sup norm (also called the uniform norm) assigns to each bounded complex-valued function on a set $S$ the supremum of the absolute values of its outputs. It is the natural norm for studying uniform convergence of sequences and series of functions. When equipped with this norm, the space of bounded functions becomes a metric space with the distance $d(f,g)=\|f-g\|$.
