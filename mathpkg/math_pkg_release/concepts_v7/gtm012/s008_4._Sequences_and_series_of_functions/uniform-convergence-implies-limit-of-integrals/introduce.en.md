Theorem 4.2 states that if a sequence of continuous functions converges uniformly on a closed interval, then the limit of the integrals equals the integral of the limit. This is a key justification for exchanging the limit and integral operations. Under uniform convergence, the integral of the pointwise limit is well-behaved, in contrast to pointwise convergence where the limit function need not even be integrable.
