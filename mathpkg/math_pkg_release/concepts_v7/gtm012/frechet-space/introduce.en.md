A Frechet space is a complete metric vector space whose topology is generated by a countable family of seminorms. It generalizes Banach spaces to cases where the topology cannot be given by a single norm. The prototypical example is $\mathcal{P}$, the space of smooth periodic functions, where convergence means uniform convergence of all derivatives.
