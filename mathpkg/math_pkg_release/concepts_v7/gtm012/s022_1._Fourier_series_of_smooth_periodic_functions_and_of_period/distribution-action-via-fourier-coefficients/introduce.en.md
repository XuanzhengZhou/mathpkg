This theorem shows how the action of a periodic distribution $F$ on a smooth periodic function $u$ can be computed directly from their respective Fourier coefficient sequences. Specifically, $F(u)$ equals the sum over all integers $n$ of $a_n b_{-n}$, which is a discrete analogue of the Parseval/Plancherel duality between a distribution and a test function. The proof relies on approximating $u$ by its Fourier partial sums $u_N$, which converge to $u$ in the $\mathcal{P}$ topology, and using the continuity and linearity of $F$.
