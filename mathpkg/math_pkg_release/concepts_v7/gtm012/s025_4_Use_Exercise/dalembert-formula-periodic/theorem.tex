\begin{corollary}
Let $G$ and $H$ be the distributions defined by functions $g$ and $h$ in $\mathcal{P}$, and suppose $h = 0$. Let $(F_t)_{t>0}$ be the family of distributions given by Theorem~5.1. Then the Fourier series for $F_t$ converges, and $F_t$ is the distribution defined by the function $u_t \in \mathcal{P}$, where
$$
u(x, t) = u_t(x) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n \cos(nt) \, e^{inx}
= \frac{1}{2}\bigl[g(x+t) + g(x-t)\bigr].
$$
This function $u$ satisfies
$$
\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}, \quad
u(x, 0) = g(x), \quad
\frac{\partial u}{\partial t}(x, 0) = 0.
$$
\end{corollary}
