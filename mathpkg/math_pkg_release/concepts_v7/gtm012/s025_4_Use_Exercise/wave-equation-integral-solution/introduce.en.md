This corollary of Theorem~5.1 gives the solution of the wave equation on periodic functions when the initial displacement is zero but the initial velocity is given by $h$. The solution is expressed as the integral of the initial velocity over the spatial interval $[x-t, x+t]$, which represents the cumulative effect of the initial velocity disturbance propagating along characteristics. Together with d'Alembert's formula, the general solution for arbitrary $g$ and $h$ is the superposition $u(x,t) = \frac{1}{2}[g(x+t)+g(x-t)] + \frac{1}{2}\int_{x-t}^{x+t} h(y)\,dy$.
