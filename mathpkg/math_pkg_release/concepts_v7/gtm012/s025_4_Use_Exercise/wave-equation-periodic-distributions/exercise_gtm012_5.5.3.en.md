# Exercise 3

Use Exercise 2 and the results of this section to prove a theorem about existence and uniqueness of classical solutions of the problem

$$\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}, \quad x \in (0, \pi), \quad t > 0,$$
$$u(0, t) = u(\pi, t) = 0,$$
$$u(x, 0) = g(x), \quad \frac{\partial u}{\partial t}(x, 0) = h(x).$$
