Lemma 4.1 constructs a concrete sequence of non-negative trigonometric polynomials $\varphi_n(x) = c_n (1 + \cos x)^n$ that forms an approximate identity on $[0, 2\pi]$. The normalization constant $c_n$ is chosen so that each $\varphi_n$ has integral $2\pi$; as $n$ grows, the mass concentrates near $0$ and $2\pi$. This approximate identity is the key tool used in Theorem 4.3 to prove that continuous periodic functions can be uniformly approximated by trigonometric polynomials.
