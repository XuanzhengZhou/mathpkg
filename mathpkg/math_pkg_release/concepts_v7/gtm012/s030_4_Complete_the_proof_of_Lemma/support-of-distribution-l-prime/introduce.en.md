The support of a distribution generalizes the notion of support for functions. A point $t$ lies outside the support of $F$ if $F$ vanishes on all test functions whose support is contained in a sufficiently small neighborhood of $t$. This definition is consistent with the support of a function when $F = F_f$. The support is always a closed set, and for distributions in $\mathcal{L}'$, it is bounded below.
