The space $\mathcal{L}$ consists of all smooth functions on $\mathbb{R}$ whose derivatives decay faster than any exponential as $t 	o +\infty$. This space serves as the domain of test functions for the theory of Laplace transforms. The rapid decay condition ensures that the Laplace transform integral converges for suitable integrands, and the smoothness allows differentiation under the integral sign. The space $\mathcal{L}$ is a vector space closed under differentiation, translation, complex conjugation, and right-integration.
