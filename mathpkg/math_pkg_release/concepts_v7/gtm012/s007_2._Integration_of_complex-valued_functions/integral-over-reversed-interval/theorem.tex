Suppose $f: [a_0, b_0] \to \mathbb{C}$ is Riemann integrable, and suppose $a, b \in [a_0, b_0]$. If $a < b$, then $f$ is integrable on $[a, b]$. If $b < a$, then $f$ is integrable on $[b, a]$ and we define
$$\int_a^b f = -\int_b^a f.$$
We also define
$$\int_a^a f = 0.$$
Then for any $a, b, c \in [a_0, b_0]$,
$$\int_a^c f = \int_a^b f + \int_b^c f.$$
