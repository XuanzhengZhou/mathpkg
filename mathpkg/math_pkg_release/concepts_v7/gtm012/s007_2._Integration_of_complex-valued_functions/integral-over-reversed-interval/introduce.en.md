To make the additivity formula $\int_a^c f = \int_a^b f + \int_b^c f$ hold for arbitrary ordering of the points $a,b,c$, two conventions are introduced. First, if the upper limit is less than the lower limit, the integral is defined as the negative of the integral over the reversed interval. Second, the integral over a degenerate interval of zero length is defined to be zero. With these conventions, additivity holds without case analysis.
