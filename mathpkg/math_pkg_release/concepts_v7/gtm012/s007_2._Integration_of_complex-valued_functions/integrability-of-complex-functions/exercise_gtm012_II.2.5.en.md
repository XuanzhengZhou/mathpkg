# Exercise 5

Suppose \(f: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}\) is continuous and \(f(x) \geq 0\) for all \(x \in [a, b]\). Show that \(\int_a^b f = 0\) implies \(f(x) = 0\) for all \(x \in [a, b]\).

*Hint:* If \(f(x_0) > 0\) for some \(x_0\), then by continuity there is a subinterval around \(x_0\) where \(f\) is bounded below by a positive number. Use additivity and the estimate \(|S(f;P)| \geq \cdots\) to derive a contradiction.
