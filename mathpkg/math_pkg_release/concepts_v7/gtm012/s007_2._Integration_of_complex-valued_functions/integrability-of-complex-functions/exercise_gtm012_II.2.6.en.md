# Exercise 6

Suppose \(f: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}\) is bounded, integrable, and real-valued. Suppose

\[
\left| \int_a^b f \right| = M(b - a), \quad \text{where} \quad M = \sup\{ |f(x)| \mid x \in [a, b] \}.
\]

Show that \(f\) is constant.

*Hint:* Consider \(\int_a^b (M - f)\) and \(\int_a^b (M + f)\) and use the result of Exercise 5.
