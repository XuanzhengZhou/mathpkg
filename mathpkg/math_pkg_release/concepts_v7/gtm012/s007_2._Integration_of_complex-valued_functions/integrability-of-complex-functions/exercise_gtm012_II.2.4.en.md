# Exercise 4

Suppose \(f: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}\) is bounded and is continuous except at some finite set of points in \([a, b]\). Show that \(f\) is integrable.

*Hint:* Partition the interval at the points of discontinuity. On each subinterval \(f\) is continuous, hence uniformly continuous on closed subintervals away from the discontinuities. Use Proposition 2.5 to glue the pieces.
