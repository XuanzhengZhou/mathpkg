# Exercise 1

Let \(f: [0, 1] \to \mathbb{R}\) with \(f(x) = 0\) if \(x\) is irrational, \(f(x) = 1\) if \(x\) is rational. Show that \(f\) is not integrable.

*Hint:* In any subinterval of a partition there are both rational and irrational points, so one can choose Riemann sums arbitrarily close to 0 or to 1.
