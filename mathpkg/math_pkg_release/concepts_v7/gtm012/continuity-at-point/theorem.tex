Let $(S, d)$ and $(S', d')$ be metric spaces. A function $f: S \to S'$ is \textit{continuous at} $x \in S$ if for each $\varepsilon > 0$ there exists $\delta > 0$ such that:
\[
d'(f(x), f(y)) < \varepsilon \quad \text{whenever } d(x, y) < \delta.
\]
Equivalently, $f(x_n) \to f(x)$ whenever $x_n \to x$ in $S$.
