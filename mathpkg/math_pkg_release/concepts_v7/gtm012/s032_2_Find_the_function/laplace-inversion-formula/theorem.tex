Suppose $f \colon \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{C}$ is continuous and satisfies
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\operatorname{supp}(f) \subset [M, \infty)$,
\item $|f(t)| \leq K e^{at}$ for all $t$.
\end{enumerate}
Let $g = Lf$ be its Laplace transform. For any $b > \max\{a, 0\}$, let $C$ be the vertical line
$$C = \{z \in \mathbb{C} \mid \operatorname{Re} z = b\}.$$
Then $f$ is the second derivative of the function $F$ defined by
$$F(t) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \int_{C} e^{zt} \, z^{-2} \, g(z) \, dz.$$
Equivalently,
$$F(t) = \int_{-\infty}^{t} (t - s) \, f(s) \, ds,$$
and therefore $D^{2}F = f$.
