A linear combination is a finite sum of scalar multiples of vectors. This is the fundamental way to build new vectors from a given set. The set of all linear combinations of vectors from a subset $S$ is called the span of $S$, and is the smallest subspace containing $S$. The concept of linear combination is central to defining linear dependence, bases, and dimension.
