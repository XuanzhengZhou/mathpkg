# Exercise 2.1.1

Prove the equivalence of the two definitions of continuity at a point:
(1) The $\varepsilon$-$\delta$ definition: $f$ is continuous at $x$ if for each $\varepsilon > 0$ there is a $\delta > 0$ such that $d'(f(x), f(y)) < \varepsilon$ if $d(x, y) < \delta$.
(2) The sequential definition: $f$ is continuous at $x$ if $f(x_n) \to f(x)$ whenever $(x_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ is a sequence in $S$ which converges to $x$.
