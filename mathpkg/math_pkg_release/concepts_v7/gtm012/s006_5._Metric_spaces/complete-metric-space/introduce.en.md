A metric space is complete if every Cauchy sequence converges to a limit within the space. Completeness is a fundamental property that distinguishes spaces like $\mathbb{R}^n$ from spaces like $\mathbb{Q}$. In complete spaces, the Cauchy criterion provides a necessary and sufficient condition for convergence. Closed subsets of complete spaces are themselves complete, and complete subsets are always closed.
