Lemma 7.2 gives an equivalent characterization of linear dependence for a finite set of vectors (with $n \geq 2$): the vectors are linearly dependent precisely when one of them can be written as a linear combination of the remaining ones. This makes the notion of redundancy precise: a linearly dependent set contains at least one vector that is "unnecessary" for the span. The forward direction follows by solving for a vector with a nonzero coefficient; the converse is immediate.
