Suppose $(S, d)$ is a metric space. A subset $A \subset S$ is said to be open (or is called an open set) if it is a neighborhood of each of its points. That is, for each $x \in A$ there exists $r > 0$ such that $B_r(x) \subset A$.

A subset $A \subset S$ is said to be a neighborhood of the point $x \in S$ if there exists $r > 0$ such that $B_r(x) \subset A$.
