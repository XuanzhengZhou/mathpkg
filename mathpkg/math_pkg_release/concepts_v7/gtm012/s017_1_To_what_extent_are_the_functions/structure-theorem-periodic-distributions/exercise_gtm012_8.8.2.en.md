# Exercise 2

Find $v \in \mathcal{C}$ such that $D^2 F_v = F$, where

$$F = \delta - T_\pi \delta,$$

i.e.,

$$F(u) = u(0) - u(\pi).$$

*Hint.* Solve the differential equation $D^2 v = \delta - T_\pi \delta$ in the sense of distributions. First find a piecewise linear continuous function whose second distributional derivative gives point masses at $0$ and $\pi$ with opposite signs. Consider the periodic extension of a suitable function on $[0, 2\pi]$.
