# Exercise 3

Find $v \in \mathcal{C}$ and a constant function $f$ such that

$$\delta = D^2 F_v + F_f.$$

*Hint.* Use the result of Exercise 2 or solve directly. Since $\delta$ is a distribution of order $0$ (i.e., of order $0+2=2$ with $k=0$), the structure theorem guarantees such a representation exists. Find an explicit continuous function $v$ on the circle whose second derivative (in the distributional sense) yields $\delta$, up to a constant term. Consider a "tent" function centered at $0$ with appropriate slope.
