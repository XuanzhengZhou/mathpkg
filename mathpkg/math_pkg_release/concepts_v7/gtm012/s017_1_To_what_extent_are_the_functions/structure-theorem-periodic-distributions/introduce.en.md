The structure theorem for periodic distributions provides a complete characterization: every periodic distribution $F$ of order $k+2$ can be expressed as $F = D^k F_v + F_f$, where $v$ is a continuous periodic function and $f$ is a constant. In other words, any periodic distribution is a finite-order derivative of a regular distribution induced by a continuous function (possibly plus a constant term). This is the periodic analog of the classical result that distributions on $\mathbb{R}$ are locally derivatives of continuous functions, and it shows that the space $\mathcal{P}'$ is precisely the union over $k$ of $D^k(\mathcal{C}) \oplus \mathbb{C}$.
