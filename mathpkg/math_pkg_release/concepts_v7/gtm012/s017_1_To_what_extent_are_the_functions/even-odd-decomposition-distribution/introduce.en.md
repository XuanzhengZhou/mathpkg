Every periodic distribution can be uniquely decomposed into an even part $G = \frac{1}{2}(F + F^\sim)$ and an odd part $H = \frac{1}{2}(F - F^\sim)$, where $F^\sim$ is the reversal (reflection) of $F$. An even distribution satisfies $F^\sim = F$, meaning it is invariant under the change of variable $x \mapsto -x$, while an odd distribution satisfies $F^\sim = -F$. For example, the delta distribution $\delta$ is even, and its derivative $D\delta$ is odd. This decomposition is useful for simplifying convolution calculations and studying symmetries.
