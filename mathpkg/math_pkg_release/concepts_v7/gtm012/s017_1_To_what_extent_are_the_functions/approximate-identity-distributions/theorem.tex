Let $(\varphi_n)_{1}^{\infty} \subset \mathcal{P}$ be an approximate identity and let $F \in \mathcal{P}'$. Set $F_n = F_{f_n}$ where $f_n = F * \varphi_n$. Then
$$F_n \to F \quad (\mathcal{P}').$$

Moreover, if $(\varphi_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ is an approximate identity consisting of trigonometric polynomials, then the functions $f_n = F * \varphi_n$ are also trigonometric polynomials. In particular, for $e_k(x) = \exp(2\pi i k x)$,
$$(F * e_k)(x) = F(e_k) \, e_k(x),$$
and
$$F\left(\sum a_k e_k\right) = \sum a_k F(e_k) e_k.$$
