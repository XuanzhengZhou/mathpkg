Theorem 7.7 is the distributional analog of the approximation theorem for functions: every periodic distribution $F$ can be approximated in $\mathcal{P}'$ by the regular distributions $F_{f_n}$ induced by $f_n = F * \varphi_n$, where $(\varphi_n)$ is any approximate identity. If the approximate identity consists of trigonometric polynomials, the approximating functions $f_n$ are also trigonometric polynomials. This yields the Fourier series representation of a periodic distribution via the formula $(F * e_k)(x) = F(e_k) e_k(x)$, where the coefficients $F(e_k)$ are the Fourier coefficients of $F$.
