Proposition 7.1 establishes the fundamental algebraic and analytic properties of the operation $F * u$ where $F$ is a periodic distribution and $u$ is a smooth periodic function. The result $F * u$ is again a smooth periodic function, and the operation is bilinear in both arguments. Crucially, differentiation can be transferred to either factor: $D^k(F * u) = (D^k F) * u = F * (D^k u)$, which is essential for solving differential equations in the setting of distributions.
