The Cauchy integral formula expresses the values of a holomorphic function inside a contour in terms of its values on the contour. A stunning consequence is that holomorphic functions are automatically infinitely differentiable, and all derivatives can be expressed by similar integral formulas. The formula also leads to power series expansions, Liouville's theorem, and the residue theorem.
