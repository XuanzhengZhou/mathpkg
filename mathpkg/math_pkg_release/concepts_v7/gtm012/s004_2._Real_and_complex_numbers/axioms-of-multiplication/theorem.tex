For any $x, y, z \in \mathbb{R}$, the following axioms hold:
\begin{itemize}
\item[M1.] $(xy)z = x(yz)$ \hfill (associativity of multiplication)
\item[M2.] $xy = yx$ \hfill (commutativity of multiplication)
\item[M3.] There exists an element $1 \in \mathbb{R}$, $1 \neq 0$, such that $x \cdot 1 = x$ for every $x \in \mathbb{R}$ \hfill (multiplicative identity)
\item[M4.] For each $x \in \mathbb{R}$ with $x \neq 0$, there exists an element $x^{-1} \in \mathbb{R}$ such that $x \cdot x^{-1} = 1$ \hfill (multiplicative inverse)
\end{itemize}
The elements $1$ and $x^{-1}$ (for $x \neq 0$) are unique.
