# Exercise 1

Prove that the additive identity $0$ and the multiplicative identity $1$ in $\mathbb{R}$ are unique. Also prove that for each $x \in \mathbb{R}$, the additive inverse $-x$ is unique, and if $x \neq 0$, the multiplicative inverse $x^{-1}$ is unique.

*Hint:* Use only the field axioms A1–A4, M1–M4, and DL. For the additive identities, suppose $0$ and $0'$ both satisfy A3 and show $0 = 0'$. A similar argument works for $1$. For inverses, suppose $y$ and $y'$ are both additive inverses of $x$, so $x + y = 0 = x + y'$; deduce $y = y'$. Similarly for multiplicative inverses.
