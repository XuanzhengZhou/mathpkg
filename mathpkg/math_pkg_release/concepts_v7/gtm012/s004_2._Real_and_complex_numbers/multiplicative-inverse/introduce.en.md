Every nonzero real number $x$ has a multiplicative inverse $x^{-1}$, which when multiplied by $x$ yields the multiplicative identity $1$. This is Axiom M4 in the field axiom system for $\mathbb{R}$. The text references this axiom when noting that $x^{-1}$ is unique, though the statement was truncated by OCR.
