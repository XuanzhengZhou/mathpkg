If $(a_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}$ is a sequence in $\mathbb{C}$ and $z_0 \in \mathbb{C}$, the series
$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (z - z_0)^n$$
is the \textbf{power series} around $z_0$ with coefficients $(a_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}$. There is a number $R$, $0 \leq R \leq \infty$, called the \textbf{radius of convergence}, such that the series converges absolutely if $|z - z_0| < R$, and diverges if $|z - z_0| > R$.
