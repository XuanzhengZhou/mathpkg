A multilinear map of $n$ variables is a function that is linear in each variable when the others are held fixed. This generalizes bilinear maps ($n=2$, such as inner products and multiplication in algebras) to arbitrary arity. For topological vector spaces, the continuity of multilinear maps is more subtle than for linear maps: continuity at the origin does not automatically imply global continuity when $n \geq 2$, unlike the linear case. Exploring these continuity conditions is the subject of the subsequent theorem.
