Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$ and let $\tau$ be a topology on $E$. Then $\tau$ is compatible with the vector space structure of $E$ if and only if
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\tau$ is compatible with the additive group structure of $E$,
\item[(ii)] for each $x \in E$, the mapping $\lambda \mapsto \lambda x$ ($\lambda \in \mathbb{K}$) is continuous at $\lambda = 0$,
\item[(iii)] for each $\lambda \in \mathbb{K}$, the mapping $x \mapsto \lambda x$ ($x \in E$) is continuous at $x = \theta$,
\item[(iv)] the mapping $(\lambda, x) \mapsto \lambda x$ ($\lambda \in \mathbb{K}, x \in E$) is continuous at $(0, \theta)$.
\end{enumerate}
