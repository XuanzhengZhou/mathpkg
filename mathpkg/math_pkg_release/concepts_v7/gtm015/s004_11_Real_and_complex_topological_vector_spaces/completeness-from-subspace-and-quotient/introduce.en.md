This theorem provides a converse to the fact that closed subspaces and quotients of complete metrizable TVS are complete. If a metrizable TVS $E$ has a closed subspace $M$ such that both $M$ and the quotient $E/M$ are complete, then $E$ itself must be complete. This is a useful tool for proving completeness of TVS constructed from simpler complete pieces. The result is an application of the corresponding theorem for topological groups.
