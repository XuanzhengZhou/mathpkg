If a set $A$ in a TVS is balanced (satisfying $\lambda A \subset A$ for all $|\lambda| \leq 1$), then its closure $\overline{A}$ is also balanced. The proof is an immediate consequence of continuity: the map $x \mapsto \lambda x$ is continuous, so $\lambda \overline{A} \subset \overline{\lambda A} \subset \overline{A}$. Combined with the existence of balanced neighborhoods, this ensures the existence of closed balanced neighborhoods of the origin, which form a particularly convenient fundamental system of neighborhoods.
