Banach spaces are stable under two fundamental operations: closed subspaces and quotients by closed subspaces. A closed subspace of a Banach space is itself a Banach space (closed subsets of complete metric spaces are complete). The quotient of a Banach space by a closed subspace is also a Banach space with the quotient norm; completeness follows from the same argument used for complete topological groups. These stability properties are essential for the categorical treatment of Banach spaces.
