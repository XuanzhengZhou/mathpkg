This theorem provides a converse to the stability of Banach spaces under closed subspaces and quotients. If a normed space $E$ has a closed subspace $M$ such that both $M$ and the quotient $E/M$ are Banach (complete), then $E$ itself must be a Banach space. This is the Banach space analog of the corresponding theorem for metrizable complete TVS and follows from the same general principle about completeness in topological groups. It is a useful tool for proving completeness of normed spaces constructed from simpler pieces.
