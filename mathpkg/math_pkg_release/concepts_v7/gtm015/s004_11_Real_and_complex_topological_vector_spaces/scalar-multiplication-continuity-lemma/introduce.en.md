This lemma is a crucial technical result for the theory of $F$-spaces. It shows that in a metrizable setting, the seemingly weaker conditions of separate continuity of scalar multiplication (at zero) together with topological group structure are sufficient to guarantee joint continuity, hence the full TVS structure. The proof uses the metrizability to extract a contradiction from a clever diagonal argument involving a compatible invariant metric.
