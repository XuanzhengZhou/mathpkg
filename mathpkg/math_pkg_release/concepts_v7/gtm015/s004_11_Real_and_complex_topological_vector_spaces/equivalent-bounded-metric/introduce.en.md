Every metric $d$ can be replaced by an equivalent bounded metric $D = d/(1+d)$ taking values in $[0,1)$. The two metrics induce the same topology, the same convergent sequences, and the same Cauchy sequences. This construction preserves completeness and is essential for metrizing countable products of metric spaces, since boundedness of the component metrics allows convergence of the weighted sum used in the product metric.
