# Exercise 16.17

Let $I = [a, b]$ be a nondegenerate closed interval of $\mathbb{R}$, and let $\mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{K}}(I)$ be the linear space of all continuous functions $x: I \to \mathbb{K}$ (such functions are bounded, by the compactness of $I$). For $x \in \mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{K}}(I)$ define

$$\|x\|_{1} = \int_{a}^{b} |x(t)| dt.$$

Then $\|x\|_{1}$ is a norm on $\mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{K}}(I)$, but $(\mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{K}}(I), \|x\|_{1})$ is not a Banach space.
