This corollary is a fundamental construction tool: given a linear map $f: E \to F$ from a vector space into a TVS, the coarsest topology on $E$ making $f$ continuous (the initial topology) is automatically a TVS topology. The proof uses the axiomatic characterization of TVS via neighborhood bases: pulling back the closed balanced neighborhoods of $F$ yields a system of subsets of $E$ satisfying the TVS neighborhood axioms. This result underlies the definition of weak topologies, where a family of linear functionals $E \to \mathbb{K}$ is used to generate a TVS topology on $E$.
