# Exercise 10.8

A complete topological group need not be bi-uniform. Indeed, there exists a metrizable complete group $G$ for which no neighborhood of the identity is invariant under all inner automorphisms, hence $G$ is not bi-uniform (10.2). One may take $G$ to be the group of upper triangular $3 \times 3$ real matrices with $1$'s on the diagonal, equipped with the Euclidean metric inherited from $\mathbb{R}^9$.
