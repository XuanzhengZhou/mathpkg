The Wiener algebra $\mathcal{A}$ consists of all continuous, $2\pi$-periodic functions on $\mathbb{R}$ whose Fourier series converges absolutely. Equivalently, it is the image of $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ under the Fourier transform. The algebra is closed under pointwise operations and is isomorphic (as an algebra) to $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ equipped with convolution. The study of $\mathcal{A}$ and its maximal ideal space provides the functional-analytic framework for Wiener's theorem.
