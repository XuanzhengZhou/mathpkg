For each $n \in \mathbb{Z}$, define $e_n \in \ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ by
$$e_n(m) = \delta_{mn} \quad (m \in \mathbb{Z}),$$
where $\delta_{mn} = 1$ if $m = n$ and $\delta_{mn} = 0$ otherwise. The Fourier transform of $e_n$ is
$$\hat{e}_n(t) = e^{int} \quad (t \in \mathbb{R}).$$
The sequence $(e_n)_{n \in \mathbb{Z}}$ forms a Schauder basis for $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$.
