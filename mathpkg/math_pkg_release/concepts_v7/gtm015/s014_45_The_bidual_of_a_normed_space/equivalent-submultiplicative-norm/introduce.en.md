This theorem addresses the question of when a normed algebra with continuous multiplication can be renormed to have a submultiplicative norm. The trick is to embed $A$ into the Banach algebra $\mathcal{B}(A)$ of bounded linear operators on $A$ via left multiplication, and pull back the operator norm. The resulting norm $|x| = \|U_x\|$ is automatically submultiplicative and equivalent to the original norm.
