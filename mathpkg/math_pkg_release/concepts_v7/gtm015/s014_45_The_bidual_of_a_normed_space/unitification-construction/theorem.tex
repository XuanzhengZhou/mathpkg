Suppose $A$ is an associative algebra over $\mathbb{C}$ (with or without a unity element). The Cartesian product $A_1 = A \times \mathbb{C}$ becomes an associative algebra under the operations:
\begin{align*}
(x, \lambda) + (y, \mu) &= (x + y, \lambda + \mu), \\
\mu(x, \lambda) &= (\mu x, \mu \lambda), \\
(x, \lambda)(y, \mu) &= (xy + \mu x + \lambda y, \lambda \mu).
\end{align*}
The element $u = (0, 1)$ is a unity element for $A_1$. Every element of $A_1$ is uniquely representable in the form $x + \lambda u$ with $x \in A$ and $\lambda \in \mathbb{C}$. The algebra $A_1$ is called the \text{unitification} of $A$, and $A$ is a maximal ideal of $A_1$ with $A_1/A \cong \mathbb{C}$.
