A normed algebra is an associative algebra equipped with a submultiplicative norm, meaning the norm of a product is bounded by the product of the norms. When the underlying normed space is complete, it is called a Banach algebra. Banach algebras form the algebraic-topological framework for the spectral theory of operators and are fundamental to modern functional analysis, having been introduced by I. M. Gelfand in 1941.
