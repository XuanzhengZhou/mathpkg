# Exercise 49.9

(i) Let $(B, \|\cdot\|)$ be a normed algebra with unity element $u$. The equivalent norm $|x| = \|u\|^{-1}\|x\|$ is submultiplicative iff $\|u\| \|xy\| \leq \|x\| \|y\|$ for all $x, y$ in $B$.

(ii) Let $(A, \|\cdot\|)$ be a normed algebra without unity, in which not all products are zero. Say $ab \neq 0$. Choose a positive real number $r$ such that $r \|ab\| > \|a\| \|b\|$ (necessarily $r > 1$). Let $A_1$ be the algebra unitification of $A$ (49.4) and define

$$\|x + \lambda u\| = \|x\| + r|\lambda|$$

for all $x \in A$, $\lambda \in \mathbb{C}$. The result is that $A_1$ becomes a normed algebra and $\|u\| = r$. The norm $|x + \lambda u| = \|u\|^{-1}\|x + \lambda u\|$ is not submultiplicative.
