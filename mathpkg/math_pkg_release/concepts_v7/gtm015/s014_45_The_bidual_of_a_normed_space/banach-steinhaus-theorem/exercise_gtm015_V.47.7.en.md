# Exercise 47.7

Let $E$ and $F$ be Banach spaces over $\mathbb{K}$, and suppose $T: E \to F$ and $S: F' \to E'$ are mappings such that $g(Tx) = (Sg)(x)$ for all $x \in E$ and $g \in F'$. Then $S$ and $T$ are continuous linear mappings, and $S = T'$ in the sense of (40.18).
