The Uniform Boundedness Principle, also known as the Banach-Steinhaus Theorem, is one of the three fundamental principles of functional analysis (alongside the Hahn-Banach Theorem and the Open Mapping/Closed Graph Theorems). It states that a pointwise bounded family of continuous linear operators from a Banach space to normed spaces is automatically uniformly bounded in operator norm. The proof applies the Baire category theorem via the lemma on uniform boundedness on an open set.
