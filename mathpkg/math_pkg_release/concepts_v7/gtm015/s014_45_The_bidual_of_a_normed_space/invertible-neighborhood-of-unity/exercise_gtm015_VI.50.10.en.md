# Exercise 50.10

In an associative ring (with product $(x, y) \mapsto xy$) one defines an auxiliary operation
$$x \circ y = x + y - xy,$$
called the circle composition. This operation is associative. An element $x$ is said to be quasi-regular if there exists $y$ such that $x \circ y = y \circ x = 0$; such an element $y$ is called a quasi-inverse of $x$.
(i) In a ring with unity $1$, $x$ is quasi-regular iff $1 - x$ is invertible.
(ii) In a Banach algebra with unity, if $\|x\| < 1$ then $x$ is quasi-regular.
