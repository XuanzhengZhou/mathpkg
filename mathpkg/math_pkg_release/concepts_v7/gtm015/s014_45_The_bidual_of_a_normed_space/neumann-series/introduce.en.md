The Neumann series is the Banach-algebraic analogue of the geometric series. If an element $z$ has norm strictly less than 1, then $1 - z$ is invertible and its inverse is given by the convergent series $\sum z^n$. This is the fundamental tool for studying invertibility in Banach algebras and underlies the proof that the group of invertible elements is open.
