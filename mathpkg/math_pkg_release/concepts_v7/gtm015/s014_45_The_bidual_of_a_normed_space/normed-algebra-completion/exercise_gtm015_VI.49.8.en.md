# Exercise 49.8

A pair $(B, \varphi)$ with the properties in (49.3) is called a completion of $A$. If $(B_1, \varphi_1)$ and $(B_2, \varphi_2)$ are completions of $A$, then there exists an isometric isomorphism $\psi: B_1 \to B_2$ such that $\psi \circ \varphi_1 = \varphi_2$. In this sense, the completion of $A$ is unique.
