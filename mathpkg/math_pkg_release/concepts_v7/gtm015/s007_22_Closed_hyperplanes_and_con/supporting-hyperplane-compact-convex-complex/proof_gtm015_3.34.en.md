# Proof of Complex variant: supporting hyperplanes for compact convex sets

With notation as in the proof of (34.5), let $g$ be the continuous $\mathbb{R}$-form obtained from (34.5), and let $f$ be the $\mathbb{C}$-form with $\operatorname{Re} f = g$ (22.6). Then $\operatorname{Re} f \le \alpha$ on $A$, $\operatorname{Re} f(a) = \alpha$ for at least one $a \in A$, and $\operatorname{Re} f(b) > \alpha$.
