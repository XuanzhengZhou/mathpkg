# Proof of Topological direct sum decomposition by closed maximal subspace

Since $M$ is closed, the quotient TVS $E/M$ is separated (11.8). Also, $M \cap N$ is closed in $N$ (for the relative topology on $N$); since $M \cap N = \{0\}$, this means that $N$ is separated (3.4). Let $\pi: E \rightarrow E/M$ be the canonical mapping, and let $\pi_0$ be the restriction of $\pi$ to $N$. As noted in (20.8), $\pi_0: N \rightarrow E/M$ is a continuous vector space isomorphism. Since $E/M$ is one-dimensional and separated, $\pi_0$ is bicontinuous by (22.1). Therefore $E$ is the topological direct sum of $M$ and $N$ (20.9).
