# Proof of Topological direct sum for closed subspaces of finite codimension

The finite codimensionality of $M$ means, by definition, that $E/M$ is finite-dimensional. Let $\pi: E \rightarrow E/M$ be the canonical mapping and let $\pi_0$ be the restriction of $\pi$ to $N$; thus, $\pi_0: N \rightarrow E/M$ is a continuous vector space isomorphism, and the problem is to show that $\pi_0$ is bicontinuous (20.9). Since $M$ is closed in $E$, $M \cap N = \{\theta\}$ is closed in $N$, therefore $N$ is separated (3.4); since, moreover, $E/M$ is separated (11.8), it follows from (23.2) that $\pi_0$ is bicontinuous.
