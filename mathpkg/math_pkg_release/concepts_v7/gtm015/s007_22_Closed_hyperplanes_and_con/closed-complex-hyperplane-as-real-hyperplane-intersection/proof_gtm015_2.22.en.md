# Proof of Closed complex hyperplane as intersection of closed real hyperplanes

If $E$ is a TVS over $\mathbb{C}$ and $H$ is a closed $\mathbb{C}$-hyperplane in $E$, then there exist closed $\mathbb{R}$-hyperplanes $H_1, H_2$ such that $H = H_1 \cap H_2$.

This follows from (22.8)(b): $H = (x_0 + M_0) \cap (y_0 + iM_0)$. Since $M_0$ is a closed, maximal $\mathbb{R}$-linear subspace, $x_0 + M_0$ and $y_0 + iM_0$ are closed $\mathbb{R}$-hyperplanes (21.8). Their intersection yields $H$.
