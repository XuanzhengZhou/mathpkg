# Proof of Open mapping for surjective continuous linear maps onto finite-dimensional spaces

Since $F$ is separated, $\{\theta\}$ is closed in $F$, therefore $N = u^{-1}(\{\theta\})$ is closed in $E$ by the continuity of $u$; consequently $E/N$ is separated (11.8). Let $\pi: E \rightarrow E/N$ be the canonical mapping and let $\tilde{u}: E/N \rightarrow F$ be the vector space isomorphism induced by $u$ (so that $u = \tilde{u} \circ \pi$). Since $F$ is finite-dimensional separated and $E/N$ has the same dimension (as $u$ is surjective), $\tilde{u}$ is bicontinuous by (23.2). Since $\pi$ is open (11.8), $u = \tilde{u} \circ \pi$ is open as the composition of open mappings. Moreover, $\tilde{u}$ being bicontinuous means that the vector space isomorphism $E/N \rightarrow F$ induced by $u$ is bicontinuous.
