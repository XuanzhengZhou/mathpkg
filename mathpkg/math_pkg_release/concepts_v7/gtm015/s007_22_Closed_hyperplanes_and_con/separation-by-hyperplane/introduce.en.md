A hyperplane separates two sets if they lie in opposite closed half-spaces, and separates them strictly if they lie in opposite open half-spaces. A set lies to one side if it is entirely contained in one closed half-space, and strictly to one side if contained in one open half-space. These definitions formalize the geometric intuition of a hyperplane cutting the space into two regions.
