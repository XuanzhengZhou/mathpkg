# Proof of Existence of tangent hyperplanes for compact sets

Let $E$ be a real TVS and let $A$ be a nonempty compact subset of $E$. If $H$ is any closed hyperplane in $E$, then there exists a hyperplane parallel to $H$ and tangent to $A$.

Write $H = \{x : g(x) = \alpha\}$ with $g$ a continuous linear form (22.5). Then $g(A)$ is a nonempty compact subset of $\mathbb{R}$, therefore it contains a smallest element and a largest element. By Lemma (30.10), there exists a hyperplane parallel to $M = \{x : g(x) = 0\}$ (hence parallel to $H$) that is tangent to $A$. (In fact, the hyperplane corresponding to the smallest or largest value of $g$ on $A$ works.)
