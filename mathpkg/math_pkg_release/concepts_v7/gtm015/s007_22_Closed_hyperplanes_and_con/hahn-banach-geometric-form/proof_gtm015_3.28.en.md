# Proof of Geometric form of the Hahn-Banach theorem

Let $E$ be a TVS over $\mathbb{R}$, $A$ a nonempty open convex set, $M$ a linear variety with $M \cap A = \varnothing$. By translation we may assume $\theta \in A$ (otherwise translate $A$ and $M$). Let $C = \bigcup_{\alpha > 0} \alpha A$ be the convex cone generated by $A$; $C$ is open and convex, with $\theta \notin C$.

Consider the family of all linear subspaces $L$ of $E$ such that $L \supset M$ and $L \cap A = \varnothing$, partially ordered by inclusion. By Zorn's lemma, there exists a maximal such subspace $H$. We claim $H$ is a maximal linear subspace (i.e., of codimension 1).

If not, passing to the quotient $E/H$, we obtain a real TVS $F$ of dimension $\ge 2$, with an open convex set $B$ (image of $A$) such that $\theta \notin B$ and every nonzero subspace of $F$ meets $B$. But Lemma (28.2) says $F - \{\theta\}$ is connected when $\dim F \ge 2$. By (28.3), a nonempty proper open convex set in a connected space must have nonempty boundary, which contradicts the maximality. Hence $\dim F = 1$, so $H$ is a hyperplane.

Since $H \cap A = \varnothing$, $H$ does not meet the open set $A$, so $H$ is closed (as the complement of an open set union of cosets). Thus $H$ is a closed hyperplane containing $M$ and disjoint from $A$.
