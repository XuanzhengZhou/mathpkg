# Proof of Initial topology into locally convex space is locally convex

If $f: E \rightarrow F$ is a linear mapping, where $E$ is a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$ and $F$ is a locally convex TVS over $\mathbb{K}$, then the initial topology for $f$ is locally convex.

The topology in question is compatible with the vector space structure of $E$ (17.13). A basic neighborhood of $\theta$ in $E$ has the form $f^{-1}(V)$, where $V$ is a convex neighborhood of $\theta$ in $F$. Since $f$ is linear, $f^{-1}(V)$ is convex by (25.6)(ii). Thus the neighborhoods of $\theta$ have a base of convex sets, so the topology is locally convex.
