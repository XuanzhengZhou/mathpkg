A salient cone is a pointed convex cone that contains no nontrivial linear subspaces; equivalently, the only vector $x$ for which both $x$ and $-x$ belong to the cone is the zero vector. Salient cones correspond precisely to partial orderings on the vector space via the construction $x \leq y \iff y-x \in A$.
