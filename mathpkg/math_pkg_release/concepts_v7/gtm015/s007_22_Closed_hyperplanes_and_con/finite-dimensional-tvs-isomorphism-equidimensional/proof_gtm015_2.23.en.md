# Proof of Bicontinuity of isomorphisms between equidimensional finite-dimensional TVS

Let $u: E \rightarrow F$ be a vector space isomorphism. Let $r = \dim E = \dim F$. Choose vector space isomorphisms $\varphi: \mathbb{K}^r \rightarrow E$ and $\psi: \mathbb{K}^r \rightarrow F$. By Tihonov's theorem (23.1), both $\varphi$ and $\psi$ are bicontinuous. Then $u = \psi \circ \varphi^{-1}$ is the composition of two bicontinuous maps, hence $u$ is bicontinuous.
