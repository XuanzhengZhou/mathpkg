On a complex topological vector space, every continuous complex-linear form corresponds uniquely to a continuous real-linear form via the real-part operation. The inverse mapping reconstructs the complex form from its real part using the formula involving multiplication by the imaginary unit. This bijection respects continuity: a complex form is continuous if and only if its real part is continuous. The correspondence is fundamental for extending results from the real to the complex case, particularly in duality theory and the Hahn-Banach theorem.
