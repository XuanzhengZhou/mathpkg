# Exercise 25.28

Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$. A subset $A$ of $E$ is said to be \emph{absolutely convex} if $\lambda A + \mu A \subset A$ whenever $|\lambda| + |\mu| \le 1$. The following conditions on a set $A$ are equivalent: (a) $A$ is absolutely convex; (b) $A$ is balanced and convex; (c) $A$ is circled (25.27) and convex. If $S$ is any subset of $E$, the absolutely convex hull of $S$ is defined in the obvious way. Prove: If $S$ is any subset of $E$, then the absolutely convex hull of $S$ coincides with the convex hull of the balanced hull of $S$.
