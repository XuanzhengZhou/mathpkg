# Proof of Uniqueness of separated compatible topology on finite-dimensional spaces

Apply (23.2) to the identity mapping $(E, \tau_1) \rightarrow (E, \tau_2)$. Since $\tau_1$ and $\tau_2$ are both separated and compatible with the vector space structure, and $\dim E$ is finite, the identity mapping is a vector space isomorphism between finite-dimensional separated TVS of the same dimension, hence it is bicontinuous by (23.2). Therefore $\tau_1 = \tau_2$.
