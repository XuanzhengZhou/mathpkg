In a locally convex space, every closed linear variety is precisely the intersection of the family of closed hyperplanes that contain it. This is the topological analogue of the algebraic fact that every linear variety is the intersection of (algebraic) hyperplanes. The proof uses the geometric Hahn-Banach theorem and the existence of convex open neighborhoods.
