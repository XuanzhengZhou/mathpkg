In any topological vector space, the closure of a convex set remains convex. The proof uses the continuity of the mapping $(x, y) \mapsto \alpha x + (1-\alpha)y$ and the fact that $\overline{A} \times \overline{A} = \overline{A \times A}$. This ensures that taking closures preserves the convexity property.
