An important consequence of the geometric Hahn-Banach theorem: if an open convex set is disjoint from a linear subspace, there exists a continuous linear form that vanishes on the subspace and is strictly positive on the convex set. The sign of the linear form can be flipped if needed, giving either strict positivity or strict negativity on the convex set.
