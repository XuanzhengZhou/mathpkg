A hyperplane divides the space into two closed half-spaces (non-strict inequalities) and two open half-spaces (strict inequalities). All half-spaces are convex because they are inverse images of intervals under a linear map. When the defining linear form is continuous, the topological and algebraic descriptions coincide: closed half-spaces are topologically closed and open half-spaces are topologically open.
