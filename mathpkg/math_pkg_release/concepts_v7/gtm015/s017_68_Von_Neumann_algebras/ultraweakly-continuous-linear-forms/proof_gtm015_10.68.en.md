# Proof of Characterization of ultraweakly continuous linear forms on L(H)

Proof. By definition (68.14), the ultraweak topology on $\mathcal{L}(H)$ is the topology $\sigma(\mathcal{L}(H), \mathcal{E})$ with respect to the bilinear form $B(T, S) = \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} (T x_i | y_i)$, where $\mathcal{E}$ is the set of all linear forms

$$f(T) = \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} (T x_i | y_i)$$

with $\sum_i \|x_i\|^2 < \infty$ and $\sum_i \|y_i\|^2 < \infty$. From (38.7), the continuous linear forms for the topology $\sigma(\mathcal{L}(H), \mathcal{E})$ are precisely the linear forms belonging to $\mathcal{E}$. Thus a linear form $f$ on $\mathcal{L}(H)$ is ultraweakly continuous iff it can be expressed as $f(T) = \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} (T x_i | y_i)$ with the stated summability conditions.

In the notation of (68.10), the formula may be written $f(T) = \omega_{x,y}(\tilde{T})$ where $x = (x_i), y = (y_i) \in K$. This identification makes the characterization transparent from the duality theory of locally convex spaces (38.6)-(38.7).
