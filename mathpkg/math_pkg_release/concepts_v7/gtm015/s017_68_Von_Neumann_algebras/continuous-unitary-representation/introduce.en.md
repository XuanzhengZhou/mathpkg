A continuous unitary representation of a locally compact group $G$ is a strongly continuous homomorphism from $G$ into the unitary operators on a Hilbert space. Irreducibility means the commutant consists only of scalar multiples of the identity. Such representations form the central objects of unitary representation theory, connected via the Gel'fand-Raikov theorem to the $*$-representations of the convolution algebra $L^1(G)$.
