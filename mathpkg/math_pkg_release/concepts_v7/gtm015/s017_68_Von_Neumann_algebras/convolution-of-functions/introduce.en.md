The convolution of two functions on a locally compact group generalizes the classical convolution on $\mathbb{R}^n$. When one function is integrable ($L^1$) and the other is $p$-integrable ($L^p$), the convolution exists almost everywhere and defines an $L^p$ function. Convolution gives $L^1(G)$ the structure of a Banach algebra.
