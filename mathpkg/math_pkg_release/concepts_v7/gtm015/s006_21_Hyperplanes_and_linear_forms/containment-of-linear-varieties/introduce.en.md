This lemma characterizes when one linear variety is contained in another. A translate $x + M$ contains $y + N$ precisely when $M$ contains $N$ and the offset difference $x - y$ lies in $M$. Equality holds exactly when the underlying subspaces coincide and the translating vectors differ by an element of that subspace. This criterion is used throughout the theory of hyperplanes to relate containments of varieties to containments of their underlying subspaces.
