A maximal linear subspace is a proper linear subspace that has no proper linear subspace strictly containing it other than the whole space. This is the natural order-theoretic notion of maximality applied to the lattice of linear subspaces of a vector space $E$. Maximal subspaces are precisely the null spaces of nonzero linear forms; they serve as the algebraic foundation for the theory of hyperplanes in both real and complex topological vector spaces.
