A duality (or dual pairing) between two vector spaces $E$ and $F$ is a nondegenerate bilinear form $B: E \times F \to \mathbb{K}$. Nondegeneracy means that the induced linear maps $x \mapsto B(x, \cdot)$ from $E$ into the algebraic dual of $F$, and $y \mapsto B(\cdot, y)$ from $F$ into the algebraic dual of $E$, are both injective. Dualities provide the general framework for weak topologies, polar topologies, and the theory of dual pairs in functional analysis.
