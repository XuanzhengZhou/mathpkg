This definition introduces a natural preorder $\prec$ on seminorms: $p \prec q$ means that $p$ is bounded by a constant multiple of $q$, which is exactly the condition for $\tau_p \subset \tau_q$. The induced equivalence relation $p \sim q$ means mutual boundedness, which corresponds exactly to the equality of the generated locally convex topologies $\tau_p = \tau_q$. For norms, this is the familiar notion of equivalent norms.
