The saturation $\mathcal{F}^*$ of a family of seminorms $\mathcal{F}$ is the set of all seminorms that are continuous for the locally convex topology $\tau(\mathcal{F})$ generated by $\mathcal{F}$. This lemma provides a concrete algebraic description: a seminorm is in the saturation exactly when it is dominated by a finite sum of seminorms from $\mathcal{F}$. This characterization is crucial for understanding which seminorms are continuous in a given locally convex topology and for proving the bijective correspondence between saturated families and locally convex topologies.
