This lemma shows that a seminorm is uniquely determined by its closed unit ball $\{x : p(x) \leq 1\}$. The proof uses a scaling argument: for any $x \in E$ and $\varepsilon > 0$, consider $y = (p(x) + \varepsilon)^{-1}x$ to obtain $q(x) \leq p(x) + \varepsilon$, and symmetry yields the reverse inequality. This uniqueness property is crucial for the Minkowski functional construction, which provides a bijection between balanced convex bodies and certain seminorms.
