The Minkowski functional (also called the gauge functional) establishes a fundamental correspondence between geometry and analysis in topological vector spaces. Given any balanced, closed, convex set $B$ with nonempty interior (a balanced convex body), the formula $p(x) = \inf\{\alpha > 0 : x \in \alpha B\}$ defines a unique seminorm whose closed unit ball is precisely $B$. This theorem is the cornerstone of the seminorm approach to locally convex spaces: showing that the neighborhood definition of local convexity and the seminorm definition are equivalent.
