# Exercise 2

(i) Let $(A_i)_{i \in I}$ be a family of $C^*$-algebras, let $A$ be the set of all families $a = (a_i)_{i \in I}$, with $a_i \in A_i$ for all $i \in I$, such that $\sup_{i \in I} \|a_i\| < \infty$. Equipped with the coordinatewise $*$-algebra operations and the norm $\|a\| = \sup_{i \in I} \|a_i\|$, $A$ is a $C^*$-algebra, called the $C^*$-sum of the family. Notation: $A = \bigoplus_{i \in I} A_i$.

(ii) If $(A_i)_{i \in I}$ and $(B_i)_{i \in I}$ are families of $C^*$-algebras and if, for each $i \in I$, $\varphi_i: A_i \to B_i$ is a $*$-homomorphism, then the formula $\varphi((a_i)) = (\varphi_i(a_i))$ defines a $*$-homomorphism $\varphi: \bigoplus_{i \in I} A_i \to \bigoplus_{i \in I} B_i$.

(iii) If $(H_i)_{i \in I}$ is a family of Hilbert spaces, then there exists a natural faithful $*$-representation $\bigoplus_{i \in I} \mathcal{L}(H_i) \to \mathcal{L}(\bigoplus_{i \in I} H_i)$.
