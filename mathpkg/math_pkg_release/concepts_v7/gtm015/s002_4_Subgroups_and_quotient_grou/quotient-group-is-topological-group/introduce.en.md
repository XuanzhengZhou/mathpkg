Given a topological group G and a normal subgroup N, the quotient group G/N inherits a natural topology via the quotient construction. The key insight is that the images of identity neighborhoods under the canonical projection satisfy the axioms for a fundamental system of neighborhoods of the identity, making G/N a topological group. The theorem also provides a precise separation criterion: the quotient is Hausdorff (separated) exactly when the normal subgroup N is closed in the original topology of G.
