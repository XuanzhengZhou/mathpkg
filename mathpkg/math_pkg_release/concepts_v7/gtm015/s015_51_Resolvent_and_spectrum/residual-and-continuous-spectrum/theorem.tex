The residual spectrum of $T$, denoted $\sigma_r(T)$, is
$$\sigma_r(T) = \sigma_{\text{com}}(T) - \sigma_p(T).$$
Thus $\lambda \in \sigma_r(T)$ iff $T - \lambda I$ is injective but its range is not dense in $E$.

The continuous spectrum of $T$, denoted $\sigma_c(T)$, is
$$\sigma_c(T) = \sigma(T) - \{\sigma_p(T) \cup \sigma_{\text{com}}(T)\}.$$
Thus $\lambda \in \sigma_c(T)$ iff $T - \lambda I$ is injective, has dense range, but is singular.
