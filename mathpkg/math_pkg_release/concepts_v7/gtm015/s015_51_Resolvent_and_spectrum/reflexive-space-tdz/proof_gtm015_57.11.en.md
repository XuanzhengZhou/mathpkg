# Proof of TDZ in reflexive Banach space

(57.11) Corollary. Let $E$ be a reflexive Banach space and let $T \in \mathcal{L}(E)$. If $T$ is singular but is not a divisor of zero in $\mathcal{L}(E)$, then $T$ is both a left TDZ and a right TDZ.

Proof. By (57.9), $T$ is a left TDZ in $\mathcal{L}(E)$. Since $E$ is reflexive, $T'$ satisfies the same hypotheses relative to $\mathcal{L}(E')$, therefore $T'$ is a left TDZ in $\mathcal{L}(E')$—whence $T$ is a right TDZ in $\mathcal{L}(E)$ (by either (57.8) or reflexivity).
