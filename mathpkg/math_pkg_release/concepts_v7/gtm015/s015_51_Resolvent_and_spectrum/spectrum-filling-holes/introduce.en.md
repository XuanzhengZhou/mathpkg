When enlarging a Banach algebra, the spectrum shrinks by "filling in" certain holes (bounded components of the resolvent set). The unbounded component of the resolvent set remains unchanged. This geometric description complements the algebraic fact that the boundary of the larger spectrum is contained in the boundary of the smaller one. The spectral radius, being the outer radius, is unchanged.
