# Proof of Injective dense range implies TDZ

(57.12) Corollary. Let $E$ be a reflexive Banach space and let $T \in \mathcal{L}(E)$. If $T$ is injective and $T(E)$ is a dense, proper subspace of $E$, then $T$ is both a left TDZ and a right TDZ.

Proof. $T$ is not a divisor or zero in $\mathcal{L}(E)$ (57.1) but is obviously singular; quote (57.11).
