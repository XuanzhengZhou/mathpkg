# Proof of Gelfand-Mazur Theorem

(51.8) Theorem. (Gel’fand-Mazur theorem) If $A$ is a division ring then it is one-dimensional over $\mathbb{C}$, and $A = \mathbb{C}$ in the sense that the mapping $\lambda \mapsto \lambda 1$ is an isometric algebra isomorphism of $\mathbb{C}$ onto $A$.
