The boundary of the set of singular elements consists entirely of two-sided topological divisors of zero. The proof approximates a boundary point $x \in \partial S$ by invertible elements $x_n \to x$, and then shows that $z_n = x_n^{-1}/\|x_n^{-1}\|$ satisfies $x z_n \to 0$ and $z_n x \to 0$. This lemma is fundamental for understanding how spectra behave under changes of algebra.
