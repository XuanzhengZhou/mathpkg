This geometric form of the Hahn-Banach theorem guarantees that for every nonzero vector in a normed space, there exists a continuous linear functional of norm one that achieves the norm of the vector. It shows that the dual space is rich enough to "see" the norm of every vector, establishing the duality between E and E'.
