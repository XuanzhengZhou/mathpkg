For linear mappings between normed spaces, continuity is equivalent to boundedness: the image of the unit ball (or unit sphere) is bounded. The suprema of the norms on the unit sphere, open unit ball, and closed unit ball all coincide, yielding the minimal Lipschitz constant for the operator. This theorem bridges the topological and geometric structures of normed spaces.
