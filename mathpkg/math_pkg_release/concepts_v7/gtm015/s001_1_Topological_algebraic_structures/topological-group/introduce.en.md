A topological group is a group endowed with a topology that makes both the group operation and the inversion map continuous. This is the central concept of Chapter 1 of the book and a fundamental object in functional analysis. The continuity of inversion distinguishes topological groups from topological monoids and enables the rich theory of homogeneous spaces, since left and right translations become homeomorphisms.
