Let $K^\star$ be a finite normal extension of $K$ and let $\mathcal{P}$ be a place of $K$. If $\mathcal{P}^\star$ and $\mathcal{P}'^\star$ are extensions of $\mathcal{P}$ in $K^\star$, then $\mathcal{P}'^\star$ is isomorphic to a conjugate of $\mathcal{P}^\star$.

Let $\mathcal{P}^\star$ and $\mathcal{P}'^\star$ be the centers of $\mathcal{P}^\star$ and $\mathcal{P}'^\star$ in the ring $K^\star$. Since $K^\star$ is integral over $K_\mathcal{P}$ and since $\mathcal{P}^\star$ and $\mathcal{P}'^\star$ both lie over the ideal $\mathfrak{M}_\mathcal{P}$ in $K_\mathcal{P}$, it follows by V, § 9, Theorem 22, that $\mathcal{P}^\star$ and $\mathcal{P}'^\star$ are conjugate prime ideals over $K$. Consequently some conjugate $\mathcal{P}_1^\star$ of the place $\mathcal{P}^\star$ will have center $\mathcal{P}'^\star$ in $K^\star$, and hence $\mathcal{P}_1^\star$ and $\mathcal{P}'^\star$ are isomorphic since, by Theorem 12, these two places have the same valuation ring.
