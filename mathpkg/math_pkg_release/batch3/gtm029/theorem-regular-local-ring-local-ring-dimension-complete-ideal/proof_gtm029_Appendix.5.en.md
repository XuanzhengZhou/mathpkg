We shall use induction with respect to the numerical character $w(A)$ introduced earlier in this appendix, for if $w(A) = 1$ then necessarily $A = m, \dagger$ and $m$ is obviously a simple ideal. The induction is based on a lemma which we shall state immediately after the following observation.

If $P$ is a simple contracted ideal (in the sense defined at the end of the proof of Proposition 4), primary for $m$, then it follows from the factorization theorem for contracted ideals (Theorem 1) that $c(P)$ is a power of an irreducible form $\bar{g}$ in $k[z_1, z_2]$ (a positive power of $\bar{g}$, unless $P = m$). Let $o'$ be the quadratic transform of $o$, relative to the directional form $\bar{g}$, and let $P'$ be the $o$-transform of $P$. We shall refer to $P'$ as the transform of $P$ (if $P = m$, then $P' = o'$).
