If, in addition to the assumptions made in Theorem 5, we also assume that the ideal $m$ admits a finite basis, then the closures of $m^n E$ in $\hat{E}$ and of $m^n$ in $\hat{A}$ are $\hat{A} m^n E = (\hat{A} m)^n E$ and $\hat{A} m^n = (\hat{A} m)^n$ respectively. We have $m^n E = (\hat{A} m)^n E \cap E$ and $m^n = (\hat{A} m)^n \cap A$. The topologies of $\hat{E}$ and $\hat{A}$ considered as completions of $E$ and $A$ are their $(\hat{A} m)$-topologies.

In fact, since $m^s m^n E = m^{n+s} E \cap m^n E$ for every $s$, the $m$-topology of $m^n E$ is induced by the $m$-topology of $E$. Thus the closure of $m^n E$ in $\hat{E}$ may be identified, as a topological $\hat{A}$-module, with the $m$-adic completion of $m^n E$. Since our hypotheses imply that $m^n E$ is a finite $A$-module, Theorem 5 shows that this completion is $\hat{A} m^n E = (\hat{A} m)^n E$. In particular, the closure of $m^n$ in $\hat{A}$ is $(\hat{A} m)^n$. Taking into account the fact that the module $m^n E$ is closed in $E$ (as it is an open submodule), the second part of the corollary follows from the well-known topological fact that, given a metric space $S$ and a subset $T$ of $S$, the intersection of $S$ and of the closure of $T$ in $\hat{S}$ is the closure of $T$ in $S$.
