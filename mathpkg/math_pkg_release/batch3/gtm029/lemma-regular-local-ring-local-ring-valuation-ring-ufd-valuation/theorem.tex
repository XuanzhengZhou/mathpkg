A regular local ring $A$ of dimension 1 or 2 is a UFD.

Let $a$ and $b$ be any two elements of $A$. Since $Aa + Ab$ is a submodule of a free module, we have $\delta(Aa + Ab) \leq \dim(A) - 1$ by the theorem on syzygies (VII, § 13, Theorem 43). Hence $\delta(Aa + Ab) \leq 1$, and we use Lemma 1.

Notice that, if $\dim(A) = 1$ (or 0), $A$ is a discrete valuation ring (or a field), and that the unique factorization properly is obvious in this case.
