Let (29) be a decomposition of a complete ideal $A$ into valuation ideals $q_i$ and let $v_i$ be a valuation (non-negative on $0$) such that $q_i$ is a $v_i$-ideal (we do not assume that the $v_i$ are discrete). Assume that $A$ is primary for $m$ and that the decomposition (29) satisfies conditions (30). Let $h$ be an arbitrary integer, let $r$ be the order of $A$ and let $\tilde{q}_i$ be the $v_i$-ideal determined by the condition: $v_i(\tilde{q}_i) = v_i(m^h A)$. Then

$$m^h A = \tilde{q}_1 \cap \tilde{q}_2 \cap \cdots \cap \tilde{q}_n \cap m^{r+h}.$$

Proof. Denote by $D$ the ideal on the right-hand side of (32). Since $m^h A$ has order $r+h$ and since $m^h A \subset D \subset m^{r+h}$, also $D$ has order $r+h$. Therefore $c(D)$ divides $c(A)$, and the difference between the order $r+h$ of $D$ and the degree of $c(D)$ is at least equal to $h$. Therefore, $m^h$ factors out from $D$ (Theorem 1). Let $D = m^h E$. Since $m^h A \subset D \subset \tilde{q}_i$ and $v_i(m^h A) = v_i(\tilde{q}_i)$, it follows that $v_i(D) = v_i(\tilde{q}_i)$. Thus $v_i(m^h) + v_i(E) = v_i(D) = v_i(m^h A) = v_i(m^h) + v_i(A)$, i.e., $v_i(E) = v_i(A)$, and $E \subset q_i$. Consequently $E \subset A$ and $D \subset m^h A$, showing that $D = m^h A$. Q.E.D.
