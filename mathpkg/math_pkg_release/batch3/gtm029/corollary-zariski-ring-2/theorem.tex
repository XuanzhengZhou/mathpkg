Let $A$ be a Zariski ring, $E$ a finite $A$-module, $E'$ a submodule of $E$ and $z$ an element of $E$. Then $\hat{A}(E':Az) = \hat{A}E':Az$.

We recall that $E':Az$ is the ideal of all elements $a$ in $A$ such that $az \in E'$. We apply Corollary 3 to the case $E=A$, $F=E$, $F'=E'$, and take for $f$ the mapping $a \rightarrow az$.

In particular, if $b$ is an ideal in $A$ and $a$ an element of $A$, we have $\hat{A}(b:Aa) = \hat{A}b:Aa$.
