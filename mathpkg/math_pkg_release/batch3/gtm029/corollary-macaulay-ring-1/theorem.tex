Let $A$ be a Macaulay ring. For a finite subset $S$ of $A$ to be a prime sequence, it is necessary and sufficient that it be a subset of some system of parameters.

In fact, if $S$ is a prime sequence, it is contained in a maximal prime sequence, i.e., in a system of parameters. The converse follows from Corollary 1 ((a) implies (c)), since any subset of a prime sequence is a prime sequence (Lemma 2).
