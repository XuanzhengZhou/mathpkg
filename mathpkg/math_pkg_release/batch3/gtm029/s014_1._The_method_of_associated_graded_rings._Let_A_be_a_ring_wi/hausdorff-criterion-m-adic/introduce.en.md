A topological module (and in particular a topological ring) is Hausdorff if and only if the intersection of all basic neighborhoods of zero is just $\{0\}$. For the $\mathfrak{m}$-adic topology, this means the intersection of all powers $\mathfrak{m}^n E$ must be the zero submodule. This criterion is used throughout the theory of completions.
