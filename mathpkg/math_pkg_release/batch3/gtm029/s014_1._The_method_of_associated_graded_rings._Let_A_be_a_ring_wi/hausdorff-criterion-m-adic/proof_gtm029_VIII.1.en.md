If $x \neq 0$, then by hypothesis $x \notin \bigcap \Sigma(E)$, so there exists $U \in \Sigma(E)$ with $x \notin U$. Then $U$ and $x+U$ are disjoint neighborhoods of $0$ and $x$, proving the space is Hausdorff. Conversely, if the space is Hausdorff and $x \neq 0$, there exist disjoint neighborhoods $U$ of $0$ and $V$ of $x$, implying $x \notin U$ and hence $x \notin \bigcap \Sigma(E)$, so $\bigcap \Sigma(E) = \{0\}$.
