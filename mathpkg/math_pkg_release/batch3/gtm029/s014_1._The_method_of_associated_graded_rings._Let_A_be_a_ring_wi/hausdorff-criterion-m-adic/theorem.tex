Let $E$ be a topological $A$-module with a basis $\Sigma(E)$ of neighborhoods of zero. Then $E$ is a Hausdorff space if and only if the intersection of all sets of the system $\Sigma(E)$ consists only of the zero of $E$. In particular, with respect to the $\mathfrak{m}$-topology, $E$ is Hausdorff if and only if

$$\bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} \mathfrak{m}^n E = (0).$$
