Let $A$ be a noetherian ring and $\mathfrak{m}$ an ideal in $A$. The following conditions are equivalent:

(a) $\mathfrak{m}$ is contained in the Jacobson radical of $A$.

(b) Every finite $A$-module is a Hausdorff space in its $\mathfrak{m}$-topology.

(c) For every ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ of $A$, $\bigcap_{n=0}^{\infty} (\mathfrak{m}^n + \mathfrak{a}) = \mathfrak{a}$.

(d) Every maximal ideal of $A$ contains $\mathfrak{m}$.

(e) Every element of $1 + \mathfrak{m}$ is a unit in $A$.

(f) If $E$ is a finite $A$-module such that $E = \mathfrak{m}E$, then $E = (0)$.

A noetherian ring $A$ with an ideal $\mathfrak{m}$ satisfying these equivalent conditions is called a Zariski ring.
