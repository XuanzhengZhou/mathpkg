A Zariski ring is a noetherian ring with an ideal $\mathfrak{m}$ satisfying several equivalent conditions: $\mathfrak{m}$ is contained in the Jacobson radical, every element of $1+\mathfrak{m}$ is a unit, every finite module is Hausdorff in the $\mathfrak{m}$-adic topology, and Nakayama's lemma holds in the strong form. Zariski rings provide the natural setting for the theory of completions in commutative algebra.
