The key is that the topology induced on $F$ by the $\mathfrak{m}$-topology of $E$ coincides with the $\mathfrak{m}$-topology of $F$ (Theorem 4), and similarly the quotient topology on $E/F \cong G$ coincides with the $\mathfrak{m}$-topology of $G$. Since completions preserve exactness of sequences where the submodule carries the induced topology and the quotient carries the quotient topology, the completed sequence remains exact. The surjectivity of $\hat{g}$ follows from the fact that the image of $\hat{g}$ is closed (as $\hat{E}/\hat{F}$ is complete) and dense (since $g$ is surjective).
