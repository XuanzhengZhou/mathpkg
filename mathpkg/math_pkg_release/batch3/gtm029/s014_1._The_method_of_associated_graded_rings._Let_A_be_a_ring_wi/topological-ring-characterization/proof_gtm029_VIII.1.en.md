Given $\Sigma(A)$ satisfying (a)-(c), define the topology by declaring that $U \subset A$ is a neighborhood of $a \in A$ if and only if $a \in U$ and there exists $W \in \Sigma(A)$ such that $W + a \subset U$. Conditions (a)-(c) guarantee that the ring operations are continuous in this topology. Uniqueness follows from the fact that a neighborhood basis determines the topology completely.
