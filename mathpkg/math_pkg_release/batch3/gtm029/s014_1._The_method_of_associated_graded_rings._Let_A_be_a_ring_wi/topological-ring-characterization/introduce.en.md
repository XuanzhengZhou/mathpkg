A system of subsets of a ring $A$ satisfying three natural conditions -- closure under intersection, existence of additive and multiplicative "square roots" for neighborhoods, and translation/scaling properties -- uniquely determines a topology making $A$ into a topological ring. This axiomatic characterization underlies the construction of the $\mathfrak{m}$-adic topology.
