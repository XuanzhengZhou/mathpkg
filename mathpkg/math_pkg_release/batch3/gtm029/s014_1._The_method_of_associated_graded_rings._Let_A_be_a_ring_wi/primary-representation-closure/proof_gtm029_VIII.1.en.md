Each $F_j$ with $\mathfrak{p}_j + \mathfrak{m} \neq A$ is closed by Theorem 8, and hence the intersection $\bigcap_j F_j$ is closed. It remains to prove $F$ is dense in $\bigcap_j F_j$. For any $x \in \bigcap_j F_j$ and any $n$, for each $\nu$ with $\mathfrak{p}_\nu + \mathfrak{m} = A$, choose $p_{\nu n} \in \mathfrak{p}_\nu^{s(\nu)}$ and $m_{\nu n} \in \mathfrak{m}^n$ with $p_{\nu n} + m_{\nu n} = 1$. The element $y = (\prod_\nu p_{\nu n})x$ lies in every $F_\nu$, hence in $F$, and $y \equiv x$ modulo $\mathfrak{m}^n$. Thus $x$ is in the closure of $F$.
