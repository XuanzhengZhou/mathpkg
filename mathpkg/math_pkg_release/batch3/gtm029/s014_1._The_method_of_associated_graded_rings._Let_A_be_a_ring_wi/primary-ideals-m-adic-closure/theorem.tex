Let $A$ be a noetherian ring, $\mathfrak{m}$ an ideal in $A$, and $\mathfrak{q}$ a primary ideal in $A$ with radical $\mathfrak{p}$. Then $\mathfrak{q}$ is closed in the $\mathfrak{m}$-topology if and only if $\mathfrak{p} + \mathfrak{m} \neq A$, i.e., if and only if no element of $\mathfrak{p}$ is congruent to $1$ modulo $\mathfrak{m}$.
