In the $\mathfrak{m}$-adic topology, a sequence is Cauchy if the differences $x_n - x_{n+i}$ eventually lie in arbitrarily high powers of $\mathfrak{m}$. Thanks to the strong triangle inequality, it suffices to check that consecutive differences tend to zero. Limits are defined via null sequences, though uniqueness of limits holds only in the Hausdorff case.
