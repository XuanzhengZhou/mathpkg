A sequence $\{x_n\}$ in $E$ is a Cauchy sequence if $x_n - x_{n+i} \in \mathfrak{m}^{N(n)} E$ for all $i \geq 0$, where $N(n) \to +\infty$ as $n \to +\infty$. In view of the strong triangle inequality, $\{x_n\}$ is a Cauchy sequence if and only if $d(x_n, x_{n+1}) \to 0$. A null sequence $\{x_n\}$ is one for which $d(x_n, 0) \to 0$. A limit of a sequence $\{x_n\}$ is any element $y$ of $E$ such that $\{x_n - y\}$ is a null sequence.
