Include $F$ in an exact sequence $0 \to F \to E \to E/F \to 0$. The submodules $\mathfrak{m}^n E \cap F$ form a basis of neighborhoods of zero for the induced topology on $F$, while the submodules $\mathfrak{m}^n F$ form a basis for the $\mathfrak{m}$-topology of $F$. The Artin-Rees Lemma (Theorem 4') provides an integer $k$ such that $\mathfrak{m}^n E \cap F = \mathfrak{m}^{n-k}(\mathfrak{m}^k E \cap F)$ for all $n \geq k$. Since $\mathfrak{m}^k E \cap F \subset F$, we obtain $\mathfrak{m}^n E \cap F \subset \mathfrak{m}^{n-k}F$. Conversely, $\mathfrak{m}^n F \subset \mathfrak{m}^n E \cap F$ holds trivially. Hence the two filtrations are equivalent, defining the same topology.
