Let $A$ be a Zariski ring with respect to $\mathfrak{m}$. The multiplicatively closed set $S = 1 + \mathfrak{m}$ has the property that its image in $\hat{A}$ consists of units. The kernel of the canonical homomorphism $\varphi: A \to A_S$ is precisely

$$\ker \varphi = \bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} \mathfrak{m}^n,$$

the closure of $(0)$ in $A$. Thus $A_S$ may be identified with a subring of the completion $\hat{A}$. If $A$ is Hausdorff ($\bigcap \mathfrak{m}^n = (0)$), then $A$ is a subring of $A_S$, and $A_S$ is a subring of $\hat{A}$.
