Introduce an indeterminate $X$ and consider the set $A' = \{\sum_i m_i X^i : m_i \in \mathfrak{m}^i\}$, which is a subring of $A[X]$. If $\{a_1, \ldots, a_q\}$ generates $\mathfrak{m}$, then $A' = A[a_1 X, \ldots, a_q X]$ is noetherian (by Hilbert basis theorem). Consider $E' = \{\sum_j z_j X^j : z_j \in \mathfrak{m}^j E\}$, which is an $A'$-module. Let $F' \subset E'$ consist of sums $\sum z_j X^j$ with $z_j \in \mathfrak{m}^j E \cap F$. Then $F'$ is an $A'$-submodule of $E'$.

Now, $E'$ is a finite $A'$-module (generated by the generators of $E$ over $A$). Since $A'$ is noetherian, $F'$ is a finite $A'$-module. Let $\{f_1, \ldots, f_r\}$ be generators of $F'$. Choose $k$ such that each $f_i$ has degree at most $k$ in $X$. Then for any element of $F_n = \mathfrak{m}^n E \cap F$, its corresponding monomial $z X^n$ in $F'$ can be expressed as an $A'$-linear combination of the generators. Comparing coefficients yields the Artin-Rees relation $\mathfrak{m}^n E \cap F = \mathfrak{m}^{n-k}(\mathfrak{m}^k E \cap F)$.
