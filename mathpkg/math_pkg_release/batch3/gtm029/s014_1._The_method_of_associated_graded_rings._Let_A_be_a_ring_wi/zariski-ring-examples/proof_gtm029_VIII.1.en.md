(1) A noetherian local ring $(A,\mathfrak{m})$: by condition (d), $\mathfrak{m}$ is the unique maximal ideal, so it contains itself. Thus condition (d) is satisfied.

(2) A noetherian semi-local ring $A$ with maximal ideals $\mathfrak{m}_i$: the intersection $\mathfrak{m} = \bigcap_i \mathfrak{m}_i$ is contained in each maximal ideal, so condition (d) holds.

(3) If $A$ is noetherian and complete Hausdorff, every $1-m \in 1+\mathfrak{m}$ has inverse $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} m^n$, which converges by completeness. Thus condition (e) holds.

(4) The factor ring of a Zariski ring inherits the Zariski property since the image of $\mathfrak{m}$ in $A/\mathfrak{a}$ is contained in the Jacobson radical of $A/\mathfrak{a}$.
