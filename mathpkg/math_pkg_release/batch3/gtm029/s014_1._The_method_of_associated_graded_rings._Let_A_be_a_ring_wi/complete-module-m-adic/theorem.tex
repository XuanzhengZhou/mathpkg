The module $E$ is complete (for the $\mathfrak{m}$-topology) if every Cauchy sequence in $E$ converges in $E$ (i.e., has a limit in $E$). In view of the strong triangle inequality, if $E$ is complete then the convergent series $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} z_n$ are precisely those whose general term $z_n$ tends to zero.
