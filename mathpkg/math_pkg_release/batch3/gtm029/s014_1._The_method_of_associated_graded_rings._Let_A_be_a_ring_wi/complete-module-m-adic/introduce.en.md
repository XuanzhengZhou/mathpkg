A module is complete with respect to the $\mathfrak{m}$-adic topology if every Cauchy sequence converges. A key consequence of the ultrametric nature of the distance is that a series converges if and only if its general term tends to zero, a property analogous to that of $p$-adic numbers and power series rings.
