Let $\mathfrak{m}$ be an ideal in a noetherian ring $A$, such that $A$ is a Hausdorff space in its $\mathfrak{m}$-topology (i.e., $\bigcap_{n=0}^{\infty} \mathfrak{m}^n = (0)$). Let $\mathfrak{a}$ be a closed ideal in $A$, let

$$\hat{A}\mathfrak{a} = \mathfrak{q}_1^* \cap \mathfrak{q}_2^* \cap \cdots \cap \mathfrak{q}_n^*$$

be an irredundant primary representation of $\hat{A}\mathfrak{a}$, and let $\mathfrak{p}_i^*$ be the prime ideal which is the radical of $\mathfrak{q}_i^*$. Then

$$\mathfrak{a} = \bigcap_i (\mathfrak{q}_i^* \cap A)$$

is a primary representation of $\mathfrak{a}$, and $\mathfrak{p}_i^* \cap A$ is the associated prime ideal of $\mathfrak{q}_i^* \cap A$ and is contained in an associated prime ideal of $\mathfrak{a}$.
