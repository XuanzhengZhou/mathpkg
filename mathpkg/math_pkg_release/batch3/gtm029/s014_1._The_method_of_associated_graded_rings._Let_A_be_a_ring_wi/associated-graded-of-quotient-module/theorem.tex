Let $A$ be a ring, $\mathfrak{m}$ an ideal in $A$, $E$ an $A$-module and $F$ a submodule of $E$. Then $G(E/F)$ is canonically isomorphic to a factor module of $G(E)$, the corresponding submodule of $G(E)$ being

$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(\mathfrak{m}^n E) \cap (\mathfrak{m}^{n+1} E + F)}{\mathfrak{m}^{n+1} E},$$

i.e., the homogeneous submodule of $G(E)$ which is generated by the initial forms of the elements of $F$, namely the leading submodule of $F$. In the particular case where $E = A$ and $F$ is an ideal $\mathfrak{a}$, we have

$$G(A/\mathfrak{a}) \cong G(A) / (\text{leading ideal of } \mathfrak{a}).$$
