The associated graded ring $G(A)$ of a ring $A$ with respect to an ideal $\mathfrak{m}$ is the direct sum of successive quotients $\mathfrak{m}^n / \mathfrak{m}^{n+1}$. Similarly, for an $A$-module $E$, the associated graded module $G(E)$ is the direct sum of $\mathfrak{m}^n E / \mathfrak{m}^{n+1} E$. These constructions capture the leading-order behavior of elements under the $\mathfrak{m}$-adic filtration and serve as a fundamental tool for studying completions, dimension theory, and multiplicity.
