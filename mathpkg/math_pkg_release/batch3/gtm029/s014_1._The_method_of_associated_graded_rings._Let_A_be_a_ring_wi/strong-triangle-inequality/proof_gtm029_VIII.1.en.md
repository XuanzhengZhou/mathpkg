By the property $v(x-z) \geq \min(v(x-y), v(y-z))$, we have $2^{-v(x-z)} \leq \max(2^{-v(x-y)}, 2^{-v(y-z)})$, which is exactly $d(x,z) \leq \max(d(x,y), d(y,z))$. The ordinary triangle inequality follows since $\max(a,b) \leq a+b$ for nonnegative $a,b$.
