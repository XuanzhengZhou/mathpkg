If $\mathfrak{m}$ is an ideal in $A$, the powers $\mathfrak{m}^n$ ($n = 0, 1, 2, \cdots$) form a system $\Sigma(A)$ satisfying:

(a) $\mathfrak{m}^n \cap \mathfrak{m}^{n'} = \mathfrak{m}^n$ if $n \geq n'$;

(b) $\mathfrak{m}^n - \mathfrak{m}^n = \mathfrak{m}^n$ and $(\mathfrak{m}^n)^2 \subset \mathfrak{m}^n$;

(c) $\mathfrak{m}^n + a \subset \mathfrak{m}^n$ and $\mathfrak{m}^n b \subset \mathfrak{m}^n$, if $a \in \mathfrak{m}^n$ and $b \in A$.

We define the $\mathfrak{m}$-topology of $A$ as being the one in which the ideals $\mathfrak{m}^n$ constitute a basis of neighborhoods of the zero of $A$. Similarly, if $E$ is an $A$-module, the $\mathfrak{m}$-topology of $E$ is the one in which the submodules $\mathfrak{m}^n E$ constitute a basis of neighborhoods of the zero of $E$. With respect to this $\mathfrak{m}$-topology, the module $E$ is a Hausdorff space if and only if $\bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} \mathfrak{m}^n E = (0)$.
