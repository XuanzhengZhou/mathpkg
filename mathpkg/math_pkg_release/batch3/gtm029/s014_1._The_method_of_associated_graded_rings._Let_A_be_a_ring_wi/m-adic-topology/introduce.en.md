The $\mathfrak{m}$-adic topology on a ring $A$ (or module $E$) is defined by taking the powers $\mathfrak{m}^n$ (or $\mathfrak{m}^n E$) as a fundamental system of neighborhoods of zero. This topology is the foundation for the theory of completions in commutative algebra and is Hausdorff precisely when the intersection of all powers of $\mathfrak{m}$ is zero.
