If $A$ is semi-local, the radical of $\mathfrak{m}$ is the intersection of the maximal ideals of $A$. Hence $A/\mathfrak{m}$ is a noetherian ring in which every prime ideal different from $(1)$ is maximal, i.e., $A/\mathfrak{m}$ satisfies the d.c.c. (Vol. I, Ch. IV, $\S$2, Theorem 2). Similarly if $A$ is local.

Conversely, if $A/\mathfrak{m}$ satisfies the d.c.c., there is only a finite number of prime ideals $\mathfrak{p}_i/\mathfrak{m}$ in $A/\mathfrak{m}$, and they are maximal. The preimages $\mathfrak{p}_i$ are maximal ideals of $A$, and their intersection is the radical of $\mathfrak{m}$, which equals $\mathfrak{m}$ since $A$ is a Zariski ring (every maximal ideal contains $\mathfrak{m}$). Thus $A$ is semi-local (or local if there is only one such prime ideal).
