If $\mathfrak{m}$ admits a finite basis, then the closures of $\mathfrak{m}^n E$ in $\hat{E}$ and of $\mathfrak{m}^n$ in $\hat{A}$ are

$$\hat{A} \mathfrak{m}^n E = (\hat{A} \mathfrak{m})^n E \quad \text{and} \quad \hat{A} \mathfrak{m}^n = (\hat{A} \mathfrak{m})^n$$

respectively. Moreover, $\mathfrak{m}^n E = (\hat{A} \mathfrak{m})^n E \cap E$ and $\mathfrak{m}^n = (\hat{A} \mathfrak{m})^n \cap A$. The topologies of $\hat{E}$ and $\hat{A}$ considered as completions are their $(\hat{A} \mathfrak{m})$-topologies.
