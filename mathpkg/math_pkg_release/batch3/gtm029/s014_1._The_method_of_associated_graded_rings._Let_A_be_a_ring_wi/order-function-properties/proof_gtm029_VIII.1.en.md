For (2): If $v(x) = m$ and $v(y) = n$, then $x \in \mathfrak{m}^m E$, $y \in \mathfrak{m}^n E$, so $x+y \in \mathfrak{m}^{\min(m,n)} E$, hence $v(x+y) \geq \min(v(x),v(y))$. The inequality $v(a+b) \geq \min(v(a),v(b))$ is proved similarly.

For (3): If $a \in \mathfrak{m}^{v(a)}$ and $x \in \mathfrak{m}^{v(x)}E$, then $ax \in \mathfrak{m}^{v(a)}\mathfrak{m}^{v(x)}E = \mathfrak{m}^{v(a)+v(x)}E$, so $v(ax) \geq v(a)+v(x)$.
