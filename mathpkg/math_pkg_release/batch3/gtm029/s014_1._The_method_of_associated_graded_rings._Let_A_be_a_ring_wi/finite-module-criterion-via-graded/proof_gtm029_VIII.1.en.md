If $E/\mathfrak{m}E$ is finite over $A/\mathfrak{m}$, choose elements $x_1,\ldots,x_r \in E$ whose residue classes generate $E/\mathfrak{m}E$. Their initial forms $G(x_i)$ are homogeneous of degree $0$ and generate $G(E)$ as a $G(A)$-module (since any homogeneous element of degree $n$ in $G(E)$ is a sum of products $G(m_1)\cdots G(m_n)G(x)$). Thus the leading submodule of $F=E$ is generated by $G(x_i)$, and Theorem 7 implies $x_i$ generate $E$.
