An integrally closed local domain $R$ in which the ideal of non-units is the only proper prime ideal is the valuation ring of a place of rank 1.

For, $R$ is a Dedekind domain (Vol. I, Ch. V, § 6, Theorem 13), and if $\mathfrak{p}$ is the ideal of non-units in $R$ then $R = R_{\mathfrak{p}}$. Note that $R$ is a discrete valuation ring of rank 1 (in the sense of Vol. I, Ch. V, end of § 6, p. 278; see also § 10 of this chapter, Theorem 16, Corollary 1).

We shall conclude this section with the derivation of another criterion for a domain to be a valuation ring. Let $\mathfrak{o}$ be an integral domain, $\mathfrak{q}$ a prime ideal in $\mathfrak{o}$, and let $\mathcal{P}$ be a place of the quotient field $K$ of $\mathfrak{o}$ which is finite on $\mathfrak{o}$ and has center $\mathfrak{q}$. Since $\mathfrak{M}_{\mathcal{P}} \cap \mathfrak{o} = \mathfrak{q}$, the integral domain $\mathfrak{o}/\mathfrak{q}$ can be canonically identified with a subring of the residue field $\Delta$ of $\mathcal{P}$. Thus $\Delta$ is an extension of the quotient field $\Delta_0$ of $\mathfrak{o}/\mathfrak{q}$. We shall say that the place $\mathcal{P}$ is of the first or of the second kind, with respect to $\mathfrak{o}$, according to whether the transcendence degree of $\Delta$ over $\Delta_0$ is zero or positive.
