Relation (8) expresses in a different form the content of Theorem 1. At this stage it will be convenient to introduce certain notations and terminology. Given the $\emptyset$-module $M$, the field $F_0$ introduced above shall be denoted by $\emptyset(M)$. The module $M$ shall be said to be homogeneous if the sum $R = \sum_{q=0}^{\infty} M^q$ is direct (so that $R$ is therefore a graded ring). It is immediately seen that if $M$ is a homogeneous module, then every element $m$ of $M$, $m \neq 0$, is transcendental over $\emptyset(M)$, and that if this last condition is satisfied by some element $m$ of $M$, $m \neq 0$, then $M$ is homogeneous.

The above transition from $M$ to $M'$ is only necessary if $M$ is not homogeneous. If $M$ itself is homogeneous, then it is not necessary to introduce a new transcendental $t$, and we can deal directly with the graded ring $R$ (instead of with $R'$).
