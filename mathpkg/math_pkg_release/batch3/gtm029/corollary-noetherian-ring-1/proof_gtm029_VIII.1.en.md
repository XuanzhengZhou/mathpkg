Let $y$ be any element of $F$. We are going to show inductively the existence, for every $n \geq 0$, of elements $a_{ni}$ of $A$ such that

$$y \equiv \sum_{i=1}^{q} a_{ni} x_i \pmod{m^n E}.$$

This is obvious for $n=0$. Suppose (1) holds for a given integer $n$ and for suitable elements $a_{ni}$ in $A$. If the element $y - \sum_{i=1}^{q} a_{ni} x_i$ is in $m^{n+1} E$, we take $a_{n+1,i} = a_{ni}$. If not, then the initial form $G\left(y - \sum_{i=1}^{q} a_{ni} x_i\right)$ is an element of degree $n$ in the leading submodule $F'$ of $F$. As $F'$ is generated by the homogeneous elements $G(x_i)$ we can write

$$G\left(y - \sum_{i=1}^{q} a_{ni} x_i\right) = \sum_{i=1}^{q} G\left(c_{ni}\right) G(x_i),$$
