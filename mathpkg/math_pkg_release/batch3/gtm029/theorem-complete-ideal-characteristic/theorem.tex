Any product of complete ideals in $o$ is a complete ideal.

Proof. Let $A_1, A_2, \cdots, A_n$ be complete ideals in $o$. It is obviously sufficient to consider the case in which each $A_i$ is primary for $m$. Using the factorization theorem for complete ideals (Theorem 1') and also Corollary 2 to Theorem 1', we achieve at once a reduction to the case in which all the characteristic forms $c(A_i)$ ($i=1, 2, \cdots, n$) are powers of one and the same irreducible form $\bar{g}$. In this case, let $o'$ be the quadratic transform of $o$ relative to the directional form $\bar{g}$ and let $A'_i$ be the $o'$-transform of $A_i$. We now use induction with respect to $\max \{w(A_1), w(A_2), \cdots, w(A_n)\}$, for the theorem is trivial when that maximum is zero. Since $w(A'_i) < w(A_i)$, our induction hypothesis implies that $A'_1 A'_2 \cdots A'_n$ is a complete ideal in $o'$.
