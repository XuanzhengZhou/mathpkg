Let $A$ be an ideal in $o$, of order $r$, and let $\bar{g}^\sigma$ be the highest power of $\bar{g}$ which divides the characteristic form $c(A)$ of $A$. Then:

(a) The order $r'$ of the transform $A'$ of $A$ in $o'$ is not greater than $\sigma$, but is positive if $\sigma$ is positive. Thus, in particular, $A'$ is the unit ideal if and only if $\bar{g}$ does not divide $c(A)$.

(b) If $A$ is primary for $m$ then $A'$ is either primary for $m'$ or is the unit ideal.
