(Later on we shall use this term in a wider sense, since we shall replace $0'$ by a semi-local ring $0'_1 \cap 0'_2 \cap \cdots \cap 0'_m$, where the local rings $0'_1, 0'_2, \cdots, 0'_m$ are quadratic transforms of $0$, relative to distinct directional forms $\bar{g}_1, \bar{g}_2, \cdots, \bar{g}_m$.) We observe that every power of $m$ is a contracted ideal, since $0' m^n = 0' t_1^n$ and clearly $0' t_1^n \cap 0 = m^n$ (every element of $0' t_1^n$ has value $\geq n$ in the $m$-adic prime divisor of $0$). We also observe that as a consequence of Proposition 2, part (a) we have the following

COROLLARY. If $A$ is a contracted ideal and $\bar{g}$ does not divide the characteristic form $c(A)$ of $A$ then $A$ is a power of $m$.

For we have then $A' = 0'$ and hence $A = 0' t_1^r \cap 0 = m^r$, where $r$ is the order of $A$.
