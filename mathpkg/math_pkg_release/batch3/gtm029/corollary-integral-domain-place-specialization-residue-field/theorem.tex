If $\varphi$ is a specialization of an integral domain $\varnothing$, and if $K$ is a field containing $\varnothing$, then there exists a place of $K$ which is an extension of $\varphi$ and whose residue field is algebraic over the quotient field of $\varnothing\varphi$.

Let $k$ be the quotient field of the $\varphi$-transform $\varnothing\varphi$ of $\varnothing$. We fix a place $\mathcal{P}$ of $K$ which is an extension of $\varphi$ and whose residue field $\Delta$ therefore contains $k$. If $\Delta$ is algebraic over $k$ then $\mathcal{P}$ is the desired place. If $\Delta$ is not algebraic over $k$, then we fix, by Corollary 2, an algebraic place $\mathcal{Q}$ of $\Delta/k$.
