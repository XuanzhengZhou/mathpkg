Let $k$ be a field, and $K = k(X)$ the field of rational functions in one indeterminate $X$ over $k$. The non-trivial places of $K/k$ are:

(a) The $p(X)$-adic places ($p(X)$, an irreducible polynomial in $k[X]$).

(b) The place $\mathcal{R}$ whose valuation ring consists of all fractions $a(X)/b(X)$ ($a, b$: polynomials) such that $\partial a \leq \partial b$.

(Equivalent places may be obtained by replacing in the rational functions $f(X)$ either

(a) $X$ by a root of the irreducible polynomial $p(X)$ or

(b) $1/X$ by 0.)
