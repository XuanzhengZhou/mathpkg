A regular local ring $A$ is an integrally closed integral domain. The passage from elements of $A$ to their initial forms preserves products. The order function $v$ relative to $m$ (see §1; $v(x)$ is defined by $x \in m^{v(x)}$, $x \notin m^{v(x)+1}$) is a valuation of the quotient field of $A$. The completion $\hat{A}$ of $A$ is an integrally closed domain.

All these assertions, except the last, follow from (b) and from Theorems 1 and 3 (§1). The last assertion follows from the fact that $\hat{A}$ is also a regular local ring.
