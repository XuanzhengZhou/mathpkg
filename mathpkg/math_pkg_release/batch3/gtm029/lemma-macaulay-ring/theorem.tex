EXAMPLES. (1) Any regular local ring $A$ is a Macaulay ring. In fact any regular system of parameters of $A$ is a prime sequence by VIII, Theorem 26, Â§ 11.

(2) Any local domain $A$ of dimension 1 is a Macaulay ring. In fact any single element $\neq 0$ of the maximal ideal of $A$ constitutes a prime sequence. More generally, for a local ring $A$ of dimension 1 to be a Macaulay ring, it is necessary and sufficient that the maximal ideal $m$ of $A$ is not an associated prime ideal of (0).
