If $K$ has finite transcendence degree $r$ over $k$, then any place $\mathcal{P}$ of $K/k$ has rank $\leq r-s$, where $s = \dim \mathcal{P}/k$.

The rank of a place $\mathcal{P}$ of $K$ is zero if and only if $\mathcal{P}$ is a trivial place of $K$.

The rank of $\mathcal{P}$ is 1 if and only if $\mathcal{P}$ is not a trivial place of $K$ and is not a proper specialization of any non-trivial place of $K$. A necessary and sufficient condition that a place $\mathcal{P}$ be of rank one is that its valuation ring be a maximal (proper) subring of $K$. We shall see later (§4, Theorem 4, Corollary 3) that any maximal (proper) subring of $K$ is in fact the valuation ring of a place of $K$, provided the subring is a proper ring, i.e., not a field.
