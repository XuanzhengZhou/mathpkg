The rings $\mathfrak{D}$ and $\mathfrak{0}$ being as in the preceding corollary, let $\mathfrak{a}$ be an ideal in $\mathfrak{0}$. Then if $\mathfrak{a} \neq \mathfrak{0}$, we have $\mathfrak{D}\mathfrak{a} \neq \mathfrak{D}$.

Since $\mathfrak{0}$ contains an identity, there exists a prime ideal $m$ in $\mathfrak{0}$ such that $a \subset m \neq \mathfrak{0}$ (for instance, there exist maximal ideals containing $\mathfrak{a}$). By Corollary 5, let $\mathfrak{M}$ be a prime ideal in $\mathfrak{D}$ such that $\mathfrak{M} \cap \mathfrak{0} = \mathfrak{m}$. Then clearly $\mathfrak{M} \neq \mathfrak{D}$, and since $\mathfrak{D}\mathfrak{a} \subset \mathfrak{D}\mathfrak{m} \subset \mathfrak{M}$, it follows that $\mathfrak{D}\mathfrak{a} \neq \mathfrak{D}$.
