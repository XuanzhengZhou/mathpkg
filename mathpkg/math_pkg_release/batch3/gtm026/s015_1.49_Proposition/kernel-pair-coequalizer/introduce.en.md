Lemma 1.53 establishes a key relationship between coequalizers and kernel pairs: if a morphism $p: A \to I$ is a coequalizer (of some pair of morphisms) and its kernel pair exists, then $p$ is in fact the coequalizer of its own kernel pair. This result is technically important for constructing coequalizer-mono factorizations, as it ensures that the morphism $p$ obtained by taking the coequalizer of the kernel pair of $f$ is itself a coequalizer -- a necessary condition for the factorization $f = p \cdot i$ to be a valid coequalizer-mono factorization.
