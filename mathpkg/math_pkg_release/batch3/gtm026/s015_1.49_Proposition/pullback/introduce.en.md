The pullback is a fundamental categorical limit that captures the notion of "fiber product." Given two morphisms $f: A \to C$ and $g: B \to C$ with a common codomain, their pullback is the universal object $P$ making the square commute. In the category of sets, the pullback is realized as the subset $\{(a,b) \in A \times B : f(a) = g(b)\}$, with the projections restricted to this subset. Pullbacks generalize many constructions: when $f = g$, the pullback yields the equivalence relation (kernel pair) of $f$. The dual concept is the pushout.
