The pushout is the categorical dual of the pullback, obtained by reversing all arrows in the pullback diagram. Where a pullback universally completes a cospan $A \to C \leftarrow B$, a pushout universally completes a span $A \leftarrow C \to B$. In concrete categories like $\mathbf{Set}$, the pushout corresponds to taking the disjoint union and then identifying the images of $C$ under the two given maps. Pushouts are fundamental in constructions such as adjunction spaces, amalgamated free products, and gluing in topology.
