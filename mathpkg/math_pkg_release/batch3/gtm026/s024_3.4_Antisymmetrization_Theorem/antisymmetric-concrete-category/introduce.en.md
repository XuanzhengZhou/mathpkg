An antisymmetric concrete category over a base category $\mathcal{K}$ is one in which every fibre (the collection of $\mathcal{A}$-objects mapping to a fixed $\mathcal{K}$-object under the forgetful functor $U$) forms a partially ordered class. This condition ensures that there is at most one admissible morphism with underlying identity between objects sharing the same base object. Antisymmetry is the key structural property needed for the Antisymmetrization Theorem, which shows that every concrete category admits a full reflective embedding into an antisymmetric one.
