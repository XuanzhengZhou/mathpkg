A semigroup is one of the simplest algebraic structures: a set equipped with a single associative binary operation. It serves as the foundational motivating example in Manes' development of algebraic theories, where the passage from the equational definition of semigroups to their associated algebraic theory $T$ is worked out in detail. The set of all parenthesis-free strings $a_1 \cdots a_n$ (with $n > 0$) over a set $A$ forms the free semigroup on $A$, and this construction generalizes to arbitrary algebraic theories via a "terms modulo equations" quotient.
