A functor is a structure-preserving map between categories: it sends objects to objects and morphisms to morphisms, respecting identities and composition. Manes introduces functors already in the Preliminaries because they arise naturally in Chapter 1 — for instance, an algebraic theory $T$ itself defines a functor on $\mathbf{Set}$. The notation $H: \mathcal{K} \to \mathcal{L}$ is used throughout the book. Functors are the morphisms of the "category of categories" and provide the framework for comparing different mathematical structures.
