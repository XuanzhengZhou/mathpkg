Since $\eta.(gh)U = \eta.gU.hU = \eta = \eta.(id_F)U$, $gh = id_F$. Symmetrically, $hg = id_{F'}$. Therefore, $g$ and $h$ are mutually inverse isomorphisms the $U$ of which converts each $\eta$ into the other; it seems hard to imagine a more stringent isomorphism statement!
