Proposition 4.11 shows that the class $M$ of an image factorization system is completely determined by the class $E$ via a universal lifting property. Specifically, a morphism $t$ belongs to $M$ if and only if it admits a diagonal fill-in against every $E$-morphism on the left side of a commutative square. This is particularly useful when constructing image factorization systems: once a natural candidate for $E$ is chosen (e.g., all regular epimorphisms), there is a unique candidate for $M$ to try. Dually, $E$ is determined by $M$ through the analogous right-lifting property.
