Trivial by duality. Reversing all arrows interchanges the roles of $E$ and $M$ while preserving each of the four axioms (4.2)–(4.5). Specifically, in $\mathcal{H}^{\mathrm{op}}$ the class $M$ (now the "$E$" of the dual system) consists of epimorphisms because each $m \in M$ was a monomorphism in $\mathcal{H}$. Similarly, $E$ (now the "$M$" of the dual system) consists of monomorphisms. The factorization $f = e \cdot m$ in $\mathcal{H}$ dualizes to $f^{\mathrm{op}} = m^{\mathrm{op}} \cdot e^{\mathrm{op}}$ in $\mathcal{H}^{\mathrm{op}}$, giving the required unique factorization.
