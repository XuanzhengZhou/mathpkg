The duality principle for image factorization systems states that passing to the opposite category interchanges the two classes $E$ and $M$. If $(E, M)$ is an image factorization system in a category $\mathcal{H}$, then $(M, E)$ automatically becomes an image factorization system in the opposite category $\mathcal{H}^{\mathrm{op}}$. This follows directly from the symmetry of the definition: epimorphisms become monomorphisms and vice versa upon dualization, and the unique factorization property is preserved.
