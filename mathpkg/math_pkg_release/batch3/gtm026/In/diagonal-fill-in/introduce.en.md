The diagonal fill-in property is the fundamental lifting lemma for image factorization systems. Given a commutative square where the top arrow belongs to $E$ and the bottom arrow belongs to $M$, there exists a unique diagonal morphism $h$ making both triangles commute. Uniqueness follows from $e$ being epic (or equivalently $m$ being monic). Existence is proved by factoring both $f$ and $g$ through their $E$-$M$ factorizations and applying the uniqueness clause of axiom (4.5) to the equation $f \cdot m = e \cdot g$. This diagonal property is the categorical analog of the familiar lifting properties in topology and algebra.
