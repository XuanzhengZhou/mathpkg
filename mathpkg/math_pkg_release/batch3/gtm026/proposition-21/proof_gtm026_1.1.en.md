If $(f, g, h; d, d')$ is a contractible coequalizer as in 1.3 then $f.d.g = f.h.d' = g.h.d' = g.d.g$. Conversely, suppose $d:B \longrightarrow A$ exists subject to 1.7. As $f.d.g = g.d.g$ and $h = \text{coeq}(f, g)$, there exists a unique $d':C \longrightarrow B$ such that $h.d' = d.g$; as $h$ is epi and $h.d'.h = d.g.h = d.f.h = h$, $d'.h = \text{id}_C$.
