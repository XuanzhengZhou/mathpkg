A symmetric monoidal category $\mathcal{K}$ is called closed when the endofunctor $-\otimes A$ (tensoring on the right by a fixed object $A$) possesses a right adjoint for every object $A$. This right adjoint provides an internal hom-object $B^A$ satisfying the natural bijection $\mathcal{K}(C \otimes A, B) \cong \mathcal{K}(C, B^A)$. In the special case $C = I$ (the monoidal unit), morphisms $I \to B^A$ correspond bijectively to morphisms $A \to B$, so $B^A$ behaves as an internal "function space". Closed symmetric monoidal categories generalize cartesian closed categories to the monoidal setting, where the monoidal product need not be the categorical product.
