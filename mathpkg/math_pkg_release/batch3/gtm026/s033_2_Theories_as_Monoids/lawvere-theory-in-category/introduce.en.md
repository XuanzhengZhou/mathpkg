A Lawvere theory in a base category $\mathcal{K}$ is Linton's generalization of Lawvere's original notion of algebraic theory. It consists of a category $\mathcal{T}$ sharing the same objects as $\mathcal{K}$, equipped with a functor $R: \mathcal{K}^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathcal{T}$ that acts as the identity on objects and possesses a left adjoint. This structure captures the idea that $\mathcal{T}$-morphisms $A \to B$ correspond to $B$-tuples of $A$-ary operations, with $R$ embedding the opposite of $\mathcal{K}$ as the "projection" mappings, and the left adjoint providing the "diagonal" or "variable-inclusion" mappings. This framework generalizes the clone-theoretic algebraic theories of the main text by identifying the associated category $\mathcal{T} = (\mathcal{K}_T)^{\mathrm{op}}$ with $R$ given by the clone structure maps.
