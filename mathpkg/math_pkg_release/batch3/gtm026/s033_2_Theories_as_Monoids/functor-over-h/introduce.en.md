A functor $\Gamma: \mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{S}} \to \mathcal{L}^{\mathbf{T}}$ between categories of algebras is said to be over a given base functor $H: \mathcal{K} \to \mathcal{L}$ if it commutes with the forgetful functors, i.e., $\Gamma \circ U^{\mathbf{T}} = U^{\mathbf{S}} \circ H$. This means that the underlying object of the image algebra $A\Gamma$ is the image under $H$ of the underlying object of $A$, so $\Gamma$ lifts $H$ to the algebra level. When $H = \mathrm{id}$, a functor over the identity is simply a functor between algebra categories that preserves the underlying object. Functors over $H$ are in natural bijection with theory maps relative to $H$, generalizing the correspondence between theory morphisms and algebra-category functors.
