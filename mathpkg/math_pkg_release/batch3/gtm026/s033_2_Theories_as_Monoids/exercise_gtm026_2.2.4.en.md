Given a monoid $(X, m, e)$ in a symmetric monoidal category $(\mathcal{K}, \otimes, I, a, b, c)$, define an endofunctor $T = A \otimes X$ on $\mathcal{K}$, the unit $\eta_A = c^{-1} \cdot (\mathrm{id}_A \otimes e)$, and the substitution $\alpha \circ \beta = \alpha \cdot (\beta \otimes \mathrm{id}_X) \cdot a^{-1} \cdot (\mathrm{id} \otimes m)$.

Show that $T = (T, \eta, \circ)$ is an algebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$.

Show that $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ is the category of $(X, m, e)$-actions. Examine the previous exercise for well-known examples of actions.

Show that each monoid homomorphism induces a theory map.
