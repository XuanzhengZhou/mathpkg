A theory map relative to a functor $H: \mathcal{K} \to \mathcal{L}$ is a natural transformation $\lambda: H \circ T \to S \circ H$ that intertwines the theory endofunctors $T$ (in $\mathcal{L}$) and $S$ (in $\mathcal{K}$) through $H$, compatibly with the monoid-theoretic structure (unit $\eta$ and multiplication $\circ$). This generalizes ordinary theory maps (the case $H = \mathrm{id}$) to situations where one changes the base category along $H$. Relative theory maps correspond bijectively to functors $\Gamma: \mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{S}} \to \mathcal{L}^{\mathbf{T}}$ over $H$ via $(A, \xi)\Gamma = (AH, A\lambda \cdot \xi H)$. An application is the interpretation of the unique continuous surjection between Stone-Cech compactifications induced by the identity function from a discrete space to a topological space as a relative theory map.
