Let $\mathcal{K}$ be the category of metric spaces and distance-decreasing maps (as in 1.9) and, similarly, let $\mathcal{L}$ be the category of metric spaces of diameter at most $M$ and distance-decreasing maps. Show that $\mathcal{K}$ fails to have binary coproducts whereas $\mathcal{L}$ has all coproducts. Show that both categories have coequalizers.

[Hint: set $\text{coeq}(f, g) = Y/R$ where $R = \{(y, y') : yh = y'h$ for all $h$ in the category with $fh = gh\}$ with metric $d(yR, zR) = \text{Sup}(d(ah, bh): fh = gh$ as above and $a \in yR, b \in zR)$.]
