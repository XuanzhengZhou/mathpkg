For each $A$ in $\mathcal{K}$, the functor $\mathcal{K}(-, A):\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}} \rightarrow \text{Set}$ corepresented by $A$ is the functor $\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}(A, -)$. Prove that $(L, \psi)$ is a colimit of $D$ if and only if for every object $A$ of $\mathcal{K}$, $(\mathcal{K}(L, A), \psi \cdot -)$ is a limit of $\mathcal{K}(D, A)$ in $\text{Set}$.
