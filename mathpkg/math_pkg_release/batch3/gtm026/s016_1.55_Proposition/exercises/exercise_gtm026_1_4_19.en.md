A subobject classifier in a category $\mathcal{K}$ possessing a terminal object $1$ is a pair $(\Omega, t)$ where $\Omega$ is an object of $\mathcal{K}$ and $t: 1 \longrightarrow \Omega$ is (necessarily) a monomorphism such that the pullback of $t$ along any morphism exists and, in fact, the passage from $\chi: X \longrightarrow \Omega$ to $[t] \chi^{-1}$ establishes a bijection between $\mathcal{K}(X, \Omega)$ and the class of monosubobjects of $X$. By pullback-stability, $[t] \chi^{-1}$ is the subclass of those monosubobjects of $X$ whose characteristic morphism is $\chi$.

Show that in $\mathbf{Set}$, $\Omega$ may be taken as any two-element set with $t(0)$ any chosen element.
