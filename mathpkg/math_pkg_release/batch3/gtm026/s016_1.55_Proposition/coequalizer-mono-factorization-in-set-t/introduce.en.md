For any algebraic theory $T$ in the category of sets, the category $\text{Set}^T$ of $T$-algebras admits coequalizer-mono factorizations. Moreover, these factorizations are constructed at the underlying set level: given a $T$-homomorphism, one takes the usual set-theoretic image factorization and lifts the algebra structure to the image using the theory's properties. This result is fundamental for working with algebraic categories over $\text{Set}$, as it guarantees that the image of a homomorphism carries a canonical $T$-algebra structure.
