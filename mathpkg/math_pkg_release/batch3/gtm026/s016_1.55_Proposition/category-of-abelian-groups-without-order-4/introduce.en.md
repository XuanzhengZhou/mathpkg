This example demonstrates that a category may have all small limits and colimits (and in particular all factorizations of the type constructed in 1.52) yet still fail to have coequalizer-mono factorizations. The category $\mathcal{K}$ consists of abelian groups that contain no element of order 4, i.e., groups where $4x = 0$ implies $2x = 0$. Limits and colimits in $\mathcal{K}$ are computed as in the category of all abelian groups. The failure of coequalizer-mono factorizations is witnessed by the morphism $4: \mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z}$, which cannot be factored through a coequalizer in $\mathcal{K}$.
