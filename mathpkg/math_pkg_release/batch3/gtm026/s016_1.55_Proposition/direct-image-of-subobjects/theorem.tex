If $\mathcal{K}$ has coequalizer-mono factorizations, each morphism $f: A \rightarrow B$ induces the "direct image map" $[i]f = [j]$ where $(p, j)$ is any coequalizer-mono factorization of $i \cdot f$. The construction is well defined: if $[i] = [i']$ and $(p', j')$ is a coequalizer-mono factorization of $i' \cdot f$ then $[j] = [j']$.
