Unlike the category of abelian groups, the category of all groups admits no cogenerator. The proof uses the existence of arbitrarily large simple groups: given any candidate cogenerator $C$, one can find a simple group $S$ of cardinality larger than that of $C$. Since $S$ is simple, any homomorphism from $S$ to $C$ must be either injective (impossible by cardinality) or zero. Thus the identity and zero morphisms on $S$ cannot be separated by morphisms into $C$, contradicting the cogenerator property.
