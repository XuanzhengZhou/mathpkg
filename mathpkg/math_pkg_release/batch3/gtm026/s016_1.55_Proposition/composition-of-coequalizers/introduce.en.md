In any category with coequalizer-mono factorizations, the class of coequalizer morphisms is closed under composition with another coequalizer, and coequalizers can be cancelled on the right. Specifically, if both $f$ and $g$ are coequalizers, their composition $f \cdot g$ is also a coequalizer. Conversely, if the composition $f \cdot g$ is known to be a coequalizer, then $g$ itself must be a coequalizer. This proposition extends the elementary composition/cancellation properties of epimorphisms to the stronger notion of coequalizers.
