The circle group $S = \{z \in \mathbb{C} : |z| = 1\}$ under multiplication serves as a cogenerator for the category of abelian groups. This is a classic result in group theory, often phrased as "there exist enough homomorphisms to the circle." As a consequence, every abelian group embeds as a subgroup of a product of circles, giving a concrete representation of arbitrary abelian groups in terms of this fundamental compact Lie group.
