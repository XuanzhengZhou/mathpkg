In the category of complete semilattices with supremum-preserving morphisms, the two-element lattice $\{0, 1\}$ is a cogenerator. The proof divides into two cases: if $a < b$ in a complete semilattice $X$, define $h: X \to \{0,1\}$ by $h(x) = 0$ when $x \leq a$ and $h(x) = 1$ otherwise; if $a \not\leq b$, define $h$ symmetrically using $b$. In both cases $h$ preserves suprema and separates $a$ from $b$.
