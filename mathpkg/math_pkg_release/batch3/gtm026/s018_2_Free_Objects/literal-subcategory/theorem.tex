A \textbf{literal subcategory} of $\mathcal{K}$ is a category $\mathcal{L}$ with $\mathrm{Obj}(\mathcal{L}) \subset \mathrm{Obj}(\mathcal{K})$ and $\mathcal{L}(L_1, L_2) \subset \mathcal{K}(L_1, L_2)$ for all $L_1, L_2 \in \mathcal{L}$, such that $\mathcal{L}$ is closed under identities and composition. That is, a literal subcategory is a subclass of $\mathcal{K}$-morphisms containing all identity morphisms of its objects and closed under composition.
