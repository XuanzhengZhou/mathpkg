A literal subcategory is the concrete, set-theoretic notion of a subcategory: its objects form a subclass of the ambient category's objects, and its morphism sets are subsets of the ambient category's hom-sets, closed under identities and composition. Every abstract subcategory (a monosubobject in the category of categories) can be represented by the inclusion functor of a literal subcategory. In practice, the term "subcategory" without qualification is almost always intended to mean a literal subcategory.
