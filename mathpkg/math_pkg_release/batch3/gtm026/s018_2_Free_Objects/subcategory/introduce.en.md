A subcategory is defined as a monosubobject in the category $\mathcal{C}$ of categories and functors, following the general notion introduced in 1.65. Concretely, a subcategory of $\mathcal{K}$ is given by a functor $H: \mathcal{L} \to \mathcal{K}$ whose induced maps on hom-sets are injective. This abstract definition captures the idea of a category sitting inside another without requiring a literal subset inclusion of objects or morphisms, though every such abstract subcategory can be represented by the inclusion functor of a literal subcategory.
