Any two free objects over the same object $K$ with respect to the same functor $U$ are isomorphic via a unique isomorphism that commutes with the universal morphisms. This is a standard consequence of the universal property: each free object's universal map factors uniquely through the other, yielding mutually inverse isomorphisms. The result justifies speaking of "the" free object once existence is established, and parallels the uniqueness statement for free $\mathbf{T}$-algebras given in 1.4.14.
