If $(F, \eta)$ and $(F', \eta')$ are free $\mathcal{A}$-objects over $K$ with respect to $U: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$, then there exist unique mutually inverse $\mathcal{A}$-isomorphisms $g: F \longrightarrow F'$ and $h: F' \longrightarrow F$ such that $\eta \cdot gU = \eta'$ and $\eta' \cdot hU = \eta$.
