In the category of abelian groups, coproducts are given by the direct sum construction: the subgroup of the direct product consisting of tuples with only finitely many nonzero entries. The injection maps embed each component by placing the element at the corresponding index with zeros elsewhere (Kronecker delta pattern). The universal property is satisfied by defining the induced homomorphism as the pointwise finite sum of the component homomorphisms. This contrasts with the product in abelian groups, which is the full direct product (unrestricted tuples).
