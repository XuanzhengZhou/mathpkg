A coequalizer is the categorical dual of an equalizer: a morphism $q: B \to Q$ coequalizes a parallel pair $f, g: A \rightrightarrows B$ if $q \circ f = q \circ g$, and it is universal with this property. Equivalently, $q$ is a coequalizer in $\mathcal{K}$ precisely when it is an equalizer in the opposite category $\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}$. Coequalizers generalize quotient constructions: in sets they correspond to quotients by the equivalence relation generated by identifying $f(a)$ with $g(a)$; in algebra they correspond to quotienting by the image of $f-g$.
