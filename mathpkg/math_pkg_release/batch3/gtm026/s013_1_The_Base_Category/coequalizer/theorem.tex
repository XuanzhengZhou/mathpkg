A diagram
$$A \mathrel{\mathop{\rightrightarrows}\limits_{g}^{f}} B \xrightarrow{q} Q$$
in a category $\mathcal{K}$ is a \textbf{coequalizer} if $q = \text{coeq}(f, g)$, meaning that $q$ is the coequalizer of $f$ and $g$. Equivalently, $q = \text{eq}(f, g)$ in $\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}$.

That is:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $q \circ f = q \circ g$;
\item For any $q': B \to Q'$ with $q' \circ f = q' \circ g$, there exists a unique $h: Q \to Q'$ such that $q \circ h = q'$:
\[
\begin{array}{c}
A \mathrel{\mathop{\rightrightarrows}\limits_{g}^{f}} B \xrightarrow{q} Q \\
\qquad \searrow_{q'} \quad \downarrow_{h} \\
\qquad\qquad Q'
\end{array}
\]
\end{enumerate}
