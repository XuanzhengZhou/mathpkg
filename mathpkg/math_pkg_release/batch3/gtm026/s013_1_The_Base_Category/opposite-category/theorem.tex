The dual or opposite of a category $\mathcal{K}$ is the category $\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}$ defined as follows:

\begin{itemize}
\item $\text{Ob}(\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}) = \text{Ob}(\mathcal{K})$;
\item $\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}(A, B) = \mathcal{K}(B, A)$;
\item For $f \in \mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}(A, B)$ and $g \in \mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}(B, C)$, composition is defined by
$$g \circ_{\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}} f = f \circ_{\mathcal{K}} g$$
where $f \in \mathcal{K}(B, A)$ and $g \in \mathcal{K}(C, B)$;
\item $\text{id}_A^{\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}} = \text{id}_A^{\mathcal{K}}$.
\end{itemize}

Moreover, $\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}$ is locally small if $\mathcal{K}$ is, and $(\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}})^{\text{op}} = \mathcal{K}$.
