The opposite (or dual) of a category $\mathcal{K}$, denoted $\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}$, is the category obtained by formally reversing all morphisms. It has the same objects as $\mathcal{K}$, but the hom-set $\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}(A,B)$ is defined as $\mathcal{K}(B,A)$. Composition in $\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}$ corresponds to the reverse composition in $\mathcal{K}$. This construction is involutive: $(\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}})^{\text{op}} = \mathcal{K}$, and locally small categories remain locally small under duality. The opposite category is the fundamental tool for dualizing categorical concepts.
