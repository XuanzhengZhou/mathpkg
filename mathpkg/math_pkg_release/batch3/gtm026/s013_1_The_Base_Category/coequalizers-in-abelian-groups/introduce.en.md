The category of abelian groups has coequalizers. Given two homomorphisms $f, g: A \to B$, the coequalizer is the canonical quotient map $B \to B/\operatorname{Im}(f-g)$. This is the abelian-group analogue of quotienting by the congruence generated by the identifications of $f(a)$ with $g(a)$. Notably, the set-theoretic coequalizer may differ: for the parallel pair $0, 2: \mathbb{Z} \rightrightarrows \mathbb{Z}$, the coequalizer in $\mathbf{Ab}$ is $\mathbb{Z}_2$, whereas in $\mathbf{Set}$ it is infinite. The category of torsion-free abelian groups also admits coequalizers, obtained by further quotienting out the torsion subgroup of the abelian-group coequalizer.
