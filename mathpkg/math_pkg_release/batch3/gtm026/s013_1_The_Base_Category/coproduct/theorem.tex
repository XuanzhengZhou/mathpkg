A diagram $(A_i \xrightarrow{\text{in}_i} A)_{i \in I}$ in a category $\mathcal{K}$ is a \textbf{coproduct} if $(A \xrightarrow{\text{in}_i} A_i)_{i \in I}$ is a product in $\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}$.

Coproducts are unique up to isomorphism. The notation $\coprod A_i$ denotes a coproduct. A binary coproduct is written $A + B$. The empty coproduct is a coterminal object, more commonly called an \textbf{initial object}, often denoted $0$.
