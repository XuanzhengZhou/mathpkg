A coproduct is the categorical dual of a product: a family of morphisms $\text{in}_i: A_i \to A$ constitutes a coproduct of the objects $A_i$ precisely when the same morphisms (with arrows reversed) form a product in the opposite category. Coproducts are unique up to isomorphism. The notation $\coprod A_i$ is used for arbitrary coproducts, $A+B$ for binary coproducts, and $0$ for the empty coproduct, which is an initial object (coterminal object). Coproducts generalize disjoint unions of sets and direct sums of algebraic structures.
