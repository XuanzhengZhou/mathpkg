The category of sets has coequalizers. Given two parallel functions $f, g: A \to B$, the coequalizer is the canonical projection onto the quotient $B/R$, where $R$ is the smallest equivalence relation on $B$ identifying $f(a)$ with $g(a)$ for all $a \in A$. The universal property is verified by noting that any other map $q'$ coequalizing $f$ and $g$ induces an equivalence relation containing $R$, and therefore factors uniquely through the quotient. This construction is the set-theoretic analogue of forming a quotient by a congruence.
