$\mathbf{Set}$ has coequalizers. Given $f, g: A \to B$, let $R$ be the intersection of all equivalence relations on $B$ containing $\{(af, ag) : a \in A\}$. Set $Q = B/R$ with canonical projection $q: B \to Q$. Then $q = \text{coeq}(f, g)$.

Clearly $f \circ q = g \circ q$ by construction of $R$.

Given $q': B \to Q'$ with $f \circ q' = g \circ q'$, the relation $S = \{(b_1, b_2) : b_1 q' = b_2 q'\}$ is an equivalence relation on $B$ containing $R$. The unique map $h: Q \to Q'$ is defined by $(bR)h = bq'$.
