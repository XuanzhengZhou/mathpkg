A cartesian closed category with a subobject classifier (see exercises 7, 1.19) is called a topos. In previous exercises it has been observed that the category $\text{Set}^M$ of $M$-sets is a topos. Prove, more generally, that $\text{Set}^A$ is a topos for any small category $\mathcal{A}$. [Hint: use the Yoneda lemma (exercise 11) to define $F^G$ and $\Omega$.]
