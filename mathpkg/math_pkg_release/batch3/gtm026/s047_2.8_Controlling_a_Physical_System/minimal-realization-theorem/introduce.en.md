Theorem 2.18 is the central minimal realization theorem in categorical automata theory. Given a functor $X$ that is both an input and output process in a category $\mathcal{K}$ with an image factorization system $(E, M)$, and assuming one of four technical conditions on $E$, every response $f: IX^@ \to Y$ admits a reachable and observable realization $M_f$. This realization is simultaneously a terminal object among reachable realizations and an initial object among observable realizations, making it unique up to isomorphism. The theorem unifies the automata-theoretic concepts of reachability and observability within a purely categorical framework.
