Let $(E, M)$ be an image factorization system in $\mathcal{K}$, let $X$ be an input process and an output process in $\mathcal{K}$, and assume that at least one of the following conditions holds:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ preserves $E$;
\item $X^@$ preserves $E$;
\item $E$ is the class of all coequalizers; or
\item $E$ is the class of all epimorphisms.
\end{enumerate}

Then for every response $f: IX^@ \longrightarrow Y$ there exists a reachable and observable realization $M_f = (Q_f, \delta_f, I, \tau_f, Y, \beta_f)$ of $f$. Any such $M_f$ is a terminal object in the category of reachable realizations of $f$ and simulations and any such $M_f$ is an initial object in the category of observable realizations of $f$ and simulations; thus $M_f$ is unique up to isomorphism.
