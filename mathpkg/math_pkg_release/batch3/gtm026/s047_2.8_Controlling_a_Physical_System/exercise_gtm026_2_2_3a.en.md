Let $X: \mathcal{K} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ preserve $M$ and fix $I$ in $\mathcal{K}$. Prove that the following three conditions are equivalent:

(i) The free dynamics $(IX^{\alpha}, I\mu_0)$ over $I$ exists.

(ii) If $Y: \mathcal{K} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ is defined by $AY = I + AX$ and if $f: I \longrightarrow IY$ is the first injection then the iteration of $f$ (as above) stops, i.e., $f_{\alpha}$ is an isomorphism for some $\alpha$.

(iii) There exists $A$ in $\mathcal{K}$ with $A \cong I + AX$.

[Hint: for (ii) implies (i) set $IX = IY^{\alpha}, I\mu_0 = \text{in}_2 \circ f_{\alpha}^{-1}$; for (i) implies (iii) use exercise 2 (b); for (iii) implies (i) construct canonical morphisms $IY^{\alpha} \longrightarrow A$ in $M$ and use well-poweredness.]
