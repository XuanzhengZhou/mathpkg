Suppose $p.i = f = p'.i'$ with $p, p'$ coequalizers and $i, i'$ mono-morphisms. There exists $a, b$ with $p = \text{coeq}(a, b)$. As $a.p'.i' = a.p.i = b.p.i. = b.p'.i'$ and $i'$ is mono, $a.p' = b.p'$ so there exists a unique $g$ such that $p.g = p'$. Since $p$ is epi, $g.i' = i$. Symmetrically, there exists $h:I' \longrightarrow I$ with $p'.h = p$ and $h.i = i'$. Either because $p$ is epi or $i$ is mono, $g.h = \text{id}_I$. Symmetrically, $h.g = \text{id}_I'$.
