The operators $\mathsf{P}$ (products), $\mathsf{M}$ ($\mathsf{M}$-morphism closure), and $\mathsf{S}$ (split epimorphism closure) each satisfy the three axioms of a closure operator on classes of objects. A general property of closure operators yields that the composite $\mathsf{O}_1\mathsf{O}_2$ is itself a closure operator whenever the operators commute in the appropriate sense. This framework is then applied to prove that both $\mathsf{MP}(\mathcal{A})$ and $\mathsf{SMP}(\mathcal{A})$ are quasivarieties, using the idempotence relation $\mathsf{SMPSMP} \subset \mathsf{SMP}$ and result 4.22.
