section: "2. If every equalizer is in M"
exercise_number: "7"

Let $(\mathsf{E}, \mathsf{M})$ be an image factorization system on $\mathcal{K}$ and let $\mathbf{T}$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$ such that $T$ preserves $\mathsf{E}$. Let $(X, \xi)$ be a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra and let $m : A \longrightarrow X$ be in $\mathsf{M}$. Show that $\langle A \rangle = \operatorname{Im}(A \to X)$, the image of the inclusion of the subalgebra generated by $A$, is the smallest $\mathbf{T}$-subalgebra of $(X, \xi)$ whose underlying object admits an $\mathsf{M}$-morphism from $A$.
