Let $\mathbf{T}$ be an algebraic theory in an arbitrary category $\mathcal{K}$. A $\mathbf{T}$-equation is an arbitrary pair of morphisms of form $e, e' : K \longrightarrow AT$. A $\mathbf{T}$-algebra $(X, \xi)$ satisfies $e = e'$ if for every $f : A \longrightarrow X$ we have $e \cdot f^{\#} = e' \cdot f^{\#}$. A full replete subcategory of $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ is equational if it consists of all $\mathbf{T}$-algebras satisfying some class of equations. Prove the following version of Birkhoff's theorem (cf. [Hatcher '70], [Herrlich and Ringel '72]): If $(\mathcal{K}, \mathsf{E}, \mathsf{M})$ is a regular category with $\mathsf{E}$ the class of all coequalizers, then the $\mathsf{E}$-Birkhoff subcategories coincide with the equational classes in $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ for any $\mathbf{T}$ such that $T$ preserves $\mathsf{E}$. [Hint: if $\mathcal{B}$ is $\mathsf{E}$-Birkhoff, the appropriate equations are the kernel pairs of the $A\lambda'$s.]
