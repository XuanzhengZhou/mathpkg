Let $\mathcal{B}$ of $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ be an $\mathsf{E}$-Birkhoff subcategory of $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ if $\mathcal{B}$ is an abstract Birkhoff subcategory of $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ such that the reflection $X\lambda : (XT, X\mu) \to (X\bar{T}, \xi_X)$ of each free $\mathbf{T}$-algebra is such that $X\lambda : XT \to X\bar{T}$ is (not only an epimorphism, but) in $\mathsf{E}$. The following two statements are true:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathcal{B}$ is an $\mathsf{E}$-Birkhoff subcategory of $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ if and only if $\mathcal{B}$ is closed under limits, closed under $\mathsf{M}$ (i.e., closed under $\mathsf{M}^{\mathbf{T}}$ in the sense of 4.17), and closed under $\mathsf{U}^{\mathbf{T}}$-split epimorphisms.

\item If every equalizer is in $\mathsf{M}$ then $\mathcal{B}$ is an $\mathsf{E}$-Birkhoff subcategory of $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ if and only if $\mathcal{B}$ is closed under products, closed under $\mathsf{M}$, and closed under $\mathsf{U}^{\mathbf{T}}$-split epimorphisms.
\end{enumerate}
