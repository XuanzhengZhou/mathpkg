Let $\mathcal{K}$ be the category of topological spaces and continuous maps. Let $\mathsf{E}$ be the class of continuous surjections (= epimorphisms) and let $\mathsf{M}$ be the class of optimal injections (= equalizers). Then $(\mathcal{K}, \mathsf{E}, \mathsf{M})$ is a regular category in which every equalizer is in $\mathsf{M}$. Let $G$ be a topological group and let $\mathbf{T}$ be the algebraic theory of 1.3.7 whose algebras (1.4.16) are topological transformation groups. If $f: X \longrightarrow Y$ is a continuous surjection then $f \times G: X \times G \longrightarrow Y \times G$ is also a continuous surjection, so that $\mathbf{T}$ preserves $\mathsf{E}$. The $\mathsf{E}$-Birkhoff subcategories of the category of $G$-transformation groups are thus characterized by Theorem 4.25.
