Example 4.26 illustrates the theory of $\mathsf{E}$-Birkhoff subcategories in the concrete setting of topological transformation groups. The category of topological spaces with continuous surjections as $\mathsf{E}$ and optimal injections as $\mathsf{M}$ forms a regular category where every equalizer is in $\mathsf{M}$. For a topological group $G$, the algebraic theory whose algebras are $G$-transformation groups satisfies the condition that $\mathbf{T}$ preserves $\mathsf{E}$ (since the product with $G$ preserves surjections), so that Theorem 4.25 characterizes the $\mathsf{E}$-Birkhoff subcategories of $G$-spaces.
