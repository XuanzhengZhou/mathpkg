Theorem 5.2 establishes that the pullback $(\mathcal{P}, U_1)$ of a fibre-complete structured category $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ along an algebraic structured category $(\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}, U^{\mathbf{T}})$ is itself algebraic over $\mathcal{A}$. Concretely, $\mathcal{P}$ is the category whose objects over $(K, s) \in \mathcal{A}$ consist of all $\mathbf{T}$-algebra structures $\xi$ on $K$ compatible with the $\mathcal{A}$-structure. This result generalizes the slogan that "topological algebras form an algebraic category over topological spaces" to arbitrary fibre-complete base categories.
