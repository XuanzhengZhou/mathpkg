Let $(\mathcal{A}, U) \in \operatorname{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ be fibre complete and let $(\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}, U^{\mathbf{T}}) \in \operatorname{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ be algebraic. Consider $(\mathcal{P}, W) = (\mathcal{A}, U) \times (\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}, U^{\mathbf{T}})$ in $\operatorname{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ (2.3.7) and the associated pullback diagram in the category of categories and functors

$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
\mathcal{P} & \xrightarrow{U_2} & \mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}} \\
{\scriptstyle U_1}\downarrow & & \downarrow {\scriptstyle U^{\mathbf{T}}} \\
\mathcal{A} & \xrightarrow{U} & \mathcal{K}
\end{array}
$$

(cf. 2.3.35). Clearly $(\mathcal{P}, U_1)$ is in $\operatorname{Struct}(\mathcal{A})$ via $\mathcal{P}(K, s) = \{\xi : (K, \xi) \text{ is a } \mathbf{T}\text{-algebra}\}$ and, similarly, $(\mathcal{P}, U_2)$ is in $\operatorname{Struct}(\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}})$. It is easily seen that $(\mathcal{P}, U_2)$ is fibre complete over $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ (cf. 2.3.36). Then $(\mathcal{P}, U_1)$ is algebraic.
