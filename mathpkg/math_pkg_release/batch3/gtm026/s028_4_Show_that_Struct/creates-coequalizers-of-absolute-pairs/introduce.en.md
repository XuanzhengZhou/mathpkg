Definition 1.8 formalizes the lifting property that characterizes algebraic functors: a functor $U$ creates coequalizers of $U$-absolute pairs if every absolute coequalizer diagram in the base category $\mathcal{K}$, when restricted along $U$, lifts uniquely to a coequalizer in $\mathcal{A}$. This is one of the key equivalent conditions in Beck's Characterization Theorem (1.9). The notion captures the idea that $\mathcal{A}$-structure is "algebraic" over $\mathcal{K}$ — coequalizers that are equationally forced in $\mathcal{K}$ can be uniquely endowed with $\mathcal{A}$-structure.
