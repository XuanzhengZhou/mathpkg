A congruence on an object $A$ in a category of sets with structure $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ is an equivalence relation $R$ on the underlying set $AU$ that can be endowed with $\mathcal{A}$-structure making the coordinate projections admissible. This generalizes the classical notion of a congruence in universal algebra (an equivalence relation compatible with the operations) to the abstract setting of structured categories. In an algebraic category, one expects that the canonical projection $A \to AU/R$ lifts uniquely to a co-optimal morphism in $\mathcal{A}$, yielding the quotient algebra.
