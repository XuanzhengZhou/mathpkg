Let $\mathbf{T}$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathbf{Set}$ and fix a set $I$ and an element $p$ of $I\mathbf{T}$. For $f, g: I \longrightarrow X$, say that $f \sim_p g$ if $f$ and $g$ agree on a support of $p$.
(a) Prove that $\sim_p$ is an equivalence relation on $X^I$.
(b) Set ${}^p X = X^I / \sim_p$. If $(X, \xi)$ is a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra show that $f \sim_p g$ implies $\langle p, f^\# \rangle = \langle p, g^\# \rangle$, so that $m(x) = \{[f] \in {}^p X : \langle p, f^\# \rangle = x\}$ is well-defined. When $\mathbf{T} = \beta$ and $p$ has no finite supports, ${}^p X$ is the ultra-product construction.
