A $\mathbf{T}$-model $(X, \xi)$ is \textbf{compact} if for every $\omega$ in $XT$ there exists $x$ in $X$ with $(\omega, x)$ in $\xi$; \textbf{Hausdorff} if whenever both $(\omega, x)$ and $(\omega, y)$ are in $\xi$ then $x = y$; \textbf{$T1$} if whenever $(\langle x, X\eta \rangle, y)$ is in $\xi$ then $y = x$. Prove that these adjectives take on their usual meaning when $\mathbf{T} = \beta$. Show that each of these properties is closed under products. Observe that the compact Hausdorff $\mathbf{T}$-models are precisely the $\mathbf{T}$-algebras.
