Lemma 1.10 is a technical but crucial result used in the proof of Beck's Characterization Theorem (1.9). It states that if a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra structure on $L$ can be "pushed forward" along an epimorphism $h$ (and its images under $T$ and $TT$) to define a map $\xi$ on $KT$, then $(K, \xi)$ is automatically a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra and $h$ is co-optimal. The lemma generalizes ideas used earlier in the book (cf. 1.2.6, 2.1.56) and provides the general mechanism for lifting coequalizers to algebra categories.
