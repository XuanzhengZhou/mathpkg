Beck's Characterization Theorem is the fundamental result in categorical universal algebra. It states that a functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{K}$ with a left adjoint is algebraic (monadic) if and only if it creates coequalizers of $U$-absolute pairs, or equivalently, of $U$-contractible pairs. This provides a purely diagrammatic test for whether a category $\mathcal{A}$ is equivalent to the category of algebras for an algebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$. The proof uses the semantics comparison functor and the contractible coequalizer associated with any algebra.
