Set $^p X = X^I / \sim_p$. If $(X, \xi)$ is a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra show that $f \sim_p g$ implies that $\langle p, f^\# \rangle = \langle p, g^\# \rangle$, so that $m(x) = \{[f] \in\, ^p X : \langle p, f^\# \rangle = x\}$ is well-defined.

When $\mathbf{T} = \beta$ and $p$ has no finite supports, $^p X$ is the well-known ultra-product construction. For $(X, \xi)$ a $\beta$-algebra, $m(x)$ is called the \textbf{monad} of $x$ in nonstandard topology. The definition of $m(x)$ extends easily to $\mathbf{T}$-models as in exercise 9.
