A functor $U$ creates coequalizers of $U$-absolute pairs if every absolute coequalizer diagram in the base category $\mathcal{K}$ that comes from applying $U$ to a parallel pair in $\mathcal{A}$ lifts uniquely to a coequalizer in $\mathcal{A}$. This property is the crucial condition in Beck's Characterization Theorem for algebraic functors, replacing the more concrete condition of creating quotients of congruences in the set-valued case.
