Let $U: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ be a functor. $U$ creates coequalizers of $U$-absolute pairs if whenever we are given a pair of maps $f, g: A \longrightarrow B$ in $\mathcal{A}$ and a morphism $h: BU \longrightarrow K$ in $\mathcal{K}$ such that $(fU, gU, h)$ is an absolute coequalizer in $\mathcal{K}$, we may conclude that $h$ has a unique lift $\bar{h}: B \longrightarrow \bar{K}$ in $\mathcal{A}$ with $\bar{h}U = h$; and, moreover, $\bar{h} = \mathrm{coeq}(f, g)$ in $\mathcal{A}$. Similarly, $U$ creates coequalizers of $U$-contractible pairs if the same holds when $(fU, gU, h)$ is a contractible coequalizer.
