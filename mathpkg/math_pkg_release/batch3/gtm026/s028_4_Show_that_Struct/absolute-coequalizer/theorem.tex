A diagram
$$\xymatrix{
A \ar@<0.5ex>[r]^{f} \ar@<-0.5ex>[r]_{g} & B \ar[r]^{h} & C
}$$
in a category $\mathcal{K}$ is an \textbf{absolute coequalizer} if for every functor $H: \mathcal{K} \to \mathcal{L}$, the image $hH = \operatorname{coeq}(fH, gH)$ in $\mathcal{L}$. Since $H$ may be the identity functor, every absolute coequalizer is a coequalizer in $\mathcal{K}$.
