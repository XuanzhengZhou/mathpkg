Let $\mathbf{T}$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$ and suppose given a commutative square
\[
\begin{array}{ccc}
L\mathbf{T} & \xrightarrow{h\mathbf{T}} & K\mathbf{T} \\
\downarrow{\gamma} & & \downarrow{\xi} \\
L & \xrightarrow{h} & K
\end{array}
\]
with $(L, \gamma)$ a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra and with $h, h\mathbf{T}$ and $h\mathbf{T}\mathbf{T}$ epi in $\mathcal{K}$. Then $(K, \xi)$ is a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra and $h: (L, \gamma) \longrightarrow (K, \xi)$ is co-optimal in $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$.
