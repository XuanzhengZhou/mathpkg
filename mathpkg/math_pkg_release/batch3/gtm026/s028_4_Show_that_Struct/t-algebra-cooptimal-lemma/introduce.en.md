This technical lemma is used in the proof of Beck's Characterization Theorem (1.9). It states that if one has a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra $(L, \gamma)$ and an epimorphic map $h: L \to K$ (with $h\mathbf{T}$ and $h\mathbf{T}\mathbf{T}$ also epi) together with a commuting square defining $\xi$, then $(K, \xi)$ is automatically a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra and $h$ is a co-optimal homomorphism. The proof is a diagram chase using the epimorphism conditions to verify the algebra axioms.
