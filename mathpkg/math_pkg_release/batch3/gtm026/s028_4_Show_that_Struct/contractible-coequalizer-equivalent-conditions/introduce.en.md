Proposition 1.6 provides a simplified characterization of contractible coequalizers: instead of requiring two contraction morphisms $d$ and $d'$, it suffices to have a single $d$ satisfying the two equations $d \cdot f = \mathrm{id}_B$ and $f \cdot d \cdot g = g \cdot d \cdot g$. The existence of $d'$ then follows from the universal property of the coequalizer. This simplification is useful when verifying the contractible coequalizer condition in concrete categories.
