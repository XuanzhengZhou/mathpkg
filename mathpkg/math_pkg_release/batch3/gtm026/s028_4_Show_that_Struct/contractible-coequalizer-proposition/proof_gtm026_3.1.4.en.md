Let $H: \mathcal{K} \longrightarrow \mathcal{L}$ be any functor. It is obvious that $(fH, gH, hH; dH, d'H)$ is again a contractible coequalizer in $\mathcal{L}$, so it suffices to prove that $h = \mathrm{coeq}(f, g)$. Suppose $f.h' = g.h'$. Then $h.d'.h' = d.g.h' = d.f.h' = h'$; the uniqueness of $d'.h'$ is secure since $h$ is (split) epi.
