This proposition simplifies the definition of a contractible coequalizer. Given that $h = \mathrm{coeq}(f, g)$, the existence of a full contraction $(d, d')$ is equivalent to the existence of a single map $d: B \to A$ satisfying $d.f = \mathrm{id}_B$ and $f.d.g = g.d.g$. The second condition is often easier to verify in practice and suffices to guarantee that the coequalizer is absolute.
