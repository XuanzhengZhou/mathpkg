Let $\mathcal{K}$ be a category and let $K$ be an object. The category $\mathcal{K}/K$ of objects over $K$ is that category in $\operatorname{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ with objects all pairs $(A, f)$ with $f: A \to K$ and admissible maps $\psi: (A, f) \to (B, g)$ all $\psi: A \to B$ satisfying $f = \psi \cdot g$ (cf. $\operatorname{Cat}/\mathcal{K}$ in exercise 5). Assume that $K \times A$ exists for all $A$. Define a coalgebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$ by $AG = K \times A$, $A\varepsilon: K \times A \to A$ the $A$-projection, $A\delta: K \times A \to K \times K \times A = \Delta \times \mathrm{id}_A$ where $\Delta: K \to K \times K$ is the "diagonal map," i.e., is $\mathrm{id}_K$ when followed by either projection. Show that the category of $\mathbf{G}$-coalgebras is isomorphic over $\mathcal{K}$ to $\mathcal{K}/K$.
