Let $H$ be as in (a) and let $J: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}$ be a functor over $\mathcal{K}$ with $\mathcal{D}$ fibre complete. Define $\Gamma$ by $\alpha\Gamma = \sigma$ in $\mathcal{D}(K)$ such that the family $a: (L, t)J \to (K, \sigma)$ indexed by all $(L, t, a)$ with $[L, t, a] = \alpha$ is co-optimal in $\mathcal{D}$. Show that $\Gamma$ is a functor over $\mathcal{K}$ satisfying $H\Gamma = J$ and "if $\Gamma'$ is another functor over $\mathcal{K}$ with $H\Gamma' = J$ then $\Gamma \leqslant \Gamma'$." It is an open question how to characterize any of the fibre-completion constructions by universal properties involving functors over $\mathcal{K}$.
