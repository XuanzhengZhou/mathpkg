In the context of nondeterministic automata, the simplest measure of the "degree of certainty" that two elements are equal is given by subsets: we know that a state $q$ belongs to some subset $A$ of the state set $Q$. This gives rise to an algebraic theory where the algebras are sets with a distinguished base point, corresponding to the nonempty subset theory. This approach provides the foundation for modeling nondeterminism via algebraic theories, later extended to the dynamics of the form $\delta: Q \times X \longrightarrow QT$ where $QT$ is the set of nonempty subsets of $Q$.
