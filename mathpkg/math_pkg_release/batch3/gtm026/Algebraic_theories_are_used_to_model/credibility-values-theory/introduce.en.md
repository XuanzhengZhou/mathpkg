Given a monoid $M$ whose elements serve as "credibility values," one can construct an associated algebraic theory $T_M$ in $\mathbf{Set}$ as described in Section 1.3. A fuzzy morphism $\alpha: A \to B \times M$ is interpreted so that $a\alpha = (b, m)$ means "$a$ maps to $b$ with credibility $m$." The composition of credibilities is governed by the monoid multiplication in $M$, making the unit interval under multiplication a particularly natural choice for $M$.
