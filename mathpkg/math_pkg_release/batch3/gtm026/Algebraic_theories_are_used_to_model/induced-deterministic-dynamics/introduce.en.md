Given a multiple-branching (nondeterministic) dynamics $\delta: Q \times X \to QT$, where $\mathbf{T}$ is a fuzzy theory, one can construct an induced deterministic dynamics $\delta^\bullet: QT \times X \to QT$ on the set of nonempty subsets of states. The construction factors through the canonical map $Q\lambda$ followed by $\delta^\#$, the unique union-preserving extension of $\delta$ to $(Q \times X)T$. This abstraction captures the dynamics of the multiple-branching machine: the state set is enlarged from $Q$ to $QT$, but the new transition function becomes an ordinary (single-valued) map, recovering determinism at the level of subsets.
