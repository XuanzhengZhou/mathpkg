This result follows immediately from Theorems 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4 (the Birkhoff Variety Theorem).

By Theorem 3.2, the common reflection data $(XT', \xi_X; X\lambda)$ determines the structure of both $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$ as subcategories of $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$. Theorem 3.3 characterizes $\mathcal{A}$ (respectively $\mathcal{B}$) as those $\mathbf{T}$-algebras that admit a reflection map from their free presentation, and Theorem 3.4 establishes the equivalence between the Birkhoff closure properties and the equational characterization. Since both $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$ satisfy the same Birkhoff conditions and share the same reflection data, they must be identical.
