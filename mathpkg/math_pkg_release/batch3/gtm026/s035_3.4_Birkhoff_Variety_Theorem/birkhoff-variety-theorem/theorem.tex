Let $V : (\mathcal{A}, U) \longrightarrow (\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}, U^{\mathbf{T}})$ be a homomorphism in $\mathrm{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$. Then the following two statements are equivalent.

\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathcal{A}$ is a full subcategory of $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ closed under the formation of products, subalgebras, and quotients; that is, $V$ is an abstract Birkhoff subcategory of $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$.
\item $\mathcal{A}$ is a full subcategory of $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ obtained by imposing additional equations to the operations and equations that present $\mathbf{T}$-algebras; that is, $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ is algebraic and the theory map $\lambda : \mathbf{T} \longrightarrow \mathbf{T}'$ corresponding to $V$ is such that $X\lambda$ is epi in $\mathcal{K}$ for all $X$, where $\mathbf{T}'$ is the theory for which $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ and $(\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}'}, U^{\mathbf{T}'})$ are isomorphic (unique up to isomorphism).
\end{enumerate}
