The Birkhoff Variety Theorem is the categorical generalization of the classical Birkhoff HSP theorem. It establishes the equivalence between two characterizations of varieties of algebras: the semantic (closure under homomorphic images, subalgebras, and products) and the syntactic (equationally definable classes). This theorem is formulated in the general setting of a category $\mathcal{K}$ with an algebraic theory $\mathbf{T}$, where varieties correspond to abstract Birkhoff subcategories of the Eilenberg-Moore category $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$. The proof uses the theory of reflections and split epimorphisms developed in the preceding sections.
