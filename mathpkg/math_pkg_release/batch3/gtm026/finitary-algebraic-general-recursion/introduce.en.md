The Principle of Finitary Algebraic General Recursion provides the fundamental tool for defining functions on the term algebra $A\Omega$. Because Omega-terms are freely generated from the set $A$ of variables using the operators in $\Omega$, any function on $A\Omega$ is uniquely determined by its values on the generators (variables) and a recursive rule specifying how to compute the value of a compound term $p_1 \cdots p_n \omega$ from the values of its subterms $p_i$ and the operator $\omega$. This is the universal algebra analogue of primitive recursion in arithmetic and structural induction in logic, and it justifies the standard technique of defining interpretations, substitutions, and evaluation maps by recursion on term structure.
