An implicit $\lambda$-automaton generalizes the $M^\bullet$ construction of Section 3.9 to provide a state-free algebraic formulation of a fuzzy system. It is defined with respect to a fixed input/output process $X: \mathcal{H} \to \mathcal{H}$, an algebraic theory $\mathbf{T} = (T, e, m)$ in $\mathcal{H}$, and a distributive law $\lambda: TX \to XT$ of $X$ over $\mathbf{T}$. The state object $\bar{Q}$ carries a $\lambda$-algebra structure $(\bar{Q}, \bar{\delta}, \xi)$, an input morphism $\bar{\tau}: I \to \bar{Q}$, and an output $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphism $\bar{\beta}: (\bar{Q}, \xi) \to (Y, \theta)$. When $\mathbf{T}$ is the identity theory and $\lambda$ is the identity, the notion reduces to that of an $X$-automaton.
