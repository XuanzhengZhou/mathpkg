A process $X$ is called an output process when the forgetful functor $U: \operatorname{Dyn}(X) \to \mathcal{K}$ possesses a right adjoint. This adjointness condition guarantees the existence of cofree $X$-dynamics, which yields the observability map (the unique dynamorphic coextension of the output morphism). Intuitively, an output process allows one to characterize the minimal realization of an automaton's external behavior.
