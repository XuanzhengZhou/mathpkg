Let $\mathcal{K}$ be an arbitrary category. For functors $F, G: \mathcal{K} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$, define $F \otimes G = FG$ (their usual composition). For natural transformations $\alpha: F \longrightarrow F'$ and $\beta: G \longrightarrow G'$, define their \text{vertical composition} $\alpha \otimes \beta$ by:
$$
F\beta \cdot \alpha G' = \alpha \otimes \beta = \alpha G \cdot F'\beta
$$
where $F\beta$, $\alpha G'$, $\alpha G$, and $F'\beta$ are the whiskering operations. This yields a well-defined functor
$$
\otimes: \mathcal{K}^\mathcal{K} \times \mathcal{K}^\mathcal{K} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}^\mathcal{K}
$$
which is strictly associative on objects and morphisms.
