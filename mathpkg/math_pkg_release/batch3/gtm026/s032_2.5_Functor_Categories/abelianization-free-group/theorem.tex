The abelianization of a free group provides an example of a theory map. Let $\mathcal{K}$ be the category of sets. The theory $T$ of groups and the theory $T'$ of abelian groups are both algebraic theories in $\mathcal{K}$. The natural transformation sending a free group to its abelianization (i.e., quotient by the commutator subgroup) defines a theory map $\lambda: T \longrightarrow T'$.
