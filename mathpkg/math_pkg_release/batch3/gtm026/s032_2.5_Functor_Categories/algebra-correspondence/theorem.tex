Let $T$ and $T'$ be algebraic theories in $\mathcal{K}$ and let $\lambda: T \longrightarrow T'$ be a theory map. Then $\lambda$ induces a functor
$$
V: \mathcal{K}^{T'} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}^T
$$
between the categories of algebras. Conversely, there is also a functor in the opposite direction. The two constructions are mutually inverse in the sense that they establish a correspondence between $T$-algebras and $T'$-algebras compatible with the theory map.
