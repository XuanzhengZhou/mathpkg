Proposition 2.8 provides two equivalent ways to understand theory maps. The first condition describes them as natural transformations compatible with the monoid structure (unit and multiplication). The second, equivalent condition expresses the same compatibility in terms of Kleisli composition, which is the clone-form perspective on algebraic theories. This equivalence bridges the monoid-form and clone-form descriptions of theory morphisms.
