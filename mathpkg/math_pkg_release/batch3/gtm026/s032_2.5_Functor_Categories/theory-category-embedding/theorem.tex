Let $\mathrm{Th}(\mathcal{K})$ be the category of algebraic theories $T$ in $\mathcal{K}$ and theory maps $\lambda: T \longrightarrow T'$, and let $\mathrm{Alg}(\mathcal{K})$ be the full subcategory of $\mathrm{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ consisting of all algebraic functors. Then the passages of Proposition 2.9 establish a full representative subcategory
$$
(\mathrm{Th}(\mathcal{K}))^{\mathrm{op}} \longrightarrow \mathrm{Alg}(\mathcal{K}).
$$
The preservation of identities and composition is immediate:
$$
X\lambda \cdot \xi = \xi \quad \text{if } X\lambda = \mathrm{id}_{XT},
$$
$$
(X\lambda \cdot X\lambda') \cdot \xi'' = X\lambda \cdot (X\lambda' \cdot \xi'').
$$
