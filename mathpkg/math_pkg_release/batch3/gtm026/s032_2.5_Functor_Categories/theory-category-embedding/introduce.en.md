Theorem 2.10 is a central structural result: the opposite of the category of algebraic theories embeds as a full representative subcategory into the category of algebraic functors. This means that every algebraic theory corresponds to an algebraic functor (its category of algebras with the forgetful functor), and theory maps correspond contravariantly to morphisms of algebraic functors. The theorem provides the rigorous link between the syntactic world of theories and the semantic world of algebras.
