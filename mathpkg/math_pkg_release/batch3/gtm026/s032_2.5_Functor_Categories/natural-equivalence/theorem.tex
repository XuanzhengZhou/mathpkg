The isomorphisms in the functor category $\mathcal{K}^\mathcal{A}$ are called \text{natural equivalences} (or natural isomorphisms). A natural transformation $\alpha: H_1 \longrightarrow H_2$ is a natural equivalence if and only if each of its components $\alpha_A: H_1(A) \longrightarrow H_2(A)$ is an isomorphism in $\mathcal{K}$.
