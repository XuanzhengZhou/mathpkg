Prove that $(\mathcal{K}^\mathcal{K}, \mathrm{comp}, \mathrm{id}_\mathcal{K}, \mathrm{id}, \mathrm{id}, \mathrm{id})$ is a monoidal category from the Godement rules (the five rules of the Godement calculus governing horizontal and vertical composition of natural transformations), and conversely that the Godement rules follow from the monoidal structure.
