A \text{theory map} $\lambda: T \longrightarrow T'$ between algebraic theories $T = (T, \eta, \mu)$ and $T' = (T', \eta', \mu')$ in $\mathcal{K}$ is a natural transformation $\lambda: T \longrightarrow T'$ such that the following two diagrams commute:
$$
\begin{CD}
\mathrm{id}_\mathcal{K} @= \mathrm{id}_\mathcal{K} \\
@V{\eta}VV @VV{\eta'}V \\
T @>>{\lambda}> T'
\end{CD}
\qquad
\begin{CD}
TT @>{\lambda \lambda}>> T'T' \\
@V{\mu}VV @VV{\mu'}V \\
T @>>{\lambda}> T'
\end{CD}
$$
The composition of theory maps $\lambda: T \longrightarrow T'$ and $\lambda': T' \longrightarrow T''$ is the vertical composition of natural transformations $\lambda \cdot \lambda': T \longrightarrow T''$.
