If $T$ is an algebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$, then the unit $\eta: \mathrm{id}_\mathcal{K} \longrightarrow T$ is a theory map
$$
\eta: (\mathrm{id}_\mathcal{K}, \mathrm{id}, \mathrm{id}) \longrightarrow T
$$
from the identity theory to $T$. The corresponding homomorphism of algebraic functors induced by Proposition 2.9 is precisely the forgetful functor $U^T: \mathcal{K}^T \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$.
