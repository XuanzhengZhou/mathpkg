Given a monoidal category $(\mathcal{K}, \otimes, I, a, b, c)$, the collection of all monoids in $\mathcal{K}$, together with monoid homomorphisms as morphisms, itself forms a category. Composition and identities are inherited from the underlying category $\mathcal{K}$. This result is fundamental to the categorical study of algebraic structures.
