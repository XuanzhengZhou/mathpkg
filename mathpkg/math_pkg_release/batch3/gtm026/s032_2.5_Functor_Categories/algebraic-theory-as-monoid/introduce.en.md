In the endofunctor category $\mathcal{K}^\mathcal{K}$ with composition as the monoidal product, a monoid object is precisely an algebraic theory $(T, \eta, \mu)$ in monoid form. The multiplication $\mu: TT \to T$ is the monoid multiplication, and the unit $\eta: \mathrm{id} \to T$ is the monoid unit. This perspective unifies the theory of monads with algebraic theories and shows that algebraic theories in a category form a category. This is a central insight of the book.
