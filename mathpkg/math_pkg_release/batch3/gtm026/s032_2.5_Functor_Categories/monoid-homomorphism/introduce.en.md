A monoid homomorphism is a morphism between two monoids in a monoidal category that preserves the monoid structure. It requires that the multiplication be preserved under the tensor product of the homomorphism with itself, and that the unit element maps to the unit element. With the evident composition and identities, monoid homomorphisms make the collection of monoids in a monoidal category into a category.
