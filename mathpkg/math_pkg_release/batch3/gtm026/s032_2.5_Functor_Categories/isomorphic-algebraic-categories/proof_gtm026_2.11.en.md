This follows directly from Theorem 2.10. If $T \cong T'$ in $\mathrm{Th}(\mathcal{K})$, then their images under the embedding $(\mathrm{Th}(\mathcal{K}))^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathrm{Alg}(\mathcal{K})$ are isomorphic in $\mathrm{Alg}(\mathcal{K})$, meaning $(\mathcal{K}^T, U^T) \cong (\mathcal{K}^{T'}, U^{T'})$ as algebraic functors. Since $(\mathcal{A}, U) \cong (\mathcal{K}^T, U^T)$ and $(\mathcal{A}', U') \cong (\mathcal{K}^{T'}, U^{T'})$ by hypothesis, we obtain $(\mathcal{A}, U) \cong (\mathcal{A}', U')$.

Conversely, if $(\mathcal{A}, U) \cong (\mathcal{A}', U')$, then $(\mathcal{K}^T, U^T) \cong (\mathcal{K}^{T'}, U^{T'})$. Since the embedding of Theorem 2.10 is fully faithful (being a full representative subcategory), the isomorphism of algebraic functors must come from an isomorphism of theories $T \cong T'$ in $\mathrm{Th}(\mathcal{K})$.
