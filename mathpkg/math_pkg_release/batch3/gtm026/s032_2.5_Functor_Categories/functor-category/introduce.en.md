A functor category $\mathcal{K}^\mathcal{A}$ collects all functors from a category $\mathcal{A}$ to a category $\mathcal{K}$ as its objects, with natural transformations serving as morphisms. Composition of morphisms is given by horizontal composition of natural transformations, and identity morphisms are the identity natural transformations. Functor categories are a fundamental construction in category theory, generalizing the idea of function spaces to the categorical setting.
