The functors between two categories $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{K}$ form a category $\mathcal{K}^\mathcal{A}$, called a \text{functor category}. Specifically:
\begin{itemize}
\item The objects of $\mathcal{K}^\mathcal{A}$ are functors $H: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$.
\item A morphism $\alpha: H_1 \longrightarrow H_2$ is a natural transformation.
\item Composition is horizontal composition (as in the Godement calculus).
\item The identity on a functor $H$ is given by $A(\mathrm{id}_H) = \mathrm{id}_{AH}$ for all objects $A \in \mathcal{A}$.
\end{itemize}
