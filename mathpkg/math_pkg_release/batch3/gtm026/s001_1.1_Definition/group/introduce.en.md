A group, in the framework of finitary universal algebra, is a set equipped with three operations: a binary multiplication, a unary inversion, and a nullary unit element. These operations are required to satisfy the familiar axioms of associativity, identity, and inverses. What distinguishes this definition is its expression in Polish (parenthesis-free) notation, where terms like $x\,y\,m$ denote $m(x, y)$ and $x\,i$ denotes $i(x)$. The definition is an equational presentation, meaning the group axioms are expressed purely as equations between terms built from the operation symbols.
