Let $A$ be a set. A word in $A$ is an $n$-tuple of elements of $A$ with $n$ an integer $> 0$; $n$ is the length of the word. We write $a_1 a_2 \cdots a_n$ instead of $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$.

Let $\Omega$ be an operator domain and set $|\Omega| = \bigcup_{n \geq 0} \Omega_n$. An $\Omega$-word in $A$ is a word in the disjoint union $A + |\Omega|$, where the disjoint union is defined as
$$
X + Y = (X \times \{0\}) \cup (Y \times \{1\}).
$$
