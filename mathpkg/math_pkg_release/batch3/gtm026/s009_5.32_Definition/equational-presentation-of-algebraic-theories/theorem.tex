Let $T$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathbf{Set}$. Then there exists an equational presentation $(\Omega, E)$ as in 5.34, with $\Omega_n = \varnothing$ if $n \notin \mathrm{Law}(T)$ (see 5.35), and every equation $n$-ary for some $n \in \mathrm{Law}(T)$, such that $T$-algebras and $(\Omega, E)$-algebras are isomorphic as categories of sets with structure (as defined in the statement of 4.15).
