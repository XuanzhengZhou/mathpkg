The Lawvere theory $\mathrm{Law}(T)$ of an algebraic theory $T$ in $\mathbf{Set}$ is a category that encodes the finitary (or bounded infinitary) operations of $T$. Its objects are cardinal numbers (all cardinals if $T$ is unbounded, or those below the regular rank if $T$ is bounded), and morphisms $\alpha: m \to n$ correspond to $n$-tuples of $m$-ary operations, defined as functions $\alpha: n \to mT$ in the Kleisli category $\mathbf{Set}_T$. Composition is inherited from the Kleisli composition, using the multiplication $\mu$ of the monad $T$.
