4.10 Diagonal Fill-In Proposition. Given the commutative square

with $e \in E$ and $m \in M$ there exists unique $h$ with $e.h = f$ and $h.m = g$.

The uniqueness assertion is clear since $e$ is epi (4.3) or since $m$ is mono and, in fact, either triangle implies the other. To establish existence let $(e_1, m_1)$ be the E-M factorization of $g$ and let $(e_2, m_2)$ be the E-M factorization of $f$ as shown below. Then $(e_2, m_2 m)$ and $(ee_1, m_1)$ are both E-M

factorizations of $fm = eg$ giving rise to $\psi$ as shown by 4.5. Now define $h: B \longrightarrow C = e_1.\psi.m_2$.
