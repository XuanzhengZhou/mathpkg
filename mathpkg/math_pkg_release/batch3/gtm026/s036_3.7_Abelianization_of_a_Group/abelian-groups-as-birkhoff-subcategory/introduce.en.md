The abelianization of a group is captured categorically by the observation that **abelian groups** form a **Birkhoff subcategory** of the category of **groups**. This means the full subcategory of abelian groups is closed under products, subalgebras, and quotient algebras within the category of all groups. The result follows immediately from the characterization of Birkhoff subcategories given in 1.4.22 (equational definability) and the fact that abelian groups are exactly the groups satisfying the commutativity equation $xy = yx$, together with the general theory of Birkhoff subcategories developed in 2.13 via 3.4.
