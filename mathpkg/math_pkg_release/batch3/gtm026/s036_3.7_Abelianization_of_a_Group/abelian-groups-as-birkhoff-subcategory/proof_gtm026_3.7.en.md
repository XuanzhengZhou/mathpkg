By Theorem 1.4.22, a subcategory of a category of algebras is Birkhoff if and only if it is equationally definable. The abelian groups are precisely the groups satisfying the additional equation $xy = yx$ (commutativity). Hence abelian groups form an equationally defined subclass of groups, and therefore a Birkhoff subcategory.

The same conclusion also follows from 2.13 via Theorem 3.4: the category of abelian groups is the category of algebras for the theory obtained from the theory of groups by imposing the commutativity equation; the quotient theory map corresponds to a Birkhoff subcategory by the correspondence established in 3.4.
