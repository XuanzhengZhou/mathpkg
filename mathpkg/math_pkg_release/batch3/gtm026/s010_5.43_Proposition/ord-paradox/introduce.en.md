The Burali-Forti paradox (stated here as the Ord paradox) shows that the collection of all ordinals cannot be a set: if it were, it would itself be an ordinal and therefore an element of itself, contradicting the well-foundedness of the membership relation on ordinals. The resolution is that $\mathbf{Ord}$ is a proper class, not a set, highlighting the need for a distinction between sets and classes in formal set theory.
