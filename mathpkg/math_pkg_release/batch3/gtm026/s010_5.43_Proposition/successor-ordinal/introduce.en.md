A successor ordinal is one that has an immediate predecessor. In von Neumann's construction, the successor of an ordinal $y$ is $s(y) = y \cup \{y\}$. This corresponds to the usual successor operation on natural numbers: $0 = \emptyset$, $1 = \{\emptyset\}$, $2 = \{\emptyset, \{\emptyset\}\}$, and so on.
