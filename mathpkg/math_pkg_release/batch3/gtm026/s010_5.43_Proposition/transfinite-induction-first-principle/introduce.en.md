The first principle of transfinite induction (Monk 10.1) generalizes ordinary mathematical induction to all ordinals. To define a function $f$ on an ordinal $x$, it is sufficient to specify $f(y)$ in terms of the values of $f$ on all ordinals $z < y$. This principle is justified by the fact that every ordinal equals the set of all smaller ordinals, so assuming the definition on all predecessors uniquely determines the value at $y$.
