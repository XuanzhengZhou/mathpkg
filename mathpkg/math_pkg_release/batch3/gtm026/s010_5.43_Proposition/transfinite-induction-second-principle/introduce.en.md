The second principle of transfinite induction (Monk 10.4) breaks the definition into three cases mirroring the structure of ordinals: the zero case, the successor case, and the limit case. This formulation is more practical for concrete constructions, as it separates the successor step (analogous to ordinary induction) from the limit step (where values are synthesized from all preceding values).
