This follows from the first principle of transfinite induction by case analysis on the ordinal $y$. Every ordinal is either $0$, a successor, or a limit ordinal. The three clauses cover all three cases, and by the first principle, specifying $f(y)$ in each case uniquely determines $f$ on the entire ordinal.
