Use Zorn's lemma to prove that for any two sets $X, Y$, either $X$ admits an injection into $Y$ or $Y$ admits an injection into $X$. [Proof outline: use the partially ordered set of all $(A, f)$ with $A \subset X$ and $f: A \longrightarrow Y$ injective; show that a maximal $(A, f)$ is such that $f$ is onto or $A = X$.]
