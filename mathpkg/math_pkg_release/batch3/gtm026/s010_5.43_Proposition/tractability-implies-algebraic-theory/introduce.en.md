Theorem 5.45 establishes that tractability is both necessary and sufficient for an equational presentation to be realizable as the category of algebras for some algebraic theory $T$ in $\mathbf{Set}$. When $(\Omega, E)$ is tractable, there exists a theory $T$ such that the category of $T$-algebras is isomorphic, as a concrete category over $\mathbf{Set}$, to the category of $(\Omega, E)$-algebras. This is a central result linking syntactic presentations to semantic theories.
