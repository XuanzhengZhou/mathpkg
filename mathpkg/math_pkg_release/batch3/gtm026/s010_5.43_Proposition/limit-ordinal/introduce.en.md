A limit ordinal is a nonzero ordinal that is not the successor of any ordinal. Equivalently, a limit ordinal has no greatest element. The smallest limit ordinal is $\omega$, the set of all natural numbers. Limit ordinals play a crucial role in transfinite induction, where they require a separate clause beyond the base and successor cases.
