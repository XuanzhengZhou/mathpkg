Prove that any variety (cf. exercise 6+ of section 4) of $\beta$-algebras which contains at least one algebra having two or more elements must be the variety of all $\beta$-algebras. [Hint: let $2$ be the unique two-element $\beta$-algebra; show that the inclusion $X\beta \subset 2^{(2^X)}$ is a $\beta$-subalgebra.]
