Every ordinal $x$ satisfies $x = \{y : y \text{ is an ordinal and } y < x\}$ (see Monk 9.13 (iii)). This representation, together with the axiom of regularity, yields the trichotomy property. The well-foundedness of the $\in$-relation on ordinals implies that any nonempty set $X$ of ordinals has an $\in$-minimal element, which turns out to be the intersection $\bigcap X$.
