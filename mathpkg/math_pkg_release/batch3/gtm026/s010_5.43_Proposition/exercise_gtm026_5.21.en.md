Let $T$ be an algebraic theory. A cardinal $n$ is a **generating cardinal** for $T$ if every element of $AT$ (for any set $A$) can be expressed as $\alpha \circ \beta$ where $\alpha \in BT$ for some set $B$ with $\text{card}(B) \leqslant n$ and $\beta: A \to B$.

(c) Prove that "generating rank $\leqslant$ rank."

(d) Let $n$ be a generating cardinal for $T$ and let $\xi: XT \longrightarrow X$ satisfy "$X\eta \circ \xi = \text{id}_X$" and "for all $\alpha, \beta: A \longrightarrow XT$ with $\text{card}(A) < n^\bullet$, if $\alpha \circ \xi = \beta \circ \xi$ then $\alpha^\# \circ \xi = \beta^\# \circ \xi$". Prove that $(X, \xi)$ is a $T$-algebra. [Hint: use exercise 4.11; let $AT_k$ be the subset of those elements of $AT$ which admit a factorization of size $k$; prove by induction on $k$ that for arbitrary $A$ and $\alpha, \beta: A \longrightarrow XT$, if $\alpha \circ \xi = \beta \circ \xi$ then $\langle p, \alpha^\# \circ \xi \rangle = \langle p, \beta^\# \circ \xi \rangle$ for all $p \in AT_k$.]
