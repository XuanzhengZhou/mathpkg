A functor $U: A \to \mathbf{Set}$ is tractable if it satisfies the size condition of being tractable at every cardinal $n$. Tractability guarantees that natural transformations between powers of $U$ always form small sets, preventing proper-class pathologies. For any algebraic theory $T$ in $\mathbf{Set}$, the forgetful functor $U^T$ is tractable by Theorem 5.5.
