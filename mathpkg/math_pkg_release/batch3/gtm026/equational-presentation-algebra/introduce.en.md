An equational presentation $(\Omega, E)$ formalizes how classes of algebraic structures are defined by operations and equations. Given an operator domain $\Omega$ specifying the arity of each operation symbol, and a set $E$ of $\Omega$-equations (pairs of $\Omega$-terms), an $(\Omega, E)$-algebra is an $\Omega$-algebra whose operations satisfy all equations in $E$ under all variable assignments. The equation satisfaction condition is defined via interpretation maps constructed by finitary algebraic general recursion. The class of all $(\Omega, E)$-algebras is called a variety or equationally-definable class.
