[Manes '72.] Let $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ in Struct$(\mathbf{Set})$ be fibre complete, let $T$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathbf{Set}$ and let $\mathcal{C}$ be a full replete subcategory of $(\mathcal{A}, U) \times (\mathbf{Set}^{\mathbf{T}}, U^{\mathbf{T}})$. Let $\mathcal{L}$ be a full replete subcategory of $\mathcal{A}$ such that if $f: (X, s) \longrightarrow (Y, t)$ is an admissible surjection in $\mathcal{A}$ with $(X, s)$ in $\mathcal{L}$ then also $(Y, t)$ is in $\mathcal{L}$.

Assume further that whenever $(Y, t, \theta)$ is in MP$(\mathcal{C})$ and $f: (X, s) \longrightarrow (Y, t)$ is admissible in $\mathcal{A}$ with $(X, s)$ in $\mathcal{L}$ there exists an $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\bar{s}$ on $XT$ such that $f^{\#}: (XT, \bar{s}) \longrightarrow (Y, t)$ is admissible in $\mathcal{A}$. Prove that $U$ has a left adjoint, $V$ is algebraic, and $\Phi$ is the semantic comparison of $U$; moreover, $\Phi$ is a full reflective subcategory. [Hint: use 1.36; the assumptions guarantee that the $\bar{T}$ of 5.3 maps $\mathcal{L}$ into $\mathcal{L}$; the left adjoint to $\Phi$ works by restricting the left adjoint SMP$(\mathcal{C}) \longrightarrow$ MP$(\mathcal{C})$.]
