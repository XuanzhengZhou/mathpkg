A partially ordered group endows a group with a partial order in a way compatible with the group operation. The two axioms ensure that the order is translation-invariant (multiplying on either side preserves inequalities) and that inversion reverses the order. This structure generalizes familiar examples such as the additive group of real numbers with the usual order. Partially ordered groups provide the natural setting for studying algebraic theories over ordered sets, as they form an algebraic category over the category of partially ordered sets.
