(b) Show that a standard presentation of "monoid" can be recovered.

[*Hint:* Since $U: \mathcal{C} \longrightarrow \text{Set}$ is given, the required $n$-ary operations will be natural transformations $U^n \longrightarrow U$; there are only two 2-ary operations which are not constant and are associative (because each such operation is a word on two symbols containing say $n$ occurrences of the first and $m$ of the second, and the equations imposed on $n$ and $m$ by associativity are very restrictive---it is perfectly valid to reason first in the "standard" category so long as all transits under isomorphism are kept track of).]
