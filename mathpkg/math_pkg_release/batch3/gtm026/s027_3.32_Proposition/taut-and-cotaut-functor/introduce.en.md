A functor $H: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}$ in $\text{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ is called taut if it preserves optimal families, and cotaut if it preserves co-optimal families. These notions measure how well a structure-valued functor respects the lifting properties that define the $\text{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ framework. Full replete subcategory embeddings of familiar categories of sets with structure (as in 3.33) are often taut but rarely cotaut. However, every category of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure admits a simultaneously taut and cotaut embedding into a fibre-complete category (Exercise 12).
