Definition 3.24 introduces the notion of an isolated element in a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra: an element that cannot be expressed using the algebraic operations applied to the remaining elements of the carrier set. This concept unifies several familiar mathematical notions: join-irreducibles in semilattice theory, extreme points in convex geometry (when viewing convex sets as algebras for the stochastic matrix theory), and vertices of convex polytopes. Isolated elements play a central role in the theory of extremal-state algebras studied in the remainder of Section 3.
