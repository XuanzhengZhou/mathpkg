The $T$-algebra $(\bar{Q}, \xi)$ is \textbf{extremal state} if the subset $Q$ of isolated elements of $(\bar{Q}, \xi)$ generates $(\bar{Q}, \xi)$. In this case, elements of $Q$ are called the \textbf{states} of $(\bar{Q}, \xi)$. By 3.25, such $Q$ is the unique minimal set of generators of $(\bar{Q}, \xi)$.

\textbf{Examples:}
\begin{itemize}
\item A nonzero vector space is not extremal state.
\item Every free monoid is extremal state, the states being the words of length one.
\item A quotient of an extremal-state algebra need not be extremal state (e.g., the three-element group is the submonoid generated by either of its nonunit elements).
\item A subalgebra of an extremal-state algebra may fail to be extremal state (e.g., the interior of a convex polygon has no extreme points).
\item An extremal-state algebra can sometimes be embedded in an extremal-state algebra with fewer states.
\end{itemize}
