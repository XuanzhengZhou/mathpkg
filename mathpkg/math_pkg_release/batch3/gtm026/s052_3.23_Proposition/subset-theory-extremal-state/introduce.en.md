Proposition 3.30 establishes that the subset theory $\mathbf{T}$ (whose algebras are complete semilattices) and its two natural subtheories -- finite subsets (finitely complete semilattices) and finite nonempty subsets (semilattices) -- are all extremal state. The proof uses an inductive construction of minimal elements to show that every finitely-generated $\mathbf{T}$-algebra is generated by its isolated elements, which in this context are precisely the join-irreducibles. This result is essential for the subsequent analysis of minimal realizations of fuzzy automata via the scoop construction (3.22).
