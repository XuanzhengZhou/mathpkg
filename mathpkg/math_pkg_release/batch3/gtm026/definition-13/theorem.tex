It is obvious that $\emptyset$, $X$ are open and that finite intersections and unions of open sets are open; therefore the collection $\mathcal{T}$ of all open sets forms a topology on $X$. Suppose $U \in X\beta$ and $N \in \mathfrak{N}_{\mu\xi}$. There exists $U \in U$ with $U^*\xi \subset N^0$. Since $\operatorname{prin}(u) \in U^*$ and $(\operatorname{prin}(u))\xi = u$ (by the first algebra axiom) for every $u \in U$, $U \subset N$ and $N \in U$. This proves that $U \xrightarrow{\mathcal{T}} U\xi$.
