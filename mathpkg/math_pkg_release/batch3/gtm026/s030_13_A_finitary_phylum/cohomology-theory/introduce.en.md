A cohomology theory assigns to each object of a category $\mathcal{K}$ an object in an abelian category $\mathcal{A}$, contravariantly. This broad definition encompasses many classical cohomology theories (group cohomology, sheaf cohomology, etc.) as special cases. The morphisms in $\mathcal{K}$ induce homomorphisms in $\mathcal{A}$ via the functoriality of $H$, and while $H$ may not be faithful, it extracts algebraic invariants that can distinguish objects or classify them up to suitable equivalence.
