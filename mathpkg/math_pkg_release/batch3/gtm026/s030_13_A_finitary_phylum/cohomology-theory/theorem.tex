In its widest sense, a **cohomology theory** on $\mathcal{K}$ is a functor

$$H: \mathcal{K}^{\text{op}} \rightarrow \mathcal{A}$$

where $\mathcal{A}$ is an abelian category. While such $H$ may "lose information" (i.e., $KH$ and $LH$ may be isomorphic without $K$ and $L$ being isomorphic), $H$ may conveniently classify important properties of objects in $\mathcal{K}$.
