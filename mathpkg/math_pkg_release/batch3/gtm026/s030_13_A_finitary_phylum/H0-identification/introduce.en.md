The zeroth triple cohomology group $H^0(X, \xi)$ is naturally isomorphic to the abelian group of $\mathbf{T}$-algebra homomorphisms from $(X, \xi)$ to the coefficient object $(Y, \theta)$. This mirrors the familiar fact in group cohomology that $H^0(G, M) = M^G$ (the $G$-invariants). The proof uses the fact that $\xi = \operatorname{coeq}(\xi T, X\mu)$ in the canonical resolution, establishing that the kernel of $d^1$ consists precisely of $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphisms.
