Let $X$ be a topological space, let $\mathcal{U}$ be an open cover of $X$, and let $Y$ be a topological abelian group. Let $Z$ be the coproduct (disjoint union) of the elements of $\mathcal{U}$ with the subspace topology, and let $\gamma: Z \rightarrow X$ be the codiagonal map (i.e., $\operatorname{in}_U \cdot \gamma$ is the inclusion for each $U \in \mathcal{U}$).

Then the pullback-along-$\gamma$ functor

$$U = \gamma^*: (\mathcal{K}, X) \rightarrow (\mathcal{K}, Z)$$

is an algebraic functor with left adjoint $F = \Sigma_\gamma$ ("composition with $\gamma$"). The corresponding algebraic theory in $(\mathcal{K}, Z)$ induces, via its triple cohomology, the Čech cohomology groups $\check{H}^n(\mathcal{U}, Y)$ of the cover $\mathcal{U}$ with coefficients in $Y$.
