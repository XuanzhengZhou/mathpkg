A finitary phylum generalizes the notion of an algebraic theory to a many-sorted setting. The set $P$ serves as a set of sorts (or "phyla"), and for each word $w = p_1 \cdots p_n$ of sorts, the set $\Omega_w$ provides operation symbols of type $w$, taking $n$ inputs of sorts $p_1, \ldots, p_n$ respectively. The term "finitary" refers to the restriction that operations have finite arity $n > 0$. This structure, introduced by Birkhoff and Lipson, Grätzer, and Higgins, provides a framework for studying many-sorted universal algebra.
