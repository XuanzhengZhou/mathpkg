A **cochain complex** in an abelian category $\mathcal{A}$ is a diagram

$$0 \xrightarrow{d^0} A_0 \xrightarrow{d^1} A_1 \xrightarrow{d^2} \cdots \xrightarrow{d^n} A_n \xrightarrow{d^{n+1}} A_{n+1} \rightarrow \cdots$$

satisfying $d^n \cdot d^{n+1} = 0$ for all $n \geq 0$. Equivalently, $\operatorname{Im}(d^n) \subset \operatorname{Ker}(d^{n+1})$.

An element of $A_n$ is called an **$n$-cochain**. An element of $\operatorname{Ker}(d^{n+1})$ is called an **$n$-cocycle**. An element of $\operatorname{Im}(d^n)$ is called an **$n$-coboundary**.
