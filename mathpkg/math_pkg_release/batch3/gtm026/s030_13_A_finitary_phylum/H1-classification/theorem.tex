Let $\mathbf{T}$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$ and let $(Y, \theta; +, i, 0)$ be an abelian group in $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$. For any $\mathbf{T}$-algebra $(X, \xi)$:

**(a)** There exist mutually inverse bijections between the group of $1$-cocycles $Z^1(X, \xi)$ and the set of all $\mathbf{T}$-algebra structures $\gamma$ on $Y \times X$ satisfying:

(i) $\operatorname{pr}_X: (Y \times X, \gamma) \rightarrow (X, \xi)$ is a $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphism;

(ii) $Y \times (Y \times X) \cong (Y \times Y) \times X \xrightarrow{+ \times \text{id}} Y \times X$ is a $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphism from $(Y, \theta) \times (Y \times X, \gamma)$ to $(Y \times X, \gamma)$.

**(b)** $H^1(X, \xi)$ is isomorphic to the group of equivalence classes of such $\gamma$ under the relation $\gamma \sim \gamma'$ if there exists $q: Y \times X \rightarrow Y$ such that $\binom{q}{\operatorname{pr}_X}: (Y \times X, \gamma) \rightarrow (Y \times X, \gamma')$ is a $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphism.
