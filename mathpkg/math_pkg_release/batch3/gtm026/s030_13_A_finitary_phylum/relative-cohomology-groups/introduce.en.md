Relative cohomology groups $H^n(X, \xi, A)$ are defined for a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra $(X, \xi)$ with a distinguished subalgebra $(A, \xi_0)$. The subcomplex $[XT^{n+1}, Y]_A$ consists of those homomorphisms from the canonical resolution terms to the coefficient object $(Y, \theta)$ that vanish when precomposed with the inclusion of the subalgebra. This generalizes relative cohomology in topology and provides a long exact sequence connecting the cohomology of $(X, \xi)$, $(A, \xi_0)$, and the relative groups, analogous to the long exact sequence of a pair.
