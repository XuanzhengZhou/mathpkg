For an object $K$ in a category $\mathcal{K}$, the **category $(\mathcal{K}, K)$ of objects over $K$** (also called the slice category $\mathcal{K}/K$) has:

- **Objects**: all pairs $(A, a)$ where $a: A \rightarrow K$ is a morphism in $\mathcal{K}$;
- **Morphisms**: $f: (A, a) \rightarrow (B, b)$ is a morphism $f: A \rightarrow B$ in $\mathcal{K}$ such that $f \cdot b = a$ (i.e., the triangle commutes).
