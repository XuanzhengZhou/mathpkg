This theorem establishes that $H^1(X, \xi)$ classifies principal objects over $(X, \xi)$ up to isomorphism, generalizing the classical fact that $H^1(G, M)$ classifies principal homogeneous spaces for a group $G$ with coefficients in a $G$-module $M$. The correspondence uses the absolute coequalizer property of $(m, \operatorname{pr}_E, p)$ and the pullback condition in the definition of a principal object to extract a $1$-cocycle, and conversely constructs a principal object from a $1$-cocycle via the semi-direct product construction.
