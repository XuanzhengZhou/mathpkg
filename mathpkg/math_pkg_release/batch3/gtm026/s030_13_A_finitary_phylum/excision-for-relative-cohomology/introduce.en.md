This excision theorem states that relative triple cohomology depends only on the complement of the distinguished subalgebra. When a morphism restricts to a bijection on the complements, it induces isomorphisms on all relative cohomology groups. The proof uses the coproduct property to construct preimages for cocycles (surjectivity) and to show that cocycles differing by a coboundary in the target must already differ by a coboundary in the source (injectivity). This is the algebraic analogue of the excision axiom in topological cohomology theories.
