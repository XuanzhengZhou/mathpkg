Let $\mathcal{K} = \mathbf{Set}$. An action $(Y, E, m)$ where $m: Y \times E \rightarrow E$ is said to be **fixpoint-free** if for each $q \in E$, the map

$$(q, -)m: Y \rightarrow E$$

is injective.

In the context of principal objects, elements of $H^1(X, \xi)$ correspond to (suitable isomorphism classes of) fixpoint-free actions whose orbit-space is a quotient $\mathbf{T}$-algebra isomorphic to $(X, \xi)$.
