A principal object is the categorical algebra analogue of a principal bundle. The object $E$ is a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra equipped with an action $m$ of the abelian group object $(Y, \theta)$, a projection $p$ to the base algebra $(X, \xi)$, and a section $s$. The pullback condition ensures that the action is free and transitive on fibers, while the first condition expresses associativity of the action. This concept provides a unified framework for principal homogeneous spaces across various algebraic contexts.
