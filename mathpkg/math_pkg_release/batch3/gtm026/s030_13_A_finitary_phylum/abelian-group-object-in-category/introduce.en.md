An abelian group object internalizes the notion of an abelian group within an arbitrary category having finite products. The operations $+$, $i$ (inverse), and $0$ (unit) must satisfy the usual axioms expressed as commutative diagrams. The equivalent functorial characterization shows that $Y$ is an abelian group object precisely when the representable functor $\mathcal{K}(-, Y)$ lifts through the forgetful functor from abelian groups, meaning every hom-set $\mathcal{K}(A, Y)$ carries a natural abelian group structure.
