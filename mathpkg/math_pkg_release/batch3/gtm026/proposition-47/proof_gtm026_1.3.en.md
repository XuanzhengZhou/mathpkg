3.24 Isolated Elements. Let $(\bar{Q}, \xi)$ be a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra. Recall from 1.4.31 that, if $A$ is a subset of $Q$, then $\langle A \rangle = \text{Im}(iT.\xi)$ is the subalgebra of $(\bar{Q}, \xi)$ generated by $A$. Say that $q$ in $\bar{Q}$ is an isolated element of $(\bar{Q}, \xi)$ if $q$ cannot be generated by applying the $\mathbf{T}$-operations to the other elements, that is, if $q \notin \langle Q - \{q\} \rangle$. When $\mathbf{T}$ is the theory whose algebras are semilattices (1.5.15), i.e., $\mathbf{QT}$ is the set of finite subsets of $Q$ qua subtheory of the theory of 1.3.5, isolated elements are more commonly called join-irreducibles.
