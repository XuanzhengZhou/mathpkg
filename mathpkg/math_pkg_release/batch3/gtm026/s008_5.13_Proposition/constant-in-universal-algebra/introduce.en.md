In the context of algebraic theories, an element $\omega \in nT$ (an $n$-ary operation symbol of the theory $\mathbf{T}$) is called constant if its interpretation in every $\mathbf{T}$-algebra yields a constant function. This is a semantic condition: no matter which algebra we choose, the operation $\omega$ always acts as a constant.
