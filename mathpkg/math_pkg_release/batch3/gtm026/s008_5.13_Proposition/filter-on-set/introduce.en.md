A filter on a set $X$ is a nonempty collection of subsets of $X$ that is closed under finite intersections and upward closure, and does not contain the empty set. Filters capture the notion of "large" subsets and are fundamental in topology (neighborhood filters) and logic (ultrafilters in the Stone-Cech compactification). The concept is used here to characterize compactness and the Hausdorff property via ultrafilter convergence.
