This statement (5.16) connects set theory with universal algebra: a cardinal $M$ is regular precisely when the truncation $T_M$ of the complete semilattice theory $\mathbf{T}$ (restricting to subsets of cardinality $< M$) is closed under composition, hence forms a subtheory. The condition that $T_M$ is a subtheory amounts to the defining property of regular cardinals: the union of fewer than $M$ sets each of size $< M$ has size $< M$.
