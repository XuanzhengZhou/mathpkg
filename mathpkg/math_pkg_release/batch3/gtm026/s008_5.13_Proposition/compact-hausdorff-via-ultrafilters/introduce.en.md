Theorem 5.29 provides elegant ultrafilter-based characterizations of two fundamental topological properties. Compactness is equivalent to every ultrafilter converging to at least one point -- intuitively, ultrafilters cannot "escape" to infinity. The Hausdorff separation property is equivalent to every ultrafilter converging to at most one point -- limits are unique. Together, a compact Hausdorff space is precisely one where every ultrafilter converges to exactly one point. This theorem can be found in many standard texts or taken as the definition.
