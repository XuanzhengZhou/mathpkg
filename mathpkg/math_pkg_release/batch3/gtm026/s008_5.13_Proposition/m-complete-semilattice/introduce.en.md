An $M$-complete semilattice generalizes the notion of a semilattice by requiring suprema to exist for all subsets of cardinality strictly less than a fixed infinite cardinal $M$. The morphisms are required to preserve all such suprema. When $M = \aleph_0$, this recovers the usual finitely complete semilattices. The concept is used to study how algebraic theories can be "truncated" at a cardinal bound.
