Let $M$ be a fixed infinite cardinal. An \text{$M$-complete semilattice} is a semilattice $(X, \leqslant)$ for which every subset of $X$ of cardinal less than $M$ has a supremum. The $M$-complete homomorphisms are required to preserve all $n$-ary suprema for $n < M$. Finitely complete semilattices are $\aleph_0$-complete semilattices.
