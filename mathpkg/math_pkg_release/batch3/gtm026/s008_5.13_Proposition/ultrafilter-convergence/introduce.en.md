An ultrafilter $\mathcal{U}$ on a topological space $X$ converges to a point $x$ if every neighborhood of $x$ belongs to $\mathcal{U}$. Since $\mathfrak{N}_x$ (the neighborhood filter at $x$) is itself a filter, convergence means that the ultrafilter refines the neighborhood filter. This notion allows topology to be reformulated purely in terms of convergence of ultrafilters, which is the key to the elegant characterizations of compactness and Hausdorff separation in Theorem 5.29.
