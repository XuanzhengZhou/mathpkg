A semilattice is a partially ordered set where every finite subset has a supremum (least upper bound). In particular, the empty set has a supremum giving a bottom element, and every pair of elements has a join. Semilattices form a variety of universal algebras where the binary join operation is associative, commutative, and idempotent.
