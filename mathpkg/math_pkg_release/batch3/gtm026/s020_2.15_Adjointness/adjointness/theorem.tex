An \textbf{adjointness} is a 6-tuple $(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{K}, U, F, \eta, \varepsilon)$ where $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{K}$ are categories, $U: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ and $F: \mathcal{K} \longrightarrow \mathcal{A}$ are functors, and $\eta: \text{id}_{\mathcal{K}} \longrightarrow FU$ and $\varepsilon: UF \longrightarrow \text{id}_{\mathcal{A}}$ are natural transformations, subject to the triangular identities:

$$
A\varepsilon U \circ AU\eta = \text{id}_{AU} \quad \text{for every object } A \text{ in } \mathcal{A},
$$
$$
\eta F \circ F\varepsilon = \text{id}_{F}.
$$

In diagrammatic (period) notation: $AU\eta \ldotp A\varepsilon U = \text{id}_{AU}$ and $\eta F \ldotp F\varepsilon = \text{id}_{FU}$.
