Theorem 2.19 is the dual statement to Theorem 2.18, obtained by applying the duality principle for adjointness (2.17). It states that a functor $F: \mathcal{K} \to \mathcal{A}$ is part of an adjointness $(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{K}, U, F, \eta, \varepsilon)$ if and only if for every object $A$ in $\mathcal{A}$ there exists a cofree $\mathcal{K}$-object over $A$ with respect to $F$. In the context of the adjointness, the cofree object over $A$ is $(AU, A\varepsilon: AUF \to A)$. This dual perspective is essential because it shows that the right adjoint $U$ provides cofree objects, just as the left adjoint $F$ provides free objects.
