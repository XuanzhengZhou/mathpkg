Given a multiple branching dynamics $\delta: Q \times X \to QT$ (where $\mathbf{T}$ is the nonempty subset theory), the induced ordinary dynamics $\delta^\bullet: QT \times X \to QT$ is obtained by first applying the canonical map $Q\lambda$ to distribute the input $x$ across the fuzzy state $A \subseteq Q$, and then applying the unique union-preserving extension $\delta^\#$ of $\delta$. The resulting map $\delta^\bullet$ is an ordinary deterministic $X$-dynamics on the set of fuzzy states $QT$, allowing the fuzzy system to be "run" as a deterministic automaton whose states are subsets of the original state set. This construction is the foundation for the $M^\bullet$ construction used throughout the theory of fuzzy automata.
