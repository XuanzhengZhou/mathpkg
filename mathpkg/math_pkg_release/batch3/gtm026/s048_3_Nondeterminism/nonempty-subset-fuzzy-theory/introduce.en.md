The nonempty subset fuzzy theory is an algebraic theory in $\mathbf{Set}$ that models nondeterministic uncertainty where the degree of certainty that $q = q_0$ is measured by whether $q$ is known to belong to a particular subset $A$ of $Q$. In this theory, the set $QT$ of "fuzzy states" over $Q$ consists of all nonempty subsets of $Q$, and the algebras are precisely sets equipped with a base point (corresponding to the singleton subset). This theory serves as one of the fundamental examples of fuzzy theories used to model multiple-branching automata.
