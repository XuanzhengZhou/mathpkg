The credibility values fuzzy theory associates to each monoid $M$ of "credibility values" an algebraic theory $T_M$ in $\mathbf{Set}$ (as constructed in 1.3). A fuzzy morphism $\alpha: A \to B \times M$ is interpreted as assigning to each input $a$ an output $b$ together with a credibility value $m \in M$ measuring the degree of certainty. The monoid multiplication determines how credibility values compose when fuzzy morphisms are chained. This theory is particularly natural when $M$ is the unit interval under multiplication, modeling degrees of belief.
