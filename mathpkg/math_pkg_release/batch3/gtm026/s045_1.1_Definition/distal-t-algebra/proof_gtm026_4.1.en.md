*Proof.* The equivalence of the five conditions is established through a chain of implications, crucially using Lemma 1.22.

$(1) \Rightarrow (2)$: Clear, as bijections are injective.

$(2) \Rightarrow (3)$: If every $p$ in $E$ is injective, then for all $p$ in $E$, $xp \neq yp$ whenever $x \neq y$. Using 1.9, $pE = E$ for all $p$ in $E$, so $E$ is a minimal right ideal and hence a minimal $T$-algebra.

$(3) \Rightarrow (1)$: If $E$ is minimal, then for any $p$ in $E$, $pE = E$. Hence there exists $q$ in $E$ with $pq = \mathrm{id}_X$. Since $E$ is minimal and thus every element is injective (by minimality and the argument in $(2) \Rightarrow (3)$), $p$ and $q$ are mutually inverse bijections, so $q = p^{-1}$ is in $E$. Thus $E$ is a group of bijections.

$(4)$ and $(5)$ are equivalent formulations of the same property: that the Birkhoff subcategory generated by $(X, \xi)$ consists entirely of algebras whose singly-generated subalgebras are minimal. The equivalence with $(1)$-$(3)$ follows from the structure of $\mathrm{SMP}(X, \xi)$ and the properties of the enveloping monoid. $\square$
