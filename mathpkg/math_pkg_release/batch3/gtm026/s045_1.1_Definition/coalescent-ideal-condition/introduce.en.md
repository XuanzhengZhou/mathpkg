Lemma 1.22 provides an alternative characterization of coalescent ideals: a minimal right ideal $I$ of a monoid $M$ is coalescent precisely when every element $p$ in $I$ has a left "almost-inverse" within $I$ — specifically, there exists an element $a$ in $I$ such that $ap$ is an idempotent. This condition connects the injectivity of left translations with the existence of idempotents, a recurring theme in compact semigroup theory.
