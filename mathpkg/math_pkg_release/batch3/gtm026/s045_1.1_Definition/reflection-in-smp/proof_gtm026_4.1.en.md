*Proof.* Clearly $E(X, \xi)$ is in $\mathrm{MP}(X, \xi)$. Let $f: (1T, 1\mu) \longrightarrow (Z, \gamma)$ be a $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphism with $q: (Y, \theta) \longrightarrow (Z, \gamma)$ a surjective $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphism from the subalgebra $(Y, \theta)$ of the product algebra $(X, \xi)^I$ and consider the diagram

$$\begin{array}{ccc}
(1T, 1\mu) & \xrightarrow{\lambda} & E(X, \xi) \\
\downarrow f & & \downarrow \\
(Z, \gamma) & \xleftarrow{q} & (Y, \theta)
\end{array}$$

By the definition of $\mathrm{SMP}(X, \xi)$, every object is a subobject of a product of copies of $(X, \xi)$ factored by an image factorization. Since $\lambda$ maps the generator $1\eta$ to $\mathrm{id}_X$, and the free algebra on one generator has the universal property with respect to $T$-algebras, the factoring property follows from the construction of the enveloping monoid as the subalgebra generated by $\mathrm{id}_X$ in $(X,\xi)^X$. $\square$
