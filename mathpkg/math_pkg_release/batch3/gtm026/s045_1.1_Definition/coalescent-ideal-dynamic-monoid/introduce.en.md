A coalescent ideal is a minimal right ideal $I$ of a monoid $M$ in which all left multiplications by elements of $I$ are injective. A monoid is called dynamic if it possesses a coalescent ideal, and an algebraic theory $T$ is dynamic if its enveloping monoid $E(1T, 1\mu)$ is dynamic. These definitions provide the monoid-theoretic framework needed to characterize when universal minimal algebras exist. The term "coalescent" reflects the intuition that elements of the ideal cannot coalesce (merge) distinct points under left multiplication.
