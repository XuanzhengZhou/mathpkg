Let $M$ be a monoid. A \textit{coalescent ideal} of $M$ is a minimal right ideal $I$ of $M$ with the property that whenever $p, q, r$ are in $I$ and satisfy $pq = pr$ then $q = r$; i.e., the left multiplications of the monoid $I$ are injective. $M$ is \textit{dynamic} if $M$ has a coalescent ideal. $T$ is \textit{dynamic} if $E(1T, 1\mu)$ is dynamic.
