Theorem 1.16 shows that compactibility is a sufficient condition for a monoid (and hence a theory) to be dynamic. The proof uses Zorn's lemma on the lattice of closed right ideals to produce a minimal closed right ideal, which is then shown to be a coalescent ideal. Lemma 1.17, which guarantees the existence of idempotents in closed subsemigroups, is a crucial ingredient. This theorem is significant because it shows that many theories arising from topological dynamics are automatically dynamic.
