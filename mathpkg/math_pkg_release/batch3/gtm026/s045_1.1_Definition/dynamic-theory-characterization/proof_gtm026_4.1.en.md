*Proof.* We first prove necessity. Let $E = E(1T, 1\mu)$ and let $I$ be a minimal subalgebra of $E$. Since $I$ satisfies the first two properties of 1.10, $I$ is the universal minimal $T$-algebra. By 1.9, $I$ is a minimal right ideal. For all $p$ in $M = 1T$, the left multiplication $L_p: E \longrightarrow E$ is a $T$-endomorphism and hence its restriction $L_p: I \longrightarrow I$ is an isomorphism by 1.10(3). In particular, $L_p: I \longrightarrow I$ is injective for $p$ in $I$, and $I$ is a coalescent ideal of $E$.

Conversely, let $I$ be a coalescent ideal of $E = E(1T, 1\mu)$. By 1.9, $I$ is a minimal $T$-algebra. Since every nonempty $T$-algebra admits a homomorphism from $(1T, 1\mu)$ to its enveloping monoid, and the image of $I$ under any such homomorphism provides a minimal subalgebra, every nonempty $T$-algebra possesses a minimal subalgebra. Moreover, $I$ itself is the universal minimal $T$-algebra because it satisfies properties 1 and 2 of 1.10. $\square$
