Proposition 1.7 gives an explicit description of the reflection map $\lambda$ from Theorem 1.6: it sends each element $g$ of the free algebra $(1T, 1\mu)$ (thought of as a unary operation symbol) to the corresponding unary operation $\xi^g$ on the algebra $(X,\xi)$. This shows concretely how the enveloping monoid $E(X,\xi)$ consists of the unary operations induced by the theory $T$ acting on $(X,\xi)$.
