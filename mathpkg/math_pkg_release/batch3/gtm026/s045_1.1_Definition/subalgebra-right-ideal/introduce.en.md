Lemma 1.9 establishes a crucial structural correspondence between the $T$-algebraic properties of the enveloping monoid $E(X,\xi)$ and its monoid-theoretic properties. Specifically, every $T$-subalgebra of $E$ is a right ideal, the subalgebra generated by a single element $p$ is exactly the principal right ideal $pE$, and minimality as a $T$-subalgebra is equivalent to minimality as a right ideal. This lemma bridges algebraic theories and monoid theory, allowing the study of minimal algebras to be conducted using the monoid structure of $E(X,\xi)$.
