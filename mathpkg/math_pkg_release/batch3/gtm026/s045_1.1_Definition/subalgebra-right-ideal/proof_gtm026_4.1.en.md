*Proof.* 

1. Consider the map $R$ sending $p$ to the right multiplication $q \mapsto qp$ from $I$ into $E$. The diagram

$$\begin{array}{ccc}
E \times I & \longrightarrow & E\\
\uparrow & & \uparrow\\
I \times I & \longrightarrow & I
\end{array}$$

shows that $R$ is a $T$-homomorphism. Thus the inverse image (pullback) of the subalgebra $I^I$ of $E^I$ under $R$, which is the subset $\{p \in E : Ip \subset I\}$, is a subalgebra of $E$ containing $\mathrm{id}_X$. Hence this set is all of $E$, which means $Ip \subset I$ for all $p \in E$, i.e., $I$ is a right ideal of $E$.

2. Thinking of $(E, \mathrm{id}_X)$ as the free algebra on one generator in $\mathrm{SMP}(X, \xi)$, the left multiplication $L_p: E \longrightarrow E$ is the unique homomorphism sending $\mathrm{id}_X$ to $p$. Hence its image is $pE$, and this image is precisely the $T$-subalgebra $\langle p \rangle$ generated by $p$.

3. This follows immediately from (1) and (2): a $T$-subalgebra is minimal iff it is a minimal right ideal, and conversely a minimal right ideal is a $T$-subalgebra and hence a minimal subalgebra. $\square$
