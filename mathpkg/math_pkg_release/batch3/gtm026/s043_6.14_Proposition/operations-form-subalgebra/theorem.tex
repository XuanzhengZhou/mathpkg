Let $T$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathbf{Set}$ and let $(X, \xi)$ be a $T$-algebra.
Let $(Y, \theta) = (X, \xi)^{(X^n)}$ be the power algebra and let
$p \colon n \to Y$ be the injective map sending $i \mapsto p_i$ (the $i$-th projection).
Then the image of the $T$-homomorphism
$$pT \cdot \theta \colon nT \longrightarrow Y$$
is precisely $\mathcal{O}_n(X, \xi)$, the set of $n$-ary $T$-operations on $(X, \xi)$.
Moreover, $pT \cdot \theta$ sends each $\omega \in nT$ to the derived operation
$(X, \xi)\tilde{\omega}$, showing that $\mathcal{O}_n(X, \xi)$ is a $T$-subalgebra of $(X, \xi)^{(X^n)}$.
