Let $T$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathbf{Set}$, let $(X, \xi)$ be a $T$-algebra, and let $A$ be a subset of $X$ with inclusion map $i \colon A \to X$. Then:

1. $\langle A \rangle = i \mathcal{O}_A(X, \xi)$, the image under $i$ of the set of all $A$-indexed families of $T$-operations on $(X, \xi)$.

2. $A$ is a $T$-subalgebra of $(X, \xi)$ if and only if for every $n$, every $n$-ary $T$-operation $\tilde{\omega}$ on $(X, \xi)$, and every map $f \colon n \to A$, the composite $\langle f, \tilde{\omega} \rangle$ factors through $i$; equivalently, there exists $\psi$ such that $\langle i, (X, \xi)\tilde{\omega} \rangle = \langle \operatorname{id}_A, \psi \rangle \in A$, which by (1) implies $\langle A \rangle = i \mathcal{O}_A(X, \xi) \subseteq A$.
