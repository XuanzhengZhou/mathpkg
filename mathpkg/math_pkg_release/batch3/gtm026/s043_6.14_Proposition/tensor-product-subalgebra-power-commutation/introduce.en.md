This theorem provides a powerful sufficient condition for the existence of the tensor product $S \otimes T$ of two algebraic theories in $\mathbf{Set}$. If $S$ has the property that subalgebras commute with powers (i.e., $\langle A^n \rangle = \langle A \rangle^n$ for all $T$-algebras and subsets $A$), then $S \otimes T$ exists for **all** theories $T$. The proof applies Lemma 6.13 by verifying the bialgebra condition. As an immediate application, compact groups and compact abelian groups are sets with algebraic structure (via 6.20).
