Let $T$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathbf{Set}$, let $(X, \xi)$ be a $T$-algebra, let $A \subset X$, and let $j \colon \langle A \rangle \to X$ be the inclusion map. As $j^n \colon \langle A \rangle^n \to (X, \xi)^n$ is a $T$-homomorphism and an inclusion map, $\langle A \rangle^n$ is a subalgebra of $(X, \xi)^n$ (1.4.32). Since $A \subset \langle A \rangle$, $A^n \subset \langle A \rangle^n$; as the latter is a subalgebra it follows that $\langle A^n \rangle \subset \langle A \rangle^n$.

We say that \textbf{subalgebras commute with powers} in $T$ if for all $T$-algebras $(X, \xi)$, subsets $A$ of $X$, and sets $n$, we have
$$\langle A^n \rangle = \langle A \rangle^n.$$
