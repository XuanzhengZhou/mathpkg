In an algebraic theory $T$ over $\mathbf{Set}$, the inequality $\langle A^n \rangle \subseteq \langle A \rangle^n$ always holds for any $T$-algebra $(X,\xi)$ and subset $A \subseteq X$. The theory $T$ is said to have the property that **subalgebras commute with powers** when the reverse inclusion also holds, i.e., $\langle A^n \rangle = \langle A \rangle^n$. This property plays a crucial role in the existence theorem for tensor products of algebraic theories (Theorem 6.19). Examples of theories with this property include those corresponding to compact groups and compact abelian groups.
