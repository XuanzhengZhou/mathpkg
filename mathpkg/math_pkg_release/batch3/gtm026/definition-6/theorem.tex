Identity morphisms are defined by

$$A\eta: A \longrightarrow AT$$

(2.9)

Throughout the book we will adopt the following notational conventions: morphisms in $\text{Set}(\Omega, E)$ are distinguished from functions by the use of single-headed arrows. The symbol for the $\text{Set}(\Omega, E)$-identity of $A$ will always be $A\eta$, whereas the symbol for the identity function of $A$ will always be $id_A$; thus $id_AT$ will never mean $AT\eta: AT \longrightarrow AT$, but one of $id_AT: AT \longrightarrow AT$ or $id_AT: AT \longrightarrow A$. The symbol $\circ$ will be used for clone composition as in 2.8, whereas ordinary composition of functions can be denoted with a period.
