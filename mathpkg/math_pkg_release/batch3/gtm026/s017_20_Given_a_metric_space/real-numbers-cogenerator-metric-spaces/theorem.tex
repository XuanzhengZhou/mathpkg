$\mathbb{R}$ equipped with the usual metric is a cogenerator in the category of metric spaces and distance-decreasing maps (as defined in Section 1.9). That is, for any metric space $(X, d)$ and any distinct morphisms $f, g: (Y, d') \to (X, d)$ with $f \neq g$, there exists a distance-decreasing map $h: (X, d) \to \mathbb{R}$ such that $f \circ h \neq g \circ h$.

Equivalently, the family of distance-decreasing maps $\{\, d(x, -): X \to \mathbb{R} \mid x \in X \,\}$ separates points of any metric space $X$.
