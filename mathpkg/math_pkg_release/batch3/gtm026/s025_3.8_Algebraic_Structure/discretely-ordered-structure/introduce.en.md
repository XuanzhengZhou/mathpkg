A category of $K$-objects with structure is discretely ordered when each fibre $(C(K), \leqslant)$ is a discrete preorder. Equivalently, whenever an admissible morphism $f$ is carried by an isomorphism in the base category $K$, its inverse is also admissible. This condition formalizes the property that "bijective admissible maps are isomorphisms," which holds for algebraic structures such as groups but fails for topological spaces.
