The text states (3.10) as a noted fact without elaboration: "Algebraic implies discretely ordered." The proof follows from the structure of $K^T$: in the category of algebras for an algebraic theory, an admissible map whose underlying $K$-morphism is an isomorphism must itself be an isomorphism of algebras, hence its inverse is also a $T$-algebra homomorphism and thus admissible. Therefore the inverse is admissible in $K^T$, establishing the discretely ordered condition via the equivalent characterization in 3.9.
