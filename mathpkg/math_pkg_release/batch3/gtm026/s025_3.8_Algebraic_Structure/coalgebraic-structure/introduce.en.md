A structured category $C$ over $K$ is coalgebraic if its dual $C^{\text{op}}$ is algebraic over the dual base category $K^{\text{op}}$. While the property of being discretely ordered is self-dual, algebraicity is not; coalgebraicity is the dual notion. This concept plays a central role in understanding interior operators and other order-theoretic structures as duals of closure operators.
