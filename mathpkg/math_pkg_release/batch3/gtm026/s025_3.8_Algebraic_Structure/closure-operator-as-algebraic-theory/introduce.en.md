When the base category $K$ is a partially ordered class, an algebraic theory (monad) $T$ in $K$ reduces to a classical closure operator: an order-preserving endomorphism that is extensive ($x \leqslant xT$) and idempotent ($xTT = xT$). The category of algebras $K^T$ is then precisely the full subcategory of closed elements. This unifies the standard order-theoretic notion of closure with the categorical theory of monads.
