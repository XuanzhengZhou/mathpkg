The property of being discretely ordered is self-dual: a structured category $C$ over $K$ is discretely ordered exactly when its dual $C^{\text{op}}$ over $K^{\text{op}}$ is. This contrasts with algebraicity, which is not self-dual. The self-duality of the discrete-order property reflects the symmetry of the defining condition involving inverse admissibility.
