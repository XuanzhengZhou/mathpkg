If $C$ is discretely ordered over $K$, then for any admissible $f^{\text{op}}$ in $C^{\text{op}}$ whose underlying $K^{\text{op}}$-morphism is an isomorphism, the corresponding $f$ in $C$ is admissible with underlying isomorphism in $K$, so $f^{-1}$ is admissible in $C$, hence $(f^{-1})^{\text{op}} = (f^{\text{op}})^{-1}$ is admissible in $C^{\text{op}}$. The converse follows by duality applied to $C^{\text{op}}$.
