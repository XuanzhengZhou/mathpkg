This proposition is the dual counterpart of the Hierarchy Theorem for Epimorphisms (1.36). It establishes that split monomorphisms form the strongest of the three injectivity notions, with equalizers in the middle and monomorphisms as the weakest. The composition properties provide useful tools for working with monomorphisms in diagrammatic reasoning: the class of monomorphisms and split monomorphisms is closed under composition, and if a composite is (split) mono, then its first factor is (split) mono. These results follow by duality from the corresponding statements about epimorphisms.
