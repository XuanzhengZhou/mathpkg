A split epimorphism is the strongest of the three categorical generalizations of surjectivity considered in this section. It requires the existence of a genuine right-sided inverse $d$ (a \textit{section} or \textit{left inverse}) such that $d \cdot f = \mathrm{id}_B$. This notion corresponds directly to the Axiom of Choice in Set: every surjection is split epi if and only if the Axiom of Choice holds. Split epimorphisms are also known as \textit{retractions} in the broader categorical literature.
