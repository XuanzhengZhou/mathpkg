An epimorphism is the categorical generalization of a surjective function. A morphism $f: A \to B$ is epic if it is right-cancellative: whenever two morphisms $t, u$ out of $B$ agree after precomposition with $f$, they must be equal. This is the weakest of the three surjectivity notions studied in this section. In concrete categories like Set, Ab, and many algebraic categories, epimorphisms coincide with surjective morphisms. However, as examples (1.40) show, epimorphisms need not be surjective in categories of algebras over a theory.
