This proposition provides a fundamental characterization of monomorphisms in the category $\mathcal{K}^T$ of $T$-algebras over a base category $\mathcal{K}$. A $T$-homomorphism is monic in the algebraic category precisely when its underlying morphism is monic in the base category. The forward direction (mono in $\mathcal{K}^T$ implies mono in $\mathcal{K}$) is nontrivial and uses the universal property of free $T$-algebras $(CT, C\mu)$ to extend arbitrary testing morphisms $t, u: C \to A$ in $\mathcal{K}$ to $T$-homomorphisms $t^\#, u^\#$.
