A functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{K}$ is called a **Beck functor** if it satisfies two conditions: it creates limits, and it creates coequalizers of $U$-absolute pairs. This notion bridges the gap between limit-creation and the tripleability (monadicity) criteria central to Beck's theorem. Every algebraic functor is Beck (1.19), and the product of any family of Beck functors is again a Beck functor (1.18).
