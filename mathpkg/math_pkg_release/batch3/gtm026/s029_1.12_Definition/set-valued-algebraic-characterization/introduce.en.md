Using the characterization theorem 1.13 (rather than the Beck tripleability theorem 1.9), one obtains a particularly clean form for set-valued functors. A functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathbf{Set}$ is algebraic exactly when it creates limits, creates quotients of congruences, and satisfies the solution set condition. In the presence of a cogenerator in $\mathcal{A}$, the solution set condition is redundant, and $U$ is algebraic iff it creates limits and creates quotients of congruences.
