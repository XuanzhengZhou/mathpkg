This lemma ensures that the hypothesis "$\mathcal{A}$ has coequalizers" in the Sandwich Theorem is automatically satisfied for set-valued algebraic functors. The proof constructs the coequalizer of $f, g: A_1 \to A_2$ by taking the intersection of all congruences on $A_2$ containing the set $\{(a_1 f, a_1 g)\}$, showing this intersection is itself a congruence (using the fact that $U$ creates limits), and then applying the property that $U$ creates quotients of congruences.
