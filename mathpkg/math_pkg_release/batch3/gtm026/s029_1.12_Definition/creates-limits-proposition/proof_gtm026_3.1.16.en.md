(1) Since $U$ creates limits, it preserves them and reflects isomorphisms, so $(\mathcal{A}, U) \in \text{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$. The discrete ordering follows from the uniqueness of lifts.

(2) If $\mathcal{K}$ is locally small, then for any two objects $A, B \in \mathcal{A}$, $\mathcal{A}(A, B)$ is in bijection with a subset of $\mathcal{K}(AU, BU)$ via $U$, hence is a small set.

(3) Let $A \in \mathcal{A}$. The passage from a monosubobject $i: S \to A$ to $iU: SU \to AU$ is well-defined. To prove injectivity, let $i: S \to A$ and $i': S' \to A$ be monos with $f: SU \to S'U$ an isomorphism of subobjects in $\mathcal{K}$. The rightmost pullback square lifts to $\mathcal{A}$, and the isomorphisms descend to show $(S, i)$ and $(S', i')$ represent the same subobject.
