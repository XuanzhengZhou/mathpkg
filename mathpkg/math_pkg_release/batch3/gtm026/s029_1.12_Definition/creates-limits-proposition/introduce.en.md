This proposition lists structural consequences of a functor creating limits. In particular, such a functor automatically belongs to $\text{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ with discrete ordering, and $\mathcal{A}$ inherits local smallness and well-poweredness from $\mathcal{K}$. These properties are important for applying the adjoint functor theorems to algebraic functors.
