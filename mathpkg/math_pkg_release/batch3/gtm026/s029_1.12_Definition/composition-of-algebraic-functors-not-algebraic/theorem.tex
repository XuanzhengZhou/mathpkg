The composition of algebraic functors need not be algebraic. Specifically, let $\mathcal{A}$ be the category of abelian groups with algebraic forgetful functor $V: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathbf{Set}$. Let $\mathcal{P}$ be the full replete subcategory of torsion-free abelian groups. Then $\mathcal{P}$ is reflective in $\mathcal{A}$ (the reflection of $A$ is $\theta: A \longrightarrow A/C$ where $C$ is the torsion subgroup of $A$). By 1.33, the inclusion functor $U: \mathcal{P} \longrightarrow \mathcal{A}$ is algebraic. Although $UV: \mathcal{P} \longrightarrow \mathbf{Set}$ has a left adjoint (2.2.30), $UV$ is not algebraic.
