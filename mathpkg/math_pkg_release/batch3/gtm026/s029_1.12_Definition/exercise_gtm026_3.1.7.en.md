[Duskin '69, Theorem 3.2.] Let $\mathcal{K}$ have finite limits and let $U: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$. Prove that $U$ is algebraic if and only if (a) $U$ has a left adjoint; (b) $\mathcal{A}$ has separators; (c) given an equivalence relation $p, q: A \longrightarrow B$ in $\mathcal{A}$ such that $(pU, qU)$ extends to a contractible coequalizer $(pU, qU, h; d, d')$ in $\mathcal{K}$, $h$ admits a unique lift which is, moreover, $\mathrm{coeq}(p, q)$ in $\mathcal{A}$.
