Say that the functor $U: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ creates limits if whenever we are given a diagram $(\Delta, D)$ in $\mathcal{A}$ and a limit $\psi: L \longrightarrow DU$ for $(\Delta, DU)$ in $\mathcal{K}$, we may conclude that there exists a unique lift $(\bar{L}, \bar{\psi})$ of $(L, \psi)$ (that is $\bar{L}U = L$, $(\bar{\psi}_i: \bar{L} \longrightarrow D_i)U = \psi_i: L \longrightarrow D_iU$); and, moreover, $(\bar{L}, \bar{\psi})$ is a limit of $D$ in $\mathcal{A}$.
