For a tractable equational presentation $(\Omega, E)$, the forgetful functor from the category of $(\Omega, E)$-algebras to $\mathbf{Set}$ is algebraic. The proof uses 1.24 and establishes that $U$ creates limits (by constructing the unique $\Omega$-algebra structure on the limit set using the universal property) and creates quotients of congruences (by showing that quotients of $(\Omega, E)$-algebras by congruences have well-defined $\Omega$-structures because the quotient maps are onto). This result explains why varieties of finitary algebras in the classical sense are algebraic over $\mathbf{Set}$.
