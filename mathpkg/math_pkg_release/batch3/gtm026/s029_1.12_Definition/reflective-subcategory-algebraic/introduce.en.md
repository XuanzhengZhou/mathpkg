This proposition shows that full replete reflective subcategories of algebraic categories are themselves algebraic (via the inclusion functor). The proof uses Beck's theorem: the inclusion creates coequalizers because the reflector preserves them, and limits are created since they exist in $\mathcal{A}$ and the subcategory is closed under them (being reflective). However, as Example 1.35 shows, the composite of such an inclusion with the algebraic forgetful functor to $\mathbf{Set}$ need not be algebraic.
