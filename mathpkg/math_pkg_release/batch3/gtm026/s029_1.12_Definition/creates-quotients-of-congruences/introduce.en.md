A set-valued functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathbf{Set}$ creates quotients of congruences if every congruence (an equivalence relation that lifts to $\mathcal{A}$) yields a co-optimal quotient in $\mathcal{A}$. This property, together with having a left adjoint and separators, characterizes algebraic functors over $\mathbf{Set}$ in Theorem 1.13. It provides the set-based analog of Beck's coequalizer-creation condition.
