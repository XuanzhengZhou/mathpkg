Even without tractability, if an equational presentation $(\Omega, E)$ is **bounded** (there are no operation symbols of arity $\geq n_0$ for some $n_0$), the underlying set functor to $\mathbf{Set}$ is algebraic. Since the proof of 1.26 already shows that $U$ creates limits and creates quotients of congruences, it suffices by 1.22(1) to verify the solution set condition. The proof constructs a transfinite tower of subsets of an algebra and uses cardinal arithmetic to bound the size of the subalgebra generated by a given set. This theorem explains why varieties of algebras with finitary operations (but possibly infinitely many such operations) are algebraic.
