Every algebraic functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{K}$ (i.e., a functor that is monadic via an algebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$) is a Beck functor. By the characterization theorem 1.9, it suffices to show that $U$ creates limits. The proof reduces to the case $U = U^{\mathbf{T}}$ for an algebraic theory $\mathbf{T}$, where one explicitly constructs the $\mathbf{T}$-algebra structure on the limit object using the universality of limits and the $\mathbf{T}$-algebra structures on the diagram objects.
