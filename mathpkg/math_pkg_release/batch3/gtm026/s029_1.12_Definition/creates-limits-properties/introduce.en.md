If a functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{K}$ creates limits, then several structural properties follow automatically. The pair $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ is a category of sets with structure over $\mathcal{K}$ (in the sense of $\text{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$) and is discretely ordered. Moreover, local smallness lifts from $\mathcal{K}$ to $\mathcal{A}$, and well-poweredness lifts whenever $\mathcal{K}$ has kernel pairs. The proof of well-poweredness uses a pullback argument on kernel pairs and shows that the passage from monosubobjects to their $U$-images is injective.
