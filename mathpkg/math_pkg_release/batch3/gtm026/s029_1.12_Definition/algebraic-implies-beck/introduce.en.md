This proposition establishes that every algebraic (monadic) functor automatically creates limits, in addition to creating coequalizers of absolute pairs (already proved in Beck's theorem). Thus every algebraic functor is a Beck functor. The proof shows that $U^{\mathbf{T}}: \mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}} \to \mathcal{K}$ creates limits using the universal property of limits to induce a unique $\mathbf{T}$-algebra structure on the limit object.
