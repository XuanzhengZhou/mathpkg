This theorem characterizes exactly when a set-valued functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathbf{Set}$ in the category of sets with structure is algebraic (monadic). The three necessary and sufficient conditions are: $U$ has a left adjoint, $\mathcal{A}$ has separators, and $U$ creates quotients of congruences. The proof reduces the problem to Beck's theorem by showing that $U$ creates coequalizers of $U$-contractible pairs, using the fact that in $\mathbf{Set}$, contractible coequalizers correspond to congruences.
