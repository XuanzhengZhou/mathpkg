For a set-valued algebraic functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathbf{Set}$, the domain category $\mathcal{A}$ always has coequalizers. The proof constructs the coequalizer of a parallel pair $f, g: A_1 \to A_2$ by taking the intersection of all congruences on $A_2$ containing the set $S = \{(a_1f, a_1g) : a_1 \in A_1U\}$, then showing this intersection is itself a congruence whose quotient yields the desired coequalizer. This lemma is essential for applying the Sandwich Theorem (1.29) over $\mathbf{Set}$.
