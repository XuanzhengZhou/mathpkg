A pair of maps $f, g: A \longrightarrow B$ is reflexive if there exists $d: B \longrightarrow A$ with $df = \text{id}_B = dg$. (In $\mathbf{Set}$, $(f, g)$ is reflexive if and only if $\{(af, bf): a \in A\}$ is a reflexive relation.) In the context of 1.29, show that it is enough to postulate that every reflexive pair of $\mathcal{A}$-morphisms has a coequalizer.
