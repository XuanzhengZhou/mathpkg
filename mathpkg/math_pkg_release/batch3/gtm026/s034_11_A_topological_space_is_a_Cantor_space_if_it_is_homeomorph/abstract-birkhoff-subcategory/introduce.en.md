An abstract Birkhoff subcategory generalizes the classical Birkhoff variety theorem to the categorical setting of algebraic theories. In the classical case over $\mathbf{Set}$, a Birkhoff subcategory is precisely a full subcategory closed under products, subalgebras, and homomorphic images (the HSP theorem). In the abstract setting, condition (1) requires every free algebra to have an epimorphic reflection into the subcategory, and condition (2) requires closure under $U^T$-split epimorphisms. These two conditions suffice to classify Birkhoff subcategories as pointwise epimorphic quotient theories $\lambda: \mathbf{T} \to \mathbf{T}'$.
