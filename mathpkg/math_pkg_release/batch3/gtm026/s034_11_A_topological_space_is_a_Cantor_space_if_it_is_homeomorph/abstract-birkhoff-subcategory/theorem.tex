Let $\mathcal{K}$ be a category and let $\mathbf{T} = (T, \eta, \circ, \mu)$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$. Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a full replete subcategory of $\mathcal{K}^T$. $\mathcal{A}$ is an \textbf{abstract Birkhoff subcategory} of $\mathcal{K}^T$ provided:

\begin{enumerate}
\item For every $X$ in $\mathcal{K}$, the $\mathbf{T}$-algebra $(XT, X\mu)$ has a reflection $X\lambda: (XT, X\mu) \longrightarrow A$ in $\mathcal{A}$ such that $(X\lambda)U^T$ is epi in $\mathcal{K}$, and
\item $\mathcal{A}$ is closed under $U^T$-split epimorphisms, that is, given $A$ in $\mathcal{A}$ and $f: A \longrightarrow (X, \xi)$ in $\mathcal{K}^T$ such that $fU^T$ is a split epimorphism in $\mathcal{K}$, then $(X, \xi)$ is in $\mathcal{A}$.
\end{enumerate}
