(Zurich school; cf. [Linton '69, page 29]). Given $U: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$, let $K$ be an object of $\mathcal{K}$. Let $(K, U)$ be the diagram scheme with nodes $(A, f)$, $f: K \longrightarrow AU$, and with edges $e: (A, f) \longrightarrow (A', f')$ all $e: A \longrightarrow A'$ such that $f \cdot eU = f'$. Consider the obvious diagram $D: (K, U) \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$, $D_{(A, f)} = AU$, $D_e = eU$. Say that $U$ is tractable if for every $K$, $D$ has a limit.

(a) Show that $U: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathbf{Set}$ is tractable in the sense above if and only if $U$ is tractable in the sense of 1.5.44.

(b) Let $U: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ be tractable and let $\psi_{K; (A, f)}: KT \longrightarrow AU$ be the corresponding limit. Define $\eta$, $\circ$ accordingly. Verify that $\mathbf{T} = (T, \eta, \circ)$ is an algebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$ and that $gT$ and $\mu$ are given by the limit construction.

(c) Show that $\Phi: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}^T$, $A\Phi = (AU, \psi_{AU; (AU, \mathrm{id}_{AU})})$ is an algebraic reflection of $U$. [Hint: use the construction of part (b) of the previous exercise.]
