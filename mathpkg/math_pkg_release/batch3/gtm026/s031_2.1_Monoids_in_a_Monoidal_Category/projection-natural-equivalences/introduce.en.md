In a category with a terminal object $1$ and binary products, the canonical projections $Ab: 1 \times A \to A$ and $Ac: A \times 1 \to A$ are natural equivalences. These isomorphisms express the fact that the terminal object acts as a unit for the product. They provide the motivation for the triangle coherence axiom involving the unit object $I$ in the definition of a monoidal category.
