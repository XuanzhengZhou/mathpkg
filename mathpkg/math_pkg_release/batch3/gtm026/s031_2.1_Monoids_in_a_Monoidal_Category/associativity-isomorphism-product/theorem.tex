For any three objects $A, B, C$ in a category $\mathcal{K}$ with binary products, the associativity isomorphism is a morphism
$$(A,B,C)a: A \times (B \times C) \to (A \times B) \times C$$
defined via the universal property of the product. Both $(A,B,C)a$ and its inverse are natural transformations of functors from $\mathcal{K} \times \mathcal{K} \times \mathcal{K}$ to $\mathcal{K}$; thus $(A,B,C)a$ is a natural equivalence.
