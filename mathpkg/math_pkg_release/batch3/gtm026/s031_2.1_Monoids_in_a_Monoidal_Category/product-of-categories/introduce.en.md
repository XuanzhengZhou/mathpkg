The product of a finite family of categories $\mathcal{K}_1, \ldots, \mathcal{K}_n$ is defined by taking ordered $n$-tuples of objects and morphisms from the constituent categories. Composition and identity morphisms are defined componentwise. When a category $\mathcal{K}$ has binary products, the bifunctor $\times: \mathcal{K} \times \mathcal{K} \to \mathcal{K}$ mapping $(A,B)$ to $A \times B$ becomes a functor whose action on morphisms is determined by the universal property of products.
