A monoidal category generalizes the cartesian product of sets to an abstract categorical setting. It consists of a category $\mathcal{K}$ equipped with a bifunctor $\otimes$, a unit object $I$, and three natural equivalences — the associator $a$, the left unitor $b$, and the right unitor $c$ — satisfying the pentagon and triangle coherence axioms. The motivating example is $(\mathsf{Set}, \times, 1, a, b, c)$ where $\times$ is the cartesian product and $1$ is a singleton set. Monoidal categories provide the minimal structure needed to define monoid objects internal to a category.
