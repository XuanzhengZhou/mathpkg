An image factorization system axiomatizes the decomposition of morphisms in a category into an epimorphism followed by a monomorphism. It consists of two subclasses $E$ (the "epi" part) and $M$ (the "mono" part) that are both subcategories, contain all isomorphisms, and provide a unique factorization $f = e \circ m$ for every morphism $f$. This concept generalizes the epi-mono and coequalizer-mono factorizations encountered in concrete settings such as sets, groups, and topological spaces.
