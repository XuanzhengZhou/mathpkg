In any category equipped with an image factorization system $(E, M)$, a morphism that belongs to both the $E$ and $M$ classes must be an isomorphism. This is a formal consequence of the uniqueness of factorizations: either the trivial factorization or the identity factorization can be compared via the uniqueness isomorphism, forcing the morphism to be invertible. The argument is the same as that used in the general epi-mono factorization context.
