If $\mathcal{L}$ is a full reflective subcategory of $\mathcal{K}$ and a diagram $D$ in $\mathcal{L}$ already has a colimit $(K, \psi)$ when computed in the larger category $\mathcal{K}$, then reflecting $K$ into $\mathcal{L}$ yields a colimit of $D$ within $\mathcal{L}$. The proof is straightforward: given any lower bound of $D$ in $\mathcal{L}$, the universal property of the $\mathcal{K}$-colimit provides a unique map from $K$, which then factors uniquely through the reflection $\eta: K \to L$ via the universal property of the reflection. This theorem shows that colimit existence lifts from an ambient category to its full reflective subcategories.
