The constant-diagram embedding functor $(\tilde{\cdot}): \mathcal{K} \to \mathcal{K}^{\Delta}$ not only provides the link between objects of $\mathcal{K}$ and constant diagrams, but also preserves all existing limits. This means that if a diagram in $\mathcal{K}$ has a limit, its image under the embedding yields the limit of the corresponding constant diagrams in $\mathcal{K}^{\Delta}$. This property is crucial for applying adjoint functor theorems to prove colimit existence.
