This theorem establishes a fundamental connection between colimits and free objects: an upper bound $(L,\psi)$ of a diagram $D$ is a colimit precisely when it is free over $D$ with respect to the constant-diagram embedding functor $(\tilde{\cdot}): \mathcal{K} \to \mathcal{K}^{\Delta}$. This equivalence bridges the universal properties of colimits (unique mediating morphisms) and free objects (unique extensions along the unit). It provides the foundation for applying adjoint functor theorems to prove the existence of colimits.
