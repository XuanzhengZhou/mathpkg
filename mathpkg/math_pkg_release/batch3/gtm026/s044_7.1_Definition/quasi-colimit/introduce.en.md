A quasi-colimit weakens the definition of a colimit by dropping the uniqueness requirement on the mediating morphism. For each upper bound $(A,\Gamma)$ of the diagram $D$, there must exist some morphism $t: C \to A$ factoring through the quasi-colimit cone $\tau$, but this $t$ need not be unique. Quasi-colimits serve as an intermediate notion in the construction of genuine colimits, particularly in the proof of Theorem 7.7, where the existence of quasi-colimits together with factorization system properties yields genuine colimits.
