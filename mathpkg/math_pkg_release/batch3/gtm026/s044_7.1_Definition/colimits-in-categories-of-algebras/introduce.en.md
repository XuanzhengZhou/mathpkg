This corollary applies Theorem 7.7 to the Eilenberg-Moore category $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ of algebras for an algebraic theory $\mathbf{T}$ in a regular category $\mathcal{K}$. The key hypotheses are that $\mathbf{T}$ preserves the $E$-morphisms of the factorization system and that $\mathcal{K}$ has small weak coproducts. Since $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ inherits small limits from $\mathcal{K}$ and the forgetful functor $U^{\mathbf{T}}$ has a left adjoint, the remaining conditions of Theorem 7.7 are verified using the structure of the regular factorization system.
