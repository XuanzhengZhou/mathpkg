*Proof.* This follows directly from Theorem 7.7. $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ has small limits by Theorems 2.1.11, 2.1.14, and 2.1.22. The forgetful functor $U^{\mathbf{T}}: \mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ has a left adjoint (the free algebra functor). The third condition of Theorem 7.7 concerning the factorization system and optimal lifts is satisfied by the regular factorization structure of $\mathcal{K}$, as shown in the proof of Proposition 4.17. Since $\mathcal{K}$ has small weak coproducts, it has quasi-colimits, and the $E$-preservation property of $T$ ensures that the remaining hypotheses of Theorem 7.7 are met. $\square$
