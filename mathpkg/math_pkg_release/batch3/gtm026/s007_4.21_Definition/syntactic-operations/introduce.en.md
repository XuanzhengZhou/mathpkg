Syntactic operations provide a linguistic or syntactic viewpoint on algebraic theories. For a set $X$, the elements of $XT$ are viewed as formal expressions or "terms" built from the variables in $X$ using the operations of the theory, modulo the equations of the theory. For finitary theories, this corresponds precisely to $E$-equivalence classes of terms. In the general case, syntactic operations in $X$ are the formal carriers of the algebraic structure, which are then interpreted in actual algebras via the structure map $\xi: XT \to X$.
