The generated $T$-subalgebra $\langle A \rangle$ is the smallest $T$-subalgebra of $(X, \xi)$ containing the subset $A$. It is obtained by "closing up $A$ under the $T$-operations," analogous to how the subsemigroup generated by a subset consists of all finite products of elements from the subset. In the language of $T$-algebras, $\langle A \rangle$ is defined via the image factorization of the composite $AT \to XT \to X$. This construction satisfies the expected properties: $A \subset \langle A \rangle$, monotonicity, and idempotence ($\langle \langle A \rangle \rangle = \langle A \rangle$).
