Theorem 5.11 establishes that the syntactic rank (the minimum number of variables needed to express an operation as a term) and the semantic rank (the minimum cardinality of a support of the corresponding natural transformation) coincide for any algebraic theory in $\mathbf{Set}$. This result reconciles the linguistic and functional perspectives on operations: the number of variables an operation "really depends on" is the same whether measured syntactically (via term representations) or semantically (via its behavior on algebras). The proof uses the bijection from Theorem 5.5 and careful analysis of factorizations through subsets of $X$, including a subtle treatment of the arity-0 case.
