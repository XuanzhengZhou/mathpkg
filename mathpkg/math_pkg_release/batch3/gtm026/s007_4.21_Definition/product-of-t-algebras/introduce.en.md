This lemma establishes that the category of $T$-algebras for an algebraic theory $T$ in monoid form has products. Given a family of $T$-algebras $(X_i, \xi_i)$, the cartesian product set carries a unique $T$-algebra structure making all projection maps $T$-homomorphisms. The proof relies on the principle that two functions into a product are equal if and only if they are equal when followed by each projection. This lemma is a key component in the proof of Theorem 4.25, showing that the class $\mathcal{A}$ of $\Omega$-algebras arising from $T$-algebras is closed under products.
