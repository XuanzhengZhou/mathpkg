Let $T$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathbf{Set}$ and let $X$ be a set. Let $\mathcal{O}_X(T)$ be the set of natural transformations from $U^X$ to $U$ (semantic operations in $X$), where $U: \mathbf{Set}^T \longrightarrow \mathbf{Set}$ is the forgetful functor and $U^X = U \circ (\cdot)^X$. Then the passage:

$$XT \longrightarrow \mathcal{O}_X(T)$$
$$\omega \longmapsto \tilde{\omega}: U^X \longrightarrow U$$

defined by $\langle f, (Y, \theta)\tilde{\omega} \rangle = \langle \omega, fT \cdot \theta \rangle$ for each $T$-algebra $(Y, \theta)$ and each $f: X \longrightarrow Y$, is a bijection.
