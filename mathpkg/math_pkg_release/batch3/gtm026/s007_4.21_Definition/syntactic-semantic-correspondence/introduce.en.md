Theorem 5.5 establishes a fundamental correspondence between two perspectives on operations in an algebraic theory. Syntactic operations are elements of $XT$, viewed as formal expressions. Semantic operations are natural transformations $\alpha: U^X \to U$, which assign to each $T$-algebra $(Y, \theta)$ a set-theoretic operation $Y^X \to Y$ in a way compatible with $T$-homomorphisms. The theorem shows these two viewpoints are equivalent: every syntactic operation $\omega \in XT$ induces a semantic operation $\tilde{\omega}$, and every semantic operation arises uniquely in this way. This bijection validates viewing algebraic theories through either the syntactic or semantic lens.
