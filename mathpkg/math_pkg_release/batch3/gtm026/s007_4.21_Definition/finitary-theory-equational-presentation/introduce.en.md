This theorem establishes the fundamental equivalence between finitary algebraic theories and equational presentations. It shows that every finitary algebraic theory $T$ in $\mathbf{Set}$ can be presented by an operator domain $\Omega$ and a set of equations $E$, such that the category of $T$-algebras is isomorphic to the category of $(\Omega, E)$-algebras. Combined with earlier results, this proves that finitary universal algebra is precisely the study of finitary algebraic theories in $\mathbf{Set}$, completing the bijective correspondence between equational presentations and finitary theories.
