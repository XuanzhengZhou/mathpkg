Let $(X, \xi)$ be a $T$-algebra and let $A$ be a subset of $X$ with inclusion map $i: A \hookrightarrow X$. $A$ is a $T$-subalgebra of $(X, \xi)$ if there exists a factorization:

$$\xymatrix{
AT \ar[r]^{\xi_0} \ar[d]_{iT} & A \ar[d]^{i} \\
XT \ar[r]_{\xi} & X
}$$

that is, there exists $\xi_0: AT \longrightarrow A$ such that $iT \cdot \xi = \xi_0 \cdot i$. Equivalently, $A$ is a $T$-subalgebra if and only if there exists a $T$-algebra structure on $A$ making the inclusion $i$ a $T$-homomorphism.
