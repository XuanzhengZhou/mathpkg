A $T$-subalgebra is the natural notion of subalgebra in the category of $T$-algebras for an algebraic theory $T$ in monoid form. A subset $A$ of a $T$-algebra $(X, \xi)$ is a $T$-subalgebra precisely when the inclusion map can be lifted to a $T$-homomorphism. Equivalently, the structure map $\xi$ restricts to a map on $AT$, making $A$ itself a $T$-algebra. This definition is used in the proof of Theorem 4.25 to show that $\Omega$-subalgebras of $T$-algebras are $T$-subalgebras, establishing closure under subalgebras for the class $\mathcal{A}$.
