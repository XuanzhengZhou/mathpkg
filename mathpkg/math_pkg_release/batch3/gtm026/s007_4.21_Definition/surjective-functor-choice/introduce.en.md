This lemma states that every set-valued functor preserves surjectivity of maps, provided the axiom of choice holds. Given a surjective function $f: X \twoheadrightarrow Y$ and any functor $H: \mathbf{Set} \to \mathbf{Set}$, the induced map $fH: XH \to YH$ remains surjective. The proof uses the axiom of choice to select a right inverse (choice function) for $f$, which is then lifted through the functor. This fact is used in the proof of Theorem 4.25 to construct the $T$-algebra structure on a quotient algebra.
