The universal property of $AT$ states that any assignment $f: A \to X$ of the variables (generators) into an $(\Omega, E)$-algebra extends uniquely to an $\Omega$-homomorphism $f^{\#}: AT \to X$. The insertion map $A\eta$ sends each variable $a$ to its equivalence class $[a]$ in the quotient $AT = A\Omega/E_A$. This says that $AT$ is the free $(\Omega, E)$-algebra on $A$ in the categorical sense: the functor $A \mapsto AT$ is left adjoint to the forgetful functor from $(\Omega, E)$-algebras to $\mathbf{Set}$.
