The clone of $(\Omega, E)$ is the category $\mathbf{Set}(\Omega, E)$ whose objects are sets $A, B, C, \ldots$ and whose morphisms $\alpha: A \longrightarrow B$ are functions $\alpha: A \to BT$, where $BT = B\Omega/E_B$ is the free $(\Omega, E)$-algebra on $B$. Composition is defined by

$$
\bigl(A \xrightarrow{\alpha} B\bigr) \circ \bigl(B \xrightarrow{\beta} C\bigr) = A \xrightarrow{\alpha} BT \xrightarrow{\beta^{\#}} CT
$$

where $\beta^{\#}: BT \to CT$ is the unique $\Omega$-homomorphism extending $\beta: B \to CT$ via the universal property of $BT$ (Theorem 2.5). Identity morphisms are defined by

$$
A\eta: A \longrightarrow AT, \qquad \langle a, A\eta\rangle = [a].
$$
