Let $G$ be a group and let
$$
C = \{ab(ba)^{-1} : a, b \in G\}
$$
be the set of all commutators of $G$. The \text{commutator subgroup} (or \text{derived subgroup}) of $G$, denoted $[G, G]$ or $G'$, is the normal subgroup generated by $C$:
$$
[G, G] = \langle C \rangle^{N} = \langle ab(ba)^{-1} : a, b \in G \rangle^{N}.
$$
