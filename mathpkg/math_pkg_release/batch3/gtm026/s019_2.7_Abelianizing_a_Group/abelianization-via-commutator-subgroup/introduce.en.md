The quotient $G/[G,G]$ of a group by its commutator subgroup is always abelian, and it is the largest abelian quotient of $G$. This construction is called the abelianization of $G$. The commutator subgroup $[G,G]$ is precisely the smallest normal subgroup whose quotient yields an abelian group; any homomorphism from $G$ to an abelian group factors uniquely through $G/[G,G]$.
