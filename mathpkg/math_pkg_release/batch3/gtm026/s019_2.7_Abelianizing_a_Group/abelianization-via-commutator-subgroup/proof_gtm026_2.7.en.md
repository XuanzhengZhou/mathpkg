Let $G$ be a group, let $C = \{ab(ba)^{-1} : a, b \in G\}$ be the set of commutators, and let $N = \langle C \rangle^{N}$ be the normal subgroup generated by $C$. For any $a, b \in G$, compute in the quotient $G/N$:

$$
aN \cdot bN \cdot (bN \cdot aN)^{-1} = aN \cdot bN \cdot (b^{-1}N \cdot a^{-1}N) = (ab)N \cdot (b^{-1}a^{-1})N = (ab(ba)^{-1})N.
$$

Since $ab(ba)^{-1} \in C \subseteq N$, we have $(ab(ba)^{-1})N = N$, which is the identity element of $G/N$. Therefore $aN \cdot bN = bN \cdot aN$ for all $a, b \in G$, so $G/N$ is abelian.
