Let $G$ be a group, let $N = [G,G]$ be its commutator subgroup, and let $\eta: G \to G/N$ be the canonical projection. We have already shown that $G/N$ is abelian.

Let $f: G \to A$ be any homomorphism with $A$ abelian. Define $f^{\#}: G/N \to A$ by $(aN)f^{\#} = af$. We must verify that $f^{\#}$ is well-defined.

Suppose $aN = bN$, i.e., $b^{-1}a \in N$. Since $N$ is the normal subgroup generated by the commutators $C = \{xy(yx)^{-1} : x, y \in G\}$, it suffices to check that $f$ vanishes on $C$. For any $x, y \in G$:

$$
(xy(yx)^{-1})f = (xy)f \cdot ((yx)^{-1})f = (xf)(yf) \cdot (yf)^{-1}(xf)^{-1}.
$$

Since $A$ is abelian, $(xf)(yf) = (yf)(xf)$, so the product equals the identity of $A$. Thus $C \subseteq \ker f$, and consequently $N \subseteq \ker f$. Therefore $b^{-1}a \in N \subseteq \ker f$, which gives $(b^{-1}a)f = e_A$, i.e., $af = bf$. Hence $(aN)f^{\#} = af = bf = (bN)f^{\#}$, establishing well-definedness.

The homomorphism property of $f^{\#}$ follows directly: $(aN \cdot bN)f^{\#} = (abN)f^{\#} = (ab)f = (af)(bf) = (aN)f^{\#} \cdot (bN)f^{\#}$.

By construction, $a(\eta \cdot f^{\#}) = (aN)f^{\#} = af$, so $\eta \cdot f^{\#} = f$. Uniqueness of $f^{\#}$ is forced: if $\eta \cdot g = f$ for some $g: G/N \to A$, then for any $aN \in G/N$ we must have $(aN)g = a(\eta \cdot g) = af = (aN)f^{\#}$, so $g = f^{\#}$.

Thus $\eta$ is the reflection map, and abelian groups form a full reflective subcategory of groups.
