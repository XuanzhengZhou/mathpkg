The abelianization construction $G \mapsto G/[G,G]$ exhibits the category of abelian groups as a full reflective subcategory of the category of all groups. The canonical projection $\eta: G \to G/[G,G]$ serves as the reflection map: any homomorphism from $G$ into an abelian group factors uniquely through $\eta$. This is a fundamental example of a reflective subcategory and illustrates how universal properties capture algebraic structure.
