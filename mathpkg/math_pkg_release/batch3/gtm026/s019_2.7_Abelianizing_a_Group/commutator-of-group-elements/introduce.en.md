The commutator $[a, b] = ab(ba)^{-1}$ measures the extent to which two group elements $a$ and $b$ fail to commute. If $a$ and $b$ commute, then $ab = ba$ and the commutator equals the identity. The set of all commutators $C = \{[a, b] : a, b \in G\}$ captures every instance of non-abelian behavior in the group $G$.
