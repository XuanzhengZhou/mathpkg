Extending but slightly the definition in 3.1, a concrete category $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ will be called a \textbf{category of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure} if there exists a literal category of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure such that $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ is isomorphic to $\mathcal{C}$ in $\operatorname{Con}(\mathcal{K})$.

Notice, incidentally, that if $H: (\mathcal{A}, U) \to (\mathcal{B}, V) \in \operatorname{Con}(\mathcal{K})$ then if $H: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}$ is an isomorphism of categories then $H^{-1}: \mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{A}$ is automatically over $\mathcal{K}$; that is, for functors over $\mathcal{K}$, there is no difference between isomorphisms in $\operatorname{Con}(\mathcal{K})$ and isomorphisms of categories.
