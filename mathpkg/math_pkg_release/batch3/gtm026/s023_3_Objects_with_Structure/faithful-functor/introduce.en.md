A functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}$ is faithful when it is injective on hom-sets: distinct morphisms in $\mathcal{A}$ are mapped to distinct morphisms in $\mathcal{B}$. Faithfulness is the essential property required of the forgetful functor in a concrete category. The text notes that the image of a faithful functor need not be closed under composition.
