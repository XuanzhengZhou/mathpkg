By 6.9, 1.19, 1.18, 3.6, and 3.3 it is sufficient to prove that the underlying set functor $U: Set^{S} \times Set^{T} \rightarrow Set$ is algebraic. By 1.5.40, present $Set^{S}$ as $(\Omega_{S}, E_{S})$-alg and $Set^{T}$ as $(\Omega_{T}, E_{T})$-alg. It is obvious that if $\Omega_{n} = (\Omega_{S})_{n} + (\Omega_{T})_{n}$ and $E = E_{S} + E_{T}$ that $U$ is isomorphic to the underlying set functor from $(\Omega, E)$-algebras. Since $S$ and $T$ are bounded, it follows from the construction in 1.5.40 that there exists a cardinal $n_0$ with $\Omega_{n} = \varnothing$ for all $n > n_0$. By 1.27, $U$ is algebraic.
