The category whose objects are nonempty sets and whose morphisms are all functions between them does not admit equalizers for all parallel pairs. This counterexample shows that the existence of equalizers is not automatic even in seemingly well-behaved concrete categories. A category theorist regards a category without equalizers as "incomplete," analogous to assuming all complex numbers are nonzero.
