Limits of a given diagram are unique up to a unique isomorphism that commutes with the cone maps. This is a standard consequence of the universal property: given two limits, each factors uniquely through the other, and the compositions must be identities by uniqueness. This justifies the common practice of referring to "the" limit and using the notation $\lim D$.
