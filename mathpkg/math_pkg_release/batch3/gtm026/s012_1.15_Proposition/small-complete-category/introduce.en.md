A category is small complete if it admits limits for all diagrams whose indexing scheme is small. This is a fundamental completeness property in category theory -- most categories of interest (sets, groups, rings, topological spaces) are small complete. The restriction to small diagrams avoids set-theoretic paradoxes that would arise from requiring limits over proper-class-sized diagrams.
