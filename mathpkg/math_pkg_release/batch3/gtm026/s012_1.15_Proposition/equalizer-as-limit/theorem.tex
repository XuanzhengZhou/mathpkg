Let $\Delta$ be the diagram scheme with two nodes $\{t, u\}$ and two edges $\alpha, \beta$ from $t$ to $u$. A diagram $(\Delta, D)$ in $\mathcal{K}$ is any pair of $\mathcal{K}$-morphisms $f, g: A \longrightarrow B$ with the same domain and codomain. A lower bound relative to the final set $\{t\}$ is a morphism $i: E \longrightarrow A$ such that $i.f = i.g$. Limits of such diagrams coincide with equalizers.
