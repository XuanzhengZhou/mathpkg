Equalizers can be understood as limits of diagrams consisting of two parallel arrows. For the diagram scheme with two nodes and two parallel edges, a lower bound relative to the final set $\{t\}$ is precisely a morphism that equalizes the two parallel arrows. The universal property of the limit then corresponds exactly to the universal property of the equalizer. This unification of concepts is a key insight of category theory.
