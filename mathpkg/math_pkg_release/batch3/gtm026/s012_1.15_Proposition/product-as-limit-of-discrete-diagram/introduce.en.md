A product in a category can be understood as the limit of a discrete diagram, i.e., a diagram whose scheme has no edges. For an $I$-indexed family of objects with no morphisms between them, a lower bound is precisely a family of projections from an object $L$ to each $D_i$, and the universal property of the limit is exactly the universal property of the product. This unifies the seemingly separate notions of products and limits.
