A diagram scheme is called small when both its set of nodes and each set of edges between any pair of nodes are small sets (i.e., sets, not proper classes). This size condition is essential for defining completeness: a category is called (small-)complete when it has limits for all small diagrams, avoiding set-theoretic issues with diagrams indexed by proper classes.
