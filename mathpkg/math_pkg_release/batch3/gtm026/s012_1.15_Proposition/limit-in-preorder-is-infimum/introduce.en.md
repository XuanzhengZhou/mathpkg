In a preordered category (where there is at most one morphism between any two objects), the categorical notion of a limit specializes to the order-theoretic notion of an infimum (greatest lower bound). A lower bound in the categorical sense is exactly a lower bound in the order sense, and the universal property of the limit translates to the property of being the greatest among all lower bounds.
