A limit of a diagram is a universal lower bound: it is the "best" way to map into the diagram compatibly with all its edges. The universal property ensures that any other lower bound factors uniquely through the limit. Limits unify many familiar constructions: products, equalizers, pullbacks, and terminal objects are all special cases of limits for appropriately chosen diagram schemes.
