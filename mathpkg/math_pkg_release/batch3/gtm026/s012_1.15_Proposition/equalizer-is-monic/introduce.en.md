An equalizer in a category is always a monomorphism. This proposition states that if $i: E \to A$ is the equalizer of two parallel morphisms $f, g: A \to B$, then $i$ is left-cancellative: whenever two morphisms $t, u: E' \to E$ satisfy $t.i = u.i$, they must be equal. This is a fundamental property used extensively in diagram chasing and in proving that limits yield monomorphisms under appropriate conditions.
