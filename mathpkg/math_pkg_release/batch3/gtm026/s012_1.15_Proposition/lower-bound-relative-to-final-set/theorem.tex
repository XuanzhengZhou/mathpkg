Let $(\Delta, D)$ be a diagram in $\mathcal{K}$ and let $F \subseteq N(\Delta)$ be a final subset. A \emph{lower bound relative to $F$} is a pair $(L, \psi)$ where $L$ is an object of $\mathcal{K}$ and $\psi$ assigns to each $i \in F$ a $\mathcal{K}$-morphism $\psi_i: L \longrightarrow D_i$ such that for all $i, i' \in F$, $j \in N(\Delta)$, $\alpha \in \Delta(i, j)$, $\alpha' \in \Delta(i', j)$, the diagram

$$\psi_i \circ D_\alpha = \psi_{i'} \circ D_{\alpha'}$$

commutes, and whenever $i, j \in F$ with $\alpha \in \Delta(i, j)$, the triangle $\psi_i \circ D_\alpha = \psi_j$ commutes.
