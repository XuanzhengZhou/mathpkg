We first show that $(YX_{\#}, \theta_{\#})$ is a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra. The diagram shows that $YX_{\#}e \cdot \theta_{\#} = \mathrm{id}_{YX_{\#}}$, since $YX_{\#}e: (YX_{\#}, YL)$ is a dynamorphism. Similarly, the diagram establishes first that $YX_{\#}m \cdot \theta_{\#} \cdot Y\Lambda$ and $\theta_{\#}T \cdot \theta_{\#} \cdot Y\Lambda$ are equal $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphisms and thus that $YX_{\#}m \cdot \theta_{\#}$ and $\theta_{\#}T \cdot \theta_{\#}$ are equal dynamorphisms. That $(YX_{\#}, YL, \theta_{\#})$ is a $\lambda$-algebra is clear since $\theta_{\#}$ is a dynamorphism. As

$$\theta_{\#} \cdot Y\Lambda = (Y\Lambda)^{\#} = Y\Lambda T \cdot \theta,$$

the map $Y\Lambda: (YX_{\#}, \theta_{\#}) \rightarrow (Y, \theta)$ is a $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphism.

Now suppose given a $\lambda$-algebra $(Q, \delta, \xi)$ and a $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphism $f: (Q, \delta) \rightarrow (Y, \theta)$. Let $\Gamma: (Q, \delta) \rightarrow (YX_{\#}, YL)$ be the unique dynamorphic coextension of $f$. It suffices to show that $\Gamma: (Q, \xi) \rightarrow (YX_{\#}, \theta_{\#})$ is a $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphism. This is clear from the diagram.
