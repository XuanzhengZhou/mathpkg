\textbf{Theorem 3.18.} Let $X$ be an output process in $\mathcal{K}$, let $\mathbf{T} = (T, e, m)$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$, and let $\lambda: TX \longrightarrow XT$ be a distributive law of $X$ over $\mathbf{T}$. Then the forgetful functor $\mathcal{K}^{\lambda} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ has a right adjoint. Using notations as in 3.15 and 2.3, the cofree $\lambda$-algebra over the $\mathbf{T}$-algebra $(Y, \theta)$ is $(YX_{\#}, YL, \theta_{\#})$ with coinclusion of the generators $Y\Lambda: (YX_{\#}, \theta_{\#}) \longrightarrow (Y, \theta)$, where $\theta_{\#}$ is obtained from $\theta$ as the unique dynamorphic coextension of the unique $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphic extension $(Y\Lambda)^{\#}$.
