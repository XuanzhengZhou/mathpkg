This theorem establishes that the functor $U$ from the category $\mathcal{K}^{\lambda}$ of $\lambda$-algebras to the category $\mathcal{K}^{\star}$ is algebraic, meaning it is monadic. The semantic comparison functor $\Phi: \mathcal{K}^{\lambda} \rightarrow \mathcal{K}^{\star}$ provides an explicit isomorphism, showing that a $\lambda$-algebra $(Q, \delta, \xi)$ corresponds to a $\mathbf{T}^{\star}$-algebra $(Q, \psi)$ where $\psi = \delta^{\alpha} T \circ \xi$. The inverse functor $\Phi^{-1}$ recovers the $\lambda$-algebra structure from a $\mathbf{T}^{\star}$-algebra via the unit and multiplication of the algebraic theory.
