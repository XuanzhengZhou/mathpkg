The Composition Theorem for Adjoints states that adjoint functors compose nicely. If $U_1: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}$ and $U_2: \mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{C}$ each have left adjoints $F_1$ and $F_2$, then the composite $U_1U_2: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{C}$ has left adjoint $F_2F_1$. The theorem also explicitly provides the unit and counit of the composite adjunction in terms of those of the factors: the unit is $\eta_2 \circ F_2\eta_1U_2$ and the counit is $U_1\varepsilon_2F_1 \circ \varepsilon_1$.
