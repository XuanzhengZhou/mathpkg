Prove the truncated adjoint functor theorem: Let $\alpha$ be any cardinal $\geqslant 1$. Let $U: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ be a functor. An $\alpha$-small set is a set of cardinal less than $\alpha$; an $\alpha$-product in $\mathcal{A}$ is an $I$-indexed product with $I$ of cardinal less than $\alpha$. Assume that $\mathcal{A}$ has $\alpha$-products and equalizers of pairs. Then $U$ has a left adjoint if and only if $U$ preserves $\alpha$-products, $U$ preserves equalizers, and for every $K$ in $\mathcal{K}$ there exists a solution set $\mathcal{S}$ such that $\mathcal{S}$ is $\alpha$-small and such that, for every $(S, f) \in \mathcal{S}$, the set of all $x: S \longrightarrow S$ with $f.xU = f$ is $\alpha$-small.
