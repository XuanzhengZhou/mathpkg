The Special Adjoint Functor Theorem (SAFT) provides an alternative set of sufficient conditions for the existence of a left adjoint, replacing the explicit solution set condition of the General Adjoint Functor Theorem with structural properties of the domain category: the existence of a cogenerator and the condition of being well-powered (every object has only a small set of subobjects). The proof constructs a solution set using the cogenerator $C$ and the well-powered condition, then applies Theorem 2.24. This variant is often easier to verify in concrete algebraic categories.
