The Godement rules provide a systematic notation for composing natural transformations with functors, also known as "whiskering." Given a natural transformation $\tau: F_1 \to F_2$ and functors $G$ and $H$ on either side, the rules specify how to form $\tau GH$, $FG\tau$, and $F\tau H$ as natural transformations between the appropriate composite functors. The fourth rule expresses compatibility with vertical composition of natural transformations. These rules are fundamental for working in functor categories and are used extensively in the calculus of adjunctions.
