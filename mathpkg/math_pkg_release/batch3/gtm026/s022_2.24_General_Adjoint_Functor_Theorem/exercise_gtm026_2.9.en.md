If $\mathcal{K}, \mathcal{L}$ are categories, the functor category $\mathcal{L}^{\mathcal{K}}$ has as objects all functors $\mathcal{K} \rightarrow \mathcal{L}$, has as morphisms natural transformations and has as composition and identities the pointwise ones. Establish that $\mathcal{L}^{\mathcal{K}}$ is a category. Isomorphisms in $\mathcal{L}^{\mathcal{K}}$ are called natural equivalences. Show that the natural transformation $\tau: F \rightarrow G$ is a natural equivalence if and only if each $A\tau$ is an isomorphism in $\mathcal{L}$. Show that the category of $M$-sets as in exercise 1.19 is the same thing as $\mathbf{Set}^M$.
