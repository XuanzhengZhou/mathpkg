The General Adjoint Functor Theorem, due to P. Freyd, gives necessary and sufficient conditions for a functor $U: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ to possess a left adjoint. Assuming $\mathcal{A}$ is locally small and has small limits, $U$ has a left adjoint if and only if it preserves small products, preserves equalizers, and satisfies the solution set condition at every object $K$ in $\mathcal{K}$. The theorem is a cornerstone of categorical universal algebra, providing the most commonly used criterion for the existence of free objects.
