Assume that $\mathcal{A}$ is a locally small category which has small limits. Let $U: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ be a functor. Then necessary and sufficient for the functor $U$ to have a left adjoint are the three conditions:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ preserves small products (i.e., if $p_i: A \longrightarrow A_i$ is a product in $\mathcal{A}$ and $i$ ranges over a small set then $p_i U: AU \longrightarrow A_i U$ is a product in $\mathcal{K}$).
\item $U$ preserves equalizers (i.e., if $h = \text{eq}(f, g)$ in $\mathcal{A}$ then $h U = \text{eq}(f U, g U)$ in $\mathcal{K}$).
\item $U$ satisfies the solution set condition at $K$ for every $K$ in $\mathcal{K}$.
\end{enumerate}
