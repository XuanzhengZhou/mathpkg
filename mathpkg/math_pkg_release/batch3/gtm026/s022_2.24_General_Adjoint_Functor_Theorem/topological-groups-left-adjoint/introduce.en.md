Example 2.26 demonstrates the power of the General Adjoint Functor Theorem (2.24) by showing that the forgetful functor from topological groups to topological spaces has a left adjoint — that is, every topological space generates a free topological group. The proof verifies the three conditions of Theorem 2.24: the category of topological groups is locally small, has products (product group with product topology) and equalizers (subspace topology on a subgroup), both preserved by the forgetful functor, and the solution set condition at a space $K$ is satisfied using cardinals bounded by the cardinality of the free group on $K$.
