Example 2.28 (due to J. R. Isbell) shows that limit preservation alone is insufficient to guarantee the existence of a left adjoint — the solution set condition is genuinely needed. The example constructs a set-valued functor from the category of groups that preserves all limits but fails to satisfy the solution set condition at some object, and therefore (by the necessity direction of Theorem 2.24) cannot possess a left adjoint. This is a crucial counterexample demonstrating that the three conditions of the General Adjoint Functor Theorem are independent.
