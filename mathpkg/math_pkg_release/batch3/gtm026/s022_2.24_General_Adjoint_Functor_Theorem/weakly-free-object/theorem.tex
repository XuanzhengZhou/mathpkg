Given a functor $U: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ and an object $K$ in $\mathcal{K}$, a weakly free $\mathcal{A}$-object over $K$ with respect to $U$ is a pair $(F', \eta')$ where $F'$ is an $\mathcal{A}$-object and $\eta': K \longrightarrow F'U$ is a $\mathcal{K}$-morphism such that for every pair $(A, f)$ with $f: K \longrightarrow AU$ there exists a morphism $\psi: F' \longrightarrow A$ in $\mathcal{A}$ with $\eta'.\psi U = f$.
