A weakly free object over $K$ with respect to a functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{K}$ is a weakening of the usual notion of a free object: instead of requiring a unique mediating morphism, it requires only the existence of some (not necessarily unique) morphism satisfying the factorization condition. This concept is introduced as an intermediate step in the proof of the General Adjoint Functor Theorem (2.24), where a weakly free object is first constructed from a solution set using products, and then refined to a genuinely free object via Lemma 2.25.
