The valency map is an integer-valued function on $\Omega$-words that assigns weight $1$ to each element of $A$ and weight $1 - m$ to each $m$-ary operator symbol $\tilde{\omega} \in \Omega_m$, with additivity over concatenation. For any well-formed $\Omega$-formula $q$, one has $\operatorname{val}(q) = 1$, and any proper left segment of $q$ has strictly positive valency. This property makes the valency map a powerful tool for proving uniqueness of factorizations of $\Omega$-words, as used in the proof of the Uncoupling Lemma.
