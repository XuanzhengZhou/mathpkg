The total description map $\delta^@: X\Omega \longrightarrow X$ is the canonical evaluation morphism associated to an $\Omega$-algebra $(X, \delta)$. It interprets each $\Omega$-word (formal term) over $X$ as an actual element of the algebra $X$ by recursively applying the operations $\delta_\omega$. On the generators $x \in X$, it acts as the identity; on a compound term $p_1 \cdots p_n \omega$, it first evaluates each subterm $p_i$ and then applies the $n$-ary operation $\delta_\omega$ to the resulting $n$-tuple. This map formalizes the passage from syntax (terms) to semantics (algebra elements), making operations out of formulas.
