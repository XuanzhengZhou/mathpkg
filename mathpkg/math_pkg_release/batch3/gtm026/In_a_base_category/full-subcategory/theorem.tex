Let $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$ be categories and let $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}$ be a functor that is injective on objects. Then $U$ is a \textbf{full subcategory} of $\mathcal{B}$ if for every pair $(A, A')$ of objects in $\mathcal{A}$, the induced map

$$\mathcal{A}(A, A') \longrightarrow \mathcal{B}(AU, A'U)$$

is surjective. Equivalently, in addition to the faithful condition, every $\mathcal{B}$-morphism between images of $\mathcal{A}$-objects comes from an $\mathcal{A}$-morphism.
