Let $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ and $(\mathcal{B}, V)$ be concrete categories over $\mathcal{K}$ and let $H: (\mathcal{A}, U) \to (\mathcal{B}, V)$ be a homomorphism in $\text{Con}(\mathcal{K})$. If $H: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}$ is an isomorphism of categories, then the inverse functor $H^{-1}: \mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{A}$ is automatically over $\mathcal{K}$, i.e., $V H^{-1} = U$. Consequently, for functors over $\mathcal{K}$, there is no difference between isomorphisms in $\text{Con}(\mathcal{K})$ and isomorphisms of the underlying categories.
