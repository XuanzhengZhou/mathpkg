For a fixed object $B$ in a category $\mathcal{B}$, the constant functor $U_B: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}$ collapses the entire source category $\mathcal{A}$ to the single object $B$, sending every morphism to $\mathrm{id}_B$. This construction is useful for counterexamples: for instance, no constant functor from the category of groups to the category of sets is faithful, illustrating that faithfulness imposes nontrivial constraints on a functor.
