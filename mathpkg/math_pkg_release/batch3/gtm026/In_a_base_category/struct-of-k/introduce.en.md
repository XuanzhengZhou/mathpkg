$\text{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ is the full replete subcategory of $\text{Con}(\mathcal{K})$ whose objects are those concrete categories that qualify as "categories of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure" — that is, those isomorphic in $\text{Con}(\mathcal{K})$ to a literal category of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure. This provides the proper universe in which to place categories of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with algebraic structure (such as $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ for an algebraic theory $\mathbf{T}$). The fact that $\text{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ is full replete reflects the principle that the invariants in a given category of structured objects are implicit in the forgetful functor.
