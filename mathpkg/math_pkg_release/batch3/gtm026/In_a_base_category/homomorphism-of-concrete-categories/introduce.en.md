A homomorphism of concrete categories over $\mathcal{K}$ is a functor between the underlying categories that respects the forgetful functors to $\mathcal{K}$. The commutativity condition $UH = V$ means that $H$ does not change the underlying $\mathcal{K}$-object of a structured object — it only transforms the additional structure. Homomorphisms are the morphisms in the category $\text{Con}(\mathcal{K})$ of concrete categories over $\mathcal{K}$, with composition inherited from functor composition and identity functors serving as identity homomorphisms.
