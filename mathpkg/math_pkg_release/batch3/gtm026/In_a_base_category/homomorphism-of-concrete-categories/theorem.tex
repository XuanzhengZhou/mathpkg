Let $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ and $(\mathcal{B}, V)$ be concrete categories over a base category $\mathcal{K}$. A \textbf{homomorphism} $H: (\mathcal{A}, U) \to (\mathcal{B}, V)$ of concrete categories is a functor $H: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}$ such that the diagram of functors

\[
\begin{CD}
\mathcal{A} @>{H}>> \mathcal{B} \\
@V{U}VV @VV{V}V \\
\mathcal{K} @= \mathcal{K}
\end{CD}
\]

commutes, i.e., $UH = V$. Such a functor $H$ is said to be \emph{over $\mathcal{K}$}.
