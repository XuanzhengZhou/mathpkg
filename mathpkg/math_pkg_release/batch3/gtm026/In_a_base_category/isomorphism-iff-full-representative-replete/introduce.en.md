A functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}$ is an isomorphism of categories precisely when it combines three properties: it is a full subcategory (every $\mathcal{B}$-morphism between images comes from $\mathcal{A}$), it is representative (every $\mathcal{B}$-object is isomorphic to an object in the image), and it is full replete (the image is closed under isomorphisms). This characterization decomposes the notion of isomorphism into three independent and meaningful categorical properties.
