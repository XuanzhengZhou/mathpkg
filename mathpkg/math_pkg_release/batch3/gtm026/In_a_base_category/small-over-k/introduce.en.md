A literal category $\mathcal{C}$ of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure is said to be small over $\mathcal{K}$ when for each $\mathcal{K}$-object $K$, the collection $\mathcal{C}(K)$ of $\mathcal{C}$-structures on $K$ is a small set rather than a proper class. This condition ensures that $\mathcal{C}$ is a locally small category (at the level of fibers) and is important for foundational considerations when one wishes to form functor categories or apply adjoint functor theorems.
