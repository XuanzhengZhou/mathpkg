The clone of an equational presentation $(\Omega, E)$ is the category $\operatorname{Set}(\Omega, E)$ whose objects are sets and whose morphisms $A \to B$ are functions from $A$ to the free $(\Omega, E)$-algebra $BT$ on $B$. Composition uses the universal property: a morphism $\beta: B \to C$ lifts uniquely to an $\Omega$-homomorphism $\beta^\#: BT \to CT$, and composing $\alpha: A \to BT$ with this lift yields a morphism $A \to CT$. The clone captures all the algebraic information of the equational presentation — it encodes how terms in the theory compose with each other via substitution — and turns out to be a presentation-independent invariant, providing the categorical formulation of an algebraic theory.
