The clone of an equational presentation $(\Omega, E)$ is the category $\operatorname{Set}(\Omega, E)$ whose objects are sets $A, B, C, \ldots$ and whose morphisms $\alpha: A \longrightarrow B$ are functions $\alpha: A \longrightarrow BT$ where $BT = B\Omega / E_B$.

Composition is defined by
$$(A \xrightarrow{\alpha} B) \circ (B \xrightarrow{\beta} C) = A \xrightarrow{\alpha} BT \xrightarrow{\beta^\#} CT$$
where $\beta^\#: BT \longrightarrow CT$ is the unique $\Omega$-homomorphism extending $\beta$ via the universal property of $BT$.

Identity morphisms are defined by the insertion-of-variables map
$$\eta_A: A \longrightarrow AT$$
