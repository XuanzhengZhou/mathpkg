For any adjointness $(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{K}, U, F, \eta, \varepsilon)$, there is a canonical functor $\Phi\colon \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ to the Eilenberg-Moore category of algebras for the induced monad $\mathbf{T} = (FU, \eta, F\varepsilon U)$. On objects, $\Phi$ sends $A$ to the $\mathbf{T}$-algebra $(AU, A\varepsilon U)$, and it makes the triangle with the two forgetful functors commute. This comparison functor is the central object in monadicity theorems, which ask when $\Phi$ is an equivalence of categories.
