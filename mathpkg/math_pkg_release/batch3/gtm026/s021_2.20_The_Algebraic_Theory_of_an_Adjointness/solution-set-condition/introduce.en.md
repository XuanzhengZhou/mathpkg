The solution set condition is a crucial technical hypothesis in the General Adjoint Functor Theorem. For a functor $U\colon \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{K}$ and an object $K \in \mathcal{K}$, it requires the existence of a small set of morphisms $s\colon K \to SU$ that jointly "cover" all morphisms from $K$ into objects of the form $AU$, in the sense that every $f\colon K \to AU$ factors through some $s$ in the solution set via a morphism $g\colon S \to A$. Together with completeness of $\mathcal{A}$, this condition guarantees that $U$ has a left adjoint.
