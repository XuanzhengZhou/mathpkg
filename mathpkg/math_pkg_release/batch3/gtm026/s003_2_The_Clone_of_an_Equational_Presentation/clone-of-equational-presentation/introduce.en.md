The clone of an equational presentation $(\Omega, E)$ is the category $\operatorname{Set}(\Omega, E)$ that encodes the algebraic operations and their substitution behavior. Its objects are sets, and a morphism from $A$ to $B$ is a function from $A$ into the free algebra $BT$. Composition uses the universal property of free algebras: $\beta \circ \alpha$ is obtained by applying $\beta^{\#}$ (the unique $\Omega$-homomorphism extending $\beta$) to the values of $\alpha$. The identity on $A$ is the insertion of variables map $A\eta$. The clone captures the abstract algebraic structure of the equational presentation independently of any particular syntactic representation.
