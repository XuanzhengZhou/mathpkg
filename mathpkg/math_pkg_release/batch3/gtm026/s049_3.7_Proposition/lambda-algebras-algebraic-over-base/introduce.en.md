Theorem 3.15 establishes that the category $\mathcal{K}^{\lambda}$ of $\lambda$-algebras is always algebraic over the base category $\mathcal{K}$, for any distributive law $\lambda \colon TX \to XT$ of an input process $X$ over an algebraic theory $\mathbf{T}$. The proof makes crucial use of the distributive law axioms from Definition 3.6, showing that the forgetful functor from $\mathcal{K}^{\lambda}$ to $\mathcal{K}$ is monadic. This result generalizes the earlier observation that $\mathbf{Set}^{\lambda}$ is isomorphic to $\mathbf{Set}^{\mathbf{X} \otimes \mathbf{T}}$, which is algebraic over $\mathbf{Set}$ by Theorem 3.6.21.
