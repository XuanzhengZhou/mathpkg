Theorem 5.6 addresses the problem that not all interesting Birkhoff subcategories of $\mathcal{A}^T$ arise from the lifting construction $\bar{\mathcal{B}}$ of Corollary 5.5. When $T$ is an algebraic theory in $\mathbf{Set}$, the theorem characterizes those full replete subcategories $\mathcal{C}$ of $\mathcal{A}^{\bar{T}}$ that are $E$-Birkhoff subcategories and algebraic over $\mathcal{A}$. The conditions required are closure under products, under optimal injective $T$-homomorphisms, and under $T$-homomorphisms that are split epimorphisms in $\mathcal{A}$. The proof relies on the regularity of $(\mathbf{Set}, \text{surjections}, \text{injections})$ and the fact that $T$ preserves surjections.
