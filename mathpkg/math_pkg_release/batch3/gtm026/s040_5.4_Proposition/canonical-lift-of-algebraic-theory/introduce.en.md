The canonical lift of an algebraic theory $T$ to a category $\mathcal{A}$ is an algebraic theory $\bar{T}$ in $\mathcal{A}$ constructed in the proof of Theorem 5.2. This construction lifts the operations of $T$ so that they become admissible morphisms in $\mathcal{A}$, preserving the algebraic structure while adapting it to the new categorical context. The canonical lift provides a fundamental tool for transferring algebraic theories between categories.
