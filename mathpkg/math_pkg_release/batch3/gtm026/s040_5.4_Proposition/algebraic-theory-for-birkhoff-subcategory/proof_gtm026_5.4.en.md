To prove this, we must only be sure that Proposition 5.10 applies. But this is clear: the subcategory $\mathcal{C}$ constructed in Theorem 5.6 is closed under products, optimal injective $T$-homomorphisms into, and $T$-homomorphisms which are split epimorphisms in $\mathcal{A}$ out of. These closure properties together with the construction of $\mathcal{C}$ as a full replete subcategory of $\mathcal{A}^{\bar{T}}$ imply that $\mathcal{C}$ satisfies the taut condition of 2.3.37. Indeed, if $\{f_i: P \to C_i\}$ is a family in $\mathcal{A}^{\bar{T}}$ with each $C_i \in \mathcal{C}$ and $\{f_i U_1\}$ optimal in $\mathcal{A}$, then the closure under optimal injections (equalizers) forces $P \in \mathcal{C}$. Hence 5.10 applies, yielding the description of $\hat{T}$. $\square$
