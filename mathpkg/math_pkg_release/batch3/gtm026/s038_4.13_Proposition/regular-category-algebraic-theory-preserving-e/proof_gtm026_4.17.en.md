All axioms are obvious except 4.5 (the diagonal fill-in property). To verify 4.5, let $f: (X, \xi) \longrightarrow (Y, \theta)$ be a $T$-homomorphism and let $(e, m)$ be an $E$-$M$ factorization of $fU^T$ in $\mathcal{K}$. Since $eT \in E$, one can lift this factorization to $\mathcal{K}^T$ using the algebraic theory structure, obtaining a commutative diagram in $\mathcal{K}^T$ with the required diagonal fill-in.

The condition that $T$ preserves $E$ is essential for this lifting argument to work. The verification that $\mathcal{K}^T$ is $E^T$-co-well-powered follows from the fact that the forgetful functor $U^T$ reflects isomorphisms and preserves the factorization structure.
