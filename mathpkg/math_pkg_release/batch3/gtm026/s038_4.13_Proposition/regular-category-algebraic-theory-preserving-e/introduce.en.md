Proposition 4.17 shows that the regular category structure lifts from a base category $\mathcal{K}$ to the Eilenberg-Moore category $\mathcal{K}^T$ of an algebraic theory $T$, provided that $T$ preserves the $E$ class ($eT \in E$ whenever $e \in E$). The factorization system $(E^T, M^T)$ on $\mathcal{K}^T$ is defined by lifting: a $T$-homomorphism belongs to $E^T$ (resp. $M^T$) if its underlying $\mathcal{K}$-morphism belongs to $E$ (resp. $M$). The key nontrivial verification is the diagonal fill-in axiom (4.5).
