We need only prove the first statement. We use 4.11. Let $t: C \longrightarrow D$ be split mono so that there exists $d: D \longrightarrow C$ with $t \circ d = \mathrm{id}_C$. Let $e \circ g = f \circ t$ be a commutative square with $e \in \mathbb{E}$. Then $e \circ (g \circ d) = f \circ t \circ d = f$. By the diagonal fill-in property (4.11), there exists a diagonal $h$ making the diagram commute, showing $t \in \mathbb{M}$.
