Theorem 4.23 combines the results of Proposition 4.17 and the Quasivariety Theorem (4.21) to characterize quasivarieties in the Eilenberg-Moore category $\mathcal{K}^T$ of an $E$-preserving algebraic theory $T$. A full replete subcategory of $\mathcal{K}^T$ is a quasivariety precisely when it is closed under products and $\mathbb{M}^T$-subobjects, where $\mathbb{E}^T$ and $\mathbb{M}^T$ are the lifted factorization classes. This theorem provides the abstract categorical framework for Birkhoff-type variety theorems in categories of algebras.
