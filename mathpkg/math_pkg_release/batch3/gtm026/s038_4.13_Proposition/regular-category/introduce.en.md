Definition 4.15 introduces the concept of a regular category, a triple $(\mathcal{H}, \mathbb{E}, \mathbb{M})$ where $(\mathbb{E}, \mathbb{M})$ is an image factorization system on $\mathcal{H}$. The key additional requirement is $\mathbb{E}$-co-well-poweredness, meaning that for every object $A$, the $\mathbb{E}$-quotient objects of $A$ form a small set. An $\mathbb{E}$-quotient object is an isomorphism class of $\mathbb{E}$-morphisms with domain $A$. Regular categories provide the natural setting for the quasivariety theorem and related results in categorical algebra.
