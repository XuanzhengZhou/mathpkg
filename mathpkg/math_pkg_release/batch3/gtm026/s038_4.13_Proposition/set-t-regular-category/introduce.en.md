Corollary 4.18 is an immediate consequence of Proposition 4.17, specialized to the case $\mathcal{K} = \mathbf{Set}$. In $\mathbf{Set}$, every algebraic theory $T$ automatically preserves surjections (1.4.29), so the condition $eT \in E$ is always satisfied. Thus for any algebraic theory $T$ in $\mathbf{Set}$, the category $\mathbf{Set}^T$ of $T$-algebras is a regular category, where $E$ is the class of surjective $T$-homomorphisms and $M$ is the class of injective $T$-homomorphisms. Furthermore, $E$ coincides with the class of coequalizers in $\mathbf{Set}^T$ and $M$ with the class of monomorphisms.
