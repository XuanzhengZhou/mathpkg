Theorem 4.21, the Quasivariety Theorem, is one of the central results motivating the definition of a regular category. It characterizes quasivarieties in a regular category $(\mathcal{K}, \mathbb{E}, \mathbb{M})$ as precisely those full replete subcategories that are closed under products and under $\mathbb{M}$-subobjects. The proof uses the image factorization system to construct reflections: given an object $A$, the reflection $r: A \longrightarrow B$ into $\mathcal{B}$ is obtained via the $\mathbb{E}$-$\mathbb{M}$ factorization, and the $E$-co-well-poweredness ensures the reflection exists by bounding the relevant objects to a small set.
