Proposition 4.13 describes closure properties of the $\mathbb{M}$ and $\mathbb{E}$ classes in an image factorization system under composition. If the composite $f \circ g$ belongs to $\mathbb{M}$, then the first factor $f$ must also belong to $\mathbb{M}$. Dually, if $f \circ g$ belongs to $\mathbb{E}$, then the second factor $g$ must belong to $\mathbb{E}$. This generalizes an earlier result (2.1.57(2)) and is a fundamental property used throughout the theory of regular categories.
