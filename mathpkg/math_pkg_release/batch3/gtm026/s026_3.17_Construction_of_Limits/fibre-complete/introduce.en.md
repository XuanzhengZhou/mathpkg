A concrete category $\mathcal{C} \in \mathbf{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ is *fibre-complete* when every family of $\mathcal{K}$-morphisms into (respectively, out of) structured objects can be lifted optimally (respectively, co-optimally). This is a strong completeness condition: for each $\mathcal{K}$-object $K$, the fibre $\mathcal{C}(K)$ of $\mathcal{C}$-structures on $K$ forms a complete lattice under the intrinsic order $\leqslant$, where infima are given by optimal lifts of the identity-indexed family and suprema by the dual co-optimal construction. Fibre-completeness implies that the forgetful functor $U$ admits both left and right adjoints and constructs all limits and colimits.
