$\mathcal{C} \in \mathbf{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ is \textbf{fibre-complete} if every (not necessarily small) family $f_i: K \longrightarrow (L_i, t_i)$ has an optimal lift and if every family $f_i: (L_i, t_i) \longrightarrow K$ has a co-optimal lift. Notice that each fibre $(\mathcal{C}(K), \leqslant)$ is indeed complete since for any subset $A$ of $\mathcal{C}(K)$, the infimum of $A$ is the optimal lift of the $A$-indexed family $\text{id}_K: K \longrightarrow (K, a)$ and the supremum of $A$ is constructed, dually, as a co-optimal lift.
