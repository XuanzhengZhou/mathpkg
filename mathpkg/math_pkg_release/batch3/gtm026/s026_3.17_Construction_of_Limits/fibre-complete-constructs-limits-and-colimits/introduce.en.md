When a concrete category $\mathcal{C} \in \mathbf{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ is fibre-complete, the forgetful functor $U: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{K}$ not only admits adjoints (Proposition 3.28) but also *constructs* all limits and colimits in the sense of Definition 3.17. Moreover, this proposition provides a clean characterization of coequalizers in $\mathcal{C}$: a morphism $h$ is a coequalizer precisely when it is co-optimal (i.e., a co-optimal quotient). This connects the abstract fibre-completeness condition directly to the computation of general categorical (co)limits.
