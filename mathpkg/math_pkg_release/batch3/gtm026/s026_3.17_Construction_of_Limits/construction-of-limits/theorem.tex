Let $\mathcal{C} \in \mathbf{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$. The functor $U: \mathcal{C} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ \textbf{constructs limits} if for every (not necessarily small) diagram $(\Delta, D)$ in $\mathcal{C}$ and every limit $(K, \psi)$ of $(\Delta, DU)$ in $\mathcal{K}$ the family $\psi_i: K \longrightarrow D_i = (L_i, t_i)$ has an optimal lift $s$. It follows at once that $\psi_i: (K, s) \longrightarrow (L_i, t_i)$ is a limit of $D$ in $\mathcal{C}$; thus, if $U$ constructs limits, then $\mathcal{C}$ has and $U$ preserves whatever sorts of limit $\mathcal{K}$ has.

Dually, $U: \mathcal{C} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ \textbf{constructs colimits} if $U^{\mathbf{op}}: \mathcal{C}^{\mathbf{op}} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{op}}$ constructs limits, that is, whenever $\psi_i: D_i \longrightarrow K$ is such that $(K, \psi)$ is a colimit of $(\Delta, D)U$ in $\mathcal{K}$ the family $\psi_i: D_i = (L_i, t_i) \longrightarrow K$ has a co-optimal lift.
