The categorical product generalizes the Cartesian product of sets. It is defined by a universal property: projections from the product to each factor, and a unique mediating morphism from any other object with compatible maps. The size of a product is the size of the index set $I$; the empty product ($I = \emptyset$) is the terminal object. A category may have products for finite families, countable families, or all families depending on context.
