The category Set has all products, given by the usual Cartesian product with coordinate projection maps. An element of a product is precisely a tuple of elements from each factor, as shown by considering morphisms from a one-element set. The subcategory of finite sets has finite products but not infinite products, since an infinite product of finite sets need not be finite. This is the prototypical example that motivates the general categorical definition.
