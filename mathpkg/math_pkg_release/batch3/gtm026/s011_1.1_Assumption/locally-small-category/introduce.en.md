A category is called locally small when each of its hom-sets is a genuine set rather than a proper class. This condition holds for all categories of structured sets -- such as sets, groups, topological spaces -- because morphisms between structured sets are determined by functions between their underlying sets. However, large functor categories (e.g., the category of functors from Set to itself with natural transformations) are not locally small. The assumption of local smallness is a concession to everyday mathematics that still covers nearly all concrete examples.
