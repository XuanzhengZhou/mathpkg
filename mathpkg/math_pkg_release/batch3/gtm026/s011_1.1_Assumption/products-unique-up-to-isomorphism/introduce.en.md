Objects defined by universal properties are always unique up to unique isomorphism. This proposition establishes the first such result: any two products of the same family of objects must be isomorphic. The proof is the prototypical "universal property argument": use the universal property of each product to induce maps in both directions, then show the compositions must be identities by appealing to uniqueness. This reasoning works for all universal properties and requires only two of the three category axioms.
