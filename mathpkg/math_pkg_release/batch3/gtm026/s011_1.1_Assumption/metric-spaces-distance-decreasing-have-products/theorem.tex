Let $\mathbf{Met}$ be the category of metric spaces and distance-decreasing maps, where $f: (X, d) \to (X', d')$ is distance-decreasing if for all $x, y \in X$,
$$d'(xf, yf) \leq d(x, y).$$
$\mathbf{Met}$ has finite products and many other --- but not all --- products. If $(A, d) = \prod (A_i, d_i)$, then $A$ must be the usual product of the sets $A_i$, and
$$d((a_i), (b_i)) = \sup\{d_i(a_i, b_i) : i \in I\}.$$
The product exists if and only if this supremum is finite for every pair $(a_i), (b_i)$ in the usual product set $A$.
