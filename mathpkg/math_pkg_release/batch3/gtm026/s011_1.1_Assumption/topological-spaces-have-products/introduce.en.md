The category of topological spaces has products: the product set is equipped with the product topology (topology of pointwise convergence). The universal property is equivalent to the characterization of convergence in the product: a net converges if and only if each coordinate projection converges. Projections are automatically continuous in the product topology, and the continuity of the mediating map follows from the pointwise convergence characterization.
