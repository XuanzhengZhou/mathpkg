A category with a single object corresponds exactly to a monoid: the morphisms are the monoid elements, composition is the monoid operation, and the identity morphism is the unit. This shows that monoids are special cases of categories and provides a useful bridge between algebraic and categorical thinking. Conversely, every monoid defines a one-object category, so the two notions are essentially the same.
