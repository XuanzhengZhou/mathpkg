This proposition is the first encounter with categorical universal algebra: the category of T-algebras (for an algebraic theory T in a base category K) inherits products from K. The product T-algebra structure on the product object in K is uniquely determined by the requirement that projections be T-homomorphisms. The proof uses the universal property of the product in K to verify the T-algebra axioms and then establishes the universal property in K^T. This pattern -- algebraic structure lifting along limits -- is a fundamental theme in categorical algebra.
