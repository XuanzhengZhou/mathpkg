A morphism $f: A \longrightarrow B$ in a category $\mathcal{K}$ is an \textbf{isomorphism} if there exists a morphism $f^{-1}: B \longrightarrow A$ in $\mathcal{K}$ such that
$$f.f^{-1} = \mathrm{id}_A \quad \text{and} \quad f^{-1}.f = \mathrm{id}_B.$$
Two objects $A, B$ in $\mathcal{K}$ are \textbf{isomorphic} (written $A \approx B$) if there exists an isomorphism $f: A \longrightarrow B$ in $\mathcal{K}$. Isomorphism is an equivalence relation on the objects of $\mathcal{K}$.
