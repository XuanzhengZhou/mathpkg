An algebraic theory (in monoid form) in a category $\mathcal{K}$ is a triple $(T, \eta, \mu)$ where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $T$ is a functor from $\mathcal{K}$ to $\mathcal{K}$,
\item $\eta$ is a natural transformation from the identity functor of $\mathcal{K}$ to $T$,
\item $\mu$ is a natural transformation from $TT$ to $T$,
\end{itemize}
all such that the three diagrams of 3.16 commute for every object $A$ of $\mathcal{K}$:

$$\xymatrix{
TTT \ar[r]^{T\mu} \ar[d]_{\mu T} & TT \ar[d]^{\mu} \\
TT \ar[r]_{\mu} & T
}$$

$$\xymatrix{
T \ar[r]^{\eta T} \ar[dr]_{\mathrm{id}_T} & TT \ar[d]^{\mu} & T \ar[l]_{T\eta} \ar[dl]^{\mathrm{id}_T} \\
& T &
}$$
