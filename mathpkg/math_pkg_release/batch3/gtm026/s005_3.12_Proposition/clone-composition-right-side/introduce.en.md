In an algebraic theory in clone form, the composition operation $\circ$ can be expressed on either side using the functorial action $fT$ of the underlying functor. This proposition establishes the right-side counterpart to the earlier formula $f^\Delta \circ \alpha = f.\alpha$, showing that precomposition with the $\Delta$-lifting of a morphism $g$ is equivalent to postcomposition with $gT$. This symmetry is fundamental to the equivalence between clone form and monoid form presentations of algebraic theories.
