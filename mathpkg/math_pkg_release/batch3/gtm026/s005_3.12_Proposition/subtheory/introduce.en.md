A subtheory $T'$ of an algebraic theory $T$ in $\mathbf{Set}$ is a subfunctor of $T$ that is closed under the unit $\eta$ and the clone composition $\circ$ of the ambient theory. Specifically, for each set $A$, $AT'$ is a subset of $AT$; the unit $\eta$ maps into $AT'$, yielding a restricted unit $\eta'$; and the composition of two morphisms factoring through $T'$ again factors through $T'$, yielding a restricted composition $\circ'$. The resulting triple $(T', \eta', \circ')$ is itself an algebraic theory. This concept captures the idea of a "sub-monad" and is useful for constructing new theories from known ones.
