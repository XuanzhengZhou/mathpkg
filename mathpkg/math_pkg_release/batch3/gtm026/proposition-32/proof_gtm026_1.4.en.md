By duality, it suffices to prove the second statement. Let $(e,m)$ be an E-M factorization of $g$ and let $(e_1,m_1)$ be an E-M factorization of $f.e$. By 4.10 there exists $h$ such that $(h.m_1).m = \text{id}_C$ as shown below.

Therefore $m$ is mono and split epi, so is an isomorphism, and $g = e.m$ is in $\mathbb{E}$.
