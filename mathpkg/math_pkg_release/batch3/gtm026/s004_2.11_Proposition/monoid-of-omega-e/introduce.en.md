The monoid $V(\Omega, E)$ is a key invariant of an equational presentation $(\Omega, E)$. It is defined as the endomorphism monoid of the countable set $V$ (of variables) in the clone category $\text{Set}(\Omega, E)$. Since $V$ serves as the set of variables used to form terms, the monoid $V(\Omega, E)$ captures the compositional structure of term substitution, and it plays a central role in Theorem 2.17 where structural equivalence of equational presentations is characterized in three equivalent ways.
