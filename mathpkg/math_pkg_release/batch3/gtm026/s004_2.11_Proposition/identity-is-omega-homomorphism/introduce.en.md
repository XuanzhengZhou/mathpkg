This lemma states that for any $\Omega$-algebra $(X, \delta)$, the identity function $\text{id}_X: X \to X$ is always an $\Omega$-homomorphism. Though trivially true, this principle is important because it guarantees that every $\Omega$-algebra has at least one endomorphism, namely the identity, and it is used together with 2.5 to derive the identity $(A\eta)^\# = \text{id}_{AT}$ for all sets $A$.
