From 2.13, the identity map $\text{id}_{AT}: AT \longrightarrow AT$ is an $\Omega$-homomorphism. By 2.5, there is a unique morphism (in the sense of $\text{Set}(\Omega, E)$) whose sharp equals $\text{id}_{AT}$. But $A\eta: A \longrightarrow AT$ is defined so that $(A\eta)^\#$ is an $\Omega$-homomorphism extending $A\eta$. Since $\text{id}_{AT}$ clearly extends $A\eta$ along the universal map, we have $(A\eta)^\# = \text{id}_{AT}$.

Therefore, for any morphism $\alpha: A \longrightarrow B$,
$$
\alpha \circ B\eta = \alpha.(B\eta)^\# = \alpha.\text{id}_{BT} = \alpha.
$$
