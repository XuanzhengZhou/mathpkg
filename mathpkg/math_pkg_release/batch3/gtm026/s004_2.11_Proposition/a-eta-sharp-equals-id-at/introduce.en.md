This corollary follows from the fact that identity maps are $\Omega$-homomorphisms (2.13) combined with the characterization of morphisms in $\text{Set}(\Omega, E)$ from 2.5. It establishes that the sharp of the unit morphism $A\eta: A \to AT$ is precisely the identity map on $AT$, which in turn implies that the unit $\eta$ acts as a right-identity for the composition $\circ$ in the clone category $\text{Set}(\Omega, E)$.
