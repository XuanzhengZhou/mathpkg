The clone $\text{Set}(\Omega, E)$ associated to an equational presentation $(\Omega, E)$ is defined in 2.7 with objects being sets $A, B, C, \ldots$ and morphisms $\alpha: A \to B$ being $\Omega$-homomorphisms $\alpha: AT \to BT$ where $T$ is the free-algebra monad induced by $(\Omega, E)$. Proposition 2.11 verifies that the composition operation $\circ$ introduced in 2.8 and the identity morphisms $A\eta: A \to AT$ of 2.9 satisfy the category axioms, so that $\text{Set}(\Omega, E)$ genuinely forms a category. This result is foundational because it allows the entire machinery of category theory to be applied to equational presentations.
