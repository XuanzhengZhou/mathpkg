Theorem 2.17 is a central "folklore theorem" of universal algebra that characterizes when two finitary equational presentations are structurally equivalent. It establishes the equivalence of three perspectives: (2.18) the categories of algebras are isomorphic as concrete categories over sets, (2.19) the clones (abstract clones over Set) are isomorphic preserving variables and composition, and (2.20) the endomorphism monoids of the countable set of variables are isomorphic in a way that respects true constants. This theorem is foundational because it shows that the algebraic content of an equational presentation can be faithfully captured at the level of clones or monoids, connecting category-theoretic, clone-theoretic, and monoid-theoretic viewpoints. The author notes that more general clone homomorphisms appear later in 3.2.8.
