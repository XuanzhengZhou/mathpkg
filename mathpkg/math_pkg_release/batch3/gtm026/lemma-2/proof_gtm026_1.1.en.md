We assume that $p = \text{coeq}(a', b')$ for some $a', b': E' \longrightarrow A$.

Let $a, b: E \longrightarrow A$ be the kernel pair of $p$. Suppose $p'$ is given with $a.p' = b.p'$. Since $a'.p = b'.p$ there exists unique $g$ with $g.a = a', g.b = b'$.
