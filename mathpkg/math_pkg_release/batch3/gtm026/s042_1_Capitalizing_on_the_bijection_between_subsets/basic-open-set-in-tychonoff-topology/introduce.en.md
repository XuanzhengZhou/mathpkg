When $2^X$ is equipped with the Tychonoff product topology (each copy of $2$ having the discrete topology), the basic open sets admit a particularly convenient description in terms of finite restrictions. Under the bijection identifying a function $X \to 2$ with the subset it is characteristic of, a basic open neighbourhood is specified by prescribing the intersection of the subset with a finite subset $G$ of $X$ to equal a given $H \subset G$. This description is crucial for analysing convergence of nets in $2^X$ and the continuity of ultrafilter $\beta$-operations.
