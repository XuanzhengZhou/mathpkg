In an $S$-$T$ bialgebra $(X, \xi, \theta)$, the $S$-structure $\xi$ and the $T$-structure $\theta$ are compatible: each $T$-operation is an $S$-homomorphism and each $S$-operation is a $T$-homomorphism. The Symmetry Proposition (6.7) demonstrates the $S$-to-$T$ direction: applying the $S$-operation $\beta$ after lifting the $S$-structure $\xi$ yields a map that respects the $T$-structure $\theta$. This symmetry is fundamental to the theory of bialgebras and the tensor product of algebraic theories.
