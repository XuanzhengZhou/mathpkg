Theorem 6.10 is the central result ensuring that the tensor product of two bounded algebraic theories again yields an algebraic category over $\mathbf{Set}$. It leverages Lemma 6.9 (the Birkhoff subcategory closure of $\mathbf{Set}^S \otimes T$) together with general facts about algebraic functors from Chapter 1. By representing each bounded theory via a finitary presentation of operations and equations, their tensor product also admits a finitary presentation, guaranteeing that the underlying set functor remains algebraic (i.e., monadic).
