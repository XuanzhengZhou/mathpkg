Theorem 6.11 gives a condition for the existence of the tensor product $S \otimes T$ of two algebraic theories in $\mathbf{Set}$. The tensor product of theories formalizes the notion of two algebraic structures that commute with each other---the $S$-$T$ bialgebras. The statement in the source is truncated, but the surrounding context of Chapter 6 indicates that the tensor product exists precisely when the underlying set functor $U^{S \otimes T}$ on the category of bialgebras is algebraic, which is guaranteed for bounded theories by Theorem 6.10.
