A free object over a given object $K$ with respect to a functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{K}$ is a pair $(F, \eta)$ where $F \in \mathcal{A}$ and $\eta: K \to FU$ satisfies a universal mapping property. For any object $A \in \mathcal{A}$ and morphism $f: K \to AU$, there exists a unique $\mathcal{A}$-morphism $\psi: F \to A$ factoring $f$ through $\eta$. This definition abstracts the construction of free algebras and captures the essence of what it means for a functor $U$ to have a left adjoint at the object $K$.
