A subcategory of a category $\mathcal{K}$ is defined categorically as a monomorphism in the category of categories and functors — equivalently, a functor $H: \mathcal{L} \to \mathcal{K}$ that is injective on hom-sets. A literal subcategory is the familiar set-theoretic notion where objects and morphisms of $\mathcal{L}$ are contained in those of $\mathcal{K}$ and closed under identities and composition. Any subcategory $H$ can be represented by the inclusion functor of a literal subcategory formed by the images under $H$. A subcategory is full when the hom-set maps are bijective.
