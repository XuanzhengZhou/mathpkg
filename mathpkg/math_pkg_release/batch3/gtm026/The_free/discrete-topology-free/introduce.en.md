Given the forgetful functor $U$ from topological spaces to sets, a discrete topological space on a set $n$ together with the identity map yields a free topological space over $n$. The universal property holds because any function $f: n \to AU$ from $n$ to the underlying set of a topological space $A$ lifts uniquely to a continuous map from the discrete space $F_n$ to $A$. This is one of the simplest nontrivial examples of a free object.
