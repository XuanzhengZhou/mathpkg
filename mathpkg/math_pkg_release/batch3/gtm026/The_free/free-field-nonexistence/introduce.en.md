There is no free field over a two-element set with respect to the forgetful functor from fields to sets. The proof proceeds by contradiction: any putative free field would force the universal morphism $\eta$ to be injective, leading to the existence of a nontrivial proper ideal, which contradicts the fact that a field has no nontrivial ideals. This example illustrates that fields are not part of universal algebra — the problem is that multiplicative inverse is not a total unary operation since division by zero is undefined.
