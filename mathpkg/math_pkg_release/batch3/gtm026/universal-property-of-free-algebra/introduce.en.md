The universal property of the free $(\Omega, E)$-algebra $AT$ states that it is the "most general" algebra generated by the set $A$: any function from $A$ to an $(\Omega, E)$-algebra $(X, \delta)$ extends uniquely to an $\Omega$-homomorphism from $AT$ to $(X, \delta)$. This makes $AT$ the free object on $A$ in the category of $(\Omega, E)$-algebras. The proof uses the interpretation map $f^\# : A\Omega \to X$ (from Definition 1.19), which respects the equivalence relation $E_A$ by construction, and therefore descends to the quotient $AT = A\Omega / E_A$. To a category theorist, this result provides the left adjoint to the forgetful functor.
