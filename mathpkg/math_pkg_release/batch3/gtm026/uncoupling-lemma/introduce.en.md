The Uncoupling Lemma establishes that every Omega-term of length greater than 1 has a unique decomposition into a sequence of subterms followed by an operator symbol. This structural result is essential for working with Polish notation in universal algebra: given an Omega-term written as $p_1 \cdots p_n \omega$, the operator $\omega$ and its arity $n$ are uniquely determined, as are the subterms $p_i$. The proof uses a valency map assigning integer weights to operators ($1-m$ for an $m$-ary operator) and variables ($1$), with the property that every Omega-term has total valency 1 and every proper left segment has positive valency.
