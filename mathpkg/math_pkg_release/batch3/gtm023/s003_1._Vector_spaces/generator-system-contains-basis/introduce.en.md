Corollary I follows immediately from Theorem I by taking $R = \emptyset$ (or any linearly independent subset). It states that every generating set can be trimmed down to a basis, guaranteeing that every nonzero vector space, regardless of dimension, possesses a basis. This is a foundational result that justifies the use of coordinates and dimension theory in all vector spaces, not just finite-dimensional ones.
