A nontrivial linear relation is an equation expressing that some nontrivial linear combination of vectors equals zero. The existence of such a relation is exactly what defines linear dependence. This concept formalizes the idea that the vectors are not "independent" — there is some algebraic redundancy among them, and at least one vector can be recovered as a linear combination of the others.
