For a pair of vectors, linear dependence reduces to a simple proportional relationship: two vectors are linearly dependent precisely when one is a scalar multiple of the other. This is the simplest case of the general characterization of linear dependence and is often used as a quick check. Geometrically, in real vector spaces this means the two vectors are collinear (lie on the same line through the origin).
