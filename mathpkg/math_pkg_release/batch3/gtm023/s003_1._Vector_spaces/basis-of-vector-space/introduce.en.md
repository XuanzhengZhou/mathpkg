A basis of a vector space is a family of vectors that is both linearly independent and spans the whole space. This means every vector can be expressed uniquely as a linear combination of the basis vectors, and the coefficients in this representation are called the components of the vector with respect to the basis. A basis provides a coordinate system for the vector space, enabling computation and classification. The standard basis of $\Gamma^n$ is the canonical example: $e_i = (0,\ldots,0,1,0,\ldots,0)$ with 1 in the $i$-th position. Every nontrivial vector space admits a basis, a fact proved using Zorn's lemma.
