Theorem I is the most general basis existence result: in any nonzero vector space, given a generating set $S$ and a linearly independent subset $R \subset S$, there exists a basis $T$ sandwiched between them ($R \subset T \subset S$). The proof uses Zorn's lemma by considering the poset of all linearly independent subsets between $R$ and $S$, ordered by inclusion. A maximal element of this poset is shown to be a basis. This theorem implies that every vector space — finite- or infinite-dimensional — admits a basis, establishing the foundation for all of linear algebra.
