Proposition III connects linear independence with uniqueness of representation: a family is linearly independent exactly when each vector expressible as a linear combination of the family has a unique such expression. This is the key property that makes bases useful — a basis is a linearly independent spanning set, so every vector admits a unique coordinate representation. The proof is a direct algebraic manipulation of linear relations.
