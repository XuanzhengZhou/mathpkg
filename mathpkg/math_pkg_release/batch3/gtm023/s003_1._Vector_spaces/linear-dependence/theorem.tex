The family $\{x_{\alpha}\}_{\alpha \in A}$ is called \textbf{linearly dependent} if there exists a nontrivial linear combination of the $x_{\alpha}$ that equals the zero vector; that is, if there exists a system of scalars $\lambda^{\alpha}$ such that

$$\sum_{\alpha} \lambda^{\alpha} x_{\alpha} = 0$$

and at least one $\lambda^{\alpha} \neq 0$. If a subfamily of the family $\{x_{\alpha}\}$ is linearly dependent, then so is the full family. An equation of this form with at least one $\lambda^{\alpha} \neq 0$ is called a non-trivial linear relation.
