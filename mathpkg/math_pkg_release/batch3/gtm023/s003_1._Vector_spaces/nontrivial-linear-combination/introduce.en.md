A nontrivial linear combination is a linear combination where not all coefficients are zero. This concept is the essential ingredient in defining linear dependence: a family of vectors is linearly dependent precisely when some nontrivial linear combination of them equals the zero vector. The trivial linear combination (all coefficients zero) always sums to zero and provides no information about the relationships among the vectors.
