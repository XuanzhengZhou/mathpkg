Let $E_1$ be a subspace of a vector space $E$. Two vectors $x \in E$ and $x' \in E$ are called \textbf{equivalent mod $E_1$}, denoted $x \sim x'$, if $x' - x \in E_1$. This is an equivalence relation; the equivalence classes are the cosets of the additive subgroup $E_1$ in $E$. Let $E/E_1$ denote the set of equivalence classes.

Define addition in $E/E_1$ by
$$\bar{x} + \bar{y} = \pi(x + y)$$
and scalar multiplication by
$$\lambda \cdot \bar{x} = \pi(\lambda x)$$
where $\pi: E \to E/E_1$ is the canonical set map $\pi x = \bar{x}$. The vector space $E/E_1$ obtained in this way is called the \textbf{factor space} of $E$ with respect to the subspace $E_1$.
