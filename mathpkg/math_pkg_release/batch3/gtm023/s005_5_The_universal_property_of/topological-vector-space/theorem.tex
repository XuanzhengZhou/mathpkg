A vector space $E$ over $\mathbb{R}$ (or $\mathbb{C}$) equipped with a topology is called a \textbf{topological vector space} if the linear operations
$$(x, y) \mapsto x + y$$
and
$$(\lambda, x) \mapsto \lambda x$$
are continuous with respect to the product topology. Example: $\mathbb{R}^n$ with the product topology induced by the topology on $\mathbb{R}$.
