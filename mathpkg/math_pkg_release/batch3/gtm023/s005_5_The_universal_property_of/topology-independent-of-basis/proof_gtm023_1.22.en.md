Any topology constructed via a basis isomorphism satisfies $T_1$ and $T_2$, as shown in the existence part of the proposition. By the uniqueness part, there is only one topology on $E$ satisfying $T_1$ and $T_2$. Therefore the topology is independent of the chosen basis.
