A subspace of a vector space $E$ is a non-empty subset that is itself a vector space under the same operations of addition and scalar multiplication. It is characterized by being closed under taking linear combinations: for any vectors $x, y$ in the subset and any scalars $\lambda, \mu$, the combination $\lambda x + \mu y$ remains in the subset. The trivial subspaces are the whole space $E$ and the zero subspace $\{0\}$; every subspace must contain the zero vector.
