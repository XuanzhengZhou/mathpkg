Let $E$ be a vector space over the field $\Gamma$. A non-empty subset $E_1 \subseteq E$ is called a \textbf{subspace} if for each $x, y \in E_1$ and every scalar $\lambda \in \Gamma$,
$$x + y \in E_1$$
and
$$\lambda x \in E_1.$$

Equivalently, a subspace is a subset $E_1 \subseteq E$ such that
$$\lambda x + \mu y \in E_1$$
whenever $x, y \in E_1$ and $\lambda, \mu \in \Gamma$. In particular, the whole space $E$ and the subset $\{0\}$ consisting of the zero vector only are subspaces. Every subspace $E_1 \subset E$ contains the zero vector.
