If $\varphi: E \to F$ is an isomorphism, then by Proposition III of sec. 1.11, $\varphi$ maps a basis of $E$ onto a basis of $F$. Hence $\dim E = \dim F$.

Conversely, if $\dim E = \dim F = n$, choose a basis $e_1, \ldots, e_n$ of $E$ and a basis $f_1, \ldots, f_n$ of $F$. Define a linear map $\varphi: E \to F$ by $\varphi(\sum \xi^i e_i) = \sum \xi^i f_i$. This map is bijective (it has an inverse given by the reverse assignment), hence an isomorphism.
