Let $E_1$ be a subspace of a vector space $E$. The \textbf{canonical injection} $i: E_1 \to E$ is defined by
$$i x = x, \quad x \in E_1.$$
In view of the definition of the linear operations in $E_1$, $i$ is a linear mapping. Since $i$ is injective, a family of vectors in $E_1$ is linearly independent (dependent) if and only if it is linearly independent (dependent) in $E$.
