A fundamental property of normal transformations is that they share all eigenvectors with their adjoint. This follows from the fact that $\varphi - \lambda \iota$ remains normal, and the kernels of a normal transformation and its adjoint coincide. Consequently, for normal transformations, the eigenspaces of $\varphi$ and $\tilde{\varphi}$ agree, and the eigenvalue of $\tilde{\varphi}$ corresponding to an eigenvector $e$ of $\varphi$ (with eigenvalue $\lambda$) is also $\lambda$.
