Every linear transformation on an inner product space induces a bilinear form via the inner product: $\Phi(x,y) = (\varphi x, y)$. This construction establishes a natural isomorphism between the space of linear transformations $L(E;E)$ and the space of bilinear forms $B(E,E)$. The injectivity follows from nondegeneracy of the inner product, and surjectivity from the Riesz representation theorem (every linear functional is given by an inner product with a fixed vector). Under this correspondence, the identity map corresponds to the inner product itself.
