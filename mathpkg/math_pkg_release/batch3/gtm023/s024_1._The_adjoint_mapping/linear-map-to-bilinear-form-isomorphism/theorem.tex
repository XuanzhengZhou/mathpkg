Let $E$ be an inner product space. For each linear transformation $\varphi: E \rightarrow E$, define a bilinear function $\Phi: E \times E \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ by
$$\Phi(x, y) = (\varphi x, y).$$
The correspondence $\varphi \mapsto \Phi$ defines a linear mapping
$$\varrho: L(E; E) \rightarrow B(E, E),$$
where $B(E, E)$ denotes the space of bilinear functions on $E \times E$. This mapping is a linear isomorphism of $L(E; E)$ onto $B(E, E)$.
