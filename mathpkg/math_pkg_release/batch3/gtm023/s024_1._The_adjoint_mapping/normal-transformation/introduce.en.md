A normal transformation is a linear operator that commutes with its adjoint. This class of transformations includes selfadjoint operators ($\tilde{\varphi} = \varphi$), skew-adjoint operators ($\tilde{\varphi} = -\varphi$), and orthogonal transformations ($\tilde{\varphi} = \varphi^{-1}$). Normal transformations are precisely those operators that are diagonalizable by an orthonormal basis (over $\mathbb{C}$), making them central to the spectral theorem. The condition $\tilde{\varphi} \circ \varphi = \varphi \circ \tilde{\varphi}$ captures the essence of when an operator admits a clean spectral decomposition.
