Let $\varphi: E \rightarrow E$ be a linear transformation of an inner product space and let $\tilde{\varphi}$ be its adjoint. Let $\Phi(x, y) = (\varphi x, y)$ and $\tilde{\Phi}(x, y) = (\tilde{\varphi} x, y)$ be the corresponding bilinear functions. Then
$$\tilde{\Phi}(x, y) = \Phi(y, x).$$
That is, the bilinear form of the adjoint is obtained from the original bilinear form by interchanging the arguments.
