For a normal transformation, eigenvectors belonging to different eigenvalues are orthogonal. This is a direct consequence of two facts: (1) a normal transformation and its adjoint share the same eigenvectors with the same eigenvalues, and (2) an eigenvector of $\varphi$ and an eigenvector of $\tilde{\varphi}$ with different eigenvalues are orthogonal. This orthogonality of eigenspaces is the key ingredient for the spectral theorem, which states that a normal transformation is diagonalizable by an orthonormal basis.
