Let $e$ and $f$ be eigenvectors of $\varphi$ with distinct eigenvalues $\lambda \neq \mu$: $\varphi e = \lambda e$, $\varphi f = \mu f$.

Since $\varphi$ is normal, $\varphi$ and $\tilde{\varphi}$ have the same eigenvectors. Thus $f$ is also an eigenvector of $\tilde{\varphi}$ with eigenvalue $\mu$: $\tilde{\varphi} f = \mu f$.

Now apply the theorem that eigenvectors of $\varphi$ and $\tilde{\varphi}$ with different eigenvalues are orthogonal. Here $e$ is an eigenvector of $\varphi$ with eigenvalue $\lambda$, and $f$ is an eigenvector of $\tilde{\varphi}$ with eigenvalue $\mu$. Since $\lambda \neq \mu$, we conclude $(e, f) = 0$.
