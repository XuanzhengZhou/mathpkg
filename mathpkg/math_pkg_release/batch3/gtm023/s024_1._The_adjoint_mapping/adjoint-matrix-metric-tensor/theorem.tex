Let $\varphi: E \rightarrow F$ be a linear mapping between inner product spaces with bases $x_v$ ($v = 1, \dots, n$) of $E$ and $y_\mu$ ($\mu = 1, \dots, m$) of $F$. Let $\alpha_v^\kappa$ and $\tilde{\alpha}_\mu^\lambda$ be the matrices of $\varphi$ and $\tilde{\varphi}$ defined by
$$\varphi x_v = \sum_\kappa \alpha_v^\kappa y_\kappa, \quad \tilde{\varphi} y_\mu = \sum_\lambda \tilde{\alpha}_\mu^\lambda x_\lambda.$$
Let $g_{v\lambda} = (x_v, x_\lambda)$ and $h_{\mu\kappa} = (y_\mu, y_\kappa)$ be the metric tensors. Then
$$\sum_\kappa \alpha_v^\kappa h_{\kappa\mu} = \sum_\lambda \tilde{\alpha}_\mu^\lambda g_{v\lambda}.$$
Multiplying by the inverse matrix $g^{v\varphi}$ yields
$$\tilde{\alpha}_\mu^\varphi = \sum_{v,\kappa} \alpha_v^\kappa h_{\kappa\mu} g^{v\varphi}.$$
