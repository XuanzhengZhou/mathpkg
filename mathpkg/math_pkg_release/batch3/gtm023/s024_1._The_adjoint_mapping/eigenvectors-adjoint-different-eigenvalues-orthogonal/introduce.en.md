A fundamental orthogonality relation connects the eigenvectors of a linear map and its adjoint: if an eigenvector of $\varphi$ and an eigenvector of $\tilde{\varphi}$ have different eigenvalues, they are orthogonal. This is proved by a simple computation using the defining relation of the adjoint. This result is a key ingredient in the spectral theory of normal and selfadjoint transformations developed later in the chapter.
