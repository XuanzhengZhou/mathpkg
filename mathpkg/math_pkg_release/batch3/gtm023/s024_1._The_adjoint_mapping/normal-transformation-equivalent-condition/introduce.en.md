The defining commutation relation $\tilde{\varphi} \circ \varphi = \varphi \circ \tilde{\varphi}$ is equivalent to a condition on the norms of images: $|\varphi x| = |\tilde{\varphi} x|$ for all vectors $x$. This reformulation is often more convenient for computations and reveals that a normal transformation and its adjoint have the same "size" when applied to any vector. The equivalence is proved by a polarization-type argument, rewriting the norm condition in terms of inner products.
