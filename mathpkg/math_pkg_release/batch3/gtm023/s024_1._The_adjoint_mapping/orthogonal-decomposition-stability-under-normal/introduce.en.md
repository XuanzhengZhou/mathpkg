Normality of a linear transformation can be characterized in terms of orthogonal decompositions: if the space decomposes as an orthogonal direct sum of $\varphi$-stable subspaces, then $\varphi$ is normal precisely when each summand is also stable under the adjoint and the restrictions are normal. This theorem provides a powerful tool for proving the spectral theorem by induction on the dimension, as it allows one to reduce the study of a normal transformation to its behavior on invariant orthogonal subspaces.
