The minimum polynomial $\mu$ of a linear transformation $\varphi$ is the monic polynomial of least degree satisfying $\mu(\varphi) = 0$. The Cayley-Hamilton theorem states that the characteristic polynomial annihilates $\varphi$, so $\mu$ divides the characteristic polynomial. The degree of $\mu$ equals the dimension of the subalgebra generated by $\varphi$.
