Let $E$ be an $n$-dimensional vector space and let $\Delta \neq 0$ be a determinant function in $E$. Let $\varphi \in L(E; E)$. Then an $n$-linear map

$$\Phi: E^n \to L(E; E)$$

is given by

$$\Phi(x_1, \ldots, x_n) x = \sum_{j=1}^{n} (-1)^{j-1} \Delta(x, \varphi x_1, \ldots, \widehat{\varphi x_j}, \ldots, \varphi x_n) \cdot x_j, \quad x_v \in E.$$

Since $\Phi$ is $n$-linear and skew symmetric it determines a unique linear transformation $\operatorname{ad}(\varphi)$ of $E$ via

$$\Phi(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = \Delta(x_1, \ldots, x_n) \operatorname{ad}(\varphi).$$

This transformation $\operatorname{ad}(\varphi)$ is called the classical adjoint of $\varphi$.
