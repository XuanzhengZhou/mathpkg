Cramer's rule provides an explicit formula for the solution of a system of $n$ linear equations in $n$ unknowns, assuming the coefficient matrix has non-zero determinant. Each unknown $\xi^i$ is expressed as $\frac{1}{\det A}$ times a sum of the right-hand side entries $\eta^j$ weighted by the corresponding cofactors $\operatorname{cof}(\alpha_i^j)$. This formula is derived directly from the cofactor expansion identities.
