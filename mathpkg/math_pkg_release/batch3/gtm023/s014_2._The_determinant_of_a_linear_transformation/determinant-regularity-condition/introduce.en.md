For a linear transformation $\varphi$ on a finite-dimensional vector space, invertibility can be characterized purely through the determinant. If $\varphi$ is regular, the images of a basis under $\varphi$ remain linearly independent, so $\Delta(\varphi e_1, \ldots, \varphi e_n) \neq 0$, which forces $\det \varphi \neq 0$. Conversely, $\det \varphi \neq 0$ implies that the images of any basis are linearly independent, making $\varphi$ an automorphism.
