Let $E$ be an $n$-dimensional vector space and define linear transformations $\varphi$ and $\psi$ of $E$ such that (with respect to a given basis) $M(\varphi) = A$ and $M(\psi) = B$. Then

$$\det(AB) = \det(M(\varphi) M(\psi)) = \det M(\psi \circ \varphi) = \det(\psi \circ \varphi)$$
$$= \det \psi \cdot \det \varphi = \det M(\psi) \cdot \det M(\varphi) = \det B \cdot \det A.$$
