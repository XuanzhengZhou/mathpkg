Viewing the determinant of a matrix as a function of its row vectors (or column vectors) reveals its fundamental algebraic properties. The determinant equals $\Delta(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ where $\Delta$ is the standard determinant function on $\Gamma^n$. This perspective yields four key properties: multilinearity in each row, alternating behavior under row swaps, invariance under shear operations, and equivalence between non-vanishing determinant and linear independence of rows. These properties hold symmetrically for columns.
