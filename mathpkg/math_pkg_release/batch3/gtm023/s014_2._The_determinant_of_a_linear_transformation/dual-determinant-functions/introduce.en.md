Given dual vector spaces $E^*$ and $E$ with determinant functions $\Delta^*$ and $\Delta$, their product relates to the determinant of the pairing matrix via a scalar $\alpha$: $\Delta^*(x^{*1}, \ldots, x^{*n}) \Delta(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = \alpha \det(\langle x^{*i}, x_j \rangle)$. When $\alpha = 1$, the determinant functions are said to be dual. For every non-trivial determinant function on $E$, there exists precisely one dual determinant function on $E^*$.
