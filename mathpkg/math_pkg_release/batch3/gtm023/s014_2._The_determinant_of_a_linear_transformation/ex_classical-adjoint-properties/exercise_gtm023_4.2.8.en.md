Prove the following properties of the classical adjoint:

(i) $\operatorname{ad}(\psi \circ \varphi) = \operatorname{ad}(\varphi) \circ \operatorname{ad}(\psi).$

(ii) $\det \operatorname{ad}(\varphi) = (\det \varphi)^{n-1}.$

(iii) If $\varphi$ has rank $n-1$, then $\operatorname{Im} \operatorname{ad}(\varphi) = \ker \varphi.$

(iv) If $\varphi$ has rank $\leq n-2$, then $\operatorname{ad}(\varphi) = 0.$

(v) $\operatorname{ad}(\operatorname{ad} \varphi) = (\det \varphi)^{n-2} \cdot \varphi.$

(vi) $\det \operatorname{ad}(\operatorname{ad} \varphi) = (\det \varphi)^{(n-1)^2}.$
