The determinant of an $n \times n$ matrix $A = (\alpha_v^\mu)$ is defined by the classical Leibniz formula: $\det A = \sum_\sigma \varepsilon_\sigma \alpha_1^{\sigma(1)} \cdots \alpha_n^{\sigma(n)}$, where the sum ranges over all permutations $\sigma$ of $\{1,\ldots,n\}$ and $\varepsilon_\sigma$ is the sign. This definition is consistent with the coordinate-free notion: if $M(\varphi) = A$ is the matrix of a linear transformation $\varphi$ with respect to some basis, then $\det \varphi = \det M(\varphi)$.
