Every $n \times m$ matrix can be converted into the normal form

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
I_r & 0 \\
0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$

by a sequence of the elementary matrix-transformations:
\begin{itemize}
  \item[(I.1)] Interchange of two rows,
  \item[(I.2)] Interchange of two columns,
  \item[(II.1)] Replacement of a row-vector $a_i$ by $a_i + \lambda a_j$ ($j \neq i$),
  \item[(II.2)] Replacement of a column-vector $b_k$ by $b_k + \lambda b_l$ ($l \neq k$),
\end{itemize}
and the scaling operations $a_v \to \lambda a_v$, $b_\mu \to \lambda b_\mu$ ($\lambda \neq 0$), where $r$ is the rank of the matrix.
