Gaussian elimination is the fundamental algorithm for solving systems of linear equations. It proceeds by successive elimination of unknowns: at each step one chooses a pivot equation with a nonzero leading coefficient, uses it to eliminate that unknown from all subsequent equations, then repeats on the reduced system. The process terminates either with a contradiction (no solution) or with a triangular system whose solution is expressed in terms of free parameters.
