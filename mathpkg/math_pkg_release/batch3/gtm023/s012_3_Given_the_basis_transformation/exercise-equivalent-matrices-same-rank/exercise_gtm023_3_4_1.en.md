Two $n \times m$-matrices $C$ and $C'$ are called equivalent if there exists a regular $n \times n$-matrix $A$ and a regular $m \times m$-matrix $B$ such that $C' = ACB$. Prove that two matrices are equivalent if and only if they have the same rank.
