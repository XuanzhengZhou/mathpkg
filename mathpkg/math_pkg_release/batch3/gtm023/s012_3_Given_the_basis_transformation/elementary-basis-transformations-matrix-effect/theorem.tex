Let $x_v\ (v = 1, \dots, n)$ and $y_\mu\ (\mu = 1, \dots, m)$ be arbitrary bases of $E$ and $F$, and let $M(\varphi)$ be the matrix of a linear mapping $\varphi: E \to F$ with respect to these bases. The following elementary basis transformations have the stated effect on $M(\varphi)$:

(I.1.) Interchange of two vectors $x_i$ and $x_j\ (i \neq j)$ results in interchange of rows $i$ and $j$.

(I.2.) Interchange of two vectors $y_k$ and $y_l\ (k \neq l)$ results in interchange of columns $k$ and $l$.

(II.1.) Adding to a vector $x_i$ an arbitrary multiple of a vector $x_j\ (j \neq i)$ results in replacement of the row-vector $a_i$ by $a_i + \lambda a_j$.

(II.2.) Adding to a vector $y_k$ an arbitrary multiple of a vector $y_l\ (l \neq k)$ results in replacement of the column-vector $b_k$ by $b_k + \lambda b_l$.
