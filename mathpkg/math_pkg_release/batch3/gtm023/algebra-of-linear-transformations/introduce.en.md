The algebra of linear transformations $A(E;E)$ is the fundamental example of an associative algebra with unit. It is isomorphic to the algebra of $n \times n$ matrices over $\Gamma$ (after choosing a basis). The multiplication operator $\mu(a)$ (left multiplication by $a$) embeds the algebra into its own endomorphism space, leading to the theory of representations.
