The space $L(E; E)$ of linear transformations of $E$ together with composition as multiplication,
$$(\psi \circ \varphi)x = \psi(\varphi x),$$
forms an algebra, called the algebra of linear transformations of $E$ and denoted by $A(E; E)$. The identity transformation $\iota$ acts as unit element.

$A(E; E)$ is associative but not commutative if $\dim E \geq 2$.

Every vector $a \in A$ induces a multiplication operator $\mu(a): A \to A$ defined by $\mu(a)x = ax$.
