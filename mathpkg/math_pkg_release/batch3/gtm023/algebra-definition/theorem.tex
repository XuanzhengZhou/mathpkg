An algebra $A$ is a vector space together with a mapping $A \times A \to A$, called multiplication and denoted $(x, y) \mapsto xy$, satisfying:
$$(M_1) \quad (\lambda x_1 + \mu x_2)y = \lambda(x_1 y) + \mu(x_2 y)$$
$$(M_2) \quad x(\lambda y_1 + \mu y_2) = \lambda(x y_1) + \mu(x y_2).$$

A homomorphism of algebras is a linear mapping $\varphi: A \to B$ that preserves products: $\varphi(xy) = \varphi x \cdot \varphi y$.
