An algebra is a vector space equipped with a bilinear multiplication. Algebras generalize both rings and vector spaces: they have addition, scalar multiplication, and vector multiplication. Examples include the algebra $A(E;E)$ of linear transformations, polynomial algebras, and the quaternions. Algebras can be associative, commutative, or have a unit element.
