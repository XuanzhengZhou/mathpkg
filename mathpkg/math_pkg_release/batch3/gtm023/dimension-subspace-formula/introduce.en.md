These dimension formulas are fundamental counting tools in linear algebra. The formula $\dim(E_1 + E_2) = \dim E_1 + \dim E_2 - \dim(E_1 \cap E_2)$ generalizes the inclusion-exclusion principle to vector spaces. The factor space formula $\dim E = \dim E_1 + \dim(E/E_1)$ shows that codimension measures how much smaller a subspace is than the whole space.
