Let $A$ and $B$ be algebras and $\varphi: A \to B$ be a fixed homomorphism. A linear mapping $\theta: A \to B$ is called a $\varphi$-derivation if
$$\theta(xy) = \theta x \cdot \varphi y + \varphi x \cdot \theta y \quad x, y \in A.$$

In particular, all derivations in $A$ are $\iota$-derivations where $\iota: A \to A$ denotes the identity map.
