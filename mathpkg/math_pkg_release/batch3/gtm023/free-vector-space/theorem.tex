Let $X$ be an arbitrary set and consider all maps $f: X \to \Gamma$ such that $f(x) \neq 0$ only for finitely many $x \in X$. Denote the set of these maps by $C(X)$. Define addition and scalar multiplication by
$$(f + g)(x) = f(x) + g(x), \quad (\lambda f)(x) = \lambda f(x).$$

Then $C(X)$ becomes a vector space. Define $f_a \in C(X)$ for $a \in X$ by $f_a(a) = 1$ and $f_a(x) = 0$ for $x \neq a$. The inclusion map $i_X: X \to C(X)$ given by $i_X(a) = f_a$ defines a bijection between $X$ and a basis of $C(X)$. Identifying $a$ with $f_a$, $X$ becomes a basis of $C(X)$. $C(X)$ is called the free vector space over $X$.
