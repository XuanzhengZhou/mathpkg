The Jordan-Chevalley decomposition (Theorem IV) guarantees that every element $x$ in a finite-dimensional associative commutative algebra admits a unique decomposition $x = x_S + x_N$ into a semisimple element $x_S$ and a nilpotent element $x_N$. The component $x_S$ is called the *semisimple part* of $x$, and $x_N$ is called the *nilpotent part* of $x$. This terminology is standard in linear algebra and Lie theory, and the decomposition generalizes the additive Jordan decomposition of a linear operator into its diagonalizable and nilpotent constituents.
