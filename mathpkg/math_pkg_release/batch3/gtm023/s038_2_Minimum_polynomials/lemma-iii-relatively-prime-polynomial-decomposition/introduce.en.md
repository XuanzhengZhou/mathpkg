Lemma III is a technical result in polynomial algebra that constructs auxiliary polynomials $u$ and $v$ for a given polynomial $g$ whose derivative $g'$ is relatively prime to $g$. The lemma guarantees that $g^k$ divides $g(u)$, while $u + gv = t$, and for $k \geq 2$ the polynomial $v$ remains relatively prime to $g$. This lemma, together with Lemma IV, provides the essential polynomial factorization machinery used in the proof of the Jordan-Chevalley decomposition (Theorem IV) for elements of finite-dimensional associative commutative algebras.
