Theorem V provides the complete structure theory for the factor algebra $\Gamma(\bar{t}) = \Gamma[t]/I_f$ of a polynomial algebra modulo a principal ideal. It shows that $\Gamma(\bar{t})$ decomposes as a direct sum of its semisimple subalgebra $A$ and its radical, and that $A$ itself decomposes uniquely as a direct sum of irreducible ideals, each isomorphic to a field $\Gamma[t]/I_{f_i}$ where the $f_i$ are the distinct prime factors of $f$. Moreover, $A$ consists precisely of all semisimple elements and is the unique subalgebra complementing the radical. This is a fundamental structure theorem that generalizes the Wedderburn-Malcev decomposition to commutative algebras.
