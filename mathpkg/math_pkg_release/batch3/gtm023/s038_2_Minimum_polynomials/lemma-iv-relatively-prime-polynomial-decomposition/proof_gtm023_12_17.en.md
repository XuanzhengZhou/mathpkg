**Proof.** Apply Lemma III to obtain polynomials $u$ and $v$ with properties (i)-(iii). Define $\omega = g \cdot v$. Then:

(i) $g^k \mid g(u)$ follows immediately from Lemma III (i).

(ii) $u + \omega = u + g v = t$ by Lemma III (ii).

(iii) $g \mid \omega$ holds since $\omega = g \cdot v$ by definition.

This completes the proof.
