Lemma IV is a variant of Lemma III that provides polynomials $u$ and $\omega$ satisfying similar but slightly different conditions: $g^k$ divides $g(u)$, $u + \omega = t$, and $g$ divides $\omega$. The proof is immediate from Lemma III by setting $\omega = g \cdot v$, where $v$ is the polynomial obtained from Lemma III. This lemma is used directly in the existence proof of the Jordan-Chevalley decomposition (Theorem IV), where the condition $g \mid \omega$ ensures that $\bar{\omega}$ is nilpotent.
