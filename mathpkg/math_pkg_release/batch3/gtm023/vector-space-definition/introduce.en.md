A vector space over a field $\Gamma$ is an algebraic structure consisting of an additive abelian group together with a scalar multiplication by elements of $\Gamma$. The axioms ensure that linear combinations of vectors behave as expected. This is the fundamental object of study in linear algebra. Examples include the $n$-space $\Gamma^n$ of $n$-tuples, spaces of continuous functions, and spaces of mappings from an arbitrary set into a vector space.
