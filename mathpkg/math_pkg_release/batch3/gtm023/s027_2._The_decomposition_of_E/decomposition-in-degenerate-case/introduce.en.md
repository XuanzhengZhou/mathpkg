When a symmetric bilinear function is degenerate, one can split off the nullspace $E_0$ and apply the non-degenerate decomposition theorem to a complementary subspace $E_1$. This yields a full splitting $E = E_1^+ \oplus E_1^- \oplus E_0$, which in turn produces a diagonal matrix representation with entries $+1$, $-1$, and $0$ along the diagonal — the canonical form for any symmetric bilinear function.
