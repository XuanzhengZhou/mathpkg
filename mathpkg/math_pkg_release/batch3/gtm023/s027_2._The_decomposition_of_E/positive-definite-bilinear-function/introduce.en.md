A positive definite symmetric bilinear function is one that assigns a strictly positive value to every nonzero vector paired with itself. Such a bilinear function is automatically non-degenerate and behaves like an inner product. Positive definiteness is the key property that distinguishes Euclidean scalar products from indefinite metric tensors like those in Minkowski spacetime.
