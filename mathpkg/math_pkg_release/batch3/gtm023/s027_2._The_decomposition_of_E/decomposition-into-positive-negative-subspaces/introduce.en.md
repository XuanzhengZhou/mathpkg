This is the fundamental structure theorem for non-degenerate indefinite symmetric bilinear functions. It states that any such form splits the vector space into a direct sum of a maximal positive definite subspace and its orthogonal complement, on which the form is negative definite. The proof constructs $E^+$ as a maximal positive definite subspace and shows via dimension counting and the Schwarz inequality that its orthogonal complement must be negative definite.
