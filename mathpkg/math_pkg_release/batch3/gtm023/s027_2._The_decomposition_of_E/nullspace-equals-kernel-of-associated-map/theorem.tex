Let $E$ be a vector space, $\Phi$ a symmetric bilinear function in $E \times E$, and define the linear mapping $\varphi: E \rightarrow E^*$ by

$$\Phi(x, y) = \langle \varphi x, y \rangle \quad x, y \in E.$$

Then:

1. The nullspace $E_0$ of $\Phi$ coincides with the kernel of $\varphi$:
   $$E_0 = \ker \varphi.$$

2. Consequently, the rank of $\Phi$ is equal to the rank of the mapping $\varphi$.

3. Let $(\alpha_{v\mu})$ be the matrix of $\Phi$ relative to a basis $x_v\ (v = 1 \dots n)$ of $E$. Then $\alpha_{v\mu}$ is the matrix of the mapping $\varphi$, so the rank of the matrix $(\alpha_{v\mu})$ equals the rank of $\Phi$.
