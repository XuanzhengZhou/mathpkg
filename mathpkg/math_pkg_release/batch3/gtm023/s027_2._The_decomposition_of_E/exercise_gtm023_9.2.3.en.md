Consider the space $L(E; E)$ of linear transformations of an $n$-dimensional Euclidean vector space $E$. Define a symmetric bilinear function $\Phi$ in $L(E; E)$ by

$$\Phi(\varphi, \psi) = \operatorname{tr}(\varphi \circ \psi).$$

Let $S(E; E)$ denote the subspace of symmetric transformations and $A(E; E)$ the subspace of skew-symmetric transformations. Prove the following:

a) $\Phi(\varphi, \varphi) > 0$ for every $\varphi \neq 0$ in $S(E; E)$,

b) $\Phi(\varphi, \varphi) < 0$ for every $\varphi \neq 0$ in $A(E; E)$,

c) $\Phi(\varphi, \psi) = 0$ if $\varphi \in S(E; E)$ and $\psi \in A(E; E)$,

d) The index of $\Phi$ is $\frac{n(n+1)}{2}$, where $n = \dim E$.
