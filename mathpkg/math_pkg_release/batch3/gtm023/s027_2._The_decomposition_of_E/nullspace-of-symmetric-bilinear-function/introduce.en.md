The nullspace (or radical) of a symmetric bilinear function $\Phi$ on a vector space $E$ is the set of all vectors that are orthogonal to the entire space under $\Phi$. This concept generalizes the notion of a degenerate direction in a bilinear form, and the dimension of the nullspace measures how far $\Phi$ is from being non-degenerate. When the nullspace is trivial, the bilinear function induces an isomorphism between $E$ and its dual space.
