Sylvester's law of inertia is a cornerstone result in the theory of real quadratic forms. It states that when a symmetric bilinear form is diagonalized, the number of positive coefficients (and similarly the number of negative and zero coefficients) is an invariant of the form, independent of the diagonalization method. This invariant is precisely the index defined earlier. The theorem justifies why the signature and rank provide a complete set of invariants for real symmetric bilinear forms.
