Given a non-degenerate symmetric bilinear form $\Phi$ in $E$, let $J$ be a subspace of maximal dimension such that $\Phi(x, x) = 0$ for every $x \in J$ (such a subspace is called totally isotropic). Prove that

$$\dim J = \min(s, n - s),$$

where $s$ is the index of $\Phi$ and $n = \dim E$.

**Hint:** Introduce two dual spaces $E^*$ and $F^*$ and linear mappings

$$\varphi_1: E \rightarrow E^* \quad \text{and} \quad \varphi_2: E \rightarrow F^*$$

defined by

$$\Phi(x, y) = \langle \varphi_1 x, y \rangle \quad \text{and} \quad \Phi(x, y) = \langle x, \varphi_2 y \rangle.$$
