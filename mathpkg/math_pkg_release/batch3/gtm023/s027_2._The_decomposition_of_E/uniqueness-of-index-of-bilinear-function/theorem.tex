Let $\Phi$ be a symmetric bilinear function in $E$. Suppose there exist two decompositions

$$E = E_1^+ \oplus E_1^- \oplus E_0 \quad \text{and} \quad E = E_2^+ \oplus E_2^- \oplus E_0$$

such that $\Phi$ is positive definite on $E_1^+$ and $E_2^+$, and negative definite on $E_1^-$ and $E_2^-$. Then

$$\dim E_1^+ = \dim E_2^+.$$

Consequently, the dimension of $E^+$ (the index) is uniquely determined by $\Phi$.
