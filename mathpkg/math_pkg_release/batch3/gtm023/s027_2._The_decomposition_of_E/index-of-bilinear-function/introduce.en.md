The index of a symmetric bilinear function is the dimension of a maximal subspace on which the form is positive definite. Sylvester's law of inertia guarantees that this number is well-defined: it is the same for any choice of maximal positive definite subspace. The index is one of the two fundamental invariants (together with the rank) that completely classify real symmetric bilinear forms up to equivalence.
