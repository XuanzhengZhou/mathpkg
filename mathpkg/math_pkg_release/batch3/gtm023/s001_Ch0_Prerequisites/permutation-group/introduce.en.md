The symmetric group $S_n$ of all permutations on $n$ objects is one of the most important examples of a finite group. Every finite group can be embedded into some $S_n$ via Cayley's theorem. Permutation groups play a crucial role in the theory of determinants, where the sign of a permutation determines the parity of a term.
