Let $G$ be a commutative group and $H$ a subgroup. The equivalence relation
$$x \sim x' \iff x - x' \in H$$
partitions $G$ into cosets $\bar{x} = H + x$. The set $G/H$ of cosets forms a group under the operation
$$\bar{x} + \bar{y} = \pi(x + y), \quad \pi x = \bar{x}, \ \pi y = \bar{y},$$
called the **factor group** of $G$ by $H$. Its identity is $H$.
