A field is an algebraic structure with two compatible group structures: addition and multiplication, connected by the distributive law. The most important examples are $\mathbb{Q}$, $\mathbb{R}$, and $\mathbb{C}$. In linear algebra, the scalars are always taken from a field, and the entire theory of vector spaces depends on the field axioms.
