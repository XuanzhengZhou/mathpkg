Group homomorphisms are classified by their set-theoretic properties: injective homomorphisms are monomorphisms, surjective ones are epimorphisms, and bijective ones are isomorphisms. An isomorphism indicates that two groups are structurally identical. The inverse of an isomorphism is automatically a homomorphism and hence an isomorphism.
