For each linear transformation $\alpha$ of the complex plane with determinant $1$, there is an associated Lorentz transformation $T_{\alpha}$ acting on the space $S$ of selfadjoint mappings. The action is defined by conjugation: $T_{\alpha} \sigma = \alpha \circ \sigma \circ \tilde{\alpha}$. The selfadjointness is preserved because taking the adjoint reverses the order of composition, and the inner product is preserved as a consequence of the multiplicativity of the determinant. Such transformations $T_{\alpha}$ provide a bridge between the group $\operatorname{SL}(2, \mathbb{C})$ and the proper orthochroneous Lorentz group.
