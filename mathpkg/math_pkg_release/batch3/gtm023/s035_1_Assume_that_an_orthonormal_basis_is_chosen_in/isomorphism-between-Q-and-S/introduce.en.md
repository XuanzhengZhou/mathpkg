This proposition establishes a canonical linear isomorphism $\Omega$ between the $4$-dimensional Euclidean space $Q$ (the space of real linear combinations of the identity and the quaternion units, equipped with a new trace-based inner product) and the space $S$ of selfadjoint mappings of the complex plane. The map $\Omega$ intertwines the indefinite inner products on both spaces, meaning it is an isometry: $\langle \Omega\varphi, \Omega\psi \rangle = \langle \varphi, \psi \rangle$. Its explicit inverse $\Psi$ is also given, showing the two spaces are isometrically isomorphic. This isomorphism is fundamental for translating between Lorentz transformations on $S$ and unitary transformations on $Q$.
