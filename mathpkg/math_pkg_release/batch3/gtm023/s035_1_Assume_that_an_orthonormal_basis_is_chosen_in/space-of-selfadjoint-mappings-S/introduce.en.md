The space $S$ consists of all selfadjoint linear transformations of the complex plane $C$, forming a real $4$-dimensional vector space. It is equipped with an indefinite inner product defined via traces and determinants, having signature $(1,3)$ (index $3$). This space serves as the foundation for studying Lorentz transformations: the inner product $\langle \sigma, \tau \rangle$ is directly related to the determinant via $\langle \sigma, \sigma \rangle = -\det \sigma$, and the trace gives the pairing with the identity via $\langle \sigma, \iota \rangle = -\frac{1}{2} \operatorname{tr} \sigma$. An orthonormal basis is provided by the Pauli matrices.
