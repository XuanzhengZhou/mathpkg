Let $S$ denote the set of all selfadjoint mappings $\sigma$ of the complex plane $C$. Then $S$ is a real $4$-dimensional linear space. In $S$ introduce a real inner product by

$$\langle \sigma, \tau \rangle = \frac{1}{2} \left( \operatorname{tr} (\sigma \circ \tau) - \operatorname{tr} \sigma \cdot \operatorname{tr} \tau \right). \tag{11.69}$$

This inner product is indefinite and has index $3$. Moreover,

$$\langle \sigma, \sigma \rangle = \frac{1}{2} \left( \operatorname{tr} \sigma^2 - (\operatorname{tr} \sigma)^2 \right) = -\det \sigma \tag{11.70}$$

and

$$\langle \sigma, \iota \rangle = -\frac{1}{2} \operatorname{tr} \sigma. \tag{11.71}$$
