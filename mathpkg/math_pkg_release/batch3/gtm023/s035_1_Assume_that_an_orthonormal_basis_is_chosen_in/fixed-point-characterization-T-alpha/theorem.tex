If $T_{\alpha} \sigma = \sigma$ for all $\sigma \in S$ (i.e., $T_{\alpha}$ is the identity on $S$), then $\alpha = \pm \iota$ where $\iota$ is the identity transformation of the complex plane $C$.

More generally, if $T_{\alpha} \iota = \iota$, then $\alpha$ commutes with every selfadjoint mapping: $\alpha \circ \sigma = \sigma \circ \alpha$ for all $\sigma \in S$, and consequently $\alpha = \pm \iota$.
