This proposition characterizes when a Lorentz transformation $T_{\alpha}$ acts as the identity on the space $S$ of selfadjoint mappings. The condition $T_{\alpha} \iota = \iota$ forces $\alpha$ to commute with every selfadjoint mapping, which in turn implies $\alpha$ must be a scalar multiple of the identity. Combined with the determinant condition $\det \alpha = 1$, this yields $\alpha = \pm \iota$. This result is used in the proof of Theorem 11.20 to handle the case where a Lorentz transformation fixes the identity element.
