This proposition verifies that the Lorentz transformations $T_{\alpha}$ defined via conjugation by $\alpha \in \operatorname{SL}(2, \mathbb{C})$ belong to the identity component of the Lorentz group. Properness ($\det = 1$) is shown by deforming $\alpha$ continuously to the identity while preserving the determinant, which forces $\det T_{\alpha} = 1$ by continuity. Orthochroneity follows from the fact that $T_{\alpha} \iota = \alpha \circ \tilde{\alpha}$ is a positive selfadjoint transformation, hence lies in the forward light cone.
