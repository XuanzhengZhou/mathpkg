A group is a fundamental algebraic structure consisting of a set equipped with a binary operation that satisfies associativity, identity, and inverse axioms. The group concept unifies many mathematical structures, from number systems to symmetry transformations. The identity element is uniquely determined, and each element has a unique inverse. The inverse of a product satisfies $(xy)^{-1} = y^{-1}x^{-1}$, a crucial property used throughout algebra.
