Let $G$ and $H$ be two groups. Then a mapping

$$\varphi: G \rightarrow H$$

is called a homomorphism if

$$\varphi(xy) = \varphi x \cdot \varphi y \quad x, y \in G.$$

A homomorphism which is injective (resp. surjective, bijective) is called a monomorphism (resp. epimorphism, isomorphism). The inverse mapping of an isomorphism is clearly again an isomorphism.
