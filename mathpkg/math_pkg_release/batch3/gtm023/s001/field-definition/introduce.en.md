A field is an algebraic structure with two binary operations (addition and multiplication) satisfying the field axioms: additive group structure, multiplicative group structure on nonzero elements, and distributivity connecting them. The rationals $\mathbb{Q}$, reals $\mathbb{R}$, and complex numbers $\mathbb{C}$ are the fundamental examples. Fields serve as the coefficient domains for vector spaces throughout linear algebra.
