Let $G$ be a commutative group and consider a subgroup $H$. Then $H$ determines an equivalence relation in $G$ given by

$$x \sim x' \quad \text{if and only if} \quad x - x' \in H.$$

The corresponding equivalence classes are the sets $\{H + x\}$ and are called the cosets of $H$ in $G$. Every element $x \in G$ is contained in precisely one coset $\bar{x}$. The set $G/H$ of these cosets is called the factor set of $G$ by $H$ and the surjective mapping

$$\pi: G \rightarrow G/H$$

defined by

$$\pi x = \bar{x}, \quad x \in \bar{x}$$

is called the canonical projection of $G$ onto $G/H$.

The element $\pi(x+y)$ depends only on $\bar{x}$ and $\bar{y}$. Hence, it makes sense to define the sum $\bar{x} + \bar{y}$ by

$$\bar{x} + \bar{y} = \pi(x + y) \quad \pi x = \bar{x}, \pi y = \bar{y}.$$

It is easy to verify that the above sum satisfies the group axioms. The group $G/H$ is called the factor group of $G$ with respect to the subgroup $H$. Its unit element is the set $H$.
