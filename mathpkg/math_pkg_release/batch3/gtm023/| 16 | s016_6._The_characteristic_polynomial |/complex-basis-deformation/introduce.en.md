Over the complex numbers, the notion of orientation disappears: any two bases of a complex vector space can be continuously deformed into each other. This fundamental difference from the real case arises because $\mathbb{C}$ is not an ordered field — there is no notion of "positive" or "negative" determinant in the same sense. The proof uses the fact that any non-zero complex number can be connected to any other by a continuous path avoiding zero.
