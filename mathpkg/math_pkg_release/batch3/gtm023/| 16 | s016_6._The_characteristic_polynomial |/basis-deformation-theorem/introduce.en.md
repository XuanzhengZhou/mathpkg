The Basis Deformation Theorem gives a complete characterization of when two bases of a real vector space can be continuously deformed into each other while maintaining linear independence at every intermediate stage. The answer is purely algebraic: the determinant of the linear transformation sending one basis to the other must be positive. This theorem establishes the fundamental link between the topological notion of deformation and the algebraic notion of orientation.
