The intersection number is a sign ($\pm 1$) that captures how two oriented subspaces $E_1$ and $E_2$ sit inside an oriented ambient space $E$ when $E = E_1 \oplus E_2$. It is computed by evaluating a determinant function on a concatenation of positive bases. The intersection number is antisymmetric in the sense that swapping the order introduces a factor of $(-1)^{pq}$, reflecting the dimension-dependent sign change from reordering basis vectors.
