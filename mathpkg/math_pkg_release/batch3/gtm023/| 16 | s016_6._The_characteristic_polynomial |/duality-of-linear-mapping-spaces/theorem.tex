Let $E$ and $F$ be linear spaces and consider the spaces $L(E;F)$ and $L(F;E)$. Define a scalar product by

$$\langle \varphi, \psi \rangle = \operatorname{tr} (\psi \circ \varphi), \quad \varphi \in L(E;F),\; \psi \in L(F;E).$$

This function is bilinear and non-degenerate: if $\langle \varphi, \psi \rangle = 0$ for a fixed $\varphi \in L(E;F)$ and all $\psi \in L(F;E)$, then $\varphi = 0$.

Thus $L(E;F)$ and $L(F;E)$ are placed in duality by this trace pairing.
