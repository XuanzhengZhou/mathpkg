An orientation-preserving linear isomorphism is one that maps a positive basis to a positive basis. For automorphisms, this is equivalent to having a positive determinant. Given an isomorphism and an orientation on the domain, a unique orientation on the codomain is induced by requiring the isomorphism to be orientation preserving. As an application, the map $-\iota$ preserves orientation exactly when the dimension is even.
