Let $E$ and $F$ be two oriented vector spaces of the same dimension $n$ and $\varphi: E \to F$ a linear isomorphism. Let $\Delta_E$ and $\Delta_F$ be representing determinant functions. Then $\Delta_F \circ \varphi$ is a determinant function on $E$, so

$$\Delta_F(\varphi x_1, \ldots, \varphi x_n) = \lambda \Delta_E(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$$

for some $\lambda \neq 0$. The isomorphism $\varphi$ is called orientation preserving if $\lambda > 0$.

Given an isomorphism $\varphi: E \to F$ and an orientation in $E$, there exists precisely one orientation in $F$ such that $\varphi$ preserves the orientation (the induced orientation).

A linear automorphism $\varphi: E \to E$ preserves the orientation if and only if $\det \varphi > 0$.
