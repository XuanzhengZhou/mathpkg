Assume that $a$ is an eigenvector of $\varphi$ and that $\lambda$ is the corresponding eigenvalue. Then

$$\varphi a = \lambda a, \quad a \neq 0.$$

This equation can be written as

$$(\varphi - \lambda \iota) a = 0$$

showing that $\varphi - \lambda \iota$ is not regular. This implies that

$$\det (\varphi - \lambda \iota) = 0.$$

Hence, every eigenvalue of $\varphi$ satisfies this equation. Conversely, assume that $\lambda$ is a solution of $\det (\varphi - \lambda \iota) = 0$. Then $\varphi - \lambda \iota$ is not regular, so there exists a vector $a \neq 0$ such that $(\varphi - \lambda \iota) a = 0$, whence $\varphi a = \lambda a$.

Thus, the eigenvalues of $\varphi$ are precisely the solutions of $\det(\varphi - \lambda \iota) = 0$.
