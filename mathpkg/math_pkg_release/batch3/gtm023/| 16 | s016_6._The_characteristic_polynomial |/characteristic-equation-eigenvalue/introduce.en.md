The characteristic equation $\det(\varphi - \lambda \iota) = 0$ provides the fundamental link between eigenvalues and determinants. A scalar $\lambda$ is an eigenvalue if and only if $\varphi - \lambda \iota$ fails to be regular (invertible), which is equivalent to its determinant vanishing. This characterization reduces the eigenvalue problem to solving a polynomial equation.
