Let $\varphi: a_v \mapsto b_v$. By the Basis Deformation Theorem (sec. 4.32), the bases are deformable iff $\det \varphi > 0$. Let $\Delta$ be a representing determinant function of the given orientation. Then $\Delta(b_1, \ldots, b_n) = \det \varphi \cdot \Delta(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$. Thus $\det \varphi > 0$ if and only if $\Delta(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ and $\Delta(b_1, \ldots, b_n)$ have the same sign, i.e., both bases are positive or both are negative. Hence the two deformation classes correspond exactly to the two orientations.
