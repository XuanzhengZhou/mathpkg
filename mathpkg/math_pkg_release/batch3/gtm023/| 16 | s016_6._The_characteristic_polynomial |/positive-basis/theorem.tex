A basis $e_v\;(v = 1, \ldots, n)$ of an oriented vector space is called positive if

$$\Delta(e_1, \ldots, e_n) > 0$$

where $\Delta$ is a representing determinant function of the given orientation.

If $(e_1, \ldots, e_n)$ is a positive basis and $\sigma$ is a permutation, then the basis $(e_{\sigma(1)}, \ldots, e_{\sigma(n)})$ is positive if and only if $\sigma$ is an even permutation.
