Let $E$ be a real vector space of dimension $n \geq 1$. Let $\Delta_1 \neq 0$ and $\Delta_2 \neq 0$ be two determinant functions in $E$. Then $\Delta_2 = \lambda \Delta_1$ where $\lambda \neq 0$ is a real number. Define an equivalence relation on the set of all non-zero determinant functions by

$$\Delta_1 \sim \Delta_2 \quad \text{if and only if} \quad \lambda > 0.$$

This relation decomposes all non-zero determinant functions into two equivalence classes. Each class is called an orientation of $E$.

A basis $e_v\;(v = 1, \ldots, n)$ of an oriented vector space is called positive if $\Delta(e_1, \ldots, e_n) > 0$ for a representing determinant function $\Delta$.
