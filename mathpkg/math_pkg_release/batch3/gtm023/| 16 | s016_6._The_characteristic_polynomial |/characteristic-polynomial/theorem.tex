Let $\Delta \neq 0$ be a determinant function in $E$. Then

$$\Delta(\varphi x_1 - \lambda x_1, \ldots, \varphi x_n - \lambda x_n) = \det(\varphi - \lambda \iota) \Delta(x_1, \ldots, x_n) \quad x_v \in E\;(v = 1, \ldots, n).$$

Expanding the left-hand side and collecting terms yields

$$\Delta(\varphi x_1 - \lambda x_1, \ldots, \varphi x_n - \lambda x_n) = \Delta(x_1, \ldots, x_n) \sum_{p=0}^{n} \alpha_p \lambda^{n-p}.$$

Hence

$$\det(\varphi - \lambda \iota) = \sum_{p=0}^{n} \alpha_p \lambda^{n-p},$$

showing that the determinant of $\varphi - \lambda \iota$ is a polynomial in $\lambda$ of degree $n$. This polynomial is called the characteristic polynomial of $\varphi$.
