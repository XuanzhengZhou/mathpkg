For real vector spaces, the existence of eigenvalues is not guaranteed (e.g., a $90^\circ$ rotation in $\mathbb{R}^2$ has none). However, when the dimension is odd, the limits of the characteristic polynomial as $\lambda \to \pm\infty$ have opposite signs, forcing at least one real root by the intermediate value theorem. Additional information about the signs of eigenvalues can be inferred from the sign of the determinant.
