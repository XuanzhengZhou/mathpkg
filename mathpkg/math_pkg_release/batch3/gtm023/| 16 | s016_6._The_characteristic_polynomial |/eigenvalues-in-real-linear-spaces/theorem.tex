Let $E$ be a real linear space and $\varphi: E \to E$ a linear transformation.

If $\dim E$ is odd, then $\varphi$ has at least one eigenvalue. Moreover,
$$f(0) = \alpha_n = \det \varphi,$$
so a linear transformation of positive determinant has at least one positive eigenvalue and a linear transformation of negative determinant has at least one negative eigenvalue.

If $\dim E$ is even and $\det \varphi < 0$, then there exists at least one positive and one negative eigenvalue.
