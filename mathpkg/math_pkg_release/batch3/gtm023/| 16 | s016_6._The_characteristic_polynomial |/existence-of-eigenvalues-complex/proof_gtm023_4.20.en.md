Let $\varphi$ be a linear transformation of the complex linear space $E$ with $\dim E = n$. Its characteristic polynomial $f(\lambda) = \det(\varphi - \lambda \iota)$ is a polynomial of degree $n \geq 1$ with complex coefficients. By the fundamental theorem of algebra, $f$ has at least one complex root $\lambda_0$. Then $f(\lambda_0) = \det(\varphi - \lambda_0 \iota) = 0$, so by the characteristic equation criterion (sec. 4.18), $\lambda_0$ is an eigenvalue of $\varphi$.
