Let $\Delta \neq 0$ be a determinant function in $E$. Consider the sum

$$\sum_{i=1}^{n} \Delta(x_1, \ldots, \varphi x_i, \ldots, x_n).$$

This sum is again a determinant function and thus can be written as

$$\sum_{i=1}^{n} \Delta(x_1, \ldots, \varphi x_i, \ldots, x_n) = \alpha \cdot \Delta(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$$

where $\alpha$ is a scalar. This scalar, which is uniquely determined by $\varphi$, is called the trace of $\varphi$ and is denoted by $\operatorname{tr} \varphi$.

The trace depends linearly on $\varphi$:
$$\operatorname{tr} (\lambda \varphi + \mu \psi) = \lambda \operatorname{tr} \varphi + \mu \operatorname{tr} \psi.$$
