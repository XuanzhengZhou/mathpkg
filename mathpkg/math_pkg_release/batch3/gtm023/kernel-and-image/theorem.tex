Suppose $E, F$ are vector spaces and let $\varphi: E \to F$ be a linear mapping.

The kernel of $\varphi$, denoted by $\ker \varphi$, is the subset of vectors $x \in E$ such that $\varphi x = 0$. $\ker \varphi$ is a subspace of $E$.

The image space of $\varphi$, denoted by $\operatorname{Im} \varphi$, is the set of vectors $y \in F$ of the form $y = \varphi x$ for some $x \in E$. $\operatorname{Im} \varphi$ is a subspace of $F$.

The mapping $\varphi$ is injective if and only if $\ker \varphi = (0)$. $\varphi$ is surjective if and only if $\operatorname{Im} \varphi = F$.
