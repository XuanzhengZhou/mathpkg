This theorem gives a complete criterion for affine equivalence of quadrics that possess a center. Two centered quadrics are affine equivalent precisely when their associated symmetric bilinear forms share the same rank and index. The proof uses the fact that an affine equivalence must map centers to centers, reducing the problem to linear equivalence of the bilinear forms -- exactly the condition characterized by rank and index.
