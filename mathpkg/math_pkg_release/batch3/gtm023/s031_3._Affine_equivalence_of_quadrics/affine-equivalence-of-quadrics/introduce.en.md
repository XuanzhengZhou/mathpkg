Affine equivalence is the natural notion of sameness for quadrics under the affine group: two quadrics are equivalent if one can be mapped onto the other by an invertible affine transformation. This equivalence relation partitions the set of all quadrics into disjoint classes, forming the basis of affine classification. The classification problem reduces to finding a complete set of normal forms and invariants that distinguish the equivalence classes.
