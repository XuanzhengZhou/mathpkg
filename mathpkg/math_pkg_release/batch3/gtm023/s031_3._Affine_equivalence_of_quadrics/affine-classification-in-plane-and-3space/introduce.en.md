Specializing the general affine classification to dimensions two and three yields the familiar taxonomy of conic sections and quadric surfaces. In the plane there are four affine types (ellipse, hyperbola, two parallel lines, parabola). In 3-space there are nine types: three non-degenerate centered quadrics (ellipsoid, one-sheeted and two-sheeted hyperboloids), three cylinders, and three paraboloids/cylinders without center. Each type is uniquely determined by its rank and index.
