For quadrics without a center, affine equivalence is still completely characterized by the rank and index of the associated symmetric bilinear form. The proof is more delicate than the centered case: an affine equivalence can introduce a non-zero scaling factor between the bilinear forms, but a detailed analysis of the allowed sign of this factor -- constrained by the index inequalities -- forces the indices to coincide. The construction of the equivalence uses an isomorphism between the tangent spaces that preserves the restricted bilinear forms.
