Consider two quadrics without center, written in the normal form using points $q_1$ and $q_2$ on the respective quadrics:
$$Q_1: \Phi_1(x - q_1) + 2\langle a_1^*, x - q_1 \rangle = 0,$$
$$Q_2: \Phi_2(x - q_2) + 2\langle a_2^*, x - q_2 \rangle = 0,$$
where $\langle a_i^*, \cdot \rangle$ are non-zero linear forms. The two quadrics $Q_1$ and $Q_2$ are affine equivalent if and only if the symmetric bilinear functions $\Phi_1$ and $\Phi_2$ have the same rank and the same index:
$$r_1 = r_2 \quad \text{and} \quad s_1 = s_2.$$
