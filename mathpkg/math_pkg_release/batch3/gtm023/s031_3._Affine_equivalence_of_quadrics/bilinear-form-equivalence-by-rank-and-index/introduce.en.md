This theorem characterizes when two symmetric bilinear forms on (possibly different) vector spaces are linearly equivalent — that is, related by a linear isomorphism. The necessary and sufficient condition is equality of two fundamental numerical invariants: the rank (dimension of the non-degenerate part) and the index (number of positive entries in a Sylvester normal form). This result underlies the affine classification of quadrics, as the bilinear form of a quadric determines its type up to affine equivalence.
