**Theorem I:** Let $E$ be a non-trivial vector space. Suppose $S$ is a system of generators and that $R$ is a linearly independent subset of $S$. Then there exists a basis $T$ of $E$ such that $R \subset T \subset S$.

**Corollary I:** Every system of generators of $E$ contains a basis. In particular, every non-trivial vector space has a basis.

**Corollary II:** Every family of linearly independent vectors of $E$ can be extended to a basis.
