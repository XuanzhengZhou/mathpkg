Two bases $a_v$ and $b_v$ ($v = 1, \ldots, n$) of a real vector space $E$ can be deformed into each other (i.e., there exist $n$ continuous mappings $x_v: [t_0, t_1] \to E$ with $x_v(t_0) = a_v$, $x_v(t_1) = b_v$, and $x_v(t)$ linearly independent for each $t$) if and only if the linear transformation $\varphi: a_v \mapsto b_v$ has positive determinant:
$$\det \varphi > 0.$$
