Let $E = E_1 \oplus E_2$ with oriented $E_1, E_2, E$. Let $\dim E_1 = p$, $\dim E = n$. The orientation induced in $E_1$ by $E_2$ coincides with the original orientation of $E_1$ if and only if $p(n-p)$ is even. Equivalently,
$$\Delta_1(e_1, \ldots, e_p) = (-1)^{p(n-p)} \Delta(e_{p+1}, \ldots, e_n, e_1, \ldots, e_p).$$
