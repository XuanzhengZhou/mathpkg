Let $E$ and $F$ be two oriented vector spaces of the same dimension $n$ and $\varphi: E \to F$ a linear isomorphism. Then $\varphi$ is called \textbf{orientation-preserving} if it maps a positive basis of $E$ to a positive basis of $F$.
Given a linear isomorphism $\varphi: E \to F$ and an orientation in $E$, there exists precisely one orientation in $F$ such that $\varphi$ preserves the orientation, called the \textbf{orientation induced by $\varphi$}.
