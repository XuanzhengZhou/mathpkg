An eigenvector of a linear transformation is a non-zero vector whose direction is preserved by the transformation: the transformation simply scales the vector by a scalar factor called the eigenvalue. Eigenvectors and eigenvalues are fundamental to understanding the structure of linear transformations, diagonalization, and spectral theory. Not every linear transformation has eigenvectors; for instance, a 90-degree rotation in the real plane has no real eigenvectors.
