Let $\tau$ be the transposition interchanging $i$ and $j$ ($i < j$). Consider the product
$$\Phi(x_1, \ldots, x_p) = \prod_{k < \ell} (x_k - x_\ell).$$
The factor $(x_i - x_j)$ appears once in $\Phi$. Under $\tau$, $x_i$ and $x_j$ are swapped, so the factor becomes $(x_j - x_i) = -(x_i - x_j)$. All other factors are either unchanged (if neither index equals $i$ or $j$) or merely reordered. Hence
$$(\tau\Phi)(x_1, \ldots, x_p) = -\Phi(x_1, \ldots, x_p),$$
which means $\varepsilon_\tau = -1$.
