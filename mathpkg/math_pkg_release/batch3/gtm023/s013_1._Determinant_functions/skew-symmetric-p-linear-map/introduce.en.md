A skew symmetric $p$-linear map transforms under permutation of its arguments by multiplication with the sign of the permutation. This is the crucial property that makes determinant functions alternating: swapping two arguments flips the sign, and if two arguments are equal, the value is zero (in characteristic $\neq 2$). Skew symmetry is the algebraic formulation of the geometric notion of signed volume.
