This proposition shows that on an $n$-dimensional space, a skew symmetric $n$-linear map has only one degree of freedom: its value on any fixed basis. All other values are obtained by multiplying this base value by the determinant of the coordinate matrix. This fact is the conceptual reason why the determinant (up to a scalar factor) is the unique skew symmetric $n$-linear function.
