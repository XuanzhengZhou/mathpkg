A determinant function is the central object of this chapter. It is an $n$-linear function on an $n$-dimensional vector space that is skew symmetric -- equivalently, it vanishes when two arguments are equal. Nontrivial determinant functions always exist (one can be constructed by choosing a basis and taking the determinant of coordinates), and any two differ only by a scalar factor.
