A $p$-linear map generalizes the notion of a bilinear map ($p=2$) to $p$ arguments. Linearity holds in each argument independently while all other arguments are held fixed. $p$-linear maps are the natural domain on which the symmetric group acts by permuting arguments, leading to the notions of symmetric and skew-symmetric multilinear maps.
