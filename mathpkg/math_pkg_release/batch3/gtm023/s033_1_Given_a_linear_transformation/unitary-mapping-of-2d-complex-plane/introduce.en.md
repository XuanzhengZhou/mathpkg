In the 2-dimensional case, unitary mappings admit an explicit parametrization in terms of complex numbers $\alpha, \beta$ satisfying $|\alpha|^2 + |\beta|^2 = 1$ and a complex phase $\varepsilon$ with $|\varepsilon| = 1$ representing the determinant. This explicit coordinate representation is useful for detailed geometric study of the unitary group $\mathrm{U}(2)$ and its special subgroup $\mathrm{SU}(2)$.
