Let $C$ be a 2-dimensional unitary space. A unitary mapping $\tau$ of $C$ can be represented with respect to an orthonormal basis $e_1, e_2$ as

$$\tau e_1 = \alpha e_1 + \beta e_2, \qquad \tau e_2 = \varepsilon \left( -\bar{\beta} e_1 + \bar{\alpha} e_2 \right),$$

where $\alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{C}$ and $\varepsilon \in \mathbb{C}$ satisfy

$$|\alpha|^2 + |\beta|^2 = 1 \quad \text{and} \quad |\varepsilon| = 1.$$

The determinant is $\det \tau = \varepsilon$. Unitary mappings with determinant $+1$ correspond to the special unitary group; for these, $\varepsilon = 1$ and the formulas reduce to

$$\tau e_1 = \alpha e_1 + \beta e_2, \qquad |\alpha|^2 + |\beta|^2 = 1.$$
