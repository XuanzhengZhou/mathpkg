Equation (11.29) shows that a unitary map is normal. Hence, by the spectral theorem for normal operators on a finite-dimensional unitary space, there exists an orthonormal basis $e_\nu$ ($\nu = 1, \ldots, n$) such that

$$\varphi e_\nu = \lambda_\nu e_\nu \quad (\nu = 1, \ldots, n),$$

where the $\lambda_\nu$ are complex numbers. Since each $\lambda_\nu$ is an eigenvalue of the unitary mapping $\varphi$, by the previous proposition every eigenvalue satisfies $|\lambda_\nu| = 1$.
