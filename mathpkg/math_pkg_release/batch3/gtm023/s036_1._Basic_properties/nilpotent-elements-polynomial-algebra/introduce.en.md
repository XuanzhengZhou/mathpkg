An element $f(a)$ in the algebra $\Gamma(a)$ is nilpotent precisely when the product of the distinct irreducible factors of the minimum polynomial (the radical of $\mu$) divides $f$. If the minimum polynomial is squarefree (all $k_i = 1$), then there are no non-zero nilpotent elements. This characterizes when $\Gamma(a)$ is a reduced algebra (has trivial nilradical).
