A polynomial $f \in \Gamma[t]$ induces a polynomial function $\tilde{f}: \Gamma \to \Gamma$ by evaluation. Over an infinite field, the map $f \mapsto \tilde{f}$ is injective, so polynomials and polynomial functions can be identified. Over finite fields, distinct polynomials may induce the same function. A root of $f$ is an element $\lambda \in \Gamma$ such that $f(\lambda) = 0$.
