The minimum polynomial of an element $a$ in a finite-dimensional algebra $A$ is the unique monic polynomial $\mu$ of minimal degree such that $\mu(a) = 0$. It generates the kernel of the evaluation homomorphism. The existence follows from the finite dimensionality of $A$ (which forces the kernel to be non-trivial) and the fact that every ideal in $\Gamma[t]$ is principal. The minimum polynomial is the polynomial analogue of the minimal polynomial of a matrix.
