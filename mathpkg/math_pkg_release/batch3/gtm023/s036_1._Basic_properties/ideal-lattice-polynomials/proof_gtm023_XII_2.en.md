Since every ideal is principal (Proposition I), the map $f \mapsto I_f$ is a bijection from $\mathcal{P}$ to $\mathcal{I}$. For monic $f, g$, we have $f \mid g \iff g = fh \iff g \in I_f \iff I_g \subset I_f$. Thus divisibility corresponds to reverse inclusion. The join in $\mathcal{I}$ is $I_f + I_g = I_{f \vee g}$ (GCD), and the meet is $I_f \cap I_g = I_{f \wedge g}$ (LCM). In $\mathcal{P}$, join is LCM and meet is GCD. The bijection reverses order, giving an anti-isomorphism of lattices.
