The set of ideals in $\Gamma[t]$ forms a lattice under inclusion. The set of monic polynomials (with zero adjoined) forms a lattice under divisibility. These two lattices are anti-isomorphic: the map sending a monic polynomial to its principal ideal reverses order, since $f \mid g$ iff $I_g \subset I_f$. Thus join in $\mathcal{P}$ (LCM) corresponds to meet in $\mathcal{I}$ (intersection), and meet in $\mathcal{P}$ (GCD) corresponds to join in $\mathcal{I}$ (sum).
