Divisibility of polynomials mirrors integer divisibility: $g$ divides $f$ if $f = gh$ for some polynomial $h$. This is equivalent to $f$ belonging to the principal ideal generated by $g$. The divisibility relation is transitive, and two monic polynomials that divide each other must be equal. This defines a partial order on monic polynomials corresponding to reverse inclusion of principal ideals.
