The greatest common divisor (GCD) of two monic polynomials is the unique monic polynomial whose principal ideal equals the sum of the ideals generated by the two polynomials. This definition ensures the GCD is a common divisor and that every common divisor divides the GCD. The definition extends naturally to any finite collection of polynomials.
