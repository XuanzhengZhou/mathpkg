A family of polynomials is relatively prime (coprime) if and only if there exists a linear combination equal to $1$. This characterization is the polynomial version of Bezout's identity: polynomials are coprime exactly when $1$ belongs to the ideal they generate. The forward direction follows from expressing the GCD (which is $1$) as a linear combination; the converse holds because any common divisor of the $f_i$ must divide $1$.
