Taylor's formula expresses $f(a+b)$ as a finite sum involving the derivatives of $f$ evaluated at $a$ and powers of $b$. Over a field of characteristic zero, division by $p!$ is valid. The formula generalizes the binomial theorem and is a purely algebraic result that holds in any commutative algebra.
