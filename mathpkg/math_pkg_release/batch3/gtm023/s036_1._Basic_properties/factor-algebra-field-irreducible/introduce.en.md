The quotient algebra $\Gamma[t]/(f)$ is a field precisely when the polynomial $f$ is irreducible. This mirrors the integer analogue: $\mathbb{Z}/(n)$ is a field iff $n$ is prime. When $f$ is reducible, the quotient has zero divisors. This theorem is fundamental in field theory, particularly in constructing field extensions by adjoining a root of an irreducible polynomial.
