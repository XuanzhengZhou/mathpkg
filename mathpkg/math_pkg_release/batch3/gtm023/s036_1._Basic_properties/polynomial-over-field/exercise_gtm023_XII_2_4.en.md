Consider the mapping $\mathbb{R}[t] \times \mathbb{R}[t] \rightarrow \mathbb{R}[t]$ defined by
$$\left( \sum_v \alpha_v t^v, \sum_v \beta_v t^v \right) \mapsto \sum_v \alpha_v \beta_v.$$

Show that this mapping makes $\mathbb{R}[t]$ into an inner product space. Prove that the induced topology makes $\mathbb{R}[t]$ into a topological algebra (addition, scalar multiplication, multiplication and division are continuous).
