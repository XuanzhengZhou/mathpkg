Consider the set of all pairs $(f, g)$ where $g \neq 0$. Define an equivalence relation in this set by
$$(f, g) \sim (\tilde{f}, \tilde{g}) \quad \text{if and only if} \quad f \tilde{g} = \tilde{f} g.$$

Show that this is indeed an equivalence relation. Denote the equivalence classes by $\overline{(f, g)}$. Prove that the operations
$$\overline{(f_1, g_1)} + \overline{(f_2, g_2)} = \overline{(f_1 g_2 + f_2 g_1, g_1 g_2)}$$
and
$$\overline{(f_1, g_1)} \, \overline{(f_2, g_2)} = \overline{(f_1 f_2, g_1 g_2)}$$
are well defined.

Show that with these operations the set of equivalence classes becomes a field, denoted by $\mathbb{Q}_\Gamma[t]$.

Prove that the mapping
$$f \mapsto \overline{(f, 1)}$$
is a monomorphism of the algebra $\Gamma[t]$ into the algebra $\mathbb{Q}_\Gamma[t]$.
