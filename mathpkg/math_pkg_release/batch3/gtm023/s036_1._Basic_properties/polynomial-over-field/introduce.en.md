A polynomial over a field $\Gamma$ is defined as an infinite sequence $(\alpha_0, \alpha_1, \ldots)$ with only finitely many non-zero terms. This algebraic construction forms the foundation of polynomial algebra. The set of all such polynomials, equipped with termwise addition and convolution multiplication, becomes a commutative associative algebra denoted $\Gamma[t]$.
