The field $\Gamma$ embeds into its polynomial algebra $\Gamma[t]$ via the map sending each scalar $\alpha$ to the constant polynomial $(\alpha, 0, 0, \ldots)$. This map is an injective algebra homomorphism, allowing us to identify $\Gamma$ as a subalgebra of $\Gamma[t]$. This identification is essential for treating scalars as polynomials of degree zero.
