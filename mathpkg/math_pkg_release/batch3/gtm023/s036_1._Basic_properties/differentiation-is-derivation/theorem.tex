For $p, q \geq 0$,
$$d(t^p \cdot t^q) = dt^p \cdot t^q + t^p \cdot dt^q.$$
Since the polynomials $t^p$ form a basis of $\Gamma[t]$, the mapping $d$ is a derivation in the algebra $\Gamma[t]$. $d$ is called the differentiation map in $\Gamma[t]$, and is the unique derivation which maps $t$ into $1$.
