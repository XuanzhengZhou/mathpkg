The differentiation map $d$ satisfies the Leibniz rule: $d(fg) = d(f)g + f d(g)$ for all polynomials $f$ and $g$. This makes $d$ a derivation on the polynomial algebra $\Gamma[t]$. The Leibniz rule is first verified on basis elements $t^p$ and then extends linearly to all polynomials. The map $d$ is the unique derivation sending $t$ to $1$.
