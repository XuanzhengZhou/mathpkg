The sum and the product of two polynomials
$$f = (\alpha_0, \alpha_1, \ldots) \quad \text{and} \quad g = (\beta_0, \beta_1, \ldots)$$
are defined by
$$f + g = (\alpha_0 + \beta_0, \alpha_1 + \beta_1, \ldots)$$
$$fg = (\gamma_0, \gamma_1, \ldots)$$
where
$$\gamma_k = \sum_{i+j=k} \alpha_i \beta_j.$$
These operations make the set of all polynomials over $\Gamma$ into a commutative associative algebra. It is called the polynomial algebra over $\Gamma$ and is denoted by $\Gamma[t]$. The unit element, $1$, of $\Gamma[t]$ is the sequence $(1, 0, \ldots)$.
