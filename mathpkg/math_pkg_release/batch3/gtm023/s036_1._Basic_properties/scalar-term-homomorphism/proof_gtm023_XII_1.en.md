For $f = \sum \alpha_v t^v$ and $g = \sum \beta_v t^v$, we have $f+g = \sum (\alpha_v+\beta_v)t^v$, so $\varrho(f+g) = \alpha_0+\beta_0 = \varrho(f)+\varrho(g)$. For the product, the constant term of $fg$ is $\gamma_0 = \sum_{i+j=0}\alpha_i\beta_j = \alpha_0\beta_0$, so $\varrho(fg) = \varrho(f)\varrho(g)$. Surjectivity follows since for any $\alpha \in \Gamma$, the constant polynomial $(\alpha,0,\ldots)$ maps to $\alpha$.
