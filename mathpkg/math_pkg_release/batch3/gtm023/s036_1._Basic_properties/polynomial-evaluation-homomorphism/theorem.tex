Let $A$ be any associative algebra with unit element $e$ and choose a fixed element $a \in A$. Then the map
$$1 \rightarrow e, \quad t \rightarrow a$$
can be extended in a unique way to an algebra homomorphism
$$\Phi: \Gamma[t] \rightarrow A.$$
Explicitly,
$$\Phi\left(\sum_k \alpha_k t^k\right) = \sum_k \alpha_k a^k.$$
The image of $\Gamma[t]$ under $\Phi$ is denoted by $\Gamma(a)$, the subalgebra of $A$ generated by $e$ and $a$. Its elements are called polynomials in $a$, denoted by $f(a)$.
