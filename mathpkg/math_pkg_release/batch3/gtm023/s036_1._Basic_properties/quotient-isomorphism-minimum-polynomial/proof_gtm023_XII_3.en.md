By the definition of $\mu$, $\ker \Phi = I_{\mu}$. The first isomorphism theorem for algebras gives the factorization $\Phi = \Psi \circ \pi$ where $\pi: \Gamma[t] \to \Gamma[t]/I_{\mu}$ is the canonical projection and $\Psi: \Gamma[t]/I_{\mu} \to \Gamma(a)$ is the induced map $\Psi(\bar{f}) = \Phi(f) = f(a)$. Since $\Phi$ is surjective onto $\Gamma(a)$ (by definition of $\Gamma(a)$ as the image of $\Phi$), $\Psi$ is surjective. Since $\ker \Psi = \pi(\ker \Phi) = \pi(I_{\mu}) = 0$, $\Psi$ is injective. Thus $\Psi$ is an isomorphism and the diagram commutes.
