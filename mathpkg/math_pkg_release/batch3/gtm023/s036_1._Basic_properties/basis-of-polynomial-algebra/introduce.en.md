The monomials $t^k$ for $k = 0, 1, 2, \ldots$ form a basis of the polynomial algebra $\Gamma[t]$ as a vector space over $\Gamma$. Every polynomial can be expressed uniquely as a finite linear combination $\sum \alpha_v t^v$. This standard representation is the familiar way of writing polynomials and shows that $\Gamma[t]$ is an infinite-dimensional vector space with countable basis.
