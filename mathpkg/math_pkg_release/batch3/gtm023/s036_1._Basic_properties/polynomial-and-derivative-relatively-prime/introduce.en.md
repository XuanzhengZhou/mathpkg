A polynomial has no repeated irreducible factors (is squarefree) precisely when it is relatively prime to its formal derivative. This is a fundamental criterion in algebra: if $f = \prod f_i$ with distinct irreducibles, then $f'$ shares no common factor with $f$. Conversely, if $f$ has a repeated factor $f_i^2$, then $f_i$ divides both $f$ and $f'$.
