Let $f = f_1^{k_1} \cdots f_s^{k_s}$ be the prime factorization. The corollary of Theorem I implies that any divisor of $f^m = f_1^{mk_1} \cdots f_s^{mk_s}$ has the form $f_1^{j_1} \cdots f_s^{j_s}$ with $j_v \leq mk_v$. Since $g$ is a product of relatively prime irreducibles, all $j_v$ are $0$ or $1$. Thus $j_v \leq k_v$ for all $v$ (since $k_v \geq 1$), meaning $g \mid f$.
