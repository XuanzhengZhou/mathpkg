If a product of distinct (relatively prime) irreducible polynomials divides $f^m$, then it already divides $f$. This means that a squarefree polynomial dividing a power of $f$ must divide $f$ itself. This property is crucial for characterizing nilpotent elements in polynomial algebras and for relating $f$ and its derivative.
