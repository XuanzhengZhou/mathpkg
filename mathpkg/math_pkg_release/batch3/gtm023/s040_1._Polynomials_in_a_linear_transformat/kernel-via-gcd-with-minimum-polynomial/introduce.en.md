Applying Proposition I with the minimum polynomial $\mu$ simplifies the study of an arbitrary kernel $K(f)$: one may replace $f$ by its greatest common divisor with $\mu$, which is a divisor of $\mu$. Thus every $K(f)$ is already of the form $K(d)$ for some divisor $d$ of the minimum polynomial.
