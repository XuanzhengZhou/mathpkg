Since $K(\mu) = E$, we have

$$K(f) = K(f) \cap E = K(f) \cap K(\mu).$$

By Proposition I, $K(f) \cap K(\mu) = K(d)$ where $d = \gcd(f, \mu)$. Hence $K(f) = K(d)$.
