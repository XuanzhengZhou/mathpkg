When two polynomials are relatively prime, their LCM is simply their product and their GCD is $1$. Proposition I then gives $K(f) \cap K(g) = K(1) = 0$, while Proposition II gives $K(fg) = K(f) + K(g)$. Together these yield a direct sum decomposition, a crucial tool for the primary decomposition theorem.
