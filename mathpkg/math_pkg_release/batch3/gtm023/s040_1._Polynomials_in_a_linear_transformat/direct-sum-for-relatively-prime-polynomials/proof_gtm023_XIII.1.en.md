Since $f$ and $g$ are relatively prime, their least common multiple is $fg$. By Proposition II,

$$K(fg) = K(f) + K(g). \tag{13.9}$$

On the other hand, the greatest common divisor of $f$ and $g$ is $1$, so Proposition I yields

$$K(f) \cap K(g) = K(1) = 0. \tag{13.10}$$

Thus the sum is direct: $K(fg) = K(f) \oplus K(g)$.
