Let $\varphi: E \to E$ be a linear transformation on a finite-dimensional vector space over a field $\Gamma$ of characteristic $0$, and let $\mu \in \Gamma[t]$ be the minimum polynomial of $\varphi$. A scalar $\lambda \in \Gamma$ is an eigenvalue of $\varphi$ if and only if

$$\mu(\lambda) = 0,$$

i.e., the eigenvalues of $\varphi$ are precisely the distinct roots of the minimum polynomial $\mu$.
