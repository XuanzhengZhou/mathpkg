A scalar $\lambda$ is an eigenvalue iff $K(t - \lambda) \neq 0$. By Corollary I to Proposition III, this happens exactly when $t - \lambda$ is not relatively prime to $\mu$, which for a linear polynomial means $t - \lambda$ divides $\mu$. Thus the eigenvalues are the roots of the minimum polynomial.
