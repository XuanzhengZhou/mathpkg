A homomorphism of short exact sequences is a commutative ladder diagram consisting of two short exact sequences and three vertical linear maps connecting corresponding terms. When all three vertical maps are isomorphisms, the diagram gives an isomorphism of short exact sequences, which is the natural notion of equivalence in this context. The fundamental example shows that every short exact sequence is isomorphic to the canonical sequence $0 \to E_1 \to E \to E/E_1 \to 0$.
