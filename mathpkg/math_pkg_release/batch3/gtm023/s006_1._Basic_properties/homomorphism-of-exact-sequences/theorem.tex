A commutative diagram of the form
$$
\begin{CD}
0 @>>> F_1 @>{\varphi_1}>> E_1 @>{\psi_1}>> G_1 @>>> 0 \\
    @VV{\varrho}V @VV{\sigma}V @VV{\tau}V \\
0 @>>> F_2 @>{\varphi_2}>> E_2 @>{\psi_2}>> G_2 @>>> 0
\end{CD}
$$
where both horizontal sequences are short exact sequences and $\varrho, \sigma, \tau$ are linear mappings, is called a \text{homomorphism of exact sequences}. If $\varrho, \sigma, \tau$ are linear isomorphisms, then the diagram is called an \text{isomorphism} between the two short exact sequences.
