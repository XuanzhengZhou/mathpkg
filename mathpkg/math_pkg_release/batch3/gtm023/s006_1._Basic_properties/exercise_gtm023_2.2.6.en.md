Let $E$ and $F$ be vector spaces and let $S_E$ and $S_F$ be sets of linear transformations of $E$ and $F$ respectively. Suppose $\alpha: E \to F$ is a linear mapping such that
$$\alpha S_E = S_F \alpha,$$
i.e., to every transformation $\varphi \in S_E$ there exists a transformation $\psi \in S_F$ such that $\alpha \circ \varphi = \psi \circ \alpha$, and conversely. Prove that either $\alpha = 0$ or $\alpha$ is a linear isomorphism of $E$ onto $F$.
