A linear isomorphism preserves all linear-algebraic structure. This collection of three properties makes precise what "preserves all linear properties" means: generating sets, linear independence, and bases are preserved (Property I); the subspace lattice and quotient structure are preserved (Property II); and the Hom-spaces transform naturally under pre- and post-composition with the isomorphism (Property III).
