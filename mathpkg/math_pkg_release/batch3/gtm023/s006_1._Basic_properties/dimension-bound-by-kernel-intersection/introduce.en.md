If a subspace $F$ is defined as the intersection of the kernels of $r$ linear functions, then the codimension of $F$ in $E$ is at most $r$. This follows from considering the linear map $\varphi: E \to \Gamma^r$ defined by $x \mapsto (f_1(x), \ldots, f_r(x))$, whose kernel is exactly $F$, and applying the First Isomorphism Theorem.
