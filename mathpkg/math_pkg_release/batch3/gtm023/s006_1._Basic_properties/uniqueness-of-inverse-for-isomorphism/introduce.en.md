If a linear map is bijective, its two-sided inverse $\varphi^{-1}$ serves as both a left and right inverse. This corollary shows that these one-sided inverses are in fact unique: any left inverse must equal $\varphi^{-1}$, and any right inverse must equal $\varphi^{-1}$.
