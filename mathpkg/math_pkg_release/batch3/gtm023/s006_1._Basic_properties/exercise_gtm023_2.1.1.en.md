Let $C$ be the space of continuous functions $f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ and define the mapping $\varphi: C \to C$ by
$$\varphi(f)(t) = \int_0^t f(s)\,ds.$$
Prove that $\operatorname{Im} \varphi$ consists of all continuously differentiable functions, while the kernel of $\varphi$ consists only of the zero function.
