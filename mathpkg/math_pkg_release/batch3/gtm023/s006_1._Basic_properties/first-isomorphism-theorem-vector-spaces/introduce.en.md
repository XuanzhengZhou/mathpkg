The First Isomorphism Theorem states that every linear map factors uniquely as the composition of a surjective projection onto the quotient by its kernel followed by an injective map to the codomain. This is the vector-space analogue of the fundamental homomorphism theorem for groups. The domain modulo the kernel is canonically isomorphic to the image.
