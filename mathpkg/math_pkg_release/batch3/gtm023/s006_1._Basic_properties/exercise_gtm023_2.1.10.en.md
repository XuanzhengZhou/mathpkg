Assume a commutative diagram of linear maps
$$
\begin{CD}
E_1 @>{\alpha_1}>> E_2 @>{\alpha_2}>> E_3 @>{\alpha_3}>> E_4 @>{\alpha_4}>> E_5 \\
@V{\varphi_1}VV @V{\varphi_2}VV @V{\varphi_3}VV @V{\varphi_4}VV @V{\varphi_5}VV \\
F_1 @>{\beta_1}>> F_2 @>{\beta_2}>> F_3 @>{\beta_3}>> F_4 @>{\beta_4}>> F_5
\end{CD}
$$
where both horizontal sequences are exact.

i) Show that if $\varphi_4$ is injective and $\varphi_1$ is surjective, then
$$\ker \varphi_3 = \alpha_2(\ker \varphi_2).$$

ii) Show that if $\varphi_2$ is surjective and $\varphi_5$ is injective, then
$$\operatorname{Im} \varphi_3 = \beta_3^{-1}(\operatorname{Im} \varphi_4).$$

iii) Conclude that if the maps $\varphi_1, \varphi_2, \varphi_4, \varphi_5$ are linear isomorphisms, then so is $\varphi_3$ (Five Lemma).
