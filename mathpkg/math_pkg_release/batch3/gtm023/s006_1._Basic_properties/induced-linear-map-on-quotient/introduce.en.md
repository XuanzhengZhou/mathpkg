Given a linear map $\varphi: E \to F$ and subspaces $E_1 \subset E$, $F_1 \subset F$ with $\varphi(E_1) \subset F_1$, there is a uniquely induced linear map between the quotient spaces $E/E_1$ and $F/F_1$. This fundamental construction is the linear-algebraic analogue of the universal property of quotients: any linear map that annihilates a subspace factors uniquely through the quotient.
