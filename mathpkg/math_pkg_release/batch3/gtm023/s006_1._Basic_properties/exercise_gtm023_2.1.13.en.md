Prove the Hexagonal Lemma: Given a diagram of linear maps

$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
& E & \\
\stackrel{h_1}{\nearrow} & & \stackrel{h_2}{\searrow} \\
E_1 & & E_2 \\
\stackrel{i_1}{\searrow} & \stackrel{k_1}{\downarrow} \quad \stackrel{k_2}{\downarrow} & \stackrel{i_2}{\nearrow} \\
& F & \\
\stackrel{j_1}{\nearrow} & & \stackrel{j_2}{\searrow} \\
F_1 & \stackrel{\varphi}{\longrightarrow} & F_2 \\
\stackrel{l_1}{\searrow} & \stackrel{\psi}{\longrightarrow} & \stackrel{l_2}{\nearrow} \\
& G &
\end{array}
$$

in which all triangles are commutative, $k_1$ and $k_2$ are isomorphisms, and the diagonals $j_2 \circ i_1$ and $j_1 \circ i_2$ are exact at $E$. Show that
$$l_1 \circ k_1 \circ h_1 + l_2 \circ k_2 \circ h_2 = \psi \circ \varphi.$$
