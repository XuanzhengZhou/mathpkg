Consider a system of linear mappings forming a commutative $3 \times 3$ diagram
$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
0 & 0 & 0 \\
\downarrow & \downarrow & \downarrow \\
0 \to E_{00} \xrightarrow{\varphi_{00}} E_{01} \xrightarrow{\varphi_{01}} E_{02} \to 0 \\
\phantom{0}\downarrow{\psi_{00}} & \phantom{0}\downarrow{\psi_{01}} & \phantom{0}\downarrow{\psi_{02}} \\
0 \to E_{10} \xrightarrow{\varphi_{10}} E_{11} \xrightarrow{\varphi_{11}} E_{12} \to 0 \\
\phantom{0}\downarrow{\psi_{10}} & \phantom{0}\downarrow{\psi_{11}} & \phantom{0}\downarrow{\psi_{12}} \\
0 \to E_{20} \xrightarrow{\varphi_{20}} E_{21} \xrightarrow{\varphi_{21}} E_{22} \to 0 \\
\downarrow & \downarrow & \downarrow \\
0 & 0 & 0
\end{array}
$$
where all rows and columns are short exact sequences. Prove the $3 \times 3$ Lemma: the induced sequence on kernels (or cokernels) of the vertical maps is exact.
