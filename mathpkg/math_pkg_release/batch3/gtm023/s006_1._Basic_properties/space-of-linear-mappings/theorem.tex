Let $E$ and $F$ be vector spaces over the same field. The set $L(E; F)$ of all linear mappings $\varphi: E \to F$ becomes a vector space under pointwise operations:
$$
(\varphi + \psi)(x) = \varphi(x) + \psi(x), \qquad (\lambda \varphi)(x) = \lambda \varphi(x) \quad (x \in E).
$$
The zero vector of $L(E; F)$ is the linear mapping $0$ defined by $0(x) = 0$ for all $x \in E$. When $F = \Gamma$ (the ground field), the space $L(E; \Gamma)$ is denoted by $L(E)$, the space of linear functions on $E$.
