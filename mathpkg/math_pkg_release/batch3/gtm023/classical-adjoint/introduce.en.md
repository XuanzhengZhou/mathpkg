The classical adjoint (or adjugate) of a linear transformation generalizes the matrix adjugate. It satisfies $\operatorname{ad}(\varphi) \circ \varphi = \varphi \circ \operatorname{ad}(\varphi) = \iota \cdot \det\varphi$. When $\varphi$ is invertible, this gives an explicit formula for the inverse: $\varphi^{-1} = (\det\varphi)^{-1}\operatorname{ad}(\varphi)$. This is the basis for Cramer's rule.
