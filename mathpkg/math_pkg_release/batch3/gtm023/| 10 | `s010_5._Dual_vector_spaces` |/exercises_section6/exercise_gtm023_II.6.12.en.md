Let $E$ and $F$ be vector spaces of finite dimension and consider a bilinear function $\Phi$ in $E \times F$. Prove that

$$\dim E - \dim N_E = \dim F - \dim N_F,$$

where $N_E$ and $N_F$ denote the null spaces of $\Phi$.

Conclude that if $\dim E = \dim F$, then $N_E = 0$ if and only if $N_F = 0$.
