Let $F, F^*$ be arbitrary dual spaces and $\varphi: E \rightarrow F$ be a linear mapping. Then a dual mapping $\varphi^*: L(E) \leftarrow F^*$ exists, and is given by

$$(\varphi^* y^*)(x) = \langle y^*, \varphi x \rangle, \quad y^* \in F^*, \quad x \in E.$$

If $F^* = L(F)$ as well, this can be written as

$$\varphi^* f = f \circ \varphi, \quad f \in L(F).$$
