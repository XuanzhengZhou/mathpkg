When the target dual space $F^*$ is arbitrary but the domain dual space is taken as the full space of linear functions $L(E)$, a dual mapping $\varphi^*: L(E) \leftarrow F^*$ always exists for any linear mapping $\varphi: E \rightarrow F$. This proposition shows that $L(E)$ serves as a universal receiving space for dual mappings, in contrast to general dual pairs where the existence of a dual mapping is not guaranteed.
