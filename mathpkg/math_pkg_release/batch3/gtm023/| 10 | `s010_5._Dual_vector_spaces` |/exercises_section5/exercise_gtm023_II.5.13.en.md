Let $\Phi: E \times F \rightarrow \Gamma$ be a bilinear map such that the linear maps $\varphi: E \rightarrow L(F)$ and $\psi: F \rightarrow L(E)$ given by $\varphi_a(y) = \Phi(a, y)$ and $\psi_b(x) = \Phi(x, b)$ are isomorphisms. Show that the canonical map $\lambda$ defined in problem 3 is given by $\lambda = (\psi^{-1})^* \circ \varphi$. Conclude that $E$ and $F$ must have the same finite dimension.
