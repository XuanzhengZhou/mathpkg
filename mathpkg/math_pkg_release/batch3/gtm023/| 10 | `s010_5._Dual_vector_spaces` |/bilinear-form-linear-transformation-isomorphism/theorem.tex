Let $E$ be a finite dimensional vector space and $E^*$ a dual space. Then there is a canonical linear isomorphism

$$B(E^*, E) \cong L(E; E),$$

where $B(E^*, E)$ denotes the space of bilinear functions $\Phi: E^* \times E \rightarrow \Gamma$. Specifically, for every bilinear function $\Phi \in B(E^*, E)$, there exists a unique linear transformation $\varphi \in L(E; E)$ such that

$$\Phi(x^*, x) = \langle x^*, \varphi x \rangle, \quad x^* \in E^*, \quad x \in E.$$
