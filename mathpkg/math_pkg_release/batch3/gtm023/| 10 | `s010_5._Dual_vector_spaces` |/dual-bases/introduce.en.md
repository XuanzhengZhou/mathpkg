Dual bases provide a concrete computational tool for working with finite-dimensional dual spaces. Given a basis of $E$, the dual basis of $E^*$ is the unique basis satisfying the biorthogonality condition $\langle x^{*v}, x_\mu \rangle = \delta_\mu^v$. The components of a vector with respect to the primal basis are recovered by evaluating the dual basis vectors, and the scalar product takes the simple Euclidean form $\sum_v \xi_v \xi^v$ when expressed in dual bases.
