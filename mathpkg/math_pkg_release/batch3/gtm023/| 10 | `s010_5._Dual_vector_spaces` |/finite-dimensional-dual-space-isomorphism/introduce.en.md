In finite dimensions, every abstract dual space $E^*$ is canonically isomorphic to the space of all linear functions $L(E)$. This fundamental result justifies the common practice of identifying the dual space with the space of linear functionals in the finite-dimensional setting. The proof uses the symmetry between $E$ and $E^*$ together with the dimension equality $\dim L(E) = \dim E$.
