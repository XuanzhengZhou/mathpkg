In finite dimensions, a linear mapping and its dual always have the same rank. This follows from the dimension formula and the relation $\operatorname{Im} \varphi^* = (\ker \varphi)^\perp$. The first orthogonal relation $\operatorname{Im} \varphi = (\ker \varphi^*)^\perp$ provides a practical criterion for solvability of linear equations: $\varphi x = y$ has a solution if and only if $y$ is orthogonal to every solution of the transposed homogeneous equation.
