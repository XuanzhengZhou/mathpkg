Proposition VI provides a converse to the direct decomposition theorem. Given a subspace $E_1 \subset E$ and a dual subspace $E_1^* \subset L(E)$ satisfying the double-orthogonal closure condition $(E_1^*)^{\perp\perp} = E_1^*$, one can reconstruct a direct sum decomposition of both $E$ and $L(E)$. This result is useful for recognizing when a subspace admits a complementary subspace via duality.
