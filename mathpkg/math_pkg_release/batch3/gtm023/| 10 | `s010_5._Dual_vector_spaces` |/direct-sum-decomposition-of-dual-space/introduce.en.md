When a vector space $E$ decomposes as a direct sum $E = E_1 \oplus E_2$, the space of linear functions $L(E)$ decomposes correspondingly as the direct sum of the orthogonal complements $E_1^\perp$ and $E_2^\perp$. The orthogonal complement $E_1^\perp$ is naturally isomorphic to $L(E_2)$, and $E_2^\perp$ to $L(E_1)$, yielding the elegant formula $L(E_1 \oplus E_2) \cong L(E_1) \oplus L(E_2)$. This shows that duality converts direct sums into direct sums.
