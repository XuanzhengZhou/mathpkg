Given a $G^p$-gradation in $E$, consider the homomorphism $\tau: G^p \rightarrow G$ given by
$$\tau(k_1, \dots, k_p) = k_1 + \dots + k_p.$$
The induced (simple) $G$-gradation of $E$ is given by
$$E = \sum_{j} F_j, \quad F_j = \sum_{k_1 + \dots + k_p = j} E_{k_1, \dots, k_p}.$$
This $G$-gradation is called the \textit{(simple) $G$-gradation induced by the given $G^p$-gradation}.
