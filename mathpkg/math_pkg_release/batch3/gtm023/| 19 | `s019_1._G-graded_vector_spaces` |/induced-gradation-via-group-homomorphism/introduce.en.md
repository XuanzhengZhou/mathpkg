Given a group homomorphism $\tau: G \rightarrow G'$, any $G$-gradation of a vector space induces a $G'$-gradation by collecting together all homogeneous components whose degrees map to the same element of $G'$ under $\tau$. This construction is a "push-forward" of the grading along the homomorphism. The directness of the induced decomposition follows from the directness of the original decomposition and the fact that distinct fibers of $\tau$ are disjoint.
