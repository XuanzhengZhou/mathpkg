Suppose $E = \sum_{k \in G} E_k$ and $F = \sum_{k \in G} F_k$ are two $G$-graded vector spaces, and assume that $\varphi: E \times F \rightarrow \Gamma$ is a bilinear function. Then $\varphi$ \textit{respects the $G$-gradations} of $E$ and $F$ if
$$\varphi(E_k \times F_j) = 0$$
for each pair of distinct degrees, $k \neq j$.

Every such bilinear function determines bilinear functions $\varphi_k: E_k \times F_k \rightarrow \Gamma$ ($k \in G$) by $\varphi|_{E_k \times F_k} = \varphi_k$. Conversely, given any bilinear functions $\varphi_k: E_k \times F_k \rightarrow \Gamma$, a unique bilinear function $\varphi: E \times F \rightarrow \Gamma$ which respects $G$-gradations is determined by these restrictions.
