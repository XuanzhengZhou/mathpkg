Two gradations (by groups $G$ and $H$) on the same vector space are compatible if their intersection yields a direct sum decomposition, i.e., each $E_j \cap F_k$ is a well-defined subspace and $E$ is the direct sum of all such intersections. This proposition establishes a one-to-one correspondence between pairs of compatible $G$ and $H$-gradations on $E$ and a single $(G \oplus H)$-gradation (a bigrading) on $E$. This is the foundational result for the theory of bigraded vector spaces.
