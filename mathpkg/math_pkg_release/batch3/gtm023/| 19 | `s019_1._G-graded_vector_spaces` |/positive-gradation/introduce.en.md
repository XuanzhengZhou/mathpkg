A positive gradation is a $\mathbb{Z}$-grading of a vector space where all negative-degree components are trivial ($E_k = 0$ for $k \leq -1$). Equivalently, the space decomposes as $E = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} E_k$. Positive gradations are the natural setting for Poincaré series and appear throughout homology theory, where the degree corresponds to the dimension of chains or the index of a filtration.
