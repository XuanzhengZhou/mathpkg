When a homogeneous linear map between $G$-graded spaces is surjective, the image space $F$ inherits a natural $G$-gradation: the degree-$j$ component of $F$ is the image under $\varphi$ of the degree-$j$ component of $E$. The proof uses that the kernel of $\varphi$ is a graded subspace to show the decomposition is direct. This gradation is uniquely characterised by the requirement that $\varphi$ be homogeneous of degree zero, making it the correct quotient gradation in the graded category.
