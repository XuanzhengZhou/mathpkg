The Poincaré series is a formal power series that encodes the dimensions of the homogeneous components of an almost finite positively graded vector space. Each coefficient $\dim E_k$ is the number of linearly independent vectors of degree $k$. When the total dimension is finite, the Poincaré series is a polynomial, and evaluating at $t=1$ recovers the total dimension. The Poincaré series plays a role analogous to generating functions in combinatorics and is a fundamental invariant in algebraic topology (Betti numbers) and commutative algebra (Hilbert series).
