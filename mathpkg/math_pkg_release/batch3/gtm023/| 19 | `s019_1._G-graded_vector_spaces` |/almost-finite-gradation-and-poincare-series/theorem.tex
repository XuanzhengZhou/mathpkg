A gradation $E = \sum_k E_k$ of a vector space $E$ is called \textit{almost finite} if the dimension of every space $E_k$ is finite. To every almost finite positive gradation we assign the formal series
$$P_E(t) = \sum_k \dim E_k \cdot t^k.$$
$P_E(t)$ is called the \textit{Poincaré series} of the graded space $E$. If the dimension of $E$ is finite, then $P_E(t)$ is a polynomial and
$$P_E(1) = \dim E.$$
