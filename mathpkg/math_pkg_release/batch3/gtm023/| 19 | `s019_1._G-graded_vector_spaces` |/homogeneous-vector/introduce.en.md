A homogeneous vector in a $G$-graded vector space is a vector that belongs to exactly one summand $E_{\alpha}$ of the direct decomposition, and its degree is the element $k(\alpha) \in G$ assigned to that summand. The zero vector is exceptional: it is declared homogeneous of every degree by convention, ensuring that subspaces of homogeneous elements remain well-behaved under addition.
