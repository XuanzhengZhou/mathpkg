When two $G$-graded vector spaces $E$ and $F$ are paired by a scalar product (a non-degenerate bilinear form) that respects the gradations, the homogeneous components $E_k$ and $F_k$ are dual vector spaces. This forces equality of dimensions: $\dim E_k = \dim F_k$ for every degree $k \in G$. In the important special case of positive $\mathbb{Z}$-gradations, this dimension equality implies that the Poincaré series of $E$ and $F$ coincide. This result underlies the duality between homology and cohomology in algebraic topology.
