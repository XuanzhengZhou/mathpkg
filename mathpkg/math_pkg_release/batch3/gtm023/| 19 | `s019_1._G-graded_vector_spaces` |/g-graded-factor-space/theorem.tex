Let $E$ be a $G$-graded vector space and let $F \subset E$ be a $G$-graded subspace. Then there is a unique $G$-gradation in the factor space $E/F$ such that the canonical projection $\pi: E \rightarrow E/F$ is homogeneous of degree zero. The factor space, together with this $G$-gradation, is called a \textit{$G$-graded factor space} of $E$.
