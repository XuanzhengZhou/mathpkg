A $G^p$-gradation (or $p$-gradation when $G = \mathbb{Z}$) is a grading of a vector space by the $p$-fold direct sum $G \oplus \cdots \oplus G$. This is the natural notion of a multi-grading: each vector is assigned a $p$-tuple of degrees. Multi-gradings arise naturally when studying tensor products of graded objects, double complexes, and spectral sequences.
