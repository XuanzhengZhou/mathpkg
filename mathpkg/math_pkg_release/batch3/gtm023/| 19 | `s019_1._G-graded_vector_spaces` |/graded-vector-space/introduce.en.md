A graded vector space (without qualification) is the special case of a $G$-graded vector space where the group of degrees is $\mathbb{Z}$. This $\mathbb{Z}$-grading is the most commonly encountered grading in algebra and topology, appearing in polynomial rings, exterior algebras, homology groups, and spectral sequences. The integer $k$ is called the degree, and vectors in $E_k$ are homogeneous of degree $k$.
