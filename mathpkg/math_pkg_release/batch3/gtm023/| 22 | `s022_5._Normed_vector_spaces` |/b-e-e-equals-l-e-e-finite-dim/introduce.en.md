In finite-dimensional normed spaces, the distinction between bounded and unbounded linear transformations disappears: every linear transformation is automatically bounded, so $B(E;E) = L(E;E)$. Furthermore, the supremum in the definition of the operator norm is actually attained as a maximum, due to the compactness of the unit sphere in finite dimensions.
