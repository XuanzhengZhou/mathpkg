Properties $N_1$, $N_2$, and $N_3$ of the norm-function imply respectively that the induced distance $\varrho(x,y) = \|x-y\|$ satisfies positive definiteness, the triangle inequality, and symmetry -- the three defining properties of a metric. Thus every normed linear space is canonically a metric space, and the resulting norm topology makes it a topological vector space.
