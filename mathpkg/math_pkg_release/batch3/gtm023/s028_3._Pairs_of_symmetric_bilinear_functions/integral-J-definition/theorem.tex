Let $\Phi$ and $\Psi$ be symmetric bilinear functions on a real vector space $E$, and let $\Phi(x) = \Phi(x,x)$, $\Psi(x) = \Psi(x,x)$ be the associated quadratic forms. Denote by $\dot{E}$ the deleted space $\{x \in E : x \neq 0\}$. For a differentiable curve $x = x(t)$ ($0 \leq t \leq 1$) in $\dot{E}$, the line integral $J$ is defined by
$$J = \int_0^1 \frac{\Phi(x)\Psi(x, \dot{x}) - \Phi(x, \dot{x})\Psi(x)}{\Phi(x)^2 + \Psi(x)^2} \, dt,$$
where $\dot{x} = \frac{dx}{dt}$ and the integrand is evaluated along the curve $x(t)$.
