This lemma handles the case where $\Psi$ may be degenerate. By considering the polynomial $f(\lambda)$ obtained from the determinant of the linear pencil $\varphi + \lambda\psi$, one shows that $f$ has degree $r = \operatorname{rank}(\psi) \geq 1$ and is not identically zero. Hence there exists a real number $\lambda_0$ such that $f(\lambda_0) \neq 0$, which means the bilinear function $\Phi + \lambda_0 \Psi$ is non-degenerate. This reduces the general simultaneous diagonalization problem to the non-degenerate case treated elsewhere.
