This is the main theorem of the section: any two symmetric bilinear functions on a real vector space of dimension at least three, satisfying a natural non-vanishing condition on their quadratic forms, can be simultaneously diagonalized. The proof uses a line-integral technique to define a continuous functional whose critical points yield eigenvectors, followed by an iterative construction. The dimension condition $n \geq 3$ is essential for the simple connectivity argument that ensures the line integral is path-independent.
