When one of two symmetric bilinear functions is non-degenerate, the question of simultaneous diagonalization reduces to the diagonalizability of the associated linear transformation $\varphi$ defined by $\Phi(x,y) = \Psi(\varphi x, y)$. This proposition establishes the equivalence between simultaneous diagonalization of the pair $(\Phi, \Psi)$ and the existence of an eigenbasis for $\varphi$. It provides a complete algebraic criterion for the special case where one form is non-degenerate.
