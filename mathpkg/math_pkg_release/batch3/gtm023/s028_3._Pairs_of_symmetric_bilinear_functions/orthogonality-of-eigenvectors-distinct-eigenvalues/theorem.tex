Let $\Phi$ and $\Psi$ be symmetric bilinear functions on a vector space $E$ over a field of characteristic $\neq 2$. Assume $\Psi$ is non-degenerate and let $\varphi: E \to E$ be the linear transformation satisfying
$$\Phi(x, y) = \Psi(\varphi x, y) \quad \text{for all } x, y \in E.$$
If $x_1$ and $x_2$ are eigenvectors of $\varphi$ with distinct eigenvalues $\lambda_1 \neq \lambda_2$, then
$$\Psi(x_1, x_2) = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad \Phi(x_1, x_2) = 0.$$
Thus eigenvectors corresponding to distinct eigenvalues are orthogonal with respect to both $\Phi$ and $\Psi$.
