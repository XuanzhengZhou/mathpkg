Let $E$ be a real vector space of dimension $n \geq 3$, and let $\Phi$ and $\Psi$ be symmetric bilinear functions on $E$ with associated quadratic forms $\Phi(x) = \Phi(x,x)$, $\Psi(x) = \Psi(x,x)$ satisfying
$$\Phi(x)^2 + \Psi(x)^2 \neq 0 \quad \text{for all } x \neq 0.$$
Then the line integral
$$J = \int_0^1 \frac{\Phi(x)\Psi(x, \dot{x}) - \Phi(x, \dot{x})\Psi(x)}{\Phi(x)^2 + \Psi(x)^2} \, dt$$
taken along any differentiable curve $x(t)$ in $\dot{E} = E \setminus \{0\}$ depends only on the endpoints $x(0)$ and $x(1)$, and not on the choice of path joining them.
