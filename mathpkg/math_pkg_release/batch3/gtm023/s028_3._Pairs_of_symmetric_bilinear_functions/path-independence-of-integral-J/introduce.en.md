The path independence of the line integral $J$ is the critical step that makes the entire construction work. By identifying $2J$ with the change in an angle function along the curve $\omega(t) = \Phi(x(t)) + i\Psi(x(t))$, one shows that the difference between two path integrals is an integer multiple of $\pi$. The hypothesis $n \geq 3$ enters via the simple connectivity of the deleted space $\dot{E} = E \setminus \{0\}$: any two curves with the same endpoints are homotopic, and a continuity argument forces the integer factor to be zero. This allows defining a single-valued functional $F$ on $\dot{E}$.
