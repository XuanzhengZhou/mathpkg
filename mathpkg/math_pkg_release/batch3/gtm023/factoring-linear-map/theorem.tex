Let $\varphi: E \to F$ be a linear mapping and consider the subspaces $E_1 = \ker \varphi$ and $F_1 = (0)$. Then a linear mapping
$$\bar{\varphi}: E / \ker \varphi \to F$$
is induced by $\varphi$ such that
$$\bar{\varphi} \circ \pi = \varphi$$
where $\pi: E \to E / \ker \varphi$ is the canonical projection. The induced map $\bar{\varphi}$ is injective, and its restriction to $\operatorname{Im} \varphi$ gives an isomorphism
$$E / \ker \varphi \xrightarrow{\cong} \operatorname{Im} \varphi.$$

**Proposition I:** Suppose $\varphi: E \to F$ and $\psi: E \to G$ are linear mappings such that $\ker \varphi \subset \ker \psi$. Then $\psi$ can be factored over $\varphi$; that is, there exists a linear mapping $\chi: F \to G$ such that $\chi \circ \varphi = \psi$.
