Let $E$ be a vector space and $G$ be an abelian group. Suppose a direct decomposition $E = \sum_{\alpha \in I} E_\alpha$ is given and each $E_\alpha$ is assigned an element $k(\alpha) \in G$ injectively. Then $E$ is a $G$-graded vector space. Vectors of $E_\alpha$ are homogeneous of degree $k(\alpha)$.

If $G = \mathbb{Z}$, $E$ is a graded vector space. A gradation with $E_k = 0$ for $k \leq -1$ is called a positive gradation.

A subspace $F \subset E$ is a $G$-graded subspace if $F = \sum_{k \in G} F \cap E_k$.
