This lemma provides the first step in the constructive proof that two symmetric bilinear functions $\Phi$ and $\Psi$, with $\Psi$ non-degenerate, can be simultaneously diagonalized. By introducing a positive definite inner product and considering the continuous function $F(x) = \Phi(x)/\Psi(x)$ on the compact unit sphere, a minimum is attained at some vector $e_1$. A variational argument shows that this minimizing vector satisfies a generalized eigenvalue equation $\Phi(e_1, y) = \lambda_1 \Psi(e_1, y)$ for all vectors $y$, where $\lambda_1 = \Phi(e_1)/\Psi(e_1)$. This reduces the simultaneous diagonalization problem to finding a common eigenvector for the pair $(\Phi, \Psi)$.
