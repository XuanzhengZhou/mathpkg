Let $E$ be an $n$-dimensional real vector space, $\Phi$ and $\Psi$ symmetric bilinear functions on $E$, with $\Psi$ non-degenerate. Introduce a positive definite inner product on $E$ and define the continuous function
$$F(x) = \frac{\Phi(x)}{\Psi(x)}$$
on the set where $\Psi(x) \neq 0$. Let $e_1$ be a vector with $|e_1| = 1$ such that
$$F(e_1) \leq F(x)$$
for all $x$ with $|x| = 1$ (and hence, by homogeneity, for all $x \neq 0$). Then $\Psi(e_1) \neq 0$, and setting
$$\lambda_1 = \frac{\Phi(e_1)}{\Psi(e_1)},$$
the vector $e_1$ satisfies the generalized eigenvalue equation
$$\Phi(e_1, y) = \lambda_1 \Psi(e_1, y) \quad \text{for all } y \in E.$$
