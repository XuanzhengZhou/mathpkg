This theorem provides a constructive simultaneous diagonalization of two symmetric bilinear functions $\Phi$ and $\Psi$ on a real vector space, assuming $\Psi$ is non-degenerate. The proof proceeds by induction on the dimension: a generalized eigenvector $e_1$ is constructed as the minimizer of the ratio $F(x) = \Phi(x)/\Psi(x)$ on the unit sphere, yielding $\Phi(e_1, y) = \lambda_1 \Psi(e_1, y)$ for all $y$. The orthogonal complement $E_1 = \{z : \Psi(e_1, z) = 0\}$ then inherits non-degeneracy of $\Psi$, allowing the construction to be iterated. After $n$ steps, one obtains a basis in which $\Psi(e_v, e_\mu) = \varepsilon_v \delta_{v\mu}$ (with $\varepsilon_v = \pm 1$ reflecting the signature) and $\Phi(e_v, e_\mu) = \lambda_v \varepsilon_v \delta_{v\mu}$.
