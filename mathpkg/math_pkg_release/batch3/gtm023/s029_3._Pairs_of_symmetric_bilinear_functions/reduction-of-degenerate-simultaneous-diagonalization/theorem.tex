Let $E$ be an $n$-dimensional real vector space, $\Phi$ and $\Psi$ symmetric bilinear functions on $E$ with $\Psi \neq 0$. Let $\varphi, \psi : E \to E^*$ be the associated linear maps defined by
$$\Phi(x, y) = \langle \varphi x, y \rangle, \qquad \Psi(x, y) = \langle \psi x, y \rangle.$$
Let $r = \operatorname{rank}(\Psi) = \operatorname{rank}(\psi)$ and let $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ be a basis of $E$ with $x_{r+1}, \ldots, x_n$ forming a basis of $\ker \psi$. Define
$$f(\lambda) = \Delta(\varphi x_1 + \lambda \psi x_1, \ldots, \varphi x_n + \lambda \psi x_n)$$
for a non-zero determinant function $\Delta$ on $E$. Then $f$ is a polynomial in $\lambda$ of degree $r$. In particular, if $r \geq 1$, there exists $\lambda_0 \in \mathbb{R}$ such that $f(\lambda_0) \neq 0$.
