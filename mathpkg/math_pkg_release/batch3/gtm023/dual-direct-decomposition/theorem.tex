**Proposition V:** Suppose $E = E_1 \oplus E_2$ is a decomposition of $E$ as a direct sum of subspaces. Then
$$L(E) = E_1^\perp \oplus E_2^\perp$$
and the pairs $E_1^\perp, E_2$ and $E_2^\perp, E_1$ are dual with respect to the induced scalar products. Moreover,
$$L(E) \cong L(E_1) \oplus L(E_2).$$

**Proposition VI:** Let $E_1 \subset E$ be any subspace, and let $E_1^* \subset L(E)$ be a subspace dual to $E_1$ such that $(E_1^*)^{\perp\perp} = E_1^*$. Then
$$E = E_1 \oplus (E_1^*)^\perp \quad \text{and} \quad L(E) = E_1^* \oplus E_1^\perp.$$
