A linear mapping $\theta: A \to A$ of an algebra into itself is called a derivation if
$$\theta(xy) = \theta x \cdot y + x \cdot \theta y \quad x, y \in A.$$

If $A$ has a unit element $e$, then $\theta e = 0$. A derivation is completely determined by its action on a system of generators.

For every derivation $\theta$, the Leibniz formula holds:
$$\theta^n(xy) = \sum_{r=0}^{n} \binom{n}{r} \theta^r x \cdot \theta^{n-r} y.$$

The commutator $[\theta_1, \theta_2] = \theta_1\theta_2 - \theta_2\theta_1$ of two derivations is again a derivation, making the space of derivations a Lie algebra.
