An involution on an algebra is an endomorphism $\omega$ satisfying $\omega^2 = \iota$, analogous to an involution on a vector space. The identity map is always an involution. Involutions play a key role in defining antiderivations, which satisfy a twisted Leibniz rule involving the involution.
