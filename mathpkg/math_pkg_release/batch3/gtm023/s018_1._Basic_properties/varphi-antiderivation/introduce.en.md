A $\varphi$-antiderivation combines the notions of $\varphi$-derivation and antiderivation, twisting the Leibniz rule with both a homomorphism $\varphi$ and an involution $\omega_A$. When the homomorphism intertwines the involutions of $A$ and $B$, the definition simplifies to a form directly analogous to an ordinary antiderivation.
