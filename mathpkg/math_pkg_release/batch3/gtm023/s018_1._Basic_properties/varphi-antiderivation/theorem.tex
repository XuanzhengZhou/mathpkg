Let $A$ and $B$ be algebras, $\varphi: A \to B$ a homomorphism, and $\omega_A$ an involution in $A$. A linear mapping $\Omega: A \to B$ is called a \textbf{$\varphi$-antiderivation} with respect to $\omega_A$ if
$$
\Omega(xy) = \Omega x \cdot \varphi y + \varphi \omega_A x \cdot \Omega y.
$$
If $\omega_B$ is an involution in $B$ such that $\varphi \omega_A = \omega_B \varphi$, then this can be rewritten as
$$
\Omega(xy) = \Omega x \cdot \varphi y + \omega_B \varphi x \cdot \Omega y.
$$
