Assume $\bar{x} \in A / \operatorname{rad} A$ satisfies $\bar{x}^k = 0$ for some $k$. Then $x^k \in \operatorname{rad} A$, so $x^k$ is nilpotent. Hence there exists $l$ such that $(x^k)^l = x^{kl} = 0$, which means $x \in \operatorname{rad} A$. Therefore $\bar{x} = 0$.
