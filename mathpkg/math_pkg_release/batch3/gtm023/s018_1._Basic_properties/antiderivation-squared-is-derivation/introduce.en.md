When an antiderivation $\Omega$ anticommutes with its defining involution $\omega$, the square $\Omega^2$ is an ordinary derivation. More generally, if $\Omega_1, \Omega_2$ both satisfy this anticommutation condition, then $\Omega_1\Omega_2 + \Omega_2\Omega_1$ is a derivation.
