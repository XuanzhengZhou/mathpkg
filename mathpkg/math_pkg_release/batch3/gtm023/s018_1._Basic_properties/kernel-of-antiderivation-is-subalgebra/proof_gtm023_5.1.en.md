For any $x, y \in \ker \Omega$, we have $\Omega x = 0$ and $\Omega y = 0$. Then

$$
\Omega(xy) = \Omega x \cdot y + \omega x \cdot \Omega y = 0 \cdot y + \omega x \cdot 0 = 0,
$$

so $xy \in \ker \Omega$. Thus $\ker \Omega$ is a subalgebra. If $A$ has a unit $e$, then $\Omega e = \Omega(e \cdot e) = \Omega e \cdot e + \omega e \cdot \Omega e = \Omega e + \Omega e$, whence $\Omega e = 0$.
