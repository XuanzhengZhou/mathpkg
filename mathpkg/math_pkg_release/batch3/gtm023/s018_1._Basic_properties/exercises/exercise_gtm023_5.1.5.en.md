Let $C$ be the algebra of continuous functions on $\mathbb{R}$ and $C^1$ the algebra of continuously differentiable functions.

a) Prove that $d: C^1 \to C$ given by $f \mapsto f'$ is an $i$-derivation where $i: C^1 \to C$ denotes the canonical injection.

b) Show that $d$ is surjective and construct a right inverse for $d$.

c) Prove that $d$ cannot be extended to a derivation in the algebra $C$.
