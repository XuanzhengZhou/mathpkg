Suppose that $\theta$ is a derivation in an associative commutative algebra $A$ with identity $e$ and assume that $x \in A$ is invertible; i.e., there exists an element $x^{-1}$ such that $x x^{-1} = x^{-1} x = e$. Prove that $x^p$ ($p \geq 1$) is invertible and that $(x^p)^{-1} = (x^{-1})^p$. Denoting the inverse of $x^p$ by $x^{-p}$, show that for every derivation $\theta$

$$
\theta(x^{-p}) = -p x^{-p-1} \theta(x).
$$
