A complex number $z$ is called algebraic if it satisfies an equation of the form

$$
\sum_{v=1}^{n} \alpha_v z^v = 0 \quad (\alpha_v \text{ rational})
$$

where not all the coefficients $\alpha_v$ are zero. Prove that the algebraic numbers form a subfield $A$ of $\mathbb{C}$ and that $A$ has infinite dimension over the rationals. Prove that there are no non-trivial derivations in $A$.
