A $\varphi$-derivation generalizes the notion of a derivation by twisting the Leibniz rule with a fixed algebra homomorphism $\varphi$. When $\varphi$ is the identity map, one recovers ordinary derivations. An example arises from the algebra of smooth functions where differentiation composed with an injection $C^1 \hookrightarrow C^0$ yields a $\varphi$-derivation.
