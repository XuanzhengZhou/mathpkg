An antiderivation generalizes a derivation by twisting one factor of the Leibniz rule with a fixed involution $\omega$. When $\omega$ is the identity map, an antiderivation reduces to an ordinary derivation. Antiderivations are determined by their action on a system of generators, and their kernels are subalgebras.
