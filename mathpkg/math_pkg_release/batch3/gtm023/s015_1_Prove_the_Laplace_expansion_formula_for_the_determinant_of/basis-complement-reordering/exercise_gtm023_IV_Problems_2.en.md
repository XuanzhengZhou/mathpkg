Let $a_1, \dots, a_n$ and $b_1, \dots, b_n$ be two bases of a vector space $E$ and let $p$ ($1 \leq p \leq n-1$) be given. Show that the vectors $b_v$ can be reordered such that

(i) $a_1, \dots, a_p, b_{v_{p+1}}, \dots, b_{v_n}$ is a basis of $E$,

(ii) $b_{v_1}, \dots, b_{v_p}, a_{p+1}, \dots, a_n$ is also a basis of $E$.
