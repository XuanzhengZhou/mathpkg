The Vandermonde determinant is the determinant of the $n \times n$ matrix whose $i$-th row consists of the $(i-1)$-th powers of $n$ given scalars. Its value is the product of all differences $(\lambda_i - \lambda_j)$ for $i > j$. This elegant formula appears throughout mathematics, with applications to polynomial interpolation (showing that a degree $\leq n-1$ polynomial is determined by $n$ distinct values), the theory of symmetric functions, and the proof that the elementary symmetric polynomials are algebraically independent. The determinant is non-zero precisely when all $\lambda_i$ are distinct.
