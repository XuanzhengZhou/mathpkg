The Laplace expansion formula generalizes cofactor expansion of a determinant along a single row or column to expansion along an arbitrary set of $p$ rows. For a fixed integer $p$ with $1 \leq p \leq n-1$, the determinant of an $n \times n$ matrix is expressed as a signed sum of products of $p \times p$ minors from the first $p$ rows and their complementary $(n-p) \times (n-p)$ minors from the remaining rows. The sign of each term depends on the column indices selected in the $p \times p$ minor. This formula is fundamental in the theory of determinants and is named after Pierre-Simon Laplace.
