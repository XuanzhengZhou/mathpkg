The matrix representing a linear mapping depends on the chosen bases of the domain and codomain. Under a change of basis in $E$ (via matrix $A$) and in $F$ (via matrix $B$), the new matrix is obtained by multiplying the original matrix on the left by the inverse of $B$ and on the right by $A$. This transformation rule is fundamental for understanding how linear operators are represented in different coordinate systems.
