Let $x_v$ and $\bar{x}_v$ ($v = 1 \ldots n$) be two bases of a vector space $E$, with the basis-transformation matrices $(\alpha_v^\mu)$ and $(\check{\alpha}_v^\mu)$ defined by

$$\bar{x}_v = \sum_{\mu} \alpha_v^\mu x_\mu, \qquad x_v = \sum_{\mu} \check{\alpha}_v^\mu \bar{x}_\mu.$$

Then the two matrices are inverse to each other:

$$\sum_{\mu} \alpha_v^\mu \check{\alpha}_\mu^\lambda = \delta_v^\lambda, \qquad \sum_{\mu} \check{\alpha}_v^\mu \alpha_\mu^\lambda = \delta_v^\lambda.$$
