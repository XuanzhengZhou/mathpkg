When a basis of a vector space is transformed by a matrix, the corresponding dual basis transforms by the inverse matrix. This contravariant behavior is a consequence of the duality pairing $\langle x^{*\sigma}, x_v \rangle = \delta_v^\sigma$. Substituting the basis transformation relations into the pairing yields the identity $\beta_v^\varrho = \check{\alpha}_v^\varrho$.
