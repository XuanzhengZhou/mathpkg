Consider two bases $x_v$ and $\bar{x}_v$ ($v = 1 \ldots n$) of the vector space $E$. Then every vector $\bar{x}_v$ can be written as

$$\bar{x}_v = \sum_{\mu} \alpha_v^\mu x_\mu.$$

Similarly,

$$x_v = \sum_{\mu} \check{\alpha}_v^\mu \bar{x}_\mu.$$

The two $n \times n$-matrices $(\alpha_v^\mu)$ and $(\check{\alpha}_v^\mu)$ defined by these relations are called the \text{change-of-basis matrices} (or basis-transformation matrices) connecting the two bases.
