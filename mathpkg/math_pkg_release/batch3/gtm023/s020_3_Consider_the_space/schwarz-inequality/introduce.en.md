The Schwarz inequality (also known as the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality) is one of the most fundamental inequalities in inner product spaces. It bounds the absolute value of the inner product of two vectors by the product of their norms. The inequality is proved by considering the nonnegative quadratic expression $|x + \lambda y|^2$ and observing that its discriminant must be non-positive. The equality case characterizes exactly when the two vectors are linearly dependent.
