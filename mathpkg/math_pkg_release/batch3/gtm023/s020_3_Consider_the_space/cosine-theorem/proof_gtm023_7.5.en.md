Expand the squared norm of the difference:

$$|x - y|^2 = (x - y, x - y) = (x, x) - 2(x, y) + (y, y) = |x|^2 - 2(x, y) + |y|^2.$$

Using the definition of the angle $\omega$ between $x$ and $y$, namely $\cos \omega = (x, y) / (|x| |y|)$, we have $(x, y) = |x| |y| \cos \omega$. Substituting this into the expansion yields

$$|x - y|^2 = |x|^2 + |y|^2 - 2|x| |y| \cos \omega.$$
