The cosine theorem (also known as the law of cosines) generalizes the Pythagorean theorem to arbitrary vectors by relating the squared distance $|x - y|^2$ to the norms and the cosine of the angle between them. It follows directly from expanding $|x - y|^2 = |x|^2 - 2(x, y) + |y|^2$ and substituting the definition of the cosine of the angle: $\cos \omega = (x, y) / (|x| |y|)$.
