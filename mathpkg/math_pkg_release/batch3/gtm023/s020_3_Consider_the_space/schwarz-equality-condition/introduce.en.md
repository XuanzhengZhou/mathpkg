The equality case of the Schwarz inequality characterizes when the inner product attains its maximal possible absolute value relative to the norms of the vectors. This occurs precisely when the two vectors are linearly dependent, meaning one is a scalar multiple of the other. The proof follows from analyzing when the discriminant of the quadratic expression $|x + \lambda y|^2$ vanishes, which forces the quadratic to have a real root and thus $x + \lambda_0 y = 0$ for some $\lambda_0$.
