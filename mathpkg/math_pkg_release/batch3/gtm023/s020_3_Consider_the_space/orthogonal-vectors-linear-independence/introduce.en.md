In an inner product space, pairwise orthogonality implies linear independence, provided none of the vectors is the zero vector. This elementary but fundamental fact follows directly from the definiteness of the inner product: if a linear combination of orthogonal vectors equals zero, taking the inner product with each vector in turn forces every coefficient to vanish. This property is essential for constructing orthogonal bases and underlies the Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization process.
