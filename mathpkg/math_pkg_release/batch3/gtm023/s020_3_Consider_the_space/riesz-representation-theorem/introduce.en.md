The Riesz representation theorem establishes a fundamental duality in finite-dimensional inner product spaces: every linear functional can be represented uniquely as taking the inner product with a fixed vector. This provides a canonical isomorphism between the inner product space $E$ and its dual space $L(E)$ of linear functionals. The theorem follows from the observation that the inner product makes $E$ self-dual, combined with the general theory of dual pairs (Corollary II to Proposition I, sec. 2.33 in Greub).
