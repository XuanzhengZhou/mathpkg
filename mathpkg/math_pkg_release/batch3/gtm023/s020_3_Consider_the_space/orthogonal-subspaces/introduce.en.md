Orthogonality of subspaces generalizes the concept of orthogonal vectors to entire linear subspaces. Two subspaces are orthogonal if every vector from the first subspace is orthogonal to every vector from the second subspace with respect to the given inner product. This relation is denoted by the symbol $\perp$ and plays a central role in the decomposition theory of inner product spaces.
