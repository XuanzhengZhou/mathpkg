The Pythagorean theorem in inner product spaces is the natural generalization of the classical geometric theorem. It follows as an immediate corollary of the cosine theorem: when two vectors are orthogonal, $\cos \omega = 0$, so the cross term $-2|x||y|\cos \omega$ vanishes, leaving the sum of squares. This result is fundamental to the geometry of inner product spaces and is used extensively in orthogonal decomposition and projection theory.
