If $x$ and $y$ are orthogonal, then $(x, y) = 0$. The result follows directly from the cosine theorem (or from the expansion of $|x - y|^2$):

$$|x - y|^2 = |x|^2 - 2(x, y) + |y|^2 = |x|^2 + |y|^2.$$

Equivalently, it follows from the cosine theorem

$$|x - y|^2 = |x|^2 + |y|^2 - 2|x| |y| \cos \omega$$

by noting that orthogonality implies $\omega = \pi/2$, so $\cos \omega = 0$.
