Equality in the triangle inequality $|x + y| = |x| + |y|$ occurs precisely when the two vectors are positively collinear, i.e. when one is a positive scalar multiple of the other. Geometrically, this means the vectors point in the same direction. The proof follows by tracing equality through the chain of inequalities used to prove the triangle inequality: equality forces equality in the Schwarz inequality and then the sign of the scalar is determined to be positive.
