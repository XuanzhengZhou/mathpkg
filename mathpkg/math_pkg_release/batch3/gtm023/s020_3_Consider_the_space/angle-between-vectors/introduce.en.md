The angle between two non-zero vectors in a real inner product space is defined via the cosine formula $\cos \omega = (x, y) / (|x| |y|)$. The Schwarz inequality guarantees that the quotient lies in the interval $[-1, 1]$, so the angle $\omega \in [0, \pi]$ is well-defined and unique. This definition generalizes the geometric notion of angle from Euclidean space to any real inner product space. Orthogonal vectors correspond to a right angle ($\omega = \pi/2$), while linearly dependent vectors correspond to angles of $0$ (same direction) or $\pi$ (opposite directions).
