The triangle inequality is a fundamental property of the norm induced by an inner product. It states that the length of the sum of two vectors is at most the sum of their lengths, which makes the inner product space a metric space via $d(x, y) = |x - y|$. The proof follows from the Schwarz inequality by expanding $|x + y|^2$ and bounding the cross term.
