A linear transformation $\varphi: E \to E$ of a normed space is called bounded if $\|\varphi x\| \leq M \|x\|$ for some $M$. It is bounded iff it is continuous.

The operator norm is $\|\varphi\| = \sup_{\|x\| = 1} \|\varphi x\|$. Under this norm, $B(E; E)$ (bounded transformations) becomes a normed space. In finite dimensions, $B(E; E) = L(E; E)$.
