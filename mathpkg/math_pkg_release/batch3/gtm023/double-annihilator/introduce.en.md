The double annihilator theorem states that taking the annihilator twice returns the original subspace when working with the space $L(E)$ of all linear functions. This is a crucial duality result: it shows that the correspondence $E_1 \mapsto E_1^\perp$ is a bijection between subspaces of $E$ and certain subspaces of $L(E)$. The corollary establishes duality between $E_1^\perp$ and $E/E_1$.
