**Proposition IV:** If $E_1 \subset E$ is any subspace and $L(E)$ is the space of linear functions in $E$, then
$$E_1^{\perp\perp} = E_1.$$

**Corollary:** The bilinear function
$$\langle x^*, \bar{x} \rangle = \langle x^*, x \rangle \quad x^* \in E_1^\perp, \bar{x} \in E/E_1$$
defines a scalar product in the pair $E_1^\perp, E/E_1$.
