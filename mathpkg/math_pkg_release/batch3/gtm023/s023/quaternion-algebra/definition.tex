Let $E$ be an oriented Euclidean space of dimension $4$. Choose a unit vector $e$, and let $E_1$ denote the orthogonal complement of $e$. Let $E_1$ have the orientation induced by the orientation of $E$ and by $e$. Every vector $x \in E$ can be uniquely decomposed as
$$x = \lambda e + x_1, \quad \lambda \in \mathbb{R}, \; x_1 \in E_1.$$

Define a bilinear map $E \times E \to E$ by
$$e \cdot x = x, \quad x \cdot e = x, \quad x \in E,$$
and for $x, y \in E_1$,
$$x \cdot y = -(x, y) e + x \times y,$$
where $\times$ denotes the cross product in the oriented $3$-dimensional Euclidean space $E_1$.

The algebra $(E, \cdot)$ is called the \textbf{algebra of quaternions} and is denoted by $\mathbb{H}$. The element $e$ is the unit element of $\mathbb{H}$. Elements of $E$ are called \textbf{quaternions}; elements of $E_1$ are called \textbf{pure quaternions}. The \textbf{conjugate} of a quaternion $x = \lambda e + x_1$ is $\bar{x} = \lambda e - x_1$.

For $x \neq 0$, the inverse is $x^{-1} = \frac{1}{|x|^2} \bar{x}$, satisfying $x \cdot x^{-1} = x^{-1} \cdot x = e$.
