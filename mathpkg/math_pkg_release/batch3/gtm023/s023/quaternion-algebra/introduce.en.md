The quaternion algebra $\mathbb{H}$ is constructed as a bilinear multiplication on an oriented 4-dimensional Euclidean space $E$. By fixing a unit vector $e$ and decomposing $E = \mathbb{R}e \oplus E_1$, the multiplication combines the inner product and cross product on the 3-dimensional subspace $E_1$ of pure quaternions. Quaternions form a non-commutative division algebra: every non-zero quaternion has a multiplicative inverse given by scaling its conjugate. The conjugation operation satisfies $\overline{x \cdot y} = \bar{y} \cdot \bar{x}$ and $x \cdot \bar{x} = |x|^2 e$.
