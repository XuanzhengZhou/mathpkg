Frobenius's theorem (Theorem 7.26) classifies all finite-dimensional associative division algebras over the real numbers: the only possibilities are $\mathbb{R}$ itself (dimension 1), the complex numbers $\mathbb{C}$ (dimension 2), and the quaternions $\mathbb{H}$ (dimension 4). The proof uses Lemma II to decompose the algebra as $A = \mathbb{R}e \oplus F$, shows that $F$ carries a Euclidean inner product derived from the anticommutator, and then demonstrates that $\dim F$ must be 0, 1, or 3 via the construction of an orthonormal basis and the quaternion multiplication law.
