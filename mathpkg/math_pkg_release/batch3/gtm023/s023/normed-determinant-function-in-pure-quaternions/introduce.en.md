In the quaternion algebra $\mathbb{H}$, the pure quaternion subspace $E_1$ carries a natural trilinear form $\Delta(x_1, x_2, x_3) = (x_1 \cdot x_2, x_3)$ obtained from quaternion multiplication followed by the Euclidean inner product. Proposition I establishes that this 3-linear function is a normed determinant function representing the orientation of $E_1$. The proof verifies skew-symmetry and shows that $\Delta$ coincides with the scalar triple product $(x_1 \times x_2, x_3)$, confirming its role as a volume form.
