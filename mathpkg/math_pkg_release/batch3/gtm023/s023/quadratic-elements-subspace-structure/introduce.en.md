Lemma II characterizes the subset $F$ of an associative division algebra consisting of elements whose square is a negative scalar multiple of the unit. It establishes that $F$ is a vector subspace, that the algebra decomposes as a direct sum $A = \mathbb{R}e \oplus F$, and that the commutator and anticommutator of elements in $F$ have specific behaviors: the anticommutator lies in the scalar multiples of $e$, while the commutator stays in $F$. This structural result is crucial for the classification of finite-dimensional division algebras.
