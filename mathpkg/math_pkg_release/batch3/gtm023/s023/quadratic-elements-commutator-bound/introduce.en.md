In an associative division algebra over $\mathbb{R}$, every element satisfies a quadratic equation. Lemma I shows that if two elements $x$ and $y$ both satisfy $x^2 = y^2 = -e$, then their anticommutator $xy + yx$ is a scalar multiple of the unit, with coefficient bounded between $-1$ and $1$. The proof uses the linear independence of $e, x, y$ (when $y \neq \pm x$) and compares the quadratic equations satisfied by $x+y$ and $x-y$.
