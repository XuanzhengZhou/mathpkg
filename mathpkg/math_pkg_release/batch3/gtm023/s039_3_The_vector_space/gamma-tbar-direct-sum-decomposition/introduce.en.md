For a linear transformation $t$, the algebra $\Gamma(\bar{t})$ admits a direct sum decomposition into a semisimple subalgebra $A$ and the radical $\operatorname{rad} \Gamma(\bar{t})$. This is a structural result forming part of Theorem V. The subalgebra $A$ is obtained from the semisimple part $\bar{t}_S$ of $\bar{t}$ and is itself a direct sum of irreducible ideals, each isomorphic to a field.
