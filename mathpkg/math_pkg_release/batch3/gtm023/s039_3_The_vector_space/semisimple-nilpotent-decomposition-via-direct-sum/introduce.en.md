For any element $x$ in $\Gamma(\bar{t})$, the component $x_N$ from the $A$-part and $x_R$ from the radical part of the direct sum decomposition $\Gamma(\bar{t}) = A \oplus \operatorname{rad} \Gamma(\bar{t})$ coincide with the unique semisimple and nilpotent parts of $x$ respectively. This corollary elegantly identifies the abstract direct sum decomposition with the intrinsic semisimple-nilpotent (Jordan-Chevalley) decomposition.
