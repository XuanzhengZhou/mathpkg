Let $T$ be a basis of $E$ containing $S$ (such a basis exists by the basis extension theorem, cf. section 1.6). Extend $\varphi_0$ in an arbitrary way to a set map $\psi_0: T \rightarrow F$ (for example, by setting $\psi_0(t) = 0$ for all $t \in T \setminus S$). By Proposition II, $\psi_0$ extends uniquely to a linear mapping $\psi: E \rightarrow F$. Since $\psi$ agrees with $\psi_0$ on $T$, and $\psi_0$ agrees with $\varphi_0$ on $S$, it follows that $\psi$ extends $\varphi_0$.
