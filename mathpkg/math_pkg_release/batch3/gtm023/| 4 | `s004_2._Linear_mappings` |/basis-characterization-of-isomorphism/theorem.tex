Let $\varphi: E \rightarrow F$ be a linear map and let $(x_{\alpha})_{\alpha \in A}$ be a basis of $E$.

If $\varphi$ is a linear isomorphism, then $(\varphi x_{\alpha})_{\alpha \in A}$ is a basis of $F$.

Conversely, if the vectors $y_{\alpha} = \varphi x_{\alpha}$ form a basis of $F$, then $\varphi$ is a linear isomorphism.
