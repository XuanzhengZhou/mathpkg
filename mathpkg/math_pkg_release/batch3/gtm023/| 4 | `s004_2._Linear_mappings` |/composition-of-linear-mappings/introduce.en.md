The composition of two linear mappings $\psi \circ \varphi$ is defined by applying $\varphi$ first and then $\psi$, i.e. $(\psi \circ \varphi)(x) = \psi(\varphi(x))$. A straightforward computation shows that $\psi \circ \varphi$ is again a linear mapping. For a linear transformation $\varphi$ in $E$, repeated composition yields powers $\varphi^k$, with the convention $\varphi^0 = \iota$, the identity transformation.
