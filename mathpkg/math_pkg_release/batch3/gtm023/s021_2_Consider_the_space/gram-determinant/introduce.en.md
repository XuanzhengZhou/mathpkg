The Gram determinant of \(p\) vectors in an inner product space is the determinant of their Gram matrix — the \(p \times p\) matrix of all pairwise inner products \((x_i, x_j)\). It is always non-negative, and it vanishes precisely when the vectors are linearly dependent. In the linearly independent case, the Gram determinant equals the square of the normed determinant function applied to those vectors: \(G(x_1, \ldots, x_p) = \Delta_1(x_1, \ldots, x_p)^2\). For \(p = 2\), this reduces to the Schwarz inequality.
