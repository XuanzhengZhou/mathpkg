Let \(E\) be a finite-dimensional inner product space with inner product \(( \; , \; )\). There exists a canonical linear isomorphism \(\tau : E \to E^*\) defined by

$$\langle \tau x, y \rangle = (x, y) \quad \text{for all } x, y \in E.$$

Using \(\tau\), a positive definite inner product is induced on \(E^*\) by

$$(x^*, y^*) = (\tau^{-1} x^*, \tau^{-1} y^*).$$

Moreover, the dual mapping \(\tau^* : E^{**} \to E^*\) coincides with \(\tau\) (under the natural identification \(E^{**} \cong E\)), so \(\tau\) is self-dual.
