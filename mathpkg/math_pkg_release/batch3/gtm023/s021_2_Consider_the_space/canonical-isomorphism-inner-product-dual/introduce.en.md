In a finite-dimensional inner product space \(E\), the inner product defines a canonical linear isomorphism \(\tau : E \to E^*\) via \(\langle \tau x, y \rangle = (x, y)\). This isomorphism is canonical because it does not depend on any choice of basis. Through \(\tau\), the dual space \(E^*\) inherits a positive definite inner product, becoming an inner product space itself. Remarkably, the dual mapping \(\tau^*\) coincides with \(\tau\), meaning that \(\tau\) is its own dual — a property that distinguishes inner product spaces from general vector spaces.
