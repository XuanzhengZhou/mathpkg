Let \(a \neq 0\) and \(b\) be two given vectors of an oriented 3-space. Prove that the equation \(x \times a = b\) has a solution if and only if \((a, b) = 0\). If this condition is satisfied and \(x_0\) is a particular solution, show that the general solution is \(x_0 + \lambda a\).
