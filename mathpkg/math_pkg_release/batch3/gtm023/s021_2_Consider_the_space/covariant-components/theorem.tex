Let \(E\) be a finite-dimensional inner product space and let \(e_{\nu}\) (\(\nu = 1, \ldots, n\)) be a basis of \(E\) with dual basis \(e^{*\nu}\) of \(E^*\). Let

$$g_{\nu\lambda} = (e_{\nu}, e_{\lambda}), \quad g^{\nu\lambda} = (e^{*\nu}, e^{*\lambda})$$

be the metric matrices. For \(\tau : E \to E^*\) the canonical isomorphism, write

$$\tau e_{\lambda} = \sum_{\nu} \alpha_{\lambda\nu} e^{*\nu}, \quad \text{with} \quad \alpha_{\lambda\mu} = g_{\lambda\mu}.$$

For any \(x = \sum_{\lambda} \xi^{\lambda} e_{\lambda} \in E\), the **covariant components** \(\xi_{\lambda}\) are defined by

$$\tau x = \sum_{\lambda} \xi_{\lambda} e^{*\lambda},$$

and they satisfy

$$\xi_{\lambda} = \sum_{\nu} g_{\lambda\nu} \xi^{\nu}, \quad \xi_{\nu} = (x, e_{\nu}).$$

If the basis is orthonormal, then \(g_{\lambda\nu} = \delta_{\lambda\nu}\), \(\tau e_{\lambda} = e^{*\lambda}\), and \(\xi_{\lambda} = \xi^{\lambda}\).
