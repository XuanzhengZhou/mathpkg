Bessel's inequality states that in an inner product space, the norm of the orthogonal projection of a vector onto a finite-dimensional subspace never exceeds the norm of the original vector. This follows directly from the Pythagorean decomposition \(|x|^2 = |p|^2 + |h|^2\), where \(h\) is the orthogonal component. Equality holds precisely when the vector already lies in the subspace (so the orthogonal component vanishes). Bessel's inequality is a fundamental estimate in the geometry of inner product spaces.
