Consider three distinct vectors \(x \neq 0\), \(y \neq 0\), and \(z \neq 0\). Prove that the equation

$$|x - y| \, |z| = |y - z| \, |x| + |z - x| \, |y|$$

holds if and only if the four points \(x, y, z, 0\) are contained on a circle such that the pairs \(x, y\) and \(z, 0\) separate each other.
