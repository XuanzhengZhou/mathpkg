Let \(E\) be an oriented \(3\)-dimensional inner product space with normed determinant function \(\Delta\) representing the orientation. The **cross product** \(x \times y\) of two vectors \(x, y \in E\) is the uniquely determined vector \(u \in E\) such that for all \(z \in E\),

$$(x \times y, z) = \Delta(x, y, z).$$

The cross product satisfies:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \((x \times y, x) = 0\) and \((x \times y, y) = 0\) (orthogonality to both factors),
\item \(x \times y = -y \times x\) (skew-symmetry),
\item \(x \times y \neq 0\) if and only if \(x\) and \(y\) are linearly independent,
\item \(|x \times y|^2 = |x|^2 |y|^2 - (x, y)^2,\)
\item \(|x \times y| = |x| |y| \sin \theta\) for \(x \neq 0, y \neq 0\), where \(\theta \in [0, \pi]\) is the angle between \(x\) and \(y\).
\end{enumerate}
