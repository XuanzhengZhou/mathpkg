The cross product in an oriented 3-dimensional inner product space is defined via the normed determinant function by the relation \((x \times y, z) = \Delta(x, y, z)\) for all vectors \(z\). This construction yields a bilinear, skew-symmetric product that is orthogonal to both factors. The magnitude of the cross product equals the area of the parallelogram spanned by the two vectors: \(|x \times y| = |x| |y| \sin \theta\). The cross product is non-zero precisely when the two vectors are linearly independent, and it endows the space with the structure of a Lie algebra.
