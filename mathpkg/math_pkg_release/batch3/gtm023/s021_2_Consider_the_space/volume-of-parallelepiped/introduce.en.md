The volume of a \(p\)-dimensional parallelepiped spanned by \(p\) linearly independent vectors in an inner product space is the square root of their Gram determinant. This generalizes the familiar formulas for area of a parallelogram (\(p=2\)) and volume of a parallelepiped (\(p=3\)). The volume can also be expressed using the orthogonal component of any one of the spanning vectors relative to the subspace generated by the others, leading to the interpretation of volume as "base times height."
