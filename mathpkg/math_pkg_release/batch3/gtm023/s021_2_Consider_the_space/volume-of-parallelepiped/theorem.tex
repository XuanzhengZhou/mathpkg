Let \(E\) be an inner product space and let \(a_1, \ldots, a_p\) be \(p\) linearly independent vectors in \(E\). The \(p\)-dimensional volume \(V(a_1, \ldots, a_p)\) of the parallelepiped spanned by the \(a_{\nu}\) is given by

$$V(a_1, \ldots, a_p)^2 = G(a_1, \ldots, a_p).$$

In particular, for a fixed index \(i\),

$$V(a_1, \ldots, a_p) = V(a_1, \ldots, a_{i-1}, h_i, a_{i+1}, \ldots, a_p)$$

where \(h_i\) is orthogonal to all \(a_{\nu}\) (\(\nu \neq i\)).
