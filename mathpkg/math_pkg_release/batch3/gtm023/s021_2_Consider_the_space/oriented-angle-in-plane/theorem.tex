Let \(E\) be a \(2\)-dimensional oriented inner product space with normed determinant function \(\Delta\) representing the given orientation. For non-zero vectors \(x, y \in E\), there exists exactly one real number \(\theta\) modulo \(2\pi\) such that

$$\cos \theta = \frac{(x, y)}{|x| \, |y|}, \quad \sin \theta = \frac{\Delta(x, y)}{|x| \, |y|}.$$

This number \(\theta\) is called the **oriented angle** between \(x\) and \(y\).
