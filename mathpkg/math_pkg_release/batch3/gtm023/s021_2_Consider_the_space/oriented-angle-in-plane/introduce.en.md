In an oriented 2-dimensional inner product space, the normed determinant function allows one to assign a signed angle (an "oriented angle") between any two non-zero vectors. The cosine of the angle is given by the standard inner product formula, while the sine is determined by the normed determinant. Together these define a unique angle \(\theta\) modulo \(2\pi\). This construction generalizes the ordinary geometric angle by incorporating orientation, making it possible to distinguish clockwise from counterclockwise rotation.
