From the defining identity (7.23) of a normed determinant function \(\Delta\),

$$|x|^2 |y|^2 - (x, y)^2 = \Delta(x, y)^2.$$

Dividing by \(|x|^2 |y|^2\) (both non-zero), we obtain

$$\frac{(x, y)^2}{|x|^2 |y|^2} + \frac{\Delta(x, y)^2}{|x|^2 |y|^2} = 1.$$

Setting

$$\cos \theta = \frac{(x, y)}{|x| |y|}, \quad \sin \theta = \frac{\Delta(x, y)}{|x| |y|},$$

we have \(\cos^2 \theta + \sin^2 \theta = 1\). Hence there exists a real number \(\theta\) satisfying both equations, and \(\theta\) is uniquely determined modulo \(2\pi\).
