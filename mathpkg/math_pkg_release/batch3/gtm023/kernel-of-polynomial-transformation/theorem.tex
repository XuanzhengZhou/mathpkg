For a polynomial $f \in \Gamma[t]$, define $K(f) = \ker f(\varphi)$. Then $K(f)$ is a $\varphi$-stable subspace of $E$.

Notable cases: $K(1) = 0$, $K(t) = \ker \varphi$, $K(\mu) = E$ where $\mu$ is the minimum polynomial.

If $g \mid f$, then $K(g) \subset K(f)$.

**Proposition I:** Let $f, g$ be non-zero polynomials and $d = \gcd(f, g)$. Then
$$K(d) = K(f) \cap K(g).$$

This follows from the Bezout identity $d = f_1 f + g_1 g$.
