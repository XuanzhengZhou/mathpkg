A family of vectors $(x_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ is called linearly independent if it is not linearly dependent; i.e., the vectors $x_\alpha$ are linearly independent if and only if the equation
$$\sum_\alpha \lambda^\alpha x_\alpha = 0$$
implies that $\lambda^\alpha = 0$ for each $\alpha \in A$.

Every subfamily of a linearly independent family of vectors is again linearly independent. If $(x_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ is a linearly independent family, then for any two distinct indices $\alpha, \beta \in A$, $x_\alpha \neq x_\beta$, and so the map $\alpha \to x_\alpha$ is injective.
