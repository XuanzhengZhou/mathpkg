Let $S_p$ be the group of permutations of $p$ elements. Define $\Phi: \mathbb{Z}^p \to \mathbb{Z}$ by
$$\Phi(x_1, \ldots, x_p) = \prod_{i < j} (x_i - x_j).$$
For every $\sigma \in S_p$, $\sigma\Phi = \varepsilon_\sigma \cdot \Phi$ where $\varepsilon_\sigma = \pm 1$. Then
$$\varepsilon_{\tau\sigma} = \varepsilon_\tau \cdot \varepsilon_\sigma, \quad \varepsilon_\iota = 1.$$
Thus $\varepsilon: S_p \to \{-1, +1\}$ is a homomorphism. A permutation $\sigma$ is called even (respectively odd) if $\varepsilon_\sigma = 1$ (respectively $\varepsilon_\sigma = -1$). Transpositions are odd.
