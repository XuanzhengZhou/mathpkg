\textbf{Proposition I.} Let $E, F, G$ be three vector spaces and suppose that linear mappings
$$\varphi_1: E \to G, \quad \varphi_2: F \to G, \quad \psi_1: G \to E, \quad \psi_2: G \to F$$
satisfy
$$\psi_1 \circ \varphi_1 = \iota_E, \quad \psi_2 \circ \varphi_2 = \iota_F, \quad \psi_2 \circ \varphi_1 = 0, \quad \psi_1 \circ \varphi_2 = 0$$
and
$$\varphi_1 \circ \psi_1 + \varphi_2 \circ \psi_2 = \iota_G.$$
Then there exists a linear isomorphism $\tau: E \oplus F \xrightarrow{\cong} G$ such that
$$\varphi_1 = \tau \circ i_1, \quad \psi_1 = \pi_1 \circ \tau^{-1}, \quad \varphi_2 = \tau \circ i_2, \quad \psi_2 = \pi_2 \circ \tau^{-1}.$$
