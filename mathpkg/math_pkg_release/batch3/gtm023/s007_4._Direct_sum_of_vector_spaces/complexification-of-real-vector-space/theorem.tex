Let $E$ be a real vector space. The direct sum $E \oplus E$ can be made into a complex vector space by defining scalar multiplication by complex numbers as
$$(\alpha + i\beta)(x, y) = (\alpha x - \beta y, \alpha y + \beta x), \quad \alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{R}.$$
The complex vector space so obtained is called the complexification of $E$ and is denoted by $E_{\mathbb{C}}$.
Every vector $(x, y) \in E_{\mathbb{C}}$ can be written as
$$(x, y) = (x, 0) + i(y, 0)$$
where $i(y, 0) = (0, y)$.
