Let $E$ and $F$ be two vector spaces and consider the set $E \times F$ of all ordered pairs $(x, y)$, $x \in E$, $y \in F$. The set $E \times F$ becomes a vector space under the operations
$$(x_1, y_1) + (x_2, y_2) = (x_1 + x_2, y_1 + y_2)$$
and
$$\lambda(x, y) = (\lambda x, \lambda y).$$
This vector space is called the (external) direct sum of $E$ and $F$ and is denoted by $E \oplus F$.

If $(x_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ and $(y_\beta)_{\beta \in B}$ are bases of $E$ and $F$ respectively then the pairs $(x_\alpha, 0)$ and $(0, y_\beta)$ form a basis of $E \oplus F$. In particular,
$$\dim(E \oplus F) = \dim E + \dim F.$$
