Every external direct sum $E \oplus F$ comes equipped with two canonical injections $i_1, i_2$ that embed $E$ and $F$ as complementary subspaces, and two canonical projections $\pi_1, \pi_2$ that extract the components. These four linear mappings satisfy simple composition identities and provide the universal language for working with direct sums. The injections allow us to identify $E$ and $F$ as subspaces of $E \oplus F$, recovering the internal direct sum perspective.
