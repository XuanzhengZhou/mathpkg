For the external direct sum $E \oplus F$, the canonical injections are the linear mappings
$$i_1: E \to E \oplus F, \quad i_1 x = (x, 0)$$
$$i_2: F \to E \oplus F, \quad i_2 y = (0, y)$$
and the canonical projections are the linear mappings
$$\pi_1: E \oplus F \to E, \quad \pi_1(x, y) = x$$
$$\pi_2: E \oplus F \to F, \quad \pi_2(x, y) = y.$$

These mappings satisfy the relations
$$\pi_1 \circ i_1 = \iota_E, \quad \pi_2 \circ i_2 = \iota_F, \quad \pi_2 \circ i_1 = 0, \quad \pi_1 \circ i_2 = 0$$
and
$$i_1 \circ \pi_1 + i_2 \circ \pi_2 = \iota_{E \oplus F}.$$

The injections $i_1, i_2$ are injective and the projections $\pi_1, \pi_2$ are surjective. Identifying $E$ with $\operatorname{Im} i_1$ and $F$ with $\operatorname{Im} i_2$, the spaces $E$ and $F$ become subspaces of $E \oplus F$, and $E \oplus F$ becomes the internal direct sum of $E$ and $F$.
