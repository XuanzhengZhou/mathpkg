A projection operator $\varphi$ (satisfying $\varphi^2 = \varphi$) decomposes the vector space $E$ as the direct sum of its kernel and image, with $\varphi$ acting as the identity on its image and as zero on its kernel. Conversely, any direct sum decomposition $E = E_1 \oplus E_2$ gives rise to a projection operator $\iota_{E_1} \oplus 0_{E_2}$ projecting onto $E_1$ along $E_2$. This establishes a one-to-one correspondence between projections and direct sum decompositions.
