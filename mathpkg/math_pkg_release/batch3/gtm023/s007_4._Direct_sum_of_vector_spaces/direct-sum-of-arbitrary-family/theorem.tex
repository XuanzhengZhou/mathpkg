Let $(E_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ be an arbitrary family of vector spaces. Consider all mappings
$$x: A \to \bigcup_{\alpha \in A} E_\alpha$$
such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item $x(\alpha) \in E_\alpha$ for all $\alpha \in A$,
\item all but finitely many $x(\alpha)$ are zero.
\end{enumerate}
Denote $x(\alpha)$ by $x_\alpha$. The sum of two such mappings $x$ and $y$ is defined by
$$(x + y)(\alpha) = x_\alpha + y_\alpha$$
and scalar multiplication by
$$(\lambda x)(\alpha) = \lambda x_\alpha.$$
Under these operations, the set of all such mappings becomes a vector space called the (external) direct sum of the family $(E_\alpha)$ and denoted by $\bigoplus_{\alpha \in A} E_\alpha$.

The canonical injection $i_\varrho: E_\varrho \to \bigoplus_{\alpha} E_\alpha$ is defined by
$$i_\varrho x: \alpha \mapsto \begin{cases} 0_\alpha & \alpha \neq \varrho \\ x & \alpha = \varrho \end{cases}, \quad x \in E_\varrho$$
and the canonical projection $\pi_\varrho: \bigoplus_{\alpha} E_\alpha \to E_\varrho$ by
$$\pi_\varrho x = x_\varrho.$$
These satisfy $\pi_\varrho \circ i_\varrho = \iota_{E_\varrho}$ and $\pi_\varrho \circ i_\sigma = 0$ for $\varrho \neq \sigma$.
