The external direct sum $\bigoplus_{\alpha \in A} E_\alpha$ generalizes the direct sum of two spaces to an arbitrary family of vector spaces. An element is a family $(x_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ with $x_\alpha \in E_\alpha$, where all but finitely many components are zero. This finiteness condition distinguishes the direct sum from the direct product. Canonical injections embed each $E_\alpha$ into the sum, and canonical projections extract individual components.
