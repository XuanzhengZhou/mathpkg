Let

$$f = f_1^{k_1} \cdots f_r^{k_r}$$

be the decomposition of $f$ into prime polynomials and let the polynomials $q_i$ be defined by

$$q_i = f_1^{k_1} \cdots \hat{f}_i^{k_i} \cdots f_r^{k_r}.$$

Then

$$\Gamma(\bar{t}) = \sum_{i=1}^{r} I_i$$

where $I_i$ denotes the ideal generated by $\bar{q}_i$. Moreover let

$$\bar{1} = \bar{e}_1 + \cdots + \bar{e}_r, \quad \bar{e}_i \in I_i$$

and

$$\bar{t} = \bar{t}_1 + \cdots + \bar{t}_r, \quad \bar{t}_i \in I_i$$

be the decompositions determined by (12.19). Then $\bar{e}_i$ is an identity in $I_i$ and for every $\bar{q} \in \Gamma(\bar{t})$

$$\bar{q} = \sum_{i=1}^{r} q(\bar{t}_i).$$

Finally, if $I$ is any ideal in $\Gamma(\bar{t})$, then

$$I = \sum_{i=1}^{r} I \cap I_i.$$
