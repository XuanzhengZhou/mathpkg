From Theorem I we have the decomposition $\bar{q} = \sum_{j=1}^{r} q(\bar{t}_j)$ for any $\bar{q} \in \Gamma(\bar{t})$. If $q(\bar{t}_i) = \bar{q}$ and $q(\bar{t}_j) = 0$ for $j \neq i$, then clearly $\bar{q} = q(\bar{t}_i) \in I_i$.

Conversely, if $\bar{q} \in I_i$, then for $j \neq i$ we have $q(\bar{t}_j) \in I_j$, but also $\bar{q} = \sum_k q(\bar{t}_k)$. Since the sum is direct, the component in $I_j$ must vanish, i.e., $q(\bar{t}_j) = 0$ for $j \neq i$, and $\bar{q} = q(\bar{t}_i)$.
