By Theorem III, the decomposition of $\Gamma(\bar{t})$ into irreducible ideals corresponds exactly to the distinct prime factors in the factorization of $f$. If $f$ has only one prime factor, i.e., $f = g^k$ for some irreducible $g$, then there is only one irreducible component, so $\Gamma(\tilde{t})$ is itself irreducible. Conversely, if $\Gamma(\tilde{t})$ is irreducible, then $f$ cannot have two distinct prime factors, because otherwise Theorem III would give a non-trivial decomposition. Hence $f$ must be a power of a single irreducible polynomial.
