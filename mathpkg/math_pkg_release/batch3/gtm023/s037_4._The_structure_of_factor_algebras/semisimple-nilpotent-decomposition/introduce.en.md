This is the Jordan-Chevalley decomposition for elements of a finite-dimensional commutative associative algebra. Given the minimum polynomial $f = \prod f_i^{k_i}$ of $x$, one constructs polynomials $u, \omega$ such that $g^k \mid g(u)$, $u + \omega = t$, and $g \mid \omega$, where $g = \prod f_i$. Projecting onto the factor algebra $\Gamma(x) \cong \Gamma[t]/I_f$, the images $x_S = \bar{u}$ and $x_N = \bar{\omega}$ give the decomposition: $x_S$ is semisimple with minimum polynomial $g$, $x_N$ is nilpotent with $x_N^k = 0$, and $x = x_S + x_N$. The decomposition is unique.
