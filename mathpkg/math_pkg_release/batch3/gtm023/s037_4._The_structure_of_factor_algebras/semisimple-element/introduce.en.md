In a finite-dimensional associative commutative algebra $A$ with unit, an element $a$ is called semisimple if its minimum polynomial $f$ factors as a product of pairwise relatively prime irreducible polynomials. Equivalently, $f$ has no repeated irreducible factors. This generalizes the notion of diagonalizable (or semisimple) linear transformations: $a$ is semisimple if and only if the factor algebra $\Gamma(a) \cong \Gamma[t]/I_f$ decomposes as a direct sum of fields.
