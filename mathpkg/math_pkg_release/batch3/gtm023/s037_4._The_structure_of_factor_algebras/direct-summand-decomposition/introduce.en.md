If an ideal $I$ is a direct summand of the factor algebra $\Gamma(\tilde{t})$ (so that $\Gamma(\tilde{t}) = I \oplus J$ for some ideal $J$), then $I$ must be a direct sum of some subset of the irreducible ideals $I_i$ from the unique decomposition. This follows because in a finite-dimensional algebra any ideal is a direct sum of irreducible ideals, and since $I \cdot J = 0$, any ideal of $I$ (or $J$) is also an ideal of $\Gamma(\tilde{t})$, so the uniqueness from Theorem III forces the conclusion.
