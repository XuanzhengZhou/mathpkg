Let $J$ be an ideal such that $I \oplus J = \Gamma(\tilde{t})$. In a finite-dimensional algebra, any ideal is a direct sum of irreducible ideals. Since $I$ and $J$ are ideals with $I \cdot J = 0$, any ideal in $I$ (respectively $J$) is also an ideal in $\Gamma(\tilde{t})$. By Theorem III, the irreducible decomposition of $\Gamma(\tilde{t})$ is unique, so the irreducible components of $I$ must be among the $I_i$. The result follows.
