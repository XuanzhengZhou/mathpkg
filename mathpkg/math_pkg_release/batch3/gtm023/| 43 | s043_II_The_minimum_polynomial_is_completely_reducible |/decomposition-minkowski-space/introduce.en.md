This classification theorem provides the complete canonical decomposition of a $4$-dimensional Minkowski space under the action of a proper orthochronous Lorentz transformation. There are exactly three mutually exclusive types: Type I (trivial, identity), Type II (a hyperbolic boost combined with a Euclidean reflection, decomposing the space into a pseudo-Euclidean plane and a Euclidean plane), and Type III (a parabolic or shear-like transformation fixing a space-like direction and acting irreducibly on a $3$-dimensional subspace of index $2$). This trichotomy exhausts all possibilities for the geometric structure of Lorentz transformations.
