Suppose $E$ is an $n$-dimensional vector space over a field $\Gamma$. Assume that a symmetric bilinear function $\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle: E \times E \to \Gamma$ is defined such that $\langle x, x \rangle \neq 0$ whenever $x \neq 0$. Prove that $\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle$ is a scalar product and thus $E$ is self-dual.
