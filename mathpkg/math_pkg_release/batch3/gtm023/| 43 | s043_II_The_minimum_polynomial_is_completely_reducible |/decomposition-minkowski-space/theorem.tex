The decomposition of a Minkowski space $E$ with respect to a proper orthochronous Lorentz transformation $\varphi$ has one of the following forms:

\textbf{I.} $E$ is completely reducible. Then $\varphi$ is the identity.

\textbf{II.} $E$ is the direct sum of an invariant Euclidean plane and an invariant pseudo-Euclidean plane. These planes are irreducible except for the case where the induced mappings are $\pm \iota$ (Euclidean plane) or $\iota$ (pseudo-Euclidean plane).

\textbf{III.} $E$ is the direct sum of a space-like $1$-dimensional stable subspace (eigenvalue $1$) and an irreducible subspace of dimension $3$ and index $2$.
