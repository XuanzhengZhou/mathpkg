Assume $k \geq 3$. Then there exists an eigenvector $e$ of $\varphi$ on the light-cone. The orthogonal complement $E_1 = e^\perp$ is a $3$-dimensional subspace of $E$ which contains $e$. The induced inner product on $E_1$ has rank $2$ and index $2$. Let $F$ be a $2$-dimensional subspace of $E_1$ in which the inner product is positive definite. Selecting an orthonormal basis $e_1, e_2$ in $F$, the mapping $\varphi$ satisfies

$$\varphi e_1 = e_1 \cos \omega + e_2 \sin \omega + \alpha_1 e,$$
$$\varphi e_2 = -e_1 \sin \omega + e_2 \cos \omega + \alpha_2 e,$$
$$\varphi e = e.$$

The coefficients $\alpha_1$ and $\alpha_2$ are not both zero.
