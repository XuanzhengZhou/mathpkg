For Case III Lorentz transformations with $k \geq 3$, a distinguished light-like eigenvector $e$ exists on the light-cone. Its orthogonal complement $E_1 = e^\perp$ is a 3-dimensional degenerate subspace. Within $E_1$, one can identify a positive definite plane $F$, and the action of $\varphi$ on the basis $\{e_1, e_2, e\}$ takes a form that combines a Euclidean rotation in $F$ (parameterized by angle $\omega$) with translations along the light-like direction $e$ (governed by coefficients $\alpha_1, \alpha_2$). The non-vanishing of at least one $\alpha_i$ is crucial: if both were zero, $F$ would be invariant and the space would decompose, contradicting $k \geq 3$.
