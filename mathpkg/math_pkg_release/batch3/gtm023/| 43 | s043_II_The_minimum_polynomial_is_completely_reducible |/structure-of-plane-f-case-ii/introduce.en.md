In Case II of the classification of Lorentz transformations, the eigenvectors $e$ and $e'$ corresponding to the reciprocal eigenvalues span an invariant plane $F$. This proposition characterizes the Minkowski inner product restricted to $F$: the quadratic form takes the simple hyperbolic form $(z,z) = 2(e,e')\xi\eta$ in terms of the coordinates relative to the light-vector basis $\{e, e'\}$. The factor $(e,e') \neq 0$ ensures the form is nondegenerate, and the signature analysis shows the induced inner product has index $1$, making $F$ a pseudo-Euclidean (hyperbolic) plane.
