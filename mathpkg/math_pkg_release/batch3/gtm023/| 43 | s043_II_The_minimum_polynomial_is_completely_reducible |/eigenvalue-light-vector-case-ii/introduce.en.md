In the classification of proper orthochronous Lorentz transformations on Minkowski space, Case II is characterized by the condition that the minimum polynomial is completely reducible but not all its roots are equal to 1. This proposition describes the resulting eigenvalue structure: the transformation possesses a pair of reciprocal eigenvalues $\lambda \neq 1$ and $1/\lambda \neq 1$, whose corresponding eigenvectors are necessarily light-vectors (vectors of zero Minkowski norm). The linear independence of these two light-vectors and the non-vanishing of their inner product are key to the subsequent geometric decomposition of the space.
