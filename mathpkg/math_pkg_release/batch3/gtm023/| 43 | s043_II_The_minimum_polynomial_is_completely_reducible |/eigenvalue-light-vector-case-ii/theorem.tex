Let $\varphi$ be a proper orthochronous Lorentz transformation of a Minkowski space $E$ such that the minimum polynomial of $\varphi$ is completely reducible and not all its roots are equal to $1$. Then $\varphi$ has eigenvalues $\lambda \neq 1$ and $\frac{1}{\lambda} \neq 1$ with corresponding eigenvectors $e$ and $e'$ satisfying

$$\varphi e = \lambda e, \quad \varphi e' = \frac{1}{\lambda} e'.$$

The condition $\lambda \neq 1$ implies that $e$ and $e'$ are light-vectors. These vectors are linearly independent, whence $(e, e') \neq 0$.
