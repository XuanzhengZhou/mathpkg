Combining the structural analysis of the invariant plane $F$ (pseudo-Euclidean of index $1$) and its orthogonal complement $F^\perp$ (Euclidean plane with rotation angle $0$ or $\pi$), one obtains the canonical matrix form for a proper orthochronous Lorentz transformation of Case II. By selecting an orthonormal basis adapted to the decomposition $E = F \oplus F^\perp$, the transformation matrix takes a block diagonal form: a hyperbolic rotation (boost) in the $F$-plane parameterized by the rapidity $\theta \neq 0$, and a Euclidean reflection $\varepsilon = \pm 1$ on $F^\perp$. This is the Lorentz boost canonical form.
