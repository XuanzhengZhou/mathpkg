A group is an algebraic structure consisting of a set equipped with an associative binary operation that has an identity element and where every element possesses an inverse. Groups formalize the notion of symmetry and are foundational across all of mathematics. The axioms of associativity, identity, and inverses completely characterize the structure, from which properties like uniqueness of identity and inverses follow as logical consequences.
