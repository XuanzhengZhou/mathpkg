Group homomorphisms are classified by their set-theoretic properties: injective homomorphisms are monomorphisms, surjective ones are epimorphisms, and bijective ones are isomorphisms. An isomorphism between two groups indicates that the groups are structurally identical. The inverse of an isomorphism is also an isomorphism, making "being isomorphic" an equivalence relation on the class of groups.
