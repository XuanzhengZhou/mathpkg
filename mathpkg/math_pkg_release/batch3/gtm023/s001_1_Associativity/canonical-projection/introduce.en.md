The canonical projection maps each element of a commutative group to its coset in the factor group. It is a surjective group homomorphism that captures the process of "modding out" by a subgroup. The canonical projection is the universal object in the category of homomorphisms from $G$ that vanish on $H$, making it central to the first isomorphism theorem.
