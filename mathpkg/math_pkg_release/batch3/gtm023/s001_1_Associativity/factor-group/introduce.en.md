A factor group (or quotient group) $G/H$ is the group whose elements are the cosets of a subgroup $H$ in a commutative group $G$, with addition induced by the group operation on $G$. The well-definedness of this operation follows from the fact that the coset of the sum depends only on the cosets of the summands, not on the chosen representatives. Factor groups are fundamental to the isomorphism theorems of group theory.
