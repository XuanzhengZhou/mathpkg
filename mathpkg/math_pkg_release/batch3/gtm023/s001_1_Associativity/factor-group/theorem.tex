Let $G$ be a commutative group and $H$ a subgroup. The set $G/H$ of cosets of $H$ in $G$ is called the \textbf{factor set} of $G$ by $H$. The sum $\bar{x} + \bar{y}$ is defined by
$$\bar{x} + \bar{y} = \pi(x + y) \quad \text{where} \quad \pi x = \bar{x}, \; \pi y = \bar{y}.$$
This addition satisfies the group axioms. The resulting group $G/H$ is called the \textbf{factor group} (or \textbf{quotient group}) of $G$ with respect to the subgroup $H$. Its unit element is the set $H$.
