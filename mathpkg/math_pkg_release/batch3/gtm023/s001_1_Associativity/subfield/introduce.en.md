A subfield is a subset of a field that is itself a field under the same addition and multiplication operations. Subfields arise naturally in field extensions and Galois theory. For example, $\mathbb{Q}$ is a subfield of $\mathbb{R}$, which is a subfield of $\mathbb{C}$. The study of subfields and field extensions is central to algebraic number theory and algebraic geometry.
