Let $G$ be a commutative group and $H$ a subgroup. Then $H$ determines an equivalence relation in $G$ given by
$$x \sim x' \quad \text{if and only if} \quad x - x' \in H.$$
The corresponding equivalence classes are the sets $\{H + x\}$ and are called the \textbf{cosets} of $H$ in $G$. Every element $x \in G$ is contained in precisely one coset $\bar{x}$.
