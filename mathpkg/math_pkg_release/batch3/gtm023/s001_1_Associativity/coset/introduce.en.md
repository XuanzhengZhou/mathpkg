A coset is an equivalence class of a group under the equivalence relation defined by a subgroup. In additive notation for abelian groups, two elements are equivalent if their difference lies in the subgroup. Cosets partition the group into disjoint subsets of equal size and form the building blocks of quotient groups, making them essential for understanding the structure of groups and their homomorphic images.
