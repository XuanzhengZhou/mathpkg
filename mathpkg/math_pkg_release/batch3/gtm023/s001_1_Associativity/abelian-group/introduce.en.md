An abelian (or commutative) group is a group in which the group operation is commutative. Abelian groups are ubiquitous in mathematics, appearing as additive groups of rings, modules, and vector spaces. The additive notation $(+)$ is commonly used for abelian groups, with $0$ denoting the identity element. The integers $\mathbb{Z}$ under addition form the prototypical example.
