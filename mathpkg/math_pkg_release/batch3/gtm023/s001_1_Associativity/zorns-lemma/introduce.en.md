Zorn's lemma is a fundamental axiom of set theory, equivalent to the Axiom of Choice, that guarantees the existence of maximal elements in partially ordered sets where every chain is bounded above. It is one of the most powerful non-constructive existence principles in mathematics, used extensively in algebra (e.g., existence of bases for arbitrary vector spaces, maximal ideals in rings) and analysis (e.g., Hahn-Banach theorem).
