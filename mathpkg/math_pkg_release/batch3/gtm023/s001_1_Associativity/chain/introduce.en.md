A chain (or linearly ordered set, or totally ordered set) is a partially ordered set in which every pair of elements is comparable. Chains are the "well-behaved" subsets within a partial order and play a critical role in Zorn's lemma, where the hypothesis requires that every chain has an upper bound. Examples include the real numbers with the usual order and any subset thereof.
