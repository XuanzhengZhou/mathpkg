An order-preserving map (or order homomorphism) between partially ordered sets is a function that respects the order relation: if one element precedes another in the domain, their images preserve this relationship in the codomain. Such maps are the natural morphisms in the category of partially ordered sets and appear throughout mathematics, particularly in fixed-point theorems and domain theory.
