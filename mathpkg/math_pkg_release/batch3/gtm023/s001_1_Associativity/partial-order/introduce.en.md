A partial order is a binary relation on a set that is reflexive, antisymmetric, and transitive. Unlike a total order, a partial order may have incomparable elements. Partial orders are fundamental in set theory, lattice theory, and topology, providing the framework for concepts like maximal elements, chains, and upper bounds that are essential to Zorn's lemma and many existence proofs in algebra and analysis.
