The spectral theorem for selfadjoint transformations asserts that every selfadjoint linear transformation on a finite-dimensional inner product space admits an orthonormal basis consisting entirely of eigenvectors. The proof uses the Rayleigh quotient $F(x) = (x, \varphi x)/(x,x)$, which attains its minimum on the compact unit sphere. This minimizer is shown to be an eigenvector, and induction on the orthogonal complement yields the full orthonormal eigenbasis. The eigenvalues are all real.
