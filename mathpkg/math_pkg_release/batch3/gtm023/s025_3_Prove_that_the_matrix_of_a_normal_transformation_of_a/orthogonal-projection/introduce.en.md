An orthogonal projection is a linear operator that is both selfadjoint and idempotent. It projects the space orthogonally onto its image subspace along its kernel. Orthogonal projections play a fundamental role in Hilbert space theory, least-squares approximation, and the spectral decomposition of selfadjoint operators. Every subspace of an inner product space has a unique orthogonal projection onto it.
