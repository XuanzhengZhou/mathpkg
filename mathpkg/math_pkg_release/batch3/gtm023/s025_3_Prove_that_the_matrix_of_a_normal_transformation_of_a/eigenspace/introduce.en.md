The eigenspace $E(\lambda)$ associated to an eigenvalue $\lambda$ of a linear transformation consists of all eigenvectors with eigenvalue $\lambda$, together with the zero vector. For a selfadjoint transformation, eigenspaces corresponding to distinct eigenvalues are orthogonal, and the whole space decomposes as the orthogonal direct sum of the eigenspaces.
