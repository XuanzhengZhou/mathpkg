Let $\varphi: E \rightarrow E$ be a selfadjoint transformation and let $\lambda_v$ ($v = 1, \ldots, r$) be the distinct eigenvalues of $\varphi$. Then the eigenspaces $E(\lambda_v)$ are pairwise orthogonal and their direct sum is the whole space:

$$E = E(\lambda_1) \oplus E(\lambda_2) \oplus \cdots \oplus E(\lambda_r).$$

The characteristic polynomial of $\varphi$ factors as

$$\det(\varphi - \lambda \iota) = (\lambda_1 - \lambda)^{k_1} (\lambda_2 - \lambda)^{k_2} \cdots (\lambda_r - \lambda)^{k_r}$$

where $k_v = \dim E(\lambda_v)$.
