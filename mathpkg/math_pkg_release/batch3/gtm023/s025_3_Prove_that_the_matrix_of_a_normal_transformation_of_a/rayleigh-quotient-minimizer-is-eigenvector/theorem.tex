Let $\varphi: E \rightarrow E$ be a selfadjoint transformation and $F(x) = (x, \varphi x)/(x,x)$ the Rayleigh quotient. If $e_1$ is a unit vector at which $F$ attains its minimum on the unit sphere, then $e_1$ is an eigenvector of $\varphi$:

$$\varphi e_1 = (e_1, \varphi e_1) e_1.$$

The corresponding eigenvalue is $\lambda_1 = F(e_1) = (e_1, \varphi e_1)$.
