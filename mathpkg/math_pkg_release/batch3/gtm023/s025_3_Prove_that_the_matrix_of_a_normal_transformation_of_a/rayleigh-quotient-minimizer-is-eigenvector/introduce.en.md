The minimizer of the Rayleigh quotient on the unit sphere is necessarily an eigenvector of the selfadjoint transformation. This is proved by differentiating the auxiliary function $f(t) = F(e_1 + ty)$ at $t = 0$ and using the selfadjoint property to deduce that $(\varphi e_1 - (e_1, \varphi e_1)e_1, y) = 0$ for all vectors $y$, forcing $\varphi e_1 = \lambda_1 e_1$. This variational argument is the heart of the constructive proof of the spectral theorem.
