For any subspace of an inner product space, there is a unique orthogonal projection whose image is exactly that subspace. The projection is defined by the identity on the subspace and the zero map on its orthogonal complement. This establishes a one-to-one correspondence between subspaces and orthogonal projections, which is fundamental to the geometry of inner product spaces and the theory of Hilbert spaces.
