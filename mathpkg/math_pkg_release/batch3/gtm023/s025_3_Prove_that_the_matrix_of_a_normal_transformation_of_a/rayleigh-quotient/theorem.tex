For a selfadjoint linear transformation $\varphi: E \rightarrow E$, the \textbf{Rayleigh quotient} is the function $F$ defined by

$$F(x) = \frac{(x, \varphi x)}{(x, x)} \quad (x \neq 0).$$

The function $F$ is continuous, homogeneous of degree zero ($F(\lambda x) = F(x)$ for $\lambda \neq 0$), and attains extremal values at the eigenvectors of $\varphi$.
