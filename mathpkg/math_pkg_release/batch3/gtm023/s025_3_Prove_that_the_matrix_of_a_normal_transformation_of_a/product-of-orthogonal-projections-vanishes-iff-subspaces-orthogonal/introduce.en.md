The composition of two orthogonal projections vanishes precisely when their image subspaces are orthogonal. If the subspaces are orthogonal, projecting onto the first subspace lands in the orthogonal complement of the second, so the second projection annihilates the result. Conversely, if the composition is zero, every vector in the first subspace must be orthogonal to the second subspace.
