A normal transformation that is also idempotent must necessarily be an orthogonal projection. Although an orthogonal projection is defined by the combination of selfadjointness and idempotence, the condition $\varphi^2 = \varphi$ alone, together with normality ($\varphi \tilde{\varphi} = \tilde{\varphi} \varphi$), forces selfadjointness. This provides an alternative characterization of orthogonal projections among normal operators.
