An orthogonal projection decomposes the inner product space into an orthogonal direct sum of its kernel and image. The projection acts as the identity on its image and annihilates its kernel. This decomposition is a special case of the general fact that for any projection, $E = \ker \pi \oplus \operatorname{Im} \pi$; the additional selfadjointness ensures the two summands are orthogonal.
