A selfadjoint transformation $\varphi$ is called positive if $(x, \varphi x) \geq 0$ for every $x \in E$. Given a positive selfadjoint transformation $\varphi$, prove that there exists exactly one positive selfadjoint transformation $\psi$ such that $\psi^2 = \varphi$.
