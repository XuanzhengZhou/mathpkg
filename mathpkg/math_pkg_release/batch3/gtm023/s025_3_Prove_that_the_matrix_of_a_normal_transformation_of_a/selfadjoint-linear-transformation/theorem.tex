A linear transformation $\varphi: E \rightarrow E$ of an inner product space is called \textbf{selfadjoint} if $\tilde{\varphi} = \varphi$, or equivalently

$$(\varphi x, y) = (x, \varphi y) \quad \text{for all } x, y \in E.$$

The matrix of a selfadjoint transformation relative to an orthonormal basis is symmetric.
