A linear transformation $\varphi$ on an inner product space $E$ is selfadjoint when it equals its own adjoint $\tilde{\varphi}$. Equivalently, the inner product $(\varphi x, y)$ is symmetric in its two arguments. Selfadjoint transformations generalize real symmetric matrices to the setting of inner product spaces, and they possess a rich spectral theory: every selfadjoint transformation on a finite-dimensional space admits an orthonormal basis of eigenvectors with real eigenvalues.
