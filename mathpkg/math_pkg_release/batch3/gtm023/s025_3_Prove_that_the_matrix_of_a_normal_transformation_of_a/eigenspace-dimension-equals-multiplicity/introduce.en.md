An important consequence of the spectral theorem is that for selfadjoint transformations, geometric and algebraic multiplicities coincide. The dimension of each eigenspace equals the multiplicity of the corresponding eigenvalue in the characteristic polynomial. This means selfadjoint transformations are always diagonalizable — there are no generalized eigenvectors needed, in contrast to general linear transformations that may have Jordan blocks.
