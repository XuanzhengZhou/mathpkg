The diagonalization theorem states that every selfadjoint linear transformation on a finite-dimensional inner product space can be represented by a real diagonal matrix when expressed in an orthonormal basis of eigenvectors. The diagonal entries are precisely the eigenvalues. This is the finite-dimensional spectral theorem: selfadjoint operators are orthogonally diagonalizable with real spectrum.
