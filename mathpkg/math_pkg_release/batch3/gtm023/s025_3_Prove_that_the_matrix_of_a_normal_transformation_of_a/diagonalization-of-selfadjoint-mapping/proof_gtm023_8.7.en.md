This follows immediately from the spectral theorem for selfadjoint transformations. By that theorem, there exists an orthonormal basis $e_v$ ($v = 1, \ldots, n$) of eigenvectors of $\varphi$ with $\varphi e_v = \lambda_v e_v$. The matrix of $\varphi$ in this basis is precisely $\operatorname{diag}(\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_n)$, since the $v$-th column of the matrix consists of the coefficients of $\varphi e_v$ expressed in the basis $\{e_\mu\}$, which is $\lambda_v$ in the $v$-th position and $0$ elsewhere.
