If $\lambda$ and $\lambda'$ are distinct eigenvalues of a selfadjoint transformation $\varphi$, then the corresponding eigenspaces $E(\lambda)$ and $E(\lambda')$ are orthogonal:

$$E(\lambda) \perp E(\lambda').$$

In particular, if $\varphi e = \lambda e$ and $\varphi e' = \lambda' e'$ with $\lambda \neq \lambda'$, then $(e, e') = 0$.
