For a selfadjoint transformation, eigenvectors corresponding to distinct eigenvalues are always orthogonal. This follows directly from the symmetry of the inner product: computing $(\varphi e, e')$ in two ways yields $(\lambda - \lambda')(e, e') = 0$, forcing $(e, e') = 0$ when $\lambda \neq \lambda'$. This orthogonality is a crucial property that makes selfadjoint operators particularly well-behaved.
