Let $z \in F^{\perp}$ be any vector. Then for every $y \in F$,
$$(\varphi z, y) = (z, \varphi y).$$
Since $F$ is stable under $\varphi$, we have $\varphi y \in F$. Because $z \in F^{\perp}$, it follows that $(z, \varphi y) = 0$. Hence $(\varphi z, y) = 0$ for all $y \in F$, which means $\varphi z \in F^{\perp}$. Therefore $F^{\perp}$ is stable under $\varphi$.
