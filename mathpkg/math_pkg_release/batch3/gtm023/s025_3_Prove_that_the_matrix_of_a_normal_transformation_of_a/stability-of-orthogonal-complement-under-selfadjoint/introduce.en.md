For a selfadjoint transformation $\varphi$, if a subspace $F$ is invariant under $\varphi$, then its orthogonal complement $F^\perp$ is also invariant. This follows directly from the symmetry of the inner product: for $z \in F^\perp$ and any $y \in F$, one has $(\varphi z, y) = (z, \varphi y) = 0$, so $\varphi z$ remains orthogonal to $F$. This property is fundamental to the inductive construction of an orthonormal eigenbasis for selfadjoint transformations.
