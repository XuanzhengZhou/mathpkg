In an inner product space, every bilinear function $\Phi$ corresponds to a unique linear transformation $\varphi$ via the relation $\Phi(x, y) = (\varphi x, y)$. This correspondence allows the notions of eigenvectors and eigenvalues to be extended from linear transformations to bilinear functions. For symmetric bilinear functions, the corresponding transformation is selfadjoint, and the spectral theorem yields a diagonalization result.
