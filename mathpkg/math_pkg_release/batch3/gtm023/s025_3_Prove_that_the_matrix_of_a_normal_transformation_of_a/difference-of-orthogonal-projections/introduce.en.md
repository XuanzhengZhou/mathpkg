The difference of two orthogonal projections is a projection exactly when the image of the subtrahend is contained in the image of the minuend. Geometrically, subtracting a projection onto a smaller subspace from a projection onto a larger subspace yields the projection onto the orthogonal complement of the smaller subspace within the larger one. This is used in the spectral decomposition to isolate individual eigenspaces.
