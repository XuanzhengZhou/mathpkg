Let $\pi_1: E \rightarrow E_1$ and $\pi_2: E \rightarrow E_2$ be orthogonal projections. The difference $\pi_1 - \pi_2$ is an orthogonal projection if and only if $E_2 \subset E_1$. In this case, $\pi_1 - \pi_2$ is the projection onto the orthogonal complement of $E_2$ relative to $E_1$:

$$\operatorname{Im}(\pi_1 - \pi_2) = E_1 \cap E_2^\perp.$$
