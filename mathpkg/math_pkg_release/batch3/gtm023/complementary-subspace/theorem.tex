**Proposition I:** If $E_1$ is a subspace of $E$, then there exists a second subspace $E_2$ such that
$$E = E_1 \oplus E_2.$$

$E_2$ is called a complementary subspace for $E_1$ in $E$.

**Corollary I:** Let $E_1$ be a subspace of $E$ and $\varphi_1: E_1 \to F$ a linear mapping. Then $\varphi_1$ may be extended (in several ways) to a linear map $\varphi: E \to F$.
