If $\Phi$ is a bilinear function in $E \times F$ and $E_1 \subset E$, $F_1 \subset F$ are subspaces, then $\Phi$ induces a bilinear function $\Phi_1$ in $E_1 \times F_1$ defined by
$$\Phi_1(x, y) = \Phi(x, y) \quad x \in E_1, \; y \in F_1.$$
$\Phi_1$ is called the restriction of $\Phi$ to $E_1 \times F_1$.
