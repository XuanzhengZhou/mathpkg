Every bilinear function $\Phi_1$ in $E_1 \times F_1$ may be extended (in several ways) to a bilinear function in $E \times F$.

In fact, let $\varrho: E \rightarrow E_1$, $\sigma: F \rightarrow F_1$ be surjective linear mappings such that $\varrho$ and $\sigma$ reduce to the identity in $E_1$ and $F_1$ respectively. Define $\Phi$ by
$$\Phi(x, y) = \Phi_1(\varrho x, \sigma y).$$
Then $\Phi$ is a bilinear function in $E \times F$ and for $x_1 \in E_1, y_1 \in F_1$,
$$\Phi(x_1, y_1) = \Phi_1(\varrho x_1, \sigma y_1) = \Phi_1(x_1, y_1).$$
Thus $\Phi$ extends $\Phi_1$.
