If $x \in E_1$ and $y^* \in E^*$ are arbitrary, we have
$$\langle y^*, ix \rangle = \langle y^*, x \rangle = \langle \bar{y}^*, x \rangle = \langle \pi y^*, x \rangle,$$
where $\bar{y}^*$ is the class of $y^*$ in $E^*/E_1^\perp$. The first equality uses that $i$ is the inclusion $E_1 \hookrightarrow E$. The second equality follows from the definition of the induced scalar product on $E^*/E_1^\perp \times E_1$ using any representative. The third equality identifies $\pi y^* = \bar{y}^*$. Since $\langle y^*, ix \rangle = \langle \pi y^*, x \rangle$ holds for all $x$ and $y^*$, we obtain $\pi = i^*$ by the definition of the dual mapping.
