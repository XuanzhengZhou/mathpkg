Let $G$ be a commutative group and $H$ a subgroup. Then $H$ determines an equivalence relation in $G$:
$$x \sim x' \quad \text{if and only if} \quad x - x' \in H.$$
The equivalence classes are the cosets $\{H + x\}$. The set $G/H$ of cosets is the factor set of $G$ by $H$, and the surjective mapping
$$\pi: G \to G/H, \quad \pi x = \bar{x}$$
is the canonical projection. Define
$$\bar{x} + \bar{y} = \pi(x + y), \quad \pi x = \bar{x}, \pi y = \bar{y}.$$
Then $G/H$ becomes a group, called the factor group of $G$ with respect to $H$. Its unit element is $H$.
