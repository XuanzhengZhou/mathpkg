This proposition characterizes when a scalar-vector product vanishes in a vector space: $\lambda x = 0$ if and only if either the scalar $\lambda = 0$ or the vector $x = 0$. The proof uses the distributive laws and the associative law of scalar multiplication, with the forward direction requiring the existence of multiplicative inverses in the coefficient field $\Gamma$.
