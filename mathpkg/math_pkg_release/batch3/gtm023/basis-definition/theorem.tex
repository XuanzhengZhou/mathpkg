A family of vectors $(x_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ in $E$ is called a basis of $E$ if it is simultaneously a system of generators and linearly independent.

Thus $(x_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ is a basis if and only if every vector $x \in E$ can be written in precisely one way as
$$x = \sum_\alpha \xi^\alpha x_\alpha, \quad \xi^\alpha \in \Gamma.$$

The scalars $\xi^\alpha$ are called the components of $x$ with respect to the basis $(x_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$.
