The matrix representation map $M$ provides a linear isomorphism between the algebra of linear transformations $A(E;E)$ and the space of $n \times n$ matrices $M^{n \times n}$. However, because $M(\psi \circ \varphi) = M(\varphi)M(\psi)$ reverses the order of multiplication, $M$ is actually an algebra isomorphism from $A(E;E)$ onto the opposite algebra $(M^{n \times n})^{\text{opp}}$, not onto $M^{n \times n}$ itself.
