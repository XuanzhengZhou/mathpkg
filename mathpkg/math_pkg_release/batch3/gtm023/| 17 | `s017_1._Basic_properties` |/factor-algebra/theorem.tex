Assume $B$ is an ideal in an algebra $A$. Then define the multiplication in $A/B$ by

$$\bar{x}\bar{y} = \pi(xy) \quad \bar{x}, \bar{y} \in A/B$$

where $x$ and $y$ are any representatives of $\bar{x}$ and $\bar{y}$ respectively. This product does not depend on the choice of representatives.

The multiplication in $A/B$ satisfies $(M_1)$ and $(M_2)$, and the canonical projection $\pi: A \to A/B$ is a homomorphism:

$$\pi(xy) = \pi x \cdot \pi y.$$

The vector space $A/B$ together with this multiplication is called the factor algebra of $A$ with respect to the ideal $B$. If $A$ is associative (commutative) then so is $A/B$. If $A$ has a unit element $e$ then $\bar{e} = \pi e$ is the unit element of $A/B$.
