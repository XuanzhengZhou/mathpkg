An algebra homomorphism is a linear mapping between algebras that preserves the multiplicative structure. The condition $\varphi(xy) = \varphi x \cdot \varphi y$ ensures compatibility with the algebra product. Special types of homomorphisms are distinguished by their set-theoretic properties: monomorphisms are injective, epimorphisms are surjective, isomorphisms are bijective, and endomorphisms map an algebra to itself.
