The First Isomorphism Theorem for algebras states that for any algebra homomorphism $\varphi: A \to B$, the kernel $\ker \varphi$ is an ideal in $A$, and the induced map $\bar{\varphi}: A/\ker \varphi \to \operatorname{Im} \varphi$ is an algebra isomorphism. This is the algebra analogue of the corresponding theorem for groups, rings, and vector spaces.
