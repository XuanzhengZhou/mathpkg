The space $L(E;E)$ of linear transformations on a vector space $E$ becomes an associative algebra under composition, denoted $A(E;E)$. The identity transformation serves as the unit element. When $\dim E \geq 2$, this algebra is not commutative, as shown by constructing explicit linear transformations $\varphi$ and $\psi$ with $\varphi\psi \neq \psi\varphi$.
