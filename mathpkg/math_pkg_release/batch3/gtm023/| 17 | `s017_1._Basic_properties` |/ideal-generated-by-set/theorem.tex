The ideal $I$ generated by a set $S$ is the intersection of all ideals containing $S$. If $A$ is associative, $I$ is the subspace of $A$ generated (linearly) by elements of the form

$$s, \; a s, \; s a \quad s \in S, \; a \in A.$$

In particular every single element $a$ generates an ideal $I_a$, called the principal ideal generated by $a$.
