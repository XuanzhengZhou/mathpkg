A subalgebra is a linear subspace closed under the algebra multiplication. An ideal is a subspace that absorbs multiplication by arbitrary algebra elements: a right ideal satisfies $xy \in I$ for $x \in I$, $y \in A$, while a left ideal satisfies $yx \in I$. A two-sided ideal (or simply ideal) satisfies both conditions. Every ideal is automatically a subalgebra. The derived algebra $A^2$, generated by all products $xy$, is always an ideal.
