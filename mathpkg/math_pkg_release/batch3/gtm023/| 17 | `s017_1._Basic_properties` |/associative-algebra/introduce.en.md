An algebra is associative if its multiplication satisfies the associative law $x(yz) = (xy)z$ for all elements $x, y, z$. It is commutative if $xy = yx$ for all $x, y$. These are the two most fundamental additional properties an algebra can possess. Familiar associative algebras include matrix algebras and the algebra of linear transformations on a vector space.
