Using the linear isomorphism $z \mapsto \varphi_z$ from $C$ onto $Q$, a multiplication among vectors in $C$ is defined by
$$
z_1 z_2 = \varphi_{z_2} z_1.
$$
Equivalently, $\varphi_{z_1 z_2} = \varphi_{z_2} \circ \varphi_{z_1}$. Then $\varphi_a = \iota$ (the identity), and the correspondence $z \mapsto \varphi_z$ preserves products. Consequently, $C$ becomes a (real) division algebra under this multiplication.
