Within the algebra $Q$, every element $\varphi$ satisfies the fundamental identity $\varphi \tilde{\varphi} = \varphi \operatorname{ad} \varphi = \iota \cdot \det \varphi$. This formula relates the composition of $\varphi$ with its adjoint (or adjugate) to the scalar multiple of the identity by the determinant. It is the cornerstone for deriving the algebraic properties of $Q$, including the quaternionic structure of its traceless subalgebra $Q_1$.
