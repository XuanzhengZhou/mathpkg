Since $Q$ is characterized by $\tilde{\varphi} = \operatorname{ad} \varphi$ (equivalent to (11.33) via sec. 4.6 and problem 8, §2, chapter IV), we have $\varphi \tilde{\varphi} = \varphi \operatorname{ad} \varphi$. The relation $\varphi \operatorname{ad} \varphi = \iota \cdot \det \varphi$ follows from the definition of the adjugate: for any linear transformation $\varphi$ of a 2-dimensional complex space, $\varphi \circ \operatorname{ad} \varphi = \operatorname{ad} \varphi \circ \varphi = \iota \cdot \det \varphi$. Thus $\varphi \tilde{\varphi} = \iota \cdot \det \varphi$ for all $\varphi \in Q$.
