For a unitary mapping $\tau$ of the complex plane $C$ and any vector $z \in C$,
$$
2 \operatorname{Re} (z, \tau z) = |z|^2 \operatorname{tr} \tau.
$$
In particular, if $\det \tau = 1$, then the real part of the inner product $(z, \tau z)$ depends only on the norm $|z|$ of $z$. This is the complex analogue of the corresponding relation for a proper rotation of the real plane.
