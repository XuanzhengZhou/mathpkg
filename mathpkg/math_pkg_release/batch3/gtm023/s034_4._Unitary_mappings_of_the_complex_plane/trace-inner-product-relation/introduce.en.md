For a unitary mapping $\tau$ of the complex plane, formula (11.31) implies $2 \operatorname{Re}(z, \tau z) = |z|^2 \operatorname{tr} \tau$. This relation shows that when $\det \tau = 1$, the real part of $(z, \tau z)$ is determined solely by the norm of $z$, paralleling the behavior of proper rotations in the real plane (formula (8.42)). The result is a key identity linking the geometry of unitary transformations to their algebraic trace.
