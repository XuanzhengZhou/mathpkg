The anticommutator (or symmetrized product) of two elements $\varphi, \psi$ in the traceless subalgebra $Q_1$ equals $-(\varphi, \psi) \iota$, directly linking the algebra multiplication to the Euclidean inner product. As a consequence, two pure quaternion-like elements anticommute precisely when they are orthogonal. This formula is the quaternionic analogue of the identity $\mathbf{u}\mathbf{v} + \mathbf{v}\mathbf{u} = -2(\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v})$ for vectors in $\mathbb{R}^3$.
