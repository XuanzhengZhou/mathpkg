Select a fixed unit vector $a$ in the complex plane $C$. For every vector $z \in C$, there exists a unique mapping $\varphi_z \in Q$ such that $\varphi_z a = z$. Explicitly, if $b$ is a unit vector orthogonal to $a$ and $z = \alpha a + \beta b$ with $\alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{C}$, then
$$
\varphi_z a = \alpha a + \beta b, \qquad
\varphi_z b = -\bar{\beta} a + \bar{\alpha} b.
$$
The correspondence $z \mapsto \varphi_z$ satisfies
$$
\varphi_{\lambda z_1 + \mu z_2} = \lambda \varphi_{z_1} + \mu \varphi_{z_2}
$$
for any real numbers $\lambda, \mu$, defining a linear isomorphism of $C$ (as a 4-dimensional real space) onto $Q$.
