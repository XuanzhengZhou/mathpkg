Fix a unit vector $a$ in the complex plane $C$. For every $z \in C$, there exists a unique mapping $\varphi_z \in Q$ sending $a$ to $z$. Given an orthonormal basis $\{a, b\}$ and writing $z = \alpha a + \beta b$, the action of $\varphi_z$ is determined by $\varphi_z a = \alpha a + \beta b$ and $\varphi_z b = -\bar{\beta} a + \bar{\alpha} b$. The map $z \mapsto \varphi_z$ is a real-linear isomorphism from $C$ (viewed as $\mathbb{R}^4$) onto the algebra $Q$, serving as the foundation for defining quaternionic multiplication on the complex plane.
