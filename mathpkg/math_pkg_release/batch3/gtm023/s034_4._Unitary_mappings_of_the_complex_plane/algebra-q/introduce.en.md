The algebra $Q$ is the set of all complex linear transformations $\varphi$ of the complex plane $C$ satisfying $\varphi + \tilde{\varphi} = \iota \cdot \operatorname{tr} \varphi$, where $\tilde{\varphi}$ denotes the adjoint and $\iota$ is the identity. These transformations form a real 4-dimensional vector space and, since the defining condition is equivalent to $\tilde{\varphi} = \operatorname{ad} \varphi$, $Q$ is closed under composition and thus an algebra. As shown later in the section, $Q$ is an associative division algebra over $\mathbb{R}$ isomorphic to the quaternions $\mathbb{H}$.
