It has already been established that $Q$ is a real $4$-dimensional vector space and an algebra under composition. From (11.34), $\varphi \tilde{\varphi} = \iota \cdot \det \varphi$. If $\varphi \neq 0$, then $\det \varphi \neq 0$ (otherwise $\varphi \tilde{\varphi} = 0$, which would imply $\varphi = 0$ since the inner product is positive definite). Hence every nonzero $\varphi \in Q$ has a two-sided inverse, making $Q$ a division algebra. By the Frobenius theorem on real division algebras, any finite-dimensional associative division algebra over $\mathbb{R}$ is isomorphic to $\mathbb{R}$, $\mathbb{C}$, or $\mathbb{H}$. Since $\dim_{\mathbb{R}} Q = 4$ and $Q$ is noncommutative (e.g., elements of $Q_1$ do not generally commute), $Q \cong \mathbb{H}$. The explicit isomorphism sends the identity $\iota$ to $1 \in \mathbb{H}$ and an orthonormal basis of $Q_1$ to the quaternion units $i, j, k$.
