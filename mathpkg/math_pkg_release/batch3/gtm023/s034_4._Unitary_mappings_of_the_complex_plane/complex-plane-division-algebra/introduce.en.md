The complex plane $C$, when equipped with the multiplication $z_1 z_2 = \varphi_{z_2} z_1$, becomes a $4$-dimensional real division algebra. The linear isomorphism $z \mapsto \varphi_z$ from $C$ onto $Q$ is product-preserving (as an anti-homomorphism), so $(C, \cdot)$ is anti-isomorphic to $Q$ and thus to the quaternions $\mathbb{H}$. This construction gives a concrete model of the quaternions whose underlying vector space is simply the complex numbers $\mathbb{C}$, with the quaternionic multiplication expressed directly in terms of the complex structure.
