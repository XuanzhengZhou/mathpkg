Under the multiplication defined by $z_1 z_2 = \varphi_{z_2} z_1$, where $\varphi_z \in Q$ is the unique mapping sending the fixed unit vector $a$ to $z$, the complex plane $C$ (viewed as a 4-dimensional real vector space) becomes a real division algebra. The correspondence $z \mapsto \varphi_z$ is a linear isomorphism and satisfies $\varphi_{z_1 z_2} = \varphi_{z_2} \circ \varphi_{z_1}$, so it establishes an anti-isomorphism between $(C, \cdot)$ and $Q \cong \mathbb{H}$. Thus $C$ with this multiplication is an explicit realization of the quaternions.
