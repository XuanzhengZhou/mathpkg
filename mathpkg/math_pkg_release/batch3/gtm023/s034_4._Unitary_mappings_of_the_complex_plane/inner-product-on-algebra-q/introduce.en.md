A positive definite inner product is defined on the algebra $Q$ by $(\varphi, \psi) = \frac{1}{2} \operatorname{tr}(\varphi \circ \tilde{\psi})$, where $\tilde{\psi}$ is the adjoint of $\psi$. This inner product makes $Q$ into a Euclidean space and is compatible with the algebra structure, enabling the geometric interpretation of the multiplication in $Q$ and its traceless subalgebra $Q_1$.
