The determinant is multiplicative: $\det(\tau_1 \tau_2) = \det \tau_1 \cdot \det \tau_2$. Hence if $\det \tau_1 = \det \tau_2 = 1$, then $\det(\tau_1 \tau_2) = 1$. Moreover, $\det(\tau^{-1}) = (\det \tau)^{-1} = 1$. The identity mapping has determinant $1$. Thus the set of unitary mappings with determinant $+1$ is closed under composition and inverses and contains the identity, so it forms a subgroup.
