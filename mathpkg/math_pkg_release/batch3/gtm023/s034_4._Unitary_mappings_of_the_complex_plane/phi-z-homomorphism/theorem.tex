For the multiplication defined on the complex plane $C$ by $z_1 z_2 = \varphi_{z_2} z_1$, the mapping $z \mapsto \varphi_z$ satisfies the homomorphism property
$$
\varphi_{z_1 z_2} = \varphi_{z_2} \circ \varphi_{z_1} \quad z_1, z_2 \in C.
$$
In particular, $\varphi_a = \iota$ for the fixed unit vector $a$, and the correspondence preserves products (with order reversed), making it an anti-homomorphism from the division algebra $C$ to the algebra $Q$.
