The mapping $z \mapsto \varphi_z$ from $C$ to $Q$ satisfies the product-preserving property $\varphi_{z_1 z_2} = \varphi_{z_2} \circ \varphi_{z_1}$. Along with $\varphi_a = \iota$, this establishes that the correspondence is a algebra anti-homomorphism from the division algebra $(C, \cdot)$ to $(Q, \circ)$. Since the map is also a linear isomorphism, it provides an explicit algebra isomorphism (up to order reversal) between the complex plane equipped with quaternionic multiplication and the algebra $Q$, and therefore with the quaternions $\mathbb{H}$.
