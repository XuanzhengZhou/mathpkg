The Lagrange identity $|\varphi|^2 |\psi|^2 = (\varphi, \psi)^2 + |\varphi \times \psi|^2$ holds in the traceless subalgebra $Q_1$ of $Q$. This fundamental relation shows that the norm of the cross product $|\varphi \times \psi|$ measures the area of the parallelogram spanned by $\varphi$ and $\psi$ in the Euclidean space $Q_1 \cong \mathbb{R}^3$. Together with the decomposition formula $\varphi \circ \psi = -(\varphi, \psi) \iota + \varphi \times \psi$, it confirms that $Q_1$ with its inner product and cross product is isometrically isomorphic to $\mathbb{R}^3$ with the standard dot and cross products.
