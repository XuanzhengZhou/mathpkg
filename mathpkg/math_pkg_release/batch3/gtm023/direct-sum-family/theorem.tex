Let $(E_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ be an arbitrary family of vector spaces. The (external) direct sum $\bigoplus_\alpha E_\alpha$ consists of all mappings
$$x: A \to \bigcup_\alpha E_\alpha$$
such that $x(\alpha) \in E_\alpha$ for all $\alpha \in A$, and all but finitely many $x(\alpha)$ are zero. Operations are defined pointwise.

The canonical injection $i_\varrho: E_\varrho \to \bigoplus_\alpha E_\alpha$ is defined by
$$i_\varrho x: \alpha \mapsto \begin{cases} 0_\alpha & \varrho \neq \alpha \\ x & \varrho = \alpha \end{cases}$$
and the canonical projection $\pi_\varrho: \bigoplus_\alpha E_\alpha \to E_\varrho$ by $\pi_\varrho x = x_\varrho$.
