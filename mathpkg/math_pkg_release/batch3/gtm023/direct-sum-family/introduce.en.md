The direct sum of an arbitrary family of vector spaces generalizes the binary direct sum. The finiteness condition on supports ensures that the direct sum is distinct from the direct product. The canonical injections and projections satisfy $\pi_\alpha \circ i_\beta = \delta_{\alpha\beta}$ and every $x$ can be written as a finite sum $\sum_\alpha i_\alpha \pi_\alpha x$.
