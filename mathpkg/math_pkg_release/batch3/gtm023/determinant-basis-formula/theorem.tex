**Proposition II:** Assume that $E$ is an $n$-dimensional vector space and let $\Phi$ be a skew symmetric $n$-linear map from $E$ to $F$. Then $\Phi$ is completely determined by its value on a basis of $E$. In particular, if $\Phi$ vanishes on a basis, then $\Phi = 0$.

Explicitly, for a basis $a_1, \ldots, a_n$,
$$\Phi(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = \left( \sum_{\sigma \in S_n} \varepsilon_\sigma \xi_{\sigma(1)}^1 \cdots \xi_{\sigma(n)}^n \right) \cdot \Phi(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$$
where $x_\lambda = \sum_{v=1}^n \xi_\lambda^v a_v$.
