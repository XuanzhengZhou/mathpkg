A skew symmetric $n$-linear map on an $n$-dimensional space is completely determined by its value on any single basis, scaled by the determinant of the coordinate matrix. This is the fundamental computational formula: evaluating a determinant function reduces to computing the Leibniz sum $\sum_\sigma \varepsilon_\sigma \prod \xi_\sigma$. It shows why the determinant formula works for matrices.
