Let $y \in F$ and $y^* \in F^\perp$ be arbitrarily chosen. Then
$$\langle \varphi^* y^*, y \rangle = \langle y^*, \varphi y \rangle.$$
Since $F$ is stable under $\varphi$, we have $\varphi y \in F$, and since $y^* \in F^\perp$, we have $\langle y^*, \varphi y \rangle = 0$. Therefore $\langle \varphi^* y^*, y \rangle = 0$ for all $y \in F$, which means $\varphi^* y^* \in F^\perp$. This proves that $F^\perp$ is stable under $\varphi^*$.
