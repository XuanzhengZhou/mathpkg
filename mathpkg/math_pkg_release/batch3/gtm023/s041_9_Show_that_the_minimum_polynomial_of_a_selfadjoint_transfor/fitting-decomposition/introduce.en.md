The Fitting decomposition splits a vector space equipped with a linear transformation into a nilpotent part and an invertible part. The nilpotent component $F_0$ corresponds to the generalized eigenspace for the eigenvalue $0$ (the kernel of some power of $\varphi$), while the $1$-component $F_1$ is the direct sum of all other generalized eigenspaces, on which $\varphi$ acts as an isomorphism. This decomposition is fundamental in the structure theory of linear transformations.
