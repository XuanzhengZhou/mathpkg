Let $E$ be a vector space and $\varphi: E \to E$ a linear transformation. Let $F_0$ be the generalized eigenspace of $\varphi$ corresponding to the irreducible polynomial $t$ (if $t$ does not divide the minimum polynomial $\mu$, then $F_0 = 0$). Let $F_1$ be the direct sum of the remaining generalized eigenspaces. The decomposition
$$E = F_0 \oplus F_1$$
is called the \text{Fitting decomposition} of $E$ with respect to $\varphi$. The spaces $F_0$ and $F_1$ are called respectively the \text{Fitting null component} and the \text{Fitting 1-component} of $E$.
