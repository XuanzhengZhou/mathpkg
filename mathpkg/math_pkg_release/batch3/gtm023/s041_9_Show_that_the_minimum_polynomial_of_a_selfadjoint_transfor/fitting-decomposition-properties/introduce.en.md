The Fitting decomposition separates a linear transformation into two fundamentally different parts: a nilpotent part (on $F_0$) and an invertible part (on $F_1$). The nilpotent part captures all generalized eigenvectors for the eigenvalue zero, while the invertible part contains all other generalized eigenspaces. Furthermore, the projection operators for this decomposition are polynomial expressions in the original transformation.
