In a 2-dimensional pseudo-Euclidean plane of index 1, proper rotations preserving the fore-cone and past-cone act by scaling the light-cone generators reciprocally. The inner product of a vector with its rotated image depends only on the norm, and the pseudo-Euclidean rotation angle is expressed in terms of hyperbolic functions of the scaling parameter $\lambda$.
