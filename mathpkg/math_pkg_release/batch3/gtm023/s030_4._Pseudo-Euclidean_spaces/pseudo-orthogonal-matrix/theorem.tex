If an orthonormal basis is chosen in a pseudo-Euclidean space $E$, the matrix $(\alpha_{\nu}^{\lambda})$ of a pseudo-Euclidean rotation $\varphi$ satisfies the relations
$$\sum_{\lambda} \varepsilon_{\lambda} \alpha_{\nu}^{\lambda} \alpha_{\mu}^{\lambda} = \varepsilon_{\nu} \delta_{\nu \mu}.$$
A matrix satisfying these relations is called \text{pseudo-orthogonal}.
