In a pseudo-Euclidean space, a normed determinant function is a determinant function that satisfies a specific normalization condition involving the Gram determinant of two $n$-tuples. Unlike the Euclidean case, the normalization factor includes a sign $(-1)^{n-s}$ determined by the index $s$. There exist exactly two such functions, differing only by sign.
