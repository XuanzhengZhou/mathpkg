A pseudo-Euclidean space generalizes the notion of a Euclidean space by allowing the inner product to be indefinite rather than positive definite. The bilinear form is still required to be non-degenerate, ensuring the space is self-dual. Vectors are classified as space-like, time-like, or light-vectors based on the sign of their self-inner product, with the light-cone consisting of all vectors orthogonal to themselves.
