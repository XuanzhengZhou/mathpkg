A basis $e_v\ (v = 1 \ldots n)$ of a pseudo-Euclidean space $E$ is called \text{orthonormal} if
$$(e_v, e_\mu) = \varepsilon_v \delta_{v\mu}$$
where
$$\varepsilon_v = \begin{cases} +1 & (v = 1 \ldots s) \\ -1 & (v = s + 1 \ldots n). \end{cases}$$

If an orthonormal basis $e_v\ (v = 1 \ldots n)$ is chosen, the inner product of two vectors
$$x = \sum_v \xi^v e_v \quad \text{and} \quad y = \sum_v \eta^v e_v$$
is given by the bilinear form
$$(x, y) = \sum_{v=1}^n \varepsilon_v \xi^v \eta^v.$$

An orthonormal basis can always be constructed (see sec. 9.8).
