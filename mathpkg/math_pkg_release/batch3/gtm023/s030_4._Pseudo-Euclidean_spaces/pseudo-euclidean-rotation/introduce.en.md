A pseudo-Euclidean rotation is a linear transformation of a pseudo-Euclidean space that preserves the indefinite inner product. As in the Euclidean case, such transformations have determinant $\pm 1$, but unlike the Euclidean case, eigenvectors lying on the light-cone may have eigenvalues different from $\pm 1$. These rotations are fundamental to the geometry of Minkowski space in special relativity.
