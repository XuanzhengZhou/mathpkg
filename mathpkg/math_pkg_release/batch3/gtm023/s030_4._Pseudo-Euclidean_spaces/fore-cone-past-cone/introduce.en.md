In a pseudo-Euclidean space of index $n-1$, the set of all time-like vectors splits naturally into two disjoint connected components called the fore-cone $T^+$ and the past-cone $T^-$. This decomposition arises from an equivalence relation defined by a negative inner product, essentially separating "future-directed" from "past-directed" time-like directions. The two cones are symmetric under the map $x \mapsto -x$.
