Let $E$ be a 2-dimensional pseudo-Euclidean space of index $1$, and let $x$ and $y$ be space-like vectors contained in the same one of the sectors $S_1$ or $S_2$. Then $(x, y) > 0$ and there exists exactly one real number $\theta$ ($-\infty < \theta < \infty$) such that
$$\cosh \theta = \frac{(x, y)}{|x| |y|} \quad \text{and} \quad \sinh \theta = \frac{\Delta(x, y)}{|x| |y|}.$$

This number $\theta$ is called the \text{pseudo-Euclidean angle} between the space-like vectors $x$ and $y$.
