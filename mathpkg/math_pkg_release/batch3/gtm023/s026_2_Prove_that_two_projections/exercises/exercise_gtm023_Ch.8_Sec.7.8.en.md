**Quaternionic exponential.** Fix a quaternion $a$ and consider $\dot{x}(t) = a x(t)$ with $x(0) = e$. Define $\exp a = x(1)$. Decompose $a = \lambda e + b$ with $\lambda \in \mathbb{R}$, $(b, e) = 0$. (i) Show: a) $\exp(\lambda e) = \exp \lambda \cdot e$, b) $\exp b = e \cos |b| + \frac{b}{|b|} \sin |b|$, c) $\exp a = \exp \lambda (e \cos |b| + \frac{b}{|b|} \sin |b|)$. (ii) Show $|\exp a| = \exp \lambda$. (iii) Prove $\exp a = e$ iff $\lambda = 0$ and $|b| = 2k\pi$, $k \in \mathbb{Z}$. (iv) Let $a_1 = \lambda_1 e + b_1$, $a_2 = \lambda_2 e + b_2$ with $b_1, b_2 \neq 0$. Show $\exp a_1 = \exp a_2$ iff $\lambda_1 = \lambda_2$ and $b_1, b_2$ generate the same 2-dim subspace and have the same norm mod $2\pi$.
