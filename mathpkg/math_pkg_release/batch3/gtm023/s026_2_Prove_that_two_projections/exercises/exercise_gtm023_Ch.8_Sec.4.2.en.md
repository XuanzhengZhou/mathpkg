section: "§4. Skew mappings"

Let $E$ be an oriented Euclidean 3-space.

(i) Show that every vector $a \in E$ determines a skew transformation $\varphi_a$ of $E$ given by $\varphi_a(x) = a \times x$.

(ii) Show that $\varphi_{a \times b} = \varphi_a \circ \varphi_b - \varphi_b \circ \varphi_a$ for $a, b \in E$.

(iii) Show that every skew map $\varphi: E \rightarrow E$ determines a unique vector $a \in E$ such that $\varphi = \varphi_a$.
