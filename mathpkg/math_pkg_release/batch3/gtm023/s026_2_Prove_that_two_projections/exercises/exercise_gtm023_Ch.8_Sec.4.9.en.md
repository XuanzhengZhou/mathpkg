Let $\varphi$ be a linear transformation of a real linear space $E$. Prove that an inner product can be defined in $E$ such that $\varphi$ becomes a skew mapping if and only if the following conditions are satisfied: 1. The space $E$ can be decomposed into $\ker \varphi$ and stable planes. 2. The mappings which are induced in these planes have positive determinant and trace zero.
