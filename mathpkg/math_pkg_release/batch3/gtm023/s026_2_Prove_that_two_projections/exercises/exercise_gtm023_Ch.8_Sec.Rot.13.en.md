Consider a non-zero vector $a \in \mathbb{R}^4$ and define the rotation $\varphi_a$ of $\mathbb{R}^3$. (i) Show that $\varphi_a$ is a proper rotation whose axis is generated by $a$ and whose rotation angle is $\Theta = \pi \cdot |a|$. (ii) Show that $\varphi_0 = \iota$ and $\varphi_a x = -x + 2a(a, x)$ if $|a| = 1$. (iii) Suppose $a \neq b$. Show $\varphi_a = \varphi_b$ iff $|a| = 1$ and $b = -a$. Conclude that there is a 1-1 correspondence between the proper rotations of $\mathbb{R}^3$ and the straight lines in $\mathbb{R}^4$.
