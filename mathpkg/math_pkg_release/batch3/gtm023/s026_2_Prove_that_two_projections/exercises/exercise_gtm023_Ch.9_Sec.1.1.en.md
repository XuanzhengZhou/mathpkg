Let $f$ and $g$ be two linearly independent linear functions in $E$ and let $\theta$ be a derivation in $\mathbb{R}$. Show that $\Psi(x) = f(x) \theta[g(x)] - g(x) \theta[f(x)]$ satisfies the parallelogram identity and $\Psi(\lambda x) = \lambda^2 \Psi(x)$. Prove that the function $\Phi$ obtained from $\Psi$ by (9.7) is bilinear if and only if $\theta = 0$.
