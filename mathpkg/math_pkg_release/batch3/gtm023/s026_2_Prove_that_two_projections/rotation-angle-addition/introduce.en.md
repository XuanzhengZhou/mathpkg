Proper rotations in an oriented Euclidean plane form an abelian group isomorphic to the circle $\mathbb{S}^1$. The composition of two proper rotations with angles $\Theta(\varphi)$ and $\Theta(\psi)$ is a proper rotation whose angle is the sum $\Theta(\varphi) + \Theta(\psi)$ modulo $2\pi$. This additivity follows directly from the trigonometric parametrization of proper rotations via the canonical complex structure.
