In an oriented Euclidean plane, the canonical complex structure $j$ is a distinguished proper rotation satisfying $j^2 = -1$. It is skew-adjoint ($\tilde{j} = -j$) and orthogonal ($\tilde{j} = j^{-1}$). The structure $j$ plays the role of multiplication by $i$ in the complex plane, enabling a trigonometric parametrization of arbitrary proper rotations: $\varphi x = x \cos \theta + j(x) \sin \theta$.
