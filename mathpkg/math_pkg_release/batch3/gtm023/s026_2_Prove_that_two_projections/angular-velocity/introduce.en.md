In classical rigid body kinematics, the angular velocity vector $u(t)$ describes the instantaneous rotational motion of a 3-dimensional rigid body. For a differentiable family of rotations $\varphi(t)$, the velocity of any point $x(t) = \varphi(t)x$ is given by the cross product $\dot{x}(t) = u(t) \times x(t)$. The direction of $u(t)$ gives the instantaneous axis of rotation, and its magnitude equals the angular speed.
