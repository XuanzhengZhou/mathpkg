The rotation vector $u$ has the following geometric significance:
1. $u$ is contained in the axis of $\varphi$.
2. The norm of $u$ is equal to $\sin \theta$, i.e. $|u| = \sin \theta$.
3. If the orientation induced by $u$ is used in the plane $F$ orthogonal to the axis, then $\theta$ is contained in the interval $0 < \theta < \pi$.

For a rotation $\varphi$ in 3-space:
$$\cos \theta = \frac{1}{2} (\operatorname{tr} \varphi - 1).$$
