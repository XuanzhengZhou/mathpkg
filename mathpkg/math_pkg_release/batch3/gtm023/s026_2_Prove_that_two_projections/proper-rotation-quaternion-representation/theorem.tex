Proposition II: Every proper rotation $\sigma$ of $E$ can be written in the form
$$\sigma(x) = a x b^{-1} \quad x \in E$$
where $a, b$ are unit quaternions. Moreover, if $a_1, b_1$ and $a_2, b_2$ are two pairs of unit vectors such that the corresponding rotations coincide, then
$$a_2 = \varepsilon a_1 \quad \text{and} \quad b_2 = \varepsilon b_1$$
where $\varepsilon = \pm 1$.
