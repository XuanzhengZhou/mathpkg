Proposition II generalizes the quaternionic representation to rotations of 4-space. Every proper rotation $\sigma$ of the quaternion algebra $\mathbb{H}$ can be expressed as $\sigma(x) = a x b^{-1}$ for unit quaternions $a, b$. Moreover, both $a$ and $b$ can be scaled by $-1$ simultaneously without changing the rotation, giving a double covering $S^3 \times S^3 \to SO(4)$ with kernel $\{(1,1), (-1,-1)\}$.
