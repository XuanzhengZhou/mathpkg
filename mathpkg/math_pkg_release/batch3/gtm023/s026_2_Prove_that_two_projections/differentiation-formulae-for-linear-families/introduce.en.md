The differentiation rules for families of linear transformations mirror those of scalar calculus: linearity of the derivative, a Leibniz/product rule for composition, and compatibility with the adjoint operation. These elementary rules are the foundation for studying differential equations in the space of linear operators, such as $\dot{\varphi}(t) = \psi(t) \circ \varphi(t)$.
