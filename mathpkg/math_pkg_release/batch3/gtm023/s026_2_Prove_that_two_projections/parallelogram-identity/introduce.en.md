The parallelogram identity relates the values of a quadratic function $\Psi$ on sums and differences of vectors: $\Psi(x+y) + \Psi(x-y) = 2(\Psi(x) + \Psi(y))$. Geometrically, this generalizes the Euclidean law that the sum of the squares of the diagonals of a parallelogram equals the sum of the squares of its four sides. The identity characterizes those functions $\Psi$ that arise from symmetric bilinear forms.
