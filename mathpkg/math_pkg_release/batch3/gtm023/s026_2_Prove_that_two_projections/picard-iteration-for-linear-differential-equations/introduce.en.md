The Picard iteration method constructs the unique solution to the linear operator differential equation $\dot{\varphi}(t) = \psi(t) \circ \varphi(t)$ with $\varphi(t_0) = \iota$. Starting from the identity and iteratively integrating, one obtains a sequence of approximations that converge uniformly in the operator norm. The limit is the desired differentiable family of linear automorphisms, establishing the fundamental existence theorem for 1-parameter families.
