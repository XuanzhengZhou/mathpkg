The Lie algebra of the orthogonal group consists of skew-symmetric transformations. For a differentiable family of rotations, the velocity generator $\psi(t) = \dot{\varphi}(t) \circ \varphi(t)^{-1}$ is always skew. Conversely, integrating a time-dependent family of skew mappings produces a family of proper rotations. This establishes the correspondence between the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{so}(n)$ and the Lie group $SO(n)$.
