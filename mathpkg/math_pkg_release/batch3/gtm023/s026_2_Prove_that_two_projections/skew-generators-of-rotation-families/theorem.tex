Let $\varphi(t)$ ($t_0 \leq t \leq t_1$) be a differentiable family of rotations such that $\varphi(t_0) = \iota$. Then the linear transformations
$$\psi(t) = \dot{\varphi}(t) \circ \varphi(t)^{-1}$$
are skew: $\tilde{\psi}(t) + \psi(t) = 0$.

Conversely, if $\dot{\varphi}(t) = \psi(t) \circ \varphi(t)$ with $\varphi(t_0) = \iota$ where $\psi(t)$ is a continuous family of skew mappings, then every automorphism $\varphi(t)$ is a proper rotation.
