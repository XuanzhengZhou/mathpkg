Since every skew mapping is normal (see sec. 8.5), the image space is the orthogonal complement of the kernel. Consequently, the induced transformation $\psi_1: \operatorname{Im} \psi \to \operatorname{Im} \psi$ is regular. Since $\psi_1$ is again skew (the skew condition $\tilde{\psi} = -\psi$ restricts to the image), $\det \psi_1$ is a skew-symmetric determinant of odd dimension would be zero. However, $\psi_1$ is regular, so its domain must have even dimension. Thus $\dim \operatorname{Im} \psi = \operatorname{rank} \psi$ is even.
