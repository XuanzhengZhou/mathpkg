**First part:** Let $\tau(x) = pxp^{-1}$. Setting $q = p - \lambda e$ where $\lambda = (p, e)$, we have $\tau(q) = q$, so $q$ generates the axis. The restriction of $\tau$ to the plane orthogonal to $q$ satisfies $\tau z = (2\lambda^2 - 1)z + 2\lambda(q \times z)$ for $z \in F$. With $\lambda = \cos \omega$ and $|q| = \sin \omega$, this yields $\cos \Theta = \cos 2\omega$ and $\sin \Theta = \sin 2\omega$, so $\Theta = 2\omega \pmod{2\pi}$. Thus two unit quaternions $p_1$ and $p_2$ determine the same rotation only if $p_2 = \pm p_1$.

**Second part:** For any proper rotation $\sigma \neq \iota$ of $E_1$, let $a$ be a unit vector on the axis and $\vartheta \in (0, 2\pi)$ the rotation angle. Set $p = e \cos(\vartheta/2) + a \sin(\vartheta/2)$. Then the rotation $\tau(x) = pxp^{-1}$ has axis generated by $a$ and angle $\Theta = 2 \cdot \vartheta/2 = \vartheta$, so $\tau = \sigma$. This completes the proof.
