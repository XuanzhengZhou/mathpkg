The polarization identity provides the unique way to recover a symmetric bilinear form $\Phi$ from its associated quadratic form $\Psi(x) = \Phi(x, x)$. The formula $\Phi(x, y) = \frac{1}{2}(\Psi(x+y) - \Psi(x) - \Psi(y))$ shows that $\Psi$ determines $\Phi$ completely, and different symmetric bilinear forms yield different quadratic forms.
