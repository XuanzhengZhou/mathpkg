The rotation vector $u$ of a proper rotation $\varphi$ in an oriented 3-space encodes both the axis of rotation and the rotation angle. Defined via the skew part $\psi = \frac{1}{2}(\varphi - \tilde{\varphi}) = u \times (\cdot)$, the vector $u$ points along the rotation axis and its norm equals $\sin \theta$, where $\theta$ is the rotation angle. The rotation vector uniquely determines the rotation together with the sign of $\cos \theta$.
