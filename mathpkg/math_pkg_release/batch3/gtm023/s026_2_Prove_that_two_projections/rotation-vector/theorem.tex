To determine the axis of a given rotation $\varphi$ consider the skew mapping
$$\psi = \frac{1}{2}(\varphi - \tilde{\varphi})$$
and introduce an orientation in $E$. Then $\psi$ can be written in the form
$$\psi x = u \times x \quad u \in E.$$
The vector $u$ which is uniquely determined by $\varphi$ is called the rotation-vector. The rotation vector is contained in the axis of $\varphi$.
