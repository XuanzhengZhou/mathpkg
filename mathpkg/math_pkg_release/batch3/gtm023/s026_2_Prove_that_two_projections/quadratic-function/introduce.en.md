A quadratic function on a real vector space is a continuous function $\Psi$ satisfying the parallelogram identity. The key theorem is that every such function arises from a unique symmetric bilinear form $\Phi$ as $\Psi(x) = \Phi(x, x)$. Thus quadratic and symmetric bilinear functions are equivalent concepts over the reals, with the correspondence given by polarization.
