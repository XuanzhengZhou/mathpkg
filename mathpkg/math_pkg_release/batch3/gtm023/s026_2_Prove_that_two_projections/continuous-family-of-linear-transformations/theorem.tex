A continuous mapping $t \rightarrow \varphi(t)$ of a closed interval $t_0 \leq t \leq t_1$ into the space $L(E; E)$ is called a continuous family of linear transformations or a continuous curve in $L(E; E)$. A continuous curve $\varphi(t)$ is called differentiable if the limit
$$\lim_{\Delta t \rightarrow 0} \frac{\varphi(t + \Delta t) - \varphi(t)}{\Delta t} = \dot{\varphi}(t)$$
exists for every $t$ ($t_0 \leq t \leq t_1$). The mapping $\dot{\varphi}$ is again linear for every fixed $t$.
