A continuous family of linear transformations is a continuous curve $t \mapsto \varphi(t)$ in the space $L(E; E)$ of linear operators on a finite-dimensional inner product space, equipped with the operator norm. If the curve is differentiable with derivative $\dot{\varphi}(t)$, we obtain a notion of velocity for one-parameter families. This provides the foundation for studying linear differential equations in operator spaces and 1-parameter groups of automorphisms.
