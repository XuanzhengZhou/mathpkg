Rotations on an inner product space are divided into two classes: proper rotations with determinant $+1$ (forming the special orthogonal group $SO(n)$) and improper rotations with determinant $-1$. Proper rotations preserve orientation and are connected to the identity, while improper rotations reverse orientation. The general orthogonal group $O(n)$ is the disjoint union of these two classes.
