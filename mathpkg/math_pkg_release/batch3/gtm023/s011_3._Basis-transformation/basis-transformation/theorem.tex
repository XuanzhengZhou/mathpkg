Let $(x_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ and $(y_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ be two bases of $E$ indexed by the same set $A$. Then there exist unique families of scalars $(\tau_\alpha^\beta)_{\alpha,\beta \in A}$ such that for each fixed $\alpha$, only finitely many $\tau_\alpha^\beta \neq 0$, and
$$y_\alpha = \sum_\beta \tau_\alpha^\beta x_\beta, \quad \alpha \in A.$$

Similarly,
$$x_\alpha = \sum_\beta \sigma_\alpha^\beta y_\beta, \quad \alpha \in A.$$

The families $(\tau_\alpha^\beta)$ and $(\sigma_\alpha^\beta)$ are called the transformation matrices between the two bases. They satisfy
$$\sum_\gamma \tau_\alpha^\gamma \sigma_\gamma^\beta = \delta_\alpha^\beta = \sum_\gamma \sigma_\alpha^\gamma \tau_\gamma^\beta.$$
