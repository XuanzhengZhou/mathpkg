Every non-degenerate indefinite symmetric bilinear form admits a Sylvester-type decomposition of the space into a direct sum of a positive-definite and a negative-definite subspace. The dimensions of $E^+$ and $E^-$ are invariants (the signature). This generalizes the spectral theorem for symmetric matrices to the case of indefinite forms.
