Proposition III generalizes the relationship between projections and direct sums to families of projection operators. A collection of projections that annihilate each other crosswise ($\varrho_i \circ \varrho_j = 0$ for $i \neq j$) and sum to the identity operator partitions the vector space into a direct sum of their respective images. This result is fundamental for the spectral decomposition of linear operators and provides the algebraic foundation for block-diagonalization: mutually orthogonal projections decompose the space into independent invariant subspaces.
