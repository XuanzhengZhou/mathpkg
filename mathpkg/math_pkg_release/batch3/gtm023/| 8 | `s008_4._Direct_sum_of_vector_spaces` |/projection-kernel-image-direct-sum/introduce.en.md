Every projection operator on a vector space decomposes the space into a direct sum of two complementary subspaces: its kernel and its image. This fundamental result shows that projection operators and direct sum decompositions are essentially equivalent notions — each projection yields a decomposition, and conversely each direct sum decomposition defines a projection. The decomposition also reveals the block-diagonal structure of a projection: it acts as the identity on its image and vanishes on its kernel.
