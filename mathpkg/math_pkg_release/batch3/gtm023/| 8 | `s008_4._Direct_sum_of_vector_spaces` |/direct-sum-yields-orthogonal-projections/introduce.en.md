This proposition shows that every direct sum decomposition of a vector space yields a family of orthogonal projection operators by composing canonical injections with canonical projections. Each $\varrho_v = i_v \circ \pi_v$ projects onto the subspace $E_v$ and annihilates all other summands. Together with Proposition III, this establishes a bijective correspondence between direct sum decompositions of $E$ and families of projection operators that are mutually orthogonal and sum to the identity — a fundamental structural insight in linear algebra.
