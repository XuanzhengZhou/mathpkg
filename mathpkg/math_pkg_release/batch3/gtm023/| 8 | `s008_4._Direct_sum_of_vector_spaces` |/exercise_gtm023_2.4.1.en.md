Assume a decomposition

$$
E = E_1 + E_2.
$$

Consider the external direct sum $E_1 \oplus E_2$ and define a linear mapping $\varphi: E_1 \oplus E_2 \to E$ by

$$
\varphi(x_1, x_2) = x_1 + x_2, \quad x_1 \in E_1,\; x_2 \in E_2.
$$

Prove that $\varphi$ is surjective, and that $\varphi$ is injective if and only if $E_1 \cap E_2 = \{0\}$ (i.e., the sum $E_1 + E_2$ is direct). Conclude that the internal sum $E_1 + E_2$ is isomorphic to the external direct sum $E_1 \oplus E_2$ if and only if the sum is direct.
