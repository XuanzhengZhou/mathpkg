This proposition provides the converse to the projection decomposition theorem: every direct sum decomposition of a vector space defines a projection operator. Given $E = E_1 \oplus E_2$, the map that acts as the identity on the first summand and zero on the second is clearly idempotent and linear, hence a projection. Together with the fact that every projection operator determines a direct sum decomposition, this establishes a complete equivalence between projection operators and direct sum decompositions into two subspaces.
