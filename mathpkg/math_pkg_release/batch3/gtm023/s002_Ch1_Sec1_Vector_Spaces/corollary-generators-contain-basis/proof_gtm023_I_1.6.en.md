Apply Theorem I with $S$ as the given system of generators and $R = \varnothing$. The theorem yields a basis $T$ with $\varnothing \subset T \subset S$, i.e., $T \subset S$. Thus $S$ contains a basis. Taking $S = E$ shows every non-trivial vector space has a basis.
