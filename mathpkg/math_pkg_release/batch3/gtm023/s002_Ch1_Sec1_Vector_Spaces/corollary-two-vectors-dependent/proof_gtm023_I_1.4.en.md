By Proposition II, $x$ and $y$ are linearly dependent iff one is a linear combination of the other. A linear combination of a single vector $x$ has the form $\lambda x$ for some $\lambda \in \Gamma$. Hence $x, y$ are dependent iff $y = \lambda x$ or $x = \lambda y$.
