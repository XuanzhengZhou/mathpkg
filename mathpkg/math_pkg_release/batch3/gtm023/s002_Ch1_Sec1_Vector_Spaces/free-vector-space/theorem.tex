Let $X$ be an arbitrary set and $C(X)$ the set of all maps $f: X \to \Gamma$ with $f(x) \neq 0$ for only finitely many $x \in X$. Under pointwise addition and scalar multiplication, $C(X)$ is a vector space. Identifying $a \in X$ with $f_a$ where $f_a(a) = 1$ and $f_a(x) = 0$ for $x \neq a$, the set $X$ becomes a basis of $C(X)$. $C(X)$ is the **free vector space** over $X$.
