A $p$-linear map $\Phi: E^p \to F$ is called skew symmetric if for every permutation $\sigma \in S_p$
$$\sigma\Phi = \varepsilon_\sigma \cdot \Phi;$$
that is,
$$\Phi(x_{\sigma(1)}, \ldots, x_{\sigma(p)}) = \varepsilon_\sigma \Phi(x_1, \ldots, x_p).$$

Every $p$-linear map $\Phi$ determines a skew symmetric $p$-linear map $\Psi$ given by
$$\Psi = \sum_{\sigma \in S_p} \varepsilon_\sigma \cdot \sigma\Phi.$$

**Proposition I:** A $p$-linear map $\Phi$ is skew symmetric if and only if:
(i) $\Phi(x_1, \ldots, x_p) = 0$ whenever $x_i = x_j$ for some $i \neq j$.
(ii) $\Phi(x_1, \ldots, x_p) = 0$ whenever the vectors $x_1, \ldots, x_p$ are linearly dependent.
