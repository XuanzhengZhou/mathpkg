Let $\varphi: E \to F$ and $\psi: E \leftarrow F$ be linear mappings. $\psi$ is called a right inverse of $\varphi$ if $\varphi \circ \psi = \iota_F$. $\psi$ is called a left inverse of $\varphi$ if $\psi \circ \varphi = \iota_E$.

**Proposition I:** A linear mapping $\varphi: E \to F$ is surjective if and only if it has a right inverse. It is injective if and only if it has a left inverse.

**Corollary:** A linear isomorphism $\varphi: E \to F$ has a uniquely determined right (left) inverse, namely, $\varphi^{-1}$.
