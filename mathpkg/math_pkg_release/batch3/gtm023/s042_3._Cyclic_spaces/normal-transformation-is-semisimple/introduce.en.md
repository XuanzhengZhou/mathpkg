Theorem I (13.28) characterizes normal transformations on inner product spaces. Since the projection operators onto generalized eigenspaces are polynomials in $\varphi$, they are normal; being also idempotent, they must be selfadjoint, implying orthogonality. The polynomial $f_i(\varphi_i)$ on each eigenspace is both normal and nilpotent, forcing it to be zero, so the minimum polynomial has distinct irreducible factors, making $\varphi$ semisimple.
