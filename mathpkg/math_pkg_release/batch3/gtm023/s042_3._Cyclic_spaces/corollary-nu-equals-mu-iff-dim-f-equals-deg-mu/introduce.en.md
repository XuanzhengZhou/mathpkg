For a cyclic subspace $F$, its minimum polynomial $\nu$ equals the full space's minimum polynomial $\mu$ precisely when $\dim F = \deg \mu$. This follows because $\dim F = \deg \nu$, and $\nu$ divides $\mu$. Hence $\nu = \mu$ is equivalent to $\deg \nu = \deg \mu$, i.e., $\dim F = \deg \mu$.
