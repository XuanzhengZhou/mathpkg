Theorem II is the additive Jordan-Chevalley decomposition: any linear transformation uniquely decomposes into commuting semisimple and nilpotent parts, both of which are polynomials in $\varphi$. This parallels the multiplicative decomposition in algebraic groups. Existence follows from the Chinese remainder theorem applied to the polynomial ring modulo $\mu$. Uniqueness follows from the uniqueness of the decomposition in the algebra $\Gamma(\varphi)$.
