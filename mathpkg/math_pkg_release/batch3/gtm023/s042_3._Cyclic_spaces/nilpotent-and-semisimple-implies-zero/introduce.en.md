Corollary I follows directly from Proposition I: if $\varphi$ is nilpotent, then $\varphi^k = 0$; if $\varphi$ is also semisimple, then $\varphi = 0$ by applying Proposition I with $f(t) = t$. This shows that the zero transformation is the only one that is both nilpotent and semisimple.
