Theorem V provides the converse to Theorem IV: in a commutative semisimple algebra of transformations, every individual transformation is semisimple. The proof constructs a complementary subspace to $K(g)$ where $g = f_1^{k_1-1} \cdots f_r^{k_r-1}$, and shows this forces all exponents $k_i = 1$ in the minimum polynomial factorization.
