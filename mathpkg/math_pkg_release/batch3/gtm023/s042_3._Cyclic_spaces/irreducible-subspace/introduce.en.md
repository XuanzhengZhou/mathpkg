An irreducible subspace is a stable subspace that cannot be decomposed further into a nontrivial direct sum of stable subspaces. In the context of a linear transformation $\varphi$, irreducibility means that the induced transformation admits no proper nontrivial stable subspace decomposition. An irreducible space is always cyclic, but the converse is not generally true.
