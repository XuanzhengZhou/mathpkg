For real vector spaces, irreducible polynomials over $\mathbb{R}$ have degree 1 or 2. In the degree-1 case, the Jordan block is the standard $k \times k$ block with real eigenvalue $\lambda$. In the degree-2 case ($\alpha^2 - 4\beta < 0$), the $2 \times 2$ companion matrix $\begin{pmatrix}0 & 1 \\ -\beta & -\alpha\end{pmatrix}$ replaces each scalar entry, yielding $k$ such $2 \times 2$ blocks along the diagonal.
