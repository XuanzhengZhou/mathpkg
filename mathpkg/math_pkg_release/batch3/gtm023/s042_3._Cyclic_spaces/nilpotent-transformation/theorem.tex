A linear transformation $\varphi$ is called nilpotent if $\varphi^k = 0$ for some integer $k$, or equivalently, if its minimum polynomial has the form
$$\mu = t^m.$$
The exponent $m$ is called the degree of $\varphi$.

If $\varphi$ and $\psi$ are two commuting nilpotent transformations, then $\varphi + \psi$ and $\psi \circ \varphi$ are again nilpotent.
