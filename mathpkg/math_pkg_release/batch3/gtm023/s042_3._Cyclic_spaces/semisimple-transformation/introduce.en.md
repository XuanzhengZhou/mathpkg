A semisimple transformation is one for which the representation of $\Gamma(\varphi)$ on $E$ is completely reducible: every invariant subspace admits an invariant complement. Examples include rotations in Euclidean spaces, selfadjoint transformations, and unitary transformations. Semisimple transformations are precisely those whose minimum polynomial is a product of distinct irreducible polynomials (no repeated factors).
