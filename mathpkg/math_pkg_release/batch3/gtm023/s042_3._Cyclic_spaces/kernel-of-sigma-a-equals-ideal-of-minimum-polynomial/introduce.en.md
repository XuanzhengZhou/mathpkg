Proposition I establishes that there always exists at least one vector $a \in E$ for which the kernel of the map $\sigma_a$ is precisely the principal ideal generated by the minimum polynomial $\mu$ of $\varphi$. This vector is a candidate for a cyclic generator, since the induced map $\Gamma[t]/I_\mu \to E_a$ becomes an isomorphism. The proof proceeds by first handling the case where $\mu = f^k$ for an irreducible $f$, using the generalized eigenspace decomposition and properties of greatest common divisors to construct the desired vector.
