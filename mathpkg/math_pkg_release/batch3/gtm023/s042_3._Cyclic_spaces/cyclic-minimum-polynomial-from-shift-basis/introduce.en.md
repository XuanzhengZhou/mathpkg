Given a cyclic shift basis $a_0, \ldots, a_{n-1}$, the minimum polynomial of $\varphi$ is determined by the last basis vector's image: $\varphi(a_{n-1}) = \sum \alpha_v a_v$ yields $\mu = t^n - \sum \alpha_v t^v$. This is the companion matrix polynomial; it annihilates $\varphi$ and has degree $n$, which matches $\dim E$, so it must be the minimum polynomial.
