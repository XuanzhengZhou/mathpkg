Theorem I provides the definitive algebraic characterization of semisimple transformations: $\varphi$ is semisimple precisely when $\mu = f_1 \cdots f_r$ with the $f_i$ distinct irreducible polynomials (no repeated factors). The forward direction uses Proposition I: $f(\varphi)$ is nilpotent for $f = f_1 \cdots f_r$, so $f(\varphi) = 0$ implies $\mu$ has no repeated factors. The converse uses that when $\mu$ is irreducible, $\Gamma(\varphi)$ is a field, making $E$ a vector space over an extension field, where any subspace has a complement.
