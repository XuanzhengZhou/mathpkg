Subalgebras are subspaces closed under multiplication. Ideals are subspaces closed under multiplication by arbitrary elements of the algebra (on one or both sides). Factor algebras $A/I$ exist precisely when $I$ is a two-sided ideal. These concepts generalize the corresponding notions from ring theory to the setting of algebras over a field.
