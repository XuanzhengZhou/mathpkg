A subspace $B \subset A$ of an algebra is called a subalgebra if it is closed under multiplication: $B \cdot B \subset B$.

A subspace $I \subset A$ is called a left ideal (right ideal) if $A \cdot I \subset I$ (resp. $I \cdot A \subset I$). If $I$ is both a left and right ideal, it is called a two-sided ideal or simply an ideal.

If $I$ is a two-sided ideal in $A$, the factor space $A/I$ inherits a natural algebra structure and is called the factor algebra.
