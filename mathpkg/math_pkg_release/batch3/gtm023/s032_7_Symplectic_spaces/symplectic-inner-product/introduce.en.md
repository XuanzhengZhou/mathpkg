A symplectic inner product is a non-degenerate, skew-symmetric bilinear form defined on a finite-dimensional vector space. It generalizes the notion of an inner product by replacing symmetry with skew-symmetry while retaining non-degeneracy. Symplectic inner products are the fundamental structure underlying symplectic geometry, with applications in classical mechanics (phase spaces), representation theory, and the study of Hamiltonian systems. The existence of a symplectic inner product forces the vector space to have even dimension.
