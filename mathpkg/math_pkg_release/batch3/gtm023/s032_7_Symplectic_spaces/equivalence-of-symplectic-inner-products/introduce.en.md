This theorem establishes that any two symplectic inner products on the same vector space are related by a linear automorphism whose matrix, in block form, satisfies specific symmetry conditions: the diagonal blocks are transpose-related with a sign ($D = -A^*$) while the off-diagonal blocks are symmetric ($B^* = B$, $C^* = C$). As a corollary, the dimensions of the Lie algebras of symplectic selfadjoint and skew-symplectic transformations are computed as $n(2n-1)$ and $n(2n+1)$ respectively, where $2n$ is the dimension of the space. These dimension formulas are fundamental for the structure theory of the symplectic Lie algebra $\mathfrak{sp}(2n)$.
