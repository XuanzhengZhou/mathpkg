This theorem asserts that every finite-dimensional symplectic vector space admits a symplectic basis. The proof proceeds by induction on the dimension, using the non-degeneracy of the symplectic form to select a pair of vectors $(a_1, b_1)$ with $\langle a_1, b_1 \rangle = 1$, then restricting to the symplectic orthogonal complement of their span and applying the induction hypothesis. The theorem is the linear-algebraic incarnation of Darboux's theorem in differential geometry, which states that all symplectic manifolds of the same dimension are locally symplectomorphic.
