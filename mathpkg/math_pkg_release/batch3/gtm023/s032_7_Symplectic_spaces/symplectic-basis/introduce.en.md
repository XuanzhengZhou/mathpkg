A symplectic basis is the canonical basis for a symplectic vector space, playing a role analogous to an orthonormal basis in an inner product space. It consists of $2n$ vectors paired into $n$ pairs $(a_i, b_i)$ such that the symplectic inner product vanishes on pairs of the same type and satisfies $\langle a_i, b_j \rangle = \delta_{ij}$. In a symplectic basis, the matrix of the symplectic form takes the standard block form $\begin{pmatrix} 0 & I \\ -I & 0 \end{pmatrix}$. The existence of symplectic bases is a fundamental structural result in symplectic linear algebra.
