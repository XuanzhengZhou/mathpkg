A vector space $[linear space]$ is an ordered triple $(X, \cdot, F)$ where $X$ is an additive Abelian group$^1$, $F$ is a field, and $\cdot$ is a function from $F \times X$ into $X$, whose value at $(\alpha, x)$ is denoted $\alpha x$, such that for $\alpha, \beta \in F$ and $x, y \in X$ we have

(i) $\alpha(x + y) = \alpha x + \alpha y$;
(ii) $(\alpha + \beta)x = \alpha x + \beta x$;
(iii) $\alpha(\beta x) = (\alpha \beta)x$;
(iv) $1x = x$, where $1$ is the multiplicative identity of $F$.

The members of $X$ are called vectors and the members of $F$ are called scalars. The operation $\cdot$ is called scalar multiplication. For short we say that $X$ is a vector space over the field $F$.
