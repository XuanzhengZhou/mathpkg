(2.16) Remark. From Theorem (2.15) it follows that the domain and range of a one-to-one function cannot be distinguished from each other by any purely set-theoretic properties. If $X$ and $Y$ are sets for which there is a one-to-one function $f$ with domain $X$ and range $Y$, then for any subset $A$ of $X$ we have $f(A') = f(A)'$. For any family $\{A_i\}_{i \in I}$ of subsets of $X$, we have $f(\bigcup_{i \in I} A_i) = \bigcup_{i \in I} f(A_i)$ and $f(\bigcap_{i \in I} A_i) = \bigcap_{i \in I} f(A_i)$. Similar statements hold for subsets of $Y$ and $f^{-1}$. Thus, all Boolean operations $(\cup, \cap, \triangle, ')$ are preserved under $f$ and $f^{-1}$.
