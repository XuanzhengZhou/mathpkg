Let $u$ and $v$ be cardinal numbers and let $U$ and $V$ be sets such that $\bar{U} = u$ and $\bar{V} = v$. We write $u \leq v$ or $v \geq u$ to mean that $U$ is equivalent to some subset of $V$. One sees by considering composite functions that this definition is unambiguous. We write $u < v$ or $v > u$ to mean that $u \leq v$ and $u \neq v$.
