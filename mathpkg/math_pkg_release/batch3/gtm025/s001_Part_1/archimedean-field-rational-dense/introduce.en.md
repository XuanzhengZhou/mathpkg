In any Archimedean ordered field, the rational elements (those of the form $(m1)(n1)^{-1}$ with integers $m, n$) are dense: between any two distinct elements there lies a rational element. This density property is fundamental for approximation arguments and is equivalent to the Archimedean property in the presence of the other ordered field axioms.
