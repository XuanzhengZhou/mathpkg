The complex numbers are constructed algebraically as the quotient ring $R[t]/(t^2+1)$, where $t^2+1$ generates a maximal ideal in the polynomial ring $R[t]$. Each coset has a unique representative of the form $a + bt$ with $a, b \in R$, and the element $t + J$ plays the role of $i = \sqrt{-1}$. This construction shows that the complex numbers form a field extending the real numbers.
