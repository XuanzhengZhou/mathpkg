Let $z = x + iy$ be a complex number different from $0$. Then $\arg(z)$ is the set of all real numbers $\theta$ such that
$$\cos(\theta) = \frac{x}{|z|}, \quad \sin(\theta) = \frac{y}{|z|}.$$
The unique $\theta \in \arg(z)$ such that $-\pi < \theta \leq \pi$ is called the principal value of the argument and is denoted by $\Arg(z)$.
