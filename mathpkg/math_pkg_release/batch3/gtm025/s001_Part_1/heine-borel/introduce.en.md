The Heine-Borel theorem characterizes compact subsets of Euclidean space as those that are closed and bounded. This is a cornerstone of real analysis: it guarantees that continuous functions on closed bounded sets attain maxima and minima, and that closed bounded sets have the Bolzano-Weierstrass property.
