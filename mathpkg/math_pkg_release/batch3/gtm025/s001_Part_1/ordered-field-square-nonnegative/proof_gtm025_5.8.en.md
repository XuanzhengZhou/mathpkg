If $a \in P$, then $a^2 = a \cdot a \in P$ by (5.7.iii). If $a \in -P$, then $-a \in P$ and $a^2 = (-a)(-a) \in P$ by the same axiom. Since $P \cup \{0\} \cup (-P) = F$ by (5.7.ii), every nonzero $a$ satisfies $a^2 \in P$. Taking $a = 1$ gives $1 = 1^2 \in P$.
