In metric spaces, three notions of compactness coincide: compactness (every open cover has finite subcover), Frechet compactness (every infinite set has a limit point), and sequential compactness (every sequence has a convergent subsequence). This equivalence is fundamental, allowing one to use whichever formulation is most convenient in metric space arguments.
