Let $F$ be an ordered field and $\varnothing \subseteq A \subset F$. An element $b \in F$ is said to be an upper [lower] bound for $A$ if $x \leq b$ $[x \geq b]$ for all $x \in A$. An upper bound $b$ is called the least upper bound or supremum of $A$, and we write $b = \sup A$, if $b$ is less than all other upper bounds for $A$. The greatest lower bound or infimum of $A$, written $\inf A$, is defined analogously.
