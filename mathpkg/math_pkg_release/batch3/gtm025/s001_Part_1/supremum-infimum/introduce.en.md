The supremum of a set is its least upper bound, and the infimum is its greatest lower bound. These concepts are fundamental to real analysis: the completeness of the real numbers is precisely the statement that every nonempty bounded set has a supremum and infimum. They provide the rigorous basis for defining limits, Riemann integrals, and many other analytic constructions.
