The triangle inequality for complex numbers states that the absolute value of a sum never exceeds the sum of the absolute values. Equality holds precisely when the two complex numbers point in the same direction. The proof computes $|z+w|^2 = (z+w)(\bar{z}+\bar{w})$ and uses the bound $\Re(z\bar{w}) \leq |z\bar{w}| = |z||w|$.
