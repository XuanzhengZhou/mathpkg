Let $F$ be any ordered field. A subset $D$ of $F$ is called a Dedekind cut in $F$ if:
(i) $\varnothing \subsetneq D \subsetneq F$;
(ii) the relations $x \in D$ and $y < x$ imply $y \in D$.

(a) Let $D$ be a Dedekind cut in $R$. Prove that $D = \{x \in R : x < a\}$ for some $a \in R$ or $D = \{x \in R : x \leq a\}$ for some $a \in R$.

(b) If $F$ is an ordered field not order isomorphic to $R$, prove that there exists a Dedekind cut $D$ in $F$ such that $D$ does not have the form described in (a).
