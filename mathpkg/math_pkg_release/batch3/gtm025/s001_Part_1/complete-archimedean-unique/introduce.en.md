Up to order-preserving field isomorphism, there is exactly one complete Archimedean ordered field. This uniqueness theorem justifies defining the real numbers axiomatically: any structure satisfying the axioms of a complete Archimedean ordered field is isomorphic to every other such structure. The isomorphism extends the unique embedding of $Q$ by mapping each element to the supremum of the rationals below it.
