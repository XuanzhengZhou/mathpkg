In a complete Archimedean ordered field, every nonempty bounded set has both a supremum and an infimum. This is the least-upper-bound property that characterizes the real numbers. The proof constructs the supremum via a Cauchy sequence obtained by repeatedly bisecting intervals, demonstrating the equivalence between metric completeness and order-completeness in Archimedean ordered fields.
