Every nonempty perfect subset of a complete metric space has cardinality at least $c$ (the cardinality of the continuum). The proof constructs an injection from $\{0,1\}^N$ into $A$ by recursively splitting the set into disjoint closed subsets of shrinking diameter, analogous to the Cantor set construction.
