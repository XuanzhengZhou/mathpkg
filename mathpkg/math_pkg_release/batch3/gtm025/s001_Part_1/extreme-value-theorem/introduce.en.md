The Extreme Value Theorem states that a continuous real-valued function on a compact space attains its supremum and infimum. This follows from the fact that $f(X)$ is compact in $R$, hence closed and bounded by the Heine-Borel theorem. It generalizes the classical result that continuous functions on closed bounded intervals attain maxima and minima.
