The absolute value of a complex number bounds both its real and imaginary parts individually. Conversely, the absolute value is bounded by the sum of the absolute values of the real and imaginary parts. These elementary inequalities follow directly from the definition $|z| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$ where $z = x + iy$, and are used repeatedly in estimates throughout complex analysis.
