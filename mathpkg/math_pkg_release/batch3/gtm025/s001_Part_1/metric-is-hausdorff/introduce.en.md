Metric spaces automatically satisfy the Hausdorff separation axiom: distinct points $x$ and $y$ are separated by the disjoint open balls $B_{\varrho(x,y)/2}(x)$ and $B_{\varrho(x,y)/2}(y)$. This makes metric spaces a particularly well-behaved class of topological spaces.
