The closure, interior, and boundary operators on a topological space satisfy numerous algebraic identities. Key facts include: closure is idempotent and monotone, the complement of the closure equals the interior of the complement, the boundary is the intersection of the closure with the closure of the complement, and closed sets are precisely those containing all their limit points. These identities are proved using De Morgan's laws applied to the axioms for open sets.
