The complex number field $K$ (commonly denoted $C$) consists of numbers of the form $a + bi$ where $a, b \in R$ and $i^2 = -1$. The real part $\Re(z)$, imaginary part $\Im(z)$, absolute value $|z|$, and complex conjugate $\bar{z}$ are the fundamental operations on complex numbers. The absolute value extends the Euclidean distance on $R^2$, while conjugation corresponds to reflection across the real axis.
