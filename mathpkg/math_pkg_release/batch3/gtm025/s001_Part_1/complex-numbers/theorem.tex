The field $R[t]/J$ is called the complex number field and is denoted by $K$. We write the coset $(a + bt) + J$ as $a + bi$. The number $a$ is called the real part and is written $\Re(a + bi)$. The number $b$ is called the imaginary part and is written $\Im(a + bi)$. The complex number $a + 0i$ is written as $a$ alone and $0 + bi$ as $bi$ alone. For $z = x + iy \in K$, the absolute value is defined as $|z| = (x^2 + y^2)^{\frac{1}{2}}$ (the nonnegative square root). The complex conjugate of $z$ is defined as $\bar{z} = x - iy$.
