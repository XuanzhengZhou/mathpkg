The notions of supremum and infimum are extended to the extended real numbers $R^{\#}$: unbounded sets have supremum $\infty$ or infimum $-\infty$. Combined with the completeness of $R$, this ensures every nonvoid subset of $R$ has both a supremum and infimum in $R^{\#}$.
