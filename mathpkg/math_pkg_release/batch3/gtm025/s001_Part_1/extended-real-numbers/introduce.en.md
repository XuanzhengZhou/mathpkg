The extended real numbers $R^{\#} = R \cup \{-\infty, \infty\}$ compactify the real line by adding endpoints at infinity. The linear order is extended naturally, and arithmetic operations are partially extended with careful conventions (e.g., $0 \cdot \infty = 0$ for measure theory). This system is indispensable for measure and integration theory, where suprema of unbounded sets and integrals of nonnegative functions naturally take values in $R^{\#}$.
