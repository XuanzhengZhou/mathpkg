Choose arbitrary positive numbers $\delta$ and $\varepsilon$. For each $n \in N$, let $S_n(\delta) = \{x \in X : |f(x) - f_n(x)| \geq \delta\}$. By (11.30), there exist $J \in \mathcal{A}$ and $n_0 \in N$ such that $\mu(J') < \varepsilon$ and $|f(x) - f_n(x)| < \delta$ for all $x \in J$

The remainder of this section is devoted to an investigation of the structure of measurable functions. As usual, $(X, \mathcal{A})$ is an arbitrary measurable space.
