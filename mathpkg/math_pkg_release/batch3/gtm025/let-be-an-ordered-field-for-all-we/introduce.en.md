(5.11) Theorem. Let $F$ be an ordered field. For all $a, b \in F$, we have $a < b$ or $a = b$ or $a > b$, and only one of these relations holds. is a theorem from Chapter 5 (Set Theory and Algebra) of the book *Real and Abstract Analysis* by Edwin Hewitt and Karl Stromberg (GTM 25). It is part of the development of set-theory.
