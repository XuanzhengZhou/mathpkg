An ordered field $F$ is said to be Archimedean ordered if for all $a \in F$ and all $b \in P$ there exists a positive integer $n$ such that $n b > a$. In intuitive language, this definition means that no matter how large $a$ is and how small $b$ is, successive repetitions of $b$ will eventually exceed $a$. There are ordered fields which are not Archimedean ordered; see (5.39).
