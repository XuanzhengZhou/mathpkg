Let $T$ be any subset of $X$. Then $\mu(T \cap A) = 0$, since $T \cap A \subset A$. Also, we have $\mu(T) \leq \mu(T \cap A) + \mu(T \cap A') = \mu(T \cap A') \leq \mu(T)$. It follows that $\mu(T) = \mu(T \cap A) + \mu(T \cap A') = \mu(T \cap A')$; the first equality shows that $A$ is measurable.
