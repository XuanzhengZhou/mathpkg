Since $A$ is countable, there exists a one-to-one mapping $g$ from $A$ into $N$. Let $a \in A$. Define $f$ on $N$ by

$$f(n) = \begin{cases} g^{-1}(n) & \text{for } n \in \text{rng}g, \\ a & \text{for } n \notin \text{rng}g. \end{cases}$$
