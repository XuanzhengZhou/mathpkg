Let $(X, \mathcal{M})$ and $(Y, \mathcal{N})$ be measurable spaces. Let $\mu$ and $\mu^\dagger$ be $\sigma$-finite measures on $(X, \mathcal{M})$ and let $v$ and $v^\dagger$ be $\sigma$-finite measures on $(Y, \mathcal{N})$. If $\mu^\dagger \ll \mu$ and $v^\dagger \ll v$, then we have $\mu^\dagger \times v^\dagger \ll \mu \times v$ and

(i) $\frac{d(\mu^\dagger \times v^\dagger)}{d(\mu \times v)}(x, y) = \frac{d\mu^\dagger}{d\mu}(x) \cdot \frac{d\nu^\dagger}{d\nu}(y)$

for all $(x, y) \in X \times Y$. If $\mu^\dagger \perp \mu$ or $v^\dagger \perp v$, then we have $\mu^\dagger \times v^\dagger \perp \mu \times v$. Writing subscript a's and s's for the Lebesgue decomposition of $\mu^\dagger$ with respect to $\mu$, etc., we thus have

(ii) $(\mu^\dagger \times v^\dagger)_a = \mu_a^\dagger \times v_a^\dagger$ and

(iii) $(\mu^\dagger \times v^\dagger)_s = (\mu_a^\dagger \times v_s^\dagger) + (\mu_s^\dagger \times v_a^\dagger) + (\mu_s^\dagger \times v_s^\dagger)$.
