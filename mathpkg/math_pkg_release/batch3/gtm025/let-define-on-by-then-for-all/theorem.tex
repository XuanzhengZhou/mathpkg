** Let $f \in \mathfrak{L}_1(R)$. Define $f$ on $R$ by $f(x) = f(-x)$. Then $f(y) = f(y)$ for all $y \in R$.

**Proof.** We have

$$\hat{f}(y) = \left(2\pi\right)^{-\frac{1}{2}} \int_{R} f(x) \exp(-iyx) \, dx$$

$$= \left(2\pi\right)^{-\frac{1}{2}} \int_{R} \overline{f(-x)} \exp(iyx) \, dx$$

$$= \left(2\pi\right)^{-\frac{1}{2}} \int_{R} f(t) \exp(-iyt) \, dt$$

$$= f(y).$$

A first step in defining $f$ for $f \in \mathfrak{L}_2(R)$ follows.
