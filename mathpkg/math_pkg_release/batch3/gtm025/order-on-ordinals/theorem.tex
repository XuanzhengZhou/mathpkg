Let \(A\) be a linearly ordered set and \(x \in A\). The \textbf{initial segment} determined by \(x\) is \(A_x = \{y \in A : y < x\}\). For ordinal numbers \(\alpha = \operatorname{ord} A\) and \(\beta = \operatorname{ord} B\), we write \(\alpha < \beta\) to mean that there exists \(x \in B\) such that \(A \approx B_x\). We write \(\alpha \leq \beta\) to mean \(\alpha < \beta\) or \(\alpha = \beta\).
