Jessen's Pointwise Convergence Theorem strengthens the mean convergence result to almost-everywhere convergence. Interpreting $f_n$ as the conditional expectation of $f$ given the first $n$ coordinates, this result is a martingale convergence theorem for the natural filtration of the product space. The proof applies the general limit theorem (20.56) to the decreasing sequence of $\sigma$-algebras $\mathcal{M}_{\Omega_n'}$.
