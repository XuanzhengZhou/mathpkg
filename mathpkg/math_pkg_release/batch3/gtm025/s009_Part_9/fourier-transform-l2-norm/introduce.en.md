This theorem is the key step toward Plancherel's theorem. It shows the Fourier transform preserves the $L^2$ norm on $\mathfrak{L}_1 \cap \mathfrak{L}_2$, a dense subspace. This isometry allows unique extension to a unitary operator on all of $\mathfrak{L}_2(R)$. The proof uses the convolution of $f$ with its reflected conjugate.
