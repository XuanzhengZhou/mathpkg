There exists a unique bounded linear transformation $T: \mathfrak{L}_2(R) \to \mathfrak{L}_2(R)$ such that $Tf = \hat{f}$ for all $f \in \mathfrak{L}_1(R) \cap \mathfrak{L}_2(R)$. Moreover:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\|Tf\|_2 = \|f\|_2$ for all $f \in \mathfrak{L}_2(R)$;
\item[(ii)] $\langle Tf, Tg \rangle = \langle f, g \rangle$ for all $f, g \in \mathfrak{L}_2(R)$;
\item[(iii)] $T$ carries $\mathfrak{L}_2(R)$ onto $\mathfrak{L}_2(R)$.
\end{enumerate}
