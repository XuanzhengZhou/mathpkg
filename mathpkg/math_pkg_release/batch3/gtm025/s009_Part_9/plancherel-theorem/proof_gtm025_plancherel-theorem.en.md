Define $Tf = \hat{f}$ on $\mathfrak{L}_1 \cap \mathfrak{L}_2$, which is dense in $\mathfrak{L}_2$. For $f \in \mathfrak{L}_2$, take $(f_n) \subset \mathfrak{L}_1 \cap \mathfrak{L}_2$ with $\|f_n - f\|_2 \to 0$. By (21.52), $\|Tf_n - Tf_m\|_2 = \|f_n - f_m\|_2$, so $(Tf_n)$ is Cauchy, defining $Tf$. (i) follows by continuity. (ii) is by polarization. For (iii), the adjoint is $T^*\varphi(x) = \widehat{\varphi}(-x)$, also an isometry, so the range is closed and dense, hence all of $\mathfrak{L}_2$.
