The Uniqueness Theorem states that the Fourier transform on $\mathfrak{L}_1(R)$ is injective: equal Fourier transforms imply equal functions almost everywhere. This ensures no information is lost when passing to the Fourier transform. The proof relies on the pointwise summability theorem with Fejer's kernel.
