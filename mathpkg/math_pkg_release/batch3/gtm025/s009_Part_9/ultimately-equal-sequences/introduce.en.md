The notion of "ultimately equal" sequences captures the tail equivalence relation on infinite product spaces. Two sequences are ultimately equal if they agree after some finite initial segment. Sets invariant under this equivalence relation are called tail events, which are the subject of Kolmogorov's Zero-One Law. This concept is central to probability theory and ergodic theory.
