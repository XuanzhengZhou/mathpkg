For $j=r$: $M_t^{(r)}$ is open, express it as a disjoint union of intervals $]\beta_k,\gamma_k[$. For each interval, using the maximal function definition and letting $x \to \beta_k$, one obtains $\int_{\beta_k}^{\gamma_k} f d\lambda = t(\gamma_k-\beta_k)$. Summing yields the equality. For (ii), $M_t = M_t^{(r)} \cup M_t^{(l)}$, so the estimate follows by subadditivity.
