(i) Since $1-x \leq e^{-x}$ for $0 \leq x \leq 1$, $\prod_{k=1}^n (1-\alpha_k) \leq \exp(-\sum_{k=1}^n \alpha_k) \to 0$. (ii) Only finitely many $\alpha_k$ exceed $1/2$. For the rest, $\log(1-\alpha_k) \geq -\alpha_k - \alpha_k^2$ for small $\alpha_k$. Since $\sum \alpha_k < \infty$ implies $\sum \alpha_k^2 < \infty$, $\sum \log(1-\alpha_k) > -\infty$, so the product is positive.
