$f$ attains $m = \min f$ and $M = \max f$ on $[a,b]$. Then $m\mu([a,b]) \leq \int f d\mu \leq M\mu([a,b])$. If $\mu([a,b]) > 0$, the average lies between $m$ and $M$, so by the intermediate value theorem there exists $\xi$ with $f(\xi)$ equal to this average. If $\mu([a,b]) = 0$, any $\xi$ works.
