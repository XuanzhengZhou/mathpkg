The Hardy-Littlewood Maximal Theorem is one of the most important results in real analysis. It states the maximal operator $f \mapsto f^\Lambda$ is bounded on $\mathfrak{L}_p(R)$ for $p>1$, with explicit constants $\frac{p}{p-1}$ and $\frac{2p}{p-1}$. The proof combines the layer cake representation, measure estimates from (21.75), Fubini, and Holder. Applications span differentiation theory, singular integrals, and PDE.
