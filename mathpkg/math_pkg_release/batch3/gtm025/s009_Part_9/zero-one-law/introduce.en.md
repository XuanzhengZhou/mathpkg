The Zero-One Law (Kolmogorov's Zero-One Law) states that any measurable set in an infinite product space that depends only on tail behavior must have measure either $0$ or $1$. This is a fundamental result in probability theory: events such as convergence of a series of independent random variables or the strong law of large numbers are tail events and therefore occur with probability $0$ or $1$.
