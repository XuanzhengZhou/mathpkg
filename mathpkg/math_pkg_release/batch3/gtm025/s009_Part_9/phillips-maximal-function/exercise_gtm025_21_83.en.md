[K. L. Phillips] Let $f$ be as in (21.74). Prove that
$$f^\Lambda(x) = \sup\left\{\frac{1}{u-t} \int_t^u f d\lambda : -\infty < t \leq x \leq u < \infty, t \neq u\right\}.$$
This shows the two-sided Hardy-Littlewood maximal function can be expressed as a supremum over intervals containing $x$.
