More on the structure of $\mathfrak{L}_1(R)$. We point out some algebraic properties analogous to (20.52) for $\mathfrak{C}_0(X)$.

(a) Let $\mathfrak{I}$ be a closed ideal in $\mathfrak{L}_1(R)$. Prove $f \in \mathfrak{I}$ and $a \in R$ imply $f_a \in \mathfrak{I}$.

(b)-(f) Let $M$ be a nonzero multiplicative linear functional on $\mathfrak{L}_1(R)$. Prove that $M$ has the form $M(f) = (2\pi)^{-\frac{1}{2}} \int_R \overline{\chi(x)} f(x) dx$ where $\chi$ is a continuous character on $R$.
[Hints for (e): By (20.19), there exists $h \in \mathfrak{L}_\infty(R)$ such that $M(f) = (2\pi)^{-\frac{1}{2}} \int_R fh d\lambda$. Take $g$ with $M(g)=1$, then $\overline{\chi(y)} = M(g_y) = (2\pi)^{-\frac{1}{2}} \int_R g(x-y)h(x)dx$. Using Fubini, $(2\pi)^{-\frac{1}{2}} \int_R f(y)\overline{\chi(y)}dy = M(g*f) = M(f)$.]
