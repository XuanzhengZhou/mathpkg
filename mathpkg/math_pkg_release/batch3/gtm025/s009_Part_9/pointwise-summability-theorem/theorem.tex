Let $(k_n)_{n=1}^{\infty} \subset \mathfrak{L}_1(R)$ and $u_n = \hat{k}_n$. Suppose $u_n \in \mathfrak{L}_1(R)$, $(2\pi)^{-\frac{1}{2}}\int_R u_n(t)dt = 1$, $u_n(-t) = u_n(t)$, and there exists $u$ with:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $u \in \mathfrak{L}_1^+([0,\infty])$;
\item[(ii)] $u$ nonincreasing and absolutely continuous on $[0,\infty]$;
\item[(iii)] $|u_n(t)| \leq n u(nt)$ for $t \geq 0$, $n \in N$.
\end{enumerate}
If $f \in \mathfrak{L}_1(R)$ and $x$ is a Lebesgue point for $f$, then
$$\lim_{n \to \infty} f * u_n(x) = f(x).$$
