The Pointwise Summability Theorem provides a general framework for reconstructing a function from its Fourier transform. Given kernels $(k_n)$ whose Fourier transforms $(u_n)$ form an approximate summability kernel, the convolution $f * u_n$ converges pointwise to $f$ at Lebesgue points. This theorem unifies classical summability methods (Fejer, Gauss-Weierstrass, Abel-Poisson) and serves as the foundation for the Fourier inversion theorem.
