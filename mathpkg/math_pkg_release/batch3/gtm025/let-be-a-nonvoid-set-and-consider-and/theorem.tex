Let $X$ be a nonvoid set, and consider $f, g \in \mathfrak{B}(X)$ and $\alpha \in K$. Then the following relations hold:

(i) $\|0\|_u = 0$, $\|f\|_u > 0$ if $f \neq 0$;

(ii) $\|\alpha f\|_u = |\alpha| \|f\|_u$;

(iii) $\|f + g\|_u \leq \|f\|_u + \|g\|_u$;

(iv) $\|fg\|_u \leq \|f\|_u \|g\|_u$.

Similar assertions hold for $f, g \in \mathfrak{B}^r(X)$ and $\alpha \in R$.
