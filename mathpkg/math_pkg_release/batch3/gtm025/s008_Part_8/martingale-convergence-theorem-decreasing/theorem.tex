Let $X$, $\mathcal{M}$, $\mu$, $\eta$, $(\mathcal{M}_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ be as in (20.55). Suppose that

(i) $\mathcal{M}_1 \supset \mathcal{M}_2 \supset \mathcal{M}_3 \supset \cdots,$

and write $\mathcal{M}_0 = \bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} \mathcal{M}_n$. Let $\mu_0$ and $\eta_0$ be $\mu$ and $\eta$, respectively, restricted to $\mathcal{M}_0$. Then both of the functions

$$f = \liminf_{n \to \infty} f_n \quad \text{and} \quad \bar{f} = \limsup_{n \to \infty} f_n$$

are $\mathcal{M}_0$-measurable and are derivatives of $\eta_0$ with respect to $\mu_0$.
