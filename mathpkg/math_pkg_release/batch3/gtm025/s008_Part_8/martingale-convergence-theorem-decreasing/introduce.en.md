This theorem is the decreasing counterpart of the martingale convergence theorem (20.56). For a decreasing sequence of sigma-algebras, the limit inferior and limit superior of the derivatives are measurable with respect to the intersection sigma-algebra and serve as derivatives for the restricted measures. Together, (20.56) and (20.59) are versions of what probabilists call martingale theorems.
