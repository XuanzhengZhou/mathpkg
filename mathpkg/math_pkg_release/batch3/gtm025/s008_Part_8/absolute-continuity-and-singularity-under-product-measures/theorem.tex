Let $(X, \mathcal{M})$ and $(Y, \mathcal{N})$ be measurable spaces. Let $\mu$ and $\mu^\dagger$ be $\sigma$-finite measures on $(X, \mathcal{M})$ and let $\nu$ and $\nu^\dagger$ be $\sigma$-finite measures on $(Y, \mathcal{N})$.

If $\mu^\dagger \ll \mu$ and $\nu^\dagger \ll \nu$, then $\mu^\dagger \times \nu^\dagger \ll \mu \times \nu$ and
$$\frac{d(\mu^\dagger \times \nu^\dagger)}{d(\mu \times \nu)}(x, y) = \frac{d\mu^\dagger}{d\mu}(x) \cdot \frac{d\nu^\dagger}{d\nu}(y).$$

If $\mu^\dagger \perp \mu$ or $\nu^\dagger \perp \nu$, then $\mu^\dagger \times \nu^\dagger \perp \mu \times \nu$.

Writing subscript $a$ and $s$ for the Lebesgue decomposition:
$$(\mu^\dagger \times \nu^\dagger)_a = \mu_a^\dagger \times \nu_a^\dagger,$$
$$(\mu^\dagger \times \nu^\dagger)_s = (\mu_a^\dagger \times \nu_s^\dagger) + (\mu_s^\dagger \times \nu_a^\dagger) + (\mu_s^\dagger \times \nu_s^\dagger).$$
