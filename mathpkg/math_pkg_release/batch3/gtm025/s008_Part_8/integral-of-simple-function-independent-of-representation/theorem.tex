Let $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ be a measure space, $\tau \in F(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$, and let
$$s = \sum_{j=1}^{m} \alpha_j \xi_{A_j} = \sum_{k=1}^{n} \beta_k \xi_{B_k}$$
be two representations of an $\mathcal{A}$-measurable simple function. Then
$$\sum_{j=1}^{m} \alpha_j \tau(A_j) = \sum_{k=1}^{n} \beta_k \tau(B_k).$$
