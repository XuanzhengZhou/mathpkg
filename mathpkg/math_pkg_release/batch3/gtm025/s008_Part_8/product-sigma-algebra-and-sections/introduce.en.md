This definition introduces the fundamental building blocks for product measure theory. The product sigma-algebra is the smallest sigma-algebra containing all measurable rectangles. The notions of x-sections and y-sections allow one to study a set or function on the product space by freezing one coordinate, which is essential for applying Fubini's theorem and for iterated integration.
