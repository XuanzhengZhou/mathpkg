Let $(X, \mathcal{M}, \mu)$ and $(Y, \mathcal{N}, \nu)$ be $\sigma$-finite measure spaces and let $(X \times Y, \mathcal{M} \times \mathcal{N}, \mu \times \nu)$ be the product measure space. Let $f$ be a complex-valued $\mathcal{M} \times \mathcal{N}$-measurable function on $X \times Y$ and suppose that at least one of the three integrals

$$\int_{X \times Y} |f(x, y)| d\mu \times \nu(x, y), \quad \int_Y \int_X |f(x, y)| d\mu(x) d\nu(y), \quad \int_X \int_Y |f(x, y)| d\nu(y) d\mu(x)$$

is finite. Then:

(i) $x \to f(x, y)$ is in $\mathfrak{L}_1(X, \mathcal{M}, \mu)$ for $\nu$-almost all $y \in Y$;

(ii) $y \to f(x, y)$ is in $\mathfrak{L}_1(Y, \mathcal{N}, \nu)$ for $\mu$-almost all $x \in X$;

(iii) $y \to \int_X f(x, y) d\mu(x)$ is in $\mathfrak{L}_1(Y, \mathcal{N}, \nu)$;

(iv) $x \to \int_Y f(x, y) d\nu(y)$ is in $\mathfrak{L}_1(X, \mathcal{M}, \mu)$;

(v) $f \in \mathfrak{L}_1(X \times Y, \mathcal{M} \times \mathcal{N}, \mu \times \nu)$ and
$$\int_{X \times Y} f d\mu \times \nu = \int_Y \int_X f(x, y) d\mu(x) d\nu(y) = \int_X \int_Y f(x, y) d\nu(y) d\mu(x).$$
