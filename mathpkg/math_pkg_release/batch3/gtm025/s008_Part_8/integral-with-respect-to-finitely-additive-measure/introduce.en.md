This definition extends integration from countably additive measures to finitely additive measures tau in F(X, A, mu). The integral is first defined for simple functions via finite sums, then extended to bounded measurable functions by uniform approximation. This construction parallels the standard definition of the Lebesgue integral but does not require countable additivity of the measure.
