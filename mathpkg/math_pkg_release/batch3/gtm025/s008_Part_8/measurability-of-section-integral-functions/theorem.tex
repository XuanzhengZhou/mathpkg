Let $(X, \mathcal{M}, \mu)$ and $(Y, \mathcal{N}, \nu)$ be $\sigma$-finite measure spaces and let $E \in \mathcal{M} \times \mathcal{N}$. Then:

(i) the function $x \to \nu(E_x)$ is $\mathcal{M}$-measurable;

(ii) the function $y \to \mu(E^y)$ is $\mathcal{N}$-measurable;

(iii) $\int_X \nu(E_x) d\mu(x) = \int_Y \mu(E^y) d\nu(y)$.
