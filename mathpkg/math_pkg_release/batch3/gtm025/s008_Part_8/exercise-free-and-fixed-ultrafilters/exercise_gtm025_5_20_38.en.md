Let $X$ be a nonvoid set. A filter $U$ in $X$ is said to be free if $\bigcap U = \varnothing$. All other filters are said to be fixed. Prove the following.

(a) If $U$ is a fixed ultrafilter in $X$, then there is a point $p \in X$ such that $U = \{A \in P(X) : p \in A\}$.

(b) If $X$ is finite, then every ultrafilter in $X$ is fixed.

(c) Use Krein's extension theorem (14.27) to extend $L$ to a nonnegative linear functional on the space $\mathfrak{B}^r([0, 1])$ of all bounded real-valued functions on $[0, 1]$.
