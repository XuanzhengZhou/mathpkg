(a) Let $I = [0, 1]$. Suppose that $E \subset I \times I$ is such that $\lambda(E_x) = \lambda(I \cap (E^y)') = 0$ for all $x, y \in I$. Prove that $E$ is not $\mathcal{M}_\lambda \times \mathcal{M}_\lambda$-measurable.

(b) Prove that sets $E$ as described in part (a) exist. [Let $\Delta$ be the least ordinal number such that $\overline{P_\Delta} = \mathfrak{c}$. Let $\alpha \to x_\alpha$ be any one-to-one mapping of $P_\Delta$ onto $I$. Define $E = \{(x_\alpha, x_\beta) : \beta < \alpha < \Delta\}$. If we accept the continuum hypothesis, then all sections are countable, hence have measure zero. In any case, if these sets are measurable, they have measure zero by (10.30), (6.66), and (6.65).]
