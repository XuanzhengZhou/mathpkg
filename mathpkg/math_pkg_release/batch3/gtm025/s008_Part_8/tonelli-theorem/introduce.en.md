Tonelli's theorem is one of the cornerstones of integration theory on product spaces. It states that for any nonnegative product-measurable function f, the order of integration can be interchanged freely, and all three integrals (product integral and both iterated integrals) are equal. The theorem requires no integrability condition beyond nonnegativity and measurability, making it a powerful tool.
