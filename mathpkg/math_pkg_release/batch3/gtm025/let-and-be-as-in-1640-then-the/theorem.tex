Let $H$ and $\mathfrak{B}(H)$ be as in (16.40). Then the mapping $T \rightarrow T^*$ of $\mathfrak{B}(H)$ into $\mathfrak{B}(H)$ has the following properties:

(i) $(T_1 + T_2)^* = T_1^* + T_2^*$;
(ii) $(\alpha T)^* = \bar{\alpha} T^*$ for $\alpha \in K$;
(iii) $(T_1 T_2)^* = T_2^* T_1^*$;
(iv) $T^{**} = T$;
(v) $\|T^* T\| = \|T\|^2$.
