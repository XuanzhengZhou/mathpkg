Let $v$ be a complex measure on $(X, \mathcal{A})$. Then there exists an $\mathcal{A}$-measurable function $f_0$ on $X$ such that:

(i) $|f_0| = 1$;

(ii) $v(A) = \int_A f_0 d|v|$ for all $A \in \mathcal{A}$;

and

(iii) $\int_X f dv = \int_X ff_0 d|v|$ for all $f \in \mathfrak{L}_1(X, \mathcal{A}, |v|)$.

Also,

(iv) $\left| \int_X f dv \right| \leq \int_X |f| d|v|$ for all $f \in \mathfrak{L}_1(X, \mathcal{A}, |v|)$.
