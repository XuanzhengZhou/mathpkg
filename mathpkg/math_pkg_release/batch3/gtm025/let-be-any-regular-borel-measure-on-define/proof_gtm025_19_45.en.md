(19.49) Remark. There is a different proof of (19.48) which does not use the regularity of $\iota$ except for the requirement that $\iota([a, b]) < \infty$ for all $a < b$ in $R$, this being needed to define $\alpha$. [Of course (12.35) implies that such an $\iota$ is regular.] This shows again that all Borel measures on $R$ satisfying $\iota([a, b]) < \infty$ are Lebesgue-Stieltjes measures and hence are regular. The alternate proof runs as follows. Use (19.48.1) to show that $\iota$ and $\lambda_\alpha$ agree on the algebra of all finite disjoint unions of intervals of any of the forms $[a, b[, ] - \infty, b[,]$ or $[a, \infty[$. Note that $\lambda_\alpha$ and $\iota$ are both $\sigma$-finite on this algebra, and finally use the uniqueness part of Hopf's extension theorem (10.39) to infer that $\lambda_\alpha$ and $\iota$ agree on the $\sigma$-algebra generated by this algebra, namely $\mathcal{B}(R)$.
