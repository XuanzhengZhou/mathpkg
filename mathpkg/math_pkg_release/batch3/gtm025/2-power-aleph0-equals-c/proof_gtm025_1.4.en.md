Binary expansions give an injection from \((0,1)\) into \(\{0,1\}^N\), showing \(\mathfrak{c} \leq 2^{\aleph_0}\). Ternary expansions give an injection from \(\{0,1\}^N\) into \([0,1]\), showing \(2^{\aleph_0} \leq \mathfrak{c}\). The Schroder-Bernstein theorem yields equality.
