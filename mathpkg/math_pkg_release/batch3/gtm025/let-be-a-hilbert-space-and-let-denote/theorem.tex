Let $H$ be a Hilbert space and let $\mathfrak{B}(H)$ denote the space of all bounded linear operators [transformations] from $H$ into $H$. Then $\mathfrak{B}(H)$ is a Banach algebra with unit, where we take composition for multiplication. Moreover, for each $T \in \mathfrak{B}(H)$ there exists a unique $T^* \in \mathfrak{B}(H)$ such that

$$\langle T(x), y \rangle = \langle x, T^*(y) \rangle$$

for all $x, y \in H$. Also $T^{**} = T$ and $\|T^*\| = \|T\|$. The operator $T^*$ is called the adjoint of $T$.
