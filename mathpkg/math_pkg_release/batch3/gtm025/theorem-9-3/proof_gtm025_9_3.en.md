For $f \in \mathfrak{C}_{00}$, write $f^+ = \max\{f, 0\}$ and $f^- = -\min\{f, 0\}$. It is clear that $f = f^+ - f^-$, $f^+ \geq 0$, and $f^- \geq 0$. Thus $I(f) = I(f^+) - I(f^-)$, so $I(f)$ is the difference of two nonnegative real numbers. This proves (i).

If $f, g \in \mathfrak{C}_{00}$ and $f \leq g$, then $g - f \geq 0$ and so $I(g) - I(f) = I(g - f) \geq 0$. This proves (ii).
