(4.35) Exercise. Let $A$ be any infinite set and let $\mathcal{F}$ be the family of all finite subsets of $A$. Prove that $\overline{\mathcal{F}} = \overline{A}$.

(4.36) Exercise. Let $A$ be any infinite set such that $\overline{A} \leq c$, and let $\mathcal{C}$ denote the family of all countable subsets of $A$. Prove that $\overline{\mathcal{C}} = c$.

(4.41) Examples. Let $\{1, 2, \ldots, n\} (n \in N), N$, and $Q$ have their usual orderings. We write ord $\varnothing = 0$, ord $\{1, 2, \ldots, n\} = n$, ord $Q = \eta$, and ord $N = \omega$.
