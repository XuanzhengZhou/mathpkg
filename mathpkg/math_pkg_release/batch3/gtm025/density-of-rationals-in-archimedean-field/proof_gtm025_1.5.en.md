Since \(b - a > 0\), choose \(n \in N\) such that \(\frac{1}{n} < b - a\). Let \(m\) be the smallest integer such that \(\frac{m}{n} > a\). Then \(\frac{m-1}{n} \leq a\), so \(\frac{m}{n} \leq a + \frac{1}{n} < a + (b-a) = b\).
