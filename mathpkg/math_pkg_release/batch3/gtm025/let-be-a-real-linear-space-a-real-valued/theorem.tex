Let $E$ be a real linear space. A real-valued function $p$ defined on $E$ is said to be a sublinear functional if

(i) $p(x + y) \leq p(x) + p(y)$ and

(ii) $p(\alpha x) = \alpha p(x)$

for all $x, y \in E$ and all positive real numbers $\alpha$. Notice that a norm is a sublinear functional.

(14.9) Hahn-Banach Theorem. Let $E$ be a real linear space and let $M$ be a linear subspace of $E$. Suppose that $p$ is a sublinear functional defined on $E$ and that $f$ is a linear functional defined on $M$ such that $f(x) \leq p(x)$ for every $x \in M$. Then there exists a linear functional $g$ defined on $E$ such that $g$ is an extension of $f$ [i.e., $f \subset g$] and $g(x) \leq p(x)$ for every $x \in E$.
