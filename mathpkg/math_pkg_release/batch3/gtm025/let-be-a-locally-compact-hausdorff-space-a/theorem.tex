Let $X$ be a locally compact Hausdorff space. A complex measure $\mu$ defined on the $\sigma$-algebra $\mathcal{B}(X)$ of Borel sets of $X$ is said to be a complex regular Borel measure on $X$ if for each $E \in \mathcal{B}(X)$ and each $\varepsilon > 0$ there exist a compact set $F$ and an open set $U$ such that $F \subset E \subset U$ and $|\mu(A)| < \varepsilon$ for all $A \in \mathcal{B}(X)$ such that $A \subset U \cap F'$. Let $M(X)$ denote the set of all complex regular Borel measures on $X$.

(20.42) Note. If $\mu \in M(X)$ and $\mu \geq 0$, then, since all complex measures are bounded (19.13.v), $\mu$ is a finite measure. Theorem (12.40) thus shows that the definitions of regularity of $\mu$ given in (20.41) and in (12.39) are equivalent.
