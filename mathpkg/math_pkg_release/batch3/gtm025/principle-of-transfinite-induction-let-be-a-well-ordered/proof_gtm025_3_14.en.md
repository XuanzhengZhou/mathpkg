Assume that $W \cap A' \neq \emptyset$ and let $a$ be the smallest member of $W \cap A'$. Then we have $I(a) \subset A$, so $a \in A$. But $a \in W \cap A'$.

The axiom of choice does not perhaps play a central role in analysis, but when it is needed, it is needed most urgently. We shall encounter several such situations in our subsequent study of measure theory and linear functionals. To give an immediate and important application of the axiom of choice, we will prove from Tukey's lemma that every vector space contains a basis. Exact definitions follow.
