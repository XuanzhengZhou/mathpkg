Let $\nu$ be a signed measure on $(X, \mathcal{A})$ and let $(P, P')$ be a Hahn decomposition of $X$ for $\nu$. Define $\nu^+, \nu^-$, and $|\nu|$ on $\mathcal{A}$ by:

$$\nu^+(E) = \nu(E \cap P);$$
$$\nu^-(E) = -\nu(E \cap P');$$

and
$$|\nu|(E) = \nu^+(E) + \nu^-(E)$$

for all $E \in \mathcal{A}$. The set functions $\nu^+, \nu^-$, and $|\nu|$ are called the positive variation of $\nu$, the negative variation of $\nu$, and the total variation of $\nu$, respectively.
