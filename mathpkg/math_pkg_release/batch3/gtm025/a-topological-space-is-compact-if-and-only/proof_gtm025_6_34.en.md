This is nothing but an application of de Morgan's laws (1.9). In fact, $\mathcal{U}$ is an open cover of $X$ if and only if $\mathcal{F} = \{U' : U \in \mathcal{U}\}$ is a family of closed sets with void intersection. Thus every open cover has a finite subcover if and only if every family of closed sets having void intersection has a finite subfamily with void intersection. $\square$
