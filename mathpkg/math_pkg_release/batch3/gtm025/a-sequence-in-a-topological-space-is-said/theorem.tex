A sequence $(x_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ in a topological space $X$ is said to converge to an element $x \in X$, or to have limit $x$, if for each neighborhood $U$ of $x$ there exists a positive integer $n_0$ such that $x_n \in U$ whenever $n \geq n_0$. We write $\lim_{n \to \infty} x_n = x$ and also $x_n \to x$ if $(x_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ converges to $x$.
