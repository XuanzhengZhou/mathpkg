Apply Lemma (4.19) to obtain mappings \(f_i: N \to A_i\) and \(g: N \to I\). Define \(h(m, n) = f_{g(m)}(n)\), which maps \(N \times N\) onto the union. Since \(N \times N\) is countable, so is the union.
