An \textbf{inner product space} is a linear space $H$ over $\mathbb{K}$ ($\mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{C}$) together with a mapping $(x, y) \mapsto \langle x, y \rangle$ of $H \times H$ into $\mathbb{K}$ such that for all $x, y, z \in H$ and $\alpha \in \mathbb{K}$:
$$\langle x + y, z \rangle = \langle x, z \rangle + \langle y, z \rangle; \quad \langle \alpha x, y \rangle = \alpha \langle x, y \rangle;$$
$$\langle y, x \rangle = \overline{\langle x, y \rangle}; \quad \langle x, x \rangle > 0 \text{ if } x \neq 0.$$
