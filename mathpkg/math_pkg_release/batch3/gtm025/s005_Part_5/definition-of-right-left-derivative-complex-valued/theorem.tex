If $f$ is a complex-valued function defined on $[a, a+\delta[$, $f$ is \textbf{right differentiable} at $a$ if
$$\lim_{h \downarrow 0} \frac{f(a+h) - f(a)}{h}$$
exists and is a complex number. Left derivatives and two-sided derivatives are defined similarly. $f$ has a derivative at $a$ iff $\operatorname{Re} f$ and $\operatorname{Im} f$ have finite derivatives at $a$, and then
$$f'(a) = (\operatorname{Re} f)'(a) + i (\operatorname{Im} f)'(a).$$
