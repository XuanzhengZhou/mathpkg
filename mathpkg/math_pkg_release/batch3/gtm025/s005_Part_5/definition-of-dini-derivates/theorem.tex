Let $a \in \mathbb{R}$ and $\delta > 0$. If $f$ is a real-valued function defined on $[a, a+\delta[$, define the four \textbf{Dini derivates} of $f$ at $a$:
$$D_+ f(a) = \varliminf_{h \downarrow 0} \frac{f(a+h) - f(a)}{h}, \quad D^+ f(a) = \varlimsup_{h \downarrow 0} \frac{f(a+h) - f(a)}{h},$$
$$D_- f(a) = \varliminf_{h \uparrow 0} \frac{f(a+h) - f(a)}{h}, \quad D^- f(a) = \varlimsup_{h \uparrow 0} \frac{f(a+h) - f(a)}{h}.$$
$D_+ f(a)$ is the lower right derivate, $D^+ f(a)$ the upper right derivate, $D_- f(a)$ the lower left derivate, and $D^- f(a)$ the upper left derivate.
