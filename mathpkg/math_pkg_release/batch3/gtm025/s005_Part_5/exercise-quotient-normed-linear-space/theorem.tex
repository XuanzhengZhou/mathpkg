Let $E$ be a normed linear space and $M$ a closed linear subspace. Define $\|x + M\| = \inf\{\|x + m\| : m \in M\}$ for $x \in E$. Prove:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] This defines a norm on $E/M$.
\item[(b)] If $E$ is a Banach space, so is $E/M$.
\item[(c)] The natural mapping $\varphi(x) = x+M$ is bounded, $\|\varphi\| \leq 1$, and sends open sets to open sets.
\item[(d)] If $E$ is Banach, $\varphi$ maps the open unit ball of $E$ onto the open unit ball of $E/M$.
\item[(e)] If $M$ and $E/M$ are complete, so is $E$.
\end{itemize}
