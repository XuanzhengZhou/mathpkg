For sets $A$ and $B$, the symmetric difference of $A$ and $B$ is the set $\{x : x \in A$ or $x \in B$ and $x \notin A \cap B\}$, and we write $A \triangle B$ for this set. Note that $A \triangle B$ is the set consisting of those points which are in exactly one of $A$ and $B$, and that it may also be defined by $A \triangle B = (A \cap B') \cup (A' \cap B)$.
