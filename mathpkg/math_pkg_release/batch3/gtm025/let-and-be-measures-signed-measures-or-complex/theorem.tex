Let $\mu$ and $v$ be measures, signed measures, or complex measures on $(X, \mathcal{A})$. We say that $\mu$ and $v$ are [mutually] singular, and we write $\mu \perp v$, if there exists a set $B \in \mathcal{A}$ such that $|\mu|(B) = 0$ and $|v|(B') = 0$. We also say that $\mu[v]$ is singular with respect to $v[\mu]$.
