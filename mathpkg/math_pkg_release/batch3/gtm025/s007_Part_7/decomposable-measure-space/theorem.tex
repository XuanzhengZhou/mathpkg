A measure space $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ is decomposable if there exists $\mathcal{F} \subset \mathcal{A}$ such that: (i) $0 \leq \mu(F) < \infty$ for all $F \in \mathcal{F}$; (ii) sets in $\mathcal{F}$ are pairwise disjoint and $\bigcup \mathcal{F} = X$; (iii) if $\mu(E) < \infty$ then $\mu(E) = \sum_{F \in \mathcal{F}} \mu(E \cap F)$; (iv) if $S \cap F \in \mathcal{A}$ for all $F \in \mathcal{F}$ then $S \in \mathcal{A}$. $\mathcal{F}$ is called a decomposition.
