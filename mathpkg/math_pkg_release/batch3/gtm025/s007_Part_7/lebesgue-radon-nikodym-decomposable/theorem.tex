Let $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ be decomposable with decomposition $\mathcal{F}$, and let $\nu$ be any measure on $(X, \mathcal{A})$ with $\nu \ll \mu$. Then there exists a nonnegative $\mathcal{A}$-measurable $f_0$ such that $\nu(A) = \int_A f_0 d\mu$ for all $A \in \mathcal{A}$, with the integral identity holding for appropriately integrable functions.
