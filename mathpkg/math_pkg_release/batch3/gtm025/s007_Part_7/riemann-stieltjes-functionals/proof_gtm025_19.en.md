Let $I$ be any nonnegative linear functional on $\mathfrak{C}_{00}(\mathbb{R})$ and $\iota$ the measure constructed from $I$ as in \S\S 9-10. By (19.48), there exists $\alpha$ with $\iota = \lambda_\alpha$ on $\mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})$. By Riesz representation (12.36), $I(f) = \int f d\iota = \int f d\lambda_\alpha = S_\alpha(f)$ for all $f \in \mathfrak{C}_{00}(\mathbb{R})$.
