If $\alpha$ is absolutely continuous on $[-p,p]$ for all $p$: for $\lambda(A)=0$, cover $A \cap [-p,p]$ by intervals with total length $< \delta$, giving $\iota(A \cap [-p,p]) < \varepsilon$; letting $p \to \infty$ and $\varepsilon \to 0$ yields $\iota(A)=0$. Conversely, if $\iota \ll \lambda$, Radon-Nikodym gives $\iota(E) = \int_E \alpha' d\lambda$, and by (18.14) one deduces absolute continuity of $\alpha$.
