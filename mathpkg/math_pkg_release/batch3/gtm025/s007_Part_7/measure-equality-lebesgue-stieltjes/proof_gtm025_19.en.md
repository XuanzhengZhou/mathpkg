Define $\beta(t) = \lambda_\alpha([0,t])$ for $t>0$, $\beta(0)=0$, $\beta(t)=-\lambda_\alpha([t,0])$ for $t<0$. Using left continuity and approximating $\xi_{[0,t]}$ by continuous functions via Riesz representation (12.36) and dominated convergence (12.24), one shows $\alpha = \beta$. Equality of the measures follows from uniqueness of measures determined by their values on intervals.
