Let $(X, \mathcal{A})$ be a measurable space and let $\mu$ and $\nu$ be measures with $\mu(X) < \infty$ and $\nu \ll \mu$. Then there exists $E \in \mathcal{A}$ such that: (i) for $A \subset E$, $\nu(A) = 0$ or $\nu(A) = \infty$; (ii) for $A \subset E$, $\mu(A) = 0$ if $\nu(A) = 0$; (iii) $\nu$ is $\sigma$-finite on $E'$.
