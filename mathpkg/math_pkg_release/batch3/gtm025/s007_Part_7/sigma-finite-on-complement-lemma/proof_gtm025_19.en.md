Consider $\mathcal{D} = \{B: C \subset B, \nu(C) > 0 \Rightarrow \mu(C) > 0\}$. Maximize $\mu(D)$ for $D \in \mathcal{D}$ using Zorn or sequential exhaustion to obtain a maximal set. Then construct $\mathcal{F} = \{F \subset D': \nu \text{ is } \sigma\text{-finite on } F\}$ and exhaust similarly. The union of the exhaustion gives a set on whose complement $\nu$ is $\sigma$-finite. The final $E$ is constructed from the remaining part where $\nu$ takes only extreme values.
