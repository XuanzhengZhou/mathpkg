Let $(X, \mathcal{A})$ be a measurable space and let $\mu$ and $\nu$ be finite measures on $(X, \mathcal{A})$ such that $\nu \ll \mu$. Then there exists $f_0 \in \mathfrak{L}_1^+(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ such that $\int_X f \, d\nu = \int_X f f_0 \, d\mu$ for all nonnegative $\mathcal{A}$-measurable $f$. In particular, $\nu(A) = \int_A f_0 \, d\mu$ for all $A \in \mathcal{A}$.
