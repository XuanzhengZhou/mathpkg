For $0 < s < t$, $\alpha(t)-\alpha(s) = \iota(]s,t]) \geq 0$, showing $\alpha$ is nondecreasing. Left continuity: $\bigcap_{n}[t-1/n,t] = \varnothing$, so by continuity of measures, $\lim_n \iota([t-1/n,t]) = 0$, giving $\lim_n \alpha(t-1/n) = \alpha(t)$. The limit statements follow from (10.13).
