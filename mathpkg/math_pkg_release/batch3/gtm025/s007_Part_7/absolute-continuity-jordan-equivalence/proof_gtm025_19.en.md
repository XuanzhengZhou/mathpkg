If $\nu \ll \mu$ and $|\mu|(E) = 0$, then $\nu(E) = 0$ and each $\nu_k(E) \leq |\nu|(E)$ yields $\nu_k \ll \mu$. Conversely, if each $\nu_k \ll \mu$, then $|\mu|(E) = 0$ implies $\nu_k(E) = 0$ for all $k$, hence $\nu(E) = \sum \alpha_k \nu_k(E) = 0$.
