Extends Radon-Nikodym to complex measures. Using the Jordan decomposition $\nu = \sum \alpha_k \nu_k$, apply the $\sigma$-finite version to each $\nu_k$ to get densities $f_k$, then set $f_0 = \sum \alpha_k f_k$. The $\mathfrak{L}_1$ norm of $f_0$ equals the total variation of $\nu$.
