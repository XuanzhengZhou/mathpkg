Use the Lebesgue decomposition (19.42) to write $\nu = \nu_1 + \nu_2$ with $\nu_1 \ll \mu$ and $\nu_2 \perp \mu$. If $\nu$ and $\mu$ are not mutually singular, then $\nu_1 \neq 0$. Take $\tilde{\nu} = \nu_1$.
