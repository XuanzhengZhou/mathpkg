Every regular Borel measure $\iota$ on $\mathbb{R}$ decomposes uniquely as $\iota = \iota_a + \iota_s + \iota_d$, where $\iota_a \ll \lambda$, $\iota_s \perp \lambda$ and is continuous, and $\iota_d$ is purely discontinuous. The corresponding function $\alpha = \alpha_a + \alpha_s + \alpha_d$ has $\alpha_a$ absolutely continuous, $\alpha_s$ continuous with $\alpha_s'=0$ a.e., and $\alpha_d$ a saltus function. Moreover, $\iota_a(E) = \int_E \alpha'(t) dt$ for all Borel $E$.
