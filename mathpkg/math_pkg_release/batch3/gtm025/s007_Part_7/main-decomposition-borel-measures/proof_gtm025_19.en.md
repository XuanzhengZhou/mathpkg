Let $\iota_d$ be the purely discontinuous part as in (19.57). The remainder $\iota_c = \iota - \iota_d$ is continuous. Apply the Lebesgue decomposition (19.42) to $\iota_c$ with respect to $\lambda$ to get $\iota_c = \iota_a + \iota_s$ with $\iota_a \ll \lambda$ and $\iota_s \perp \lambda$. Then $\alpha = \alpha_a + \alpha_s + \alpha_d$ where each corresponds to its measure. Formula (iii) follows from Radon-Nikodym and the definition of $\alpha_a$.
