If $\iota$ and $\lambda$ are not mutually singular, by (19.42) $\iota = \iota_1 + \iota_2$ with $\iota_1 \neq 0$, $\iota_1 \ll \lambda$. The function $\alpha_1$ corresponding to $\iota_1$ is absolutely continuous, with $\alpha_1' > 0$ on a set of positive measure, so $\alpha' \neq 0$ a.e. Conversely, if $\alpha' = 0$ a.e., the absolutely continuous part must vanish, forcing $\iota \perp \lambda$.
