Let $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ be a measure space, let $1 \leq p < \infty$, and let $f$ and $(f_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ be functions in $\mathfrak{L}_p(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$. If $p > 1$, then $(f_n)$ is said to converge to $f$ weakly [in $\mathfrak{L}_p$] if

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \int_X f_n g d\mu = \int_X f g d\mu$$

for every $g \in \mathfrak{L}_{p'}$. If $p = 1$, then $(f_n)$ is said to converge to $f$ weakly [in $\mathfrak{L}_1$] if

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \int_X f_n g d\mu = \int_X f g d\mu$$

for every bounded $\mathcal{A}$-measurable function $g$ on $X$.
