The inequality

$$|F(v) G(v) - F(u) G(u)| \leq \|F\|_u |G(v) - G(u)| + \|G\|_u |F(v) - F(u)|$$

shows that $FG$ is absolutely continuous. Hence $FG$ is differentiable a.e., and

$$(FG)' = FG' + F'G,$$

as an elementary calculation shows. By (18.3) we have $G' = g$ a.e. and $F' = f$ a.e.; thus (i) follows from (18.16).
