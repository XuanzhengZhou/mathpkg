For subsets $A$ and $B$ of $R$, let $A + B = \{x + y : x \in A, y \in B\}$, $A - B = \{x - y : x \in A, y \in B\}$, and $-A = \{-x : x \in A\}$. For $x \in R$, the set $\{x\} + A$ will be written $x + A$, and is called the translate of $A$ by $x$. The sets $AB, A^{-1}$ [if $0 \notin A$], $AB^{-1}$, and $xA$ are defined analogously.
