Let $X$ be a locally compact Hausdorff space, and let $(X, \mathcal{M}_i, \iota)$ be a measure space constructed as in §§ 9 and 10. There exists a family $\mathcal{F}_0$ of subsets of $X$ with the following properties:

(i) the sets in $\mathcal{F}_0$ are compact and have [finite!] positive measure;

(ii) the sets in $\mathcal{F}_0$ are pairwise disjoint;

(iii) if $F \in \mathcal{F}_0$, $U$ is open, and $U \cap F \neq \emptyset$, then $\iota(U \cap F) > 0$;

(iv) if $E \in \mathcal{M}_i$ and $\iota(E) < \infty$, then $E \cap F$ is nonvoid for only a countable number of sets $F \in \mathcal{F}_0$;

(v) the set $D = X \cap (\cup \mathcal{F}_0)'$ is $\iota$-measurable and is locally $\iota$-null;

(vi) if $Y$ is a subset of $X$ such that $Y \cap F \in \mathcal{M}_i$ for all $F \in \mathcal{F}_0$, then $Y \in \mathcal{M}_i$.
