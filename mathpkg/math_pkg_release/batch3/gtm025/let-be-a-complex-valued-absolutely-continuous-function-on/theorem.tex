Let $\varphi$ be a complex-valued, absolutely continuous function on $[a, b]$. For $[x, y] \subset [a, b]$, let

$$\omega_{\varphi}(x, y) = \sup \{ |\varphi(u) - \varphi(v)| : u, v \in [x, y] \}.$$

Then for every $\varepsilon > 0$, there is a $\delta > 0$ such that

(i) $\sum_{k=1}^{n} \omega_{\varphi}(c_k, d_k) < \varepsilon$

for every finite, pairwise disjoint, family $\{]c_k, d_k[\}_{k=1}^{n}$ of open subintervals of $[a, b]$ for which

(ii) $\sum_{k=1}^{n}(d_k - c_k) < \delta.$
