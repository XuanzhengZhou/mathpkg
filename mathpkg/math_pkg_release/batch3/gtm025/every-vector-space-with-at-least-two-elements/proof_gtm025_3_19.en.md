Let $X$ be a vector space with at least two elements. Let $x \neq 0$ in $X$. Then (3.16) shows that $\{x\}$ is a linearly independent set. Thus the family $\mathcal{F}$ of all linearly independent subsets of $X$ is nonvoid. The definition of linear independence shows at once that $\mathcal{F}$ is of finite character. Tukey's lemma proves that $\mathcal{F}$ contains a maximal member, i.e., $X$ contains a basis.
