Let $X$ be a compact space. Assume that $X$ has an infinite subset $A$ with no limit points in $X$. Then $A$ is a closed set (6.7.v). Moreover each $a \in A$ has a neighborhood $U_a$ containing no point of $A \cap \{a'\}$. Then $\{U_a : a \in A\} \cup \{A'\}$ is an open cover of $X$ with no finite subcover. This contradiction completes the proof. $\square$
