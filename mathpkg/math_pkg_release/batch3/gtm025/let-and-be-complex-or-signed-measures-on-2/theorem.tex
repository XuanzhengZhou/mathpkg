Let $\mu, v$, and $\sigma$ be complex or signed measures on $(X, \mathcal{A})$ such that $v + \sigma$ is defined, and let $\alpha$ be in $K$. Then:

(i) $v \ll \mu$ and $\sigma \ll \mu$ imply $\alpha v \ll \mu$ and $(v + \sigma) \ll \mu$; and

(ii) $v \perp \mu$ and $\sigma \perp \mu$ imply $\alpha v \perp \mu$ and $(v + \sigma) \perp \mu$.
