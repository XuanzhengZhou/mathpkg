(10.18) Note. In (10.3), as we trust the reader has noted, countably additive measures are defined on algebras $A$ that need not be $\sigma$-algebras. This is a technicality, but it is occasionally quite useful. There is a technique for extending a countably additive measure to a $\sigma$-algebra containing $A$. This is not an essential point in our development of measure theory, and we relegate it to an exercise (10.36). Note however that the term measure space is reserved for a triple $(X, A, \mu)$ in which $A$ is a $\sigma$-algebra and $\mu$ is countably additive on $A$.
