Let $X$ be a locally compact Hausdorff space and $\iota$ a measure as in Section 9. Let $E \subset X$ be $\iota$-measurable with $\iota(E) < \infty$, and let $f$ be an $\mathcal{M}_\iota$-measurable function on $X$ such that $f(x) = 0$ for $x \in E'$. Then for each $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists $g \in \mathfrak{C}_{00}(X)$ such that
$$\iota(\{x \in X : f(x) \neq g(x)\}) < \varepsilon.$$
Moreover, if $\|f\|_u < \infty$, then $g$ can be chosen so that $\|g\|_u \leq \|f\|_u$.
