Let $\mathcal{L}$ be a lattice of subsets of $X$ (closed under $\cup$ and $\cap$, containing $X$ and $\emptyset$). Let $\mathcal{D}$ be proper differences $B \cap A'$ ($A, B \in \mathcal{L}$, $A \subset B$) and $\mathcal{U}$ the finite disjoint unions from $\mathcal{D}$. Prove that $\mathcal{U}$ is the smallest algebra containing $\mathcal{L}$. If $\mu$ and $\nu$ are measures on a $\sigma$-algebra containing $\mathcal{L}$ that agree on $\mathcal{L}$, they agree on $\mathcal{U}$.
