Let $(X, \mathcal{M}, \mu)$ be a measure space and $\tilde{\mathcal{M}} = \{E \in \mathcal{M} : \mu(E) < \infty\}$. For $A, B \in \tilde{\mathcal{M}}$, define $\varrho(A, B) = \mu(A \triangle B)$. Identifying sets with $\mu(A \triangle B) = 0$, prove that $(\tilde{\mathcal{M}}, \varrho)$ is a complete metric space.
