Let $X$ be a set and $\mathcal{A}$ an algebra of subsets of $X$. Let $\mu$ be a countably additive measure on $\mathcal{A}$. Define $\bar{\mu}(E) = \inf\{\sum \mu(A_n) : E \subset \bigcup A_n, A_n \in \mathcal{A}\}$ for all $E \subset X$. Prove that $\bar{\mu}$ is an outer measure extending $\mu$, that every $A \in \mathcal{A}$ is $\bar{\mu}$-measurable, and that $\mu$ can be extended to a countably additive measure on a $\sigma$-algebra containing $\mathcal{A}$ (E. Hopf's extension theorem).
