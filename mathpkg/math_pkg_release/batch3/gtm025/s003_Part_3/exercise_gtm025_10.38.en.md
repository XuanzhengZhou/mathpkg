Let $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ be a measure space. Prove that $\mu$ can be extended to a measure $\bar{\mu}$ on a $\sigma$-algebra $\bar{\mathcal{A}}$ such that every subset of every set of $\mu$-measure $0$ is $\bar{\mu}$-measurable and has $\bar{\mu}$-measure $0$ (completion of a measure space).
