**Proof.** Each case follows from (11.7) by choosing an appropriate Borel measurable $\varphi$:
(i) $\varphi(t) = t + \alpha$ for $t \in \mathbb{R}$, $\varphi(\pm\infty) = \pm\infty$.
(ii) $\varphi(t) = \alpha t$ for $t \in \mathbb{R}$, with sign-dependent handling at $\pm\infty$.
(iii) $\varphi(t) = |t|^p$ for $t \in \mathbb{R}$, $\varphi(\pm\infty) = \infty$ if $p > 0$; $\varphi(t) = t^m$ for integer $m$.
(iv) $\varphi(t) = 1/t$ for $t \neq 0, \pm\infty$, with $\varphi(0) = \beta_0$, $\varphi(\pm\infty) = \beta_{\pm}$ arbitrary.
Each $\varphi$ is continuous on $\mathbb{R}$, hence Borel measurable. $\square$
