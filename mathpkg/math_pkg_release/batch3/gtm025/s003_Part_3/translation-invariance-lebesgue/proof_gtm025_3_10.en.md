**Proof.** By (9.24) and (9.23),
$$\lambda(A) = \inf \left\{ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (b_n - a_n) : A \subset \bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty} ]a_n, b_n[ \right\}.$$
The inclusions $A \subset \bigcup ]a_n, b_n[$, $x + A \subset \bigcup ]a_n + x, b_n + x[$, and $-A \subset \bigcup ]-b_n, -a_n[$ are mutually equivalent, and all three interval unions have the same total length. Hence $\lambda(x + A) = \lambda(A) = \lambda(-A)$. $\square$
