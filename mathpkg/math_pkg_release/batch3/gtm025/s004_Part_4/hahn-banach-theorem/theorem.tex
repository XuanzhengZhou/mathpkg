Let $E$ be a real linear space and let $M$ be a linear subspace of $E$. Suppose that $p$ is a sublinear functional defined on $E$ and that $f$ is a linear functional defined on $M$ such that $f(x) \le p(x)$ for every $x \in M$. Then there exists a linear functional $g$ defined on $E$ such that $g$ is an extension of $f$ [i.e., $f \subset g$] and $g(x) \le p(x)$ for every $x \in E$.
