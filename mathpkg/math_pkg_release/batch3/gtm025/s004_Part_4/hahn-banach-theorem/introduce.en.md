The Hahn-Banach theorem is one of the most important results in functional analysis. It guarantees that a linear functional defined on a subspace and dominated by a sublinear functional can be extended to the whole space while preserving the domination. This theorem has countless applications, including the existence of bounded linear functionals, separation of convex sets, and the duality theory of Banach spaces.
