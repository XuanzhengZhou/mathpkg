Suppose $f \geq 0$, $f \in \mathfrak{L}_p$. By (11.35) there exists a nondecreasing sequence $(s_n)$ of nonnegative simple functions with $s_n \to f$ a.e. Since $|f - s_n|^p \leq f^p \in \mathfrak{L}_1$, Lebesgue's dominated convergence theorem gives $\lim \|f - s_n\|_p = 0$. For complex-valued $f$, apply to real and imaginary parts.
