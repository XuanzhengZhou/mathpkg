Let $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ be a measure space.

(a) Find a function $f$ on $X$ such that $f \in \mathfrak{L}_p$ and $f \notin \mathfrak{L}_q$ if $p \neq q$.

(b) Find a function $f$ on $X$ such that $f \in \mathfrak{L}_{p-\delta}$ for all $\delta > 0$ and $f \notin \mathfrak{L}_p$. [Recall that $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} n^{-\alpha}$ converges if $\alpha > 1$ and diverges if $0 < \alpha \le 1$.]

(c) Find a nonnegative real-valued function that is in no $\mathfrak{L}_p$.
