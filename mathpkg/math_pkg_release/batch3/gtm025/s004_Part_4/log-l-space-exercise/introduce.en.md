Define $\Phi$ on $[0, \infty[$ by $\Phi(t) = 0$ if $0 \le t \le 1$ and $\Phi(t) = t \cdot \log t$ if $t \ge 1$. The Birnbaum-Orlicz space $\mathfrak{L}_{\Phi}(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ [see (13.36)] is often denoted $\mathfrak{L} \log^+ \mathfrak{L}$. Prove that for the measure space $([0, 1], \mathcal{M}_{\lambda}, \lambda)$, the space $\mathfrak{L} \log^+ \mathfrak{L}$ is strictly contained in $\mathfrak{L}_1$.
