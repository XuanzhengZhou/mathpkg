This theorem is the key to proving countable additivity of the Lebesgue integral. It states that if a nondecreasing sequence of nonnegative simple functions $(g_n)$ pointwise dominates a simple function $h$ in the limit, then the limit of the integrals $L(g_n)$ dominates $L(h)$. The proof involves a careful case analysis based on whether $\mu(X)$ is finite or infinite and whether the maximum coefficient of $h$ is finite or infinite.
