In the nontrivial case $0 < \int g^{p'} d\mu < \infty$, since $p' < 0$, $g(x) > 0$ a.e. Let $q = 1/p$, and define $\varphi = g^{-1/q}$ and $\psi = g^{1/q} f^{1/q}$. Then $\varphi^{q'} = g^{p'}$, so $\varphi \in \mathfrak{L}_q$. Apply Holder's inequality (13.4) to $\varphi$ and $\psi$ with exponent $q > 1$ to obtain the reverse inequality.
