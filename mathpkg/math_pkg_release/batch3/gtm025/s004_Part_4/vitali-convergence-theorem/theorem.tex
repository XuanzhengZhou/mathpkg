Let $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ be a measure space, $1 \leq p < \infty$, $(f_n) \subset \mathfrak{L}_p$, and $f_n \to f$ $\mu$-a.e. Then $f \in \mathfrak{L}_p$ and $\|f - f_n\|_p \to 0$ iff: (i) for every $\varepsilon > 0$ there exists $A \in \mathcal{A}$ with $\mu(A) < \infty$ such that $\int_{A'} |f_n|^p d\mu < \varepsilon$ for all $n$; (ii) for every $\varepsilon > 0$ there exists $\delta > 0$ such that $\mu(E) < \delta$ implies $\int_E |f_n|^p d\mu < \varepsilon$ for all $n$.
