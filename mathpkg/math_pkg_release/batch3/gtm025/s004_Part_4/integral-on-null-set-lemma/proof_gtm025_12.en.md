Assume first that $f \geq 0$ and $L(f)$ is defined. Let $\{B_k\}_{k=1}^m$ be a measurable dissection of $E$. Extend to a dissection of $X$ by adding $E'$ with infimum 0. This gives $L_E(f) \leq L(f)$. Conversely, suppose $\gamma < L_E(f)$. Take a dissection $\{B_k\}$ of $E$ with $\gamma < \sum \inf_{B_k} f \cdot \mu(B_k)$. Appending $E'$ gives $\gamma < L(f)$, so $L_E(f) \leq L(f)$. The assertion for arbitrary functions follows immediately.
