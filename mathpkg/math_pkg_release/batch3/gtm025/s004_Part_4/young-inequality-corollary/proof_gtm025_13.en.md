For $u \in [0,\infty[$, define $\varphi(u) = u^{p-1}$; $\varphi$ is continuous and strictly increasing, $\lim \varphi(u) = \infty$, $\varphi(0) = 0$. The inverse is $\psi(v) = v^{1/(p-1)}$. Then $\Phi(a) = \int_0^a u^{p-1} du = a^p/p$ and $\Psi(b) = \int_0^b v^{1/(p-1)} dv = b^{p'}/p'$. The corollary follows from (13.2).
