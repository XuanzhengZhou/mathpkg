Let $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ be a measure space and $(X, \bar{\mathcal{A}}, \bar{\mu})$ its completion (11.21).

(a) Let $\bar{f}$ be a complex- or extended real-valued $\bar{\mathcal{A}}$-measurable function defined on $X$. Prove that there is an $\mathcal{A}$-measurable function $f$ on $X$ such that $f = \bar{f}$ $\bar{\mu}$-almost everywhere.

(b) Prove that $\bar{f} \in \mathcal{L}_1(X, \bar{\mathcal{A}}, \bar{\mu})$ if and only if there exists $f \in \mathcal{L}_1(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ such that $f = \bar{f}$ $\bar{\mu}$-a.e.

(c) Let $X$ be a locally compact, $\sigma$-compact Hausdorff space and let $\iota$ be the measure defined from a nonnegative linear functional $I$ as in \S 9. Prove that $(X, \mathcal{M}_i, \iota)$ is the completion of $(X, \mathcal{B}(X), \iota)$. What does this tell you about Borel measurable functions and arbitrary $\mathcal{M}_i$-measurable functions?

(d) Drop the hypothesis in part (c) that $X$ be $\sigma$-finite. Let $f$ be an arbitrary function in $\mathfrak{L}_1(X, \mathcal{M}_i, \iota)$. Prove that there is a Borel measurable function $f_1$ on $X$ such that $f_1 = f$ $\iota$-almost everywhere and such that $|f_1| \le |f|$.
