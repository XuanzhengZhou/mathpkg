For any nonnegative simple function $f = \sum_{k=1}^{n} \alpha_k \xi_{E_k}$ expressed as a linear combination of characteristic functions of pairwise disjoint measurable sets $E_k$, the Lebesgue upper integral $L(f)$ exists and equals the natural weighted sum $\sum_{k=1}^{n} \alpha_k \mu(E_k)$. This theorem establishes the fundamental link between the abstract definition of the Lebesgue integral via measurable dissections and the concrete computation for simple functions. It is the cornerstone upon which the entire Lebesgue integration theory is built.
