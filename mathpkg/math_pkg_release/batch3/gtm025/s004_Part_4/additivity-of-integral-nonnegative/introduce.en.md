The Lebesgue upper integral is additive for nonnegative $\mathcal{A}$-measurable functions. The proof approximates $f$ and $g$ by nondecreasing sequences of nonnegative simple functions, uses the additivity of $L$ on simple functions (12.7), and applies the limit theorem (12.11).
