Lebesgue's Dominated Convergence Theorem (DCT) is arguably the most important convergence theorem in analysis. It states that if a sequence of integrable functions converges pointwise almost everywhere and is dominated by an integrable function, then the limit is integrable and the integral of the limit equals the limit of the integrals.
