For type G2 we have $\langle \alpha, \beta \rangle = -1$, $\langle \beta, \alpha \rangle = -3$. The proof proceeds through a series of 35 equations, systematically applying Lemma 32.5 and the actions of Int $n_{\alpha}$ and Int $n_{\beta}$ to determine the structural constants. The key steps involve comparing the two ways to rewrite certain products of root group elements and using the fact that certain root combinations are not in $\Phi$ (e.g., $\alpha - \beta, 2\alpha - \beta \notin \Phi$). The final result confirms that all constants are $\pm 1$ or $\pm 2$ and that $(n_{\alpha}n_{\beta})^{6} = e$, establishing the Weyl group order for type G2.
