Let $I$ be the ideal in $K[G]$ vanishing on $H$. Then $I$ is finitely generated, and the span of a finite generating set lies in a finite dimensional subspace $W \subset K[G]$ stable under all $\rho_x$ ($x \in G$) (Proposition 8.6). Set $M = W \cap I$, which generates $I$ as an ideal. Notice that $M$ is stable under all $\rho_x$ ($x \in H$), since $H = \{x \in G \mid \rho_x I = I\}$ (Lemma 8.5). Moreover, $M$ is stable under all $*X$ ($X \in \mathfrak{h}$) (Proposition 10.2).

We claim that in fact:
$$H = \{x \in G \mid \rho_x M = M\}, \qquad \mathfrak{h} = \{X \in \mathfrak{g} \mid M * X \subset M\}.$$

If $\rho_x M = M$ ($x \in G$), then since $I$ is the ideal generated by $M$, and $\rho_x$ is an algebra automorphism, $\rho_x I = I$. By Lemma 8.5, this means $x \in H$. Conversely, if $x \in H$, then $\rho_x I = I$, and since $M = W \cap I$ with $W$ stable under $\rho_x$, we have $\rho_x M = M$. The argument for the Lie algebra statement is similar, using Proposition 10.2.

Now let $d = \dim M$ and set $V = \wedge^d W$. Let $L = \wedge^d M$, a $1$-dimensional subspace of $V$. Define $\varphi$ as the natural action of $G$ on $\wedge^d W$ induced by $\rho$. By Lemma 11.1:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\varphi(x)L = L \iff \rho_x M = M \iff x \in H$.
\item $d\varphi(X)L \subset L \iff M * X \subset M \iff X \in \mathfrak{h}$.
\end{itemize}
This completes the proof.
