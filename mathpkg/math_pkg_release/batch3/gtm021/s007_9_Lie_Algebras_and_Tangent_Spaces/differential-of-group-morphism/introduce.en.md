The differential $d\varphi$ of a morphism $\varphi: G \to G'$ of algebraic groups is defined as the tangent map at the identity. Via the isomorphism $\theta$ of Theorem 9.1, $d\varphi$ corresponds to the natural map $\mathcal{L}(G) \to \mathcal{L}(G')$ induced by composition with $\varphi^*$. This makes the assignment $G \mapsto \mathcal{L}(G)$ into a functor from algebraic groups to Lie algebras.
