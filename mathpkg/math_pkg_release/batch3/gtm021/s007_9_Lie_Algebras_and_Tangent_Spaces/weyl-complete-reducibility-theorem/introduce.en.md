Weyl's theorem on complete reducibility, originally proved for semisimple Lie algebras, implies the corresponding statement for semisimple algebraic groups in characteristic 0 (via the correspondence of Theorem 13.2). This means every finite dimensional representation splits as a direct sum of irreducible representations. A key application: if $G$ acts on the polynomial ring $R = K[T_1,\ldots,T_n]$, the ring of invariants $R^G$ is finitely generated (solution of Hilbert's 14th Problem for semisimple or, more generally, reductive groups). The proof shows that $R = R^G \oplus S$ where $S$ is the sum of nontrivial irreducible submodules, and deduces that $R^G$ is Noetherian.
