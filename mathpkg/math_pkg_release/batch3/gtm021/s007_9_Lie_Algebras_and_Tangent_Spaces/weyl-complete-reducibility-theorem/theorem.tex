Assume $\operatorname{char} K = 0$. Let $G$ be a connected semisimple algebraic group. Every (finite dimensional) rational representation $\rho: G \to \operatorname{GL}(V)$ is completely reducible: each subspace of $V$ stable under $G$ has a complementary $G$-stable subspace. Equivalently, $V$ decomposes as a direct sum of irreducible $G$-modules. This applies in particular to the adjoint representation and has important consequences, such as the solution of Hilbert's 14th Problem for reductive groups.
