The general linear Lie algebra $\mathfrak{gl}(n,K)$ is the fundamental example of a Lie algebra: the space of all $n \times n$ matrices with the commutator bracket. It serves as the Lie algebra of the general linear group $\mathrm{GL}(n,K)$ and is the ambient Lie algebra within which the Lie algebras of all closed subgroups of $\mathrm{GL}(n,K)$ are contained.
