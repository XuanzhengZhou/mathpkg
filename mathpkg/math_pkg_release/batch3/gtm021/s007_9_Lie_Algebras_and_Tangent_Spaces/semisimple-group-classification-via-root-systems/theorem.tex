Assume $\operatorname{char} K = 0$. Let $G$ be a connected semisimple algebraic group. The Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ is determined up to isomorphism by its root system, which is a disjoint union of irreducible root systems belonging to the simple ideals of $\mathfrak{g}$. The irreducible root systems comprise four infinite families $A_\ell, B_\ell, C_\ell, D_\ell$ and five exceptional types $E_6, E_7, E_8, F_4, G_2$.

The group $G$ is determined up to "local isomorphism" (isomorphism up to a finite central subgroup) by $\mathfrak{g}$. The possible central subgroups $Z(G)$ are isomorphic to subgroups of the fundamental group of the root system (the weight lattice modulo the root lattice).
