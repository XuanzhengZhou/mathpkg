The construction of $G/N$ uses Chevalley's theorem on the existence of a representation $G \to \operatorname{GL}(V)$ such that $N$ is the stabilizer of a line. Then $G/N$ is realized as the orbit of that line in the projective space. The Lie algebra statement follows from the surjectivity of $d\pi: \mathfrak{g} \to \mathcal{L}(G/N)$ with kernel $\mathfrak{n}$.
