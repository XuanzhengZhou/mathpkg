This proposition computes the differential of the multiplication map at the identity. The result $d\mu_{(e,e)}(X,Y) = X+Y$ shows that addition in the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ is the infinitesimal analogue of the group multiplication. The proof uses the decomposition $\mu^* f = \sum f_i \otimes g_i$ and the identity $f = \sum g_i(e) f_i = \sum f_i(e) g_i$.
