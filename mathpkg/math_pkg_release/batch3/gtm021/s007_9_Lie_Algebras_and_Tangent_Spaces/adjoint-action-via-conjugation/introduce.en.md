This proposition gives an explicit formula for the adjoint action on $\mathcal{L}(G)$. Since right and left translations commute, conjugating a left invariant derivation by a right translation preserves left invariance. The formula is natural because $\operatorname{Int} x$ acts on functions by $f \mapsto f \circ \operatorname{Int} x^{-1}$, which intertwines right and left translations in the expected way.
