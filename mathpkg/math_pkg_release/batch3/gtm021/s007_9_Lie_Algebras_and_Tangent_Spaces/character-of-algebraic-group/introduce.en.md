Characters are algebraic group homomorphisms from $G$ to the multiplicative group $\mathbf{G}_m$. The character group $\mathbf{X}(G)$ is an important invariant. For example, $\mathbf{X}(\mathbf{G}_m) \cong \mathbb{Z}$ is infinite cyclic, while $\mathbf{G}_a$ and $\operatorname{SL}(n,K)$ have no nontrivial characters. Characters appear naturally when studying the action of a normal subgroup $N$ on the line $L$ obtained from Chevalley's theorem: $N$ acts by scalar multiplication, giving an associated character $\chi_0 \in \mathbf{X}(N)$.
