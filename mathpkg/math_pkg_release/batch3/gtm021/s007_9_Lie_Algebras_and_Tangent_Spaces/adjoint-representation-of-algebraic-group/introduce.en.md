The adjoint representation $\operatorname{Ad}: G \to \operatorname{Aut}(\mathfrak{g})$ is obtained by differentiating the inner automorphism $\operatorname{Int} x$ at the identity. Because $(\operatorname{Ad} x)(\operatorname{Ad} x^{-1}) = d(\operatorname{Int} e) = 1_{\mathfrak{g}}$, each $\operatorname{Ad} x$ is invertible. The multiplicativity follows from the chain rule. The adjoint representation encodes how the group acts on its own Lie algebra and is essential for studying normal subgroups, centralizers, and the structure theory of algebraic groups.
