For an algebraic group $G$ and $x \in G$, define the inner automorphism $\operatorname{Int} x (y) = xyx^{-1}$. Its differential
$$\operatorname{Ad} x = d(\operatorname{Int} x)_e: \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{g}$$
is an automorphism of the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g} = \mathcal{L}(G)$. The map $\operatorname{Ad}: G \to \operatorname{Aut} \mathfrak{g} \subset \operatorname{GL}(\mathfrak{g})$ is a homomorphism of abstract groups, called the adjoint representation of $G$.
