Since $H \trianglelefteq G$, we have $xHx^{-1} \subset H$ for all $x \in G$. Differentiating the conjugation action at $e$ yields $\operatorname{Ad} x(\mathfrak{h}) \subset \mathfrak{h}$ for all $x \in G$. Differentiating further at $x = e$ gives $\operatorname{ad} x(\mathfrak{h}) \subset \mathfrak{h}$ for all $x \in \mathfrak{g}$, i.e., $[\mathfrak{g}, \mathfrak{h}] \subset \mathfrak{h}$.
