In characteristic 0, the Lie algebra faithfully reflects the subgroup structure of a connected algebraic group. The map $H \mapsto \mathfrak{h}$ is injective: two distinct connected subgroups have distinct Lie algebras. Moreover, $H_1 \subset H_2$ iff $\mathfrak{h}_1 \subset \mathfrak{h}_2$. A subalgebra that arises as $\mathcal{L}(H)$ for some closed connected subgroup $H$ is called an algebraic subalgebra. Determining which subalgebras are algebraic is a subtle problem.
