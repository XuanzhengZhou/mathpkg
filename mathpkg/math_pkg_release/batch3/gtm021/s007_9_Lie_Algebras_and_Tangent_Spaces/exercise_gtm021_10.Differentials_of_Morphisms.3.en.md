Let $\operatorname{char} K = p > 0$. Show that the following example demonstrates that $\operatorname{Ker} \operatorname{Ad}$ may be larger than $Z(G)$: Let $G \subset \mathrm{GL}(3, K)$ consist of all matrices
$$\begin{pmatrix} a & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & a^p & b \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}, \quad a \neq 0.$$
Prove that $G$ is a closed, connected, noncommutative group of dimension $2$, that its Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ consists of all matrices
$$\begin{pmatrix} \alpha & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & \beta \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \quad (\alpha, \beta \in K),$$
and that $\mathfrak{g}$ is commutative while $\operatorname{Ker} \operatorname{Ad} = G$.
