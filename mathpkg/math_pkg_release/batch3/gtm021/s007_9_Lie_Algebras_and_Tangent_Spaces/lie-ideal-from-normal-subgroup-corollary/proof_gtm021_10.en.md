Since $H \trianglelefteq G$, for any $x \in G$, $\operatorname{Int} x(H) = H$. Therefore $\operatorname{Ad} x (\mathfrak{h}) = \mathcal{L}(\operatorname{Int} x(H)) = \mathfrak{h}$.

Now consider the homomorphism $\operatorname{Ad}|_G: G \to \operatorname{GL}(\mathfrak{g})$. Its differential is $\operatorname{ad}$. The image of $\operatorname{Ad}$ is contained in the closed subgroup of $\operatorname{GL}(\mathfrak{g})$ that stabilizes $\mathfrak{h}$. The Lie algebra of this stabilizer consists of those endomorphisms of $\mathfrak{g}$ that map $\mathfrak{h}$ into $\mathfrak{h}$. Therefore $\operatorname{ad} X (\mathfrak{h}) \subset \mathfrak{h}$ for all $X \in \mathfrak{g}$, which means $[X, Y] \in \mathfrak{h}$ for all $X \in \mathfrak{g}, Y \in \mathfrak{h}$.
