This proposition identifies the Lie algebras of the stabilizers of subspaces and vectors under the natural action of $\operatorname{GL}(V)$. Remarkably, these equalities hold in any characteristic. The proof is based on dimension comparisons: in a suitable basis, $G_W$ (resp. $G_v$) is an affine open subset of a linear variety whose dimension matches that of $\mathfrak{g}_W$ (resp. $\mathfrak{g}_v$), so the Lie algebras have the same dimension and the inclusion $\mathcal{L}(G_W) \subset \mathfrak{g}_W$ (which always holds) must be an equality.
