$T(n,K)$ is the subgroup of $\mathrm{GL}(n,K)$ consisting of invertible upper triangular matrices. It can be viewed as the principal open set in affine $n(n+1)/2$-space (embedded in $\mathbb{A}^{n^2}$) defined by the non-vanishing of the determinant. Therefore its tangent space at $e$ is this affine space, and after translating from $e$ to $0$, its Lie algebra is $\mathfrak{t}(n,K)$, the space of all upper triangular matrices.
