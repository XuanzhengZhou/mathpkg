The Lie algebra $\mathcal{L}(G)$ of an algebraic group $G$ is defined as the space of all left invariant derivations of the coordinate ring $K[G]$. Left invariance means that the derivation commutes with all left translation operators. This construction is functorial: a morphism of algebraic groups induces a homomorphism of the corresponding Lie algebras. The bracket of two left invariant derivations remains left invariant, so $\mathcal{L}(G)$ is a Lie subalgebra of $\operatorname{Der} K[G]$.
