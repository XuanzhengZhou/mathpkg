The map $G(K) \to \mathcal{T}(G)$ sends $g \in G(K)$ to the composite $K[G] \xrightarrow{g} K \hookrightarrow K[\delta]$. The map $\mathcal{T}(G) \to G(K)$ sends a homomorphism $\varphi: K[G] \to K[\delta]$ to the composite $K[G] \xrightarrow{\varphi} K[\delta] \to K$. The kernel consists of homomorphisms $f \mapsto \varepsilon_e(f) + \delta x(f)$, which correspond to $x \in \mathfrak{g}$. The splitting is given by the inclusion $K \hookrightarrow K[\delta]$.
