In characteristic 0, normality at the group level is equivalent to the ideal property at the Lie algebra level. The forward direction ($H \trianglelefteq G \implies \mathfrak{h}$ ideal) holds in any characteristic (Corollary 10.4A). The converse uses the connectedness of $G$ and the Lie correspondence: if $\mathfrak{h}$ is an ideal, then $\operatorname{ad} X(\mathfrak{h}) \subset \mathfrak{h}$ for all $X$, so $d(\operatorname{Ad} x)$ preserves $\mathfrak{h}$, which by connectedness implies $\operatorname{Ad} x$ preserves $\mathfrak{h}$, and Theorem 13.1 then forces $\operatorname{Int} x(H) = H$.
