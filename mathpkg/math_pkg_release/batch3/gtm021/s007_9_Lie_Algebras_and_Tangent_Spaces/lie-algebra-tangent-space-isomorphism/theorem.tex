Let $G$ be an algebraic group over $K$, and let $\mathfrak{g} = \mathcal{T}(G)_e$ be the tangent space at the identity $e$. Define $\theta: \mathcal{L}(G) \to \mathfrak{g}$ by
$$\theta(\delta)(f) = (\delta f)(e) \quad (\delta \in \mathcal{L}(G), f \in K[G]).$$
Then $\theta$ is an isomorphism of vector spaces. Via $\theta$, the Lie bracket in $\mathcal{L}(G)$ corresponds to the bracket in $\mathfrak{g}$ given by
$$[x, y](f) = (f * y * x)(e) - (f * x * y)(e),$$
where $(f * x)(y) = x(\lambda_{y^{-1}} f)$. Moreover, if $\varphi: G \to G'$ is a morphism of algebraic groups, then $d\varphi: \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{g}'$ preserves the bracket operation, so $\theta$ is a natural isomorphism of Lie algebras.
