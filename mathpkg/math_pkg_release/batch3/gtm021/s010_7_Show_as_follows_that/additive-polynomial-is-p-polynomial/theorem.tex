Let $f(T_1, \ldots, T_n) \in K[T_1, \ldots, T_n]$ be an additive polynomial, i.e., $f(a_1 + b_1, \ldots, a_n + b_n) = f(a_1, \ldots, a_n) + f(b_1, \ldots, b_n)$ for all $(a_i), (b_i) \in K^n$. Then $f(T)$ is a $p$-polynomial, i.e., $f(T)$ is a linear combination of terms $T_i^{p^r}$ ($r \geqslant 0$).
