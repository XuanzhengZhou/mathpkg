(a) Embed G in T(n,K) via Lie-Kolchin, use exact sequence 1 to U(n,K) to T(n,K) to D(n,K) to 1. Restrict to G: 1 to G_u to G to T' to 1, where G_u = G intersect U(n,K) and T' = pi(G) is a torus. G_u is connected since (G,G) and G'_u are connected. The chain comes from intersecting G_u with a normal series of U(n,K). (b) G contains a torus T mapping onto T', so the sequence splits: G = T semidirect G_u. For conjugacy, let T,S be maximal tori. By Proposition 16.4, exists s in S with C_G(s)=C_G(S). Conjugating S, assume s in T. Then T subset C_G(s) = C_G(S), so (ST)^circ is a torus containing both S and T, forcing S = T. Conjugacy of semisimple elements: write s = t n (t in T, n in G_u), apply Theorem 18.3(b) to t^{-1} on G_u: n = z gamma_{t^{-1}}(u), so s = z(u t u^{-1}). Since z is unipotent and G_u abelian, the right side is the Jordan decomposition, forcing z=e, so u^{-1} s u = t in T.
