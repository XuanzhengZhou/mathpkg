If dim G = 0, G = e. Proceed by induction. H = (G,G) is connected (17.2), solvable, and of lower dimension. By induction, the fixed point set Y of H is nonempty. Y is closed (8.2), hence complete (21.1a). G stabilizes Y since H is normal. Now H subset G_x for all x in Y, so all isotropy groups are normal, and G/G_x is affine. Choose x with closed orbit G.x (8.3), which is complete. The canonical morphism G/G_x to G.x is bijective, with affine left side and complete right side. By Lemma 21.1, G/G_x is complete, hence 0-dimensional (21.1c). Thus G = G_x, and x is a fixed point.
