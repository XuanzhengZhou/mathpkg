If B = B_s or B = B_u, then B is a torus or a unipotent group (Theorem 19.3), hence nilpotent. Whenever B is nilpotent of positive dimension, its center has positive dimension (Proposition 17.4(a)). But Z(G)^circ is contained in Z(B) which is contained in Z(G). Pass to G/Z(G)^circ, where B/Z(G)^circ is a nilpotent Borel subgroup. By induction these groups are equal, so G = B.
