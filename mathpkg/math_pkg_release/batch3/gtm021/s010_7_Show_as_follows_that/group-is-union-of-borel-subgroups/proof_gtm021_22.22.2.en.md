Define X = union of conjugates of H. Use auxiliary morphisms: G x G ->(phi) G x G ->(psi) (G/H) x G ->(pr1) G/H where phi(x,y)=(x,xyx^{-1}), psi(x,y)=(xH,y). Let M = psi(phi(G x H)); then X = pr2(M). M is closed: psi is open, phi is isomorphism, so phi(G x H) is closed and equals psi^{-1}(M). For density: X is constructible, dim M = dim G/H + dim H = dim G. The general fiber of pr2: M -> X has dim H, so dim X = dim G. X is dense. Since X is also closed, X = G.
