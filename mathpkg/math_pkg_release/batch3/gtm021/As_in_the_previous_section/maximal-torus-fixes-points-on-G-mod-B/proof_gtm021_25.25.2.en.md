As remarked at the beginning of this section, $G$ may be embedded into some $\operatorname{GL}(V)$ in such a way that $G/P$ is isomorphic to the orbit $X$ of some point in $\mathbf{P}(V)$; we can even guarantee that $X$ lies in no hyperplane. Since $G/P$ is complete and irreducible, $X$ is closed and irreducible (21.1)(a). $T$ stabilizes $X$, so the theorem applies.
