The proof proceeds by showing $R_u(G) \subset I(T)_u$ (which is clear since $R_u(G)$ lies in every Borel subgroup) and the reverse inclusion. For the reverse inclusion, suppose there exists a unipotent element in $I(T)_u$ not in $R_u(G)$. Using Lemma 26.1 and the structure of root groups, one derives a contradiction by constructing a 1-parameter subgroup whose associated Borel subgroup does not contain this element, contradicting that it lies in $I(T)$.
