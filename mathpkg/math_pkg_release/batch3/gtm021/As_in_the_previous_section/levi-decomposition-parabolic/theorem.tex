Let $P$ be a parabolic subgroup of a reductive group $G$. Then $P = L \ltimes R_u(P)$, where $L$ is a reductive subgroup, called a Levi factor. If $P = P_I$ is a standard parabolic, one can take $L = C_G(Z)$ where $Z = (\bigcap_{\alpha \in I} \operatorname{Ker} \alpha)^\circ$, and $L$ is reductive with $R_u(P) \cap L = e$. Moreover, any two Levi factors of $P$ are conjugate by an element of $R_u(P)$.
