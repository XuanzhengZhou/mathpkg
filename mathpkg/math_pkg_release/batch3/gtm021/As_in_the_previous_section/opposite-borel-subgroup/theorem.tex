Let $G$ be a reductive group, $B$ a Borel subgroup containing a maximal torus $T$, and let $U = B_u$. Then there exists a unique Borel subgroup $B^{-}$ containing $T$ such that $B \cap B^{-} = T$, called the opposite Borel subgroup. Moreover, $B^{-} = T U^{-}$ where $U^{-}$ is generated by the root subgroups $U_{-\alpha}$ for positive roots $\alpha$.
