Fix a maximal torus $T$ of a connected algebraic group $G$, and assign to each regular 1-parameter subgroup $\lambda \in Y(T)_{\text{reg}}$ a Borel subgroup $B(\lambda)$ containing $T$, by taking the Borel subgroup whose unipotent part is generated by root subgroups corresponding to roots $\alpha$ with $\langle \alpha, \lambda \rangle > 0$. Then the correspondence $\lambda \mapsto B(\lambda)$ is $W$-equivariant and surjective onto $\mathfrak{B}^T$. The sets $\mathfrak{C}(B) = \{\lambda \in Y(T)_{\text{reg}} \mid B = B(\lambda)\}$ form a disjoint partition of $Y(T)_{\text{reg}}$, called Weyl chambers.
