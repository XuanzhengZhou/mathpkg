For a regular $\lambda \in Y(T)_{\text{reg}}$, define $B(\lambda)$ as the Borel subgroup containing $T$ such that its unipotent radical is generated by those root subgroups $U_\alpha$ for which $\langle \alpha, \lambda \rangle > 0$. The $W$-equivariance follows from the fact that $W$ permutes the eigenspaces of $T$ in $V$, hence permutes the weights and the integers $m_i = \langle \gamma_i, \lambda \rangle$. For $\sigma \in W$ with representative $n$, one checks that $nB(\lambda)n^{-1} = B(\sigma\lambda)$. The surjectivity follows from the simply transitive action of $W$ on $\mathfrak{B}^T$.
