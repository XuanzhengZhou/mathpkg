For $U$ take $B_u$ (the unipotent radical of a Borel subgroup). Then apply Theorem 29.5 using Theorem 27.5 to verify that $G = (G, G)$ and that the conjugates of $U$ generate $G$. The Weyl group of $G$ is irreducible because $G$ is simple, and $B_u$ is a normal solvable subgroup of $B$.
