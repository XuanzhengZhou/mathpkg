A Tits system is said to be \emph{saturated} if $\bigcap_{x \in N} xBx^{-1} = T$ (in general, this intersection includes $T$). Call the intersection $T'$, and let $N' = \langle N, T' \rangle$. Prove that $W' = N'/T'$ is canonically isomorphic to $W = N/T$. If $S'$ corresponds to $S$ (under this isomorphism), prove that $(G, B, N', S')$ is a saturated Tits system (with Weyl group $W'$).
