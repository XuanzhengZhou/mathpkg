Let $(G, B, N, S)$ have rank 1, $S = \{\rho\}$, so $G = B \cup B\rho B$. $G$ permutes $G/B$ (via left multiplication), with $B = \{x \in G \mid x B = B\}$ and $N$ the subgroup of $G$ stabilizing the set $\{B, \rho B\}$. Prove that $G$ acts doubly transitively on $G/B$(cf. Exercise 6).
