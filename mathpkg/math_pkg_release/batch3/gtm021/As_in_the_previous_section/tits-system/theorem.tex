Let $G$ be a group generated by two subgroups $B$ and $N$, where $T = B \cap N$ is normal in $N$. Denote the quotient group $N/T$ by $W$, and suppose that $W$ is generated by a subset $S$ consisting of involutions. We call $(G, B, N, S)$ a Tits system (or BN-pair) provided the following axioms are satisfied:
(T1) If $\rho \in S$ and $\sigma \in W$, then $\rho B\sigma \subset B\sigma B \cup B\rho\sigma B$.
(T2) If $\rho \in S$, then $\rho B\rho \neq B$.
