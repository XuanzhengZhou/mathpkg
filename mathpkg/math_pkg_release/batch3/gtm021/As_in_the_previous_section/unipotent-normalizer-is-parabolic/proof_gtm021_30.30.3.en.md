Let $B$ be a Borel subgroup of $G$ which includes $V$, and set $S = (B \cap N)^\circ$. Then $S$ lies in a Borel subgroup $B_1$ of $N$, and we can choose an opposite $nB_1n^{-1}$ ($n \in N$), i.e., $R_u(B_1 \cap nB_1n^{-1}) = R_u(N)$ (apply Corollary 26.2C to $N^\circ/R_u(N)$). Evidently this choice implies that $R_u(N \cap B \cap nBn^{-1}) \subset R_u(N) \subset V$. On the other hand, $n$ normalizes $V$, so $V$ lies in the unipotent part of the solvable group $N \cap B \cap nBn^{-1}$. This forces equality to hold throughout; in particular, $V = R_u(N)$ is connected. Moreover, $V$ lies in the intersection $B \cap B'$ ($B' = nBn^{-1}$). If $V$ were smaller than $R_u(B \cap B')$, a dimension argument yields a contradiction. It follows that $N$ is parabolic.
