This proposition provides a criterion for a subgroup to be parabolic: if a closed unipotent subgroup contained in a Borel subgroup is its own unipotent radical (up to connected components), then its normalizer is a parabolic subgroup. This is used to prove that maximal unipotent subgroups are precisely the unipotent radicals of Borel subgroups.
