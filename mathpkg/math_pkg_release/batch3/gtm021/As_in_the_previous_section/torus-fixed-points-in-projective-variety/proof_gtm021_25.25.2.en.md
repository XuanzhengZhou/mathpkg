Let $(v_1, \ldots, v_n)$ be a basis of $V$ consisting of eigenvectors for $T$, with corresponding weights $\gamma_i \in X(T)$. Choose $\lambda \in Y(T)$ so that $m_i = \langle \gamma_i, \lambda \rangle$ are distinct when the $\gamma_i$ are distinct. As remarked at the end of (25.1), $T$ and $\lambda(\mathbf{G}_m)$ then have precisely the same fixed points in $\mathbf{P}(V)$. So we may as well assume that $T = \lambda(\mathbf{G}_m)$.

(a) Since $\dim X \geqslant 1$, it follows from Lemma 25.2 that $X$ meets each hyperplane $\mathbf{P}(W_k)$, where $W_k$ is spanned by all $v_i$ with $m_i \neq m_k$. The intersection points are fixed by the 1-parameter subgroup. Choosing the maximal and minimal $m_i$ yields at least two distinct fixed points.

(b) When $\dim X \geqslant 2$, one obtains at least three fixed points by considering intermediate weight values.
