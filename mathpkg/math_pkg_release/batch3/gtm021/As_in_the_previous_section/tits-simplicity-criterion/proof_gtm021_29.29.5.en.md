Let $H$ be a normal subgroup of $G$ not included in $Z$. Thanks to a lemma (29.5) and the irreducibility of $W$, $G = HB$. The normality of $U$ in $B$ forces the subgroup $HU$ already to contain all $HB$-conjugates of $U$ (i.e., all $G$-conjugates); the latter generate $G$, so in fact $G = HU$.

A standard isomorphism theorem shows that $U/U \cap H \cong HU/H \cong G/H$. The left side is solvable (since $U$ is), while the right side is its own derived group (since this is true of $G$), forcing $G = H$.
