Let $G$ be a reductive algebraic group, $T$ a maximal torus, $\alpha \in \Phi = \Phi(G, T)$.
(a) There exists a unique connected $T$-stable subgroup $U_{\alpha}$ of $G$ having Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}_{\alpha}$ (and $U_{\alpha} \subset Z_{\alpha}$).
(b) If $n \in N_G(T)$ represents $\sigma \in W$, then $nU_{\alpha}n^{-1} = U_{\sigma(\alpha)}$.
(c) There exists an isomorphism $\varepsilon_{\alpha} : \mathbf{G}_a \rightarrow U_{\alpha}$ such that for all $t \in T$ and $x \in \mathbf{G}_a$, $t\varepsilon_{\alpha}(x)t^{-1} = \varepsilon_{\alpha}(\alpha(t)x)$.
