Let $G$ be a reductive algebraic group, $T$ a maximal torus, $\Phi = \Phi(G, T)$. Then:
(a) $\Phi = \Psi$, and $\Phi = -\Phi$.
(b) $\mathfrak{g} = \mathfrak{t} \oplus \bigsqcup_{\alpha \in \Phi} \mathfrak{g}_{\alpha}$, with $\dim \mathfrak{g}_{\alpha} = 1$.
(c) The singular tori of codimension 1 in $G$ are the $T_{\alpha} = (\operatorname{Ker} \alpha)^{\circ}$, $\alpha \in \Phi$.
(d) Let $Z_{\alpha} = C_G(T_{\alpha})$ ($\alpha \in \Phi$). Then $Z_{\alpha}$ is a reductive group of semisimple rank 1, with $\mathfrak{z}_{\alpha} = \mathfrak{t} \oplus \mathfrak{g}_{\alpha} \oplus \mathfrak{g}_{-\alpha}$. Moreover, the $Z_{\alpha}$ generate $G$.
(e) $Z(G) = \bigcap_{\alpha \in \Phi} T_{\alpha}$.
(f) The rank of the subgroup $R$ of $X(T)$ generated by $\Phi$ equals the semisimple rank of $G$.
