(a), (b), (c), and the first part of (d) are all immediate, in view of Corollary A and (25.3). That the $Z_{\alpha}$ generate $G$ follows from the fact that they generate a closed subgroup of $G$ having Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ (cf. (b)).

(e) According to Corollary A, $Z(G) \subset T$, forcing $Z(G) \subset T_{\alpha}$ for all $\alpha \in \Phi$. Conversely, if $x \in \bigcap T_{\alpha}$, then $x$ centralizes each $Z_{\alpha}$ (since $T_{\alpha}$ is the connected center of $Z_{\alpha}$). Since the $Z_{\alpha}$ generate $G$, $x \in Z(G)$.

(f) This follows from the structure of the character group and the fact that the torus part corresponds to characters trivial on all roots.
