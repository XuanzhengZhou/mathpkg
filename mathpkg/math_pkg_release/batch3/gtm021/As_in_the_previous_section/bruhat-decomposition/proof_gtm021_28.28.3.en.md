According to Theorem 27.3, $G$ is generated by the groups $Z_\alpha$ for $\alpha \in \Delta$. Proposition 28.2 shows that $Z_\alpha \subset BWB$. To show that $G = BWB$, it therefore suffices to show that $BWB$ is closed under left multiplication by $Z_\alpha$ for $\alpha \in \Delta$. At the end of (28.1) we observed that $U = U_{\sigma_\alpha} U_\alpha$ and that $Z_\alpha$ normalizes $U_{\sigma_\alpha}$. So any element of $Z_\alpha$ (notably a representative of $\sigma_\alpha$) can be moved past $U_{\sigma_\alpha}$.

The uniqueness follows by induction on the length $\ell(\sigma)$ of a reduced expression, using the key property (Corollary of the proof): if $\alpha \in \Delta$ and $\sigma \in W$, then $\sigma_\alpha B\sigma \subset B\sigma B \cup B\sigma_\alpha\sigma B$.
