The Bruhat decomposition partitions a reductive group into double cosets indexed by the Weyl group: $G = \bigsqcup_{\sigma \in W} B\sigma B$. This fundamental decomposition generalizes the fact that an invertible matrix can be reduced to a permutation matrix by row and column operations. It is the foundation for studying the geometry of flag varieties.
