Since $U^{-} \cap B = e$, $\pi$ is certainly injective. Moreover, $\dim \Omega = \dim \mathfrak{u}^{-} + \dim \mathfrak{b} = \dim \mathfrak{g} = \dim G$, so the constructible set $\Omega$ contains a dense open subset of $G$. But $\Omega$ is the translate by $\sigma_0$ (the longest Weyl group element) of the double coset $B\sigma_0B$. Thus it suffices to show that $B\sigma_0B$ is open, or that $B\sigma_0 \cdot x_0$ is open in $G/B$ ($x_0$ the point corresponding to $B$). Being a full $B$-orbit as well as a constructible set, $B\sigma_0 \cdot x_0$ is actually open in its closure (8.3); but we observed that the latter is $G/B$.

That $\pi$ is an isomorphism follows from separability (its differential maps $\mathfrak{u}^{-} \oplus \mathfrak{b}$ isomorphically onto $\mathfrak{g}$) and Zariski's Main Theorem.
