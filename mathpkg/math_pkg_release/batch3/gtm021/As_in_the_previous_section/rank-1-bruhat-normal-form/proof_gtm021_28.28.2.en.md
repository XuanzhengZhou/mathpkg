The proof uses the structure of $G/B \cong \mathbf{P}^1$ and the action of $U$ on it. $U$ fixes the point corresponding to $B$, and its orbit of the other $T$-fixed point exhausts the complement. The uniqueness follows from analyzing the stabilizers and the intersection properties of the Bruhat cells.
