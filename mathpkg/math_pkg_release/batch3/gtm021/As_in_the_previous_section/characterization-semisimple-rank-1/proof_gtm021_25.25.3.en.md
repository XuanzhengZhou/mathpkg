(a) $\Rightarrow$ (b): Since every Borel subgroup includes $R(G)$, the Borel subgroups of $G$ including $T$ are in 1--1 correspondence with those of $G' = G/R(G)$ including $T' = T/T \cap R(G)$, and the number in each case is the order of the respective Weyl group $W$, $W'$ (Proposition 24.1B). But $\dim T' = 1$, by assumption, and $\operatorname{Aut} G_m = \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ (Exercise 7.4), so $|W| = |W'| \leqslant 2$. On the other hand, $G$ is nonsolvable by assumption, so $|W| \geqslant 2$ by Corollary 25.2B.

(b) $\Rightarrow$ (c): The equivalence was noted in (24.1).

(c) $\Rightarrow$ (d): Since $T$ lies in two distinct Borel subgroups, $G/B$ has dimension at least 1. If $\dim G/B \geqslant 2$, then Corollary 25.2A says that $\operatorname{Card} \mathfrak{B}^T \geqslant 3$, contrary to (c).

(d) $\Rightarrow$ (e): Let $\dim G/B = 1$. As in (25.1), we may construct a nonconstant morphism $\mathbf{P}^1 \rightarrow G/B \subset \mathbf{P}(V)$ relative to any regular 1-psg $\lambda \in Y(T)$, $T$ a maximal torus of $G$. Its image is closed, because $\mathbf{P}^1$ is complete (21.1)(b), hence equal to $G/B$, because $G/B$ is irreducible and of dimension 1. This implies that $G/B$ is isomorphic to $\mathbf{P}^1$ (6.3).

(e) $\Rightarrow$ (f): $G$ acts as a group of automorphisms of $G/B \cong \mathbf{P}^1$. But $\operatorname{Aut} \mathbf{P}^1 \cong \operatorname{PGL}(2, K)$.
