For $G = \mathrm{SL}(n, \mathbb{K})$, describe the parabolic subgroups including $B = G \cap \mathrm{T}(n, \mathbb{K})$, and deduce that the number of conjugacy classes of parabolics in $G$ is $2^{n-1}$ ($n-1 = \mathrm{rank}\ G$). Treat the other classical groups similarly.
