Let $G$ be a variety endowed with the structure of a group. If the multiplication map $\mu: G \times G \rightarrow G$ ($\mu(x, y) = xy$) and the inversion map $\iota: G \rightarrow G$ ($\iota(x) = x^{-1}$) are morphisms of varieties, we call $G$ an algebraic group.

Unless stated otherwise, an algebraic group is assumed to be affine.
