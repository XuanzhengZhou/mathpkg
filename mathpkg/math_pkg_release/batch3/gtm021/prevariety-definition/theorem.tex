An irreducible prevariety $X$ is an irreducible noetherian topological space, endowed with a sheaf $\mathcal{O}_X$ of $K$-valued functions, such that $X$ is the union of finitely many open subsets $U_i$, each isomorphic to an affine variety when given the restricted sheaf of functions.

A prevariety is a noetherian topological space $X$ whose irreducible components $X_i$ are irreducible prevarieties such that $\mathcal{O}_{X_i}$ and $\mathcal{O}_{X_j}$ induce the same sheaf of functions on $X_i \cap X_j$.
