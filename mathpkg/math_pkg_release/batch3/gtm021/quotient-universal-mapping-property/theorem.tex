Let $H$ be a closed subgroup of an algebraic group $G$. There exists a separable, $G$-equivariant morphism $\pi: G \rightarrow Y$ (where $Y$ is a $G$-orbit in some $\mathbf{P}(V)$) whose fibres are precisely the cosets $xH$, satisfying the universal mapping property:

Given any variety $X$ and morphism $\varphi: G \rightarrow X$ whose nonempty fibres are unions of cosets $xH$, there exists a unique morphism $\psi: Y \rightarrow X$ such that $\psi \circ \pi = \varphi$.
