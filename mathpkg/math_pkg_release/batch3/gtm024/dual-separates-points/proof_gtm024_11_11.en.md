Given distinct $x, y \in X$, let $z = x - y \neq \theta$. Choose a convex $\theta$-neighborhood $V$ that does not contain $z$. By the topological separation theorem, $z$ and $V$ can be separated by a closed hyperplane $[\psi; \alpha]$. Since $\psi(V)$ is an interval containing $0$ in its interior, $\psi(z) \neq 0$, so $\psi(x) \neq \psi(y)$.
