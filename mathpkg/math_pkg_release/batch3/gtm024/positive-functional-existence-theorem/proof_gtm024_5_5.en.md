Choose $x \in X \setminus P$ and apply the basic separation theorem to separate $x$ and $P$ by a hyperplane $[\phi; \alpha]$. A linear functional that is bounded below on a wedge must be non-negative there. Thus $\phi \in P^+$ and $\phi \neq \theta$.
