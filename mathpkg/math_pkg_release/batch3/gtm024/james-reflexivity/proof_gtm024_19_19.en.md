Suppose $X$ is not reflexive. a) From exercise 3.33, $X$ contains a nonreflexive separable subspace $M$. The unit ball $U(M)$ is not weakly compact, so by James' theorem, $\exists\phi\in M^*$ that fails to attain its norm on $U(M)$. The hyperplane $H=[\phi;\|\phi\|]$ has no minimal element, hence is not proximinal. b) $H$ and $U(M)$ cannot be strictly separated.
