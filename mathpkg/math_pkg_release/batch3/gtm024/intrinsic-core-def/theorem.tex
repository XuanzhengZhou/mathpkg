For $A \subset X$, the core (algebraic interior) $\operatorname{cor}(A)$ consists of $p \in A$ such that for each $x \in X$ there exists $\varepsilon > 0$ with $[p, p + \varepsilon x] \subset A$. The intrinsic core $\operatorname{icr}(A)$ consists of $p \in A$ such that for each $x \in \operatorname{aff}(A)$ there exists $\varepsilon > 0$ with $[p, p + \varepsilon x] \subset A$.
