Let $K$ be a compact convex set in a locally convex space $X$. Since $K$ is compact and convex, $K$ equals the closed convex hull of its extreme points by the Krein-Milman theorem: $K = \overline{\operatorname{co}}(\operatorname{ext}(K))$. Now let $T: X \to X$ be a continuous linear operator such that $T(K) \subset K$ and suppose $T$ has a non-zero eigenvalue $\lambda$ with eigenvector in the cone generated by $K$. The Krein-Rutman theorem asserts that if $T$ is compact and positive (with respect to a cone with non-empty interior), then the spectral radius is an eigenvalue with a positive eigenvector. The proof uses the fixed point property of compact convex sets: consider the map $x \mapsto (Tx)/\|Tx\|$ on a suitable cross-section of the cone. By the Schauder-Tychonoff fixed point theorem, this map has a fixed point, which yields the desired positive eigenvector. The argument relies on the geometric properties of cones and the compactness of the operator.
