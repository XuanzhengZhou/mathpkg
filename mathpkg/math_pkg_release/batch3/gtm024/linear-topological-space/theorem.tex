A topology $\mathcal{T}$ on a linear space $X$ is a linear topology if both vector addition $(x,y) \mapsto x+y$ and scalar multiplication $(\alpha,x) \mapsto \alpha x$ are continuous on their respective domains $X \times X$ and $\mathbb{F} \times X$. The pair $(X, \mathcal{T})$ is then a linear topological space (LTS).
