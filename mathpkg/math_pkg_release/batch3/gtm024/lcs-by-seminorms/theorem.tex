Let $\mathcal{U}$ be a family of absolutely convex absorbing sets in $X$ such that: given $U, V \in \mathcal{U}$ there exists $W \in \mathcal{U}$ with $W \subset U \cap V$, and $\alpha U \in \mathcal{U}$ whenever $\alpha \neq 0$. Then $\mathcal{U}$ is a local base for a unique locally convex topology on $X$.
