As usual, it suffices to prove $U(M)$ is weakly compact for any separable closed subspace $M$. Let $\phi \in M^*$ and choose $\{x_n\} \subset U(M)$ with $\phi(x_n) \to \|\phi\|$. Uniform rotundity implies $\{x_n\}$ is a Cauchy sequence, so its limit is where $\phi$ attains its norm. By James' theorem, $U(M)$ is weakly compact.
