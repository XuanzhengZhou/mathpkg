Reduce to separable case. Let $\phi \in X^*$ and let $\alpha = \min\phi(A)$, $\beta = \max\phi(A)$, attained at $u,v \in A$. Then $\phi(\operatorname{aco}(A)) = [-\gamma,\gamma]$ where $\gamma = \max(|\alpha|,|\beta|)$, and $\phi(\overline{\operatorname{aco}}(A))$ also equals $[-\gamma,\gamma]$. Thus $\phi$ attains its supremum on $\overline{\operatorname{aco}}(A)$, and James' theorem applies.
