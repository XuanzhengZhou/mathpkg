A relatively free algebra is the free object within a variety $V$ — it satisfies the same universal property as an absolutely free algebra, but the target algebra is restricted to lie in $V$. It is constructed as a quotient of the absolutely free algebra by the congruence that identifies terms that are provably equal under the laws of $V$. This construction yields the "most general" algebra in $V$ generated by $X$.
