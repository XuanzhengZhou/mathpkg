Let $V$ be a variety of $T$-algebras defined by a set $L$ of laws. A $T$-algebra $R$ in the variety $V$ is the (relatively) free algebra of $V$ on the set $X$ of (relatively) free generators (where a function $\sigma: X \to R$ is given) if, for every $T$-algebra $A$ in $V$ and function $\tau: X \to A$, there exists a unique homomorphism $\varphi: R \to A$ such that $\varphi\sigma = \tau$.

Equivalently, $R$ is constructed as the quotient $F/{\sim}$ where $F$ is the absolutely free $T$-algebra on $X$, and $\sim$ is the congruence relation defined by $u \sim v$ if $\varphi(u) = \varphi(v)$ for every homomorphism $\varphi: F \to A$ into every algebra $A$ in $V$.
