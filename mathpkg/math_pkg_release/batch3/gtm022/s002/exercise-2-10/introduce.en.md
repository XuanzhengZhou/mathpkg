This exercise generalizes the previous ones: if the type $T$ is countable, has at least one constant (nullary) operation and at least one non-nullary operation, and the generating set $X$ is countable, then the free $T$-algebra is countable. The proof uses the inductive layer construction from Theorem 2.2 — each $F_n$ is countable, and the countable union of countable sets is countable.
