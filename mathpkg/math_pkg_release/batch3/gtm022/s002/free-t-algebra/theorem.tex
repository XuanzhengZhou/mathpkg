Let $X$ be any set, let $F$ be a $T$-algebra and let $\sigma: X \to F$ be a function. We say that $F$ (more strictly $(F, \sigma)$) is a free $T$-algebra on the set $X$ of free generators if, for every $T$-algebra $A$ and function $\tau: X \to A$, there exists a unique homomorphism $\varphi: F \to A$ such that $\varphi\sigma = \tau$.

If $(F, \sigma)$ is free, then $\sigma$ is injective.
