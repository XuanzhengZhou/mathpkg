An identical relation is a formal equation between two terms (elements of the free algebra) that may be imposed as an axiom on $T$-algebras. A $T$-algebra satisfies the relation if every interpretation of the variables makes the equation true. A set of identical relations defines a variety — the class of all $T$-algebras satisfying those relations. This framework unifies the axiomatic definition of algebraic structures such as semigroups, groups, and rings.
