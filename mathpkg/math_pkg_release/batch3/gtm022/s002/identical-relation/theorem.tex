An identical relation (or law) on $T$-algebras is a pair $(u, v)$ of elements of the free $T$-algebra $F$ on a countable set $X = \{x_1, x_2, \ldots\}$ of variables. A $T$-algebra $A$ satisfies the identical relation $(u, v)$ if for every homomorphism $\varphi: F \to A$, we have $\varphi(u) = \varphi(v)$.
