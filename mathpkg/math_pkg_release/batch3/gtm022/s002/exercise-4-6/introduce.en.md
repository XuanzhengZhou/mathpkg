This exercise shows that $K$-vector spaces form a variety and that every vector space is a free algebra in this variety. Vector spaces are modeled as abelian groups with a unary operation for each scalar, satisfying the expected axioms. Since every vector space has a basis, the universal property of a basis coincides exactly with the universal property of a free algebra in the variety.
