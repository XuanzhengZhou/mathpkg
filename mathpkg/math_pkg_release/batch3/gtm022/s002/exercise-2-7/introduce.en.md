This exercise demonstrates the trivial case: when the type $T$ has no operations, the free $T$-algebra on $X$ is simply $X$ itself, with the identity map as the universal map. There are no operations to define, so any function from $X$ to a $T$-algebra trivially extends (since a $T$-algebra in this case is just a set with no structure).
