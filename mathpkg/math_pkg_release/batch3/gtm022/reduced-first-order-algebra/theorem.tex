Let $w_1, w_2 \in \tilde{P}(V, \mathcal{R})$. Define $w_1 \approx w_2$ if
(a) $d(w_1) = d(w_2) = 0$ and $w_1 = w_2$, or
(b) $d(w_1) = d(w_2) > 0$, $w_1 = a_1 \Rightarrow b_1$, $w_2 = a_2 \Rightarrow b_2$, $a_1 \approx a_2$ and $b_1 \approx b_2$, or
(c) $w_1 = (\forall x)a$, $w_2 = (\forall y)b$, and there exists $z \notin V(w_1) \cup V(w_2)$ such that $a[z/x] \approx b[z/y]$.
Then $\approx$ is a congruence relation. The quotient algebra $P(V, \mathcal{R}) = \tilde{P}(V, \mathcal{R})/{\approx}$ is called the reduced first-order algebra on $(V, \mathcal{R})$.
