Minimalisation (also called unbounded search or the $\mu$-operator) is one of the three fundamental operations used to generate recursive functions from initial functions. Given two functions $f$ and $g$ on $\mathbb{N}^{n+1}$ that are guaranteed to agree somewhere for every tuple of the first $n$ arguments, minimalisation returns the least $y$ where they coincide. Unlike composition and primitive recursion, minimalisation can produce partial functions if the existence condition is not satisfied, making it the operation responsible for the full power of general recursive functions.
