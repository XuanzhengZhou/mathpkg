Given $f, g: \mathbb{N}^{n+1} \to \mathbb{N}$ ($n \in \mathbb{N}^+$) satisfying the condition that for each $(x_1, \ldots, x_n) \in \mathbb{N}^n$ there exists at least one $y$ such that $f(x_1, \ldots, x_n, y) = g(x_1, \ldots, x_n, y)$, \textit{minimalisation} yields the function $h: \mathbb{N}^n \to \mathbb{N}$ given by

$$
h(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = \min_y \bigl( f(x_1, \ldots, x_n, y) = g(x_1, \ldots, x_n, y) \bigr)
$$

$$
= \text{the least } y \in \mathbb{N} \text{ such that } f(x_1, \ldots, x_n, y) = g(x_1, \ldots, x_n, y).
$$
