A proposition $p \in P(X)$ is called a tautology (or valid) if it evaluates to true under every possible valuation $v: P(X) \to \mathbb{Z}_2$. In symbols, $\models p$ means that $\emptyset \models p$, i.e., $p$ is a semantic consequence of the empty set of assumptions. The notion of tautology captures the idea of logical truth — a statement that is true by virtue of its logical form alone, independent of the truth values assigned to its atomic constituents. Note that $\models p$ is a statement in the metalanguage about the model, not a proposition in $P(X)$ itself.
