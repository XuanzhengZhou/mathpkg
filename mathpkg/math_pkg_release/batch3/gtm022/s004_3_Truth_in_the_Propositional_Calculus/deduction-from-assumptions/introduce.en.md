The syntactic consequence operator $\text{Ded}$ maps a set $A$ of assumptions to the set $\text{Ded}(A)$ of all propositions that can be formally proved from $A$ (together with the axioms) using modus ponens. The notation $A \vdash q$ expresses that $q$ is deducible from $A$. This syntactic notion of consequence is the proof-theoretic counterpart to the semantic consequence relation $\models$ introduced in Section 3, and the relationship between $\vdash$ and $\models$ — soundness and completeness — is the central concern of the propositional calculus.
