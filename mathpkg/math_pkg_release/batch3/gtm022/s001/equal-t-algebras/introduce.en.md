Two $T$-algebras are equal precisely when they are identical as sets and all their corresponding operations coincide. This definition emphasizes that an algebra is more than just its underlying set — it is the set together with a specific interpretation of each operation symbol in the type $T$. The equality condition requires both the carrier sets and the structural operations to be identical.
