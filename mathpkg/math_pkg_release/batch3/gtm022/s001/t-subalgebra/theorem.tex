If $A$ is a $T$-algebra, a subset $B$ of $A$ is called a $T$-subalgebra of $A$ if it forms a $T$-algebra with operations the restrictions to $B$ of those on $A$; i.e., if for all $n$ and for all $t \in T_n$ and $b_1, \ldots, b_n \in B$, we have

$$t_A(b_1, \ldots, b_n) \in B.$$

Any intersection of subalgebras is a subalgebra, and so, given any subset $X$ of $A$, there is a unique smallest subalgebra containing $X$.
