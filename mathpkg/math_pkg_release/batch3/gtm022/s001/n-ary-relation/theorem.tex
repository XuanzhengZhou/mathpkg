An $n$-ary relation $\rho$ on the sets $A_1, \ldots, A_n$ is a subset of the product set $A_1 \times \cdots \times A_n$. That is, $\rho$ is specified by giving those ordered $n$-tuples $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ of elements $a_i \in A_i$ which are in the relation $\rho$.

For binary relations, we write $(a_1, a_2) \in \rho$ or $\rho(a_1, a_2)$, each notation extending naturally to $n$-ary relations for any $n$.
