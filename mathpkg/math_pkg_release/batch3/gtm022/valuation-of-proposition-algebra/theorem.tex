A valuation of $P(X)$ is a proposition algebra homomorphism $v: P(X) \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}_2$. We say that $p \in P(X)$ is true with respect to $v$ if $v(p) = 1$, and that $p$ is false with respect to $v$ if $v(p) = 0$.

Since $X$ is a set of free generators of $P(X)$, the values $v(x)$ for $x \in X$ may be assigned arbitrarily and determine $v$ uniquely. In $\mathbb{Z}_2$, the operations are $F = 0$ and $a \Rightarrow b = 1 + a(1 + b)$, with addition and multiplication mod 2.
