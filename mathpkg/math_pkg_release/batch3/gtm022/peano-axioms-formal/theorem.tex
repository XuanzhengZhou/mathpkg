The first-order theory $\mathcal{P}$ of Peano arithmetic has one unary relation symbol $\theta$ ($\theta(x)$ means $x = 0$) and one binary relation symbol $s$ ($s(x, y)$ means $x$ is the successor of $y$), with axioms:
$$P_1: (\exists!x)\theta(x), \quad P_2: (\forall x)(\exists!y)s(y, x),$$
$$P_3: (\forall x)(\forall y)(\forall z)((s(z, x) \wedge s(z, y)) \Rightarrow x = y),$$
$$P_4: (\forall x)(\forall y)(s(x, y) \Rightarrow \sim\theta(x)),$$
and the induction axiom scheme $P_5$.
