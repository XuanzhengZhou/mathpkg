Proof. (Sketch) Suppose $\mathcal{T} = (\mathcal{R}, A, C)$. Choose $V_0 \supset C$ with $|V_0 - C| = \aleph$. Then $|P(V_0, \mathcal{R})| = \aleph$. Add distinctness axioms $\sim\mathcal{I}(x,y)$ for all distinct $x,y \in V_0 - C$. The resulting set is consistent (by compactness, since $\mathcal{T}$ has an infinite model), so has a model of cardinality at least $\aleph$. The downward part uses the Tarski-Vaught criterion to extract an elementary substructure of cardinality exactly $\aleph$. $\square$
