This lemma asserts that when two special words in the semigroup associated with a Turing machine are equivalent and the target word is terminal, there exists a derivation consisting entirely of forward steps. The proof uses a minimal-length path argument: if any backward step occurs, the determinism of the Turing machine (at most one forward step from any special word) allows the path to be shortened, contradicting minimality. This lemma is crucial for reducing the word problem to the halting problem.
