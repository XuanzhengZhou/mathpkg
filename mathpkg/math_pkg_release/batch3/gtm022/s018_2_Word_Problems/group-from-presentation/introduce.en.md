Given a presentation $(X, R)$, one constructs the free group $F$ on the generators $X$ and takes the smallest normal subgroup $K$ of $F$ containing all the relations $R$. The group presented by $(X, R)$ is the quotient $F/K$, commonly denoted $\langle X \mid R \rangle$. The same group admits many different presentations, and to avoid ambiguity a word is understood as an element of the free group $F$, while the element it represents in $G = F/K$ is called the group element represented by that word.
