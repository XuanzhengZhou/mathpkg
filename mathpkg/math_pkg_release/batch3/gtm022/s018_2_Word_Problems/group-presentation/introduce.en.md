A group presentation specifies a group by giving a set of generators $X$ and a set of relations $R$ among those generators. Each relation is a group-theoretic word $w(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ in the generators that is set equal to the identity. The presentation is finite when both the generator set and the relation set are finite. Every presentation determines a unique group: the factor group $F/K$ where $F$ is the free group on $X$ and $K$ is the smallest normal subgroup of $F$ containing $R$.
