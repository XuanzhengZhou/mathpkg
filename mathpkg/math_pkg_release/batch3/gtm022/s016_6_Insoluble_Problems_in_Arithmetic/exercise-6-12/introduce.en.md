This exercise asks to prove that the set of Godel numbers of formulas in the language of arithmetic that are true in the standard model $\mathbb{N}$ is not recursively enumerable. This is a corollary of Tarski's undefinability theorem and Godel's incompleteness results: if this set were recursively enumerable, then truth in $\mathbb{N}$ would be arithmetically definable, contradicting the undecidability established in Theorem 6.10.
