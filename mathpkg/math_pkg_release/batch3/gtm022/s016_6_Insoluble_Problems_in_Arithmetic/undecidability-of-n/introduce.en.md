Theorem 6.10 establishes that any theory $\mathcal{T}$ extending recursive arithmetic $N_0$ and having the natural numbers as a model is undecidable -- there is no algorithm to determine whether an arbitrary formula is a theorem of $\mathcal{T}$. The proof reduces the halting problem to the decision problem for $\mathcal{T}$: using Theorem 6.9, the computation relation of Turing machines is strongly definable, so a decision procedure for $\mathcal{T}$ would yield a decision procedure for the recursively insoluble halting problem. In particular, the theory $N$ of recursive arithmetic itself is undecidable.
