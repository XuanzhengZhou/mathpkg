Godel's First Incompleteness Theorem states that any consistent, effectively axiomatised theory $\mathcal{T}$ extending recursive arithmetic $N_0$ and having the natural numbers as a model is necessarily incomplete. The proof constructs a self-referential formula $q$ (essentially asserting "I am not provable") using the strong definability of recursive functions established in Theorem 6.9. The theorem shows that no effective axiomatisation of arithmetic can capture all arithmetic truths, and that adding the undecided formula as a new axiom only produces a new theory with its own Godel sentence, demonstrating an essential limitation of formal systems.
