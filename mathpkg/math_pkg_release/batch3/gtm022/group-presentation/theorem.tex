A group presentation is a set $X$ (generators) together with a set $R$ of group theoretical words on $X$ (relations). The presentation $(X, R)$ is called finite if both $X$ and $R$ are finite. The group determined by $(X, R)$ is $G = F/K$, where $F$ is the free group on $X$ and $K$ is the smallest normal subgroup of $F$ containing $R$. We write $G = \langle X \mid R \rangle$.
