Let $p \in P(X)$. We say that $p$ is valid, or is a tautology, if $v(p) = 1$ for every valuation $v$ of $P(X)$. We write $\models p$ to denote that $p$ is a tautology.

More generally, if $A \subseteq P(X)$ and $p \in P(X)$, we write $A \models p$ if, for every valuation $v$ with $v(a) = 1$ for all $a \in A$, we have $v(p) = 1$.
