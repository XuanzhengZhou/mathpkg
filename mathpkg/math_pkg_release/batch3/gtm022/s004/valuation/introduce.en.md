A valuation is a homomorphism from the proposition algebra $P(X)$ into the two-element Boolean algebra $\mathbb{Z}_2$. Since $X$ freely generates $P(X)$, a valuation is uniquely determined by its values on the propositional variables. The value $1$ represents truth and $0$ represents falsehood. This formalizes the intuitive notion of assigning truth values to propositions in a way that respects the logical connectives.
