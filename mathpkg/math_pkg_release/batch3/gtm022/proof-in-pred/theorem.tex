A \emph{proof} of length $n$ of $p$ from $A$ in $\operatorname{Pred}(V, \mathcal{R})$ is a sequence $p_1, \ldots, p_n$ such that $p_n = p$ and for each $i$, either (a) $p_i \in \mathcal{A} \cup A$, or (b) $p_j = p_k \Rightarrow p_i$ for some $j, k < i$ (Modus Ponens), or (c) $p_i = (\forall x)w$ where a proof of $w$ from some $A_0 \subseteq A$ with $x \notin \operatorname{var}(A_0)$ appears as a subsequence (Generalisation).
