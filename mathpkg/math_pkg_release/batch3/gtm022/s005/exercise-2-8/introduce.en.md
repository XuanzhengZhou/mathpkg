This exercise asks the reader to prove six basic theorems of propositional logic, establishing fundamental properties of disjunction and conjunction: introduction rules ($p \Rightarrow p \lor q$, $q \Rightarrow p \lor q$), commutativity of $\lor$ and $\land$, and elimination rules ($p \land q \Rightarrow p$, $p \land q \Rightarrow q$). These theorems confirm that the algebraic definitions of the connectives behave as expected.
