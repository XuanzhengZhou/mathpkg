Proof. To show $S/\mathcal{F}$ is a model of $\mathcal{T}$, one must verify that the defining conditions for universal quantifiers hold within $S$. For any $a \in S$ and formula $(\forall y)q(x,y)$, if $a$ fails to satisfy the universal statement, one constructs $b \in S$ witnessing the failure. Since $a$ is definable, $b$ (defined via minimization) is also definable. The countability follows from $S$ being countable. Non-standardness for non-principal $\mathcal{F}$ follows from the construction of an infinite element. $\square$
