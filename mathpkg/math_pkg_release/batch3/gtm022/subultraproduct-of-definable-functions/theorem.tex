Let $\mathcal{T}$ be the theory of $\mathbb{N}$. Let $S$ be the set of all functions $s: \mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{N}$ definable in $\mathcal{T}$. Let $\mathcal{F}$ be an ultrafilter on $\mathbb{N}$. Then the subultraproduct $S/\mathcal{F}$ is a countable model of $\mathcal{T}$. If $\mathcal{F}$ is not principal, then $S/\mathcal{F} \neq \mathbb{N}$.
