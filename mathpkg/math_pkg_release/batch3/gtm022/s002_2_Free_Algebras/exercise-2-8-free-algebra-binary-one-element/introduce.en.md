Exercise 2.8 from GTM022 §2. The similarity type $T$ contains exactly one binary operation symbol (e.g., a multiplication with no axioms). Let $X = \{x\}$ be a singleton set. The exercise asks to determine the cardinality of the free $T$-algebra $F$ on $X$. Unlike the unary case in Exercise 2.6, the binary operation allows forming expressions such as $x$, $x \cdot x$, $(x \cdot x) \cdot x$, $x \cdot (x \cdot x)$, and so on, yielding a countably infinite set of distinct binary trees.
