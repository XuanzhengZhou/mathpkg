This theorem guarantees that for any set $X$ of generators and any similarity type $T$ (a set of operation symbols with prescribed arities), a free $T$-algebra on $X$ always exists. Furthermore, any two free $T$-algebras on the same set $X$ are uniquely isomorphic via an isomorphism that respects the generator embeddings. The proof is constructive: the free algebra is built as a union of layers $F_n$, where elements of $F_n$ are formal $T$-expressions of increasing complexity.
