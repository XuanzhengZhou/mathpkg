Let $X$ be any set, let $F$ be a $T$-algebra and let $\sigma: X \rightarrow F$ be a function. We say that $F$ (more strictly $(F, \sigma)$) is a \text{free $T$-algebra on the set $X$ of free generators} if, for every $T$-algebra $A$ and function $\tau: X \rightarrow A$, there exists a unique homomorphism $\varphi: F \rightarrow A$ such that $\varphi \sigma = \tau$.
