Exercise 2.7 from GTM022 §2. When the similarity type $T$ is empty (no operations at all), a $T$-algebra is simply a set with no algebraic structure. The exercise asks to prove that $X$ itself, with the identity embedding, is the free $T$-algebra on $X$. This follows because any function $\tau: X \to A$ to another $T$-algebra $A$ (which is just a set) extends uniquely to a homomorphism, namely $\tau$ itself.
