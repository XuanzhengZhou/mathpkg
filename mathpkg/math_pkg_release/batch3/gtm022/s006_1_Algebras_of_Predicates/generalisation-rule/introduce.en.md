The Generalisation rule of inference formalizes the common mathematical argument: having proved $p(x)$ for an arbitrary $x$, we may conclude $(\forall x)p(x)$. The critical restriction is that $x$ must be "general" — it cannot appear free in any of the assumptions used in deriving $p(x)$. Without this restriction, one could illegally generalize from a statement that only holds under specific assumptions about $x$, leading to unsound deductions.
