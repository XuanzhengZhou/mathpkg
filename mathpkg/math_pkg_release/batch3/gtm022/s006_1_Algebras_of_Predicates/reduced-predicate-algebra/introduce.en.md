The reduced predicate algebra $P(V, \mathcal{R})$ is obtained by quotienting the free predicate algebra $\tilde{P}(V, \mathcal{R})$ by the syntactic congruence $\approx$. This quotient identifies formulas that are essentially the same up to renaming of bound variables, yielding the proper algebraic structure in which the operations of implication and universal quantification are well-defined on equivalence classes. The elements of $P(V, \mathcal{R})$ are the formal propositions of the predicate calculus.
