The congruence relation $\approx$ on $\tilde{P}(V, \mathcal{R})$ identifies formulas that differ only by the renaming of bound variables (a form of $\alpha$-equivalence adapted to the algebraic framework). It is defined by induction on the depth of quantification: formulas of depth $0$ (atomic formulas and $F$) are congruent only if identical; implications are congruent if their respective components are congruent; and universally quantified formulas are congruent if they quantify the same variable over congruent subformulas. This relation is the key to constructing the reduced predicate algebra as a quotient.
