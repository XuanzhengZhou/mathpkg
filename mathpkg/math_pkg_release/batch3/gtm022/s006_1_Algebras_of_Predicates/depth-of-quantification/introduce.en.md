The depth of quantification $d(w)$ measures the maximum nesting level of quantifiers in a formula $w$. Atomic formulas and the constant $F$ have depth $0$, implication preserves the maximum depth of its subformulas, and each universal quantifier increases the depth by $1$. This numerical invariant is used to define the congruence relation on $\tilde{P}$ by induction on depth, and later to define truth valuations by induction on the depth of quantification.
