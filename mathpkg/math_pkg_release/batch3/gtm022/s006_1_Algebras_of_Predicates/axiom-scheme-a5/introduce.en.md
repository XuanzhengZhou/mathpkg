Axiom scheme $\mathcal{A}_5$ formalizes the principle of universal instantiation: if a property holds for all $x$, then it holds for any particular $y$. In the algebraic setting, $p(y)$ denotes the result of substituting $y$ for the free occurrences of $x$ in a representative of $p(x)$, and the axiom is stated with the implicit understanding that a representative of $[(\forall x)p(x)]$ is chosen in which $(\forall y)$ does not appear, avoiding variable capture.
