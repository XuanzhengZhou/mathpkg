This exercise establishes the fundamental semantic theorem for the predicate calculus: given an interpretation $(U, \varphi, \psi)$, there exists a unique truth valuation $v$ on $P(V, \mathcal{R})$ satisfying the defining conditions. The proof proceeds by induction on the depth of quantification, using the inductive definition of the conditions $(c_k)$. Uniqueness follows because each condition determines $v$ on formulas of depth $k$ in terms of its values on formulas of lower depth.
