The free predicate algebra $\tilde{P}(V, \mathcal{R})$ is the syntactic universe from which all formulas of the predicate calculus are built. Its free generators are the atomic formulas $r(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$, and it is equipped with the logical constant $F$ (false), the binary implication operation $\Rightarrow$, and a unary universal quantifier operation $(\forall x)$ for each individual variable $x \in V$. This construction mirrors the free algebra approach used for proposition algebras, extended to accommodate quantification.
