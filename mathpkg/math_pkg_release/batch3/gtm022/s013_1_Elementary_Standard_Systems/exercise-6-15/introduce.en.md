Exercise 6.15 asks the reader to analyze why the non-standard proof method for uniform continuity breaks down when the domain $U$ is an open interval $\{x : p < x < q\}$ instead of a closed set. The key issue is that the non-standard extension $*U$ may contain points infinitesimally close to the endpoints $p$ and $q$ that have no standard counterpart in $U$, breaking the transfer argument.
