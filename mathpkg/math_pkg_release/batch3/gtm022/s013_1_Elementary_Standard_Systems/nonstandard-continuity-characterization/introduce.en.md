A function $f$ is continuous at $a$ in the standard sense if and only if its non-standard extension $*f$ preserves the monad: points infinitely close to $a$ are mapped to points infinitely close to $*f(a)$. This elegant reformulation of continuity is an immediate consequence of the non-standard limit characterization.
