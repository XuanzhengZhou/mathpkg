In the non-standard natural numbers $*\mathbb{N}$, there is no smallest infinite natural number: if $n$ is infinite, then $n = m + 1$ for some natural number $m$ (a theorem of $\mathcal{T}(\mathbb{N})$), and $m$ is also infinite. Moreover, the set of infinite natural numbers cannot be internal (it is external), since any internal set of natural numbers that is unbounded would, by overspill, contain all sufficiently large finite numbers but no infinite ones.
