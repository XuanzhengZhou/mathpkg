In the non-standard real numbers $*\mathbb{R}$, elements are classified as finite (bounded by some standard real), infinite (not bounded by any standard real), or infinitesimal (smaller in absolute value than every positive standard real). Zero is infinitesimal, and for nonzero $a$, $a$ is infinitesimal if and only if $1/a$ is infinite. This classification is fundamental to Robinson's non-standard analysis.
