Let $\mathbb{S} \leqslant \mathbb{S}_1$, and let $\sigma, \sigma_1$ be sets of concurrent relations of $\mathbb{S}$, $\mathbb{S}_1$ such that $\sigma \leqslant \sigma_1$. Let $\mathbb{S}_1^{\sigma_1}$ be an enlargement of $\mathbb{S}_1$ with respect to $\sigma_1$. Then $\mathbb{S}_1^{\sigma_1}$ contains an enlargement $\mathbb{S}^\sigma$ of $\mathbb{S}$ with respect to $\sigma$. In particular, any full enlargement of $\mathbb{S}_1$ contains a full enlargement of $\mathbb{S}$.
