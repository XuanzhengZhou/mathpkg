When a standard system $\mathbb{S}$ is a subsystem of $\mathbb{S}_1$, their enlargements respect this inclusion. If $\sigma$ and $\sigma_1$ are matching sets of concurrent relations, any enlargement of $\mathbb{S}_1$ with respect to $\sigma_1$ contains an enlargement of $\mathbb{S}$ with respect to $\sigma$. As a consequence, any full enlargement of the larger system contains a full enlargement of the subsystem.
