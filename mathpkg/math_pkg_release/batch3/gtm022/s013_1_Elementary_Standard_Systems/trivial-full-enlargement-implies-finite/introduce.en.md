If the full enlargement of $\mathcal{T}(\mathbb{S})$ is trivial (adds no new elements beyond those already in $\mathbb{S}$), then $\mathbb{S}$ must be finite. This is because an infinite system always possesses concurrent relations whose witnesses cannot all be standard elements. The result highlights why non-standard models are inevitable for infinite standard systems.
