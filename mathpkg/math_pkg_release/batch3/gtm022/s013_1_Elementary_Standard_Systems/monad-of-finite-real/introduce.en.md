For a finite real number $a$, the monad $\mu(a)$ is the set of all non-standard reals infinitesimally close to $a$. The relation $a \cong b$ (meaning $a - b$ is infinitesimal) is an equivalence relation on $*\mathbb{R}$. Monads are the equivalence classes of this relation, and they partition the finite non-standard reals.
