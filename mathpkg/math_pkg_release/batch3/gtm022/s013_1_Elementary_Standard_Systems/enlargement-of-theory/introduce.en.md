Given a set $\sigma$ of concurrent relations on a standard system $\mathbb{S}$, the enlargement $\mathcal{T}^\sigma$ extends the theory $\mathcal{T}(\mathbb{S})$ by adding a new constant $c_\rho$ for each concurrent relation $\rho$, together with axioms asserting that $c_\rho$ is a witness for the concurrency of $\rho$. The full enlargement $*\mathcal{T}$ is obtained when $\sigma$ consists of all concurrent relations. Enlargements are the key tool for constructing useful non-standard models via the compactness theorem.
