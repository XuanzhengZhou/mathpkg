An $n$-ary relation on sets $A_1, \ldots, A_n$ is defined as a subset of their Cartesian product $A_1 \times \cdots \times A_n$. This unifies the common notations for binary relations (such as $a_1 \rho a_2$, $(a_1, a_2) \in \rho$, or $\rho(a_1, a_2)$) with the general $n$-ary case. This formulation generalizes the concept of relation to any finite arity and serves as the foundation for defining functions and operations in universal algebra.
