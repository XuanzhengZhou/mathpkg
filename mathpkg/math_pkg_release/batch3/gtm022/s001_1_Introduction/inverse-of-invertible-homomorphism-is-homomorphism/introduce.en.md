If a homomorphism $\varphi: A \rightarrow B$ of $T$-algebras is bijective, its set-theoretic inverse $\varphi^{-1}: B \rightarrow A$ is automatically a homomorphism. This follows by applying $\varphi^{-1}$ to the homomorphism equation $\varphi(t_A(a_1, \ldots, a_n)) = t_B(\varphi(a_1), \ldots, \varphi(a_n))$, yielding $\varphi^{-1}(t_B(b_1, \ldots, b_n)) = t_A(\varphi^{-1}(b_1), \ldots, \varphi^{-1}(b_n))$ for all $b_i \in B$. This fact, together with the closure of homomorphisms under composition, establishes that the isomorphisms in the category of $T$-algebras are precisely the bijective homomorphisms.
