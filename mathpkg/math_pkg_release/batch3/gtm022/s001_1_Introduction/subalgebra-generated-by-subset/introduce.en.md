Given a $T$-algebra $A$ and any subset $X \subseteq A$, the intersection of all $T$-subalgebras containing $X$ is itself a $T$-subalgebra (since arbitrary intersections of subalgebras are subalgebras). This intersection is the unique smallest subalgebra containing $X$, called the subalgebra generated by $X$. This construction is fundamental in universal algebra, generalizing the notion of the subgroup generated by a set of elements in group theory.
