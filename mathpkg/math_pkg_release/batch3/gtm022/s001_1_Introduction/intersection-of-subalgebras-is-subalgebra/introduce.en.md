This proposition states that the collection of $T$-subalgebras of a given $T$-algebra $A$ is closed under arbitrary intersections. The proof is straightforward: if a family of subsets $\{B_i\}$ each satisfy the closure condition $t_A(b_1, \ldots, b_n) \in B_i$ for all $t \in T_n$, then the intersection $\bigcap_i B_i$ also satisfies it. This closure property guarantees the existence of a unique smallest subalgebra containing any given subset of $A$.
