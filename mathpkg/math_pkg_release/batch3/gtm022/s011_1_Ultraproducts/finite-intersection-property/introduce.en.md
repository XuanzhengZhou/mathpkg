A family $S$ of subsets of a set $I$ has the finite intersection property (FIP) if the intersection of any finitely many members of $S$ is nonempty. The FIP is the key condition characterizing when a set of subsets can be extended to a filter. It plays a crucial role in the construction of ultrafilters via Zorn's Lemma, and in the ultraproduct proof that every field has an algebraic closure.
