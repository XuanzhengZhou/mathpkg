A filter on a set $I$ is a nonempty collection of subsets closed under taking supersets and finite intersections, with the empty set excluded. Filters arise naturally in the ultraproduct construction when analyzing which indices satisfy a given formula. An ultrafilter is a maximal filter, characterized by the additional property that for every subset $A \subseteq I$, exactly one of $A$ or its complement belongs to the filter. Ultrafilters are central to the ultrapower and ultraproduct constructions in model theory.
