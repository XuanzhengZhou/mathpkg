Theorem 3.1 proves that every field admits an algebraic closure. The proof uses the ultraproduct construction: one takes the family of all splitting fields $F_r$ of polynomials $r \in F[x]$, forms the ultraproduct $\prod F_r / \mathcal{F}$ with respect to a suitably chosen ultrafilter $\mathcal{F}$ on the index set $R = F[x]$, and verifies that the resulting extension field of $F$ is algebraically closed. This is a striking application of model-theoretic methods (ultraproducts) to obtain a purely algebraic result.
