Lemma 2.4 characterizes exactly when a collection of subsets can be extended to a filter: a set $S$ of subsets of $I$ is contained in some filter on $I$ if and only if $S$ has the finite intersection property. The forward direction is immediate since filters are closed under finite intersections and exclude the empty set. The converse constructs a filter by taking all supersets of finite intersections of members of $S$. This lemma is a key step in the proof that every family with the FIP can be extended to an ultrafilter (via Zorn's Lemma).
