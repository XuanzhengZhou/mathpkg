Proof. Since $\mathcal{T}$ is consistent, it has a model $M$. Put $A' = \{p \in \mathcal{L}(\mathcal{T}) \mid p \text{ is true in } M\}$. Then $(\mathcal{R}, A', C)$ has $M$ as a model, so is consistent. It is complete because for each sentence $p$, either $p$ or $\sim p$ is true in $M$, hence in $A'$. $\square$
