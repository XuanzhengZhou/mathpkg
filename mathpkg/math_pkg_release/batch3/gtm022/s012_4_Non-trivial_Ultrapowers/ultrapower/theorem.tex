An ultraproduct $\prod_{i \in I} M_i / \mathcal{F}$ in which $M_i = M$ for all $i \in I$ is called an \text{ultrapower} of $M$ and denoted by $M^I / \mathcal{F}$. There is a natural embedding $\theta: M \rightarrow M^I / \mathcal{F}$ given by $\theta(m) = f_m \mathcal{F}$, where $f_m: I \rightarrow M$ is the constant function $f_m(i) = m$ for all $i \in I$. By identifying $m$ with $\theta(m)$, we may regard $M$ as a subset of $M^I / \mathcal{F}$.
