The first-order predicate calculus Pred(V,R) extends the propositional calculus with quantifier axioms and the rule of Generalisation. Axiom A4 handles quantifier distribution, A5 allows instantiation, and A6 permits vacuous quantification. Generalisation allows universal quantification when the variable is not free in assumptions.
