The consistent theory $\mathcal{T}$ is effectively axiomatised and complete. Show that $\mathcal{T}$ is decidable. Show further that there is a Turing machine which, given the Gödel number of an element $p \in \mathcal{L}(\mathcal{T})$, answers the question of whether or not $p$ is a theorem, and also provides a proof of its answer.
