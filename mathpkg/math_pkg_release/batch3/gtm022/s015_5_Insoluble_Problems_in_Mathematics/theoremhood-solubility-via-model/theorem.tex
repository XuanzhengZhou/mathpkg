Let $\mathcal{T} = (\mathcal{R}, A, C)$ be an effectively axiomatised first-order theory and let $p \in \mathcal{L}(\mathcal{T})$. The problem of deciding whether or not $p$ is a theorem of $\mathcal{T}$ is soluble: either find a proof of $p$ in $\mathcal{T}$, or, if $p$ is not a theorem, construct a model of the consistent theory $\mathcal{T}' = (\mathcal{R}, A \cup \{\sim p\}, C)$ in which $p$ is false.
