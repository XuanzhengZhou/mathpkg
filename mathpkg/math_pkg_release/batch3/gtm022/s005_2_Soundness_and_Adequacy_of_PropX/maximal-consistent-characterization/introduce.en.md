Lemma 2.10 characterizes maximal consistent subsets of $P(X)$ by three equivalent conditions: $F$ is not in $A$, $A$ equals its own deductive closure $\text{Ded}(A)$, and for every proposition $p$, exactly one of $p$ or its negation $\sim p$ belongs to $A$. This "completeness" property — that every proposition is decided — is the key structural feature that allows maximal consistent sets to serve as the building blocks for constructing valuations in the proof of the Adequacy Theorem.
