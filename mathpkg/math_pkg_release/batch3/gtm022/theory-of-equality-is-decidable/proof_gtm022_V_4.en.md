Proof. A quantifier reduction process is described that reduces any sentence to a Boolean combination of statements of the form $\operatorname{al}(n)$ (there are at least $n$ elements). Since a sentence has no free variables, the result is a Boolean combination of finitely many $\operatorname{al}(n)$. The truth of such a combination depends only on the cardinality of the model. A finite check of all possible cardinalities up to the maximum $n$ occurring settles the validity of the sentence. $\square$
