A standard model is isomorphic to the intended system; a non-standard model is not. Since first-order theories extending T(S) are not categorical (by Lowenheim-Skolem), non-standard models always exist for infinite systems. These models contain 'ideal' elements that behave like standard elements at the first-order level.
