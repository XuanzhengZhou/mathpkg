The reduction process described in the text eliminates quantifiers, reducing every sentence to a propositional combination of $\operatorname{al}(n)$ formulas ($n \geqslant 1$). Since the only possible combinations of truth values for $\operatorname{al}(1), \ldots, \operatorname{al}(k)$ in any model are those where truth of $\operatorname{al}(n+1)$ implies truth of $\operatorname{al}(n)$, one checks the finitely many possibilities to determine if the formula is a theorem.
