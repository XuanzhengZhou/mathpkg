The Axiom of Infinity (ZF6) asserts the existence of an infinite set. It states that there exists a set $a$ containing the empty set $\emptyset$ and closed under the successor operation $x \mapsto x \cup \{x\}$. This axiom guarantees the existence of an inductive set, from which the natural numbers can be constructed. (Note: the OCR artifact $(3y)$ in the original text has been corrected to $(\exists y)$.)
