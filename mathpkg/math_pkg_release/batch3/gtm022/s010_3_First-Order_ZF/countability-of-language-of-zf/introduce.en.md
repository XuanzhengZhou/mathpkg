The language $\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{S})$ of the first-order ZF theory is independent of the choice of the infinite set $V$ of variables used, provided $V$ contains a countable subset $V_0$. Any predicate $p \in P(V, \mathcal{R})$ with no free variables already belongs to $P(V_0, \mathcal{R})$. Since there are no individual constants and $\mathcal{R} = \{\mathcal{I}, \in\}$ has only two relation symbols, $P(V, \mathcal{R})$ is countable, and consequently $\mathcal{S}$ is a countable first-order theory.
