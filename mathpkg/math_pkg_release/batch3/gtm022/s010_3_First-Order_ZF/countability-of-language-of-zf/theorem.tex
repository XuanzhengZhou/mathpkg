$$
\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{S}) \text{ is independent of the choice of the infinite set } V \text{ of variables.}
$$
$$
\text{If } V_0 \subseteq V \text{ is countable and } p \in P(V, \mathcal{R}), \text{ var}(p) = \varnothing \Rightarrow p \in P(V_0, \mathcal{R}).
$$
$$
\text{Since } \mathcal{R} = \{\mathcal{I}, \in\} \text{ has 2 relation symbols and no individual constants, } P(V, \mathcal{R}) \text{ is countable.}
$$
$$
\text{Hence } \mathcal{S} \text{ is a countable first-order theory.}
$$
