The formal first-order ZF theory $\mathcal{S}$ is consistent under the assumption that informal set theory (within which the book is written) is consistent. The argument is that any proof of a contradiction $F$ in $\mathcal{S}$ would have an informal equivalent, contradicting the assumed consistency of informal set theory. The consistency of $\mathcal{S}$ is therefore conditional on the consistency of ordinary mathematical set theory.
