The Axiom of Extensionality is the first axiom of Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory formalized in first-order logic. It asserts that if two sets have exactly the same elements, then they are equal. In the formal language $\mathcal{S}$, this is written as $(\forall a)(\forall b)(((\forall x)(x \in a \Leftrightarrow x \in b)) \Rightarrow a = b)$, where $x \in y$ abbreviates the membership relation $\in(x,y)$ and $x = y$ abbreviates the equality relation $\mathcal{I}(x,y)$.
