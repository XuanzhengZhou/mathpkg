We have shown that $\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{S})$ is independent of the choice of $V$ and that we may suppose $V$ is countable. Since $\mathcal{R} = \{\mathcal{I}, \in\}$ has only two relation symbols and there are no individual constants, $P(V, \mathcal{R})$ is countable. Hence $\mathcal{S}$ is a countable first-order theory. By the Löwenheim-Skolem Theorem, every countable first-order theory that is consistent has a countable model. Assuming the consistency of $\mathcal{S}$ (which follows from the assumed consistency of informal set theory), $\mathcal{S}$ has a countable model.
