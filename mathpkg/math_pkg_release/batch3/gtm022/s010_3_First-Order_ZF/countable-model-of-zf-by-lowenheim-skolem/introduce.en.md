Since the formal ZF theory $\mathcal{S}$ is a countable first-order theory (having a countable language with no individual constants and only two relation symbols $\mathcal{I}$ and $\in$), the Löwenheim-Skolem Theorem guarantees the existence of a countable model for $\mathcal{S}$. This means that although ZF proves the existence of uncountable sets, the entire theory can be interpreted in a countable domain.
