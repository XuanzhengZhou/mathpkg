Let $\mathcal{T} \supseteq \mathcal{N}_0$ be a consistent, effectively axiomatised theory with $\mathbb{N}$ as model. Then there exists an element $q \in \mathcal{L}(\mathcal{T})$ such that neither $\mathcal{T} \vdash q$ nor $\mathcal{T} \vdash \sim q$. That is, $\mathcal{T}$ is incomplete. This incompleteness cannot be cured by adding finitely many axioms.
