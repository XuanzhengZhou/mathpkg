First-order ZF theory formalizes Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory in the predicate calculus with identity. The formal versions of the ZF axioms are written in the language with equality and membership. The theory is countable and, by Lowenheim-Skolem, has a countable model despite proving the existence of uncountable sets (Skolem's paradox).
