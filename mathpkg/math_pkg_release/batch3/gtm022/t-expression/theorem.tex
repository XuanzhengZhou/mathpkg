Let $A$ be any $T$-algebra, and let $F$ be the free $T$-algebra on the set $X = \{x_1, \ldots, x_n\}$. An element $w \in F$ is called a \emph{$T$-expression} (or \emph{word}) in $X$. For each such $w$, there is a unique associated function $f_w: A^n \to A$ called the \emph{coordinate function} defined by $f_w(a_1, \ldots, a_n) = \varphi(w)$ where $\varphi: F \to A$ is the unique homomorphism with $\varphi(x_i) = a_i$.
