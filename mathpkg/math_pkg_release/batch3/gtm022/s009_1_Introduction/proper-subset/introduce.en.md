The proper subset relation $a \subset b$ holds when $a$ is a subset of $b$ but $a$ is not equal to $b$, i.e., $a \subseteq b$ and $a \neq b$. This defines a strict partial order on the universe of sets. The distinction between $\subseteq$ and $\subset$ is important in ordinal theory, where statements such as $n = \{x \in \omega \mid x \subset n\}$ characterize the von Neumann natural numbers.
