The Axiom of Choice (ZF7), often abbreviated as AC, asserts that for any set $a$, there exists a choice function $f$ defined on the non-empty subsets of $a$ such that $f(x) \in x$ for every non-empty $x \subseteq a$. This axiom is independent of the other ZF axioms and is essential for many results in modern mathematics, including the well-ordering theorem, Zorn's lemma, and the existence of bases for arbitrary vector spaces.
