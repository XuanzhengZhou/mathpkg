Suppose $\lim s(n) = r$. For any standard $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists $n_0 \in \mathbb{N}$ such that $|s(n) - r| < \varepsilon$ for $n > n_0$. This statement transfers to $*\mathbb{R}$: for $n \in *\mathbb{N}$ with $n > n_0$, $|*s(n) - r| < \varepsilon$. So for infinite $n$, $|*s(n) - r| < \varepsilon$ for all standard $\varepsilon > 0$, i.e., $*s(n) \in \mu(r)$. The converse uses the same transfer principle in reverse.
