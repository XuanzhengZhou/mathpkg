A proposition algebra $P(X)$ is the free $T$-algebra on a set $X$ of propositional variables, with type $T$ consisting of a nullary operation $F$ (falsehood) and a binary operation $\Rightarrow$ (implication). All other standard logical connectives — negation, disjunction, conjunction, and equivalence — are defined as abbreviations in terms of $F$ and $\Rightarrow$. This algebraic approach to propositional logic simplifies the theory by reducing the primitive operations to just two.
