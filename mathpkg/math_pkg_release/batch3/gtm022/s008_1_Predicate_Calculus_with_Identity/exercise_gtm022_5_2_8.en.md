Let $\mathcal{T}$ be the first-order plane projective geometry of Example 2.3. The \textit{dual} $\bar{w}$ of an element $w \in P(V, \mathcal{R})$ is the element obtained from $w$ by interchanging $p$ and $\ell$ (the predicates for "point" and "line") and replacing $\in(x, y)$ by $\in(y, x)$ (for all $x, y \in V$) throughout. Show that if $\alpha$ is the map $\alpha(w) = \bar{w}$, and if $\beta$ is the identity map, then $(\alpha, \beta)$ is a semi-homomorphism (in fact an automorphism) of $P(V, \mathcal{R})$, satisfying the condition of Exercise 2.7. Hence prove that the dual of a theorem of $\mathcal{T}$ is a theorem of $\mathcal{T}$.
