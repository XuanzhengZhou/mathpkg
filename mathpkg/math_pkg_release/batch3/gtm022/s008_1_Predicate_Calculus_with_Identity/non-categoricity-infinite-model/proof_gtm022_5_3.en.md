\textbf{Proof.} Since $\mathcal{T}$ has an infinite model, by the Lowenheim-Skolem Theorem (Theorem 3.18) it has models of cardinality $\aleph_0$ and also models of cardinality $\aleph_1$ (or any other distinct infinite cardinals no smaller than $|\mathcal{T}|$). Models of different cardinalities cannot be isomorphic. Therefore $\mathcal{T}$ has non-isomorphic models and is not categorical.
