This result (a form of the Łoś-Vaught test) provides a powerful method for proving that a theory is complete: if $\mathcal{T}$ has only infinite models and is categorical in some infinite cardinal $\aleph$ no smaller than the cardinality of the language, then $\mathcal{T}$ must be complete. The proof uses the Löwenheim-Skolem theorem to obtain models of size $\aleph$ both of $\mathcal{T} \cup \{p\}$ and of $\mathcal{T} \cup \{\sim p\}$, which would be non-isomorphic if neither $p$ nor $\sim p$ were provable, contradicting categoricity.
