The Löwenheim-Skolem Theorem is the fundamental result of model theory. It states that if a first-order theory $\mathcal{T}$ of cardinality $\chi$ has an infinite model, then it has models of every infinite cardinality $\aleph \geqslant \chi$. In particular, first-order logic cannot distinguish between different infinite cardinalities — no first-order theory can have only countably infinite models, or only models of a particular uncountable cardinality. This has profound consequences for the foundations of mathematics.
