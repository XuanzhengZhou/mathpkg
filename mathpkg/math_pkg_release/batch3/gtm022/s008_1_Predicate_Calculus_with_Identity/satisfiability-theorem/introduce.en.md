The Satisfiability Theorem for $\text{Pred}_g$ establishes the equivalence between syntactic provability ($A \vdash_g p$) and semantic consequence ($A \vDash_g p$) in the predicate calculus with identity. Despite the restriction to proper interpretations making the semantic condition appear weaker than $A \cup I \vdash p$, the theorem asserts they are in fact equivalent. This is the completeness theorem for the logic of first-order mathematics with equality.
