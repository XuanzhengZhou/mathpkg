\textbf{Definition 3.6.} Let $(M_1, v_1, \psi_1)$ and $(M_2, v_2, \psi_2)$ be models of the theory $\mathcal{T}$. We say that $M_1$ is \textit{isomorphic} to $M_2$ if there exists a bijective map $\alpha: M_1 \to M_2$ such that $\alpha v_1 = v_2$ (i.e., $\alpha$ preserves constants) and for every relation symbol $r \in \mathcal{R}_t$, the map $\alpha$ preserves $r$: for all $(m_1, \ldots, m_t) \in M_1^t$, we have $(m_1, \ldots, m_t) \in \psi_1 r$ if and only if $(\alpha m_1, \ldots, \alpha m_t) \in \psi_2 r$.
