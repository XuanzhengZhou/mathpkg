Two models of a theory $\mathcal{T}$ are isomorphic if there exists a bijection between their underlying sets that preserves the interpretation of all constants and all relation symbols. This notion is central to classification problems in model theory: isomorphic models are considered structurally identical, and a theory is categorical in a cardinality if all its models of that size are isomorphic.
