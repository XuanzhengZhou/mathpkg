Definability captures the idea that a relation or function on a model can be expressed using the formal language of the theory. A relation is definable if it coincides with the interpretation $\psi(p)$ of some formula $p$, while a function $f$ is definable if there exists a formula $p(x_1, \ldots, x_n, y)$ that functionally describes the graph of $f$ and the theory proves the existence and uniqueness condition $(\exists! y)p(x_1, \ldots, x_n, y)$.
