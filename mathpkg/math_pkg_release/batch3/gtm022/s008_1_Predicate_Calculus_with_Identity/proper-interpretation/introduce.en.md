A proper interpretation restricts the class of admissible interpretations of $P(V, \mathcal{R})$ to those in which the distinguished binary relation symbol $\mathcal{I}$ is actually interpreted as the genuine identity (equality) relation on the underlying set $U$. This semantic restriction is the key step in building the predicate calculus with identity $\text{Pred}_g(V, \mathcal{R})$, as it ensures that the identity axioms are reflected in every model under consideration.
