The theory of pure equality $\mathcal{E}$, whose language contains only the identity symbol $\mathcal{I}$ and which has no non-logical axioms besides the identity axioms, is decidable. This is proved by exhibiting a syntactic reduction procedure that transforms any sentence into an equivalent propositional combination of cardinality statements $\text{al}(n)$ (asserting at least $n$ elements). Since truth of such combinations depends only on the size of the model, checking validity reduces to a finite case analysis.
