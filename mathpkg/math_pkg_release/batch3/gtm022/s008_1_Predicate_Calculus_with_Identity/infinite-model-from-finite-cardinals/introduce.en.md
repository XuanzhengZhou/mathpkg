If a first-order theory $\mathcal{T}$ possesses finite models of arbitrarily large size, then by a compactness argument one can construct an infinite model. The proof adds to the axioms of $\mathcal{T}$ the statements $\text{al}(n)$ (asserting the existence of at least $n$ distinct elements) for all natural numbers $n$, and shows that every finite subset of this enlarged axiom set is consistent by appealing to a sufficiently large finite model of $\mathcal{T}$.
