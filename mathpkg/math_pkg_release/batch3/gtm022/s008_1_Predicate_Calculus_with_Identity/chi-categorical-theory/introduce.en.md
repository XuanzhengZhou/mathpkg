A theory is $\chi$-categorical if it has, up to isomorphism, at most one model of cardinality $\chi$. Categoricity is a strong structural property: for example, elementary group theory is categorical in cardinal $1$ (there is only the trivial group), but not in cardinal $4$ (there exist two non-isomorphic groups of order $4$). The Löwenheim-Skolem theorem shows that no theory with an infinite model can be categorical in all infinite cardinals.
