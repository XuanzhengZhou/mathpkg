This exercise asks the reader to prove that the function mapping each natural number to the Gödel number of the corresponding Turing machine (in the ordering by Gödel number) is recursive. This establishes that the enumeration of all Turing machines is computable, a key technical fact used in diagonalization arguments for undecidability proofs.
