A Turing machine is a formal model of computation consisting of a finite set of internal states, a finite alphabet of symbols, and a deterministic transition function specified by a set of quadruples. The machine operates on a potentially infinite tape divided into squares, reading and writing symbols while transitioning between states. This definition formalizes the intuitive notion of a mechanical decision process as described in Section 1, providing the foundation for studying computability and decidability in mathematical logic.
