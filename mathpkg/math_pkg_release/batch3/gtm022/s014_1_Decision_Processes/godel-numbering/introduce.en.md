Gödel numbering is a systematic encoding scheme that assigns a unique natural number to each symbol, string, and finite sequence of strings, enabling Turing machines (and formal logical expressions) to be represented as integers. The encoding uses prime factorization to ensure uniqueness via the fundamental theorem of arithmetic: symbol numbers are mapped to prime exponents, string numbers to products of primes raised to symbol numbers, and sequence numbers to products of primes raised to string numbers. This arithmetization of syntax is the essential technical device for proving limitative results such as the unsolvability of the halting problem.
