A subset $U$ of $\mathbb{N}$ is called \textbf{recursive} if its characteristic function is recursive. Prove that $U \subseteq \mathbb{N}$ is recursive if and only if both $U$ and $\mathbb{N} - U$ are recursively enumerable.
