The Substitution Theorem for the predicate calculus $\text{Pred}(V, \mathcal{R})$ generalizes the corresponding result from the Propositional Calculus (Theorem 4.11). It states that a semi-homomorphism $(\alpha, \beta)$ between first-order algebras preserves both syntactic consequence ($\vdash$) and semantic consequence ($\vDash$). A semi-homomorphism is a less restrictive notion than a full homomorphism, which is necessary because a homomorphism would require identical sets of individual variables and would forbid natural substitutions such as interchanging two variables $x$ and $y$.
