If $A \vDash p$, then by the Adequacy Theorem (the converse of the Soundness Theorem, which together with Soundness gives the Gödel-Henkin Completeness Theorem: $A \vDash p$ if and only if $A \vdash p$), we have $A \vdash p$. Any proof of $p$ from $A$ uses only finitely many formulas from $A$ as assumptions. Let $A_0$ be this finite set of assumptions. Then $A_0 \vdash p$, and by the Soundness Theorem, $A_0 \vDash p$.
