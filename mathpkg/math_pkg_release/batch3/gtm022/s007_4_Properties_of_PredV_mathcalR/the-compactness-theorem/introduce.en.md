The Compactness Theorem for predicate calculus states that if a formula $p$ is a semantic consequence of a (possibly infinite) set $A$ of formulas, then $p$ is already a semantic consequence of some finite subset $A_0 \subseteq A$. This follows from the Completeness Theorem (Gödel-Henkin): semantic consequence $\vDash$ is equivalent to syntactic consequence $\vdash$, and any proof uses only finitely many assumptions. The Compactness Theorem is a fundamental result in model theory with far-reaching applications.
