Every module $M$ contains a trivial submodule consisting solely of the zero element, denoted by $(0)$. It is the smallest possible submodule, analogous to the trivial subgroup $\{0\}$ in group theory. The zero submodule plays the role of the additive identity in the lattice of submodules formed by the operations of sum and intersection.
