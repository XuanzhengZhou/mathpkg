A ring $R$ is an abelian group under addition. Define scalar multiplication as the ring multiplication $R \times R \to R$. The module axioms follow directly from the ring axioms: $(a+b)x = ax+bx$ by distributivity, $a(x+y) = ax+ay$ by distributivity, $a(bx) = (ab)x$ by associativity of multiplication, and $1 \cdot x = x$ by the identity property. Thus $R$ is an $R$-module. A subset $I \subseteq R$ is a submodule if and only if it is closed under subtraction and under multiplication by arbitrary elements of $R$, which is precisely the definition of an ideal.
