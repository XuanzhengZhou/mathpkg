Let $R$ be a ring and $M$ an $R$-module. An $R$-submodule (or simply a submodule) of $M$ is a non-empty subset $N$ of $M$ such that

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] If $x_1, x_2 \in N$, then $x_1 - x_2 \in N$.
\item[(b)] If $a \in R$ and $x \in N$, then $ax \in N$.
\end{enumerate}

If $N$ is a submodule of $M$, then it is also an $R$-module if, for $a \in R$ and $x \in M$, we define the product of $a$ and $x$ to be $ax$, the product which is already defined in virtue of the fact that $M$ is an $R$-module.
