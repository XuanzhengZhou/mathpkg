The sum of submodules is a fundamental operation on the set of submodules of a given module $M$. For two submodules, their sum consists of all pairwise sums of elements. For an arbitrary collection $\{N_\alpha\}$, the sum is defined using finite linear combinations: each element of the sum has only finitely many non-zero components. The resulting sum is the smallest submodule containing every $N_\alpha$, making sum the join operation in the lattice of submodules.
