Let $R$ be an integral domain satisfying $\mathrm{UF}_1$ (that is, every element of $R$ which is not a unit is a finite product of irreducible elements of $R$). Let $F$ be the field of fractions of $R$. For a given quotient ring $R_M$ (with respect to a multiplicative system $M$ in $R$), let $S$ denote the set of all irreducible elements of $R$ which divide elements of $M$, where associate elements are not regarded as distinct. Then $S$ is uniquely determined by $R_M$. Conversely, given an arbitrary set $S$ of irreducible elements of $R$, let $M_0$ be the multiplicative system generated by $S$; then $R_{M_0}$ is a quotient ring of $R$. These two maps establish a $(1,1)$ correspondence between the quotient rings of $R$ (in $F$) with respect to multiplicative systems in $R$, and the sets of irreducible elements of $R$.
