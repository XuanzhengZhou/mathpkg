This theorem provides a complete classification of all quotient rings (localizations) of a unique factorization domain. In a UF-domain, the multiplicative structure is governed by the irreducible elements up to association. The theorem establishes that specifying which irreducible elements to invert is equivalent to specifying a quotient ring. This is analogous to the fact that in the ring of integers, every subring containing $\mathbb{Z}$ and contained in $\mathbb{Q}$ is determined by which prime numbers are allowed in denominators.
