A multiplicative system in a ring $R$ is a multiplicatively closed subset of $R$ that excludes zero and consists entirely of regular elements. This concept is the foundation for constructing rings of fractions, also known as localization. The requirement that all elements be regular ensures that the canonical map from $R$ to the resulting quotient ring is injective, avoiding the zero ring collapse that occurs when inverting zero divisors.
