Let $R$ be a ring. A \textbf{multiplicative system} (abbreviation: m.s.) in $R$ is a non-empty subset $M$ of $R$ which does not contain the zero of $R$ and which is closed under multiplication—that is, if $m_1 \in M$, $m_2 \in M$, then $m_1m_2 \in M$. Additionally, all the elements of $M$ are required to be regular in $R$.
