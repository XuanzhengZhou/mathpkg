This proposition characterizes exactly which elements of the original ring $R$ become invertible after localizing at a multiplicative system $M$. An element $b'$ becomes a unit in $R_M$ precisely when it divides some element of $M$. Geometrically, this says that $M'$ is the saturation of $M$: it is the largest multiplicative system that yields the same quotient ring as $M$. In particular, $R_{M'} = R_M$, so one may always replace $M$ by its saturation without changing the localization.
