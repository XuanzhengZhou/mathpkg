\begin{enumerate}
\item If $M$ is the set of all units of $R$, then for any $a \in R$ and $m \in M$, the quotient $a/m = a \cdot m^{-1} \in R$ since $m$ is a unit in $R$. Hence every element of $R_M$ already lies in $R$, and $R \subseteq R_M$ always, so $R_M = R$.

\item If $M$ is the set of all regular elements of $R$, then $R_M$ consists of all quotients $a/m$ where $a \in R$ and $m$ is regular in $R$. By definition, the total quotient ring $F$ consists precisely of all such quotients. Hence $R_M = F$.
\end{enumerate}
