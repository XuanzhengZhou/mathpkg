This corollary (sometimes called the supplementary law of quadratic reciprocity) gives a closed-form formula for determining whether $2$ is a quadratic residue modulo an odd prime $p$. The result $\left(\frac{2}{p}\right) = (-1)^{(p^2-1)/8}$ means that $2$ is a quadratic residue modulo $p$ precisely when $p \equiv \pm 1 \pmod{8}$. Together with the main quadratic reciprocity law, this completely solves the problem of evaluating Legendre symbols.
