For $p$ an odd prime, $\left(\frac{2}{p}\right) = (-1)^{(p^2-1)/8}$. This follows from evaluating $2^{(p-1)/2} \pmod{p}$ using Gauss sums or by noting that $2$ is a square mod $p$ iff $p \equiv \pm 1 \pmod{8}$.
