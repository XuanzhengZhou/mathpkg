A quadratic field is the simplest nontrivial type of algebraic number field, obtained by adjoining a square root to the rational numbers. Every quadratic field is a normal (Galois) extension of $\mathbb{Q}$ with Galois group of order 2, generated by the conjugation map $a + b\sqrt{m} \mapsto a - b\sqrt{m}$. Quadratic fields serve as the primary testing ground for algebraic number theory, and the study of prime decomposition in them leads naturally to the Legendre symbol and quadratic reciprocity.
