A \textbf{quadratic field} $K$ is an extension of degree 2 of the rational number field $\mathbb{Q}$. It is generated over $\mathbb{Q}$ by the square root of a rational number. Multiplying or dividing this rational number by the square of a suitable integer, we may assume that it is a \textit{square-free} integer $m$, that is, an integer $m$ without square factors. Let $K = \mathbb{Q}(e)$ where $e^2 = m$.

Any element $x$ of $K$ is of the form $x = a + be$ with $a, b \in \mathbb{Q}$. The mapping $a + be \mapsto a - be$ is an automorphism of $K$ over $\mathbb{Q}$. Thus $K$ is a normal extension of $\mathbb{Q}$ with a cyclic Galois group of order $2$.
