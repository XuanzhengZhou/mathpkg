Let $p$ be an odd prime and let $\mathbb{Q}(z)$ be the cyclotomic field of $p$-th roots of unity. The Galois group of $\mathbb{Q}(z)$ over $\mathbb{Q}$ is a cyclic group of order $p-1$, and therefore it contains one and only one subgroup of index $2$. To this subgroup corresponds a quadratic subfield $K$ of $\mathbb{Q}(z)$, uniquely determined by $p$.

Furthermore:
$$K = \begin{cases}
\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{p}) & \text{if } p \equiv 1 \pmod{4},\\[4pt]
\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{-p}) & \text{if } p \equiv 3 \pmod{4}.
\end{cases}$$

The discriminant of the ring $R$ of algebraic integers of $K$ over $\mathbb{Z}$ is $p$.
