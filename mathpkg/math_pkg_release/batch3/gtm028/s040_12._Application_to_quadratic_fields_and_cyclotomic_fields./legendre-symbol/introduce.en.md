The Legendre symbol $\left(\frac{s}{p}\right)$ is a fundamental multiplicative character modulo an odd prime $p$ that encodes whether an integer $s$ is a quadratic residue modulo $p$. It takes the value $+1$ when $s$ is a square in the finite field $\mathbb{F}_p$ and $-1$ otherwise. The Legendre symbol provides a compact notation for expressing the decomposition behavior of primes in quadratic fields and is the central object in the formulation of the quadratic reciprocity law.
