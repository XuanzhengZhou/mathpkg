This proposition gives explicit necessary and sufficient conditions for an element of a quadratic field to be an algebraic integer. The characterization depends on the congruence class of the square-free integer $m$ modulo 4, leading to two distinct cases: $m \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$ and $m \equiv 2,3 \pmod{4}$. These conditions determine the ring of integers $R = \mathcal{O}_K$ and the discriminant of the quadratic field, which are essential for analyzing prime decomposition.
