Let $p$ be an odd prime, let $z$ be a primitive $p$-th root of unity, and let $R'$ be the ring of algebraic integers in the cyclotomic field $\mathbb{Q}(z)$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $[\mathbb{Q}(z) : \mathbb{Q}] = p - 1$.
\item The discriminant of $R'$ over $\mathbb{Z}$ is $p^{p-2}$.
\item $\{1, z, \ldots, z^{p-2}\}$ is an integral basis of $R'$ over $\mathbb{Z}$.
\end{enumerate}
