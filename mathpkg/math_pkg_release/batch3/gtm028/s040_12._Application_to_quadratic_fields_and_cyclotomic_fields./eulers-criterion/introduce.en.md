Euler's criterion provides an explicit computational formula for the Legendre symbol using exponentiation modulo $p$. It equates $\left(\frac{s}{p}\right)$ with $s^{(p-1)/2} \bmod p$, reducing the problem of determining quadratic residuacity to modular exponentiation. The proof relies on the fact that the multiplicative group of $\mathbb{F}_p$ is cyclic of order $p-1$, which is a basic result from finite field theory.
