Let $q \neq p$ be a prime. Since $q$ does not divide the discriminant $p^{p-2}$ of $\mathbb{Q}(z)$, the prime $q$ is unramified in $\mathbb{Q}(z)$ (ramified primes are divisors of the discriminant).

By Theorem 22 of §9, for an unramified prime $q$ with $g$ distinct prime factors in $R'$, each having the same relative degree $f$, we have $fg = [\mathbb{Q}(z) : \mathbb{Q}] = p-1$.

Let $\mathfrak{D}$ be any prime ideal of $R'$ lying over $(q)$. The residue field $R'/\mathfrak{D}$ is a finite field of characteristic $q$, and it is obtained from $\mathbb{Z}/(q)$ by adjoining the $p$-th roots of unity. The $p$-th roots of unity in a field of characteristic $q \neq p$ are distinct, and they lie in the extension $\mathbb{F}_{q^f}$ where $f = [R'/\mathfrak{D} : \mathbb{Z}/(q)]$ is the relative degree.

Since the multiplicative group of $\mathbb{F}_{q^f}$ has order $q^f - 1$, the field $\mathbb{F}_{q^f}$ contains primitive $p$-th roots of unity if and only if $p \mid (q^f - 1)$, i.e., $q^f \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$. Therefore $f$ is the smallest positive integer satisfying this congruence, for if a smaller $f' < f$ satisfied $q^{f'} \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$, then $\mathbb{F}_{q^{f'}}$ would already contain the $p$-th roots of unity, contradicting the minimality of $f = [R'/\mathfrak{D} : \mathbb{Z}/(q)]$.

Finally, the decomposition field $L$ of $q$ in $\mathbb{Q}(z)$ corresponds to the subgroup of the Galois group generated by the Frobenius automorphism. The degree $[L:\mathbb{Q}]$ equals $g$, the number of prime factors. If $g$ is even, then $L$ contains the unique quadratic subfield $K$ (since the subgroup of index $2$ contains the decomposition group), meaning $q$ is decomposed in $K$. Conversely, if $g$ is odd, the decomposition group has odd order and cannot contain the subgroup of index $2$, so $q$ is inertial in $K$.
