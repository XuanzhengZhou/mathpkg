Let $p$ be an odd prime and let $q \neq p$ be a prime. Let $R'$ be the ring of algebraic integers in the cyclotomic field $\mathbb{Q}(z)$. Let $g$ be the number of distinct prime ideal factors of $q$ in $R'$, and let $f$ be their common relative degree. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $q$ is unramified in $\mathbb{Q}(z)$, and $fg = p - 1$.
\item $f$ is the smallest positive integer such that $q^f \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$.
\item The decomposition field of $q$ in $\mathbb{Q}(z)$ contains the quadratic subfield $K$, and $q$ is decomposed in $K$ if and only if $g$ is even.
\end{enumerate}
