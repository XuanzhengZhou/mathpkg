This proposition describes the decomposition of a prime $q \neq p$ in the $p$-th cyclotomic field. The prime $q$ is unramified, and its decomposition is governed purely by the order $f$ of $q$ modulo $p$ (the smallest positive integer with $q^f \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$). The number of distinct prime ideal factors $g$ satisfies $fg = p-1$, and the parity of $g$ determines whether $q$ is decomposed or inertial in the unique quadratic subfield. This connection is the key to proving quadratic reciprocity.
