It follows directly from Euler's criterion:
$$\left(\frac{s}{p}\right)\left(\frac{t}{p}\right) \equiv s^{\frac{1}{2}(p-1)} \cdot t^{\frac{1}{2}(p-1)} = (st)^{\frac{1}{2}(p-1)} \equiv \left(\frac{st}{p}\right) \pmod{p}.$$

Since both sides take values in $\{\pm 1\}$ and $p > 2$, the congruence modulo $p$ implies equality as integers.
