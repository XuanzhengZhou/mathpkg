The multiplicativity of the Legendre symbol, $\left(\frac{s}{p}\right)\left(\frac{t}{p}\right) = \left(\frac{st}{p}\right)$, is a direct consequence of Euler's criterion. This property shows that the Legendre symbol defines a homomorphism from the multiplicative group of $\mathbb{F}_p$ to the group $\{\pm 1\}$, making it a Dirichlet character modulo $p$. Multiplicativity is essential for reducing the computation of Legendre symbols to prime arguments, a key step in proving the quadratic reciprocity law.
