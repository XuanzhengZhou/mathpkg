For an odd prime $p$, let $z$ be a primitive $p$-th root of unity. The \textbf{cyclotomic field} of $p$-th roots of unity is the simple extension $\mathbb{Q}(z)$ of $\mathbb{Q}$. The other roots of unity are $z^2, z^3, \ldots, z^{p-1}$ with $z^p = 1$.

The $p-1$ primitive $p$-th roots of unity are the roots of the cyclotomic polynomial:
$$F(Z) = Z^{p-1} + Z^{p-2} + \cdots + Z + 1 = (Z - z)(Z - z^2) \cdots (Z - z^{p-1}).$$
