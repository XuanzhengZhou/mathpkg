A cyclotomic field is generated by adjoining a primitive $p$-th root of unity to the rational numbers. These fields are abelian (in fact cyclic) extensions of $\mathbb{Q}$ and play a central role in class field theory. The $p$-th cyclotomic field $\mathbb{Q}(z)$ has degree $p-1$ over $\mathbb{Q}$, and its ring of integers admits a particularly simple integral basis $\{1, z, \ldots, z^{p-2}\}$. The cyclotomic fields provide the setting for proving the quadratic reciprocity law via Galois theory.
