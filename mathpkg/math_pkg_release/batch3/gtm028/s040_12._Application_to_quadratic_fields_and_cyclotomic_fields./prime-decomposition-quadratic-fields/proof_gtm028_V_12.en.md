For a quadratic field $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{m})$, an odd prime $p \nmid m$ is decomposed if $m$ is a quadratic residue mod $p$ (i.e., $\left(\frac{m}{p}\right) = 1$), and inertial if $m$ is a non-residue mod $p$ (i.e., $\left(\frac{m}{p}\right) = -1$). For $p=2$ when $m \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$, $2$ is decomposed iff $(m-1)/4$ is even; otherwise $2$ is ramified. This follows from analyzing the factorization of the minimal polynomial modulo $p$ and the Dedekind-Kummer theorem on prime decomposition.
