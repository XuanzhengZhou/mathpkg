Theorem 32 is the main result on prime decomposition in quadratic fields, providing a complete classification of how rational primes behave when extended to a quadratic field $K = \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{m})$. The theorem distinguishes three possibilities for each prime $p$: ramified, decomposed (split), or inertial (inert). The criteria are expressed in terms of the discriminant $\mathfrak{d}$ and the Legendre symbol $\left(\frac{m}{p}\right)$, establishing a direct link between the arithmetic of quadratic fields and the theory of quadratic residues.
