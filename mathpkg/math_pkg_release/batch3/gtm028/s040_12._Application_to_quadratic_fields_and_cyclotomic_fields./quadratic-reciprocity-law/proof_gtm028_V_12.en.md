For distinct odd primes $p, q$, let $K = \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{p^*})$ where $p^* = (-1)^{(p-1)/2}p$ is the unique quadratic subfield of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_p)$. The decomposition of $q$ in $K$ is governed by $\left(\frac{p^*}{q}\right)$. On the other hand, $q$ decomposes in $K$ iff the Frobenius at $q$ acts trivially on $K$, which occurs iff $q$ is a square mod $p$, i.e., $\left(\frac{q}{p}\right) = 1$. Equating these conditions yields $\left(\frac{p^*}{q}\right) = \left(\frac{q}{p}\right)$, which expands to $\left(\frac{p}{q}\right)\left(\frac{q}{p}\right) = (-1)^{(p-1)(q-1)/4}$.
