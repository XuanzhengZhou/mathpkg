If $f(X)$ is an irreducible polynomial in $k[X]$, of degree $n \geq 1$, then we know that there exists an algebraic extension $K$ of $k$, of relative degree $n$ over $k$ (Theorem 3', \S\ 2). If $k$ is algebraically closed we must have $K = k$, whence $n = 1$. Thus (a) implies (b).

It is clear that (b), (c), and (d) are equivalent. Finally, if $K$ is any algebraic extension of $k$ and $x$ is any element of $K$, then the minimal polynomial of $x$ over $k$ is irreducible and therefore has degree $1$ if (b) holds. Therefore $x \in k$, $K = k$, that is, (b) implies (a).
