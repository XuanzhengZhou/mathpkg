Let $M$ be an $R$-module. A normal series in $M$ is a descending (but not necessarily strictly descending) finite chain of submodules
$$M = M_0 \supset M_1 \supset M_2 \supset \cdots \supset M_r = (0),$$
beginning with $M$ and ending with $(0)$; the integer $r$ is called the length of the normal series. The inclusions need not be proper. If all inclusions are proper, i.e.,
$$M = M_0 > M_1 > M_2 > \cdots > M_r = (0),$$
then the normal series is said to be without repetitions. A refinement of a normal series is obtained by inserting additional terms.
