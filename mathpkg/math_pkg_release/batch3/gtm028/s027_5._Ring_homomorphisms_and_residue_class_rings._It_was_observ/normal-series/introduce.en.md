A normal series of a module is a finite descending chain of submodules from the module itself down to the zero submodule. The terms need not be distinct — repetitions are allowed. A refinement inserts additional submodules between existing ones. Normal series provide the framework for defining composition series, which are the module-theoretic analogue of composition series in group theory and are fundamental for the Jordan-Hölder theory.
