Given any ideal $N$ in a ring $R$, one can construct a homomorphic image $\bar{R} = R/N$ whose kernel is precisely $N$. The elements of $R/N$ are the cosets $a + N$, with addition and multiplication induced from $R$. This construction shows that the converse of the earlier observation holds: every ideal is the kernel of some ring homomorphism.
