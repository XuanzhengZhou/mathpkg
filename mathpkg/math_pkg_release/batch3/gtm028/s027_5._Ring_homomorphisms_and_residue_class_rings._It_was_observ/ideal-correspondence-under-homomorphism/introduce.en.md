Theorem 6 establishes the fundamental correspondence between ideals in a ring and ideals in its homomorphic image. Given a surjective ring homomorphism $T: R \to R'$ with kernel $N$, there is a bijective, inclusion-preserving correspondence between ideals of $R'$ and ideals of $R$ containing the kernel $N$. The theorem also provides canonical isomorphisms $\mathfrak{A}/N \cong \mathfrak{A}'$ and $R/\mathfrak{A} \cong R'/\mathfrak{A}'$, extending the isomorphism theorems to the setting of ring homomorphisms.
