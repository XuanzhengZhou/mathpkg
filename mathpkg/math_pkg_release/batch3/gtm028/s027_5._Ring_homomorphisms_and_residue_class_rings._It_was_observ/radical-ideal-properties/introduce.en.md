The radical $\sqrt{\mathfrak{A}}$ of an ideal $\mathfrak{A}$ consists of all elements whose some power lies in $\mathfrak{A}$. It is itself an ideal and the operation is idempotent ($\sqrt{\sqrt{\mathfrak{A}}} = \sqrt{\mathfrak{A}}$). The radical respects intersections and products, making it a fundamental tool for relating ideals to their "nilpotent-free" counterparts. Ideals equal to their own radical are called radical ideals.
