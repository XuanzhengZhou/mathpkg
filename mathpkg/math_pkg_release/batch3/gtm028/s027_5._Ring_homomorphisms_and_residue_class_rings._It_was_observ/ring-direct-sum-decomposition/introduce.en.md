Theorem 30 characterizes when a ring with identity decomposes as a direct sum of ideals. The key condition is that the subrings $R_i$ mutually annihilate each other ($R_i R_j = (0)$ for $i \neq j$). In this case, the identity decomposes into a sum of orthogonal idempotents $e_i$, and each $R_i = Re_i$ is a ring with identity $e_i$. This decomposition is fundamental for understanding the structure of Artinian rings and plays a crucial role in primary decomposition theory — when the ring decomposes, ideals and their primary components also decompose accordingly.
