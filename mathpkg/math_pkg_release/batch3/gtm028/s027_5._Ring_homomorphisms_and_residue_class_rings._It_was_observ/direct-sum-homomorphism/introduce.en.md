Theorem 23 expresses the universal property of the direct sum: a homomorphism from a direct sum is uniquely determined by its restrictions to the summands. Given homomorphisms on each $M_i$, there exists a unique extension to the whole direct sum, defined componentwise. Moreover, if each component map is onto (resp. an isomorphism) and the target sum is direct, then the assembled map is onto (resp. an isomorphism). This makes the direct sum a coproduct in the category of $R$-modules.
