In any ring $R$, the radical $\sqrt{(0)}$ of the zero ideal (the nilradical of $R$) is the intersection of all prime ideals of $R$.
$$\sqrt{(0)} = \bigcap_{\mathfrak{p} \text{ prime}} \mathfrak{p}.$$
Equivalently, an element of $R$ is nilpotent if and only if it is contained in every prime ideal of $R$.
