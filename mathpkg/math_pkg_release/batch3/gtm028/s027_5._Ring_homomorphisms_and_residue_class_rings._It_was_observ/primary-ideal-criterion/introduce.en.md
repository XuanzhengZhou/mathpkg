Theorem 13 provides a convenient three-part criterion for verifying that an ideal $\mathfrak{Q}$ is primary with radical $\mathfrak{P}$. The conditions require that $\mathfrak{Q}$ is contained in $\mathfrak{P}$, every element of $\mathfrak{P}$ is nilpotent modulo $\mathfrak{Q}$, and the "zero-divisor" condition for primary ideals holds with $\mathfrak{P}$ as the radical. This criterion is often more practical to verify than the direct definition of primary ideals.
