Let $M$ be a module over a ring $R$ and let $\mathfrak{A}$ be any ideal contained in the order of $M$. Define $\bar{R} = R/\mathfrak{A}$. For $\bar{c} \in \bar{R}$ arising as the image of $c \in R$ and for $x \in M$, set $\bar{c}x = cx$. This definition is independent of the choice of representative $c$, and makes $M$ into an $\bar{R}$-module. Any $R$-submodule of $M$ is an $\bar{R}$-submodule, and conversely. Thus the study of $M$ as an $R$-module is equivalent to its study as an $\bar{R}$-module.
