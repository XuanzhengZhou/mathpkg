When an ideal $\mathfrak{A}$ is contained in the order (annihilator) of a module $M$, the ring of operators $R$ can be replaced by the smaller residue class ring $R/\mathfrak{A}$ without changing the module structure. This is because elements of $\mathfrak{A}$ act as zero on $M$, so the action factors through the quotient. This construction is crucial because $R/\mathfrak{A}$ often has a simpler structure than $R$, making the module theory more tractable.
