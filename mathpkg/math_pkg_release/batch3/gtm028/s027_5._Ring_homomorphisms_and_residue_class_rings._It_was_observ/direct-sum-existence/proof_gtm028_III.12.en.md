The uniqueness follows from Theorem 23, Corollary. To prove existence, define $M$ to consist of all ordered $r$-tuples $x = (x_1, x_2, \cdots, x_r)$ with $x_i \in M'_i$. If $y = (y_1, \cdots, y_r)$ is another member of $M$ and $a \in R$, we define $x + y = (x_1+y_1, \cdots, x_r+y_r)$ and $ax = (ax_1, \cdots, ax_r)$. Thus $M$ clearly becomes an $R$-module. We define $M_i$ to consist of all $(x_1, \cdots, x_r)$ such that $x_j = 0$ for $j \neq i$. It is obvious that $M = M_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus M_r$ and that $x_i \mapsto (0, \cdots, 0, x_i, 0, \cdots, 0)$ is an $R$-isomorphism of $M'_i$ onto $M_i$.
