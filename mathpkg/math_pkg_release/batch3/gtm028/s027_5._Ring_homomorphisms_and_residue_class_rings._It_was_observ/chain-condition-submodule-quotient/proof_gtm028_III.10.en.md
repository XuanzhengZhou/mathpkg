If the a.c.c. holds in $M$, then it obviously holds in $N$ (any ascending chain in $N$ is also an ascending chain in $M$) and in $M - N$ (by the correspondence between submodules of $M - N$ and submodules of $M$ containing $N$).

Conversely, assume the a.c.c. holds in $N$ and in $M - N$. Let $\{L_i\}$ be an ascending sequence of submodules of $M$. Then the ascending sequences $\{L_i + N\}$ and $\{L_i \cap N\}$ remain ultimately constant. For the latter sequence this follows from the a.c.c. in $N$. For the former, it follows from the a.c.c. in $M - N$ in view of the correspondence between submodules of $M - N$ and those of $M$ containing $N$.

Thus the theorem is proved for the a.c.c. The d.c.c. is treated similarly.
