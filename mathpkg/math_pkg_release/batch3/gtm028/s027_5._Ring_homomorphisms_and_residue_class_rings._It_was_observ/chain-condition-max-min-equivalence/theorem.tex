A module $M$ satisfies the ascending (descending) chain condition if and only if it satisfies the maximum (minimum) condition. Here:
\begin{itemize}
\item The ascending chain condition (a.c.c.) means every strictly ascending chain $M_1 \subset M_2 \subset \cdots$ of submodules is finite.
\item The descending chain condition (d.c.c.) means every strictly descending chain $M_1 \supset M_2 \supset \cdots$ is finite.
\item The maximum condition means every non-empty collection $C$ of submodules has a maximal element.
\item The minimum condition means every non-empty collection $C$ of submodules has a minimal element.
\end{itemize}
