By induction it is sufficient to consider the case $n = 2$. In view of Theorem 16 it is then enough to show that $M - M_1$ satisfies the chain condition in question. That it does, follows from
$$M - M_1 = (M_1 + M_2) - M_1 \cong M_2 - (M_1 \cap M_2),$$
since this last module satisfies the chain condition, by Theorem 16.
