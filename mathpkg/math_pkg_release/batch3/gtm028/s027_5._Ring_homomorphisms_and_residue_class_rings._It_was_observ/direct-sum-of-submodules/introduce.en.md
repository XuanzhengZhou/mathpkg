The direct sum is the fundamental construction for decomposing a module into simpler pieces. Submodules are independent if each intersects the sum of the others only in zero. When a module is the sum of independent submodules, every element can be written uniquely as a sum of components, one from each summand. Direct sums are the module-theoretic analogue of the decomposition of a vector space into a direct sum of subspaces.
