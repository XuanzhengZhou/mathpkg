This theorem characterizes completely reducible modules of finite length precisely as finite direct sums of simple modules. The result is the module-theoretic analog of the Wedderburn-Artin theorem: semisimple modules decompose uniquely into simple summands. The number of simple summands equals the length of the module, providing a complete invariant of the decomposition.
