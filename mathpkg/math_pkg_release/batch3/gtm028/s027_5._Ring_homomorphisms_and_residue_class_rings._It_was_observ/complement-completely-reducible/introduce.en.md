A complement of a submodule is a direct summand that together with the submodule spans the whole module. The concept generalizes the notion of a complementary subspace in linear algebra. A module in which every submodule has a complement is called completely reducible -- this is equivalent to being a direct sum of simple submodules and is the module-theoretic analog of semisimplicity.
