A primary ideal generalizes the concept of a power of a prime number in ordinary arithmetic to arbitrary rings. An ideal $\mathfrak{Q}$ is primary if whenever a product lies in $\mathfrak{Q}$ but one factor does not, some power of the other factor must lie in $\mathfrak{Q}$. The radical of a primary ideal is always a prime ideal, establishing a natural link between primary and prime ideals. Primary ideals are the building blocks of primary decomposition theory, the analogue of factorization into prime powers.
