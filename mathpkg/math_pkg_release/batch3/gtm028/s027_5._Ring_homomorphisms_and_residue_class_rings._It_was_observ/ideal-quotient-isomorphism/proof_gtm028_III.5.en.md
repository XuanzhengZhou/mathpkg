Apply Theorem 6 with $T$ being the natural homomorphism of $R$ onto $R/N$ and with kernel $N$. The ideal $\mathfrak{A}$ of $R$ contains $N$, so by the theorem, $\mathfrak{A}T = \mathfrak{A}/N$ is an ideal in $R/N$. Furthermore, the isomorphism $R/\mathfrak{A} \cong R'/\mathfrak{A}'$ from Theorem 6 becomes $R/\mathfrak{A} \cong (R/N) / (\mathfrak{A}/N)$.
