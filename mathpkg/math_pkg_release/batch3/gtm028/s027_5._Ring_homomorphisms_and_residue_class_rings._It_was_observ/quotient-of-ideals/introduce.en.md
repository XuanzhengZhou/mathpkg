The quotient of two ideals $\mathfrak{A} : \mathfrak{B}$ is the fourth fundamental operation on ideals in commutative algebra, alongside sum, product, and intersection. It consists of all ring elements that multiply $\mathfrak{B}$ into $\mathfrak{A}$. The ideal quotient is the algebraic analogue of the set-theoretic notion of a "residual" and plays an essential role in primary decomposition theory and the study of Dedekind domains.
