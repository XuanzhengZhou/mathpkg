Congruence modulo an ideal generalizes the familiar notion of congruence modulo an integer from elementary number theory. If $N$ is an ideal in a ring $R$, two elements $a, b \in R$ are congruent modulo $N$ when their difference lies in $N$. This equivalence relation is compatible with both addition and multiplication, making it the fundamental tool for constructing residue class rings.
