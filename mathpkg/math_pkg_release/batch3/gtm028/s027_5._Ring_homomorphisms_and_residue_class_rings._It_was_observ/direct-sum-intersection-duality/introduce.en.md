This theorem establishes a duality between direct sum decompositions and "direct intersection" representations. Given a direct sum decomposition $M = \bigoplus M_i$, the complementary sums $N_i = \bigoplus_{j \neq i} M_j$ satisfy intersection conditions that are formal duals of the direct sum conditions (interchanging sum and intersection, $M$ and $(0)$). This duality is particularly useful in ring theory where both formulations arise naturally.
