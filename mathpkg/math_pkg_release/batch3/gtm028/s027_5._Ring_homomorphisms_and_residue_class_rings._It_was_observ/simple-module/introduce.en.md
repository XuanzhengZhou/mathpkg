A simple module is the most basic non-trivial module — it has no proper non-zero submodules. Simple modules are the "atoms" of module theory: just as prime numbers are the building blocks of integers, simple modules are the building blocks (composition factors) that appear in composition series. Every simple module is cyclic (generated by any non-zero element), and in the case of vector spaces, the simple modules are precisely the one-dimensional vector spaces.
