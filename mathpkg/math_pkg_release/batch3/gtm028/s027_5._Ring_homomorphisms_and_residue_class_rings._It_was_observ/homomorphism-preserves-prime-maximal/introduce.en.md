Theorem 11 establishes that the properties of being prime or maximal are preserved under the ideal correspondence induced by a surjective ring homomorphism. An ideal $\mathfrak{A}$ containing the kernel is prime (resp. maximal) in $R$ exactly when its image $\mathfrak{A}T$ is prime (resp. maximal) in $R'$. Conversely, the preimage of a prime or maximal ideal under a surjective homomorphism retains that property. This is essential for understanding how these important classes of ideals behave under quotient constructions.
