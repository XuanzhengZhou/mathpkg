Hölder's theorem completes the Jordan-Hölder theorem by establishing that not only is the length of a composition series invariant, but the composition factors (simple quotients) themselves are uniquely determined up to isomorphism and multiplicity. Two composition series are equivalent if their successive quotients can be paired into isomorphic modules. This is the module-theoretic analogue of the uniqueness of prime factorization.
