Kummer's Theorem provides a practical method for determining how a prime ideal of an integrally closed domain decomposes in an integral extension, provided an integral basis of the form {1, y, ..., y^{n-1}} exists. The decomposition of the prime ideal is read directly from the factorization of the minimal polynomial of y modulo the prime ideal. This theorem is foundational in algebraic number theory, allowing the computation of ramification indices and residue field degrees for number fields and function fields.
