This corollary to Theorem 1 establishes the essential uniqueness of the irreducible polynomial vanishing at an algebraic element $x$. At most one such polynomial exists, up to multiplication by a nonzero constant in $k$. Normalizing to be monic selects a unique representative, which is precisely the minimal polynomial of $x$.
