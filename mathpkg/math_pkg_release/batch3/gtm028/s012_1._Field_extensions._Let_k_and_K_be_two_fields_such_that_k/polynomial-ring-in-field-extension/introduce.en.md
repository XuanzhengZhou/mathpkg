Given a field extension $K/k$ and elements $x_1, \ldots, x_n \in K$, the ring $k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ is the smallest subring of $K$ containing both the ground field $k$ and the elements $x_1, \ldots, x_n$. It consists of all polynomial expressions in $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ with coefficients in $k$. Since $K$ has no zero divisors, $k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ is an integral domain.
