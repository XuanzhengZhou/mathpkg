Given a field extension $K/k$ and elements $x_1, \ldots, x_n \in K$, the field $k(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ is obtained by adjoining these elements to $k$. It is defined as the set of all quotients $f(x_1,\ldots,x_n)/g(x_1,\ldots,x_n)$ where $f,g \in k[X_1,\ldots,X_n]$ and $g(x_1,\ldots,x_n) \neq 0$. Equivalently, it is the quotient field of the integral domain $k[x_1,\ldots,x_n]$ inside $K$, and it is the smallest subfield of $K$ containing both $k$ and the elements $x_1,\ldots,x_n$.
