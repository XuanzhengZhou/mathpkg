Theorem 2 describes the structure of a simple algebraic extension $k(x)$. For an algebraic element $x$, the field $k(x)$ equals the ring $k[x]$, meaning every rational expression in $x$ can be reduced to a polynomial. Furthermore, if the minimal polynomial has degree $n$, then $k(x)$ is an $n$-dimensional vector space over $k$ with basis $1, x, \ldots, x^{n-1}$. Every element has a unique representation as a polynomial in $x$ of degree less than $n$.
