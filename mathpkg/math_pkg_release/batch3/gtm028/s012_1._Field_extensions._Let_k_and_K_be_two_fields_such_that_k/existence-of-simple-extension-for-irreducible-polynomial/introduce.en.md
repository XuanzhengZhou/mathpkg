Theorem 3' complements Theorem 3 by showing existence: given any irreducible polynomial $f(X)$ over $k$, there exists an extension field of $k$ containing a root of $f$. The proof is constructive: it builds the extension as the quotient ring $k[X]/(f(X))$, made into a field by defining a new multiplication (the "circle product") on the set of polynomials of degree less than $n$. Together with Theorem 3, this shows that simple algebraic extensions are classified precisely by irreducible polynomials.
