Theorem 3 states that a simple algebraic extension $k(x)$ is determined up to $k$-isomorphism by the minimal polynomial of $x$. If two algebraic elements $x$ and $x'$ share the same minimal polynomial over $k$, then $k(x)$ and $k(x')$ are isomorphic via a map fixing $k$ pointwise and sending $x$ to $x'$. The converse also holds: any such $k$-isomorphism implies the minimal polynomials coincide. This is a foundational result for the construction of extension fields by adjoining roots of irreducible polynomials.
