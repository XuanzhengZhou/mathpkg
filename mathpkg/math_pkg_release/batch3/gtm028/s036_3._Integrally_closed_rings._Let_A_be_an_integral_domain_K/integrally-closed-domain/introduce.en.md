An integrally closed domain is an integral domain that contains every element of its quotient field which satisfies a monic polynomial equation over it. This property is fundamental in commutative algebra: it characterizes unique factorization domains among Noetherian domains and is a key axiom of Dedekind domains. Integral closure ensures that the domain is "normal," meaning the quotient field fractions integral over the domain already lie within it, which is essential for the study of algebraic integers in number theory.
