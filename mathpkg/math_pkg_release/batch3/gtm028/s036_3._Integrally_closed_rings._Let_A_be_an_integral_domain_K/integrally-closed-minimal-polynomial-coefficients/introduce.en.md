This result strengthens Theorem 4 when $A$ is integrally closed. Instead of merely concluding that the coefficients of the minimal polynomial are integral over $A$ (and hence lie in the integral closure of $A$), the hypothesis that $A$ is integrally closed forces those coefficients to lie in $A$ itself. The proof uses the Gauss-type lemma (Theorem 5) to factor an integral dependence polynomial through the minimal polynomial.
