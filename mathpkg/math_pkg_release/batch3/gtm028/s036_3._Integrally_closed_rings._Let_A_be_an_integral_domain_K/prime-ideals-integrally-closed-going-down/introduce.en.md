This is the "Going-Down" theorem for integral extensions of integrally closed domains. Given a chain of prime ideals $\mathfrak{p} \subset \mathfrak{q}$ in $A$ and a prime $\mathfrak{q}'$ in $A'$ lying over $\mathfrak{q}$, one can find a prime $\mathfrak{p}'$ in $A'$ lying over $\mathfrak{p}$ and contained in $\mathfrak{q}'$. The hypothesis that $A$ is integrally closed is essential; the theorem fails for arbitrary integral extensions. The proof uses the Gauss-type lemma (Theorem 5) to analyze the minimal polynomial of elements in the multiplicatively closed set $S = (A \setminus \mathfrak{q})(A' \setminus \mathfrak{q}')$ and shows that $\mathfrak{p}$ does not meet $S$ in the localization.
