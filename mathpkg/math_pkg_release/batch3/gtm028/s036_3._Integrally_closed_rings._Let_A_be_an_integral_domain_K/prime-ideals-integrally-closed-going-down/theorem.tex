Let $A$ be an integrally closed domain, and $A'$ an overring of $A$ integral over $A$ such that no non-zero element of $A$ is a zero-divisor in $A'$. If $\mathfrak{p}$ and $\mathfrak{q}$ are prime ideals in $A$ such that $\mathfrak{p} \subset \mathfrak{q}$, and if $\mathfrak{q}'$ is a prime ideal in $A'$ lying over $\mathfrak{q}$ (i.e., $\mathfrak{q}' \cap A = \mathfrak{q}$), then there exists a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}'$ in $A'$ lying over $\mathfrak{p}$ such that $\mathfrak{p}' \subset \mathfrak{q}'$.
