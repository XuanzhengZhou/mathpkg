The Going-Down Theorem is a fundamental result in commutative algebra concerning the behavior of prime ideal chains under integral extensions over integrally closed domains. While the Going-Up Theorem holds for arbitrary integral extensions, the going-down property requires the base ring to be integrally closed. This theorem is essential for dimension theory and the study of Cohen-Macaulay rings, ensuring that chains of prime ideals can be extended downward in integral extensions.
