A unique factorization domain (UFD) is always integrally closed. The proof uses the fact that in a UFD, any element has a unique prime factorization: if a fraction x/y in lowest terms satisfies a monic polynomial equation, the denominator y must divide 1 and therefore be a unit. This property makes UFDs and, by extension, polynomial rings over UFDs, fundamental examples of integrally closed domains.
