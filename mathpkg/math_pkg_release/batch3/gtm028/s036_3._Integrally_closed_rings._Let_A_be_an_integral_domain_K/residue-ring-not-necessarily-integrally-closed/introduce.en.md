Unlike localization, the property of being integrally closed is not preserved under passage to residue class rings. Every finite integral domain $k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ over a field $k$ is of the form $R/\mathfrak{p}$ where $R = k[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$ is a UFD (hence integrally closed). Yet such finite integral domains need not be integrally closed. The simplest counterexample for $n = 2$ is $\mathfrak{p} = (X_1^2 - X_2^3)$, giving $k[x_1, x_2]$ with $x_1^2 = x_2^3$, where $x_1/x_2$ is integral over the ring but does not belong to it.
