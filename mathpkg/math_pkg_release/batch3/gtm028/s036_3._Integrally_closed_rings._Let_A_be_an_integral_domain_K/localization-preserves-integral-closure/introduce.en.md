The property of being integrally closed is preserved under localization. Given an integrally closed domain R and a multiplicative set S, the quotient ring R_S remains integrally closed. The proof relies on the fact that any element of the quotient field integral over R_S can be scaled by an element of S to obtain an element integral over R, which then lies in R by integral closure.
