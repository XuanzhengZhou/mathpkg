A ring automorphism is an isomorphism from a ring onto itself. The collection of all automorphisms of a ring $R$ forms a group under composition, denoted $\operatorname{Aut}(R)$. Automorphisms capture the internal symmetries of the ring structure. The definition applies equally to groups: the automorphism group $\operatorname{Aut}(G)$ of a group $G$ is a fundamental invariant.
