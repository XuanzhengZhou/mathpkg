The identity mapping $I$ on a set $S$ maps each element to itself. It serves as the neutral element for composition of transformations: a transformation $T: S \to \bar{S}$ is a one-to-one mapping precisely when there exists an inverse transformation $T'$ such that $TT' = I$ and $T'T = \bar{I}$. This characterizes bijections in terms of the existence of a two-sided inverse.
