The identity mapping $I$ of a set $S$ is defined by $aI = a$ for all $a$ in $S$. If $S$ and $\bar{S}$ are two sets, $I$ and $\bar{I}$ their respective identity mappings, then a transformation $T$ of $S$ into $\bar{S}$ is a one to one mapping of $S$ if and only if there exists a transformation $T'$ of $\bar{S}$ into $S$ such that $TT' = I$ and $T'T = \bar{I}$.
