The kernel of a ring homomorphism $T: R \to \bar{R}$ is the set of elements of $R$ that map to the zero element of $\bar{R}$. The kernel is always a subring of $R$ and has the stronger property: if one factor of a product belongs to the kernel, the entire product belongs to the kernel. This property is fundamental to the concept of an ideal.
