Theorem 3 catalogs the fundamental properties of ring homomorphisms: the zero maps to zero, negatives map to negatives, the image is a subring, and the kernel is a subring. Most importantly, a ring homomorphism is an isomorphism precisely when its kernel is trivial. These properties are the ring-theoretic analogues of the corresponding group-theoretic facts and are used constantly in commutative algebra.
