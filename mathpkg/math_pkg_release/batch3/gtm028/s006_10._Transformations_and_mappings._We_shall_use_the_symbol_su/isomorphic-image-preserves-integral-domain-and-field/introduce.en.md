This corollary confirms that the algebraic properties of being an integral domain or a field are isomorphism-invariant: if $R$ is an integral domain (resp. field) and $R \cong \bar{R}$, then $\bar{R}$ is also an integral domain (resp. field). This follows because all algebraic properties expressible in terms of $+$ and $\cdot$ are preserved by isomorphisms.
