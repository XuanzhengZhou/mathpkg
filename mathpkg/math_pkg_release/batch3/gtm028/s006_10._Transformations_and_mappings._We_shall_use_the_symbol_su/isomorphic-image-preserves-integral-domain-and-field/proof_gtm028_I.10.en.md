**Proof.** From a formal algebraic standpoint, isomorphic rings are not essentially distinct rings, because it is clear that an isomorphic mapping of a ring $R$ preserves the algebraic properties of $R$ (that is, those properties of $R$ which can be formally expressed in terms of the ring operations $+$ and $\cdot$). Thus, for instance, an isomorphic image of an integral domain or of a field is again respectively an integral domain or a field.

On the other hand, a homomorphism which is not an isomorphism may affect some algebraic properties of a ring. For instance, a homomorphic image of an integral domain need not be an integral domain, and a ring which is not an integral domain may have an integral domain as a homomorphic image.
