Given two groups $G, \bar{G}$, we say that $\bar{G}$ is a homomorphic or isomorphic image (or map) of $G$ according as there exists a homomorphism or an isomorphism of $G$ onto $\bar{G}$. If $T$ is an isomorphism of $G$ onto $\bar{G}$, then $T^{-1}$ is an isomorphism of $\bar{G}$ onto $G$. Hence if $\bar{G}$ is an isomorphic image of $G$, then also $G$ is an isomorphic image of $\bar{G}$. We say then that $G$ and $\bar{G}$ are isomorphic groups.
