A group $\bar{G}$ is a homomorphic image of $G$ if there exists a surjective homomorphism $G \to \bar{G}$, and an isomorphic image if there exists a surjective isomorphism $G \to \bar{G}$. The isomorphic image relation is symmetric: if $\bar{G}$ is an isomorphic image of $G$, then $G$ is also an isomorphic image of $\bar{G}$ via the inverse map. Groups related by isomorphism are called isomorphic and are algebraically identical for all group-theoretic purposes.
