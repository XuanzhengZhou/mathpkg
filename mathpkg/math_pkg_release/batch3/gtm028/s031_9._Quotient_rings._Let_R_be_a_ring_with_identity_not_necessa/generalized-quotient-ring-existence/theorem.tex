Let $R$ be a ring and let $M$ be a multiplicative system in $R$. There exists a ring $R_M$ and a homomorphism $h: R \to R_M$ such that:

\begin{enumerate}
\item The kernel $\mathfrak{n}$ of $h$ is the set of all elements $x \in R$ for which there exists $m \in M$ such that $xm = 0$.
\item The elements of $h(M)$ are units in $R_M$.
\item Every element of $R_M$ may be written as a quotient $h(x)/h(m)$ with $x \in R$, $m \in M$.
\end{enumerate}
