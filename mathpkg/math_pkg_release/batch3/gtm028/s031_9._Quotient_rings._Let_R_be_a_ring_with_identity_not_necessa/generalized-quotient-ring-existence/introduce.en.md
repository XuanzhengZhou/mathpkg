This theorem extends the construction of quotient rings (localizations) to the case where the multiplicative system $M$ may contain zero divisors. The key idea is to first factor out the ideal $\mathfrak{n}$ of elements annihilated by some member of $M$, reducing to the regular case on the quotient $R/\mathfrak{n}$. The theorem establishes the existence of a ring $R_M$ and a canonical homomorphism $h$ satisfying the universal properties expected of a localization.
