Let $R$ be a ring, $M$ a multiplicative system in $R$, and $f: R \to S$ a ring homomorphism such that $f(m)$ is a unit in $S$ for every $m \in M$. If $x \in R$ and there exists $m \in M$ such that $mx = 0$, then $f(x) = 0$. Consequently, the kernel of $f$ contains the set
$$
\mathfrak{n} = \{ x \in R \mid \exists m \in M \text{ such that } xm = 0 \}.
$$
