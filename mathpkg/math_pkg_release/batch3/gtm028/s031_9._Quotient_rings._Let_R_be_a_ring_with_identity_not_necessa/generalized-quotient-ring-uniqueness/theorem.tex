Let $R$ be a ring, $M$ a multiplicative system in $R$, and let $h: R \to R_M$ be a homomorphism satisfying conditions (1), (2), and (3) of the existence theorem (in particular, $h = \varphi \circ \psi$ as constructed above). If $S$ is any ring and $f: R \to S$ is a homomorphism such that:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $\ker f = \{ x \in R \mid \exists m \in M,\; xm = 0 \}$,
\item the elements of $f(M)$ are units in $S$,
\item every element of $S$ is of the form $f(x)/f(m)$ with $x \in R$, $m \in M$,
\end{enumerate}

then there exists an isomorphism $f': R_M \to S$ such that $f = h \circ f'$.
