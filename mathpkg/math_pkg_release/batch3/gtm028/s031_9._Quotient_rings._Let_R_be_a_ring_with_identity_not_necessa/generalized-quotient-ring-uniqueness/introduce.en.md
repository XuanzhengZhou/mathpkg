This theorem establishes that the generalized quotient ring $(R_M, h)$ is unique up to unique isomorphism. Any pair $(S, f)$ satisfying the same three universal properties as $(R_M, h)$ must be isomorphic to it via a map $f'$ that makes the triangle commute ($f = h \circ f'$). This is the universal property of the generalized quotient ring construction.
