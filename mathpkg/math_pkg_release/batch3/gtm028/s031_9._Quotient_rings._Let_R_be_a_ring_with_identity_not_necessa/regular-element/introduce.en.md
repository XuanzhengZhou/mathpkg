A regular element in a ring $R$ is an element that is not a zero divisor. In other words, $b \in R$ is regular if $bx = 0$ implies $x = 0$ and $xb = 0$ implies $x = 0$ for all $x \in R$. Regular elements are precisely the elements that can become denominators when forming quotients in the total quotient ring.
