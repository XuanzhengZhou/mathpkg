A binary operation is called commutative if $ab = ba$ for all elements $a, b$ in the set. This is the defining property of abelian groups and commutative rings — the entire subject of commutative algebra rests on this concept. When commutativity holds, the binomial theorem $(ab)^n = a^n b^n$ becomes valid, and many algebraic manipulations simplify dramatically. The distinction between left and right multiplication disappears, making the algebraic theory much more symmetric and tractable.
