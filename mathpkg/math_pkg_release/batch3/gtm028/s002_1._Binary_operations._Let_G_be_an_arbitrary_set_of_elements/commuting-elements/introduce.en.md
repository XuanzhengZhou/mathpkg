Two elements $a$ and $b$ of a set $G$ equipped with a binary operation are said to commute if $ab = ba$, i.e., their product is independent of the order in which they are multiplied. The commuting relation is symmetric but not necessarily transitive. The concept is central to commutative algebra: when all pairs of elements commute, the operation itself is called commutative, and the resulting algebraic structure has much stronger properties, such as the binomial theorem for powers of products.
