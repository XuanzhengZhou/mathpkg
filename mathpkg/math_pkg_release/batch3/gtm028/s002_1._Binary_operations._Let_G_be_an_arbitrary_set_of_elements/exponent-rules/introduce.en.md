The rules of exponents are the fundamental algebraic identities governing powers of elements under an associative binary operation. Rule (1) $a^m a^n = a^{m+n}$ expresses that multiplication of powers corresponds to addition of exponents, and rule (2) $(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$ expresses that iterated exponentiation corresponds to multiplication of exponents. Both are proved by induction using associativity. Rule (3) $(ab)^n = a^n b^n$ holds only when $a$ and $b$ commute, which is why commutativity is such a powerful property in algebra. Once an identity and inverses are introduced, the rules extend to all integer exponents via the definition $a^0 = e$ and $a^{-n} = (a')^n$.
