This theorem characterizes $p$-bases in terms of the existence of "dual" derivations. Given a $p$-basis $S$, for each $x \in S$ there is a derivation $D_x$ that acts as a coordinate functional: $D_x(x) = 1$ and $D_x(y) = 0$ for $y \neq x$. Conversely, the existence of such derivations guarantees $p$-independence. In the finitely generated case, these derivations form a basis of the derivation space $\mathcal{D}_{F/F^p}$, establishing that $\dim_F \mathcal{D}_{F/F^p} = |S|$, the $p$-degree of $F$.
