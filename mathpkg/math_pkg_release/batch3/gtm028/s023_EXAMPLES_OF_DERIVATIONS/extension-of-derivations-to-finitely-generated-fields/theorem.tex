Let $K$ be a field, $F = K(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ a finitely generated extension, and $D$ a derivation of $K$ with values in a field $L$ containing $F$. Let $D_i = \partial/\partial X_i$ denote the formal partial derivatives on $K[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$, and for $f \in K[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$ let $f^D$ be the polynomial obtained by applying $D$ coefficientwise.

For any derivation $D'$ of $F$ extending $D$, we have for every $g \in K[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$:
$$D'(g(x_1, \ldots, x_n)) = g^D(x_1, \ldots, x_n) + \sum_{i=1}^n D'(x_i) (D_i g)(x_1, \ldots, x_n). \tag{7}$$

Hence a necessary condition for the existence of $D'$ with prescribed values $D'(x_i) = u_i$ is
$$f_j^D(x_1, \ldots, x_n) + \sum_{i=1}^n u_i (D_i f_j)(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = 0 \quad \text{for every } j, \tag{5}$$
where $\{f_j\}$ is any set of generators of the ideal of algebraic relations satisfied by $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ over $K$. If this condition is satisfied, then $D'$ is uniquely determined. Conversely, if $\{f_j\}$ is a basis of the ideal of algebraic relations, then condition (5) is also sufficient for the existence of $D'$.
