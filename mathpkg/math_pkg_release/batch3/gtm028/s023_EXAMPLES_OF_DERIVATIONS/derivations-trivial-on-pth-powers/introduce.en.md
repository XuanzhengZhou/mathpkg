The formula $D(x^p) = p x^{p-1} D(x)$ shows that in characteristic $p$, the factor $p$ vanishes, so $D(x^p) = 0$ for every derivation $D$. Consequently, every derivation is trivial on the subfield $L^p$ of $p$-th powers. This has profound consequences: perfect fields (where $L^p = L$) admit no non-trivial derivations, and the subfield $L^p$ is precisely the intersection of the kernels of all derivations of $L$.
