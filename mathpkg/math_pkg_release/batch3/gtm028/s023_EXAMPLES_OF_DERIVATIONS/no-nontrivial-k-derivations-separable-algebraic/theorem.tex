Let $K(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ be a separable algebraic extension of $K$. Then there exist no non-trivial $K$-derivations of $K(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$. Equivalently, $\mathcal{D}_{K(x_1, \ldots, x_n)/K} = 0$.

More generally, if $K(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ is finitely generated and separable algebraic over $K$, then the system of homogeneous equations
$$\sum_{i=1}^n u_i (D_i f)(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = 0$$
obtained by letting $f$ vary over all polynomials vanishing at $(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ has only the trivial solution $u_1 = \cdots = u_n = 0$.
