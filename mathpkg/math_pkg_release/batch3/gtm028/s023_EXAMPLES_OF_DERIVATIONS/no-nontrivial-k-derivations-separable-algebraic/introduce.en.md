This corollary establishes that finitely generated separable algebraic extensions are "rigid" with respect to derivations: the only $K$-derivation is the zero derivation. The proof uses Theorem 39 to show that the system of linear equations governing the values $D(x_i)$ has vanishing determinant (by separability, the Jacobian criterion ensures the partial derivatives span a system with non-zero Jacobian determinant), forcing all $D(x_i) = 0$.
