Let $K$ be a field of characteristic $p \neq 0$ and let $F = K(x)$ ($F \neq K$) be a simple purely inseparable extension of $K$. Let $e$ be the smallest integer $e \geq 1$ such that $y = x^{p^e} \in K$. Then a derivation $D$ of $K$ has an extension $D'$ to $F$ if and only if $D(y) = 0$. If $D$ satisfies this condition (with values in a field $L$ containing $F$), then the value of $D'(x)$ can be assigned arbitrarily in $L$.
