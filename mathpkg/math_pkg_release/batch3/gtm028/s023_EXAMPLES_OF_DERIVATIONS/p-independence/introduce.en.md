$p$-independence is the characteristic $p$ analogue of algebraic independence, but relative to the subfield $F^p$ of $p$-th powers. A set of elements is $p$-independent when all monomials with exponents in $\{0, 1, \ldots, p-1\}$ are linearly independent over $F^p$. This notion is fundamental for understanding the structure of purely inseparable extensions and for describing bases of the derivation space $\mathcal{D}_{F/F^p}$.
