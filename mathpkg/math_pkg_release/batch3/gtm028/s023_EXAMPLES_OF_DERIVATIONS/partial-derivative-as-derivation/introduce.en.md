The familiar partial derivative operator from calculus has a purely algebraic analogue on a polynomial ring $R = R'[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$. By defining the formal partial derivative $\partial/\partial X_1$ via the power rule on monomials and extending $R'$-linearly, one obtains an $R'$-derivation of $R$. This derivation vanishes on polynomials not involving $X_1$, making it a fundamental example of a derivation that is not the identity on the full ring.
