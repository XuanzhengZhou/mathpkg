Let $R'$ be a ring and $R = R'[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$ the polynomial ring. Define the partial derivative with respect to $X_1$ by
$$f'_{X_1}(X_1, \ldots, X_n) = \sum a_{q_1, \ldots, q_n} \, q_1 X_1^{q_1-1} X_2^{q_2} \cdots X_n^{q_n}$$
for $f(X_1, \ldots, X_n) = \sum a_{q_1, \ldots, q_n} X_1^{q_1} \cdots X_n^{q_n}$. Then the mapping $D_1 = \partial/\partial X_1 \colon f \mapsto f'_{X_1}$ is an $R'$-derivation of $R$, trivial on $R'[X_2, \ldots, X_n]$.
