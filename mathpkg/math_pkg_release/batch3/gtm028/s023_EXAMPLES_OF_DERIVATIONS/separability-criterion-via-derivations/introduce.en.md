Theorem 42 provides a beautiful cohomological characterization of separable extensions in characteristic $p$: $F/K$ is separable precisely when every derivation of $K$ extends to $F$. The forward direction constructs the extension explicitly using a $K^p$-basis of $F^p$, while the converse shows that if some derivation fails to extend, the fields $F$ and $K^{1/p}$ cannot be linearly disjoint, producing a $p$-dependence relation.
