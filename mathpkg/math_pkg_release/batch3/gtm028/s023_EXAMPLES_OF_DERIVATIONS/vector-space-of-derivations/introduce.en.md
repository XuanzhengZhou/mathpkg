The set of derivations from a field $K$ into an extension field $L$ carries a natural vector space structure over $L$, with pointwise addition and scalar multiplication. This makes it possible to study derivations using the tools of linear algebra. Moreover, fixing a subfield $K'$ of $K$ and restricting to derivations trivial on $K'$ yields a subspace $\mathcal{D}_{K/K'}$, which plays a central role in the Galois-type theory of derivations.
