The finite basis condition is the third of three equivalent finiteness conditions that characterize noetherian rings. It requires that every ideal of the ring can be generated by a finite set of elements, called a basis. In modern terminology for rings with identity, this is precisely the condition that every ideal is finitely generated. The finite basis condition is equivalent to the ascending chain condition and the maximum condition, and it is often the most practical characterization to verify or apply in algebraic constructions.
