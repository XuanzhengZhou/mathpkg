This proposition states that the noetherian property is preserved under taking homomorphic images. The proof follows immediately from the finite basis condition: every ideal of the homomorphic image $R'$ is the image of an ideal of $R$, and the image of a finite set of generators of that ideal provides a finite set of generators for the corresponding ideal in $R'$. This property is one of the three elementary consequences of the definition of a noetherian ring presented at the beginning of the chapter.
