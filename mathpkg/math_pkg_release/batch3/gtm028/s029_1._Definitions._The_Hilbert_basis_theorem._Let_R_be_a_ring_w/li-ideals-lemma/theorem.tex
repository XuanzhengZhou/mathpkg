Let $R$ be a commutative ring and let $S = R[x]$ be the polynomial ring in one indeterminate over $R$. For an ideal $\mathfrak{A}$ of $S$ and an integer $i \geq 0$, let $L_i(\mathfrak{A})$ denote the set of elements of $R$ consisting of $0$ and of the coefficients of $x^i$ of all elements of $\mathfrak{A}$ which are of degree $i$.

Then $\{L_i(\mathfrak{A})\}_{i \geq 0}$ is an increasing sequence of ideals in $R$. Moreover, if $\mathfrak{B}$ is any other ideal in $S$ such that $\mathfrak{A} \subseteq \mathfrak{B}$ and $L_i(\mathfrak{A}) = L_i(\mathfrak{B})$ for all $i = 0, 1, 2, \dots$, then $\mathfrak{A} = \mathfrak{B}$.
