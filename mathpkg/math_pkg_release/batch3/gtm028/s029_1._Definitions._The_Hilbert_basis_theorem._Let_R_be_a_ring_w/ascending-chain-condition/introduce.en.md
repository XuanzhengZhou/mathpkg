The ascending chain condition (a.c.c.) for ideals is one of three equivalent finiteness conditions on a commutative ring that characterize noetherian rings. It requires that every strictly ascending chain of ideals must terminate after finitely many steps, or equivalently, that any infinite ascending chain stabilizes. This condition is fundamental in commutative algebra as it ensures that certain inductive constructions on ideals cannot continue indefinitely, providing a powerful tool for proving the existence of maximal elements and for establishing properties of noetherian rings.
