This proposition states that in any noetherian ring, an ideal always contains some power of its own radical. The proof uses the finite basis condition: take a finite set of generators of the radical, each generator becomes nilpotent modulo the original ideal after a suitable exponent, and by the pigeonhole principle a sufficiently high power of the radical falls into the ideal. This is one of the three elementary consequences of the noetherian property presented at the beginning of Chapter IV of Zariski-Samuel.
