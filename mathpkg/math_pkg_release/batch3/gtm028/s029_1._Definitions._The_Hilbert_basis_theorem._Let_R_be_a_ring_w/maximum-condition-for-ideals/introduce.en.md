The maximum condition for ideals is the second of three equivalent finiteness conditions that characterize noetherian rings. It states that any non-empty collection of ideals of the ring possesses a maximal element with respect to inclusion. This condition guarantees in particular that every proper ideal is contained in some maximal ideal, a fact of fundamental importance throughout commutative algebra. Together with the ascending chain condition and the finite basis condition, the maximum condition provides an equivalent characterization of noetherianity.
