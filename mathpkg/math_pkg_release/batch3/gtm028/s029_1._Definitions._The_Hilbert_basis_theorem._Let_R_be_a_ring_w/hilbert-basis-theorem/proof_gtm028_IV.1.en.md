Two proofs are given. We present both.

---

**First proof (using the maximum condition).**

By induction, it suffices to prove that $S = R[x]$ is noetherian when $R$ is noetherian. Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an ideal of $S$ and define the ideals $L_i(\mathfrak{A})$ of $R$ as in the Lemma (coefficients of $x^i$ in elements of $\mathfrak{A}$ of degree $i$). The ideals $L_i(\mathfrak{A})$ form an increasing sequence. Let $\mathfrak{L} = \bigcup_{i \geq 0} L_i(\mathfrak{A})$; then $\mathfrak{L}$ is an ideal of $R$. (Proof: if $a,b \in \mathfrak{L}$, there exist $f(x), g(x) \in \mathfrak{A}$ with leading terms $a x^r$, $b x^s$ respectively. Assuming $r \geq s$, the polynomial $f(x) - x^{r-s} g(x) \in \mathfrak{A}$ has leading term $(a-b)x^r$, so $a-b \in \mathfrak{L}$; for $c \in R$, $c f(x) \in \mathfrak{A}$ gives $ca \in \mathfrak{L}$.)

Since $R$ is noetherian, $\mathfrak{L}$ has a finite basis $\{a_1, \dots, a_n\}$. Let $f_1(x), \dots, f_n(x) \in \mathfrak{A}$ be elements having respectively $a_1, \dots, a_n$ as their leading coefficients. Let $q$ be the maximum of the degrees of the $f_i(x)$, and let $\mathfrak{A}'$ be the ideal generated by $f_1, \dots, f_n$; clearly $\mathfrak{A}' \subseteq \mathfrak{A}$.

Now let $g(x) \in \mathfrak{A}$ have degree $s \geq q$ with leading term $a x^s$. Since $a \in \mathfrak{L}$, write $a = \sum_i c_i a_i$ with $c_i \in R$. Form
$$
g_1(x) = \sum_{i=1}^n c_i f_i(x) x^{s - d_i} \in \mathfrak{A}',
$$
where $d_i = \deg f_i(x)$. Then $g(x) - g_1(x) \in \mathfrak{A}$ has degree at most $s-1$. By descending induction on degree, we can subtract elements of $\mathfrak{A}'$ to reduce the degree of $g(x)$ below $q$. Thus every element of $\mathfrak{A}$ differs from an element of $\mathfrak{A}'$ by a polynomial of degree less than $q$.

For each $j = 0, 1, \dots, q-1$, the leading-coefficient ideal $L_j(\mathfrak{A})$ is finitely generated as an ideal of the noetherian ring $R$. For each such $j$, pick finitely many polynomials in $\mathfrak{A}$ of degree $j$ whose leading coefficients generate $L_j(\mathfrak{A})$. Adding these finitely many polynomials to the generators of $\mathfrak{A}'$, we obtain a finite set that generates all of $\mathfrak{A}$. Hence $\mathfrak{A}$ is finitely generated, and $S$ is noetherian.

---

**Second proof (using the finite basis condition — Hilbert's original approach).**

Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an ideal of $S = R[x]$. For each integer $k \geq 0$, let $\mathfrak{a}_k$ be the set of leading coefficients of elements of $\mathfrak{A}$ of degree exactly $k$, together with $0$. Then $\mathfrak{a}_k$ is an ideal of $R$, and $\mathfrak{a}_0 \subseteq \mathfrak{a}_1 \subseteq \mathfrak{a}_2 \subseteq \cdots$ (since multiplying by $x$ raises degree without changing the leading coefficient).

Since $R$ is noetherian, the ascending chain $\mathfrak{a}_k$ stabilizes: there exists $N$ such that $\mathfrak{a}_k = \mathfrak{a}_N$ for all $k \geq N$.

For each $k = 0, 1, \dots, N$, the ideal $\mathfrak{a}_k$ is finitely generated; choose generators $a_{k,1}, \dots, a_{k, r_k} \in R$ and corresponding polynomials $f_{k,j}(x) \in \mathfrak{A}$ of degree $k$ with leading coefficient $a_{k,j}$. Let $\mathfrak{A}_0$ be the ideal generated by all these finitely many $f_{k,j}$; then $\mathfrak{A}_0 \subseteq \mathfrak{A}$.

We claim $\mathfrak{A}_0 = \mathfrak{A}$. Let $g(x) \in \mathfrak{A}$ have degree $d$. If $d \leq N$, then the leading coefficient of $g$ is a linear combination of the $a_{d,j}$, and subtracting the corresponding combination of $f_{d,j}$ reduces the degree. If $d > N$, then the leading coefficient of $g$ lies in $\mathfrak{a}_d = \mathfrak{a}_N$, so it is a linear combination of $a_{N,j}$. Subtracting the combination of $x^{d-N} f_{N,j}(x)$ reduces the degree. By induction on the degree, $g(x) \in \mathfrak{A}_0$. Thus $\mathfrak{A} = \mathfrak{A}_0$ is finitely generated.

Therefore $R[x]$ is noetherian, and by induction $R[x_1, \dots, x_n]$ is noetherian for any finite number of indeterminates.
