An element $x$ in a ring $\Delta$ that is unitary over a base ring $R$ is called algebraic over $R$ if the evaluation homomorphism $f \mapsto f(x)$ from the polynomial ring $R[X]$ to $\Delta$ is a proper homomorphism (i.e., not an isomorphism). Equivalently, $x$ satisfies some non-zero polynomial equation with coefficients in $R$. This is the fundamental notion that distinguishes elements that satisfy polynomial relations from those that are free of such relations.
