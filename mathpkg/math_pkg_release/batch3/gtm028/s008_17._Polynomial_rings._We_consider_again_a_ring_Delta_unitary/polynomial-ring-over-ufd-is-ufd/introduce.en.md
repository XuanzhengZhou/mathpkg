Theorem 10 is one of the most important results in commutative algebra: it states that if the coefficient ring $R$ is a UFD, then the polynomial ring $R[X]$ is also a UFD. This generalizes the corollary that $F[X]$ is a UFD when $F$ is a field. The proof relies on Gauss's Lemma and the lemmas about primitive polynomials to establish unique factorization in $R[X]$ using the UF conditions.
