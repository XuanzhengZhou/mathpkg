A polynomial ring $R[x]$ over $R$ is a ring containing $R$ together with a distinguished transcendental element $x$ such that every element can be uniquely written as a polynomial $\sum a_i x^i$ with coefficients $a_i \in R$. All rings $R[x]$ with $x$ transcendental over $R$ are $R$-isomorphic to the formal polynomial ring $R[X]$, making this the canonical model of a simple transcendental extension.
