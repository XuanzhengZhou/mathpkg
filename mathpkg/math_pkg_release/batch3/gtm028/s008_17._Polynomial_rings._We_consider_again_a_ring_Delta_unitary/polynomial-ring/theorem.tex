Let $R$ be a ring, and let $S$ be a ring containing $R$. An element $x \in S$ is a \text{transcendental} (or \text{indeterminate}) over $R$, and $S$ is called a \text{polynomial ring} in $x$ over $R$, if the evaluation homomorphism $f(X) \mapsto f(x)$ from $R[X]$ to $S$ is an $R$-isomorphism of $R[X]$ onto $S$. The ring $S$ is then denoted by $R[x]$ and its elements are uniquely expressible in the form $\sum_{i=0}^n a_i x^i$ with $a_i \in R$.
