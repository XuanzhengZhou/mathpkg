**Proof.** Since $x - a$ is of degree $1$, by Theorem 9 there exist $q(x) \in R[x]$ and $b \in R$ such that $f(x) = q(x)(x - a) + b$. Then $f(a) = q(a)(a - a) + b = b$. Hence $f(a) = 0$ if and only if $b = 0$, which means $x - a$ divides $f(x)$.
