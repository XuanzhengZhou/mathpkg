Let $R$ be a ring with identity and let $g(x) = b_0 + b_1 x + \cdots + b_n x^n$ be a polynomial of $R[x]$ whose leading coefficient $b_n$ is a regular element of $R$. Then, given any $f(x) \in R[x]$, there exist an integer $k \geq 0$ and polynomials $q(x), r(x) \in R[x]$ such that $\partial r < n$ and $b_n^k f(x) = q(x) g(x) + r(x)$. Moreover, if $b_n$ is regular and we also have $b_n^{k'} f = q' g + r'$ with $\partial r' < n$, then $q = q'$ and $r = r'$.
