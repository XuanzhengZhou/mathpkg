A primitive polynomial over a UFD $R$ is one whose coefficients are coprime — no non-unit of $R$ divides all coefficients simultaneously. This notion is central to Gauss's Lemma, which states that the product of primitive polynomials remains primitive. Using the content function, a polynomial $f$ is primitive precisely when $c(f) \in R^\times$.
