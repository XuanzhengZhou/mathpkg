Let $R$ and $\bar{R}$ be rings, let $T_0$ be a homomorphism of $R$ onto $\bar{R}$, and let $S' = R[x]$ be a polynomial ring in $x$ over $R$. Let $\bar{R}[y]$ be a polynomial ring in $y$ over $\bar{R}$. Then there exists a unique homomorphism $T$ of $S'$ onto $\bar{R}[y]$ such that $aT = aT_0$ for all $a \in R$ and $xT = y$. Moreover, $T$ is an isomorphism if and only if $T_0$ is an isomorphism and $y$ is transcendental over $\bar{R}$.
