This theorem characterizes all possible generators of a polynomial ring $R[x]$ when $R$ is an integral domain. The only elements $x'$ that can serve as a new indeterminate — i.e., such that $R[x] = R[x']$ with $x'$ still transcendental — are the affine linear expressions $x' = a_0 + a_1 x$ where $a_1$ is a unit. When $R$ has zero divisors, more exotic generators (like $x + a x^2$ with $a^2 = 0$) become possible.
