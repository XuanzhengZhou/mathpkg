An element $x$ of a ring $\Delta$ unitary over $R$ is said to be \text{transcendental over $R$} if it is not algebraic over $R$ — that is, if the evaluation homomorphism $f \mapsto f(x)$ from $R[X]$ to $\Delta$ is an isomorphism. Equivalently, no non-zero polynomial $g(X) \in R[X]$ satisfies $g(x) = 0$.
