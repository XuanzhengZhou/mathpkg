Gauss's Lemma is the cornerstone of the proof that $R$ UFD implies $R[x]$ UFD. It consists of two equivalent statements: the content is multiplicative ($c(fg) = c(f)c(g)$), and the product of primitive polynomials is primitive. The proof uses the irreducibility (primeness) in a UFD: if an irreducible element $p$ divided all coefficients of the product, one identifies the first coefficients of each factor not divisible by $p$, leading to a contradiction.
