Let $f(X) \in R[X]$ be a polynomial in one indeterminate over an integral domain $R$. If $a_1, \dots, a_m$ are distinct roots of $f(X)$ in $R$, then the product $(X - a_1) \cdots (X - a_m)$ divides $f(X)$ in $R[X]$. Consequently, if $f(X) \neq 0$, the number of roots of $f(X)$ in $R$ is at most equal to the degree of $f(X)$.
