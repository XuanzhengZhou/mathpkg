When a field $k$ has positive characteristic $p \neq 0$, the subring $E$ of integral multiples of the identity turns out to be a field itself. It consists of exactly the $p$ distinct elements $0, e, 2e, \dots, (p-1)e$, and is isomorphic to the finite prime field $\mathbb{F}_p$. The crucial observation in the proof is that for any $ne \neq 0$ in $E$, the integer $n$ is not divisible by $p$, so there exist integers $m, q$ with $mn - qp = 1$, giving $(me)(ne) = e$ as the inverse.
