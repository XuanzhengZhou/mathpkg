Let $k$ be a field with identity $e$, and consider the homomorphism
$$
\varphi: \mathbb{Z} \to k, \quad n \mapsto ne
$$
from the ring of integers onto the subring $E$ of integral multiples of $e$ in $k$. Two cases are possible:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\varphi$ is an isomorphism. Then $ne \neq 0$ whenever $n \neq 0$, and we say that $k$ has \textbf{characteristic zero}.
\item[(b)] $\varphi$ is a proper homomorphism. Then its kernel is a nonzero ideal of $\mathbb{Z}$, hence of the form $p\mathbb{Z}$ for some integer $p > 1$, and $p$ is necessarily a prime number. The prime number $p$ is called the \textbf{characteristic} of $k$.
\end{enumerate}
Every field $k$ has a well-defined characteristic $p$ which is either $0$ or a prime number ($p > 1$).
