This is an immediate reformulation of Theorem 3. By Theorem 3, for every prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ of $A$, there exists a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}'$ of $A'$ such that $\mathfrak{p}' \cap A = \mathfrak{p}$. By definition, this means $\mathfrak{p}'$ lies over $\mathfrak{p}$. Hence the contraction map $\mathfrak{p}' \mapsto \mathfrak{p}' \cap A$ from the set of prime ideals of $A'$ to the set of prime ideals of $A$ is surjective.
