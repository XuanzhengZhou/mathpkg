This corollary to the Lying Over theorem restates the result in the language of the prime spectrum. If $A'$ is an overring integrally dependent on $A$, then every prime ideal of $A$ is the contraction of some prime ideal of $A'$. In other words, the natural contraction map $\operatorname{Spec}(A') \to \operatorname{Spec}(A)$ is surjective. This is an immediate consequence of Theorem 3 together with the definition of "lying over," and it provides the geometric interpretation that the associated map of affine schemes is surjective on the underlying topological spaces.
