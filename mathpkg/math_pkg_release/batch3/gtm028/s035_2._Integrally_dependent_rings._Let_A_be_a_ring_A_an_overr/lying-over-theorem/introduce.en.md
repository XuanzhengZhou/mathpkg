The Lying Over theorem is one of the foundational results in the theory of integral extensions of rings. It asserts that if $A'$ is an overring integrally dependent on $A$, then for every prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ of $A$, the extended ideal $A'\mathfrak{p}$ is a proper ideal of $A'$. Consequently, there exists a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}'$ of $A'$ that "lies over" $\mathfrak{p}$, meaning $\mathfrak{p}' \cap A = \mathfrak{p}$. Moreover, if $\mathfrak{p}$ is maximal, then $\mathfrak{p}'$ is also maximal. This theorem — together with the Going Up and Going Down theorems — forms the Cohen-Seidenberg theory describing the relationship between prime ideal spectra under integral extensions.
