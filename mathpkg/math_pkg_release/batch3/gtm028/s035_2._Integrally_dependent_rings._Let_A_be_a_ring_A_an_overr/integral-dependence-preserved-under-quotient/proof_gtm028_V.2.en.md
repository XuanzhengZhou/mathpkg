We first recall that $A/(\mathfrak{J}' \cap A)$ can be canonically identified with a subring of $A'/\mathfrak{J}'$ (III, §5, Theorem 8). Now, for any $x$ in $A'$, it suffices to reduce modulo $\mathfrak{J}'$ an equation of integral dependence for $x$ over $A$. More precisely, if

$$x^n + a_{n-1}x^{n-1} + \cdots + a_0 = 0 \quad (a_i \in A)$$

is an equation of integral dependence for $x$ over $A$, then reducing modulo $\mathfrak{J}'$ yields

$$\bar{x}^n + \bar{a}_{n-1}\bar{x}^{n-1} + \cdots + \bar{a}_0 = 0,$$

where $\bar{x}$ denotes the image of $x$ in $A'/\mathfrak{J}'$ and $\bar{a}_i$ denotes the image of $a_i$ in $A/(\mathfrak{J}' \cap A)$ under the canonical identification. This is an equation of integral dependence for $\bar{x}$ over $A/(\mathfrak{J}' \cap A)$. Since every element of $A'/\mathfrak{J}'$ arises as $\bar{x}$ for some $x \in A'$, it follows that $A'/\mathfrak{J}'$ is integrally dependent on $A/(\mathfrak{J}' \cap A)$.
