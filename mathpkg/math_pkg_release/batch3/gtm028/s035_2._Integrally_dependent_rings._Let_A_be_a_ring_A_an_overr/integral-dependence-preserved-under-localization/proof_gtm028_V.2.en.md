Let $\mathfrak{n}'$ be the ideal formed by the elements of $A'$ which are annihilated by at least one element of $S$. Since $A'_S$ and $A_S$ are ordinary quotient rings of $A'/\mathfrak{n}'$ and of $A/(\mathfrak{n}' \cap A)$ (IV, §9), we are reduced, by Lemma 1, to the case in which $A$ and $A'$ are subrings of $A_S$ and $A'_S$ respectively.

Let then $x/s$ ($x \in A'$, $s \in S$) be an arbitrary element of $A'_S$. From an integral dependence equation

$$x^n + a_{n-1}x^{n-1} + \cdots + a_0 = 0 \quad (a_i \in A)$$

of $x$ over $A$, we deduce, upon division by $s^n$:

$$\left(\frac{x}{s}\right)^n + \frac{a_{n-1}}{s}\left(\frac{x}{s}\right)^{n-1} + \cdots + \frac{a_0}{s^n} = 0.$$

Since each coefficient $a_i/s^{n-i}$ belongs to $A_S$, this is an equation of integral dependence for $x/s$ over $A_S$. As every element of $A'_S$ is of the form $x/s$ with $x \in A'$ and $s \in S$, we conclude that $A'_S$ is integrally dependent on $A_S$.
