The set of all separable algebraic elements over $k$ within an extension $K$ is closed under field operations: sums, products, and inverses of separable algebraic elements remain separable algebraic. This subfield is called the maximal separable extension of $k$ in $K$ (or the separable algebraic closure of $k$ in $K$). This theorem justifies treating separability as a property that can be studied within a single field, and it is fundamental to the decomposition of any algebraic extension into a separable part followed by a purely inseparable part.
