For a finite algebraic extension $K/k$ in characteristic $p$, the total degree $[K:k]$ factors as $n_0 \cdot p^e$, where $n_0$ (the separable degree) is the degree of the maximal separable subextension, and $p^e$ (the inseparable degree) measures how far the extension is from being separable. The separable degree equals the number of distinct $k$-embeddings of $K$ into an algebraic closure, while the inseparable degree measures the multiplicity of each embedding. These invariants are multiplicative in towers and are fundamental numerical invariants in algebraic number theory and algebraic geometry.
