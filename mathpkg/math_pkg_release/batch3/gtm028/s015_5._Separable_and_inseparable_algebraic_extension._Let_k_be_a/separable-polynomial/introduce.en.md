The separability of a polynomial is defined via its formal derivative. An irreducible polynomial is separable exactly when its formal derivative does not vanish identically. Over fields of characteristic zero, every irreducible polynomial is separable. Over fields of characteristic $p > 0$, an irreducible polynomial can be inseparable; this happens precisely when the polynomial lies in $k[X^p]$. The concept generalizes to arbitrary polynomials by requiring each irreducible factor to be separable. This definition, due to Steinitz, is the foundation for distinguishing between "well-behaved" (separable) and "pathological" (inseparable) algebraic extensions.
