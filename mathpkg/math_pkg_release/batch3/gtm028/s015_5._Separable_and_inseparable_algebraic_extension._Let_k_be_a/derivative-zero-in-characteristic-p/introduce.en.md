In characteristic zero, a non-constant polynomial always has a non-zero derivative, just as in calculus. In characteristic $p > 0$, however, the formal derivative can vanish for non-constant polynomials. This happens precisely when the polynomial is actually a polynomial in $X^p$, meaning every exponent of $X$ is a multiple of $p$. This phenomenon is the algebraic root of inseparability: an irreducible polynomial with vanishing derivative cannot have distinct roots in any extension field.
