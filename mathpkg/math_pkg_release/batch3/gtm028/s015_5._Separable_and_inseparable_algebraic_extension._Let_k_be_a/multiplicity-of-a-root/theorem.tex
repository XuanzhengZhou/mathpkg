Let $k$ be a field, $K$ an extension of $k$, and $x \in K$ an algebraic element over $k$. Let $g(X) \in k[X]$ be any polynomial such that $g(x) = 0$. Since $x \in K$ and $g(X)$ is also a polynomial in $K[X]$, the factor $X - x$ divides $g(X)$ in $K[X]$. Let $s$ be the largest integer such that $(X - x)^s$ divides $g(X)$ in $K[X]$:

$$g(X) = (X - x)^s g_1(X),$$

where $g_1(X) \in K[X]$ and $g_1(x) \neq 0$. The integer $s$ depends only on $x$ and $g(X)$ and not on the choice of the extension field $K$ of $k$ containing $x$. This integer $s$ is called the **multiplicity** of the root $x$ of $g(X)$. If $s = 1$, $x$ is called a **simple root**; if $s > 1$, $x$ is called a **multiple root** of $g(X)$.
