The multiplicity of a root $x$ of a polynomial $g(X)$ is the highest power of $(X - x)$ dividing $g(X)$ in any extension field containing $x$. This integer is well-defined and independent of the choice of extension field. A root of multiplicity 1 is called simple; roots of higher multiplicity are called multiple. The concept of multiplicity is essential for distinguishing separable elements (whose minimal polynomials have only simple roots) from inseparable ones. It generalizes the familiar notion from calculus to arbitrary fields using purely algebraic methods.
