An algebraic element $x$ over a field $k$ is separable if its minimal polynomial has non-zero formal derivative. Equivalently, $x$ is separable over $k$ if it is a simple root of its minimal polynomial in every extension field. Over a perfect field (characteristic zero or fields where every element is a $p$-th power), all algebraic elements are separable. The separability of an element ensures that its conjugates over $k$ are all distinct.
