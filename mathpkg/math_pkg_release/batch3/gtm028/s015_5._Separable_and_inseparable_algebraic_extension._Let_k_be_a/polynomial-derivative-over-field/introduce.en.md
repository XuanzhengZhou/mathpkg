The formal derivative of a polynomial over an arbitrary field is defined purely algebraically, without any appeal to limits or continuity. This mimics the usual analytic formula but operates in the polynomial ring $k[X]$. For a polynomial $f(X) = a_0 X^n + a_1 X^{n-1} + \cdots + a_n$, the derivative is $f'(X) = n a_0 X^{n-1} + (n-1)a_1 X^{n-2} + \cdots + a_{n-1}$, where the coefficients $n, n-1, \ldots$ are interpreted as elements of the field $k$ via the natural map $\mathbb{Z} \to k$. The behavior of this formal derivative depends critically on the characteristic of $k$, which is the basis for the theory of separable and inseparable extensions.
