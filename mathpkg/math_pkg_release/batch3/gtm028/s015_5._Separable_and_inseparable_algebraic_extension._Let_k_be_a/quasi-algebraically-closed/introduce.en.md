A field $k$ is quasi-algebraically closed in an extension $K$ if no proper separable algebraic extension of $k$ is contained in $K$. This is a relative notion: $k$ may be quasi-algebraically closed in some extension but not in another. For example, a separably closed field is quasi-algebraically closed in every extension. The concept is useful in Galois theory and in the study of regular extensions in algebraic geometry, where one often needs to know whether the base field is "maximally separable" inside a function field.
