This lemma establishes that separable and purely inseparable extensions are essentially complementary: the only elements that belong to both categories are those already in the base field. In characteristic zero, every algebraic element is separable, and being "purely inseparable" simply means being in $k$. In characteristic $p$, the lemma is a key tool for decomposing algebraic extensions into a separable part followed by a purely inseparable part.
