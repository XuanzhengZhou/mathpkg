Let $k$ be a field of characteristic $p \neq 0$ and let $x$ be purely inseparable over $k$. Let $e$ be the least non-negative integer such that $x^{p^e} \in k$, and set $a = x^{p^e}$. Then the minimal polynomial of $x$ over $k$ is $X^{p^e} - a$, and consequently $[k(x) : k] = p^e$. In particular, the degree of a purely inseparable extension is a power of $p$.
