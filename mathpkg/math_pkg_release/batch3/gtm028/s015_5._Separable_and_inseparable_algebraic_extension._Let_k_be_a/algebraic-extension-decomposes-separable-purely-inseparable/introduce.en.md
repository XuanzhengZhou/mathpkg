Any algebraic extension can be uniquely factored as a separable extension followed by a purely inseparable one. The intermediate field $k_0$ (maximal separable extension of $k$ in $K$) is the "separability frontier": everything above it is purely inseparable. This decomposition is fundamental in algebraic geometry (where separable extensions correspond to etale morphisms and purely inseparable ones to radicial morphisms) and in Galois theory. When $K/\bar{k}$ is finite, the separable degree and inseparable degree quantify the sizes of these two steps.
