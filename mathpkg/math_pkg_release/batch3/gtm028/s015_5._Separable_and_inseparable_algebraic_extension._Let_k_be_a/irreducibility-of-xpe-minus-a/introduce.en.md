In a field of prime characteristic $p$, the polynomial $X^{p^e} - a$ is the prototypical example of a purely inseparable polynomial. Zariski and Samuel prove that whenever $a$ is not a $p$-th power in $k$, the polynomial $X^{p^e} - a$ is irreducible for every $e \geq 0$. When $e = 0$, the statement is trivial since $X - a$ is always irreducible. For $e \geq 1$, the derivative vanishes identically, so the polynomial is inseparable. This theorem provides the canonical form for minimal polynomials of purely inseparable elements and shows that $[k(\sqrt[p^e]{a}) : k] = p^e$.
