A purely inseparable algebraic element over a field of characteristic $p$ is one whose minimal polynomial has the form $X^{p^e} - a$. Such an element $x$ satisfies $x^{p^e} = a \in k$, and its minimal polynomial has a single root (with multiplicity $p^e$) in any extension. Over a field of characteristic zero, the only purely inseparable elements are those already in $k$ (i.e., $e = 0$). The theory of purely inseparable extensions is a phenomenon unique to positive characteristic and complements the theory of separable extensions.
