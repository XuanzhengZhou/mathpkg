Let $F$ be a field. By $F_3$, every $a \in F$, $a \neq 0$, has a multiplicative inverse $a^{-1} \in F$, so every nonzero element is a unit. Suppose $a, b \in F$ with $ab = 0$ and $a \neq 0$. Then multiplying both sides by $a^{-1}$ gives $b = 0$. Hence $F$ has no proper zero divisors. By $F_2$, $F$ has an identity $1 \neq 0$, so $F$ satisfies the definition of an integral domain.
