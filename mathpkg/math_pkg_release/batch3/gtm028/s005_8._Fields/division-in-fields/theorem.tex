Let $F$ be a field.

\begin{enumerate}
\item For any $a, b \in F$ with $a \neq 0$, the equation $ax = b$ has a unique solution in $F$, denoted $b/a$ and called the \textbf{quotient} of $b$ by $a$.
\item If $a \neq 0$, then $0/a = 0$.
\item Division by zero is impossible: the equation $0 \cdot x = b$ has no solution if $b \neq 0$, and every element of $F$ is a solution if $b = 0$.
\end{enumerate}
