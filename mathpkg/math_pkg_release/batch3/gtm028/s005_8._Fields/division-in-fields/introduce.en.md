In a field, division by any nonzero element is always well-defined. For $a \neq 0$, the quotient $b/a$ is the unique solution to $ax = b$, which exists because $a$ has a multiplicative inverse. The case $a = 0$ is excluded because the equation $0 \cdot x = b$ either has no solution (when $b \neq 0$) or is satisfied by every element (when $b = 0$), so division by zero cannot yield a unique result. This property distinguishes fields from general rings.
