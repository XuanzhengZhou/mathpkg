This fundamental theorem states that the multiplicative group $GF(p^n)^\times$ of nonzero elements of any finite field is cyclic. The group has order $h = p^n - 1$. The proof proceeds by decomposing $h$ into prime powers and constructing an element whose order is exactly $h$, using the fact that a polynomial equation $X^d = 1$ can have at most $d$ roots in a field. This result implies that every finite field has a primitive element whose powers generate all nonzero elements.
