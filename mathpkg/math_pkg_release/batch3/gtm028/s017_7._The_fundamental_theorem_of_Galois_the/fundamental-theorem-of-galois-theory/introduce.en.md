The Fundamental Theorem of Galois Theory establishes a bijective correspondence between subgroups of the Galois group $G(K/k)$ and intermediate fields between $k$ and $K$, where $K/k$ is a finite normal (hence separable) extension. For each subgroup $H$, the corresponding intermediate field is the fixed field $L = K^H$, and the theorem asserts that $H = G(K/L)$ and $[K:L] = |H|$. This correspondence reverses inclusions and is the cornerstone of classical Galois theory, allowing one to translate problems about field extensions into problems about finite groups.
