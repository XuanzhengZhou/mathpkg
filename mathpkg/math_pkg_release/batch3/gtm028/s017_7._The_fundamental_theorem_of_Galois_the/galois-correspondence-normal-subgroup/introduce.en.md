This corollary of the Fundamental Theorem of Galois Theory characterizes when an intermediate field $L$ (with $k \subset L \subset K$) is itself a normal extension of $k$. The condition is precisely that the corresponding subgroup $G(K/L)$ be an invariant (normal) subgroup of the full Galois group $G(K/k)$. Furthermore, in this case the Galois group of the intermediate extension is given by the factor group $G(L/k) \cong G(K/k)/G(K/L)$. This result elegantly translates the field-theoretic property of normality into group-theoretic language.
