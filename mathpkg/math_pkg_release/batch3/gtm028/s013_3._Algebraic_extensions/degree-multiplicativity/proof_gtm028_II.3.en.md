Let $\{\omega_1, \ldots, \omega_n\}$ be a basis of $K$ over $k$, so $[K:k] = n$. Let $\{\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m\}$ be a basis of $L$ over $K$, so $[L:K] = m$. By the Tower Theorem, the $mn$ elements $\omega_i \xi_j$ ($i = 1, \ldots, n$; $j = 1, \ldots, m$) form a basis of $L$ over $k$. Hence
$$[L:k] = mn = [L:K] \cdot [K:k].$$
