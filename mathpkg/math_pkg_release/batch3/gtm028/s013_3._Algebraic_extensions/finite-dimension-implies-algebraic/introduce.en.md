If a field extension $K/k$ is finite-dimensional as a vector space, then every element of $K$ is algebraic over $k$. Moreover, each $x \in K$ satisfies a polynomial equation of degree at most $[K:k]$. This theorem connects the purely linear-algebraic notion of finite dimension with the field-theoretic notion of algebraicity, and it shows that the minimal polynomial of any element has degree bounded by the extension degree.
