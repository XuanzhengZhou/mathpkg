The Tower Theorem (or "tower law") states that if $k \subset K \subset L$ is a tower of finite field extensions, then $L/k$ is also finite. Explicitly, a basis of $L/k$ is obtained by multiplying basis elements of $K/k$ with basis elements of $L/K$. This is the field-theoretic analogue of the composition of finite-dimensional vector spaces and is fundamental for computing extension degrees in iterated field constructions.
