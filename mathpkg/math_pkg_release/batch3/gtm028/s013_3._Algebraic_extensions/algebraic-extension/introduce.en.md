A field extension $K/k$ is algebraic if every element of $K$ satisfies a non-zero polynomial equation with coefficients in $k$. This generalizes the notion of an algebraic element to the entire extension field. Extensions that fail this condition---those containing at least one transcendental element---are called transcendental extensions. The simplest example of an algebraic extension is the simple extension $k(x)$ where $x$ is algebraic over $k$.
