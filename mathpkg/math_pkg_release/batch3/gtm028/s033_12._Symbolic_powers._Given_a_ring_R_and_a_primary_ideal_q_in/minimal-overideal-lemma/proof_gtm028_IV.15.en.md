From the hypothesis concerning the ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ and from Corollary 1 to Theorem 28 (§13), it follows that $\mathfrak{a}\mathfrak{m} \subset \mathfrak{b}$ and consequently $\mathfrak{a}/\mathfrak{b}$ is a one-dimensional vector space over $R/\mathfrak{m}$. The colon ideal operation yields $(\mathfrak{q} : \mathfrak{a}) \subset (\mathfrak{q} : \mathfrak{b})$ and one verifies that there can be no ideal properly between them using the one-dimensionality of the quotient.
