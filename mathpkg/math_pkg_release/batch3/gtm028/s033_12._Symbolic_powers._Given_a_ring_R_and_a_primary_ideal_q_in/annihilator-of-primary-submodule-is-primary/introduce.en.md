This theorem establishes a crucial link between primary submodules and primary ideals: the annihilator (order) of the difference module $M - N$ is a primary ideal whenever $N$ is a primary submodule. This allows the rich theory of primary decomposition for ideals to be lifted to the module setting, as the associated primes of a primary submodule are well-defined via this annihilator.
