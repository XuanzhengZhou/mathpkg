Krull's Principal Ideal Theorem (Theorem 29) is one of the foundational results of commutative algebra. It establishes that in a noetherian domain, isolated prime ideals of proper principal ideals are exactly the minimal prime ideals. This theorem is the starting point for dimension theory and lies at the heart of the relationship between the number of generators of an ideal and the heights of its associated primes.
