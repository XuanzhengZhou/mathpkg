Let $\mathfrak{a}$ be an ideal in $R$ which admits a primary representation without imbedded components. Let $M$ be the complement of the union of the associated prime ideals of $\mathfrak{a}$. The $n$-th symbolic power $\mathfrak{a}^{(n)}$ is defined by $\mathfrak{a}^{(n)} = (\mathfrak{a}^n)^{ec}$, where extension and contraction are taken with respect to the quotient ring $R_M$. The ideal $\mathfrak{a}^{(n)}$ is the set of all elements $x$ in $R$ for which there exists an element $m \in M$ such that $mx \in \mathfrak{a}^n$. The ideal $\mathfrak{a}^{(n)}$ has the same associated prime ideals as $\mathfrak{a}$; all these prime ideals are isolated.
