The definition of symbolic powers can be generalized from primary ideals to any ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ admitting a primary representation without imbedded components. Instead of localizing at a single prime $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$, one localizes at the multiplicative system $M$ which is the complement of the union of all associated primes of $\mathfrak{a}$. The resulting $n$-th symbolic power $\mathfrak{a}^{(n)}$ preserves the associated primes and has only isolated components, making it a natural extension of the primary ideal case.
