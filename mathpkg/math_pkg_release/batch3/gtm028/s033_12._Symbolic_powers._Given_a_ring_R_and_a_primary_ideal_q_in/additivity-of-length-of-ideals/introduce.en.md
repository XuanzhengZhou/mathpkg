Theorem 25 shows that the length $\lambda(\mathfrak{a})$ of an ideal is additive across its isolated primary components. If $\mathfrak{a} = \bigcap \mathfrak{q}_i$ is an irredundant primary representation without imbedded components, then the length of $\mathfrak{a}$ is the sum of the lengths of the individual primary ideals $\mathfrak{q}_i$. This reduces the study of the length of an ideal to the study of primary ideals.
