The height $h(\mathfrak{p})$ of a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ is the maximal length of an ascending chain of prime ideals terminating at $\mathfrak{p}$, while the depth $d(\mathfrak{p})$ is the maximal length of a descending chain starting from $\mathfrak{p}$ and ending before $R$. Height measures "how far up" $\mathfrak{p}$ is from $(0)$, and depth measures "how far down" from the maximal ideals. Prime ideals of height 0 are minimal; prime ideals of depth 0 are maximal.
