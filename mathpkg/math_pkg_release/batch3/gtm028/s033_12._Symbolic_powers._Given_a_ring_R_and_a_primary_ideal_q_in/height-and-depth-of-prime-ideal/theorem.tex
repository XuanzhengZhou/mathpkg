Let $R$ be an arbitrary ring with identity. A prime ideal $\mathfrak{p} \neq R$ is said to have \textit{height} $h$ (respectively, \textit{depth} $d$) if there exists at least one chain
$$\mathfrak{p}_0 \subsetneq \mathfrak{p}_1 \subsetneq \cdots \subsetneq \mathfrak{p}_{h-1} \subsetneq \mathfrak{p}_h = \mathfrak{p}$$
(respectively, $\mathfrak{p} = \mathfrak{p}_d \subsetneq \mathfrak{p}_{d-1} \subsetneq \cdots \subsetneq \mathfrak{p}_1 \subsetneq \mathfrak{p}_0 \subsetneq R$)
where the $\mathfrak{p}_i$ are prime ideals, and there exists no such chain with more than $h + 1$ (respectively, $d + 1$) ideals. The height (respectively, depth) of a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ is denoted by $h(\mathfrak{p})$ (respectively $d(\mathfrak{p})$).
