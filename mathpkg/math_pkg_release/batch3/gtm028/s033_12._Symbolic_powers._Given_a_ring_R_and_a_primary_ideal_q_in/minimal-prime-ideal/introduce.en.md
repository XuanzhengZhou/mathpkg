A minimal prime ideal in an integral domain is a non-zero prime ideal that does not properly contain any other non-zero prime ideal. In a noetherian domain, every proper principal prime ideal is minimal; in a unique factorization domain, all minimal prime ideals are principal and are generated by irreducible elements. This concept is fundamental to the dimension theory of commutative rings.
