Theorem 31 is the converse of Theorem 30. It asserts that the bound $h(\mathfrak{p}) \leq r$ for an $r$-generated ideal is sharp: every prime ideal of height $h$ can be realized as an isolated prime ideal of some ideal generated by exactly $h$ elements. This result completes the fundamental relationship between heights and numbers of generators in noetherian rings.
