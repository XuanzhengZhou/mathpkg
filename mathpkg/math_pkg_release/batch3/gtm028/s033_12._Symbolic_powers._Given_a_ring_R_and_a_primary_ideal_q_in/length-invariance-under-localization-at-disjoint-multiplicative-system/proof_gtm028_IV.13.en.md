Apply Theorem 16 of §10: since $M$ is disjoint from $\mathfrak{q}$, the extension $\mathfrak{q}^e$ in $R_M$ is primary and belongs to the same prime as $\mathfrak{q}$. The $R_M$-module $R_M/\mathfrak{q}^e$ has the same composition series length as the $R$-module formed by taking the difference module with respect to the same underlying additive group structure, because localization at $M$ does not affect the chain of submodules when $M$ is disjoint from the radical. Hence $\lambda(\mathfrak{q}) = \lambda(\mathfrak{q}^e)$.
