The appendix to Chapter IV extends the theory of primary decomposition from ideals to modules. The radical $\mathfrak{r}(N)$ of a submodule $N$ generalizes the radical of an ideal: it consists of elements $a \in R$ whose power sends the whole module $M$ into $N$. A submodule $N$ is primary if, whenever a scalar multiple $ax$ lies in $N$, either the element $x$ is already in $N$ or the scalar $a$ belongs to the radical. This generalizes the ideal case where $M = R$.
