Let $R$ be a commutative ring, $M$ an $R$-module, and $N$ a submodule of $M$.

The \textit{radical} of the submodule $N$, denoted $\mathfrak{r}(N)$, is the set of all $a \in R$ for which there exists an exponent $n$ such that $a^n \cdot M \subset N$. This set is an ideal in $R$, equal to the radical of the annihilator $\mathfrak{b}$ of the difference module $M - N$ (where $\mathfrak{b} = \{b \in R : bM \subset N\}$).

A submodule $N$ of $M$ is said to be \textit{primary} if the relation $ax \in N$ (with $a \in R$, $x \in M$) implies either $x \in N$ or $a \in \mathfrak{r}(N)$.
