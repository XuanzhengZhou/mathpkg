Theorem 30 generalizes Krull's Principal Ideal Theorem from one generator to $r$ generators: if an ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ can be generated by $r$ elements, then every isolated prime ideal of $\mathfrak{a}$ has height $\leq r$. This is a fundamental result in dimension theory, establishing a crucial link between the number of generators and the height of associated primes.
