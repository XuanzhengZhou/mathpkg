The proof proceeds by induction on $r$. For $r = 0$, $(0)$ is the only ideal generated by 0 elements, and its isolated prime ideals are of height 0.

In the general case, let $\mathfrak{a}$ be an ideal with basis $\{x_1, \ldots, x_r\}$ and $\mathfrak{p}_0$ an isolated prime ideal of $\mathfrak{a}$. Consider the ideal $\mathfrak{b}$ generated by $\{x_1, \ldots, x_{r-1}\}$. Let $\mathfrak{p}_1, \ldots, \mathfrak{p}_t$ be the isolated prime ideals of $\mathfrak{b}$ contained in $\mathfrak{p}_0$, and let $\mathfrak{v}_1, \ldots, \mathfrak{v}_s$ be the imbedded prime ideals of $\mathfrak{b}$ contained in $\mathfrak{p}_0$. By the induction hypothesis, $h(\mathfrak{p}_i) \leq r-1$ for all $i$. Now consider the case when $\mathfrak{a} = \mathfrak{b} + R x_r$. One shows that $\mathfrak{p}_0$ cannot be an isolated prime of $\mathfrak{b}$ (unless $x_r \notin \mathfrak{p}_0$), and then constructs a chain showing $h(\mathfrak{p}_0) \leq r$ by inserting a suitable prime between an isolated prime of $\mathfrak{b}$ and $\mathfrak{p}_0$, using the Principal Ideal Theorem.
