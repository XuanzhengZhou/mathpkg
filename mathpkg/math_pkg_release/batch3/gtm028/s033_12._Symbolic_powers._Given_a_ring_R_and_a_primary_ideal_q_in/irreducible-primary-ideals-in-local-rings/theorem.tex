Let $R$ be a local ring and let $\mathfrak{q}$ be a primary ideal in $R$ belonging to the ideal $\mathfrak{m}$ of non-units of $R$. The following conditions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathfrak{q}$ is irreducible.
\item $(\mathfrak{q} : \mathfrak{m})/\mathfrak{q}$ is a one-dimensional vector space over $R/\mathfrak{m}$.
\item $\mathfrak{q} : \mathfrak{m}$ is the smallest ideal properly containing $\mathfrak{q}$.
\item The mapping $\mathfrak{a} \mapsto \mathfrak{q} : \mathfrak{a}$ is a $(1, 1)$ correspondence of the set of ideals containing $\mathfrak{q}$ onto itself, and $\mathfrak{a} = \mathfrak{q} : (\mathfrak{q} : \mathfrak{a})$ for every ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ containing $\mathfrak{q}$.
\end{enumerate}
