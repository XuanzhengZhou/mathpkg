(1) Every proper prime ideal $\mathfrak{p} = Rp$ is generated by an irreducible element $p$, because if $p = ab$, then $a \in \mathfrak{p}$ or $b \in \mathfrak{p}$, implying one factor is a unit. Since $R$ is a PID, any ideal containing $Rp$ must be generated by a divisor of $p$; by irreducibility, no proper containment exists, so $Rp$ is maximal.

(2) The GCD assertion: since $Ra + Rb$ is principal, it equals $Rd$ for some $d = au + bv$. Then $a, b \in Rd$, so $d$ is a common divisor. Any common divisor of $a$ and $b$ divides any linear combination, hence divides $d$. Thus $d$ is the greatest common divisor.

(3) For UF1 (factorization into irreducibles): If $a$ is a non-unit, $Ra$ is contained in a maximal ideal $Rp_1$ (since $R$ is noetherian), so $a = p_1 a_1$. If $a_1$ is a non-unit, continue. The process terminates since an infinite chain $Ra \subsetneq Ra_1 \subsetneq \cdots$ would violate the noetherian property.

(4) For $R/\mathfrak{a}$ with $\mathfrak{a} = Rx$, ideals correspond to classes of associated divisors of $x$. By unique factorization, these classes are finite in number, hence $R/\mathfrak{a}$ has only finitely many ideals and thus satisfies DCC.
