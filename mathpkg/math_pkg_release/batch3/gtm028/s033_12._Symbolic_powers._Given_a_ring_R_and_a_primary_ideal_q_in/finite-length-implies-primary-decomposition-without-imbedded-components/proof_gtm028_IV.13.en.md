By Theorem 2 (§2), the ring $R/\mathfrak{a}$ satisfies both chain conditions since $\ell(R - \mathfrak{a})$ is finite. Thus $R/\mathfrak{a}$ is noetherian, and every prime ideal of this ring is maximal. This gives a primary representation without imbedded components for $(0)$ in $R/\mathfrak{a}$, whence for $\mathfrak{a}$ in $R$ (see Remark at the end of §5, p. 213). Since the associated prime ideals of $\mathfrak{a}$ are maximal, the ring $R/\mathfrak{a}$ is its own quotient ring $(R/\mathfrak{a})_{M'}$ where $M'$ is the complement of their union. Hence $\lambda(\mathfrak{a}) = \ell(R - \mathfrak{a})$.
