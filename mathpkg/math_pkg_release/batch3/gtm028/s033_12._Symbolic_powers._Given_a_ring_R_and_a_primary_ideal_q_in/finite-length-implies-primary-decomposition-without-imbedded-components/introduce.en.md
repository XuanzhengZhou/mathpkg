Theorem 27 connects the module-theoretic notion of length with the ideal-theoretic notion of primary decomposition. If the difference $R$-module $R - \mathfrak{a}$ has finite length, then $\mathfrak{a}$ automatically admits a primary decomposition without imbedded components, and its ideal-theoretic length $\lambda(\mathfrak{a})$ coincides with the module length $\ell(R - \mathfrak{a})$.
