Corollary 1 combines Theorem 7 with the Noetherian hypothesis to obtain a strong finiteness result. When the base ring $A$ is Noetherian, the integral closure $A'$ is not merely contained in a finite $A$-module but is itself a finite $A$-module. This implies $A'$ inherits the ascending chain condition and is therefore a Noetherian ring. This corollary is crucial for the proof of Theorem 9 and for establishing that integral closures of Noetherian domains in separable extensions are again Noetherian.
