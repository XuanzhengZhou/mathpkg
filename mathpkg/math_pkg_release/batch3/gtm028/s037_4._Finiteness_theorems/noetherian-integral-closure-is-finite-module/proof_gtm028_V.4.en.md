By Theorem 7, $A'$ is contained in the $A$-module $\sum_i A x_i$ for some basis $\{x_1, \cdots, x_n\}$ of $F$ over $K$. Since $A$ is Noetherian, the finite $A$-module $\sum_i A x_i$ is a Noetherian $A$-module. The submodule $A'$ of this Noetherian $A$-module is therefore a finite $A$-module. Thus $A'$ satisfies the ascending chain condition as an $A$-module (III, §10, Theorem 18), and *a fortiori* as an $A'$-module, whence $A'$ is a Noetherian ring.
