Let $A$ be an integral domain and let $A'$ be the integral closure of $A$ in its quotient field $F$. The set
$$\mathfrak{f} = \{ z \in A \mid zA' \subset A \}$$
is called the **conductor** of $A$ in $A'$, or the **conductor of the integral closure** of $A$.

The conductor $\mathfrak{f}$ is an ideal in $A$ and also an ideal in $A'$. If $\mathfrak{b}$ is any ideal of $A$ which is also an ideal of $A'$, then $\mathfrak{b} \subset \mathfrak{f}$; thus $\mathfrak{f}$ is the largest ideal of $A$ which is also an ideal of $A'$. In particular, $A' = A$ if and only if $\mathfrak{f}$ is the unit ideal.
