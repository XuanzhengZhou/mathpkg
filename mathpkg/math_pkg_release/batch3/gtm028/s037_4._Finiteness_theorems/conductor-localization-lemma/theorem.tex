Let $A$ be an integral domain, $A'$ its integral closure, $\mathfrak{f}$ the conductor of $A$ in $A'$, and $S$ a multiplicative system in $A$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A'_S$ is the integral closure of $A_S$.
\item For $A_S$ to be integrally closed, it is sufficient that $\mathfrak{f} \cap S \neq \emptyset$.
\item If $A'$ is a finite $A$-module, then the conductor of $A_S$ in $A'_S$ is $\mathfrak{f} \cdot A_S$.
\item If, moreover, $A_S$ is integrally closed, then $A_S = A'_S$.
\end{enumerate}
