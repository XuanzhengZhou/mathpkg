This lemma describes how the conductor behaves under localization by a multiplicative system $S$. It states that integral closure commutes with localization ($A'_S$ is the integral closure of $A_S$), and that $A_S$ is integrally closed whenever $S$ meets the conductor. When $A'$ is a finite $A$-module, the conductor of the localized ring is the localized conductor $\mathfrak{f} \cdot A_S$. These facts are essential for reducing questions about integral closedness to the local case, since one can localize at primes not containing the conductor.
