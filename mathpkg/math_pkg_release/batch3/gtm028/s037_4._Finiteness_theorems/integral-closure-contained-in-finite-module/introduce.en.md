Theorem 7 establishes a fundamental finiteness result for integral closures in separable field extensions. Given an integrally closed domain $A$ and a finite separable extension $F$ of its quotient field, the integral closure $A'$ of $A$ in $F$ is always contained in a finitely generated $A$-module. The proof uses the trace form $T(xy)$ and the non-degeneracy of the trace pairing in separable extensions to construct an explicit basis of $F$ over $K$ whose $A$-span contains $A'$. This theorem underpins the Noetherianity of integral closures in the separable case and is essential for the finiteness theorems that follow.
