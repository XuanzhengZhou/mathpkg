Let $k[x_1, \cdots, x_n]$ be a finite integral domain over a field $k$, of transcendence degree $d$ over $k$. If the quotient field $k(x_1, \cdots, x_n)$ is separably generated over $k$, then there exist $d$ linear combinations $y_1, \cdots, y_d$ of the $x_i$ with coefficients in $k$ such that $k[x_1, \cdots, x_n]$ is integral over the polynomial ring $k[y_1, \cdots, y_d]$, and $k(x_1, \cdots, x_n)$ is a separable extension of $k(y_1, \cdots, y_d)$. Thus $\{y_1, \cdots, y_d\}$ is a separating transcendence basis of $k(x_1, \cdots, x_n)$ over $k$.
