Theorem 35 establishes that tensor product commutes with quotient by ideals: $(A/\mathfrak{A}) \otimes_k (B/\mathfrak{B}) \cong (A \otimes_k B) / (\mathfrak{A}, \mathfrak{B})$, where $(\mathfrak{A}, \mathfrak{B})$ is the ideal generated by the images of $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ in $A \otimes_k B$. The proof uses the canonical homomorphisms from $A$ and $B$ onto their quotients, combined with the universal property of the tensor product, and invokes Corollary 2 of Theorem 34 to confirm the constructed map is an isomorphism.
