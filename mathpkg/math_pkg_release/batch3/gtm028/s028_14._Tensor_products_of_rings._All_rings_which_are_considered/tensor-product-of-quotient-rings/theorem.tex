Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an ideal in $A$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ an ideal in $B$. Denote by $(\mathfrak{A}, \mathfrak{B})$ the ideal in $A \otimes_k B$ generated by the elements of $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$. Then there is a canonical isomorphism
$$(A/\mathfrak{A}) \otimes_k (B/\mathfrak{B}) \cong (A \otimes_k B) / (\mathfrak{A}, \mathfrak{B}).$$
