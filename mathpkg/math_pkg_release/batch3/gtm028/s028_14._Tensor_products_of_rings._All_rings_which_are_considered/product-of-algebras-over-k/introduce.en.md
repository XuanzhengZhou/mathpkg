A product of two $k$-algebras $A$ and $B$ is a triple $(C, \varphi, \psi)$ where $C$ is another $k$-algebra and $\varphi, \psi$ are $k$-algebra embeddings of $A$ and $B$ into $C$, such that $C$ is generated as a ring by the images $\varphi(A)$ and $\psi(B)$. This notion captures the idea of combining two algebras inside a common overring with the requirement that the original algebras embed faithfully and jointly generate the ambient ring. The concept of a product serves as the foundation for defining the tensor product of algebras, which imposes the additional universal mapping property.
