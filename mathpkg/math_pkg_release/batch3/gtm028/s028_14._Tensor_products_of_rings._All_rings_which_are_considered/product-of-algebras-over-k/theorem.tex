Let $A$ and $B$ be two algebras over a field $k$. By a \textbf{product} of $A$ and $B$ (over $k$) we mean the composite concept $(C, \varphi, \psi)$ consisting of:
\begin{itemize}
\item an algebra $C$ over $k$,
\item a $k$-isomorphism $\varphi$ of $A$ into $C$,
\item a $k$-isomorphism $\psi$ of $B$ into $C$,
\end{itemize}
such that $C = [A\varphi, B\psi]$, where $[A\varphi, B\psi]$ denotes the smallest subring of $C$ containing both $A\varphi$ and $B\psi$.
