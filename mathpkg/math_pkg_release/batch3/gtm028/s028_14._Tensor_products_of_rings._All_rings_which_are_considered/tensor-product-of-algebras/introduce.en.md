The tensor product $A \otimes_k B$ of two $k$-algebras $A$ and $B$ is a product $(C, \varphi, \psi)$ that satisfies a universal mapping property: any pair of $k$-algebra homomorphisms from $A$ and $B$ into a common target ring $R$ factors uniquely through $C$. This makes the tensor product the "most general" way to combine $A$ and $B$ inside a single ring while keeping them embedded as $k$-subalgebras. The universal property determines the tensor product uniquely up to equivalence, and it serves as the fundamental construction for base change, extension of scalars, and the study of algebraic varieties over non-algebraically closed fields.
