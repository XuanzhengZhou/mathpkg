A product $(C, \varphi, \psi)$ of $A$ and $B$ (over $k$) is called a \textbf{tensor product} of $A$ and $B$ (over $k$) if it satisfies the following universal property: given any two $k$-homomorphisms $g: A \to R$ and $h: B \to R$ of $A$ and $B$ respectively into a ring $R$, there exists a homomorphism $f: C \to R$ such that $f = \varphi^{-1}g$ on $A\varphi$ and $f = \psi^{-1}h$ on $B\psi$.
