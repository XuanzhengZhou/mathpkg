Corollary 1 to Theorem 34 asserts that any two tensor products of $A$ and $B$ over $k$ are equivalent. This follows immediately from the universal mapping property: given two tensor products, each admits a unique homomorphism to the other that respects the embeddings, and these homomorphisms are mutually inverse. This justifies the standard notation $A \otimes_k B$ for "the" tensor product, as it is unique up to canonical isomorphism.
