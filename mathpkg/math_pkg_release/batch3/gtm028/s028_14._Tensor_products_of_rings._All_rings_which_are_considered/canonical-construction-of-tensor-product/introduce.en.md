The canonical construction of $A \otimes_k B$ proceeds by forming the free $k$-vector space on the set $A \times B$, introducing a ring structure via componentwise multiplication $(a,b)(a',b') = (aa', bb')$, and then quotienting by the ideal that enforces $k$-bilinearity. The resulting ring $T$ admits natural embeddings $g: A \to T$, $a \mapsto a \otimes 1$ and $h: B \to T$, $b \mapsto 1 \otimes b$, and satisfies $T = [Ag, Bh]$. Unlike the basis-dependent construction in the proof of Theorem 33, this construction is intrinsic to the rings $A$ and $B$. The universal property is verified by mapping the canonical map $\sigma: \sum c_i(a_i,b_i) \mapsto \sum c_i \varphi(a_i)\psi(b_i)$ through $T$.
