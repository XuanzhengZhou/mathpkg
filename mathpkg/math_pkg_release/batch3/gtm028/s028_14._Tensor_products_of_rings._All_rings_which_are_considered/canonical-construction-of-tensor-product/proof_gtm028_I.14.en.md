Let $M$ be the vector space of formal finite linear combinations $\sum_i c_i(a_i, b_i)$ with $c_i \in k$, $a_i \in A$, $b_i \in B$, where addition and scalar multiplication are defined in the obvious way:
$$\left( \sum_i c_i(a_i, b_i) \right) + \left( \sum_i d_i(a_i, b_i) \right) = \sum_i (c_i + d_i)(a_i, b_i),$$
$$d\left( \sum_i c_i(a_i, b_i) \right) = \sum_i dc_i(a_i, b_i).$$

Define a multiplication in $M$ by $(a, b)(a', b') = (aa', bb')$ and extend bilinearly. This makes $M$ an associative ring. Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be the ideal generated by the bilinearity relations:
$$(a + a', b) - (a, b) - (a', b), \quad (a, b + b') - (a, b) - (a, b'), \quad (ca, b) - c(a, b), \quad (a, cb) - c(a, b).$$

Set $T = M/\mathfrak{R}$ and denote the coset of $(a, b)$ by $a \otimes b$. The maps $g: A \to T$, $a \mapsto a \otimes 1$, and $h: B \to T$, $b \mapsto 1 \otimes b$, are ring homomorphisms. Since $(a, b) = (a, 1)(1, b)$ in $M$, we have $a \otimes b = (a \otimes 1)(1 \otimes b)$ in $T$, hence every element of $T$ is a sum of products of elements from $Ag$ and $Bh$. Thus
$$T = [Ag, Bh].$$

To show $g$ and $h$ are isomorphisms onto their images: fix a tensor product $(C, \varphi, \psi)$. The map
$$\sigma: \sum_i c_i(a_i, b_i) \mapsto \sum_i c_i \varphi(a_i)\psi(b_i)$$
is a ring homomorphism of $M$ onto $C$. The bilinearity of $\varphi$ and $\psi$ implies that $\mathfrak{R} \subseteq \ker \sigma$. Hence $\sigma = \rho \circ \tau$ where $\rho: M \to T$ is the canonical projection and $\tau: T \to C$ is a homomorphism. For any $a \in A$, $\varphi(a) = \sigma((a,1)) = \tau(a \otimes 1) = (g \circ \tau)(a)$. Since $\varphi$ is an isomorphism, $g$ is an isomorphism of $A$ onto $Ag$ and $\tau|_{Ag}$ is an isomorphism onto $A\varphi$. Similarly, $h$ is an isomorphism of $B$ onto $Bh$.

Finally, the universal property: given $g_0: A \to R$, $h_0: B \to R$, define $f: T \to R$ by $f(\sum a_i \otimes b_i) = \sum g_0(a_i)h_0(b_i)$. This is well-defined because the bilinearity relations map to zero under this definition. Hence $(T, g, h)$ is a tensor product.
