Theorem 34 establishes that the tensor product of two $k$-algebras $A$ and $B$ is characterized by a universal mapping property: a product $(C, \varphi, \psi)$ is a tensor product if and only if every pair of $k$-algebra homomorphisms $g: A \to R$, $h: B \to R$ factors through $C$ via a homomorphism $f$ that agrees with $\varphi^{-1}g$ on the image of $A$ and $\psi^{-1}h$ on the image of $B$. The necessity direction uses the fact that every element of $C$ is a sum of products $\varphi(a_i)\psi(b_i)$; the map $f$ is defined by $f(\sum \varphi(a_i)\psi(b_i)) = \sum g(a_i)h(b_i)$, and its well-definedness follows from Lemma 2 of I, §11. The sufficiency direction follows from the definition of tensor product.
