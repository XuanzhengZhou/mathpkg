Theorem 36 describes the total quotient ring of the tensor product of two integral domains. When $A$ and $B$ are integral domains over $k$ whose quotient fields $K$ and $K'$ are linearly disjoint over $k$, then $A \otimes_k B$ is an integral domain and its field of fractions is $K \otimes_k K'$. The proof uses the fact that regular elements of $A \otimes_k B$ remain regular after passing to $K \otimes_k K'$, which follows from expressing elements in terms of $k$-bases and analyzing the linear disjointness condition. This theorem is fundamental for understanding the behavior of tensor products in algebraic geometry, particularly for products of varieties over non-algebraically closed fields.
