Two products $(C, \varphi, \psi)$ and $(C', \varphi', \psi')$ of the same pair of $k$-algebras $A$ and $B$ are equivalent when there is a $k$-isomorphism $\lambda: C \to C'$ that intertwines the structure maps: $\lambda \circ \varphi = \varphi'$ and $\lambda \circ \psi = \psi'$. This notion of equivalence ensures that the way $A$ and $B$ sit inside $C$ is structurally the same as the way they sit inside $C'$. In categorical terms, equivalent products are isomorphic in the comma category of pairs of maps from $A$ and $B$ into a common target.
