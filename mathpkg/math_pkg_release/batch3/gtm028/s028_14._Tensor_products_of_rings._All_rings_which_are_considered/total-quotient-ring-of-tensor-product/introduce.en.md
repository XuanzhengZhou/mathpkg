This corollary to the zero-divisor lemma shows that the total quotient ring of the tensor product $A \otimes_k B$ is large enough to contain the total quotient rings of both factors $A$ and $B$. Since every regular element of $A$ remains regular in $A \otimes_k B$, it has an inverse in the total quotient ring $K$, and the quotient ring of $A$ in $K$ is a total quotient ring of $A$. Moreover, these embedded total quotient rings of $A$ and $B$ are linearly disjoint over $k$ inside $K$.
