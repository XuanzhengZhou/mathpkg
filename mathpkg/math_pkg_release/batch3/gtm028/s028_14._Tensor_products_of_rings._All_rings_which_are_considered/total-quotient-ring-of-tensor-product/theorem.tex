The total quotient ring $K$ of $A \otimes_k B$ contains the total quotient rings of $A$ and $B$. More precisely: the quotient ring of $A$ in $K$ is a total quotient ring of $A$, and similarly for $B$. Furthermore, these total quotient rings of $A$ and $B$ are linearly disjoint over $k$ (as subrings of $K$).
