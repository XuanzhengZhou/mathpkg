If a given product $(C, \varphi, \psi)$ of $A$ and $B$ admits a homomorphism $f$ into a tensor product $(C', \varphi', \psi')$ of $A$ and $B$ such that $f = \varphi^{-1}\varphi'$ on $A\varphi$ and $f = \psi^{-1}\psi'$ on $B\psi$, then $(C, \varphi, \psi)$ is itself a tensor product of $A$ and $B$, and $f$ is an isomorphism of $C$ onto $C'$.
