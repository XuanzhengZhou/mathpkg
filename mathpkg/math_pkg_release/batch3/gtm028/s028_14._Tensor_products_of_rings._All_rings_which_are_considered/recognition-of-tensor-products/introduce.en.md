Corollary 2 to Theorem 34 provides a practical recognition criterion for tensor products: if a product $(C, \varphi, \psi)$ admits a homomorphism $f$ into a known tensor product $(C', \varphi', \psi')$ that is compatible with the embeddings ($f\varphi = \varphi'$, $f\psi = \psi'$), then $(C, \varphi, \psi)$ is automatically a tensor product and $f$ is an isomorphism. This is established in the second half of the proof of Theorem 34 and is useful for verifying that a constructed product is indeed the tensor product.
