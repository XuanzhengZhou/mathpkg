This lemma states that regular elements (non-zero-divisors) of $A$ remain regular in the tensor product $A \otimes_k B$. The proof is immediate: if $a\xi = 0$ in $A \otimes B$, write $\xi = \sum a_i \otimes b_i$ with the $b_i$ linearly independent over $k$ (hence over $A$). Then $\sum (a a_i) \otimes b_i = 0$ implies $a a_i = 0$ for all $i$, and since $a$ is regular in $A$, each $a_i = 0$, so $\xi = 0$.
