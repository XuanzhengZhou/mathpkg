Theorem 27 shows that the differential exponent (the exponent of a prime ideal in the factorization of the different) is a local invariant: it does not change when passing to the localization at the prime below. This justifies the computation of the different via local methods, reducing to the case of discrete valuation rings where explicit formulas are available.
