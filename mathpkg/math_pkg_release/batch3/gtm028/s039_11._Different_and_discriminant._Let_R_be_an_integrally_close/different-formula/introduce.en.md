Theorem 28 (and the surrounding results) provides the fundamental formula relating the different to the complementary module: the different is the inverse of the complementary module. In the tame ramification case, the differential exponent of a prime ideal is one less than the ramification index, giving a clean relation between ramification theory and the different. The different thus detects precisely which primes are ramified.
