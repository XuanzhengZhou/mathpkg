For any basis $\{u_1, \cdots, u_n\}$ of $K'$ over $K$ which is contained in $R'$, the element $d(u)$, discriminant of the basis $\{u_1, \cdots, u_n\}$, is contained in the ideal $\mathfrak{d}_{R'/R}$, discriminant of $R'$ over $R$. The ideal $\mathfrak{d}_{R'/R}$ is generated by the elements $d(u)$. In order that $\mathfrak{d}_{R'/R} = R \cdot d(u)$, it is necessary and sufficient that $\{u_1, \cdots, u_n\}$ be a module basis of $R'$ over $R$.
