The complementary module is a fundamental construction in the theory of Dedekind domains and algebraic number theory. For an integral extension R' of an integrally closed ring R, it collects all elements of the quotient field whose traces (multiplied by any element of R') lie in R. The complementary module serves as the building block for defining the different, which measures ramification in the extension.
