The tower formulas express the transitivity of the different and discriminant in a tower of finite separable extensions $K \subset K' \subset K''$ of the quotient field of a Dedekind domain. The different is multiplicative up to extension of ideals, while the discriminant formula involves the norm from the intermediate field and a power of the lower discriminant. These relations are computationally essential for determining ramification in composite extensions.
