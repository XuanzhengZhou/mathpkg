Let $R$ be a Dedekind domain, $K$ its quotient field, $K'$ a finite algebraic separable extension of $K$, and $R'$ the integral closure of $R$ in $K'$.

Suppose $R' = R[y]$ for some $y \in R'$, and let $F(X)$ be the minimal polynomial of $y$ over $K$. Let $F'(X)$ be its formal derivative.

Then the complementary module $\mathfrak{C}$ of $R'$ with respect to $R$ satisfies

$$F'(y) \cdot \mathfrak{C} \subset R'.$$

In particular, $\mathfrak{C} \subset R' \cdot F'(y)^{-1}$.
