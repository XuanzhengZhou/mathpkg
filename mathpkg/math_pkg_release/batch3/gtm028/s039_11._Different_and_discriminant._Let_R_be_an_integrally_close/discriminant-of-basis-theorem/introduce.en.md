Theorem 30 establishes the relationship between the discriminant ideal (the norm of the different) and the discriminants of individual bases. The discriminant ideal is generated by the discriminants of all bases of K' over K that lie in R'. When a basis is a bona fide integral basis (an R-basis of R'), its discriminant generates the discriminant ideal. This provides both a theoretical link between discriminants and differents and a practical tool for computing discriminant ideals.
