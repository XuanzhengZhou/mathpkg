When the integral closure R' of R in K' is monogenic (i.e., of the form R[y]), the different can be computed explicitly as the principal ideal generated by the derivative F'(y) of the minimal polynomial. This is a computationally effective formula: the different measures ramification, and F'(y) vanishes precisely at those prime ideals where the reduction of F modulo the prime has multiple roots, i.e., where ramification occurs.
