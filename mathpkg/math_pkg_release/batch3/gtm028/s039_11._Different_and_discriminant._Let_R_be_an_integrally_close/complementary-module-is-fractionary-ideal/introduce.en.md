Theorem 26 establishes that the complementary module is a fractionary ideal of R'. The proof uses the existence of a K-basis of K' contained in R', and the non-degeneracy of the trace bilinear form in separable extensions. Since the complementary module is a fractionary ideal, its inverse (the different) is also a well-defined fractionary ideal, making the theory of ramification work smoothly.
