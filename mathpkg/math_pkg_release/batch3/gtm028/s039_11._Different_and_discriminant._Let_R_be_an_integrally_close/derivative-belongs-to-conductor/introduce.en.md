Theorem 29 refines the previous result: $F'(y)$ actually belongs to the conductor $\mathfrak{F}$ of the integral closure $R''$ into $R'$. This is stronger because $\mathfrak{F}$ is generally larger than the different $\mathfrak{D}_{R'/R}$ — in fact, $\mathfrak{D}_{R'/R} \cdot R'' \subset R'$, so $\mathfrak{D}_{R'/R} \subset \mathfrak{F}$. When $R$ is a Dedekind domain, we have $R' = R''$ and the result states that the different divides the principal ideal generated by $F'(y)$. This gives a practical way to compute the different: find a primitive element $y$, take $F'(y)$, and factor it into prime ideals.
