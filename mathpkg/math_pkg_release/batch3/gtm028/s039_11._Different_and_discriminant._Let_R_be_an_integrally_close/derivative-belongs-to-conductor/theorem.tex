Let $R$ be an integrally closed ring (not necessarily a Dedekind domain), $K$ its quotient field, $K'$ a finite algebraic separable extension of $K$, and $R'$ an integral extension of $R$ with quotient field $K'$. Let $R''$ be the integral closure of $R$ in $K'$, and let $\mathfrak{F}$ be the conductor of $R''$ into $R'$ (i.e., the set of all $x \in R'$ such that $x R'' \subset R'$).

Suppose $R' = R[y]$ for some $y \in R'$, and let $F(X)$ be the minimal polynomial of $y$ over $K$.

Then $F'(y) \in \mathfrak{F}$, and the different $\mathfrak{D}_{R'/R}$ is contained in $\mathfrak{F}$. In particular, if $R$ is a Dedekind domain (so that $R' = R''$), then $\mathfrak{D}_{R'/R}$ divides the principal ideal $R' F'(y)$.
