Theorem 27 establishes that the differential exponent $m(\mathfrak{P})$ is a local invariant: when we localize at the complement $M$ of $\mathfrak{p}$, the differential exponent of the extended prime ideal $\mathfrak{P}R'_M$ over the local ring $R_M$ remains exactly $m(\mathfrak{P})$. Equivalently, the different $\mathfrak{D}_{R'_M/R_M}$ equals the extended ideal $\mathfrak{D}_{R'/R} R'_M$. This compatibility with localization is fundamental for computing differents via completions and for the local-to-global principle in Dedekind domains.
