The transitivity formulas show how the different and discriminant behave in a tower of field extensions. The different of the full extension factors as the different of the upper extension times the norm of the different of the lower extension. The discriminant formula involves both norms and powers. These formulas allow ramification information to be analyzed step by step through intermediate fields, and are essential for computations in class field theory and the study of higher ramification groups.
