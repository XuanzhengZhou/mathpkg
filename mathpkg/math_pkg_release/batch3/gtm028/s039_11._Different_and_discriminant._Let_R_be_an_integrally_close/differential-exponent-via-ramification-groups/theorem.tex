Let $R$ be a Dedekind domain, $K$ its quotient field, $K'$ a finite Galois extension of $K$ with Galois group $G$, and $R'$ the integral closure of $R$ in $K'$. Let $\mathfrak{P}$ be a prime ideal of $R'$ and $\mathfrak{p} = \mathfrak{P} \cap R$. Assume $R'/\mathfrak{P}$ is separable over $R/\mathfrak{p}$.

Let $G_Z$, $G_T$, $G_{V_i}$ be the decomposition, inertia, and $i$-th ramification groups of $\mathfrak{P}$, with corresponding subfields $K_Z$, $K_T$, $K_i$. Let $n_i = [K' : K_i] = |G_{V_i}|$.

Suppose $R_{K_i}' = R_{K_i}[u]$ for some element $u$, where $R_{K_i}'$ is the integral closure of $R$ in $K_i$. Let $F(X)$ be the minimal polynomial of $u$ over $K_i$. Then the different of $K'$ over $K_i$ is the ideal

$$\mathfrak{D}_{K'/K_i} = R' F'(u) = R' \prod_{s \in G_{V_i}, s \neq 1} (u - s(u)).$$

Hence the differential exponent $m(K', K_i)$ equals

$$m(K', K_i) = \sum_{s \in G_{V_i}, s \neq 1} v_{\mathfrak{P}}(u - s(u)),$$

where $v_{\mathfrak{P}}$ is the normalized $\mathfrak{P}$-adic valuation.
