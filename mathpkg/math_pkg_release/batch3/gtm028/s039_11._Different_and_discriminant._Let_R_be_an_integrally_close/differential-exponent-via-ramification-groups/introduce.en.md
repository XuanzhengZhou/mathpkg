In the Galois case, when the ring of integers is monogenic over each intermediate ramification field ($R'_{K_i} = R_{K_i}[u]$), the different can be expressed directly in terms of the action of the ramification groups on a primitive element $u$. The differential exponent $m(K', K_i)$ is the sum of the $\mathfrak{P}$-adic valuations of $u - s(u)$ over all non-identity elements $s$ of the $i$-th ramification group $G_{V_i}$. This connects the ideal-theoretic notion of the different with the group-theoretic filtration given by the ramification groups.
