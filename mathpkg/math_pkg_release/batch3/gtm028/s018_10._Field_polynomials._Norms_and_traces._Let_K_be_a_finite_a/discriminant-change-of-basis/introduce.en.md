Under a change of basis, the discriminant transforms by the square of the determinant of the transition matrix. This is analogous to how a bilinear form's matrix transforms under a change of basis, with the trace form $T(xy)$ playing the role of the bilinear form. The factor $(\det A)^2$ means the vanishing or non-vanishing of the discriminant is independent of the choice of basis.
