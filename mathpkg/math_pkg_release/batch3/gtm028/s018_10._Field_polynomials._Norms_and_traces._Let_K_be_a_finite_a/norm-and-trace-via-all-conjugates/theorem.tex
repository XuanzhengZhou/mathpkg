Let $K/k$ be a finite algebraic extension of degree $n$, and let $K^\star$ be a normal extension of $k$ containing $K$. Let $\tau_1, \tau_2, \dots, \tau_n$ be the $n$ distinct $k$-isomorphisms of $K$ into $K^\star$ (counting multiplicities via extensions). Set $x_i = x^{\tau_i}$ for $x \in K$. Then the field polynomial of $x$ over $k$ factors as
$$f(X) = \prod_{i=1}^{n} (X - x_i),$$
and consequently
$$T_{K/k}(x) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i, \qquad N_{K/k}(x) = \prod_{i=1}^{n} x_i.$$
If there are $\nu$ isomorphisms leaving $x$ invariant, then $x$ has exactly $m = n/\nu$ distinct conjugates and the minimal polynomial of $x$ has degree $m$.
