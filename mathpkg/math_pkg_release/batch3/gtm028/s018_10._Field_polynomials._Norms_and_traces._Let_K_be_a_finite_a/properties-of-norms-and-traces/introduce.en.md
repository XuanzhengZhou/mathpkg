These five properties characterize the basic algebraic behavior of the norm and trace maps. The norm is multiplicative while the trace is $k$-linear. Both act as expected on elements of the base field $k$: the norm raises to the $n$-th power and the trace multiplies by the degree. These properties follow directly from the matrix definitions.
