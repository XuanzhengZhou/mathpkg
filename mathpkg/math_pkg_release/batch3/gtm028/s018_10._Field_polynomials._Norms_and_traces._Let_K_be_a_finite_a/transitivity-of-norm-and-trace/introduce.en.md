The transitivity law states that norm and trace are functorial with respect to towers of extensions: computing the norm (or trace) from $\Delta$ all the way down to $k$ is the same as first computing it from $\Delta$ to the intermediate field $L$, then from $L$ down to $k$. This generalizes formulas (8) and (9) (where the element lies in the intermediate field) to the case where the element lies in the top field.
