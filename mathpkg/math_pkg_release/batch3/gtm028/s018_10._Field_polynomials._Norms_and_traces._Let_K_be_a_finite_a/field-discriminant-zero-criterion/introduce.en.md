Theorem 21 gives a clean equivalent condition for the discriminant to vanish: the trace map must be identically zero. Since a finite extension is separable if and only if the trace form is nondegenerate, this theorem is equivalent to the statement that $K/k$ is separable if and only if its field discriminant is nonzero. This is one of the most important characterizations of separability.
