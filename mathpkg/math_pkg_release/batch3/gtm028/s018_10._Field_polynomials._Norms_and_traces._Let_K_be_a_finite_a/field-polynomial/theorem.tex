Let $K/k$ be a finite algebraic extension of degree $n$, and let $\omega_1, \omega_2, \dots, \omega_n$ be a basis of $K/k$. For $x \in K$, define the $n \times n$ matrix $A = \|a_{ij}\|$ with entries $a_{ij} \in k$ by
$$x\omega_i = \sum_{j=1}^{n} a_{ij}\omega_j, \quad i = 1, 2, \dots, n.$$
The \textbf{field polynomial} of $x$ relative to $k$ (or over $k$) is the monic polynomial
$$f(X) = \det(X E_n - A),$$
where $E_n$ is the $n \times n$ identity matrix. This polynomial is monic, of degree $n$, has coefficients in $k$, satisfies $f(x) = 0$, and is independent of the choice of basis $\{\omega_1, \dots, \omega_n\}$ of $K/k$.
