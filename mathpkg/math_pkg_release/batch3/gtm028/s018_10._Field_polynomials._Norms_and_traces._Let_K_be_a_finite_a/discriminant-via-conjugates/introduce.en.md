This elegant formula expresses the discriminant as the square of a determinant whose entries are the conjugates of the basis elements. It follows from writing $T(\omega_i \omega_j) = \sum_{\alpha} \omega_i^{(\alpha)} \omega_j^{(\alpha)}$ and recognizing that the matrix $(T(\omega_i \omega_j))$ factors as the product of the conjugate matrix and its transpose. The formula generalizes the familiar Vandermonde expression for the discriminant of a polynomial.
