A proper zero divisor is a non-zero element that can annihilate another non-zero element. This definition distinguishes genuine zero divisors from the trivial case of the zero element. A ring with proper zero divisors fails to have the cancellation property; a ring without proper zero divisors is an integral domain (if also commutative with identity).
