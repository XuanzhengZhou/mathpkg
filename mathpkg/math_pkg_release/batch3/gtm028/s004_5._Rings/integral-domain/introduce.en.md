An integral domain is a commutative ring with identity in which the product of non-zero elements is always non-zero. Integral domains generalize the arithmetic of integers, where the cancellation property holds. They form the foundational setting of commutative algebra and number theory, serving as the natural domain for divisibility arguments, prime factorization, and the construction of fields of fractions.
