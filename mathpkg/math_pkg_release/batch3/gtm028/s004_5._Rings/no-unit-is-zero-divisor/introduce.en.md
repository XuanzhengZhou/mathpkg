This proposition establishes that units and zero divisors are mutually exclusive in any ring with identity. If an element has a multiplicative inverse, it cannot annihilate any non-zero element. This fact underlies the important distinction between invertible elements and zero divisors, and is critical for the theory of localization, where one inverts elements that are not zero divisors.
