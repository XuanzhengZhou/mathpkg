This proposition states that multiplying an element by the additive inverse of another element is the same as taking the additive inverse of their product. It extends the usual arithmetic rule $a \cdot (-c) = -(a \cdot c)$ to all rings, and follows directly from the distributive laws and the fact that $a \cdot 0 = 0$.
