The subgroup criterion provides a simple necessary and sufficient condition for a non-empty subset $H_0$ of a group $G$ to form a subgroup: for any $a, b \in H_0$, the element $ab^{-1}$ must also belong to $H_0$. This single condition elegantly encapsulates closure under the group operation, containment of the identity, and closure under inverses. It is one of the most practical tools for verifying that a given subset is a subgroup.
