In any group $G$, the equations $ax = b$ and $xa = b$ each have exactly one solution for arbitrary $a, b \in G$. This property is a direct consequence of the group axioms: multiplying on the left by $a^{-1}$ gives $x = a^{-1}b$ as the unique solution to $ax = b$, and multiplying on the right by $a^{-1}$ yields $x = ba^{-1}$ for $xa = b$. The solvability of these equations is in fact equivalent to the existence of inverses (axiom G$_3$) in the presence of associativity and non-emptiness.
