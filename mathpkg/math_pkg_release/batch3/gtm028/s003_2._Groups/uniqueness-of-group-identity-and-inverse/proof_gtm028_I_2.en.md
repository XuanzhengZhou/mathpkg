From the group axioms, the identity $e$ satisfies $ea = a$ for all $a \in G$. If $e'$ is another element satisfying $e'a = a$ for all $a$, then in particular $e'e = e$. But also $ee' = e'$ (since $e$ is an identity). By the associative law and the definition of identity, these two properties force $e = e'$. Similarly, if $a'$ and $a''$ are both inverses of $a$ (so that $a'a = e$ and $a''a = e$), then $a' = a'e = a'(aa'') = (a'a)a'' = ea'' = a''$. Thus the inverse is uniquely determined and is denoted by $a^{-1}$.
