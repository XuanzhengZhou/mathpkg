In any group, both the identity element and the inverse of each element are uniquely determined. This follows directly from the group axioms: if $e$ and $e'$ are both identities, then $e = ee' = e'$; if $a'$ and $a''$ are both inverses of $a$, then $a' = a'e = a'(aa'') = (a'a)a'' = ea'' = a''$. The uniqueness justifies the notation $a^{-1}$ for the inverse of $a$.
