Let $e$ be the identity of $G$ and let $e'$ be the identity of the subgroup $H$. Since $e' \in H \subseteq G$, we have $e' \cdot e' = e'$ (as identity in $H$) and also $e' \cdot e = e'$ (since $e$ is the identity in $G$). Hence $e' \cdot e' = e' \cdot e$. By the cancellation law in $G$, we obtain $e' = e$. Thus the identity element of $G$ belongs to $H$ and is also the identity of $H$.
