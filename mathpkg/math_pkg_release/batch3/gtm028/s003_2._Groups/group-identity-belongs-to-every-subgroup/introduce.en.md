If $H$ is a subgroup of a group $G$, then the identity element of $G$ must also lie in $H$ and serve as the identity for $H$. This follows because the identity of $H$, call it $e'$, satisfies $e' \cdot e' = e'$ in $H$, and also $e' \cdot e = e'$ in $G$ (where $e$ is the identity of $G$). By the cancellation law, $e' = e$. This lemma ensures that a subgroup shares the identity element with its ambient group.
