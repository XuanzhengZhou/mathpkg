When an element $a$ has finite order $m$, the order can be characterized as the smallest positive integer such that $a^m = 1$. The $m$ powers $1, a, a^2, \ldots, a^{m-1}$ are all distinct and constitute the entire cyclic subgroup. Moreover, $a^n = 1$ exactly when $m$ divides $n$. This characterization is fundamental for working with finite cyclic groups and for proving divisibility relations involving element orders.
