For any element $a$ in a group $G$, the powers $a^n$ (for non-negative integers $n$) form a subgroup called the cyclic subgroup generated by $a$. This is the smallest subgroup of $G$ containing $a$. If this subgroup is finite, its order is called the order of the element $a$; otherwise, $a$ is said to have infinite order. Cyclic subgroups are the simplest and most fundamental type of subgroup.
