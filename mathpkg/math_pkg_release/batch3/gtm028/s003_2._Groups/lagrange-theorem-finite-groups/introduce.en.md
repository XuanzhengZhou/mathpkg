Lagrange's theorem is one of the fundamental results of finite group theory: the order of any subgroup $H$ of a finite group $G$ must divide the order of $G$. The proof relies on the fact that the cosets of $H$ partition $G$ into disjoint subsets, each having the same cardinality as $H$. The number of cosets, called the index of $H$ in $G$, is therefore $|G|/|H|$. This theorem severely constrains the possible orders of subgroups and elements in a finite group.
