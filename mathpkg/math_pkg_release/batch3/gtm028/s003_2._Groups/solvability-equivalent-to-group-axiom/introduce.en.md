This theorem provides an alternative characterization of groups: in a set with an associative binary operation, the existence of identity and inverses is equivalent to the unique solvability of the equations $ax = b$ and $xa = b$. This equivalence is practically useful because the solvability of equations sometimes follows more directly from the nature of a given binary operation than verifying the existence of identity and inverses explicitly. The theorem thus simplifies the task of proving that a given set forms a group.
