A coset is formed by multiplying all elements of a subgroup $H$ by a fixed element $a$ of the group, either on the right (giving the right coset $Ha$) or on the left (giving the left coset $aH$). Cosets partition the group into disjoint subsets of equal size, and they are the fundamental building blocks for Lagrange's theorem, which states that the order of a subgroup divides the order of a finite group. The number of distinct cosets is called the index of $H$ in $G$.
