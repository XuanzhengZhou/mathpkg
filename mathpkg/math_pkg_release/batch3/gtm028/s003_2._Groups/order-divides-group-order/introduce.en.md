In any finite group $G$, every element has finite order, and moreover the order of each element divides the order of the group. This follows immediately from Lagrange's theorem: the order of an element $a$ is the order of the cyclic subgroup $\langle a \rangle$ generated by $a$, and since $\langle a \rangle$ is a subgroup of $G$, its order must divide $|G|$. This corollary is one of the most frequently used facts in finite group theory.
