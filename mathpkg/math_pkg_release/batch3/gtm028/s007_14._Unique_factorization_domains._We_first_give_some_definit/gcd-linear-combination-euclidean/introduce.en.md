In a euclidean domain, the greatest common divisor of any two non-zero elements can be expressed as a linear combination of those elements (a Bezout identity). The proof selects a non-zero element $d$ of minimal $\varphi$-value in the set of all linear combinations of $a$ and $b$, then shows $d$ must divide both $a$ and $b$. This lemma is the crucial step in proving that euclidean domains satisfy UF3 (every irreducible is prime).
