An element $a$ in a commutative ring $R$ is called irreducible if it is a non-unit whose only divisors are improper — that is, units and associates of $a$. Irreducible elements are the "atoms" of factorization theory, and every non-unit in a unique factorization domain can be expressed as a finite product of irreducibles.
