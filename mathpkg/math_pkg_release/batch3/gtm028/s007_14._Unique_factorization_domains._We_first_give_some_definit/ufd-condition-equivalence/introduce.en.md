The uniqueness condition UF2 in the definition of a UFD can be equivalently replaced by the prime divisor property UF3: every irreducible element is prime, i.e., if an irreducible $p$ divides a product $ab$, then $p$ divides $a$ or $p$ divides $b$. This equivalence is proved by induction on the number of irreducible factors, using the cancellation property in integral domains.
