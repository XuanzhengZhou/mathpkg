The polynomials with coefficients in $R$ form a ring $S$ in which $R$ can be imbedded as a subring via the mapping $a \mapsto (a, 0, 0, \cdots)$. $S$ has an identity if and only if $R$ has an identity; and if that is so, then $(1, 0, 0, \cdots)$ is the identity of $S$, where $1$ is the identity of $R$.

For non-zero polynomials $f, g \in S$, either $fg = 0$ or $\partial(fg) \leq \partial f + \partial g$. Equality $\partial(fg) = \partial f + \partial g$ holds if and only if the product $a_n b_m$ of the leading coefficients of $f$ and $g$ is not zero; in that case $a_n b_m$ is the leading coefficient of $fg$.

If $R$ is an integral domain, then $S$ is also an integral domain, and the units of $S$ arise from the units of $R$ under the embedding $a \mapsto (a, 0, 0, \cdots)$. If $R$ is regarded as a subring of $S$, then the element $1$ of $R$ is also the identity of $S$, and if $R$ is an integral domain, the units of $R$ are the only units of $S$.
