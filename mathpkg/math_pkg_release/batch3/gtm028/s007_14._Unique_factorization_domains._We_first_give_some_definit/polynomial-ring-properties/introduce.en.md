Theorem 6 summarizes the fundamental algebraic properties of the polynomial ring $R[X]$. It states that $R$ embeds as a subring, that $S$ inherits the identity from $R$, and characterizes the degree of a product in terms of the leading coefficients. A key consequence is that when $R$ is an integral domain, so is $R[X]$, and the only units in $R[X]$ are the units of $R$ (constant polynomials).
