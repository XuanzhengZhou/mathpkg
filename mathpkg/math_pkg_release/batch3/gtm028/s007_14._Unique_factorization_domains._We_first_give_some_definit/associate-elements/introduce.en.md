Two elements $a$ and $b$ in a commutative ring $R$ are called associate elements if $a = b\epsilon$ for some unit $\epsilon \in R$. Associates are equivalently characterized by the property that each divides the other. This relation is fundamental for understanding uniqueness of factorization, where factorizations are considered unique only up to order and multiplication by units.
