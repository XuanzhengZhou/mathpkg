In a commutative ring $R$ with identity, an element $b$ is said to divide $a$ if $a = bc$ for some $c \in R$. This is the fundamental relation studied in divisibility theory. The units of $R$ are exactly those elements that divide $1$. This definition sets the stage for the study of factorization, irreducibility, and unique factorization domains.
