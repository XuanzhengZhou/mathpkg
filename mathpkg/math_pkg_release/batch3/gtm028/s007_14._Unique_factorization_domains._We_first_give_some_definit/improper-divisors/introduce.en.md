In a commutative ring $R$, every unit $\epsilon$ divides every element $a$, since $a = \epsilon(\epsilon^{-1}a)$. Together with the associates of $a$, the units of $R$ are called the improper divisors of $a$. This terminology distinguishes trivial divisors from proper, non-trivial ones, and is essential for defining irreducible elements.
