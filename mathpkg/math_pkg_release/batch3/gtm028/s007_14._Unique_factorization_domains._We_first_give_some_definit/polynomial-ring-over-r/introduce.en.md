The polynomial ring $R[X]$ over a commutative ring $R$ is formally constructed as the set of all finite sequences of elements of $R$, with addition defined componentwise and multiplication given by the Cauchy product (convolution). The zero element is the all-zero sequence, and the ring $R$ can be embedded as a subring via the map $a \mapsto \{a, 0, 0, \cdots\}$. This construction makes the notion of "polynomial" rigorous without reference to an indeterminate variable.
