If $f = \{a_i\}$ is a non-zero polynomial (that is, not all $a_i$ are zero) and if $n$ is the greatest integer such that $a_n \neq 0$ ($n \geq 0$), then $n$ is called the \text{degree} of $f$, denoted by $\partial f$. The coefficient $a_n$ is called the \text{leading coefficient} of $f$. No degree is assigned to the zero polynomial.
