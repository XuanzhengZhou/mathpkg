Assume $R$ has an identity $1$. Denote by $X$ the polynomial $(0, 1, 0, \cdots)$. For $a \in R$ and a non-negative integer $m$, we have $aX^m = \{c_i\}$ where $c_m = a$ and $c_i = 0$ for $i \neq m$.

Every polynomial $f = \{a_0, a_1, a_2, \cdots\}$ can then be written uniquely as a finite sum:
$$f = a_0 + a_1 X + a_2 X^2 + \cdots + a_n X^n.$$

The ring $S$ will be denoted by $R[X]$, and $X$ is called an \text{indeterminate}.
