A unique factorization domain (UFD) is an integral domain in which every non-unit element can be expressed as a finite product of irreducible elements, and this factorization is unique up to the order of factors and multiplication by units. The ring of integers $\mathbb{Z}$ and polynomial rings over a field $F[X]$ are classical examples of UFDs. The concept generalizes the fundamental theorem of arithmetic to arbitrary integral domains.
