An integral domain $R$ is a \text{UNIQUE FACTORIZATION DOMAIN} (or briefly, a \text{UFD}) if it satisfies the following conditions:

\text{UF1.} Every non-unit of $R$ is a finite product of irreducible factors.

\text{UF2.} The foregoing factorization is unique to within order and unit factors; that is, if $\prod_{i=1}^{m} p_i = \prod_{j=1}^{n} p'_j$ where the $p_i$ and $p'_j$ are irreducible, then $m = n$ and by a suitable ordering $p_i$ and $p'_i$ are associates $(i = 1, 2, \cdots, m)$.
