In a unique factorization domain, any two elements possess a greatest common divisor (GCD), which can be constructed from their irreducible factorizations by taking the minimum exponent for each irreducible factor. This property generalizes the familiar GCD from the integers to all UFDs, and it follows directly from the existence and uniqueness of factorization into irreducibles.
