Theorem 14 is a key intermediate result in the proof of the classical characterization of Dedekind domains (Theorem 13). It states that in an integrally closed Noetherian domain, every associated prime of a nonzero principal ideal is a minimal prime ideal. The proof uses localization to reduce to the case where the prime ideal is maximal, and then applies Lemma 6 to establish invertibility and subsequently minimality.
