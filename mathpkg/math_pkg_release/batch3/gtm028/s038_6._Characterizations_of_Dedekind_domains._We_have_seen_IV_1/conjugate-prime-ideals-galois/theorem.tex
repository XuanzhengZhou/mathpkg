Let $R$ be a Dedekind domain with quotient field $K$, let $L$ be a finite normal extension of $K$ with Galois group $G$, and let $R'$ be the integral closure of $R$ in $L$. Let $\mathfrak{p}$ be a proper prime ideal of $R$.

Then the prime ideals $\mathfrak{P}$ of $R'$ lying over $\mathfrak{p}$ form a complete set of conjugate ideals under $G$. All these prime ideals have the same ramification index $e$ and the same relative degree $f$. Denoting by $g$ the number of these prime ideals, we have
$$e f g \leq n = [L:K].$$

If $L$ is a separable extension of $K$, then $e f g = n$.
