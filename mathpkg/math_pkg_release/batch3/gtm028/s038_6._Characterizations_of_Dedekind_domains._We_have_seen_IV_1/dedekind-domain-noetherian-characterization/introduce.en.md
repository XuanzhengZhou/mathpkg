Theorem 13 gives the classical characterization of Dedekind domains: a domain is Dedekind if and only if it is Noetherian, integrally closed, and of Krull dimension one (every nonzero prime ideal is maximal). The necessity of (1) and (2) follows from Theorems 11 and 10 respectively. For (3), integral closedness is proved using the valuation functions $v_{\mathfrak{p}}$: if $x \in K$ is integral over $R$, the condition $v_{\mathfrak{p}}(d) + n v_{\mathfrak{p}}(x) \geq 0$ for all $n$ forces $v_{\mathfrak{p}}(x) \geq 0$, hence $x \in R$.
