If $\mathfrak{a} \cdot \mathfrak{a}' = R$, we have $\mathfrak{a}' \subset (R:\mathfrak{a})$ by definition of the conductor. On the other hand $\mathfrak{a} \cdot (R:\mathfrak{a}) \subset R$, whence, if $\mathfrak{a}'$ is an inverse of $\mathfrak{a}$, we have

$$(R:\mathfrak{a}) = \mathfrak{a}' \cdot \mathfrak{a} \cdot (R:\mathfrak{a}) \subset \mathfrak{a}' \cdot R = \mathfrak{a}'.$$

Thus $(R:\mathfrak{a}) = \mathfrak{a}'$, proving both uniqueness and the explicit form of the inverse. Q.E.D.
