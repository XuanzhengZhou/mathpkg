If $\mathfrak{a}$ is an invertible fractionary ideal, then $\mathfrak{a}$ has a unique inverse, and this inverse is equal to $R:\mathfrak{a} = \{x \in K : x\mathfrak{a} \subset R\}$. Hence a necessary and sufficient condition for $\mathfrak{a}$ to be invertible is that

$$\mathfrak{a} \cdot (R:\mathfrak{a}) = R.$$
