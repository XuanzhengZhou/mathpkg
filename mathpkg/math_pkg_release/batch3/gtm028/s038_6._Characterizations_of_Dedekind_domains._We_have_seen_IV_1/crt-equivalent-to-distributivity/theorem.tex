Given an arbitrary ring $R$, the Chinese Remainder Theorem property (CRT) is equivalent to the distributivity of each of the operations $+$ and $\cap$ with respect to the other in the set of all ideals of $R$.

Concretely, the following two distributive laws are equivalent to (CRT):
$$(D_1) \quad \mathfrak{a} + (\mathfrak{b} \cap \mathfrak{b}') = (\mathfrak{a} + \mathfrak{b}) \cap (\mathfrak{a} + \mathfrak{b}'),$$
$$(D_2) \quad \mathfrak{a} \cap (\mathfrak{b} + \mathfrak{b}') = (\mathfrak{a} \cap \mathfrak{b}) + (\mathfrak{a} \cap \mathfrak{b}').$$
