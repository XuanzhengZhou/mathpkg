Let $L = \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{-1})$, and let $R' = \mathbb{Z} + i\mathbb{Z}$ be the ring of Gaussian integers. For a rational prime $p$, the factorization of $R'p$ into prime ideals is classified by the residue of $p$ modulo $4$:
\begin{itemize}
\item $R'p = \mathfrak{P}_1 \mathfrak{P}_2$ with $R'/\mathfrak{P}_1 \cong R'/\mathfrak{P}_2 \cong \mathbb{Z}/(p)$ (decomposed), if $p \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$;
\item $R'p = \mathfrak{P}^2$ with $R'/\mathfrak{P} \cong \mathbb{Z}/(p)$ (ramified), if $p = 2$;
\item $R'p = \mathfrak{P}$ with $[R'/\mathfrak{P} : \mathbb{Z}/(p)] = 2$ (inertial), if $p \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$.
\end{itemize}
Moreover, if $p \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$, then $p = a^2 + b^2$ for some rational integers $a, b$.
