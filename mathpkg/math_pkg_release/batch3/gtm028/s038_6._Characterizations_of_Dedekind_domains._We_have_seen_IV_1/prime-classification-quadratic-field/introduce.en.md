In the ring of Gaussian integers $\mathbb{Z}[i]$, the integral closure of $\mathbb{Z}$ in $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{-1})$, the factorization of a rational prime $p$ is governed by the residue of $p$ modulo 4. This yields the classical theorem that primes congruent to 1 mod 4 are sums of two squares. The three cases—decomposed, ramified, and inertial—exemplify the general decomposition theory of prime ideals in extensions of Dedekind domains.
