Theorem 16 states that a semilocal Dedekind domain (one with only finitely many maximal ideals) is a principal ideal domain. The proof shows that each prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}_i$ is principal by finding an element in $\mathfrak{p}_i$ not in $\mathfrak{p}_i^2$ and not in any other $\mathfrak{p}_j$, using the Chinese Remainder Theorem. Since every ideal factors uniquely into prime powers, principality of primes implies principality of all ideals.
