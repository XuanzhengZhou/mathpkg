This lemma shows that the invertibility of all nonzero integral ideals implies that every fractionary ideal is also invertible, making the set of all fractionary ideals a multiplicative group. The proof writes an arbitrary fractionary ideal as $(1/d)\mathfrak{b}$ with $\mathfrak{b}$ integral, and constructs its inverse as $d \mathfrak{b}^{-1}$.
