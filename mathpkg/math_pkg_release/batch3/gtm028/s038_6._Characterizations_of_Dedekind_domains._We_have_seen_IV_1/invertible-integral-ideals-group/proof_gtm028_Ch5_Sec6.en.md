Every fractionary ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ may be written in the form $(1/d)\mathfrak{b}$, where $\mathfrak{b}$ is an integral ideal and $d$ is a non-zero element of $R$. If $\mathfrak{b}$ has an inverse $\mathfrak{b}^{-1}$, then $\mathfrak{a}$ admits $d \mathfrak{b}^{-1}$ as an inverse. Since multiplication of ideals is associative, and since every element of $\mathcal{I}$ admits an inverse, $\mathcal{I}$ is a group.
