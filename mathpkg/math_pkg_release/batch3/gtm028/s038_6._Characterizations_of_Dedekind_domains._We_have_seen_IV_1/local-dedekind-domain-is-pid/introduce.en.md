Theorem 15 shows that a local Dedekind domain (one with a unique nonzero prime ideal) is necessarily a principal ideal domain. Every proper ideal is a power of the maximal ideal, and the maximal ideal is principal. Such rings are precisely the discrete valuation rings. The proof uses the invertibility of the maximal ideal to produce elements $m \in \mathfrak{m}$ and $m' \in R:\mathfrak{m}$ with $mm' = 1$, from which $\mathfrak{m} = Rm$ follows.
