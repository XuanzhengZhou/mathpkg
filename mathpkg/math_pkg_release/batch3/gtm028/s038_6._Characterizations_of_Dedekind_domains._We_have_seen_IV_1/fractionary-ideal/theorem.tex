Given an integral domain $R$ and its quotient field $K$, a sub-$R$-module $\mathfrak{b}$ of $K$ is said to be a \textit{fractionary ideal} of $R$ if the elements of $\mathfrak{b}$ admit a common denominator $d \neq 0$ in $R$ — more precisely, if there exists $d \neq 0$ in $R$ such that

$$\mathfrak{b} \subset \frac{1}{d}R.$$

Then we have $\mathfrak{b} = \frac{1}{d}\mathfrak{a}$ where $\mathfrak{a}$ is an ordinary ideal in $R$.
