A fractionary ideal is a generalization of an ordinary (integral) ideal in an integral domain. While ordinary ideals are subsets of the ring $R$, fractionary ideals are $R$-submodules of the quotient field $K$ whose elements share a common denominator in $R$. This concept is fundamental for the study of Dedekind domains, where the set of all fractionary ideals forms a group under multiplication, allowing unique factorization of ideals into prime ideal powers.
