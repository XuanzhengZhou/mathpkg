Theorem 10 is the foundational structural result for Dedekind domains: every nonzero proper prime ideal is both invertible and maximal (i.e., the Krull dimension is 1). The proof first shows that any invertible proper prime ideal is maximal by reducing modulo the prime ideal and using Lemma 4 on the invertibility of prime factors, then establishes that all proper prime ideals are invertible using the Dedekind domain factorization property.
