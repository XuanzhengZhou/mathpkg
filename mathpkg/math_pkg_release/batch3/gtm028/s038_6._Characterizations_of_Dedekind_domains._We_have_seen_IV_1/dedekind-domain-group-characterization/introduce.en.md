Theorem 12 provides an elegant algebraic characterization of Dedekind domains: an integral domain is Dedekind if and only if every nonzero ideal is invertible, i.e., the fractionary ideals form a group. Necessity follows from Theorem 11. Sufficiency relies on Lemma 3 (invertible ideals are finitely generated, hence Noetherian) and an indirect argument showing that the invertibility of all ideals forces every proper prime ideal to be maximal and every proper ideal to factor into prime ideals.
