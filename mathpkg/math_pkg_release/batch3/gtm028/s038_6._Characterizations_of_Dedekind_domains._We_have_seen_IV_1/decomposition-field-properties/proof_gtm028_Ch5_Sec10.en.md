By definition of $G_Z$, the conjugate prime ideals of $\mathfrak{P}$ over $K_Z$ consist of $\mathfrak{P}$ only. Thus, by Theorem 22, $\mathfrak{P}$ is the only prime ideal of $R'$ lying over $\mathfrak{P}_Z$. (Note that $R_Z$ is integrally closed and that consequently Theorem 22 is applicable to the pair of rings $R_Z, R'$.) Therefore $R'\mathfrak{P}_Z$ is a power $\mathfrak{P}^{e(Z)}$ of $\mathfrak{P}$. The relative degree $f(Z)$ of $\mathfrak{P}$ over $\mathfrak{P}_Z$ satisfies $e(Z)f(Z) = [L:K_Z]$. By comparison with $efg = n$ and $[K_Z:K] = g$, we obtain $e(Z) = e$ and $f(Z) = f$.

If $G_Z$ is normal in $G$, then $K_Z/K$ is Galois. By the transitivity of the Galois action on primes lying over $\mathfrak{p}$ (Theorem 22), the factorization of $R_Z\mathfrak{p}$ consists of $g$ distinct conjugate prime factors, each appearing with exponent 1 and relative degree 1 (since the decomposition group over $K_Z$ is trivial for each of these primes).
