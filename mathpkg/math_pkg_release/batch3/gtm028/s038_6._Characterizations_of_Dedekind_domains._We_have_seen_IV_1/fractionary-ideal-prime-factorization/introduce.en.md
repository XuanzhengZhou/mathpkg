Theorem 11 is the central result of the theory of Dedekind domains. It states that every nonzero fractionary ideal factors uniquely as a product of prime ideal powers with integer exponents. This generalizes the unique factorization of integers into prime powers to the ideal-theoretic setting. The exponent functions $n_{\mathfrak{p}}$ behave like valuations, satisfying familiar formulas for sum, intersection, product, and quotient of ideals. The theorem also shows that the set of fractionary ideals is a lattice-ordered abelian group.
