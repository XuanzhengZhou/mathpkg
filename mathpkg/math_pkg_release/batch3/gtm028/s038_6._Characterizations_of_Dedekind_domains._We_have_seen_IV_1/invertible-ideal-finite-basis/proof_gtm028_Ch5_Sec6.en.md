Since $\mathfrak{a} \cdot \mathfrak{a}^{-1} = R$, there exist two finite families $\{x_i\}, \{x_i'\}$ ($i = 1, \cdots, n$) of elements of $\mathfrak{a}$ and $\mathfrak{a}^{-1}$ respectively such that $\sum_i x_i x_i' = 1$. For any $x \in \mathfrak{a}$, we have $x = \sum_i (x x_i') x_i$, and since $x x_i' \in R$, the $x_i$ form a finite basis of $\mathfrak{a}$ as an $R$-module.
