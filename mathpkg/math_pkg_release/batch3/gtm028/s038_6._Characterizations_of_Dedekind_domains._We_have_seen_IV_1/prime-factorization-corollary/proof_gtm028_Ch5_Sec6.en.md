This follows immediately from Theorem 10 and from Lemma 5. Theorem 10 ensures that every proper prime ideal in a Dedekind domain is invertible, and the definition of Dedekind domain ensures every proper integral ideal factors as a product of prime ideals. Lemma 5 then guarantees the uniqueness of this factorization.
