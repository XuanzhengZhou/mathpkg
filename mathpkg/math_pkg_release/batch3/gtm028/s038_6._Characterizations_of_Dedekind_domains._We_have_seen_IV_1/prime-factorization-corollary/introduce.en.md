This corollary follows immediately from Theorem 10 and Lemma 5: since every proper prime ideal is invertible (by Theorem 10) and the prime factorization of invertible ideals is unique (by Lemma 5), every proper integral ideal in a Dedekind domain admits a unique factorization into prime ideals.
