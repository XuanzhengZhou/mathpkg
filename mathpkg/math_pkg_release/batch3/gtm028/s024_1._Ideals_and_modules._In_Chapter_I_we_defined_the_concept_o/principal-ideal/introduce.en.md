A principal ideal is the simplest type of ideal, generated by a single element of the ring. In a ring with identity, the principal ideal $Ra$ consists of all multiples of $a$ by ring elements. When the ring lacks an identity, the notation $(a)$ is used for the smallest ideal containing $a$, which must also include integer multiples $na$ to ensure additive closure.
