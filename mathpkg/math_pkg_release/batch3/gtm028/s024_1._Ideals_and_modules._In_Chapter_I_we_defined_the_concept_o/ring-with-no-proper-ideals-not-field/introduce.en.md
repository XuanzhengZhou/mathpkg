This theorem classifies the exceptional case where a ring has trivial ideal structure yet fails to be a field. The only such rings are those with prime-order cyclic additive groups and trivial (zero) multiplication. These pathological rings are important as counterexamples showing that the converse of "fields have only two ideals" requires the presence of a multiplicative identity.
