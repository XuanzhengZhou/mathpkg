If a ring has no proper ideals but is not a field, then its additive group must be cyclic of prime order, and the multiplication is identically zero. This shows that the "trivial multiplication" example (an abelian group with $ab=0$) is essentially the only counterexample to the converse of "a field has exactly two ideals."
