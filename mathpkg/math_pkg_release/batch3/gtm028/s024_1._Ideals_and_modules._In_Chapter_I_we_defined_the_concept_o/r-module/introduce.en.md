An $R$-module generalizes the notion of a vector space by allowing the scalars to come from an arbitrary commutative ring rather than a field. The definition requires $M$ to be a commutative group equipped with a scalar multiplication $R \times M \to M$ satisfying distributivity and associativity axioms. Every ideal of $R$ is an $R$-module, and vector spaces are precisely unitary modules over fields.
