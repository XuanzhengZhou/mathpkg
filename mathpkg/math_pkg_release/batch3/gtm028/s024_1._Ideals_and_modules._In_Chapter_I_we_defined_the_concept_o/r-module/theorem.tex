Let $R$ be a ring. A set $M$ is called an $R$-module if:

(a) $M$ is a commutative group (written additively).

(b) With every ordered pair $(a, x)$ where $a \in R$ and $x \in M$, there is associated a unique element of $M$, denoted by $ax$, such that:

(1) $a(x + y) = ax + ay$

(2) $(a + b)x = ax + bx$

(3) $(ab)x = a(bx)$

for all $a, b \in R$ and $x, y \in M$.
