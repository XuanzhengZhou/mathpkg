Each ring element induces an additive group endomorphism on any module via scalar multiplication. This perspective connects module theory to the theory of operator groups and explains why modules are sometimes called "modules with operators." The distributivity axiom $a(x+y) = ax + ay$ is precisely the statement that scalar multiplication by $a$ is a group homomorphism.
