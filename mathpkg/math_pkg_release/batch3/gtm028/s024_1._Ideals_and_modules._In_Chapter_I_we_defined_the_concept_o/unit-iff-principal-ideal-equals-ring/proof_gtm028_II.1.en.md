If $a$ is a unit, then $a^{-1} \in R$ and for any $b \in R$, $b = (ba^{-1})a \in Ra$, so $R \subseteq Ra$, hence $Ra = R$. Conversely, if $Ra = R$, then $1 \in Ra$, so $1 = xa$ for some $x \in R$, which means $a$ is a unit with inverse $x$.
