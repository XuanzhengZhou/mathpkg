If $\mathfrak{A}$ contains a unit $u$, then $u^{-1} \in R$. For any $b \in R$, we have $b = b u^{-1} u$. Since $b u^{-1} \in R$ and $u \in \mathfrak{A}$, by condition (b) of the definition of an ideal, $(b u^{-1}) u = b \in \mathfrak{A}$. Thus $R \subseteq \mathfrak{A}$, and since $\mathfrak{A} \subseteq R$ by definition, $\mathfrak{A} = R$.
