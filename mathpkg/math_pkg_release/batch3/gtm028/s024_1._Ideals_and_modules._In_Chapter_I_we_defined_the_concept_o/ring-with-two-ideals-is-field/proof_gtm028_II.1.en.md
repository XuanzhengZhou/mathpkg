Let $R$ have an identity and exactly two ideals. The two ideals must be $(0)$ and $R$ itself. For any $a \in R$ with $a \neq 0$, the principal ideal $Ra$ is nonzero, so $Ra \neq (0)$. Since $R$ has only two ideals, $Ra$ must equal $R$. Hence $1 \in Ra$, so $1 = xa$ for some $x \in R$, which means $a$ is a unit. Since every nonzero element is a unit, $R$ is a field.
