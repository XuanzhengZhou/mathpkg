A module over a ring $R$ generalizes the concept of a vector space by replacing the field of scalars with an arbitrary ring. Just as vector spaces are modules over fields, abelian groups can be viewed as modules over the integers. The axioms require the ring action to be compatible with the additive group structure through distributivity and associativity conditions.
