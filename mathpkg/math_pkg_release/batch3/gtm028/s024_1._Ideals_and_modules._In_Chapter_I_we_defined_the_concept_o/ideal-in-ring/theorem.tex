Let $R$ be a ring. An ideal in $R$ is a non-empty subset $\mathfrak{A}$ of $R$ such that

(a) If $a_1, a_2 \in \mathfrak{A}$, then $a_1 - a_2 \in \mathfrak{A}$.

(b) If $a \in \mathfrak{A}$ and $b \in R$, then $ab \in \mathfrak{A}$.

Condition (a) states that $\mathfrak{A}$ is a subgroup of the additive group of $R$. Condition (b), taken together with (a), implies that $\mathfrak{A}$ is a subring of $R$.
