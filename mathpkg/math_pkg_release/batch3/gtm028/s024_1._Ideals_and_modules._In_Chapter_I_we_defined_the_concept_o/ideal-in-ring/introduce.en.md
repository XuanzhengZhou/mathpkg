An ideal is a special kind of subring that is closed under multiplication by arbitrary ring elements, not just elements from within itself. This absorption property makes ideals the natural kernels of ring homomorphisms and the fundamental building blocks for constructing quotient rings. The concept generalizes the notion of multiples in the integers: the set of all even integers forms an ideal in $\mathbb{Z}$ because any integer times an even number remains even.
