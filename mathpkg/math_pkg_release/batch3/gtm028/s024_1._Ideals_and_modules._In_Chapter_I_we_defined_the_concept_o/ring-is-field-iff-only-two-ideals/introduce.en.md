This theorem gives a purely ideal-theoretic characterization of fields: a commutative ring with identity is a field exactly when its only ideals are the trivial ones. This provides a bridge between the theory of fields and the theory of ideals, and explains why fields have such simple ideal structure -- there simply is not enough room for nontrivial ideals when every nonzero element is invertible.
