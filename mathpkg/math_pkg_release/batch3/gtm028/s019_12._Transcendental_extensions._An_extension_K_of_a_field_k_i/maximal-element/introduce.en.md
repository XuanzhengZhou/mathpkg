A maximal element $a_0$ of a partially ordered set $S$ is an element that is not strictly less than any other element; if some $a \in S$ satisfies $a_0 \prec a$, then necessarily $a = a_0$. Zorn's Lemma guarantees the existence of maximal elements in any inductive partially ordered set, a powerful non-constructive existence principle used throughout algebra to prove the existence of bases, maximal ideals, and transcendence bases.
