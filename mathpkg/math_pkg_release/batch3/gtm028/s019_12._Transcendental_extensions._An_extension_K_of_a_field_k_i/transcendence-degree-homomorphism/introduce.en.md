Theorem 28 states that if $R'$ is a $k$-homomorphic image of an integral domain $R$ (both containing $k$), then the transcendence degree does not increase: $\operatorname{tr.d.} R'/k \leq \operatorname{tr.d.} R/k$. The proof lifts a transcendence basis of $R'/k$ back to $R$ via the homomorphism, showing that the preimage set is algebraically independent and can be extended to a transcendence basis of $R/k$ using Theorem 23.
