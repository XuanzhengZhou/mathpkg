The transcendence degree $\operatorname{tr.d.} K/k$ is the cardinality of any transcendence basis of $K/k$. By Theorem 24, this is well-defined independent of the choice of basis. It measures the "size" of the transcendental part of the extension: $K/k$ is algebraic if and only if $\operatorname{tr.d.} K/k = 0$. The transcendence degree is additive in towers (Theorem 26) and is a fundamental invariant in algebraic geometry, where it equals the dimension of the corresponding algebraic variety.
