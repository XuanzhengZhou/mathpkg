Theorem 29 states that a surjective $k$-homomorphism $\tau: R \to R'$ between integral domains of equal finite transcendence degree $n$ over $k$ is necessarily an isomorphism (injective). The proof uses the notation of Theorem 28: a transcendence basis $L'$ of $R'/k$ lifts to a transcendence set $L \subset R$ of the same cardinality $n$. Since $\operatorname{tr.d.} R/k = n$, $L$ must already be a transcendence basis of $R/k$, from which injectivity of $\tau$ follows. If $R$ is a field, the statement holds trivially without any condition on transcendence degrees.
