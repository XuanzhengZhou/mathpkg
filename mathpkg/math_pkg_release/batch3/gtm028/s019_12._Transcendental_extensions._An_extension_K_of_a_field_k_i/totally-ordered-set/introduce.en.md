A totally ordered subset (also called a chain) of a partially ordered set is a subset in which every pair of elements is comparable under the order relation. This concept is essential for the definition of an inductive set: a poset is inductive if every totally ordered subset (chain) has an upper bound, which is precisely the hypothesis of Zorn's Lemma.
