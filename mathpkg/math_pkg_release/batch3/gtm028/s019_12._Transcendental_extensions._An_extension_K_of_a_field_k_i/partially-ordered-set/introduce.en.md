A partially ordered set (poset) is a set equipped with a binary relation $\prec$ that is reflexive, antisymmetric, and transitive. The relation need not be defined for all pairs of elements, distinguishing it from a total order. This general notion of order is a prerequisite for stating Zorn's Lemma, which is used in Section 12 to prove the existence of transcendence bases and the uniqueness of their cardinality.
