We apply Corollary 2 to the case $S = K$. Since $K$ is an algebraic extension of $k(K) = K$ (every element is algebraic over itself), $S = K$ satisfies the hypothesis of Corollary 2. Therefore $K$ contains a transcendence basis of $K/k$.
