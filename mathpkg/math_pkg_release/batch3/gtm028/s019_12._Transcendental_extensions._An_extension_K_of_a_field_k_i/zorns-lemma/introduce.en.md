Zorn's Lemma states that every inductive (chain-complete) partially ordered set possesses at least one maximal element. Equivalent to the Axiom of Choice, it is a fundamental non-constructive existence principle in algebra. In Section 12, Zorn's Lemma is applied to the set of transcendence sets partially ordered by inclusion, which is inductive because the union of a chain of transcendence sets remains a transcendence set. This yields the existence of maximal transcendence sets -- i.e., transcendence bases.
