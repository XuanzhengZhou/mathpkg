A transcendental extension is a field extension that is not algebraic, meaning the larger field contains at least one element that is transcendental over the base field. The prototypical example is the field of rational functions $k(X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n)$ in $n$ indeterminates over $k$, or any $k$-isomorphic image thereof. This definition sets the stage for the study of transcendence bases and transcendence degree.
