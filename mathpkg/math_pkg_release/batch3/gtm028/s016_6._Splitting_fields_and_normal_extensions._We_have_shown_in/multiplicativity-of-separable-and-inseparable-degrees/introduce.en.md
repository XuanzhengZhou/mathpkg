Both the separable degree and the inseparable degree are multiplicative in towers of finite algebraic extensions. This is analogous to the multiplicativity of the ordinary degree $[\Delta:k] = [\Delta:L][L:k]$. The formula for the separable degree follows from Theorem 16 by counting $k$-isomorphisms into a normal extension; the inseparable degree formula then follows from the relation $[K:k] = [K:k]_s \cdot [K:k]_i$.
