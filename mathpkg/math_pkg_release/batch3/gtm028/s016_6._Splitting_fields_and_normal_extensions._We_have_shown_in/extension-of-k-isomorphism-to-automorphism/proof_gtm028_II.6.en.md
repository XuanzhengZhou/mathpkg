Apply the corollary of Theorem 12, taking for $k'$, $k$, and $k_0$ the fields $K$, $L$, and $k$ respectively. Since $K$ is a finite normal extension of $k$, $K$ is a splitting field over $L$ (as well as over $k$) of some polynomial. The given $k$-isomorphism of $L$ into $K$ can then be extended to a $k$-isomorphism of $K$ onto $K$, which is a $k$-automorphism of $K$.
