Let $k$, $\bar{k}$ and $\tau$ have the same meaning as in the preceding lemma, and let $f(X)$ be an arbitrary monic polynomial in $k[X]$, of degree $n$. Let $\bar{f}(X) = [f(X)]\tau$ be the corresponding polynomial in $\bar{k}[X]$ and let $k'$, $\bar{k}'$ be splitting fields over $k$ and $\bar{k}$ of $f(X)$ and $\bar{f}(X)$ respectively. Then the isomorphism $\tau$ can be extended to an isomorphism $\rho$ of $k'$ onto $\bar{k}'$, and any such extension $\rho$ sends each root of $f(X)$ in $k'$ into a root of $\bar{f}(X)$ in $\bar{k}'$ (and similarly $\rho^{-1}$ sends each root of $\bar{f}(X)$ in $\bar{k}'$ into a root of $f(X)$ in $k'$).
