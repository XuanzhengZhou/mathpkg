Let $K = k(\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n)$ and let $f_i(X)$ be the minimal polynomial of $\alpha_i$ over $k$. Set $f(X) = \prod_{i=1}^n f_i(X)$ and let $K'$ be a splitting field of $f(X)$ over $k$ containing $K$. By Theorem 13, $K'$ is a finite normal extension of $k$. If $K''$ is any normal extension of $k$ containing $K$, then each $f_i(X)$ has a root in $K''$ and hence factors completely in $K''[X]$, so $K''$ contains a splitting field of $f(X)$ over $k$. Therefore the splitting field of $f(X)$ over $k$ contained in $K''$ is $K$-isomorphic to $K'$. Thus $K'$ is a least normal extension, and any two such are $K$-isomorphic by Theorem 12.
