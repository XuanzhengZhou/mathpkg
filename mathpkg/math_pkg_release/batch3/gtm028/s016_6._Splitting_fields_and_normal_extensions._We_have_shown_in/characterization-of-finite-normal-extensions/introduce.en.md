A finite extension is normal precisely when every $k$-embedding of it into any larger field actually maps it onto itself. This provides an alternative, functional characterization of normality: the extension is "closed" under $k$-isomorphisms into any ambient field. The forward direction is immediate from the definition; the converse uses the existence of a least normal extension.
