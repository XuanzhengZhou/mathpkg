Given an isomorphism between two fields and irreducible polynomials that correspond under this isomorphism, one can lift the isomorphism to the simple algebraic extensions obtained by adjoining roots of these polynomials. The extended isomorphism sends the chosen root of the first polynomial to the chosen root of the second, and this extension is uniquely determined. When the base fields coincide and the isomorphism is the identity, this lemma reduces to the standard uniqueness theorem for simple algebraic extensions (Theorem 3 of §2).
