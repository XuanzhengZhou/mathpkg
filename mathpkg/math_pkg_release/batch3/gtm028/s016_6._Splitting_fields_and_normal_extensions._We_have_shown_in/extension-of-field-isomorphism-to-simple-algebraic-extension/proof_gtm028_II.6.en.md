If $k = \bar{k}$ and $\tau$ is the identity, then the lemma coincides with Theorem 3 of §2. In the general case, the proof of the lemma is similar to the proof of Theorem 3 and may be left to the reader. The isomorphism $\tau$ extends coefficient-wise to the polynomial rings and then to the quotient fields $k[X]/(\varphi(X)) \cong k(x)$ and $\bar{k}[X]/(\bar{\varphi}(X)) \cong \bar{k}(\bar{x})$, yielding the unique extension $\rho$ with $x\rho = \bar{x}$.
