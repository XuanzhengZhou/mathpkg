The number of distinct $k$-isomorphisms of a finite algebraic extension into a normal extension is precisely its separable degree. This fundamental result connects the structural notion of separability (measured by the separable degree) with the Galois-theoretic notion of embeddings. The proof proceeds by induction on the separable degree and uses Lemma 2 on isomorphism extensions in towers.
