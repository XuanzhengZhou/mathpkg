A normal extension is an algebraic field extension that contains all conjugates of each of its elements. Equivalently, every irreducible polynomial over the base field that has at least one root in the extension must split completely into linear factors over the extension. This property is fundamental to Galois theory, where normal extensions together with separable extensions define Galois extensions.
