This theorem provides the converse to Corollary 1 of Definition 2: not only are finite normal extensions splitting fields, but every splitting field of a polynomial is automatically a finite normal extension. The proof uses the uniqueness theorem on splitting fields (Theorem 12) to show that any irreducible polynomial with a root in the splitting field must split completely there.
