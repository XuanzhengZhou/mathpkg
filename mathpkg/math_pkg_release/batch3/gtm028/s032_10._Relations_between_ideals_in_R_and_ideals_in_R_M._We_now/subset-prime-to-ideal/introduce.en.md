A subset $E$ of a ring $R$ is prime to an ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ if every element of $E$ is individually prime to $\mathfrak{a}$. For ideals that are finite intersections of primary ideals, this is equivalent to $E$ being disjoint from the set of associated primes of $\mathfrak{a}$. This notion is particularly important when $E$ is a multiplicative system $M$: the condition $M \cap \mathfrak{a} = \emptyset$ is closely related to the extension $\mathfrak{a}^e$ being a proper ideal in the quotient ring $R_M$.
