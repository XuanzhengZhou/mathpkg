A subset $E$ of a ring $R$ is said to be \emph{prime to an ideal} $\mathfrak{a}$ if each one of its elements is prime to $\mathfrak{a}$.

When $\mathfrak{a}$ is a finite intersection of primary ideals, a subset $E$ of $R$ is prime to $\mathfrak{a}$ if and only if it is disjoint from the union of the associated prime ideals of $\mathfrak{a}$.
