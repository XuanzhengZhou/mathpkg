We notice that $\mathfrak{a}^e = R_M$ is equivalent to $1 \in \mathfrak{a}^{ec}$. By Theorem 15(a), $1 \in \mathfrak{a}^{ec}$ if and only if there exists $m \in M$ such that $1 \cdot m \in \mathfrak{a}$, i.e., $m \in \mathfrak{a} \cap M$. Thus $\mathfrak{a}^e = R_M$ if and only if $\mathfrak{a} \cap M \neq \emptyset$. The contrapositive gives $\mathfrak{a}^e \neq R_M$ if and only if $\mathfrak{a} \cap M = \emptyset$.
