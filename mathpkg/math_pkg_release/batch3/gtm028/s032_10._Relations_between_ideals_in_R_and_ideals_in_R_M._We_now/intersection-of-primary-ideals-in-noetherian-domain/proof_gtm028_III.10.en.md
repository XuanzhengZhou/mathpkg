In a noetherian domain, $(0)$ is a prime ideal. The only primary component of $(0)$ is $(0)$ itself, with radical $(0)$. Since $(0) \subset \mathfrak{p}$ for any prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$, Theorem 20 tells us that the intersection $\mathfrak{a}$ of all $\mathfrak{p}$-primary ideals equals the intersection $\mathfrak{b}$ of those primary components of $(0)$ contained in $\mathfrak{p}$. But the only such component is $(0)$ itself, so $\mathfrak{b} = (0)$, and thus $\mathfrak{a} = (0)$.
