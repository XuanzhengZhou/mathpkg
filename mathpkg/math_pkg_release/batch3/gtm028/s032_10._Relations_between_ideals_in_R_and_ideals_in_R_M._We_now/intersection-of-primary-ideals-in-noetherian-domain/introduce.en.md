This corollary to Theorem 20 specializes to the case of an integral domain. In a noetherian domain, the intersection of all $\mathfrak{p}$-primary ideals is the zero ideal for any prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$. This follows immediately from Theorem 20 because in a domain, the only primary component of $(0)$ is $(0)$ itself (with radical $(0)$), which is contained in every prime ideal.
