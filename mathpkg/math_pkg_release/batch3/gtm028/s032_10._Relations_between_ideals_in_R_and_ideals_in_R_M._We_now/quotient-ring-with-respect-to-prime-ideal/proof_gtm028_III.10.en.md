This theorem is a summary of Theorems 15, 16, 17, and 18 for the special case $M = R \setminus \mathfrak{p}$.

(a) Follows from Theorem 15. The condition $\mathfrak{a}^e = R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ is equivalent to $\mathfrak{a} \cap (R \setminus \mathfrak{p}) \neq \emptyset$, i.e., $\mathfrak{a} \not\subset \mathfrak{p}$. The contractedness condition follows from Theorem 15(a): $\mathfrak{a}^{ec} = \{b \in R : bs \in \mathfrak{a} \text{ for some } s \in R \setminus \mathfrak{p}\}$, and $\mathfrak{a} = \mathfrak{a}^{ec}$ precisely when no element outside $\mathfrak{p}$ is a zero divisor modulo $\mathfrak{a}$.

(b) This is Theorem 15(d) specialized; contracted ideals here are precisely those contained in $\mathfrak{p}$ and satisfying the condition in (a).

(c) That $\mathfrak{p}^e$ is maximal follows from (a): any ideal properly containing $\mathfrak{p}^e$ must meet $R \setminus \mathfrak{p}$ and hence equals $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$. The prime correspondence follows from Corollary 1 to Theorem 16.

(d) Theorem 16 specialized.

(e) Theorem 17 specialized: only those $\mathfrak{q}_i$ with $\mathfrak{p}_i \subset \mathfrak{p}$ survive (since $\mathfrak{q}_i \cap (R \setminus \mathfrak{p}) = \emptyset$ iff $\mathfrak{q}_i \subset \mathfrak{p}$ iff $\mathfrak{p}_i \subset \mathfrak{p}$).

(f) Theorem 18 specialized: the primary components of $(0)$ disjoint from $R \setminus \mathfrak{p}$ are exactly those with radicals contained in $\mathfrak{p}$.
