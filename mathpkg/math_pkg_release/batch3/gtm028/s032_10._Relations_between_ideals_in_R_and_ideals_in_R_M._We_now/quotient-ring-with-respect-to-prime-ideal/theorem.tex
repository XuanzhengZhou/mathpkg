Let $\mathfrak{p}$ be a prime ideal in a ring $R$ and let $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ be the quotient ring of $R$ with respect to the multiplicative system $M = R \setminus \mathfrak{p}$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] If $\mathfrak{a}$ is an ideal in $R$, its extension $\mathfrak{a}^e$ in $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ consists of all fractions $a/s$ with $a \in \mathfrak{a}$, $s \in R \setminus \mathfrak{p}$. Then $\mathfrak{a}^e = R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ if and only if $\mathfrak{a} \not\subset \mathfrak{p}$; if $\mathfrak{a} \subset \mathfrak{p}$, then $\mathfrak{a}$ is contracted if and only if no element of $R \setminus \mathfrak{p}$ is a zero divisor modulo $\mathfrak{a}$.

\item[(b)] Every ideal in $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ is the extension of its contraction; the mapping $\mathfrak{a}' \mapsto \mathfrak{a}'^{c}$ is a $1$-$1$ correspondence between ideals of $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ and contracted ideals of $R$ contained in $\mathfrak{p}$, preserving ideal operations.

\item[(c)] The ideal $\mathfrak{p}^e$ is the unique maximal ideal of $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$. The mapping $\mathfrak{p}' \mapsto \mathfrak{p}'^{c}$ is a $1$-$1$ correspondence between prime ideals of $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ and prime ideals of $R$ contained in $\mathfrak{p}$.

\item[(d)] If $\mathfrak{q}$ is a $\mathfrak{p}$-primary ideal, then $\mathfrak{q}^e$ is $\mathfrak{p}^e$-primary in $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$, and every primary ideal in $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ is the extension of a primary ideal in $R$.

\item[(e)] If $\mathfrak{a} = \bigcap \mathfrak{q}_i$ is an irredundant primary representation and $\mathfrak{p}_i$ are the radicals of $\mathfrak{q}_i$, then $\mathfrak{a}^e = \bigcap_{\mathfrak{p}_i \subset \mathfrak{p}} \mathfrak{q}_i^e$ is an irredundant primary representation of $\mathfrak{a}^e$.

\item[(f)] The kernel of $R \to R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ is the intersection of all primary ideals disjoint from $R \setminus \mathfrak{p}$, i.e., the intersection of all $\mathfrak{r}$-primary components of $(0)$ with $\mathfrak{r} \subset \mathfrak{p}$.
\end{enumerate}
