This lemma is the key technical tool behind the proof that imbedded primary components admit infinite variation (Theorem 22). It states that given two primary ideals $\mathfrak{u}$ and $\mathfrak{q}$ with radicals satisfying a strict containment $\mathfrak{v} < \mathfrak{p}$, one can find a strictly smaller $\mathfrak{p}$-primary ideal $\mathfrak{q}'$ that intersects $\mathfrak{u}$ in exactly the same way as $\mathfrak{q}$. The proof passes to the quotient by $\mathfrak{q} \cap \mathfrak{u}$ and uses the fact that the intersection of all $\mathfrak{p}$-primary ideals is zero (Theorem 20).
