Let $R$ be a noetherian ring, and let $\mathfrak{p}_1, \dots, \mathfrak{p}_n$ be prime ideals of $R$ none of which is an isolated prime ideal of $(0)$ (in a domain they may thus be arbitrary proper prime ideals). There exists an ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ in $R$ whose associated prime ideals are exactly the given ideals $\mathfrak{p}_1, \mathfrak{p}_2, \dots, \mathfrak{p}_n$.
