This follows from Theorem 16 and the following remarks. Every ideal in $R_M$ is the extension of its contraction (Theorem 15(c)), so in particular every prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}'$ in $R_M$ satisfies $\mathfrak{p}' = \mathfrak{p}'^{ce}$. Moreover, the contraction of a prime ideal is a prime ideal (a basic property of extension and contraction). Hence $\mathfrak{p}'^{c}$ is a prime ideal of $R$ disjoint from $M$ (by Theorem 16(a)). Conversely, if $\mathfrak{p}$ is a contracted prime ideal of $R$ disjoint from $M$, then $\mathfrak{p}^e$ is prime in $R_M$ (Theorem 16(b) applied to the primary ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ itself, since a prime ideal is primary with itself as radical). The mapping $\mathfrak{p}' \mapsto \mathfrak{p}'^{c}$ gives the desired $1$-$1$ correspondence.
