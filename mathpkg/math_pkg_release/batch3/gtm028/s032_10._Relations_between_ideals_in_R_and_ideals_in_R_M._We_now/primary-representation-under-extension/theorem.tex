Let $\mathfrak{a}$ be an ideal of $R$ admitting an irredundant primary representation
$$\mathfrak{a} = \bigcap_{i=1}^{n} \mathfrak{q}_i.$$
Suppose that, for $1 \leq i \leq r$, we have $\mathfrak{q}_i \cap M = \emptyset$, and that for $r+1 \leq i \leq n$, we have $\mathfrak{q}_i \cap M \neq \emptyset$. Then:
$$\mathfrak{a}^e = \bigcap_{i=1}^{r} \mathfrak{q}_i^e$$
is an irredundant primary representation of $\mathfrak{a}^e$ in $R_M$.
