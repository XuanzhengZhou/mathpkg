The first assertion (that the mapping is $1$-$1$ and onto) follows from Theorem 16 exactly as in Corollary 1. For the second assertion about isomorphism with respect to $\cap$ and $:$, observe that if $\mathfrak{q}_1$ and $\mathfrak{q}_2$ are primary for $\mathfrak{p}$, then $\mathfrak{q}_1 \cap \mathfrak{q}_2$ is also primary for $\mathfrak{p}$ (intersection of $\mathfrak{p}$-primary ideals is $\mathfrak{p}$-primary). Similarly, $\mathfrak{q}_1 : \mathfrak{q}_2$ is primary for $\mathfrak{p}$, except in the trivial case where $\mathfrak{q}_2 \subset \mathfrak{q}_1$, which gives $\mathfrak{q}_1 : \mathfrak{q}_2 = R$. In that case we also have $\mathfrak{q}_2^e \subset \mathfrak{q}_1^e$ and $\mathfrak{q}_1^e : \mathfrak{q}_2^e = R_M = R^e$, so the correspondence is preserved. Since extension preserves intersections and ideal quotients (for the non-trivial cases), the mapping is indeed an isomorphism for these operations.
