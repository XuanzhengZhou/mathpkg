Two proofs are available. \textbf{First proof:} Use the $1$-$1$ mapping defined in Theorem 15(d). Since every ideal in $R_M$ is the extension of its contraction (Theorem 15(c)), and contractions are ideals in $R$, the ascending chain condition in $R$ lifts to $R_M$ via the order-preserving $1$-$1$ correspondence of Theorem 15(d).

\textbf{Second proof:} By Theorem 15(c), every ideal $\mathfrak{a}'$ in $R_M$ satisfies $\mathfrak{a}' = \mathfrak{a}'^{ce}$. Since $R$ is noetherian, $\mathfrak{a}'^{c}$ is finitely generated. The extension of a finitely generated ideal is finitely generated, so $\mathfrak{a}'$ is finitely generated. Thus $R_M$ has the finite basis property and is noetherian.
