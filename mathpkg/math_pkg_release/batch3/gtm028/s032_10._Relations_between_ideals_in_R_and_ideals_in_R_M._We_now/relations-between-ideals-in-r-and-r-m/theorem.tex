Let $M$ be a multiplicative system in a ring $R$ with identity and let $R_M$ be the quotient ring of $R$ with respect to $M$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] If $\mathfrak{a}$ is an ideal in $R$, then $\mathfrak{a}^{ec}$ consists of all elements $b$ in $R$ such that $bm \in \mathfrak{a}$ for some $m$ in $M$.

\item[(b)] $\mathfrak{a}^e = R_M$ if and only if $\mathfrak{a} \cap M \neq \emptyset$, i.e., if and only if $M$ is not prime to $\mathfrak{a}$.

\item[(c)] Every ideal in $R_M$ is the extension of its contraction; that is, for any ideal $\mathfrak{a}'$ in $R_M$, we have $\mathfrak{a}'^{ce} = \mathfrak{a}'$.

\item[(d)] The mapping $\mathfrak{a}' \to \mathfrak{a}'^{c}$ is a $1$-$1$ mapping of the set of all ideals in $R_M$ onto the set of all contracted ideals in $R$, and this mapping is an isomorphism with respect to the ideal-theoretic operations: addition, multiplication, intersection, and quotient.
\end{enumerate}
