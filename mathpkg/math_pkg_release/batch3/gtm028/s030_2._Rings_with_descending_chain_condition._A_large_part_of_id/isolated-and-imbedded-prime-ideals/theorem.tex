Let $\mathfrak{a}$ be an ideal in a noetherian ring $R$ and let $\{\mathfrak{p}_i\}$ be its associated prime ideals. A minimal element in the family of associated prime ideals of $\mathfrak{a}$ (i.e., an associated prime ideal of $\mathfrak{a}$ which contains no other associated prime ideal of $\mathfrak{a}$) is called an \text{isolated prime ideal} of $\mathfrak{a}$. An associated prime ideal of $\mathfrak{a}$ which is not isolated is said to be \text{imbedded}.
