The "determinant trick" is a fundamental lemma in commutative algebra, closely related to Nakayama's Lemma. If a finitely generated module $\mathfrak{a}$ satisfies $\mathfrak{a} = \mathfrak{m}\mathfrak{a}$, then there exists an element $z \in \mathfrak{m}$ such that $(1-z)$ annihilates $\mathfrak{a}$. The proof uses Cramer's rule: write each generator as an $\mathfrak{m}$-linear combination of all generators, form the matrix $I - Y$, take its determinant $d$, which expands to $1 - z$ with $z \in \mathfrak{m}$. This lemma is the key step in proving Krull's Intersection Theorem.
