Let $\mathfrak{a}$ be generated by $x_1, \ldots, x_n$. Since $\mathfrak{a} = \mathfrak{m}\mathfrak{a}$, we have relations $x_i = \sum_{j=1}^{n} y_{ij} x_j$ with $y_{ij} \in \mathfrak{m}$. This gives $\sum_j (\delta_{ij} - y_{ij}) x_j = 0$, where $\delta_{ij}$ is $1$ if $i = j$ and $0$ otherwise.

Let $d = \det(\delta_{ij} - y_{ij})$. By Cramer's rule (which holds in any commutative ring), $d x_j = 0$ for all $j$, i.e., $d \mathfrak{a} = (0)$. Expanding the determinant shows $d = 1 - z$ with $z \in \mathfrak{m}$ (each term in the expansion except the diagonal product $1$ contains at least one factor from $\mathfrak{m}$). Hence $(1 - z)\mathfrak{a} = (0)$.
