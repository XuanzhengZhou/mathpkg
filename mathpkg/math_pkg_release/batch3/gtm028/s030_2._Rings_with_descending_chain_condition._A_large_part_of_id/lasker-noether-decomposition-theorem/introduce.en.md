The Lasker-Noether decomposition theorem is the cornerstone of ideal theory in Noetherian rings. It states that every ideal can be expressed as a finite intersection of primary ideals. The theorem combines Lemma 1 (every ideal is a finite intersection of irreducibles) with Lemma 2 (every irreducible ideal is primary in a Noetherian ring). The theorem was first proved for polynomial rings by Emanuel Lasker (the chess master) and later generalized to all Noetherian rings by Emmy Noether. It does not extend to non-Noetherian rings.
