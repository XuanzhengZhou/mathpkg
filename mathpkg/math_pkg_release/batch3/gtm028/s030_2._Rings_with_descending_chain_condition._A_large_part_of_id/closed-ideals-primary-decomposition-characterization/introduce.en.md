Theorem 13 describes the $\mathfrak{m}$-adic closure of an ideal in terms of its primary decomposition: the closure equals the intersection of precisely those primary components whose associated primes are not comaximal with $\mathfrak{m}$. Components whose radicals are comaximal with $\mathfrak{m}$ are "dense" and do not affect the closure. This provides a structural understanding of the closure operation in the $\mathfrak{m}$-adic topology.
