In a Noetherian ring where every proper prime ideal is maximal (such as an Artinian ring), the primary decomposition is completely unique -- both the associated primes and the primary components are uniquely determined. Furthermore, in this setting the intersection can be replaced by a product: the ideal is uniquely a product of primary ideals with distinct associated primes. This is because imbedded components cannot exist when all primes are maximal and distinct maximal ideals are comaximal.
