In a Noetherian local ring (where the non-units form an ideal $\mathfrak{m}$), all powers of the maximal ideal intersect to zero. This follows directly from Krull's Intersection Theorem because in a local ring, every element of $1 - \mathfrak{m}$ is a unit (if $1 - m$ were a non-unit, it would lie in $\mathfrak{m}$, implying $1 \in \mathfrak{m}$), and units are never zero-divisors.
