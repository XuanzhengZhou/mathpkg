In a local ring, the non-units form an ideal $\mathfrak{m}$. If $1 - z \in 1 - \mathfrak{m}$ were not a unit, it would lie in $\mathfrak{m}$, giving $1 \in \mathfrak{m}$, a contradiction. Hence every element of $1 - \mathfrak{m}$ is a unit. Units are not zero-divisors. By Krull's Intersection Theorem (Theorem 12), $\bigcap_n \mathfrak{m}^n = (0)$.
