If $a$ belongs to all maximal ideals and $1 + xa$ is not a unit for some $x$, then $1 + xa$ lies in some maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$. Since $a \in \mathfrak{m}$, we have $xa \in \mathfrak{m}$, so $1 = (1 + xa) - xa \in \mathfrak{m}$, a contradiction. Hence $1 + xa$ is a unit.

Conversely, if $1 + xa$ is a unit for every $x$ but $a$ is not contained in some maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$, then $\mathfrak{m} + (a) = R$. Hence there exists $x \in R$ such that $1 + xa \in \mathfrak{m}$, contradicting that $1 + xa$ is a unit. Therefore $a$ belongs to all maximal ideals.
