Theorem 12' generalizes Krull's Intersection Theorem from the zero ideal to an arbitrary ideal $\mathfrak{a}$. The intersection of $\mathfrak{a} + \mathfrak{m}^n$ over all $n$ equals $\mathfrak{a}$ precisely when $\mathfrak{m}$ is not comaximal with any associated prime of $\mathfrak{a}$. This is obtained by applying Theorem 12 to the residue class ring $R/\mathfrak{a}$. This generalized form is the foundation for the theory of closed ideals.
