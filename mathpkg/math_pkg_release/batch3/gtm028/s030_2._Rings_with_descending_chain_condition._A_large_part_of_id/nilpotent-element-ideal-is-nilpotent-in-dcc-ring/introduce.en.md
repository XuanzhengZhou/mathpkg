In an Artinian ring, an ideal consisting entirely of nilpotent elements must itself be nilpotent. The proof uses the d.c.c. on the descending chain of powers of the ideal, showing that if the chain stabilizes at a nonzero ideal, then one can construct a minimal counterexample leading to a contradiction.
