Given a ring homomorphism $f: R \to S$, the contraction of an ideal $\mathfrak{B}$ of $S$ is its inverse image $f^{-1}(\mathfrak{B})$ in $R$. It is always an ideal of $R$. Along with extension, contraction forms a Galois connection between the lattices of ideals of $R$ and $S$.
