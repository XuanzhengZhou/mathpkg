Property A is a basic but useful fact about prime ideals and intersections: if a prime ideal contains a finite intersection, it must contain one of the intersected ideals. When the intersected ideals are primary, the prime ideal contains the radical (associated prime) of one of these primary ideals. This property is fundamental for studying the uniqueness of primary decompositions.
