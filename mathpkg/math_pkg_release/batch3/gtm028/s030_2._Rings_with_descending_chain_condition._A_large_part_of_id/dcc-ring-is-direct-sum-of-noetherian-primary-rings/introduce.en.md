Theorem 3 provides the structure theorem for Artinian rings: every Artinian ring with identity decomposes uniquely as a direct sum of Noetherian primary rings. The proof uses the fact that in a d.c.c. ring, the zero ideal is a product of powers of maximal ideals, and that coprime powers of maximal ideals are comaximal. This theorem parallels the primary decomposition of ideals but at the level of the ring itself.
