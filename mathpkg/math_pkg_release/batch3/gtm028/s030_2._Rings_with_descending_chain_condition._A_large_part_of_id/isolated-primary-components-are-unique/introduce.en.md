Theorem 8 establishes the second uniqueness theorem for primary decompositions: while the full decomposition is not unique (imbedded components can vary), the isolated primary components are uniquely determined by the ideal. The proof constructs an ideal-theoretic characterization of each isolated component using elements outside the corresponding prime ideal. This complements Theorem 6, which established uniqueness of the associated primes themselves.
