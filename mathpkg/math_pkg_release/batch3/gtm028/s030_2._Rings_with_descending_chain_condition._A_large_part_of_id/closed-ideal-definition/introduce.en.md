In the $\mathfrak{m}$-adic topology on a ring $R$, the sets $\mathfrak{a} + \mathfrak{m}^n$ form a neighborhood basis for the ideal $\mathfrak{a}$. An ideal is "closed" if it equals the intersection of all its neighborhoods, i.e., if it is closed in this topological sense. By Krull's Intersection Theorem (Theorem 12'), an ideal is closed precisely when $\mathfrak{m}$ is not comaximal with any of its associated primes.
