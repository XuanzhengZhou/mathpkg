In commutative ring theory, the descending chain condition (d.c.c.) is a strong finiteness condition. For integral domains, the d.c.c. is extremely restrictive: the only integral domains that satisfy the d.c.c. are precisely the fields. This fact is proved by considering the descending chain of principal ideals generated by powers of any nonzero element. If the chain stabilizes, the element must be invertible.
