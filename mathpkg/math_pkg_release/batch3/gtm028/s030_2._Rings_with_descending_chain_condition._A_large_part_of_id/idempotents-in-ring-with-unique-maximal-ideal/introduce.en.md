In a ring with a unique maximal ideal (a local ring), the only idempotents are the trivial ones: 0 and 1. This follows because if $e$ is a nontrivial idempotent, then both $e$ and $1-e$ are zero-divisors, hence non-units, and must lie in the unique maximal ideal, forcing $1 = e + (1-e)$ to also lie in that maximal ideal — a contradiction.
