If $e \neq 0$ and $e \neq 1$, then $e^2 = e$ is equivalent to $e(1-e) = 0$. Thus $e$ and $1-e$ are zero-divisors and cannot be units in $R$. Hence the ideals $Re$ and $R(1-e)$ are proper ideals, contained in maximal ideals. Since there is only one maximal ideal $\mathfrak{M}$, we have $e \in \mathfrak{M}$ and $1-e \in \mathfrak{M}$. Then $1 = e + (1-e) \in \mathfrak{M}$, a contradiction. Therefore $e = 0$ or $e = 1$.
