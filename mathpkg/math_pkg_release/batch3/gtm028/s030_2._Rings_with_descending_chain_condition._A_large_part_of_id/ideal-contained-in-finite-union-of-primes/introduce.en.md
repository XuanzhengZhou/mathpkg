This is the classical "prime avoidance" lemma: an ideal cannot be covered by a finite union of prime ideals unless it is already contained in one of them. The proof proceeds by induction, assuming without loss of generality that no $\mathfrak{p}_i$ is contained in another, and constructing an element in the ideal that lies outside every $\mathfrak{p}_i$ if the ideal were not contained in any single one. This lemma is fundamental in commutative algebra.
