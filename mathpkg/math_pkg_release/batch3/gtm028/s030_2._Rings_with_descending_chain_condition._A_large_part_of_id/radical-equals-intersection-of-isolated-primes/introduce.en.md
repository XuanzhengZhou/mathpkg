Theorem 10 shows that the radical of an ideal is exactly the intersection of its isolated (minimal) associated prime ideals. The imbedded primes can be omitted from the intersection without changing the result. This provides a clean relationship between the radical operation and primary decomposition, and implies that $\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}$ is prime if and only if $\mathfrak{a}$ has exactly one isolated prime ideal.
