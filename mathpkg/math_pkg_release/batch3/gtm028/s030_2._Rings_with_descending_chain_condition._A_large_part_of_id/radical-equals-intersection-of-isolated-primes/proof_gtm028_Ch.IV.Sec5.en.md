Since the radical of a finite intersection is the intersection of the radicals, $\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} = \bigcap_i \mathfrak{p}_i$ where $\mathfrak{p}_i$ are the associated prime ideals of $\mathfrak{a}$. From this intersection, the imbedded prime ideals can be deleted without changing the result, since each imbedded prime contains some isolated prime. Thus $\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}$ is the intersection of the isolated prime ideals of $\mathfrak{a}$.
