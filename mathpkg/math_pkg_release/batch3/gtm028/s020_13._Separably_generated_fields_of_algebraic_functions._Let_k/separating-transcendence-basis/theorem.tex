A transcendence basis $\{z_1, z_2, \cdots, z_r\}$ of a field $K/k$ of algebraic functions of $r$ independent variables is called a \text{separating transcendence basis of $K/k$} if $K$ is a separable (algebraic) extension of $k(z_1, z_2, \cdots, z_r)$.

The field $K$ is said to be \text{separably generated over $k$} if there exists a separating transcendence basis of $K/k$.
