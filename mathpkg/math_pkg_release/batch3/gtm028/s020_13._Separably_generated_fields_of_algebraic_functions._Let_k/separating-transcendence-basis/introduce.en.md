A separating transcendence basis is a transcendence basis $\{z_1, \ldots, z_r\}$ of $K/k$ such that $K$ is a separable algebraic extension of the purely transcendental subfield $k(z_1, \ldots, z_r)$. When such a basis exists, the extension $K/k$ is called separably generated. This definition refines the notion of a field of algebraic functions by imposing a separability condition on the algebraic part of the extension. The concept is fundamental in algebraic geometry and commutative algebra, particularly when the ground field $k$ is not perfect; over perfect fields every finitely generated extension is automatically separably generated.
