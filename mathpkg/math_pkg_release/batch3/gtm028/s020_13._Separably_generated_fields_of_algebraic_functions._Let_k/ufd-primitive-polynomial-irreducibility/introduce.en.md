Lemma 1 establishes the equivalence of irreducibility for primitive polynomials when considered over a unique factorization domain $R$ versus its quotient field $K$. Part (1) states that if a primitive polynomial is irreducible over $R$, it remains irreducible over $K$ (a consequence of Gauss's Lemma). Part (2) provides the converse. Part (3) is a divisibility refinement: if a primitive polynomial $g(X)$ divides $f(X)$ in $K[X]$, then the division already holds in $R[X]$, citing Lemma 2 of I, \S 17. This lemma is a key technical tool used in the proof of Theorem 30.
