Let $x_1, x_2, \cdots, x_n, x_{n+1}$ be elements of an extension field $K$ of a field $k$ and assume that these $n+1$ elements $x_i$ are algebraically dependent over $k$ but that the $n$ elements $x_1, x_2, \cdots, x_n$ are algebraically independent over $k$. Then the set $A$ of polynomials $g(X_1, X_2, \cdots, X_n, X_{n+1})$ in $k[X_1, X_2, \cdots, X_n, X_{n+1}]$ such that $g(x_1, x_2, \cdots, x_n, x_{n+1}) = 0$ is a principal ideal, generated by an irreducible polynomial $f(X_1, \cdots, X_n, X_{n+1})$ which involves $X_{n+1}$ effectively. We refer to the relation $f(x_1, x_2, \cdots, x_n, x_{n+1}) = 0$ as the \text{irreducible algebraic relation} between the $x_i$, over $k$.
