Lemma 2 characterizes the ideal of algebraic relations among $n+1$ elements $x_1, \ldots, x_{n+1}$ that are algebraically dependent over $k$, when the first $n$ are algebraically independent. Under these hypotheses, the vanishing ideal in $k[X_1, \ldots, X_{n+1}]$ is a principal ideal generated by a single irreducible polynomial $f$ that involves $X_{n+1}$ non-trivially. This irreducible polynomial defines what is called the irreducible algebraic relation among the $x_i$. The lemma is essential for constructing and analyzing transcendence bases, and is invoked in the proof of Theorem 30.
