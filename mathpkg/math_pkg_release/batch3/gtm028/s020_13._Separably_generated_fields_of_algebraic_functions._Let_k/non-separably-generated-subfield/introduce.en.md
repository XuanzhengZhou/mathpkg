Lemma 3 provides a reduction step for analyzing non-separably generated extensions. If $K = k(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ has transcendence degree $r$ and is not separably generated over $k$, then one can select $r+1$ of the generators such that the subfield they generate still has transcendence degree $r$ and remains non-separably generated. This lemma is preparatory for the application to perfect ground fields $k$: if $k$ is perfect, then every finitely generated extension is separably generated, and this lemma helps establish that result by contradiction.
