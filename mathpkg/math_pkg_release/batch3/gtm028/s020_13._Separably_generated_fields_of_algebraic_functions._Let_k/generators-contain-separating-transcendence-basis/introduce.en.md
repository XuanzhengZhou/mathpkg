Theorem 30 is the central result of this section. It states that if $K = k(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ is a finitely generated extension that is separably generated over $k$, then one can always select a separating transcendence basis from among the given generators $\{x_1, \ldots, x_n\}$ themselves. This means one does not need to look for a separating transcendence basis outside the given generating set. The proof proceeds by induction on the transcendence degree $r$, with the base case $r = 1$ relying on Lemmas 1 and 2 of the same section and on Theorem 10 of \S 5 about inseparable elements.
