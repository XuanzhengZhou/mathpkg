The notion of a derivation of $R$ may be generalized to the case where the ring $S$ is replaced by an $R$-module $M$. In that case, a mapping $D: R \to M$ is a derivation if:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D(x + y) = D(x) + D(y)$ for all $x, y \in R$.
\item $D(xy) = x D(y) + y D(x)$ for all $x, y \in R$,
\end{enumerate}
where the products $x D(y)$ and $y D(x)$ on the right-hand side of (2) are the scalar multiplication of elements of the $R$-module $M$ by elements of $R$.
