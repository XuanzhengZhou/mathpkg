This lemma gives a practical criterion for verifying linear disjointness: two subspaces are linearly disjoint over $k$ precisely when the products of elements from chosen $k$-bases of each subspace form a linearly independent set. The criterion holds for both finite and infinite-dimensional subspaces. The proof reduces to the finite-dimensional case by noting that any finite set of elements from $L$ and $L'$ lies in finite-dimensional subspaces spanned by finite subsets of the respective bases.
