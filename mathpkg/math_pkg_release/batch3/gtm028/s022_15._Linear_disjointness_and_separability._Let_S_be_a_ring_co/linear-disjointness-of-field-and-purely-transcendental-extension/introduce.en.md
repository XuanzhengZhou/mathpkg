This corollary establishes that if a set $X$ is algebraically independent over a field $K$, then the subfield $k(X)$ generated by $X$ over a subfield $k \subset K$ is linearly disjoint from $K$ over $k$. The proof uses the basis criterion for linear disjointness: the monomials in the elements of $X$ form a $k$-basis of $k[X]$, and by assumption these monomials are also independent over $K$, so the lemma on basis criteria for linear disjointness applies directly.
