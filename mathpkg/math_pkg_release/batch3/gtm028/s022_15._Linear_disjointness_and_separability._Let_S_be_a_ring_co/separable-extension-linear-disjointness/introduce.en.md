This definition generalizes the notion of separable algebraic extensions to arbitrary (possibly transcendental) field extensions in characteristic $p$. An extension $K/k$ is separable precisely when $K$ and the purely inseparable extension $k^{p^{-1}}$ are linearly disjoint over $k$. For algebraic extensions this coincides with the usual definition of separability (Theorem 34), and for finitely generated extensions it coincides with separable generation (Theorem 35). For non-finitely generated extensions, separability in this sense is strictly weaker than separable generation.
