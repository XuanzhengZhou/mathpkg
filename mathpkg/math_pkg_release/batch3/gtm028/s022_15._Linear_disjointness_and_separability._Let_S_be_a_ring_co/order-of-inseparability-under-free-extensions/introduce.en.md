Theorem 38 describes how the order of inseparability behaves when the ground field is extended to a field $K$ that is free over $k(x)$. The key result is that the ratio of inseparable degrees $[K(x):K(z)]_i / [k(x):k(z)]_i$ is independent of the choice of transcendence basis $\{z\}$ and equals $[K(x):K]_i / [k(x):k]_i$. The proof proceeds by first treating the algebraic case (where $K/k$ is algebraic) using Lemma 1, and then reducing the general case to the algebraic case via a transcendence basis of $K/k$.
