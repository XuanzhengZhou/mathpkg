The order of inseparability generalizes the degree of inseparability from algebraic extensions to finitely generated extensions. It selects the transcendence basis that minimizes the inseparable degree of the extension over the purely transcendental subfield generated by that basis. This invariant is fundamental in algebraic geometry for measuring how far a function field is from being separably generated, and it plays a key role in the study of varieties in characteristic $p$.
