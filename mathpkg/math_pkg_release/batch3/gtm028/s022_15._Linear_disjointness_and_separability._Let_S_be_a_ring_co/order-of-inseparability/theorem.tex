Let $k(x) = k(x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n)$ be a finitely generated extension of $k$. The \textbf{order of inseparability} of $k(x)/k$, denoted $[k(x):k]_i$, is the minimum value of the degree of inseparability $[k(x):k(z)]_i$ over all possible choices of a transcendence basis $\{z\} = \{z_1, z_2, \dots, z_r\}$ of $k(x)/k$.

In the special case where $k(x)$ is an algebraic extension of $k$, $\{z\}$ is the empty set, $k(z) = k$, and the order of inseparability reduces to the degree of inseparability $[k(x):k]_i$ of the algebraic extension.
