Two subrings $R$ and $R'$ of a ring containing $k$ are free over $k$ when algebraically independent sets from each ring, taken together, remain algebraically independent. This notion is the transcendental analogue of linear disjointness. If either ring is algebraic over $k$, the condition is automatic. For integral domains of finite transcendence degree, freeness is equivalent to the condition $\operatorname{tr.deg}([R,R']/k) = \operatorname{tr.deg}(R/k) + \operatorname{tr.deg}(R'/k)$, i.e., the transcendence bases are disjoint and independent.
