Let $S$ be a ring containing a field $k$. Two subrings $R$ and $R'$ of $S$ which contain $k$ are said to be \textbf{free over $k$} if the following condition is satisfied: whenever $\{x_1, x_2, \dots, x_r\}$ and $\{x'_1, x'_2, \dots, x'_s\}$ are finite subsets of $R$ and $R'$ respectively such that the elements of each set are algebraically independent over $k$, then also the $r + s$ elements $x_1, x_2, \dots, x_r, x'_1, x'_2, \dots, x'_s$ are algebraically independent over $k$.

An equivalent asymmetric formulation is: the integral domains $R$ and $R'$ are free over $k$ if and only if any set of algebraically independent elements of $R$ over $k$ remains algebraically independent over $R'$.
