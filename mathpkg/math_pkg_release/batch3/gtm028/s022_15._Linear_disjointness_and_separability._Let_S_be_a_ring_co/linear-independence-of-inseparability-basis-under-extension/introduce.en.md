This technical lemma is a key step in the proof of Theorem 38. It states that under the hypothesis that the inseparable degree of $k(x)/k(z)$ is preserved when extending the ground field from $k$ to $K$, a basis for the purely inseparable part over $L(k)$ remains linearly independent when the coefficient field is enlarged to $L(K)$. The proof uses a finite approximation argument: any finite set of elements of $L(K)$ is contained in some $L(k')$ with $k'/k$ finite.
