**Proof.** Let $\bar{k}$ be the algebraic closure of $k$ in some algebraic closure of $k(x)$. Since $\bar{k}$ is a perfect field, $k(x)$ is separably generated over $\bar{k}$ (Theorem 31, §13). By Theorem 30, §13, the set of generators $\{x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n\}$ contains a separating transcendence basis of $k(x)/\bar{k}$. Let $\{x_1, x_2, \dots, x_r\}$ be such a basis. Then the $x_i$ are separably algebraic over $\bar{k}(x_1, x_2, \dots, x_r)$, $i = 1, 2, \dots, n$. If we denote by $k'$ the field obtained by adjoining to $k$ the coefficients of the minimal polynomials of the $x_i$ over $\bar{k}(x_1, \dots, x_r)$, then $k'$ is a finite algebraic extension of $k$, and $\{x_1, \dots, x_r\}$ is a transcendence basis of $k'(x)/k'$ such that $[k'(x):k'(x_1, \dots, x_r)]_i = 1$. Then $[k(x):k(x_1, \dots, x_r)]_i$ is the minimum possible value, i.e., equals $[k(x):k]_i$.
