Theorem 37 asserts that one can always find a transcendence basis for a finitely generated extension within the original set of generators that attains the minimal possible inseparable degree. The proof uses passage to the algebraic closure to obtain a perfect ground field, applies the existence of separating transcendence bases over perfect fields (Theorem 30, §13), and then relates the inseparable degree back to the original field via the notion of the algebraic closure of $k$ in $k(x)$.
