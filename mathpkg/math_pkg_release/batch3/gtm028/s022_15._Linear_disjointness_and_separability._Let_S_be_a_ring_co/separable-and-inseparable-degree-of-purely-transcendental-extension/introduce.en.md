This corollary shows that adjoining algebraically independent elements does not affect the separable or inseparable degree of a finite algebraic extension. More precisely, if $k'/k$ is finite algebraic and $B$ is algebraically independent over $k'$, then passing from $k(B)$ to $k'(B)$ preserves both the separable and inseparable degrees. The result follows from Lemma 1 combined with the fact that $k(B)$ and $k'$ are linearly disjoint over $k$ (Corollary 1 to the lemma of §15).
