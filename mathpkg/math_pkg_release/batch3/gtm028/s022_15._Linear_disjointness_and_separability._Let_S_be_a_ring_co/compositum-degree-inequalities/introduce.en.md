This lemma controls how the degree, separable degree, and inseparable degree of a finite field extension behave when both the base and extension fields are composed with a third field $k'$. The compositum cannot increase any of these invariants beyond their original values. Equality holds precisely when $K$ and $k'$ are linearly disjoint over a common subfield, which is a key technical tool for analyzing how inseparability behaves under base change.
