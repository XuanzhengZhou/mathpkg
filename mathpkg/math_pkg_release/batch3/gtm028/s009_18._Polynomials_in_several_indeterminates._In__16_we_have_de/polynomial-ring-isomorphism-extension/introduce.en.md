An isomorphism between coefficient rings $R$ and $\bar{R}$ extends uniquely to an isomorphism between corresponding polynomial rings $S = R[x_1,\dots,x_n]$ and $\bar{S} = \bar{R}[y_1,\dots,y_n]$ sending each indeterminate $x_i$ to $y_i$, provided the $y_i$ are algebraically independent over $\bar{R}$. This follows from Theorem 7 of §17 and the universal property of polynomial rings.
