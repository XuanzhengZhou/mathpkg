A polynomial in $n$ indeterminates over a ring $R$ is formalized as a finitely-supported mapping from the set $I_n$ of ordered $n$-tuples of non-negative integers into $R$. Addition is defined pointwise, and multiplication is defined via convolution: the coefficient of $(i)$ in the product is the sum of products of coefficients whose index pairs sum to $(i)$. This mapping-based definition avoids any reliance on the informal notion of "expressions" and provides a rigorous foundation for the polynomial ring $R[X_1, \dots, X_n]$.
