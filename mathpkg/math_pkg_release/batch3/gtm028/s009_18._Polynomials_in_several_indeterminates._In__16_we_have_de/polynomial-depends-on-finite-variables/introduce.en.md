In the polynomial ring with infinitely many indeterminates, each individual polynomial involves only finitely many variables. This follows from the finite-support condition in the definition: a polynomial $f$ is non-zero on only finitely many multi-indices, and each such multi-index has only finitely many non-zero coordinates. Thus the polynomial ring is the directed union of its finitely-generated polynomial subrings, allowing many properties (degree, homogeneity, UFD) to be transferred from the finite case.
