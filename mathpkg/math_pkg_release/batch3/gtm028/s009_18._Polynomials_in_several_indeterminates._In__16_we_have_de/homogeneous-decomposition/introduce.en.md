Every polynomial $f$ of degree $m$ admits a unique decomposition as a sum $f = f_0 + f_1 + \cdots + f_m$, where each $f_i$ is either zero or a homogeneous polynomial (form) of degree $i$. This decomposition groups monomials by their total degree and is central to the graded algebra structure of polynomial rings.
