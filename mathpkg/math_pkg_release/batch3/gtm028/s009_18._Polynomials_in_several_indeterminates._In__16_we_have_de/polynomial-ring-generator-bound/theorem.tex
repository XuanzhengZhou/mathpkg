**Theorem 15.** Let $S$ be a polynomial ring in elements $x_1, \cdots, x_n$ over a ring $R$, and let $y_1, \cdots, y_m$ be elements of $S$ such that $S = R[y_1, \cdots, y_m]$. Then $m \geq n$, and equality holds if and only if $S$ is a polynomial ring in $y_1, \cdots, y_m$ (i.e., the $y_j$ are algebraically independent over $R$).
