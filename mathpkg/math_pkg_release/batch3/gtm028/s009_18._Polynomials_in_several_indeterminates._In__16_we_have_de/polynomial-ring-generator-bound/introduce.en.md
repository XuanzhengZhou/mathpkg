If a polynomial ring $S = R[x_1,\dots,x_n]$ (with $x_i$ algebraically independent) is generated as an $R$-algebra by $m$ elements $y_1,\dots,y_m$, then necessarily $m \geq n$. Moreover, $m = n$ precisely when the $y_j$ are also algebraically independent, i.e., $S$ is also a polynomial ring in the $y_j$. This result is foundational for dimension theory and the invariance of the number of indeterminates.
