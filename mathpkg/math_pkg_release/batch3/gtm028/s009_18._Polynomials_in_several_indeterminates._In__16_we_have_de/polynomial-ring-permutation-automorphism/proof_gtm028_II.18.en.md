Apply Corollary 1 with $\bar{S} = S = R[x_1, \dots, x_n]$ and $y_i = x_{h_i}$. Since $\{h_1, \dots, h_n\}$ is a permutation of $\{1, \dots, n\}$, the elements $x_{h_1}, \dots, x_{h_n}$ are also algebraically independent over $R$ and generate $S$. Thus $S$ is a polynomial ring in both $\{x_1, \dots, x_n\}$ and $\{x_{h_1}, \dots, x_{h_n}\}$. By Corollary 1, there is a unique $R$-automorphism $T$ of $S$ sending $x_i \mapsto x_{h_i}$ for $i = 1, \dots, n$.
