Any permutation of the indeterminates of a polynomial ring $S = R[x_1, \dots, x_n]$ induces a unique $R$-automorphism of $S$. This follows from Corollary 1 by taking $\bar{S} = S$ and $y_i = x_{h_i}$, and shows that the symmetric group $S_n$ acts naturally on $R[X_1, \dots, X_n]$ by $R$-algebra automorphisms.
