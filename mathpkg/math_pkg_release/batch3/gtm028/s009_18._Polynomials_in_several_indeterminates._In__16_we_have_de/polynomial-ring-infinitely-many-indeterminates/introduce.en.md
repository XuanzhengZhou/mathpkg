The construction of polynomial rings extends from finitely many to arbitrarily many (even uncountably many) indeterminates. Given a set $E$ indexing the indeterminates, one considers finitely-supported functions from $I_E$ (the set of finitely-supported multi-indices) into the coefficient ring $R$. Each individual polynomial involves only finitely many variables, and the full ring is the union of all its finitely-generated polynomial subrings. This construction is fundamental in algebraic geometry for studying function rings on infinite-dimensional spaces.
