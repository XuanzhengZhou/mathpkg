The ring $S$ of all polynomials over $R$ in $n$ indeterminates is denoted by $R[X_1, \cdots, X_n]$. For each $\nu = 1, \dots, n$, the **indeterminate** $X_\nu$ is the element of $S$ which assigns the identity element $1$ of $R$ to the $n$-tuple $(j^\nu) = (0, \dots, 0, 1, 0, \dots, 0)$ (with $1$ at the $\nu$-th position) and the zero element $0$ of $R$ to every other $n$-tuple.

If $a \in R$, and $i_1, i_2, \cdots, i_n$ are non-negative integers, then $aX_1^{i_1}X_2^{i_2} \cdots X_n^{i_n}$ is the element of $S$ which associates $a$ with the $n$-tuple $(i) = (i_1, i_2, \cdots, i_n)$ and $0$ with every other one. Such elements are called **monomials**.

Every element $f$ of $S$ is a sum of a finite number of monomials of the form
$$a_{(i)}X_1^{i_1}X_2^{i_2} \cdots X_n^{i_n},$$
and $f$ is the zero element of $S$ if and only if all the coefficients $a_{(i)}$ are zero.
