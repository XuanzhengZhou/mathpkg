When two non-zero polynomials are multiplied, the lexicographically first monomial in their product arises from the product of the first monomials of their highest-degree homogeneous components. Since the coefficient ring is an integral domain, this product monomial has a non-zero coefficient, proving that $f_p g_q \neq 0$. This lemma is the crucial step in showing that a polynomial ring over an integral domain is again an integral domain.
