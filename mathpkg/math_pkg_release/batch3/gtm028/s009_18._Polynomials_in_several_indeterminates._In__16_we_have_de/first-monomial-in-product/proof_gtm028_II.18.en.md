Let $M_1 = X_1^{\alpha_1} \cdots X_n^{\alpha_n}$ be the lexicographically first monomial in $f_p$ and $M_2 = X_1^{\beta_1} \cdots X_n^{\beta_n}$ the first in $g_q$. Consider any other monomial $M = X_1^{\gamma_1} \cdots X_n^{\gamma_n}$ occurring in $f_p$ and $N = X_1^{\delta_1} \cdots X_n^{\delta_n}$ occurring in $g_q$, not both first. Then $M \succ M_1$ (or $N \succ N_1$) in lexicographic order. Let $s$ be the first index where $(\gamma,\delta)$ differs from $(\alpha,\beta)$. Since the order is determined by the first differing position and both $M, N$ contribute additively to exponents, the product $M N = X_1^{\gamma_1+\delta_1} \cdots X_n^{\gamma_n+\delta_n}$ is lexicographically strictly greater than $M_1 M_2$. Hence $M_1 M_2$ with coefficient $ab \neq 0$ is the first monomial in $f_p g_q$, proving $f_p g_q \neq 0$.
