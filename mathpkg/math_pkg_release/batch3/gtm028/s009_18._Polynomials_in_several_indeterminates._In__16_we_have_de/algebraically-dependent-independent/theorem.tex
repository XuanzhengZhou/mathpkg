**DEFINITION 1.** Let $R$ be a ring with identity and let $\Delta$ be a ring unitary over $R$. Elements $x_1, \cdots, x_n \in \Delta$ are called **algebraically dependent** over $R$ if the substitution homomorphism $f \mapsto f(x_1, \cdots, x_n)$ is not injective (i.e., is a proper homomorphism). Otherwise, they are called **algebraically independent** over $R$.

Equivalently, $x_1, \cdots, x_n$ are algebraically dependent over $R$ if and only if there exists a non-zero polynomial $g(X_1, \dots, X_n) \in R[X_1, \dots, X_n]$ such that $g(x_1, \dots, x_n) = 0$.
