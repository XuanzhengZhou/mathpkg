Elements $x_1, \dots, x_n$ in an $R$-algebra are **algebraically independent** over $R$ when the substitution homomorphism from $R[X_1, \dots, X_n]$ is injective — that is, no non-zero polynomial relation holds among them. They are **algebraically dependent** otherwise, meaning some non-zero polynomial vanishes when evaluated at $(x_1, \dots, x_n)$. This generalizes linear independence to polynomial relations.
