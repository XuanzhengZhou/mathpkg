The degree of a monomial is the sum of its exponents $i_1 + \cdots + i_n$, measuring its total order. The degree $\partial f$ of a non-zero polynomial $f$ is the maximum degree among its constituent monomials. A polynomial whose monomials all share the same degree is called homogeneous, or a form. This generalizes the familiar notion from single-variable polynomials to the multivariate setting and is essential for studying graded ring structures.
