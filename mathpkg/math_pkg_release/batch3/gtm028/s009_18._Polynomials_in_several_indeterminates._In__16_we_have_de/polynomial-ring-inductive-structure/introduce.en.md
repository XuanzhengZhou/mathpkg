Polynomial rings admit an inductive description: $R[X_1, \dots, X_n]$ is naturally isomorphic to $(R[X_1, \dots, X_{n-1}])[X_n]$, the ring of polynomials in a single variable $X_n$ with coefficients in the polynomial ring in the remaining $n-1$ variables. This inductive structure enables proofs by induction on the number of indeterminates, which is a standard technique in commutative algebra.
