Let $\Delta$ be a ring unitary over $R$, and let $x_1, \cdots, x_n$ be elements of $\Delta$. For any polynomial $f \in R[X_1, \cdots, X_n]$ expressed as
$$f = \sum a_{(i)} X_1^{i_1} \cdots X_n^{i_n},$$
define
$$f(x_1, \cdots, x_n) = \sum a_{(i)} x_1^{i_1} \cdots x_n^{i_n}.$$
Then $f(x_1, \cdots, x_n) \in \Delta$ is called the **result of substituting** $x_1$ for $X_1, \cdots, x_n$ for $X_n$ in $f$.

For fixed $x_1, \cdots, x_n$, the mapping $f \mapsto f(x_1, \cdots, x_n)$ is an $R$-homomorphism of $R[X_1, \cdots, X_n]$ into $\Delta$. The image is denoted by $R[x_1, \cdots, x_n]$, a subring of $\Delta$ consisting of all elements of the form $\sum a_{(i)} x_1^{i_1} \cdots x_n^{i_n}$; it is the smallest subring of $\Delta$ containing $\{x_1, \cdots, x_n\} \cup R$.
