This theorem establishes the fundamental properties of $R$-homomorphisms between modules. It states that $R$-homomorphisms preserve the submodule structure in both directions: the forward image of a submodule is a submodule, and the inverse image of a submodule is likewise a submodule. Additionally, the kernel is always an $R$-submodule, not merely an additive subgroup. The theorem also relates the action of an ideal $A$ on a submodule $L$ with the homomorphic image: applying $T$ after multiplying by $A$ is the same as multiplying the image by $A$.
