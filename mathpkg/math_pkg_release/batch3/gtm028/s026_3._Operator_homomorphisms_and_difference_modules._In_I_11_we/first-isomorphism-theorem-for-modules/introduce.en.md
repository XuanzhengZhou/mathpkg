This is the first isomorphism theorem for $R$-modules. It states that every homomorphic image of an $R$-module $M$ is isomorphic to a quotient module $M/N$, where $N$ is the kernel of the homomorphism. More precisely, if $S \colon M \to M'$ is a surjective $R$-homomorphism with kernel $N$, then the map $x+N \mapsto xS$ establishes an $R$-isomorphism between the difference module $M - N$ (equivalently $M/N$) and $M'$.
