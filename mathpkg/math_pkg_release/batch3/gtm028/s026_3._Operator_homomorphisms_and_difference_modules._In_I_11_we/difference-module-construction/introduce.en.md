This theorem constructs the quotient module of an $R$-module $M$ by a submodule $N$. The underlying additive group is the group of cosets $x + N$, with addition defined by $(x+N) + (y+N) = (x+y)+N$. The $R$-module structure is given by scalar multiplication $a(x+N) = ax+N$, which is well-defined precisely because $N$ is a submodule (and not merely an additive subgroup). The natural projection $x \mapsto x+N$ is then an $R$-homomorphism of $M$ onto the quotient module with kernel $N$.
