The difference module (or factor module, or quotient module) $M/N$ is the $R$-module whose elements are the cosets $x+N$ of a submodule $N$ in $M$. The notation $M - N$ is used in Zariski--Samuel, while $M/N$ is the more common modern notation. The canonical (or natural) homomorphism is the surjective $R$-homomorphism $M \to M/N$ sending each element $x \in M$ to its coset $x+N$. When the submodule $N$ is trivial, i.e., $N = (0)$, the canonical homomorphism reduces to the identity map, which is an isomorphism.
