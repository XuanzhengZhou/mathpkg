The $R$-module $\bar{M}$ constructed from an $R$-module $M$ and an $R$-submodule $N$ is denoted by $M - N$ and is called the \text{difference module} of $M$ with respect to $N$. The difference module $M - N$ is also called the \text{factor module} of $M$ by $N$ and is denoted by $M/N$. The mapping $x \mapsto x + N$ is called the \text{canonical} (or \text{natural}) homomorphism of $M$ onto $M - N$.

If $N = (0)$, then this natural homomorphism is an isomorphism.
