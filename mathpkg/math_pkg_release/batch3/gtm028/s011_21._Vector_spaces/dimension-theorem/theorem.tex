If $V$ admits a finite basis $B$ of $n$ elements then every basis $B'$ of $V$ also has $n$ elements, and any two bases of $V$ have the same number of elements. This number is called the dimension of $V$ over $F$, and is denoted by $[V:F]$ or by $\dim(V)$. A vector space which admits a finite basis is said to be finite-dimensional. If a vector space $V$ does not admit any finite basis, we say that $V$ is infinite-dimensional, and we set $[V:F] = \infty$ in this case.
