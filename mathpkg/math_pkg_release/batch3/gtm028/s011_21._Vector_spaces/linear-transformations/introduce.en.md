A linear transformation (or homomorphism) between vector spaces $V$ and $W$ over the same field $F$ is a mapping $T: V \to W$ that preserves the vector space structure: it respects vector addition and scalar multiplication. The kernel $\ker(T)$ and image $\operatorname{im}(T)$ are vector subspaces of $V$ and $W$ respectively. An injective homomorphism is called an isomorphism (into). A homomorphism from $V$ to itself is an endomorphism, and a bijective endomorphism is an automorphism. These concepts generalize group homomorphisms to the setting of vector spaces.
