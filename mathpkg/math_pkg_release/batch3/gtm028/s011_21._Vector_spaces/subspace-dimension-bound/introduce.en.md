This lemma, embedded within the proof of Theorem 23, states that any vector subspace $W$ of a finite-dimensional vector space $V$ is itself finite-dimensional, and its dimension cannot exceed that of $V$. Equality $\dim(W) = \dim(V)$ occurs precisely when $W = V$. This result is essential for the rank-nullity theorem and for the general theory of finite-dimensional vector spaces, ensuring that the finite-dimensional property is inherited by subspaces.
