A basis of a vector space $V$ is a subset $X$ that is both free (linearly independent) and a system of generators (spanning $V$). Equivalently, every element of $V$ admits a unique representation as a finite linear combination of elements of $X$. The freeness condition guarantees the uniqueness of this representation: if two linear combinations of basis elements are equal, their coefficients must coincide. Bases are the fundamental tool for coordinatizing vector spaces.
