This corollary to Theorem 21 guarantees that every finitely generated vector space possesses a basis consisting entirely of elements from the given finite generating set. The proof is immediate: take the free subset $L$ in Theorem 21 to be the empty set; then there exists $S' \subseteq S$ such that $S' = \emptyset \cup S'$ is a basis of $V$. This result is fundamental: it shows that finitely generated vector spaces are precisely those admitting finite bases.
