A vector subspace $W$ of a vector space $V$ over a field $F$ is a non-empty subset that is itself a vector space under the inherited operations. Equivalently, $W$ is closed under subtraction (making it a subgroup of the additive group of $V$) and closed under scalar multiplication by elements of $F$. This two-condition characterization is the standard way to verify that a subset forms a subspace.
