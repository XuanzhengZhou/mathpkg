A subset $X$ of a vector space $V$ is called free, and its elements are said to be linearly independent, if no element of $X$ belongs to the span of the remaining elements. Equivalently, the only linear combination of elements of $X$ that yields the zero vector is the trivial one (all coefficients zero). This condition implies that all elements of a free set are distinct and non-zero. Conversely, any single non-zero vector forms a free set. Linear independence is the fundamental notion underlying bases and dimension theory.
