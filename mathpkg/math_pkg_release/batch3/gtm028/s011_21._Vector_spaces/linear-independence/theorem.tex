In a vector space $V$ with span operation $s$, a subset $X$ of $V$ is called free (or the elements of $X$ are said to be linearly independent) if the following condition holds:

(I) For every $x \in X$, we have $x \notin s(X - x)$.

Equivalently, no non-trivial linear relation exists among the elements of $X$: a relation $\sum a_i x_i = 0$ with $x_i \in X$ distinct and $a_i \in F$ implies $a_i = 0$ for all $i$.

The elements of a free subset must be all distinct and all $\neq 0$. As a partial converse, if a vector $x \neq 0$, then the subset $\{x\}$ is free, since $ax = 0$ $(a \in F)$ implies $a = 0$.
