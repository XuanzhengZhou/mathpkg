Let $V$ be a vector space over a field $F$. The operation $s$ ("span") is a mapping of the set of all subsets of $V$ into itself which has the following properties:

$(S_1)$ If $X \subset Y$, then $s(X) \subset s(Y)$.

$(S_2)$ If $x$ is an element of $V$ and $X$ a subset of $V$ such that $x \in s(X)$, then there exists a finite subset $X'$ of $X$ such that $x \in s(X')$.

$(S_3)$ For every subset $X$ of $V$ we have $X \subset s(X)$.

$(S_4)$ For every subset $X$ of $V$ we have $s(s(X)) = s(X)$.

$(S_5)$ The relations $y \in s(X, x)$ and $y \notin s(X)$ imply $x \in s(X, y)$ ("exchange property"). Here $s(X, x)$ stands for $s(X \cup \{x\})$.
