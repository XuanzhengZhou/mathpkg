Let $x, y \in B$ be integral over $A$. By Theorem 1, $A[x, y]$ is a finite $A$-module.

Since $x - y \in A[x, y]$ and $xy \in A[x, y]$, and $A[x, y]$ is a finite $A$-module, condition (c'') (with $R = A[x, y]$) implies that both $x - y$ and $xy$ are integral over $A$.

Every element $a \in A$ is integral over $A$ (as $a$ is a root of $X - a$), so $A$ is contained in the set of integral elements.

Thus the set of all elements of $B$ integral over $A$ is closed under subtraction and multiplication, contains $1$, and hence forms a subring of $B$ containing $A$. $\square$
