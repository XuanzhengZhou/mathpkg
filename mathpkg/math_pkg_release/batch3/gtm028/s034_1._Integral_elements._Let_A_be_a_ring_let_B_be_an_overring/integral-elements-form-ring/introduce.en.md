If $x$ and $y$ are elements of $B$ integral over $A$, then by Theorem 1 the ring $A[x, y]$ is a finite $A$-module. Condition (c') then implies that $x - y$ and $xy$ are integral over $A$. Since every element of $A$ is trivially integral over $A$, the set of integral elements forms a subring of $B$ containing $A$. This subring is called the integral closure of $A$ in $B$.
