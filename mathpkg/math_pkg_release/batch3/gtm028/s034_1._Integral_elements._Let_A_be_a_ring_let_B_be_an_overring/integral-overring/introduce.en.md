An overring $B$ is integral over $A$ when every element of $B$ satisfies a monic polynomial equation with coefficients in $A$. This is a relative notion: $B$ is an integral extension of $A$. Integral extensions generalize algebraic field extensions to arbitrary commutative rings. Unlike field extensions, an integral domain can admit integral extensions that are not finitely generated as modules. A fundamental result is that integral dependence is transitive: if $C$ is integral over $B$ and $B$ is integral over $A$, then $C$ is integral over $A$.
