An element $x$ in an overring $B$ of a ring $A$ is called integral over $A$ if $x$ is a root of a monic polynomial with coefficients in $A$. This generalizes the notion of an algebraic element from field theory to arbitrary commutative rings. Note that every element $a \in A$ is trivially integral over $A$, since $a$ is a root of $X - a$. An element integral over $A$ is also algebraic over $A$, but the converse does not hold in general for rings that are not fields.
