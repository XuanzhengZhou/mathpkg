Given a ring $A$ and an overring $B$, the integral closure of $A$ in $B$ is the set of all $x \in B$ that satisfy a monic polynomial equation with coefficients in $A$. By the Corollary to Theorem 1, this set forms a subring of $B$ containing $A$. The integral closure is the largest subring of $B$ that is integral over $A$. When $B$ is the total ring of fractions of $A$, this construction yields the normalization of $A$, a fundamental operation in commutative algebra.
