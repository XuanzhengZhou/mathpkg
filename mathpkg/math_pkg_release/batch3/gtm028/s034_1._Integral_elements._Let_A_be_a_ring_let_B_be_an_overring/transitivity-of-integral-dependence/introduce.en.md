Integral dependence is transitive: if $B$ is integral over $A$ and $C$ is integral over $B$, then $C$ is integral over $A$. This generalizes the corresponding fact for algebraic field extensions. The proof takes an arbitrary $x \in C$, which satisfies a monic equation with coefficients in $B$, and uses Theorem 1 to show that the subring generated by $A$ and the coefficients (together with $x$) is a finite $A$-module, hence $x$ is integral over $A$ by condition (c'').
