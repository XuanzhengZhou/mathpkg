If finitely many elements $x_1, \dots, x_n$ of an overring $B$ are each integral over $A$, then the subring they generate $A[x_1, \dots, x_n]$ is finitely generated as an $A$-module. This follows by induction on $n$: for $n=1$ this is equivalent to the definition of integral dependence (condition c'), and the induction step uses the fact that an element integral over $A$ is a fortiori integral over any intermediate ring, together with the transitivity of the "finite module" property (Remark 3).
