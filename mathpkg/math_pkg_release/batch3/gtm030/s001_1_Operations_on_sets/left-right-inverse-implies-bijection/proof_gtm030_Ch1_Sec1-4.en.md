First, $\alpha$ is injective: if $s_1\alpha = s_2\alpha$, then applying $\beta$ gives $(s_1\alpha)\beta = (s_2\alpha)\beta$, so $s_1(\alpha\beta) = s_2(\alpha\beta)$, hence $s_1 1_S = s_2 1_S$, so $s_1 = s_2$. Second, $\alpha$ is surjective: for any $t \in T$, let $s = t\beta$. Then $s\alpha = (t\beta)\alpha = t(\beta\alpha) = t 1_T = t$. Thus $\alpha$ is bijective and $\beta = \alpha^{-1}$ by uniqueness of inverses.
