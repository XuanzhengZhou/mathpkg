Let $S$ be a set. If $A$ and $B$ are two subsets of $S$, then $A$ is contained in $B$ (or $B$ contains $A$), denoted $A \subseteq B$ or $B \supseteq A$, if every $a \in A$ is also in $B$. The statement $A = B$ means that $A \supseteq B$ and $B \supseteq A$. We write $A \supset B$ (proper containment) if $A \supseteq B$ but $B \neq A$.
