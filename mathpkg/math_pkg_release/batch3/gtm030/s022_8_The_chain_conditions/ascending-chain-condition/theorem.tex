Let $\mathfrak{G}$ be an $M$-group and let $\mathfrak{G}$ be any term of a normal series. If
\[
\mathfrak{G}_1 \subseteq \mathfrak{G}_2 \subseteq \mathfrak{G}_3 \subseteq \cdots
\]
is an increasing sequence of $M$-subgroups all of which are invariant in $\mathfrak{G}$, then there exists an integer $N$ such that
\[
\mathfrak{G}_N = \mathfrak{G}_{N+1} = \cdots.
\]
