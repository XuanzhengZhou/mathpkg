The descending and ascending chain conditions together characterize exactly when an $M$-group possesses a composition series. Sufficiency is proved by constructing a maximal descending chain of invariant $M$-subgroups, which must terminate by the DCC, yielding a composition series. Necessity follows from the Jordan-Hölder and Schreier refinement theorems: any properly descending chain of invariant subgroups can contain at most as many terms as the length of a composition series.
