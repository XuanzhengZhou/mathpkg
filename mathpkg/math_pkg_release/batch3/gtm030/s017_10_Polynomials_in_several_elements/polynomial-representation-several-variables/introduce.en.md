This theorem describes the internal structure of $A[u_1, \ldots, u_r]$: every element can be written as a finite sum of monomials $a_{i_1 \cdots i_r} u_1^{i_1} \cdots u_r^{i_r}$ with coefficients in $A$, using the ring operations to combine terms. The representation follows from the iterated adjunction identity $A[u_1, \ldots, u_r] = A[u_1][u_2] \cdots [u_r]$, which reduces the several-variable case to repeated single-variable polynomial rings. When the $u_i$ are algebraically independent over $A$, the representation is unique.
