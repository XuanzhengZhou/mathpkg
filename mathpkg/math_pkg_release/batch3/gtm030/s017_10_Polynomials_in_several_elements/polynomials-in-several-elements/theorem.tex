Let $A$ be a subring (containing $1$) of a ring $B$ with identity, and let $u_1, \cdots, u_r$ be commuting elements of $B$ that also commute with every element of $A$. Then $A[u_1, u_2, \cdots, u_r]$ is the set of all finite sums of the form

$$\sum a_{i_1 i_2 \cdots i_r} u_1^{i_1} u_2^{i_2} \cdots u_r^{i_r}$$

where the coefficients $a_{i_1 i_2 \cdots i_r} \in A$ and the exponents $i_j$ are non-negative integers. Such expressions are called polynomials in $u_1, \cdots, u_r$ with coefficients in $A$.
