Left multiplication by an element $a$ in a group is the mapping $x \mapsto ax$. Unlike right multiplication, the left multiplications form a transformation group but the natural mapping $a \mapsto a_l$ is an anti-isomorphism rather than an isomorphism, since $a_l b_l = (ba)_l$. To obtain an isomorphism, one must compose with the inversion map.
