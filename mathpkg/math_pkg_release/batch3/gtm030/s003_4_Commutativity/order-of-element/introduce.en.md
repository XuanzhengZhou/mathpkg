The order of a group element $a$ is the order (cardinality) of the cyclic subgroup it generates. For finite order $r$, this is the smallest positive integer such that $a^r = 1$. The order of an element divides the order of any finite group containing it (Lagrange's theorem), making it a fundamental invariant in group theory.
