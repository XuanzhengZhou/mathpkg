Let $e$ be a left identity and $f$ be a right identity in $\mathfrak{S}$. Then $ef = f$ because $e$ is a left identity (it acts as identity when on the left of any element, including $f$). Also $ef = e$ because $f$ is a right identity (it acts as identity when on the right of any element, including $e$). Hence $e = f$. It follows that if two two-sided identities $1$ and $1'$ exist, both are left identities and both are right identities, so $1 = 1'$.
