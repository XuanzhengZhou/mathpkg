Suppose the mapping $n \to a^n$ is not 1-1. Then $a^m = a^n$ for $m \neq n$. We may assume $n > m$. Then $a^{n-m} = a^n a^{-m} = a^m a^{-m} = 1$. Let $r$ be the smallest positive integer such that $a^r = 1$. Then the elements $1, a, \cdots, a^{r-1}$ are distinct and every element of $\mathfrak{Z}$ is in this set. Thus $\mathfrak{Z}$ is a finite group of order $r$.
