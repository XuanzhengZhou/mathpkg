If a cyclic group generated by $a$ is not infinite, then some positive power of $a$ equals the identity. The smallest such positive integer $r$ is called the order of $a$ (and of the group). In this case, the group has exactly $r$ distinct elements: $1, a, a^2, \ldots, a^{r-1}$, and every element can be reduced modulo $r$. Any two finite cyclic groups of the same order are isomorphic.
