In a semigroup, if each element $a_i$ in a product commutes with a fixed element $b$, then the entire product commutes with $b$. This is proved by a straightforward induction on $n$, the number of factors. The result is a basic but useful lemma that extends the commutativity of individual elements to the commutativity of products.
