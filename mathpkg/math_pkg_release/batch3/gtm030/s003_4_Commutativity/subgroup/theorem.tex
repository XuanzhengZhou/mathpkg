A subset $\mathfrak{H}$ of a group $\mathfrak{G}$ is called a \text{subgroup} if it is closed under the group operation and itself forms a group under the induced operation. The following conditions are necessary and sufficient for a subset $\mathfrak{H}$ of $\mathfrak{G}$ to be a subgroup:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $a, b \in \mathfrak{H}$ implies $ab \in \mathfrak{H}$ (closure).
\item $1 \in \mathfrak{H}$ (contains identity).
\item $a \in \mathfrak{H}$ implies $a^{-1} \in \mathfrak{H}$ (closed under inverses).
\end{enumerate}
