A fundamental consequence of the associative law is that left and right multiplications commute with each other: for any $a, b$ in a group, $a_l b_r = b_r a_l$. This means every right multiplication commutes with every left multiplication. The converse is also true: any transformation that commutes with all left (resp. right) multiplications must itself be a right (resp. left) multiplication.
