In a group, the equations $ax = b$ and $ya = b$ always have unique solutions, namely $x = a^{-1}b$ and $y = ba^{-1}$ respectively. This property is characteristic of groups — a semigroup in which these equations are always solvable is necessarily a group. The unique solvability also implies the cancellation laws.
