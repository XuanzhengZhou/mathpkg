For $ax = b$, multiply both sides on the left by $a^{-1}$:
$$a^{-1}(ax) = a^{-1}b.$$
By associativity, $(a^{-1}a)x = 1 \cdot x = x$, so $x = a^{-1}b$ is a solution.

For uniqueness, if $ax = ax'$, then multiply on the left by $a^{-1}$:
$$a^{-1}(ax) = a^{-1}(ax') \implies (a^{-1}a)x = (a^{-1}a)x' \implies x = x'.$$
This shows the left cancellation law holds. The right cancellation law is proved analogously for $ya = b$.
