The classification of cyclic groups is complete: up to isomorphism, there is exactly one cyclic group of order $r$ for each positive integer $r$, and exactly one infinite cyclic group (isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}$). This makes cyclic groups the best-understood class of groups: their entire structure is determined by a single number (the order, or "infinite").
