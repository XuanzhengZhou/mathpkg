Suppose $a$ is both left regular and right regular. Then there exist $a'$ with $aa' = 1$ (right inverse) and $a''$ with $a''a = 1$ (left inverse). Now compute:

$$a'' = a'' \cdot 1 = a''(aa') = (a''a)a' = 1 \cdot a' = a'.$$

Thus $a' = a''$. This common element is the unique two-sided inverse, denoted $a^{-1}$.
