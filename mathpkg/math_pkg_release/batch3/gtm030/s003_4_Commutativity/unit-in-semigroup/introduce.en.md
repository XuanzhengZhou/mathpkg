A unit (or regular element) in a semigroup with identity is an element that possesses both a left inverse and a right inverse. When both exist, they must coincide, yielding a unique two-sided inverse. This property is the defining characteristic of elements in a group: a group is precisely a semigroup in which every element is a unit.
