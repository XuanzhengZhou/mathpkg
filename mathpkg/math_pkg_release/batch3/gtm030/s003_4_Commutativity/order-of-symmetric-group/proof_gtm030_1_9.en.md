Represent a permutation in the two-row notation. The number in the first position of the second row can be chosen in $n$ different ways. Since no repetitions are allowed in the second row, we have $n-1$ choices for the second position, $n-2$ for the third, etc. Hence in all we have $n!$ symbols and consequently $n!$ elements in $S_n$.
