Let $S$ be an arbitrary set and let $\mathfrak{T}(S)$ be the semi-group of transformations of $S$ into itself. The subgroup $\mathfrak{G}(S)$ of units of $\mathfrak{T}(S)$ is just the set of 1-1 mappings of $S$ onto itself. We call $\mathfrak{G}(S)$ the \text{group of 1-1 transformations} or \text{permutations} of the set $S$.
