The group of permutations (or symmetric group) on a set $S$, denoted $\mathfrak{G}(S)$, consists of all bijective mappings from $S$ to itself. It is the group of units of the semigroup of all transformations of $S$. When $S$ is a finite set of $n$ elements, this group is the familiar symmetric group $S_n$ of degree $n$, which plays a central role in group theory and combinatorics.
