Let $S$ be an arbitrary set. A \text{transformation group} (in $S$) is any subgroup of the group $\mathfrak{G}(S)$ of all bijective (1-1 and onto) transformations of $S$ into itself. The group $\mathfrak{G}(S)$ consists of all bijections $\alpha : S \to S$, with composition as the group operation, identity $1_S$, and inverse $\alpha^{-1}$.
