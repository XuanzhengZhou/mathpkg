Every infinite cyclic group is isomorphic to the additive group of integers $\mathbb{Z}$. The isomorphism is given by $n \mapsto a^n$, which is a bijection when all powers are distinct. This means that up to isomorphism, there is exactly one infinite cyclic group. This classification result is fundamental: the structure of any infinite cyclic group is completely determined by this isomorphism.
