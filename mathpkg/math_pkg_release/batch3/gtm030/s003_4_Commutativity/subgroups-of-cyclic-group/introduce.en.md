Every subgroup of a cyclic group is cyclic. For finite cyclic groups of order $r$, subgroups correspond bijectively to the divisors of $r$: for each divisor $t$ of $r$, there is exactly one subgroup of order $t$. For infinite cyclic groups, subgroups correspond to non-negative integers.
