Let $\mathfrak{Z}$ be a cyclic group of finite order $r$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item The order of any subgroup of $\mathfrak{Z}$ is a divisor of $r$.
\item For every positive divisor $t$ of $r$, $\mathfrak{Z}$ possesses exactly one subgroup of order $t$.
\end{enumerate}

If $\mathfrak{Z}$ is infinite cyclic, then every subgroup is cyclic. The mapping $\mathfrak{W} \mapsto s$ (where $s$ is the smallest positive integer with $a^s \in \mathfrak{W}$) gives a bijection between subgroups of $\mathfrak{Z}$ and non-negative integers.
