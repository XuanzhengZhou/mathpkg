A left identity in a semigroup is an element that, when multiplied on the left by any element, leaves that element unchanged. Not every semigroup possesses a left identity; for example, the positive integers under addition have no identity. A semigroup may have multiple left identities but no right identity, as shown by defining $ab = b$ for all $a, b$ in any set with more than one element.
