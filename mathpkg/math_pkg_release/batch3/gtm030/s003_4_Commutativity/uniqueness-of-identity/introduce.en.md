In a semigroup, the existence of both a left identity and a right identity forces them to be equal, and furthermore it forces the identity to be unique. This is a fundamental result that underpins the definition of a group, where the identity is always unique. The proof is elegantly simple: if $e$ is a left identity and $f$ a right identity, then $ef = f$ (by left identity property) and $ef = e$ (by right identity property), so $e = f$.
