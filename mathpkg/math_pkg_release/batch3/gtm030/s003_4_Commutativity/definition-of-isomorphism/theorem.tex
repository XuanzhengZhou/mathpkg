\textbf{Definition 3.} Two groups $\mathfrak{G}$ and $\mathfrak{G}'$ are said to be \text{isomorphic} if there exists a 1-1 mapping $x \to x'$ of $\mathfrak{G}$ onto $\mathfrak{G}'$ such that
$$(xy)' = x'y'.$$
A mapping satisfying this condition is called an \text{isomorphism} of $\mathfrak{G}$ onto $\mathfrak{G}'$.
