Two groups are isomorphic if there exists a bijective mapping between them that preserves the group operation. Isomorphic groups are "essentially the same" from the abstract viewpoint — they may have different elements, but their algebraic structure is identical. The concept of isomorphism is fundamental throughout algebra: it allows us to classify groups up to structural equivalence rather than by the particular nature of their elements.
