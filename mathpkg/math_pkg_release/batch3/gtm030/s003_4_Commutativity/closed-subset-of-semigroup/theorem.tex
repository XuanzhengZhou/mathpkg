A subset $\mathfrak{S}'$ of a semi-group $\mathfrak{S}$ is said to be \text{closed} if $ab \in \mathfrak{S}'$ for every $a$ and $b$ in $\mathfrak{S}'$. It is clear that the associative law holds in $\mathfrak{S}'$. Hence the pair $(\mathfrak{S}', \cdot)$ consisting of $\mathfrak{S}'$ and the induced mapping $(a, b) \to ab$, $a, b$ in $\mathfrak{S}'$, is a semi-group.
