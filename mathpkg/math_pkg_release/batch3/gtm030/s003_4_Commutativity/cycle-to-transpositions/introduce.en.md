Every cycle of length $r$ can be decomposed into a product of $r-1$ transpositions. The standard decomposition uses a fixed first element: $(i_1 i_2 \cdots i_r) = (i_1 i_2)(i_1 i_3) \cdots (i_1 i_r)$. This formula, combined with the factorization of any permutation into disjoint cycles, shows that every permutation can be written as a product of transpositions, with the number of transpositions congruent modulo 2 to a well-defined invariant (the parity).
