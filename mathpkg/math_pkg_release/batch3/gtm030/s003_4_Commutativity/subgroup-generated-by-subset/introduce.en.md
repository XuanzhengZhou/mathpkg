The subgroup generated by a subset $M$ of a group $\mathfrak{G}$, denoted $[M]$, is defined as the intersection of all subgroups containing $M$. Since the intersection of subgroups is a subgroup, $[M]$ is the unique smallest subgroup containing $M$. This construction is fundamental: it allows us to build subgroups from generating sets, leading to important concepts like cyclic groups (generated by a single element) and finitely generated groups.
