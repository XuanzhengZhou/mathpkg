In a semigroup with identity, the set of all units (elements possessing two-sided inverses) forms a group under the restricted operation. This group, called the group of units, is a key source of groups in algebra. For example, the multiplicative group of nonzero real numbers is the group of units of the semigroup of all real numbers under multiplication.
