Every permutation can be uniquely decomposed (up to order of factors) as a product of disjoint cycles. The algorithm is straightforward: start with any element, follow its orbit under repeated application of the permutation until it returns to the starting point, forming a cycle; then pick an element not yet visited and repeat. This cycle decomposition is a fundamental tool for analyzing permutations, computing their orders, and studying the structure of symmetric groups.
