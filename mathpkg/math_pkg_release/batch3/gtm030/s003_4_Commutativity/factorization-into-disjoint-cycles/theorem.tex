Any permutation $\alpha$ can be written as a product of disjoint cycles. For any $\alpha$, begin with any number $i_1$ in $\{1, 2, \cdots, n\}$ and form $i_1\alpha = i_2, i_2\alpha = i_3, \cdots$, until reaching a repetition. The first such repetition occurs when $i_{r+1} = i_r\alpha = i_1$, giving the cycle $(i_1 i_2 \cdots i_r)$. Repeating this process for remaining numbers yields a factorization of $\alpha$ into disjoint cycles.
