Two groups $\mathfrak{G}$ and $\mathfrak{G}'$ are said to be \text{isomorphic} if there exists a bijective (1-1 and onto) mapping $x \mapsto x'$ of $\mathfrak{G}$ onto $\mathfrak{G}'$ such that

$$(xy)' = x'y'$$

for all $x, y \in \mathfrak{G}$. Such a mapping is called an \text{isomorphism} of $\mathfrak{G}$ onto $\mathfrak{G}'$.
