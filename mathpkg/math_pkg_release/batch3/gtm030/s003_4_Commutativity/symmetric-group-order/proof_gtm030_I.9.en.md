A permutation is written in the two-row notation where the first row lists $1, 2, \cdots, n$ and the second row lists their images. No repetitions are allowed in the second row. The first position in the second row can be chosen in $n$ ways, the second in $n - 1$ ways (excluding the already chosen value), the third in $n - 2$ ways, and so on. Thus the total number of permutations is

$$n (n-1) (n-2) \cdots 2 \cdot 1 = n!$$

and consequently $|S_n| = n!$.
