Let $\mathfrak{S}$ be a semigroup with identity $1$. An element $a$ of $\mathfrak{S}$ is called \text{right regular} if there exists $a' \in \mathfrak{S}$ such that $aa' = 1$; $a'$ is a \text{right inverse} of $a$. \text{Left regular} and \text{left inverse} are defined similarly. An element that is both left regular and right regular is called a \text{unit} (or \text{regular}). In this case, the left inverse and right inverse coincide and this unique element is denoted $a^{-1}$.
