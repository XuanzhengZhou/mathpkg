For a permutation $\alpha$ with disjoint cycle decomposition $\alpha = \gamma_1 \gamma_2 \cdots \gamma_k$ where $\gamma_i$ has length $r_i$, define

$$N(\alpha) = \sum_{i=1}^k (r_i - 1).$$

If $N(\alpha)$ is even (resp. odd), then every factorization of $\alpha$ as a product of transpositions contains an even (resp. odd) number of factors. Thus the parity of a permutation is well-defined.
