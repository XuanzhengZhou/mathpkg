Let $M$ be a non-vacuous subset of a group $\mathfrak{G}$. The subgroup $[M]$ \text{generated by} $M$ is the intersection of all subgroups of $\mathfrak{G}$ that contain $M$. It consists of all finite products $a_1 a_2 \cdots a_n$ ($n \geq 1$) where each $a_i \in M$ or $a_i$ is the inverse of an element of $M$.

When $M = \{a\}$ is a singleton, the subgroup $[a]$ is called the \text{(cyclic) group generated by} $a$, and $a$ is called a \text{generator}. A group $\mathfrak{Z}$ is a \text{cyclic group} if $\mathfrak{Z} = [a]$ for some $a \in \mathfrak{Z}$. The elements of $[a]$ are the integral powers $a^n$ ($n \in \mathbb{Z}$), where $a^0 = 1$ and $a^{-n} = (a^{-1})^n$ for $n > 0$.
