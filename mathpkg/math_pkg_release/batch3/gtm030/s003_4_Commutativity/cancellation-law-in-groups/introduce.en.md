In a group, one can "cancel" common factors from equations: if $ax = ax'$, then $x = x'$ (left cancellation), and if $ya = y'a$, then $y = y'$ (right cancellation). These laws follow directly from the existence of inverses and are a hallmark of group structure. The converse is not generally true — a finite semigroup with cancellation is a group, but an infinite one need not be.
