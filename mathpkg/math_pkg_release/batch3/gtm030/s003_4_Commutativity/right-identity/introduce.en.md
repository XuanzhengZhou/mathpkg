A right identity in a semigroup is an element that, when multiplied on the right by any element, leaves that element unchanged. Together with the concept of a left identity, this leads to the notion of a two-sided identity. A semigroup can have several right identities but no left identities, as demonstrated by defining $ab = a$ for all $a, b$.
