In a Boolean algebra (a complemented distributive lattice), complements are unique, unlike in general complemented lattices. The complementation map $a \mapsto a'$ is an involutive bijection that reverses the order and satisfies the De Morgan laws: the complement of a join is the meet of complements, and vice versa. These properties are fundamental to the duality principle in Boolean algebras and establish complementation as the algebraic analogue of set-theoretic complement.
