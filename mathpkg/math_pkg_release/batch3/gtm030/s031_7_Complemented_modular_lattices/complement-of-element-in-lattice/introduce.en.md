In a bounded lattice with least element $0$ and greatest element $1$, a complement of an element $a$ is an element $a'$ such that the join of $a$ and $a'$ equals $1$ and their meet equals $0$. Not every element in a general bounded lattice has a complement; those lattices in which every element has a complement are called complemented lattices. The complement, when it exists, may not be unique in general lattices, though it is unique in distributive (and hence Boolean) lattices.
