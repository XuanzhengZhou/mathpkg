An integral domain is a ring in which the product of any two non-zero elements is non-zero. Equivalently, the set of non-zero elements forms a sub-semi-group of the multiplicative semi-group. Integral domains generalize the arithmetic property that ab = 0 implies a = 0 or b = 0.
