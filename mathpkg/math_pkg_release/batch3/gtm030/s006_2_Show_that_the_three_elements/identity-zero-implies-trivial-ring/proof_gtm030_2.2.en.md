If the identity $1 = 0$ in a ring $\mathfrak{A}$, then for any element $a \in \mathfrak{A}$:
$$a = a \cdot 1 = a \cdot 0 = 0,$$
where we used the property that $a \cdot 0 = 0$, which holds in every ring. Hence every element equals $0$, and $\mathfrak{A}$ consists of only the zero element.

Taking the contrapositive: if $\mathfrak{A} \neq 0$ (i.e., the ring has more than one element), then $1 \neq 0$.
