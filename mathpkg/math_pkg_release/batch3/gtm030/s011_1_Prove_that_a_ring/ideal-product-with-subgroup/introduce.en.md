This proposition gives sufficient conditions for the product of two additive subgroups of a ring to be an ideal. It generalizes the fact that the product of two left ideals is a left ideal: one factor may be merely a subgroup (not necessarily an ideal) and the product still carries a one-sided ideal structure. When one factor is a left ideal and the other a right ideal, the product becomes a full two-sided ideal.
