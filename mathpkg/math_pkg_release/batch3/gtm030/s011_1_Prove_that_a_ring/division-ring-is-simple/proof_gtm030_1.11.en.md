Let $\mathfrak{D}$ be a division ring and let $\mathfrak{I}$ be a non-zero two-sided ideal of $\mathfrak{D}$. Take any non-zero element $a \in \mathfrak{I}$. Since $\mathfrak{D}$ is a division ring, $a$ has a multiplicative inverse $a^{-1} \in \mathfrak{D}$. Because $\mathfrak{I}$ is a two-sided ideal, $a^{-1}a = 1 \in \mathfrak{I}$. Then for any $x \in \mathfrak{D}$, we have $x = x \cdot 1 \in \mathfrak{I}$, since $\mathfrak{I}$ is an ideal. Hence $\mathfrak{I} = \mathfrak{D}$. Thus $\mathfrak{D}$ has no proper non-zero two-sided ideals, i.e., $\mathfrak{D}$ is simple.
