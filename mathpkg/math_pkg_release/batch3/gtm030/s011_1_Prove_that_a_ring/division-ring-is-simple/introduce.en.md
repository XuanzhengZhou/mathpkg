A division ring is a ring in which every non-zero element has a multiplicative inverse. This theorem states that every division ring is simple as a ring, meaning it contains no two-sided ideals other than the zero ideal and the ring itself. The result follows from the fact that any non-zero element in a two-sided ideal of a division ring would be a unit, forcing the ideal to be the whole ring.
