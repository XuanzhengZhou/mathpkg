The negative of an endomorphism is obtained by composing the endomorphism with the inversion automorphism of the group. Since the composition of two endomorphisms is again an endomorphism, $-\eta$ belongs to the endomorphism set. Together with the zero endomorphism, the existence of negatives ensures that the endomorphisms of an abelian group form an additive group, which is a necessary step in verifying the ring structure.
