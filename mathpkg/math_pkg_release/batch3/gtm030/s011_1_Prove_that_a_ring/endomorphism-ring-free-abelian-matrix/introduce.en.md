This theorem generalizes the infinite cyclic group example to free abelian groups of arbitrary finite rank. Every endomorphism of a free abelian group of rank $n$ can be represented by an $n \times n$ integer matrix. The mapping from endomorphisms to matrices is one-to-one, preserves addition (matrix addition), and preserves composition (matrix multiplication). This shows that the endomorphism ring is isomorphic to the full matrix ring $I_n = M_n(\mathbb{Z})$, establishing a fundamental connection between abelian group theory and matrix algebra.
