Let $\mathfrak{D}$ be a division ring. By the first isomorphism theorem for rings, every homomorphic image of $\mathfrak{D}$ is isomorphic to $\mathfrak{D}/\mathfrak{I}$ for some two-sided ideal $\mathfrak{I}$ of $\mathfrak{D}$. Since $\mathfrak{D}$ is simple (it has no proper non-zero two-sided ideals), the only possibilities for $\mathfrak{I}$ are $\{0\}$ and $\mathfrak{D}$ itself. If $\mathfrak{I} = \{0\}$, then $\mathfrak{D}/\mathfrak{I} \cong \mathfrak{D}$. If $\mathfrak{I} = \mathfrak{D}$, then $\mathfrak{D}/\mathfrak{I}$ is the zero ring. Therefore the only homomorphic images of $\mathfrak{D}$ are $0$ and $\mathfrak{D}$ itself.
