A homomorphic image of a ring is the image under a ring homomorphism, which is isomorphic to a quotient ring modulo the kernel ideal. Since a division ring has no proper non-zero two-sided ideals (it is simple), its only quotient rings are the zero ring (by the unit ideal) and the division ring itself (by the zero ideal). Thus the only homomorphic images are the trivial ones.
