When a commutative group is free abelian of finite rank $n$, each endomorphism can be represented by an $n \times n$ integer matrix with respect to a chosen basis. The $i$-th row of the matrix records the image of the $i$-th basis element expressed as an integer linear combination of basis elements. This matrix representation generalizes the familiar linear algebra correspondence between linear transformations and matrices, now over the integers rather than a field. The mapping from endomorphisms to matrices respects addition and multiplication (composition).
