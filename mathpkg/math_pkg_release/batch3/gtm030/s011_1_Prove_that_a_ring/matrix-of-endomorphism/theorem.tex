Let $\mathfrak{G}$ be a free abelian group with basis $e_1, e_2, \ldots, e_n$, so every element of $\mathfrak{G}$ can be uniquely expressed as $\sum_{i} m_i e_i$ with $m_i \in \mathbb{Z}$. Let $\eta$ be an endomorphism of $\mathfrak{G}$. For each basis element $e_i$, write

$$e_i \eta = \sum_{j=1}^{n} a_{ij} e_j, \quad a_{ij} \in \mathbb{Z}.$$

The $n \times n$ integer matrix $(a_{ij})$ is called the \textbf{matrix of the endomorphism} $\eta$ with respect to the basis $\{e_i\}$.
