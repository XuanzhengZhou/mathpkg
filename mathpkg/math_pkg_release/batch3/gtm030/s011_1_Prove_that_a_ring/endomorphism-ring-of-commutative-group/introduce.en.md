This theorem establishes that the set of all endomorphisms of an abelian group forms a ring under pointwise addition and composition. The additive structure makes $\mathfrak{E}$ into a commutative group, and composition provides an associative multiplication that distributes over addition. The endomorphism ring is a central construction in algebra, as it generalizes the relationship between an abelian group and its symmetries, analogous to how transformation groups describe symmetries in group theory.
