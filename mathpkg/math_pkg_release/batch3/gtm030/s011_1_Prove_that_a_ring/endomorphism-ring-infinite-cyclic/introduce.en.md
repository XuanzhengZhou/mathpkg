This theorem computes the endomorphism ring of the simplest non-trivial abelian group: the infinite cyclic group (the additive group of integers). Every endomorphism corresponds to multiplication by some integer $u$, where the image of the generator $1$ is $u$. Under this correspondence, addition of endomorphisms corresponds to addition of integers, and composition of endomorphisms corresponds to multiplication of integers. This shows that the ring of integers itself arises naturally as an endomorphism ring.
