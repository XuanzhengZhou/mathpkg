Over a Gaussian subring $\mathfrak{g}$ (a unique factorization domain that is integrally closed), an element $a$ of the ambient field is integral if and only if its minimal polynomial has coefficients in $\mathfrak{g}$. The forward direction uses Gauss's Lemma: if $f(x) \in \mathfrak{g}[x]$ is monic with $f(a)=0$, the minimal polynomial $\mu(x)$ over $\mathfrak{J}_0$ divides $f(x)$ in $\mathfrak{J}_0[x]$, and by Gauss's Lemma the irreducible factor of $f$ in $\mathfrak{g}[x]$ must be associate to $\mu(x)$ with leading coefficient 1, forcing $\mu(x) \in \mathfrak{g}[x]$.
