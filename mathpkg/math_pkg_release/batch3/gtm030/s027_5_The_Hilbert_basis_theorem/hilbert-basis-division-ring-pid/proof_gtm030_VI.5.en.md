If $\mathfrak{A}$ is a division ring, the only left ideals are $0$ and $\mathfrak{A}$, so the ascending chain condition holds trivially. If $\mathfrak{A}$ is a principal ideal domain, every left ideal is generated by a single element; hence any ascending chain of left ideals $(a_1) \subseteq (a_2) \subseteq \cdots$ corresponds to a chain of divisibility $a_2 \mid a_1, a_3 \mid a_2, \dots$, which must terminate since $\mathfrak{A}$ is a unique factorization domain. Thus $\mathfrak{A}$ satisfies the ascending chain condition. In both cases, by Corollary 1, every left (right) ideal of $\mathfrak{A}[x_1, \dots, x_r]$ has a finite set of generators.
