If two primary ideals share the same associated prime (radical), then their intersection is again a primary ideal with that same radical. This lemma is used in the proof of the First Uniqueness Theorem to combine primary ideals with coinciding radicals, thereby ensuring that in an irredundant primary decomposition, the associated primes can be taken to be distinct.
