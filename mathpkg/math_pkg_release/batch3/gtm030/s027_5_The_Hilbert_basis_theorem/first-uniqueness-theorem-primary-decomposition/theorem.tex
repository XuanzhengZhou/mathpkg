Let $\mathfrak{B} = \mathfrak{Q}_1 \cap \mathfrak{Q}_2 \cap \cdots \cap \mathfrak{Q}_r = \mathfrak{Q}_1' \cap \mathfrak{Q}_2' \cap \cdots \cap \mathfrak{Q}_s'$ be two irredundant intersections of $\mathfrak{B}$ into primary ideals whose associated primes (radicals $\mathfrak{P}_i = \mathfrak{R}(\mathfrak{Q}_i)$ and $\mathfrak{P}_j' = \mathfrak{R}(\mathfrak{Q}_j')$) are distinct. Then $r = s$ and the sets of associated primes of the two decompositions are identical:
$$\{\mathfrak{P}_1, \dots, \mathfrak{P}_r\} = \{\mathfrak{P}_1', \dots, \mathfrak{P}_s'\}.$$
