The integral closure $\mathfrak{G}$ of a subring $\mathfrak{g}$ in $\mathfrak{A}$ (the set of all $\mathfrak{g}$-integral elements) is itself integrally closed. This "transitivity of integrality" uses the module criterion (Theorem 7): if $a$ is integral over $\mathfrak{G}$ with equation $a^n = g_0 + g_1 a + \cdots + g_{n-1}a^{n-1}$ where $g_i \in \mathfrak{G}$, then there exists a finitely generated $\mathfrak{g}$-submodule containing all monomials in the $g_i$, and all powers of $a$ lie in a larger finitely generated $\mathfrak{g}$-module, hence $a \in \mathfrak{G}$.
