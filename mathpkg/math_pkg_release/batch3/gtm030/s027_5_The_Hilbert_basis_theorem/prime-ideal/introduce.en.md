A prime ideal generalizes the notion of a prime element from principal ideal domains to arbitrary commutative rings. An ideal $\mathfrak{P}$ is prime if whenever the product of two elements lies in $\mathfrak{P}$, at least one of the factors must belong to $\mathfrak{P}$. Equivalently, the quotient ring $\mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{P}$ is an integral domain. In the ring of integers, the prime ideals are exactly $(p)$ for prime numbers $p$ and the zero ideal.
