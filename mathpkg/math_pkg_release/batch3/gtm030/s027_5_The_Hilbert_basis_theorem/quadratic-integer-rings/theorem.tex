Let $m$ be a square-free integer, let $\theta^2 = m$, and let $R_0(\theta) = \{\alpha + \beta\theta \mid \alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{Q}\}$ be the quadratic field obtained by adjoining $\theta$ to $\mathbb{Q}$.

If $m \equiv 2$ or $3 \pmod{4}$, then the ring $\mathfrak{G}$ of algebraic integers of $R_0(\theta)$ is
$$\mathfrak{G} = \{\alpha + \beta\theta \mid \alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{Z}\}.$$

If $m \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$, then the ring of integers is
$$\mathfrak{G} = \left\{\alpha + \beta\theta \;\middle|\; \alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{Z}\right\} \cup \left\{\alpha + \beta\theta \;\middle|\; \alpha = \frac{2n+1}{2},\; \beta = \frac{2q+1}{2},\; n,q \in \mathbb{Z}\right\}.$$

That is, when $m \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$, the integers also include elements where both $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are halves of odd integers.
