This theorem classifies the ring of algebraic integers $\mathfrak{G}$ in a quadratic field $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{m})$ for square-free $m$. Using Theorem 11, an element $u = \alpha + \beta\theta$ is integral over $\mathbb{Z}$ if and only if its trace $T(u) = 2\alpha$ and norm $N(u) = \alpha^2 - \beta^2 m$ are integers. Analysis of these conditions yields two cases: when $m \equiv 2,3 \pmod{4}$, the integer ring is simply $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{m}]$; when $m \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$, the integer ring includes additional elements with half-integer coefficients, namely $\mathbb{Z}[(1+\sqrt{m})/2]$.
