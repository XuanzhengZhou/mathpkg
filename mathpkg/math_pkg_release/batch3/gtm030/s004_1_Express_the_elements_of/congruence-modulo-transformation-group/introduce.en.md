A transformation group $\mathfrak{G}$ acting on a set $S$ induces a natural equivalence relation on $S$. Two elements $x, y \in S$ are congruent modulo $\mathfrak{G}$ if one can be mapped to the other by some element of $\mathfrak{G}$. This relation is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive, as follows directly from the group axioms. The equivalence classes under this relation are called the transitivity sets of $S$ relative to $\mathfrak{G}$.
