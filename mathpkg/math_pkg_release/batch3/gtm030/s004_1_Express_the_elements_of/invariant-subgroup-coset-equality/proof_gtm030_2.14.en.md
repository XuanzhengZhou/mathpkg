Suppose $\mathfrak{H}$ is invariant, i.e., $y^{-1}\mathfrak{H}y \subseteq \mathfrak{H}$ for all $y \in \mathfrak{G}$. Then for any $h \in \mathfrak{H}$, $y^{-1}hy \in \mathfrak{H}$, so $hy \in y\mathfrak{H}$. This shows $\mathfrak{H}y \subseteq y\mathfrak{H}$ for all $y$. Replacing $y$ by $y^{-1}$, we get $\mathfrak{H}y^{-1} \subseteq y^{-1}\mathfrak{H}$. Multiplying on the left and right by $y$ yields $y\mathfrak{H} \subseteq \mathfrak{H}y$. Together with $\mathfrak{H}y \subseteq y\mathfrak{H}$, we obtain $\mathfrak{H}y = y\mathfrak{H}$ for all $y \in \mathfrak{G}$.
