A subgroup $\mathfrak{H}$ of $\mathfrak{G}$ is called \textit{invariant} (or \textit{normal}) if for every $y \in \mathfrak{G}$ and every $h \in \mathfrak{H}$, $y^{-1}hy \in \mathfrak{H}$. Equivalently, $\mathfrak{H}y = y\mathfrak{H}$ for all $y \in \mathfrak{G}$; that is, the right coset determined by any element coincides with the left coset determined by that element.
