An invariant subgroup (also called a normal subgroup) $\mathfrak{H}$ of $\mathfrak{G}$ is one that is closed under conjugation by any element of $\mathfrak{G}$. The defining condition $y^{-1}\mathfrak{H}y \subseteq \mathfrak{H}$ for all $y \in \mathfrak{G}$ is equivalent to the equality of left and right cosets: $\mathfrak{H}y = y\mathfrak{H}$. Invariant subgroups are precisely those subgroups for which the set of cosets can be made into a group, called the factor group $\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{H}$.
