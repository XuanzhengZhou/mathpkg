This exercise tests understanding of well-definedness of maps on cosets. The rule $x\mathfrak{H} \mapsto x\mathfrak{H}$ attempts to map each right coset to a left coset using the same representative $x$. The question is whether this gives a well-defined function, i.e., whether $x\mathfrak{H} = y\mathfrak{H}$ implies $\mathfrak{H}x = \mathfrak{H}y$. This is not true in general and holds precisely when $\mathfrak{H}$ is invariant.
