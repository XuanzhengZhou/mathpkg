This corollary follows directly from Lagrange's theorem: the order of the cyclic subgroup generated by any element $x$ divides the order $n$ of the group. If $m$ is the order of $[x]$, then $x^m = 1$ and $n = mr$ for some integer $r$, so $x^n = (x^m)^r = 1$. This result is fundamental to finite group theory.
