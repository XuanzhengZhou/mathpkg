If $\mathfrak{H}$ is an invariant subgroup of $\mathfrak{G}$, then $(x\mathfrak{H})(y\mathfrak{H}) = x\mathfrak{H}y\mathfrak{H} = xy\mathfrak{H}\mathfrak{H} = xy\mathfrak{H}$. Hence the set of cosets of $\mathfrak{H}$ is closed under set multiplication. The collection $\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{H}$ of cosets with this composition is a group: the associative law holds, $\mathfrak{H}$ is the identity, and $x^{-1}\mathfrak{H}$ is the inverse of $x\mathfrak{H}$.
