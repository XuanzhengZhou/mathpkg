The product $AB$ of two subsets $A$ and $B$ of a group $\mathfrak{G}$ is defined as the set of all pairwise products $ab$ with $a \in A$ and $b \in B$. This notation is associative: $(AB)C = A(BC)$. It provides a convenient algebraic language for expressing cosets: the right coset of $x$ relative to $\mathfrak{H}$ is simply $x\mathfrak{H}$, and the left coset is $\mathfrak{H}x$.
