Let $\mathfrak{H}_1$ and $\mathfrak{H}_2$ be two subgroups of $\mathfrak{G}$. Show that any coset relative to $\mathfrak{H}_1 \cap \mathfrak{H}_2$ is the intersection of a coset relative to $\mathfrak{H}_1$ with a coset relative to $\mathfrak{H}_2$. Use this result to prove Poincaré's theorem: if $\mathfrak{H}_1$ and $\mathfrak{H}_2$ have finite index in $\mathfrak{G}$, then so does $\mathfrak{H}_1 \cap \mathfrak{H}_2$.
