An isomorphism into is an injective homomorphism from one group to another. It identifies the domain group with a subgroup of the codomain. When the map is also surjective, it is an isomorphism onto, and the two groups are abstractly identical. The natural map from a group to its factor group is a homomorphism onto but is not an isomorphism into (unless the subgroup is trivial).
