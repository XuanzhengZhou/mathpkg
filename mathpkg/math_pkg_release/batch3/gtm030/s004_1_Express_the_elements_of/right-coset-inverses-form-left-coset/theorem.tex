The set of inverses of the elements in any right coset $x\mathfrak{H}$ constitutes a left coset. Specifically, for $(xh)^{-1} = h^{-1}x^{-1} \in \mathfrak{H}x^{-1}$ and as $h$ ranges over $\mathfrak{H}$, $h^{-1}x^{-1}$ ranges over $\mathfrak{H}x^{-1}$. Thus the left coset $\mathfrak{H}x^{-1}$ is uniquely determined by $x\mathfrak{H}$. Moreover, the correspondence $x\mathfrak{H} \mapsto \mathfrak{H}x^{-1}$ is one-to-one.
