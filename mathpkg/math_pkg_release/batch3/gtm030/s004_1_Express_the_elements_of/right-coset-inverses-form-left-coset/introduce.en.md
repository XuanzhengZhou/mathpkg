There is a natural bijection between the set of right cosets and the set of left cosets of a subgroup $\mathfrak{H}$, given by mapping each right coset $x\mathfrak{H}$ to the left coset $\mathfrak{H}x^{-1}$ formed by the inverses of its elements. This shows that the number of right cosets equals the number of left cosets, i.e., the index of $\mathfrak{H}$ is well-defined regardless of which side is used.
