The right cosets of a subgroup $\mathfrak{H}$ form a partition of the group $\mathfrak{G}$. Every element of $\mathfrak{G}$ belongs to exactly one right coset, and two cosets are either identical or disjoint. This partition theorem is the foundation for Lagrange's theorem and the entire theory of coset decompositions. The analogous statement holds for left cosets.
