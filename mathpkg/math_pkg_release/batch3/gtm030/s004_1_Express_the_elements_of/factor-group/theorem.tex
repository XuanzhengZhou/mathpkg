Let $\mathfrak{H}$ be an invariant subgroup of $\mathfrak{G}$. The collection $\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{H}$ of cosets of $\mathfrak{H}$ with the composition $(x\mathfrak{H})(y\mathfrak{H}) = xy\mathfrak{H}$ forms a group, called the \textit{factor group} (or \textit{quotient group}) of $\mathfrak{G}$ relative to $\mathfrak{H}$. The coset $\mathfrak{H}$ acts as the identity, and the inverse of $x\mathfrak{H}$ is $x^{-1}\mathfrak{H}$.
