A ring is an algebraic structure with two binary operations — addition and multiplication — that generalizes the arithmetic of integers. The additive structure forms a commutative group, the multiplicative structure forms a semigroup, and the two operations are linked by the distributive laws. Rings are fundamental objects in abstract algebra, with examples including the integers, matrix rings, and polynomial rings.
