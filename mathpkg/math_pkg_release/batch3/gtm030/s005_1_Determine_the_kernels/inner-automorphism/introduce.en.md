An inner automorphism of a group is the conjugation map by a fixed element: $x \mapsto a^{-1}xa$. It is always a bijective homomorphism and thus an automorphism. Inner automorphisms form a subgroup of the full automorphism group, and the map $a \mapsto C_a$ is a homomorphism from the group to its automorphism group.
