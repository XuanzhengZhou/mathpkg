From the formula $\beta^{-1} \alpha \beta = (i_1 \beta \; i_2 \beta \; \cdots \; i_r \beta) \cdots$, it is clear that if $r > 1$ and $n > 2$, then $\beta$ can be chosen so that $\beta^{-1} \alpha \beta \neq \alpha$. Hence, if $\alpha \neq 1$, then there exists a $\beta$ such that $\beta \alpha \neq \alpha \beta$. This shows that the center of $S_n$, $n > 2$, consists only of the identity element.
