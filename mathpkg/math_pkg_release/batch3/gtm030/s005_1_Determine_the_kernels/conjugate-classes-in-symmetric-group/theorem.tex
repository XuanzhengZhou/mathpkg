Two permutations $\alpha$ and $\alpha'$ are conjugates in $S_n$ if and only if the associated sets of cycle lengths $r, s, \cdots, u$ (a partition of $n$) are the same for these two permutations. Hence there is a $1$-$1$ correspondence between the conjugate classes in $S_n$ and the partitions of $n$. The number of conjugate classes coincides with the number $p(n)$ of distinct partitions of $n$.
