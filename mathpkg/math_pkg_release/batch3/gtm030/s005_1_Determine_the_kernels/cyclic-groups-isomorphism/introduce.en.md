Every cyclic group is determined up to isomorphism by its order. If a cyclic group is infinite, it is isomorphic to the additive group of integers $I_+$. If it is finite of order $m$, it is isomorphic to the quotient $I_+/[m]$. Consequently, any two cyclic groups of the same order are isomorphic to each other.
