For any given ring $(\mathfrak{A}, +, \cdot)$, define a new multiplication $\times$ on the set $\mathfrak{A}$ by
$$a \times b = b \cdot a.$$
Then $(\mathfrak{A}, +, \times)$ is a ring, and the identity mapping $\iota: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{A}$ is an anti-isomorphism of $(\mathfrak{A}, +, \cdot)$ onto $(\mathfrak{A}, +, \times)$.
