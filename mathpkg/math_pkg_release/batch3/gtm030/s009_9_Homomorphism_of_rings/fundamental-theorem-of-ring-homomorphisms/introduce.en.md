The Fundamental Theorem of Ring Homomorphisms (Theorem 6) is the ring-theoretic analogue of the First Isomorphism Theorem for groups. It states that every ring homomorphism factors through the quotient by its kernel: any homomorphism $$\eta: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{A}'$$ can be written as the composition of the natural projection $$\nu: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{K}$$ followed by an injective homomorphism (in fact, an isomorphism into) $$\bar{\eta}: \mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{K} \to \mathfrak{A}'$$. More generally, the theorem holds when factoring through any ideal $$\mathfrak{B}$$ contained in the kernel, with injectivity precisely when $$\mathfrak{B}$$ equals the kernel.
