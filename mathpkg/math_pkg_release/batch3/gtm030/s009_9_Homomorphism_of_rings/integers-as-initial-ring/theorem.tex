Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be any ring with identity $1$. The mapping $n \mapsto n \cdot 1$ from the ring of integers $I = \mathbb{Z}$ into $\mathfrak{A}$ is a ring homomorphism. Its kernel is the ideal $(m)$ for some integer $m \geq 0$, and the image is a subring of $\mathfrak{A}$ isomorphic to $I/(m)$. Consequently,

$$\mathfrak{A} \supseteq \text{a subring isomorphic to } I/(m).$$

In particular, if $\mathfrak{A}$ is generated by its identity as a ring, then $\mathfrak{A} \cong I/(m)$. Thus either $\mathfrak{A}$ is infinite and isomorphic to the ring of integers $I$ (when $m = 0$), or $\mathfrak{A}$ is finite with $m$ elements and isomorphic to the finite ring $I/(m)$ (when $m > 0$).
