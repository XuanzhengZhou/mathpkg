The characteristic of a ring is a numerical invariant that captures the additive behavior of the ring's identity element. If every element of the additive group $(\mathfrak{A},+)$ has finite order and there is a maximum such order $m > 0$, then $m$ is the characteristic. Otherwise, the ring has characteristic $0$. The characteristic governs how the ring of integers $\mathbb{Z}$ maps into $\mathfrak{A}$ via $n \mapsto n \cdot 1$.
