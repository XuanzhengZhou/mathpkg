A ring isomorphism is a bijective ring homomorphism. When two rings are isomorphic, they are algebraically indistinguishable — every ring-theoretic property of one holds in the other. The isomorphism relation is an equivalence relation on the class of rings, as the composition of isomorphisms is an isomorphism and the inverse of an isomorphism is again an isomorphism.
