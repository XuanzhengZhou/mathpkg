Theorem 4 establishes that the homomorphic image of a ring is always a subring of the target ring. The proof is straightforward: since a ring homomorphism is also a homomorphism of the underlying additive groups, the image is an additive subgroup; and because the homomorphism preserves multiplication, the image is closed under the product operation. This theorem parallels the corresponding result for groups.
