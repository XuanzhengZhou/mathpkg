For matrices over a commutative ring, the transpose operation is an anti-isomorphism of the matrix ring onto itself. While it preserves addition, the transpose of a product is the product of the transposes in reverse order. This provides a canonical example of an anti-isomorphism and shows that every matrix ring over a commutative ring is anti-isomorphic to itself.
