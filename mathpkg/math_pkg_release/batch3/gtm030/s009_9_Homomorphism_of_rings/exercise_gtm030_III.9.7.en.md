Show that, if $a \mapsto \bar{a}$ is an anti-isomorphism of $\mathfrak{R}$ onto $\bar{\mathfrak{R}}$, then the mapping
$$(a) \mapsto (\bar{a})',$$
where the $(i,j)$ element of $(\bar{a})'$ is $\bar{a}_{ji}$, is an anti-isomorphism of $\mathfrak{R}_n$ onto $\bar{\mathfrak{R}}_n$.
