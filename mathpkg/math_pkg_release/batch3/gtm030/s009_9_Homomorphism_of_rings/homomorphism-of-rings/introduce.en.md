A ring homomorphism is a structure-preserving map between two rings that respects both addition and multiplication. It is simultaneously a homomorphism of the underlying additive groups and a map that sends products to products: the image of a sum is the sum of the images, and the image of a product is the product of the images. Ring homomorphisms are the natural morphisms in the category of rings, playing a role analogous to that of group homomorphisms in group theory.
