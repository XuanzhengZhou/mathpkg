This exercise establishes two fundamental identities relating least common multiples and greatest common divisors in a Gaussian semi-group. The first identity $(a, b) \cdot [a, b] \sim a b$ shows that the product of the gcd and lcm of two elements is their product (up to units), generalizing the familiar integer identity $\gcd(a,b) \cdot \operatorname{lcm}(a,b) = |ab|$. The second identity $[a, (b, c)] \sim ([a, b], [a, c])$ is a distributive law showing that the lcm distributes over the gcd (up to associates), dual to the gcd-associativity established in Lemma 2.
