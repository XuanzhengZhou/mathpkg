An element $m$ in a commutative semi-group is called a \textit{least common multiple} (l.c.m.) of elements $a$ and $b$ if:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $a \mid m$ and $b \mid m$,
  \item if $n$ is any element such that $a \mid n$ and $b \mid n$, then $m \mid n$.
\end{enumerate}

Prove that any two elements of a Gaussian semi-group have a least common multiple.
