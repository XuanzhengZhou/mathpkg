An element $m$ is called a least common multiple (l.c.m.) of the elements $a$ and $b$ if $a \mid m$ and $b \mid m$, and if $n$ is any element such that $a \mid n$ and $b \mid n$, then $m \mid n$. Prove that any two elements of a Gaussian semi-group have a l.c.m.
