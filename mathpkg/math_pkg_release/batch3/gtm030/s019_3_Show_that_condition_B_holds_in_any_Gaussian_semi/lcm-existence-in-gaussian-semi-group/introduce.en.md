This exercise asks the reader to prove that in a Gaussian semi-group (one possessing unique factorization into irreducibles), any two elements possess a least common multiple. The proof typically uses the unique factorization of the two elements into prime powers and constructs the l.c.m. by taking the maximum exponent for each prime factor. This result is dual to the existence of greatest common divisors established in the section.
