Lemma 4 is the critical step in proving that Condition C implies Condition B (every irreducible is prime). It states that if an element is coprime (i.e., has g.c.d. one) with each of two elements individually, then it is also coprime to their product. This is an analogue of the Euclidean lemma in the context of a general commutative semi-group possessing greatest common divisors. The proof relies on the associativity and distributivity properties of the g.c.d. established in Lemmas 2 and 3.
