Condition C is the third in a series of three fundamental properties (A, B, C) that characterize the arithmetic of commutative semi-groups. While Condition A asserts the existence of factorization into irreducibles and Condition B asserts that every irreducible is prime, Condition C requires that every pair of elements possesses a greatest common divisor. Jacobson shows in this section that Condition C together with Condition A implies Condition B, and consequently a semi-group satisfying A and C is Gaussian (i.e., has unique factorization).
