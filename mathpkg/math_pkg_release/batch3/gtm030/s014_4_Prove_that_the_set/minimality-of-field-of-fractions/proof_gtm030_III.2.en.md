Let $\mathfrak{F}$ be the field of fractions of the commutative integral domain $\mathfrak{A}$, and let $\overline{\mathfrak{A}} = \{\overline{a} = ab/b : a \in \mathfrak{A}\}$ be the isomorphic copy of $\mathfrak{A}$ in $\mathfrak{F}$.

Take any element $a/b \in \mathfrak{F}$. Then
$$a/b = (ab/b)(b/b^2) = (ab/b)(b^2/b)^{-1} = \overline{a} \cdot \overline{b}^{-1}.$$
Thus every element of $\mathfrak{F}$ can be expressed as a quotient of two elements from $\overline{\mathfrak{A}}$. The subfield of $\mathfrak{F}$ generated by $\overline{\mathfrak{A}}$ therefore equals $\mathfrak{F}$ itself. Since any field containing an isomorphic copy of $\mathfrak{A}$ must contain a subfield isomorphic to $\mathfrak{F}$ that is generated by (the image of) $\mathfrak{A}$, $\mathfrak{F}$ is minimal in this sense.
