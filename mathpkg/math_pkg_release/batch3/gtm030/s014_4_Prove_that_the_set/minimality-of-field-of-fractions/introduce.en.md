The field of fractions $\mathfrak{F}$ of an integral domain $\mathfrak{A}$ is minimal among all fields containing an isomorphic copy of $\mathfrak{A}$. This follows from the fact that any element $a/b$ equals $\overline{a} \cdot \overline{b}^{-1}$ where $\overline{a} = ab/b$ and $\overline{b} = b^2/b$ are the canonical images in the subring $\overline{\mathfrak{A}} \cong \mathfrak{A}$. Thus the subfield generated by $\overline{\mathfrak{A}}$ is $\mathfrak{F}$ itself, establishing minimality.
