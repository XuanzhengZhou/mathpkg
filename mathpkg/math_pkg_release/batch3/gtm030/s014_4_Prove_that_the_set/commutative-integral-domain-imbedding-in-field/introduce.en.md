Theorem 2 states that every non-zero commutative integral domain $\mathfrak{A}$ can be embedded in a field. The proof constructs the field of fractions $\mathfrak{F}$ from equivalence classes of pairs $(a, b)$ with $b \neq 0$ in $\mathfrak{A}$. The mapping $a \mapsto \overline{a} = ab/b$ (for any $b \neq 0$) defines an injective ring homomorphism from $\mathfrak{A}$ into $\mathfrak{F}$, establishing that $\mathfrak{A}$ is isomorphic to a subring of $\mathfrak{F}$. This is a fundamental result generalizing the construction of $\mathbb{Q}$ from $\mathbb{Z}$.
