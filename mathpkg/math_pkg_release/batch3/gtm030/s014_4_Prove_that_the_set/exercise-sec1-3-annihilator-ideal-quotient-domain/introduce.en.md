This exercise studies the annihilator $\mathfrak{Z} = \{z \in \mathfrak{B} : z\mathfrak{A} = 0\}$ in the ring extension $\mathfrak{B}$ constructed in Section 1. It asks to prove that $\mathfrak{Z}$ is an ideal and that the quotient $\mathfrak{B}/\mathfrak{Z}$ is an integral domain with identity. This shows how factoring out the annihilator eliminates zero-divisors from the extension ring.
