This exercise generalizes the field of fractions construction to the localization of a commutative ring at a multiplicative set $S$ of non-zero-divisors. The construction produces a ring extension in which every element of $S$ becomes a unit (invertible). A key observation is that the product of two non-zero-divisors is again a non-zero-divisor, so the set $S$ forms a multiplicative system (sub-semigroup), and the fraction construction carries through.
