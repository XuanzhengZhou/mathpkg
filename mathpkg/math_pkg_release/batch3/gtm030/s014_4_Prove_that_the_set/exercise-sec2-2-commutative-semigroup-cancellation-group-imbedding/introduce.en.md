This exercise generalizes the field of fractions construction to the purely multiplicative setting: any commutative semigroup with the cancellation law (i.e., $ab = ac \implies b = c$) can be embedded in a group. The construction mirrors the field of fractions by taking equivalence classes of ordered pairs $(a, b)$ with the relation $(a, b) \sim (c, d) \iff ad = bc$, yielding the Grothendieck group of the semigroup.
