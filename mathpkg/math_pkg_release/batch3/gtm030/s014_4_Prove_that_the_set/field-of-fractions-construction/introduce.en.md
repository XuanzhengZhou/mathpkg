The field of fractions (or field of quotients) of a commutative integral domain $\mathfrak{A}$ is the smallest field containing an isomorphic copy of $\mathfrak{A}$. It is constructed by taking equivalence classes of ordered pairs $(a, b)$ with $b \neq 0$, where $(a, b) \sim (c, d)$ exactly when $ad = bc$. The equivalence class $a/b$ generalizes the notion of a rational number from the integers. Addition and multiplication are defined by the familiar fraction arithmetic: $a/b + c/d = (ad+bc)/bd$ and $(a/b)(c/d) = ac/bd$. This construction shows that every integral domain can be embedded in a field, analogous to how $\mathbb{Z}$ is embedded in $\mathbb{Q}$.
