Any commutative integral domain $\mathfrak{A} \neq 0$ can be imbedded in a field. Specifically, the set $\mathfrak{F}$ of equivalence classes of fractions $a/b$ ($a \in \mathfrak{A}, b \in \mathfrak{A}, b \neq 0$) under the equivalence relation $a/b \sim a'/b' \iff ab' = ba'$, with operations

$$\frac{a}{b} + \frac{c}{d} = \frac{ad + bc}{bd}, \qquad \frac{a}{b} \cdot \frac{c}{d} = \frac{ac}{bd},$$

forms a field, and the map $a \mapsto a/1$ is an isomorphism of $\mathfrak{A}$ onto a subring of this field.
