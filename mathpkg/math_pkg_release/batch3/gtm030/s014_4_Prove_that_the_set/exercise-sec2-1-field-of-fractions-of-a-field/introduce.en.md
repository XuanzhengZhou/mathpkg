If $\mathfrak{A}$ is already a field, applying the field of fractions construction yields nothing new: the resulting field $\mathfrak{F}$ is exactly the isomorphic image $\overline{\mathfrak{A}}$ of $\mathfrak{A}$. This exercise verifies that the field of fractions construction is idempotent on fields.
