Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a ring and $m$ a positive integer such that $ma = 0$ for all $a \in \mathfrak{A}$. Let $\mathfrak{C}$ be the set of pairs $(\bar{n}, a)$ where $\bar{n}$ is a residue class mod $m$ and $a \in \mathfrak{A}$, with addition $(\bar{n}, a) + (\bar{q}, b) = (\bar{n} + \bar{q}, a + b)$ and multiplication $(\bar{n}, a)(\bar{q}, b) = (\bar{n}\bar{q}, nb + qa + ab)$. Show that multiplication is single-valued and that $\mathfrak{C}$ is a ring with an identity which is an extension of $\mathfrak{A}$ and that $mc = 0$ for all $c \in \mathfrak{C}$.
