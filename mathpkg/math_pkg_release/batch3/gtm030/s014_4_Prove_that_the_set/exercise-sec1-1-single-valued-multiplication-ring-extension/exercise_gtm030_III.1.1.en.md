Define multiplication in $\mathfrak{C}$ by
$$(\bar{n}, a)(\bar{q}, b) = (\bar{n}\bar{q}, nb + qa + ab).$$
Show that:
1. Multiplication is single-valued (well-defined).
2. $\mathfrak{C}$ is a ring with an identity.
3. $\mathfrak{C}$ is an extension of $\mathfrak{A}$ (i.e., $\mathfrak{A}$ is isomorphic to a subring of $\mathfrak{C}$).
4. $mc = 0$ for all $c \in \mathfrak{C}$, where $m$ is the characteristic associated with the quotient used for $\bar{n}, \bar{q}$.
