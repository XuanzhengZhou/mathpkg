The least common multiple (LCM) of two integers $a$ and $b$ is the smallest positive integer that is a multiple of both $a$ and $b$. It can be expressed in terms of the greatest common divisor $(a,b)$ via the formula $m = ab/(a,b)$. The defining property is twofold: $m$ is a common multiple of $a$ and $b$, and any other common multiple of $a$ and $b$ is itself a multiple of $m$.
