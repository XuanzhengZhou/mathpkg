The Division Algorithm states that for any integer $a$ and any non-zero integer $b$, there exist unique integers $q$ (the quotient) and $r$ (the remainder) satisfying $a = bq + r$ with $0 \leq r < |b|$. This result establishes that division with remainder is always possible in the ring of integers. It is the foundational result for elementary number theory, providing the basis for the Euclidean algorithm, greatest common divisors, and ultimately the fundamental theorem of arithmetic.
