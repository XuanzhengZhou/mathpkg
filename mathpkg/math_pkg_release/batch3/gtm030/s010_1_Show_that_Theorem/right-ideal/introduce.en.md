A right ideal is an additive subgroup of a ring that is closed under right multiplication by arbitrary ring elements. If $B$ is a right ideal in a ring $A$, then for any $a \in A$ and $b \in B$, the product $ba$ remains in $B$. Together with left ideals, right ideals form the basic building blocks for studying the two-sided ideal structure of non-commutative rings.
