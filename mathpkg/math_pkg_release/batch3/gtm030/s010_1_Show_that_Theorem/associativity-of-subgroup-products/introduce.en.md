The product operation on additive subgroups of a ring is associative. This follows from the characterization of subgroup products as sets of finite sums of triple products: both $(AB)C$ and $A(BC)$ consist precisely of all finite sums $\sum a_i b_i c_i$ with $a_i \in A$, $b_i \in B$, $c_i \in C$. This associativity is essential for the algebra of ideals in ring theory.
