In the additive group of a ring, one can multiply subgroups by considering all products of elements. Given subgroups $A$ and $B$, the product $AB$ is defined as the subgroup generated by all products $ab$ with $a \in A$ and $b \in B$. A key observation is that this product coincides with the set of all finite sums $\sum a_i b_i$, which provides a concrete description of the subgroup structure.
