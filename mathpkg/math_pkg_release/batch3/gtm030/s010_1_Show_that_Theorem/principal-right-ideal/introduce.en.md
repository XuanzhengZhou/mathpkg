A principal right ideal $(b)_r$ is the smallest right ideal containing a given element $b$ of a ring. In rings with identity, it consists of all right multiples $bx$. In rings without identity, one must also include integer multiples $nb$ to ensure the set forms a right ideal. The parallel definition to principal left ideals provides a complete picture of one-sided principal ideals in general rings.
