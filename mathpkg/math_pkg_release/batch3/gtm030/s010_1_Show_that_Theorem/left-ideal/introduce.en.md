A left ideal is a special type of additive subgroup of a ring that is closed under left multiplication by arbitrary ring elements. If $B$ is a left ideal in a ring $A$, then for any $a \in A$ and $b \in B$, the product $ab$ remains in $B$. Left ideals generalize the notion of the set of left multiples of an element and play a fundamental role in the structure theory of non-commutative rings.
