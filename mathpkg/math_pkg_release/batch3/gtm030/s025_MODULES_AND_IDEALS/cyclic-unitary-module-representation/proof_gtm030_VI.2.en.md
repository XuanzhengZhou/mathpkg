This follows directly from Theorem 1 by taking the generating set $X$ to be a singleton $\{x\}$. By Theorem 1, every element of $\mathfrak{M}$ can be written in the form $a_1 x_1 + \cdots + a_r x_r$ with $a_i \in \mathfrak{A}$ and $x_i \in X$. Since $X = \{x\}$, each $x_i = x$, and the expression reduces to $(a_1 + \cdots + a_r)x = a x$ where $a = a_1 + \cdots + a_r \in \mathfrak{A}$. Hence every element of $\mathfrak{M}$ is a multiple $ax$ of the single generator $x$. Furthermore, since $\mathfrak{M}$ is unitary, $x$ itself can be written as $x = e x$ for a suitable $e \in \mathfrak{A}$.
