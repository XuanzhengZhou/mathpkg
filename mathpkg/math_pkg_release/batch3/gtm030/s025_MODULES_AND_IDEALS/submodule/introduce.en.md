A submodule of an $\mathfrak{A}$-module $\mathfrak{M}$ is an $\mathfrak{A}$-subgroup, i.e., an additive subgroup $\mathfrak{N}$ of $\mathfrak{M}$ that is closed under the scalar action of $\mathfrak{A}$. Submodules are the natural substructures in module theory, analogous to subgroups in group theory. Key examples arise from homomorphisms: the image of a module homomorphism is a submodule of the codomain, and the kernel is a submodule of the domain. An important special case is that the submodules of the left module of a ring $\mathfrak{A}$ (the ring acting on its own additive group) are exactly the left ideals of $\mathfrak{A}$.
