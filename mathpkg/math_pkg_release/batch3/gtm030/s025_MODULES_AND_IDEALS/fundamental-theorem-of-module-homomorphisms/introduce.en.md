The fundamental theorem of module homomorphisms states that for any $\mathfrak{A}$-module homomorphism $\eta: \mathfrak{M} \to \mathfrak{M}'$, the image $\mathfrak{M}\eta$ is isomorphic to the quotient $\mathfrak{M} / \ker \eta$. This is the natural generalization of the first isomorphism theorem for groups and rings to the module setting. The kernel and image of a module homomorphism are both submodules, making the theorem a central tool for analyzing the structure of modules via homomorphisms.
