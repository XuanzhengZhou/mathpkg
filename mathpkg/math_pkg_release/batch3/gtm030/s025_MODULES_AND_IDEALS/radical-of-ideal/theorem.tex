The \text{radical} $\mathfrak{R}$ of an ideal $\mathfrak{B}$ in a commutative ring $\mathfrak{A}$ is the set of elements $z \in \mathfrak{A}$ such that $z^m \equiv 0 \pmod{\mathfrak{B}}$ for some positive integer $m$. The radical $\mathfrak{R}$ is an ideal containing $\mathfrak{B}$, and in a Noetherian ring, $\mathfrak{R}^N \subseteq \mathfrak{B}$ for some $N$.
