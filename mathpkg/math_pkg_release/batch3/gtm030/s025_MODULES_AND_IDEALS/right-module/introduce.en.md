A right module over a ring $\mathfrak{A}$ is a commutative group $M$ where the ring acts on the right: the mapping $a \mapsto a_r$ sending each $a \in \mathfrak{A}$ to the endomorphism $x \mapsto xa$ is a ring homomorphism (rather than an anti-homomorphism as in the left module case). The defining axioms are right-distributivity $(x+y)a = xa + ya$, left-distributivity over scalar addition $x(a+b) = xa + xb$, and associativity $x(ab) = (xa)b$. For commutative rings, left and right modules coincide. In general, any left $\mathfrak{A}$-module can be regarded as a right module over an anti-isomorphic ring $\mathfrak{A}'$, and vice versa.
