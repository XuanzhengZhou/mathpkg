The left module of a ring $\mathfrak{A}$ is the canonical construction that turns the additive group $(\mathfrak{A}, +)$ of the ring itself into a left $\mathfrak{A}$-module by using the ring multiplication as the scalar action. This is the analogue of a group acting on itself by left translation. An important consequence is that the submodules of this module are exactly the left ideals of $\mathfrak{A}$, providing a unified perspective between ideal theory and module theory.
