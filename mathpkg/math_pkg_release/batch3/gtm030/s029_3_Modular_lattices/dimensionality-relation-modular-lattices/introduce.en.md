The fundamental dimensionality relation for modular lattices of finite length states that $l(a \cup b) = l(a) + l(b) - l(a \cap b)$, where $l(x)$ denotes the length (rank) of the principal ideal generated by $x$. This formula is the lattice-theoretic generalization of the familiar dimension formula for subspaces of a vector space: $\dim(U + W) = \dim U + \dim W - \dim(U \cap W)$. The proof relies on Theorem 4, which asserts that the intervals $I[a \cup b, a]$ and $I[b, a \cap b]$ are isomorphic and therefore have equal length.
