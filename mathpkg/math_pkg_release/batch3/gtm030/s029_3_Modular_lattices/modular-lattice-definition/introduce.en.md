A modular lattice, also called a Dedekind lattice, is a lattice satisfying the modular law $L_5$: whenever $a \geq b$, the distributive identity $a \cap (b \cup c) = b \cup (a \cap c)$ holds. This condition is strictly weaker than full distributivity, yet strong enough to guarantee many structural results. Many of the most important lattices occurring in algebra -- such as the lattice of invariant subgroups of a group, the lattice of submodules of a module, and the lattice of ideals of a ring -- are modular but not distributive.
