This lemma is the lattice-theoretic analogue of Zassenhaus' lemma (also known as the butterfly lemma or third isomorphism theorem) from group theory. It states that for any four elements of a modular lattice satisfying the natural ordering conditions, three particular intervals are projective (i.e., connected by a chain of transposed intervals). The lemma is the crucial technical ingredient in the proof of the Schreier refinement theorem for modular lattices.
