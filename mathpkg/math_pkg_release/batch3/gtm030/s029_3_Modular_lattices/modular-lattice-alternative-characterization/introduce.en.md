Theorem 3 gives an equivalent formulation of modularity: a lattice is modular exactly when equality of joins and meets with a common third element forces equality of two comparable elements. This characterization can be seen as a cancellation property and is often easier to verify in concrete lattice-theoretic settings than the $L_5$ condition directly.
