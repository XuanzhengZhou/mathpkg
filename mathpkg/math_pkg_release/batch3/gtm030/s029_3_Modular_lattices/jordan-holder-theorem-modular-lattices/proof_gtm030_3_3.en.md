By the Schreier refinement theorem (Theorem 5), any two finite descending chains connecting $a$ and $b$ have equivalent refinements. A composition chain is, by definition, a chain in which each successive element is a cover of the next, meaning that each interval $I[a_i, a_{i+1}]$ has length 1. Consequently, a composition chain admits no proper refinement -- any refinement must simply insert repeated elements, which does not change the set of quotient intervals. Therefore, if two composition chains between $a$ and $b$ are given, their equivalent refinements must be essentially the same as the original chains (up to insertion of repetitions). Hence the original composition chains themselves are equivalent. The descending and ascending chain conditions guarantee the existence of composition chains and that they have finite length.
