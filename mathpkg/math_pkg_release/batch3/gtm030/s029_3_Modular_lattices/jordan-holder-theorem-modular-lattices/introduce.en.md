Theorem 6 is the lattice-theoretic Jordan-Hölder theorem. In a modular lattice satisfying both the ascending and descending chain conditions, any two composition chains between the same endpoints are equivalent -- that is, their successive quotient intervals can be paired into projective (hence isomorphic) pairs. The proof follows from the Schreier refinement theorem, since composition chains by definition admit no proper refinement. This yields the fundamental dimensionality relation for modular lattices of finite length.
