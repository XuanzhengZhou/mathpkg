Let $L$ be a modular lattice. A \textit{composition chain} connecting $a, b$ with $a > b$ is a finite sequence

$$a = a_1 > a_2 > a_3 > \cdots > a_{n+1} = b$$

in which each $a_i$ is a cover of $a_{i+1}$.

\textbf{Theorem 6 (Jordan-Hölder).} Assume $L$ satisfies the descending chain condition (no infinite properly descending chain $a_1 > a_2 > a_3 > \cdots$) and the ascending chain condition (no infinite properly ascending chain $a_1 < a_2 < a_3 < \cdots$). Then any two composition chains between $a$ and $b$ are equivalent.
