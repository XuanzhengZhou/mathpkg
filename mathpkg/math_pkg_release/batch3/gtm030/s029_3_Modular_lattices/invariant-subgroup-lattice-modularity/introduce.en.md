Theorem 2 establishes that the lattice of invariant (normal) subgroups of any group is modular. This is the primary reason modular lattices are important for applications to algebra. As immediate consequences, the lattice of invariant $M$-subgroups of any $M$-group, the lattice of submodules of any module, and the lattices of left, right, and two-sided ideals of any ring are all modular. However, the lattice of all subgroups of a group is generally not modular.
