Let $\tilde{\Pi} = \prod_{\alpha \in J} \mathfrak{G}_{\alpha}$ be the complete direct product of the $M$-groups $\mathfrak{G}_{\alpha}$, and let $\mathfrak{H}$ be a subgroup of $\tilde{\Pi}$. For each $\alpha \in J$, the projection mapping $h \mapsto h(\alpha)$ is a homomorphism of $\mathfrak{H}$ into $\mathfrak{G}_{\alpha}$; its image $\mathfrak{H}_{\alpha}$ is a subgroup of $\mathfrak{G}_{\alpha}$. Then $\mathfrak{H}$ is called a **subdirect product** of the $\mathfrak{G}_{\alpha}$ if $\mathfrak{H}_{\alpha} = \mathfrak{G}_{\alpha}$ for all $\alpha \in J$, that is, if the homomorphism $h \mapsto h(\alpha)$ is surjective for every $\alpha \in J$. In any case, $\mathfrak{H}$ is a subdirect product of the image groups $\mathfrak{H}_{\alpha}$.
