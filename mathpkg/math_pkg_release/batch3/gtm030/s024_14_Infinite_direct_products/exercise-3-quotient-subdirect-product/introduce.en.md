This exercise shows that any $M$-group with a family of invariant subgroups intersecting trivially can be embedded as a subdirect product of the corresponding quotient groups. The natural map $g \mapsto (\cdots, g \mathfrak{R}_{\alpha}, \cdots)$ sends each element to the tuple of its cosets; the trivial intersection condition ensures injectivity, while the definition of the quotient guarantees that each projection is surjective onto $\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{R}_{\alpha}$.
