The complete direct product, also called the unrestricted direct product, endows the product set with pointwise operations inherited from each factor group. The identity element is the constant function mapping every $\alpha$ to $1_{\mathfrak{G}_\alpha}$, and inverses are taken pointwise. When the index set $J$ is finite, the complete direct product coincides with the usual finite direct product. For infinite $J$, the complete direct product contains elements with infinitely many non-identity components.
