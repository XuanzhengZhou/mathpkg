This exercise extends the primary decomposition from Exercise 1 to the ring setting. When the commutative torsion group is the additive group of a ring, each primary component is not just a subgroup but an ideal, and the ring becomes a direct sum of these ideals. Moreover, the product of distinct primary components is zero, reflecting the fact that adding elements of coprime orders cannot produce interactions across the ideals.
