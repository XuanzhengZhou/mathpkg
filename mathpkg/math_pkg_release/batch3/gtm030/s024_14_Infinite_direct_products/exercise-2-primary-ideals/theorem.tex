Let $\mathfrak{G}$ be the additive group of a ring, and suppose $\mathfrak{G}$ is a commutative torsion group (no elements of infinite order). For each prime $p$, let $\mathfrak{G}_p$ be the set of elements of $\mathfrak{G}$ of order a power of $p$. Then each $\mathfrak{G}_p$ is an ideal of the ring. Hence the ring $\mathfrak{G}$ is the direct sum

$$\mathfrak{G} = \sum_p \oplus \mathfrak{G}_p,$$

and $\mathfrak{G}_p \mathfrak{G}_q = 0$ whenever $p \neq q$.
