Let $\tilde{\Pi} = \prod_{\alpha \in J} \mathfrak{G}_{\alpha}$ be the complete direct product of the groups $\mathfrak{G}_{\alpha}$. The **restricted direct product** (also called the direct product with finite support), denoted by $\prod_{\alpha \in J} \mathfrak{G}_{\alpha}$, is the subset of elements $g \in \tilde{\Pi}$ satisfying:

$$g(\alpha) = 1 \quad \text{for all but a finite number of } \alpha \in J.$$

This set is closed under multiplication and contains the identity; it is a subgroup of the complete direct product $\tilde{\Pi}$.
