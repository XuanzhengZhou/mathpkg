The restricted direct product consists of those elements of the complete direct product that differ from the identity in only finitely many coordinates. When the index set $J$ is finite, the restricted and complete direct products coincide. For infinite $J$, the restricted product is a proper subgroup. In the additive setting for abelian groups or modules, this construction is called the **direct sum** and denoted $\bigoplus_{\alpha \in J} \mathfrak{G}_{\alpha}$.
