Let $\mathfrak{G}$ be a commutative group without elements of infinite order (a torsion group). For each prime $p$, let $\mathfrak{G}_p$ be the subset of elements of $\mathfrak{G}$ whose order is a power of $p$. Then $\mathfrak{G}_p$ is a subgroup of $\mathfrak{G}$, and

$$\mathfrak{G} = \prod_p \mathfrak{G}_p,$$

the restricted direct product of the primary components $\mathfrak{G}_p$ over all primes $p$.
