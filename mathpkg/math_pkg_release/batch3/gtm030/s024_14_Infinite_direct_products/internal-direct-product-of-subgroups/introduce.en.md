This theorem gives an internal criterion for recognizing when a group is isomorphic to the direct product of certain of its subgroups. The two conditions require that the subgroups together generate the whole group (completeness) and that each subgroup intersects the product of the others only in the identity (independence). When these hold, every element of $\mathfrak{G}$ can be uniquely expressed as a finite product of elements from distinct $\mathfrak{G}_{\alpha}'$, mirroring the external direct product construction.
