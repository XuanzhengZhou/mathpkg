The ascending chain condition (ACC) for modules requires that every strictly increasing sequence of submodules eventually stabilizes. A module satisfying the ascending chain condition is called a **Noetherian module**, named after Emmy Noether. The ACC is equivalent to the maximum condition and, as shown in Theorem 2 of this section, to the condition that every submodule is finitely generated. The ascending chain condition on ideals is the foundational hypothesis for the ideal decomposition theorems in polynomial rings over a field.
