The descending chain condition (DCC) for modules requires that every strictly decreasing sequence of submodules eventually stabilizes. A module satisfying the descending chain condition is called an **Artinian module**, named after Emil Artin. The DCC is equivalent to the minimum condition, which asserts that every nonempty collection of submodules contains a minimal element. This finiteness condition plays a fundamental role in the structure theory of rings and modules, particularly in the decomposition theory of Noetherian and Artinian rings.
