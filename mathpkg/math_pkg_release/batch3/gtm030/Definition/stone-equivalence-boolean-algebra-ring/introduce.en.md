Stone's Theorem establishes a categorical equivalence between the category of Boolean algebras and the category of Boolean rings with identity. The translation from a Boolean algebra to a ring uses symmetric difference as addition and meet as multiplication; the reverse translation recovers the lattice operations and complementation from the ring operations. This equivalence allows one to apply ring-theoretic methods to problems in lattice theory and logic, and vice versa.
