Fermat's Little Theorem states that for any prime $p$ and any integer $a$ not divisible by $p$, the $(p-1)$-th power of $a$ is congruent to $1$ modulo $p$. Equivalently, $a^p \equiv a \pmod{p}$ holds for all integers $a$, which is a trivial statement when $p$ divides $a$. The theorem is a direct corollary of the Euler-Fermat theorem applied to the multiplicative group of the finite field $\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z}$, where the group has order $p-1$.
