Let $\eta$ be a homomorphism of the ring $\mathfrak{A}$ into the ring $\mathfrak{A}'$ with kernel $\mathfrak{R}$, and let $\mathfrak{B}$ be an ideal of $\mathfrak{A}$ contained in $\mathfrak{R}$. Then the correspondence $a + \mathfrak{B} \mapsto a\eta$ defines a homomorphism $\bar{\eta}$ of the ring $\bar{\mathfrak{A}} = \mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{B}$ into $\mathfrak{A}'$, and $\eta = \nu\bar{\eta}$ where $\nu$ is the natural homomorphism of $\mathfrak{A}$ onto $\bar{\mathfrak{A}}$. The mapping $\bar{\eta}$ is 1-1 if and only if $\mathfrak{B} = \mathfrak{R}$. In this case $\bar{\eta}$ is an isomorphism of $\mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{R}$ into $\mathfrak{A}'$.
