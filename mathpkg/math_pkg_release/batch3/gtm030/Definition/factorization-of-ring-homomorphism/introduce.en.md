This fundamental theorem for ring homomorphisms establishes the factorization of any ring homomorphism through its kernel. Given a homomorphism $\eta: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{A}'$ with kernel $\mathfrak{R}$, and any ideal $\mathfrak{B} \subseteq \mathfrak{R}$, the map factors as $\eta = \nu \circ \bar{\eta}$, where $\nu: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{B}$ is the natural projection and $\bar{\eta}: \mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{B} \to \mathfrak{A}'$ is an induced homomorphism. When $\mathfrak{B} = \mathfrak{R}$, the induced map is an isomorphism onto its image.
