Show that, if $a \mapsto \bar{a}$ is an anti-isomorphism of a commutative ring $\Re$ onto $\Re$, then the mapping $(a) \mapsto (\bar{a})'$, where the $(i,j)$-element of $(\bar{a})'$ is $\bar{a}_{ji}$ (the transpose), is an anti-isomorphism of $\Re_n$ onto $\Re_n$.
