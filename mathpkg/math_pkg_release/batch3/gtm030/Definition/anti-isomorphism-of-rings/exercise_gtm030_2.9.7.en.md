Show that, if $a \mapsto \bar{a}$ is an anti-isomorphism of a commutative ring $\Re$ onto itself, then the mapping $(a) \mapsto (\bar{a})'$, where the $(i,j)$-element of $(\bar{a})'$ is $\bar{a}_{ji}$, is an anti-isomorphism of the matrix ring $\Re_n$ onto itself.
