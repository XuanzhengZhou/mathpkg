An anti-isomorphism of rings is a bijective mapping that preserves addition but reverses the order of multiplication. That is, it sends products to products in reversed order. Every commutative ring is anti-isomorphic to itself via any isomorphism. For noncommutative rings, the transpose map on matrix rings provides a canonical example of an anti-isomorphism.
