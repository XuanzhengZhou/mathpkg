A zero-ring is a ring in which the product of any two elements is the zero element. Given any commutative group, one can define a trivial multiplication $ab = 0$ to obtain a zero-ring. The existence of zero-rings demonstrates that the additive structure of a ring can be essentially arbitrary without additional constraints on multiplication. However, imposing conditions such as the existence of a multiplicative identity forces constraints on the additive group.
