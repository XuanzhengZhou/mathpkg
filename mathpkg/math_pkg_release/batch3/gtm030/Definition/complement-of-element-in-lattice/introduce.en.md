The complement of an element $a$ in a bounded lattice is an element $a'$ that acts as a "dual" to $a$: their join is the top element and their meet is the bottom element. In a complemented lattice, every element has at least one complement. The relative complement generalizes this notion to intervals $[0, a]$ within the lattice. In Boolean algebras, complements are unique, but in general complemented lattices they need not be.
