Let $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{A}'$ be rings. A mapping $\eta: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{A}'$ is called a \text{homomorphism} if
$$(a + b)\eta = a\eta + b\eta, \quad (ab)\eta = (a\eta)(b\eta).$$

If $\eta$ is 1-1, it is called an \text{isomorphism}. Two rings $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{A}'$ are said to be \text{isomorphic} ($\mathfrak{A} \cong \mathfrak{A}'$) if there exists an isomorphism of $\mathfrak{A}$ onto $\mathfrak{A}'$.

An isomorphism of a ring onto itself is called an \text{automorphism}.
