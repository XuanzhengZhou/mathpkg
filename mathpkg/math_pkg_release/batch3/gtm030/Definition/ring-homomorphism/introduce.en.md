A ring homomorphism is a structure-preserving map between two rings that respects both addition and multiplication. It generalizes the notion of group homomorphism to rings by additionally requiring the preservation of multiplicative structure. When a homomorphism is bijective, it is called an isomorphism, establishing that the two rings have identical algebraic structure. An isomorphism from a ring to itself is an automorphism, representing a symmetry of the ring. The isomorphism relation is an equivalence relation on the class of rings.
