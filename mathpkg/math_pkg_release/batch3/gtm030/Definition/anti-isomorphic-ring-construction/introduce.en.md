Given any ring, one can construct an anti-isomorphic copy by simply reversing the order of multiplication; this yields the so-called opposite ring. The verification that the reversed multiplication is associative follows from $(a \times b) \times c = c(ba) = (cb)a = a \times (b \times c)$. This construction shows that every ring has a naturally anti-isomorphic counterpart, and it is fundamental in the study of non-commutative ring theory.
