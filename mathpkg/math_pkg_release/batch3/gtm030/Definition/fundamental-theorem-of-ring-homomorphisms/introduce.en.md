The Fundamental Theorem of Ring Homomorphisms provides a factorization of any ring homomorphism $\eta: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{A}'$ through the quotient ring by an ideal contained in the kernel. For any ideal $\mathfrak{B} \subseteq \ker \eta$, the induced map $\bar{\eta}: \mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{B} \to \mathfrak{A}'$ is a well-defined ring homomorphism. The most important special case takes $\mathfrak{B} = \ker \eta$, yielding an isomorphism $\mathfrak{A}/\ker \eta \cong \operatorname{im} \eta$, which is the ring-theoretic analogue of the first isomorphism theorem for groups.
