Let $\eta$ be a homomorphism of the ring $\mathfrak{A}$ into the ring $\mathfrak{A}'$ with kernel $\mathfrak{R}$, and let $\mathfrak{B}$ be an ideal of $\mathfrak{A}$ contained in $\mathfrak{R}$. Then the mapping
$$a + \mathfrak{B} \mapsto a\eta$$
defines a ring homomorphism $\bar{\eta}: \mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{B} \to \mathfrak{A}'$. Moreover, $\eta = \nu \bar{\eta}$ where $\nu$ is the natural homomorphism of $\mathfrak{A}$ onto $\mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{B}$.

In particular, taking $\mathfrak{B} = \mathfrak{R}$, we obtain a factorization $\eta = \nu \bar{\eta}$ where $\nu: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{R}$ is the natural homomorphism and $\bar{\eta}: \mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{R} \to \mathfrak{A}'$ is an isomorphism onto the image $\mathfrak{A}\eta$.
