A Gaussian semigroup is the most general algebraic setting in which unique factorization into irreducibles holds. It requires three conditions: commutativity with identity and cancellation, existence of a factorization into irreducibles for every non-unit, and essential uniqueness of such factorizations. This generalizes the property of the integers (or any UFD) to the purely multiplicative setting of semigroups. In Gaussian semigroups, one can determine all factors of a given element up to unit multiples, once a factorization into irreducibles is known.
