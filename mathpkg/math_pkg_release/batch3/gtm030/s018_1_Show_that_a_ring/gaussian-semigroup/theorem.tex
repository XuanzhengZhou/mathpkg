**Definition 1.** A semigroup $\mathfrak{S}$ is called **Gaussian** if

(1) $\mathfrak{S}$ is commutative, has an identity, and satisfies the cancellation law;

(2) every non-unit of $\mathfrak{S}$ can be factored into a product of irreducible elements;

(3) factorization into irreducible elements is essentially unique.
