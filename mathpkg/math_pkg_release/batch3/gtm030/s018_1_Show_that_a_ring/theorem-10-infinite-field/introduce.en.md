Theorem 10 states that over an infinite field, a non-zero polynomial cannot vanish everywhere: there always exists some choice of field elements that yields a non-zero evaluation. Equivalently, the coordinate functions $s_1, \cdots, s_r$ are algebraically independent over the constant functions when the coefficient field is infinite. This contrasts sharply with the finite field case, where the polynomial $\prod_{a \in \mathfrak{F}}(x - a)$ is non-zero yet vanishes on every element. The result is fundamental for distinguishing formal polynomials from polynomial functions.
