Two elements $a$ and $b$ in a commutative semigroup are called associates, denoted $a \sim b$, if each divides the other. Equivalently, $a$ and $b$ differ by multiplication by a unit. This is an equivalence relation that partitions the semigroup into associate classes. In factorization theory, factorizations differing only by associates are regarded as essentially the same, since unit factors are the trivial ambiguities in any multiplicative decomposition.
