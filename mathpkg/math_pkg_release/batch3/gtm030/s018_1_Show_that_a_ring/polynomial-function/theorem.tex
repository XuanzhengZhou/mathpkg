Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a commutative ring with identity. The ring of functions $(\mathfrak{A}, \mathfrak{A})$ consists of all mappings $f : \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{A}$ with pointwise addition and multiplication.

If $f(x_1, \cdots, x_r) \in \mathfrak{A}[x_1, \cdots, x_r]$ where the $x_i$ are algebraically independent, the corresponding \text{polynomial function} $f(s_1, \cdots, s_r)$ is obtained by evaluating $f$ at the identity functions $s_i : \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{A}$ defined by $s_i(a) = a$. The set of all such functions is $\mathfrak{A}[s_1, \cdots, s_r]$.
