Let $S$ be an arbitrary non-vacuous set and let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an arbitrary ring. The totality $(\mathfrak{A}, S)$ of functions with domain $S$ and range contained in $\mathfrak{A}$ consists of all mappings $f: S \to \mathfrak{A}$, $s \mapsto f(s)$. Equality $f = g$ means $f(s) = g(s)$ for all $s \in S$. Addition and multiplication are defined pointwise:

$$(f + g)(s) = f(s) + g(s), \quad (fg)(s) = f(s)g(s).$$

The constant functions (mapping every $s \in S$ to a fixed element of $\mathfrak{A}$) constitute a subring of $(\mathfrak{A}, S)$ isomorphic to $\mathfrak{A}$. If $\mathfrak{A}$ has an identity, the associated constant function acts as the identity in the whole ring $(\mathfrak{A}, S)$.
