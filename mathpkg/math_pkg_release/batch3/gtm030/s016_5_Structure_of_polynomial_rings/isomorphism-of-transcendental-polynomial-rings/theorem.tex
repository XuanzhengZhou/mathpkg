If $x$ and $y$ are transcendental over $\mathfrak{A}$, then $\mathfrak{A}[x]$ and $\mathfrak{A}[y]$ are isomorphic. More generally, any ring of the form $\mathfrak{A}[u]$ for arbitrary $u$ is isomorphic to a difference ring $\mathfrak{A}[x]/\mathfrak{R}$ where $\mathfrak{R}$ is an ideal in $\mathfrak{A}[x]$ with $\mathfrak{A} \cap \mathfrak{R} = 0$, and $x$ is transcendental.
