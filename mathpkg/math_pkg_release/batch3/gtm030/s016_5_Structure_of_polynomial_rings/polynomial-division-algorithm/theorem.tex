Let $g(x) = b_0 + b_1x + \cdots + b_mx^m$ be a non-zero polynomial in $\mathfrak{A}[x]$ whose leading coefficient $b_m$ is a unit. Then for any $f(x) \in \mathfrak{A}[x]$, there exist unique polynomials $q_1(x), r_1(x) \in \mathfrak{A}[x]$ such that $\deg r_1(x) < \deg g(x)$ and
$$f(x) = q_1(x)g(x) + r_1(x).$$
Similarly, there exist unique $q_2(x), r_2(x)$ such that $\deg r_2(x) < \deg g(x)$ and
$$f(x) = g(x)q_2(x) + r_2(x).$$
