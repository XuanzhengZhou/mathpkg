A simple transcendental extension of a field $\mathfrak{F}$ is obtained by first forming the polynomial ring $\mathfrak{F}[x]$ and then taking its field of fractions $\mathfrak{F}(x)$. The resulting field consists of all rational functions in the transcendental element $x$ with coefficients in $\mathfrak{F}$. This construction parallels the construction of the rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$ from the integers $\mathbb{Z}$.
