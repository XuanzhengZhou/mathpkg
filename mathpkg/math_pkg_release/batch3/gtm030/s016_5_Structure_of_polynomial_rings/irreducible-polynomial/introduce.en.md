An irreducible polynomial over a field is a non-constant polynomial that cannot be factored as a product of two polynomials, each of positive degree. Irreducible polynomials play the role of prime numbers in the polynomial ring $\mathfrak{F}[x]$ and are essential for constructing field extensions via Kronecker's method.
