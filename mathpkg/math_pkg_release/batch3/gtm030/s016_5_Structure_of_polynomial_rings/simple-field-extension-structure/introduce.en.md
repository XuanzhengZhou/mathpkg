Any field can be understood as a tower of simple extensions starting from its prime field. Each step adjoins a single element, which may be transcendental (forming a field of rational functions) or algebraic (forming a quotient of the polynomial ring by an irreducible polynomial). This structural decomposition, together with the classification of prime fields, gives a complete description of the architecture of arbitrary fields.
