Let $f(x) = a_0 + a_1 x + \cdots + a_n x^n$ with $a_n \neq 0$, so $\deg f = n$, and $g(x) = b_0 + b_1 x + \cdots + b_m x^m$ with $b_m \neq 0$, so $\deg g = m$. The product $f(x)g(x)$ has leading term $a_n b_m x^{n+m}$. Since $\mathfrak{A}$ is an integral domain, $a_n \neq 0$ and $b_m \neq 0$ imply $a_n b_m \neq 0$, so $\deg(fg) = n + m = \deg f + \deg g$. If either $f = 0$ or $g = 0$, the result follows from the convention $\deg 0 = -\infty$.
