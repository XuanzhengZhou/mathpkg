Let $\mathfrak{F}$ be a field and $f(x) \in \mathfrak{F}[x]$ an irreducible (prime) polynomial of positive degree. Form the difference ring $\mathfrak{E} = \mathfrak{F}[x]/(f(x))$ where $(f(x))$ denotes the principal ideal generated by $f(x)$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathfrak{E}$ is a field (a field extension of $\mathfrak{F}$).
\item The natural homomorphism $\mathfrak{F}[x] \to \mathfrak{E}$ induces an isomorphism $\mathfrak{F} \cong \overline{\mathfrak{F}} \subset \mathfrak{E}$, and we identify $a \in \mathfrak{F}$ with the coset $\overline{a} = a + (f(x))$.
\item The element $\overline{x} = x + (f(x))$ is algebraic over $\mathfrak{F}$ and satisfies $f(\overline{x}) = 0$.
\item $\mathfrak{E} = \mathfrak{F}[\overline{x}]$, a simple algebraic extension.
\end{enumerate}
