Let $\mathfrak{F}$ be a field and let $[[1]]$ be the subring generated by the identity $1$. Then exactly one of the following holds:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(I)] $[[1]] \cong I$ (the ring of integers). In this case $\mathfrak{F}$ has characteristic $0$, and the prime field $\mathfrak{P}$ is isomorphic to the field of rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$, consisting of all elements $(m1)(n1)^{-1}$ with $m, n \in I$, $n \neq 0$.
\item[(II)] $[[1]] \cong I/(p)$ where $p$ is a prime. In this case $\mathfrak{F}$ has characteristic $p$, and $[[1]]$ itself is already a field, hence equals the prime field $\mathfrak{P}$.
\end{enumerate}
