In a polynomial ring over an integral domain, a polynomial is a unit (invertible) if and only if it is a constant whose constant term is already a unit in the coefficient ring. This follows directly from the additivity of degree with respect to multiplication. Over the integers, the only units are $\pm 1$; over a field, every non-zero constant is a unit.
