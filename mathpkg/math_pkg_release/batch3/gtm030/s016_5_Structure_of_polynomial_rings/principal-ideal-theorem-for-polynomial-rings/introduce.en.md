The polynomial ring $\mathfrak{F}[x]$ over a field $\mathfrak{F}$ is a principal ideal domain: every ideal is generated by a single polynomial. The proof uses the division algorithm and the fact that every non-zero element of a field is a unit. A non-zero ideal is generated by any non-zero polynomial of minimal degree within it.
