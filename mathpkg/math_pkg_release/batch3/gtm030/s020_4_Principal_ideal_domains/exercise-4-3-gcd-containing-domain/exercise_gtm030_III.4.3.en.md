Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a principal ideal domain and let $\mathfrak{B}$ be any commutative integral domain containing $\mathfrak{A}$. Show that, if the elements $a, b \in \mathfrak{A}$ have a greatest common divisor $d \in \mathfrak{A}$, then $d$ is also a greatest common divisor of $a$ and $b$ in $\mathfrak{B}$.
