Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a Gaussian domain and let $x_1, x_2, \dots, x_r$ be algebraically independent over $\mathfrak{A}$. Then the polynomial ring $\mathfrak{A}[x_1, x_2, \dots, x_r]$ is also a Gaussian domain.

In particular:
\begin{itemize}
\item If $\mathfrak{F}$ is any field, then $\mathfrak{F}[x_1, x_2, \dots, x_r]$ is Gaussian.
\item $I[x_1, x_2, \dots, x_r]$ (where $I = \mathbb{Z}$) is Gaussian.
\end{itemize}

Note that for $r > 1$, the rings $\mathfrak{F}[x_1, \dots, x_r]$ are not principal ideal domains. For $r \geq 1$, $I[x_1, \dots, x_r]$ is also not a principal ideal domain. Hence the class of Gaussian domains is strictly larger than the class of principal ideal domains.
