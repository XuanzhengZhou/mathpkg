Theorem 4 states that if $\mathfrak{A}$ is a Gaussian domain (UFD) and $x$ is transcendental over $\mathfrak{A}$, then the polynomial ring $\mathfrak{A}[x]$ is also Gaussian. The proof uses Gauss's Lemma: every non-zero polynomial factors as a constant times a primitive polynomial, and primitive polynomials factor into irreducibles of positive degree. The uniqueness of factorization follows from comparing factorizations in $\mathfrak{A}[x]$ with those in the polynomial ring over the field of fractions $\mathfrak{J}[x]$, using Gauss's Lemma to relate irreducibility in the two rings.
