Prove the following irreducibility criterion due to Eisenstein: If $f(x) = a_0 + a_1x + \cdots + a_nx^n \in I[x]$ is primitive and there exists a prime $p$ in $I$ such that $p \mid a_0, p \mid a_1, \cdots, p \mid a_{n-1}$ but $p \nmid a_n$ and $p^2 \nmid a_0$, then $f(x)$ is irreducible in $I[x]$ and hence in $R_0[x]$, $R_0$ the field of rational numbers.
