Schreier's refinement theorem states that any two normal series of an $M$-group can be refined to equivalent normal series -- series whose factors can be paired as isomorphic $M$-groups. The proof uses the third isomorphism theorem to construct the refinements: given series $\mathfrak{G} = \mathfrak{G}_1 \supseteq \cdots \supseteq \mathfrak{G}_{s+1} = 1$ and $\mathfrak{G} = \mathfrak{H}_1 \supseteq \cdots \supseteq \mathfrak{H}_{t+1} = 1$, one inserts the intersections $(\mathfrak{G}_i \cap \mathfrak{H}_k)\mathfrak{G}_{i+1}$ and $(\mathfrak{G}_i \cap \mathfrak{H}_k)\mathfrak{H}_{k+1}$, and the third isomorphism theorem gives $\mathfrak{G}_{ik}/\mathfrak{G}_{i,k+1} \cong \mathfrak{H}_{ki}/\mathfrak{H}_{k,i+1}$.
