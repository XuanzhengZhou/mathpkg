A composition series is a normal series that cannot be properly refined — each term is maximal in the previous one, so the factors are simple $M$-groups. Not every $M$-group admits a composition series. A simple commutative group must be cyclic of prime order, and consequently any ordinary commutative group with a composition series is necessarily finite. An $M$-group that does have a composition series has its composition factors uniquely determined, up to isomorphism and order, by the Jordan-Hölder theorem.
