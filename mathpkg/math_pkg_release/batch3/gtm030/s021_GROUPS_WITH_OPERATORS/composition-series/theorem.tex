A \textbf{composition series} for an $M$-group $\mathfrak{G}$ is a normal series

$$\mathfrak{G} = \mathfrak{G}_1 \supseteq \mathfrak{G}_2 \supseteq \cdots \supseteq \mathfrak{G}_{s+1} = 1$$

with the property that each $\mathfrak{G}_{i+1}$ is maximal in $\mathfrak{G}_i$. Equivalently, a composition series is a normal series whose factors $\mathfrak{G}_i/\mathfrak{G}_{i+1}$ are simple $M$-groups $\neq 1$.

An $M$-group need not have a composition series. For example, if $M$ is vacuous and $\mathfrak{G}$ is an infinite commutative group, then $\mathfrak{G}$ does not have a composition series.
