This alternative definition of a group with operators replaces the mapping $(a,m) \mapsto am$ with a mapping $m \mapsto \bar{m}$ from the operator set $M$ into the set of endomorphisms of $\mathfrak{G}$. The two definitions are equivalent: from the first definition, $x \mapsto xm$ defines an endomorphism $\bar{m}$, and conversely, given endomorphisms $\bar{m}$, one defines $am = a\bar{m}$. This formulation is particularly convenient for constructing examples, such as taking $M$ to be the set of inner automorphisms, all automorphisms, or all endomorphisms of $\mathfrak{G}$ with the identity mapping.
