Let $\mathfrak{G}$ and $\mathfrak{G}'$ be $M$-groups. A homomorphism $\eta : \mathfrak{G} \to \mathfrak{G}'$ is an \text{$M$-homomorphism} if

$$(am)\eta = (a\eta)m$$

for all $a \in \mathfrak{G}$ and $m \in M$. Equivalently, $\eta$ commutes with all operator endomorphisms: $\bar{m} \eta = \eta \bar{m}$.
