The Jordan-Hölder theorem is a cornerstone of group theory, establishing that the composition factors of an $M$-group are uniquely determined up to isomorphism and order. The proof follows directly from Schreier's refinement theorem: the two composition series have equivalent refinements, but since a composition series cannot be properly refined (any refinement merely inserts factors equal to 1), the original series are already equivalent after pairing off the trivial factors.
