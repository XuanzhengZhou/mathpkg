This is the analogue for $M$-groups of the fundamental theorem of homomorphisms for ordinary groups. It establishes a two-way relationship: factor groups by invariant $M$-subgroups yield $M$-homomorphic images, and every $M$-homomorphic image is $M$-isomorphic to a factor group. The factorization $\eta = \nu\bar{\eta}$ carries over, where $\nu$ is the natural $M$-homomorphism onto the factor group and $\bar{\eta}$ is an $M$-isomorphism precisely when the kernel equals the subgroup by which one factors.
