As a special case of Theorem 2, take $\mathfrak{G}'$ to be the $M$-factor group $\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{R}$ and $\eta = \nu$ the natural $M$-homomorphism. If $\mathfrak{H}$ is an invariant $M$-subgroup of $\mathfrak{G}$ containing $\mathfrak{R}$, then $\mathfrak{H}\eta = \mathfrak{H}\nu$ is the factor group of cosets $h\mathfrak{R}$ with $h \in \mathfrak{H}$, i.e., $\mathfrak{H}/\mathfrak{R}$.

By Theorem 2, the $M$-factor groups $\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{H}$ and $(\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{R})/(\mathfrak{H}/\mathfrak{R})$ are isomorphic under the correspondence $g\mathfrak{H} \mapsto (g\nu)(\mathfrak{H}\nu) = (g\mathfrak{R})(\mathfrak{H}/\mathfrak{R})$.
