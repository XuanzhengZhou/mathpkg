This lemma is the critical step in proving that the alternating group $A_n$ is simple for $n \geq 5$. The proof uses a minimal counterexample argument: choose a non-identity permutation in $\mathfrak{H}$ that fixes as many elements as possible. If this permutation is not a three-cycle, one constructs via conjugation a different permutation that contradicts the maximality of fixed points. The construction depends on whether the permutation contains a cycle of length at least 3 or is a product of disjoint transpositions.
