Let $\eta$ be an $M$-homomorphism of $\mathfrak{G}$ into $\mathfrak{G}'$ and let $a\eta$ be any element of the image set $\mathfrak{G}\eta$. If $m \in M$, then

$$(a\eta)m = (am)\eta \in \mathfrak{G}\eta.$$

Since $\mathfrak{G}\eta$ is a subgroup, this shows that $\mathfrak{G}\eta$ is an $M$-subgroup of $\mathfrak{G}'$.

Now consider the kernel $\mathfrak{R}$ of $\eta$. We know that $\mathfrak{R}$ is an invariant subgroup of $\mathfrak{G}$. Also if $k \in \mathfrak{R}$ and $m \in M$, then

$$(km)\eta = (k\eta)m = 1'm = 1'.$$

Hence $km \in \mathfrak{R}$, so $\mathfrak{R}$ is an $M$-subgroup of $\mathfrak{G}$. Being an invariant subgroup that is also an $M$-subgroup, $\mathfrak{R}$ is an invariant $M$-subgroup.
