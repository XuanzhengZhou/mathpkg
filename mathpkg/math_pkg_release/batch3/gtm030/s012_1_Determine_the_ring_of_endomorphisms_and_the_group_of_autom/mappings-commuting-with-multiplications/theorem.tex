**Theorem 11.** Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a ring with an identity. If a mapping $T$ in the endomorphism ring of the additive group $(\mathfrak{A}, +)$ commutes with all left multiplications, then $T$ is a right multiplication. Similarly, if $T$ commutes with all right multiplications, then $T$ is a left multiplication.

In symbols: if $T \in \operatorname{End}(\mathfrak{A}, +)$ and $T a_l = a_l T$ for all $a \in \mathfrak{A}$, then $T = b_r$ for some $b \in \mathfrak{A}$. If $T a_r = a_r T$ for all $a \in \mathfrak{A}$, then $T = b_l$ for some $b \in \mathfrak{A}$.
