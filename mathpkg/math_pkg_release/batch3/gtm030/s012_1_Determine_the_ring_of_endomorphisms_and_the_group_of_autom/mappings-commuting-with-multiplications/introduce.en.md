Theorem 11 establishes a duality between left and right multiplications in a ring with identity: the centralizer of all left multiplications in the endomorphism ring consists precisely of the right multiplications, and vice versa. This result is the ring-theoretic analogue of a corresponding group-theoretic result and highlights the symmetric relationship between the two types of multiplications.
