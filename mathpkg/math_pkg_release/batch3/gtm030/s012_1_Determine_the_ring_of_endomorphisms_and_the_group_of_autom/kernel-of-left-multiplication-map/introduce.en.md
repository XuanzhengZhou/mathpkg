The kernel of the left multiplication anti-homomorphism $a \mapsto a_l$ is precisely the set of elements that annihilate the entire ring from the left. This ideal $\mathfrak{Z}_l$ measures how far the anti-homomorphism is from being injective. For rings with identity, the kernel is trivial, ensuring that left multiplication gives an anti-isomorphism.
