The greatest lower bound (also called infimum or meet) of a subset $A$ of a partially ordered set is the maximum among all lower bounds of $A$. When it exists, it is unique. In lattice theory, the greatest lower bound of two elements $a$ and $b$ is denoted $a \cap b$ and is the fundamental operation capturing the "meet" of two elements. Together with the least upper bound, these two operations define the structure of a lattice.
