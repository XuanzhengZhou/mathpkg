The least upper bound (also called supremum or join) of a subset $A$ of a partially ordered set is the minimum among all upper bounds of $A$. When it exists, it is unique. In lattice theory, the least upper bound of two elements $a$ and $b$ is denoted $a \cup b$ and is the fundamental operation capturing the "join" of two elements.
