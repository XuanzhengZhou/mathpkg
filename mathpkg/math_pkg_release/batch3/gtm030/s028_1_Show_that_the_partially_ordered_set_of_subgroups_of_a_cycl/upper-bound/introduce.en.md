An upper bound of a subset $A$ of a partially ordered set $S$ is an element $u \in S$ that dominates every element of $A$ with respect to the partial order. This notion is fundamental for defining suprema, lattices, and completeness properties. An upper bound need not belong to $A$, and a subset may have many upper bounds or none at all.
