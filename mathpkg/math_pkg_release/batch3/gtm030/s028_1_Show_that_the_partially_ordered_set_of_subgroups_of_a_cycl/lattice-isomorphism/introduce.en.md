A lattice isomorphism is a bijective lattice homomorphism; that is, a one-to-one correspondence between two lattices that preserves both join and meet operations. Isomorphic lattices have identical order-theoretic and algebraic properties. The isomorphism criterion (Theorem 1) provides an equivalent characterization: a bijection is an isomorphism if and only if it preserves and reflects the partial order.
