The product of a square matrix with its adjoint yields a scalar multiple of the identity matrix, where the scalar is the determinant of the original matrix. This theorem unifies the cofactor expansion formulas (7) and (8) into a single matrix equation. It is the foundation for Cramer's rule and shows that a matrix is invertible if and only if its determinant is a unit in the base ring, with $a^{-1} = (\det a)^{-1} \operatorname{adj}(a)$.
