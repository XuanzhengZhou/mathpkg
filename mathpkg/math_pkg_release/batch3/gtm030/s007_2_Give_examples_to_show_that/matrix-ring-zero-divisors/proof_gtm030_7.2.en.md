The proof is deferred to Exercise 3: Prove that if $\Re \neq 0$ and $n > 1$, then $\Re_n$ has zero-divisors $\neq 0$. A concrete construction: take the matrix unit $E_{11}$ (with $1$ at position $(1,1)$ and zeros elsewhere) and $E_{22}$. Both are non-zero, but $E_{11}E_{22} = 0$. For general $n > 1$, pick any two distinct matrix units $E_{ii}$ and $E_{jj}$ with $i \neq j$; their product is zero while neither factor is zero.
