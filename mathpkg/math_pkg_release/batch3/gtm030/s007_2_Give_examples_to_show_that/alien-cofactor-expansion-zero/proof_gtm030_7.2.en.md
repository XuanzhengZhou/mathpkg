This theorem is cited as a well-known property of determinants, dual to the cofactor expansion. The sum $\sum_k a_{ik} A_{jk}$ for $i \neq j$ is the determinant of the matrix obtained by replacing the $j$-th row of $(a)$ with the $i$-th row. This modified matrix has two identical rows, so its determinant is zero. The same argument applies to columns.
