When expanding a determinant using cofactors from a row different from the one being expanded, the result is always zero. This "alien cofactor" property reflects the fact that replacing one row of a matrix with another row yields a matrix with two identical rows, whose determinant is zero. It is a fundamental complement to the standard Laplace expansion and is essential for deriving the relationship between a matrix and its adjoint.
