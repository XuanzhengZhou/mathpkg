When the base ring $\Re$ possesses a multiplicative identity $1$, the matrix ring $\Re_n$ also has a multiplicative identity. This identity is the $n \times n$ diagonal matrix $I_n$ with $1$ on all diagonal entries and $0$ elsewhere. It satisfies $I_n A = A I_n = A$ for every matrix $A \in \Re_n$, as follows directly from the definition of matrix multiplication.
