This theorem is cited as a well-known property of determinants. The determinant of an $n \times n$ matrix $(a)$ is defined by
$$\det(a) = \sum_{(i_1,\ldots,i_n)} \pm a_{1i_1} a_{2i_2} \cdots a_{ni_n}$$
where the summation is over all permutations $(i_1,\ldots,i_n)$ of $(1,\ldots,n)$ and the sign is $+$ or $-$ according as the permutation is even or odd. The cofactor $A_{ij}$ of $a_{ij}$ is $(-1)^{i+j}$ times the determinant of the $(n-1) \times (n-1)$ matrix obtained by deleting the $i$th row and $j$th column. The cofactor expansion formulas follow by regrouping the terms in the determinant sum according to the element from a fixed row or column.
