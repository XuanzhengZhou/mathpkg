The determinant of an $n \times n$ matrix can be computed by expanding along any row or column using cofactors. For each entry $a_{ij}$, its cofactor $A_{ij}$ is $(-1)^{i+j}$ times the determinant of the $(n-1) \times (n-1)$ submatrix obtained by deleting the $i$-th row and $j$-th column. The Laplace expansion states that the sum of the products of the entries of any row (or column) with their corresponding cofactors equals the determinant.
