Let the $\alpha_i$ in
$$(\alpha) = \begin{bmatrix}
\alpha_1 & 0 \\
\alpha_2 & \cdot \\
\cdot & \cdot \\
0 & \alpha_n
\end{bmatrix}$$
be distinct rational numbers. Show that $C(\alpha)$ in the matrix ring $R_{0n}$, where $R_0$ is the field of rational numbers, is the set of diagonal matrices, that is, the set of matrices that have the same form as $(\alpha)$.
