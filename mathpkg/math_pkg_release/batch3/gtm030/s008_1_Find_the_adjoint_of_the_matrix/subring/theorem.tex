**Definition 2.** If $\mathfrak{B}$ is a subset of a ring $\mathfrak{A}$ that is closed under the compositions of the ring and $\mathfrak{B}, +, \cdot$ (induced compositions) is a ring, then $\mathfrak{B}, +, \cdot$ is called a subring of $\mathfrak{A}, +, \cdot$.

A subset $\mathfrak{B}$ determines a subring if $\mathfrak{B}, +$ is a group and $\mathfrak{B}$ is closed under multiplication. The first condition is satisfied if either (1) $\mathfrak{B}$ is closed under $+$, contains $0$ and the negative of any element in $\mathfrak{B}$, or (2) $\mathfrak{B}$ is closed under subtraction.
