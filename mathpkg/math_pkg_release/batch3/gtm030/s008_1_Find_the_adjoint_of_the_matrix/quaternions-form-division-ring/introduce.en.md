The quaternions are realized as a set of $2 \times 2$ complex matrices of a specific form. This set is closed under addition and multiplication, making it a subring of the matrix ring $C_2$. Moreover, every nonzero quaternion matrix has nonzero determinant $\alpha_0^2 + \alpha_1^2 + \alpha_2^2 + \alpha_3^2$, which yields an explicit inverse formula. Thus the quaternions form a division ring: a ring in which every nonzero element is a unit.
