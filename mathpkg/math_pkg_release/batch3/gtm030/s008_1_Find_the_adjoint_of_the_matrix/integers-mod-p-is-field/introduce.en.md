When $p$ is prime, the ring of integers modulo $p$ is a field. Every nonzero residue class $\bar{a} \neq 0$ is a unit because $\gcd(a,p) = 1$, so there exist integers $b,q$ with $ab + pq = 1$, giving $\bar{a}\bar{b} = \bar{1}$. This constructs a finite field of $p$ elements. In contrast, when the modulus is composite, zero divisors appear and the quotient ring fails to be an integral domain.
