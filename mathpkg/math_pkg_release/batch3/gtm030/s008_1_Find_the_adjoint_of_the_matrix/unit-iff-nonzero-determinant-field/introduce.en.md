Over a field, a square matrix is invertible if and only if its determinant is nonzero. This follows immediately from the multiplicative property of determinants: if a matrix is invertible its determinant must be a unit (hence nonzero in a field), and conversely a nonzero determinant provides an explicit formula for the inverse via the adjoint.
