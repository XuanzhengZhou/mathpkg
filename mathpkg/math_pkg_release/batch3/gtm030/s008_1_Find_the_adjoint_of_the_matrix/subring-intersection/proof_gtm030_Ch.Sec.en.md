Let $\{\mathfrak{B}_\alpha\}$ be a collection of subrings of $\mathfrak{A}$. Let $\mathfrak{B} = \bigcap_\alpha \mathfrak{B}_\alpha$. For any $b_1, b_2 \in \mathfrak{B}$, we have $b_1, b_2 \in \mathfrak{B}_\alpha$ for each $\alpha$, hence $b_1 - b_2 \in \mathfrak{B}_\alpha$ and $b_1 b_2 \in \mathfrak{B}_\alpha$ for each $\alpha$. Thus $b_1 - b_2 \in \mathfrak{B}$ and $b_1 b_2 \in \mathfrak{B}$. Also $0 \in \mathfrak{B}_\alpha$ for all $\alpha$, so $0 \in \mathfrak{B}$. Therefore $\mathfrak{B}$ is closed under subtraction (hence is an additive subgroup) and under multiplication, making it a subring.
