If the original ring has a multiplicative identity, then the coset of the identity serves as the multiplicative identity in the quotient ring. This is because for any coset $a + \mathfrak{B}$, we have $(a + \mathfrak{B})(1 + \mathfrak{B}) = a \cdot 1 + \mathfrak{B} = a + \mathfrak{B}$.
