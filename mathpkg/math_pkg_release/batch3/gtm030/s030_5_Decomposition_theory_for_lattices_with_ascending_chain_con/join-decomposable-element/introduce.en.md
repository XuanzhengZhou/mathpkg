An element of a lattice is join-decomposable if it can be expressed as the join of two proper, independent subelements. This notion is dual to meet-reducibility and provides the foundation for a direct decomposition theory in modular lattices, analogous to the decomposition of a group into a direct product of indecomposable subgroups. The descending chain condition ensures that every element admits a finite decomposition into join-indecomposable elements.
