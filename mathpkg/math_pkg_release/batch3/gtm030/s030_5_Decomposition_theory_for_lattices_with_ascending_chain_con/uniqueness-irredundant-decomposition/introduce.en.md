Theorem 8 states that in a modular lattice, any two irredundant representations of the same element as a meet of irreducibles must involve the same number of terms. This is the lattice-theoretic analogue of the invariance of the number of primary components in a minimal primary decomposition in a Noetherian ring. The proof follows directly from the replacement theorem (Theorem 7) by iteratively substituting terms from one decomposition into the other.
