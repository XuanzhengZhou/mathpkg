In a lattice satisfying the descending chain condition, every element admits a representation as a finite join of independent, join-indecomposable elements. This is the lattice-theoretic analogue of the Krull-Schmidt theorem for groups: any element can be decomposed into a direct sum of indecomposable components. The proof uses the DCC to argue that an infinite chain of proper decompositions cannot exist, guaranteeing termination at indecomposable factors.
