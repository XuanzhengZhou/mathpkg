If $a = q_1 \cap q_2 \cap \cdots \cap q_m = r_1 \cap r_2 \cap \cdots \cap r_n$ are two representations of an element of a modular lattice as a greatest lower bound of meet-irreducible elements, then for each $q_i$ there exists an $r_{i'}$ such that
$$a = q_1 \cap \cdots \cap q_{i-1} \cap r_{i'} \cap q_{i+1} \cap \cdots \cap q_m.$$
