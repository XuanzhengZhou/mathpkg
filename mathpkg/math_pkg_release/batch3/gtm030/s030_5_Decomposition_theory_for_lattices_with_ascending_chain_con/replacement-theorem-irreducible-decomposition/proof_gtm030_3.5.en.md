Consider the intervals $I[q_2 \cap \cdots \cap q_m, a]$ and $I[q_2 \cap \cdots \cap q_m, q_1 \cap q_2 \cap \cdots \cap q_m]$, which are isomorphic. By modularity we also have that
$$I[q_1 \cup (q_2 \cap \cdots \cap q_m), q_1]$$
is isomorphic to these intervals. Since $q_1$ is irreducible in $I[q_1 \cup (q_2 \cap \cdots \cap q_m), q_1]$, it follows that $a$ is irreducible in $I[q_2 \cap \cdots \cap q_m, a]$. But the decomposition $a = r_1 \cap r_2 \cap \cdots \cap r_n$ is valid in this interval, hence $a = r_{i'}$ for some suitable index $i'$. Replacing $q_1$ by this $r_{i'}$ yields the required decomposition. By symmetry, the same argument applies to any $q_i$, giving the full statement.
