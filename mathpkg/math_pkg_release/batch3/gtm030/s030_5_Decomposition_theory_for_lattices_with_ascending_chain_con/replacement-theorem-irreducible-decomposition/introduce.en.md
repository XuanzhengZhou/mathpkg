Theorem 7 is a replacement theorem for meet-irreducible decompositions in modular lattices. Given two (not necessarily irredundant) representations of the same element $a$ as a meet of irreducibles, any $q_i$ from the first representation can replace some $r_{i'}$ from the second, yielding another valid decomposition of $a$. This is the lattice-theoretic analogue of the replacement lemma for bases in vector spaces, and it underpins the uniqueness of the number of terms in irredundant decompositions.
