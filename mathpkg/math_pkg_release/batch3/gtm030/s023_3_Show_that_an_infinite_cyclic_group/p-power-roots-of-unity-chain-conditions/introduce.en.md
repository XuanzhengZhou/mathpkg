The group $U_{(p)}$ of all $p^k$-th complex roots of unity (for all $k \geq 0$) is an infinite abelian $p$-group, often called the quasicyclic or Prüfer $p$-group. Every proper subgroup is finite cyclic, consisting of the $p^n$-th roots of unity for some fixed $n$. Consequently, any descending chain of subgroups must stabilize (each proper subgroup is finite), but an infinite ascending chain $U_{p} \subset U_{p^2} \subset U_{p^3} \subset \cdots$ shows the ACC fails.
