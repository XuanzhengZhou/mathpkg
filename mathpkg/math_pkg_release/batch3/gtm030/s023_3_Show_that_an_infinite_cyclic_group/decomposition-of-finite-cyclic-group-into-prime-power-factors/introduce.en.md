Any finite cyclic group decomposes as a direct product of cyclic groups of prime-power orders, corresponding to the prime factorization of its order. This is a concrete illustration of the criterion for recognizing direct products from Theorem 5. The proof uses the fact that a cyclic group of order $n$ has a unique subgroup of order $p_i^{e_i}$ for each prime power dividing $n$, and these subgroups intersect trivially and generate the whole group.
