Direct products are associative up to $M$-isomorphism: factors can be regrouped into larger blocks without changing the isomorphism type of the overall group. For example, if $\mathfrak{G} = \mathfrak{G}_1 \times \mathfrak{G}_2 \times \mathfrak{G}_3 \times \mathfrak{G}_4$, then $\mathfrak{G} \cong (\mathfrak{G}_1 \times \mathfrak{G}_2) \times (\mathfrak{G}_3 \times \mathfrak{G}_4)$. The proof is immediate from the definition of the direct product, since componentwise multiplication respects arbitrary partition of the coordinates.
