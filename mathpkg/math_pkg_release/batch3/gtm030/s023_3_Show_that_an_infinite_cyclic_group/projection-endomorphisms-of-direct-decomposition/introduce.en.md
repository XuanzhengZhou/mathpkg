Every direct decomposition $\mathfrak{G} = \mathfrak{G}_1 \times \cdots \times \mathfrak{G}_n$ gives rise to $n$ orthogonal projection endomorphisms $\epsilon_i$ that project onto the $i$-th factor. These projections are orthogonal idempotents ($\epsilon_i^2 = \epsilon_i$, $\epsilon_i\epsilon_j = 0$ for $i \neq j$) and sum to the identity ($\sum \epsilon_i = 1$). Conversely, any collection of normal $M$-endomorphisms satisfying these properties determines a direct decomposition of $\mathfrak{G}$.
