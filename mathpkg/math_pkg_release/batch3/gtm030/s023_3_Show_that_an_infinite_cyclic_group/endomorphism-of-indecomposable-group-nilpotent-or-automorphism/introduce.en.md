This corollary of Fitting's lemma establishes a striking dichotomy for endomorphisms of indecomposable groups satisfying both chain conditions: a normal $M$-endomorphism is either nilpotent (some power is the zero map) or an automorphism. No middle ground exists. This "all-or-nothing" property is fundamental to the proof of the Krull-Schmidt theorem, where it is used to show that certain endomorphisms constructed from two direct decompositions must be isomorphisms.
