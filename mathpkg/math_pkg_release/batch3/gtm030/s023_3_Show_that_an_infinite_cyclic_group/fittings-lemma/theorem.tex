Let $\mathfrak{G}$ be an $M$-group that satisfies the chain conditions for invariant $M$-subgroups and let $\eta$ be a normal $M$-endomorphism of $\mathfrak{G}$. Then for sufficiently large $m$, $\mathfrak{G}$ is the direct product of the kernel $\beta_m$ of $\eta^m$ and the image $\mathfrak{G}\eta^m$:

$$\mathfrak{G} = \beta_m \times \mathfrak{G}\eta^m,$$

where $\eta$ is nilpotent on $\beta_m$ and an automorphism on $\mathfrak{G}\eta^m$.
