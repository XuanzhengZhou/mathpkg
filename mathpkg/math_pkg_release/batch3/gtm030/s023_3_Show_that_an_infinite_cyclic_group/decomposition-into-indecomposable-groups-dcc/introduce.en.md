Under the descending chain condition on invariant $M$-subgroups, any non-trivial $M$-group admits a finite direct product decomposition into indecomposable factors. The proof proceeds by repeatedly factoring off indecomposable summands: if $\mathfrak{G}$ is not indecomposable, it splits as $\mathfrak{G}_1 \times \mathfrak{G}_2'$ with $\mathfrak{G}_1$ indecomposable; continuing with $\mathfrak{G}_2'$ yields a strictly descending chain of invariant subgroups, which must terminate by the DCC.
