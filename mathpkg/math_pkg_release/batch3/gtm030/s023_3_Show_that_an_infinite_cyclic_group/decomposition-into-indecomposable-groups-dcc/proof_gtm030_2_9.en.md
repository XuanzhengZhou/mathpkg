If $\mathfrak{G}$ is not indecomposable, then $\mathfrak{G} = \mathfrak{G}_1 \times \mathfrak{G}_2'$ where $\mathfrak{G}_1 \neq 1$ is indecomposable and $\mathfrak{G}_2' \neq 1$ is an invariant $M$-subgroup. Next either $\mathfrak{G}_2' = 1$ or $\mathfrak{G}_2' = \mathfrak{G}_2 \times \mathfrak{G}_3'$ where $\mathfrak{G}_2$ is indecomposable. As before, $\mathfrak{G}_3'$ is invariant in $\mathfrak{G}$. This process leads to a properly descending chain of invariant $M$-subgroups

$$\mathfrak{G} \supset \mathfrak{G}_2' \supset \mathfrak{G}_3' \supset \cdots.$$

By the descending chain condition $I'$, we conclude that $\mathfrak{G}_n' = 1$ for some integer $n$. Hence

$$\mathfrak{G} = \mathfrak{G}_1 \times \mathfrak{G}_2 \times \cdots \times \mathfrak{G}_n$$

where each $\mathfrak{G}_i$ is indecomposable and $\neq 1$.
