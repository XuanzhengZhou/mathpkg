The Krull-Schmidt theorem asserts that any direct product decomposition of an $M$-group (satisfying both chain conditions) into indecomposable factors is unique up to isomorphism and ordering of the factors. This is the group-theoretic analogue of the fundamental theorem of arithmetic: just as every integer factors uniquely into primes, every such group factors uniquely into indecomposable "building blocks". The proof uses Fitting's lemma and relies on a subtle induction that replaces factors from one decomposition with isomorphic factors from the other one at a time.
