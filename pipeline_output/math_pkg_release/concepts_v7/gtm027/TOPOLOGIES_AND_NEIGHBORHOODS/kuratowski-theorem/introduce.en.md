Kuratowski's theorem establishes the equivalence between topologies and closure operators. Given any operator satisfying the four Kuratowski axioms, the family of sets fixed by the operator forms the closed sets of a unique topology. Moreover, the topological closure in this topology coincides exactly with the original operator. This theorem is foundational: it shows that one can define a topological space entirely in terms of an abstract closure operation, without any prior notion of open sets.
