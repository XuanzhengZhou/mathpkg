The closure operator $A \mapsto A^{-}$ maps each subset to its closure. It determines the topology uniquely since closed sets are exactly the fixed points of the operator. The operator distributes over finite unions: $(A \cup B)^{-} = A^{-} \cup B^{-}$, a property essential to the Kuratowski axiomatization. This operator perspective shows that a topology can be defined entirely in terms of a closure operation satisfying four simple axioms.
