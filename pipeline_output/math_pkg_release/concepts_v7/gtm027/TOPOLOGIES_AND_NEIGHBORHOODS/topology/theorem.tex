A \textbf{topology} is a family $\mathfrak{J}$ of sets which satisfies the two conditions:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The intersection of any two members of $\mathfrak{J}$ is a member of $\mathfrak{J}$.
\item The union of the members of each subfamily of $\mathfrak{J}$ is a member of $\mathfrak{J}$.
\end{enumerate}
The set 
$$X = \bigcup\{U : U \in \mathfrak{J}\}$$
is necessarily a member of $\mathfrak{J}$ because $\mathfrak{J}$ is a subfamily of itself, and every member of $\mathfrak{J}$ is a subset of $X$. The set $X$ is called the \textit{space} of the topology $\mathfrak{J}$ and $\mathfrak{J}$ is a topology \textit{for} $X$.
