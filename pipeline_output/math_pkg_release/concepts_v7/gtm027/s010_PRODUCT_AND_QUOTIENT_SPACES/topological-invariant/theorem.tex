A property which when possessed by a topological space is also possessed by each homeomorph (each homeomorphic image) is a \textbf{topological invariant}. Any property defined in terms of the members of the space and the topology is automatically a topological invariant. Examples include connectedness, having a countable base, having a countable local base at each point, being a $T_1$-space, and being a Hausdorff space.
