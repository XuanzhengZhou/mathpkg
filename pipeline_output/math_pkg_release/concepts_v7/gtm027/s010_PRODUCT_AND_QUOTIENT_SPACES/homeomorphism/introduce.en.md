A homeomorphism is the fundamental notion of equivalence in topology: two spaces are homeomorphic when there exists a continuous bijection between them whose inverse is also continuous. Homeomorphic spaces are topologically indistinguishable — they share all properties defined purely in terms of open sets. For example, the real line $\mathbb{R}$ is homeomorphic to the open interval $(0,1)$, but $(0,1)$ is not homeomorphic to $(0,1) \cup (1,2)$ because one is connected and the other is not. Determining whether two spaces are homeomorphic is a central problem in topology, typically resolved by comparing topological invariants.
