The projection maps are the canonical functions from a Cartesian product to its coordinate spaces, mapping each tuple (or function) to its value at a particular index. In the product topology, projections are always continuous, and the product topology is precisely the smallest topology that makes all projections continuous. This universal property of the product topology — that continuity of a function into a product is equivalent to continuity of each coordinate function — depends fundamentally on the projection maps.
