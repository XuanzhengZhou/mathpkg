Continuity at a point is the localized version of global continuity: a function is continuous at $x$ precisely when the preimage of every neighborhood of $f(x)$ is a neighborhood of $x$. This local condition is equivalent to the $\varepsilon$-$\delta$ definition in metric spaces and unifies to global continuity: a function is continuous on $X$ if and only if it is continuous at every point of $X$. The localized form is particularly useful when one needs to prove continuity by examining behavior near individual points.
