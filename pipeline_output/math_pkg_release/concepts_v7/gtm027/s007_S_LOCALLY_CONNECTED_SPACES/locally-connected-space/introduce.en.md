A topological space is locally connected when every point has a base of connected neighborhoods. This is a local property satisfied by many familiar spaces such as Euclidean spaces and manifolds. Local connectedness ensures that the connected components of open sets behave well: components of open sets are themselves open. The property is strictly weaker than connectedness, and also independent of it — a space can be locally connected but not connected, or connected but not locally connected (as in the topologist's sine curve).
