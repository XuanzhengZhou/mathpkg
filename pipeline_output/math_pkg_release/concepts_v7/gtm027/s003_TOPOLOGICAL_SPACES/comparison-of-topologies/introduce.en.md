Topologies on a fixed set $X$ form a partially ordered set under inclusion. A coarser topology has fewer open sets; a finer topology has more. The discrete and indiscrete topologies are the extreme bounds of this ordering. Not all pairs of topologies are comparable, and this partial order is fundamental to understanding relationships between different topological structures on the same underlying set.
