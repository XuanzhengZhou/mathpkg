The derived set, a notion introduced by Cantor, collects all accumulation points of a given set. It provides an alternative route to defining the closure: the closure of $A$ is precisely $A$ together with its derived set. In the usual topology on the reals, the derived set of the reciprocals of integers is $\{0\}$.
