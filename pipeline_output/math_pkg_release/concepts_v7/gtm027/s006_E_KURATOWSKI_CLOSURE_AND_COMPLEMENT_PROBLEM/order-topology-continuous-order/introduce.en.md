The order topology on a linearly ordered set $X$ admits an elegant characterization: it is the coarsest (smallest) topology such that whenever $a < b$, the two points can be separated by neighborhoods whose elements preserve the order relation. This captures the intuition that the order topology is the minimal topology making the order "topologically visible." The standard subbase of open rays generates precisely this topology.
