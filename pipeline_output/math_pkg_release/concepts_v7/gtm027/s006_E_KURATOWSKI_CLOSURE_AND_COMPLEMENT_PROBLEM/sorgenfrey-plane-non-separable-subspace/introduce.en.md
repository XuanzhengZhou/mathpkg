A striking property of the Sorgenfrey plane: although the whole space is separable, it contains closed subspaces that are not separable. The anti-diagonal $x + y = 1$ is a classic example: in the subspace topology inherited from the Sorgenfrey plane, this set is discrete and uncountable, hence cannot be separable. This shows that separability is not a hereditary property.
