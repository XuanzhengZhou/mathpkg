The countable chain condition (CCC) is a cardinality restriction on the topology of a space: any collection of pairwise disjoint nonempty open sets must be at most countable. The term originates from the Boolean algebra of regular open sets. The CCC is a weak form of separability and is often used in forcing and set-theoretic topology.
