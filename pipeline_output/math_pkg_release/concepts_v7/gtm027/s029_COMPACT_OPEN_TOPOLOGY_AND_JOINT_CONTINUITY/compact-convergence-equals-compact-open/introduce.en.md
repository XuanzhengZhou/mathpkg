Theorem 7.11 unifies two apparently distinct function space topologies: for continuous functions into a uniform space, the topology of uniform convergence on compacta coincides with the compact-open topology. The proof shows that each compact-open subbasic set $\{f : f[K] \subset U\}$ is open in the uniform-convergence-on-compacta topology (using that $f[K]$ is compact and can be covered by uniform neighborhoods), and conversely each basic entourage of the compact-convergence uniformity contains a finite intersection of compact-open subbasic neighborhoods of $f$.
