A family is equicontinuous on a set $A$ if the family of restrictions to $A$ is equicontinuous. A family that is equicontinuous at every point of $A$ is equicontinuous on $A$, but the converse is false in general. However, a family equicontinuous on $A$ is equicontinuous at each point of the interior of $A$. This notion is used in the Ascoli theorem for $k$-spaces, which requires equicontinuity on every compact subset.
