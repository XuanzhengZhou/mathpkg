Let $C$ be the family of all continuous functions on a regular locally compact space $X$ to a uniform space $Y$, and let $C$ have the topology of uniform convergence on compacta. Then a subfamily $F$ of $C$ is compact if and only if

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $F$ is closed in $C$,
\item[(b)] $F[x]$ has a compact closure for each member $x$ of $X$, and
\item[(c)] the family $F$ is equicontinuous.
\end{enumerate}
