Theorem 7.10 collects the key properties of the uniformity of uniform convergence on members of a family $\alpha$ of subsets. It interpolates between pointwise and uniform convergence (a), shares the Cauchy+pointwise characterization of convergence (b), preserves completeness (c)-(d), and is jointly continuous on each member of $\alpha$ (e). Note that the family of continuous functions may fail to be complete under $\mathfrak{U}\mid\alpha$; however, if continuity on each member of $\alpha$ implies continuity on $X$, then completeness of the continuous functions follows from (d).
