Let $(X, d)$ be a pseudo-metric space and let $A$ be a compact subset of $X$. If $\mathfrak{u}$ is an open cover of $A$, then there exists a positive number $r > 0$ (called a \emph{Lebesgue number}) such that for each point $x \in A$, the open $r$-sphere about $x$ is contained in some member of $\mathfrak{u}$.
