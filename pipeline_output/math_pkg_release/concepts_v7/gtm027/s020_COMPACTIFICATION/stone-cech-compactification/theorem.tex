Let $X$ be a topological space. Let $F(X)$ be the family of all continuous functions from $X$ to the closed unit interval $Q = [0, 1]$. Let $Q^{F(X)}$ be the product cube (compact by the Tychonoff theorem). Define the \emph{evaluation map} $e: X \to Q^{F(X)}$ by setting the $f$-th coordinate of $e(x)$ to be $f(x)$ for each $f \in F(X)$. The \emph{Stone-Čech compactification} of $X$ is the pair $(e, \beta(X))$, where $\beta(X)$ is the closure of $e[X]$ in $Q^{F(X)}$. If $X$ is a Tychonoff space, then $e$ is a homeomorphism of $X$ onto a subspace of $Q^{F(X)}$.
