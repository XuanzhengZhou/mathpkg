This lemma is a key technical tool in the theory of paracompactness. It shows that in spaces where every open cover is even, one can enlarge each member of a locally finite (or discrete) family to an open set while preserving the local finiteness (or discreteness) of the family. This "controlled enlargement" property is crucial for establishing the equivalence between even covers and paracompactness.
