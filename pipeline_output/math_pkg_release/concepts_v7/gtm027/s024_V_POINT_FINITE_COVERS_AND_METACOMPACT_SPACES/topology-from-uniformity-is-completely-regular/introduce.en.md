A topological space admits a compatible uniformity precisely when it is completely regular. One direction follows from Theorem 16: every uniform topology embeds into a product of pseudo-metrizable spaces, hence is completely regular. The converse uses the fact that completely regular spaces embed into products of unit intervals, each carrying a natural uniformity, and the induced uniformity works. This characterizes exactly which topologies can arise from uniform structures.
