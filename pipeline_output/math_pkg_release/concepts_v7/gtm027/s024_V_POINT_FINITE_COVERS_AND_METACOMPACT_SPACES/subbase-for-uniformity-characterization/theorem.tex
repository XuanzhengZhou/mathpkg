A family $\mathcal{S}$ of subsets of $X \times X$ is a subbase for some uniformity for $X$ if and only if:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] each member of $\mathcal{S}$ contains the diagonal $\Delta$;
\item[(b)] for each $U \in \mathcal{S}$ the set $U^{-1}$ contains a member of $\mathcal{S}$;
\item[(c)] for each $U \in \mathcal{S}$ there is $V \in \mathcal{S}$ such that $V \circ V \subset U$.
\end{enumerate}
In particular, the union of any collection of uniformities for $X$ is a subbase for a uniformity for $X$.
