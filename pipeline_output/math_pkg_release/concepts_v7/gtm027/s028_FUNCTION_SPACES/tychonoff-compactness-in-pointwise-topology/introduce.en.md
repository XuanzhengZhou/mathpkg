This theorem, an immediate consequence of the Tychonoff product theorem, gives a complete characterization of compact subsets in the pointwise topology. A family $F$ of functions is pointwise compact precisely when it is closed in the product space $Y^X$ and each "vertical slice" $F[x]$ has compact closure. The sufficiency follows from Tychonoff: the product of the compact closures contains $F$, and $F$ is closed there, hence compact. The necessity uses that the evaluation maps are continuous, so each $F[x]$ is the continuous image of a compact space. This result is fundamental to the study of function spaces and underlies the Ascoli theorem.
