A net is a function whose domain is a directed set. Nets generalize sequences by allowing index sets more general than the natural numbers, making them the correct tool for studying convergence in arbitrary topological spaces. The pair notation $(S, \geq)$ emphasizes that both the mapping $S$ and the directing order $\geq$ are part of the structure. Nets are the central objects of Moore-Smith convergence theory, introduced by Moore and Smith in 1922 and later popularized by Kelley.
