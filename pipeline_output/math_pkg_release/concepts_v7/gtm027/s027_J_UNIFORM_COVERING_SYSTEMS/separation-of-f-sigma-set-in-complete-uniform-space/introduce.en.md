In a complete uniform space, \sigma$ sets can be separated from points outside them by continuous real-valued functions. Specifically, given an \sigma$ set $ and a point $ not in $, there exists a continuous function that is strictly positive on $ and zero at $. This result relies on the device used earlier in the chapter (6.K(a)) and has consequences for the existence of complete uniform subspaces.
