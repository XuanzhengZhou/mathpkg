# Largest Uniformity Compatible with Topology

For every completely regular topological space, there exists a largest uniformity that induces the given topology. This maximal uniformity can be characterized in two equivalent ways: as the smallest uniformity making all continuous maps into metric spaces uniformly continuous, or as the smallest uniformity making all continuous maps into uniform spaces uniformly continuous. The explicit neighborhood-of-the-diagonal condition requires a countable descending sequence of symmetric diagonal neighborhoods satisfying the usual "halving" condition of uniformities. This uniformity contrasts with the one given by the Stone-Čech compactification, which is the smallest such uniformity.
