Every pseudo-metric space can be turned into a metric space by identifying points at zero distance. The quotient space $\mathfrak{D}$ consists of the closures of singletons, and the induced distance $D$ is a genuine metric. The quotient map is both open and continuous, and the quotient topology coincides with the metric topology. This construction shows that pseudo-metric spaces differ from metric spaces only by the presence of distinct points at distance zero.
