Tychonoff's lemma states that every regular Lindelof space is normal. A Lindelof space is one in which every open cover has a countable subcover. This lemma provides a crucial bridge: it allows the construction of continuous functions (via Urysohn's lemma) on regular spaces that satisfy the second axiom of countability, since second-countable spaces are Lindelof.
