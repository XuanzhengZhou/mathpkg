The Urysohn Metrization Theorem is one of the most celebrated results in point-set topology. It provides sufficient conditions for metrizability: a regular $T_1$-space with a countable base is metrizable. The proof constructs a countable family of continuous functions to $[0,1]$ that distinguishes points and closed sets, then applies the Embedding Lemma to embed the space into the metrizable cube $[0,1]^\omega$. The theorem characterizes exactly when a topological space arises from a metric.
