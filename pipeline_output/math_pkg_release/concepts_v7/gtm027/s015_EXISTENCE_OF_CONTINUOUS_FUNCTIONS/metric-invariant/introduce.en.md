A metric invariant is a property of metric spaces preserved under isometries -- bijections that preserve distances exactly. This is a stronger notion than topological invariance (preservation under homeomorphisms). For example, completeness and boundedness (finite diameter) are metric invariants but not topological invariants, since a metric space can be homeomorphic to a space with different metric properties. Kelley makes this distinction to highlight that topological spaces abstract away metric-specific features, and that additional structure (uniformities) is needed to capture metric-like properties topologically.
