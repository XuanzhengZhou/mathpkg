Let $(X,d)$ be a pseudo-metric space and define $e : X \times X \to \mathbb{R}$ by $e(x,y) = \min[1, d(x,y)]$. Then $(X,e)$ is a pseudo-metric space whose topology is identical to that of $(X,d)$, and the $e$-diameter of $X$ is at most one.

Consequently, every pseudo-metric space is homeomorphic to a pseudo-metric space of diameter at most one.
