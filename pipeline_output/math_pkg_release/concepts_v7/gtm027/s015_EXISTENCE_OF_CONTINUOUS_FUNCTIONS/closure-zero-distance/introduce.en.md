This theorem gives a purely metric characterization of topological closure: a point belongs to the closure of a set $A$ if and only if its distance to $A$ is zero. The proof uses the continuity of the distance function (Theorem 8) and the fact that points outside the closure have neighborhoods disjoint from $A$, hence are at positive distance. This result is fundamental for working with closures in metric and pseudo-metric spaces.
