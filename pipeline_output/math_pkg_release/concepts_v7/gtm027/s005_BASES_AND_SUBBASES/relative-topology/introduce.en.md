The relative topology (or subspace topology) on a subset $Y$ of a topological space $X$ is defined by declaring a set open in $Y$ precisely when it is the intersection of an open set in $X$ with $Y$. This is the coarsest topology on $Y$ making the inclusion map $Y \hookrightarrow X$ continuous. The relative topology is fundamental for studying subsets as topological spaces in their own right.
