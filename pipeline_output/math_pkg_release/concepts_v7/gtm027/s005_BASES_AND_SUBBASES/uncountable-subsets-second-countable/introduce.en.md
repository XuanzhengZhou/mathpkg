Theorem 13 asserts that in any second countable space, every uncountable subset must contain at least one accumulation point of itself. The proof proceeds by contradiction: if no point of $A$ is an accumulation point, one can construct a countable cover that forces $A$ to be countable. This is a fundamental result linking cardinality to topological structure.
