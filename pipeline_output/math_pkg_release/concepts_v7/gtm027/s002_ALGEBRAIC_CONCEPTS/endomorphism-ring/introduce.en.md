For an abelian group $(X, \oplus)$, the set of all group endomorphisms (homomorphisms from the group to itself) forms a ring under pointwise addition and composition. This endomorphism ring is crucial for the definition of a linear space: scalar multiplication is precisely a ring homomorphism from the field $F$ into the endomorphism ring.
