Order-completeness is the defining property of the real numbers: every non-empty bounded-above subset has a supremum. This property distinguishes $\mathbb{R}$ from $\mathbb{Q}$ and is the foundation of analysis. Kelley takes the real numbers to be an ordered field that is order-complete, using this to prove properties about integers, induction, and b-adic expansions.
