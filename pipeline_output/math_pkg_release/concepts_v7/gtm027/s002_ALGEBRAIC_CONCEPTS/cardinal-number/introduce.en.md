A cardinal number is an equipollence class of sets. Cardinal numbers are well-ordered under the relation $C < D$ (meaning $C$ is equipollent to a proper subset of $D$). Kelley sketches this theory to motivate the Schroeder-Bernstein theorem. The full construction is given in the appendix.
