The restriction of a function $f$ to a subset $A$ of its domain, denoted $f|A$, is the function that agrees with $f$ on $A$ and is undefined elsewhere. Set-theoretically, it is simply $f \cap (A \times Y)$, the intersection of $f$ with the cartesian product of $A$ and the codomain. A function $g$ is a restriction of $f$ exactly when $g \subset f$. Restriction is the dual of extension and is used pervasively in topology for relativizing concepts to subspaces.
