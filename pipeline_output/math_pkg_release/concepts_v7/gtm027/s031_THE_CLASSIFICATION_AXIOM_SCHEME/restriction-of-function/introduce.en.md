The restriction $f|x$ of a function $f$ to a class $x$ is a function whose domain is the intersection of $x$ with the domain of $f$, and whose values agree with $f$ on that intersection. The restriction of a relation is defined similarly (for any relation $r$, $r|x$ is the restriction).
