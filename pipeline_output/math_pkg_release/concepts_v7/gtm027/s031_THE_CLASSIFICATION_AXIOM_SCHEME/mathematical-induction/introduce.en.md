The Principle of Mathematical Induction is derived as a theorem within Kelley's set theory. The set $\omega$ of integers is defined as the intersection of all inductive sets (whose existence is guaranteed by the Axiom of Infinity). The induction principle follows immediately: any inductive subset of $\omega$ must equal $\omega$. Together with the successor properties, this yields all of Peano's postulates as theorems of set theory.
