The Axiom of Infinity asserts the existence of an inductive set: a set containing $0$ (the empty set) and closed under the successor operation $x \mapsto x \cup \{x\}$. This axiom guarantees the existence of infinite sets. From it, the set $\omega$ of all integers (finite ordinals) is constructed as the intersection of all inductive sets. The Peano axioms for the integers are then derived as theorems within set theory.
