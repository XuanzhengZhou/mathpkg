The Axiom of Extent (also called Extensionality) is the first axiom of Kelley's set theory system. It asserts that two classes are equal precisely when they have the same members. This axiom captures the fundamental principle that a class is determined by its elements, and it is the basis for proving set equality throughout the development. The axiom is stated for classes generally, not just sets.
