The Kuratowski ordered pair $(x,y)$ is defined as $\{\{x\}, \{x,y\}\}$. This ingenious definition reduces the ordered pair to an unordered set, with the crucial property that $(x,y) = (u,v)$ implies $x = u$ and $y = v$ (when $x$ and $y$ are sets). The first and second coordinates are then recoverable via the operations $\bigcap\bigcap z$ and a more complex expression. Ordered pairs are the building blocks for relations and functions.
