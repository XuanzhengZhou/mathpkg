A filter converges to a point if every neighborhood of that point belongs to the filter. This definition allows the entire topological structure to be recovered from filter convergence: open sets are exactly those sets belonging to every filter converging to a point within them, and the neighborhood system of a point is the intersection of all filters converging to that point. Filters and nets lead to essentially equivalent convergence theories.
