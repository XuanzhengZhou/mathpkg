A lattice is a partially ordered set in which every pair of elements has both a least upper bound (join, $\vee$) and a greatest lower bound (meet, $\wedge$). Lattices arise throughout mathematics: the power set of any set forms a lattice under inclusion, as do the open sets of a topological space, the ideals of a ring, and the subspaces of a vector space.
