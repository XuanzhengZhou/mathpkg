The Darboux sums provide upper and lower approximations to the Riemann integral using suprema and infima of the function on subdivision intervals. Viewing the subdivisions as a directed set under refinement, the Darboux sums form nets, and the existence of the integral corresponds to the convergence of these nets to a common limit. This net-theoretic perspective unifies Riemann and Lebesgue integration by varying the notion of subdivision and the measure of intervals.
