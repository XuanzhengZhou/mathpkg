Let $X$ be an order-complete chain; that is, $X$ is linearly ordered by a relation $>$ such that each non-void subset of $X$ which has an upper bound has a supremum. Let $X$ have the order topology. A net $(S, >)$ in $X$ is monotone increasing (resp. decreasing) if and only if whenever $m > n$, then $S_m \geq S_n$ (resp. $S_n \geq S_m$). Each monotone increasing net in $X$ whose range is bounded above converges to the supremum of its range.
