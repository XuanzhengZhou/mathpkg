Let $f: G \to J$ be a continuous homomorphism of topological groups. Then the map $\tilde{f}: G \to f[G]$ obtained by endowing $f[G]$ with the quotient topology induced by the surjection $G \to f[G]$ is a continuous open homomorphism. Moreover, the identity map $\iota: f[G] \to J$ (where $f[G]$ has the quotient topology and $J$ has its original topology) is a continuous one-to-one homomorphism. Thus every continuous homomorphism $f$ factors as $f = \iota \circ \tilde{f}$, a continuous open homomorphism followed by a continuous one-to-one homomorphism.
