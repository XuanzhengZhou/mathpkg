This theorem establishes a relationship between upper semi-continuity of a non-negative function and upper semi-continuity of the decomposition of its subgraph into vertical slices. If the vertical slice decomposition of the region under the graph is upper semi-continuous in the sense of decomposition spaces, then the function itself is upper semi-continuous. The converse also holds and requires Theorem 5.12 of Kelley.
