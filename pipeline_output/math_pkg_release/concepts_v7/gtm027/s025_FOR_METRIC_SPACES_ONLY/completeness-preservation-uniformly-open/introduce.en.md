This theorem is the concluding result of the chapter on metric spaces. It states that completeness of pseudo-metric spaces is preserved under continuous uniformly open surjections, provided the target space is Hausdorff. Unlike the Baire theorem, this result is peculiar to pseudo-metric spaces — it fails for arbitrary uniform spaces, as shown by Köthe's example of a complete linear topological space with a non-complete quotient. The proof relies on the Closed Relation Ball Lemma, a formalization of an argument due to Banach, which also yields the closed graph and open mapping theorems of functional analysis.
