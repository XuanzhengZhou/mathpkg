This example demonstrates that a uniform space may fail to be metrizable even though its induced topology is metrizable. The construction uses the set of all countable ordinals, with entourages defined by a tail-equivalence condition: two ordinals are U_a-related if they are equal or both are at least a. The resulting uniform topology is discrete (hence metrizable), but the uniformity itself is not generated by any metric. This shows that the metrizability of a uniform space is a strictly stronger condition than the metrizability of its topology.
