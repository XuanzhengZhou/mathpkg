A subset of a topological space is meager (or of the first category) if it can be written as a countable union of nowhere dense sets. Meager sets are considered "small" in the sense of Baire category, though they can be dense. In a complete metric space, the complement of a meager set is always dense — this is the category-theoretic formulation of Baire's theorem. The complement of a meager set is called residual or comeager.
