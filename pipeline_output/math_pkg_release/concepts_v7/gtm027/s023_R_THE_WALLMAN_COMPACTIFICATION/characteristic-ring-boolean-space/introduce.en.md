The characteristic ring of a Boolean space is the Boolean ring formed by its clopen subsets. Under Stone duality, this construction is the forward functor from Boolean spaces to Boolean algebras: every Boolean space gives rise to the Boolean algebra of its clopen sets. Conversely, every Boolean algebra yields a Boolean space (its Stone space) whose points are the ultrafilters of the algebra. The Wallman compactification generalizes this: for any $T_1$-space $X$, the space of closed ultrafilters on $X$ with the Wallman topology is a compact $T_1$-space containing $X$ as a dense subspace.
