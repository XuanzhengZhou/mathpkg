The induced map $F^*$ associated to a continuous map $F: X \to Y$ between compact Hausdorff spaces is an algebra homomorphism from $C(Y)$ to $C(X)$. This is the starting point of Gelfand duality: the passage from continuous maps to algebra homomorphisms between function algebras is contravariant and structure-preserving.
