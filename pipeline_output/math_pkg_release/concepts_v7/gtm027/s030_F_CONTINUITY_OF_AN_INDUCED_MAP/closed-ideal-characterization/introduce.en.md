Every closed ideal $I$ in $C(X)$ for a compact Hausdorff space $X$ consists precisely of all continuous functions that vanish on the common zero set $Z$ of $I$. This establishes a one-to-one correspondence between closed ideals of $C(X)$ and closed subsets of $X$, with maximal ideals corresponding to singleton sets (points). This is a central result in Gelfand duality.
