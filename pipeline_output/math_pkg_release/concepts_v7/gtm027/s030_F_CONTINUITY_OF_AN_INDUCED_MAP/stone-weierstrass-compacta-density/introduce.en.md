This is the Stone-Weierstrass theorem in the topology of uniform convergence on compacta. A subalgebra of $C(X)$ that separates points (the two-point property: for any distinct $x, y \in X$, there exists a function in the subalgebra taking different values at $x$ and $y$) is dense in $C(X)$ under compact convergence. Kelley notes this as "unquestionably the most useful known result on $C(X)$."
