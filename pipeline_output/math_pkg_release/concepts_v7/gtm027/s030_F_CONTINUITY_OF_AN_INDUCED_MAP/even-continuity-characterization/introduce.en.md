Even continuity of a family of functions is characterized by a net convergence condition: whenever a net of points converges to $x$ and the corresponding net of function values at $x$ converges to $y$, then the net of function values evaluated at the moving points must also converge to $y$. This condition captures the simultaneous continuity of the family in both the function and point arguments.
