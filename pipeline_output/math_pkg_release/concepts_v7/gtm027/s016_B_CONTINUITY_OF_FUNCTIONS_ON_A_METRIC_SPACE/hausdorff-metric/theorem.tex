Let $(X, d)$ be a metric space of finite diameter, and let $\alpha$ be the family of all closed subsets. For $r > 0$ and $A$ in $\alpha$ let
$$V_r(A) = \{ x : \operatorname{dist}(x, A) < r \},$$
and define, for members $A$ and $B$ of $\alpha$,
$$d'(A, B) = \inf \{ r : A \subset V_r(B) \text{ and } B \subset V_r(A) \}.$$
Then $d'$ is the Hausdorff metric on $\alpha$.
