Tychonoff's theorem states that the cartesian product of any collection of compact topological spaces is compact in the product topology. It is widely regarded as the single most important theorem in general topology, with deep consequences throughout analysis and functional analysis. The proof relies on the Alexander subbase theorem and the finite intersection property, constructing a point in the product whose coordinates lie in the closure of projections of a maximal family with the finite intersection property. The theorem is equivalent to the axiom of choice.
