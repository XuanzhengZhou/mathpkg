A subset of a pseudo-metric (or metric) space is bounded if its diameter — the supremum of distances between any two points in the set — is finite. In the special case of the real line with the usual metric, boundedness is equivalent to the set having both an upper and a lower bound. Boundedness plays a key role in the Heine-Borel-Lebesgue theorem, which characterizes compact subsets of Euclidean space as precisely those that are closed and bounded.
