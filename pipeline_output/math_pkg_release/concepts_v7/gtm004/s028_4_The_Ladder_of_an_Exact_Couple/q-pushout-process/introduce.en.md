The $Q_{\sigma}$-process is a categorical pushout construction that "factors out" a subobject from the middle term of a two-step sequence $A \to B_{0,1} \to C_1$ along an epimorphism $A \twoheadrightarrow A_1$. Given that the composite factors through the epimorphism, one forms the pushout of $\beta_{0,1}$ along $\sigma$, yielding $B_1 \cong B_{0,1}/\beta_{0,1}\sigma^{-1}(0)$ together with induced maps $\beta_1 : A_1 \to B_1$ and $\gamma_1 : B_1 \to C_1$. Along with the dual $Q^{\varepsilon}$-pullback process, it provides the building block for iteratively deriving the exact couple ladder and the associated spectral sequence.
