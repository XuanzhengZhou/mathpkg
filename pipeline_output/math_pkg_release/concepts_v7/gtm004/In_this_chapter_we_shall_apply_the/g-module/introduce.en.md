A $G$-module is an abelian group equipped with a linear action of the group $G$. The universal property of the integral group ring $\mathbb{Z}G$ establishes that $G$-modules are precisely modules over $\mathbb{Z}G$, unifying group actions with ring-theoretic module theory. A trivial $G$-module is one where every group element acts as the identity.
