The semi-direct product $A \rtimes G$ of a group $G$ by a $G$-module $A$ is a group constructed on the Cartesian product $A \times G$ with multiplication twisted by the $G$-action on $A$. It fits into a split short exact sequence $A \to A \rtimes G \to G$, where the splitting $s(x) = (0,x)$ is a group homomorphism. Unlike the abelian case, the projection $q(a,x) = a$ onto $A$ is not a group homomorphism but is instead a derivation. The semi-direct product is the universal example of a split extension of $G$ by $A$.
