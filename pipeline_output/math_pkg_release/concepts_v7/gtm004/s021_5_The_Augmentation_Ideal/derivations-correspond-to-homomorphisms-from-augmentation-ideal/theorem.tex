There is a natural isomorphism
$$\operatorname{Der}(G, A) \cong \operatorname{Hom}_G(IG, A),$$
given by $\eta : \operatorname{Hom}_G(IG, A) \to \operatorname{Der}(G, A)$ where
$$\eta(\varphi)(y) = \varphi(y - 1) \quad \text{for } \varphi \in \operatorname{Hom}_G(IG, A),\; y \in G.$$
The inverse map sends a derivation $d : G \to A$ to the $G$-module homomorphism $\varphi_d : IG \to A$ defined by
$$\varphi_d(y - 1) = d(y).$$
