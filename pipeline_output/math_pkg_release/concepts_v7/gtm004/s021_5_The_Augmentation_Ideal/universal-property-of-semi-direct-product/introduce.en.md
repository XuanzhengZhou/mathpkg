Proposition 5.3 gives the universal property of the semi-direct product $A \rtimes G$: any group homomorphism $f : X \to G$ together with an $f$-derivation $d : X \to A$ factors uniquely through $A \rtimes G$. This means $A \rtimes G$ is the representing object for pairs $(f, d)$ consisting of a homomorphism to $G$ and a compatible derivation to $A$. The universal property formalizes the idea that the semi-direct product is the "free" split extension.
