Let $F: \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{D}$ and $G: \mathfrak{D} \to \mathfrak{C}$ be functors. We say that $F$ is \text{left adjoint} to $G$ (and $G$ is \text{right adjoint} to $F$), and write $\eta: F \dashv G$, if there is a natural equivalence
$$\eta = \eta_{XY}: \mathfrak{D}(FX, Y) \xrightarrow{\cong} \mathfrak{C}(X, GY)$$
of functors $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}} \times \mathfrak{D} \to \mathfrak{S}$. The natural equivalence $\eta$ is called the \text{adjugant equivalence} or simply the \text{adjugant}.
