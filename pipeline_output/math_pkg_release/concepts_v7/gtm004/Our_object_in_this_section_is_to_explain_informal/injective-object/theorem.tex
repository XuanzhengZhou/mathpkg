An object $I$ in a category $\mathfrak{C}$ is called \text{injective} if it is projective in $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$. Equivalently, given any diagram
$$\begin{array}{ccc}
A & \xrightarrow{\mu} & B \\
{\scriptstyle\varphi}\downarrow & & \\
I & &
\end{array}$$
in $\mathfrak{C}$ with $\mu$ monomorphic, there exists a morphism $\psi: B \to I$ such that $\psi \mu = \varphi$.
