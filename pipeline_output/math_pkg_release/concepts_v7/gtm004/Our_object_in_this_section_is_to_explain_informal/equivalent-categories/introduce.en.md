Equivalence of categories is the proper notion of "sameness" for categories, weaker than isomorphism but strong enough that equivalent categories share all categorical properties. Unlike isomorphic categories, equivalent categories may have non-isomorphic object collections, as long as every object is isomorphic to one in the image of the equivalence. A skeleton of a category is always equivalent to the original category, though generally not isomorphic to it.
