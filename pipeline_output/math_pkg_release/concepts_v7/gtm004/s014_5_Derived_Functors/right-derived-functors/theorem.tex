Let $T : \mathfrak{M}_A \rightarrow \mathfrak{Ub}$ be an additive covariant functor. For any $\Lambda$-module $A$, take an injective resolution $I$ of $A$, form the cochain complex $TI$, and take cohomology:
$$R^n TA = H^n(TI), \quad n = 0, 1, \ldots$$
The resulting functors $R^n T : \mathfrak{M}_A \rightarrow \mathfrak{Ub}$, $n = 0, 1, \ldots$, are called the \textbf{$n$-th right derived functors} of $T$.

For a homomorphism $\alpha : A \rightarrow A'$ and injective resolutions $I, I'$ of $A, A'$ respectively, there exists a cochain map $\alpha : I \rightarrow I'$ inducing $\alpha$. The induced map $T\alpha : TI \rightarrow TI'$ defines
$$\alpha_* = H^n(T\alpha) : R^n TA \rightarrow R^n TA', \quad n = 0, 1, \ldots$$
which is independent of the choice of $\alpha$.
