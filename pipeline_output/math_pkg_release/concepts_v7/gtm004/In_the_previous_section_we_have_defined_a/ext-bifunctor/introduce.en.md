Theorem 2.3 of Hilton--Stammbach establishes that $\operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}(-, -)$ is a bifunctor from $\Lambda$-modules to abelian groups. The contravariance in the first argument means that a map $\alpha : A' \to A$ induces $\alpha^* : \operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}(A, B) \to \operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}(A', B)$; the covariance in the second argument means that a map $\beta : B \to B'$ induces $\beta_* : \operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}(A, B) \to \operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}(A, B')$. Together with the natural equivalence of Corollary 2.2, this makes $\operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}$ a well-defined bifunctor independent of the choice of projective presentations.
