Let $\alpha : A' \to A$ be a homomorphism of $\Lambda$-modules. Choose projective presentations $R' \xrightarrow{\mu'} P' \xrightarrow{\varepsilon'} A'$ and $R \xrightarrow{\mu} P \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} A$, and let $\pi : P' \to P$ be any homomorphism lifting $\alpha$ with induced map $\sigma : R' \to R$. Then the induced homomorphism
$$\pi^* : \operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}^{\varepsilon}(A, B) \longrightarrow \operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}^{\varepsilon'}(A', B), \quad [\varphi] \mapsto [\varphi \circ \sigma]$$
does not depend on the chosen lifting $\pi$, but only on $\alpha : A' \to A$.
