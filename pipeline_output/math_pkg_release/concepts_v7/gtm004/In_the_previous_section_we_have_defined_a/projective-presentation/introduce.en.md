A projective presentation of a $\Lambda$-module $A$ is a short exact sequence $R \to P \to A$ in which $P$ is a projective module. It resolves $A$ by a projective object $P$, with $R$ (the "relation" module) measuring the failure of $P$ to be isomorphic to $A$. Projective presentations are the fundamental building blocks for defining the Ext functor: every module admits one (since every module is a quotient of a free, hence projective, module), and the resulting Ext groups are independent of the chosen presentation up to natural equivalence.
