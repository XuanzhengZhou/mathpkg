Theorem 5.2 states that for a semi-simple Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ over a field of characteristic zero, the trace form of any faithful representation is non-degenerate. This deep result is closely related to Cartan's criterion for solvability and generalizes the fact that the Killing form of a semi-simple Lie algebra is non-degenerate. The theorem is used in proving the complete reducibility of representations (Weyl's theorem).
