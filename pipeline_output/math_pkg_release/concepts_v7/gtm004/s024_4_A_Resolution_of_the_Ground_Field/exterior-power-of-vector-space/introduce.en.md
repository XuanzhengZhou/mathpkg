The exterior power $E_n V$ of a vector space $V$ is the quotient of the $n$-fold tensor product by the subspace identifying elements that differ by a sign change according to a permutation. It captures the alternating multilinear nature of forms. Elements are denoted by $\langle x_1, \ldots, x_n \rangle$ and satisfy $\langle x_1, \ldots, x_n \rangle = (\operatorname{sig} \sigma) \langle x_{\sigma 1}, \ldots, x_{\sigma n} \rangle$. This construction is fundamental for defining the Chevalley--Eilenberg complex used in Lie algebra cohomology.
