A Lie algebra is semi-simple if it has no non-zero abelian ideals. Over a field of characteristic zero, this definition is equivalent to the Killing form being non-degenerate (Cartan's criterion) and to $\mathfrak{g}$ being a direct sum of simple Lie algebras. Semi-simple Lie algebras play a central role in the structure theory of Lie algebras, as every finite-dimensional Lie algebra is a split extension of a semi-simple algebra by a solvable radical (the Levi decomposition).
