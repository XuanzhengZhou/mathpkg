A Lie algebra is solvable if its derived series terminates at zero after finitely many steps. The derived length is the smallest $n$ with $\mathfrak{g}_n = 0$. Solvability is preserved under extensions (Lemma 6.4), and the sum of two solvable ideals is solvable (Lemma 6.5), guaranteeing the existence of a unique maximal solvable ideal — the radical.
