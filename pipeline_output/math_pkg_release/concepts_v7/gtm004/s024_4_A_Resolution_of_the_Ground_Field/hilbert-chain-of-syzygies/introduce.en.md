Theorem 7.1 (Hilbert's Chain-of-Syzygies Theorem) computes the global dimension of the polynomial ring $P = K[x_1, \ldots, x_m]$ as exactly $m$. The theorem further asserts that projective graded $P$-modules are automatically free (the Serre conjecture, proven independently by Quillen and Suslin, handles the ungraded case). The proof uses the Koszul resolution (Proposition 7.2) as the standard $P$-free resolution of $K$, and the Tor-vanishing criterion (Theorem 7.4) to establish freeness of graded modules with $\operatorname{Tor}_1^P(B, K) = 0$.
