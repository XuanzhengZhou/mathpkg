Theorem 5.2 establishes a six-term exact sequence linking the Hom and Ext functors when the second argument varies over a short exact sequence. Given a short exact sequence $B' \to B \to B''$ and a fixed module $A$, there is a connecting homomorphism $\omega: \operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(A, B'') \to \operatorname{Ext}_\Lambda(A, B')$ yielding an exact sequence that starts at $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(A, B')$ and ends at $\operatorname{Ext}_\Lambda(A, B'')$. The proof uses a projective presentation of $A$ and applies Lemma 5.1 to the resulting diagram of Hom groups. This is one of the two fundamental long exact sequences that make Ext a derived functor.
