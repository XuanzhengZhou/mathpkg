Theorem 2.4 of Hilton-Stammbach is the fundamental result that justifies the name Ext: it establishes a natural equivalence of bifunctors between the set $E(A, B)$ of equivalence classes of module extensions and the group $\text{Ext}_\Lambda(A, B)$. The proof constructs explicit inverse maps $\eta : E(A, B) \to \text{Ext}_\Lambda(A, B)$ and $\xi : \text{Ext}_\Lambda(A, B) \to E(A, B)$ using pull-backs and push-outs respectively, and verifies using a 3-dimensional diagram chase that $\eta$ is natural in both variables and independent of the chosen projective presentation.
