Corollary 2.5 of Hilton-Stammbach observes that the set $E(A, B)$ of equivalence classes of module extensions inherits a natural abelian group structure via the natural equivalence $E(-, -) \cong \text{Ext}_\Lambda(-, -)$ established in Theorem 2.4. The neutral element is the split extension $B \to B \oplus A \to A$. An extension $B \to E \to A$ represents the zero element if and only if the map $\psi : R \to B$ constructed in the proof of Theorem 2.4 factors through $P$. This group structure coincides with the Baer sum of extensions.
