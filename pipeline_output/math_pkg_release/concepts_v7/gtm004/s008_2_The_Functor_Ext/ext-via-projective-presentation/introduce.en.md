Given a projective presentation $R \to P \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} A$ of a $\Lambda$-module $A$, the group $\text{Ext}_\Lambda^\varepsilon(A, B)$ is defined as the cokernel of the induced map $\mu^* : \text{Hom}_\Lambda(P, B) \to \text{Hom}_\Lambda(R, B)$. The superscript $\varepsilon$ serves as a reminder that this definition depends on the chosen projective presentation. A central result of the theory is that different choices of projective presentation yield naturally isomorphic Ext groups, allowing one to drop the superscript and speak simply of $\text{Ext}_\Lambda(A, B)$.
