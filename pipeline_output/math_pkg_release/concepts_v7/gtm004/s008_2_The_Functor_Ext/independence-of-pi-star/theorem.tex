Let $\alpha : A' \to A$ be a homomorphism of $\Lambda$-modules, and let $R' \to P' \xrightarrow{\varepsilon'} A'$ and $R \to P \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} A$ be projective presentations. Suppose $\pi_1, \pi_2 : P' \to P$ are two homomorphisms lifting $\alpha$ (i.e., $\varepsilon \pi_i = \alpha \varepsilon'$ for $i = 1, 2$), inducing $\sigma_1, \sigma_2 : R' \to R$ respectively. Then the induced maps on Ext coincide:
$$\pi_1^* = \pi_2^* : \text{Ext}_\Lambda^\varepsilon(A, B) \to \text{Ext}_\Lambda^{\varepsilon'}(A', B).$$
Thus $\pi^*$ depends only on $\alpha$ and not on the chosen lifting $\pi$.
