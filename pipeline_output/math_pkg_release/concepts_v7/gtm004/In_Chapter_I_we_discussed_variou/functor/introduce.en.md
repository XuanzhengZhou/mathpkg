A (covariant) functor is a structure-preserving map between categories. It assigns to each object of the source category an object of the target category, and to each morphism a morphism in the target, while preserving identity morphisms and composition. Functors are the morphisms of the "category of categories." Familiar examples include the abelianization functor from groups to abelian groups, and representable functors $\mathfrak{C}(A, -): \mathfrak{C} \to \mathbf{Set}$.
