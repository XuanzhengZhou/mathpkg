Lemma 1.2 records two useful consequences of a commutative square being a pullback. Part (i) states that the vertical map $\beta$ restricts to the kernels of the horizontal maps, which is proved in full generality in Theorem II.6.2. Part (ii) is crucial for the extension theory: it guarantees that pulling back an epimorphism (a projection $E \to A$) along a homomorphism $\alpha: A' \to A$ again yields an epimorphism, so the pullback construction indeed produces a new extension.
