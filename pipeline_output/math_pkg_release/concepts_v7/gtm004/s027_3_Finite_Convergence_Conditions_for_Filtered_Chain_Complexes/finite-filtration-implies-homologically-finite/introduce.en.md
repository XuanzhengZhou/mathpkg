Proposition 3.4 establishes that finiteness of a filtration is a stronger condition than homological finiteness. The proof is straightforward: condition (i) of a finite filtration ($C_q^{(p)} = 0$ for $p \leq p_0$) trivially forces $H_q(C^{(p)}) = 0$ for the same range, giving homological finiteness condition (i). For condition (ii), if $C_q^{(p)} = C_q$ for $p \geq p_1$, then $C_q^{(p)} = C_q$ for all large $p$, and the homology groups stabilize accordingly, yielding $H_q(C^{(p)}) = H_q(C)$. Thus every finite filtration is homologically finite, enabling the application of Theorem 3.5 to any finite filtration.
