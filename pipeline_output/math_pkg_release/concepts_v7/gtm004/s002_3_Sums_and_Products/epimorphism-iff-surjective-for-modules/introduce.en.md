In the category of $\Lambda$-modules, the categorical notion of epimorphism coincides with the set-theoretic notion of surjectivity. Surjectivity clearly implies the right-cancellation property. Conversely, if $\varepsilon: B \to C$ is epimorphic, consider the canonical projection $\pi: C \to C / \varepsilon B$ and the zero map $0: C \to C / \varepsilon B$; since $\pi \circ \varepsilon = 0 = 0 \circ \varepsilon$, right-cancellation forces $\pi = 0$, hence $\varepsilon B = C$. This fortunate coincidence simplifies the categorical treatment of modules.
