Maschke's theorem in its classical form states that the group algebra $kG$ of a finite group $G$ over a field $k$ is semisimple if and only if the characteristic $p$ of $k$ does not divide the group order $|G|$. This homological reformulation expresses the same fact in terms of the vanishing of the first extension functor: nontrivial extensions between $kG$-modules exist precisely when $p \mid |G|$. It marks the entry point of homological algebra into modular representation theory.
