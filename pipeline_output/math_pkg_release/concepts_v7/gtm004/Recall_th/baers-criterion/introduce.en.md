Baer's Criterion provides a practical test for injectivity: instead of checking the extension property for all monomorphisms into all modules, it suffices to check only the inclusions of left ideals into the ring itself. This dramatically simplifies the verification that a module is injective. The criterion is essential in the proof of Theorem 7.1, where it is used to show that divisible modules over a PID satisfy the injectivity condition by reducing the problem to principal ideals.
