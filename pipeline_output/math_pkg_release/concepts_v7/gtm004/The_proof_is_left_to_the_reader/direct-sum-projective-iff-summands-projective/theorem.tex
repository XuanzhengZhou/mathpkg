A direct sum $\bigoplus_{i \in I} P_i$ of $\Lambda$-modules is projective if and only if each summand $P_i$ is projective. In particular, for a direct sum of two modules $P \oplus Q$:

\begin{itemize}
\item If $P$ and $Q$ are projective, then $P \oplus Q$ is projective.
\item If $P \oplus Q$ is projective, then both $P$ and $Q$ are projective.
\end{itemize}

The forward direction uses the universal property of the direct sum: given a surjection $\varepsilon : B \rightarrow C$ and $\gamma : P \oplus Q \rightarrow C$, the lifts for the restrictions $\gamma|_P$ and $\gamma|_Q$ combine to a lift for $\gamma$. The converse holds because summands of a projective are projective (lifting to $P$ by extending to $P \oplus Q$ with the zero map on the other summand).
