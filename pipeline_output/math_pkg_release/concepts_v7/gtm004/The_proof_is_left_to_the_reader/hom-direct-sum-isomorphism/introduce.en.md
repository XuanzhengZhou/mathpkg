This proposition establishes that the Hom functor converts a direct sum in the first argument into a direct product. Specifically, a homomorphism out of a direct sum is uniquely determined by a family of homomorphisms out of each summand, and this correspondence is an isomorphism of abelian groups. The isomorphism is natural in all variables and encapsulates the universal property of the direct sum in the language of the Hom functor. It is a special case of the more general adjunction between the Hom and tensor product functors.
