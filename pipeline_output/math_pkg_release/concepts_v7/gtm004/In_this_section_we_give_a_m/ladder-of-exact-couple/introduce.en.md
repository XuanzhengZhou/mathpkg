The ladder of an exact couple is a two-parameter grid of objects and morphisms constructed by iteratively factoring the endomorphism $\alpha$ and applying the Q-processes. Starting from the original exact couple, each iteration splits $\alpha_n$ as $\varrho_n\sigma_n$ and defines $\alpha_{n+1} = \sigma_n\varrho_n$, producing the derived couples along the diagonal. The off-diagonal entries $E_{m,n}$ for $m \neq n$ provide an enriched structure that facilitates the analysis of convergence of the associated spectral sequence.
