To construct the ladder of a (bi)graded exact couple while keeping vertical morphisms degree-preserving, factorize $\alpha$ as

$$\alpha = \varrho\omega\sigma, \quad D \xrightarrow{\sigma} D' \xrightarrow{\omega} D'' \xrightarrow{\varrho} D,$$

where $\omega$ is an isomorphism carrying the (bi)degree of $\alpha$. More generally,

$$\alpha^n = \varrho_n \omega_n \sigma_n,$$

where $\omega_n: D'_n \xrightarrow{\cong} D''_n$ carries the degree of $\alpha^n$.

The graded ladder sequence then takes the form

$$D'_m \xrightarrow{\alpha'} D'_m \xrightarrow{\beta_{m,n}} E_{m,n} \xrightarrow{\gamma_{m,n}} D''_n \xrightarrow{\alpha''} D''_n$$

with $\alpha''_n \omega_n = \omega_n \alpha'_n$. By convention, one chooses $D_n = D''_n$ and sets

$$\alpha_n = \alpha''_n, \quad \beta_n = \beta'_n \omega_n^{-1}, \quad \gamma_n = \gamma''_n,$$

so that the degree rules of the original exact couple are preserved at each rung.
