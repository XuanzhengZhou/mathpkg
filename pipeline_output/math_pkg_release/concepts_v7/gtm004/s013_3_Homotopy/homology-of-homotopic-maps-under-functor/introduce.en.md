Corollary 3.5 of Hilton--Stammbach combines Proposition 3.1 and Lemma 3.4 to state that if two chain maps $\varphi, \psi : C \to D$ are homotopic, then for any additive functor $F$ the induced maps in homology coincide: $H(F(\varphi)) = H(F(\psi))$. The practical value is its contrapositive: to show two chain maps are not homotopic, it suffices to find an additive functor $F$ such that $H(F(\varphi)) \neq H(F(\psi))$. This technique is illustrated by the example with $-\otimes \mathbb{Z}_2$ in the same section.
