This proposition identifies the graded homology $\operatorname{Gr} \circ H(C)$ of a filtered chain complex $C$ within the data of its associated Rees system. Writing $A = A(C)$ for the direct sum of the filtration subcomplexes and $B = B(C)$ for the shifted sum, the graded homology equals the quotient of the images of homology under the inclusion maps: $i_A H(A) / i_B H(B)$. This is also equal to $\ker j_A / \ker j_B$, where $j_A$ and $j_B$ are the morphisms from the exact couple structure. The formula makes explicit how the filtration on $C$ induces a filtration on its homology.
