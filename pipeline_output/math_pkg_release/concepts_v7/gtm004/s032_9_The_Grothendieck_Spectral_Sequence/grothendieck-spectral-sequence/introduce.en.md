The Grothendieck spectral sequence is a fundamental tool in homological algebra that relates the derived functors of a composite functor $G \circ F$ to the derived functors of $F$ and $G$ individually. Under the key hypothesis that $F$ preserves injectives up to $G$-acyclicity, it provides a first-quadrant spectral sequence with $E_1^{p,q} = (R^p G)(R^{q-p}F)(A)$ converging to $R^q(GF)(A)$. Its most celebrated application is the Lyndon--Hochschild--Serre spectral sequence in group cohomology, and it also yields the Hochschild--Serre spectral sequence for Lie algebra cohomology. Variants exist for left derived functors and contravariant functors.
