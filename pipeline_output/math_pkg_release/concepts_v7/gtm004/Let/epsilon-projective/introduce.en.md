An $\mathcal{E}$-projective object is one that is projective with respect to all epimorphisms in a specified class $\mathcal{E}$. This generalizes the absolute notion of projectivity (where $\mathcal{E}$ is the class of all epimorphisms). Different choices of $\mathcal{E}$ give different relative homological algebra theories, such as $\mathcal{E}_2$ (epimorphisms splitting as abelian group maps) in module categories.
