If $\{T_j\}$ is an $\mathcal{E}$-connected sequence of functors (with connecting morphisms $\omega_j : T_j(A'') \to T_{j-1}(A')$ for each short $\mathcal{E}$-exact sequence $0 \to A' \to A \to A'' \to 0$), then the long sequence
\[
\cdots \to T_j(A') \to T_j(A) \to T_j(A'') \xrightarrow{\omega_j} T_{j-1}(A') \to T_{j-1}(A) \to \cdots
\]
is differential (i.e., every composite of two consecutive maps is zero).
