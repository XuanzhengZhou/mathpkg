Proposition 4.3 is a corollary of Theorem 4.1: any two projective resolutions of a module $A$ are homotopy equivalent, with the homotopy equivalence being canonical (uniquely determined up to homotopy). This is the foundational result that makes derived functors well-defined: different projective resolutions yield isomorphic derived functor values because homotopy equivalent complexes have isomorphic homology after applying any additive functor.
