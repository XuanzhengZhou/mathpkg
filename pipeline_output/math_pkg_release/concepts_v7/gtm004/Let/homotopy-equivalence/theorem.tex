Two chain complexes $C$ and $D$ are of the \emph{same homotopy type} (or are \emph{homotopy equivalent}) if there exist chain maps $\varphi : C \to D$ and $\psi : D \to C$ such that
$$\psi \varphi \simeq 1_C \quad \text{and} \quad \varphi \psi \simeq 1_D.$$
The map $\varphi$ (or $\psi$) is then called a \emph{homotopy equivalence}. Homotopy equivalent complexes have isomorphic homology: $H(C) \cong H(D)$.
