A homotopy equivalence is a chain map that admits an inverse up to homotopy. It is a stronger notion than a quasi-isomorphism (which only requires an isomorphism in homology). Homotopy equivalences are the isomorphisms in the homotopy category, and they play a crucial role in the comparison of resolutions.
