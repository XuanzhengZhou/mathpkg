Proposition 3.1 is the fundamental property of homotopy: homotopic chain maps induce identical homomorphisms in homology. This is because the homotopy relation $\psi - \varphi = \partial \Sigma + \Sigma \partial$ shows that the difference $\psi - \varphi$ sends cycles to boundaries. This result justifies working in the homotopy category when only homological information is relevant.
