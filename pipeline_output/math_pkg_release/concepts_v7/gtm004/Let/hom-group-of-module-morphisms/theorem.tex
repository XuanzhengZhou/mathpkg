Let $A$ and $B$ be $\Lambda$-modules. The set $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(A, B)$ of all $\Lambda$-module homomorphisms from $A$ to $B$ has the structure of an abelian group: if $\varphi, \psi : A \to B$ are $\Lambda$-module homomorphisms, then
$$(\varphi + \psi)(a) = \varphi(a) + \psi(a)$$
for all $a \in A$. The zero element is the zero homomorphism, and the inverse of $\varphi$ is $-\varphi$ defined by $(-\varphi)(a) = -\varphi(a)$.
