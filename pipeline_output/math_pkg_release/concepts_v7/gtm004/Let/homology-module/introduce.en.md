The homology module $H_n(C)$ measures the failure of exactness of a chain complex at position $n$, being the quotient of cycles by boundaries. Homology is the central invariant of homological algebra: a chain complex is exact precisely when all its homology modules vanish. The homology functor $H(-)$ maps the category of chain complexes to graded modules, and a chain map $\varphi$ induces a well-defined map $H(\varphi)$ in homology.
