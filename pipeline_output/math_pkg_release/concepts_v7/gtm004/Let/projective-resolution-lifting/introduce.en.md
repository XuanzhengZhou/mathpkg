Theorem 4.1 is the fundamental comparison theorem for projective resolutions. Given a projective complex $C$ and an acyclic complex $D$, any map on $H_0$ lifts to a chain map, and this lift is unique up to homotopy. This theorem is used to prove that projective resolutions are unique up to homotopy equivalence and to define derived functors independent of the choice of resolution.
