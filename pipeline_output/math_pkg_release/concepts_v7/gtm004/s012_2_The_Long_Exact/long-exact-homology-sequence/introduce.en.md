A short exact sequence of chain complexes $0 \to A \to B \to C \to 0$ induces a long exact sequence connecting the homology groups $H_n(A)$, $H_n(B)$, and $H_n(C)$ in every degree. The connecting homomorphism $\omega_n : H_n(C) \to H_{n-1}(A)$ is constructed via the snake lemma applied to the diagram of kernels and cokernels. This theorem is fundamental to homological algebra, as it allows one to relate the homology of related complexes through exact sequences, and is the key tool for developing derived functors.
