The spectral sequence functor $SS: \mathfrak{EC} \to \mathfrak{E}$ is the bridge between exact couples and spectral sequences. Given an exact couple $\{D, E, \alpha, \beta, \gamma\}$, one forms the derived couple by setting $D_1 = \alpha D$, $E_1 = H(E, \beta\gamma)$, and defining new structure maps $\alpha_1, \beta_1, \gamma_1$ via the formulas $\beta_1(\alpha x) = [\beta x]$ and $\gamma_1[z] = \gamma(z)$. Iterating this process yields the spectral sequence $\{(E_n, d_n)\}$. This functorial construction is the reason exact couples are studied: they provide a systematic algebraic mechanism for generating spectral sequences.
