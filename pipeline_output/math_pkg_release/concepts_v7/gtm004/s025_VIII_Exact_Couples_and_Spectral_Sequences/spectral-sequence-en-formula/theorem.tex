Let $EC = \{D, E, \alpha, \beta, \gamma\}$ be an exact couple and let $\{(E_n, d_n)\}$ be the associated spectral sequence. Then for each $n \geq 0$,
$$E_n = \gamma^{-1}(\alpha^n D) / \beta(\alpha^{-n}(0)),$$
and $d_n: E_n \to E_n$ is induced by $\beta \alpha^{-n} \gamma$.

Moreover, for each $n$ there is an exact sequence
$$\alpha^n D \xrightarrow{\alpha_n} \alpha^n D \xrightarrow{\beta_n} E_n \xrightarrow{\gamma_n} \alpha^n D \xrightarrow{\alpha_n} \alpha^n D,$$
where $\alpha_n$ is induced by $\alpha$, $\beta_n$ is induced by $\beta\alpha^{-n}$, and $\gamma_n$ is induced by $\gamma$. Consequently, there is a short exact sequence
$$0 \to \operatorname{coker} \alpha_n \xrightarrow{\bar{\beta}_n} E_n \xrightarrow{\bar{\gamma}_n} \ker \alpha_n \to 0,$$
where
$$\operatorname{coker} \alpha_n = \alpha^n D / \alpha^{n+1} D \cong D / (\alpha D \cup \alpha^{-n}(0)), \qquad \ker \alpha_n = \alpha^n D \cap \alpha^{-1}(0).$$
