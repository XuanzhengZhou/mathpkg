Let $E = \{(E_n, d_n)\}$ be a spectral sequence. An element $x \in E_n$ (with $x \neq 0$) is said to \textbf{survive to infinity} if $x$ is a cycle for every $d_r$ ($r \geq n$) and a boundary for no $d_r$ ($r \geq n$).

Equivalently, $x \in E_{n,\infty}$ and $x$ does not represent the zero element in $E_\infty = \lim\limits_{\longrightarrow} (E_{n,\infty}, \sigma)$.
