The category $\mathfrak{EC}(\mathfrak{A})$ of exact couples in an abelian category $\mathfrak{A}$ provides the algebraic setting for constructing spectral sequences. Its objects are exact couples $\{D, E, \alpha, \beta, \gamma\}$ and its morphisms are pairs $(\kappa, \lambda)$ that commute with the structure maps. The significance of this category lies in the existence of the spectral sequence functor $SS: \mathfrak{EC} \to \mathfrak{E}$, which maps each exact couple to a spectral sequence through an iterative derivation process.
