Theorem 4.6 is one of the first substantive results in group cohomology. For a trivial coefficient module $A$, the map $\iota^*$ in Proposition 4.5 is zero, so $H^1(G, A) \cong \operatorname{Hom}_G(IG, A)$. Using Lemma 4.1, this is isomorphic to $\operatorname{Hom}(G_{\text{ab}}, A)$. Thus the first cohomology with trivial coefficients classifies group homomorphisms from the abelianization of $G$ into $A$. Moreover, the kernel of $\iota^*$ describes when a derivation is principal.
