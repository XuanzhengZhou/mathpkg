Lemma 4.1 establishes a fundamental isomorphism between the quotient $IG/(IG)^2$ and the abelianization $G_{\text{ab}}$ of the group $G$. The map sending a group element $x$ to $(x-1) + (IG)^2$ induces a well-defined group homomorphism from $G$, which factors through the commutator subgroup because $(x-1)(y-1) = (xy-1) - (x-1) - (y-1)$ lies in $(IG)^2$. This lemma is the key to relating first (co)homology to group-theoretic data.
