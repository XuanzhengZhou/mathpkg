A $G$-module is an abelian group equipped with a group action by automorphisms. The concepts of $G$-module and $\mathbb{Z}G$-module are equivalent, since a group homomorphism $G \to \operatorname{Aut} A$ extends uniquely to a ring homomorphism $\mathbb{Z}G \to \operatorname{End} A$. A trivial $G$-module is one where every group element acts as the identity.
