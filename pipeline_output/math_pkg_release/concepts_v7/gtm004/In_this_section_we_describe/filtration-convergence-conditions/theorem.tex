Let $M$ be a filtered object in a concrete abelian category with filtration
$$
\cdots \subseteq M^{(p-1)} \subseteq M^{(p)} \subseteq \cdots \subseteq M.
$$
The filtration is said to satisfy the \textbf{convergence conditions} if
$$
\text{(i)} \quad \bigcap_{p} M^{(p)} = 0, \qquad
\text{(ii)} \quad \bigcup_{p} M^{(p)} = M.
$$
When both conditions hold, every non-zero element $x \in M$ is represented by a unique homogeneous non-zero element in the associated graded object $Gr(M)$, and conversely every homogeneous non-zero element of $Gr(M)$ represents a non-zero element of $M$. Thus the only information lost in passing from $M$ to $Gr(M)$ is the extension data describing how $M^{(p-1)}$ embeds into $M^{(p)}$.
