A right module over a ring $\Lambda$ is defined by having the scalar multiplication act on the right rather than the left. The formal definition uses the opposite ring $\Lambda^{\mathrm{opp}}$: a right $\Lambda$-module is precisely a left module over the opposite ring. This elegant device allows the entire theory of left modules to be applied to right modules without duplication of effort.
