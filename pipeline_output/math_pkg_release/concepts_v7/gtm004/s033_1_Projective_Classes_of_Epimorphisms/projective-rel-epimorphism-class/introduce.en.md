In relative homological algebra, the notion of projectivity is refined by considering
a designated class $\mathcal{E}$ of epimorphisms rather than all epimorphisms.
An object $P$ is projective relative to a specific epimorphism $\mathcal{E}: B 	o C$
if every morphism from $P$ to $C$ lifts through $\mathcal{E}$ along the Hom-functor.
This concept generalizes the absolute notion of projectivity by relativizing with
respect to a chosen class of epimorphisms.
