The closure operator $C(\mathcal{E})$ on classes of epimorphisms defines a Galois-like connection: it collects all epimorphisms that are "projectively equivalent" to those in $\mathcal{E}$ in the sense that they allow lifts from all $\mathcal{E}$-projective objects. The fixed points of this operator are the closed classes, which provide the natural setting for relative homological algebra. Working with closed classes ensures that the relative projective objects behave consistently, just as ordinary projectives do in the absolute case.
