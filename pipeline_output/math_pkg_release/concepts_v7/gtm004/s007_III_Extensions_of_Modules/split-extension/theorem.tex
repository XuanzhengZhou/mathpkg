An extension of $A$ by $B$ is called a \text{split extension} if it is equivalent to the extension
$$
B \xrightarrow{\iota_B} A \oplus B \xrightarrow{\pi_A} A
$$
where $\iota_B: B \to A \oplus B$ is the canonical inclusion and $\pi_A: A \oplus B \to A$ is the canonical projection. Equivalently, there exist maps $\sigma: A \to E$ and $\rho: E \to B$ such that $\nu \circ \sigma = 1_A$ and $\rho \circ \kappa = 1_B$.
