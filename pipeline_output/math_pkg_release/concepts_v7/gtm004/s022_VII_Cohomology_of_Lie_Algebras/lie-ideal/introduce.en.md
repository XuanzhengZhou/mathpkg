A Lie ideal is a subalgebra that is closed under bracketing with arbitrary elements of the ambient Lie algebra — analogous to a two-sided ideal in ring theory or a normal subgroup in group theory. Lie ideals are precisely the kernels of Lie algebra homomorphisms, and the quotient by a Lie ideal inherits a natural Lie algebra structure.
