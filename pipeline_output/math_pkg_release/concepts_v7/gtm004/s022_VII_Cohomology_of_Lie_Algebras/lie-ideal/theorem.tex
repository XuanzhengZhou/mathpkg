Let $\mathfrak{g}$ be a Lie algebra over a field $K$. A Lie subalgebra $\mathfrak{h} \subseteq \mathfrak{g}$ is called a \textbf{Lie ideal} of $\mathfrak{g}$ if
$$
[x, y] \in \mathfrak{h}, \qquad \forall x \in \mathfrak{g}, \; \forall y \in \mathfrak{h}.
$$
If $\mathfrak{h}$ is a Lie ideal of $\mathfrak{g}$, then the quotient space $\mathfrak{g}/\mathfrak{h}$ carries a natural Lie algebra structure induced by the Lie bracket in $\mathfrak{g}$.
