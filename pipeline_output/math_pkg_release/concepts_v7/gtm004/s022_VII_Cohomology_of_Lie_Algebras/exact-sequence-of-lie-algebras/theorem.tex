A sequence of Lie algebra homomorphisms
$$
\mathfrak{n} \xrightarrow{\;f\;} \mathfrak{g} \xrightarrow{\;g\;} \mathfrak{h}
$$
over a field $K$ is called \textbf{exact} if $\operatorname{im} f = \ker g$. In particular, we say the sequence $\mathfrak{n} \to \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{h}$ is exact if $\mathfrak{n}$ (identified with its image) is a Lie ideal of $\mathfrak{g}$ and $\mathfrak{h} \cong \mathfrak{g}/\mathfrak{n}$.
