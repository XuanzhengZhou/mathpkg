Let $G$ be a finite group and $k$ a field of characteristic $p$. Over the group algebra $kG$, the notions of projective and injective module coincide. The indecomposable modules that are projective (equivalently, injective) over $kG$ are precisely the indecomposable direct summands of the regular module $kG$. These are called the {\it principal indecomposable modules} (PIMs). They correspond bijectively to the isomorphism classes of simple $kG$-modules.
