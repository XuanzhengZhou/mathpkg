Let $G$ be a finite group, $k$ a field of characteristic $p$, and $M$ an indecomposable $kG$-module. The {\it complexity} $c_G(M)$ is a numerical invariant associated to $M$, defined via the growth rate of a minimal projective resolution of $M$. One has:
\[
c_G(M) = 0 \iff M \text{ is $kG$-projective},
\]
and $c_G(k)$ equals the Krull dimension of $H^*(G, k)$.
