A block is an equivalence class of simple $kG$-modules determined by the connected components of the extension graph $\Gamma(G)$. Introduced by R. Brauer in the 1940s via characters, blocks were later recognized as fundamentally homological in nature. They partition the category of $kG$-modules into indecomposable direct summands of the group algebra.
