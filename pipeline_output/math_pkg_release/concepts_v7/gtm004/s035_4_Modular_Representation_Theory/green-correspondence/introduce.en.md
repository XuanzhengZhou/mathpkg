The Green correspondence establishes a remarkable bijection between indecomposable modules of a group $G$ and those of the normalizer $N_G(Q)$ of a $p$-subgroup $Q$, when attention is restricted to modules with vertex $Q$. This reduces the study of $G$-modules with a given vertex to the study of modules over a generally smaller group. The correspondence is given by conceptually simple operations: restriction and induction. It extends Brauer's earlier correspondence from character theory and is one of the most powerful tools in modular representation theory.
