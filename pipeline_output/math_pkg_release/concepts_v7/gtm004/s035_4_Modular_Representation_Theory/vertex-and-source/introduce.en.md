The vertex-source theory, developed by J.A. Green, provides a fundamental classification tool for indecomposable $kG$-modules in characteristic $p$. The vertex $V$ is the minimal subgroup relative to which the module is projective (well-defined up to $G$-conjugacy), and the source $N$ is the uniquely determined indecomposable $kV$-module from which $M$ is induced as a direct summand. This theory generalizes the class of projective modules and has deep connections with Brauer's defect groups.
