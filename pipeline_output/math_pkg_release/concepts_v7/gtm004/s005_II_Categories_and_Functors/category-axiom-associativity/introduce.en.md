Axiom A2 is the associative law for composition of morphisms in a category. It states that when composing three morphisms $f$, $g$, $h$, the result is independent of the order of composition — one may first compose $f$ with $g$ and then with $h$, or first compose $g$ with $h$ and then with $f$. This mirrors the associativity of ordinary function composition.
