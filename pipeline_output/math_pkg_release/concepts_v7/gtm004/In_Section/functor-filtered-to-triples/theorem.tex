Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an abelian category and let $(\mathfrak{A}, d, f)$ denote the category of filtered differential objects. Define the functor
$$F : (\mathfrak{A}, d, f) \to \mathfrak{T}(\mathfrak{A}^{\mathbb{Z}}, d)$$
on a filtered object
$$C = \left( \cdots \subseteq C^{(p-1)} \subseteq C^{(p)} \subseteq \cdots \subseteq C \right)$$
by setting
\begin{align*}
G(C) &= \bigoplus_{p \in \mathbb{Z}} C, \\
A(C) &= \bigoplus_{p \in \mathbb{Z}} C^{(p)},
\end{align*}
and taking the automorphism $\theta : G(C) \to G(C)$ to be the shift operator that sends the $p$-th copy of $C$ to the $(p+1)$-st copy.

The triple $(G(C), A(C), \theta)$ is an object of $\mathfrak{T}(\mathfrak{A}^{\mathbb{Z}}, d)$, where the differential and filtration are extended componentwise.
