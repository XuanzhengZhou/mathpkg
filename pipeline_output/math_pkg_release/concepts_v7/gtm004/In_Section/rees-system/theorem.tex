A \textbf{Rees system} in an abelian category $\mathfrak{A}$ consists of three exact couples
\begin{align*}
\textcircled{1} &: D \xrightarrow{\alpha} D \xrightarrow{\beta} E \xrightarrow{\gamma} D, \\
\textcircled{2} &: \bar{D} \xrightarrow{\bar{\alpha}} \bar{D} \xrightarrow{\bar{\beta}} E \xrightarrow{\bar{\gamma}} \bar{D}, \\
\textcircled{3} &: \bar{D} \xrightarrow{\xi} D \xrightarrow{\varphi} \Gamma \xrightarrow{\bar{\varphi}} \bar{D} \xrightarrow{\xi} D,
\end{align*}
together with morphisms connecting them, satisfying the following relations:
\begin{align*}
\alpha \xi &= \xi \bar{\alpha}, \\
\beta \xi &= \bar{\beta}, \\
\bar{\alpha} \bar{\varphi} &= \bar{\varphi} \varphi \alpha, \\
\bar{\beta} \bar{\varphi} &= 0, \\
\gamma \bar{\varphi} &= 0,
\end{align*}
and the additional condition that $\textcircled{3}$ is exact (so that $\xi, \varphi, \bar{\varphi}$ form an exact triangle).

Equivalently, a Rees system is a diagram
\begin{align*}
D &\xrightarrow{\alpha} D \xrightarrow{\beta} E \xrightarrow{\gamma} D, \\
\bar{D} &\xrightarrow{\bar{\alpha}} \bar{D} \xrightarrow{\bar{\beta}} E \xrightarrow{\bar{\gamma}} \bar{D}, \\
\bar{D} &\xrightarrow{\xi} D \xrightarrow{\varphi} \Gamma \xrightarrow{\bar{\varphi}} \bar{D} \xrightarrow{\xi} D,
\end{align*}
where the first two rows are exact couples, the third row is an exact triangle, and the morphisms satisfy the commutativity relations above.

Every exact couple $EC$ may be regarded as a Rees system by setting $\textcircled{1} = EC$, $\textcircled{2} = EC$, $\Gamma = 0$, $\xi = 1$.
