A \textbf{special Rees system} is a Rees system (6.9) together with an automorphism $\theta : \Gamma \to \Gamma$ satisfying the relations:
\begin{align*}
\theta \varphi &= \varphi \alpha, \\
\bar{\varphi} \theta &= \bar{\alpha} \bar{\varphi}, \\
\bar{\varphi} \theta^{-1} \varphi &= \bar{\gamma} \beta.
\end{align*}

The categories of Rees systems and special Rees systems are denoted $\mathfrak{R}(\mathfrak{A}, d)$ and $\mathfrak{S}(\mathfrak{A}, d)$ respectively, with an underlying forgetful functor $U : \mathfrak{S} \to \mathfrak{R}$.

Every exact couple $EC$ yields a special Rees system by setting $\textcircled{1} = EC$, $\textcircled{2} = EC$, $\Gamma = 0$, $\xi = 1$, giving a full embedding $E : \mathfrak{EC}(\mathfrak{A}) \to \mathfrak{S}(\mathfrak{A}, d)$.
