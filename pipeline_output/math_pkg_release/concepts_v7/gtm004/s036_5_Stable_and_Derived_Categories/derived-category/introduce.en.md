The derived category is the fundamental construction of modern homological algebra, introduced by J.L. Verdier in the context of sheaf theory. It is obtained from the homotopy category of complexes by formally inverting quasi-isomorphisms -- maps that induce isomorphisms on cohomology. Unlike the homotopy category, objects that are quasi-isomorphic become isomorphic in the derived category, making it the correct setting for derived functors. The original abelian category embeds fully and faithfully via the functor $J$, and the derived category carries a triangulated structure that generalizes the long exact sequences of classical homological algebra.
