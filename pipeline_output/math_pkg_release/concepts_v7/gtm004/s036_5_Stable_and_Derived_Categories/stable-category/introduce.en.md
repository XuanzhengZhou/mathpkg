The stable module category $\operatorname{StM}_R$ is obtained from the ordinary module category by identifying morphisms that differ by a projective homomorphism. In this category, projective modules become isomorphic to zero, and two modules are isomorphic precisely when they differ by projective direct summands. This construction eliminates the "projective noise" and reveals the essential structure of modules. The stable category is not abelian in general, but it carries additional structure -- notably, the Heller operator $\Omega$ becomes a well-defined functor here.
