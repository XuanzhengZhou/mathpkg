Morita equivalence identifies rings that have the same representation theory: their module categories are equivalent. A classic example is the equivalence between modules over a commutative ring $K$ and modules over the matrix ring $M_n(K)$. In modular representation theory, Morita equivalences appear frequently -- for instance, when passing from a group algebra to a block algebra. Morita theory shows that the equivalence is always given by tensoring with a suitable bimodule, and the rings are characterized as endomorphism rings of projective generators in each other's module categories.
