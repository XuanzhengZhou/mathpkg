Let $\mathfrak{C}$ be the category of cochain complexes over an abelian category $\mathfrak{A}$. The \textbf{homotopy category} $\mathfrak{K}$ of cochain complexes has the same objects as $\mathfrak{C}$, and its morphisms are the homotopy classes of cochain maps. The homology functor $H$ on $\mathfrak{C}$ factors through $\mathfrak{K}$; in particular, two cochain complexes $\mathbf{C}, \mathbf{C}'$ which are isomorphic in $\mathfrak{K}$ (i.e., homotopy equivalent) have isomorphic homology: $H(\mathbf{C}) \cong H(\mathbf{C}')$.

The homotopy category $\mathfrak{K}$ is additive but not in general abelian. It carries the structure of a triangulated category.
