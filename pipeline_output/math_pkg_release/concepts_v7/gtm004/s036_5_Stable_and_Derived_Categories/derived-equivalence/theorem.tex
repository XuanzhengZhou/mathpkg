Two abelian categories $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ are said to be {\it derived equivalent} if their (bounded) derived categories are equivalent as triangulated categories:
\[
\mathfrak{D}^b(\mathfrak{A}) \cong \mathfrak{D}^b(\mathfrak{B}).
\]
In modular representation theory, an important example is given by: if $G$ is a finite group with cyclic $p$-Sylow subgroup $P$ and $H = N_G(P)$, then the derived categories of the principal blocks of $kG$ and $kH$ are equivalent.
