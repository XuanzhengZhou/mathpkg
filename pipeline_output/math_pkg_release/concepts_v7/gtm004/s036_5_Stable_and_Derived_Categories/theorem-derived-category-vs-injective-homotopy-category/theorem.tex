\textbf{Theorem 5.1.} If the abelian category $\mathfrak{A}$ admits enough injectives, then the homotopy category $\mathfrak{K}(\mathcal{I})^{+}$ of bounded-below cochain complexes consisting of injective objects is equivalent to the bounded-below derived category $\mathfrak{D}^{+}(\mathfrak{A})$:
$$
\mathfrak{K}(\mathcal{I})^{+} \simeq \mathfrak{D}^{+}(\mathfrak{A}).
$$
The equivalence is induced by the natural functor $\mathfrak{K}(\mathcal{I})^{+} \to \mathfrak{D}^{+}(\mathfrak{A})$ sending an injective complex to its image in the derived category.
