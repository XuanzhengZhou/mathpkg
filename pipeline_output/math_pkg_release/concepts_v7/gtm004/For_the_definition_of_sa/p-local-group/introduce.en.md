A $P$-local group is one in which every element has a unique $q$-th root for every integer $q$ whose prime factors lie outside the set $P$. This generalizes the notion of a uniquely divisible group (the case $P = \emptyset$, where $P'$-numbers are all integers). For abelian groups, $P$-locality is equivalent to being a $\mathbb{Z}_P$-module, where $\mathbb{Z}_P$ is the ring of integers localized at the prime set $P$.
