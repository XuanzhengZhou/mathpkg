A $P$-local abelian group is one that is uniquely divisible by all integers whose prime factors lie outside the given set $P$ of primes. This condition is equivalent to the group being a module over the localized ring $\mathbb{Z}_P$. The $P$-localization functor $(-)_P = \mathbb{Z}_P \otimes (-)$ sends any abelian group to a $P$-local abelian group and serves as the abelian foundation for the $P$-localization theory of nilpotent groups.
