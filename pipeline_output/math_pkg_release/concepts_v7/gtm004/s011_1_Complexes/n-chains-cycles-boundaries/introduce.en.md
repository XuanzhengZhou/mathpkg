In the context of a chain complex $C = \{C_n, \partial_n\}$, the elements of $C_n$ are called **$n$-chains**, the elements of $\ker \partial_n$ are called **$n$-cycles**, and the elements of $\operatorname{im} \partial_{n+1}$ are called **$n$-boundaries**.

The notation $Z_n = Z_n(C) = \ker \partial_n$ and $B_n = B_n(C) = \operatorname{im} \partial_{n+1}$ is standard. The condition $\partial \partial = 0$ ensures $B_n \subseteq Z_n$, so the homology module is simply $H_n(C) = Z_n / B_n$.
