Given a projective presentation $R \xrightarrow{\mu} P \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} A$ of a module $A$, one can construct a short cochain complex using the contravariant Hom functor. The cohomology of this 2-term complex recovers the familiar functors: $H^0$ yields $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(A, B)$ and $H^1$ yields $\operatorname{Ext}_\Lambda^1(A, B)$.

This example motivates the general theory of derived functors: Ext groups, previously defined via equivalence classes of extensions, can be computed as cohomology of an appropriate cochain complex built from a projective resolution.
