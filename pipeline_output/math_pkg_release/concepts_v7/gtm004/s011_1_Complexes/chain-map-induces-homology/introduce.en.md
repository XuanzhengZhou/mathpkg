A chain map $\varphi: C \rightarrow D$ between chain complexes induces a well-defined homomorphism $\varphi_*: H_n(C) \rightarrow H_n(D)$ on homology in each degree. The map sends the homology class of an $n$-cycle $z \in \ker \partial_n^C$ to the homology class of $\varphi_n(z)$ in $H_n(D)$.

This induced map is well-defined because $\varphi$ commutes with the differentials, ensuring that cycles map to cycles and boundaries map to boundaries. The construction makes homology a functor from the category of chain complexes to the category of graded modules.
