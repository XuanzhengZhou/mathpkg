An $n$-extension is a long exact sequence of length $n+2$ beginning at $B$ and ending at $A$, generalising the familiar notion of a short exact sequence (a $1$-extension). This concept is fundamental for the Yoneda interpretation of $\operatorname{Ext}^n(A,B)$: elements of $\operatorname{Ext}^n(A,B)$ correspond to equivalence classes of $n$-extensions of $A$ by $B$ under a suitable equivalence relation.
