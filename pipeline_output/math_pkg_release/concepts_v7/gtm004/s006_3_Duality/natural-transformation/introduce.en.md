A natural transformation is a morphism between functors. It provides a way to transform the image of one functor into the image of another in a way that is compatible with all morphisms in the source category. The concept was introduced by Eilenberg and MacLane and is the fundamental reason they developed the language of categories and functors. The collection of natural transformations between functors $F$ and $G$ is denoted by $[F, G]$.
