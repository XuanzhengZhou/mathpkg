Let $F : \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{D}$ and $G : \mathfrak{D} \to \mathfrak{C}$ be functors. The categories $\mathfrak{C}$ and $\mathfrak{D}$ are \textit{equivalent} if there exist natural equivalences
$$
GF \simeq I_{\mathfrak{C}} : \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{C}, \qquad FG \simeq I_{\mathfrak{D}} : \mathfrak{D} \to \mathfrak{D},
$$
where $I$ denotes the identity functor.
