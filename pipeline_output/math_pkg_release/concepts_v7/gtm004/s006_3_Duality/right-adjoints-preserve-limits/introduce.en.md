A fundamental theorem in category theory: right adjoints preserve limits. This means that if a diagram has a limit in $\mathfrak{D}$, its image under a right adjoint $G$ is a limit of the image diagram in $\mathfrak{C}$. Dually, left adjoints preserve colimits. This theorem unifies many ad hoc preservation results in algebra and topology and is used repeatedly in homological algebra.
