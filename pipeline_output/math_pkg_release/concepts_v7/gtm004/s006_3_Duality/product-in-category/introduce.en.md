The product in a category generalizes the Cartesian product of sets, the direct product of groups, and the topological product. It is defined by a universal property: projections to each factor, and a unique morphism into the product for any family of morphisms into the factors. Products do not always exist, but when they do, they are unique up to unique isomorphism.
