In an additive category, finite products and finite coproducts coincide; the resulting object is called a biproduct or direct sum, denoted by $\oplus$. This is a hallmark of additive categories and is a consequence of the abelian group structure on morphism sets. The injections and projections satisfy matrix-like relations. This is why the direct sum in module categories is both a product and a coproduct.
