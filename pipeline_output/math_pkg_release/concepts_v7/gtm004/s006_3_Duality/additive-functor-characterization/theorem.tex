Let $F : \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{B}$ be a functor between additive categories. The following conditions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $F$ preserves sums (coproducts) of two objects.
\item[(ii)] $F$ preserves products of two objects.
\item[(iii)] For each $A, A'$ in $\mathfrak{A}$, the map $F : \mathfrak{A}(A, A') \to \mathfrak{B}(FA, FA')$ is a group homomorphism.
\end{enumerate}
A functor satisfying any of these conditions is called an \textit{additive functor}.
