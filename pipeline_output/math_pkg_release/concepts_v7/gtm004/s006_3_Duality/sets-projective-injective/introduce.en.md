The category of sets has the remarkable property that every set is both projective and injective. The projectivity relies on the axiom of choice: given a surjection onto a set, a function to that set can be lifted. The injectivity is straightforward: given an injection, any function from its domain can be extended to the codomain. This property is the foundation for the proof that free objects (which are images of sets under a free functor left adjoint to a forgetful functor) are projective.
