A natural equivalence (or natural isomorphism) is a natural transformation where each component is an isomorphism. This is the correct notion of "sameness" for functors. If two functors are naturally equivalent, they behave identically from the categorical perspective, even though their actual definitions may differ significantly. The notation $F \simeq G$ is used to denote natural equivalence.
