Theorem 3.3 establishes the fundamental interpretation of the second cohomology group $H^2(\mathfrak{h}, A)$: it is in natural bijection with the set $M(\mathfrak{h}, A)$ of equivalence classes of extensions of $\mathfrak{h}$ by the $\mathfrak{h}$-module $A$. The zero element corresponds to the semidirect product $A \rtimes \mathfrak{h}$. This result is the Lie-algebraic analogue of the classical group-cohomological theorem (Section VI.10) and provides the essential link between cohomology and the structure theory of Lie algebras.
