The sum of two independent Poisson random variables is again Poisson distributed, with parameter equal to the sum of the individual parameters. This property, known as the reproductive property of the Poisson distribution, is easily proved using generating functions: the generating function of a Poisson random variable with parameter $\lambda$ is $e^{-\lambda(1-x)}$, and the product of generating functions for independent variables yields $e^{-(\lambda_1+\lambda_2)(1-x)}$, confirming the result.
