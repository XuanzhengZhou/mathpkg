The extinction probability of a Galton–Watson branching process is characterized as the smallest fixed point of the offspring generating function $G(x)$ in $[0,1]$. The behavior depends critically on the mean offspring number $E\eta$: in the subcritical and critical cases ($E\eta \leq 1$), extinction is almost sure ($q = 1$); in the supercritical case ($E\eta > 1$), there is a positive probability of survival ($q < 1$). This result is fundamental to the theory of branching processes.
