When modeling an infinite sequence of independent coin tosses, the natural sample space is the set of all infinite binary sequences. This space is uncountable, having the cardinality of the continuum, which is established via the binary expansion correspondence with the interval $[0, 1)$. The uncountability of this space forces a fundamental shift from the elementary (finite) probability model to the measure-theoretic framework of Kolmogorov's axioms, where probabilities are assigned to events (measurable subsets) rather than to individual outcomes.
