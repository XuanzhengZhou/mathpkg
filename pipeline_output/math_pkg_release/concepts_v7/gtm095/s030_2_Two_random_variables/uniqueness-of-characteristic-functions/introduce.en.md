The uniqueness theorem establishes that the characteristic function is a faithful representation of a probability distribution: two distributions with the same characteristic function are identical. This is the fundamental reason why characteristic functions are so powerful in probability theory — one can work entirely with characteristic functions (which are analytically tractable) without losing information about the underlying distribution. The proof uses the Weierstrass-Stone theorem to uniformly approximate test functions by trigonometric polynomials.
