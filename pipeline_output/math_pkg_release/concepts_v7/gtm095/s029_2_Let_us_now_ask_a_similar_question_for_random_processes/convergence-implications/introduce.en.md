This theorem establishes the hierarchy among the four fundamental modes of convergence. Almost sure convergence implies convergence in probability (via the tail supremum condition). $L^p$ convergence implies convergence in probability by Markov's inequality. Convergence in probability implies convergence in distribution (proved using a truncation argument with bounded continuous test functions). None of the reverse implications hold in general; counterexamples show that convergence in probability does not imply almost sure convergence, and convergence in distribution does not imply convergence in probability.
