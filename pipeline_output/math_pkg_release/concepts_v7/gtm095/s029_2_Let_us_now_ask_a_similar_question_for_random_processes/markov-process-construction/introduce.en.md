This corollary to Kolmogorov's existence theorem constructs a continuous-time Markov process from a given initial distribution $\pi$ and a family of transition probabilities $\{P(s, x; t, B)\}$ satisfying the Kolmogorov–Chapman equation. The finite-dimensional distributions are expressed as iterated integrals. This construction shows that the Kolmogorov–Chapman equation is the precise consistency condition needed to apply Kolmogorov's extension theorem in the Markovian setting.
