A family of transition probabilities $\{P(s, x; t, B)\}$ defined for $s, t \in T$, $t > s$, $x \in \mathbb{R}$, $B \in \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})$ satisfies the \textbf{Kolmogorov–Chapman equation} if for all $0 \leq s < t < \tau$ and $B \in \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})$:

$$P(s, x; \tau, B) = \int_{\mathbb{R}} P(s, x; t, dy) \, P(t, y; \tau, B).$$
