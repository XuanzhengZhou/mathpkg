The expectation of a nonnegative random variable is defined by extending the elementary expectation from simple functions to all nonnegative measurable functions via monotone approximation. This is the standard Lebesgue integral construction: approximate from below by simple functions, then take the supremum (limit) of their expectations. The consistency of this definition — independence from the choice of approximating sequence — is the first nontrivial result of integration theory.
