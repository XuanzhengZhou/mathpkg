Kolmogorov's extension theorem is a cornerstone result in measure-theoretic probability, providing the existence and uniqueness of a probability measure on the infinite-dimensional space $(R^\infty, \mathcal{B}(R^\infty))$ from a consistent family of finite-dimensional distributions $\{P_n\}_{n \geq 1}$. The consistency condition $P_{n+1}(B \times R) = P_n(B)$ ensures that the finite-dimensional marginals are compatible. The measure $P$ is defined on cylinder sets via $P(\mathcal{I}_n(B)) = P_n(B)$ and then extended uniquely to the full $\sigma$-algebra $\mathcal{B}(R^\infty)$ via Carathéodory's theorem.
