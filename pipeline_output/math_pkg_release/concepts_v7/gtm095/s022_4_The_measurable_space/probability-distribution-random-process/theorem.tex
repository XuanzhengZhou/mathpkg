Let $X = (\xi_t)_{t \in T}$ be a random process. The probability measure $P_X$ on $(R^T, \mathcal{B}(R^T))$ defined by

$$P_X(B) = P\{\omega : X(\omega) \in B\}, \quad B \in \mathcal{B}(R^T),$$

is called the probability distribution of $X$.

The probabilities

$$P_{t_1, \ldots, t_n}(B) \equiv P\{\omega : (\xi_{t_1}, \ldots, \xi_{t_n}) \in B\}, \quad B \in \mathcal{B}(R^n)$$

with $t_1 < t_2 < \cdots < t_n$, $t_i \in T$, are called finite-dimensional probabilities (or finite-dimensional probability distributions).
