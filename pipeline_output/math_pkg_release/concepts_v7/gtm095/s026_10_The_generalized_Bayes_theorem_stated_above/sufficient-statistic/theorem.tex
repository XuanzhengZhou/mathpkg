Let $\mathcal{P} = \{P_{\theta}, \theta \in \Theta\}$ be a family of probability measures on $(\Omega, \mathcal{F})$ and let $T = T(\omega)$ be an $\mathcal{F}$-measurable function (a \text{statistic}) depending on the outcome $\omega$. Denote $\mathcal{G} = \sigma(T(\omega))$ the $\sigma$-algebra generated by $T$. If $\mathcal{G}$ is sufficient for the family $\mathcal{P}$, then the statistic $T = T(\omega)$ is called \text{sufficient} for the family $\mathcal{P}$.
