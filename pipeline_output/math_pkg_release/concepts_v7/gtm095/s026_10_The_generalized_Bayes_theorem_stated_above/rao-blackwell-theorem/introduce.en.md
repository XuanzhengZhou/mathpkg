The Rao-Blackwell theorem provides a procedure for improving any unbiased estimator: condition it on a sufficient statistic. The resulting estimator remains unbiased and has mean squared error no larger than the original. This is a foundational result in the search for optimal estimators, as it implies that uniformly minimum variance unbiased estimators (UMVUEs), if they exist, must be functions of a sufficient statistic.
