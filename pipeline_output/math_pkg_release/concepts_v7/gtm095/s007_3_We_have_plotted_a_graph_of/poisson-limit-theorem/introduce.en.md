Poisson's theorem, also known as the law of small numbers, states that when the success probability $p$ in a Bernoulli scheme tends to zero while the expected number of successes $np$ stays constant at $\lambda$, the binomial probabilities converge to the Poisson distribution with parameter $\lambda$. This theorem provides an excellent approximation for rare events and is particularly useful when $p$ is small and the binomial distribution is highly asymmetric, where the normal approximation from the de Moivre–Laplace theorem performs poorly. The Poisson distribution is the first example encountered of a discrete distribution concentrated on a countably infinite set of points.
