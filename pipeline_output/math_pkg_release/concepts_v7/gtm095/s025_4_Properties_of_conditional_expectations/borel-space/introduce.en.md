Definition 9. A measurable space $(E, \mathcal{E})$ is a Borel space if it is Borel equivalent to a Borel subset of the real line, i.e., there is a one-to-one mapping $\varphi = \varphi(e) : (E, \mathcal{E}) \rightarrow (R, \mathcal{B}(R))$ such that. This definition introduces a fundamental concept in probability theory.
