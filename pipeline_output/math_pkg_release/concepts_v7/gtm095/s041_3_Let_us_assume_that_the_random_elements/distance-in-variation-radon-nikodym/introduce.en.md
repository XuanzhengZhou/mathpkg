This lemma expresses the distance in variation between two probability measures $P$ and $\tilde{P}$ as the $L^1(Q)$ distance between their Radon-Nikodym derivatives with respect to any dominating $\sigma$-finite measure $Q$. A convenient choice is $Q = (P + \tilde{P})/2$, yielding $\|P - \tilde{P}\| = 2\mathbb{E}_Q|1 - z| = 2\mathbb{E}_Q|1 - \tilde{z}|$. This representation is fundamental in mathematical statistics, linking the total variation distance to the optimal error probabilities in hypothesis testing.
