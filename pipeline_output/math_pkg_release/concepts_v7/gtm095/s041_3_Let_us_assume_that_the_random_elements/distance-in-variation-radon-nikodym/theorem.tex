Let $P$ and $\tilde{P}$ be probability measures on $(\Omega, \mathcal{F})$, and let $Q$ be a $\sigma$-finite measure such that $P \ll Q$ and $\tilde{P} \ll Q$. Let $z = dP/dQ$ and $\tilde{z} = d\tilde{P}/dQ$ be the Radon-Nikodym derivatives of $P$ and $\tilde{P}$ with respect to $Q$. Then

$$\|P - \tilde{P}\| = \mathbb{E}_Q|z - \tilde{z}|.$$

In particular, taking $Q = (P + \tilde{P})/2$, we have

$$\|P - \tilde{P}\| = \mathbb{E}_Q|z - \tilde{z}| = 2\,\mathbb{E}_Q|1 - z| = 2\,\mathbb{E}_Q|1 - \tilde{z}|.$$
