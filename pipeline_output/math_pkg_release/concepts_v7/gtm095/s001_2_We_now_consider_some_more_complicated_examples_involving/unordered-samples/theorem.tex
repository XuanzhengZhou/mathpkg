Consider selecting $n$ balls from an urn containing $M$ distinguishable balls, where at each step one ball is drawn at random and returned. An \text{unordered sample with replacement} is a collection $[a_1, \ldots, a_n]$ where the order of elements is disregarded. The number $N(M,n)$ of distinct unordered samples of size $n$ from $M$ types (called \text{combinations of $n$ out of $M$ elements with repetitions}) is
$$N(M,n) = C_{M+n-1}^{n} = \binom{M+n-1}{n}.$$
