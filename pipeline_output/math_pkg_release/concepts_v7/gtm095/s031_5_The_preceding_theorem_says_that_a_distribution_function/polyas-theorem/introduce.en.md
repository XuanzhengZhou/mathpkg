Pólya's Theorem gives a simple sufficient condition for a function to be a characteristic function. If an even, continuous, nonnegative function equals 1 at the origin, tends to 0 at infinity, and is convex on the negative half-line, then it is the characteristic function of some probability distribution. Standard examples include $\varphi_1(t)=e^{-|t|}$ (the Laplace characteristic function) and the triangular function $\varphi_2(t)=\max(1-|t|,0)$. The theorem also shows that two different characteristic functions can coincide on a finite interval without the corresponding distribution functions being identical.
