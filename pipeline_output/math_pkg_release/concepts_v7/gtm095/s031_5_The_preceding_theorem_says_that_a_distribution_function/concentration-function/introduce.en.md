The concentration function $Q(X; l)$ measures the maximal probability that a random variable $X$ falls in an interval of length $l$. It is a useful tool for studying the smoothness of distributions. For independent random variables, the concentration function of the sum is bounded by the minimum of the individual concentration functions. A distribution is continuous (atomless) precisely when $Q(X; 0) = 0$.
