A multivariate Gaussian distribution is specified by a mean vector $m$ and a covariance matrix $\mathbb{R}$ (symmetric, positive semi-definite). The characteristic function has the elegant form $\exp(i(t,m) - \frac{1}{2}(\mathbb{R}t,t))$. When $\mathbb{R}$ is nonsingular, the density is given by the standard formula involving $\mathbb{R}^{-1}$. In the Gaussian case, uncorrelated components are automatically independent, making Hilbert space methods particularly powerful.
