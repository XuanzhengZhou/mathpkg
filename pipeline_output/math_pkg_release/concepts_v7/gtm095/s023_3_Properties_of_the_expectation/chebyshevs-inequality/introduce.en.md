Chebyshev's inequality provides a universal tail bound on the probability that a random variable deviates from its expectation. The first form bounds the probability of large absolute values by the second moment; the second form bounds the probability of deviation from the mean by the variance. Both are direct consequences of Markov's inequality applied to $\xi^2$ and $(\xi - E\xi)^2$ respectively.
