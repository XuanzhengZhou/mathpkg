Theorem 7 (Change of Variables) shows that the expectation of a function $g(X)$ can be computed using only the distribution $P_X$ of $X$ rather than the underlying probability space. This is the fundamental principle that allows probability theory to work with distributions: $E g(X) = \int_{\mathbb{R}} g(x) \, P_X(dx)$. When $P_X$ has a density $f_X$, this reduces to the familiar formula $E g(X) = \int g(x) f_X(x) \, dx$.
