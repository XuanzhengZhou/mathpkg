The Cauchy–Bunyakovskii inequality (also known as the Cauchy–Schwarz inequality in probability) bounds the expectation of the absolute product of two random variables by the square roots of their second moments. The proof normalizes the variables and uses the elementary inequality $2|\tilde{\xi}\tilde{\eta}| \leq \tilde{\xi}^2 + \tilde{\eta}^2$. It is a special case of Hölder's inequality with $p = q = 2$.
