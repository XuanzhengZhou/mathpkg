Fatou's Lemma gives inequalities relating the expectation of limit inferior (or limit superior) to the limit inferior (or limit superior) of expectations. It provides a fundamental tool for passing expectations through limits. The lemma comes in three variants: one-sided lower bound (a), one-sided upper bound (b), and two-sided dominated bound (c). Part (a) is proved by considering $\zeta_n = \inf_{m \geq n} \xi_m$, which increases to $\liminf \xi_n$, and applying the monotone convergence theorem.
