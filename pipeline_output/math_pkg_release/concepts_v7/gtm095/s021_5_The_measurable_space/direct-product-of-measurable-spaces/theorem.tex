Let $T$ be an arbitrary index set and let $(\Omega_t, \mathcal{F}_t)$ be measurable spaces for each $t \in T$. Set

$$\Omega = \prod_{t \in T} \Omega_t = \{\omega = (\omega_t)_{t \in T} : \omega_t \in \Omega_t \text{ for each } t \in T\}.$$

The collection of finite unions of disjoint cylinder sets

$$\mathcal{I}_{t_1, \ldots, t_n}(B_1 \times \cdots \times B_n) = \{\omega : \omega_{t_1} \in B_1, \ldots, \omega_{t_n} \in B_n\},$$

where $B_{t_i} \in \mathcal{F}_{t_i}$, forms an algebra. The smallest $\sigma$-algebra containing all these cylinder sets is denoted by

$$\bigotimes_{t \in T} \mathcal{F}_t,$$

and the measurable space $(\prod_{t \in T} \Omega_t, \bigotimes_{t \in T} \mathcal{F}_t)$ is called the \textit{direct product} of the measurable spaces $(\Omega_t, \mathcal{F}_t)$, $t \in T$.
