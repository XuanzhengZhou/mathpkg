An $n$-dimensional probability density $f_n(t_1, \ldots, t_n)$ generalizes the one-dimensional density to random vectors. The distribution function is obtained by integrating the density over the region $(-\infty, x_1] 	imes \cdots 	imes (-\infty, x_n]$. The most important example is the multivariate Gaussian (normal) density, parameterized by a mean vector $(m_1, \ldots, m_n)$ and a positive definite covariance matrix $\mathbf{R}$. The normalizing constant $|A|^{1/2}/(2\pi)^{n/2}$ ensures the density integrates to $1$ over $R^n$.
