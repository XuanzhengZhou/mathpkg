Theorem 1 establishes that every distribution function $F$ determines a unique probability measure $P$ on the Borel $\sigma$-algebra of the real line satisfying $P(a, b] = F(b) - F(a)$. The proof uses Caratheodory's extension theorem: one first defines a finitely additive set function $P_0$ on the algebra of finite disjoint unions of half-open intervals $(a, b]$, then verifies $\sigma$-additivity via continuity at the empty set, and finally extends to $\mathcal{B}(R)$. This theorem is the cornerstone of probability theory on the real line.
