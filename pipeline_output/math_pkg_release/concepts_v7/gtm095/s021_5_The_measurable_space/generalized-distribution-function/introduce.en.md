A generalized distribution function relaxes the boundary conditions of a standard distribution function: it is only required to be nondecreasing and right-continuous, with values in $(-\infty, \infty)$ rather than $[0,1]$. This generalization accommodates $\sigma$-finite measures (such as Lebesgue measure) that are not probability measures. The generalized Theorem 1 establishes a one-to-one correspondence between Lebesgue-Stieltjes measures and generalized distribution functions, with the formula $\mu(a, b] = G(b) - G(a)$.
