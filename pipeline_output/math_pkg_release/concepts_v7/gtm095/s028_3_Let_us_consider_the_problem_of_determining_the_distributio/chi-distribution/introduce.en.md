The chi distribution is the distribution of the positive square root of a chi-square random variable. Its density is obtained from the chi-square density via the transformation formula. The chi distribution describes the Euclidean norm of an $n$-dimensional standard normal vector. For $n=2$, it specializes to the Rayleigh distribution, which describes the magnitude of a two-dimensional vector whose components are independent standard normals.
