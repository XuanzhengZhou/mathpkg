Let $\xi$ and $\eta$ be independent random variables with distribution functions $F_\xi$ and $F_\eta$, and let $\zeta = \xi + \eta$. The distribution function of the sum is the convolution of $F_\xi$ and $F_\eta$, denoted by $F_\xi * F_\eta$:

$$F_\zeta(z) = (F_\xi * F_\eta)(z) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} F_\eta(z - x) \, dF_\xi(x) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} F_\xi(z - y) \, dF_\eta(y).$$

The convolution operation is commutative and associative.
