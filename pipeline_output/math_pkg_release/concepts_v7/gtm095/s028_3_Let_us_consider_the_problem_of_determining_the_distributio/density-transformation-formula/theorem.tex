Let $\xi$ be a random variable with density $f_\xi(x)$ supported on an open interval $I = (a,b)$. Let $\varphi: I \to \mathbb{R}$ be continuously differentiable and strictly monotone with inverse $h = \varphi^{-1}$. Then the density of $\eta = \varphi(\xi)$ is
$$f_\eta(y) = f_\xi(h(y)) \, |h'(y)|.$$
If $\varphi$ is piecewise strictly monotone on intervals $I_k = (a_k, b_k)$ with inverses $h_k$, then
$$f_\eta(y) = \sum_{k=1}^{n} f_\xi(h_k(y)) \, |h_k'(y)| \cdot I_{D_k}(y),$$
where $D_k$ is the domain of $h_k$.
