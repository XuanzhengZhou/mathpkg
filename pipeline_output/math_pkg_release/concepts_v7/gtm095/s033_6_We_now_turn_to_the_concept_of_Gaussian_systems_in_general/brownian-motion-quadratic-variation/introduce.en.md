This theorem establishes that the quadratic variation of standard Brownian motion over a dyadic partition of $[0,T]$ converges almost surely to $T$. The proof uses Chebyshev's inequality to bound tail probabilities and the Borel-Cantelli lemma to upgrade convergence in probability to almost-sure convergence. This result underpins the Ito calculus, where the formal rule $(dB_t)^2 = dt$ captures the fact that Brownian motion accumulates quadratic variation at rate one per unit time.
