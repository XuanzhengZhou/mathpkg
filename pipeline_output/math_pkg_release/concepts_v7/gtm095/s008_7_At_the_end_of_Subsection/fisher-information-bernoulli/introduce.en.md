Fisher information $I_n(\theta)$ measures the amount of information that an observable sample carries about the unknown parameter $\theta$. It is defined as the expected squared derivative of the log-likelihood (the score function). For the Bernoulli scheme with $n$ independent trials, the Fisher information equals $n/(\theta(1-\theta))$. This quantity appears in the Cramér–Rao lower bound and quantifies the fundamental limit on how precisely $\theta$ can be estimated from $n$ observations.
