An estimator $T_n = T_n(\omega)$ is called consistent if for every $\theta \in \Theta$ and any $\varepsilon > 0$,

$$P_\theta \left\{ |T_n - \theta| \geq \varepsilon \right\} \to 0, \quad n \to \infty.$$

In the Bernoulli scheme, the sample mean $T_n^* = S_n/n$ is a consistent estimator of $\theta$ by the law of large numbers.
