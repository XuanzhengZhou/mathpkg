Applying the exponential Chebyshev inequality to the Bernoulli scheme yields remarkably sharp exponential bounds on the probability of large deviations. The resulting inequalities, $P\{|S_n/n - p| \geq \varepsilon\} \leq 2e^{-2n\varepsilon^2}$, are a special case of Hoeffding's inequality. These exponential bounds are far tighter than the polynomial bounds obtained from the classical Chebyshev inequality, especially for small $\alpha$ when determining the sample size needed to guarantee a given confidence level.
