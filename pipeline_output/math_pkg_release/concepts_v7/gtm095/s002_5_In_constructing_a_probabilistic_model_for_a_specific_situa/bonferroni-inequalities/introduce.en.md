Bonferroni inequalities provide two-sided bounds on the probability of a union of events using truncated alternating sums from the inclusion-exclusion formula. Truncating after an odd number of terms gives an upper bound; after an even number, a lower bound. These inequalities are widely used in multiple testing corrections, extreme value theory, and wherever exact inclusion-exclusion computation is infeasible.
