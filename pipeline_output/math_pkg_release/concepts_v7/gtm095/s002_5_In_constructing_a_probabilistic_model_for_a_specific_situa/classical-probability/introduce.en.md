The classical definition of probability, dating back to Laplace, assigns equal weight to every elementary outcome. Under this assumption, probability becomes a purely combinatorial exercise: the probability of an event is the ratio of the number of favorable outcomes to the total number of possible outcomes. While limited to finite sample spaces, this approach underpins most of elementary probability and provides the intuition behind more general measure-theoretic formulations.
