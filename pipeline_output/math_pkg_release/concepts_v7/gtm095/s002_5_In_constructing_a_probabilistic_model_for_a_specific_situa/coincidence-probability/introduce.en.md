The coincidence problem, famously known as the birthday problem, asks for the probability that in a group of $n$ people, at least two share a birthday. The surprising result is that only 23 people are needed for the probability to exceed 1/2. This illustrates a general principle: collisions become likely much sooner than intuition suggests, a fact exploited in the "birthday attack" in cryptography and in analyzing hash table collisions.
