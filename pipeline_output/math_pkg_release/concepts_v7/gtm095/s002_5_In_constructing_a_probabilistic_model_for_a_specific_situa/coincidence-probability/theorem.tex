Consider an ordered sample of size $n$ drawn with replacement from $M$ balls. The probability that all $n$ draws are distinct (no repetition) is
$$\text{P}(A) = \frac{(M)_n}{M^n} = \left(1 - \frac{1}{M}\right)\left(1 - \frac{2}{M}\right) \cdots \left(1 - \frac{n-1}{M}\right).$$
In the \text{birthday problem} ($M = 365$), the probability $P_n$ that at least two people in a group of $n$ share a birthday is
$$P_n = 1 - \frac{(365)_n}{365^n}.$$
For $n = 23$, $P_{23} \approx 1/2$.
