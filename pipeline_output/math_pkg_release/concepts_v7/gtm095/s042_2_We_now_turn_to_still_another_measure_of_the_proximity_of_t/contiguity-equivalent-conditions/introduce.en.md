This theorem gives a complete characterization of contiguity $(\tilde{P}^n) \triangleleft (P^n)$ through several equivalent conditions involving the Hellinger integrals $H(\alpha; P^n, \tilde{P}^n)$ and the Lebesgue derivative $Z^n = d\tilde{P}^n/dP^n$. The equivalence (a) $\Leftrightarrow$ (b) is proved via a truncation argument bounding $\tilde{P}^n(A^n)$ in terms of $P^n(A^n)$ and $\tilde{P}^n(z^n \leq \varepsilon)$. The equivalence with (c) uses the identity $\tilde{P}^n(z^n \geq \varepsilon) \geq (\delta/2)^\alpha H(\alpha; P^n, \tilde{P}^n) - 2\delta/2^\alpha$ and properties of dominated convergence as $\alpha \downarrow 0$. For product measures $P^n = P_1 \times \cdots \times P_n$, $\tilde{P}^n = \tilde{P}_1 \times \cdots \times \tilde{P}_n$, the theorem yields the criterion $(\tilde{P}^n) \triangleleft (P^n) \iff \lim_{\alpha \downarrow 0} \limsup_n \sum_{k=1}^n [1 - H(\alpha; P_k, \tilde{P}_k)] = 0$.
