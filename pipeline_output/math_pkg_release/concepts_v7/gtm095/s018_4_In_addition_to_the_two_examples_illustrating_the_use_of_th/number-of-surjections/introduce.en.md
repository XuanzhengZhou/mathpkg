Counting functions between finite sets is a fundamental problem in combinatorics. The number of all functions from an $n$-set to an $m$-set is $m^n$. Injections (one-to-one functions) require $n \leq m$ and are counted by the falling factorial $(m)_n$. The number of surjections (onto functions) is given by an inclusion–exclusion formula and is related to Stirling numbers of the second kind. Bijections require $n = m$ and number $n!$. These formulas provide the foundation for occupancy problems in probability theory.
