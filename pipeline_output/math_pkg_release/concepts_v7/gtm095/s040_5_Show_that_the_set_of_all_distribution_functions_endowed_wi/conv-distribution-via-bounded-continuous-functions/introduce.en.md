This proposition provides a fundamental equivalence characterizing convergence in distribution of random elements in terms of expectations of bounded continuous test functions. It follows from the definition of weak convergence of probability measures together with the change-of-variables theorem for the Lebesgue integral. This characterization serves as a bridge between the measure-theoretic notion of weak convergence and the functional-analytic perspective, and is a key tool for proving that almost sure convergence implies convergence in distribution (via Lebesgue's dominated convergence theorem).
