This is Theorem 135 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke.

If $p$ and $q$ are odd positive primes, then we have the relations

(I) $(\frac{-1}{p}) = (-1)^{(p-1)/2},$
(II) $(\frac{p}{q}) = (\frac{q}{p})(-1)^{((p-1)/2)((q-1)/2)},$
(III) $(\frac{2}{p}) = (-1)^{(p^2-1)/8},$
