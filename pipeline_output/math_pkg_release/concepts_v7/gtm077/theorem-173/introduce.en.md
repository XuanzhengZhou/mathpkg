This is Theorem 173 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke.

In order that an odd ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ can be made into a primary number of the field by multiplication by a square of an ideal, it is necessary and sufficient that the conditions

$$Q(\varepsilon, \mathfrak{a}) = +1$$

are satisfied for each totally positive singular number $\varepsilon$.
