Let $d = (a, b)$ be the greatest common divisor of two integers $a$ and $b$, not both zero. Then there exist integers $x$ and $y$ such that
$$ax + by = d.$$
The set of all integral linear combinations $ax + by$ (with $x, y \in \mathbb{Z}$) is precisely the set of all multiples of $d$.

Consequently, every common divisor of $a$ and $b$ divides the GCD of $a$ and $b$.
