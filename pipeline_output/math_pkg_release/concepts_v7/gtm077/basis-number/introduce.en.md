Theorem 27 states that the number of elements of order dividing $p$ in a finite Abelian group is exactly $p^e$, where $e$ is the number of cyclic factors in the basis whose order is a power of $p$. This $p$-rank is an invariant of the group — it does not depend on the choice of basis. For example, in $(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^e$, every non-identity element has order $p$, so there are $p^e$ solutions to $A^p = E$.
