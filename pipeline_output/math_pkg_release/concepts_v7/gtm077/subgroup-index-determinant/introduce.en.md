Theorem 39 (combining 35-38) is a cornerstone of algebraic number theory: the index of a subgroup $\mathfrak{U}$ in a torsion-free Abelian group equals the absolute value of the determinant of any matrix expressing a basis of $\mathfrak{U}$ in terms of a basis of $\mathfrak{G}$. In number fields, this gives the norm of an ideal as the index of the ideal in the ring of integers, and the discriminant as the square of such a determinant.
