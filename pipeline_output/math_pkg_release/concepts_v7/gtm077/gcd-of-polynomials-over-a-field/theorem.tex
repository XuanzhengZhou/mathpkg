Two arbitrary nonzero polynomials $f_1(x)$ and $f_2(x)$ over a number field $k$ have a uniquely determined greatest common divisor $d(x)$ with leading coefficient $1$, such that $d(x) \mid f_1(x)$ and $d(x) \mid f_2(x)$, and every common divisor of $f_1$ and $f_2$ also divides $d(x)$. Moreover, $d(x)$ can be represented in the form
$$d(x) = g_1(x) f_1(x) + g_2(x) f_2(x),$$
where $g_1(x)$ and $g_2(x)$ are polynomials over $k$.
