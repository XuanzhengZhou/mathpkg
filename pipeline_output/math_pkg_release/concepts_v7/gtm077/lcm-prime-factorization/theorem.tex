The numbers divisible by both $a$ and $b$ form a module. Let $v$ be the smallest positive number in this module; then every common multiple of $a$ and $b$ is a multiple of $v$. The number $v$ is called the least common multiple (LCM) of $a$ and $b$.

In terms of prime factorizations $a = \prod p_i^{a_i}$, $b = \prod p_i^{b_i}$:
$$v = \operatorname{lcm}(a, b) = \prod p_i^{\max(a_i, b_i)}.$$
Moreover, $ab = (a, b) \cdot \operatorname{lcm}(a, b)$.
