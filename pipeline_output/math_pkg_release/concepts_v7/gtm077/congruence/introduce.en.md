A congruence $a \equiv b \pmod{n}$ means that $a$ and $b$ differ by a multiple of $n$, i.e., they have the same remainder modulo $n$. Congruences behave like equations under addition, subtraction, and multiplication, making modular arithmetic a powerful tool. Any polynomial with integer coefficients preserves congruence: if $a \equiv b \pmod{n}$, then $f(a) \equiv f(b) \pmod{n}$.
