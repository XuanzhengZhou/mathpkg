Every finite Abelian group $\mathfrak{G}$ of order $h$ possesses a basis $B_1, B_2, \ldots, B_r$ of orders $h_1, h_2, \ldots, h_r$ ($h_i > 1$) such that each element of $\mathfrak{G}$ is obtained in exactly one way in the form
$$C = B_1^{x_1} B_2^{x_2} \cdots B_r^{x_r},$$
where the integers $x_i$ each run through a complete system of residues modulo $h_i$ independently. Moreover, the $h_i = p^{k_i}$ are prime powers and $h = h_1 h_2 \cdots h_r$. The $h_i$ are uniquely determined up to ordering.
