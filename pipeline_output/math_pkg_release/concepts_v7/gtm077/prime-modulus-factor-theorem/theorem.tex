Let $p$ be a prime. If $f(x) \equiv g(x) g_1(x) \pmod{p}$, then every root of $f(x)$ modulo $p$ is a root of at least one of the two polynomials $g(x)$ or $g_1(x)$ modulo $p$.

This follows because $g(a) g_1(a) \equiv f(a) \equiv 0 \pmod{p}$ implies $p \mid g(a) g_1(a)$, and a prime dividing a product must divide one of the factors.
