Each odd ideal $\alpha$, which can be made into a totally positive primary number by multiplication by a square ideal, has the property

$$\left( \frac{\varepsilon}{\alpha} \right) = +1$$

for all units and squares of ideals $\varepsilon$ as long as they are relatively prime to $\alpha$.

We will prove in the next section that the converse of this theorem also holds.
