If $\theta$ is an algebraic number of degree $n$ over $k$, then every number $\alpha$ in $K(\theta)$ can be represented uniquely in the form
$$\alpha = c_0 + c_1 \theta + \cdots + c_{n-1} \theta^{n-1}$$
with coefficients $c_i \in k$. The degree $n$ is thus the dimension of $K(\theta)$ as a vector space over $k$.
