The set of all $h$ characters of a finite Abelian group $\mathfrak{G}$ forms an Abelian group under pointwise multiplication:
$$(\chi_1 \chi_2)(A) = \chi_1(A) \cdot \chi_2(A).$$
This group, denoted $\widehat{\mathfrak{G}}$, has order $h$ and is isomorphic to $\mathfrak{G}$.

Basis characters $\chi_q$ defined by $\chi_q(B_1^{x_1} \cdots B_r^{x_r}) = \zeta_q^{x_q}$ (where $\zeta_q$ is a primitive $h_q$th root of unity) form a basis for $\widehat{\mathfrak{G}}$.
