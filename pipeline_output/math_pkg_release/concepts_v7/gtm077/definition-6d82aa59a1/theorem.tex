A class in the wider sense thus splits into at most two classes relative to the strict concept of equivalence. Hence the class number $h_0$ in the strict sense is also finite and $\leq 2h$.

Since the number $\mu$ is determined only up to a unit factor by the ideal equation $a = \mu b$, the two concepts of equivalence are identical if in each complete sequence of associated numbers some with positive norm occur. That is, if $k$ is imaginary or $k$ is real and the basic unit in $k$ has norm $-1$, then $h_0 = h$.

In the cases yet remaining in which $k$ is real and each unit in $k$ has norm $\pm 1$, $a$ and $a\sqrt{d}$ are obviously not equivalent in the strict sense, and then $h_0 = 2h$.
