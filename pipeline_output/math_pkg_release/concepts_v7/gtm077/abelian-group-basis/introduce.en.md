A basis of a finite Abelian group is a minimal generating set of independent elements whose orders are prime powers. Every element is uniquely expressible as a product of powers of the basis elements with exponents reduced modulo the respective orders. The orders of the basis elements (the invariants) determine the group up to isomorphism. This is the finite Abelian group analogue of a basis in linear algebra.
