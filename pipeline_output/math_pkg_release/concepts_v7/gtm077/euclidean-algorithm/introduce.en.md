The Euclidean algorithm is the classic method for computing the greatest common divisor of two integers. By repeated application of the division algorithm, one produces a strictly decreasing sequence of non-negative remainders. The process must terminate after finitely many steps, and the last non-zero remainder equals the GCD. The algorithm dates back to Euclid's Elements and remains fundamental in computational number theory.
