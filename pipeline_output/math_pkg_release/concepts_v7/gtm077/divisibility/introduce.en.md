Divisibility is the most fundamental relation in elementary number theory. For integers $a$ and $b$, we write $b \mid a$ when $b$ divides $a$ without remainder. Each nonzero integer $a$ has the trivial divisors $\pm a, \pm 1$, and the only numbers that divide every integer are the units $1$ and $-1$. This concept underpins the entire theory of prime factorization and modular arithmetic.
