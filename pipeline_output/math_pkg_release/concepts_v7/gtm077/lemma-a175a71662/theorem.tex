The numbers $1 - \zeta^a$ ($a \neq 0$ (mod $l$) are all associated. They satisfy the ideal equation

$$(l) = (1 - \zeta)^{l-1}.$$

To see this let $a$ and $a_1$ be rational integers coprime to $l$. Then we determine a positive rational integer $b$ such that

$$ab \equiv a_1 \pmod{l}, \quad \text{thus} \zeta^{a_1} = \zeta^{ab}.$$

Consequently,

$$\frac{1 - \zeta^{a_1}}{1 - \zeta^a} = \frac{1 - \zeta^{ab}}{1 - \zeta^a} = 1 + \zeta^a + \zeta^{2a} + \cdots + \zeta^{(b-1)a}$$

is an integer and the same follows for the inverse quotient; thus this quotient must be a unit.
