Each finite Abelian group $\mathfrak{G}$ can be represented as a direct product of cyclic groups whose orders are prime powers. Equivalently, there exist elements $B_1,\ldots,B_r$ of orders $h_1,\ldots,h_r$ (each a prime power) such that each element $C$ is uniquely representable as
$$C = B_1^{x_1}B_2^{x_2}\cdots B_r^{x_r},$$
where $x_i$ runs through a complete residue system modulo $h_i$, and $h = h_1h_2\cdots h_r$.
