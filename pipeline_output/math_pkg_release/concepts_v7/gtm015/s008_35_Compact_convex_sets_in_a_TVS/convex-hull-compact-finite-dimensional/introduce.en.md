In a finite-dimensional separated topological vector space, the pathology of a non-compact convex hull of a compact set cannot occur. This follows from Caratheodory's theorem, which bounds the number of points needed to represent any element of the convex hull, together with the compactness of the standard simplex and the continuity of the finite convex combination map. The result is a useful contrast to the infinite-dimensional case, where the convex hull of a compact set may fail to be even closed.
