In a complete locally convex space, precompactness together with completeness yields compactness. This corollary applies that principle to the balanced convex hull: if a set has compact closure, then its balanced convex hull is precompact (by the main theorem of the section), and its closure — which is precisely the closed balanced convex hull — is therefore compact. As an immediate consequence, the closed convex hull of such a set is also compact, being a closed subset of the compact balanced convex hull.
