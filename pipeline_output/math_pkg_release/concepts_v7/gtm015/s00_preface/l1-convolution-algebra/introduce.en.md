$\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ with convolution as multiplication forms a commutative Banach algebra with unity $e_0$. The algebraic properties (commutativity, associativity, bilinearity) are inherited from the Wiener algebra $\mathcal{A}$ via the Fourier transform isomorphism, since pointwise multiplication of functions enjoys these properties. The norm inequality $\|xy\|_1 \leq \|x\|_1 \|y\|_1$ makes the multiplication continuous. The completeness of $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ (an elementary exercise) is the crucial analytic property that distinguishes it as a Banach algebra and enables the application of Gel'fand's theory of maximal ideals in the proof of Wiener's theorem.
