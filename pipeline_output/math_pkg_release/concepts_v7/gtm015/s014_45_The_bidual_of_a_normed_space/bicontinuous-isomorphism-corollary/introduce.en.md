This corollary of the Open Mapping Theorem is frequently used in functional analysis: if a continuous linear map between Banach spaces (or more generally complete metrizable TVS) is bijective, then its inverse is automatically continuous. This means that the algebraic notion of isomorphism and the topological notion coincide for such spaces — a powerful fact that eliminates the need to separately verify continuity of the inverse.
