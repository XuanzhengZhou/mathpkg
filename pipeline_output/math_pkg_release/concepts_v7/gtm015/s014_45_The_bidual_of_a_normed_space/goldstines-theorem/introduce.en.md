Goldstine's theorem is a fundamental result in the theory of normed spaces that describes the relationship between a normed space and its bidual. It states that under the canonical embedding of $E$ into its bidual $E''$, the image of the closed unit ball of $E$ is dense in the closed unit ball of $E''$ with respect to the weak* topology $\sigma(E'',E')$. This theorem is a key ingredient in the proof that a Banach space is reflexive if and only if its closed unit ball is weakly compact.
