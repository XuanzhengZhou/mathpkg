In a Banach space, surjectivity of an operator $T$ is equivalent to the adjoint $T'$ being bounded below. The proof uses the Hahn-Banach separation theorem to show that if $T'$ is bounded below, then the closure of the image of the unit ball under $T$ contains a neighborhood of zero, forcing $T$ to be surjective via completeness.
