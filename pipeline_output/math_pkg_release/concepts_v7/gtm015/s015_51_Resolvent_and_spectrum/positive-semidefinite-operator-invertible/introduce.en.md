If an operator has nonnegative quadratic form, then adding the identity yields an invertible operator. More generally, $T^*T + I$ is always invertible. This simple but powerful fact follows from the inequality $\|(T+I)x\|^2 \geq \|x\|^2$ when $T$ is positive, showing $T+I$ is bounded below and self-adjoint.
