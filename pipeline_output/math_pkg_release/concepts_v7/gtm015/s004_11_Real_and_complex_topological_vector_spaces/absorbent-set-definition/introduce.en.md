An absorbent (or absorbing) set is one that can "swallow" every point of the space after sufficiently scaling it down toward the origin. More precisely, for any vector $x$, all sufficiently small scalar multiples $\lambda x$ belong to $A$. This property is one of the key axioms characterizing the neighborhood system of zero in a TVS: every neighborhood of the origin must be absorbent, because scalar multiplication $\lambda \mapsto \lambda x$ is continuous at $\lambda = 0$. Absorbent sets are also fundamental in the theory of locally convex spaces and Minkowski functionals.
