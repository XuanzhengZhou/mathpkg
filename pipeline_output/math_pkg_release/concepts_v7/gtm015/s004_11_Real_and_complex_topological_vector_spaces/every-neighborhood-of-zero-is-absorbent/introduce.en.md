This simple but essential lemma follows directly from the continuity of the map $\lambda \mapsto \lambda x$ at $\lambda = 0$: since $0x = \theta$ and $V$ is a neighborhood of $\theta$, there must exist $\varepsilon > 0$ such that $\lambda x \in V$ for all $|\lambda| \leq \varepsilon$. Thus every neighborhood of the origin in a TVS is absorbent. This property is one of the necessary conditions in the axiomatic characterization of TVS topologies via neighborhood bases at zero.
