In any TVS, the collection $\mathcal{B}$ of all closed balanced neighborhoods of the origin forms a fundamental system (neighborhood base) at zero. Moreover, nonzero scalar multiples of such neighborhoods remain in $\mathcal{B}$. This theorem provides the standard "canonical" neighborhood base for a TVS and is used to characterize TVS topologies axiomatically: given a filter base of subsets satisfying appropriate axioms (balanced, absorbent, etc.), there is a unique compatible TVS topology having them as a neighborhood base at the origin.
