This theorem provides three equivalent descriptions of a hyperplane: (a) the geometric definition as a maximal proper linear variety, (b) as a translate of a maximal linear subspace, and (c) as a level set of a nonzero linear form. The equivalence between (a) and (c) is the algebraic foundation for the geometric form of the Hahn-Banach theorem, where hyperplanes serve to separate convex sets. The theorem also establishes that the defining linear form for a hyperplane is unique up to a nonzero scalar multiple.
