This theorem is the affine counterpart of Theorem 21.8: every linear variety is the intersection of all hyperplanes that contain it. The proof uses the bijection between hyperplanes containing $V = x + N$ and maximal subspaces containing $N$, via hyperplanes of the form $x + M$. Taking $V$ to be a linear subspace yields the result that every subspace is an intersection of hyperplanes, which will be refined in the topological setting via the Hahn-Banach theorem.
