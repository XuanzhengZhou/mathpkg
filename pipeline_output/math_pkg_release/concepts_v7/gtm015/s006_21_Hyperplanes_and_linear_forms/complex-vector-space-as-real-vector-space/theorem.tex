Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{C}$. By restricting scalar multiplication to $\mathbb{R} \subset \mathbb{C}$, $E$ can also be viewed as a vector space over $\mathbb{R}$. The terms $\mathbb{C}$-linear subspace and $\mathbb{R}$-linear subspace are self-explanatory, as are the terms $\mathbb{C}$-variety, $\mathbb{R}$-variety, $\mathbb{C}$-hyperplane, and $\mathbb{R}$-hyperplane. A $\mathbb{C}$-form is a linear form $f: E \to \mathbb{C}$ for the complex vector space structure; an $\mathbb{R}$-form is a linear form $g: E \to \mathbb{R}$ for the real vector space structure.
