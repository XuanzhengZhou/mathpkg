This theorem gives five equivalent descriptions of a maximal linear subspace: (a) the order-theoretic definition, (b) as the null space of a nonzero linear form, (c) via a one-dimensional quotient, (d) via codimension-1 decomposition $E = M + \mathbb{K}z$, and (e) that every vector outside $M$ generates the whole space together with $M$. The theorem also establishes that the linear form having a given maximal subspace as null space is unique up to a nonzero scalar multiple.
