Every compact subset of a real TVS admits a tangent (supporting) hyperplane parallel to any given closed hyperplane. The proof is immediate: a continuous linear form attains its minimum and maximum on a compact set, and the preceding lemma yields the tangent hyperplane. The result requires only quasicompactness, not necessarily separatedness.
