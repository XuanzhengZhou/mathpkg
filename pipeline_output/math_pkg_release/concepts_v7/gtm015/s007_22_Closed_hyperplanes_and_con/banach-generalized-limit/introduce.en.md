A Banach generalized limit is a continuous linear functional on the space of bounded sequences that extends the ordinary limit in a translation-invariant way. It assigns a limiting value to every bounded sequence, generalizing the notion of limit to sequences that do not converge. The existence of such functionals follows from the amenability of the additive semigroup of positive integers and the Hahn-Banach theorem.
