Kolmogorov's celebrated theorem provides a complete topological characterization of normable spaces among topological vector spaces: a TVS admits a norm generating its topology precisely when it is separated and has a bounded convex neighborhood of the origin. The proof constructs the Minkowski functional, which serves as the norm. This criterion is fundamental for understanding which metrizable TVS are normed spaces.
