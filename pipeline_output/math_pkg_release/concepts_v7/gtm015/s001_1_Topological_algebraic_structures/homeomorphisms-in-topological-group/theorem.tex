If $a$ and $b$ are fixed elements of a topological group $G$, then each of the following mappings is a homeomorphism of $G$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $x \mapsto x^{-1}$,
\item[(2)] $x \mapsto ax$,
\item[(3)] $x \mapsto xb$,
\item[(4)] $x \mapsto axb$,
\item[(5)] $x \mapsto axa^{-1}$.
\end{enumerate}
For fixed $a \in G$, the mapping $x \mapsto xax^{-1}$ is continuous.
