This definition assigns standard names to the fundamental mappings of a topological group: inversion, left and right translation, and inner automorphism. These mappings are all homeomorphisms by Theorem (2.8). The fact that translations are homeomorphisms implies topological homogeneity of the group, meaning the local structure at any point determines the local structure everywhere.
