A seminorm generalizes the concept of a norm by allowing nonzero vectors to have seminorm zero. Theorem 37.1 establishes the fundamental geometric properties of the "closed unit ball" $\{x : p(x) \leq 1\}$ and "open unit ball" $\{x : p(x) < 1\}$ associated with a seminorm: they are always balanced and convex. The inequality $|p(x) - p(y)| \leq p(x-y)$ is the seminorm analogue of the reverse triangle inequality and implies uniform continuity when $p$ is continuous on a topological vector space.
