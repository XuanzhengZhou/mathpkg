The $\mathcal{L}^p$ spaces are among the most important examples in functional analysis. For a measure space $(X, \mathcal{S}, \mu)$ and $p \geq 1$, $\mathcal{L}^p$ consists of measurable functions whose $p$-th power is integrable. The quantity $\|f\|_p$ defined here is initially only a seminorm (it can vanish for nonzero functions that are zero almost everywhere). The conjugate exponent $q$ satisfying $1/p + 1/q = 1$ is essential for Hölder's inequality. After quotienting by the null functions, $\mathcal{L}^p$ becomes the Banach space $L^p$.
