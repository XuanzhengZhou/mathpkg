This definition introduces the polar topology on the continuous dual space $E'$, a fundamental construction in duality theory. For each bounded subset $A$ of $E$, the "support seminorm" $p_A(f) = \sup_{x \in A} |f(x)|$ measures how large a linear functional can be on $A$. By choosing different families $\mathfrak{S}$ of bounded sets, one obtains different topologies on $E'$: taking $\mathfrak{S}$ as all finite sets gives the weak* topology $\sigma(E',E)$, while taking all bounded sets gives the strong topology $\beta(E',E)$.
