This corollary gives a concrete description of which seminorms are weakly continuous: a seminorm on $E$ is $\sigma(E,F)$-continuous exactly when it is dominated by a finite sum of "evaluation seminorms" $p_y(x) = |B(x,y)|$. This follows from the characterization of the saturation (Lemma 37.15) and the fact that $\sigma(E,F)$ is generated by the seminorms $\{p_y : y \in F\}$. The result is essential for describing weakly continuous linear functionals.
