Let $B: E \times F \to \mathbb{K}$ be a duality.
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] The initial topology on $E$ for the family of linear forms $(B^y)_{y \in F}$ is denoted $\sigma(E, F)$; it is called the weak topology on $E$ induced by $F$ and the duality $B$.
\item[(ii)] Similarly, $\sigma(F, E)$ denotes the initial topology on $F$ for the family $(B_x)_{x \in E}$.
\end{enumerate}
Both $\sigma(E,F)$ and $\sigma(F,E)$ are locally convex topologies, and they are separated.
