\begin{theorem}[von Neumann Bicommutant Theorem]
Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a $*$-subalgebra of $\mathcal{L}(H)$ such that the intersection of the null spaces of the operators in $\mathcal{A}$ is $\{\theta\}$. Then $\mathcal{A}''$ is the ultrastrong closure of $\mathcal{A}$ in $\mathcal{L}(H)$. In particular, for a von Neumann algebra $\mathcal{A}$, $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{A}''$ is both the bicommutant and the ultrastrong closure of $\mathcal{A}$.
\end{theorem}
