A C*-algebra is a Banach algebra equipped with an involution whose norm satisfies the *-quadratic identity $\|x^*x\| = \|x\|^2$. This single condition links the algebraic structure (involution), the geometric structure (norm), and the analytic structure (completeness) in a remarkably tight way. The canonical example is any norm-closed *-subalgebra of the bounded linear operators $\mathcal{L}(H)$ on a Hilbert space $H$. The Gel'fand-Naimark theorem (the climax of this chapter) proves the converse: every C*-algebra can be represented isometrically as such an operator algebra.
