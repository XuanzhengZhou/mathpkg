If $E$ is an inner product space (real or complex), then a norm on $E$ satisfying the parallelogram law is obtained by defining

$$\|x\| = (x, x)^{1/2} \quad \text{(real case)}, \qquad \|x\| = (x|x)^{1/2} \quad \text{(complex case)}.$$

Explicitly, for all $x, y \in E$:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\|x + y\|^2 + \|x - y\|^2 = 2\|x\|^2 + 2\|y\|^2$ (parallelogram law);
\item[(ii)] $\|x + y\| \leq \|x\| + \|y\|$ (triangle inequality);
\item[(iii)] $\|\lambda x\| = |\lambda|\,\|x\|$ (absolute homogeneity);
\item[(iv)] $\|x\| \geq 0$, and $\|x\| = 0$ iff $x = \theta$ (positive definiteness).
\end{enumerate}

Thus every inner product space is a pre-Hilbert space.
