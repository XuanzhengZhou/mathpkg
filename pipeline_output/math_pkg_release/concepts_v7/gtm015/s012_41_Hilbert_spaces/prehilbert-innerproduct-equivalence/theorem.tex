The notions of pre-Hilbert space and inner product space are coextensive. More precisely, for a (complex) vector space $E$:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] If $E$ is a pre-Hilbert space (normed space satisfying the parallelogram law), then the polarization formula (41.4)(2) defines an inner product $(x|y)$ on $E$, and applying Theorem 41.11 to this inner product recovers the original norm.

\item[(ii)] If $E$ is an inner product space with inner product $(x|y)$, then $\|x\| = (x|x)^{1/2}$ defines a pre-Hilbert space structure (norm satisfying the parallelogram law), and the polarization formula (41.4)(2) applied to this norm recovers the original inner product.
\end{enumerate}

The same holds for the real case, using the real polarization formula and real inner product.
