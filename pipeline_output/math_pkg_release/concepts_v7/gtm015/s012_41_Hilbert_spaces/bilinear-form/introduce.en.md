A bilinear form on a real vector space is a real-valued function of two vector arguments that is linear in each argument separately. This is the fundamental algebraic structure underlying real inner products. By imposing symmetry (commutativity of arguments) and strict positivity (positive definiteness on the diagonal), one obtains the axioms for a real inner product.
