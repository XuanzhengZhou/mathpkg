For a group homomorphism between topological groups, continuity at a single point (or at the identity) already implies uniform continuity with respect to the left uniform structures. This powerful result shows that the algebraic structure of a homomorphism forces a "uniform" behavior: given any neighborhood $V$ in the codomain, there is a neighborhood $U$ in the domain that works at every point simultaneously.
