Let $L_0$ and $L_1$ be languages with $L_1$ relational. The language $L_1$ is \text{interpreted} in an $L_0$-structure $A$ if for some $n$, there exist:
\begin{itemize}
\item an $L_0$-formula $Q(\bar{x})$ with $n$ free variables,
\item an $L_0$-formula $E(\bar{x}, \bar{y})$ with $2n$ free variables,
\item for every $m$-ary predicate symbol $p_i$ in $L_1$, an $L_0$-formula $P_i(\bar{x}_1, \ldots, \bar{x}_m)$ with $mn$ free variables,
\end{itemize}
such that $E(A)$ is an equivalence relation on $Q(A)$ and the $P_i(A)$ are relations on $Q(A)$ preserved by $E(A)$. The domain $Q(A)/E(A)$ with the interpretation of $p_i$ given by $P_i(A)$ is called a \text{definable structure}.
