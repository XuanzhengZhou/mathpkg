A formal deduction is the model-theoretic analogue of a mathematical proof, representing it as a finite sequence of formulas where each step is either an axiom, a hypothesis, or follows from previous steps by rules of inference. The completeness theorem of Godel establishes that semantic truth and syntactic deducibility coincide for first-order logic.
