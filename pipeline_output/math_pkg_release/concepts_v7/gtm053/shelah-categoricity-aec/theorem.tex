There exists a Hanf number $\mu$ (depending only on the L\"owenheim-Skolem number $\operatorname{LS}(K)$) such that if an AEC $K$ has arbitrarily large models, satisfies the amalgamation property and the joint embedding property, and is categorical in some successor cardinal $\lambda > \mu$, then $K$ is categorical in all larger cardinals.
  
Moreover, under the mild set-theoretic assumption $2^{\aleph_n} < 2^{\aleph_{n+1}}$ for all $n$, if an $L_{\omega_1,\omega}$-sentence $\Sigma$ is categorical in $2^{\aleph_n}$ for every $n$, then $\Sigma$ has a unique model in every infinite cardinal.
