The strong Lefschetz principle states that a first-order sentence in the language of fields holds in the complex numbers iff it holds in all algebraically closed fields of characteristic zero, and this is equivalent to holding in the algebraic closures of all sufficiently large prime fields. It formalizes the classical Lefschetz principle of algebraic geometry.
