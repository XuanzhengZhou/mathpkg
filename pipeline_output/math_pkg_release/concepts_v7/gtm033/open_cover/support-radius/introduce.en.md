The support radius of a compactly supported function $\theta: \mathbb{R}^m \to \mathbb{R}$ is the smallest radius $\sigma \geq 0$ such that the entire support of $\theta$ lies within the closed ball $B_\sigma(0)$ centered at the origin. This notion is essential in approximation theory, particularly when using convolution kernels: the support radius controls how "localized" the kernel is, and taking $\sigma \to 0$ yields increasingly sharp approximations of a target function.
