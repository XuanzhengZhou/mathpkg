Let $U \subset \mathbb{R}^m$ be open and $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^n$ be a continuous map. Let $\theta: \mathbb{R}^m \to \mathbb{R}$ be a $C^s$ convolution kernel ($0 \leqslant s \leqslant \infty$). Then the convolution $\theta * f$ is $C^s$, and for any compact subset $K \subset U$ and $\varepsilon > 0$, if the support radius of $\theta$ is sufficiently small,
$$
\|\theta * f - f\|_{0, K} < \varepsilon.
$$
In particular, $\theta * f \to f$ uniformly on compact subsets of $U$ as the support radius tends to $0$. Consequently, every $C^r$ map from an open subset of $\mathbb{R}^m$ to $\mathbb{R}^n$ can be $C^r$-approximated by $C^\infty$ maps in neighborhoods of compact sets.
