The $C^r$ norm $\|f\|_{r,K}$ of a $C^r$ map $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^n$ on a subset $K \subset U$ is the supremum over $x \in K$ and all derivative orders $0 \leq k \leq r$ of the operator norms $\|D^k f(x)\|$. This notation provides a unified way to measure the closeness of two $C^r$ maps together with all their derivatives up to order $r$, which is essential for defining the strong $C^r$ topology and stating approximation theorems in differential topology.
