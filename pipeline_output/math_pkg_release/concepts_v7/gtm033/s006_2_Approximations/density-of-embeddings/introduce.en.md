Theorem 2.13 establishes that embeddings are dense in the strong $C^r$ topology under the dimension condition $\dim N \geqslant 2 \dim M + 1$. For compact $M$, every $C^r$ map $M \to N$ can be approximated by an embedding; for noncompact $M$, the same holds within the subspace of proper maps. The proof uses the structure functor formalism: $A$-germs of embeddings form a continuous and locally extendable structure functor, and the Globalization Theorem 2.11 yields the global result. The compact case is simpler because continuity of the structure functor is automatic.
