A shrinking of an open cover $\mathcal{U}$ is a refined cover $\mathcal{V}$ with the same index set whose closed members $\bar{V}_i$ are contained in the corresponding $U_i$. From a partition of unity subordinate to $\mathcal{U}$, one obtains a shrinking by taking the interiors of the supports: $\{\operatorname{Int} \operatorname{Supp} \lambda_i\}$. This shows that existence of partitions of unity implies every open cover has a shrinking.
