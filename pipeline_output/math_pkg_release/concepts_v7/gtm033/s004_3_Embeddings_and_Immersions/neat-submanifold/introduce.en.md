A neat submanifold of a $\partial$-manifold $M$ is a submanifold whose boundary is the intersection of its own boundary with $\partial M$, and which meets $\partial M$ transversely. The neatness condition ensures that the submanifold sits "cleanly" within the ambient manifold with boundary—its boundary lies exactly on the ambient boundary and is not tangent to it. This is the correct generalization of submanifolds to the $\partial$-manifold setting.
