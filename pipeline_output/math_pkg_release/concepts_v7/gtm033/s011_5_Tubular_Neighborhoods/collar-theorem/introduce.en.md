The Collar Theorem (Theorem 6.1) asserts that every boundary of a smooth manifold admits a collar — a diffeomorphism from $\partial M \times [0,\infty)$ onto a neighborhood of $\partial M$ in $M$ that is the identity on $\partial M \times 0$. The proof constructs a retraction onto the boundary and a function with the boundary as a regular level set, then combines them into a local diffeomorphism. This theorem is fundamental for extending techniques from closed manifolds to manifolds with boundary. The analogous statement for $C^0$ manifolds (Brown's theorem) is much deeper.
