Theorem 6.2 refines the Collar Theorem for the case of a neat submanifold pair $(V,M)$. It states that one can find a collar of $\partial V$ in $V$ whose restriction to $M$ gives a collar of $\partial M$ in $M$. This compatibility between the collar structures of the ambient manifold and the submanifold is essential for transferring tubular neighborhood constructions from the boundaryless case to manifolds with boundary, and is a key ingredient in the proof that neat submanifolds admit tubular neighborhoods (Theorem 6.3).
