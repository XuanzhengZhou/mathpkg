A collar of a manifold boundary provides a product neighborhood structure $\partial M \times [0,\varepsilon)$ around the boundary. It is the analogue for manifolds with boundary of a tubular neighborhood: just as a tubular neighborhood identifies a neighborhood of a submanifold with a vector bundle, a collar identifies a neighborhood of the boundary with the product of the boundary and a half-open interval. The existence of collars for $C^\infty$ manifolds (Theorem 6.1) is a fundamental result that enables the extension of results about closed manifolds to manifolds with boundary.
