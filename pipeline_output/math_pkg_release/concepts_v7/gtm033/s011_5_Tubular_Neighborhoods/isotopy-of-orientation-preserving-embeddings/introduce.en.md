Theorem 6.6 states that any two embeddings of an $n$-disk (or $\mathbb{R}^n$) into an $n$-dimensional manifold are isotopic relative to the basepoint, provided their Jacobian determinant at $0$ is positive — i.e., they are orientation-preserving at the origin. The proof uses isotopy of tubular neighborhoods to reduce $f_1^{-1}f_0$ to a linear automorphism $L \in GL(n)$, and then connects $L$ to the identity via a path in $GL(n)$ (possible precisely when $\det L > 0$). The isotopy is then $f_1 L_t^{-1} f_1^{-1} f_0$. This is a key local-to-global result used in constructing orientations and in transversality arguments.
