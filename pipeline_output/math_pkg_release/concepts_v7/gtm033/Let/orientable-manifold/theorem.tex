Let $M$ be a manifold. $M$ is called orientable if its tangent bundle $TM$ is orientable. An orientation of $M$ is an orientation of $TM$. A manifold has just two orientations if it is orientable. If one of these is called $\omega$, the other is called $-\omega$.

Every manifold $M$ has an oriented double covering $\tilde{M}$. The natural map $p: \tilde{M} \rightarrow M$ is a submersion. If $M$ is orientable, then each orientation $\omega$ of $M$ defines a section $s_\omega: M \rightarrow \tilde{M}$ by $s_\omega(x) = (x, \omega_x)$. Conversely, every section defines an orientation of $M$.
