Theorem 3.2 is the dual of Theorem 3.1, obtained by replacing the Morse function $f$ with $-f$. A critical point of index $k$ for $f$ corresponds to an $(n-k)$-cell when approaching from above rather than below. The two cells $e^k$ and $e_*^{n-k}$ intersect transversely at the critical point and are called dual cells. This duality is fundamental to the handle decomposition of manifolds via Morse theory.
