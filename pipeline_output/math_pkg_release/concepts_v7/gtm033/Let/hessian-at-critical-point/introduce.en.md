At a critical point of a $C^2$ function on a manifold, the Hessian is a well-defined quadratic form on the tangent space, independent of the choice of coordinates. It is defined by composing the derivative of a chart with the classical Hessian of the local representation. The invariance follows from the fact that at a critical point, the Hessian transforms correctly under coordinate changes, unlike at regular points.
