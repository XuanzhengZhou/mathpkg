Let $P, Q$ be manifolds. An isotopy of $P$ in $Q$ is a homotopy

$$F: P \times I \rightarrow Q, \quad F(x,t) = F_t(x)$$

such that the related map

$$\hat{F}: P \times I \rightarrow Q \times I, \quad (x,t) \mapsto (F_t(x),t)$$

is an embedding. $\hat{F}$ is called the track of $F$. $F$ is an isotopy from $F_0$ to $F_1$.

If $A \subset P$ is such that $F_t(x) = F_0(x)$ for all $(x,t) \in A \times I$, then $F$ is a rel $A$ isotopy.
