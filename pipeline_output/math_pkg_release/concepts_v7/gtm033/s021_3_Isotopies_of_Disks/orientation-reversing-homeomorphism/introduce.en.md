A surface obtained by handle attachment via an orientable embedding admits an orientation reversing diffeomorphism, constructed by reflecting the cylinder $D^1 \times S^1$ and extending by a diffeomorphism on the remainder. Consequently, if the original surface $M$ is reversible, the new surface $M[f]$ is also reversible. This is used inductively to show that orientable surfaces of genus $p$ are reversible.
