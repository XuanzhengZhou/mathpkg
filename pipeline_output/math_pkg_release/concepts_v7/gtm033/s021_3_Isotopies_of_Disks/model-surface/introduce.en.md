Model surfaces are the canonical representatives for the diffeomorphism classification of compact surfaces. A model surface is specified by its genus $p$, orientability (orientable or nonorientable), and the number of boundary components. A $\partial$-surface with boundary is built by removing disks from a closed model surface. The main theorem of surface theory states that every compact connected surface is diffeomorphic to exactly one model surface.
