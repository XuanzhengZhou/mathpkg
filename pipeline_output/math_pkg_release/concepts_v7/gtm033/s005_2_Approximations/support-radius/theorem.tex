Let $\theta: \mathbb{R}^m \to \mathbb{R}$ be a map having compact support. There is a smallest $\sigma \geqslant 0$ such that $\operatorname{Supp}(\theta)$ is contained in the closed ball $B_\sigma(0) \subset \mathbb{R}^m$ of radius $\sigma$ and center $0$. We call $\sigma$ the \textbf{support radius} of $\theta$.
