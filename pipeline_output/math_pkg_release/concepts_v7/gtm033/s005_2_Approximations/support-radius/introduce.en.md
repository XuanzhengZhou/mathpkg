The support radius of a compactly supported map $\theta: \mathbb{R}^m \to \mathbb{R}$ is the smallest radius $\sigma \geq 0$ such that the support of $\theta$ is contained in the closed ball $B_\sigma(0)$. This notion is used to control the localization of convolution kernels when approximating functions.
