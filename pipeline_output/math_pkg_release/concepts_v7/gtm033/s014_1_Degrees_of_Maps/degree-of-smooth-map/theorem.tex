Let $(M, \omega)$ and $(N, \theta)$ be compact oriented $n$-manifolds without boundary, with $N$ connected. Let $f: M \to N$ be a $C^\infty$ map and $y \in N$ a regular value of $f$. For each $x \in f^{-1}(y)$, define $\operatorname{sign}_x(f) = +1$ if the derivative $Df_x: M_x \to N_y$ preserves orientation, and $\operatorname{sign}_x(f) = -1$ otherwise. The \textbf{degree} of $f$ at $y$ is
$$
\deg(f, y) = \sum_{x \in f^{-1}(y)} \operatorname{sign}_x(f).
$$
If $M$ is not connected with components $M_1, \ldots, M_k$, then
$$
\deg f = \sum_i \deg(f|_{M_i}),
$$
where each $M_j$ is given the orientation $\omega|_{M_j}$.
