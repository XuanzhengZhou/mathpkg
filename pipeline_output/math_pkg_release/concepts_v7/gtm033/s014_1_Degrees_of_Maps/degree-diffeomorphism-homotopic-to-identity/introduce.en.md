Theorem 1.3 establishes that any diffeomorphism of a compact connected oriented manifold which is homotopic to the identity must have degree 1. This result is used in the proof that the degree of a map is independent of the choice of regular value, a crucial step in making the degree a well-defined invariant of the homotopy class.
