This proposition captures the approximation-theoretic content of the easy Whitney embedding theorem. For a compact $C^r$ manifold $M$, any $C^r$ map into a sufficiently high-dimensional Euclidean space can be approximated arbitrarily closely (in the weak $C^r$ topology) by an embedding (when $q > 2n$) or by an immersion (when $q \geq 2n$). The weak topology controls closeness of derivatives only over compact sets, which suffices when the domain is compact.
