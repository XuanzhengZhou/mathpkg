Theorem 4.1 generalizes the classic regular value theorem to manifolds with boundary. When $y$ is a regular value for both $f$ and its restriction to the boundary $\partial M$, the preimage $f^{-1}(y)$ is a neat submanifold — meaning it meets the boundary cleanly. This is one of the fundamental tools for constructing submanifolds in the boundary setting.
