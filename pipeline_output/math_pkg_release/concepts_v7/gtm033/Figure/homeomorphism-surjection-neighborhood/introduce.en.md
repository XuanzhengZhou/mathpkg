Theorem 1.7 shows that in the strong $C^0$ topology, a homeomorphism between manifolds has a neighborhood consisting entirely of surjective maps. The proof reduces to showing that maps sufficiently close to the identity on a closed ball cover the ball. This is a purely topological result, contrasting with the fact that homeomorphisms are not open in $C^0_S$ when $\dim M > 0$.
