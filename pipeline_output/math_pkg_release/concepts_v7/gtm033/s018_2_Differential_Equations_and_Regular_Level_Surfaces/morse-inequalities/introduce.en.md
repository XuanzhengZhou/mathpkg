The Morse inequalities (Theorem 3.4) relate the type numbers $v_k$ of a Morse function to the Betti numbers $eta_k$ of the manifold pair $(M, f^{-1}(a))$. Part (a) gives a family of alternating-sum inequalities, while part (b) states that the alternating sum of type numbers equals the homological Euler characteristic. These inequalities constrain the number of critical points a Morse function must have.
