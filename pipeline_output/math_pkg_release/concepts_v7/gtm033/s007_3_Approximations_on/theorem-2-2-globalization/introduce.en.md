The Globalization Theorem 2.2 is an abstract gluing result for $C^r$ mapping classes. A mapping class $\mathcal{X}$ assigns to each triple $(L,U,V)$ (with $L$ closed in $U$) a set of maps $\mathcal{X}_L(U,V)$ satisfying a localization axiom. If $\mathcal{X}$ is "rich"---meaning that locally over compact sets these sets are dense and open in the weak topology---then globally $\mathcal{X}_L(M,N)$ is dense and open in the strong topology (and also weak when $L$ is compact). This theorem is the abstract engine behind transversality density proofs.
