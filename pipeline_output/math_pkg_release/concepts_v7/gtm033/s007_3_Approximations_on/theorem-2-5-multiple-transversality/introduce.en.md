Theorem 2.5 extends the transversality density result from one submanifold to finitely many submanifolds. It states that the set of maps simultaneously transverse to a finite collection of $C^r$ submanifolds $A_0, \ldots, A_q$ is residual (contains a countable intersection of dense open sets) in the strong $C^r$ topology. The proof is immediate: each transversality set is residual, and the intersection of finitely many residual sets is residual. This theorem underlies many generic position arguments.
