This is the fundamental technical lemma for the classification of compact surfaces. If a compact connected surface admits a Morse function with exactly two local minima and one saddle (and no other critical points), then the surface must be diffeomorphic to the 2-disk. The proof constructs a homeomorphism between $M$ and a model disk $D^2$ by matching level curves and gradient lines of the two Morse functions, then smooths this homeomorphism to a diffeomorphism.
