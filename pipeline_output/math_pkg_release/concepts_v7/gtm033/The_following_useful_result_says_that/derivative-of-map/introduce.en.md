The derivative (or Fréchet derivative) of a map between Euclidean spaces is the best linear approximation to the map at a given point. It is defined as the unique linear transformation $L$ such that the remainder $f(x+h) - f(x) - Lh$ vanishes faster than $|h|$ as $h \to 0$.
