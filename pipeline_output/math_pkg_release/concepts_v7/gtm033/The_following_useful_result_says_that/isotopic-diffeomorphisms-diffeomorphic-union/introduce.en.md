If two attaching maps from the boundary of $Q$ to the boundary of $P$ are isotopic, then the resulting manifolds obtained by gluing $P$ and $Q$ along these maps are diffeomorphic. This follows from extending the isotopy $g^{-1}f$ on $\partial Q$ to a diffeomorphism of $Q$ using a collar neighborhood, reducing to the case where the attaching maps are equal.
