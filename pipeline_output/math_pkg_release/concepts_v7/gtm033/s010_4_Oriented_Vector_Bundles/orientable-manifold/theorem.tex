Let $M$ be a manifold. $M$ is called \text{orientable} if its tangent bundle $TM$ is orientable. An \text{orientation} of $M$ is an orientation of $TM$. Just as for a general vector space $V$, a manifold has exactly two orientations if connected and of positive dimension; if one is denoted $\omega$, the other is $-\omega$.

Every manifold $M$ has an \text{oriented double covering} $\tilde{M}$, obtained by taking the oriented double covering of $TM$. The covering bundle $\tilde{\xi} = \widetilde{TM}$ is naturally isomorphic to $T\tilde{M}$, so $\tilde{M}$ is an orientable manifold. The natural projection $p: \tilde{M} \to M$ is a submersion.

If $M$ is orientable, each orientation $\omega$ of $M$ defines a section $s_\omega: M \to \tilde{M}$ by $s_\omega(x) = (x, \omega_x)$. Conversely, every section of $p: \tilde{M} \to M$ defines an orientation of $M$.
