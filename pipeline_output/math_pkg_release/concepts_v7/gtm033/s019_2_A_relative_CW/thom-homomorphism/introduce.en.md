The Thom homomorphism establishes a fundamental isomorphism between cobordism groups and homotopy groups of Thom spaces (one-point compactifications of universal vector bundles). This reduces the geometric problem of classifying manifolds up to cobordism to the algebraic problem of computing homotopy groups. The proof uses transversality to represent cobordism classes as inverse images of the Grassmann manifold, and the tubular neighborhood theorem to show that bordant manifolds correspond to homotopic maps.
