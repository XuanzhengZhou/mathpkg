The Grassmann manifold $G_{n,k}$ parametrizes all $k$-dimensional linear subspaces of $\mathbb{R}^n$. It is constructed as an analytic manifold by representing nearby $k$-planes as graphs of linear maps from a given $k$-plane $E$ to its orthogonal complement $E^\perp$. The resulting manifold has dimension $k(n-k)$ and is a fundamental example of a moduli space in differential topology and algebraic geometry.
