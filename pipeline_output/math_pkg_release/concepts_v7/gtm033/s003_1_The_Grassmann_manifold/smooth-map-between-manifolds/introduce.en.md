A $C^r$ map between $C^r$ manifolds is a continuous map whose local coordinate representations are $C^r$ functions between Euclidean spaces. This definition uses adapted charts to lift the notion of differentiability from Euclidean space to abstract manifolds. The composition of $C^r$ maps is $C^r$, and the identity map is $C^r$, forming the category of $C^r$ manifolds.
