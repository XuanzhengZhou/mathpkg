A regular point of a $C^1$ map $f: M \to N$ is a point where the derivative $T_x f$ is surjective; otherwise it is a critical point. A regular value is a point in $N$ whose entire preimage consists solely of regular points (or which has empty preimage). The regular value theorem states that the preimage of a regular value is a submanifold, making regular values a powerful tool for constructing manifolds. Sard's theorem guarantees that almost every point is a regular value.
