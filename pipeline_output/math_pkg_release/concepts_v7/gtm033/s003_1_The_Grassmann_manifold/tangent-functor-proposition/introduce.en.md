The tangent functor $T$ assigns to each $C^{r+1}$ manifold $M$ its tangent bundle $TM$ and to each $C^{r+1}$ map $f: M \to N$ its tangent map $Tf: TM \to TN$. It is a covariant functor because it respects composition and identity: $T(g \circ f) = Tg \circ Tf$ and $T(1_M) = 1_{TM}$. The functor also respects the projection maps, making the diagram $f \circ p_M = p_N \circ Tf$ commutative. This functoriality is the global expression of the chain rule.
