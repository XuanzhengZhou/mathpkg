The dimension (or codimension) relation provides a fundamental inequality governing intersections of algebraic sets. It states that the codimension of an intersection is bounded above by the sum of the codimensions of the intersecting sets. For curves in the complex projective plane, this inequality forces any two irreducible algebraic curves to intersect nontrivially, since the dimension of their intersection must be at least zero. This simple but powerful fact underlies the geometric intuition that algebraic curves in projective space cannot be disjoint, paving the way for Bézout's theorem.
