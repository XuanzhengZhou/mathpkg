This lemma states that when a coordinate ring $R = \mathbb{C}[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ is embedded in a larger polynomial ring $R^* = \mathbb{C}[x_1, \ldots, x_m]$ with $m \geqslant n$, the operation of contracting (intersecting with $R$) a maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}^*$ of $R^*$ always yields a maximal ideal of $R$. The proof uses the fact that $\mathfrak{m}^* = (x_1 - a_1, \ldots, x_m - a_m)$ so that $R^*/\mathfrak{m}^* \cong \mathbb{C}$, and since $R/(\mathfrak{m}^* \cap R)$ embeds naturally as a subring of $\mathbb{C}$ containing $\mathbb{C}$, it must be isomorphic to $\mathbb{C}$, forcing $\mathfrak{m}^* \cap R$ to be maximal. This result underpins the geometric interpretation that each point of the larger variety projects to a well-defined point of the smaller variety.
