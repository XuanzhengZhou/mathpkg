Definition 10.6 provides the concrete geometric formulation of the "lying over" relation for affine varieties. Given an embedding of affine spaces $\mathbb{C}^n \subset \mathbb{C}^n \times \mathbb{C}^m$, the natural projection $\pi$ onto the first $n$ coordinates sends points of $V^*$ into $\mathbb{C}^n$. Rather than requiring $\pi(V^*) = V$ exactly (which is too restrictive), the definition only requires that $\pi(V^*)$ be Zariski-dense in $V$. This allows for the possibility that some points of $V$ may not be hit by the projection, as long as they are limit points of the image. This definition pairs algebraically with the ideal-theoretic notion in Definition 10.7 and is shown equivalent to it in Theorem 10.8.
