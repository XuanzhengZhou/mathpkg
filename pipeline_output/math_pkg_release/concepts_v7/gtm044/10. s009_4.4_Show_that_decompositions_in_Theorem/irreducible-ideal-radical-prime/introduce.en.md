This result combines two facts: in a Noetherian ring, every irreducible ideal is primary (Exercise 4.5), and the radical of a primary ideal is prime (Exercise 5.2). Together they imply that the radical of any irreducible ideal in a Noetherian ring is prime. This is another piece of the structural relationship between irreducible, primary, and prime ideals that underpins primary decomposition theory.
