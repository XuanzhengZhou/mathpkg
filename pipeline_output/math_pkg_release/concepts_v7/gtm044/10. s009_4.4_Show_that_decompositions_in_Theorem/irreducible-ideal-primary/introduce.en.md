This theorem establishes that in Noetherian rings, the notion of irreducibility (cannot be written as a proper intersection of two strictly larger ideals) implies the notion of being primary. The proof uses the ideal quotient construction and the ascending chain condition (Noetherian property). This result is a key step toward the Lasker-Noether theorem, which states that every ideal in a Noetherian ring admits a primary decomposition.
