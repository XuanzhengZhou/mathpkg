This lemma warns that homogenization is not a left inverse of dehomogenization. A simple counterexample is $q(X_1, X_2) = X_1 X_2$: dehomogenizing at $X_1$ gives $D_1(q) = X_2$, and re-homogenizing gives $H_1(D_1(q)) = X_2 \neq X_1 X_2$. This asymmetry reflects the fact that dehomogenization may lose information about factors containing the dehomogenized variable.
