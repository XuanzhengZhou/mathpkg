Let $V \subset \mathbb{C}_{X_1, \ldots, X_m}$ and $W \subset \mathbb{C}_{Y_1, \ldots, Y_n}$ be two varieties.

(1) The set-theoretic product $V \times W \subset \mathbb{C}_{X_1, \ldots, X_m, Y_1, \ldots, Y_n}$ is a variety (called a product variety).

(2) Let $V$ and $W$ be irreducible with generic points $(x) = (x_1, \ldots, x_m)$ and $(y) = (y_1, \ldots, y_n)$, respectively, and suppose that $\mathbb{C}[x] \cap \mathbb{C}[y] = \mathbb{C}$. Then $V \times W$ is irreducible and has $(x, y)$ as a generic point.

(3) $\dim V \times W = \dim V + \dim W$.
