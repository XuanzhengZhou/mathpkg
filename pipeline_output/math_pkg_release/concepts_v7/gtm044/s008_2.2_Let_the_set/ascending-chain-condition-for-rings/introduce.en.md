The ascending chain condition (a.c.c.) is a fundamental finiteness property in ring theory. A ring satisfies the a.c.c. if every strictly increasing chain of ideals eventually stabilizes — there is no infinite, properly ascending sequence of ideals. Rings satisfying the a.c.c. are called Noetherian rings, and this condition is central to the algebraic foundations of algebraic geometry, where it guarantees that every variety decomposes uniquely into finitely many irreducible components.
