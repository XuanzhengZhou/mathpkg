Theorem 3.14 gives the geometric interpretation of the contraction-extension operation $\mathfrak{a} \mapsto \mathfrak{a}^{ec}$: it sends the ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ defining a subvariety $X \subset V$ to the ideal defining the germ $X_{(W)}$, which consists of those irreducible components of $X$ that contain $W$. Algebraically, $\mathfrak{a}^{ec}$ is exactly the intersection of all prime ideals containing $\mathfrak{a}$ and contained in $\mathfrak{p}$. The image of $\mathcal{J}(R_{\mathfrak{p}})$ under contraction consists precisely of $R$ together with those closed ideals that are intersections of prime ideals contained in $\mathfrak{p}$.
