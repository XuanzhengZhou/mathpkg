Theorem 3.16 establishes that the maps $G^*$ and $J^*$ form a lattice-reversing isomorphism between the closed ideal lattice $\mathcal{J}(R_{\mathfrak{p}})$ and the germ lattice $\mathcal{G}(R_{\mathfrak{p}})$, mirroring the global Hilbert Nullstellensatz correspondence. The four identities show that $G^*$ converts intersections to unions and sums to intersections, while $J^*$ does the reverse. These are the local analogues of the $V$ and $J$ operators used in the classic ideal-variety correspondence.
