This theorem establishes a deep geometric connection between ideal theory and intersection theory. On a nonsingular plane curve $C$, the factorization of the principal ideal generated by a polynomial $p$ into prime ideals corresponds exactly to the intersection cycle of the curve $\mathbf{C}(p)$ with $C$. The exponent of each prime ideal factor equals the intersection multiplicity at the corresponding point. This result is proved by analyzing fractional-power series expansions and resultants, showing that the order of $p(x_1,x_2)$ at a point equals the order of the resultant.
