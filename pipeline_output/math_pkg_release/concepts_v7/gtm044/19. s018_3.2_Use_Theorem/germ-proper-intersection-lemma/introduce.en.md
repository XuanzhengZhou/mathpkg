This lemma is a version of the prime avoidance principle used in the proof of Theorem 4.8. Given a finite set of prime ideals $\mathfrak{p}_1, \ldots, \mathfrak{p}_t$ and an ideal $\mathfrak{q}$ not contained in any of them, one can construct an element of $\mathfrak{q}$ that avoids all the $\mathfrak{p}_i$ simultaneously. Geometrically, for a pure $r$-dimensional germ, this implies that it is the proper intersection of $n-r$ germs of dimension $n-1$, a germ-theoretic converse to the fact that the proper intersection of $r$ hypersurfaces has pure dimension $n-r$.
