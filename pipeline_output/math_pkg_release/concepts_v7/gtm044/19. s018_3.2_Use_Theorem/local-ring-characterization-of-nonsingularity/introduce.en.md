This theorem provides a purely ring-theoretic characterization of nonsingularity: an irreducible subvariety $W$ of $V$ is nonsingular in $V$ exactly when the maximal ideal of the local ring $\mathfrak{o}(W;V)$ can be generated by $r-s$ elements, where $r=\dim V$ and $s=\dim W$. Such a local ring is called regular. The proof reduces the problem to computing the dimension of $\mathfrak{m}/\mathfrak{m}^2$ as a vector space over $R/\mathfrak{m}$, and connects this to the rank of the Jacobian matrix. This characterization allows the notions of singularity and nonsingularity to be generalized to arbitrary Noetherian rings.
