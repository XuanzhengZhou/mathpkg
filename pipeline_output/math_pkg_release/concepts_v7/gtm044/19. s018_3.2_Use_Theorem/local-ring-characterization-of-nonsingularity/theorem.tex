Let $W \subset V$ be irreducible varieties in $\mathbb{C}^n$ of dimensions $s$ and $r$, respectively, and let $\mathfrak{m}$ be the maximal ideal of the local ring $R = \mathfrak{o}(W; V)$ of $V$ at $W$. Then $W$ is nonsingular in $V$ if and only if $\mathfrak{m}$ is $R$-generated by some set of $r - s$ elements. (The local ring $\mathfrak{o}(W; V)$ is then said to be regular.)
