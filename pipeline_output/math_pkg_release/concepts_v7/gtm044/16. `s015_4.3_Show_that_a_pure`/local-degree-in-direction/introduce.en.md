The local degree of a variety $V$ at a point $P$ in the direction of a complementary-dimensional subspace $L_0$ is a numerical invariant measuring how many times $V$ meets a generic small perturbation of $L_0$ near $P$. For example, at the origin, the parabola $V(Y - X^2)$ has local degree $2$ in the $X$-axis direction (a tangent direction), but local degree $1$ in all other directions, illustrating that this invariant detects tangency.
