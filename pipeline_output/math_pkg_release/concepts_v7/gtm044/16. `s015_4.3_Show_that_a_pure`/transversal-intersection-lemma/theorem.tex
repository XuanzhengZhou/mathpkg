Let $V, W \subset \mathbb{P}^n(\mathbb{C})$ be pure-dimensional varieties and let $V^{\dagger\dagger} \subset \mathbb{C}^{3(n+1)^2} \times \mathbb{C}^{n+1}$ be the incidence variety parametrizing simultaneous independent linear transforms of $V$, $W$, and an auxiliary linear subspace $L$ (as in Notation 7.4). Let $P \in \mathbb{C}^{(n+1)^2}$ be a transformation degenerating $V$ to a union $V'$ of $\deg V$ distinct subspaces. There is a point $Q_0 \in \{P\} \times \mathbb{C}^{2(n+1)^2}$ such that exactly $\deg V \cdot \deg W$ distinct points of $V^{\dagger\dagger}$ lie over $Q_0$, and for each such point $Q_0^*$ and each $Q \in \mathbb{C}^{3(n+1)^2}$ sufficiently near $Q_0$, there is exactly one point of $V^{\dagger\dagger}$ arbitrarily near $Q_0^*$ lying over $Q$.
