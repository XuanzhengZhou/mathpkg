Let $V = V^r$ be a pure $r$-dimensional variety in $\mathbb{P}^n(\mathbb{C})$ or $\mathbb{C}^n$, let $P \in V$, and let $L$ be an $(n-r)$-dimensional projective or affine subspace properly intersecting $V$ at $P$. For almost all transforms $L'$ sufficiently near $L$, the intersection $V \cap L'$ near $P$ consists of a common, fixed number of distinct points.
