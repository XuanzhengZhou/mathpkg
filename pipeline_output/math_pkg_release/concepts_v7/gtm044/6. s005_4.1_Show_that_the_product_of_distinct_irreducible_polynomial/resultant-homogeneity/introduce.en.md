This theorem describes the homogeneity property of the resultant of two homogeneous polynomials. When $p$ and $q$ are homogeneous of degrees $m$ and $n$ in $\mathbb{C}[X_0, \ldots, X_r]$, written as polynomials in $X_r$ with leading coefficients in $\mathbb{C} \setminus \{0\}$, their resultant $\mathcal{R}_{X_r}(p, q)$ is either zero or a homogeneous polynomial of degree $mn$ in the remaining variables $X_0, \ldots, X_{r-1}$. The proof uses the scaling trick $X \mapsto TX$ and the determinantal definition of the resultant, factoring powers of $T$ from rows and columns.
