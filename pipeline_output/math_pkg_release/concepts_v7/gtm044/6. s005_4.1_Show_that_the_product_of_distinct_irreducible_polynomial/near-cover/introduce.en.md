A near cover relaxes the cover definition: it allows finitely many exceptional points $P_1, \ldots, P_r$ in the base $M$ where the covering property may fail. Outside the preimages of these exceptional points, the restricted map is required to be a genuine cover. When the cover away from the exceptional set is $s$-sheeted, we speak of a near $s$-sheeted cover. This concept is central to representing algebraic curves as branched covers of the Riemann sphere.
