Let $S$ be a topological space. A finite sequence of connected open subsets $(\mathcal{O}_1, \mathcal{O}_2, \ldots, \mathcal{O}_m)$ such that $\mathcal{O}_i \cap \mathcal{O}_{i+1}$ is connected and nonempty for $i = 1, \ldots, m-1$ is called a **chain in $S$**, a **chain from $\mathcal{O}_1$ to $\mathcal{O}_m$**, or simply a **chain**. If $P_1 \in \mathcal{O}_1$ and $P_m \in \mathcal{O}_m$, we say $(\mathcal{O}_1, \ldots, \mathcal{O}_m)$ is a **chain from $P_1$ to $P_m$**.
