This definition gives a purely algebraic characterization of singular and nonsingular points on an affine plane curve. Given a curve defined as the zero set $V(p)$ of a square-free polynomial $p(X,Y)$, a point $P$ on the curve is called singular if both first-order partial derivatives of $p$ vanish at $P$, and nonsingular otherwise. This definition is motivated by Theorem 7.4 (the Jacobi criterion) and provides a convenient computational tool: smoothness of the curve at a point reduces to checking whether the gradient of $p$ is nonzero there.
