Complex differentiability is a much stronger condition than real differentiability. While in the real case, differentiability at a point gives a linear approximation, complex differentiability forces the function to satisfy the Cauchy-Riemann equations, which in turn implies that all partial derivatives exist and that the function is analytic (holomorphic). A deep theorem of Hartogs states that separate analyticity in each variable implies joint analyticity, making complex-differentiable functions remarkably rigid. This rigidity is exactly what makes complex-analytic implicit mapping theorems so powerful in algebraic geometry.
