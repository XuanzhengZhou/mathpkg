Let $q(X_1, \ldots, X_{n+1}) \in k[X_1, \ldots, X_{n+1}]$ be a homogeneous polynomial. The dehomogenization of $q$ at $X_i$, denoted $D_{X_i}(q)$ or $D_i(q)$, is defined by

$$D_{X_i}(q)(X_1, \ldots, \widehat{X_i}, \ldots, X_{n+1}) = q(X_1, \ldots, X_{i-1}, 1, X_{i+1}, \ldots, X_{n+1}).$$

When the variable to dehomogenize at is clear from context, we write $D(q)$.
