A smooth 2-manifold is orientable if one can consistently assign a direction of rotation at every point. This is made precise by requiring that all coordinate transition maps have positive Jacobian determinant, so that the orientation induced by one chart agrees with that induced by any overlapping chart. The definition extends naturally to smooth $n$-manifolds and provides the analytic framework needed to study orientability of algebraic curves.
