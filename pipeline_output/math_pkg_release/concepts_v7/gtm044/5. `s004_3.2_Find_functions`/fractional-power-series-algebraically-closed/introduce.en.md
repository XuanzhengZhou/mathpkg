Theorem 4.16 states that the field $\mathbb{C}(X)^*$ of formal fractional-power series (also known as Puiseux series) is algebraically closed. This is a fundamental result generalizing the fact that $\mathbb{C}$ is algebraically closed and that every algebraic function admits a Puiseux series expansion. The theorem is stated without proof in this section, with a reference to Walker's text for the constructive proof. It generalizes Theorem 4.13 by asserting that any polynomial equation over $\mathbb{C}(X)^*$ has a solution within $\mathbb{C}(X)^*$ itself.
