\textbf{Proposition 2.1.} Let $f: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}$ be bounded. Then $f$ is Riemann integrable on $[a, b]$ if and only if $\operatorname{Re} f$ and $\operatorname{Im} f$ are Riemann integrable on $[a, b]$. In that case,
$$\int_a^b f = \int_a^b \operatorname{Re} f + i \int_a^b \operatorname{Im} f.$$
