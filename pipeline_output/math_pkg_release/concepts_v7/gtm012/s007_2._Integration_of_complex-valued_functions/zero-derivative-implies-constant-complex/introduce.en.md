If a complex-valued function has zero derivative everywhere on an interval, it must be constant. The proof reduces to the real case by considering the real and imaginary parts separately: by Proposition 3.2, both parts have zero derivative, and applying the Mean Value Theorem (Theorem 3.4) to each real part on any subinterval shows they are constant.
