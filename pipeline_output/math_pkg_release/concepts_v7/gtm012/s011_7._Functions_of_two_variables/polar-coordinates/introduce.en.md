Polar coordinates $(r, \theta)$ provide an alternative to Cartesian coordinates $(x, y)$ for describing points in the plane. The radial coordinate $r$ measures distance from the origin, while the angular coordinate $\theta$ measures the counterclockwise angle from the positive $x$-axis. This coordinate system is essential for problems with circular symmetry and for the change of variables formula in double integration.
