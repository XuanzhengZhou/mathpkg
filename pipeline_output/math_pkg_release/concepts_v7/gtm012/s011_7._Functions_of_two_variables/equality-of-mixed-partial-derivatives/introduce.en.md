Theorem 7.1 states that for $C^2$ functions, the order of taking partial derivatives does not matter: $D_1 D_2 f = D_2 D_1 f$. This is sometimes called Clairaut's theorem or Schwarz's theorem. The proof uses the fundamental theorem of calculus applied iteratively. The $C^2$ hypothesis (continuity of both mixed partials) is essential; counterexamples exist when this condition fails.
