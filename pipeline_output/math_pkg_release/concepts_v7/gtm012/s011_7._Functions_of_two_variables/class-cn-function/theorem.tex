Let $A$ be an open subset of $\mathbb{R}^2$ and let $f: A \to \mathbb{C}$. An $(n+1)$-order partial derivative of $f$ is $D_1 g$ or $D_2 g$, where $g$ is an $n$-order partial derivative of $f$. The function $f$ is said to be **$n$-times continuously differentiable**, or of **class $C^n$**, if all the partial derivatives of $f$ of order $\leq n$ exist and are continuous at each point of $A$. If this holds for every integer $n$, then $f$ is said to be **infinitely differentiable**, or of **class $C^\infty$**.
