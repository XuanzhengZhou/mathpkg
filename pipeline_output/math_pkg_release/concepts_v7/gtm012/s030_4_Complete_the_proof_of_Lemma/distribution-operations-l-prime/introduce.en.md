Basic operations on ordinary functions—translation, complex conjugation, and differentiation—are extended to arbitrary distributions in $\mathcal{L}'$ by duality: the operation is transferred to the test function. For instance, the derivative $DF$ is defined by $DF(u) = -F(Du)$, which mirrors integration by parts for functions. This definition ensures that when $F$ is defined by a differentiable function, the distributional derivative coincides with the classical derivative.
