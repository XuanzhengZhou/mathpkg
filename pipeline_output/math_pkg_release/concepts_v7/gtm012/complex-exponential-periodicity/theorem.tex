For all $z \in \mathbb{C}$:
\begin{align}
\exp(iz) &= \cos z + i \sin z, \\
\exp(z + 2\pi i) &= \exp z, \\
\sin(z + 2\pi) &= \sin z, \quad \cos(z + 2\pi) = \cos z.
\end{align}
Every nonzero $w \in \mathbb{C}$ can be written as $w = \exp(z)$ for some $z$, and if also $w = \exp(z')$, then $z' = z + 2n\pi i$ for some integer $n$.
