A periodic distribution $F$ is even if it is invariant under reflection: $F = F^\sim$. This generalizes the notion of an even function $v(-x) = v(x)$. Evenness and oddness partition the space $\mathcal{P}'$: every distribution can be uniquely decomposed as the sum of an even and an odd distribution. Even distributions satisfy $F(u) = 0$ whenever $u$ is an odd test function.
