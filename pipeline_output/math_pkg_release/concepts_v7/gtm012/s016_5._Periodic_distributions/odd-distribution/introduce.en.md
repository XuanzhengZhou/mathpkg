A periodic distribution $F$ is odd if it changes sign under reflection: $F = -F^\sim$. This generalizes the notion of an odd function $v(-x) = -v(x)$. Together with even distributions, this provides a complete symmetry decomposition of $\mathcal{P}'$. Odd distributions satisfy $F(u) = 0$ whenever $u$ is an even test function, and every distribution can be uniquely written as the sum of an even and an odd part.
