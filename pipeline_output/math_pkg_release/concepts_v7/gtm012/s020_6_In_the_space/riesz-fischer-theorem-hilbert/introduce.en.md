The Riesz-Fischer theorem is the converse direction of the orthogonal expansion theorem: every square-summable sequence of scalars corresponds to a unique element of the Hilbert space. Combined with Bessel's equality and Parseval's identity, this establishes that every separable Hilbert space is unitarily isomorphic to $\ell^2$, the space of square-summable sequences. This is a cornerstone result showing that all separable infinite-dimensional Hilbert spaces are isomorphic.
