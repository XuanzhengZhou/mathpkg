Theorem 4.5 is the classical Weierstrass polynomial approximation theorem: every continuous function on a closed bounded interval can be uniformly approximated by polynomials. The proof in Beals proceeds by reducing to the case $[0, \pi]$, extending the function to a $2\pi$-periodic continuous function, applying the trigonometric approximation theorem (Theorem 4.3), and then approximating the resulting trigonometric polynomials by ordinary polynomials via power series expansions of $\cos x$ and $\sin x$.
