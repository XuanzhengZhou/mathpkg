The ratio test uses the asymptotic behavior of successive term ratios to determine convergence. If the limsup of the absolute ratios is strictly less than 1, the terms eventually decay faster than a convergent geometric series, guaranteeing convergence. If the liminf of the absolute ratios exceeds 1, the terms grow and the series diverges. The test is inconclusive when the ratios approach 1 (as with $\sum 1/n$ and $\sum 1/n^2$). The ratio test is especially effective for series involving factorials and exponentials.
