Fourier coefficients are the fundamental numerical invariants associated to a periodic function or distribution via its Fourier series expansion. For a distribution $F \in L^2$, the $n$-th Fourier coefficient is obtained by evaluating $F$ at the exponential test function $e_{-n}$, i.e., $a_n = F(e_n)$. When $F$ arises from an integrable function $u$, this evaluation reduces to the classical integral formula $a_n = \frac{1}{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi} u(x)e^{-inx}\,dx$. The sequence of Fourier coefficients encodes the full information of the original object, as the Fourier series expansion theorem guarantees unique reconstruction in the $L^2$ sense.
