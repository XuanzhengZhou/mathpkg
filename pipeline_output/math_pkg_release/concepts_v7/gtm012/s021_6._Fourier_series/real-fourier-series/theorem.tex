Let $F \in L^2$ have Fourier coefficients $a_n = F(e_n)$. Define the real Fourier coefficients
$$b_n = a_n + a_{-n}, \quad c_n = i(a_n - a_{-n}), \quad n = 0, 1, 2, \ldots$$
Equivalently,
$$a_n = \tfrac{1}{2}(b_n - i c_n), \quad a_{-n} = \tfrac{1}{2}(b_n + i c_n), \quad n > 0,$$
with $a_0 = \tfrac{1}{2}b_0$. The Fourier series of $F$ can then be written in real form as
$$\frac{1}{2}b_0 + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left( b_n \cos nx + c_n \sin nx \right).$$
If $F$ is real (i.e., $F = F^*$), then $b_n, c_n$ are real numbers, and the series involves only real-valued functions.
