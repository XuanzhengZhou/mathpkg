Let $e_n(x) = \exp(inx)$, $n \in \mathbb{Z}$. The sequence $(e_n)_{-\infty}^{\infty}$ is an orthonormal basis for $L^2$.

For any $F \in \mathbf{L}^2$, the \textbf{Fourier series} is
$$F = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n e_n, \quad a_n = F(e_n),$$
in the sense that $\sum_{-N}^{N} a_n e_n \to F$ in $L^2$. Moreover,
$$\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} |a_n|^2 = \|F\|^2 \quad \text{(Parseval identity)}.$$
Conversely, if $\sum |a_n|^2 < \infty$, there is a unique $F \in \mathbf{L}^2$ with Fourier coefficients $a_n$.

If $u \in \mathbb{C}$ is continuously differentiable, the partial sums converge uniformly to $u$.
