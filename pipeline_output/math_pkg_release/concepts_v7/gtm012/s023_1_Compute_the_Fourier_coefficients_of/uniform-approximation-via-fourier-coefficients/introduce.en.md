Theorem 2.2 provides a general criterion for deducing uniform convergence of a sequence of smooth periodic functions from its Fourier coefficients. If the Fourier coefficients $(a_{m,n})$ of a sequence $(u_m)$ in $\mathcal{P}$ satisfy a uniform rapid-decrease bound $|a_{m,n}| \leq c|n|^{-r}$ (for some $r > 0$ and all $m$) and converge pointwise to a limiting sequence $(a_n)$, then the limit sequence consists of Fourier coefficients of some $u \in \mathcal{P}$, and $u_m$ converges to $u$ uniformly along with all its derivatives. This theorem provides a flexible tool for constructing smooth functions with prescribed Fourier behavior.
