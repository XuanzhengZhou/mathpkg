The wave equation describes propagation of disturbances at finite speed. Unlike the heat equation, the wave equation preserves regularity: singularities in initial data propagate along characteristics without smoothing. The d'Alembert formula provides an explicit closed-form solution for the Cauchy problem on the line; on the circle, Fourier series give the periodic analog.
