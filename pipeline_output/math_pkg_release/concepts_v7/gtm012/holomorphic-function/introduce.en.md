A holomorphic function is complex differentiable on an open set. Complex differentiability is much stronger than real differentiability: it implies infinite differentiability and analyticity (representability by power series). The Cauchy-Riemann equations link complex differentiability to real partial derivatives. Holomorphic functions are the central objects of complex analysis.
