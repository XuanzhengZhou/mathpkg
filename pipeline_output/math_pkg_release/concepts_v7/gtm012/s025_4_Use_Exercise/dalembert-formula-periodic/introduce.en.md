This is d'Alembert's classical formula for the solution of the one-dimensional wave equation on periodic functions with zero initial velocity. It expresses the solution $u(x,t)$ as the average of the initial displacement $g$ evaluated at the two points $x+t$ and $x-t$, reflecting the fact that disturbances propagate along characteristics at unit speed. The formula follows directly from Theorem~5.1 by expanding the Fourier coefficients and using the identity $\cos(nt) e^{inx} = \frac{1}{2}[e^{in(x+t)} + e^{in(x-t)}]$.
