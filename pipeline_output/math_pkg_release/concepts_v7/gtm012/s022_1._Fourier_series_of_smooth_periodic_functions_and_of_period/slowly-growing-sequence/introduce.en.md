A two-sided sequence of complex numbers is said to be of slow growth if its terms are bounded in absolute value by some polynomial in $|n|$. This is a much weaker condition than rapid decrease, and it characterizes exactly those sequences that can occur as the Fourier coefficients of a periodic distribution $F \in \mathcal{P}'$. The definition mirrors the classical growth condition for tempered distributions on $\mathbb{R}$ but adapted to the periodic setting.
