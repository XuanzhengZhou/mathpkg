Compactness implies both closedness and boundedness in any metric space. The converse is not true in general metric spaces (e.g., in infinite-dimensional normed spaces), but it holds in $\mathbb{R}^n$ by the Heine-Borel theorem. This result gives a quick necessary condition for compactness.
