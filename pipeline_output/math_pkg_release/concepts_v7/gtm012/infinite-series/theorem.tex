Suppose $(z_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ is a sequence in $\mathbb{C}$. The associated sequence $(s_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ of partial sums is
$$s_n = \sum_{m=1}^{n} z_m = z_1 + z_2 + \cdots + z_n.$$
If $(s_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ converges to $s$, the formal symbol $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} z_n$ denotes the \textbf{infinite series} and $s$ is its \textbf{sum}. The series \textbf{converges} if $(s_n)$ converges, and \textbf{diverges} otherwise. If $\sum |z_n|$ converges, the series is \textbf{absolutely convergent}.
