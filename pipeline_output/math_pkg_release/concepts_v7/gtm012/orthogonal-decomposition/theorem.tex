Suppose $H_1$ is a closed subspace of the Hilbert space $H$ and $u \in H$. Then there is a unique $v \in H_1$ closest to $u$:
$$\|u - v\| \leq \|u - w\|, \quad \text{all } w \in H_1.$$
Moreover, $v$ is the unique element of $H_1$ such that $u - v \perp H_1$ (i.e., $(u-v, w) = 0$ for all $w \in H_1$).
\textbf{Corollary.} If $H_1 \neq H$, there exists a nonzero $u \in H$ such that $u \perp H_1$.
