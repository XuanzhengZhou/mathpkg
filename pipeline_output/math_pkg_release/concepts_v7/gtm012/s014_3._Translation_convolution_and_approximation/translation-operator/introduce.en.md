The translation operator $T_t$ shifts a periodic continuous function $u \in \mathcal{C}$ by $t$ units to the right on the real line. This operator is fundamental to the study of convolution and approximation of periodic functions, as averaging translated copies of a function yields smoother approximations. The translation operator preserves continuity and periodicity, and its commutation with the differentiation operator $D$ plays a key role in proving that convolution with smooth functions produces smooth functions.
