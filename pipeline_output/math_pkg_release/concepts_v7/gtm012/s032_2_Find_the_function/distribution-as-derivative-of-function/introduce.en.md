Every distribution of finite order can be expressed as a finite-order distributional derivative of a regular distribution induced by a continuous function. Concretely, a distribution of order $k-2$ is the $k$-th derivative of some $F_f$, the distribution generated by a continuous function $f$. This structural result bridges the gap between abstract distributions and classical functions, showing that distributions are essentially generalized derivatives of continuous functions. The uniqueness part guarantees that the representation is well defined up to the function $f$ itself.
