Theorem 7.2 shows that the Green's function $G(t)$, defined as an improper contour integral, converges and takes a particularly simple form. For $t < 0$ it vanishes (causality), while for $t > 0$ it is a linear combination of terms $t^k e^{z_j t}$ where $z_j$ are the roots of the characteristic polynomial $p$ and $k$ ranges up to one less than the multiplicity. This makes $G(t)$ explicitly computable once the roots are known.
