Let $X$ be a vector space and let $S \subset X$ be a subset. The span of $S$, denoted $\operatorname{span}(S)$, is the set of all linear combinations of finitely many elements of $S$:

$$\operatorname{span}(S) = \left\{ \sum_{j=1}^{n} a_j x_j \;\middle|\; n \in \mathbb{N},\ a_j \text{ scalars},\ x_j \in S \right\}.$$

The span of $S$ is the smallest subspace of $X$ containing $S$. If $S$ is empty, $\operatorname{span}(\emptyset) = \{0\}$.
