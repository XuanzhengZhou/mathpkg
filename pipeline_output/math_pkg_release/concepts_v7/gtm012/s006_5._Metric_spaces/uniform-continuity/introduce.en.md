Uniform continuity strengthens ordinary continuity by requiring that the same $\delta$ works uniformly for all points in the domain. While continuity allows $\delta$ to depend on both $\varepsilon$ and the point $x$, uniform continuity demands a $\delta$ that depends only on $\varepsilon$. The distinction is crucial: functions like $f(x) = 1/x$ on $(0, 1]$ are continuous but not uniformly continuous. Theorem 1.3 shows that on compact domains, the two notions coincide.
