Proposition 6.1 states that in any nonempty metric space, every compact subset must be both closed and bounded. These are necessary conditions for compactness. However, they are not sufficient in general metric spaces: for example, $(0, 1]$ is closed and bounded as a subset of itself but is not compact. The Heine-Borel theorem characterizes the special case of $\mathbb{R}^n$ where closed and bounded is equivalent to compactness.
