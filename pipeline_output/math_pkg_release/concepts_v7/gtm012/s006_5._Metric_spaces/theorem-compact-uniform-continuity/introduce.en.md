Theorem 1.3 (sometimes called the Heine-Cantor theorem) states that on a compact domain, continuity implies uniform continuity. The proof uses the open cover formulation of compactness: for each point $x$, continuity provides a neighborhood where $f$ varies by at most $\varepsilon/2$. A finite subcover yields points $x_1, \ldots, x_n$, and taking $\delta$ as the minimum of the corresponding radii ensures the uniform bound. This theorem provides one of the most important applications of compactness in analysis.
