Suppose $(S, d)$ is a metric space and $A \subset S$. A point $x \in S$ is said to be a limit point of $A$ if for every $r > 0$ there is a point of $A$ with distance from $x$ less than $r$:

$$B_r(x) \cap A \neq \emptyset \quad \text{for all } r > 0.$$

In other words, every ball centered at $x$ contains a point of $A$ (possibly $x$ itself).
