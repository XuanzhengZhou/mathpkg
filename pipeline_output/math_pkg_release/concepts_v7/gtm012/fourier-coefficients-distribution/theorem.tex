A sequence $(a_n)_{-\infty}^{\infty} \subset \mathbb{C}$ is the sequence of Fourier coefficients of a periodic distribution $F \in \mathcal{P}'$ if and only if it is of slow growth.

Moreover, if $F \in \mathcal{P}'$ has coefficients $(a_n)$ and $u \in \mathcal{P}$ has coefficients $(b_n)$, then:
\[
F(u) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n b_{-n} = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_{-n} b_n.
\]
