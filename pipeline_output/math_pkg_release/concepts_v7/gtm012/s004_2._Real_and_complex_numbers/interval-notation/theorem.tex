Let $a, b \in \mathbb{R}$ with $a < b$. The following subsets of $\mathbb{R}$ are called intervals:
$$
\begin{aligned}
(a, b) &= \{x \mid x \in \mathbb{R},\ a < x < b\}, \\
(a, b] &= \{x \mid x \in \mathbb{R},\ a < x \leq b\}, \\
[a, b) &= \{x \mid x \in \mathbb{R},\ a \leq x < b\}, \\
[a, b] &= \{x \mid x \in \mathbb{R},\ a \leq x \leq b\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Additionally, the unbounded intervals are defined by
$$
\begin{aligned}
(a, \infty) &= \{x \mid x \in \mathbb{R},\ a < x\}, \\
(-\infty, a] &= \{x \mid x \in \mathbb{R},\ x \leq a\},
\end{aligned}
$$
and similarly for $[a, \infty)$, $(-\infty, a)$, $(-\infty, \infty) = \mathbb{R}$.
