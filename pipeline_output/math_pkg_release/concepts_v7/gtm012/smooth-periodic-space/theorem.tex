$\mathcal{P}$ is the subspace of $\mathcal{C}$ consisting of infinitely differentiable periodic functions. A sequence $(u_n) \subset \mathcal{P}$ \textit{converges in the sense of $\mathcal{P}$} if for each $k \geq 0$, $\|D^k u_n - D^k u\| \to 0$ uniformly. $\mathcal{P}$ with this notion of convergence is a complete metric space (a Fr\'echet space), with metric:
\[
d'(u, v) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} 2^{-k-1} \|D^k u - D^k v\| \left[1 + \|D^k u - D^k v\|\right]^{-1}.
\]
