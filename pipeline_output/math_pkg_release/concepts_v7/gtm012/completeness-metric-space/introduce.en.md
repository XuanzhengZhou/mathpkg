A metric space is complete if every Cauchy sequence has a limit within the space. This generalizes the Cauchy convergence criterion for $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{C}$. Complete metric spaces (Banach spaces when they are also normed vector spaces) are the natural setting for many existence theorems in analysis.
