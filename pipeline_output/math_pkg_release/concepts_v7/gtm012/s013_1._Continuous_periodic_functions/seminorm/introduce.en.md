A seminorm generalizes the notion of a norm by dropping the requirement that only the zero vector has zero seminorm. Seminorms are the fundamental building blocks of locally convex topological vector spaces: the topology is generated by a family of seminorms, with basic open neighborhoods of zero given by $\{u : p_i(u) < \varepsilon\}$. A countable separating family of seminorms defines a metrizable topology and, when complete, yields a Frechet space.
