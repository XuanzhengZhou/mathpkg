The \textbf{heat equation} on the circle is the partial differential equation
$$\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}, \quad u(x, 0) = f(x),$$
where $f$ is a periodic distribution. For $f \in \mathcal{P}'$ with Fourier coefficients $a_n$, the unique distribution solution is
$$u(x, t) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n e^{-n^2 t} e^{inx}, \quad t > 0.$$
For $t > 0$, $u(\cdot, t)$ is a smooth function. If $f$ is continuous, $u$ is a classical solution for $t > 0$.
