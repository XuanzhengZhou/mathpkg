The heat equation models diffusion of temperature. On the circle (periodic boundary conditions), the Fourier method reduces the PDE to an infinite system of decoupled ODEs. The solution is smoothing: for any positive time, the solution becomes $C^\infty$, even if the initial data is merely a distribution. This "infinite propagation speed with smoothing" is characteristic of parabolic equations.
