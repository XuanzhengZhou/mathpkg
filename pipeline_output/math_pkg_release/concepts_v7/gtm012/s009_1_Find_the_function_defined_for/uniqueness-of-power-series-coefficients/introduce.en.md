Setting $x = x_0$ in the formula for the $k$-th derivative of a power series collapses all terms except the constant term, yielding the familiar Taylor coefficient relation $a_k = f^{(k)}(x_0)/k!$. This means a convergent power series representation of a function is unique — if two power series centered at $x_0$ agree on a neighborhood of $x_0$, all their coefficients must coincide. This justifies identifying a function with its power series expansion and is the basis for methods that determine solutions of differential equations by matching coefficients.
