The functional equation $e^{z+w} = e^z e^w$ extends from real to complex arguments, confirming that the complex exponential is a group homomorphism from the additive group $(\mathbb{C}, +)$ to the multiplicative group $(\mathbb{C}\setminus\{0\}, \cdot)$. Additionally, the complex exponential respects conjugation: $e^{z^*} = (e^z)^*$, which follows from the series definition since conjugation distributes over sums and products. These identities are used to unify the solution formulas for linear ODEs with constant coefficients, expressing them compactly as $f(x) = ce^{ax} + \int_0^x e^{a(x-t)}h(t)dt$.
