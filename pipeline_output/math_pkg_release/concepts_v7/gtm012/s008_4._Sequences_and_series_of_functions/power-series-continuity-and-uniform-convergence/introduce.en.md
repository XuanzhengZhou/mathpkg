Theorem 4.3 shows that a power series with radius of convergence $R$ defines a continuous function on the entire open disc of convergence. The key technique is proving that the partial sums converge uniformly on each strictly smaller closed subdisc $D_r$ with $0<r<R$, via a comparison with a geometric series. Combined with Theorem 4.1, this uniform convergence on subdiscs implies continuity of the limit function.
