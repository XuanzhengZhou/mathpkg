Theorem 4.1 establishes two fundamental properties of uniform convergence. First, the space of bounded complex-valued functions on any set is complete with respect to the sup norm: every uniform Cauchy sequence converges uniformly to a unique bounded limit function. Second, the uniform limit of a sequence of continuous functions on a metric space is itself continuous. Together these results justify the central role of uniform convergence in analysis.
