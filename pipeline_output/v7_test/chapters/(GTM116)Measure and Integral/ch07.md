THE INTEGRAL $I_{\mu}$ ON $L_1(\mu)$

This section is devoted to the construction of an integral $I_{\mu}$ from a measure $\mu$, to the relationships between $\mu$ and $I_{\mu}$ (especially for Borel measures $\mu$ for $\mathbb{R}$), and to a brief consideration of the vector spaces $L_p(\mu), 1 \leq p \leq \infty$, associated with $\mu$.

There is no difficulty in obtaining an integral from a measure. If $\mu$ is a measure on a $\delta$-ring $\mathcal{A}$ of subsets of $X$, $L^{\mathcal{A}}$ is the class of $\mathcal{A}$ simple functions on $X$ and $I^{\mu}$ is the linear functional on $L^{\mathcal{A}}$ such that $I^{\mu}(\chi_A) = \mu(A)$ for all $A$ in $\mathcal{A}$, then $\mu$ is also a pre-measure and therefore, according to theorem 2.7, $I^{\mu}$ is a pre-integral. Hence, by theorem 3.4, the Daniell extension of $I^{\mu}$ is an integral. Thus $I^{\mu}$ has an integral extension, and $I_{\mu}$, the integral w.r.t. $\mu$, is to be the minimal extension of $I^{\mu}$.

The construction just outlined for $I_{\mu}$ is not efficient—it fails to use the fact that $\mu$ is supposed to be a measure, not just a pre-measure. We give a direct construction for $I_{\mu}$ and a simple proof that $I_{\mu}$ is an integral.

A sequence $\{f_n\}_n$ of real valued functions on $X$ converges $\mu$ almost uniformly to a function $f$ iff for each $e > 0$ there is a member $A$ of $A$ with $I_\mu(\chi_A) < e$ such that $\{f_n\}_n$ converges uniformly to $f$ on $X \setminus A$. In general, almost uniform convergence is uniform convergence outside the union $A$ of a disjoint sequence $\{A_n\}_n$ in $A$ with $\sum_n \mu(A_n)$ small; we sometimes say $A$ is of small standardized $\mu$ measure.
