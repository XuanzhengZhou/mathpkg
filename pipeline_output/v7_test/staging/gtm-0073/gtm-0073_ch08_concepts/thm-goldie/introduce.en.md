Goldie's Theorem is a landmark result that extends the Wedderburn-Artin theorems to a wider class of rings. It characterizes semiprime left Goldie rings as exactly those rings that have a semisimple left Artinian left quotient ring. Since the structure of semisimple left Artinian rings is completely known (Wedderburn-Artin), this gives as good a description of semiprime left Goldie rings as possible: they are "orders" in semisimple Artinian rings. The proof (due to Procesi and Small) uses Ore's Theorem.
