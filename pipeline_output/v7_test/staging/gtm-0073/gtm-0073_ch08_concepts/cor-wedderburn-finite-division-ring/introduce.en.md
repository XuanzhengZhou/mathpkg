Wedderburn's famous "little theorem": every finite division ring is commutative, hence a finite field. This is a fundamental result in algebra showing that finiteness forces commutativity for division rings. The proof uses the fact that all maximal subfields are isomorphic (by the Noether-Skolem theorem) and conjugate, together with a group-theoretic lemma that a finite group cannot be covered by conjugates of a proper subgroup.
