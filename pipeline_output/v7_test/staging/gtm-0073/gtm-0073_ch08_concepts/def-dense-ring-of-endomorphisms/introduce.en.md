A dense ring of endomorphisms on a vector space V over a division ring D is a subring of the full endomorphism ring that can "interpolate" any finite set of linearly independent vectors to arbitrary target vectors. This is the key property that generalizes the full endomorphism ring -- the full ring Hom_D(V,V) is always dense in itself, and the Jacobson Density Theorem shows that every primitive ring is isomorphic to such a dense ring.
