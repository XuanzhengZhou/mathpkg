K is obviously a left ideal. For a in K, let T = {r + ra | r in R}. T is a regular left ideal (with e = -a). If T != R, then by Lemma 2.4, T is contained in a regular maximal left ideal I_0. Since a in K subset I_0, ra in I_0 for all r. But r + ra in T subset I_0, so r in I_0 for all r, making R = I_0, contradiction. Thus T = R. Hence there exists r with r + ra = -a, i.e., r + a + ra = 0. So a is left quasi-regular.
