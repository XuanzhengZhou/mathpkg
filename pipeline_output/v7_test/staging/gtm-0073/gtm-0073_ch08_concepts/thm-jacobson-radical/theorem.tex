If $R$ is a ring, then there is an ideal $J(R)$ of $R$ such that:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $J(R)$ is the intersection of all the left annihilators of simple left $R$-modules;
\item[(ii)] $J(R)$ is the intersection of all the regular maximal left ideals of $R$;
\item[(iii)] $J(R)$ is the intersection of all the left primitive ideals of $R$;
\item[(iv)] $J(R)$ is a left quasi-regular left ideal which contains every left quasi-regular left ideal of $R$;
\item[(v)] Statements (i)--(iv) are also true if "left" is replaced by "right".
\end{itemize}
