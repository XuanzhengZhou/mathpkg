Let J(R) be the intersection of all left annihilators of simple left R-modules. J(R) is an ideal by Theorem 1.4. If R has no simple left modules, J(R) = R and all properties hold vacuously. Assuming J(R) != R: equivalence of (i) and (iii) follows from definition (left primitive ideal = annihilator of simple module). For (ii), regular maximal left ideals correspond to simple modules via Theorem 1.3, and their intersection equals J(R) by Lemmas 2.5 and 2.6. For (iv), J(R) is left quasi-regular (Lemma 2.5) and contains every left quasi-regular left ideal (Lemma 2.6). Right-left symmetry follows from Lemma 2.8 (left quasi-regular implies right quasi-regular) and the analogous right-hand statements.
