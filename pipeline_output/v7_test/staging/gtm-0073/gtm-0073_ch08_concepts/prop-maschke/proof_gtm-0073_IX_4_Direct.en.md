Suppose char K = 0 or p not dividing |G|. Embed K(G) into Hom_K(K(G), K(G)) via left multiplication. Define a trace-like map and use averaging over the group to construct a K-linear projection onto the fixed points. If K(G) had nonzero radical, averaging would give a contradiction. For characteristic p dividing |G|, the element w = sum_{g in G} g satisfies w^2 = |G|w = 0, so K(G)w is a nonzero nilpotent left ideal, hence contained in J(K(G)).
