If R is nonzero semisimple, let O be the set of all left primitive ideals. Then 0 = J(R) = cap_{P in O} P by Theorem 2.3(iii). Map R -> prod_{P in O} R/P via r -> {r+P}. This is a monomorphism, and each projection is surjective, so R is a subdirect product of the primitive rings R/P. Conversely, a subdirect product of primitive (= semisimple) rings is semisimple because the radical of a product is the product of radicals, and the radical of a subring of a semisimple ring is zero.
