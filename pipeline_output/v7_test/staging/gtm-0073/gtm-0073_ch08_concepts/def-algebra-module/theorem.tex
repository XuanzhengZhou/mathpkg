Let $A$ be an algebra over a commutative ring $K$ with identity.
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] A \textbf{left (algebra) $A$-module} is a unitary left $K$-module $M$ such that $M$ is a left module over the ring $A$ and $k(rc) = (kr)c = r(kc)$ for all $k \in K$, $r \in A$, $c \in M$.
\item[(ii)] An \textbf{$A$-submodule} of an $A$-module $M$ is a subset of $M$ which is itself an algebra $A$-module.
\item[(iii)] An algebra $A$-module $M$ is \textbf{simple} (or \textit{irreducible}) if $AM \neq 0$ and $M$ has no proper $A$-submodules.
\item[(iv)] An \textbf{$A$-homomorphism} $f: M \to N$ of algebra $A$-modules is a map that is both a $K$-module and an $A$-module homomorphism.
\end{itemize}
