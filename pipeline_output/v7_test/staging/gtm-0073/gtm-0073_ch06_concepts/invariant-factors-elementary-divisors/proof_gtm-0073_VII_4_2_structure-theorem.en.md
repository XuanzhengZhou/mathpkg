View $E$ as a $K[x]$-module with $x \cdot u = \phi(u)$. Since $E$ is finite-dimensional over $K$ and $K \subset K[x]$, $E$ is finitely generated over $K[x]$. The minimal polynomial $q_\phi$ annihilates $E$ ($q_\phi \neq 0$), so $E$ is a torsion $K[x]$-module. By Theorem IV.6.12(i), $E$ decomposes as a direct sum of cyclic $K[x]$-modules $E_i$ of orders $q_i$ with $q_1 \mid \cdots \mid q_t$. Each $E_i$ is $\phi$-invariant and $\phi$-cyclic. The monic generators $q_i$ are unique. Part (ii) follows from the primary decomposition (Theorem IV.6.12(ii)): each cyclic module further decomposes into primary cyclic modules whose orders are powers of irreducible polynomials.
