A finite-dimensional vector space $E$ equipped with a linear transformation $\phi$ becomes a finitely generated torsion module over the PID $K[x]$ (with $x$ acting as $\phi$). Applying the structure theorem for modules over a PID yields a decomposition of $E$ into cyclic $K[x]$-submodules. The orders of these cyclic submodules are the invariant factors $q_1 \mid \cdots \mid q_t$, and their prime power factorizations give the elementary divisors. These polynomials are complete invariants for the pair $(E, \phi)$ up to isomorphism.
