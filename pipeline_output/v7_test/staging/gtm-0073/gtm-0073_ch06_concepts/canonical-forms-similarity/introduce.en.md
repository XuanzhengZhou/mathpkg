For square matrices over a field, similarity is completely classified by invariant factors or elementary divisors. Each similarity class contains a unique rational canonical form (direct sum of companion matrices of invariant factors), a unique primary rational canonical form (direct sum of companion matrices of elementary divisors), and when applicable, a unique Jordan canonical form (direct sum of Jordan blocks). Two matrices are similar exactly when they share the same invariant factors (or equivalently, the same elementary divisors).
