Let $\phi : K^n \to K^n$ be the linear transformation with matrix $A$ relative to the standard basis (Theorem 1.2). By Corollary 1.7, two matrices are similar iff they represent the same $\phi$ in different bases. By Theorem 4.6, $\phi$ has a rational canonical form $R$ (direct sum of companion matrices of invariant factors), a primary rational canonical form $P$, and (when applicable) a Jordan canonical form $J$. Thus $A$ is similar to $R, P, J$. If $A$ is also similar to another matrix in one of these forms, uniqueness of the invariant factors/elementary divisors (Theorem 4.2) forces the forms to coincide (up to block order).
