Let $A$ be an $n \times m$ matrix over a principal ideal domain $R$. Then $A$ is equivalent to a diagonal matrix
\[
\begin{pmatrix}
d_1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
0 & d_2 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \cdots & \vdots \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & d_r & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \cdots & \vdots & \vdots & \cdots & \vdots \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0
\end{pmatrix}
\]
with each $d_i \neq 0$ and $d_1 \mid d_2 \mid \cdots \mid d_r$. The ideals $(d_i)$ are uniquely determined by the equivalence class of $A$. The elements $d_i$ are called the invariant factors of $A$.
