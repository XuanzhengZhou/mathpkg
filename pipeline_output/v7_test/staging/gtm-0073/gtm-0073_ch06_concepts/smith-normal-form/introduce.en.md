Every matrix over a principal ideal domain is equivalent to a diagonal matrix whose diagonal entries satisfy the divisibility chain $d_1 \mid d_2 \mid \cdots \mid d_r$. These invariant factors are uniquely determined. This is the Smith normal form theorem, a generalization of the canonical forms for matrices over a division ring (Theorem 2.6), and is fundamental to the structure theory of finitely generated modules over a PID.
