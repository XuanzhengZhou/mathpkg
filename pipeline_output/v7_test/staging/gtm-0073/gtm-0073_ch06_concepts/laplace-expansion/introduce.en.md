The determinant can be computed by expanding along any row or column: the determinant equals the sum over entries in that row (or column) of the entry times its cofactor $(-1)^{i+j}|A_{ij}|$. This recursive formula reduces the computation of an $n \times n$ determinant to $n$ determinants of size $(n-1) \times (n-1)$, and is the basis for the classical adjoint and Cramer's rule.
