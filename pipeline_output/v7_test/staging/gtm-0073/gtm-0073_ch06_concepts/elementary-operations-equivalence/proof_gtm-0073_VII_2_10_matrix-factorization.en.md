Since each row [column] operation used to obtain $B$ from $A$ is given by left [right] multiplication by an appropriate elementary matrix (Theorem 2.8), we have $B = (E_p \cdots E_1)A(F_1 \cdots F_q) = PAQ$ with each $E_i, F_j$ an elementary matrix and $P = E_p \cdots E_1$, $Q = F_1 \cdots F_q$. $P$ and $Q$ are products of invertible matrices (Corollary 2.9) and hence invertible.
