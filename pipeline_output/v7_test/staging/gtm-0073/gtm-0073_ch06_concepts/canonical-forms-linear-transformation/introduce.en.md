Every linear transformation on a finite-dimensional vector space admits three canonical matrix representations. The rational canonical form is a direct sum of companion matrices of the invariant factors. The primary rational canonical form further refines this by using companion matrices of the elementary divisors (prime power factors). When all irreducible factors are linear (e.g., over an algebraically closed field), one obtains the Jordan canonical form, where each block is a Jordan block with an eigenvalue on the diagonal and ones on the superdiagonal.
