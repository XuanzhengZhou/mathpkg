Let $\phi : E \to E$ be a linear transformation of an $n$-dimensional vector space $E$ over a field $K$.
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ has a basis relative to which the matrix of $\phi$ is the direct sum of the companion matrices of the invariant factors $q_1, \ldots, q_t \in K[x]$ of $\phi$. This is the \textbf{rational canonical form}.
\item $E$ has a basis relative to which the matrix of $\phi$ is the direct sum of the companion matrices of the elementary divisors $p_i^{m_{ij}}$ of $\phi$. This is the \textbf{primary rational canonical form}.
\item If the irreducible factors of the invariant factors of $\phi$ are all linear polynomials (which always happens when $K$ is algebraically closed), then $E$ has a basis relative to which the matrix of $\phi$ is the direct sum of the elementary Jordan matrices associated with the elementary divisors. This is the \textbf{Jordan canonical form}.
\end{enumerate}
In each case, the form is uniquely determined by $\phi$ up to the order of the diagonal blocks.
