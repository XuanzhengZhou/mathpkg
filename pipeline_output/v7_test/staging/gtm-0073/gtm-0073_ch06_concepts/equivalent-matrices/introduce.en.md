Two matrices represent the same homomorphism with respect to different choices of ordered bases if and only if they are equivalent, meaning one can be obtained from the other by multiplying on the left and right by invertible matrices. These invertible matrices are precisely the change-of-basis matrices. This establishes the fundamental relationship between equivalence of matrices and change of bases.
