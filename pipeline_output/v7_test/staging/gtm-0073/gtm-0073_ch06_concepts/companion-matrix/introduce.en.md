When a vector space $E$ is cyclic with respect to a linear transformation $\phi$ (meaning $E = K[x]v$ for some cyclic vector $v$), then $\phi$ is represented by the companion matrix of its minimal polynomial in the basis $\{v, \phi(v), \ldots, \phi^{r-1}(v)\}$. The companion matrix of a monic polynomial $q$ has ones on the superdiagonal and the negated coefficients of $q$ (excluding the leading term) in the last row.
