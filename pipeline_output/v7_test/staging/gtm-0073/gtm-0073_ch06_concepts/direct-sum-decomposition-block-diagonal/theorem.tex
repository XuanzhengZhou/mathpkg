Let $\phi : E \to E$ be a linear transformation of a finite dimensional vector space $E$ over a field $K$. Then $E = E_1 \oplus E_2 \oplus \cdots \oplus E_t$ with each $E_i$ a $\phi$-invariant subspace and $M_i$ the matrix of $\phi|_{E_i}$ relative to some ordered basis of $E_i$, if and only if $\phi$ has a matrix of the form
\[
M = \begin{pmatrix}
M_1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
0 & M_2 & \cdots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & M_t
\end{pmatrix}
\]
relative to some ordered basis of $E$. The matrix $M$ is the \textbf{direct sum} of $M_1, \ldots, M_t$ (in this order).
