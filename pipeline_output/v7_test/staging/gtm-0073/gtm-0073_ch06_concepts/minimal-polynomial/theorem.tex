Let $K$ be a field and $\phi : E \to E$ a linear transformation of a finite dimensional $K$-vector space $E$. The \textbf{minimal polynomial} $q_\phi \in K[x]$ of $\phi$ is the unique monic polynomial generating the kernel of the evaluation homomorphism $\zeta_\phi : K[x] \to \operatorname{Hom}_K(E,E)$ given by $f \mapsto f(\phi)$. Equivalently, $q_\phi$ is the monic polynomial of least degree such that $q_\phi(\phi) = 0$.

Similarly, for $A \in \operatorname{Mat}_n K$, the minimal polynomial $q_A$ is the unique monic polynomial such that $(q_A) = \operatorname{Ker} \zeta_A$, where $\zeta_A : K[x] \to \operatorname{Mat}_n K$ is the evaluation homomorphism $f \mapsto f(A)$. If $A$ is the matrix of $\phi$ relative to some basis, then $q_\phi = q_A$.
