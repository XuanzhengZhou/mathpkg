The minimal polynomial of a linear transformation $\phi$ (or its matrix $A$) is the monic polynomial of least degree that annihilates $\phi$ (or $A$). It exists because $\operatorname{Hom}_K(E,E)$ is finite-dimensional over $K$ while $K[x]$ is infinite-dimensional, forcing the evaluation homomorphism to have a nontrivial kernel. The minimal polynomial is the generator of the principal ideal $\operatorname{Ker}(\zeta_\phi)$ in $K[x]$.
