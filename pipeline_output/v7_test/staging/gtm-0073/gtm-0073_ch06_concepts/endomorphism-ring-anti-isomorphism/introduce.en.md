The set of all $R$-linear endomorphisms of a free module of rank $n$ forms a ring under addition and composition. Mapping each endomorphism to its matrix (relative to a fixed basis) yields an anti-isomorphism of rings: the matrix of a composition $fg$ is the product of matrices in the reverse order. Composing with the transpose yields an actual ring isomorphism with the opposite matrix ring.
