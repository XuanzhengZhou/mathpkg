Let $\phi : \operatorname{Hom}_R(E,E) \to \operatorname{Mat}_n R$ be the anti-isomorphism that assigns to each map $f$ its matrix relative to the given basis. Verify that the map $\psi : \operatorname{Mat}_n R \to \operatorname{Mat}_n R^{\mathrm{op}}$ given by $\psi(A) = A^t$ is an anti-isomorphism of rings. Then the composite $\psi\phi : \operatorname{Hom}_R(E,E) \to \operatorname{Mat}_n R^{\mathrm{op}}$ is an isomorphism of rings. The last statement of the theorem is a consequence of Theorem IV.2.4 and Exercise III.1.17.
