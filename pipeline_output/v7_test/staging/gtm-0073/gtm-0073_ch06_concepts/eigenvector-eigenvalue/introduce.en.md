An eigenvector of a linear transformation $\phi$ is a nonzero vector that is merely scaled by $\phi$, with the scaling factor being the corresponding eigenvalue. The eigenvalues are precisely the roots of the characteristic polynomial in the base field $K$. The spectrum is the set of all eigenvalues. These concepts are fundamental for understanding the action of a linear transformation and for determining when it can be diagonalized.
