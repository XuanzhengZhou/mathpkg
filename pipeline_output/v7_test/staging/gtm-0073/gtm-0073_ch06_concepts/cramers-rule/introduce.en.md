When the coefficient matrix $A$ of a square linear system has nonzero determinant, Cramer's Rule gives an explicit formula for the unique solution: each variable $x_j$ equals the determinant of the matrix obtained by replacing the $j$th column of $A$ with the constant vector, divided by $|A|$. This follows directly from the adjugate formula for the inverse.
