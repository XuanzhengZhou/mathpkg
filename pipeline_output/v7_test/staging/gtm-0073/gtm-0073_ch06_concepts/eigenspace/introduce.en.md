For a given eigenvalue $k$, the set of all vectors that $\phi$ scales by $k$ (together with the zero vector) forms a subspace called the eigenspace $C(\phi, k)$. Its dimension is the geometric multiplicity of the eigenvalue. The eigenspace is always nonzero by definition of an eigenvalue and is the kernel of $\phi - k \cdot 1_E$.
