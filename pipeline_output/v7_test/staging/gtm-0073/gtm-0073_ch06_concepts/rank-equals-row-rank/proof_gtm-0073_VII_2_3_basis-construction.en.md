Let $A$ be the $n \times m$ [resp. $m \times n$] matrix of $f$ relative to ordered bases $U = \{u_1, \ldots, u_n\}$ of $E$ and $V = \{v_1, \ldots, v_m\}$ of $F$. The rank of $f$ is the dimension of $\operatorname{Im} f$, which is spanned by $\{f(u_1), \ldots, f(u_n)\}$. For each $i$, $f(u_i) = \sum_j r_{ij} v_j$ (or the right-module analogue). Thus the coordinates of $f(u_i)$ relative to $V$ form the $i$th row of $A$. The map sending each $f(u_i)$ to its coordinate vector is an isomorphism from $\operatorname{Im} f$ to the row space of $A$, so their dimensions are equal.
