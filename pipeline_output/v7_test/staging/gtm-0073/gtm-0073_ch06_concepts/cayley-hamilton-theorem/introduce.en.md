The Cayley-Hamilton Theorem states that every linear transformation (or square matrix) satisfies its own characteristic polynomial: $p_\phi(\phi) = 0$. More fundamentally, the characteristic polynomial factors as the product of the invariant factors. Consequently, the minimal polynomial divides the characteristic polynomial, and they share the same irreducible factors. This theorem establishes the deep relationship between the characteristic polynomial, minimal polynomial, and invariant factors.
