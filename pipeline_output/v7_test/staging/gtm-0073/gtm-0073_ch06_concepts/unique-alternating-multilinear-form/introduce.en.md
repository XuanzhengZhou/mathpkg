There is exactly one alternating $n$-linear form on $n$ copies of $R^n$ that takes a prescribed value $r$ on the standard basis vectors (in order). This theorem is the theoretical foundation for the determinant: when $r = 1_R$, the unique form is the determinant function. The result justifies defining the determinant by its multilinearity and alternating properties.
