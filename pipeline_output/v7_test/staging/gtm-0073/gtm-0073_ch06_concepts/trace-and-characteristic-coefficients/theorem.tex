Let $K$ be a commutative ring with identity. The \textbf{trace} of an $n \times n$ matrix $A = (a_{ij})$ over $K$ is
\[
\operatorname{Tr} A = a_{11} + a_{22} + \cdots + a_{nn} \in K.
\]
The \textbf{trace} of an endomorphism $\phi$ of a free $K$-module of rank $n$ is $\operatorname{Tr} \phi = \operatorname{Tr} A$, where $A$ is the matrix of $\phi$ relative to some ordered basis. Since $p_\phi = p_A$ is independent of basis, so is $\operatorname{Tr} \phi$.

For any $A, B \in \operatorname{Mat}_n K$ and $k \in K$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\operatorname{Tr}(A + B) = \operatorname{Tr} A + \operatorname{Tr} B$;
\item $\operatorname{Tr}(kA) = k \operatorname{Tr} A$;
\item $\operatorname{Tr}(AB) = \operatorname{Tr}(BA)$.
\end{enumerate}
Similar matrices have the same trace.
