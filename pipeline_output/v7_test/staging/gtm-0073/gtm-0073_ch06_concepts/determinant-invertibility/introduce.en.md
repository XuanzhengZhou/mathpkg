Over a commutative ring, invertibility of a square matrix is equivalent to its determinant being a unit (not necessarily nonzero, since the ring may have non-unit nonzero elements). The inverse is given explicitly by the classical adjoint formula $A^{-1} = |A|^{-1} \operatorname{adj}(A)$, where the adjugate $\operatorname{adj}(A)$ is the transpose of the cofactor matrix.
