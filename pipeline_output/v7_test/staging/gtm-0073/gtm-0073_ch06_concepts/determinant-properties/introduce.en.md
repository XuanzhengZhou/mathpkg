The determinant satisfies the key algebraic properties: it is multiplicative ($|AB| = |A||B|$), invariant under similarity, and invariant under transposition. A matrix is invertible if and only if its determinant is a unit. For triangular matrices, the determinant is simply the product of the diagonal entries. Every alternating multilinear form is a scalar multiple of the determinant.
