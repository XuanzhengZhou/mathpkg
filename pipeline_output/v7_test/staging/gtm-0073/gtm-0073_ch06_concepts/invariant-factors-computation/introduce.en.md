The invariant factors of a matrix $A$ can be computed by finding the Smith normal form of the characteristic matrix $xI_n - A$ over the Euclidean domain $K[x]$. Using elementary row and column operations on $xI_n - A$, one obtains a diagonal matrix whose nonzero diagonal entries (the invariant factors) satisfy the divisibility chain. The non-constant entries are precisely the invariant factors of $A$. This provides a constructive algorithm for computing rational and Jordan canonical forms.
