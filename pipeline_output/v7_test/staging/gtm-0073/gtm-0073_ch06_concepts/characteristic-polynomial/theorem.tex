If $A$ is an $n \times n$ matrix over a commutative ring $K$ with identity, then $xI_n - A$ is an $n \times n$ matrix over $K[x]$, and the \textbf{characteristic polynomial} of $A$ is
\[
p_A = |xI_n - A| \in K[x].
\]
$p_A$ is a monic polynomial of degree $n$. Similar matrices have the same characteristic polynomial: if $B = PAP^{-1}$, then $p_B = p_A$.

If $\phi : E \to E$ is an endomorphism of a free $K$-module $E$ of finite rank $n$, the \textbf{characteristic polynomial} $p_\phi$ is $p_A$ where $A$ is the matrix of $\phi$ relative to some ordered basis. This is independent of the choice of basis.
