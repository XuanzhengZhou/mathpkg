The dual homomorphism $\bar{f}$ induced by $f$ on the dual modules has the same matrix as $f$ when expressed relative to the dual bases (using the right-module convention). When $R$ is commutative and duals are treated as left modules, the matrix of $\bar{f}$ is the transpose $A^t$. This connects duality theory with matrix representations.
