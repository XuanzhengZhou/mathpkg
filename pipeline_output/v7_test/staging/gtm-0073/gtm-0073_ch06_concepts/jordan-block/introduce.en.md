When the minimal polynomial of a cyclic transformation is a pure power of a linear factor $(x - b)^r$, the companion matrix can be transformed into the elementary Jordan matrix (Jordan block): a matrix with $b$ on the diagonal, $1$ on the superdiagonal, and zeros elsewhere. This is obtained by shifting $\phi = \psi - b \cdot 1_E$, applying Theorem 4.3 to $\phi$ (with minimal polynomial $x^r$), and translating back. Jordan blocks are the building blocks of the Jordan canonical form.
