A linear transformation is diagonalizable (can be represented by a diagonal matrix in some basis) precisely when its eigenvectors span the entire space, which is equivalent to the existence of a basis consisting entirely of eigenvectors. In such a basis, the diagonal entries are the eigenvalues, each repeated according to the dimension of its eigenspace (geometric multiplicity). This theorem connects the algebraic notion of eigenvectors to the matrix property of diagonalizability.
