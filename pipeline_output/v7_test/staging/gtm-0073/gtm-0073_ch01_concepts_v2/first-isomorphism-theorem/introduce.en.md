The First Isomorphism Theorem states that every group homomorphism induces an isomorphism between the quotient of the domain by the kernel and the image. This is one of the most fundamental results in group theory, establishing that every homomorphic image of a group is isomorphic to a quotient of that group. The theorem follows directly from the universal property of quotient groups (Theorem 5.6) by taking the normal subgroup to be the kernel of the homomorphism.
