If $N$ is a subgroup of a group $G$, then the following conditions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] Left and right congruence modulo $N$ coincide (that is, $a \equiv_r b \pmod{N}$ if and only if $a \equiv_l b \pmod{N}$);
\item[(ii)] the left coset $aN$ of $N$ in $G$ equals the right coset $Na$ of $N$ in $G$ for every $a \in G$;
\item[(iii)] $aNa^{-1} \subseteq N$ for all $a \in G$;
\item[(iv)] $aNa^{-1} = N$ for all $a \in G$.
\end{enumerate}
A subgroup satisfying these equivalent conditions is called a \textbf{normal subgroup} of $G$, denoted $N \trianglelefteq G$.
