A normal subgroup is a subgroup for which left and right cosets coincide. Theorem 5.1 gives four equivalent characterizations: equality of left and right congruence, equality of left and right cosets, invariance under conjugation by any group element, and conjugation mapping the subgroup onto itself. These equivalences make normality the central concept for constructing quotient groups and understanding homomorphisms. Every subgroup of an abelian group is normal, and kernels of homomorphisms are always normal.
