The Correspondence Theorem (also called the Fourth Isomorphism Theorem or Lattice Theorem) establishes a bijection between the subgroups of a quotient group and the subgroups of the original group that contain the kernel. This correspondence preserves normality: a subgroup in the quotient is normal if and only if its preimage is normal in the original group. The theorem is a powerful structural tool for understanding the subgroup lattice of a group through its homomorphic images and quotients.
