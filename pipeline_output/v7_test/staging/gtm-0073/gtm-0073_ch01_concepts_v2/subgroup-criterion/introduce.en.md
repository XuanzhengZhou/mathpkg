The subgroup criterion provides a concise necessary and sufficient condition for a nonempty subset of a group to be a subgroup: the subset must be closed under the operation of taking $ab^{-1}$. This single condition simultaneously encodes closure under the group operation, containment of the identity element, and closure under inverses. The criterion is extremely practical for verifying that a given subset forms a subgroup without checking all group axioms individually.
