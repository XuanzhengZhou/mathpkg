Cyclic groups are completely classified up to isomorphism: every infinite cyclic group is isomorphic to the integers under addition, and every finite cyclic group of order $m$ is isomorphic to the integers modulo $m$ under addition. This classification theorem shows that the structure of cyclic groups is remarkably simple; there are essentially only two prototypes, $\mathbb{Z}$ and $\mathbb{Z}_m$, from which all cyclic groups can be understood. The proof constructs an epimorphism from $\mathbb{Z}$ onto any cyclic group and analyzes its kernel using Theorem 3.1.
