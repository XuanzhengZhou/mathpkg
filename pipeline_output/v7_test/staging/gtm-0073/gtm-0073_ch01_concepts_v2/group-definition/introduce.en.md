The concept of a group is of fundamental importance in algebra. A group is defined as a nonempty set equipped with an associative binary operation, a two-sided identity element, and two-sided inverses for every element. This definition generalizes familiar structures like the integers under addition and nonzero real numbers under multiplication. The intermediate notions of semigroup (associative only) and monoid (associative with identity) are convenient for stating certain theorems in the greatest generality. A group whose operation is commutative is called abelian.
