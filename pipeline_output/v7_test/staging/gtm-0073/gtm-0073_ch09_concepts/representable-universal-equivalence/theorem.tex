Let $\mathcal{C}$ be a category, $T : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{S}$ a covariant functor, and $A$ an object of $\mathcal{C}$. Then the class $X$ of all representations $(A, \alpha)$ of $T$ is in bijection with the class $Y$ of all universal elements $(A, u)$ of the category $\mathcal{C}_T$ (the category of elements of $T$). The bijection $\phi : X \to Y$ is given by $(A, \alpha) \mapsto (A, \alpha_A(1_A))$ and its inverse $\psi : Y \to X$ is given by $(A, u) \mapsto (A, \beta)$, where $\beta$ is the natural transformation of Lemma 1.5(ii).
