This theorem establishes the equivalence between two fundamental concepts: representability of a set-valued functor $T$ and the existence of a universal element in the category of elements $\mathcal{C}_T$. A representation $(A, \alpha)$ of $T$ corresponds to the element $\alpha_A(1_A) \in T(A)$, and conversely, a universal element $(A, u)$ determines a representation via the map $g \mapsto T(g)(u)$.
