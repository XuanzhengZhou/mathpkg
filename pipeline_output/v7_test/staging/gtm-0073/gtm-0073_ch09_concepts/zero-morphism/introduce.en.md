In a category with a zero object, there is a canonical family of zero morphisms $0_{C,D} : C \to D$, each defined as the composite of the unique morphisms $C \to 0 \to D$. Zero morphisms behave like trivial homomorphisms: pre- and post-composition with any morphism yields another zero morphism. They are essential for defining kernels and cokernels in arbitrary categories.
