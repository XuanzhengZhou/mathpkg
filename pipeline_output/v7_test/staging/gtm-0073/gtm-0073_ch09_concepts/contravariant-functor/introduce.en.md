A contravariant functor reverses the direction of morphisms: it sends a morphism $f : C \to C'$ to a morphism $S(f) : S(C') \to S(C)$. Composition is also reversed: $S(g \circ f) = S(f) \circ S(g)$. Every contravariant functor on $\mathcal{C}$ corresponds to a covariant functor on the opposite category $\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{op}}$, which allows many results to be dualized.
