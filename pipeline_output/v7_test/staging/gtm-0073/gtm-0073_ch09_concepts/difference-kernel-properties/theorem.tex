Let $f, g : C \to D$ be morphisms of a category $\mathcal{C}$.
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] Any difference kernel of $(f, g)$ is a monomorphism.
\item[(ii)] If $i : B \to C$ and $j : A \to C$ are both difference kernels of $(f, g)$, then $B$ and $A$ are equivalent (isomorphic) via a unique isomorphism compatible with $i$ and $j$.
\end{enumerate}
Dually, any difference cokernel is an epimorphism, and any two difference cokernels of the same pair have equivalent (isomorphic) codomains.
