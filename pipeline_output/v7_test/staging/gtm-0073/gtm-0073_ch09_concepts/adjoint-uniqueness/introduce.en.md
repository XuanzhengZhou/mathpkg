Left adjoints are unique up to natural isomorphism. This follows from the Yoneda Lemma: if $S_1$ and $S_2$ are both left adjoints to $T$, then for each $C$, both $S_1(C)$ and $S_2(C)$ represent the same functor $\hom_{\mathcal{C}}(C, T(-))$, so they are naturally isomorphic. The natural isomorphisms assemble into a natural isomorphism between the functors $S_1$ and $S_2$.
