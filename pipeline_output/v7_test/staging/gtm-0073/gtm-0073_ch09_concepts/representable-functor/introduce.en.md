A set-valued functor is representable if it is naturally isomorphic to a hom functor. For covariant functors, this means $T(-) \cong \hom_{\mathcal{C}}(A, -)$ for some object $A$; for contravariant functors, $S(-) \cong \hom_{\mathcal{C}}(-, B)$. Representability captures the idea that the functor is completely determined by morphisms to or from a single "representing object."
