A zero object is one that is simultaneously initial (unique morphism from it to any object) and terminal (unique morphism to it from any object). Examples include the trivial group in the category of groups, the zero module in the category of $R$-modules, and the zero ring (with $1 = 0$) in the category of rings. Zero objects are unique up to equivalence.
