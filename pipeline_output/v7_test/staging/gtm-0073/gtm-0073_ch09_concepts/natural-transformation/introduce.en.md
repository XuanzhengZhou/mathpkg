A natural transformation provides a way of "mapping" one functor to another while respecting the categorical structure. For each object $C$, it gives a morphism $\alpha_C : S(C) \to T(C)$, and these morphisms must be compatible with the action of the functors on morphisms (the naturality square commutes). When each $\alpha_C$ is an isomorphism, the transformation is a natural isomorphism, indicating that the two functors are essentially the same.
