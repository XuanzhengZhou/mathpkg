The bijection $\psi_A : \operatorname{Nat}(h_A, T) \to T(A)$ of Corollary 1.7 is natural in both $A$ and $T$. Specifically, for any morphism $f : A \to B$ of $\mathcal{C}$ and any natural transformation $\sigma : T \to S$ of functors $\mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{S}$, the following diagrams commute:
\[
\begin{CD}
\operatorname{Nat}(h_A, T) @>{\psi_A}>> T(A) \\
@V{N^*(f)}VV @VV{T(f)}V \\
\operatorname{Nat}(h_B, T) @>{\psi_B}>> T(B)
\end{CD}
\qquad
\begin{CD}
\operatorname{Nat}(h_A, T) @>{\psi_A}>> T(A) \\
@V{N^*(\sigma)}VV @VV{\sigma_A}V \\
\operatorname{Nat}(h_A, S) @>{\psi_A}>> S(A)
\end{CD}
\]
where $N^*(f)(\beta)_C(g) = \beta_C(g \circ f)$ for $g \in h_B(C) = \hom_{\mathcal{C}}(B, C)$, and $N^*(\sigma)(\beta) = \sigma \beta$.
