This theorem characterizes when a functor $T : \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{C}$ admits a left adjoint: precisely when the composite functor $\hom_{\mathcal{C}}(C, T(-)) : \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{S}$ is representable for every object $C$ of $\mathcal{C}$. Applying Theorem 1.9, the representing objects assemble into a functor $S : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}$ which is the left adjoint of $T$.
