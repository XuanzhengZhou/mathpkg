A monomorphism is a morphism that is left-cancellable; an epimorphism is right-cancellable. These generalize the notions of injective and surjective homomorphisms from concrete categories to arbitrary categories. While monics coincide with injective maps in categories of sets, groups, and modules, and epics with surjective maps in sets, groups, and modules, this is not true in all categories (e.g., the inclusion $\mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Q}$ is epic in the category of rings but not surjective).
