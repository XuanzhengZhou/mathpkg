In a category with zero morphisms, the kernel of $f$ is the equalizer of $f$ and the zero morphism $0_{C,D}$ -- it is the universal morphism whose composition with $f$ yields zero. Dually, the cokernel is the coequalizer of $f$ and the zero morphism. These generalize the familiar notions of kernel and cokernel from abelian categories: the kernel is the "largest subobject" annihilated by $f$, and the cokernel is the "largest quotient" annihilating $f$.
