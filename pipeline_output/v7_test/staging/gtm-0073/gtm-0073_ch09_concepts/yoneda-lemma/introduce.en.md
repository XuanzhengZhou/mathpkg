The Yoneda Lemma (in the form of Corollary 1.7) states that natural transformations from a hom functor $h_A$ to a set-valued functor $T$ are in natural bijection with elements of $T(A)$ via the evaluation $\alpha \mapsto \alpha_A(1_A)$. This is one of the most fundamental results in category theory, showing that an object $A$ is completely determined (up to isomorphism) by the functor $\hom(A, -)$ it represents.
