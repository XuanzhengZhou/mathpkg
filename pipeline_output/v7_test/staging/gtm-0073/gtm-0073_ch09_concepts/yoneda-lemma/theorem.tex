Let $\mathcal{C}$ be a category, $T : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{S}$ a covariant functor, and $A$ an object of $\mathcal{C}$. Then there is a bijection
\[
\psi_A : \operatorname{Nat}(h_A, T) \xrightarrow{\cong} T(A),
\]
where $\operatorname{Nat}(h_A, T)$ is the class of all natural transformations from the covariant hom functor $h_A = \hom_{\mathcal{C}}(A, -)$ to $T$. The map $\psi_A$ is given by $\alpha \mapsto \alpha_A(1_A)$. Furthermore, this bijection is natural in both $A$ and $T$ (as described in Corollary 1.8).
