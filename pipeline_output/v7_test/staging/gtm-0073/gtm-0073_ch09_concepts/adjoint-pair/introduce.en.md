An adjoint pair $(S, T)$ consists of functors $S : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}$ (left adjoint) and $T : \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{C}$ (right adjoint) together with a natural isomorphism $\hom_{\mathcal{D}}(S(C), D) \cong \hom_{\mathcal{C}}(C, T(D))$. This fundamental concept generalizes many constructions in algebra, such as the tensor-hom adjunction, free-forgetful adjunctions, and the relationship between universal properties and representing objects.
