Let $\mathcal{C}$ and $\mathcal{D}$ be categories and $S : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}$ and $T : \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{C}$ covariant functors. $S$ is said to be a left adjoint of $T$ (or $T$ a right adjoint of $S$, or $(S,T)$ an adjoint pair) if there is a natural isomorphism of bifunctors
\[
\hom_{\mathcal{D}}(S(-), -) \cong \hom_{\mathcal{C}}(-, T(-)).
\]
Thus for each object $C$ of $\mathcal{C}$ and $D$ of $\mathcal{D}$, there is a bijection
\[
\alpha_{C,D} : \hom_{\mathcal{D}}(S(C), D) \xrightarrow{\cong} \hom_{\mathcal{C}}(C, T(D))
\]
which is natural in both $C$ and $D$.
