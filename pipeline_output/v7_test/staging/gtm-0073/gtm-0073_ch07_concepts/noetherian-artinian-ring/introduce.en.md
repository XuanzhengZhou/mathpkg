When a ring is considered as a module over itself, its submodules are precisely its left (or right) ideals. Consequently, the chain conditions specialize to conditions on ideals: a ring is left Noetherian if its left ideals satisfy ACC, and left Artinian if they satisfy DCC. A ring that is both left and right Noetherian (resp. Artinian) is simply called Noetherian (resp. Artinian).
