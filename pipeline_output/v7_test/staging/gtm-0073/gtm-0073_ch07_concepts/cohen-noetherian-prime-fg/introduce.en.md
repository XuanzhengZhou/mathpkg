Cohen's theorem provides a remarkable simplification for checking the Noetherian property: it suffices to verify that every prime ideal is finitely generated, rather than checking all ideals. The proof uses Proposition 2.4 (a maximal ideal among non-finitely-generated ideals is prime) together with Zorn's Lemma: if the ring were not Noetherian, the set of non-finitely-generated ideals would have a maximal element, which must be prime by Proposition 2.4, contradicting the hypothesis.
