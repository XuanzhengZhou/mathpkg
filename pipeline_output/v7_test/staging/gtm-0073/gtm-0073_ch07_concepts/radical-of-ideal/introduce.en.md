The radical \(\operatorname{Rad} I\) of an ideal \(I\) consists of all ring elements some power of which lies in \(I\). It is itself an ideal (and contains \(I\)). Key properties: the radical operation is idempotent; it commutes with finite intersections and products; and the radical of a power equals the radical of the original ideal. An ideal \(I\) with \(I = \operatorname{Rad} I\) is called a radical ideal.
