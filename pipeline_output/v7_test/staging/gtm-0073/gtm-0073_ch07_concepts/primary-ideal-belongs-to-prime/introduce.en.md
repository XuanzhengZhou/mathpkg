An ideal \(Q\) is said to be primary for the prime ideal \(P\) when \(P\) is precisely the radical of \(Q\). Theorem 2.10 gives an equivalent characterization: \(Q \subset P \subset \operatorname{Rad} Q\) and the defining property that \(ab \in Q, a \notin Q \implies b \in P\). This refines the definition of a primary ideal by explicitly identifying the associated prime ideal.
