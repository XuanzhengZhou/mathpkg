If \(s, t \in \hat{R}\), then \(s, t \in R[s,t]\), whence \(t-s \in R[s,t]\) and \(ts \in R[s,t]\). Since \(s\) and \(t\) are integral over \(R\), \(R[s]\) and \(R[t]\) are finitely generated \(R\)-modules. Then \(R[s,t] = R[s][t]\) is a finitely generated \(R[s]\)-module and \(R[s]\) is a finitely generated \(R\)-module, so \(R[s,t]\) is a finitely generated \(R\)-module. Therefore \(t-s\) and \(ts\) are integral over \(R\) by Theorem 5.3(iii). Hence \(\hat{R}\) is a subring. By construction, \(\hat{R}\) is integral over \(R\) and contains every subring of \(S\) integral over \(R\).
