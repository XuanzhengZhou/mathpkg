Let $S$ be an extension ring of $R$ and let $\hat{R}$ be the set of all elements of $S$ that are integral over $R$. Then $\hat{R}$ is a subring of $S$ containing $R$, called the \textbf{integral closure} of $R$ in $S$. $\hat{R}$ is an integral extension ring of $R$ which contains every subring of $S$ that is integral over $R$.

$R$ is said to be \textbf{integrally closed} in $S$ if $\hat{R} = R$. An integral domain $R$ is \textbf{integrally closed} if it is integrally closed in its quotient field.
