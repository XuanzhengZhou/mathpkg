Since \(P\) is prime, \(T = R \setminus P\) is a multiplicative set in \(R\), hence also in \(S\). The ideal \(PS\) of \(S\) is disjoint from \(T\) (otherwise some \(p \in P\) would equal some \(t \in T\) in \(S\), forcing \(t \in P \cap T = \emptyset\)). By Theorem 2.2, there exists a prime ideal \(Q\) of \(S\) maximal among ideals containing \(PS\) and disjoint from \(T\). Then \(Q \cap R\) is a prime ideal of \(R\) containing \(P\) and disjoint from \(T = R \setminus P\), hence \(Q \cap R = P\).
