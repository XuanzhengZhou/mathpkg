The Lying Over Theorem guarantees that in an integral extension \(S\) of \(R\), every prime ideal \(P\) of \(R\) has at least one prime ideal \(Q\) of \(S\) lying over it (i.e., \(Q \cap R = P\)). The proof uses Theorem 2.2: \(R \setminus P\) is a multiplicative set in both \(R\) and \(S\); one takes an ideal of \(S\) maximal with respect to being disjoint from \(R \setminus P\) and containing \(PS\), then shows this ideal is prime and its contraction to \(R\) is exactly \(P\).
