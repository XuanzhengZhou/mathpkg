Integral dependence is transitive: if \(T\) is integral over \(S\) and \(S\) is integral over \(R\), then \(T\) is integral over \(R\). The proof is a nice application of the finite generation criterion: given \(t \in T\), its monic polynomial over \(S\) has coefficients \(s_i\) that are integral over \(R\). The ring \(R[s_0, \ldots, s_{n-1}]\) is finitely generated over \(R\), and \(R[s_0, \ldots, s_{n-1}, t]\) is finitely generated over it, hence finitely generated over \(R\). By Theorem 5.3, \(t\) is integral over \(R\).
