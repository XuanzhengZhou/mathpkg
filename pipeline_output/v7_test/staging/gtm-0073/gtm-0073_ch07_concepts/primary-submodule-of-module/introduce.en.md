A primary submodule of a module is the natural generalization of a primary ideal to the module setting. A proper submodule \(A\) of an \(R\)-module \(B\) is primary if whenever a ring element \(r\) sends some element outside \(A\) into \(A\), then some power of \(r\) annihilates the quotient module \(B/A\). The associated ideal \(Q_A\) is primary in \(R\), and its radical is a prime ideal \(P\) to which \(A\) belongs.
