The Going-Up Theorem extends the Lying Over theorem to chains: given a chain \(P_1 \subset P\) of prime ideals in \(R\) and a prime \(Q_1\) of \(S\) lying over \(P_1\), one can find a prime \(Q\) of \(S\) containing \(Q_1\) that lies over \(P\). The proof applies the Lying Over theorem to the integral extension \(S/Q_1\) of \(R/P_1\). Together with the Incomparability Theorem, Going-Up shows that the map \(\operatorname{Spec} S \to \operatorname{Spec} R\) is surjective and order-preserving in a controlled way.
