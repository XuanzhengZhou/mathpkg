The Noether Normalization Lemma states that any finitely generated algebra over a field can be expressed as a finitely generated (in fact, integral) extension of a polynomial ring. Geometrically, this means every affine variety admits a finite surjective map to affine space of the appropriate dimension. The proof is constructive: starting from a set of generators, if they are algebraically dependent, one makes a change of variables to reduce the degree, eventually obtaining algebraically independent elements over which the ring is integral.
