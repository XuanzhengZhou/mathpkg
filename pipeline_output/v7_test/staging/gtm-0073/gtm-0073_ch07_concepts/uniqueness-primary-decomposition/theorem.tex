Let $R$ be a commutative ring with identity and $B$ an $R$-module. If a submodule $C$ of $B$ ($C \neq B$) has two reduced primary decompositions
\[
C = A_1 \cap A_2 \cap \cdots \cap A_k = A_1' \cap A_2' \cap \cdots \cap A_s',
\]
with $A_i$ being $P_i$-primary and $A_j'$ being $P_j'$-primary, then $k = s$ and (after reindexing) $P_i = P_i'$ for all $i$.

Moreover, the isolated prime ideals are uniquely determined by $C$.
