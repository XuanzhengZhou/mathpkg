The intersection of finitely many primary ideals, all belonging to the same prime ideal \(P\), is again a \(P\)-primary ideal. The proof uses the fact that the radical of an intersection is the intersection of the radicals (Theorem 2.7(ii)), so \(\operatorname{Rad}(\bigcap Q_i) = \bigcap \operatorname{Rad} Q_i = \bigcap P = P\). One then verifies the primary property directly for the intersection.
