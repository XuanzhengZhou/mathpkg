To prove \(\operatorname{Rad} Q\) is prime: suppose \(ab \in \operatorname{Rad} Q\) but \(a \notin \operatorname{Rad} Q\). Then \((ab)^n = a^n b^n \in Q\) for some \(n > 0\). Since \(a \notin \operatorname{Rad} Q\), \(a^n \notin Q\) for all \(n\). Because \(a^n b^n \in Q\) with \(a^n \notin Q\), the primary property of \(Q\) implies there exists \(m > 0\) such that \((b^n)^m = b^{nm} \in Q\). Hence \(b \in \operatorname{Rad} Q\), so \(\operatorname{Rad} Q\) is prime.
