A Dedekind domain is an integral domain in which every nonzero proper ideal factors uniquely as a product of finitely many prime ideals. This generalizes the fundamental theorem of arithmetic in \(\mathbb{Z}\) to a much wider class of rings. Every principal ideal domain (PID) is Dedekind, but the converse fails — there exist Dedekind domains that are not PIDs, such as \(\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-5}]\) and the ring of integers of any algebraic number field whose ideal class group is nontrivial.
