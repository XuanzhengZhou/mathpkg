This is a definition; no proof is required. The fact that every PID is Dedekind follows from Lemma III.3.6 (every PID is Noetherian) and the unique factorization property of PIDs. Counterexamples to the converse exist: for instance \(\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-5}]\) is Dedekind but not a PID.
