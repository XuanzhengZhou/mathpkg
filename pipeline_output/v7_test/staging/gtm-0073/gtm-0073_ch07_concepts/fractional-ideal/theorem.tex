Let $R$ be an integral domain with quotient field $K$. A \textbf{fractional ideal} of $R$ is a nonzero $R$-submodule $I$ of $K$ such that $aI \subset R$ for some nonzero $a \in R$.

A fractional ideal $I$ is \textbf{invertible} if there exists a fractional ideal $J$ such that $IJ = R$. The inverse is unique and given by $I^{-1} = \{a \in K \mid aI \subset R\}$.
