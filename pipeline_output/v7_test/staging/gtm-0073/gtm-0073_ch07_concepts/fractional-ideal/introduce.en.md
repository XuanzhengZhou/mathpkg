A fractional ideal is an \(R\)-submodule of the quotient field \(K\) that, when multiplied by a suitable nonzero element of \(R\), becomes an ordinary ideal of \(R\). Ordinary ideals are precisely the fractional ideals contained in \(R\). Every nonzero finitely generated \(R\)-submodule of \(K\) is a fractional ideal. An invertible fractional ideal is one that has a multiplicative inverse among fractional ideals; its inverse is \(I^{-1} = \{a \in K \mid aI \subset R\}\). Every nonzero principal ideal is invertible.
