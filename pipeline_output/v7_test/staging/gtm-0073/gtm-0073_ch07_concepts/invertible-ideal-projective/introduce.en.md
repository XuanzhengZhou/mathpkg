A fundamental connection between ideal theory and homological algebra: a fractional ideal is invertible exactly when it is projective as an \(R\)-module. The forward direction uses the invertibility to construct an explicit splitting, showing \(I\) is a direct summand of a free module. The converse uses a dual basis argument: projectivity provides a family of coordinate functionals, which are used to construct the inverse ideal.
