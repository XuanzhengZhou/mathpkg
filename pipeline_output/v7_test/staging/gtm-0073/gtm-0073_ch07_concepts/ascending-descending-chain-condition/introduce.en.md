A module is Noetherian if every ascending chain of submodules eventually stabilizes. Artinian is the dual notion: every descending chain must eventually stabilize. The \(\mathbb{Z}\)-module \(\mathbb{Z}\) is Noetherian but not Artinian, while \(\mathbb{Z}(p^\infty)\) is Artinian but not Noetherian. These finiteness conditions are fundamental to the study of ring and module structure.
