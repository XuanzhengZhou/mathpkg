Over a local ring, every finitely generated projective module is free. The proof chooses a free module \(F\) with a basis of minimal cardinality mapping onto \(P\), and shows that the kernel is contained in \(MF\) (where \(M\) is the unique maximal ideal). If not, an element of the kernel not in \(MF\) would allow one to eliminate a basis element, contradicting minimality. Then Nakayama's Lemma forces the kernel to be zero. Kaplansky later generalized this to arbitrary (not necessarily finitely generated) projective modules over local rings.
