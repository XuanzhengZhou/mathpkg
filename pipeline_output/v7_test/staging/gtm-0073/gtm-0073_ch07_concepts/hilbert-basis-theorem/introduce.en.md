The Hilbert Basis Theorem states that the polynomial ring in one variable over a Noetherian ring is again Noetherian. By induction, \(R[x_1, \ldots, x_n]\) is Noetherian whenever \(R\) is. The proof takes an ideal \(J\) of \(R[x]\) and constructs a finite generating set by considering the ideal \(I\) of leading coefficients of polynomials in \(J\), which is finitely generated since \(R\) is Noetherian. The generators lift to polynomials in \(J\) that, together with a Noetherian induction on degree, generate \(J\).
