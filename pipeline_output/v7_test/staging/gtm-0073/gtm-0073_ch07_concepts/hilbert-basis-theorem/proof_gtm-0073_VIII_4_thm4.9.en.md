Let \(J\) be an ideal of \(R[x]\). For each \(k \geq 0\) let \(I_k\) be the set of leading coefficients of polynomials in \(J\) of degree at most \(k\), together with 0. Each \(I_k\) is an ideal of \(R\) and \(I_0 \subset I_1 \subset \cdots\). Since \(R\) is Noetherian, there exists \(t\) such that \(I_k = I_t\) for all \(k \geq t\). Each \(I_k\) is finitely generated; choose generators and corresponding polynomials in \(J\). The set \(X\) of all such polynomials (for \(k = 0, \ldots, t\)) is finite. One shows by induction on degree that every \(g \in J\) lies in the ideal generated by \(X\): using the leading coefficient ideal, one subtracts an appropriate linear combination to reduce the degree.
