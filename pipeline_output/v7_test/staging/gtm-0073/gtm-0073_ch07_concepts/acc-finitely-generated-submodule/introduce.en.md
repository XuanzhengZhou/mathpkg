A module satisfies the ascending chain condition (ACC) precisely when every submodule is finitely generated. The forward direction uses the maximal condition: among all finitely generated submodules of a given submodule, pick a maximal one and show it equals the whole submodule. The converse direction shows that the union of an ascending chain is itself finitely generated, forcing the chain to stabilize. For commutative rings, this provides the standard characterization of Noetherian rings as those in which every ideal is finitely generated.
