(i) \(\Rightarrow\) (ii) If \(s^n + r_{n-1}s^{n-1} + \cdots + r_0 = 0\) with \(r_i \in R\), then \(R[s]\) is generated by \(\{1, s, \ldots, s^{n-1}\}\) as an \(R\)-module.

(ii) \(\Rightarrow\) (iii) Take \(T = R[s]\). The annihilator of \(R[s]\) in \(R[s]\) is zero since \(1 \in R[s]\).

(iii) \(\Rightarrow\) (i) Let \(T\) be generated by \(b_1, \ldots, b_n\) as an \(R\)-module. Write \(s b_i = \sum_j r_{ij} b_j\) with \(r_{ij} \in R\). Then \(\sum_j (r_{ij} - s\delta_{ij}) b_j = 0\). Let \(M = (r_{ij})\) and \(d = \det(M - sI_n)\). Then \(d b_i = 0\) for all \(i\) by Exercise VII.3.8. Since \(T\) is generated by the \(b_i\) and the annihilator of \(T\) in \(R[s]\) is zero, we must have \(d = 0\). If \(f(x) = |M - xI_n| \in R[x]\), then \(\pm f(s) = \pm d = 0\) and one of \(\pm f\) is monic. Hence \(s\) is integral over \(R\).
