Theorem 6.10 provides nine equivalent characterizations of Dedekind domains, unifying several perspectives. The most commonly used is (viii): a domain is Dedekind iff it is Noetherian, integrally closed, and every nonzero prime ideal is maximal (i.e., has Krull dimension 1). Another important characterization is (ix): a domain is Dedekind iff it is Noetherian and all its localizations at nonzero primes are discrete valuation rings. This localization property makes Dedekind domains the natural setting for algebraic number theory, where rings of integers of number fields are Dedekind.
