For an integral domain $R$ which is not a field, the following are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $R$ is a Dedekind domain;
  \item every proper ideal in $R$ is uniquely a product of a finite number of prime ideals;
  \item every nonzero ideal in $R$ is invertible;
  \item every fractional ideal of $R$ is invertible;
  \item the set of all fractional ideals of $R$ is a group under multiplication;
  \item every ideal in $R$ is projective;
  \item every fractional ideal of $R$ is projective;
  \item $R$ is Noetherian, integrally closed, and every nonzero prime ideal is maximal;
  \item $R$ is Noetherian and for every nonzero prime ideal $P$, the localization $R_P$ is a discrete valuation ring.
\end{enumerate}
