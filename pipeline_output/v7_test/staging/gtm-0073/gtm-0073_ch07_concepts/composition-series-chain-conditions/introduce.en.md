A nonzero module admits a composition series (a maximal finite chain of submodules with simple quotients) exactly when it is both Noetherian and Artinian — that is, it satisfies both the ascending and descending chain conditions. The forward direction uses the Schreier Refinement Theorem: if either chain condition fails, one can construct a normal series longer than the composition series, contradicting equivalence of refinements. The converse constructs a descending chain by repeatedly taking maximal proper submodules (using the maximum condition); the ACC guarantees termination after finitely many steps.
