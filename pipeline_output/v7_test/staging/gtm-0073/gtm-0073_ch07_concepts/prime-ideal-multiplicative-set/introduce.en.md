A proper ideal \(P\) in a commutative ring \(R\) is prime exactly when its complement \(R \setminus P\) is closed under multiplication. This restates the defining property of a prime ideal — that \(ab \in P\) implies \(a \in P\) or \(b \in P\) — in terms of the multiplicative structure of the complement. The set of all prime ideals of \(R\) is called the **spectrum** of \(R\).
