Every proper submodule of a Noetherian module over a Noetherian ring admits a primary decomposition (and hence a reduced one). This is the module-theoretic analogue of the fact that every ideal in a Noetherian ring has a primary decomposition. The proof uses the ascending chain condition to find a maximal counterexample, then exploits the module structure to construct submodules \(B_k\) and \(D\) whose proper containment of \(C\) forces them to have primary decompositions, producing a decomposition for \(C\) itself.
