This theorem describes how linear disjointness behaves in towers. If $E$ and $F$ are linearly disjoint over $K$, then $E$ is also linearly disjoint from any intermediate field $L \supset K$ contained in $F$, and the compositum $EL$ remains linearly disjoint from $F$ over the larger base field $L$. This is essential for the proof of Theorem 2.10 on separable extensions.
