Let $F$ be an extension field of $K$ and let $S$ be a transcendence base of $F$ over $K$.
Let $T \subseteq F$ be an algebraically independent set over $K$.
Then there exists a subset $S' \subseteq S$ such that $T \cup S'$ is a transcendence base of $F$ over $K$ and $T \cap S' = \varnothing$.

In particular, for a finite transcendence base $S = \{s_1, \ldots, s_n\}$:
if $t \in F$ is transcendental over $K$, then there exists an index $i$ such that $(S \setminus \{s_i\}) \cup \{t\}$ is also a transcendence base of $F$ over $K$.
More precisely, there exists $i$ such that $t$ is transcendental over $K(S \setminus \{s_i\})$ and $s_i$ is algebraic over $K((S \setminus \{s_i\}) \cup \{t\})$.
