This is the field-theoretic analogue of the Steinitz Exchange Lemma for vector spaces, with algebraic independence playing the role of linear independence. The key step in the proof (for the finite case) shows: given a transcendence base $\{s_1, \ldots, s_n\}$ and any other transcendence base $T$, some $t_1 \in T$ is transcendental over $K(s_2, \ldots, s_n)$, so $\{t_1, s_2, \ldots, s_n\}$ is algebraically independent. Then $s_1$ is algebraic over $K(t_1, s_2, \ldots, s_n)$, making $\{t_1, s_2, \ldots, s_n\}$ also a transcendence base. Iterating this exchange yields Theorem 1.8 (invariance of cardinality).
