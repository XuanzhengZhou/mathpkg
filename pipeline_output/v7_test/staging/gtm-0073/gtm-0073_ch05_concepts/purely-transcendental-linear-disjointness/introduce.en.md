This theorem shows that purely transcendental extensions are "orthogonal" to purely inseparable extensions $K^{1/p^n}$ in the sense of linear disjointness. The proof uses the monomial basis of the polynomial ring: the monomials over a transcendence base form a $K$-basis that remains linearly independent over $K^{1/p^n}$ because raising to $p^n$ merely multiplies exponents by $p^n$, preserving distinctness of monomials.
