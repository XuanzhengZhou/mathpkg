In characteristic $p > 0$, $K^{1/p^n}$ is the set of all $p^n$-th roots of elements of $K$ in a fixed algebraic closure. These form a field (since $(u \pm v)^{p^n} = u^{p^n} \pm v^{p^n}$ in characteristic $p$), and the union $K^{1/p^\infty}$ is the maximal purely inseparable extension of $K$ (it is the perfect closure). These fields play a central role in the general theory of separability via linear disjointness.
