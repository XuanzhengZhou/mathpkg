This theorem establishes that the cardinality of a transcendence base is an invariant of the field extension, analogous to the invariance of dimension for vector spaces. The proof uses an exchange property: if $S = \{s_1, \ldots, s_n\}$ is a finite transcendence base and $T$ is any transcendence base, then there exists $t_1 \in T$ such that $\{t_1, s_2, \ldots, s_n\}$ is also a transcendence base, and the process can be continued.
