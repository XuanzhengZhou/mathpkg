These are the fundamental properties of separable extensions. Part (i) states that separability is inherited by intermediate fields (subextensions). Part (ii) states that separability is transitive in towers. Part (iii) states that if $F/K$ is separable and $E/K$ is algebraic, then $F/E$ remains separable. Note that (iii) may fail if $E/K$ is not algebraic.
