This is the main theorem of the section, giving the general definition of a separable (not necessarily algebraic) field extension. In characteristic $0$, all extensions are separable by convention. In characteristic $p > 0$, separability is characterized by linear disjointness from $K^{1/p}$ (or equivalently from $K^{1/p^n}$ or $K^{1/p^\infty}$), or by the property that every finitely generated subextension is separably generated. For algebraic extensions, this agrees with the classical definition (Theorem 2.8).
