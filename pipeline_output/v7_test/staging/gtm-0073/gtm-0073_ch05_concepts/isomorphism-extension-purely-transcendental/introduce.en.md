This corollary generalizes Theorem 1.2 (which deals with a single field $K$) to the case of an arbitrary field isomorphism $\sigma \colon K_1 \cong K_2$. Given algebraically independent sets $S_1, S_2$ of equal cardinality, the isomorphism $\sigma$ lifts to the purely transcendental extensions $K_1(S_1)$ and $K_2(S_2)$. The proof proceeds by composing the $K_i$-isomorphisms $K_i(S_i) \cong K_i(\{x_j\})$ from Theorem 1.2 with the map induced by $\sigma$ on the rational function fields. This result is used in the proof of Theorem 1.12 to extend isomorphisms to algebraically closed fields with the same transcendence degree.
