The transcendence degree is a well-defined cardinal invariant of a field extension, independent of the choice of transcendence base. It measures the maximal number of algebraically independent elements. In the analogy between algebraic and linear independence, tr.d.$F/K$ is the analogue of the vector space dimension $[F:K]$. One always has tr.d.$F/K \leq [F:K]$, and tr.d.$F/K = 0$ if and only if $F$ is algebraic over $K$.
