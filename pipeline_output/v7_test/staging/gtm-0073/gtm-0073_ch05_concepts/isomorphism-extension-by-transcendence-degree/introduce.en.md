This theorem generalizes the fact that algebraic closures are unique up to isomorphism. It shows that two algebraically closed fields containing isomorphic subfields are isomorphic if and only if they have the same transcendence degree over those subfields. In particular, any two algebraically closed fields of the same characteristic and same uncountable cardinality are isomorphic.
