Linear disjointness is a key technical notion for extending separability to nonalgebraic extensions. Two extensions $E$ and $F$ of $K$ (both contained in some algebraically closed field $C$) are linearly disjoint if $K$-linear independence in $E$ is preserved when viewing elements as vectors over the larger field $F$. The definition is symmetric in $E$ and $F$ (Theorem 2.2). An equivalent formulation in terms of tensor products states that $E$ and $F$ are linearly disjoint over $K$ if and only if the canonical map $E \otimes_K F \to EF$ is injective.
