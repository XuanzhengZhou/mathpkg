Mac Lane's Criterion states that for finitely generated extensions, separability is equivalent to being separably generated. Moreover, a separating transcendence base can always be chosen from among the given generators. This provides a very concrete way to check separability for finitely generated extensions: find a subset of generators that forms a transcendence base and check that the remaining generators are separable algebraic over the field generated by that subset.
