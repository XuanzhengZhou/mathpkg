If $F$ is a free abelian group of rank $n$ and $G$ is a nonzero subgroup of $F$, then there exists a basis $\{x_1, \ldots, x_n\}$ of $F$, an integer $r$ $(1 \leq r \leq n)$, and positive integers $d_1, \ldots, d_r$ such that $d_1 \mid d_2 \mid \cdots \mid d_r$ and $G$ is isomorphic to the free abelian group with basis $\{d_1x_1, \ldots, d_rx_r\}$. In particular, $G$ is free of rank $r \leq n$.
