Let $p$ and $q$ be primes with $p > q$. Then there are (up to isomorphism) exactly two distinct groups of order $pq$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The cyclic group $\mathbb{Z}_{pq}$.
\item A non-abelian group $K$ generated by elements $c$ and $d$ such that
$$|c| = p, \quad |d| = q, \quad dc = c^s d,$$
where $s \not\equiv 1 \pmod{p}$ and $s^q \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$.
\end{enumerate}
The second case can occur only if $q \mid p-1$.
