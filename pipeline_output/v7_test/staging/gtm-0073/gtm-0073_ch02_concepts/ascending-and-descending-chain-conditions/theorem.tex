A group $G$ is said to satisfy the \emph{ascending chain condition} (ACC) on normal subgroups if for every chain $G_1 < G_2 < \cdots$ of normal subgroups of $G$ there is an integer $n$ such that $G_i = G_n$ for all $i \geq n$.

$G$ is said to satisfy the \emph{descending chain condition} (DCC) on normal subgroups if for every chain $G_1 > G_2 > \cdots$ of normal subgroups of $G$ there is an integer $n$ such that $G_i = G_n$ for all $i \geq n$.
