\begin{theorem}
If $\{G_i \mid i \in I\}$ is a family of groups, then the direct product $\prod_{i \in I} G_i$ is a product in the category of groups (with the canonical projections $\pi_k : \prod G_i \rightarrow G_k$ given by $\{a_i\}_{i \in I} \mapsto a_k$). Consequently the direct product is uniquely determined up to isomorphism.
\end{theorem}
