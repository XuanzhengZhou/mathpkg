In a cyclic group, the order of an element (the smallest positive exponent yielding the identity) determines the structure of the subgroup it generates. For finite cyclic groups, the order of $a^k$ is $m/\gcd(k,m)$, and distinct powers coincide precisely when their exponents differ by multiples of the order.
