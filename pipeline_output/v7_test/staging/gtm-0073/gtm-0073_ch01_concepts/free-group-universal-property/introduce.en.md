The free group satisfies a universal property: any function from the generating set $X$ to a group $G$ extends uniquely to a homomorphism from the free group to $G$. This means that to define a homomorphism from a free group, it suffices to specify the images of the generators. Free groups are thus the free objects in the category of groups.
