The subgroup generated by a set $X$ is the smallest subgroup containing $X$, and it consists of all finite products of elements of $X$ and their inverses. When the set consists of a single element $a$, the generated subgroup is the cyclic subgroup $\langle a \rangle = \{a^n : n \in \mathbb{Z}\}$, whose structure is fully described in Section 3.
