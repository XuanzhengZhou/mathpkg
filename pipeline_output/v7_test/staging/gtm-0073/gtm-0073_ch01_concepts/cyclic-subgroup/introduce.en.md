A cyclic group is one that can be generated by a single element. Cyclic groups are the simplest nontrivial groups and are completely classified up to isomorphism: every cyclic group is isomorphic either to $\mathbb{Z}$ (infinite case) or to $\mathbb{Z}_m$ (finite case of order $m$).
