The kernel of any homomorphism is always a normal subgroup, establishing that normal subgroups are precisely the kernels of homomorphisms. Conversely, given any normal subgroup $N$, the canonical projection $\pi: G \to G/N$ is a surjective homomorphism with kernel $N$. This tight correspondence is the foundation of the isomorphism theorems.
