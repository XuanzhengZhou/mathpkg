Any homomorphism $f: G \to H$ factors uniquely through the quotient $G/N$ whenever $N \subseteq \operatorname{Ker} f$. The induced map $\bar{f}$ has the same image as $f$ and its kernel is $(\operatorname{Ker} f)/N$. This universal property of the quotient is the key to all three isomorphism theorems.
