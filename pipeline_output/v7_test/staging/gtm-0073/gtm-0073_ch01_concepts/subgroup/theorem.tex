\begin{definition}
Let $G$ be a group and $H$ a nonempty subset that is closed under the binary operation of $G$. If $H$ is itself a group under the binary operation of $G$, then $H$ is a \textbf{subgroup} of $G$, denoted $H < G$. A subgroup $H$ such that $H \neq G$ and $H \neq \langle e \rangle$ is a \textbf{proper subgroup}.
\end{definition}
