The index is multiplicative in towers of subgroups. If $K$ is contained in $H$ which is contained in $G$, then the index of $K$ in $G$ equals the product of the index of $H$ in $G$ and the index of $K$ in $H$. This generalizes Lagrange's Theorem and is useful for computing indices step by step.
