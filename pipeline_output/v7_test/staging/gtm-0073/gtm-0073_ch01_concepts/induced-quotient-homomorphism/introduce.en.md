A homomorphism $f: G \to H$ that sends $N$ into $M$ descends to a homomorphism between the quotient groups $G/N$ and $H/M$. This result generalizes the First Isomorphism Theorem and encompasses the Second and Third Isomorphism Theorems as special cases. It provides a flexible tool for comparing quotients.
