The alternating group $A_n$ consists of all even permutations in $S_n$. It is a normal subgroup of index 2, hence of order $n!/2$. The sign homomorphism provides a short proof: $\operatorname{sgn}: S_n \to \{\pm 1\}$ has kernel $A_n$, so by the First Isomorphism Theorem $S_n/A_n \cong \{\pm 1\}$. $A_n$ is the unique index-2 subgroup of $S_n$.
