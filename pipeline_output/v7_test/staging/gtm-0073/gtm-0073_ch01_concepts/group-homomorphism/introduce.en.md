A group homomorphism is a structure-preserving map between groups: it respects the binary operation. Monomorphisms are injective, epimorphisms are surjective, and isomorphisms are bijective homomorphisms. Endomorphisms are homomorphisms from a group to itself, and automorphisms are isomorphisms from a group to itself. These concepts formalize the idea that groups can be "the same" up to relabeling.
