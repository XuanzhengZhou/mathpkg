A permutation is even (resp. odd) if it can be expressed as a product of an even (resp. odd) number of transpositions. The sign is a homomorphism from $S_n$ to $\{1, -1\}$. This classification is well-defined (Theorem 6.7) and gives rise to the alternating group $A_n$.
