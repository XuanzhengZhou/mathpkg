\begin{definition}
Let $X$ be a set and $Y$ a set of (reduced) words on $X$. A group $G$ is said to be the \textbf{group defined by the generators} $x \in X$ \textbf{and relations} $w = e$ ($w \in Y$) provided $G \cong F/N$, where $F$ is the free group on $X$ and $N$ the normal subgroup of $F$ generated by $Y$. One says that $(X \mid Y)$ is a \textbf{presentation} of $G$.
\end{definition}
