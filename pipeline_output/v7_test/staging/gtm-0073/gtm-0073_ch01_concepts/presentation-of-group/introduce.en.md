A group presentation $(X \mid Y)$ specifies generators $X$ and relations $Y$ (words set equal to the identity). The group presented is $F(X)/N$ where $N$ is the normal closure of $Y$ in the free group $F(X)$. This is the standard way to describe groups compactly: for instance, $D_n \cong \langle a,b \mid a^n = b^2 = e,\; bab^{-1} = a^{-1} \rangle$.
