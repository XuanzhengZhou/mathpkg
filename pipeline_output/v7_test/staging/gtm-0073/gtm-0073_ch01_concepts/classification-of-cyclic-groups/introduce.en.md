Cyclic groups are completely classified up to isomorphism: there is exactly one infinite cyclic group ($\mathbb{Z}$) and exactly one finite cyclic group of order $m$ for each positive integer $m$ ($\mathbb{Z}_m$). This means that to study a cyclic group, one can always work with the concrete models $\mathbb{Z}$ or $\mathbb{Z}_m$.
