Lagrange's Theorem is one of the most fundamental results in finite group theory: the order of a subgroup divides the order of the group. The index $[G:H]$ is the number of distinct cosets. The theorem follows from the fact that $G$ is partitioned into $[G:H]$ right cosets, each of the same cardinality as $H$.
