\begin{definition}
Let $\{A_i \mid i \in I\}$ be a family of objects in a category $\mathcal{C}$. A \textbf{product} for the family is an object $P$ of $\mathcal{C}$ together with a family of morphisms $\{\pi_i : P \rightarrow A_i \mid i \in I\}$ such that for every object $B$ and family of morphisms $\{\varphi_i : B \rightarrow A_i \mid i \in I\}$, there is a unique morphism $\varphi : B \rightarrow P$ such that $\pi_i \circ \varphi = \varphi_i$ for all $i \in I$.
\end{definition}
