Products in a category are unique up to equivalence (isomorphism). The proof is a classic diagram chase: the universal property yields maps $f: P \to Q$ and $g: Q \to P$, and uniqueness forces $g \circ f = 1_P$ and $f \circ g = 1_Q$. This pattern recurs throughout category theory.
