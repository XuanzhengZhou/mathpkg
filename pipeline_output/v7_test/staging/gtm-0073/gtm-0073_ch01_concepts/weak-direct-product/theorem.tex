\begin{definition}
The \textbf{(external) weak direct product} of a family of groups $\{G_i \mid i \in I\}$, denoted $\prod_{i \in I}^w G_i$, is the set of all $f \in \prod_{i \in I} G_i$ such that $f(i) = e_i$, the identity in $G_i$, for all but a finite number of $i \in I$. If all the groups $G_i$ are (additive) abelian, $\prod_{i \in I}^w G_i$ is usually called the \textbf{(external) direct sum} and is denoted $\sum_{i \in I} G_i$.
\end{definition}
