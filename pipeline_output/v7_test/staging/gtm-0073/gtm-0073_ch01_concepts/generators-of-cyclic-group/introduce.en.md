An infinite cyclic group has exactly two generators ($a$ and $a^{-1}$). A finite cyclic group of order $m$ has $\varphi(m)$ generators (Euler's totient function), namely those powers $a^k$ where $k$ is coprime to $m$. This completely characterizes which elements can generate the whole group.
