The alternating group $A_n$ is simple for all $n \geq 5$. This is a fundamental result in group theory: it provides an infinite family of nonabelian finite simple groups. The proof proceeds by case analysis on the cycle structure of a nontrivial element in a normal subgroup, eventually reducing to the presence of a 3-cycle, which forces the subgroup to be all of $A_n$.
