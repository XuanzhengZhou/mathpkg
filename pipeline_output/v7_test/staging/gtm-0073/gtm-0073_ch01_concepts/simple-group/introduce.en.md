A simple group has no normal subgroups other than the trivial subgroup and the whole group. Simple groups are the "atoms" of group theory: every finite group can be built from simple groups via composition series. The only simple abelian groups are $\mathbb{Z}_p$ for prime $p$. The classification of finite simple groups is one of the great achievements of 20th-century mathematics.
