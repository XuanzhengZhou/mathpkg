\begin{definition}
Let $f: G \rightarrow H$ be a homomorphism of groups. The \textbf{kernel} of $f$ (denoted $\operatorname{Ker} f$) is $\{a \in G \mid f(a) = e \in H\}$. If $A$ is a subset of $G$, then $f(A) = \{b \in H \mid b = f(a) \text{ for some } a \in A\}$ is the \textbf{image} of $A$. The \textbf{image} of $f$ is $\operatorname{Im} f = f(G)$.
\end{definition}
