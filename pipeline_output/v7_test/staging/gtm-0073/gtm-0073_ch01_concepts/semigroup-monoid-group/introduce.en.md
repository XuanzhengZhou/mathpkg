A semigroup is a nonempty set with an associative binary operation. Adding an identity element yields a monoid, and further requiring inverses for every element yields a group. If the operation is commutative, the structure is called abelian. These three algebraic structures form a hierarchy of increasing strength, with groups being the primary object of study in this chapter.
