($\Rightarrow$) Trivial. ($\Leftarrow$) Note that Theorem 1.2(i) is true under these hypotheses. $G \neq \emptyset$ since $e \in G$. If $a \in G$, then by (ii) $(aa^{-1})(aa^{-1}) = a(a^{-1}a)a^{-1} = a(ea^{-1}) = aa^{-1}$ and hence $aa^{-1} = e$ by Theorem 1.2(i). Thus $a^{-1}$ is a two-sided inverse of $a$. Since $ae = a(a^{-1}a) = (aa^{-1})a = ea = a$ for every $a \in G$, $e$ is a two-sided identity. Therefore $G$ is a group by Definition 1.1.
