\begin{definition}
Given a family of groups $\{G_i \mid i \in I\}$ (assumed mutually disjoint), let $X = \bigcup_{i \in I} G_i$. The \textbf{free product} $\prod_{i \in I}^* G_i$ is the set of all reduced words on $X$, where reduced means:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item[(i)] no $a_i \in X$ is the identity element in its group $G_j$;
    \item[(ii)] adjacent letters belong to different groups $G_j$;
    \item[(iii)] the word is finite, padded with $1$'s.
\end{enumerate}
The group operation is juxtaposition followed by multiplication of adjacent letters from the same group and removal of identities where necessary.
\end{definition}
