The free product of a family of groups is the coproduct in the category of groups. It is constructed analogously to free groups but with the additional rule that adjacent elements from the same factor group are multiplied together. Free groups are free products of infinite cyclic groups. The free product is nonabelian whenever at least two factors are nontrivial.
