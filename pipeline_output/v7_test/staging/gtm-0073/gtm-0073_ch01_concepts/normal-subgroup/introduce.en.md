A normal subgroup is one whose left and right cosets coincide. The set of cosets then forms a group, the quotient group $G/N$. Normal subgroups are the kernels of homomorphisms and are central to the isomorphism theorems. Examples include subgroups of abelian groups (all are normal) and subgroups of index 2.
