The sign of a permutation is well-defined: no permutation can be expressed as both an even and an odd number of transpositions. The proof uses the discriminant $\Delta$ and shows that applying a transposition changes its sign, so a product of an even number of transpositions preserves $\Delta$ while an odd number flips its sign.
