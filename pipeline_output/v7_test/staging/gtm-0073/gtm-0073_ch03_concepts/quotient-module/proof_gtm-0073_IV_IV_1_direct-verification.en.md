Since $A$ is an additive abelian group, $B$ is a normal subgroup, and $A/B$ is a well-defined abelian group. If $a + B = a' + B$, then $a - a' \in B$. Since $B$ is a submodule, $ra - ra' = r(a - a') \in B$ for all $r \in R$. Thus $ra + B = ra' + B$ by Corollary I.4.3 and the action of $R$ on $A/B$ is well defined. The remainder of the proof verifies the module axioms.
