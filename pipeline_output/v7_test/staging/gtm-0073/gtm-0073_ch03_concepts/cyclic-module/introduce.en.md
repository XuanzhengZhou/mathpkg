A cyclic module is an $R$-module generated by a single element. Over a ring with identity, every unitary cyclic module is isomorphic to a quotient $R/J$ of the ring by a left ideal. Cyclic modules are the building blocks in the structure theorem for finitely generated modules over a principal ideal domain, where every such module decomposes as a direct sum of a free module and finitely many cyclic torsion modules.
