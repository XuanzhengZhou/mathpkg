If $X$ is a subset of a module $A$ over a ring $R$, then the intersection of all submodules of $A$ containing $X$ is called the \emph{submodule generated by $X$} (or \emph{spanned by $X$}). If $X$ is finite and generates the module $B$, $B$ is said to be \emph{finitely generated}. If $X = \emptyset$, then the submodule generated by $X$ is the zero submodule.
