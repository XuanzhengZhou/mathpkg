Let $R$ be a ring and
\[
\begin{array}{ccccccccc}
0 & \to & A & \xrightarrow{f} & B & \xrightarrow{g} & C & \to & 0 \\
& & \alpha \downarrow & & \beta \downarrow & & \gamma \downarrow & & \\
0 & \to & A' & \xrightarrow{f'} & B' & \xrightarrow{g'} & C' & \to & 0
\end{array}
\]
a commutative diagram of $R$-modules with exact rows. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] If $\alpha$ and $\gamma$ are monomorphisms, then $\beta$ is a monomorphism.
\item[(ii)] If $\alpha$ and $\gamma$ are epimorphisms, then $\beta$ is an epimorphism.
\item[(iii)] If $\alpha$ and $\gamma$ are isomorphisms, then $\beta$ is an isomorphism.
\end{enumerate}
