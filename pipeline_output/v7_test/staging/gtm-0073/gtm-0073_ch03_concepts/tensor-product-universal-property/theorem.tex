Let $A_R$ and ${}_R B$ be modules over a ring $R$, and let $C$ be an abelian group. If $g : A \times B \to C$ is a middle linear map, then there exists a unique group homomorphism $\bar{g} : A \otimes_R B \to C$ such that $\bar{g}(a \otimes b) = g(a,b)$. $A \otimes_R B$ is uniquely determined up to isomorphism by this property.
