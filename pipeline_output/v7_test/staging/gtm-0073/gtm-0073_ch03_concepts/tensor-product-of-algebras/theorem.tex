Let $A$ and $B$ be algebras [with identity] over a commutative ring $K$ with identity. Then $A \otimes_K B$ is a $K$-algebra [with identity] with product map $\pi$ given by
\[
(A \otimes_K B) \otimes_K (A \otimes_K B) \xrightarrow{1_A \otimes \alpha \otimes 1_B} (A \otimes_K A) \otimes_K (B \otimes_K B) \xrightarrow{\pi_A \otimes \pi_B} A \otimes_K B,
\]
where $\alpha(a \otimes b) = b \otimes a$. For generators, $(a \otimes b)(a_1 \otimes b_1) = aa_1 \otimes bb_1$, and if $A,B$ have identities, $1_A \otimes 1_B$ is the identity.
