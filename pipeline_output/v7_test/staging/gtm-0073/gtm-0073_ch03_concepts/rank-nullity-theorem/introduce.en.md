The Rank-Nullity Theorem is the fundamental dimension formula for linear transformations. It states that for a linear transformation $f: V \to W$ between vector spaces over a division ring, the dimension of the domain equals the sum of the dimensions of the kernel (nullity) and the image (rank). This follows from the first isomorphism theorem together with the subspace dimension formula $\dim V = \dim W + \dim(V/W)$ for a subspace $W \subseteq V$.
