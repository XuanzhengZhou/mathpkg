Let $K$ be a commutative ring with identity. A \emph{$K$-algebra} $A$ is a ring $A$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $(A,+)$ is a unitary left $K$-module;
\item[(ii)] $k(ab) = (ka)b = a(kb)$ for all $k \in K$ and $a,b \in A$.
\end{enumerate}
A $K$-algebra that is a division ring is called a \emph{division algebra}. A finite-dimensional algebra over a field $K$ is a \emph{finite dimensional algebra over $K$}.
