An $R$-module $P$ is said to be \emph{projective} if given any diagram of $R$-module homomorphisms
\[
\begin{array}{ccc}
& & P \\
& & f \downarrow \\
A & \xrightarrow{g} & B \to 0
\end{array}
\]
with bottom row exact ($g$ an epimorphism), there exists an $R$-module homomorphism $h : P \to A$ such that $gh = f$.
