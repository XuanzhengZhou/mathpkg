Let $K$ be a commutative ring with identity and $A$, $B$ $K$-algebras.
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] A $K$-algebra homomorphism $f: A \to B$ is both a ring homomorphism and a $K$-module homomorphism.
\item[(ii)] A $K$-algebra ideal $I$ of $A$ is both a ring ideal and a $K$-submodule. If $A$ has an identity, every ideal is also an algebra ideal.
\end{enumerate}
