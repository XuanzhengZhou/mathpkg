Given $f \in F^*$, let $s_i = f(x_i) \in R$. For any $u = \sum r_i x_i$, $\langle u, f \rangle = \sum r_i s_i$. On the other hand, $\langle u, \sum f_i s_i \rangle = \sum r_i s_i$. Thus $f = \sum f_i s_i$, so $\{f_i\}$ generates $F^*$. Linear independence: if $\sum f_i r_i = 0$, evaluate at $x_j$ to get $r_j = 0$. Hence $\{f_x\}$ is a basis.
