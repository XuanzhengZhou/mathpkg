(i) $\Rightarrow$ (ii) Let $X$ be a basis. For each $x \in X$, $Rx \cong R$. Since $X$ is linearly independent and spans $F$, $F = \sum_{x \in X} Rx$ is an internal direct sum. (ii) $\Rightarrow$ (iii) By definition of internal direct sum. (iii) $\Rightarrow$ (i) The elements $\theta_x$ with $1_R$ in coordinate $x$ and $0$ elsewhere form a basis. (i) $\Rightarrow$ (iv) Given $f: X \to A$, define $\bar{f}(\sum r_i x_i) = \sum r_i f(x_i)$. Linear independence ensures well-definedness. Uniqueness follows since $X$ generates $F$. (iv) $\Rightarrow$ (iii) Construct $\sum_{x \in X} R$ and use the free object property.
