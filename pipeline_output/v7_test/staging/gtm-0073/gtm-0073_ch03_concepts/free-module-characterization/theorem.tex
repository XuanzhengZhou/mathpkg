Let $R$ be a ring with identity. The following conditions on a unitary $R$-module $F$ are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $F$ has a nonempty basis;
\item[(ii)] $F$ is the internal direct sum of a family of cyclic $R$-modules, each isomorphic to $R$;
\item[(iii)] $F$ is $R$-module isomorphic to a direct sum of copies of $R$;
\item[(iv)] there exists a nonempty set $X$ and a function $\iota: X \to F$ with the property: given any unitary $R$-module $A$ and function $f: X \to A$, there exists a unique $R$-module homomorphism $\bar{f}: F \to A$ such that $\bar{f}\iota = f$.
\end{enumerate}
