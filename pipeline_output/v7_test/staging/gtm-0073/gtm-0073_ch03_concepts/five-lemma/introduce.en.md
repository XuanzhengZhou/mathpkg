The Five Lemma is a fundamental diagram-chasing result in homological algebra. Given a commutative diagram with exact rows of length five, it asserts that the middle vertical map inherits monomorphism or epimorphism properties from the outer four vertical maps. Together with the Short Five Lemma (for exact rows of length three), these lemmas form the backbone of proofs involving exact sequences and commutative diagrams.
