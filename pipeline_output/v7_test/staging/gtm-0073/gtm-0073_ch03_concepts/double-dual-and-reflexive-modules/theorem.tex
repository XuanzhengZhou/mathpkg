Let $A$ be a left module over a ring $R$.
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] There is an $R$-module homomorphism $\theta : A \to A^{**}$.
\item[(ii)] If $R$ has an identity and $A$ is free, then $\theta$ is a monomorphism.
\item[(iii)] If $R$ has an identity and $A$ is free with a finite basis, then $\theta$ is an isomorphism.
\end{enumerate}
A module $A$ such that $\theta : A \to A^{**}$ is an isomorphism is said to be \emph{reflexive}.
