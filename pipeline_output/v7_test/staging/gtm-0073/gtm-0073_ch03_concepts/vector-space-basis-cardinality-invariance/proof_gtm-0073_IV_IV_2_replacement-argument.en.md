If either basis is infinite, Theorem 2.6 applies. For finite bases $X = \{x_1,\ldots,x_n\}$ and $Y = \{y_1,\ldots,y_m\}$, suppose $m$ minimal. Express $y_m$ in terms of $X$; a nonzero coefficient identifies an $x_k$ that can be replaced by $y_m$ while preserving spanning. Repeating this process replaces all $x_i$ with $y_j$, showing $m = n$ by minimality.
