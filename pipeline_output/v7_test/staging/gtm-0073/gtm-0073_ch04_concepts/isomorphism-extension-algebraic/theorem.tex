Let $\sigma : K \to L$ be an isomorphism of fields and let $K(u)$ and $L(v)$ be simple extension fields.

(i) If $u$ is transcendental over $K$ and $v$ is transcendental over $L$, then $\sigma$ extends to an isomorphism $K(u) \cong L(v)$ that maps $u$ onto $v$.

(ii) If $u$ is a root of an irreducible polynomial $f \in K[x]$ and $v$ is a root of $\sigma f \in L[x]$, then $\sigma$ extends to an isomorphism $K(u) \cong L(v)$ that maps $u$ onto $v$.
