A field isomorphism $\sigma: K \to L$ can be extended to simple extensions $K(u) \cong L(v)$ under natural conditions: either both $u,v$ are transcendental, or both are algebraic with $u$ being a root of $f$ and $v$ a root of the corresponding polynomial $\sigma f$. This is fundamental for the uniqueness of splitting fields.
