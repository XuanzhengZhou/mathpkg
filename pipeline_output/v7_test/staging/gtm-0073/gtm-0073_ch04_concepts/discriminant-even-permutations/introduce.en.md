The subfield $K(\Delta)$ corresponds to $G \cap A_n$ under the Galois correspondence. Thus the Galois group is contained in the alternating group $A_n$ exactly when the discriminant $\Delta^2$ is already a square in $K$. This is the key computational tool for determining Galois groups.
