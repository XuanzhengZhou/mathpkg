Let $K$ be a field and $f \in K[x]$ a polynomial of positive degree. An extension field $F$ of $K$ is said to be a splitting field of $f$ over $K$ if $f$ splits over $F$ (that is, $f$ can be written as a product of linear factors in $F[x]$) and $F = K(u_1, \ldots, u_n)$ where $u_1, \ldots, u_n$ are all the roots of $f$ in $F$.

More generally, if $S \subset K[x]$ is a set of polynomials of positive degree, an extension field $F$ of $K$ is a splitting field of $S$ over $K$ if every $f \in S$ splits over $F$ and $F$ is generated by $K$ and all roots of all polynomials in $S$.
