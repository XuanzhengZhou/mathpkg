The only proper algebraic extension of $\mathbb{R}$ is $\mathbb{C}$ (up to isomorphism). Since any algebraic element not in $\mathbb{R}$ has an irreducible polynomial that splits in $\mathbb{C}$, its extension embeds into $\mathbb{C}$, and dimension considerations force it to be $\mathbb{C}$ itself.
