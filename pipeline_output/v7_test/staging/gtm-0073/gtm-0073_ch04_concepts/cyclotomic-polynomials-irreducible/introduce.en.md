Over $\mathbb{Q}$, the cyclotomic polynomial $g_n(x)$ has integer coefficients, degree $\varphi(n)$, and is irreducible. The Galois group of the $n$th cyclotomic extension of $\mathbb{Q}$ is the full unit group $(\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^\times$, giving $[\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n):\mathbb{Q}] = \varphi(n)$. The proof that $g_n$ is irreducible uses reduction mod primes.
