Let $\zeta$ be a primitive $n$th root of unity over $\mathbb{Q}$ and let $g_n(x) \in \mathbb{Q}[x]$ be the irreducible polynomial of $\zeta$ over $\mathbb{Q}$. Then:

(i) $g_n(x) = \prod (x - \zeta^i)$ where $1 \leq i < n$ and $(i,n) = 1$, so that $\deg g_n(x) = \varphi(n)$ (the Euler totient function);

(ii) $g_n(x) \in \mathbb{Z}[x]$ and satisfies $x^n - 1 = \prod_{d \mid n} g_d(x)$;

(iii) the Galois group $\mathrm{Aut}_{\mathbb{Q}} \mathbb{Q}(\zeta)$ is isomorphic to the full group of units $\mathbb{Z}_n^\times$, so that $[\mathbb{Q}(\zeta) : \mathbb{Q}] = \varphi(n)$.
