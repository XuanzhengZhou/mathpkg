Every algebraic extension decomposes into a separable part $S$ and a purely inseparable part (over $S$). The field $S$ is the maximal separable subextension, and $P$ is the maximal purely inseparable subextension. When $F$ is normal over $K$, the Galois groups coincide: $\mathrm{Aut}_K S \cong \mathrm{Aut}_K F$.
