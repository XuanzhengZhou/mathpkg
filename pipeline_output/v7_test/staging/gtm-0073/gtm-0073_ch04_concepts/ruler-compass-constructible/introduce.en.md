A finite sequence of ruler and compass constructions produces a tower of quadratic extensions, so the final point $(c,0)$ has degree $[\mathbb{Q}(c):\mathbb{Q}]$ equal to a power of $2$. This gives a necessary algebraic condition for constructibility: any constructible number must have degree a power of $2$ over $\mathbb{Q}$.
