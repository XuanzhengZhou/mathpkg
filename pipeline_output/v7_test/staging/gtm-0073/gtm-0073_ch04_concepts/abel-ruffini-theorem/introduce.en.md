For $n \geq 5$, there is no formula using radicals for the roots of the general polynomial of degree $n$. The proof identifies the Galois group of the general equation as $S_n$ (via symmetric functions), notes that $S_n$ is not solvable for $n \geq 5$, and applies the solvability criterion (Corollary 9.7). This is the historical culmination of Galois theory.
