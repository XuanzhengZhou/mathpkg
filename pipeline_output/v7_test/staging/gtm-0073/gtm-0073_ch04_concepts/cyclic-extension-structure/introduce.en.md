When $K$ contains a primitive $n$th root of unity, cyclic extensions of degree dividing $n$ are precisely the "radical extensions" $K(\sqrt[n]{a})$. The proof uses Hilbert's Theorem 90: if $\sigma$ generates the Galois group and $\zeta$ is a primitive $n$th root of unity, then $N(\zeta) = \zeta^n = 1$ yields $u = v\sigma(v)^{-1}$ and $v^d \in K$.
