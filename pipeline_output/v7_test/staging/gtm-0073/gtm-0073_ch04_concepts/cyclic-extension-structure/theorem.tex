Let $K$ be a field and $n$ a positive integer such that $\mathrm{char}\,K \nmid n$ and $K$ contains a primitive $n$th root of unity. Then the following conditions on an extension field $F$ of $K$ are equivalent:

(i) $F$ is cyclic of degree $d$, where $d \mid n$;

(ii) $F = K(u)$ where $u$ is a root of some $x^n - a \in K[x]$;

(iii) $F = K(u)$ where $u$ is a root of some irreducible $x^d - b \in K[x]$ with $d = [F : K]$.
