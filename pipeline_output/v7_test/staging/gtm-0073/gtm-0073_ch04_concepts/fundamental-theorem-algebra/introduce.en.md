Every nonconstant polynomial over $\mathbb{C}$ has a root in $\mathbb{C}$. The proof given here uses Galois theory and basic analysis: it reduces to showing that every polynomial over $\mathbb{R}$ splits over $\mathbb{C}$, which follows from the fact that $\mathbb{C}$ has no degree 2 extensions and Sylow theory.
