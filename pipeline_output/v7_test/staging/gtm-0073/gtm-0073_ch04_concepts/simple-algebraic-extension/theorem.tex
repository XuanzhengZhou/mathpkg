If $F$ is an extension field of $K$ and $u \in F$ is algebraic over $K$, then:

(i) $K(u) = K[u]$;

(ii) $K(u) \cong K[x]/(f)$, where $f \in K[x]$ is an irreducible monic polynomial of degree $n \geq 1$ uniquely determined by the conditions that $f(u) = 0$ and $g(u) = 0$ ($g \in K[x]$) if and only if $f$ divides $g$;

(iii) $[K(u):K] = n$;

(iv) $\{1_K, u, u^2, \ldots, u^{n-1}\}$ is a basis of the vector space $K(u)$ over $K$;

(v) every element of $K(u)$ can be written uniquely in the form $a_0 + a_1u + \cdots + a_{n-1}u^{n-1}$ ($a_i \in K$).
