An irreducible polynomial over $\mathbb{Q}$ of prime degree $p$ with exactly two nonreal roots must have Galois group $S_p$. The proof combines Cauchy's Theorem (giving a $p$-cycle) with complex conjugation (giving a transposition), which together generate $S_p$.
