Let $F$ be a subfield of the field of real numbers and let $L_1, L_2$ be nonparallel lines in the plane of $F$ and $C_1, C_2$ circles in the plane of $F$. Then:

(i) $L_1 \cap L_2$ is a point in the plane of $F$;

(ii) $L_1 \cap C_1 = \varnothing$ or consists of one or two points in the plane of $F(\sqrt{u})$ for some $u \in F$ ($u \geq 0$);

(iii) $C_1 \cap C_2 = \varnothing$ or consists of one or two points in the plane of $F(\sqrt{u})$ for some $u \in F$ ($u \geq 0$).
