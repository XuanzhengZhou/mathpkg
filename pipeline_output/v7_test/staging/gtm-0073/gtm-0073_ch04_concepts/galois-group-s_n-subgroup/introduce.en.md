The Galois group of a polynomial embeds into $S_n$ by its action on the $n$ distinct roots. For an irreducible separable polynomial, the group is transitive (any root can be mapped to any other) and its order is divisible by the degree. This bounds the possible Galois groups of low-degree polynomials.
