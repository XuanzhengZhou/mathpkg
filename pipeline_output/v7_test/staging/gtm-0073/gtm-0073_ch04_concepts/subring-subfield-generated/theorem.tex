If $F$ is an extension field of a field $K$, $u, u_i \in F$, and $X \subset F$, then:

(i) the subring $K[u]$ consists of all elements of the form $f(u)$, where $f \in K[x]$ is a polynomial with coefficients in $K$;

(ii) the subring $K[u_1, \ldots, u_n]$ consists of all elements of the form $f(u_1, \ldots, u_n)$, where $f \in K[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$;

(iii) the subring $K[X]$ consists of all elements of the form $f(u_1, \ldots, u_m)$, where $u_i \in X$ and $f \in K[x_1, \ldots, x_m]$;

(iv) $K(u_1, \ldots, u_n)$ is the quotient field of the ring $K[u_1, \ldots, u_n]$;

(v) $K(X)$ is the quotient field of the ring $K[X]$;

(vi) $K(X) = \{f(u_1, \ldots, u_n)/g(u_1, \ldots, u_n) \mid u_i \in X; f,g \in K[x_1, \ldots, x_n]; g(u_1, \ldots, u_n) \neq 0\}$;

(vii) for each $u \in K(X)$ there is a finite subset $X' \subset X$ such that $u \in K(X')$.
