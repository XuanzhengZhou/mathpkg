An algebraic extension cannot be too much larger than the base field in cardinality: $|F| \leq \aleph_0|K|$. This set-theoretic bound is essential for applying Zorn's Lemma to a suitably restricted set of algebraic extensions in the proof of the existence of algebraic closures.
