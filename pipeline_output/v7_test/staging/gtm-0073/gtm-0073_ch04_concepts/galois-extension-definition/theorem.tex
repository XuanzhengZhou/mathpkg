Let $F$ be an extension field of $K$ such that the fixed field of the Galois group $\mathrm{Aut}_K F$ is $K$ itself. Then $F$ is said to be a Galois extension (field) of $K$ or to be Galois over $K$.

Equivalently, $F$ is Galois over $K$ if and only if for any $u \in F - K$, there exists a $K$-automorphism $\sigma \in \mathrm{Aut}_K F$ such that $\sigma(u) \neq u$.
