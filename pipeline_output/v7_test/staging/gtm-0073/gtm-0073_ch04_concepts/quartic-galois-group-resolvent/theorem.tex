Let $K$ be a field and $f \in K[x]$ an (irreducible) separable quartic with Galois group $G$ (considered as a transitive subgroup of $S_4$). Let $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$ be the roots of the resolvent cubic. Then:

(i) If the resolvent cubic has a root in $K$, then $G$ is a subgroup of $D_4$ (the dihedral group of order 8).

(ii) If the resolvent cubic splits completely over $K$, then $G$ is isomorphic to $V$ (the Klein four group).

(iii) If the resolvent cubic is irreducible over $K$, then $G$ is either $S_4$ or $A_4$.
