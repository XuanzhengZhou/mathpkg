In a linearly ordered set equipped with the order topology, connectedness admits a purely order-theoretic description: the space is connected precisely when the order is complete (every nonempty bounded-above subset has a supremum) and there are no gaps (between any two distinct points there exists a third). This result generalizes the familiar fact that the real line $\mathbb{R}$ is connected, which follows from the completeness of the real numbers and the density of the rationals.
