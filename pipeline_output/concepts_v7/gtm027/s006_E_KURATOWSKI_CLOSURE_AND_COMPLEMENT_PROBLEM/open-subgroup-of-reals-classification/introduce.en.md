An elementary but important fact in topological group theory: the only open subgroup of $\mathbb{R}$ is $\mathbb{R}$ itself. This follows from the observation that any open subgroup must contain a neighborhood of $0$, which then generates the entire group by translation. This property holds more generally in any connected topological group.
