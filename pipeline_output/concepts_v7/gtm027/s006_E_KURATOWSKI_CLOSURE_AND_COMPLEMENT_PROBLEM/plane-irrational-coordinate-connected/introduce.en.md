Despite the fact that the rational points are removed (on the axes), the remainder---all points with at least one irrational coordinate---forms a connected subspace of the Euclidean plane. This is a non-obvious connectedness result: the set of points with both coordinates rational is totally disconnected, but removing just the 'fully rational' points (those with both coordinates rational) from the rational-coordinate lines actually leaves the complement connected.
