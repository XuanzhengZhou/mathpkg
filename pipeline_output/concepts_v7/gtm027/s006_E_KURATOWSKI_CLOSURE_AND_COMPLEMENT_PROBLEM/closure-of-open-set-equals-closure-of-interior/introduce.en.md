A useful lemma in the proof of Kuratowski's closure-complement theorem states that if a set is already the closure of an open set, then taking its interior and closing again returns the same set. In terms of complementation (denoted by $\prime$) and closure (denoted by $-$), this is expressed succinctly as $A = A^{\prime - \prime}$. This identity reduces the number of distinct sequences of operations one must consider when analyzing the monoid generated by closure and complement.
