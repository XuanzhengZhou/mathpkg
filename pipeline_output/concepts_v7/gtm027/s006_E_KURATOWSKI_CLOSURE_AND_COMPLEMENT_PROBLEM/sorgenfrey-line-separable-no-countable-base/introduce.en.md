The Sorgenfrey line provides an important counterexample distinguishing separability from second-countability. The rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$ are dense in the Sorgenfrey line, making it separable. However, no countable family of open sets can serve as a base: for each real $x$, a base must contain a basic open set whose infimum is exactly $x$, requiring uncountably many distinct elements.
