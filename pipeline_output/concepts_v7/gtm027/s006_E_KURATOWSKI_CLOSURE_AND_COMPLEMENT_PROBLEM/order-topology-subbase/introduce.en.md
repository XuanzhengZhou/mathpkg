The order topology on a linearly ordered set $X$ is generated by the subbase of open rays: all sets of elements strictly less than some fixed point $a$, and all sets of elements strictly greater than $a$. These "open rays" form the simplest building blocks of the order topology; finite intersections of rays yield the open intervals $(a, b)$ that form the usual base. This definition generalizes the standard topology on the real line to any totally ordered set.
