A fundamental classification of additive subgroups of the real line: any nontrivial additive subgroup of $\mathbb{R}$ either accumulates everywhere (is dense) or is discrete and cyclic (has a smallest positive element). The rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$ exemplify the dense case, while the integers $\mathbb{Z}$ exemplify the discrete case. This dichotomy underlies many results in real analysis and topological group theory.
