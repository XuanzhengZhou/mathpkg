Separability is a stronger condition than the countable chain condition: any countable dense set must intersect every nonempty open set, so disjoint open sets force distinct elements of the dense set, giving countability. The converse fails, as demonstrated by the co-countable topology on an uncountable set, or more sophisticated examples such as the Helly space. The distinction between CCC and separability is important in set-theoretic topology.
