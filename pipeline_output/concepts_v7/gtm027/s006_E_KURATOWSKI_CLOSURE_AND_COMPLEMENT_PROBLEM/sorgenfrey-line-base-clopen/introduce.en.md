A distinctive feature of the Sorgenfrey line is that its basic open sets $[a, b)$ are simultaneously closed. This is because the complement of $[a, b)$ can be expressed as $(-\infty, a) \cup [b, \infty)$, which is open in the Sorgenfrey topology. The abundance of clopen sets implies that the Sorgenfrey line is not connected.
