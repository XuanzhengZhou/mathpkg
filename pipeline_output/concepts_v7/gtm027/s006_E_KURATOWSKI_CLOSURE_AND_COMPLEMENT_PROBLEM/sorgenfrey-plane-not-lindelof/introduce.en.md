Despite the Sorgenfrey line being hereditarily Lindelof, its square fails to be Lindelof. This is a classic counterexample showing that the Lindelof property is not preserved under products. The anti-diagonal provides an uncountable closed discrete subspace, whose complement can be covered by open sets that cannot be reduced to a countable subcover.
