The Sorgenfrey plane is the product of two copies of the Sorgenfrey line, with the product topology. Its basic open sets are half-open rectangles $[a, b) 	imes [c, d)$. This space serves as an important counterexample: it is separable but not Lindelof, and it contains a non-separable subspace. These properties illustrate that separability and the Lindelof property are not preserved under products, even for very well-behaved spaces.
