Kuratowski's closure-complement theorem is a classical result in general topology establishing that the monoid generated by the operations of topological closure and set complementation is finite. Starting from any subset $A$ of a topological space, at most $14$ distinct sets can be produced by iterating these two operations in any order. The bound $14$ is sharp, as demonstrated by a carefully chosen subset of the real line under the usual topology. This problem illuminates the algebraic structure underlying topological operations and is often presented as an exercise in introductory topology courses.
