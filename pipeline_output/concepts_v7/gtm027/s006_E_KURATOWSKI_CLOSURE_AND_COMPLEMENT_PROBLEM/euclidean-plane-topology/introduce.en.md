The Euclidean plane is the product $\mathbb{R} 	imes \mathbb{R}$ with the product topology derived from the usual topology on the real line. A countable base is obtained by taking products of open intervals with rational endpoints, making the plane second-countable and separable. The plane also admits an equivalent base of open discs with rational centers and radii.
