The Sorgenfrey line, also known as the lower-limit topology on $\mathbb{R}$, is defined by taking as a base all half-open intervals $[a, b)$. This space is a classic source of counterexamples in general topology. It refines the usual topology and possesses several surprising properties: it is separable but not second-countable, every subspace is Lindelof, and every subspace is separable.
