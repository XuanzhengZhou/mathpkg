Another remarkable hereditary property of the Sorgenfrey line: every subspace is separable (has a countable dense subset). Combined with the hereditary Lindelof property, this makes the Sorgenfrey line a particularly well-behaved counterexample. It shows that a space can be hereditarily separable and hereditarily Lindelof without being second-countable.
