Let $X$ be the following subset of the Euclidean plane with the relativized usual topology: for each positive integer $n$, let $A_n = \{1/n\} \times [0,1]$ where $[0,1]$ is the closed interval, and let $X$ be the union of the sets $A_n$ together with the points $(0,0)$ and $(0,1)$. Then $\{(0,0)\}$ and $\{(0,1)\}$ are distinct components of $X$, but every open and closed (clopen) subset of $X$ contains either both points or neither.
