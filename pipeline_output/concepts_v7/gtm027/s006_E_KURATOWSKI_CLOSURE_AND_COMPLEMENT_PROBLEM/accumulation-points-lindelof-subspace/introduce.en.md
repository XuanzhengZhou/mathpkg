This result, appearing as a problem in Kelley, describes the structure of uncountable subsets in spaces where every subspace is Lindel\"of (a property satisfied, for instance, by the Sorgenfrey line). Given an uncountable set $A$, almost all of its points (all but countably many) have the property that every neighborhood meets $A$ in uncountably many points. Moreover, the set $B$ of such "condensation points" is closed under this property within itself. The result is a topological analogue of the Cantor-Bendixson derivative.
