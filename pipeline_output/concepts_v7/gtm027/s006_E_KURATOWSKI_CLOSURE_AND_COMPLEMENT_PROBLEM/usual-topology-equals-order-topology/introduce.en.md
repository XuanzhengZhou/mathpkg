The standard topology on the real line can be described in two equivalent ways: as the metric topology induced by the absolute value, and as the order topology arising from the natural ordering. The equivalence of these two descriptions is a basic fact in elementary topology. Additionally, the intervals with rational endpoints provide a countable base, making $\mathbb{R}$ a second-countable space.
