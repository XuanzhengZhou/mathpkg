An open cover is a cover whose members are all open sets in the ambient topological space. Open covers are central to the definitions of compactness, Lindelöf, and paracompactness. The distinction between arbitrary covers and open covers is crucial: compactness is defined in terms of open covers, while the Lindelöf property concerns the existence of countable subcovers from open covers.
