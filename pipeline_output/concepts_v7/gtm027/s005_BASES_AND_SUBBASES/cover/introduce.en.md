A cover of a set $B$ is a family of sets whose union contains $B$. This fundamental set-theoretic notion is essential for defining compactness and related topological properties. Covers provide the language for expressing that a space or subset can be "covered" by a collection of sets, with special importance attached to open covers and the existence of finite or countable subcovers.
