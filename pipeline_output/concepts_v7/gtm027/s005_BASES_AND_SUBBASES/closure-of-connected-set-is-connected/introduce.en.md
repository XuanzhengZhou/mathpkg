Theorem 20 states that if $Y$ is a connected subset of a topological space, then its closure $Y^{-}$ is also connected. Moreover, any set $Z$ satisfying $Y \subset Z \subset Y^{-}$ is connected. The proof uses the characterization of connectedness via relatively open and closed sets and the fact that closed subsets of the closure restrict to closed subsets of the original set. This result shows that connectedness is preserved under the operation of taking closures.
