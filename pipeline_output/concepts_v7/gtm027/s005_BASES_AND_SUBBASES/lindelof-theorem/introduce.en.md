Lindelöf's theorem (Theorem 15) states that in a second countable space, every open cover of any subset admits a countable subcover. The proof uses the countable base to select, for each point of the subset, a basic open set containing it and contained in some member of the open cover. The countability of the base then yields a countable subcover. This result motivates the definition of Lindelöf spaces.
