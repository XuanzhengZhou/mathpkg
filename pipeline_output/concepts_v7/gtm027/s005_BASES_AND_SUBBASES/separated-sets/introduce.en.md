Two sets are separated if neither intersects the closure of the other. This condition is stronger than mere disjointness: it requires that no point of one set is an accumulation point of the other. Separation is the fundamental concept underlying the definition of connectedness: a space is connected if it cannot be expressed as the union of two non-void separated sets. The open intervals $(0,1)$ and $(1,2)$ are a standard example of separated sets in the real line.
