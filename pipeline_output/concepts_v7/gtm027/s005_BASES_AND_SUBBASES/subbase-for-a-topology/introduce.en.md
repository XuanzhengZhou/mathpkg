A subbase is a family of sets whose finite intersections form a base for the topology. Equivalently, every open set can be written as a union of finite intersections of subbase members. The notion of subbase is extremely flexible: every non-empty family of sets is a subbase for some topology, making it a universal tool for constructing topological spaces from arbitrary collections of sets.
