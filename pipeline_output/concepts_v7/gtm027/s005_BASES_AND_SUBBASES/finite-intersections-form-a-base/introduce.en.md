This theorem (implicitly Theorem 12) states that for any family of sets, the collection of all finite intersections of members of that family automatically satisfies the base condition and therefore generates a topology. The key observation is that the intersection of two finite intersections is again a finite intersection, so the condition from Theorem 11 is satisfied. This result directly motivates the definition of a subbase.
