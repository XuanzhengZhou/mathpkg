This proposition (implicitly Theorem 17) provides an equivalent characterization of separated sets purely in terms of the relative topology on their union: $A$ and $B$ are separated precisely when they are disjoint and each is closed (and therefore open) in $A \cup B$. This formulation is sometimes more convenient because it avoids explicit reference to closures in the original space, highlighting that the notion of separation is intrinsic to the union.
