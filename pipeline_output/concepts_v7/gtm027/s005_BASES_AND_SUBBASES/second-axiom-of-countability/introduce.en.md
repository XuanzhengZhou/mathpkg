A topological space satisfies the second axiom of countability if it admits a countable base for its topology. This is a strong countability condition that implies many desirable properties: second countable spaces are separable, first countable, and Lindelöf. The real line with its usual topology is the canonical example, with the countable base consisting of all open intervals with rational endpoints.
