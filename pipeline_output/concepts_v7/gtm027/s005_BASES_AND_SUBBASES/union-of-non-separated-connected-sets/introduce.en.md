Theorem 21 is a fundamental gluing result for connectedness: if a family of connected sets has the property that no two members are separated, then their union is also connected. In particular, this implies that the union of connected sets with a common point, or more generally with overlapping closures, is connected. This theorem is essential for building connected spaces from connected pieces and for defining connected components.
