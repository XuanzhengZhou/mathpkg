A topological space is separable if it contains a countable dense subset. The real line with its usual topology is separable because the rational numbers form a countable dense set. Separability is a weaker condition than second countability: every second countable space is separable, but the converse is false, as demonstrated by uncountable sets with the cofinite topology.
