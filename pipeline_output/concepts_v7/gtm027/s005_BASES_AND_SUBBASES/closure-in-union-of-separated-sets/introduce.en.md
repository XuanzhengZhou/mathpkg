Theorem 18 provides a decomposition formula for the closure of a set when the space is the union of two subsets whose set-theoretic differences are separated. Under this hypothesis, the closure of any subset $A$ can be computed locally: it is the union of the closures of $A \cap Y$ in $Y$ and $A \cap Z$ in $Z$. This is a powerful tool for gluing local closure information into global closure information.
