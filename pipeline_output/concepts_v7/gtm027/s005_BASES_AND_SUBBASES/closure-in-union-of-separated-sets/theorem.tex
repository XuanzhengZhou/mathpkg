Let $X$ be a topological space which is the union of subsets $Y$ and $Z$ such that $Y \sim Z$ and $Z \sim Y$ are separated. Then the closure of a subset $A$ of $X$ is the union of the closure in $Y$ of $A \cap Y$ and the closure in $Z$ of $A \cap Z$:
$$A^{-} = (A \cap Y)^{-}_{Y} \cup (A \cap Z)^{-}_{Z}.$$
