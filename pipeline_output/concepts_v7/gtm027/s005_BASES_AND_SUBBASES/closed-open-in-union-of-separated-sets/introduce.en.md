Corollary 19 is a direct consequence of Theorem 18 and provides a practical criterion for verifying that a set is closed or open by checking its restrictions to each piece of a suitable decomposition. If $X = Y \cup Z$ with $Y \sim Z$ and $Z \sim Y$ separated, then a set $A$ is closed (resp. open) in $X$ precisely when its intersections with $Y$ and $Z$ are closed (resp. open) in the relative topologies. This is a fundamental gluing principle.
