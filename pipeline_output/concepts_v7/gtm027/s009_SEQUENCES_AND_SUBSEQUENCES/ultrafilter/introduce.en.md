An ultrafilter is a maximal filter — it cannot be properly extended to a larger filter. Ultrafilters satisfy the characteristic prime property: if a finite union belongs to the ultrafilter, then one of the sets belongs to it. Consequently, for any subset $A$, exactly one of $A$ and its complement belongs to an ultrafilter. Ultrafilters are the filter-theoretic counterpart of universal nets and play a fundamental role in compactness via the ultrafilter lemma.
