Let $f$ be a bounded real-valued function on the closed interval $[a,b]$. A subdivision $S$ of $[a,b]$ is a finite family of closed intervals covering $[a,b]$ such that any two intervals have at most one point in common. For each $I \in S$, let $|I|$ denote the length of $I$, let $M_f(I) = \sup\{f(x) : x \in I\}$, and let $m_f(I) = \inf\{f(x) : x \in I\}$.

The upper and lower Darboux sums corresponding to the subdivision $S$ are:
$$D_f(S) = \sum\{|I| \, M_f(I) : I \in S\}, \qquad d_f(S) = \sum\{|I| \, m_f(I) : I \in S\}.$$

The family of subdivisions is directed by refinement: $S \geq S'$ if and only if each member of $S$ is a subset of a member of $S'$.
