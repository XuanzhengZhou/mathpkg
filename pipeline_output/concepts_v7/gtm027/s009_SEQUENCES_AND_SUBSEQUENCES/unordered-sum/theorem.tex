Let $A$ be an index set and let $f : A \to \mathbb{R}$ be a real-valued function. For each finite subset $F$ of $A$, let $S_F = \sum\{f(a) : a \in F\}$. The family $\{S_F : F \text{ finite}, F \subset A\}$ directed by inclusion is a net. If this net converges, $f$ is summable over $A$ and the limit is the unordered sum of $f$ over $A$, denoted $\sum\{f(a) : a \in A\}$ or $\sum_A f$.
