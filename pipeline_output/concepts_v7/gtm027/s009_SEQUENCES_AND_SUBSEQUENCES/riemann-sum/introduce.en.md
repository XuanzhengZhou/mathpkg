Riemann sums are formulated as nets indexed by pairs $(S, c)$ of subdivisions and choice functions. Two natural directions are compared: refinement ordering and mesh ordering. The mesh-directed net is a subnet of the refinement-directed net, and both converge to the same limit when the Darboux upper and lower sums converge to a common value. This net-theoretic treatment reveals that refinement-based integration generalizes to Lebesgue integration and vector-valued integration, while mesh-based integration does not.
