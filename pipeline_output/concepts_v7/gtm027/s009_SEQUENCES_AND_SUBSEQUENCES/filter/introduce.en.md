A filter on a set $X$ is a nonempty collection of nonempty subsets closed under finite intersections and upward inclusion. Filters provide an alternative foundation for convergence theory parallel to nets: they are proper dual ideals in the Boolean ring of subsets of $X$. The filter approach to topology was developed by Henri Cartan and offers a complementary perspective to Moore-Smith convergence.
