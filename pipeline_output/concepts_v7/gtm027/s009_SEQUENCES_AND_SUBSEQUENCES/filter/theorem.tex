A filter $\mathcal{F}$ in a set $X$ is a family of non-void subsets of $X$ such that:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] If $A, B \in \mathcal{F}$, then $A \cap B \in \mathcal{F}$.
\item[(ii)] If $A \in \mathcal{F}$ and $A \subset B \subset X$, then $B \in \mathcal{F}$.
\end{enumerate}

In the terminology of Boolean rings, a filter is a proper dual ideal in the Boolean ring of all subsets of $X$.
