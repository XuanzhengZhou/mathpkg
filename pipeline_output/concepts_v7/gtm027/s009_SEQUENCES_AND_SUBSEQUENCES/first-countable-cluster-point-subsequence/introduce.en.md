In a first countable space, every cluster point of a sequence is the limit of some subsequence. This is part (c) of Theorem 8 and is a key distinguishing property: in general topological spaces, a sequence may have cluster points that are not limits of any subsequence. The proof uses a nested countable neighborhood base and a diagonal selection argument.
