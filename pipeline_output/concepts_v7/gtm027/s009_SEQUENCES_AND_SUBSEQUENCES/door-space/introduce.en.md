A door space is a topological space in which every subset is either open or closed — the topology is as extreme as possible in terms of the open/closed dichotomy. In the Hausdorff case, door spaces are highly constrained: they admit at most one accumulation point, and any non-accumulation point is isolated. This concept illustrates the tension between separation axioms and extreme topological structure.
