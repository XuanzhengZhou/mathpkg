A Boolean ring is a ring $(R, +, \cdot)$ with unit such that every element is idempotent: $r \cdot r = r$ for all $r \in R$. The natural ordering of a Boolean ring is defined by $r \geq s$ if and only if $r \cdot s = s$. With this ordering, the join and meet are given by:
$$r \vee s = r + s + r \cdot s, \qquad r \wedge s = r \cdot s.$$
The family of all subsets of a set $X$ forms a Boolean ring $(\mathcal{P}(X), \Delta, \cap)$ where $\Delta$ is symmetric difference and $\cap$ is intersection.
