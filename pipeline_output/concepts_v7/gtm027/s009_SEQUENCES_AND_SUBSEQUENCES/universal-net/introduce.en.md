A universal net is a net that, for every subset $A$ of the space, is eventually either entirely inside $A$ or entirely outside $A$. Universal nets are the net-theoretic analogue of ultrafilters and share their key property: if frequently in a set, then eventually in it. Every universal net converges to each of its cluster points, and the image of a universal net under any function remains universal.
