A Boolean algebra is a complemented distributive lattice. The natural ordering of a Boolean algebra $(R, \geq)$ satisfies that the join and meet are associative and the following distributive laws hold:
$$r \wedge (s \vee t) = (r \wedge s) \vee (r \wedge t), \qquad r \vee (s \wedge t) = (r \vee s) \wedge (r \vee t).$$
An ideal $S$ in a Boolean ring $(R, +, \cdot)$ is an additive subgroup such that $r \cdot s \in S$ whenever $r \in R$ and $s \in S$. Not all Boolean algebras are isomorphic to an algebra of all subsets of a set.
