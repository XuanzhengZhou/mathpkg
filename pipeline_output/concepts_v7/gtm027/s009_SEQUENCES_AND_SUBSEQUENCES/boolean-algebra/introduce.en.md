A Boolean algebra is a complemented distributive lattice, equivalent to a Boolean ring via the correspondence $r \vee s = r + s + rs$ and $r \wedge s = rs$. While every power set algebra is a Boolean algebra, not all Boolean algebras are isomorphic to a full power set — countable Boolean algebras provide counterexamples. Stone's representation theorem shows that every Boolean algebra is isomorphic to the algebra of clopen sets of a totally disconnected compact Hausdorff space.
