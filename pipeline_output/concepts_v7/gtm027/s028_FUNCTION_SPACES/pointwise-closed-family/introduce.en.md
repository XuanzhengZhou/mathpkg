A family $F$ of functions from $X$ to $Y$ is pointwise closed when it forms a closed subset of the full product space $Y^X$. This notion provides the closure condition in the compactness criterion for the pointwise topology: a family is compact in the pointwise topology if and only if it is pointwise closed and each pointwise fiber $F[x]$ has compact closure. The term "pointwise" reflects that closedness is assessed in the topology of pointwise convergence.
