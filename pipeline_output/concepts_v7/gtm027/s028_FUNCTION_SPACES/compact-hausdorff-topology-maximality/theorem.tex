Let $F$ be a set equipped with two topologies $\mathfrak{j}$ and $\varphi$ such that $\varphi \subset \mathfrak{j}$ (i.e., $\mathfrak{j}$ is finer than $\varphi$). If $(F, \mathfrak{j})$ is compact and $(F, \varphi)$ is Hausdorff, then $\mathfrak{j} = \varphi$.

Equivalently, the identity map $i : (F, \mathfrak{j}) \to (F, \varphi)$ is a continuous bijection from a compact space to a Hausdorff space, hence a homeomorphism.

In the context of function spaces: if $(F, \mathfrak{j})$ is compact, $(F, \wp)$ is Hausdorff (where $\wp$ is the pointwise topology), and $\mathfrak{j}$ is finer than $\wp$, then $\mathfrak{j} = \wp$.
