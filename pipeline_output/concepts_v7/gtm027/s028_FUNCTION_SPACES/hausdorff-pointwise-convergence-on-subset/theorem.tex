Let $F$ be a family of functions, each on a set $X$ to a Hausdorff space $Y$, and let $A \subset X$. Let $\wp$ denote the topology of pointwise convergence on $X$, and let $\wp_A$ denote the topology of pointwise convergence on $A$.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(i)] The family $F$ with the topology $\wp_A$ is a Hausdorff space if and only if $A$ distinguishes members of $F$ (i.e., the restriction map $R : F \to Y^A$ is injective).
  \item[(ii)] If $F$ is compact relative to the topology $\wp$ and $A$ distinguishes members of $F$, then the topologies $\wp$ and $\wp_A$ are identical on $F$.
\end{enumerate}
