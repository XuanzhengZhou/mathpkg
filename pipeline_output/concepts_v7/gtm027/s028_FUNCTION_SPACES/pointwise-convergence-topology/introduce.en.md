The topology of pointwise convergence is the coarsest topology on a family of functions that makes evaluation at each point continuous. It is defined as the relative product topology inherited from the product space $Y^X$. Under this topology, a net of functions converges precisely when it converges at every point of the domain, which explains the name "pointwise." A subbase consists of sets of functions whose value at a fixed point lies in a fixed open set of the codomain.
