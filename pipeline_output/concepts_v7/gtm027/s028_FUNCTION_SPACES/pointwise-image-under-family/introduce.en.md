Given a family $F$ of functions from $X$ to $Y$, the pointwise image $F[A]$ collects all values $f(x)$ as $f$ ranges over $F$ and $x$ ranges over $A$. The special case $F[x] = \{f(x) : f \in F\}$ is the fiber of the family at $x$, which coincides with the image of $F$ under the evaluation map $e_x$. These sets play a key role in the compactness criterion: a family $F$ is compact in the pointwise topology precisely when $F$ is pointwise closed and each fiber $F[x]$ is compact.
