Let $F$ be a family of functions, each on a set $X$ to a topological space $Y$. Then $F$ is compact relative to the topology of pointwise convergence if and only if:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(a)] $F$ is pointwise closed; that is, $F$ is a closed subset of the product space $Y^X$.
  \item[(b)] For each $x \in X$, the closure of $F[x] = \{f(x) : f \in F\}$ in $Y$ is compact.
\end{enumerate}

If $Y$ is a Hausdorff space and $F$ is compact relative to the pointwise topology, then $F[x]$ is compact (and closed) for each $x \in X$, because the evaluation map $e_x$ is continuous and maps the compact space $F$ into the Hausdorff space $Y$.
