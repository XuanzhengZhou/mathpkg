Let $F$ be a family of functions, each on a set $X$ to a set $Y$, and let $A \subset X$. The subset $A$ is said to \textbf{distinguish members of} $F$ if and only if, whenever $f$ and $g$ are distinct members of $F$, there exists a point $x \in A$ such that $f(x) \neq g(x)$.

Equivalently, $A$ distinguishes members of $F$ if and only if the restriction map $R : F \to Y^A$ defined by $R(f) = f|_A$ is injective (one-to-one).
