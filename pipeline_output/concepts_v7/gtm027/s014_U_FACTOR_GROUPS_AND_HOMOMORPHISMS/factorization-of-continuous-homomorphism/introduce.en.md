Any continuous homomorphism $f: G \to J$ between topological groups can be canonically factored into two maps: first, the corestriction $\tilde{f}: G \to f[G]$ where the image $f[G]$ carries the quotient topology from $G$, which is a continuous open homomorphism; second, the inclusion $\iota: f[G] \hookrightarrow J$, which is a continuous one-to-one homomorphism. This factorization separates the "surjectivity" aspect (captured by the open map $\tilde{f}$) from the "embedding" aspect (captured by $\iota$). If $f$ itself is already open, then $\iota$ is a topological embedding.
