When the subgroup $H$ is invariant (normal), the quotient $G/H$ is not merely a homogeneous space but a topological group in its own right. The group operations on $G/H$ are continuous with respect to the quotient topology, and the canonical projection $\pi: G \to G/H$ is simultaneously a group homomorphism and a continuous open map. This result is fundamental: it establishes that the category of topological groups is closed under taking quotients by normal subgroups.
