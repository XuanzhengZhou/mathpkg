Theorem 11 relates the Hausdorff separation property of a quotient space to topological properties of the equivalence relation itself. If the quotient is Hausdorff, the relation $R$ must be a closed subset of $X \times X$. Conversely, when the projection is an open map and $R$ is closed in the product, the quotient is guaranteed to be Hausdorff. The condition that $R$ is closed means that any two points from distinct equivalence classes can be separated by saturated neighborhoods.
