The quotient topology is the finest topology on the codomain that makes a given function continuous. Given a topological space $X$ and a function $f: X \to Y$, a set $U \subseteq Y$ is declared open precisely when its preimage $f^{-1}[U]$ is open in $X$. This construction is dual to the product topology: while the product topology is the coarsest making all projections continuous, the quotient topology is the finest making a given map continuous.
