An upper semi-continuous decomposition is a partition of a topological space where each member $D$ admits a neighborhood basis consisting of saturated open sets (i.e., sets that are unions of decomposition members). This property is the natural topological analogue of continuity for set-valued maps. Despite the name, the condition has no direct relationship with semi-continuous functions; the term originates from the upper semi-continuity of the associated decomposition-valued mapping.
