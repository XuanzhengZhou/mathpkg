# Uniform Neighborhood System from a Gage

This proposition constructs a uniform neighborhood system from the gage (family of pseudometrics) of a uniformity. The index set is the product of the gage $P$ and the positive reals, directed in a natural way that respects both the ordering of pseudometrics (pointwise domination) and the radius. The neighborhood assigned to $(p, r)$ at a point $x$ is the open $r$-ball under the pseudometric $p$. The resulting uniform neighborhood system recovers the original uniformity, providing a metric-flavored description of any uniform space.
