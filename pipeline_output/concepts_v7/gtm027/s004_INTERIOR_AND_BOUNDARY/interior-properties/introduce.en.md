Theorem 9 establishes the core properties of the interior operator on a topological space. The interior $A^{0}$ is always open and is maximal among open subsets of $A$, yielding a crisp criterion for openness: $A$ is open exactly when it equals its own interior. The theorem also connects the interior to accumulation points and the closure of the complement, foreshadowing the duality between the interior and closure operators.
