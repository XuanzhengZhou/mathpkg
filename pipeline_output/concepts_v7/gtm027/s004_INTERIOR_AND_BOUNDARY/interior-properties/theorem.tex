Let $A$ be a subset of a topological space $X$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The interior $A^{0}$ of $A$ is open and is the largest open subset of $A$.
\item A set $A$ is open if and only if $A = A^{0}$.
\item The set of all points of $A$ which are not points of accumulation of $X \sim A$ is precisely $A^{0}$.
\item The closure of $X \sim A$ is $X \sim A^{0}$.
\end{enumerate}
