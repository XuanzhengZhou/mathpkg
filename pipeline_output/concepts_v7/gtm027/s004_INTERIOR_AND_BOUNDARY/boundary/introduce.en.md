The boundary (or frontier) of a subset $A$ of a topological space consists of those points that lie in neither the interior of $A$ nor the interior of its complement. Equivalently, a point belongs to the boundary of $A$ precisely when every neighborhood of the point meets both $A$ and its complement. The boundary thus captures the "edge" of a set, separating it from its complement in the topological sense.
