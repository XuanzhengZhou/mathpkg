An interior point of a subset $A$ of a topological space is a point for which $A$ serves as a neighborhood. This notion is the inverse of the neighborhood relation: $A$ is a neighborhood of $x$ if and only if $x$ is an interior point of $A$. Interior points collectively form the interior of $A$, a fundamental operator in topology dual to the closure operator.
