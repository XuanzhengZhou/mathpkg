The interior and closure operators on a topological space are dual to each other via complementation. The interior of $A$ is exactly the complement of the closure of the complement of $A$, and symmetrically the closure of $A$ is the complement of the interior of the complement of $A$. This duality means that any theorem about closure immediately yields a corresponding theorem about interior, and vice versa.
