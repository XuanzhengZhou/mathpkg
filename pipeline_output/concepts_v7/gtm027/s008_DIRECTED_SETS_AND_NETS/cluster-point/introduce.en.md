A cluster point of a net generalizes the notion of an accumulation point from sequences to nets. A point $s$ is a cluster point of a net $S$ if $S$ is frequently in every neighborhood of $s$ -- that is, for each neighborhood $U$ of $s$ and each index $m$, there exists a later index $n \geq m$ with $S_n \in U$. Every limit point of a convergent net is a cluster point, but a net may have a unique cluster point without converging to it. The concept is essential for characterizing compactness via nets.
