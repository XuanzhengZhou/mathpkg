Lemma 5 is a key technical result in the theory of nets. It states that if a net $S$ is frequently in each member of a family $\alpha$ of sets that is closed under finite intersections (a filter base), then there exists a subnet of $S$ that is eventually in every member of $\alpha$. The proof constructs the subnet explicitly using the product directed set $D \times \alpha$ ordered componentwise, with the isotone projection map $N(m, A) = m$. This lemma is applied immediately to characterize cluster points of nets: a point $s$ is a cluster point of $S$ if and only if some subnet of $S$ converges to $s$.
