A cofinal subset of a directed set is one that contains elements arbitrarily far out in the order: for every element of the directed set, there is an element of the cofinal subset that follows it. Cofinal subsets are themselves directed by the same order, and they play a crucial role in net theory. A net is frequently in a set precisely when a cofinal subset of its domain maps into that set, and the simplest subnets are obtained by restricting a net to a cofinal subset of its index domain.
