A subset $E$ of a directed set $(D, \geq)$ is cofinal if and only if for each $m \in D$ there is $p \in E$ such that $p \geq m$.

Each cofinal subset $E$ of $D$ is also directed by $\geq$: for elements $m$ and $n$ of $E$ there is $p$ in $D$ such that $p \geq m$ and $p \geq n$, and there is then an element $q$ of $E$ which follows $p$.
