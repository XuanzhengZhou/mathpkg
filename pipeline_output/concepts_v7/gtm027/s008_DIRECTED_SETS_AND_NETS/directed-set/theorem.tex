A binary relation $\geq$ directs a set $D$ if $D$ is non-void and

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] if $m, n$ and $p$ are members of $D$ such that $m \geq n$ and $n \geq p$, then $m \geq p$;
\item[(b)] if $m \in D$, then $m \geq m$; and
\item[(c)] if $m$ and $n$ are members of $D$, then there is $p$ in $D$ such that $p \geq m$ and $p \geq n$.
\end{enumerate}

A directed set is a pair $(D, \geq)$ such that $\geq$ directs $D$. This is sometimes called a directed system.
