Theorem 3 establishes a fundamental equivalence: a topological space satisfies the Hausdorff separation axiom precisely when every net in the space has at most one limit. The forward direction is straightforward since a net cannot be eventually in two disjoint neighborhoods. The converse constructs, for any non-Hausdorff space, a net that converges to two distinct points by taking the directed set of neighborhood pairs ordered by inclusion. This theorem is one of the earliest and most important applications of net convergence.
