Theorem 4 provides a construction that reduces iterated limits to a single limit of a net. Given a doubly-indexed family $S(m,n)$ with an iterated limit, one builds a product directed set $F = D \times \prod_m E_m$ and a map $R(m,f) = (m, f(m))$ so that the composed net $S \circ R$ converges to the iterated limit. The construction uses a variant of the diagonal process and requires Moore-Smith convergence: for double sequences, no sequence whose range lies in $\omega \times \omega$ can have this property, illustrating why nets are essential for general iterated limits.
