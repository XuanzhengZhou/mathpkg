An isotone subnet provides a simple sufficient condition for constructing subnets that is adequate for almost all purposes. Given a function $N$ from a directed set $E$ to a directed set $D$, if $N$ is isotone (order-preserving) and its range is cofinal in $D$, then $S \circ N$ is automatically a subnet of $S$ for any net $S$ on $D$. This construction is used in the proof of Lemma 5 and was noted by K. T. Smith. It is more restrictive than the general definition of subnet but easier to work with in practice.
