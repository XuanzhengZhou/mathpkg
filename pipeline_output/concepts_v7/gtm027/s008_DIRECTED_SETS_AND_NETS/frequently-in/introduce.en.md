A net is frequently in a set when it visits that set arbitrarily far out in the directed index. Formally, this means a cofinal subset of the index domain maps into the set, or equivalently, the net is not eventually in the complement. The "frequently in" property is the dual of "eventually in" and is essential for defining cluster points of nets. A point is a cluster point of a net precisely when the net is frequently in every neighborhood of that point.
