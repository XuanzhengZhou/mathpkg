A family S of subsets of X×X is a subbase for a uniformity when each member contains the diagonal, inverse images are roughly in S, and the V∘V⊂U condition holds within S. The uniformity is then obtained by taking all finite intersections of members of S as a base. This result implies that the union of any collection of uniformities generates a uniformity, giving a supremum operation on the class of uniformities for a fixed set.
