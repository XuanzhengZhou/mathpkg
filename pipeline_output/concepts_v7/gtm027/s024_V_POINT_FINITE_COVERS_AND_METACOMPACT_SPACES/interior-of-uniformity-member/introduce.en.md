In a uniform space, the interior of any member of the uniformity (taken in the product topology on X×X) again belongs to the uniformity. As a consequence, the family of all open symmetric members of the uniformity forms a base. This means one can always work with open symmetric entourages without loss of generality, simplifying many arguments in the theory of uniform spaces.
