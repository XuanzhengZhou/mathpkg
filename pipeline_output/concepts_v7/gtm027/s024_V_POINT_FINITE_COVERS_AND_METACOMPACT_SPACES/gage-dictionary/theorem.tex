Let $(X, \mathfrak{u})$ be a uniform space and let $P$ be the gage of $\mathfrak{u}$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] The family of all sets $V_{p,r}$ for $p \in P$ and $r > 0$ is a base for $\mathfrak{u}$.
\item[(b)] The uniform topology is the topology generated by the pseudo-metric topologies for $p \in P$.
\item[(c)] The closure of a set $A \subset X$ is $\{x : p\text{-dist}(x,A) = 0 \text{ for all } p \in P\}$.
\item[(d)] A net is Cauchy iff it is $p$-Cauchy for each $p \in P$.
\item[(e)] A function $f : X \to (Y, \mathcal{V})$ is uniformly continuous iff for each $q$ in the gage $Q$ of $\mathcal{V}$, the pseudo-metric $q \circ f_2$ belongs to $P$.
\item[(f)] The gage of a product uniformity is generated by the pseudo-metrics $q(x,y) = p_a(x_a, y_a)$ for coordinates $a$ and $p_a \in P_a$.
\end{enumerate}
