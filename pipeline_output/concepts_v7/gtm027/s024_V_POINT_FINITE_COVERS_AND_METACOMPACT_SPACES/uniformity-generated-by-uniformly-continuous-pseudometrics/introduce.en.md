Every uniformity is generated by the collection of all pseudo-metrics that are uniformly continuous on the product space. This means the family P of all uniformly continuous pseudo-metrics—called the gage of the uniformity—completely determines the uniformity, and the uniformity is the smallest one making each member of P uniformly continuous. This representation theorem shows that the study of uniform spaces is essentially equivalent to the study of families of pseudo-metrics.
