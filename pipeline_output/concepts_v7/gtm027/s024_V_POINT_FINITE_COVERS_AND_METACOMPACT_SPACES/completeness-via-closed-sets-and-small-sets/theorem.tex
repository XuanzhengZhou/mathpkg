A uniform space $(X, \mathfrak{u})$ is complete if and only if every family $\mathcal{A}$ of closed subsets of $X$ which has the finite intersection property and contains arbitrarily small sets has a non-void intersection. (Here $\mathcal{A}$ \text{contains small sets} means: for each $U \in \mathfrak{u}$ there exists $A \in \mathcal{A}$ such that $A \subset U[x]$ for some $x \in X$.)
