A non-void family $\mathcal{B}$ of subsets of $X \times X$ is a base for some uniformity for $X$ if and only if:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] each member of $\mathcal{B}$ contains the diagonal $\Delta$;
\item[(b)] if $U \in \mathcal{B}$, then $U^{-1}$ contains a member of $\mathcal{B}$;
\item[(c)] if $U \in \mathcal{B}$, then there is $V \in \mathcal{B}$ such that $V \circ V \subset U$;
\item[(d)] the intersection of two members of $\mathcal{B}$ contains a member of $\mathcal{B}$.
\end{enumerate}
