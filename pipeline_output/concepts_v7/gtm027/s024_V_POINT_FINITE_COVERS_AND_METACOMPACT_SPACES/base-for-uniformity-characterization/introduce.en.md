A family of subsets of X×X serves as a base for a uniformity precisely when: every member contains the diagonal, the family is closed under taking inverses (up to containment), the "half-size" condition V∘V⊂U holds, and binary intersections are controlled. These axioms abstract the behavior of the sets V_{d,r} = {(x,y): d(x,y) < r} in metric spaces, where the triangle inequality yields V_{d,r/2}∘V_{d,r/2} ⊂ V_{d,r}.
