Every uniform space can be uniformly embedded as a subspace of a product of pseudo-metric spaces. In the Hausdorff case, the target can be taken to be a product of genuine metric spaces. The embedding is constructed using the gage: each uniformly continuous pseudo-metric gives a coordinate map into the associated metric identification. This result, due to Weil, is the uniform analog of the Tychonoff embedding theorem for completely regular spaces.
