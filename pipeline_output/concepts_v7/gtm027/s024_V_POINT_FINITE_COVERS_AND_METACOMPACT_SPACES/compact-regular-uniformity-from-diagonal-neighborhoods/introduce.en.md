On a compact regular topological space, the topology determines exactly one compatible uniformity: the family of all neighborhoods of the diagonal. This uniqueness means that every topological invariant of a compact regular space is automatically a uniform invariant. In particular, the completion, total boundedness, and Cauchy net notions become purely topological for compact regular spaces, dramatically simplifying the theory.
