Every metric space admits a completion: it embeds isometrically as a dense subset of a complete metric space. The construction takes X* to be the set of all Cauchy sequences in X, with the distance defined as the limit of pointwise distances. This completion is unique up to isometry. Combined with Theorem 16, this yields a completion for every Hausdorff uniform space by embedding into a product of complete metric spaces and taking the closure.
