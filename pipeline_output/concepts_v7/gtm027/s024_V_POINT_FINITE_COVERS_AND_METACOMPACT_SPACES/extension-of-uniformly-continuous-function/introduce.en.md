A uniformly continuous function from a subset of a uniform space into a complete Hausdorff uniform space extends uniquely and uniformly continuously to the closure of its domain. The extension is constructed by taking the closure of the graph of the original function in the product space. This is the uniform analog of the Tietze extension theorem and is a cornerstone result: it implies uniqueness of the Hausdorff completion up to uniform isomorphism.
