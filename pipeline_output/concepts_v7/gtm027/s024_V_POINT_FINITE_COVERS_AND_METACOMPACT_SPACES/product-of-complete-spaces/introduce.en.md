A product of uniform spaces is complete precisely when every factor is complete. This follows from the fact that a net in a product is Cauchy if and only if its projection onto each coordinate is Cauchy. This theorem is fundamental for constructing complete spaces: products of complete spaces remain complete, mirroring the Tychonoff theorem for compactness but holding for arbitrary (not necessarily compact) complete spaces.
