A family of subsets of a topological space is point finite if every point of the space belongs to at most finitely many members of the family. This notion weakens local finiteness: every locally finite family is point finite, but the converse fails. Point finiteness serves as the defining ingredient for metacompact spaces, where every open cover admits a point finite open refinement.
