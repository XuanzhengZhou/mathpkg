Local compactness is preserved by maps that are simultaneously open and continuous: the image of a compact neighborhood under an open continuous map is again a compact neighborhood of the image point. Note that continuity alone is insufficient — every topological space is the continuous one-to-one image of a discrete (hence locally compact) space, yet not every space is locally compact. The openness condition is essential to ensure neighborhoods are mapped to neighborhoods.
