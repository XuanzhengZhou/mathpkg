Theorem 18 provides a powerful separation result for regular locally compact spaces: given a closed compact set $A$ contained in an open neighborhood $U$, one can find an intermediate closed compact neighborhood $V$ with $A \subseteq V \subseteq U$, together with a continuous Urysohn function that is $0$ on $A$ and $1$ outside $V$. This generalizes Urysohn's lemma to the setting of compact subsets in locally compact regular spaces and is a key tool for proving complete regularity.
