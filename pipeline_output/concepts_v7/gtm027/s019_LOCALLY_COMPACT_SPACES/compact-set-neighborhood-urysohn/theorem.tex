Let $X$ be a regular locally compact topological space, let $A \subseteq X$ be a closed compact subset, and let $U$ be a neighborhood of $A$. Then there exists a closed compact neighborhood $V$ of $A$ such that $A \subseteq V \subseteq U$. Moreover, there exists a continuous function $f: X \to [0,1]$ such that $f(x) = 0$ for all $x \in A$ and $f(x) = 1$ for all $x \in X \setminus V$.
