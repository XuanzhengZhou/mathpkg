Theorem 19 gives a precise necessary condition for a product space to be locally compact: each factor must be locally compact (which follows because projections are open continuous maps), and only finitely many factors can be non-compact. The latter condition is proved by contrapositive: if infinitely many factors are non-compact, then every compact subset of the product is nowhere dense (by a result from the Tychonoff product theorem section), preventing any point from having a compact neighborhood.
