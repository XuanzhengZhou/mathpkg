Theorem 20 establishes that forming a quotient space via an upper semi-continuous decomposition with compact members preserves several important topological properties: the Hausdorff property, regularity, local compactness, and second countability. The proof uses the fact that in such decompositions, admissible neighborhoods (unions of decomposition members) can be used to transfer separation properties from $X$ to the quotient space, and projection maps closed sets to closed sets. This result ensures that quotients of "nice" spaces by "nice" decompositions remain well-behaved.
