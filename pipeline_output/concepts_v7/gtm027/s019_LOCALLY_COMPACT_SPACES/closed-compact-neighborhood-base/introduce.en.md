In a locally compact space that is either Hausdorff or regular, every point admits a neighborhood base consisting entirely of closed compact sets. This theorem is a fundamental tool: it guarantees that locally compact Hausdorff or regular spaces have an abundance of "nice" neighborhoods around each point, enabling compactness arguments to be applied locally. The result fails without the Hausdorff or regularity assumption.
