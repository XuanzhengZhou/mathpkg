This theorem gives an equivalent formulation of local connectedness: a space is locally connected precisely when its topology admits a base consisting entirely of open connected sets. The forward direction follows from the fact that components of open sets are open and connected, while the reverse direction is almost immediate from the definition of a base. This characterization is often the most practical way to verify local connectedness and is used frequently in applications to manifolds and polyhedra.
