An inductive property is one that is preserved under taking countable intersections of nested closed sets. This notion is central to the Brouwer Reduction Theorem, which guarantees the existence of minimal closed subsets possessing a given inductive property. The second axiom of countability ensures that the space is hereditarily Lindelöf, which is the key topological ingredient enabling the reduction argument. Common examples of inductive properties include being nonempty, being uncountable, and having a particular topological dimension.
