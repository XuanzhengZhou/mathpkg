In a locally connected space, the connected components of any open set are themselves open. This is a direct consequence of the definition: for each point in a component, the component of the containing open set relative to that point is a neighborhood. Since the component of the open set coincides locally with these smaller component-neighborhoods, it is a union of open sets and therefore open. This property is one of the most useful consequences of local connectedness and underlies many arguments in the topology of manifolds and CW complexes.
