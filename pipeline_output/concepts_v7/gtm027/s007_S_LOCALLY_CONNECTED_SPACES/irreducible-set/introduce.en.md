An irreducible set for a property $P$ is a minimal closed set possessing $P$, in the sense that no strictly smaller closed subset also has $P$. This is the notion of minimality that appears in the conclusion of the Brouwer Reduction Theorem. Irreducibility is always relative to a specific property; a set irreducible for one property may have proper closed subsets satisfying a different property. The concept is fundamental to reduction arguments throughout topology and analysis, including the proof of the Cantor-Bendixson theorem and dimension theory.
