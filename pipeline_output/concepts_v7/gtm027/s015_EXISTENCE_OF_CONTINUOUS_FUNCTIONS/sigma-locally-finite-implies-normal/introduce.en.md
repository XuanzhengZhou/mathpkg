This theorem is a crucial lemma in the proof of the Bing-Nagata-Smirnov metrization theorem. It establishes that a regular space with a $\sigma$-locally finite base is automatically normal. The proof uses the $\sigma$-locally finite base to construct disjoint open neighborhoods for arbitrary disjoint closed sets, iterating through the locally finite families. This result shows that the normality requirement in the metrization theorem is actually redundant -- regularity plus a $\sigma$-locally finite base already implies normality. More generally, it connects to the theory of paracompact spaces, since every regular space with a $\sigma$-locally finite base is paracompact.
