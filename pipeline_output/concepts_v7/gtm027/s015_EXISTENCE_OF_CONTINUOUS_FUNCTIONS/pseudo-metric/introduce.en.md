A pseudo-metric is a generalization of a metric that satisfies all the metric axioms except the condition that $d(x,y) = 0$ implies $x = y$. Thus distinct points may be at zero distance in a pseudo-metric space. Pseudo-metrics arise naturally in analysis and topology, particularly in function spaces. Every pseudo-metric induces a topology via open spheres, and the quotient by the relation $d(x,y) = 0$ yields a metric space.
