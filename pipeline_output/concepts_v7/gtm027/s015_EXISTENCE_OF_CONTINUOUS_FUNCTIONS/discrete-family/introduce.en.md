A discrete family is a strong form of local finiteness: each point has a neighborhood meeting at most one member of the family. A $\sigma$-discrete family is a countable union of discrete families, and similarly for $\sigma$-locally finite. These concepts are central to the general metrization theorem (Bing-Nagata-Smirnov), which characterizes metrizable spaces as regular $T_1$-spaces with a $\sigma$-locally finite (or $\sigma$-discrete) base.
