The Tychonoff Embedding Theorem provides a complete characterization of Tychonoff spaces: a topological space is Tychonoff if and only if it can be embedded as a subspace of a cube. The sufficiency follows because cubes are Tychonoff (being products of the unit interval) and subspaces of Tychonoff spaces are Tychonoff. The necessity uses the Embedding Lemma with the family of all continuous functions to $[0,1]$.
