This lemma provides the fundamental mechanism for constructing a real-valued function from an increasing family of sets indexed by a dense subset of the reals. Given such a family $F_t$, the function $f(x) = \inf\{t : x \in F_t\}$ has the property that its sublevel sets $\{x : f(x) < s\}$ are precisely unions of $F_t$ for $t < s$. This technique is central to the proof of Urysohn's lemma and the construction of continuous functions on normal spaces.
