This theorem shows that any pseudo-metric can be replaced by a bounded one (diameter at most 1) without changing the topology, by truncating distances at 1. The open $r$-spheres for $r < 1$ are identical under both metrics, ensuring the topologies coincide. This construction is technically useful for metrization of countable products and for restricting to spaces of finite diameter.
