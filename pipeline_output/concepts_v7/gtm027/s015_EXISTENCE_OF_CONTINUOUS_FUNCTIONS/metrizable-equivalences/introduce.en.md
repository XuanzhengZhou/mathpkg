This theorem establishes the equivalence of three fundamental properties: having a countable base (second countability), embeddability into the Hilbert cube $Q^\omega$, and separable metrizability. The circle of implications uses the Urysohn metrization theorem for (a) to (b), the metrizability and second countability of $Q^\omega$ for (b) to (c), and the fact that separable metric spaces are second countable for (c) to (a).
