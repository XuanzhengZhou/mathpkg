Let $(X,d)$ be a pseudo-metric space, let $\mathfrak{D}$ be the family of all sets $\overline{\{x\}}$ for $x \in X$, and for $A, B \in \mathfrak{D}$ let $D(A,B) = \operatorname{dist}(A,B) = \inf \{d(x,y) : x \in A, y \in B\}$. Then $(\mathfrak{D}, D)$ is a metric space, and the projection of $X$ onto $\mathfrak{D}$ is both continuous and open relative to the pseudo-metric topology and the metric topology derived from $D$, which coincide.
