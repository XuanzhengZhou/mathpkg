An isometry is a distance-preserving map between pseudo-metric spaces. Isometries are stronger than homeomorphisms: they preserve the full metric structure, not just the topology. On metric spaces, isometries are always injective. A metric invariant is a property preserved by isometries; metric invariants need not be topological invariants (e.g., having infinite diameter is metric but not topological).
