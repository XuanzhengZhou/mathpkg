An \textbf{isometry} between pseudo-metric spaces $(X,d)$ and $(Y,e)$ is a function $f : X \to Y$ such that $e(f(x), f(y)) = d(x,y)$ for all $x, y \in X$. On a metric space, an isometry is necessarily injective, and a surjective isometry between metric spaces is a homeomorphism. The composition of two isometries is an isometry, and the inverse of a bijective isometry is an isometry.

A property which is preserved under isometries is called a \textbf{metric invariant}. A metric invariant need not be a topological invariant.
