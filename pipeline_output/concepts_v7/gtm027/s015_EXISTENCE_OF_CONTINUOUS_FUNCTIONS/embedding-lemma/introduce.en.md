The Embedding Lemma provides a general method for embedding a topological space into a product space via a family of continuous functions. The evaluation map sends each point to its tuple of function values. If the family distinguishes points and closed sets, the map is a homeomorphic embedding. This lemma is fundamental for compactification theory (Stone-Cech) and the Urysohn metrization theorem.
