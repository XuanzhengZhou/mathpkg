This theorem establishes the equivalence of three central properties for topological spaces. (a) implies (b) by the Urysohn Metrization Theorem; (b) implies (c) because $[0,1]^\omega$ is metrizable and second countable; (c) implies (a) because every metrizable space is regular and separability plus metrizability implies second countability (Theorem 11). This clean equivalence is a hallmark of classical point-set topology.
