This theorem establishes that the Tychonoff property is productive: an arbitrary product of Tychonoff spaces is again a Tychonoff space. The proof uses the fact that if $f$ is a Urysohn function for a point and neighborhood in a factor space, then composing with the projection yields a Urysohn function in the product. Since singletons are closed in each factor, they are closed in the product.
