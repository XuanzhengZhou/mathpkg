Every pseudo-metric space can be turned into a metric space by identifying points at distance zero. The quotient map is continuous, open, and closed, and the induced metric on the quotient yields the same topology as the quotient topology. This shows that pseudo-metric spaces differ from metric spaces only by the presence of distinct points at distance zero, which can always be collapsed.
