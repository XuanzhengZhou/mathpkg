A family of functions distinguishes points and closed sets if for every closed set $A$ and every point $x$ outside $A$, some function in the family maps $x$ outside the closure of the image of $A$. This condition ensures that the evaluation map is an open map onto its image, and together with distinguishing points, guarantees that the evaluation map is a homeomorphic embedding.
