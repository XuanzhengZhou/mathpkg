The diameter of a subset of a pseudo-metric space is the supremum of distances between pairs of points in the set. A set has finite diameter if this supremum exists as a real number. Importantly, having finite diameter is a metric property, not a topological one: a space can be homeomorphic to a space of finite diameter even if its original metric has infinite diameter, simply by truncating the metric.
