A family of functions on a topological space distinguishes points if for any two distinct points there is a function in the family that takes different values at them. This condition is exactly what is needed for the evaluation map into a product space to be injective. It is one of the two key conditions (along with distinguishing points and closed sets) in the embedding lemma.
