A normal space is a topological space in which disjoint closed sets can be separated by disjoint open sets. Normality is a key separation axiom that enables the construction of continuous real-valued functions via Urysohn's lemma. Every pseudo-metric space and every compact Hausdorff space is normal. Normality is not hereditary: subspaces of normal spaces need not be normal.
