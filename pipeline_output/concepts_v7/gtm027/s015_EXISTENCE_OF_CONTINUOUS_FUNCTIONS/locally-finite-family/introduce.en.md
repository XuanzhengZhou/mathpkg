A family of subsets is locally finite if every point has a neighborhood intersecting only finitely many members. Local finiteness is a key technical property in general topology: it ensures that closures commute with unions of the family, and it allows local-to-global arguments for partitions of unity and metrization. It is weaker than discreteness but strong enough for many covering and refinement arguments.
