A topological space $(X, \mathfrak{J})$ is \textbf{metrizable} iff there exists a metric $d$ on $X$ such that $\mathfrak{J}$ is the metric topology induced by $d$. The space is \textbf{pseudo-metrizable} iff there exists a pseudo-metric inducing the topology.

A space is metrizable if and only if it is $T_1$ and pseudo-metrizable.
