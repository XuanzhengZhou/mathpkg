A topological space is metrizable if its topology can be generated by a metric. Pseudo-metrizability is the analogous notion using pseudo-metrics. A space is metrizable exactly when it is $T_1$ and pseudo-metrizable, since the $T_1$ condition forces distinct points to have positive distance. Metrization theorems give necessary and sufficient conditions for metrizability.
