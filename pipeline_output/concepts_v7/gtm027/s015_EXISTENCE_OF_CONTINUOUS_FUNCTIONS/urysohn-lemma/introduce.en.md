Urysohn's lemma is a cornerstone of point-set topology. It asserts that in a normal space, any two disjoint closed sets can be separated by a continuous real-valued function taking values in the unit interval. This lemma guarantees the existence of non-trivial continuous functions on normal spaces and is essential for the proof of the Urysohn metrization theorem and the Tietze extension theorem. The function constructed is often called a Urysohn function.
