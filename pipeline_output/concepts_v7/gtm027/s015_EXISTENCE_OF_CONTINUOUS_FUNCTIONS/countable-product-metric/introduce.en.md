The countable product of pseudo-metric spaces is pseudo-metrizable via a weighted sum of coordinate distances. The formula $d(x,y) = \sum 2^{-n} d_n(x_n, y_n)$ yields a pseudo-metric whose induced topology is exactly the product topology. This result is essential for the Urysohn metrization theorem, where a space is embedded into the countable product $Q^\omega$ of unit intervals. The weighting by $2^{-n}$ ensures convergence of the series.
