A Tychonoff space (also called $T_{3\frac{1}{2}}$) is a completely regular $T_1$-space. Tychonoff spaces are precisely those spaces that can be embedded into a cube $[0,1]^\kappa$ for some cardinal $\kappa$. This characterization makes Tychonoff spaces the natural domain for studying rings of continuous functions and compactifications. Every normal $T_1$-space is Tychonoff by Urysohn's lemma, and products and subspaces of Tychonoff spaces are Tychonoff.
