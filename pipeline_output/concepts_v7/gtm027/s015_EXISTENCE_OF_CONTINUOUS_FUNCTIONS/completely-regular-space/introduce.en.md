A completely regular space is one in which points can be separated from closed sets by continuous real-valued functions to the unit interval. This property, also called $T_{3\frac{1}{2}}$, is stronger than regularity: every completely regular space is regular. The family of all continuous functions to $[0,1]$ on a completely regular space distinguishes points and closed sets, enabling an embedding into a cube via the Embedding Lemma.
