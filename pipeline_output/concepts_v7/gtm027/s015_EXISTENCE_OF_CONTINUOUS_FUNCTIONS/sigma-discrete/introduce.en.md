A $\sigma$-discrete family is a countable union of discrete families, where a discrete family is one in which each point has a neighborhood meeting at most one member. This concept, together with $\sigma$-locally-finite families, is central to the Bing-Nagata-Smirnov metrization theorem. Every $\sigma$-discrete family is $\sigma$-locally finite, and in metrizable spaces, every open cover has a $\sigma$-discrete open refinement. The equivalence of $\sigma$-discrete and $\sigma$-locally-finite bases for regular spaces is a key step in the metrization characterization.
