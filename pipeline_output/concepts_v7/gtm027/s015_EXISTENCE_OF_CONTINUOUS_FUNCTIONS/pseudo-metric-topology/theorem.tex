Let $(X,d)$ be a pseudo-metric space. For $x \in X$ and $r > 0$, the \textbf{open $r$-sphere} about $x$ is $\{y \in X : d(x,y) < r\}$. The family of all open spheres is a base for a topology on $X$, called the \textbf{pseudo-metric topology}. Each closed sphere $\{y : d(x,y) \leq r\}$ is closed in this topology.
