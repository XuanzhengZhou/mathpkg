This theorem provides an explicit pseudo-metric for a countable product of pseudo-metric spaces of diameter at most one. The metric is a weighted sum of the coordinate metrics with exponentially decaying weights $2^{-n}$. This construction is the key step in the Urysohn metrization theorem: since the cube $[0,1]^\omega$ is metrizable, any subspace of it is metrizable, and any second-countable regular $T_1$-space embeds into it.
