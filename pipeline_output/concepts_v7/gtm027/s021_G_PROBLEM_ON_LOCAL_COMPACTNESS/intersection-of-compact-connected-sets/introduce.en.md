This theorem establishes that connectedness is preserved under arbitrary intersections of compact sets in a Hausdorff space, provided all finite intersections within the family are connected. The result bridges compactness and connectedness, two of the most fundamental topological properties. The Hausdorff condition is essential: it ensures that compact subsets are closed, which plays a key role in the proof. This is a standard exercise in general topology illustrating how separation axioms interact with covering properties.
