This theorem states that in a Hausdorff space, any dense subspace that is locally compact in its own right must actually be an open subset. Intuitively, local compactness forces each point of the subspace to have a compact neighborhood whose interior in the ambient space is contained in the subspace; density then propagates this openness to the whole subspace. The result highlights a subtle interplay between density, local compactness, and the Hausdorff separation axiom in general topology.
