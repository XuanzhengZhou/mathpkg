The Helly space $H$ consists of all non-decreasing functions from $[0,1]$ to $[0,1]$, topologized as a subspace of the product space $[0,1]^{[0,1]}$. It is compact Hausdorff because it is a closed subspace of the compact Hausdorff product space (Tychonoff's theorem). The Helly space serves as an important example in functional analysis and topology: it satisfies the first axiom of countability (hence is sequentially compact) yet fails to be separable, providing a counterexample to the converse of "separable implies second countable."
