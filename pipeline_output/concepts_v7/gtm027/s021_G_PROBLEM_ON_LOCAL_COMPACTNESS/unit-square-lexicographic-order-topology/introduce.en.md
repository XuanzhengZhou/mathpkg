Problem 5.J presents the unit square with the lexicographic (dictionary) order topology, a classic example in general topology. The space is compact, connected, and Hausdorff with the first countability axiom, yet it is not separable. This provides a counterexample to the natural conjecture that a compact Hausdorff first-countable space should be separable (and hence metrizable). Each vertical slice $\{a\} \times [0,1]$ is both open and closed, giving uncountably many disjoint open sets, which prevents separability. The space illustrates the subtle interplay between order-theoretic and topological properties.
