This theorem provides an equivalent formulation of compactness in terms of nests---families of closed sets linearly ordered by inclusion. A space is compact precisely when every such nest has a non-empty intersection. The proof sketch given by Kelley involves passing to a maximal family of closed sets with the finite intersection property and then extracting a maximal nest within it. An alternative proof can be based on well-ordering. This characterization illuminates the connection between compactness and order-theoretic properties of the lattice of closed sets.
