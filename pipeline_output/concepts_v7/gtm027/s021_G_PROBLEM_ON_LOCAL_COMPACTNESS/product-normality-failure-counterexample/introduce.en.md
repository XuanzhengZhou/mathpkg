Problem 5.K provides a classic counterexample showing that normality is not preserved by products, even under favorable conditions. While the product of a compact Hausdorff space with a paracompact Hausdorff space is paracompact (hence normal), the product of a compact Hausdorff space with a merely normal (even locally compact) space can fail to be normal. The counterexample uses $\Omega'$ (the compact space of ordinals up to and including $\Omega$) and $\Omega_0$ (the locally compact normal space of countable ordinals). This counterexample, due to Tychonoff, was historically important in the development of general topology.
