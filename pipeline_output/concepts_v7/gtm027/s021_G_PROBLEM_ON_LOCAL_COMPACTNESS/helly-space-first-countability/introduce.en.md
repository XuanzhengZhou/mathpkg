The Helly space provides an important counterexample in the hierarchy of countability axioms. Despite being a compact Hausdorff space, it satisfies the first axiom of countability (every point has a countable neighborhood base) and is therefore sequentially compact, yet it fails to be separable. This shows that for compact Hausdorff spaces, first countability does not imply separability, and separability does not imply second countability. The Helly space thus demonstrates the subtlety of relationships between countability and separation properties.
