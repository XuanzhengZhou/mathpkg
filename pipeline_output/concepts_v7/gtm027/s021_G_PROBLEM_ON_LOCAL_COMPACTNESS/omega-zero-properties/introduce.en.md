The space $\Omega_0$ of all countable ordinals (ordinals less than the first uncountable ordinal $\Omega$) with the order topology is a fundamental example in general topology. It exhibits a cluster of properties---local compactness, the Hausdorff separation axiom, first countability, and sequential compactness---while notably failing to be compact. This combination makes $\Omega_0$ a canonical counterexample distinguishing sequential compactness from compactness, and it plays a central role in the study of ordinal spaces and their topological behavior.
