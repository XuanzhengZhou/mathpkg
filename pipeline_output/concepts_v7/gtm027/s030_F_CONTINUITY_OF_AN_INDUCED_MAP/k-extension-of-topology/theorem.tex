Let $(X, \mathcal{J})$ be a Hausdorff space. The $k$-extension of $\mathcal{J}$ is defined to be the family $\mathcal{J}_k$ of all subsets $U$ of $X$ such that $U \cap C$ is open in $C$ for every compact set $C$ (equivalently, $A$ is $\mathcal{J}_k$-closed iff $A \cap C$ is $\mathcal{J}$-compact for every $\mathcal{J}$-compact set $C$).
