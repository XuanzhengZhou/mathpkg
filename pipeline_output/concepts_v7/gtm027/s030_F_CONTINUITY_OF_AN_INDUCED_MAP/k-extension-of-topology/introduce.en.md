The $k$-extension of a Hausdorff topology $\mathcal{J}$ is the family $\mathcal{J}_k$ of subsets whose intersection with every compact set is relatively open. Equivalently, a set is $\mathcal{J}_k$-closed precisely when its intersection with every $\mathcal{J}$-compact set is $\mathcal{J}$-compact. This construction yields the finest topology that agrees with the original topology on all compact subsets.
