If a map $T: X \to Y$ carries each member of a family $\alpha$ of subsets of $X$ into some member of a family $\beta$ of subsets of $Y$, then the induced map $T^*$ between function spaces is uniformly continuous when both function spaces are equipped with the uniformities of uniform convergence on members of $\alpha$ and $\beta$, respectively. This theorem provides the key link between mappings of underlying sets and the continuity properties of the associated composition operators on function spaces. As a special case, the induced map is always uniformly continuous for the uniformity of uniform convergence and always continuous for pointwise convergence.
