If $\phi$ is a continuous homomorphism of $G$ into a compact topological group $H$, then there is a continuous homomorphism $\theta$ of $\alpha[G]$ into $H$ such that $\phi = \theta \circ L$. More generally, for $H$ arbitrary, $\phi$ induces a natural homomorphism $\theta$ of $\alpha[G]$ into $\alpha[H]$ such that $\theta \circ L = L \circ \phi$.
