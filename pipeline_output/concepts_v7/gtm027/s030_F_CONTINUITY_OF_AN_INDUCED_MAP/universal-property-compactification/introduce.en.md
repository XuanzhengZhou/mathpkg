The compactification $\alpha[G]$ satisfies the universal property of the Bohr compactification: any continuous homomorphism from $G$ into a compact group $H$ factors uniquely through the canonical map $L: G \to \alpha[G]$. More generally, any continuous homomorphism $\phi: G \to H$ induces a natural homomorphism between the corresponding compactifications, making the assignment $G \mapsto \alpha[G]$ functorial. This is the group-theoretic analogue of the Stone-Cech compactification.
