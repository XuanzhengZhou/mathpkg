Let $X$ be a set, let $\alpha$ be a cover of $X$ which is directed by $\supset$ (that is, for $A$ and $B$ in $\alpha$ there is $C$ in $\alpha$ such that $C \supset A \cup B$), let $(Y, \mathcal{U})$ be a uniform space, and let $F$ be the family of functions on $X$ to $Y$ with the uniformity $\mathcal{U} \mid \alpha$ of uniform convergence on members of $\alpha$. Suppose that $S$ is a net in $F$ and that for each member $A$ of $\alpha$ there is given a subnet $\{S \circ T_A(m), m \in E_A\}$ of $S$ which converges to a member $s$ of $F$ uniformly on $A$. Then there exists an explicit formula for a subnet of $S$ which converges to $s$ relative to the topology of $\mathcal{U} \mid \alpha$.
