The left translate of a bounded real-valued function $f$ on a topological group $G$ by an element $x \in G$ is the function $L_x(f)$ defined by $(L_x(f))(y) = f(x^{-1}y)$. This defines a left action of $G$ on the space of bounded functions. The orbit of $f$ under this action and its closure properties are central to the theory of almost periodic functions.
