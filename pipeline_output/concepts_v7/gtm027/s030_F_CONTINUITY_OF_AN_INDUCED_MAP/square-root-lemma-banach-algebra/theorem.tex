If the Banach algebra $A$ has a unit $u$ and if $\|x - u\| \leq 1$, then there is an element $y$ in $A$ such that $x = y^2$. Explicitly, $y$ may be taken to be $\sum \{a_n (u - x)^n : n \in \omega\}$, where $a_n$ are the binomial coefficients from the expansion of $(1-t)^{1/2}$. The element $y$ is the limit of polynomials in $x$ and these polynomials may be taken to be without constant coefficients.
