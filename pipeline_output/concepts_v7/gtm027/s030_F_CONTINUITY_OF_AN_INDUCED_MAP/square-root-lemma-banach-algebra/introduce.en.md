In a unital Banach algebra, every element within distance $1$ of the unit has a square root expressible as a norm-convergent power series. The square root $y$ of $x$ is given by the binomial series $y = \sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n (u - x)^n$, which converges absolutely because $\|u - x\| \leq 1$. This lemma is fundamental for the functional calculus in Banach algebras and underpins the proof of the Stone-Weierstrass theorem.
