The star operation that sends a continuous map $F$ to the induced algebra homomorphism $F^*$ is contravariantly functorial: the homomorphism induced by a composition is the reverse composition of the induced homomorphisms. This is the fundamental property that makes the assignment $X \mapsto C(X)$, $F \mapsto F^*$ a contravariant functor from compact Hausdorff spaces to Banach algebras.
