Any linear subspace of $C(X)$ that possesses the two-set property is uniformly dense in $C(X)$. This lemma is a key ingredient in the proof of the Stone-Weierstrass theorem. The proof strategy: given an arbitrary function $g \in C(X)$, one shows that the distance to the subspace can be reduced by a factor of $2/3$ iteratively, forcing the distance to be zero.
