If $g$ is a continuous real function on $\alpha[G]$, then $g \circ L \in A$. If $f \in A$ and $g$ is the function on $\alpha[G]$ defined by $g(R) = R^{-1}(f)(e)$ (where $e$ is the identity of $G$), then $f = g \circ L$ and $g$ is continuous. The family of continuous real functions on $\alpha[G]$ is isometric (and isomorphic) to $A$.
