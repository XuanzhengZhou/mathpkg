The binomial coefficients $a_n$ from the power series expansion of $(1-t)^{1/2}$ satisfy specific combinatorial identities. All coefficients beyond $a_0 = 1$ are negative, the series sums to zero at $t = 1$, and the convolution of the sequence with itself yields a simple pattern: $1, -1, 0, 0, \ldots$. These identities are essential for constructing square roots in Banach algebras via power series.
