Let $X$ be a topological space, let $C(X)$ be the space of all bounded continuous real-valued functions on $X$, and let $C(X)$ have the topology of uniform convergence. A subset $L$ of $C(X)$ is said to have the two-set property iff for closed disjoint subsets $A$ and $B$ of $X$ and for each closed interval $[a,b]$, there is a member $f$ of $L$ such that $f$ maps $X$ into $[a,b]$, $f$ equals $a$ on $A$, and $f$ equals $b$ on $B$.
