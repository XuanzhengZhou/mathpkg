A subset $L$ of $C(X)$ has the two-set property if, for any pair of disjoint closed subsets $A, B$ of $X$ and any closed interval $[a,b]$, there exists a function in $L$ that takes the constant value $a$ on $A$, the constant value $b$ on $B$, and maps the whole space into $[a,b]$. This property captures the ability of $L$ to separate disjoint closed sets with functions that respect prescribed boundary values.
