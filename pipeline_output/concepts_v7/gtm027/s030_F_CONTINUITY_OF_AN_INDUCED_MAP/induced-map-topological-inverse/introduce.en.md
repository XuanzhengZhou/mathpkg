When $F: X \to Y$ is a homeomorphism between compact Hausdorff spaces, the induced algebra homomorphism $F^*: C(Y) \to C(X)$ is an isomorphism, and the homomorphism induced by the inverse map is precisely the inverse homomorphism. This shows that the functor $X \mapsto C(X)$ sends isomorphisms (homeomorphisms) to isomorphisms (algebra isomorphisms).
