A continuous map $F: X \to Y$ between compact Hausdorff spaces is injective if and only if the induced homomorphism $F^*: C(Y) \to C(X)$ is surjective. In the Gelfand duality framework, this means that injective continuous maps correspond to quotient homomorphisms of function algebras.
