A bounded real-valued function on a topological group is left almost periodic if the uniform closure of its left translates forms a compact set in the sup-norm topology. This definition captures the idea that the function's behavior under translation is "almost periodic" in the sense that the set of translates is precompact. The theory of almost periodic functions, pioneered by Harald Bohr, provides a compactification of the group known as the Bohr compactification.
