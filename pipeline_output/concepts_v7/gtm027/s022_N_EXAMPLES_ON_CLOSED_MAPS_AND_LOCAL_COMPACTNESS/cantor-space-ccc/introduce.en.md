The countable chain condition (ccc) is a cardinality restriction on the topology: a space has ccc if every family of pairwise disjoint nonempty open sets is countable. This theorem asserts that every Cantor space $2^A$, regardless of the size of the index set $A$, satisfies ccc. The proof reduces a disjoint family of open sets to basic open sets (determined by finitely many coordinates), then uses an inductive argument on the number of coordinates involved to show that any uncountable disjoint family would lead to a contradiction. A shorter proof uses the observation that $2^A$ with coordinatewise addition modulo 2 forms a compact topological group, and compact groups satisfy ccc via the Haar measure argument.
