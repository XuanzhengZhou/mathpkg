Let $X = \mathbb{R}$ with the usual topology, let $I \subset X$ be the set of integers, and let $\Omega$ be the decomposition of $X$ whose members are the set $I$ and all singletons $\{x\}$ for $x \in X \setminus I$. Then the natural projection $\pi : X \to X/\Omega$ onto the quotient space is continuous and closed, but the quotient space $X/\Omega$ is neither locally compact nor first countable.
