This example uses the space $\Omega_0$ of countable ordinals with the order topology to construct a quotient space that is compact but fails first countability. By identifying a closed uncountable set $A$ whose complement is also uncountable to a single point, the resulting quotient is compact by the properties of $\Omega_0$, yet the quotient topology at the collapsed point fails to have a countable local base. The proof relies on the interlacing lemma (4.E) from Kelley's text, which concerns the structure of closed unbounded subsets of $\Omega_0$.
