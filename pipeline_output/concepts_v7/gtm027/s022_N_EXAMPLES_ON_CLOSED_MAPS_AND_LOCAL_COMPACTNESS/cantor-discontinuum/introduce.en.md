The Cantor discontinuum, also known as the middle third set, is the classical Cantor set constructed from the closed unit interval $[0,1]$ by removing the open middle third $(1/3, 2/3)$, then removing the open middle thirds of the two remaining intervals, and continuing this process ad infinitum. Equivalently, it consists of all numbers in $[0,1]$ whose base-3 (triadic) expansion contains only the digits 0 and 2, with the convention that only irrational triadic expansions are used to ensure uniqueness. The Cantor discontinuum is a fundamental example in topology: it is uncountable, perfect, totally disconnected, compact, and nowhere dense in $\mathbb{R}$.
