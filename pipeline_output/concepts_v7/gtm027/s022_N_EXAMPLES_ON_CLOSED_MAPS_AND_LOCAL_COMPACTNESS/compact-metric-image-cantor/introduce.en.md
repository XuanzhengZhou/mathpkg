This corollary of theorem N(d) specializes the representation of compact Hausdorff spaces to the metric case. Since a compact metric space is second countable, one can replace the general family $F$ of closed-cover pairs with a countable family indexed by a countable base $\{U_n\}$ for the topology. Each $f_n$ codes whether a point belongs to the closure of $U_n$ or its complement, yielding a map from the countable-product Cantor space $2^\omega$ onto $X$. This result is a cornerstone of descriptive set theory, showing that every compact metric space is a continuous image of the classical Cantor space.
