This theorem characterizes compact Hausdorff spaces as continuous images of closed subsets of Cantor spaces. The construction uses a family $F$ of pairs of closed sets that cover $X$; each such pair determines a coordinate in the Cantor cube $2^F$. The map $\phi$ sends a point in the Cantor space to the intersection of the corresponding closed sets, and the preimage of any open set $U \subset X$ consists of those $x$ for which the entire intersection is contained in $U$. This result shows that every compact Hausdorff space is a dyadic space, establishing Cantor spaces as universal objects for the class of compact Hausdorff spaces.
