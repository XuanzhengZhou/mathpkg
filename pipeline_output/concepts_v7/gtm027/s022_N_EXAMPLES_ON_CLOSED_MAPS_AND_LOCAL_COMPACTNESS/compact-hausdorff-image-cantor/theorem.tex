Let $X$ be a compact Hausdorff space. Let $F$ be the family of all functions $f$ on $2 = \{0,1\}$ such that $f(0)$ and $f(1)$ are closed subsets of $X$ and $f(0) \cup f(1) = X$. For $x \in 2^F$ and $f \in F$, define $\phi(x)$ to be the unique point in $\bigcap \{f(x_f) : f \in F\}$ whenever this intersection is nonempty. Then the domain of $\phi$ is a closed subset of the Cantor space $2^F$, and $\phi$ is a continuous surjection from this closed subset onto $X$.
