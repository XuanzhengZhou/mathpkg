A Cantor space, also called a Cantor cube, generalizes the classical Cantor discontinuum to arbitrary index sets. For any set $A$, the Cantor space $2^A$ is the set of all functions from $A$ into the two-point discrete space $\{0,1\}$, equipped with the product topology. When $A$ is countably infinite, $2^\omega$ is homeomorphic to the classical middle-third Cantor set. Cantor spaces are fundamental in set-theoretic topology: they are compact, totally disconnected, perfect Hausdorff spaces, and every compact metric space arises as a continuous image of $2^\omega$.
