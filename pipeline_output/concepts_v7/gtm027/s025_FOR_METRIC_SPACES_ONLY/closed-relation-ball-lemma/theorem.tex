\begin{lemma}[Closed Relation Ball Lemma]
Let $X$ be a complete metric space with metric $d$, let $Y$ be a topological space, and let $R \subset X \times Y$ be a closed relation. For $r > 0$ and $x \in X$, denote by $U_r[x]$ the open ball of radius $r$ about $x$. Then the following critical fact holds: if $A \subset X$ and $v \in R[A]^{-}$, then for every $r > 0$ there exists a set $B \subset X$ of diameter less than $r$ such that $v \in R[B]^{-}$ and $A \cap B \neq \varnothing$.

Consequently, if $v \in R[U_r[x]]^{-}$, then for every $\varepsilon > 0$ it holds that $v \in R[U_{r+\varepsilon}[x]]$.
\end{lemma}
