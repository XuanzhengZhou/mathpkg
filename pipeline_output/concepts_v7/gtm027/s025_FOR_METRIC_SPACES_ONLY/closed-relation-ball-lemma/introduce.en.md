This lemma, formalizing an argument of Banach, is a key technical result about closed relations on complete metric spaces. It states that for a closed relation R from a complete metric space X to a topological space Y, the closure of the image of a set under R can be approximated by images of sets of arbitrarily small diameter intersecting the original set. As a consequence, if a point v lies in the closure of R-image of an open ball of radius r, then v actually lies in the R-image of the closed ball of radius r (or any slightly larger ball). This lemma underpins the completeness preservation theorem, the closed graph theorem, and the open mapping theorem for normed linear spaces.
