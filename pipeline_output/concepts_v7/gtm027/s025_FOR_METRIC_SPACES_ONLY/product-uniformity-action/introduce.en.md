This proposition gives an explicit formula for how a product entourage acts on a relation (a subset of the product). The W(U,V)-neighborhood of a relation R equals the composition V after R after the inverse of U, which unpacks to the union of Cartesian products of U-neighborhoods and V-neighborhoods over all pairs in R. This formula is essential for computing closures in the product uniformity and for analyzing properties of relations between uniform spaces.
