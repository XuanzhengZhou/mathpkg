This is the category-theoretic formulation of Baire's theorem: in a complete pseudo-metric space, the complement of any meager set is dense. Equivalently, a complete pseudo-metric space is of the second category in itself (it is not meager). This formulation is the one most frequently used in functional analysis, where it implies that a complete metric space cannot be written as a countable union of nowhere dense closed sets.
