A subset of a topological space is nowhere dense if its closure contains no nonempty open set. In other words, the interior of its closure is empty. An equivalent characterization is that the complement of its closure is dense. Nowhere dense sets are the building blocks of the Baire category theory; a set is of the first category (meager) exactly when it can be expressed as a countable union of nowhere dense sets. A finite union of nowhere dense sets remains nowhere dense.
