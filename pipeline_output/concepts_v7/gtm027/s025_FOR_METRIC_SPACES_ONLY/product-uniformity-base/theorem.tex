\begin{definition}[Product Uniformity]
Let $(X, \mathfrak{u})$ and $(Y, \mathfrak{v})$ be uniform spaces. For each $U \in \mathfrak{u}$ and each $V \in \mathfrak{v}$, define
\[
W(U, V) = \{((x, y), (u, v)) \in (X \times Y) \times (X \times Y) : (x, u) \in U \text{ and } (y, v) \in V\}.
\]
Then the family of all sets of the form $W(U, V)$ for $U \in \mathfrak{u}$ and $V \in \mathfrak{v}$ is a base for the product uniformity for $X \times Y$.
\end{definition}
