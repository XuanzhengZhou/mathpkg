The product uniformity on the product of two uniform spaces is generated by entourages that act coordinate-wise. For entourages U and V from the two factor spaces, the product entourage W(U,V) relates two pairs exactly when the first coordinates are U-related and the second coordinates are V-related. The collection of all such W(U,V) forms a fundamental system of entourages for the product uniformity. This construction extends naturally to arbitrary products of uniform spaces.
