Let $X$ be a Boolean space. The family $\mathcal{R}$ of all clopen (simultaneously open and closed) subsets of $X$, with the operations of symmetric difference $A \triangle B = (A \sim B) \cup (B \sim A)$ as addition and intersection $A \cap B$ as multiplication, forms a Boolean ring. This ring is called the \textbf{characteristic ring} of the Boolean space $X$.
