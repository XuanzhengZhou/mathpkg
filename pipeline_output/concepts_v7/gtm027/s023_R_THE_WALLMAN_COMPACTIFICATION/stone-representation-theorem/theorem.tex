\textbf{Stone Representation Theorem.} Every Boolean algebra (equivalently, every Boolean ring) is isomorphic to a field of sets. More precisely, if $B$ is a Boolean algebra, let $S(B)$ be the set of all ultrafilters on $B$, topologized by taking as a base all sets of the form $\{U \in S(B) : b \in U\}$ for $b \in B$. Then $S(B)$ is a Boolean space, and $B$ is isomorphic to the Boolean algebra of all clopen subsets of $S(B)$ via the map $b \mapsto \{U \in S(B) : b \in U\}$.
