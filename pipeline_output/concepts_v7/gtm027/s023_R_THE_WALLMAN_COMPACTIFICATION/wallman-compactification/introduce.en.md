The Wallman compactification of a $T_1$-space $X$ is constructed using maximal families of closed sets with the finite intersection property (ultrafilters in the lattice of closed sets). The points of $w(X)$ are these maximal families, and the embedding $\phi(x)$ sends each point to the family of all closed sets containing it. The Wallman compactification has the advantage that it preserves finite intersections and unions of the original topology, making it useful for dimension theory and Cech homology.
