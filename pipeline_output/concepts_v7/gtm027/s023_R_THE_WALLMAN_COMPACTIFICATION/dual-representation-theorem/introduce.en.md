Stone's duality theorem establishes a profound connection between algebra and topology: the category of Boolean algebras is dually equivalent to the category of Boolean spaces (compact Hausdorff totally disconnected spaces). Every Boolean algebra can be represented as the algebra of clopen subsets of a uniquely determined Boolean space (its Stone space), and every Boolean space is the Stone space of its algebra of clopen subsets. Kelley presents this as an extension of the Wallman compactification framework, where the Wallman space of a discrete $T_1$-space recovers the Stone-Cech compactification of that discrete space.
