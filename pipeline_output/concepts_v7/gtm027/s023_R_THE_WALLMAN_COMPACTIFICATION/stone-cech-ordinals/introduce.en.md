This example shows that the Stone-Cech compactification of the space $\Omega_0$ of all countable ordinals is precisely the one-point compactification obtained by adding the first uncountable ordinal $\Omega$. The proof uses the fact that every bounded continuous real-valued function on $\Omega_0$ is eventually constant. This result is a classic illustration of compactification theory in the context of ordinal spaces.
