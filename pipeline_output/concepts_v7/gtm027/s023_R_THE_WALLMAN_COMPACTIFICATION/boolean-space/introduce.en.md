A Boolean space (also called a Stone space) is a compact Hausdorff space possessing a base consisting of clopen (simultaneously open and closed) sets. Equivalently, it is a totally disconnected compact Hausdorff space. Boolean spaces are the topological side of Stone duality: the category of Boolean algebras is dually equivalent to the category of Boolean spaces. Every Boolean space arises as the space of ultrafilters of some Boolean algebra. The Wallman compactification constructs a Boolean space from any $T_1$-space by taking the Wallman topology on the set of maximal closed ultrafilters.
