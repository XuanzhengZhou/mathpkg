This theorem states that connectedness is a topological property preserved by continuous maps. If a topological space cannot be partitioned into two disjoint nonempty open sets, then its continuous image inherits this same indivisibility. The result is fundamental in topology and is often used to prove that certain spaces are connected by exhibiting them as continuous images of known connected spaces (such as intervals). It also underlies the intermediate value property: the continuous image of an interval in $\mathbb{R}$ is an interval.
