Zorn's Lemma is the most widely cited equivalent of the Axiom of Choice. It states that in a poset where every chain has an upper bound, there exists a maximal element. The proof follows from the Kuratowski Lemma: take a maximal chain and an upper bound of it, which must be maximal. Zorn's Lemma is used throughout algebra (existence of maximal ideals, bases for vector spaces) and topology (Tychonoff's theorem, compactifications).
