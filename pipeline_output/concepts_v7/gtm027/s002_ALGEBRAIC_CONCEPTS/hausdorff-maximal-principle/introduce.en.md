The Hausdorff Maximal Principle states that every nest (chain of sets under inclusion) in a family can be extended to a maximal nest. Kelley takes this as an axiom and derives the other equivalents of the Axiom of Choice from it (Theorem 25). The principle was independently used by Kuratowski, R.L. Moore, and Zorn in similar forms. It is equivalent to the Axiom of Choice.
