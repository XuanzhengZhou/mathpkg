A choice function for a family $\alpha$ of sets selects one element from each member of $\alpha$. The existence of a choice function for every family of non-empty sets is precisely the Axiom of Choice. Kelley presents this to motivate the equivalence results in Theorem 25, where the Axiom of Choice, Zorn's Lemma, and the Well-Ordering Principle are shown to be equivalent.
