A group homomorphism is a structure-preserving map between groups: the image of a product is the product of the images. Homomorphisms are the morphisms in the category of groups and encode the notion of groups being "alike." Kelley also uses the term "representation" synonymously. Every homomorphism has a kernel (an invariant subgroup) and an image (a subgroup of the codomain).
