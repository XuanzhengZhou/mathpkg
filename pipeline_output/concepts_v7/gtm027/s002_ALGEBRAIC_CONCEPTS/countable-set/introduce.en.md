A countable set is one that can be injected into $\omega$; it is either finite or countably infinite. Countability is a central notion in topology (first-countable and second-countable spaces), analysis (countable dense subsets), and set theory. Kelley proves several fundamental theorems about countable sets, including that countable unions of countable sets are countable.
