The supremum of a set is its least upper bound -- the smallest element that is greater than or equal to every element of the set. In an order-complete field like $\mathbb{R}$, every non-empty bounded-above set has a supremum. This property is used throughout analysis and is equivalent to the convergence of bounded monotone sequences.
