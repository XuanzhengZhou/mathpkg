A ring is an algebraic structure with two binary operations, addition and multiplication. The additive structure is an abelian group, while multiplication is associative and distributes over addition. Rings generalize integers, polynomials, and matrices. Kelley uses rings as a stepping stone to linear spaces (vector spaces), which are fundamental to functional analysis and topology.
