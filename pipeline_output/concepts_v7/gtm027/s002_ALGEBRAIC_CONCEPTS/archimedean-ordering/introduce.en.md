An ordered field is Archimedean if the natural numbers are cofinal -- every element is exceeded by some integer. The real numbers are Archimedean (as proved by Kelley from order-completeness). This property is used in the construction of b-adic expansions and is equivalent to the statement that $1/n \to 0$.
