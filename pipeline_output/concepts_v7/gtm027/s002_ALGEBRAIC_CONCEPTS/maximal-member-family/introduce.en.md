A maximal member of a family of sets is one not properly contained in any other member. Existence of maximal members is not guaranteed in general and requires additional hypotheses, such as those in the Hausdorff Maximal Principle or Zorn's Lemma. This concept is central to many non-constructive existence proofs in mathematics.
