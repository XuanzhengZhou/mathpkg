Theorem 14 establishes that every real number can be represented by a $b$-adic expansion. The map $\bar{r}$ from expansions to real numbers is order-preserving, unique, and surjective. It is injective on non-terminating expansions ($E \setminus R$). The proof uses the density of $b$-adic rationals and order-completeness. The theorem shows that the $b$-adic expansion system provides a complete representation of the real numbers.
