The kernel of a homomorphism $f \colon G \to H$ is the preimage of the identity element in $H$. It is always a normal subgroup of $G$. The kernel measures how far a homomorphism is from being injective: $f$ is one-to-one if and only if its kernel is trivial. This concept generalizes to many algebraic structures.
