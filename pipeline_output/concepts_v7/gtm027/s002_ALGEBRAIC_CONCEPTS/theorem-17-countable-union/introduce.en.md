Theorem 17 states that a countable union of countable sets is countable. The proof reduces to showing $\omega \times \omega$ is countable, accomplished by enumerating along diagonals $B_n = \{(p,q) : p+q = n\}$, each containing $n+1$ points. This result is crucial for showing that the rationals are countable and that many topological constructions preserve countability.
