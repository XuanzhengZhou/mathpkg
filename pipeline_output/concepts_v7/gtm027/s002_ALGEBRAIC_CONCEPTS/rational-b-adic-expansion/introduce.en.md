A rational $b$-adic expansion terminates: it has only finitely many non-zero digits. Each rational expansion corresponds to a sum $\sum a_p b^{-p}$ with only finitely many non-zero terms, yielding a rational number. The set $R$ of rational expansions is countable, while the full set $E$ of all expansions is uncountable (as shown in Theorem 18).
