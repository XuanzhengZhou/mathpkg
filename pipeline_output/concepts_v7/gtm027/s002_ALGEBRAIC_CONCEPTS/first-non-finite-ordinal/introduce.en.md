The first non-finite ordinal $\omega$ is the smallest ordinal with infinitely many predecessors. Kelley notes a key feature of the von Neumann ordinal construction: this ordinal literally is the set of non-negative integers. Thus the symbol $\omega$ serves double duty as both the set of natural numbers and the first infinite ordinal, elegantly unifying the two perspectives.
