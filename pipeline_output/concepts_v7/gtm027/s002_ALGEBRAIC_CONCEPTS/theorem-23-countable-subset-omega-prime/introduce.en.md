Theorem 23 states that every countable subset of $\Omega'$ not containing the maximum $\Omega$ has supremum strictly less than $\Omega$. This reflects the fact that $\Omega$ has uncountable cofinality—it cannot be reached by a countable sequence. This property is essential for constructing topological counterexamples where countable operations remain within the "nice" part of $\Omega'$.
