A set is countably infinite if there exists a bijection between it and the natural numbers $\omega$. The integers, the rationals, and the algebraic numbers are all countably infinite. Countably infinite sets have the smallest infinite cardinality, $\aleph_0$. Together with finite sets, they form the countable sets.
