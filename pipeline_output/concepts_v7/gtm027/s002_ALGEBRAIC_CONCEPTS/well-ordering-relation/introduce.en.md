A well-ordering is a linear ordering where every non-empty subset has a least element. This is a stronger condition than being totally ordered: $\mathbb{Z}$ is totally ordered but not well-ordered. The Well-Ordering Principle (equivalent to the Axiom of Choice) asserts that every set admits a well-ordering. Well-orderings are the backbone of transfinite induction.
