A $b$-adic expansion represents a real number as a sequence of digits in $\{0,1,\dots,b-1\}$ indexed by integers, with only finitely many non-zero digits before the "decimal" point. Kelley uses these to construct the real numbers from rational expansions via order-completeness. Special cases include dyadic ($b=2$), triadic ($b=3$), and decimal ($b=10$) expansions.
