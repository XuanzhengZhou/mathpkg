For each rational $b$-adic expansion $a$, the sum $\sum \{a_p b^{-p} : p \text{ an integer}\}$ (which has only finitely many non-zero terms) is the real number $r(a)$. A real number of this form is a \textbf{$b$-adic rational}. These numbers are precisely those of the form $q b^{-p}$ for integers $p$ and $q$.
