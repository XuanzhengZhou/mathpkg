A $b$-adic rational is a real number of the form $q b^{-p}$ with $p$ and $q$ integers. These correspond to terminating $b$-adic expansions. The $b$-adic rationals are dense in the real numbers, a fact used in Theorem 14 to show that every real number has a unique non-terminating $b$-adic expansion.
