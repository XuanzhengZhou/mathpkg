Tukey's Lemma states that every non-empty family of finite character has a maximal member. The proof uses the Maximal Principle: the union of a nest in a finite-character family is again in the family. Applications include: every vector space has a basis (maximal linearly independent set), and every group contains a maximal abelian subgroup. Kelley notes the common form given may not directly imply all cited applications.
