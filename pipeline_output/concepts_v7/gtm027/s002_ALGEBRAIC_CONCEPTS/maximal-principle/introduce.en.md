The Maximal Principle guarantees the existence of a maximal member in a family of sets where every nest has an upper bound in the family. It follows from the Hausdorff Maximal Principle by taking the union of a maximal nest. The principle is widely applicable; for instance, in a ring, the set of proper ideals satisfies the condition, yielding existence of maximal ideals.
