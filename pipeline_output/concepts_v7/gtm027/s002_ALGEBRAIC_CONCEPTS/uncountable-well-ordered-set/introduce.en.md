Summary 22 asserts the existence of an uncountable well-ordered set $\Omega'$ with a greatest element $\Omega$, where every proper initial segment is countable. This set (essentially $\omega_1 + 1$, the first uncountable ordinal plus its supremum) is a crucial source of counterexamples in topology, such as the long line and the Tychonoff plank. The construction is detailed in the appendix.
