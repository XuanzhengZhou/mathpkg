The infimum of a set is its greatest lower bound -- the largest element that is less than or equal to every element of the set. In the real numbers, every non-empty bounded-below set has an infimum. The concepts of supremum and infimum are dual, and Kelley notes that order-completeness via suprema implies the existence of infima (by 0.9).
