Theorem 18 contains two results. First, the family of all finite subsets of a countable set is countable (proved by writing each finite subset as a finite union of finite sets). Second, the full power set of a countably infinite set is uncountable, proved by Cantor's diagonal argument using dyadic expansions: the map $a_p = 1 - f(p)_p$ produces a dyadic expansion not in the enumeration. This is the classic proof that $2^{\aleph_0} > \aleph_0$.
