Theorem 13 establishes the legitimacy of recursive definitions on the natural numbers. Given an initial value $a$ and a rule $F$ that determines the value at $p+1$ from all previous values, there exists a unique function $f$ satisfying the recurrence. Kelley proves this using the family of all "fragment" functions. This theorem justifies definitions like exponentiation $b^p$ and is used systematically to develop elementary properties of real numbers.
