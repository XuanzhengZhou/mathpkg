A chain in a partially ordered set is a subset that is totally ordered by the induced order. Chains are the "linearly ordered" subsets and play the central role in Zorn's Lemma: if every chain has an upper bound, then the poset has a maximal element. The concept generalizes nests of sets (where the order is inclusion).
