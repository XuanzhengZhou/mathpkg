A linear space (or vector space) over a field $F$ is an abelian group equipped with scalar multiplication by elements of $F$. Kelley defines this via a ring homomorphism from $F$ into the endomorphism ring of the abelian group, a formulation that elegantly encodes all the usual vector space axioms. Linear spaces are fundamental to functional analysis, the study of topological vector spaces, and much of modern mathematics.
