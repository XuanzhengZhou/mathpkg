The cartesian product of a family of sets $\{X_a\}_{a \in A}$ is the set of all choice functions $x \colon A \to \bigcup_a X_a$ with $x(a) \in X_a$. This generalizes the familiar finite product. When all $X_a = Y$, the product is $Y^A$, the set of all functions from $A$ to $Y$. The product is fundamental for the product topology in Kelley's later chapters.
