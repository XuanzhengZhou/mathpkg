The Well-Ordering Principle asserts that every set admits a well-ordering. Kelley gives Zermelo's proof using a choice function: construct an ordering inductively where the first element after any initial segment $A$ is $c(X \setminus A)$. The proof shows that the class of all such reflexive linear orderings has a unique maximal element whose domain is the whole set. This principle is equivalent to the Axiom of Choice and enables transfinite induction on arbitrary sets.
