A hereditary property is a topological property that is preserved when passing to subspaces. If a space has a hereditary property, then every subspace of that space also enjoys the same property. For example, the Hausdorff property ($T_2$) and first countability are hereditary, while normality is not, as demonstrated by the Tychonoff Plank counterexample.
