A productive property is a topological property that is preserved under arbitrary products. If every factor space in a product has a productive property, then the product space itself also enjoys that property. The Tychonoff theorem famously establishes that compactness is productive. Other productive properties include the Hausdorff property, regularity, and complete regularity, while normality and separability are not productive.
