This theorem gives the classical epsilon-delta characterization of continuity for functions between pseudo-metric spaces. It states that a function is continuous precisely when points that are close in the domain (as measured by $d$) map to points that are close in the codomain (as measured by $e$). This extends the familiar definition from real analysis to the general setting of pseudo-metric spaces, where the distance function need not separate distinct points.
