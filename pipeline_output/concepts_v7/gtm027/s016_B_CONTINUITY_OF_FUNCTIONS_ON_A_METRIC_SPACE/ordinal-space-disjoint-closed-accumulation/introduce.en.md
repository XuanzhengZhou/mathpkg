This proposition states a separation-like property of the ordinal space $\Omega_0$: given two disjoint closed subsets, the point $\Omega$ (the first uncountable ordinal, often denoted $\omega_1$) cannot be an accumulation point of both sets simultaneously. This result is used in establishing the normality of ordinal spaces and highlights the special role of the endpoint $\Omega$ in the topology of $\Omega_0$.
