Let $(X, d)$ be a metric space of finite diameter, and let $\alpha$ be the family of all closed subsets with the Hausdorff metric $d'$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $(\alpha, d')$ is a metric space.
\item The map which carries each $x$ in $X$ into $\{x\}$ in $\alpha$ is an isometry of $X$ onto a subspace of $\alpha$.
\end{enumerate}
