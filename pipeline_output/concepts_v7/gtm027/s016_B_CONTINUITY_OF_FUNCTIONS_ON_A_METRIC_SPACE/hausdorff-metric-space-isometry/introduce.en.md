This theorem establishes two fundamental properties of the Hausdorff metric. First, the family of all closed subsets of a metric space of finite diameter, equipped with the Hausdorff metric $d'$, is itself a metric space. Second, the original space embeds isometrically into this new space via the singleton map $x \mapsto \{x\}$, meaning the distance between points is preserved when they are viewed as singleton closed sets.
