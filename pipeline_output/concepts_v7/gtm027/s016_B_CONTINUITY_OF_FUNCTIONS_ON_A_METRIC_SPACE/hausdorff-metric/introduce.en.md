The Hausdorff metric $d'$ measures the distance between two closed subsets of a metric space of finite diameter. It is defined as the infimum of all radii $r$ such that each set is contained in the $r$-neighborhood of the other. This metric generalizes the notion of distance from points to sets, and it is not the same as the point-to-set distance function used elsewhere in the text.
