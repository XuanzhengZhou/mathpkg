This proposition addresses the relationship between products and quotients in topology. Given an equivalence relation $S$ on $X$, there is a natural map from $X \times X$ to $(X/S) \times (X/S)$. The question is whether this map is open, which is equivalent to asking if the product of quotient spaces is homeomorphic to the quotient of the product. The counterexample shows that this map fails to be open in general, meaning the operations of taking products and taking quotients do not commute.
