Let $f$ be defined on a subset $X_0$ of a topological space $X$ with values in a Hausdorff space $Y$. Then $f$ is continuous at a point $x \in X$ if and only if $x$ belongs to the closure $\overline{X}_0$ and there exists a point $y \in Y$ such that for every neighborhood $V$ of $y$, the inverse image $f^{-1}(V)$ is the intersection of $X_0$ with some neighborhood of $x$.

Equivalently, $f$ is continuous at $x$ if and only if $x \in \overline{X}_0$ and whenever $S$ and $T$ are nets in $X_0$ converging to $x$, the nets $f \circ S$ and $f \circ T$ converge to the same point of $Y$.
