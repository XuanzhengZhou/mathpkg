Let $f$ be a non-negative real-valued function on a topological space $X$, let $\mathbb{R}$ carry the usual topology, and let
\[
G = \{\, (x, t) \in X \times \mathbb{R} : 0 \leq t \leq f(x) \,\}
\]
have the relativized product topology of $X \times \mathbb{R}$. Let $\mathfrak{D}$ be the decomposition of $G$ into \emph{vertical slices}, that is, sets of the form $(\{x\} \times \mathbb{R}) \cap G$. If the decomposition $\mathfrak{D}$ is upper semi-continuous, then $f$ is upper semi-continuous.
