The upper topology on the real numbers is the topology generated by all open rays extending to negative infinity. It is a non-Hausdorff topology that plays a natural role in the theory of upper semi-continuous functions: a function is upper semi-continuous with respect to the usual topology if and only if it is continuous when the codomain carries the upper topology.
