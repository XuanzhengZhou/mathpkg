A quotient map is a continuous surjection that identifies points to create a quotient topology. While every quotient map sends saturated open sets to open sets, not every quotient map is open or closed. Open and closed quotient maps are special cases where the map additionally preserves openness or closedness of arbitrary (not necessarily saturated) sets. These maps are important in the study of product spaces (products of quotient maps need not be quotient maps, but products of open quotient maps are) and in the theory of upper semi-continuous decompositions.
