This proposition collects the fundamental algebraic properties of continuous real-valued functions on a topological space. Scalar multiplication, absolute value, sums, differences, products, quotients (where defined), and pointwise maxima and minima all preserve continuity. These facts follow from continuity of the corresponding operations on the real numbers and the fact that composition of continuous functions is continuous.
