If $f$ is a bounded real-valued function on a topological space $X$, then there exists a smallest upper semi-continuous function $f^{-}$ such that $f^{-} \geq f$ pointwise. Explicitly, if $\mathcal{V}$ denotes the family of neighborhoods of a point $x$, then
\[
f^{-}(x) = \lim_{V \in \mathcal{V}} \sup \{\, f(y) : y \in V \,\},
\]
where the limit is taken over the net of neighborhoods directed by reverse inclusion.
