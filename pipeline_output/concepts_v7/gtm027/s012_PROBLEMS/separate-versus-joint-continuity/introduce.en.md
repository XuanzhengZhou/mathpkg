Joint continuity of a function of two variables always implies separate continuity in each variable, but the converse fails in general. The standard counterexample $xy/(x^2+y^2)$ is separately continuous yet discontinuous at the origin when approached along different paths. This distinction is important in analysis and topology, particularly when studying function spaces and compact-open topology.
