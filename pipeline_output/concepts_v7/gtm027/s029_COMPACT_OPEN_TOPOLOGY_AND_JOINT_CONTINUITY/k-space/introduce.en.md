A $k$-space (or compactly generated space) is one in which the topology is determined by the family of all compact subsets: a set is closed precisely when its intersection with each closed compact set is closed. The two most important examples are locally compact Hausdorff spaces and first countable Hausdorff spaces. For $k$-spaces, continuity of a function is equivalent to continuity on each compact set, making $k$-spaces the natural setting for the Ascoli theorem.
