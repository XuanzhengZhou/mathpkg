Given a family $F$ of functions from a set $X$ to a uniform space $(Y,\mathcal{V})$, the uniformity of uniform convergence is the largest uniformity considered on function spaces. It is defined by the base $\{W(V) : V \in \mathcal{V}\}$ where $W(V) = \{(f,g) : (f(x),g(x)) \in V \text{ for all } x \in X\}$. This uniformity is larger than the uniformity of pointwise convergence, and a net converges relative to it if and only if it is a Cauchy net that converges pointwise. If the range space is complete, so is the function space under this uniformity.
