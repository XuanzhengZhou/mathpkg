A topology for a function family is jointly continuous on compacta if the evaluation map is continuous on $F \times K$ for every compact subset $K$ of the domain. This concept is the key bridge between joint continuity and the compact-open topology: every topology that is jointly continuous on compacta is larger than the compact-open topology, and under mild hypotheses the compact-open topology itself is jointly continuous on compacta.
