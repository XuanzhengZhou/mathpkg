A topology for a function family $F$ is jointly continuous on a subset $A$ of the domain if the restriction of the evaluation map $P$ to $F \times A$ is continuous. Care is needed: this condition does not mean that $P$ is continuous at the points of $F \times A$, but rather that the restricted map $P \mid (F \times A)$ is continuous. This notion provides a useful relaxation of full joint continuity and is central to characterizing the compact-open topology.
