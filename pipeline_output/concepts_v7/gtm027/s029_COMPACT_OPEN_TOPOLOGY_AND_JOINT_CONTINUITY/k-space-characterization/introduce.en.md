Theorem 7.13 establishes that the two most important classes of topological spaces — locally compact Hausdorff spaces and first countable Hausdorff spaces — are $k$-spaces. The proof argues by contrapositive: if $B$ is non-closed with accumulation point $x \notin B$, then in the locally compact case a compact neighborhood of $x$ witnesses non-closedness of $B \cap C$, and in the first countable case a convergent sequence together with its limit provides the required compact set.
