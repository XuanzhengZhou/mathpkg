Let $C$ be the family of all continuous functions on a $k$-space $X$ which is either Hausdorff or regular to a Hausdorff uniform space $Y$, and let $C$ have the topology of uniform convergence on compacta. Then a subfamily $F$ of $C$ is compact if and only if

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $F$ is closed in $C$,
\item[(b)] the closure of $F[x]$ is compact for each $x$ in $X$, and
\item[(c)] $F$ is equicontinuous on every compact subset of $X$.
\end{enumerate}
