Theorem 7.9 establishes two important facts. Part (a) is the classical result that a uniform limit of continuous functions is continuous: the set of non-continuous functions is open in the u.c. topology, so the continuous functions form a closed subset. Part (b) shows that the evaluation map is jointly continuous when $F$ carries the u.c. topology. The proof of (a) uses a three-entourage argument: if $f$ is discontinuous at $x$, any function sufficiently close to $f$ in the u.c. uniformity is also discontinuous at $x$.
