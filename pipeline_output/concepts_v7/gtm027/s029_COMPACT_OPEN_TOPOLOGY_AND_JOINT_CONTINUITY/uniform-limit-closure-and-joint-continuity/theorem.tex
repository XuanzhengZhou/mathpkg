Let $F$ be a family of continuous functions on a topological space $X$ to a uniform space $(Y, \mathcal{V})$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $F$ is closed in the space of all functions on $X$ to $Y$ relative to the topology of uniform convergence. That is, the uniform limit of continuous functions is continuous.

\item[(b)] The map $P : F \times X \to Y$ defined by $P(f,x) = f(x)$ is continuous when $F$ is given the topology of uniform convergence.
\end{enumerate}
