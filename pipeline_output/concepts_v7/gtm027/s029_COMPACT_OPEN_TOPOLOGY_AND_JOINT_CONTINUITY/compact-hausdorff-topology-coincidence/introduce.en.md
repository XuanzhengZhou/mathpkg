This corollary establishes that when a family of functions $F$ into a Hausdorff space $Y$ is compact under a topology that is jointly continuous on compacta, and $Y$ is Hausdorff, then all natural function space topologies coincide: the given topology equals the compact-open topology, which equals the pointwise topology. The proof uses the fact that for Hausdorff $Y$, the pointwise topology is Hausdorff, and a continuous bijection from a compact space to a Hausdorff space is a homeomorphism. This is a powerful uniqueness result for function space topologies under compactness hypotheses.
