Let $F$ be a family of functions on a set $X$ to a uniform space $(Y, \mathfrak{U})$ and let $\mathfrak{U}$ be the uniformity of uniform convergence. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] The uniformity $\mathfrak{U}$ is generated by the family of all pseudo-metrics of the form
$$d^*(f,g) = \sup \{d(f(x),g(x)) : x \in X\},$$
where $d$ is a bounded member of the gage of $(Y,\mathfrak{U})$.

\item[(b)] A net $\{f_n, n \in D\}$ in $F$ converges uniformly to $g$ if and only if it is a Cauchy net relative to $\mathfrak{U}$ and $\{f_n(x), n \in D\}$ converges to $g(x)$ for each $x$ in $X$.

\item[(c)] If $(Y,\mathfrak{U})$ is complete, then the uniform space $(F,\mathfrak{U})$ is also complete.
\end{enumerate}
