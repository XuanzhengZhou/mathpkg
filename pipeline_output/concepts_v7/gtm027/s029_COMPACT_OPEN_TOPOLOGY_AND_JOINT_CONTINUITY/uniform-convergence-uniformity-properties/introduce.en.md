Theorem 7.8 gives three fundamental properties of the uniformity of uniform convergence. Part (a) expresses the gage using suprema of bounded pseudo-metrics from $Y$. Part (b) states that uniform convergence of a net is equivalent to being Cauchy plus pointwise convergence. Part (c) establishes that completeness of $(Y,\mathfrak{U})$ implies completeness of the function space under the u.c. uniformity. These properties parallel those of the pointwise convergence uniformity (Theorem 7.3) but with stronger conclusions.
