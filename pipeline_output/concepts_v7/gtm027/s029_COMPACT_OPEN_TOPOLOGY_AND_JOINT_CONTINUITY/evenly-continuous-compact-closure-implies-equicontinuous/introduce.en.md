Theorem 7.23 provides the converse to Theorem 7.22 under a compactness hypothesis: if $F$ is evenly continuous and $F[x]$ has compact closure, then $F$ is equicontinuous at $x$. The proof uses even continuity to cover the compact closure $F[x]^{-}$ with finitely many neighborhoods $W_i$ of points $y_i$, each paired with a neighborhood $V_i$ of $x$. The intersection of the $V_i$ then yields the equicontinuity condition. Together, Theorems 7.22 and 7.23 show that equicontinuity and even continuity are equivalent when $F[x]$ has compact closure.
