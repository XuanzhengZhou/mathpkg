Theorem 7.6 provides necessary and sufficient conditions for a family $F$ of functions to be compact under the compact-open topology. The conditions are: closedness in $C$ (the family of functions continuous on compacta), pointwise compact closure of $F[x]$ for each $x$, and joint continuity on compacta of the compact-open topology for $F$. This theorem is a precursor to the Ascoli theorem and shows that when $F$ is pointwise compact and $Y$ is Hausdorff, the compact-open topology coincides with the pointwise topology.
