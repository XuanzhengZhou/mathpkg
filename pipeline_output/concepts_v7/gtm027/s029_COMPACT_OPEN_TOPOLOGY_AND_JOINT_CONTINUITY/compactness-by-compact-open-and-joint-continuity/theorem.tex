Let $X$ be a topological space which is either regular or Hausdorff, let $Y$ be a Hausdorff space, let $C$ be the family of all functions on $X$ to $Y$ which are continuous on each compact subset of $X$, and let $\mathcal{C}$ and $\wp$ be respectively the compact open and the pointwise topologies. Then a subfamily $F$ of $C$ is $\mathcal{C}$-compact if and only if

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $F$ is $\mathcal{C}$-closed in $C$,
\item[(b)] $F[x]^{-}$ is compact for each $x$ in $X$, and
\item[(c)] the topology $\mathcal{C}$ for $F$ is jointly continuous on compacta.
\end{enumerate}
