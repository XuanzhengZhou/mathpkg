Theorem 7.17 is the classical Ascoli theorem: a family of continuous functions on a regular locally compact space to a uniform space, endowed with the topology of uniform convergence on compacta (equivalently, the compact-open topology), is compact precisely when it is closed, pointwise bounded (each $F[x]$ has compact closure), and equicontinuous. The proof combines earlier results: equicontinuity makes pointwise topology jointly continuous (7.15), compactness in jointly continuous implies equicontinuity (7.16), and the Tychonoff theorem controls pointwise compactness.
