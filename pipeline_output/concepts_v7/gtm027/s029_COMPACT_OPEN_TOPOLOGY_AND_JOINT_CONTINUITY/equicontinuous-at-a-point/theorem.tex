Let $F$ be a family of maps of a topological space $X$ into a uniform space $(Y, \mathcal{V})$. The family $F$ is **equicontinuous at a point** $x \in X$ iff for each member $V$ of $\mathcal{V}$ there is a neighborhood $U$ of $x$ such that $f[U] \subset V[f(x)]$ for every member $f$ of $F$. Equivalently, $F$ is equicontinuous at $x$ iff
$$\bigcap \{f^{-1}[V[f(x)]] : f \in F\}$$
is a neighborhood of $x$ for each $V$ in $\mathcal{V}$.
