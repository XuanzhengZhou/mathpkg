Let $F$ be a family of functions on a set $X$ to a uniform space $(Y, \mathcal{V})$ and let $A$ be a subset of $X$. Then the uniformity $\sigma_A$ of pointwise convergence on $A$ has the properties:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] The family of all sets of the form $\{(f, g): (f(x), g(x)) \in V\}$ for $V$ in $\mathcal{V}$ and $x$ in $A$ is a subbase for the $\sigma_A$ uniformity.
\item[(b)] The topology of the $\sigma_A$ uniformity is the topology of pointwise convergence on $A$.
\item[(c)] A net $\{f_n, n \in D\}$ is a Cauchy net if and only if $\{f_n(x), n \in D\}$ is a Cauchy net for each $x$ in $A$.
\item[(d)] If $(Y, \mathcal{V})$ is complete and $R[F]$ is closed in $Y^A$ relative to pointwise convergence on $A$, then $F$ is complete relative to the $\sigma_A$ uniformity.
\end{enumerate}
