Given a family $F$ of functions from a topological space $X$ to a topological space $Y$, the notation $W(K, U)$ denotes the set of all functions in $F$ that map the subset $K$ of $X$ into the subset $U$ of $Y$. When $K$ is compact and $U$ is open, these sets form a subbasis for the compact-open topology on $F$. The fact that singletons are compact makes comparison with the pointwise topology straightforward, since $W(\{x\}, U) = \{f : f(x) \in U\}$.
