This corollary to Theorem 7.5 states that for continuous functions on a locally compact regular space, the compact-open topology is precisely the smallest jointly continuous topology. This follows by combining three facts: on a locally compact space, joint continuity equals joint continuity on compacta (previous corollary); the compact-open topology is jointly continuous on compacta (Theorem 7.5); and every jointly continuous on compacta topology is larger than the compact-open topology (Theorem 7.5). While there is generally no smallest jointly continuous topology, the compact-open topology fills this role when the domain is locally compact and regular.
