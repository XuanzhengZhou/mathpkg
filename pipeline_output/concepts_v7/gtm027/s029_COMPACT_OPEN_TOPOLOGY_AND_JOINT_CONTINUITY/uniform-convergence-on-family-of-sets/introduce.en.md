Uniform convergence on members of a family $\alpha$ of subsets of the domain interpolates between pointwise convergence (when $\alpha$ is all singletons) and uniform convergence (when $\alpha = \{X\}$). For each $A \in \alpha$ and $V$ in the uniformity of the range, one considers pairs $(f,g)$ such that $(f(x),g(x)) \in V$ for all $x \in A$. When $\alpha$ is the family of all compacta, this yields the uniformity of uniform convergence on compacta, whose topology coincides with the compact-open topology.
