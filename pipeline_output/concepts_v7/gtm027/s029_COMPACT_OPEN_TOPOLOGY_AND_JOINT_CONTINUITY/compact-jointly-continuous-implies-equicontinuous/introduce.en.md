Theorem 7.16 establishes the reverse implication needed for the Ascoli theorem: compactness in a jointly continuous topology implies equicontinuity. The proof uses joint continuity to find, for each $f \in F$, a neighborhood $G$ of $f$ and $W$ of $x$ such that $g[W] \subset V \circ V[g(x)]$ for $g \in G$. Compactness of $F$ then yields a finite cover $G_1,\dots,G_n$ with corresponding $W_1,\dots,W_n$, and $U = \bigcap W_i$ gives the required neighborhood of $x$.
