Even continuity is a purely topological concept introduced to prove an Ascoli theorem without reference to uniformities. Intuitively: for each $x$, $y$, and $f$, if $f(x)$ is near $y$, then $f$ maps points near $x$ into points near $y$. The definition is closely related to joint continuity: in the reformulation, the set of functions mapping $x$ into a neighborhood $W$ of $y$, multiplied by a neighborhood $V$ of $x$, is carried into the target neighborhood $U$. Every equicontinuous family is evenly continuous, and the converse holds under a compactness condition on $F[x]$.
