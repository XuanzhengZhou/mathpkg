A subset relationship is the most basic comparison between sets: $A$ is a subset of $B$ if every element of $A$ also belongs to $B$. Kelley notes the chain of inclusions that holds universally: the empty set is a subset of any intersection, which is a subset of each operand, which in turn is a subset of the union. This simple observation is used repeatedly throughout the book.
