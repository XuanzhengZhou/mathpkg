Theorem 9 establishes that order-completeness defined in terms of upper bounds (every non-empty bounded-above set has a supremum) is logically equivalent to the dual statement for lower bounds (every non-empty bounded-below set has an infimum). The proof proceeds by considering the set of lower bounds and applying the upper-bound completeness to it. This symmetry is a standard and useful fact in order theory, allowing one to freely switch between suprema and infima in complete ordered sets.
