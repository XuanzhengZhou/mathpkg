Kelley defines a relation simply as a set of ordered pairs — a remarkably economical definition that unifies orders, functions, equivalences, and graphs under one concept. This set-theoretic reduction means every theorem about sets automatically applies to relations. The notation $xRy$ is used interchangeably with $(x,y) \in R$, emphasizing both the relational and set-theoretic perspectives.
