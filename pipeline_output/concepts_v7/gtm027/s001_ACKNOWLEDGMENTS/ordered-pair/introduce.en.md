The ordered pair is the fundamental construct for building relations. Its defining property is that equality of ordered pairs reduces to equality of their respective coordinates — this is what makes the pair "ordered" as opposed to an unordered set. Kelley notes that ordered pairs can be defined purely in terms of sets (e.g., the Kuratowski definition $(x,y) = \{\{x\},\{x,y\}\}$), but for topological purposes only the characteristic property matters.
