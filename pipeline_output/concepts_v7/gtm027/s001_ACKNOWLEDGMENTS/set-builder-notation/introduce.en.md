Set-builder notation is the primary mechanism for defining sets by specifying a property that their elements must satisfy. Kelley emphasizes an important rule: if two propositions are logically equivalent, the sets they define are identical. He also clarifies that the variable after the opening brace is a bound (dummy) variable, and its scope must be carefully managed to avoid ambiguity.
