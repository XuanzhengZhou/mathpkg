Composition chains relations together: $(x,z) \in R \circ S$ means one can go from $x$ to $z$ by first applying $S$ (getting to some intermediate $y$) and then applying $R$. Kelley's ordering convention has $R \circ S$ meaning "$S$ then $R$". Composition is generally non-commutative — a simple example with singleton relations demonstrates this. The composition operation is associative, which is proved in Theorem 5.
