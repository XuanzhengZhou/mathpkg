Transitivity allows chaining: if $x$ relates to $y$ and $y$ relates to $z$, then $x$ must relate to $z$. The composition characterization $R \circ R \subset R$ is concise and powerful. An ordering is defined simply as a transitive relation. When combined with reflexivity, transitivity implies idempotence ($R \circ R = R$), a property used extensively in the theory of closure operators (Kuratowski axioms).
