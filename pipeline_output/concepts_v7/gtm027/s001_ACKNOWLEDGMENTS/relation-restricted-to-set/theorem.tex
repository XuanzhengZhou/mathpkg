Given a set $X$ and a relation $R$, the \text{restriction} of $R$ to $X$ is:
$$
R \cap (X \times X).
$$
We say that $R$ has a property \text{on} $X$ (or $R$ restricted to $X$ has the property) iff $R \cap (X \times X)$ has the property. For example, $R$ is transitive on $X$ iff $R \cap (X \times X)$ is transitive.
