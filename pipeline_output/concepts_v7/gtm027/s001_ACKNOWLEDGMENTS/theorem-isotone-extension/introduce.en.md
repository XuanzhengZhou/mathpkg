Theorem 10 gives a necessary and sufficient condition for extending an order-preserving function from a subset of a chain to the entire chain, assuming the codomain is order-complete. The condition requires that the function map order-bounded subsets to order-bounded subsets. The proof constructs the extension explicitly: for each $x$ in $X$, define $f^-(x) = \sup f[L_x]$ where $L_x$ consists of all domain points less than or equal to $x$. This theorem is used in constructing decimal expansions and has connections to the compactification of topological spaces.
