An upper bound of a subset $A$ in an ordered set $X$ is an element of $X$ that is greater than or equal to every element of $A$. A lower bound is defined dually. These concepts are fundamental to order theory and lead naturally to the notions of supremum (least upper bound) and infimum (greatest lower bound). In Kelley's broad definition of ordering as a transitive relation, bounds always exist vacuously for many subsets, but in chains with antisymmetry they become meaningful and unique when they exist as suprema or infima.
