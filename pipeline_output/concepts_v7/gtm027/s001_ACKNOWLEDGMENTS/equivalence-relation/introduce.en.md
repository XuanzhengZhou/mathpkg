An equivalence relation captures the notion of "being the same" with respect to some property. Kelley shows that every equivalence relation decomposes its domain into a partition of equivalence classes — the relation is exactly the union of $A \times A$ over all classes. This structure theorem is the foundation for quotient constructions throughout topology: quotient spaces, quotient groups, and identification spaces all arise from equivalence relations.
