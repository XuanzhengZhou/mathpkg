Theorem 2 collects the fundamental algebraic identities of set theory: commutativity, associativity, distributivity of union and intersection, the De Morgan laws for relative complement, and the double complement law. These are the set-theoretic analogues of Boolean algebra laws and are proved by showing that the sets on each side have exactly the same members. These identities are used extensively in topological arguments throughout the book.
