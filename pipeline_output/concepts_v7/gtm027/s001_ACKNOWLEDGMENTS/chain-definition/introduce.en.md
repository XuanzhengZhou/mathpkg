A chain is a set equipped with a linear (total) ordering. In a chain, any two distinct elements are comparable, and the ordering is antisymmetric. Chains are fundamental in order theory and appear throughout mathematics, particularly in applications of Zorn's Lemma where one considers chains in partially ordered sets. The real numbers with the usual ordering are a prototypical example of a chain.
