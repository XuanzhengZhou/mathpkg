The identity relation pairs each element with itself. It serves as the neutral element for relational composition, analogous to the identity function. The alternate name "diagonal" comes from its geometric position in $X \times X$: it is the set of points on the line $y = x$. In topology, the diagonal plays a crucial role — a space is Hausdorff precisely when its diagonal is closed in the product topology.
