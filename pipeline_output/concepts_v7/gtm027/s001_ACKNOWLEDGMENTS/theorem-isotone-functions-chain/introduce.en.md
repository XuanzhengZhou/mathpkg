Theorem 11 provides a uniqueness result for isotone functions on chains. If two order-preserving functions agree on a dense subset of a chain, they agree everywhere. Here "dense" means that between any two distinct points of the chain there lies a point of the subset. This is analogous to the fact that a continuous function on a topological space is determined by its values on a dense subset. The theorem is applied in Kelley's text to establish uniqueness of the correspondence between $b$-adic expansions and real numbers.
