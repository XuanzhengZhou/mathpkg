A countably compact space is one in which the compactness condition is required only for countable open covers. This is a weaker property than full compactness. Equivalent characterizations include: every sequence has a cluster point, and (in $T_1$ spaces) every infinite subset has a limit point (i.e., the space is limit point compact).
