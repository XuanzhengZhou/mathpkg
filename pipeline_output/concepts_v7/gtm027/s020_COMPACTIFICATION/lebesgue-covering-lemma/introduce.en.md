Lebesgue's covering lemma states that for any open cover of a compact subset of a pseudo-metric space, there exists a uniform radius such that every open ball of that radius is contained within a single member of the cover. This "uniformity" of covering is a crucial property linking compactness to metric structure and leads to the concept of even covers and paracompactness.
