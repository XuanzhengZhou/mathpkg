The Stone-Čech compactification $\beta(X)$ is constructed by embedding $X$ into a product of unit intervals via the evaluation map. Each point of $X$ is sent to the tuple of its values under all continuous $[0,1]$-valued functions, and $\beta(X)$ is the closure of this image. For Tychonoff spaces, this construction yields the maximal Hausdorff compactification.
