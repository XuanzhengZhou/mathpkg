This technical lemma shows that for any neighborhood of the diagonal, there exists a symmetric neighborhood whose self-composition is contained in the original. Intuitively, if being in $V$ means two points are "close," then $V \circ V \subset U$ means that if $x$ is close to $y$ and $y$ is close to $z$ (in the sense of $V$), then $x$ and $z$ are close in the sense of $U$.
