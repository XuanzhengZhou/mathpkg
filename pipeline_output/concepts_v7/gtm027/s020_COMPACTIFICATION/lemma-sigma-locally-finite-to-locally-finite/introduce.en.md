This lemma completes the cycle of implications in the characterization of paracompactness. It shows that if every open cover admits an open $\sigma$-locally finite refinement (a countable union of locally finite families), then every open cover actually admits a single locally finite refinement. The construction removes overlaps between the countably many families to produce the desired refinement.
