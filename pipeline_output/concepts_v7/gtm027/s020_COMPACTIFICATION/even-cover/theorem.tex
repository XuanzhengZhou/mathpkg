Let $X$ be a topological space and let $\Delta = \{(x, x) : x \in X\}$ be the diagonal in $X \times X$. An open cover $\mathfrak{u}$ of $X$ is called an \emph{even cover} iff there exists a neighborhood $V$ of $\Delta$ in $X \times X$ such that for each $x \in X$, the set $V[x] = \{y : (x, y) \in V\}$ is contained in some member of $\mathfrak{u}$. In other words, the family of all sets of the form $V[x]$ refines $\mathfrak{u}$.
