A compactification of a space $X$ is formally defined as a pair consisting of a compact space $Y$ and a homeomorphic embedding $f$ of $X$ onto a dense subspace of $Y$. This formulation allows topological embeddings rather than requiring the original space to be a literal subspace, and the density condition ensures the compactification is not unnecessarily large.
