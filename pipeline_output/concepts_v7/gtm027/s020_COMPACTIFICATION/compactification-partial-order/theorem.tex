Let $X$ be a topological space. Define a relation $\geq$ on the collection of all compactifications of $X$ by: $(f, Y) \geq (g, Z)$ iff there exists a continuous map $h$ of $Y$ onto $Z$ such that $h \circ f = g$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The collection of all compactifications of $X$ is partially ordered by $\geq$.
\item If $(f, Y)$ and $(g, Z)$ are Hausdorff compactifications of $X$ and $(f, Y) \geq (g, Z) \geq (f, Y)$, then $(f, Y)$ and $(g, Z)$ are topologically equivalent (i.e., the map $h$ can be taken to be a homeomorphism).
\end{enumerate}
