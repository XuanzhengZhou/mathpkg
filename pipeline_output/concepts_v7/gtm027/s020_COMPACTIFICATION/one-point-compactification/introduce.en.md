The one-point compactification (also called the Alexandroff compactification) adjoins a single point at infinity to a topological space. The topology is defined so that the complements of closed compact subsets of the original space become neighborhoods of the new point. This construction generalizes the familiar process of adding $\infty$ to the complex plane to obtain the Riemann sphere.
