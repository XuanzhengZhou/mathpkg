The canonical well-ordering of $R \times R$ (the class of all ordinal pairs) is a fundamental construction in set theory. The ordering proceeds by the maximum of the two ordinals, then lexicographically. The crucial property is that the set of predecessors of any point is a subset of $(\max[u,v] + 1) \times (\max[u,v] + 1)$, which has cardinality at most the maximum. This yields the key result $P(\kappa \times \kappa) = \kappa$ for infinite cardinals $\kappa$, which underpins cardinal arithmetic.
