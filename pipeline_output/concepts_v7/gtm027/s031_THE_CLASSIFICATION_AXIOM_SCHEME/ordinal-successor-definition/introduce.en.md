The successor of an ordinal $x$ is defined as $x \cup \{x\}$, which is again an ordinal. Under this definition, $0 = \emptyset$, $1 = \{0\}$, $2 = \{0,1\}$, and so on, yielding the standard von Neumann representation of the natural numbers.
