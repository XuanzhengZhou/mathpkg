This theorem computes the various iterated unions and intersections of an ordered pair, showing how the Kuratowski encoding encodes both coordinates. If either $x$ or $y$ is not a set, the results follow the boundary conventions: $\bigcup \bigcap (x,y) = 0$, $\bigcap \bigcap (x,y) = \mathcal{U}$, $\bigcup \bigcup (x,y) = \mathcal{U}$, and $\bigcap \bigcup (x,y) = 0$.
