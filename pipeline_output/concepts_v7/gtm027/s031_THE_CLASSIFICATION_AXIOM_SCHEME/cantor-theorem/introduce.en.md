Cantor's Theorem states that the cardinality of any set is strictly less than the cardinality of its power set. The proof is the classic diagonal argument: assuming a bijection $f: x \to 2^x$, the set $\{v \in x : v \notin f(v)\}$ cannot be in the range of $f$. This theorem implies there is no largest cardinal number and that the class $C$ of all cardinal numbers is not a set. The proof is structurally similar to Russell's paradox.
