A relation is simply a class of ordered pairs. This definition unifies the notion of relation with the rest of set theory: relations are just special classes. All the usual operations on relations -- composition, inverse, restriction, domain, range -- are defined set-theoretically. Functions are then defined as a special kind of relation (single-valued). This reductionist approach is characteristic of Kelley's set-theoretic foundations.
