\textbf{Definition.} $x$ is \textbf{full} iff each member of $x$ is a subset of $x$.

$x$ is an \textbf{ordinal} iff $x$ is full and the $\in$-relation $E = \{(x,y) : x \in y\}$ connects $x$.

$x$ is an \textbf{ordinal number} iff $x$ is an ordinal and $x$ is a set (i.e., $x \in R$ where $R$ is the class of all ordinals that are sets).

\textbf{Theorem.} The relation $E$ well-orders every ordinal, and ordinals are comparable: if $x$ and $y$ are ordinals, then $x \in y$ or $y \in x$ or $x = y$.
