\textbf{Definition.} $x$ is a \textbf{cardinal number} iff $x$ is an ordinal number and for every ordinal $y < x$, it is false that $x \approx y$ (where $\approx$ denotes equipotence, i.e., the existence of a one-to-one correspondence).

The \textbf{power} (or \textbf{cardinal}) $P(x)$ of a set $x$ is the unique cardinal number equipotent to $x$.
