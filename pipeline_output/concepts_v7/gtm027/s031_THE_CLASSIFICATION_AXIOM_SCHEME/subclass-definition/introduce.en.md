A class $x$ is a subclass of $y$ (written $x \subset y$) if every member of $x$ is also a member of $y$. The inclusion relation $\subset$ must be carefully distinguished from the membership relation $\in$; for example $0 \subset 0$ is true but $0 \in 0$ is false.
