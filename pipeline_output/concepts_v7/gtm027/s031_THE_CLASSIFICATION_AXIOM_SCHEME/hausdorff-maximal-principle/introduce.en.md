The Hausdorff Maximal Principle states that every partially ordered set contains a maximal chain (nest). The proof uses transfinite induction and the Axiom of Choice, similar to the Well-Ordering Theorem. This principle is equivalent to Zorn's Lemma and the Axiom of Choice, and it is used extensively in algebra and topology for existence proofs of maximal ideals, bases, compactifications, and other maximal objects.
