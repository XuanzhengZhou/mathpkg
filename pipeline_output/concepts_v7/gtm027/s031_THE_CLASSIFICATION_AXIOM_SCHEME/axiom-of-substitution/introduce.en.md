The Axiom of Substitution (also called Replacement) asserts that the range of a function whose domain is a set is itself a set. This is a powerful axiom that ensures "small" images of "small" domains. It is used extensively: together with the Axiom of Amalgamation it yields that Cartesian products of sets are sets, that functions with set domains are sets, and in the proof of the Well-Ordering Theorem via transfinite induction.
