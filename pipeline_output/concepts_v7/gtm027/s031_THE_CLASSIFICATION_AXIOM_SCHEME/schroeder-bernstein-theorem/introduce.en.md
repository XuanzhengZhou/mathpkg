The Schroeder-Bernstein Theorem states that if two sets can each be injected into the other, then they are equipotent (have the same cardinality). This establishes that the cardinal ordering $\leq$ is antisymmetric: $P(x) \leq P(y)$ and $P(y) \leq P(x)$ implies $P(x) = P(y)$. The proof uses cardinal arithmetic and does not require the Axiom of Choice (a direct proof constructing a bijection is also possible).
