The value $f(x)$ of a function $f$ at $x$ is defined as the intersection of all second coordinates of ordered pairs in $f$ whose first coordinate is $x$. If $f$ is a function and $x$ is in its domain, this yields the unique $y$ such that $(x,y) \in f$; otherwise $f(x) = \mathcal{U}$.
