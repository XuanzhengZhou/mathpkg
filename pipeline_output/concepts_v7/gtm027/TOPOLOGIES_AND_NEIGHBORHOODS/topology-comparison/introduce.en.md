Topologies on a fixed set are partially ordered by inclusion: a topology with fewer open sets is coarser (smaller, weaker), and one with more open sets is finer (larger, stronger). The indiscrete topology is the coarsest and the discrete topology is the finest. Two topologies may be incomparable if neither contains the other, reflecting the fact that the family of all topologies on a set forms a complete lattice under inclusion.
