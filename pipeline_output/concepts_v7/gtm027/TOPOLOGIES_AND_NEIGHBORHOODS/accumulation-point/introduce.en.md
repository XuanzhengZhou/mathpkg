An accumulation point (also called a cluster point or limit point) of a set $A$ is a point whose every neighborhood contains some point of $A$ distinct from itself. Intuitively, there are points of $A$ arbitrarily near $x$. Together with the points of $A$ itself, accumulation points characterize the closure. In the usual topology on $\mathbb{R}$, every point of $[0,1]$ is an accumulation point of $(0,1)$, while $0$ is the unique accumulation point of $\{1/n : n \in \mathbb{N}\}$.
