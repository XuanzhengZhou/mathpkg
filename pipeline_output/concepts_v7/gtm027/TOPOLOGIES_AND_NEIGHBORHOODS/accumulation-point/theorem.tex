A point $x$ is an \textbf{accumulation point} (sometimes called \textbf{cluster point} or \textbf{limit point}) of a subset $A$ of a topological space $(X, \mathfrak{J})$ iff every neighborhood of $x$ contains points of $A$ other than $x$.

It is true that each neighborhood of a point $x$ intersects $A$ if and only if $x$ is either a point of $A$ or an accumulation point of $A$.
