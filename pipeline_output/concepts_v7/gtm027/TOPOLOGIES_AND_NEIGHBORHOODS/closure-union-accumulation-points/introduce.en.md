This fundamental theorem reveals the structure of the closure: $\overline{A} = A \cup A'$, where $A'$ is the derived set of accumulation points. The proof uses two facts: every accumulation point of $A$ belongs to every closed superset of $A$ (hence to $\overline{A}$), and the set $A \cup A'$ is closed (by Theorem 6) and contains $A$, so it must contain $\overline{A}$. Thus the closure simply adjoins all accumulation points.
