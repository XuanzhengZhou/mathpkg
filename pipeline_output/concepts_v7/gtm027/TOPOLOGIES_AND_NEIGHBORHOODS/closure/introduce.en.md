The closure of a set is the intersection of all closed supersets—equivalently, the smallest closed set containing the set. It is denoted $\overline{A}$ or $A^{-}$. A set is closed exactly when it coincides with its closure. The closure operator is idempotent, monotone, and distributes over finite unions, making it a Kuratowski closure operator. Closure is the fundamental operation for defining topological concepts like density, separability, and the frontier.
