A topological space is a set equipped with a topology—a distinguished family of subsets satisfying the axioms of closure under arbitrary unions and finite intersections. The set $X$ is called the space, and the members of the topology $\mathfrak{J}$ are the open sets. This minimal structure is sufficient to define continuity, convergence, compactness, and connectedness.
