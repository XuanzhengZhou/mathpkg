An open set is a member of the topology. In any topological space, the whole space and the empty set are always open—the former because the topology is a subfamily of itself, and the latter as the union over the empty subfamily. Open sets generalize the notion of open intervals in $\mathbb{R}$ and serve as the building blocks for defining continuity in abstract spaces.
