The members of the topology $\mathfrak{J}$ are called \textbf{open} relative to $\mathfrak{J}$, or $\mathfrak{J}$-open, or if only one topology is under consideration, simply \textbf{open sets}. The space $X$ of the topology is always open, and the void set is always open because it is the union of the members of the void family.
