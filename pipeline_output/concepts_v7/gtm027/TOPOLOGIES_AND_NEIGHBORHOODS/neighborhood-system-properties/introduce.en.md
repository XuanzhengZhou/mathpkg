This theorem establishes that the neighborhood system at any point is a filter: it is closed under finite intersections and upward-closed with respect to inclusion. The proof relies on the fact that the intersection of two open neighborhoods contains another open neighborhood (since open sets are closed under finite intersections), and any superset of a neighborhood contains the same open neighborhood and is thus itself a neighborhood.
