A closed set is the complement of an open set. By De Morgan's laws, the family of closed sets is closed under finite unions and arbitrary intersections—the dual properties to those of open sets. The whole space and the empty set are always both open and closed (clopen). In the usual topology on $\mathbb{R}$, closed intervals are closed, open intervals are not closed, and half-open intervals are neither.
