The Kuratowski closure axioms provide an equivalent foundation for topology: any operator satisfying these four axioms—preservation of the empty set, extensivity ($A \subseteq A^c$), idempotence ($A^{cc} = A^c$), and additivity over finite unions—defines a unique topology whose closed sets are the fixed points of the operator. This axiomatization reveals that the essence of a topology lies in its closure operation.
