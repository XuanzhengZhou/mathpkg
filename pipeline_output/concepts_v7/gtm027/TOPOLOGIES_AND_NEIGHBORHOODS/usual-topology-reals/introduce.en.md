The usual topology on $\mathbb{R}$ is the canonical topology generated by open intervals. A set is open precisely when every point admits an open interval around it contained entirely within the set. This topology is the archetypal example motivating the abstract definition of a topological space, and it gives rise to the familiar notions of open intervals, closed intervals, and accumulation points in real analysis.
