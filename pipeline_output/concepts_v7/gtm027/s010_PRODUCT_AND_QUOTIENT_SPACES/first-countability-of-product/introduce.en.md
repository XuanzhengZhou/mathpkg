This theorem gives a precise necessary and sufficient condition for an arbitrary product space to satisfy the first axiom of countability. The sufficiency direction constructs a countable local base from the countable local bases of the non-indiscrete factors (of which there are only countably many). The necessity direction uses a cardinality argument: if uncountably many factors each have a proper open neighborhood at a point, no countable family of open sets can form a local base, because each member of a countable family would project onto the entire space for all but finitely many coordinates, leaving uncountably many coordinates uncovered.
