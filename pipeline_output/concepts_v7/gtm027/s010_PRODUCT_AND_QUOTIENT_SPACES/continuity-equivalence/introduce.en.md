This theorem provides eight equivalent characterizations of continuity for functions between topological spaces. The conditions range from the standard open-set definition (via subbase elements) to formulations in terms of closed sets, neighborhoods, net convergence, and closure operations. This equivalence is fundamental because different characterizations are convenient in different proofs: the net characterization is useful for arguments involving limits, while the closure formulation is powerful in set-theoretic arguments about continuity.
