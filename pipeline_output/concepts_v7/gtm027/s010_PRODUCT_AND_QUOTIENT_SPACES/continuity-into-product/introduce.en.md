This theorem provides an extremely useful characterization: a function into a product space is continuous precisely when each of its coordinate functions is continuous. The forward direction follows from the continuity of projections and the fact that compositions of continuous functions are continuous. The reverse direction holds because the preimage of each subbase element $P_a^{-1}[U]$ is $(P_a \circ f)^{-1}[U]$, which is open when each $P_a \circ f$ is continuous. This universal property essentially defines the product topology and is used constantly in analysis and topology.
