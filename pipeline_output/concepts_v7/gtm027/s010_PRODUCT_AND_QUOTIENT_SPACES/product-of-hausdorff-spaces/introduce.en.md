The Hausdorff separation property is preserved under arbitrary products: if every coordinate space is Hausdorff, so is the product. The proof is simple and elegant — distinct points in the product differ in at least one coordinate, and disjoint neighborhoods in that coordinate space pull back via the projection to give disjoint neighborhoods in the product. The converse also holds: if a product space is Hausdorff, each coordinate space (being homeomorphic to a subspace of the product) is Hausdorff. Thus the Hausdorff property is a topological invariant that behaves well with respect to products.
