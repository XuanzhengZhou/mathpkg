Suppose that a topology $\mathfrak{J}_a$ is given for each coordinate set $X_a$ in the Cartesian product $\bigwedge \{X_a : a \in A\}$. The \textbf{product topology} is the topology having as a subbase the family of all sets of the form
$$P_a^{-1}[U] = \{x : x_a \in U\},$$
where $U$ is an open subset of $X_a$. A base for the product topology is the family of all finite intersections of subbase elements. A member of this base is of the form
$$\bigcap \{P_a^{-1}[U_a] : a \in F\} = \{x : x_a \in U_a \text{ for each } a \text{ in } F\},$$
where $F$ is a finite subset of $A$ and $U_a$ is open in $X_a$ for each $a$ in $F$.

The product topology is the smallest topology for which all projections $P_a$ are continuous. The \textbf{product space} is the Cartesian product endowed with the product topology.
