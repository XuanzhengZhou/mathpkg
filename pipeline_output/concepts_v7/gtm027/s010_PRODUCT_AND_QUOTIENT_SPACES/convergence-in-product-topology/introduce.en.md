Convergence in the product topology is equivalent to convergence in each coordinate separately — hence the name coordinatewise convergence. This follows directly from the definition of the product topology: a basic neighborhood of a point restricts only finitely many coordinates, so a net eventually enters each basic neighborhood precisely when each coordinate projection eventually enters the corresponding coordinate neighborhood. When all coordinate spaces are identical, this is called pointwise convergence, and the product space $X^A$ is the space of all functions from $A$ to $X$.
