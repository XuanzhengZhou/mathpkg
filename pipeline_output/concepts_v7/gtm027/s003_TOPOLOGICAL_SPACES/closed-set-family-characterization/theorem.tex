Let $\mathfrak{F}$ be a family of subsets of a set $X$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The union of any finite subfamily of $\mathfrak{F}$ is a member of $\mathfrak{F}$.
\item The intersection of any arbitrary non-void subfamily of $\mathfrak{F}$ is a member of $\mathfrak{F}$.
\item $X = \bigcup \{F : F \in \mathfrak{F}\}$ is a member of $\mathfrak{F}$.
\end{enumerate}
Then $\mathfrak{F}$ is precisely the family of closed sets in $X$ relative to the topology consisting of the complements of members of $\mathfrak{F}$.
