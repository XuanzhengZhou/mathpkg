Kuratowski's theorem establishes a profound equivalence: a topology on a set $X$ can be defined entirely in terms of a closure operator satisfying the four axioms. The closed sets are exactly the fixed points of the operator, and the topology is recovered by taking complements. This result is foundational—it shows that the axiomatic study of closure is logically equivalent to the axiomatic study of open sets, providing an alternative and often more algebraic entry point to general topology.
