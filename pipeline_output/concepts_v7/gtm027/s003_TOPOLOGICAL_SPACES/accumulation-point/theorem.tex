A point $x$ is an \textbf{accumulation point} (also called \textbf{cluster point} or \textbf{limit point}) of a subset $A$ of a topological space iff every neighborhood of $x$ contains points of $A$ other than $x$ (i.e., distinct from $x$). Every neighborhood of $x$ intersects $A$ iff $x$ is either a point of $A$ or an accumulation point of $A$.
