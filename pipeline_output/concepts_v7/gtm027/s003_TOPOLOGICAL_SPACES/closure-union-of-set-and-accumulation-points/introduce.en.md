This theorem bridges two fundamental characterizations of the closure: the order-theoretic definition (smallest closed superset) and the geometric definition (adjoining all limit points). It justifies the intuitive view that the closure "fills in the holes" of a set by including every point that can be approximated from within the set. This result is foundational—it is used throughout point-set topology.
