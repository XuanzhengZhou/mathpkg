A topology on a set $X$ is a collection of subsets, called open sets, that is closed under arbitrary unions and finite intersections. This abstract structure captures the notion of nearness and continuity without requiring a metric. The pair $(X, \mathfrak{J})$ forms a topological space, the fundamental object of study in general topology.
