A \textbf{topology} is a family $\mathfrak{J}$ of sets which satisfies:

\begin{enumerate}
\item The intersection of any two members of $\mathfrak{J}$ is a member of $\mathfrak{J}$.
\item The union of the members of each subfamily of $\mathfrak{J}$ is a member of $\mathfrak{J}$.
\end{enumerate}

The set $X = \bigcup \{U : U \in \mathfrak{J}\}$ is called the \textit{space} of the topology $\mathfrak{J}$, and $\mathfrak{J}$ is a topology for $X$.
