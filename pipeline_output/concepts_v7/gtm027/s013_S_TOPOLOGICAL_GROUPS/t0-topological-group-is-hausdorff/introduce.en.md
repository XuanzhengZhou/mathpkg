This theorem states that in a topological group, the weakest separation axiom ($T_0$) already implies full Hausdorff separation ($T_2$). This is a special property of topological groups that does not hold for general topological spaces. The key observation is that the group structure allows one to translate a separating neighborhood from one point to separate all pairs of distinct points. Consequently, for topological groups the $T_0$, $T_1$, and $T_2$ separation axioms are equivalent.
