This proposition establishes that multiplying an open set $U$ by any subset $X$ (on either side) yields an open set. This follows because left and right translation maps are homeomorphisms in a topological group, so $U \cdot X$ is a union of open translates of $U$. The second part provides an important warning: the analogous statement for closed sets is false. The product of two closed sets need not be closed, as shown by the example $X = Y = \{(x,y) : y = 1/x^2\}$ in the Euclidean plane under addition, where both are closed but their sum is not.
