This theorem provides a converse construction: if one specifies a family of subsets to serve as neighborhoods of the identity element and this family satisfies the four axioms of propositions (c) through (e), then there is a unique topology making the group a topological group with precisely that neighborhood system at the identity. This is a fundamental result because it shows that the topology of a topological group is completely determined by the neighborhoods of the identity element.
