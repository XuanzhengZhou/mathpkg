This theorem gives a purely neighborhood-based reformulation of the continuity condition defining a topological group. Instead of requiring the map $(x, y) \mapsto x \cdot y^{-1}$ to be continuous in the product topology, one can equivalently demand that for any neighborhood $W$ of the product $x \cdot y^{-1}$ there exist neighborhoods $U$ of $x$ and $V$ of $y$ whose set difference (in the group sense) lies within $W$. This formulation is often more convenient for working with topological groups at the local level.
