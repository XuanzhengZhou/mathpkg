This proposition records two elementary algebraic facts about subset multiplication in a topological group. Subset multiplication is associative, just as the underlying group operation is. Moreover, the inverse of a product of subsets is the product of the inverses taken in reverse order, mirroring the familiar identity $(xy)^{-1} = y^{-1} x^{-1}$ for group elements. These identities are used routinely when manipulating neighborhoods in topological groups.
