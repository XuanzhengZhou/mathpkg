A topological group is a group equipped with a topology such that the algebraic operations are continuous. Specifically, the map sending $(x, y)$ to $x \cdot y^{-1}$ must be continuous with respect to the product topology on $G \times G$. This definition unifies algebraic and topological structures, making it possible to study continuity properties of group operations. The notation for products of subsets $X \cdot Y$ and inverses $Y^{-1}$ extends the group operation naturally to the power set of $G$.
