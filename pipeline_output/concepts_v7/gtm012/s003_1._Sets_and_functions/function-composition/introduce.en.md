The composition $g \circ f$ of two functions $f: A \to B$ and $g: B \to C$ is the function obtained by applying $f$ first and then $g$. Composition is associative: $h \circ (g \circ f) = (h \circ g) \circ f$ whenever the compositions are defined. It is not commutative in general. Composition of functions is one of the most fundamental operations in mathematics.
