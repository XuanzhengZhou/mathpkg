The complement of $A$ in $B$, written $B \setminus A$, is defined when $A$ is a subset of $B$ and consists of all elements of $B$ that do not belong to $A$. The complement satisfies the characteristic properties $A \cup (B \setminus A) = B$ and $A \cap (B \setminus A) = \varnothing$. Together with union and intersection, the complement forms the basic Boolean algebra of sets.
