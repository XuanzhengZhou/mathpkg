The union of two sets $A$ and $B$ is the set consisting of all elements that belong to $A$, to $B$, or to both. This binary operation generalizes to arbitrary families of sets via an index set $J$: the union $\bigcup_{j \in J} A_j$ collects all elements that appear in at least one of the sets $A_j$. Union is commutative, associative, and idempotent, and the empty set acts as the identity element.
