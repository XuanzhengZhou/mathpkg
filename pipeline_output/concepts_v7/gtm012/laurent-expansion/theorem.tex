If $f$ is holomorphic on an annulus $r < |z - z_0| < R$, then $f$ has a unique expansion
$$f(z) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n (z - z_0)^n,$$
converging uniformly on compact subsets of the annulus. The coefficients are
$$a_n = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_\gamma \frac{f(w)}{(w - z_0)^{n+1}} \, dw,$$
where $\gamma$ is any circle $|w - z_0| = \rho$ with $r < \rho < R$.
