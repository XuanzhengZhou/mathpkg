Proposition 1.2 states the fundamental equivalence between continuity and boundedness for linear functionals on normed spaces. Boundedness implies Lipschitz continuity with constant $c$; conversely, continuity at zero yields a $\delta > 0$ such that $\|u\| \leq \delta$ implies $|F(u)| \leq 1$, and scaling by $\delta^{-1}\|u\|$ shows $|F(u)| \leq \delta^{-1}\|u\|$, establishing boundedness. A linear functional is therefore continuous precisely when its operator norm is finite.
