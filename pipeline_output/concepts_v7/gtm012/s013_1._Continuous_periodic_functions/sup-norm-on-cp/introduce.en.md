The supremum norm (also called the uniform norm or $L^\infty$ norm) on $\mathbb{C}$ measures the maximum absolute value of a continuous $2\pi$-periodic function. By periodicity and continuity, the supremum over $\mathbb{R}$ equals the supremum over the compact interval $[0, 2\pi]$, guaranteeing finiteness. This norm induces the topology of uniform convergence and satisfies all three norm axioms: positivity, homogeneity, and the triangle inequality.
