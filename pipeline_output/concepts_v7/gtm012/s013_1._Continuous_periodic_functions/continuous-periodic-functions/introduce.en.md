The space $\mathbb{C}$ of continuous $2\pi$-periodic complex-valued functions is the fundamental function space for the theory of Fourier series. It contains all classical trigonometric functions and is closed under pointwise addition and scalar multiplication, forming a vector space. Equipped with the supremum norm, $\mathbb{C}$ becomes a Banach space, providing the analytic foundation for studying convergence of Fourier series.
