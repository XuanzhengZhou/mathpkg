A bounded linear functional is a linear map from a normed space to the scalar field whose operator norm $\|F\| = \sup_{\|u\| \leq 1} |F(u)|$ is finite. Boundedness is equivalent to continuity for linear maps between normed spaces, making it the central concept for studying dual spaces. The collection of all bounded linear functionals on $X$ forms the dual space $X^*$, which is always a Banach space under the operator norm.
