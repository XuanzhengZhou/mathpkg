The metric $d'$ on $\mathcal{P}$ is constructed as a weighted sum of bounded metrics derived from each seminorm $p_k(u) = \|D^k u\|$. The factors $2^{-k-1}$ ensure convergence of the infinite series, and the transformation $t \mapsto t/(1+t)$ makes each term bounded by $1$ while preserving the topology near zero. This construction is the standard technique for metrizing a topology generated by a countable separating family of seminorms.
