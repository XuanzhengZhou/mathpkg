Let $\mathcal{P}$ denote the subset of $\mathbb{C}$ consisting of all functions $u \in \mathbb{C}$ that are infinitely differentiable ($C^\infty$). Such a function is called a \emph{smooth periodic function}. If $u \in \mathcal{P}$, then all derivatives $Du, D^2u, \ldots$ are also in $\mathcal{P}$. The set $\mathcal{P}$ is a linear subspace of $\mathbb{C}$, and $\mathcal{P} \neq \mathbb{C}$ (for example, $|\sin x| \in \mathbb{C}$ but $|\sin x| \notin \mathcal{P}$).
