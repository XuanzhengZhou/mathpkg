Theorem 4.3 is the main structure theorem for distributions of type $\mathcal{L}'$. It states that every distribution $F \in \mathcal{L}'$ of sufficiently high order can be represented as the $k$-th derivative of a distribution defined by an ordinary function $f$. This provides a converse to the observation that $D^k F_f$ is always a distribution of type $\mathcal{L}'$: up to differentiation, all such distributions come from functions.
