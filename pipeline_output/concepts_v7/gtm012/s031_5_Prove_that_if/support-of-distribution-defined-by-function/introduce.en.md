When a distribution $F$ is defined by a locally integrable function $f$ via the usual integral pairing, the support of the distribution coincides with the support of the function. This result confirms that the distributional notion of support is a proper extension of the classical support for functions. The statement appears as an exercise in the context of distributions of type $\mathcal{L}'$.
