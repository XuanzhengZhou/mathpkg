Differentiability is a stronger condition than continuity: it requires the existence of a linear approximation (the tangent line) at each point. For complex-valued functions, differentiability reduces to differentiability of the real and imaginary parts. The class $C^k$ consists of functions with $k$ continuous derivatives; $C^\infty$ functions are infinitely differentiable.
