A Cauchy sequence is one whose terms eventually become arbitrarily close to each other. This is an intrinsic property of the sequence itself, requiring no knowledge of its limit. In complete spaces like $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{C}$, a sequence converges if and only if it is Cauchy.
