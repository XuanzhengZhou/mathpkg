An open subset of $\mathbb{R}^2$ generalizes the notion of an open interval in $\mathbb{R}$ to the plane. The definition uses open discs (the 2-dimensional analogue of open intervals) as the basic notion of neighborhoods. This concept provides the natural domain for defining differentiability of functions of two real variables.
