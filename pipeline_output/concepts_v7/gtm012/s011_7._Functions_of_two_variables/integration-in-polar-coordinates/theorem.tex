**Theorem 7.4.** Suppose $f$ is a continuous complex-valued function defined on the disc
$$
D_R = \{(x, y) \mid x^2 + y^2 < R\}.
$$
Suppose $g$ is related to $f$ by $g(r, \theta) = f(r \cos \theta, r \sin \theta)$. Then
$$
\int_{-R}^{R} \left\{ \int_{-(R^2 - y^2)^{1/2}}^{(R^2 - y^2)^{1/2}} f(x, y) \, dx \right\} \, dy = \int_{0}^{R} \left\{ \int_{0}^{2\pi} g(r, \theta) \, r \, d\theta \right\} \, dr.
$$
