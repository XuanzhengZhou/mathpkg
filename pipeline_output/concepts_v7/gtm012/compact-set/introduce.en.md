Compact sets generalize the notion of closed and bounded intervals in $\mathbb{R}$. Every open cover has a finite subcover. Sequential compactness (every sequence has a convergent subsequence) is equivalent to compactness in metric spaces. Compact sets are always closed and bounded; in $\mathbb{R}^n$, the converse (Heine-Borel) holds.
