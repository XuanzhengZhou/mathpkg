Theorem 6.4 provides the fundamental pointwise and uniform convergence result for Fourier series of smooth periodic functions. If a continuous periodic function $u$ has a continuous derivative, then its Fourier coefficients decay rapidly enough to guarantee absolute summability via the Schwarz inequality, and Lemma 6.3 then yields uniform convergence. The proof uses integration by parts to relate the Fourier coefficients of $u$ and its derivative $Du$, showing $a_n(Du) = in\, a_n(u)$, which combined with Parseval's identity yields $\sum |a_n| < \infty$. The second part extends convergence to the distributional sense for smooth periodic functions in $\mathcal{P}$.
