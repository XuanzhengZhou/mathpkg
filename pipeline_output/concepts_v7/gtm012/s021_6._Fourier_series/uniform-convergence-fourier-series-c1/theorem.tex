If $u \in \mathcal{C}$ and $u$ is continuously differentiable, then the partial sums of the Fourier series of $u$ converge uniformly to $u$. Thus
$$u(x) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} (u, e_n) \exp(inx), \quad \text{for all } x \in \mathbb{R}.$$
Moreover, if $u \in \mathcal{P}$, then the partial sums converge to $u$ in the sense of $\mathcal{P}$ (i.e., in the topology of $\mathcal{P}'$).
