If $(a_n)_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} \subset \mathbb{C}$ has the property
$$\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} |a_n| < \infty,$$
then the partial sums
$$u_N(x) = \sum_{n=-N}^{N} a_n e_n(x), \quad e_n(x) = \exp(inx)$$
converge uniformly to a continuous periodic function $u \in \mathcal{C}$, and $(a_n)_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}$ is precisely the sequence of Fourier coefficients of $u$.
