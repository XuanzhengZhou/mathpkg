Lemma 6.3 provides a simple sufficient condition for the uniform convergence of a trigonometric series: absolute summability of the coefficients. Since $|e_n(x)| = 1$ for all $x$, the condition $\sum |a_n| < \infty$ allows one to apply the Weierstrass M-test, yielding uniform convergence of the partial sums to a continuous periodic function. Moreover, the uniform convergence guarantees that term-by-term integration is valid, which confirms that the coefficients $a_n$ are indeed the Fourier coefficients of the limit function $u$. This lemma serves as a key technical tool in proving the uniform convergence of Fourier series for sufficiently regular functions.
