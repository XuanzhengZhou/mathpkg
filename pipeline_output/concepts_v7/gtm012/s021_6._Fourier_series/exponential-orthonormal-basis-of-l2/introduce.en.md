The exponential functions $e_n(x) = e^{inx}$ form the fundamental building blocks of Fourier analysis on the circle. Lemma 6.1 establishes that this two-sided sequence constitutes an orthonormal basis for the Hilbert space $L^2$ of periodic distributions. This means every element of $L^2$ can be uniquely expanded as an infinite linear combination of these exponentials, with convergence in the $L^2$ norm. The orthonormality follows from the elementary integral $\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi} e^{i(n-m)x}\,dx = \delta_{nm}$, while completeness relies on the density of continuous periodic functions and the Weierstrass approximation theorem.
