The real form of the Fourier series expresses the expansion in terms of sines and cosines rather than complex exponentials. Using Euler's formula $e^{inx} = \cos nx + i\sin nx$, the complex coefficients $a_n$ are transformed into real coefficients $b_n = a_n + a_{-n}$ and $c_n = i(a_n - a_{-n})$. This formulation is particularly natural when the original function or distribution is real-valued, as the coefficients $b_n, c_n$ then become real numbers, yielding a series $\frac{1}{2}b_0 + \sum (b_n \cos nx + c_n \sin nx)$ of real functions. The symmetry relations $b_{-n} = b_n$ and $c_{-n} = -c_n$ reflect the even/odd character of cosine and sine respectively.
