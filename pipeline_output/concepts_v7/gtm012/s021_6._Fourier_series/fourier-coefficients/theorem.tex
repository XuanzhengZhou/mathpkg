For a periodic distribution $F \in L^2$, its Fourier coefficients are the numbers
$$a_n = F(e_n), \quad e_n(x) = \exp(inx), \quad n = 0, \pm 1, \pm 2, \ldots$$
For an integrable function $u: [0, 2\pi] \to \mathbb{C}$, its Fourier coefficients are
$$a_n = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{0}^{2\pi} u(x) e^{-inx} \, dx, \quad n = 0, \pm 1, \pm 2, \ldots$$
which coincides with $F_u(e_n)$ where $F_u$ is the distribution defined by $u$.
