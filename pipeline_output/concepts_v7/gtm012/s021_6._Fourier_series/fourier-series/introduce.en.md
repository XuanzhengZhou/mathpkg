The Fourier series is the formal infinite trigonometric series $\sum a_n e^{inx}$ formed from the Fourier coefficients of a periodic function or distribution. It provides the spectral decomposition of the object into harmonics of frequencies $n \in \mathbb{Z}$. While the series is written as a formal pointwise sum, its convergence is understood in the $L^2$ sense for general $L^2$ distributions, and may hold in stronger senses (uniform, pointwise) under additional regularity assumptions on the function. The Fourier series is the central object of study in harmonic analysis on the circle.
