The Fourier series of a periodic distribution $F \in L^2$ (or of an integrable function $u$) is the formal trigonometric series
$$\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n \exp(inx),$$
where $(a_n)_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}$ are the Fourier coefficients of $F$ (respectively, of $u$). Convergence of the series is understood in the sense of $L^2$, i.e., the symmetric partial sums $\sum_{n=-N}^{N} a_n e_n$ converge to $F$ in the $L^2$ norm as $N \to \infty$.
