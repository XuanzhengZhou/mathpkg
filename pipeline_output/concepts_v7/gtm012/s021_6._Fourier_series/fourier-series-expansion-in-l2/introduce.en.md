Theorem 6.2 is the central result of Fourier analysis in the Hilbert space framework. It states that every periodic distribution $F \in L^2$ possesses a unique Fourier series expansion that converges in the $L^2$ norm, with coefficients given by pairing $F$ against the exponential basis functions. Parseval's identity $\sum |a_n|^2 = \|F\|^2$ expresses the fact that the Fourier transform is a unitary isomorphism between $L^2$ and the sequence space $\ell^2(\mathbb{Z})$. The converse direction guarantees that every square-summable sequence arises as the Fourier coefficients of some $L^2$ distribution, establishing the Riesz-Fischer theorem in this context.
