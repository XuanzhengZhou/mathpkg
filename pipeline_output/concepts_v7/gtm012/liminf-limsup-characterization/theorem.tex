Suppose $(x_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ is a bounded sequence in $\mathbb{R}$. Then $\liminf x_n$ is the unique number $x'$ such that:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i')] for any $\varepsilon > 0$, there is $N$ such that $x_n > x' - \varepsilon$ whenever $n \geq N$,
\item[(ii')] for any $\varepsilon > 0$ and any $N$, there is $n \geq N$ such that $x_n < x' + \varepsilon$.
\end{itemize}
Similarly, $\limsup x_n$ is the unique number $x''$ such that:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i'')] for any $\varepsilon > 0$, there is $N$ such that $x_n < x'' + \varepsilon$ whenever $n \geq N$,
\item[(ii'')] for any $\varepsilon > 0$ and any $N$, there is $n \geq N$ such that $x_n > x'' - \varepsilon$.
\end{itemize}
