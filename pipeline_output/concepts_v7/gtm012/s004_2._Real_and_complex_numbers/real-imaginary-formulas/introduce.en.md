The real and imaginary parts of a complex number can be recovered algebraically from the number and its conjugate, without reference to the decomposition $z = x + iy$. These formulas show that $\operatorname{Re}$ and $\operatorname{Im}$ are $\mathbb{R}$-linear maps and are fundamental in complex analysis for expressing functions in terms of $z$ and $\bar{z}$.
