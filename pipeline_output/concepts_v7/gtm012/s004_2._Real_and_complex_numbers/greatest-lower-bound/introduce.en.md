The greatest lower bound (also called infimum) of a set $A$ is the largest among all lower bounds of $A$. When it exists, it is unique. The notation $\operatorname{glb} A$ is used in this text; the standard notation in modern analysis is $\inf A$. As a consequence of the completeness axiom, every nonempty, bounded-below subset of $\mathbb{R}$ has a greatest lower bound.
