The axioms of multiplication make $(\mathbb{R} \setminus \{0\}, \cdot)$ into an abelian group. M1 (associativity) and M2 (commutativity) mirror the corresponding addition axioms. M3 guarantees a multiplicative identity $1$ distinct from $0$, and M4 guarantees a multiplicative inverse $x^{-1}$ for every nonzero real number. Together with the addition axioms and the distributive law, these define $\mathbb{R}$ as a field, the starting point for all of real analysis.
