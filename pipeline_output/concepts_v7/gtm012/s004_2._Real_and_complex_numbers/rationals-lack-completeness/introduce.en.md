Theorem 2.1 establishes the fundamental distinction between $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{Q}$: the completeness axiom fails for the rational numbers. The proof pivots on the irrationality of $\sqrt{2}$: the set of rationals whose square is less than $2$ is bounded above but admits no rational least upper bound. This demonstrates that the completeness axiom is the essential property that separates the real numbers from the rationals.
