Complex conjugation is an involutive field automorphism of $\mathbb{C}$ that fixes $\mathbb{R}$ pointwise. It distributes over addition and multiplication, is its own inverse, and the product $z^* z$ equals $|z|^2$, which is a nonnegative real number vanishing only at $z = 0$. These properties are fundamental for all algebraic manipulations in $\mathbb{C}$.
