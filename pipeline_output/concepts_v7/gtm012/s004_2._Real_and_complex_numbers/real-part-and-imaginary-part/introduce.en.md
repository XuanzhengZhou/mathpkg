Every complex number $z$ is uniquely expressible as $z = x + iy$ where $x$ and $y$ are real numbers. The real part $\operatorname{Re}(z) = x$ is the projection onto the real axis, and the imaginary part $\operatorname{Im}(z) = y$ is the projection onto the imaginary axis. These two real-valued functions on $\mathbb{C}$ are linear over $\mathbb{R}$ and satisfy $\operatorname{Re}(z) = \frac{1}{2}(z + z^*)$ and $\operatorname{Im}(z) = \frac{1}{2i}(z - z^*)$, where $z^*$ denotes the complex conjugate.
