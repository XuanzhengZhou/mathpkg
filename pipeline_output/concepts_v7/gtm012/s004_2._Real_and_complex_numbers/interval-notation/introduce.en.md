An interval is a connected subset of the real line $\mathbb{R}$, defined by specifying lower and upper bounds with either strict or non-strict inequality. Open intervals $(a,b)$ exclude the endpoints, closed intervals $[a,b]$ include them, and half-open intervals like $(a,b]$ or $[a,b)$ mix the two conventions. Unbounded intervals extend to $+\infty$ or $-\infty$, which are not real numbers themselves but serve as notational conveniences for sets with no finite upper or lower bound.
