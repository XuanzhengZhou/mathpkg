The least upper bound (also called supremum) of a set $A$ is the smallest among all upper bounds of $A$. When it exists, it is unique. The notation $\operatorname{lub} A$ is used in this text; the standard notation in modern analysis is $\sup A$. The completeness axiom guarantees existence for every nonempty, bounded-above subset of $\mathbb{R}$.
