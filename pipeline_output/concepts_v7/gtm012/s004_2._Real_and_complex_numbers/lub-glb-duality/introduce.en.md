The least upper bound property and the greatest lower bound property are equivalent in $\mathbb{R}$ via the negation map. If a set $A$ is bounded below, then reflecting it about the origin yields a set that is bounded above, and the negative of its supremum is the infimum of $A$. Thus the completeness axiom implies existence of both suprema and infima for appropriately bounded sets.
