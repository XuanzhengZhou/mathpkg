Complex conjugation is the involution on $\mathbb{C}$ that sends $x + iy$ to $x - iy$. It is an $\mathbb{R}$-linear, involutive, multiplicative automorphism of $\mathbb{C}$. The conjugate is fundamental to defining the modulus, computing inverses, and expressing real and imaginary parts algebraically.
