The order on $\mathbb{R}$ is introduced axiomatically via a distinguished subset $P$ of positive elements, rather than by defining $<$ directly. O1 (trichotomy) asserts that every real number is either positive, zero, or negative, with no overlap. O2 guarantees that the sum of two positive numbers remains positive. Together these axioms make $\mathbb{R}$ into an ordered field, and the usual inequality $x < y$ is then defined by the condition $y - x \in P$.
