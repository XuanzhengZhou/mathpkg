The modulus of a complex number generalizes the absolute value of a real number. Geometrically, $|z|$ is the Euclidean distance from the point representing $z$ to the origin in the complex plane. The modulus satisfies $|z| \geq 0$, with equality only for $z = 0$, and $|zw| = |z|\,|w|$.
