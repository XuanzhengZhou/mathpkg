The completeness axiom is the defining property that distinguishes $\mathbb{R}$ from $\mathbb{Q}$. It states that every nonempty subset of $\mathbb{R}$ that is bounded above possesses a least upper bound (supremum). Equivalently, every nonempty subset bounded below has a greatest lower bound (infimum). This axiom is the foundation for all limit arguments in real analysis.
