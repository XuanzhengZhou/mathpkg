Let $\mathbb{C} = \mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}$, the set of all ordered pairs $(x, y)$ with $x, y \in \mathbb{R}$.
Define addition and multiplication on $\mathbb{C}$ by
$$
(x, y) + (x', y') = (x + x',\; y + y'),
$$
$$
(x, y) \cdot (x', y') = (xx' - yy',\; xy' + x'y).
$$
Then $(\mathbb{C}, +, \cdot)$ is a field. Writing a real number $x$ as $(x, 0)$ and defining $i = (0, 1)$, one obtains $i^2 = (-1, 0) = -1$, and every element can be written uniquely as
$$
z = (x, y) = x + iy, \qquad x, y \in \mathbb{R}.
$$
