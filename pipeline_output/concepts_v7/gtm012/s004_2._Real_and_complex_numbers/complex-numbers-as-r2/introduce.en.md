The complex numbers are constructed rigorously as the Cartesian product $\mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}$ equipped with component-wise addition and a twisted multiplication that encodes the relation $i^2 = -1$. This construction avoids postulating a mysterious quantity $i$ and instead defines $i$ simply as the ordered pair $(0,1)$. The resulting set $\mathbb{C}$ forms a field containing a copy of the real numbers as the subset $\{(x,0) : x \in \mathbb{R}\}$, and every complex number admits the standard representation $z = x + iy$ with real and imaginary parts $x$ and $y$.
