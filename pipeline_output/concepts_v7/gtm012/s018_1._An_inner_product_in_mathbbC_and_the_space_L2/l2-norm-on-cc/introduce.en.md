The $L^2$ norm on the space $\mathbb{C}$ of continuous complex-valued periodic functions is the norm induced by the inner product: it is the square root of the inner product of a function with itself. Equivalently, it is the root-mean-square of the function, normalized by the length of the period interval. This norm is bounded above by the supremum norm, reflecting that pointwise control implies $L^2$ control.
