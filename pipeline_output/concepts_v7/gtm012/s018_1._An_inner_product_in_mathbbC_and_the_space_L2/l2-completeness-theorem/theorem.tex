The inner product in $\mathbf{L}^2$ defined by
$$
(F, G) = \lim_{n \to \infty} (u_n, v_n)
$$
where $u_n \to F$, $v_n \to G$ in $\mathbf{L}^2$, satisfies the identities
\begin{align}
(aF, G) &= a(F, G) = (F, a^*G), \\
(F_1 + F_2, G) &= (F_1, G) + (F_2, G), \\
(F, G_1 + G_2) &= (F, G_1) + (F, G_2), \\
(G, F) &= (F, G)^*, \\
(F, F) &\geq 0, \quad (F, F) = 0 \text{ only if } F = 0.
\end{align}

Define
$$
\|F\| = (F, F)^{1/2}.
$$

Then $\|\cdot\|$ is a norm on $\mathbf{L}^2$, and $\mathbf{L}^2$ is complete with respect to this norm. Consequently, $\mathbf{L}^2$ is a Hilbert space.
