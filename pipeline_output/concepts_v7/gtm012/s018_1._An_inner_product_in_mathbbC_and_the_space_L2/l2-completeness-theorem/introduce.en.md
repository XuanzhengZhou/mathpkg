Theorem 1.3 is the culminating result of the section, establishing that $\mathbf{L}^2$ with the extended inner product is a Hilbert space. The inner product on $\mathbf{L}^2$ satisfies the same algebraic identities (linearity, conjugate symmetry, positive definiteness) as on $\mathbb{C}$, the induced norm satisfies the norm axioms, and crucially, $\mathbf{L}^2$ is complete — every Cauchy sequence converges in the space. This is in contrast to $\mathbb{C}$, which is not complete with respect to the $L^2$ norm.
