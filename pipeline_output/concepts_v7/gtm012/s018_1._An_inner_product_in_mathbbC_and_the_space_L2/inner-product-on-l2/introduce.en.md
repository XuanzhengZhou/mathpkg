The inner product originally defined on the space $\mathbb{C}$ of continuous periodic functions is extended to the larger space $\mathbf{L}^2$ by taking limits. For two $L^2$ distributions $F$ and $G$ approximated by Cauchy sequences $(u_n)$ and $(v_n)$, their inner product is the limit of the inner products $(u_n, v_n)$. Lemma 1.2 guarantees that this limit is well-defined and independent of the particular choice of approximating sequences.
