Let $F, G \in \mathbf{L}^2$ and let $(u_n)_{1}^{\infty}, (v_n)_{1}^{\infty} \subset \mathbb{C}$ be Cauchy sequences such that
$$
u_n \to F \quad (\text{in } \mathbf{L}^2), \qquad v_n \to G \quad (\text{in } \mathbf{L}^2).
$$

The inner product on $\mathbf{L}^2$ is defined by
$$
(F, G) = \lim_{n \to \infty} (u_n, v_n).
$$

The limit exists and is independent of the choice of the approximating sequences $(u_n)_{1}^{\infty}$ and $(v_n)_{1}^{\infty}$.
