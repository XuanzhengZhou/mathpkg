The space $\mathbf{L}^2$ is defined as the set of all periodic distributions that can be approximated by Cauchy sequences of continuous periodic functions with respect to the $L^2$ norm. This construction realizes the metric completion of $\mathbb{C}$ as a subspace of the space of periodic distributions $\mathcal{P}'$. The space $\mathbf{L}^2$ is a vector space: it is closed under scalar multiplication and addition, with the operations inherited from the approximating sequences.
