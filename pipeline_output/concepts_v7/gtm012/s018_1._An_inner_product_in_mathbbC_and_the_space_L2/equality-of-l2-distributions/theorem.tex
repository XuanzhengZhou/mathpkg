Let $(u_n)_{1}^{\infty}, (v_n)_{1}^{\infty} \subset \mathbb{C}$ be Cauchy sequences with respect to the $L^2$ norm, and let $F, G \in \mathbf{L}^2$ be the corresponding periodic distributions:
$$
F_{u_n} \to F, \qquad F_{v_n} \to G \quad (\text{in } \mathcal{P}').
$$

Then $F = G$ if and only if
$$
\|u_n - v_n\| \to 0 \quad \text{as } n \to \infty.
$$
