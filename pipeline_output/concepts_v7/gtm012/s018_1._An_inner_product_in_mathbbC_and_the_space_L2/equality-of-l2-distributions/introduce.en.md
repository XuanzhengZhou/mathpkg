Lemma 1.2 provides a criterion for when two $L^2$ distributions defined by different Cauchy sequences of continuous periodic functions are equal: the $L^2$ norm of the difference of the approximating sequences must converge to zero. This result is essential for establishing that the extension of the inner product to $\mathbf{L}^2$ is well-defined, as it guarantees that the limiting inner product does not depend on the choice of approximating sequences.
