The Schwarz inequality (also known as the Cauchy–Schwarz inequality) states that for any two continuous periodic functions, the absolute value of their inner product is bounded by the product of their $L^2$ norms. This is Lemma 1.1 of the text and is a cornerstone inequality in Hilbert space theory. The proof uses the non-negativity of the norm of $u - av$ for a suitably chosen complex scalar $a$.
