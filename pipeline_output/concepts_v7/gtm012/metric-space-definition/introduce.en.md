A metric space abstracts the notion of distance. The three axioms — positivity, symmetry, and the triangle inequality — capture the essential properties of Euclidean distance. Key examples include $\mathbb{R}^n$ with Euclidean distance, $\mathbb{C}$ with $d(z,w) = |z-w|$, and function spaces with the supremum metric.
