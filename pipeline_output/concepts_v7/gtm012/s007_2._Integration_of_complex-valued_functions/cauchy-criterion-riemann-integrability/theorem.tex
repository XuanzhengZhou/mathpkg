\textbf{Proposition 2.3.} A bounded function $f: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}$ is Riemann integrable on $[a, b]$ if and only if for every $\varepsilon > 0$ there exists $\delta > 0$ such that
$$|S(f; P) - S(f; Q)| < \varepsilon$$
whenever $P$ and $Q$ are partitions of $[a, b]$ with $|P| < \delta$ and $|Q| < \delta$.
