The Mean Value Theorem states that for a real-valued function continuous on a closed interval and differentiable on its interior, the slope of the secant line equals the derivative at some interior point. The theorem is proved by reducing to Rolle's theorem via an auxiliary function $g(x) = f(x) - (x-a)[f(b)-f(a)](b-a)^{-1}$. It is a fundamental tool in real analysis that does not generalize directly to complex-valued functions.
