The mesh of a partition, also called the norm of the partition, measures the coarseness of the subdivision by taking the length of the largest subinterval. As the mesh tends to zero, every subinterval becomes arbitrarily small. This notion is essential for the epsilon-delta definition of the Riemann integral, where the limit is taken as the mesh approaches zero.
