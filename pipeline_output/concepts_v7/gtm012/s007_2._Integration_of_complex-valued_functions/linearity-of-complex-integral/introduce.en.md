The Riemann integral for complex-valued functions is linear: the integral of a sum equals the sum of the integrals, and a constant complex factor can be pulled out of the integral. The proof follows directly from the corresponding linearity of Riemann sums: $S(f+g;P) = S(f;P) + S(g;P)$ and $S(cf;P) = cS(f;P)$, then taking the limit as the mesh tends to zero.
