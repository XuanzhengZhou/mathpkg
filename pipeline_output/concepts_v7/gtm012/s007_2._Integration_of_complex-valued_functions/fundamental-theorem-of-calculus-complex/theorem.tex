\textbf{Theorem 3.6.} \text{(Fundamental Theorem of Calculus).} Suppose $f: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}$ is continuous and suppose $c \in [a, b]$. Define $F: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}$ by
$$F(x) = \int_c^x f(t)\,dt.$$
Then $F$ is differentiable at each point of $(a, b)$ and
$$F'(x) = f(x), \qquad x \in (a, b).$$
