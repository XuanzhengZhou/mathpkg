The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus extends to complex-valued continuous functions: the integral function $F(x) = \int_c^x f$ is differentiable and its derivative recovers the original function $f$. The proof reduces to the real case by applying the real FTC to the real and imaginary parts of $f$, using Proposition 2.1 for the integral and Proposition 3.2 for the derivative.
