For complex-valued functions of a real variable, differentiability at a point implies continuity at that point, just as in the real case. The proof uses the definition of the derivative: if $f'(x)$ exists, then $|f(y)-f(x)| \leq |f(y)-f(x)-f'(x)(y-x)| + |f'(x)||y-x|$, and the first term is $o(|y-x|)$ while the second tends to zero as $y \to x$.
