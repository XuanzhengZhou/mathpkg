The Riemann integral is additive with respect to the domain of integration: a function is integrable over a larger interval if and only if it is integrable over each of two adjacent subintervals, and the integral over the whole equals the sum of the integrals over the parts. The forward direction uses the Cauchy criterion; the converse direction constructs partitions that include the splitting point $b$.
