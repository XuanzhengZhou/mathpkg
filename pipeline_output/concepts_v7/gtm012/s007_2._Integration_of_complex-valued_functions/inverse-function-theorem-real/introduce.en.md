The Inverse Function Theorem for real functions of a real variable states that if a function has a strictly positive derivative on an interval, it is strictly increasing and has a differentiable inverse. The derivative of the inverse is the reciprocal of the derivative of the original function evaluated at the corresponding point. Strict monotonicity follows from the Mean Value Theorem, and differentiability of the inverse follows from the continuity of $f^{-1}$ and the limit definition of the derivative.
