A bounded function $f: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}$ is said to be \text{integrable} (in the sense of Riemann) if there is a number $z \in \mathbb{C}$ such that
$$\lim_{|P| \to 0} S(f; P) = z.$$
More precisely, for any $\varepsilon > 0$ there is a $\delta > 0$ such that
$$|S(f; P) - z| < \varepsilon \quad \text{if } |P| < \delta.$$
If this is the case, the number $z$ is called the \text{integral} of $f$ on $[a, b]$ and denoted by
$$\int_a^b f \quad \text{or} \quad \int_a^b f(x) \, dx.$$
