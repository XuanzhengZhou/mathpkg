The Riemann integral for complex-valued functions is defined exactly as for real-valued functions, using the limit of Riemann sums as the mesh of the partition tends to zero. A bounded complex-valued function is Riemann integrable if this limit exists in the complex plane. The integral is then a complex number, and the notation $\int_a^b f$ or $\int_a^b f(x)\,dx$ is used.
