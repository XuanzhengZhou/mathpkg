A Hilbert space is a complete inner product space — an abstraction of Euclidean geometry to possibly infinite dimensions. Every Hilbert space has a notion of orthogonality, and the general theory rests on the closest-point lemma and the Riesz representation theorem. Examples include $L^2$, $\ell^2$, and $\mathbb{C}^n$. Every Hilbert space is a Banach space, but the inner product structure provides much richer geometry.
