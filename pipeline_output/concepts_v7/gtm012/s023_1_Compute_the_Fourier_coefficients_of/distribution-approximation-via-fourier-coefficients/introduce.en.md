Theorem 2.3 is the distributional counterpart of Theorem 2.2. Given a sequence $(F_m)$ of periodic distributions whose Fourier coefficients $(a_{m,n})$ satisfy a uniform polynomial-growth bound $|a_{m,n}| \leq c|n|^r$ and converge pointwise to $(a_n)$, the limiting coefficients determine a distribution $F \in \mathcal{P}'$, and $F_m$ converges to $F$ in the sense of distributions. The proof reduces the distribution case to the smooth-function case by integrating (differentiating in Fourier) the coefficients $k \geq r+2$ times. Together with Theorem 2.2, this establishes a unified approximation framework for both $\mathcal{P}$ and $\mathcal{P}'$.
