Let $F, G \in \mathcal{P}'$ have Fourier coefficients $(a_n)_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}$ and $(b_n)_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}$, respectively.
Let $u \in \mathcal{P}$ have Fourier coefficients $(c_n)_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}$.

Then the convolution $F * u$ has Fourier coefficients $(a_n c_n)_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}$,
and the convolution $F * G$ has Fourier coefficients $(a_n b_n)_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}$.
