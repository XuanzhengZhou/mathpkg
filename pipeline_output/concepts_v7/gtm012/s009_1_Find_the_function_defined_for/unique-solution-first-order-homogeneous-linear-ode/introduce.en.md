The simplest nontrivial differential equation is $f' = af$, whose solutions are scalar multiples of the exponential function $E(ax)$. By combining the power series definition of $E$ with termwise differentiation, one obtains both existence (the series satisfies the equation) and uniqueness (any solution must match the series expansion). This theorem provides the foundation for all linear constant-coefficient differential equations of higher order.
