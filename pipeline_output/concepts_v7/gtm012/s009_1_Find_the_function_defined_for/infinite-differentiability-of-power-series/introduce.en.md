A power series with a positive radius of convergence is not merely differentiable once or twice — it is infinitely differentiable within its open disc of convergence. Moreover, the derivatives can be computed by differentiating the series term-by-term, and each resulting differentiated series also converges on the same interval. This property is fundamental because it shows that power series represent analytic functions, which are among the most well-behaved objects in analysis and form the foundation for the theory of differential equations via series methods.
