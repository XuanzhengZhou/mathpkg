Let
$$f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n(x - x_0)^n, \quad |x - x_0| < R,$$
where $R > 0$ is the radius of convergence. Then $f$ is infinitely differentiable on $(x_0 - R, x_0 + R)$, and for each $k \in \mathbb{N}$,
$$f^{(k)}(x) = \sum_{n=k}^{\infty} n(n-1)(n-2) \cdots (n-k+1)\,a_n\,(x - x_0)^{n-k}, \quad |x - x_0| < R.$$
