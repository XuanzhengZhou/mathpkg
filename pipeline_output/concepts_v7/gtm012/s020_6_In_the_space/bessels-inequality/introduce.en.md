Bessel's inequality provides an upper bound for finite partial sums of squared Fourier coefficients: the sum never exceeds the squared norm. It holds for any orthonormal set (not necessarily a basis) and is a consequence of the fact that the projection onto a finite-dimensional subspace reduces norm. In the presence of an orthonormal basis, Bessel's inequality becomes Bessel's equality in the limit.
