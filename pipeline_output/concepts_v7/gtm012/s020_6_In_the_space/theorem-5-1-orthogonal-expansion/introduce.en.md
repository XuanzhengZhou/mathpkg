Theorem 5.1 is the foundational result for orthogonal expansions in Hilbert space. It states that every element of a separable Hilbert space can be uniquely represented as an infinite series of its Fourier coefficients with respect to any orthonormal basis. The theorem encompasses Bessel's equality ($\|u\|^2 = \sum |(u,e_n)|^2$), Parseval's identity ($(u,v) = \sum a_n b_n^*$), and the Riesz-Fischer theorem (every $\ell^2$ sequence corresponds to an element of $H$). This generalizes the classical Fourier series theory to any Hilbert space.
