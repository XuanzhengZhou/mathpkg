Bessel's equality states that the squared norm of any Hilbert space element equals the sum of the squared moduli of its Fourier coefficients with respect to any orthonormal basis. It is a direct consequence of the orthogonal expansion theorem. The identity generalizes the Pythagorean theorem to infinite dimensions and is fundamental to the theory of Fourier series, where it becomes Parseval's theorem for periodic functions.
