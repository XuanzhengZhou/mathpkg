Suppose $H$ is a Hilbert space with an orthonormal basis $(e_n)_{-\infty}^{\infty}$ indexed by all integers. If $u \in H$, then
$$u = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} (u, e_n) e_n$$
in the sense that
$$\left\| u - \sum_{n=-N}^{N} (u, e_n) e_n \right\| \to 0 \quad \text{as} \quad N \to \infty.$$
The coefficients satisfy
$$\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} |(u, e_n)|^2 = \|u\|^2.$$
More generally, if $u = \sum a_n e_n$ and $v = \sum b_n e_n$, then
$$(u, v) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n b_n^* = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} (u, e_n)(e_n, v).$$
Conversely, if $(a_n)_{-\infty}^{\infty}$ satisfies $\sum |a_n|^2 < \infty$, there is a unique $u \in H$ with this expansion.
