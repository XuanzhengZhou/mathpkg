In any Hilbert space with an orthonormal basis, the Fourier coefficients of an element are simply its inner products with the basis vectors. This generalizes the classical Fourier coefficients of periodic functions. The orthogonal expansion theorem guarantees that these coefficients determine the element uniquely and that the series converges in norm. This unification of Fourier analysis under Hilbert space theory is one of the central achievements of functional analysis.
