Convolution of a periodic distribution $F$ with a smooth periodic function $u$ is defined by evaluating $F$ on the translated reversal of $u$. This definition extends the classical convolution of functions to distributions, motivated by the identity $(v * u)(x) = F_v(T_x \tilde{u})$ when $v$ is a continuous periodic function. The resulting object $F * u$ is a smooth periodic function, forming the bridge between distribution theory and classical analysis on the circle.
