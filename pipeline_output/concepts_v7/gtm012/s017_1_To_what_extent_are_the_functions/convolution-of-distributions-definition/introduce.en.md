The convolution of two periodic distributions $F$ and $G$ is defined by $(F * G)(u) = F(G^\sim * u)$, where $G^\sim$ is the reversal of $G$. This definition is motivated by Proposition 7.4 and ensures that when $G$ is a regular distribution $F_v$ induced by a smooth function $v$, the convolution $F * F_v$ coincides with the regular distribution induced by $f = F * v$. The result $F * G$ is again a periodic distribution, making $\mathcal{P}'$ a convolution algebra.
