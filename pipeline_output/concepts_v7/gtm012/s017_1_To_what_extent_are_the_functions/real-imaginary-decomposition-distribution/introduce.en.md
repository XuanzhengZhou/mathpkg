A periodic distribution is called real if it equals its complex conjugate, where the conjugate is defined by applying complex conjugation to both the test function and the resulting value. Every periodic distribution admits a unique decomposition $F = G + iH$ into real and imaginary parts, given explicitly by $G = \frac{1}{2}(F + F^*)$ and $H = \frac{1}{2i}(F - F^*)$. This decomposition mirrors the familiar decomposition of complex numbers and is compatible with all distribution operations, including convolution and differentiation.
