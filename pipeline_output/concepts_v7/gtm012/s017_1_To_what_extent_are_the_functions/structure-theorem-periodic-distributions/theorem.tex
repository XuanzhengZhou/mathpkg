If $v \in \mathcal{C}$ and $f$ is a constant function, then
$$D^k F_v + F_f \in \mathcal{P}'.$$

Conversely, if $F \in \mathcal{P}'$ is of order $k + 2$ with $k \geq 0$, then there exist a continuous periodic function $v \in \mathcal{C}$ and a constant function $f$ such that
$$F = D^k F_v + F_f.$$

Equivalently, every periodic distribution can be represented as a finite sum of derivatives of continuous functions.
