The Dirac delta distribution $\delta$ serves as the multiplicative identity in the convolution algebra $(\mathcal{P}', *)$. For any periodic distribution $F$, convolving with $\delta$ on either side returns $F$ itself: $\delta * F = F * \delta = F$. This extends the analogous identity for smooth functions ($\delta * u = u$) to arbitrary distributions and confirms that the convolution of distributions is a natural generalization of the classical operation.
