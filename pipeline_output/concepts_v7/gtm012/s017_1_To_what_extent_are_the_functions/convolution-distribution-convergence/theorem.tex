Suppose $(f_n)_{1}^{\infty} \subset \mathcal{P}$, $(g_n)_{1}^{\infty} \subset \mathcal{P}$, and set
$$F_n = F_{f_n}, \quad G_n = F_{g_n}.$$
If
$$F_n \to F \quad (\mathcal{P}') \quad \text{and} \quad G_n \to G \quad (\mathcal{P}'),$$
then
$$F_n * G \to F * G \quad (\mathcal{P}') \quad \text{and} \quad F * G_n \to F * G \quad (\mathcal{P}').$$
