Corollary 7.6 states that convolution of distributions is separately continuous with respect to convergence in $\mathcal{P}'$: if a sequence of regular distributions $F_n$ converges to $F$, then $F_n * G \to F * G$ for any fixed $G$, and similarly $F * G_n \to F * G$ when $G_n \to G$. This result is fundamental for approximating convolutions of arbitrary distributions by convolutions of smooth functions, which is the main technique used to prove algebraic properties like commutativity and associativity in Proposition 7.8.
