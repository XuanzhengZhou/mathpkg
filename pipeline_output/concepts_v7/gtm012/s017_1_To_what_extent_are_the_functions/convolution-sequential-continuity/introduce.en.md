Lemma 7.5 establishes that for a fixed periodic distribution $F$, the map $u \mapsto F * u$ from smooth periodic functions to themselves is sequentially continuous. This continuity property is essential for proving that the convolution of two distributions $F * G$ is again a periodic distribution, as it guarantees that the defining formula $(F * G)(u) = F(G^\sim * u)$ yields a continuous linear functional.
