The convolution of the Dirac delta distribution with a smooth periodic function returns the function itself, making $\delta$ the identity element for this operation. More generally, convolution with the $k$-th derivative $D^k \delta$ yields the $k$-th derivative of the function: $(D^k \delta) * u = D^k u$. These identities show that differentiation of smooth functions can be expressed as convolution with a distribution, unifying the algebraic and analytic viewpoints.
