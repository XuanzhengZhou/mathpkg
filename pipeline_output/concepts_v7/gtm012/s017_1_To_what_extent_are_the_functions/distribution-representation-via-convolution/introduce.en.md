Proposition 7.4 provides a duality formula: if one convolves a distribution $F$ with a smooth function $u$ to obtain $f = F * u$, then the regular distribution $F_f$ induced by $f$ acts on a test function $v$ in the same way that $F$ acts on the convolution $\tilde{u} * v$. This formula is central to the definition of convolution of two distributions, as it shows how the convolution of a distribution with a function is compatible with the embedding of functions into distributions.
