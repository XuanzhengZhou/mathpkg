The order of a periodic distribution quantifies the highest number of derivatives of a test function that appear in the estimate controlling $|F(u)|$. A distribution of order $0$ is a measure (bounded by the supremum norm of $u$), while the $k$-th derivative of the delta distribution has order $k$. The fundamental theorem states that every periodic distribution is of finite order, which is a consequence of the completeness of $\mathcal{P}$ and the uniform boundedness principle. This concept is central to the structure theorem for periodic distributions.
