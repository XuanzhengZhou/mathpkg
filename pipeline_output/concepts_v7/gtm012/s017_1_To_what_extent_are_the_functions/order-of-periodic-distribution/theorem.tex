A periodic distribution $F \in \mathcal{P}'$ is said to be of \textbf{order} $k$ if there exists a constant $c$ such that
$$|F(u)| \leq c\left(|u| + |Du| + \cdots + |D^k u|\right), \quad \text{for all } u \in \mathcal{P},$$
where $|u| = \sup_x |u(x)|$. Every $F \in \mathcal{P}'$ is of finite order, i.e., of order $k$ for some $k \geq 0$.
