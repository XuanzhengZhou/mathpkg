Proposition 7.8 establishes that the space $\mathcal{P}'$ of periodic distributions, equipped with the convolution operation, forms a commutative associative algebra with the delta distribution $\delta$ as its unit. Convolution commutes with scalar multiplication, translation, and differentiation in both arguments. The proofs of commutativity and associativity rely on approximation of distributions by smooth functions and the corresponding properties for classical convolution of functions, illustrating the power of the approximation technique developed by Theorem 7.7 and Corollary 7.6.
