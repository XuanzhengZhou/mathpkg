The inner product on the space of continuous periodic functions is defined as the average of the product $u(x)v(x)^*$ over one period. The Schwarz inequality (also called Cauchy-Schwarz) bounds the inner product by the product of norms. This inner product makes $\mathbb{C}$ a pre-Hilbert space, though it is incomplete; its completion yields $L^2$.
