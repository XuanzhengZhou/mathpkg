This theorem characterizes exactly which two-sided complex sequences can arise as the Fourier coefficients of a periodic distribution $F \in \mathcal{P}'$. The condition of slow growth (polynomial boundedness) is necessary because every periodic distribution is a finite-order derivative of a continuous periodic function plus a constant term. It is sufficient because a slowly growing sequence can be integrated termwise enough times to yield the Fourier coefficients of a continuous function, and then differentiated back to obtain the desired distribution.
