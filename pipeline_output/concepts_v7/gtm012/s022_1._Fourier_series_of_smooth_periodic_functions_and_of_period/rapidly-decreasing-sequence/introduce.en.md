A sequence of complex numbers indexed by all integers is said to be of rapid decrease if its absolute value decays faster than any negative power of $|n|$ as $n \to \pm\infty$. This condition characterizes exactly those sequences which arise as the Fourier coefficients of smooth ($C^\infty$) periodic functions. The definition is central to the Fourier-theoretic approach to the space $\mathcal{P}$ of smooth periodic functions and its dual $\mathcal{P}'$ of periodic distributions.
