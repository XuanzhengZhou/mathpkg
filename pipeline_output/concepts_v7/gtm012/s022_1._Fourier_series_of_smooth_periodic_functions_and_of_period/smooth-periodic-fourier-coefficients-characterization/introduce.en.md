This theorem provides a complete characterization of the sequences that can arise as Fourier coefficients of smooth ($C^\infty$) $2\pi$-periodic functions. The forward direction uses the fact that differentiation multiplies Fourier coefficients by $in$, so rapid decrease follows from boundedness of derivatives. The converse follows by noting that rapid decrease guarantees uniform convergence of the Fourier series and all its term-by-term derivatives, hence the limit is a smooth periodic function whose Fourier coefficients are the given sequence.
