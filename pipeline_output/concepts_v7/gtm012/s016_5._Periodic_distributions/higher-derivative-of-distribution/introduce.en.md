Higher derivatives of a periodic distribution are defined inductively by repeatedly applying the derivative operation: $(D^k F)(u) = (-1)^k F(D^k u)$. This means every periodic distribution is infinitely differentiable. The alternating sign $(-1)^k$ arises from $k$ successive integrations by parts. This definition is fundamental to the theory, as it allows any periodic distribution to be represented as a finite-order derivative of a continuous function (Theorem 6.1).
