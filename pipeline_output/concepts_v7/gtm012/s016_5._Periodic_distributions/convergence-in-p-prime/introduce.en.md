Convergence in $\mathcal{P}'$ is defined pointwise on test functions: a sequence of periodic distributions converges if it converges when evaluated at each smooth periodic function. This is the weak-* topology on the continuous dual of $\mathcal{P}$. Under this notion of convergence, the operations of differentiation, translation, and reflection are all continuous.
