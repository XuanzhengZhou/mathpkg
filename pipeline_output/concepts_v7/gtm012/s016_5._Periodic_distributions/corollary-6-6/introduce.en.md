Corollary 6.6 states that the only periodic distributions with zero derivative are the constant function distributions. This is the distributional analogue of the elementary calculus fact that a function with zero derivative is constant. The proof uses Lemma 6.5: if $DG = 0$, let $f = (2\pi)^{-1} G(e) e$ and consider $H = G - F_f$, which satisfies $DH = 0$ and $H(e) = 0$, forcing $H = 0$ by uniqueness. This result is crucial for the uniqueness part of the structure theorem.
