The space $\mathcal{P}'$ of all periodic distributions forms a vector space under pointwise addition and scalar multiplication. This is the continuous dual of the space $\mathcal{P}$ of smooth $2\pi$-periodic functions. The embedding $v \mapsto F_v$ of continuous periodic functions into $\mathcal{P}'$ is a linear injection, so $\mathcal{C}$ can be regarded as a subspace of $\mathcal{P}'$.
