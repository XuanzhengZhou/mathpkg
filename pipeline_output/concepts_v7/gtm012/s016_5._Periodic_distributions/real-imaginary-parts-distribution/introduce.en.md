The real and imaginary parts of a periodic distribution are defined by analogy with the corresponding decomposition for functions: $\operatorname{Re} F = \frac{1}{2}(F + F^*)$ and $\operatorname{Im} F = \frac{1}{2i}(F - F^*)$, where $F^*$ is the complex conjugate distribution. A distribution $F$ is called real if $F = F^*$, which implies $F(u)$ is real whenever $u$ is a real-valued test function. Every distribution decomposes uniquely as $F = \operatorname{Re} F + i \operatorname{Im} F$.
