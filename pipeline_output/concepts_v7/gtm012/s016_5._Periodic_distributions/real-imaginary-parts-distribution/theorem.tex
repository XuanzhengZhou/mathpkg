The real and imaginary parts of a function $v \in \mathcal{C}$ are defined by

$$\operatorname{Re} v = \frac{1}{2}(v + v^*), \quad \operatorname{Im} v = \frac{1}{2i}(v - v^*).$$

Similarly, the real and imaginary parts of a periodic distribution $F \in \mathcal{P}'$ are defined by

$$\operatorname{Re} F = \frac{1}{2}(F + F^*), \quad \operatorname{Im} F = \frac{1}{2i}(F - F^*),$$

where $F^*$ is the complex conjugate distribution defined by $F^*(u) = \overline{F(\overline{u})}$.
