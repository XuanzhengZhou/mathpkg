Lemma 6.5 is the antiderivative lemma for periodic distributions: given a distribution $F$ of order $k \geq 1$, there exists a unique distribution $G$ of order $k-1$ whose derivative is $F$ and which vanishes on the constant function $e(x) = 1$. Uniqueness follows from the condition $G(e) = 0$, which fixes the integration constant. Existence is proved by explicitly constructing $G(u) = -F(Su)$ using a smoothing operator $S$. This lemma provides the inductive step for proving the structure theorem.
