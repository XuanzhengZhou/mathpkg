The Dirac $\delta$-distribution is the periodic distribution defined by point evaluation at the origin: $\delta(u) = u(0)$. It is the prototypical example of a distribution that is not induced by any continuous function. One can prove this by constructing a sequence of smooth functions converging to zero pointwise almost everywhere, yet constantly equal to $1$ at the origin — the functionals $F_v$ and $\delta$ then differ on this sequence.
