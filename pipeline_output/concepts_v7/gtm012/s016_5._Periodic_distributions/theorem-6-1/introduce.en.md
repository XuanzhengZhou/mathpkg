Theorem 6.1 is the structure theorem for periodic distributions: every periodic distribution can be represented as a finite-order derivative of a continuous periodic function, plus a constant function distribution. This means that, up to a constant, all periodic distributions arise by repeatedly differentiating continuous periodic functions. The theorem provides the fundamental link between the abstract definition of distributions and concrete function representations.
