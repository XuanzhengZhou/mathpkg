Lemma 6.4 provides a representation for order-zero periodic distributions that vanish on constant functions. Such a distribution can be written as the second derivative of a function distribution: $F = D^2 F_v$ for some continuous periodic $v$. The proof constructs $v$ explicitly using an integral kernel involving the positive part function $(x-s)^+$ and the extension of $F$ to a continuous linear functional on $\mathcal{C}$. This lemma is a key step in proving the full structure theorem (Theorem 6.1).
