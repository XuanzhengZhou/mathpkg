The order of a periodic distribution measures how many derivatives of a test function are needed to bound the distribution's action. A distribution $F$ has order $k$ if $|F(u)|$ is controlled by the sup-norms of $u$ and its first $k$ derivatives. For example, the Dirac $\delta$-distribution has order $0$, while $D^k F_v$ for $v \in \mathcal{C}$ has order $k$. A fundamental theorem (6.2) asserts that every periodic distribution has some finite order.
