Differentiation is a continuous operation on the space of periodic distributions: if a sequence $F_n$ converges to $F$ in $\mathcal{P}'$, then the derivatives $D^k F_n$ converge to $D^k F$ for every order $k$. This follows directly from the definition: $(D^k F_n)(u) = (-1)^k F_n(D^k u) \to (-1)^k F(D^k u) = (D^k F)(u)$. This continuity property is one of the key advantages of the distributional framework over classical function spaces.
