The topology on $\mathcal{L}$ is defined by the countable family of seminorms $|u|_{k,a,M}$. A function belongs to $\mathcal{L}$ precisely when all these seminorms are finite. Convergence in $\mathcal{L}$ means simultaneous convergence in all seminorms, making $\mathcal{L}$ a Fr'{e}chet space. This seminorm family is partially ordered by the comparison inequalities of Lemma 2.4.
