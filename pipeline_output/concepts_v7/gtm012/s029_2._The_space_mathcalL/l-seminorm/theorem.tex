For $u \in \mathcal{L}$, integers $k \geq 0$, and real numbers $a, M$, define

$$|u|_{k,a,M} = \sup \{\, e^{at} |D^{k} u(t)| \mid t \in [M, \infty) \,\}.$$

These are finite for all $u \in \mathcal{L}$, and conversely, if $|u|_{k,a,M}$ is finite for every integer $k \geq 0$ and every $a, M \in \mathbb{R}$, then $u \in \mathcal{L}$.

A sequence $(u_n)_{1}^{\infty} \subset \mathcal{L}$ is said to converge to $u \in \mathcal{L}$ in the sense of $\mathcal{L}$, denoted $u_n \to u (\mathcal{L})$, if for every $k, a, M$,

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} |u_n - u|_{k,a,M} = 0.$$
