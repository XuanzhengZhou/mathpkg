Although the exponential functions $e_z(t) = e^{-zt}$ do not belong to $\mathcal{L}$ (they do not decay at $+\infty$ when $\operatorname{Re} z \leq 0$), Lemma 2.3 shows they can be approximated in the seminorm topology by functions in $\mathcal{L}$. This approximation property is essential for extending the Laplace transform from functions to distributions: one approximates $e_z$ by a sequence in $\mathcal{L}$ and applies the distribution to each approximant.
