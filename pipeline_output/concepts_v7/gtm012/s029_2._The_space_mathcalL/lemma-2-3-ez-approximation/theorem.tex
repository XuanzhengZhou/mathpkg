For $z \in \mathbb{C}$, define $e_z(t) = e^{-zt}$, $t \in \mathbb{R}$. The functions $e_z$ are not in $\mathcal{L}$ for any $z \in \mathbb{C}$. However, if $\operatorname{Re} z > a$, then there exists a sequence $(u_n)_{1}^{\infty} \subset \mathcal{L}$ such that for every integer $k \geq 0$ and every $M \in \mathbb{R}$,

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} |u_n - e_z|_{k,a,M} = 0.$$
