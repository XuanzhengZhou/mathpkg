$\mathcal{P}$ is not a Banach space because its topology cannot be given by a single norm. Instead, it is a Frechet space whose topology is defined by a countable family of seminorms $\|D^k u\|$. Convergence means that all derivatives converge uniformly. This space is the natural domain for periodic distributions.
