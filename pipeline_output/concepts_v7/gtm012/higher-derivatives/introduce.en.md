The class $C^k$ consists of functions with $k$ continuous derivatives. $C^\infty$ (smooth) functions have derivatives of all orders. Polynomials, exponentials, and trigonometric functions are $C^\infty$. The hierarchy $C^0 \supset C^1 \supset C^2 \supset \cdots \supset C^\infty$ is strict: there exist functions in each class that are not in the next.
