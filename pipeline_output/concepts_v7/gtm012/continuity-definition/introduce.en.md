Continuity captures the idea that small changes in input produce small changes in output. The $\varepsilon$-$\delta$ definition makes this precise in any metric space. Equivalent characterizations include: the inverse image of every open set is open, and $f(x_n) \to f(x)$ whenever $x_n \to x$. Continuous functions on compact sets are uniformly continuous and attain their bounds.
