This theorem establishes that $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{C}$ are complete metric spaces: convergence is equivalent to the Cauchy property. It provides a criterion for existence of a limit based solely on the terms of the sequence, without knowing the limit value. For example, it justifies defining $\pi$ as the limit of a Cauchy sequence of rational approximations.
