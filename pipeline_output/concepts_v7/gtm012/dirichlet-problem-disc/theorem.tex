Suppose $F$ is a periodic distribution. There exists a unique function $u(r, \theta)$ smooth in the open unit disc, satisfying Laplace's equation in polar coordinates, such that the distributions defined by $u_r(\theta) = u(r, \theta)$ converge to $F$ in $\mathcal{P}'$ as $r \to 1$. The solution is given by convolution with the Poisson kernel:
\[
u_r = F * P_r.
\]
If $F = F_g$ with $g \in \mathcal{C}$, then $u_r \to g$ uniformly as $r \to 1$.
