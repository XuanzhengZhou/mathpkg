A polynomial differential operator is a linear differential operator with constant coefficients, formed by substituting the differentiation operator $D$ into a polynomial $p(z)$. This notation compactly represents an $n$th-order linear ordinary differential equation as $p(D)u = f$, where $a_n \neq 0$ ensures genuine order $n$. The operator acts linearly on both smooth functions and distributions, and its algebraic properties (factoring, inversion via Laplace transform) mirror those of the polynomial $p(z)$.
