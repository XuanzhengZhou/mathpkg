The Green's function for a polynomial differential operator $p(D)$ is defined as a contour integral of $e^{tz} p(z)^{-1}$. It arises naturally as the inverse Laplace transform of $1/p(z)$ and serves as the fundamental solution to $p(D)G = \delta$ (in the distributional sense). Once computed, it allows solving the inhomogeneous equation $p(D)u = f$ via convolution $u = G * f$.
