Theorem 7.1 guarantees that every linear constant-coefficient ODE $p(D)F = H$ has a unique solution in the space $\mathcal{L}'$ of distributions (duals of rapidly decreasing smooth functions). The proof uses the Laplace transform: since distributions in $\mathcal{L}'$ are uniquely determined by their Laplace transforms, the equation reduces to $p(z) LF(z) = LH(z)$, which is solved by $LF(z) = p(z)^{-1} LH(z)$ for $\operatorname{Re} z$ large enough that $p(z) \neq 0$.
