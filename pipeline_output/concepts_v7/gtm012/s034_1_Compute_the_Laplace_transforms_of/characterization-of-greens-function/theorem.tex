Let $p$ be a polynomial of degree $n \geq 1$ with leading coefficient $a_n \neq 0$, and let $z_1, \ldots, z_r$ be its distinct roots with multiplicities $m_1, \ldots, m_r$. The \textbf{Green's function} $G$ for $p(D)$ is the unique function satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(7)] $p(D)G(t) = 0$ for $t > 0$.
\item[(8)] $D^k G(0+) = 0$ for $0 \leq k \leq n-2$.
\item[(9)] $G(t)$ is a linear combination of the functions $t^k e^{z_j t}$, $0 \leq k < m_j$, for $t > 0$.
\item[(10)] $D^{n-1} G(0+) = a_n^{-1}$.
\item[(11)] $G(t) = 0$ for $t < 0$.
\end{enumerate}
