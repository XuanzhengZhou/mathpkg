Fourier coefficients decompose a function or distribution into frequency components. For $L^2$ distributions, the coefficients are given by the inner product with the complex exponentials. The sequence $(e_n)_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}$ forms an orthonormal basis for $L^2$, meaning every element has a unique Fourier expansion.
