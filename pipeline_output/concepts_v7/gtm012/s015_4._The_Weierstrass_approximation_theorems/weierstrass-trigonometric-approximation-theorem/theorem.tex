If $u \in \mathcal{C}$ and $v \in \mathcal{P}$, there are sequences $(u_n)_{1}^{\infty}$ and $(v_n)_{1}^{\infty}$ of trigonometric polynomials such that

$$|u_n - u| \to 0$$

and

$$v_n \to v \quad (\mathcal{P}).$$

If $u, v$ are real-valued, then so are the sequences $(u_n)_{1}^{\infty}, (v_n)_{1}^{\infty}$ constructed here.
