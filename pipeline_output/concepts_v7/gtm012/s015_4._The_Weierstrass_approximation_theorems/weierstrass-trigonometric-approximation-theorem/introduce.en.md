Theorem 4.3, the Weierstrass trigonometric approximation theorem, states that every continuous $2\pi$-periodic function can be uniformly approximated by trigonometric polynomials. Moreover, smooth periodic functions can be approximated in the stronger $\mathcal{P}$-topology, meaning all derivatives also converge uniformly. The proof uses convolution with the approximate identity constructed in Lemma 4.1 together with the fact (Lemma 4.2) that the convolution of a trigonometric polynomial with any periodic function yields a trigonometric polynomial.
