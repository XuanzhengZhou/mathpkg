A trigonometric polynomial is a finite linear combination of complex exponentials $\exp(ikx)$ with integer frequencies $k$. Equivalently, it can be expressed as a polynomial in $\cos x$ and $\sin x$, since $\exp(\pm ikx) = (\cos x \pm i \sin x)^k$. Trigonometric polynomials are $2\pi$-periodic, infinitely differentiable, and serve as the fundamental building blocks for approximating continuous periodic functions in Weierstrass-type theorems.
