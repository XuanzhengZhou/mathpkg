Corollary 4.4 follows directly from Theorem 4.3: since every continuous $2\pi$-periodic function can be uniformly approximated by trigonometric polynomials, and every trigonometric polynomial is itself a smooth periodic function (belongs to $\mathcal{P}$), it follows that $\mathcal{P}$ is dense in the space $\mathcal{C}$ of continuous $2\pi$-periodic functions in the uniform topology. This density result is fundamental for Fourier analysis and the theory of distributions on the circle.
