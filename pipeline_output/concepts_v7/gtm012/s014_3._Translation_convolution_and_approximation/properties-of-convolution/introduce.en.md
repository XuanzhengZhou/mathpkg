Proposition 3.1 establishes the fundamental algebraic properties of convolution on the space $\mathcal{C}$ of continuous $2\pi$-periodic functions. Convolution is commutative, associative, and bilinear, giving $\mathcal{C}$ the structure of a commutative algebra under convolution. Moreover, convolution commutes with the translation operator $T_t$, a fact that is essential for proving that convolution with a smooth function yields a smooth function.
