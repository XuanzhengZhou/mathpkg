Theorem 3.6 is the main approximation result of this section. It states that convolving any continuous $2\pi$-periodic function $u$ with an approximate identity $(\varphi_n)$ yields uniform convergence $\varphi_n * u \to u$. Moreover, if $u$ is already smooth ($u \in \mathcal{P}$), the convergence holds in the stronger $\mathcal{P}$ topology, meaning all derivatives converge uniformly. This theorem provides the systematic procedure promised at the beginning of the section: by choosing a smooth approximate identity (constructed in the next section), one obtains a sequence of smooth functions converging uniformly to any given continuous periodic function, proving that $\mathcal{P}$ is dense in $\mathcal{C}$.
