Convolution on the circle is defined for two continuous $2\pi$-periodic functions $u, v \in \mathcal{C}$ as a normalized integral of the product of $u$ with a translated copy of $v$. This operation is the continuous analog of taking weighted averages of translates, and it forms the foundation for the approximation theory developed in this chapter. Convolution preserves continuity, commutes with translations, and satisfies the norm inequality $|u * v| \leq |u|\,|v|$, making it a well-behaved bilinear operation on $\mathcal{C}$.
