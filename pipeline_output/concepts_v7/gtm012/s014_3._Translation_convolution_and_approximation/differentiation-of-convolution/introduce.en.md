Proposition 3.4 is a cornerstone of the approximation theory: if one convolves a smooth periodic function $u \in \mathcal{P}$ with an arbitrary continuous periodic function $v \in \mathcal{C}$, the result is smooth, and derivatives act only on the smooth factor: $D^k(u * v) = (D^k u) * v$. The proof uses Proposition 3.1 to express the difference quotient of the convolution as a convolution with the difference quotient of $u$, then applies Lemma 3.2 to pass to the limit. This proposition justifies the smoothing technique used throughout the remainder of the chapter.
