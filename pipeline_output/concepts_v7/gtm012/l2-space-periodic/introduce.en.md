$L^2$ is the completion of $\mathcal{C}$ with respect to the $L^2$ norm. Unlike $\mathcal{C}$, which is not complete in this norm, $L^2$ is a Hilbert space—a complete inner product space. Its elements are periodic distributions, and the identification of functions with distributions gives a natural embedding $\mathcal{C} \subset L^2$.
