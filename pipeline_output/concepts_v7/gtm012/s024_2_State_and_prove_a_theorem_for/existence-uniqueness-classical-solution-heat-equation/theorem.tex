\textbf{Theorem 4.2.} For each continuous function $g \in C[0, \pi]$ with $g(0) = g(\pi) = 0$, there exists a unique classical solution $u(x, t)$ of the heat equation problem

$$\frac{\partial}{\partial t} u(x, t) = \left( \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \right)^2 u(x, t), \quad x \in (0, \pi), \quad t > 0,$$

$$u(x, 0) = g(x), \quad x \in [0, \pi],$$

$$u(0, t) = u(\pi, t) = 0, \quad t \geq 0.$$

Moreover, this classical solution coincides with the restriction to $[0, \pi]$ of the distribution solution obtained from Theorem 3.1 by extending $g$ to an odd periodic function in $\mathcal{C}$.
