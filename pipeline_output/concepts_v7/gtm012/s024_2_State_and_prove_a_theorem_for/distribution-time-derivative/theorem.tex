Suppose that to each $t$ in some interval $(a, b) \subset \mathbb{R}$ we have assigned a distribution $F_t \in \mathcal{P}'$. If $s \in (a, b)$, $G \in \mathcal{P}'$, and

$$(t - s)^{-1}(F_t - F_s) \rightarrow G \quad (\mathcal{P}')$$

as $t \rightarrow s$, then $G$ is called the \textbf{derivative of $F_t$ with respect to $t$ at $t = s$}, and we write

$$G = \frac{d}{dt} F_t \bigg|_{t=s}.$$
