A classical solution to the heat equation requires $u$ to be continuous on the closed domain $[0,\pi] \times [0,\infty)$ and to satisfy the PDE in the pointwise sense on the open interval $(0,\pi)$. Two types of boundary conditions are considered: Dirichlet conditions (3) where the ends are held at zero temperature, and Neumann conditions (3)' where the ends are insulated. The classical solution framework is contrasted with the distribution solution framework of Theorem 3.1, which treats the problem on the whole real line with periodic boundary conditions.
