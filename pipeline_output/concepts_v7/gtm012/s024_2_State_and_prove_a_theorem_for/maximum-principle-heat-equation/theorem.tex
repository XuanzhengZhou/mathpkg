\textbf{Theorem 4.1 (Maximum Principle).} Let $u(x,t)$ be a real-valued classical solution of the heat equation on the rectangle $[0,\pi] \times [0,T]$ satisfying

$$\frac{\partial}{\partial t} u(x, t) = \left( \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \right)^2 u(x, t), \quad x \in (0, \pi), \quad t \in (0, T],$$

$$u(0, t) = u(\pi, t) = 0, \quad t \in [0, T],$$

$$u(x, 0) = g(x), \quad x \in [0, \pi].$$

Then for all $(x, t) \in [0, \pi] \times [0, T]$,

$$u(x, t) \leq \max \left\{ \, \sup_{x \in [0,\pi]} g(x), \; 0 \, \right\}.$$

In particular, the maximum of $u$ on $[0,\pi] \times [0,T]$ occurs on the parabolic boundary $\{ t = 0 \} \cup \{ x = 0 \} \cup \{ x = \pi \}$.
