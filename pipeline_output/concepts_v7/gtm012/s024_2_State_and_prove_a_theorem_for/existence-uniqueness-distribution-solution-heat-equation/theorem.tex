\textbf{Theorem 3.1.} For each $G \in \mathcal{P}'$ there is a unique family $(F_t)_{t > 0} \subset \mathcal{P}'$ such that

$$\frac{d}{dt} F_t \bigg|_{t=s} = D^2 F_s, \quad \text{all } s > 0,$$

$$F_t \rightarrow G \quad (\mathcal{P}') \quad \text{as } t \rightarrow 0.$$

For each $t > 0$, $F_t$ is a function $u_t(x) = u(x, t)$ which is infinitely differentiable in both variables $x$ and $t$ on $\mathbb{R} \times (0, \infty)$. Moreover, the Fourier coefficients of $F_t$ are given by

$$a_n(t) = b_n \exp(-n^2 t), \quad n \in \mathbb{Z},$$

where $(b_n)_{-\infty}^{\infty}$ are the Fourier coefficients of $G$.
