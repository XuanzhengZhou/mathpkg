Let $u(x,t)$ be defined for $x \in \mathbb{R}$, $t \geq 0$, periodic in $x$. The \textbf{periodic heat equation} is the problem: find $u$ such that

$$\frac{\partial}{\partial t} u(x, t) = \left( \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \right)^2 u(x, t), \quad x \in \mathbb{R}, \quad t > 0,$$

$$u(x, 0) = g(x), \quad x \in \mathbb{R},$$

where $g \in \mathcal{C}$ is given.

The \textbf{analogous problem for periodic distributions} is: given $G \in \mathcal{P}'$, find distributions $F_t \in \mathcal{P}'$ for each $t > 0$, such that

$$\frac{d}{dt} F_t \bigg|_{t=s} = D^2 F_s, \quad \text{all } s > 0,$$

$$F_t \rightarrow G \; (\mathcal{P}') \quad \text{as } t \rightarrow 0.$$
