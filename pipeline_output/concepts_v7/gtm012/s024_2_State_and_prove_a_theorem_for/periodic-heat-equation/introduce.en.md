The periodic heat equation (also called the diffusion equation) models the evolution of a temperature distribution in a thin homogeneous rod of length $\pi$, extended periodically to the whole real line. The classical formulation seeks a continuous function $u(x,t)$ satisfying the PDE $\partial_t u = \partial_x^2 u$ with a given initial temperature $g(x)$. The distribution formulation generalizes this by replacing the initial function $g$ with an arbitrary periodic distribution $G \in \mathcal{P}'$ and requiring $F_t \to G$ in the sense of $\mathcal{P}'$ as $t \to 0$. This generalization later permits the construction of a smooth solution $u_t(x) = u(x,t)$ for any periodic distribution initial data, via Fourier series.
