Theorem 6.1 establishes the complete solution of the Dirichlet problem on the unit disc in the framework of periodic distributions. Given any periodic distribution $F$ (which may be as rough as a general distribution), there is a unique smooth harmonic function $u$ in the interior of the disc whose boundary values converge to $F$ in the distributional sense as $r \to 1$. The solution is given explicitly by convolution of $F$ with the Poisson kernel: $u_r = F * P_r$. When the boundary data comes from a continuous function $g$, the convergence is uniform, recovering the classical Dirichlet problem solution. The proof uses the derivation of ordinary differential equations for Fourier coefficients, leading to the ansatz $a_n(r) = b_n r^{|n|}$, and the identification of $P_r$ as an approximate identity in the space of periodic distributions.
