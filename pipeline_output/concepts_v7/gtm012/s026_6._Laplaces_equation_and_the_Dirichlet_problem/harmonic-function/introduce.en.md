A harmonic function is a twice continuously differentiable function $u(x,y)$ defined on an open subset of $\mathbb{R}^2$ that satisfies Laplace's equation. These functions arise naturally in physics as equilibrium states — for example, the steady-state temperature distribution on a metal plate. Harmonic functions are the central objects of study in potential theory and are intimately connected to holomorphic functions in complex analysis, as every harmonic function is locally the real part of a holomorphic function.
