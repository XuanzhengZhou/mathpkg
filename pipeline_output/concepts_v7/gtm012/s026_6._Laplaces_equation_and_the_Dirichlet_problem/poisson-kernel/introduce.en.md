The Poisson kernel $P_r(\theta)$ is a family of functions on the circle indexed by $0 \leq r < 1$ that provides the fundamental solution to the Dirichlet problem on the unit disc. Convolving boundary data $g$ with $P_r$ yields the unique harmonic extension of $g$ into the interior of the disc. As $r \to 1$, the Poisson kernel forms an approximate identity, meaning the convolution $g * P_r$ converges to $g$ uniformly for continuous $g$ and in the sense of distributions for more general boundary data. The closed-form expression $(1-r^2)/(1-2r\cos\theta+r^2)$ also satisfies the semigroup property $P_r * P_s = P_{rs}$, reflecting the nesting of concentric discs.
