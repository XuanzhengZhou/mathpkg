Laplace's equation is one of the three fundamental partial differential equations of mathematical physics, alongside the heat equation and the wave equation. In two variables it reads $\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} + \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2} = 0$. Solutions of Laplace's equation are called harmonic functions. The equation describes equilibrium phenomena, such as the steady-state distribution of temperature in a homogeneous medium, the electrostatic potential in a charge-free region, and the velocity potential of an incompressible irrotational fluid.
