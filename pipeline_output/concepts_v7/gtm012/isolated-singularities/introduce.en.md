Isolated singularities of holomorphic functions are completely classified by their Laurent series. Removable singularities can be "filled in" to obtain a holomorphic extension. Poles behave like $1/(z-z_0)^m$ near the singularity. Essential singularities exhibit wild behavior: near an essential singularity, a holomorphic function comes arbitrarily close to every complex value (Picard's theorem strengthens this).
