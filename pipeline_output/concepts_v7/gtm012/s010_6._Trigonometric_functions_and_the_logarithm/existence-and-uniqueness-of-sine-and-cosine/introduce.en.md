Theorem 6.1 establishes the existence and uniqueness of two functions S and C (later identified as sine and cosine) as the unique solutions to the second-order linear differential equation f'' + f = 0 with prescribed initial conditions. The functions are required to be of class C^2 on the real line and take values in the complex numbers. The proof follows from the general existence and uniqueness theorem for n-th order linear ODEs (Theorem 5.3 in the source text).
