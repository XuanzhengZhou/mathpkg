The tangent, secant, cotangent, and cosecant functions are defined for complex arguments through algebraic combinations of the complex sine and cosine functions. For real arguments these definitions reduce to the familiar trigonometric ratios. In the complex domain, these are meromorphic functions whose poles occur precisely where the denominator (cosine or sine) vanishes.
