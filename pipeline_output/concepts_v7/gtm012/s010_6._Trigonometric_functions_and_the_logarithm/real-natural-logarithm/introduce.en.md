The real natural logarithm is defined on the positive real numbers as the inverse of the real exponential function. It is the unique real-valued function on (0, ∞) whose value at x is the exponent needed for e to produce x. By the inverse function theorem (Theorem 3.7 in the source), the logarithm is differentiable with derivative 1/x, which follows from the fact that exp is its own derivative.
