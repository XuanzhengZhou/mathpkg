For $x > 0$, the real natural logarithm $\log x$ is defined as the unique real number $y$ such that $\exp y = x$. Thus as a function from $(0, \infty)$ to $\mathbb{R}$, the logarithm is the inverse of the exponential function. It is differentiable, with derivative
$$
\frac{d}{dx} (\log x) = \left( \left. \frac{d}{dy} e^y \right|_{y = \log x} \right)^{-1} = e^{-\log x} = \frac{1}{x}.
$$
