In a Hilbert space, every closed subspace admits an orthogonal complement. For any point $u$, there is a unique closest point in the subspace, and the error $u-v$ is orthogonal to the subspace. This generalizes the geometric fact that the shortest distance from a point to a plane is along the perpendicular. The orthogonal decomposition $H = H_1 \oplus H_1^\perp$ is fundamental.
