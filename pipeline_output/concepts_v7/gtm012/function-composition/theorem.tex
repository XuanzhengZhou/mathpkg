If $f: A \to B$ and $g: B \to C$, the \textbf{composition} of $f$ and $g$ is the function denoted by $g \circ f$:
$$g \circ f: A \to C, \quad (g \circ f)(x) = g(f(x)), \quad \text{for all } x \in A.$$

If $f: A \to B$ is bijective, there is a unique \textbf{inverse function} $f^{-1}: B \to A$ with the properties:
$$f^{-1} \circ f(x) = x, \quad \forall x \in A; \quad f \circ f^{-1}(y) = y, \quad \forall y \in B.$$
