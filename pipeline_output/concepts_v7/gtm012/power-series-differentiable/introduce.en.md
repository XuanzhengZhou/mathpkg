Power series can be differentiated term-by-term within their radius of convergence, and the resulting series has the same radius. This implies power series define $C^\infty$ (in fact, analytic) functions, and the coefficients are uniquely determined by the derivatives at the center: $a_n = f^{(n)}(x_0)/n!$.
