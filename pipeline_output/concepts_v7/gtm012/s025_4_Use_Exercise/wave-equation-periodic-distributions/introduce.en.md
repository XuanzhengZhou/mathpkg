This theorem establishes the existence and uniqueness of solutions to the wave equation in the space of periodic distributions $\mathcal{P}'$. Given initial data $G$ (initial displacement) and $H$ (initial velocity) in $\mathcal{P}'$, there exists a unique one-parameter family $(F_t)_{t>0}$ of periodic distributions that satisfies the wave equation $\partial_t^2 F_t = D^2 F_t$ and attains the prescribed initial conditions in the sense of distributional limits. The proof constructs the Fourier coefficients $a_n(t) = b_n \cos nt + n^{-1}c_n \sin nt$ from the Fourier coefficients of $G$ and $H$, showing that this choice uniquely satisfies the required differential equation and initial conditions.
