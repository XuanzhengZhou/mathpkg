\begin{corollary}
Let $G$ and $H$ be the distributions defined by functions $g$ and $h$ in $\mathcal{P}$, and suppose $g = 0$. Let $(F_t)_{t>0}$ be the family of distributions given by Theorem~5.1. Then $F_t$ is the distribution defined by the function
$$
u(x, t) = \frac{1}{2} \int_{x-t}^{x+t} h(y) \, dy,
$$
where the constant of integration is chosen so that $u$ is periodic in $x$. This function $u$ satisfies
$$
\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}, \quad
u(x, 0) = 0, \quad
\frac{\partial u}{\partial t}(x, 0) = h(x).
$$
\end{corollary}
