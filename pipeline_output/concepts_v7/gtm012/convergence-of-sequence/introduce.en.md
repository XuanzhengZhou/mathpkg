A sequence converges to a limit $z$ if its terms eventually stay arbitrarily close to $z$. In $\mathbb{C}$, this means the terms eventually lie inside any given circle centered at $z$. The limit is unique: if two sequences have the same limit, they must be equal. This definition works identically in $\mathbb{R}$.
