A function-defined distribution is a distribution $F_f \in \mathcal{L}'$ obtained by integrating test functions against an ordinary continuous function $f$ that vanishes on a left half-line and has at most exponential growth. Such functions embed naturally into the space of distributions, and operations on the distribution $F_f$ (translation, conjugation, differentiation) correspond to the analogous operations on $f$.
