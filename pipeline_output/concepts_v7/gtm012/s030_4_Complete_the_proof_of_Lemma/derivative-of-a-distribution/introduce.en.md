The derivative of a distribution is defined by transferring the derivative onto the test function via integration by parts (with a sign change). This is one of the most powerful features of distribution theory: every distribution is infinitely differentiable. For a function-defined distribution $F_f$ where $f$ is continuously differentiable with appropriate growth, we have $DF_f = F_{Df}$, so the distributional derivative extends the classical derivative.
