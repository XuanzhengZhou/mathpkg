The space $\mathcal{L}'$ is the continuous dual of $\mathcal{L}$, consisting of all linear functionals that are continuous with respect to the Fr'{e}chet topology defined by the seminorms $|\cdot|_{k,a,M}$. Elements of $\mathcal{L}'$ are called distributions of type $\mathcal{L}'$, and they generalize ordinary functions. This space provides the framework for extending the Laplace transform to a much larger class of objects than integrable functions.
