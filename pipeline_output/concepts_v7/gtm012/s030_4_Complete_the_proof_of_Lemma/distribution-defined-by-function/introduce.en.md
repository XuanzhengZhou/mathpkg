Many distributions arise from ordinary functions via integration. A continuous function $f$ that vanishes to the left of some point and grows at most exponentially to the right defines a distribution $F_f$ by integration against test functions. This embedding of functions into $\mathcal{L}'$ is consistent with the operations of translation, conjugation, and differentiation, meaning that the distributional operations extend the classical ones.
