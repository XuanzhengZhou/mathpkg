The translation operator on distributions is defined by duality: shifting a distribution by $s$ is equivalent to evaluating the original distribution on the test function shifted by $-s$. This definition extends the classical translation of functions, since for a function-defined distribution $F_f$, we have $T_s F_f = F_{T_s f}$. Translation is a continuous operation with respect to convergence in $\mathcal{L}'$.
