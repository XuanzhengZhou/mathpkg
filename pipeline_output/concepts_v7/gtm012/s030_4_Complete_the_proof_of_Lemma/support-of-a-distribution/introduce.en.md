The support of a distribution generalizes the support of a function: a point is outside the support of $F$ if $F$ vanishes on all test functions whose support lies in a neighborhood of that point. For a function-defined distribution $F_f$, we have $\operatorname{supp}(F_f) = \operatorname{supp}(f)$. The support is closed by definition and plays a crucial role in the local theory of distributions.
