Suppose $F_n \to F (\mathcal{L}')$ and $G_n \to G (\mathcal{L}')$, and suppose $a \in \mathbb{C}$, $s \in \mathbb{R}$. Then:
\begin{align*}
aF_n &\to aF \quad (\mathcal{L}'), \\
F_n + G_n &\to F + G \quad (\mathcal{L}'), \\
T_s F_n &\to T_s F \quad (\mathcal{L}'), \\
D^k F_n &\to D^k F \quad (\mathcal{L}').
\end{align*}
Moreover, the difference quotient converges to the derivative in $\mathcal{L}'$:
$$s^{-1}[T_{-s}F - F] \to DF \quad (\mathcal{L}')$$
as $s \to 0$.
