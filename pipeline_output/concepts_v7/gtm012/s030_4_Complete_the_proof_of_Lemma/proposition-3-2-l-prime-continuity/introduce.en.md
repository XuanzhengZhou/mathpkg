Proposition 3.2 establishes that the basic operations on $\mathcal{L}'$—scalar multiplication, addition, translation, and differentiation—are continuous with respect to the weak-* topology. The proof relies on transferring each operation to the test function via the duality definitions. Notably, the difference quotient of distributions converges to the distributional derivative, confirming that the derivative is indeed the limit of difference quotients in the distributional sense.
