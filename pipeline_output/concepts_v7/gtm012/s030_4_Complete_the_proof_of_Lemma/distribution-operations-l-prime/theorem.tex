For $F \in \mathcal{L}'$, $s \in \mathbb{R}$, define:

Translation: $(T_s F)(u) = F(T_{-s} u)$, where $(T_s u)(t) = u(t - s)$.

Complex conjugate: $F^*(u) = F(u^*)^*$, where $u^*(t) = \overline{u(t)}$.

Real and imaginary parts:
$$\operatorname{Re} F = \tfrac{1}{2}(F + F^*), \quad \operatorname{Im} F = \tfrac{i}{2}(F^* - F).$$

$F$ is called real if $F = F^*$.

Derivative: $DF(u) = -F(Du)$, where $Du$ is the ordinary derivative of $u$.
