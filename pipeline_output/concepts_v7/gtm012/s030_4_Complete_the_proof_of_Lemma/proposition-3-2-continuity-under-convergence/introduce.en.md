Proposition 3.2 establishes that all fundamental operations on distributions \textemdash{} scalar multiplication, addition, translation, and differentiation \textemdash{} are continuous with respect to convergence in $\mathcal{L}'$. The final statement shows that the distributional derivative can be obtained as the limit of difference quotients, just as for ordinary functions, providing a consistency check for the definition of the distributional derivative.
