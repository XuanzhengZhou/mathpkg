Suppose $F_n \rightarrow F \; (\mathcal{L}')$ and $G_n \rightarrow G \; (\mathcal{L}')$, and suppose $a \in \mathbb{C}$, $s \in \mathbb{R}$. Then
$$aF_n \rightarrow aF \; (\mathcal{L}'),$$
$$F_n + G_n \rightarrow F + G \; (\mathcal{L}'),$$
$$T_s F_n \rightarrow T_s F \; (\mathcal{L}'),$$
$$D^k F_n \rightarrow D^k F \; (\mathcal{L}').$$
Moreover, the difference quotient converges:
$$s^{-1}[T_{-s}F - F] \rightarrow DF \; (\mathcal{L}') \quad \text{as} \quad s \rightarrow 0.$$
