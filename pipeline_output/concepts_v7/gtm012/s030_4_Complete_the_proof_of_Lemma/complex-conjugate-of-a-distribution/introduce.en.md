The complex conjugate of a distribution is defined by duality: applying $F^*$ to a test function $u$ yields the complex conjugate of $F$ applied to the conjugate test function $u^*$. For a function-defined distribution $F_f$, this yields $F_f^* = F_{f^*}$, consistent with the classical complex conjugate of a function.
