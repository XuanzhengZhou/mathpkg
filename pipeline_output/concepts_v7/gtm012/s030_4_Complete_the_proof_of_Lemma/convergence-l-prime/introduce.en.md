Convergence in $\mathcal{L}'$ is pointwise convergence on test functions—the weak-* topology. A sequence of distributions converges if and only if its evaluations on each fixed test function converge as complex numbers. This notion of convergence is natural for duality and ensures that the operations of scalar multiplication, addition, translation, and differentiation are all continuous with respect to it.
