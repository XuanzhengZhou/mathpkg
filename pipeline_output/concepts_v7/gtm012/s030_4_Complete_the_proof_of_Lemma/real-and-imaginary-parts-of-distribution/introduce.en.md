The real and imaginary parts of a distribution decompose it into two distributions, generalizing the decomposition of complex-valued functions. A distribution $F$ can be expressed as $F = \operatorname{Re} F + i \operatorname{Im} F$, and both $\operatorname{Re} F$ and $\operatorname{Im} F$ belong to $\mathcal{L}'$ whenever $F$ does.
