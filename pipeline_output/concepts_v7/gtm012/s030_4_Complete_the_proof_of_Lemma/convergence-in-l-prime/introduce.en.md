Convergence in $\mathcal{L}'$ is the weak-* topology on the continuous dual of the Schwartz space: a sequence of distributions converges if it converges pointwise on every test function. This notion of convergence makes $\mathcal{L}'$ a sequentially complete space and is compatible with all the algebraic operations (scalar multiplication, addition, translation, differentiation).
