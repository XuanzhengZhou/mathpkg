Theorem 3.3 provides the fundamental characterization of continuity for linear functionals on the Schwartz space $\mathcal{L}$. It states that a linear functional is continuous precisely when it is bounded by one of the seminorms $|\cdot|_{k,a,M}$ that define the topology of $\mathcal{L}$. This is the distribution-theoretic analogue of the statement that a linear functional on a normed space is continuous iff it is bounded. The theorem is essential for verifying that concretely defined functionals belong to $\mathcal{L}'$.
