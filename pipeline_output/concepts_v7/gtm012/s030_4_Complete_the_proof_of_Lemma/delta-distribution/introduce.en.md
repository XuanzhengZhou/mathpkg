The Dirac $\delta$-distribution is the simplest example of a distribution that is not given by integration against a function. It evaluates a test function at the origin. Despite its simplicity, $\delta$ plays a fundamental role: its derivative $\delta'$ and higher derivatives, as well as its translates $T_s\delta$, generate many important distributions. The $\delta$-distribution is the identity element for convolution in $\mathcal{L}'$.
