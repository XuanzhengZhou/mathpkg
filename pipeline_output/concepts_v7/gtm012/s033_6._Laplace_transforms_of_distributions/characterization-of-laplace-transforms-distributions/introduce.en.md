Theorem 6.3 addresses the inverse problem for the distributional Laplace transform: given a holomorphic function $g$ defined in a right half plane, when does there exist a distribution $F \in \mathcal{L}'$ such that $LF = g$? The theorem provides a characterization in terms of polynomial growth bounds on vertical lines, generalizing the classical Paley--Wiener type theorems to the space $\mathcal{L}'$. The statement in the source is truncated, but the standard formulation requires that $|g(z)| \leq K(1+|z|)^k$ for $\operatorname{Re} z > a$.
