The exponential multiplication of a distribution $F \in \mathcal{L}'$ by $e_w(t) = e^{-wt}$ is defined by duality: $(e_w F)(u) = F(e_w u)$ for every test function $u \in \mathcal{L}$. This definition is natural because the space $\mathcal{L}$ is closed under pointwise multiplication by exponential functions, and the operation $u \mapsto e_w u$ is continuous in the topology of $\mathcal{L}$. The construction is used in Proposition 6.1 to express how the Laplace transform interacts with exponential weights, and it appears in the proof of Theorem 6.2 for reducing the case $a < 0$ to the case $a \geq 0$.
