Theorem 6.2 generalizes the classical holomorphicity and inversion theorems for the Laplace transform to distributions in $\mathcal{L}'$. The first part asserts that $LF$ is holomorphic in the half plane $\operatorname{Re} z > a$, where the bound $a$ comes from the continuity estimate of $F$. The second part provides a representation theorem: every such distribution $F$ can be written as $D^{k+2} F_f$, where $f$ is recovered from $LF$ by a contour integral along a vertical line to the right of the origin and $a$. The proof proceeds by factoring $LF(z) = z^{k+2} Lf(z)$ and applying the classical inversion result (Theorem 5.2) to $f$.
