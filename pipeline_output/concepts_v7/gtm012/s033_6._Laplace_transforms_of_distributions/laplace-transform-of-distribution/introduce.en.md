The Laplace transform of a distribution $F \in \mathcal{L}'$ extends the classical Laplace transform to the space of continuous linear functionals on the test function space $\mathcal{L}$. It is defined as the limit $LF(z) = \lim_{n \to \infty} F(u_n)$ where $(u_n)$ is any sequence in $\mathcal{L}$ approximating the exponential function $e_z(t) = e^{-zt}$ in the seminorm $|\cdot|_{k,a,M}$. The definition is well-posed because the bound $|F(u)| \leq K|u|_{k,a,M}$ ensures that $(F(u_n))$ is Cauchy and the limit is independent of the choice of approximating sequence. This construction allows one to apply Laplace transform techniques to generalized functions that arise as derivatives of ordinary functions.
