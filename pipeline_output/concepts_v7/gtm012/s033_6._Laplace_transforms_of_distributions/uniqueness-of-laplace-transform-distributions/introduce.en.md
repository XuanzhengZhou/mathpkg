This corollary follows immediately from Theorem 6.2 and states that the Laplace transform is injective on the space of distributions $\mathcal{L}'$ satisfying a continuity bound. If the Laplace transform of such a distribution vanishes identically on its half plane of definition, then the distribution itself must be the zero distribution. This generalizes the classical uniqueness theorem for the Laplace transform of functions to the distributional setting.
