Let $\mathbf{L}^2$ be the set of all periodic distributions $F$ with the property that there is a sequence $(u_n)_{1}^{\infty} \subset \mathbb{C}$ such that
$$\|u_n - u_m\| \to 0 \quad \text{as } n, m \to \infty,$$
and $F_{u_n} \to F$ in the sense of $\mathcal{P}'$.

\textbf{Theorem.} The inner product on $\mathbf{L}^2$ extends the inner product on $\mathbb{C}$ via
$$(F, G) = \lim (u_n, v_n)$$
when $u_n \to F(\mathbf{L}^2)$, $v_n \to G(\mathbf{L}^2)$. With $\|F\| = (F,F)^{1/2}$, $\mathbf{L}^2$ is a complete inner product space (a Hilbert space).
