Theorem 4.4 establishes that a power series can be differentiated term by term within its interval (or disc) of convergence. The differentiated series $\sum_{n=1}^\infty n a_n (x-x_0)^{n-1}$ has the same radius of convergence as the original series. The proof uses uniform convergence of the differentiated series on compact subintervals and a careful estimate involving the difference quotient $[f(y)-f(x)](y-x)^{-1}$.
