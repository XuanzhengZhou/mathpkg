Let $R$ be the radius of convergence of the power series
$$
f(z) = \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} a_m(z - z_0)^m.
$$
Then the function $f$ defined by this series is continuous on $\{z \mid |z - z_0| < R\}$. Moreover, the partial sums
$$
f_n(z) = \sum_{m=0}^{n} a_m(z - z_0)^m
$$
converge to $f$ uniformly on each disc
$$
D_r = \{z \mid |z - z_0| < r\}, \quad 0 < r < R.
$$
