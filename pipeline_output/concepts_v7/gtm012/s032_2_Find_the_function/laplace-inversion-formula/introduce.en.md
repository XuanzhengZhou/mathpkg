Theorem 5.2 provides the classical Bromwich integral formula that recovers a function from its Laplace transform. The original function $f$ is obtained as the second distributional derivative of a contour integral of $e^{zt} z^{-2} g(z)$ along a vertical line in the complex plane to the right of all singularities. The $z^{-2}$ factor ensures absolute convergence of the integral; applying two derivatives removes this factor, yielding $f$ itself. This inversion formula also reveals that $F$ is the twice-integrated version of $f$, giving a constructive link between the original function and its transform.
