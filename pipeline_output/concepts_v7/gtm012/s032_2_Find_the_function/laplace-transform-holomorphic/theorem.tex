Suppose $f \colon \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{C}$ is continuous and satisfies
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\operatorname{supp}(f) \subset [M, \infty)$,
\item $|f(t)| \leq K e^{at}$ for all $t$.
\end{enumerate}
Then the Laplace transform $Lf$ is holomorphic in the half-plane
$$\{z \in \mathbb{C} \mid \operatorname{Re} z > a\}.$$
Its derivative is given by
$$(Lf)'(z) = -\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-zt} \, t \, f(t) \, dt, \qquad \operatorname{Re} z > a.$$
Moreover, $Lf$ satisfies the estimate
$$|Lf(z)| \leq K \, (\operatorname{Re} z - a)^{-1} \, \exp\!\bigl(M(a - \operatorname{Re} z)\bigr), \qquad \operatorname{Re} z > a.$$
