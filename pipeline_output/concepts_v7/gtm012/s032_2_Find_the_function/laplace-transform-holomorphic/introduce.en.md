Theorem 5.1 establishes the fundamental analytic properties of the Laplace transform. Holomorphicity follows by differentiating under the integral sign, justified by the exponential bound on $f$. The derivative formula shows that differentiation of the Laplace transform corresponds to multiplication of the original function by $-t$. The explicit exponential estimate controls $|Lf(z)|$ in terms of the growth parameters of $f$ and the real part of $z$, which is essential for contour deformation arguments in the inversion theory.
