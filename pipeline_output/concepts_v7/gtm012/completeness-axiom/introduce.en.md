The completeness axiom is what separates $\mathbb{R}$ from $\mathbb{Q}$. Every nonempty bounded-above set of real numbers has a least upper bound (supremum). This single axiom implies the existence of $\sqrt{2}$, the convergence of Cauchy sequences, and is the foundation of all limit arguments in real analysis.
