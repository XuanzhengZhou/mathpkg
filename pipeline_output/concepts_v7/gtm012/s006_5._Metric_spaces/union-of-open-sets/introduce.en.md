An arbitrary union (finite or infinite, countable or uncountable) of open sets in a metric space is always an open set. The proof is straightforward: if a point $x$ lies in the union, it lies in some particular open set $A_\beta$, and since $A_\beta$ is open, there exists a ball around $x$ contained in $A_\beta$ and hence in the union. Unlike intersections, no finiteness restriction is needed for unions.
