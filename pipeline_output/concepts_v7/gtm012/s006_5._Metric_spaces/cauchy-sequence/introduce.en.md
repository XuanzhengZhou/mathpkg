A Cauchy sequence is one whose terms eventually become arbitrarily close to each other. Unlike the definition of convergence, a Cauchy sequence does not require specifying a limit point in advance. In any metric space, every convergent sequence is Cauchy, but the converse holds only in complete metric spaces. The Cauchy criterion provides a way to test for convergence without knowing the limit.
