The span of a set of vectors is the collection of all finite linear combinations of those vectors. It forms a subspace and is, in fact, the smallest subspace containing the given set. A set $S$ is said to span a subspace $Y$ if $Y = \operatorname{span}(S)$. The span operation is fundamental for constructing subspaces and for the concept of basis.
