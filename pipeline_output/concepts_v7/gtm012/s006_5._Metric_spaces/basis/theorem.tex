A basis of a vector space $X$ is a linearly independent subset of $X$ that spans $X$.

If $(\mathbf{x}_1, \mathbf{x}_2, \ldots, \mathbf{x}_n)$ is a basis of $X$, then every $\mathbf{x} \in X$ can be written uniquely as a linear combination

$$\mathbf{x} = \sum_{j=1}^{n} a_j \mathbf{x}_j,$$

where the scalars $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n$ are called the coordinates of $\mathbf{x}$ with respect to the basis.

A vector space is said to be finite-dimensional if it has a finite basis. The number of elements in any basis of a finite-dimensional vector space is the same, called the dimension of the space.
