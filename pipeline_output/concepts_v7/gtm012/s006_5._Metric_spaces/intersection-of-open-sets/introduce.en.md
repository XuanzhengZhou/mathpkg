The intersection of finitely many open sets in a metric space is again an open set. The proof uses the fact that each point in the intersection belongs to each of the finitely many open sets $A_m$, so one can choose a radius $r(m) > 0$ for each $m$ such that $B_{r(m)}(x) \subset A_m$. Taking $r$ to be the minimum of these finitely many positive radii yields $B_r(x)$ contained in the intersection. Finiteness is essential: an infinite intersection of open sets need not be open.
