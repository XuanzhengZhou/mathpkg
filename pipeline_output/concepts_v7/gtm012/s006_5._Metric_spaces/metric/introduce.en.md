A metric is a distance function on a set that satisfies three natural properties. The first property D1 says that the distance from a point to itself is zero, and the distance between distinct points is positive. The second property D2 requires symmetry: the distance from $x$ to $y$ equals the distance from $y$ to $x$. The third property D3 is the triangle inequality, which generalizes the geometric fact that the length of one side of a triangle cannot exceed the sum of the lengths of the other two sides.
