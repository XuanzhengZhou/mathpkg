A non-empty subset $Y$ of a (complex) vector space $X$ is called a subspace of $X$ if it is closed with respect to the operations of addition and scalar multiplication. That is, if $x, y \in Y$ and $a \in \mathbb{C}$, then $x + y \in Y$ and $ay \in Y$.

If $Y$ is a subspace of $X$, then $Y$ itself is a vector space over $\mathbb{C}$ (or $\mathbb{R}$), with the operations inherited from $X$.
