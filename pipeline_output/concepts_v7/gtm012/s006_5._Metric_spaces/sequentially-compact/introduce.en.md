Sequential compactness is a notion of compactness formulated in terms of sequences rather than open covers. A subset of a metric space is sequentially compact if every sequence in the set has a convergent subsequence whose limit also lies in the set. In metric spaces, sequential compactness is equivalent to the usual (open cover) definition of compactness, though this equivalence requires proof (Exercise 3 in the text).
