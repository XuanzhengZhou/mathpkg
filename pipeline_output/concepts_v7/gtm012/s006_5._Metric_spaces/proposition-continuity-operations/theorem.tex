Suppose $(S, d)$ is any metric space, and suppose $f, g: S \to \mathbb{C}$ are functions which are continuous at $x \in S$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $f + g$ is continuous at $x$.
\item $af$ is continuous at $x$ for any $a \in \mathbb{C}$.
\item $fg$ is continuous at $x$.
\item If $g(x) \neq 0$, then $f/g$ is defined on some ball $B_r(x)$ and is continuous at $x$.
\end{enumerate}
