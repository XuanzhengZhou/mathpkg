Proposition 1.1 states that continuity is preserved under the usual algebraic operations: sums, scalar multiples, products, and quotients (where the denominator is nonzero) of continuous functions are continuous. This is proved using $\varepsilon$-$\delta$ estimates. For products, a common technique is used: add and subtract a cross term, then bound using the local boundedness of continuous functions. For quotients, one first shows $1/g$ is continuous when $g(x) \neq 0$, then applies the product rule.
