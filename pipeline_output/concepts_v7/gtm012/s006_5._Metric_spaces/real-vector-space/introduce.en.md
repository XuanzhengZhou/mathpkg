A real vector space is an algebraic structure consisting of a set of vectors equipped with two operations: vector addition and scalar multiplication by real numbers. The eight axioms V1-V8 ensure that these operations behave in the expected way. Axioms V1-V4 make $(X, +)$ an abelian group, while V5-V8 govern the interaction of scalars with vectors. Complex vector spaces are defined analogously with $\mathbb{C}$ in place of $\mathbb{R}$.
