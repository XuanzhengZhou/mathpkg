A metric space is a set equipped with a notion of distance between its elements. The pair $(S, d)$ provides a general framework in which concepts such as convergence, limits, and continuity can be studied uniformly. Familiar examples include the real line $\mathbb{R}$, the complex plane $\mathbb{C}$, and Euclidean space $\mathbb{R}^n$, each with their standard distance functions.
