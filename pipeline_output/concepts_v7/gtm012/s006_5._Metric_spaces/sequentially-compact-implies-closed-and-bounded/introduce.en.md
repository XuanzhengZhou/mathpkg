Proposition 6.3 is the sequential analog of Proposition 6.1: every sequentially compact subset of a nonempty metric space must be both closed and bounded. The proof for closedness uses the fact that any limit point can be approached by a sequence, which must have a subsequence converging in $A$. The proof for boundedness uses a diagonal argument constructing a sequence without convergent subsequences if the set is unbounded.
