A limit point (also called an accumulation point) of a subset $A$ of a metric space is a point $x$ such that every open ball around $x$ intersects $A$ in at least one point. This concept captures the idea of points that can be "approached" by points of $A$. Note that a limit point of $A$ need not belong to $A$ itself.
