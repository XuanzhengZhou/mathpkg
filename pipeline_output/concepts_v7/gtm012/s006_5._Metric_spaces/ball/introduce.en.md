In a metric space, the open ball $B_r(x)$ is the set of all points whose distance from the center $x$ is strictly less than $r$. When $S = \mathbb{R}$ with the usual metric, $B_r(x)$ is the open interval $(x - r, x + r)$. When $S = \mathbb{R}^2$ or $\mathbb{C}$, $B_r(z)$ is the open disc of radius $r$ centered at $z$.
