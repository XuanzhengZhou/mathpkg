In a metric space, every open ball is an open set. This fundamental fact is proved using the triangle inequality: given any point $y$ inside $B_r(x)$, one can find a smaller ball centered at $y$ with radius $s = r - d(y, x)$ that is entirely contained within $B_r(x)$. This establishes that $B_r(x)$ is a neighborhood of each of its points, and hence an open set.
