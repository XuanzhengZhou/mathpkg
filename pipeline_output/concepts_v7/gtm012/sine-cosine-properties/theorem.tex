The functions $S$ (sine) and $C$ (cosine) are real-valued, of class $C^\infty$, and satisfy:
\begin{align}
S' &= C, \quad C' = -S, \\
S(x)^2 + C(x)^2 &= 1, \\
S(x + 4p) &= S(x), \quad C(x + 4p) = C(x),
\end{align}
where $p$ is the smallest positive number with $C(p) = 0$. The number $\pi$ is defined as $\pi = 2p$.
