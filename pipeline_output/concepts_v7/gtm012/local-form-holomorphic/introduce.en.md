Near any point, a nonconstant holomorphic function behaves like a polynomial of degree $m$ plus a constant, where $m$ is the order of the first nonvanishing derivative. This factorization isolates the first nonconstant term and a unit factor $h$, and is fundamental to understanding the local mapping properties of holomorphic functions.
