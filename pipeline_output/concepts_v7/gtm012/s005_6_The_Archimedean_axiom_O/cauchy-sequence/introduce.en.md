A Cauchy sequence is one whose terms eventually become arbitrarily close to each other — not necessarily to any fixed limit. The key insight is that convergence (approaching some external limit) can be characterized purely in terms of the internal behavior of the sequence. In complete spaces like $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{C}$, every Cauchy sequence converges, making the Cauchy condition an intrinsic criterion for convergence that does not require knowing the limit in advance.
