A sequence of complex numbers $(z_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ converges to a limit $z \in \mathbb{C}$ if the terms $z_n$ eventually stay arbitrarily close to $z$. More precisely, for any positive tolerance $\varepsilon > 0$, all but finitely many terms of the sequence lie within distance $\varepsilon$ of $z$. This definition extends the familiar notion of convergence from real numbers to the complex plane, where distance is measured by the modulus $|z_n - z|$.
