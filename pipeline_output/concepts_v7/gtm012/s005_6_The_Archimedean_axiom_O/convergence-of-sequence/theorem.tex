A sequence $(z_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ of complex numbers is said to \textbf{converge} to $z \in \mathbb{C}$ if for each $\varepsilon > 0$, there is an integer $N$ such that
$$|z_n - z| < \varepsilon \quad \text{whenever } n \geq N.$$
If this is the case we write
$$z_n \rightarrow z, \quad \text{or} \quad \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} z_n = z.$$
The number $z$ is called the \textbf{limit} of the sequence $(z_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$.
