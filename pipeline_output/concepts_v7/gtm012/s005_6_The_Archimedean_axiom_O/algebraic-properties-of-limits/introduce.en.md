The limit operation respects the algebraic structure of the complex numbers. If two sequences converge, then their termwise sum, scalar multiple, and product also converge to the corresponding operations on the limits. Moreover, reciprocals of convergent sequences converge to the reciprocal of the limit, provided the limit is nonzero. These properties make it possible to compute limits of complicated expressions by breaking them into simpler parts.
