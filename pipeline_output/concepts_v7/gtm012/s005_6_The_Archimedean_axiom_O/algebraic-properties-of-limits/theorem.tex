Suppose $(z_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ and $(w_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ are sequences in $\mathbb{C}$ and $z_n \to z$, $w_n \to w$. Then:

(a) $z_n + w_n \to z + w$.

(b) $c z_n \to c z$ for any $c \in \mathbb{C}$.

(c) $z_n w_n \to z w$.

(d) If $z \neq 0$, then there exists $M$ such that $z_n \neq 0$ for $n \geq M$, and $z_n^{-1} \to z^{-1}$.

(e) $z_n / w_n \to z / w$ provided $w \neq 0$ and $w_n \neq 0$ for all $n$.
