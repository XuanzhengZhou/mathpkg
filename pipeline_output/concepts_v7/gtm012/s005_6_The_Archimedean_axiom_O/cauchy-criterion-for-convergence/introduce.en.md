The Cauchy criterion states that convergence in $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{C}$ is equivalent to the Cauchy property: a sequence converges if and only if its terms eventually become arbitrarily close to each other. This is a hallmark of completeness — the property that $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{C}$ have "no holes." The forward direction is straightforward; the reverse direction uses the Bolzano-Weierstrass theorem for real sequences and extends to complex sequences by separating real and imaginary parts.
