The comparison test provides a simple sufficient condition for convergence: if the terms of a complex series are bounded in magnitude by (a constant multiple of) the terms of a convergent nonnegative real series, then the complex series converges. The proof uses the Cauchy criterion: the tail sums of the dominant series control the tail sums of the given series. This test is particularly useful when combined with known convergent series like geometric series or $p$-series.
