If a sequence converges, its limit is unique. The proof uses the triangle inequality: if a sequence were to approach two distinct limits $z$ and $w$, then for sufficiently large indices the terms would be simultaneously close to both $z$ and $w$, forcing $|z-w|$ to be arbitrarily small — which is possible only when $z = w$.
