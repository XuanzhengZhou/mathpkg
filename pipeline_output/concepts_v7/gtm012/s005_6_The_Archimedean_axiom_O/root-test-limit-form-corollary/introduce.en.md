When the limit $\lim |z_n|^{1/n}$ exists, the root test simplifies to checking whether this limit is less than or greater than 1. This is an immediate consequence of Proposition 4.5, since for a convergent sequence the limsup equals the ordinary limit. However, when the limit equals 1, the test is inconclusive — both convergence (as with $\sum 1/n^2$) and divergence (as with $\sum 1/n$) are possible.
