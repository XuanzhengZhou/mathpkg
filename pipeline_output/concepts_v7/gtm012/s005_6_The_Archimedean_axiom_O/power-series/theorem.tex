If $(a_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}$ is a sequence in $\mathbb{C}$ and $z_0$ a fixed element of $\mathbb{C}$, then the series
$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (z - z_0)^n$$
is called a \textbf{power series} around $z_0$ with coefficients $(a_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}$. Here we use the convention that $w^0 = 1$ for all $w \in \mathbb{C}$, including $w = 0$. The series is defined for each $z \in \mathbb{C}$.
