A power series is an infinite series of the form $\sum a_n (z - z_0)^n$, where the terms are powers of $(z - z_0)$ weighted by complex coefficients $a_n$. Power series are the fundamental building blocks of analytic function theory: they define functions that are infinitely differentiable and can be manipulated termwise within their radius of convergence. The convention $w^0 = 1$ (even for $w = 0$) ensures the series is well-defined at the center $z = z_0$, where it evaluates to $a_0$.
