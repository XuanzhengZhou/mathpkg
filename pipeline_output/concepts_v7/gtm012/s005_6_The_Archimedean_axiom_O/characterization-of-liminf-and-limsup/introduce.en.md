Proposition 3.4 provides an equivalent characterization of liminf and limsup without reference to infima and suprema of tail sets. For $\liminf x_n = x'$, the sequence eventually stays above any number less than $x'$ (property i'), yet infinitely many terms dip below any number greater than $x'$ (property ii'). Dually, for $\limsup x_n = x''$, the sequence eventually stays below any number greater than $x''$, while infinitely many terms exceed any number less than $x''$. These properties make liminf and limsup essential tools for analyzing oscillatory behavior of bounded sequences.
