Suppose $(x_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ is a bounded sequence in $\mathbb{R}$.

Then $\liminf_{n \to \infty} x_n$ is the unique number $x'$ such that:

(i') for any $\varepsilon > 0$, there is an $N$ such that $x_n > x' - \varepsilon$ whenever $n \geq N$,

(ii') for any $\varepsilon > 0$ and any $N$, there is an $n \geq N$ such that $x_n < x' + \varepsilon$.

Similarly, $\limsup_{n \to \infty} x_n$ is the unique number $x''$ such that:

(i'') for any $\varepsilon > 0$, there is an $N$ such that $x_n < x'' + \varepsilon$ whenever $n \geq N$,

(ii'') for any $\varepsilon > 0$ and any $N$, there is an $n \geq N$ such that $x_n > x'' - \varepsilon$.
