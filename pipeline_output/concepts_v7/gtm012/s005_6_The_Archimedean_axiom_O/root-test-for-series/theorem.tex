Suppose $(z_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ is a sequence in $\mathbb{C}$ and suppose $(|z_n|^{1/n})_{n=1}^{\infty}$ is a bounded sequence, so that the upper and lower limits exist.

(a) If $\displaystyle \limsup_{n \to \infty} |z_n|^{1/n} < 1$, then $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} z_n$ converges.

(b) If $\displaystyle \limsup_{n \to \infty} |z_n|^{1/n} > 1$, then $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} z_n$ diverges.

If the sequence $(|z_n|^{1/n})$ is not bounded, then in particular $|z_n| \geq 1$ for infinitely many values of $n$, and the series diverges.
