An infinite series is a formal expression $\sum z_n$ together with its associated sequence of partial sums $s_n = z_1 + \cdots + z_n$. The series is said to converge if the sequence of partial sums converges, and the limit is called the sum of the series. This construction reduces the study of infinite summation to the theory of sequence convergence, allowing the powerful tools developed for sequences to be applied to series.
