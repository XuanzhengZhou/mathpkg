A bounded sequence is one whose terms all lie within some fixed distance from the origin. Geometrically, all terms are contained in a disk of radius $M$ centered at $0$. Every convergent sequence is bounded, but the converse is false — for example, the sequence $(-1)^n$ is bounded but does not converge. Boundedness is a necessary condition for convergence and plays a key role in the Bolzano-Weierstrass theorem.
