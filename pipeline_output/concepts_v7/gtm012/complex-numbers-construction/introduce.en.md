Complex numbers are constructed as ordered pairs of real numbers with a specific multiplication rule encoding $i^2 = -1$. The complex conjugate and modulus extend the notion of absolute value. Every nonzero complex number has a multiplicative inverse $z^{-1} = z^*|z|^{-2}$, making $\mathbb{C}$ a field.
