If $\mathfrak{a}$ and $\mathfrak{b}$ are relatively prime ideals (i.e., $\mathfrak{a} + \mathfrak{b} = (1)$), then for any $\alpha, \beta$ there exists an integer $\xi$ such that
$$\xi \equiv \alpha \pmod{\mathfrak{a}}, \quad \xi \equiv \beta \pmod{\mathfrak{b}},$$
and $\xi$ is uniquely determined modulo $\mathfrak{a}\mathfrak{b}$. Moreover, $\varphi(\mathfrak{a}\mathfrak{b}) = \varphi(\mathfrak{a})\varphi(\mathfrak{b})$ for coprime ideals.
