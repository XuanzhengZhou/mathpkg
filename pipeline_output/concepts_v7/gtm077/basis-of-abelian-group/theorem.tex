A system of $r$ elements $B_1,\ldots,B_r$ of orders $h_1,\ldots,h_r$ (powers of primes) is called a basis for the finite Abelian group $\mathfrak{G}$ if each element of $\mathfrak{G}$ is obtained exactly once in the form
$$C = B_1^{x_1}B_2^{x_2}\cdots B_r^{x_r},$$
where $x_i$ runs through a complete residue system modulo $h_i$ independently.
