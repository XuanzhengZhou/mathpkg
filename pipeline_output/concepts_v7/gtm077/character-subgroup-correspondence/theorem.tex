If $\mathfrak{U}$ is a subgroup of a finite Abelian group $\mathfrak{G}$ and the factor group $\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{U}$ has $r_0$ basis elements, then there exist $r_0$ characters $\chi_i$ of $\mathfrak{G}$ with orders $h_i$ (prime powers) such that the $r_0$ conditions
$$\chi_i(A) = 1 \quad (i = 1, 2, \ldots, r_0)$$
characterize membership in $\mathfrak{U}$: they hold for all $A \in \mathfrak{U}$ and only for $A \in \mathfrak{U}$.
