This is Theorem 68 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke.

If $ab = ac$, then if $a \neq 0$, $b = c$.

To see this we determine an ideal $m$ such that $am = (\delta)$ is a principal ideal. Then

$$amb = amc, \quad (\alpha)b = (\alpha)c.$$

The latter equation asserts that $\alpha$ times every number from $b$ is of the form $\alpha$ times a number from $c$,
