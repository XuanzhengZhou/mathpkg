For two odd ideals $\mathfrak{a}$ and $\mathfrak{b}$ in an arbitrary algebraic number field $k$,
$$\left(\frac{\mathfrak{a}}{\mathfrak{b}}\right) = \left(\frac{\mathfrak{b}}{\mathfrak{a}}\right) \prod_{\mathfrak{p} \mid \infty} (-1)^{\text{sgn}_\mathfrak{p}(\mathfrak{a},\mathfrak{b})} \prod_{\mathfrak{p} \mid 2} \text{(local factor at } \mathfrak{p}),$$
where the product runs over infinite primes (real embeddings) and primes dividing $2$. In particular, for totally positive numbers in a totally real field, the simple reciprocity $\left(\frac{a}{b}\right) = \left(\frac{b}{a}\right)$ holds.
