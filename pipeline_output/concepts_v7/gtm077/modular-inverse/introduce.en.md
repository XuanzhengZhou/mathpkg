The modular inverse of $a$ modulo $n$ is the unique residue $a'$ such that $aa' \equiv 1 \pmod{n}$, which exists precisely when $\gcd(a,n) = 1$. This makes the residue classes coprime to $n$ into the multiplicative group $(\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^\times$. The inverse can be computed via the extended Euclidean algorithm or, theoretically, via Euler's theorem as $a^{\varphi(n)-1} \bmod n$.
