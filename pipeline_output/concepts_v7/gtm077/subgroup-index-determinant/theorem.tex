Let $\mathfrak{G}$ be a torsion-free Abelian group with finite basis $B_1, \ldots, B_n$, and let $\mathfrak{U}$ be a subgroup of finite index $j$. Then $\mathfrak{U}$ also has a basis $U_1, \ldots, U_n$, and for any such basis expressed as
$$U_i = B_1^{a_{i1}} B_2^{a_{i2}} \cdots B_n^{a_{in}} \quad (i = 1, 2, \ldots, n),$$
the absolute value of the determinant $|\det(a_{ik})|$ equals the index $j$.

In particular, a basis can be chosen in triangular form (Theorem 35): $U_i = B_i^{r_{ii}} B_{i+1}^{r_{i,i+1}} \cdots B_n^{r_{in}}$ with $r_{ii} \neq 0$, and $j = |r_{11} r_{22} \cdots r_{nn}|$.
