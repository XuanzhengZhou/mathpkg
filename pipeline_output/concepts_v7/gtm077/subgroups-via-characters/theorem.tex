**Theorem 33.** Every subgroup $\mathfrak{U}$ of a finite Abelian group $\mathfrak{G}$ can be characterized by characters. Specifically, if the factor group $\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{U}$ has $r_0$ basis elements, then there exist $r_0$ characters $\chi_1, \ldots, \chi_{r_0}$ of $\mathfrak{G}$ (with prime-power orders) such that
$$\mathfrak{U} = \{ A \in \mathfrak{G} \mid \chi_1(A) = \cdots = \chi_{r_0}(A) = 1 \}.$$
Moreover, for arbitrary $h_i$th roots of unity $\zeta_i$, there exists a coset $A\mathfrak{U}$ with $\chi_i(A\mathfrak{U}) = \zeta_i$.
