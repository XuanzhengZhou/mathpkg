Theorem 33 establishes a fundamental duality: every subgroup of a finite Abelian group is the common kernel of finitely many characters. In fact, if the factor group $\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{U}$ has $r_0$ basis elements, exactly $r_0$ independent characters are needed. This is a finite analogue of the Galois correspondence and is essential for class field theory, where subgroups of the idele class group correspond to Abelian extensions.
