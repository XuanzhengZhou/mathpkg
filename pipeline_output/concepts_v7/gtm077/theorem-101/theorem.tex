The set of numbers $\lambda$ in $K$, for which the trace

$$S(\lambda \alpha) = \sum_{p=1}^{n} \lambda^{(p)} \alpha^{(p)} = \text{integer}$$

for each number $\alpha$ in $\alpha$, forms an ideal $\mathfrak{m}$. Here $\mathfrak{m}\alpha$ is an ideal independent of $\alpha$, determined only by the field $K$, and it is the reciprocal of an integral ideal $\mathfrak{d}$. A basis for $\mathfrak{m}$ can be formed from the $n$ numbers $\beta_1, \ldots, \beta_n$ which are determined along with their conjugates by the equations

$$S(\beta_i \alpha_k) = e_{ik} \quad (i, k = 1, 2, \ldots, n)$$

where $e_{ik} = 1$ if $i = k$, otherwise $e_{ik} = 0$.
