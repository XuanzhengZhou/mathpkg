For odd $q$ and odd $a$, the congruence $x^q \equiv a \pmod{2^\alpha}$ always has exactly one solution. For $q = 2$ and odd $a$, $x^2 \equiv a \pmod{2^\alpha}$ is solvable for $\alpha \geq 3$ if and only if $a \equiv 1 \pmod{8}$, with 4 solutions. For $\alpha = 2$, $a \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$ gives 2 solutions.
