Every finite Abelian group $\mathfrak{G}$ possesses a basis. That is, $\mathfrak{G}$ is isomorphic to a direct product of cyclic groups of prime-power orders. The orders $h_1, h_2, \ldots, h_r$ of the basis elements (the invariants of $\mathfrak{G}$) are uniquely determined up to ordering, and satisfy $h_i = p_i^{k_i}$ with $h = h_1 h_2 \cdots h_r$.

The proof reduces to groups of prime-power order $h = p^k$, where the orders of all elements are powers $p^\alpha$. The key lemmas establish that a maximal independent system is a basis.
