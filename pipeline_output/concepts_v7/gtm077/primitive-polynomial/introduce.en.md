A primitive polynomial is an integral polynomial whose coefficients have no common prime factor — their GCD is $1$. This concept is central to Gauss's Lemma, which states that the product of two primitive polynomials is again primitive. Primitive polynomials bridge the gap between factorization over $\mathbb{Z}[x]$ and $\mathbb{Q}[x]$, ensuring that irreducibility over $\mathbb{Z}$ implies irreducibility over $\mathbb{Q}$.
