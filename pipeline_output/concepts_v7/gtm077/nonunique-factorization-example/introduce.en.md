The field $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{-5})$ is the classic example where unique factorization fails. The number 21 factors as $3 \cdot 7$ and $(1+2\sqrt{-5})(1-2\sqrt{-5})$, both into irreducibles, demonstrating that irreducibles are not prime. This motivates the introduction of ideal numbers.
