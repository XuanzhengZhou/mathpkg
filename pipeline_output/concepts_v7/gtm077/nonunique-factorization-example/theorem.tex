In the field $K(\sqrt{-5})$, the integers are of the form $m + n\sqrt{-5}$ with $m, n \in \mathbb{Z}$. The units are only $\pm 1$. The number $21$ has two essentially distinct factorizations into irreducibles:
$$21 = 3 \cdot 7 = (1 + 2\sqrt{-5})(1 - 2\sqrt{-5}),$$
where each factor is irreducible in $K(\sqrt{-5})$ but no factor on one side is a unit multiple of a factor on the other.
