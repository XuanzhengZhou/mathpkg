Theorem 11 captures the crucial fact that for prime moduli, the ring $(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})[x]$ is an integral domain (in fact, a UFD). If a product of two polynomials vanishes at $a$ modulo $p$, then at least one factor must vanish. This property fails for composite moduli (e.g., mod 4, $x^2$ has root 2 but $x-2$ does not), making the theory of congruences modulo a prime much cleaner.
