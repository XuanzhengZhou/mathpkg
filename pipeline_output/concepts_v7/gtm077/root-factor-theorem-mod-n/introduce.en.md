Theorem 10 is the modular analogue of the polynomial factor theorem: if $a$ is a root of $f(x)$ modulo $n$, then $x - a$ divides $f(x)$ modulo $n$. The proof uses the identity $f(x) - f(a) = (x-a)g(x)$ where $g(x)$ is an integral polynomial (since $x^m - a^m$ factors with $x-a$). Note that the converse direction ($x-a \mid f(x) \mod n \implies f(a) \equiv 0$) requires care for composite moduli.
