Theorem 35 provides a "triangular" or "Hermite normal form" for expressing a basis of a subgroup in terms of a basis of the ambient group. Each generator $U_i$ involves only $B_i, B_{i+1}, \ldots, B_n$, and the diagonal coefficients $r_{ii}$ are nonzero. The product of these diagonal entries equals the index, making index computation mechanical. This theorem is fundamental for computing ideal norms and discriminants in algebraic number theory.
