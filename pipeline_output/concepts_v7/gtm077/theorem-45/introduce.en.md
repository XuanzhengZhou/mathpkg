This is Theorem 45 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke.

The groups $\Re(2)$ and $\Re(4)$ are cyclic. If $\alpha \geq 3$ then the group $\Re(2^\alpha)$ of order $h = \varphi(2^\alpha) = 2^{\alpha-1}$ has exactly two basis classes. One is of order 2, the other of order $h/2 = 2^{\alpha-2}$.
