Theorem 79. For two ideals $a$ and $b$, we always have

$$N(a b) = N(a) \cdot N(b).$$

Let $\alpha$ be a number divisible by $a$ such that $(\alpha, a b) = a$. If we let $\xi_i$ ($i = 1, 2, \ldots, N(b)$) run through a complete system of residues mod $b$ and let $\eta_k$ ($k = 1, 2, \ldots, N(a)$) run through a complete system of residues mod $a$, then no two of the numbers $\alpha \xi_i + \eta_k$ are congruent mod $a b$. On the other hand, each integer $\rho$ is congruent mod $a b$ to one of these numbers $\alpha \xi_i + \eta_k$. For let $\eta_k$ be determined so that

$$\eta_k \equiv \rho \pmod{a}$$

and then let $\xi$ be determined so that

$$\alpha \xi \equiv \rho - \eta_k \pmod{a b}.$$

Since $(\alpha, a b) = a$ and $a | \rho - \eta_k$ this congruence can be solved by Theorem 78 and $\xi$ can be determined mod $b$ so that $\xi$ can be chosen equal to $\xi_i$. Consequently the $N(a) \cdot N(b)$ numbers $\alpha \xi_i + \eta_k$ form a complete system of residues mod $a b$ and thus there must also be $N(a b)$ of them.

Theorem 80. If $(a, b) = 1$, then $\varphi(a b) = \varphi(a) \cdot \varphi(b)$ and in general

$$\varphi(a) = N(a) \prod_{p \mid a} \left(1 - \frac{1}{N(p)}\right)$$

where $p$ runs through the distinct prime divisors of $a$.

To see this let $\alpha$ be chosen so that $(\alpha, a b) = a$ and $\beta$ so that $(\beta, a b) = b$. Then if $\xi$ runs through a complete system of residues mod $b$ and $\eta$ runs through such

factors $p = \mathfrak{p}_1 \mathfrak{p}_2 \cdots \mathfrak{p}_r$, then the positive rational integers $N(\mathfrak{p}_1) \cdots N(\mathfrak{p}_r)$ have as product $N(p) = p^n$, while none of these integers is $= 1$; thus their number $r$ must be $\leq n$.

In this way we obtain one of the few statements which connect the degree of a field with other properties of the numbers of the field. If it is known that a rational prime $p$ is decomposable into $k$ ideal factors in a number field, then the degree of the field is at least $k$.
