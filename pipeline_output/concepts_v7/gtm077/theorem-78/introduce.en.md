This is Theorem 78 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke.

For given $\alpha$ and $\beta$ the congruence

$$\alpha\xi \equiv \beta \pmod{\alpha}$$

can be solved by an integer $\xi$ in $K$ if and only if $(\alpha, \alpha)|\beta$. If $(\alpha, \alpha) = 1$, then the solution is completely determined mod $\alpha$.
