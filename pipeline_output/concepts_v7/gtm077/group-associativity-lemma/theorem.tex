For elements $A_1, A_2, \ldots, A_k$ of a group ($k \geq 3$),
$$A_1 \cdot A_2 \cdots A_k = A_1 \cdot (A_2 \cdot A_3 \cdots A_k).$$

More generally, for $1 < l < k$,
$$(A_1 \cdots A_l)(A_{l+1} \cdots A_k) = (A_1 \cdots A_{l-1})(A_l A_{l+1} \cdots A_k),$$
i.e., inner parentheses can be shifted arbitrarily without changing the result.
