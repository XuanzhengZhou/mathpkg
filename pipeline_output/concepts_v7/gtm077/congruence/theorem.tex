Two integers $a$ and $b$ are called congruent modulo an integer $n \neq 0$, written
$$a \equiv b \pmod{n},$$
if $n \mid (a - b)$, i.e., if $a$ and $b$ have the same remainder upon division by $n$.

Congruences modulo the same $n$ may be added, subtracted, and multiplied:
\begin{itemize}
\item $a \equiv b \pmod{n},\ c \equiv d \pmod{n} \implies a \pm c \equiv b \pm d \pmod{n}$
\item $a \equiv b \pmod{n},\ c \equiv d \pmod{n} \implies ac \equiv bd \pmod{n}$
\item $a \equiv b \pmod{n} \implies a^k \equiv b^k \pmod{n}$ for $k \geq 0$
\item $a \equiv b \pmod{n} \implies f(a) \equiv f(b) \pmod{n}$ for any polynomial $f(x) \in \mathbb{Z}[x]$
\end{itemize}
