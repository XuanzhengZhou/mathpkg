If we replace one of the $B$, say $B_m$, in the system $B_1, \ldots, B_r$ by

$$A = B_m^u B_{m+1}^{x_{m+1}} \cdots B_r^{x_r},$$

where $u \neq 0$ (mod $p$), but the $x_i$ are arbitrary integers, then the rank does not change and the new system is again a maximal system.

$A$ has the same order as $B_m$, since the orders of $B_{m+1}, \ldots, B_r$ are not larger than that of $B_m$, and thus are divisors of the order of $B_m$. Moreover, each product of powers from $A, B_{m+1}, \ldots, B_r$ is representable as a product of powers of $B_m, B_{m+1}, \ldots, B_r$, and conversely. Consequently the new system is also independent and thus it is a maximal system.
