There are infinitely many prime numbers.

*Proof sketch.* If $p_1, p_2, \ldots, p_n$ were all primes, consider
$$z = p_1 \cdot p_2 \cdot \cdots \cdot p_n + 1.$$
The number $z$ is not divisible by any of $p_1, \ldots, p_n$, so it must be divisible by a prime distinct from all of them. Hence there are more than $n$ primes for any $n$.
