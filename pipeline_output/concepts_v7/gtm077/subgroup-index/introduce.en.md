The index of a subgroup is the number of distinct cosets into which it partitions the group. For finite groups, it equals the quotient of the group order by the subgroup order. In infinite groups, the index can be finite even when orders are infinite — e.g., $n\mathbb{Z}$ has index $n$ in $\mathbb{Z}$. The index is fundamental in the theory of group extensions and Galois theory.
