Let $S \neq \{0\}$ be a module of integers. Let $d$ be the smallest positive integer in $S$. Then every element of $S$ is a multiple of $d$, i.e.,
$$S = \{ qd \mid q \in \mathbb{Z} \}.$$
Thus every module of integers is principal, generated by its smallest positive element. Consequently, for any $n \in S$, the remainder of $n$ modulo $d$ is zero; otherwise it would be a smaller positive element of $S$.
