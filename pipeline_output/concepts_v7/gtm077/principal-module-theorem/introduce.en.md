Theorem 2 states that every module (additive subgroup) of the integers is principal — it consists precisely of all multiples of its smallest positive element. This result is the integer analogue of the statement that $\mathbb{Z}$ is a principal ideal domain. The proof uses the division algorithm: if the module contained an element not divisible by the minimal positive element, its remainder would be a smaller positive member of the module, a contradiction.
