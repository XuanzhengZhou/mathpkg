Fermat's Little Theorem is the special case of Euler's theorem when $n = p$ is prime: $a^{p-1} \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$ for $a$ not divisible by $p$. Equivalently, $a^p \equiv a \pmod{p}$ for all integers $a$. This theorem is one of the most famous results in number theory and underpins primality testing and the RSA cryptosystem.
