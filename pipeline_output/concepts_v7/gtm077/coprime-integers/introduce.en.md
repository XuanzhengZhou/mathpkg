Two integers are coprime (or relatively prime) if their greatest common divisor is $1$. This simple condition is one of the most important relations in number theory, appearing in the Chinese Remainder Theorem, Euler's theorem, and the definition of reduced residue systems. The equivalence with the solvability of $ax + by = 1$ (Bezout's identity) is a crucial algebraic characterization.
