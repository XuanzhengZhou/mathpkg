A system of elements in an Abelian group is independent if the only way a product of their powers can equal the identity is when each exponent is a multiple of the corresponding order. This generalizes linear independence from vector spaces to finite Abelian groups. Independent elements generate a subgroup whose order is the product of their individual orders, analogous to the dimension of a subspace spanned by independent vectors.
