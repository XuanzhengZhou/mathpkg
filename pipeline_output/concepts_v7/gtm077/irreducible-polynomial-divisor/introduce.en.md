This is Theorem 49 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke. If a polynomial $f(x)$, irreducible over $k$, has a common zero $x = \alpha$ with a polynomial $g(x)$ over $k$, then $f(x)$ is a divisor of $g(x)$ and hence all zeros of $f(x)$ are zeros of $g(x)$.
