A finite group is a group with finitely many elements; its order is the count of distinct elements. Examples include the residue classes modulo $n$ under addition (order $n$) and the reduced residue classes modulo $n$ under multiplication (order $\varphi(n)$). In a finite group, the solvability axiom follows from closure and cancellation because the map $X \mapsto AX$ is a bijection on a finite set.
