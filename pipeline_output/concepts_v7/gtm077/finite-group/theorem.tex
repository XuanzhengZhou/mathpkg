A group is called finite if it contains only finitely many distinct elements. The number $h$ of elements in a finite group is called its order.

A group with infinitely many elements is called an infinite group.

For a finite group of order $h$, condition (iv) of the group definition follows automatically from (i) and (iii): letting $X$ run through the $h$ elements, the $h$ products $AX$ are all distinct (by cancellation) and thus exhaust the group.
