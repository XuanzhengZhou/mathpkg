The orthogonality relations for characters are among the most important identities in mathematics. They state that the sum of all values of a non-principal character over the group is zero, and dually, the sum of all characters evaluated at a non-identity element is zero. These relations underpin Dirichlet's theorem on primes in arithmetic progressions and the proof that the Dedekind zeta-function has a simple pole at $s=1$.
