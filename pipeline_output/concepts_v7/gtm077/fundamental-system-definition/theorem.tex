$n$ numbers $\omega^{(1)}, \omega^{(2)}, \ldots, \omega^{(n)}$ of $K(\theta)$ form a fundamental system if every number $\alpha$ in $K(\theta)$ can be represented in the form
$$\alpha = \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i \omega^{(i)}$$
with coefficients $x_i$ in $k$. This is equivalent to the $\omega^{(i)}$ being a basis of $K(\theta)$ as a vector space over $k$.
