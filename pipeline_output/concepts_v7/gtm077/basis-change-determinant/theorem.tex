If $B_1, \ldots, B_n$ and $B'_1, \ldots, B'_n$ are two bases of a torsion-free Abelian group $\mathfrak{G}$, then the transformation matrix $C = (c_{ik})$ defined by
$$B'_i = B_1^{c_{i1}} B_2^{c_{i2}} \cdots B_n^{c_{in}}$$
has determinant $\det(C) = \pm 1$. Conversely, any integer matrix with determinant $\pm 1$ transforms a basis into another basis.
