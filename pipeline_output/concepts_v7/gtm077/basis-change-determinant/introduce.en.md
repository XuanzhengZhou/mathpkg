Theorem 38 states that the change-of-basis matrix between two bases of a torsion-free Abelian group of rank $n$ has determinant $\pm 1$ — it belongs to $\operatorname{GL}_n(\mathbb{Z})$. This is the integral analogue of the fact that change-of-basis matrices in vector spaces are invertible. The result guarantees that quantities like the discriminant of a number field (the determinant of a basis-change matrix squared) are well-defined invariants.
