A root of a polynomial $f(x)$ modulo $n$ is an integer $a$ such that $f(a) \equiv 0 \pmod{n}$. Unlike roots over fields, a polynomial modulo a composite modulus can have more roots than its degree. However, modulo a prime, Lagrange's theorem (Theorem 12) limits the number of roots to the degree. Roots are naturally defined on residue classes: if $a$ is a root, so is every integer congruent to $a$ modulo $n$.
