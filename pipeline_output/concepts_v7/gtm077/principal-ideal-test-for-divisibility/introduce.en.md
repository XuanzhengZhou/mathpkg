In Dedekind domains, 'to divide is to contain': $\mathfrak{c} \mid \mathfrak{a} \iff \mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathfrak{c}$. This inverts the intuition from integers: the larger the ideal as a set, the smaller it is as a divisor. A principal ideal $(lpha)$ divides $(eta)$ iff $lpha \mid eta$ in the usual sense.
