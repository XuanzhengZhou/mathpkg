The least common multiple (LCM) of two integers is the smallest positive integer divisible by both. Like the GCD, it has a clean description via prime factorization: take the maximum exponent for each prime. The relationship $ab = \gcd(a,b) \cdot \operatorname{lcm}(a,b)$ connects the two concepts and follows directly from the identity $\min(a_i, b_i) + \max(a_i, b_i) = a_i + b_i$.
