A prime number is an integer greater than $1$ (in absolute value) that cannot be factored nontrivially into integers — its only divisors are the units $\pm 1$ and its associates $\pm p$. Primes are the multiplicative building blocks of the integers, analogous to atoms in chemistry. The defining property $p \mid ab \implies p \mid a$ or $p \mid b$ is the key to unique factorization.
