Theorem 17 states that every group element has a unique two-sided inverse. Using the solvability axiom, one finds elements $X$ and $Y$ with $AX = E$ and $YA = E$; multiplying $YAX = YE$ shows $X = Y$, so the right and left inverses coincide. The inverse plays the role of reciprocal in multiplicative notation.
