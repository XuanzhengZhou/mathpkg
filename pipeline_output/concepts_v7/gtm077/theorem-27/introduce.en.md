This is Theorem 27 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke.

If $p$ is a prime, then the number of different elements of $\mathfrak{G}$ with the property

$$A^p = 1$$

is equal to $p^e$, where $e$ is the basis number belonging to $p$.
