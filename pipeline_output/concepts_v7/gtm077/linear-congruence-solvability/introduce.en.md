Theorem 14 (together with its extension Theorem 15) gives a complete theory for solving linear congruences $ax + b \equiv 0 \pmod{n}$. When $a$ is coprime to $n$, there is a unique solution; when $\gcd(a,n) = d > 1$, solvability requires $d \mid b$, and there are exactly $d$ solutions modulo $n$. The solution method reduces to the coprime case by dividing through by the GCD. Solving linear congruences is equivalent to solving linear Diophantine equations $ax - nz = -b$.
