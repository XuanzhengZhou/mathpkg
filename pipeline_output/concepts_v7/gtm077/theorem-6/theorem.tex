If $ca \equiv cb \pmod{n}$, then

$$a \equiv b \pmod{\frac{n}{d}}, \quad \text{where} \quad (c, n) = d,$$

and conversely.

In connection with this

Since a complete system of residues mod $n$ consists of $|n|$ distinct numbers, $|n|$ incongruent numbers modulo $n$ always form a complete system of residues mod $n$, e.g., the numbers $0, 1, 2, \ldots, |n| - 1$. More generally
