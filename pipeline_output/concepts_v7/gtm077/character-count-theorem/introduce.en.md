This is Theorem 30 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke. There are exactly $h$ distinct functions $\chi(A)$ which have the property: $\chi(AB) = \chi(A) \cdot \chi(B)$ and $\chi(A)$ is not $= 0$ for all elements $A$ of $\mathfrak{G}$.
