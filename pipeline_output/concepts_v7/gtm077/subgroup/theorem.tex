A subset $\mathfrak{U}$ of a group $\mathfrak{G}$ is called a subgroup of $\mathfrak{G}$ if $\mathfrak{U}$ itself forms a group under the same composition rule as $\mathfrak{G}$.

For finite groups, a nonempty subset $S$ is a subgroup as soon as it is closed under the group operation (i.e., the product of any two elements of $S$ belongs to $S$), since the remaining group axioms follow automatically.
