A subgroup is a subset of a group that is itself a group under the same operation. For finite groups, closure under the operation suffices to guarantee the subgroup property, since associativity and cancellation are inherited from the ambient group, and finiteness ensures solvability of equations. Subgroups are the basic substructures in group theory and are essential for Lagrange's theorem.
