An ideal $\mathfrak{p} \neq (1)$ in a number field $K$ is called a prime ideal if whenever a product $\alpha\beta$ of integers in $K$ lies in $\mathfrak{p}$, at least one of the factors $\alpha, \beta$ belongs to $\mathfrak{p}$. Equivalently, the residue class ring modulo $\mathfrak{p}$ is an integral domain (and hence a finite field).
