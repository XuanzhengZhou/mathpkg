The order of a group element $A$ is the smallest positive integer $a$ such that $A^a = E$. The exponents for which $A^q = E$ form a module (an additive subgroup of $\mathbb{Z}$), hence consist precisely of the multiples of $a$. By Lagrange's theorem, the order of any element divides the order of the group. In infinite groups, an element may have infinite order (no positive power equals the identity).
