Euler's totient function $\varphi(n)$ counts the number of integers between $1$ and $n$ that are coprime to $n$ — or algebraically, the order of the multiplicative group of units $(\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^\times$. For prime powers, the formula is simple: $\varphi(p^k) = p^k - p^{k-1}$, since exactly the $p^{k-1}$ multiples of $p$ are not coprime to $p^k$. This function is multiplicative and appears throughout number theory.
