For a fundamental system $\omega^{(1)}, \ldots, \omega^{(n)}$ of $K(\theta)$, the determinant
$$\Delta(\omega^{(1)}, \ldots, \omega^{(n)}) = \det\begin{pmatrix}
\omega_1^{(1)} & \omega_2^{(1)} & \cdots & \omega_n^{(1)} \\
\omega_1^{(2)} & \omega_2^{(2)} & \cdots & \omega_n^{(2)} \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
\omega_1^{(n)} & \omega_2^{(n)} & \cdots & \omega_n^{(n)}
\end{pmatrix}$$
is nonzero. Its square $\Delta^2$ belongs to the ground field $k$ and is independent of the numbering of conjugates. For the standard basis $\{1, \theta, \ldots, \theta^{n-1}\}$,
$$\Delta^2 = \prod_{1 \leq i < k \leq n} (\theta_i - \theta_k)^2,$$
the discriminant of the defining polynomial.
