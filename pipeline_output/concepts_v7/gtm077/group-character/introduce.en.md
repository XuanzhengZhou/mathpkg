A character of a finite Abelian group $\mathfrak{G}$ is a homomorphism from $\mathfrak{G}$ to the multiplicative group of complex roots of unity. Characters transform the group operation into multiplication of numbers, enabling the application of analytic methods to group-theoretic problems. Theorem 30 shows there are exactly $h = |\mathfrak{G}|$ distinct characters, each determined by assigning roots of unity to a basis. Characters are the foundation of Dirichlet's L-functions and Fourier analysis on finite groups.
