This is Definition None from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke.

The ideal

$$N_k(\mathfrak{A}) = \mathfrak{A}^{(1)} \cdot \mathfrak{A}^{(2)} \cdot \cdots \cdot \mathfrak{A}^{(m)}$$

is called the relative norm of $\mathfrak{A}$ with respect to $k$. We have $N_k(\mathfrak{UB}) = N_k(\mathfrak{A}) \cdot N_k(\mathfrak{B})$.

This concept is part of the foundational material in algebraic number theory, appearing in the section on Relative Norms of Numbe.
