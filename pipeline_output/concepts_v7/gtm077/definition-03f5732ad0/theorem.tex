If $n$ is the relative degree of $K(\theta)$ with respect to $k$ and if $\alpha = g(\theta)$ is a number of $K(\theta)$, of degree $n/q$, then the system of $n$ numbers $\alpha_i = g(\theta_i)$ ($i = 1, 2, \ldots, n$) will be called the conjugates of $\alpha$ in the field $K(\theta)$ with respect to $k$. These are the conjugates of $\alpha$ with respect to $k$, each one taken $q$ times.

Accordingly the system of these conjugates as a whole depends only on $\alpha$, the ground field $k$, and the field $K$, but it is independent of the choice of the generating $\theta$. Since in the future we will be dealing exclusively with this concept of conjugate, the qualifier "in the field $K(\theta)$ with respect to $k$" will be omitted in general for the sake of simplicity.
