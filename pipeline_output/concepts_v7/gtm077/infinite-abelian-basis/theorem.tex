A system of elements (finitely or infinitely many) $A_i$ (of infinite order) and $T_k$ (of finite order $h_k$) is called a basis for an Abelian group $\mathfrak{G}$ if every element $C \in \mathfrak{G}$ can be written uniquely as
$$C = A_1^{x_1} A_2^{x_2} \cdots T_1^{y_1} T_2^{y_2} \cdots$$
where the $x_i$ are uniquely determined integers (only finitely many nonzero), and the $y_k$ are uniquely determined modulo $h_k$. A basis is called finite if it consists of finitely many elements.
