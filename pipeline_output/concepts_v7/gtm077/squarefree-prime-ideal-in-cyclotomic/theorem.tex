If the prime $p$ does not divide $D \cdot m$, then $p$ is not divisible by the square of a prime ideal in $K(\zeta)$.

For if $\mathfrak{p}^2 \mid p$, then let us choose a number $\omega$ which is divisible by $\mathfrak{p}$ but not by $\mathfrak{p}^2$. It follows from the lemma that

$$\omega^{pf} \equiv \omega \pmod{\mathfrak{p}^2}.$$

Since $p^f \geq 2$, and therefore $\omega^{pf} \equiv 0 \pmod{\mathfrak{p}^2}$,

$$\omega \equiv 0 \pmod{\mathfrak{p}^2},$$

contrary to the hypothesis.
