The division algorithm states that for any integers $a$ (dividend) and $b \neq 0$ (divisor), there exist unique integers $q$ (quotient) and $r$ (remainder) with $0 \leq r < |b|$ such that $a = qb + r$. This seemingly simple fact about integers is the cornerstone of the Euclidean algorithm and all of modular arithmetic.
