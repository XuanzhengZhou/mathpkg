For integers $a$ and $b$ with $b \neq 0$, there exist uniquely determined integers $q$ and $r$ such that
$$a = qb + r, \quad 0 \leq r < |b|.$$
The integer $r$ is called the remainder of the division of $a$ by $b$, or the remainder of $a$ modulo $b$. The statement $b \mid a$ is equivalent to $r = 0$.
