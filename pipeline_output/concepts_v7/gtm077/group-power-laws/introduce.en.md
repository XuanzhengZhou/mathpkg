Theorem 18 establishes the familiar laws of exponents for group elements: $A^{m+n} = A^m A^n$ and $(A^m)^n = A^{mn}$ for all integers $m, n$. These laws follow from the definition of powers and generalized associativity. Notably, powers of a single element commute ($A^m A^n = A^n A^m$), even in a non-abelian group.
