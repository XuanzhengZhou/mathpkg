The number of residue classes modulo a nonzero ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ is finite and is called the norm $N(\mathfrak{a})$. If $\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_n$ is a basis of $\mathfrak{a}$ and $\omega_1,\ldots,\omega_n$ an integral basis of $K$, then
$$N(\mathfrak{a}) = \left|\frac{\Delta(\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_n)}{\sqrt{d}}\right|.$$
For a principal ideal $(\alpha)$, $N((\alpha)) = |N_{K/\mathbb{Q}}(\alpha)|$, the absolute value of the field norm of $\alpha$. The norm is multiplicative: $N(\mathfrak{a}\mathfrak{b}) = N(\mathfrak{a})N(\mathfrak{b})$.
