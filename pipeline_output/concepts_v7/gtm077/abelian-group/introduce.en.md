An Abelian (commutative) group is a group where the order of composition does not matter: $AB = BA$ for all elements. Almost all groups arising in number theory — residue class groups, ideal class groups, unit groups — are Abelian. Commutativity dramatically simplifies the structure theory: finite Abelian groups decompose into direct products of cyclic groups of prime-power order.
