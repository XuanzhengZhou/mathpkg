The numbers of an ideal $a$ which is not (0) (the case $a = (0)$ is trivial) obviously form an infinite Abelian group, under composition by addition, which is a subgroup of the group of all integers in $K$. By the product $ab$ of two ideals $a = (\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_r)$ and $b = (\beta_1, \ldots, \beta_p)$ we mean the ideal
$ab = (\alpha_1\beta_1, \ldots, \alpha_i\beta_k, \ldots, \alpha_r\beta_p)$

thus defined uniquely by $a$ and $b$.

It follows directly from this definition that multiplication of ideals is commutative and associative:

$ab = ba, \quad a(bc) = (ab)c$.
