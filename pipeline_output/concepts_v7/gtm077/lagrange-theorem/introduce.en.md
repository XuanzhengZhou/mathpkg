Lagrange's theorem is one of the most fundamental results in finite group theory: the order of any subgroup divides the order of the group. The proof partitions the group into cosets, all of which have the same size as the subgroup. The quotient $h/N = j$ is the index — the number of distinct cosets. An immediate corollary is that the order of any element (the size of the cyclic subgroup it generates) divides the group order.
