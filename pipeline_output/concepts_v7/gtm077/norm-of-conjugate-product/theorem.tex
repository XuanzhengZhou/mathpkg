For each ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ in a number field $K$ of degree $n$, the product of all conjugate ideals satisfies
$$\mathfrak{a}^{(1)} \mathfrak{a}^{(2)} \cdots \mathfrak{a}^{(n)} = (N(\mathfrak{a})).$$
In particular, for a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ of degree $f$,
$$\mathfrak{p}^{(1)} \cdots \mathfrak{p}^{(n)} = (p^f),$$
where $p$ is the rational prime divisible by $\mathfrak{p}$ and $n = f \cdot r$ with $r$ being the number of distinct conjugate prime ideals dividing $p$.
