The set of numbers $\Delta$ in $K$, such that for each integer $A$ in $K$ the relative trace $S_k(\Delta A)$ is an integer forms an ideal $\mathfrak{M}$ in $K$. Furthermore,

$$\frac{1}{\mathfrak{M}} = \mathfrak{D}_k$$

is an integral ideal and is called the relative different of $K$ with respect to $k$.

The proof that $\mathfrak{M}$ and $\mathfrak{D}_k$ are ideals runs parallel to that of Theorem 101.
