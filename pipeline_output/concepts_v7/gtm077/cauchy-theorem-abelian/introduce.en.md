Theorem 22 is Cauchy's theorem for Abelian groups: if a prime $p$ divides the group order $h$, then some element has order $p$. The proof considers the product of all element orders and uses the counting argument that each element appears equally often when expressed in terms of a power product. This result is the starting point for the structure theory of finite Abelian groups.
