A system of simultaneous congruences $x \equiv a_i \pmod{n_i}$ may or may not have a solution depending on compatibility conditions between the moduli. When a solution exists, it is uniquely determined modulo the least common multiple of the moduli. In the special case of pairwise coprime moduli, a solution always exists — this is the classical Chinese Remainder Theorem.
