Consider the system of simultaneous congruences
$$x \equiv a_1 \pmod{n_1},\quad x \equiv a_2 \pmod{n_2},\quad \ldots,\quad x \equiv a_k \pmod{n_k}.$$

If a solution exists, any two solutions differ by a multiple of the least common multiple $v = \operatorname{lcm}(n_1, \ldots, n_k)$; i.e., the solution is unique modulo $v$.

In the special case where the $n_i$ are pairwise coprime (so $v = n_1 n_2 \cdots n_k$), a solution always exists (Chinese Remainder Theorem).
