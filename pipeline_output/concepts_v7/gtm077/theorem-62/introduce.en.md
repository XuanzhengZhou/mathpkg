This is Theorem 62 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke.

If $\omega$ is a root of an equation

$$x^m + \alpha x^{m-1} + \beta x^{m-2} + \cdots + \lambda = 0$$

where $\alpha, \beta, \ldots, \lambda$ are integers, then $\omega$ is also an integer.

For example the $m$th root of an integer is again an integer.

This result is part of the foundational material in algebraic number theory, appearing in the section on Definition of Algebraic Integers Divisibility and Units.
