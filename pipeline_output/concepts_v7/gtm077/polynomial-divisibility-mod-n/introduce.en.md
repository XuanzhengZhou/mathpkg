An integral polynomial $f(x)$ is divisible by $g(x)$ modulo $n$ if $f(x) \equiv g(x)g_1(x) \pmod{n}$ for some integral polynomial $g_1(x)$. This extends the notion of divisibility from integers to polynomials with respect to a modulus.
