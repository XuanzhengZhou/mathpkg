Euler's theorem generalizes Fermat's little theorem to arbitrary moduli: if $a$ is coprime to $n$, then $a^{\varphi(n)} \equiv 1 \pmod{n}$. In group-theoretic terms, this says the order of any element of the multiplicative group $(\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^\times$ divides the group order $\varphi(n)$. The theorem is foundational for modular exponentiation and modern cryptography.
