Let $\omega_1, \ldots, \omega_n$ be an integral basis of the number field $K$. The discriminant $d$ of $K$ is defined as
$$d = \Delta^2(\omega_1, \ldots, \omega_n) = \det(\omega_i^{(j)})^2,$$
where $\omega_i^{(j)}$ denotes the $j$-th conjugate of $\omega_i$. This number is a nonzero rational integer independent of the choice of integral basis. The absolute value $|d|$ is minimal among all $|\Delta^2(\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_n)|$ for independent integers $\alpha_i$.
