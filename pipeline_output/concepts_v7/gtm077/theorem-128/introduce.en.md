This is Theorem 128 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke.

If $\chi(n)$ denotes a residue character of $n$ mod $m$, then the Dirichlet series

$$L(s, \chi) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{\chi(n)}{n^s}$$

is absolutely convergent for $s > 1$ and as long as $s > 1$ we have the product representation

$$L(s, \chi) = \prod_{p} \frac{1}{1 - \frac{\chi(p)}{p^s}},$$


This result is part of the foundational material in algebraic number theory, appearing in the section on The Distribution of Prime Ideals of Degree 1 in Particula.
