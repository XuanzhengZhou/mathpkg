The ideal $N_k(\mathfrak{A})$ is an ideal in $k$. If $k$ is the field of rational numbers, then $N_k(\mathfrak{A}) = (N(\mathfrak{A}))$.

To begin let $\mathfrak{A} = (A_1, \ldots, A_s)$ be an integral ideal, where the $A_i$ are numbers in $K$. Then, by §28, for any variables $u_1, \ldots, u_s$, the content of the conjugate polynomials

$$F^{(i)}(u) = A_1^{(i)}u_1 + \cdots + A_s

Thus Theorem 88 of §29, which was only proved in the case of Galois fields, is now seen as valid for every number field, and at the same time the terminology "norm of $\mathfrak{A}$" for the number of residue classes mod $\mathfrak{A}$ has received its justification.
