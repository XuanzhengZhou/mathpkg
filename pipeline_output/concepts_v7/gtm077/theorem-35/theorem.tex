If $\mathfrak{G}$ is a torsion-free Abelian group with finite basis $B_1, \ldots, B_h$, then every subgroup $\mathfrak{U}$ of $\mathfrak{G}$ with finite index has a basis $U_1, \ldots, U_n$ of the form

$$U_1 = B_1^{r_{11}} B_2^{r_{12}} \cdots B_n^{r_{1n}},$$

$$U_2 = B_2^{r_{22}} \cdots B_n^{r_{2n}},$$

$$\vdots$$

$$U_n = B_n^{r_{nn}},$$

with $r_{ii} \neq 0$ for $i = 1, 2, \ldots, n$.
