The factor (quotient) group $\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{U}$ is the group whose elements are the cosets of a subgroup $\mathfrak{U}$, with composition inherited from $\mathfrak{G}$. This construction requires commutativity (or normality of $\mathfrak{U}$ in the non-Abelian case). Factor groups are fundamental throughout algebra — in number theory they appear as residue class groups, ideal class groups, and Galois groups. Theorem 29 relates the factor group by $p$th powers to the basis number.
