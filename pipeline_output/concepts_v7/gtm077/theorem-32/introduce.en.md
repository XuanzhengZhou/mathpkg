This is Theorem 32 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke.

If $A$ is an element of order $f$, then $\chi_n(A)$ is an $f$th root of unity. Among the $h$ numbers $\chi_n(A)$, $n = 1, \ldots, h$, all $f$th roots of unity occur equally often, namely $h/f$ times.
