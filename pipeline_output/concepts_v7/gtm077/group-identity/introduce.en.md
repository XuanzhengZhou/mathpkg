Theorem 16 establishes that every group has a unique two-sided identity element $E$, which satisfies $AE = EA = A$ for all $A$. The proof uses the solvability axiom: pick any $C$, solve $XC = C$ to get a right identity $E$, then show it is also a left identity and is unique. This element behaves like multiplication by $1$ and may be omitted as a factor in products.
