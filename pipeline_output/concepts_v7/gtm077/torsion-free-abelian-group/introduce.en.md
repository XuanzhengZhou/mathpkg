A torsion-free Abelian group has no non-identity elements of finite order. When finitely generated, such a group is isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}^n$ for some $n$ (the rank). These groups appear naturally in number theory: the additive group of integers in a number field (as a $\mathbb{Z}$-module) is a torsion-free Abelian group of rank equal to the field degree.
