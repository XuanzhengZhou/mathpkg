A number $\theta$ is called algebraic over a field $k$ if it is a root of a nonzero polynomial with coefficients in $k$. Among all polynomials over $k$ with leading coefficient $1$ having $\theta$ as a root, the one of smallest degree is irreducible and is uniquely determined by $\theta$ and $k$. Its degree $n$ is called the degree of $\theta$ with respect to $k$. The $n$ roots of this polynomial are called the conjugates of $\theta$ with respect to $k$.
