A group is an algebraic structure consisting of a set equipped with an associative binary operation, where every equation $AX = B$ and $YA = B$ is solvable. Hecke's definition uses the cancellation laws and the solvability of equations as axioms. For finite sets, the solvability condition follows from the closure and cancellation properties. Groups are the unifying framework for studying symmetries, permutations, residue classes, and many other mathematical objects.
