Nonzero ideals obey the cancellation law: if $\mathfrak{a}\mathfrak{b} = \mathfrak{a}\mathfrak{c}$, then $\mathfrak{b} = \mathfrak{c}$. This follows from the existence of an inverse to $\mathfrak{a}$ (up to principal ideals) and is essential for unique factorization.
