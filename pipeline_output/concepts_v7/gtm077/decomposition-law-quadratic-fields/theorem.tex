Let $K = \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{d})$ be a quadratic field with discriminant $d$, and let $p$ be a rational prime not dividing $d$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $p$ splits into two distinct prime ideals $(p) = \mathfrak{p}\mathfrak{p}'$ iff $x^2 \equiv d \pmod{4p}$ is solvable (i.e., $\left(\frac{d}{p}\right) = 1$).
\item $p$ remains prime (is inert) in $K$ iff $\left(\frac{d}{p}\right) = -1$.
\item If $p \mid d$, then $p$ ramifies: $(p) = \mathfrak{p}^2$.
\end{enumerate}
