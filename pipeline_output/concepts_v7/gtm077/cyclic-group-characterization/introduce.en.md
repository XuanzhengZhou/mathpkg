This is Theorem 28 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke. An Abelian group $\mathfrak{G}$ of order $h$ is cyclic if and only if for each prime $p$ dividing $h$, the number of elements $A$ with $A^p = 1$ is equal to $p$.
