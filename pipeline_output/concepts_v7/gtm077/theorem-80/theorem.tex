If $(a, b) = 1$, then $\varphi(a b) = \varphi(a) \cdot \varphi(b)$ and in general

$$\varphi(a) = N(a) \prod_{p \mid a} \left(1 - \frac{1}{N(p)}\right)$$

where $p$ runs through the distinct prime divisors of $a$.

To see this let $\alpha$ be chosen so that $(\alpha, a b) = a$ and $\beta$ so that $(\beta, a b) = b$. Then if $\xi$ runs through a complete system of residues mod $b$ and $\eta$ runs through such

factors $p = \mathfrak{p}_1 \mathfrak{p}_2 \cdots \mathfrak{p}_r$, then the positive rational integers $N(\mathfrak{p}_1) \cdots N(\mathfrak{p}_r)$ have as product $N(p) = p^n$, while none of these integers is $= 1$; thus their number $r$ must be $\leq n$.

In this way we obtain one of the few statements which connect the degree of a field with other properties of the numbers of the field. If it is known that a rational prime $p$ is decomposable into $k$ ideal factors in a number field, then the degree of the field is at least $k$.
