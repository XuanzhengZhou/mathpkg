Let $p$ be a prime. The number of distinct elements $A$ of a finite Abelian group $\mathfrak{G}$ satisfying $A^p = E$ is $p^e$, where $e = e(p)$ is the number of basis elements whose order is divisible by $p$. The number $e$ is called the basis number belonging to $p$ and is an invariant of $\mathfrak{G}$ (independent of the choice of basis).
