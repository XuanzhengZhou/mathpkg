The group $\mathfrak{W}$ of all roots of unity in $K$ is finite, and indeed it is a cyclic group of order $w \geq 2$.

Since all roots of unity, including all conjugates, have absolute value 1, the first assertion follows from the lemma. Moreover if $p$ is a prime dividing the order of $\mathfrak{W}$, then the number of solutions of $x^p = 1$ is equal to $p^1$, and thus, by Theorem 28, the basis number of the group $\mathfrak{W}$ belonging to $p$ is equal to 1. Thus the group is cyclic.
