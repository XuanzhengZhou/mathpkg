This is Theorem 68 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke. If $g_1$ and $g_2$ are arbitrary ideals, $g_1 \neq (0)$, then there is exactly one $r$ such that  $$g_1r = g_2.$$  One writes $r = g_2/g_1$, and calls $r$ the quotient of $g_2$ and $g_1$.
