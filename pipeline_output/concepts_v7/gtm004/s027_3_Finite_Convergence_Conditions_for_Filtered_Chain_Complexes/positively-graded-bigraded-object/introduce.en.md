A bigraded object is called positively graded if for each fixed second index $q$, all components $A^{p,q}$ with sufficiently small first index $p$ vanish. Negatively graded is defined dually, requiring vanishing for all sufficiently large $p$. These grading conditions provide a convenient reformulation of the stationarity properties of the exact couple maps: as Proposition 3.2 and Theorem 3.3 show, positive grading of $D$ (or $\bar{D}$) corresponds to negative stationarity of $\alpha$ (or $\bar{\alpha}$), while negative grading of $E$ is equivalent to positive stationarity of $\alpha$. The interplay between these dual grading conditions is essential for establishing finite convergence of spectral sequences.
