A filtration of a chain complex is homologically finite if, on the level of homology, it stabilizes at both ends in each degree: for sufficiently small $p$, the homology of the $p$-th filtration layer $C^{(p)}$ vanishes, while for sufficiently large $p$, the inclusion $C^{(p)} \hookrightarrow C$ induces an isomorphism on homology. This condition is weaker than finiteness of the filtration itself—finite filtrations are always homologically finite (Proposition 3.4)—yet it is precisely the hypothesis needed in Theorem 3.5 to guarantee that the associated spectral sequence converges finitely, that the induced filtration on $H(C)$ is finite, and that the $E_\infty$ term is canonically isomorphic to the graded object $\operatorname{Gr} \circ H(C)$.
