A filtration of a chain complex is called finite if, in each homological degree $q$, the filtration stabilizes at both ends: there is a lower bound $p_0$ beyond which all filtration layers $C_q^{(p)}$ vanish, and an upper bound $p_1$ beyond which they equal the whole chain group $C_q$. This is the strongest and most easily verified finiteness condition one can impose on a filtered chain complex. It implies homological finiteness (Proposition 3.4) and therefore, via Theorem 3.5, guarantees that the associated spectral sequence converges finitely to the graded object associated with the homology of $C$. In practice, many filtrations arising from algebraic topology (e.g., skeletal filtrations of CW-complexes) are finite in this sense.
