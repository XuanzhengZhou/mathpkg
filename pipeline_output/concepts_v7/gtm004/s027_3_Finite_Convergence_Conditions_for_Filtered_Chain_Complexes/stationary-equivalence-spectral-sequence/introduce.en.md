Theorem 3.3 establishes a three-way equivalence that is central to understanding finite convergence of spectral sequences associated with filtered chain complexes. The positive stationarity of the map $\alpha$ in the exact couple derived from the filtration $C^{(p)}$ is equivalent to the negative grading of the $E^1$ term (i.e., $E^{p,q}$ vanishes for all sufficiently large $p$ at each fixed $q$), which is in turn equivalent to the positive stationarity of the map $\bar{\alpha}$ in the exact couple built from the quotient complexes $C/C^{(p-1)}$. This theorem thus connects the algebraic condition of stationarity with a concrete geometric condition on the $E$ term, and also relates the two natural exact couples (one from subobjects, one from quotients) that can be associated to a filtration.
