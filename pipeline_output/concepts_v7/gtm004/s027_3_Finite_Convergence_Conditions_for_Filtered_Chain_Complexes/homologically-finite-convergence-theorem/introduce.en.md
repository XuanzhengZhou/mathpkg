Theorem 3.5 is the central convergence result for spectral sequences of filtered chain complexes. It states that if the filtration is homologically finite—meaning that the homology of the filtration layers $C^{(p)}$ stabilizes at both ends in each degree—then three desirable conclusions hold simultaneously. First, the spectral sequence converges finitely at each bidegree $(p,q)$. Second, the induced filtration on the homology $H(C)$ is itself finite. Third, and most importantly, the $E_\infty$ term is canonically isomorphic to the graded object associated with the filtered homology group: $E_\infty^{p,q} \cong \operatorname{im} H_q(C^{(p)}) / \operatorname{im} H_q(C^{(p-1)})$. This theorem justifies the notation $E_1^{p,q} \Rightarrow H(C)$ and is the workhorse that makes spectral sequences a practical computational tool in homological algebra and algebraic topology.
