Theorem 3.1 provides a fundamental sufficient condition for finite convergence of a spectral sequence arising from an exact couple. If the map $\alpha$ in the exact couple is stationary—meaning that for each fixed $q$, the components $\alpha: D^{p,q} \to D^{p+1,q}$ are isomorphisms for all sufficiently large and all sufficiently small $p$—then at each fixed bidegree $(p,q)$ the spectral sequence terms $E_r^{p,q}$ stabilize after finitely many pages. This result is the starting point for all subsequent convergence criteria in the filtered chain complex setting, as it abstracts the essential algebraic mechanism behind finite convergence without yet invoking the specific structure of the underlying chain complex.
