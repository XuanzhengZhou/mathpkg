Let $K$ be a field. A \textbf{Lie algebra} $\mathfrak{g}$ over $K$ is a vector space over $K$ together with a $K$-bilinear map $[\;,\;] : \mathfrak{g} \times \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{g}$, called the \textbf{Lie bracket}, satisfying the following two identities for all $x, y, z \in \mathfrak{g}$:
$$
[x, x] = 0,
$$
$$
[[x, y], z] + [[y, z], x] + [[z, x], y] = 0.
$$
The second identity is called the \textbf{Jacobi identity}. Note that $[x, x] = 0$ and bilinearity imply $[x, y] = -[y, x]$ for all $x, y \in \mathfrak{g}$.
