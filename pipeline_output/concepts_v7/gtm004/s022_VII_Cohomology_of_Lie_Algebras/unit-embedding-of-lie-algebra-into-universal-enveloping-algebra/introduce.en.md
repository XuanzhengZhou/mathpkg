This corollary of the Poincaré–Birkhoff–Witt theorem states that every Lie algebra embeds into the Lie algebra associated to its universal enveloping algebra. Consequently, every Lie algebra is isomorphic to a Lie subalgebra of $LA$ for some associative $K$-algebra $A$. This is the Lie algebra analogue of Cayley's theorem for groups.
