A left $\mathfrak{g}$-module (or representation of a Lie algebra) is a vector space on which the Lie algebra acts by linear endomorphisms in a way compatible with the bracket. The key identity translates the Lie bracket into the commutator of endomorphisms. By the universal property of $U\mathfrak{g}$, left $\mathfrak{g}$-modules correspond bijectively to left $U\mathfrak{g}$-modules, making the representation theory of $\mathfrak{g}$ equivalent to the module theory of $U\mathfrak{g}$.
