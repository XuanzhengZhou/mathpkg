Let $\mathfrak{g}$ be a Lie algebra over a field $K$. A \textbf{left $\mathfrak{g}$-module} $A$ is a $K$-vector space $A$ together with a Lie algebra homomorphism
$$
\varrho : \mathfrak{g} \longrightarrow L(\operatorname{End}_K A),
$$
where $L(\operatorname{End}_K A)$ denotes the Lie algebra associated to the associative algebra $\operatorname{End}_K A$ with commutator bracket. We write $x \circ a$ for $\varrho(x)(a)$, where $x \in \mathfrak{g}$, $a \in A$. Equivalently, $A$ is a left $\mathfrak{g}$-module if $x \circ a$ is $K$-linear in both arguments and satisfies
$$
[x, y] \circ a = x \circ (y \circ a) - y \circ (x \circ a), \qquad \forall x, y \in \mathfrak{g}, \; \forall a \in A.
$$
