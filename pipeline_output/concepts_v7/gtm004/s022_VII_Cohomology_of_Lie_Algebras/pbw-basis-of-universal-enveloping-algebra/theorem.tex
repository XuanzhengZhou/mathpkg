Let $\mathfrak{g}$ be a Lie algebra over a field $K$, let $\mathfrak{h}$ be a Lie subalgebra of $\mathfrak{g}$, and let $J$ be a totally ordered basis of a vector space complement of $\mathfrak{h}$ in $\mathfrak{g}$. Let $J'$ be a totally ordered basis of $\mathfrak{h}$. Order the disjoint union $J' \cup J$ by setting for $i, j$ in this union:
$$
i \leq j \iff 
\begin{cases}
i, j \in J' \text{ and } i \leq j \text{ in } J', \\
i \in J' \text{ and } j \in J, \\
i, j \in J \text{ and } i \leq j \text{ in } J.
\end{cases}
$$
Then the elements $e_i$ corresponding to all finite increasing sequences in $J$ form a basis of $U\mathfrak{g}$ as an $\mathfrak{h}$-module.
