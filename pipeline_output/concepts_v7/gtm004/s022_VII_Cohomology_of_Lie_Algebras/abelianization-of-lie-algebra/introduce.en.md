The abelianization of a Lie algebra is the universal abelian quotient — it is the largest quotient that is abelian. The kernel of the projection is the derived subalgebra $[\mathfrak{g}, \mathfrak{g}]$, which is always a Lie ideal. This construction parallels the abelianization of a group and the commutator ideal in ring theory.
