A morphism of exact couples is a pair $(\kappa, \lambda)$ of morphisms making the two exact triangles commute in the natural sense. The three commutativity conditions $\alpha'\kappa = \kappa\alpha$, $\beta'\kappa = \lambda\beta$, and $\gamma'\lambda = \kappa\gamma$ ensure that the morphism respects the exact triangle structure. This defines the category $\mathfrak{EC}(\mathfrak{A})$ of exact couples, from which there is a functor to the category of spectral sequences.
