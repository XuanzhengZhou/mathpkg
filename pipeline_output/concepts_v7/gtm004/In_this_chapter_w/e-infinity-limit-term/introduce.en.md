$E_\infty$ is the limit (or abutment) of a spectral sequence, defined as the colimit of the system $(E_{n,\infty}, \sigma)$ where $E_{n,\infty}$ consists of elements that survive as cycles through all subsequent differentials. An element of $E_\infty$ is said to "survive to infinity" if it is a cycle for every $d_r$ ($r \geq n$) and a boundary for no $d_r$. In applications, $E_\infty$ is typically the object one wishes to compute — it is closely related to the homology of the original filtered complex or the target of the spectral sequence.
