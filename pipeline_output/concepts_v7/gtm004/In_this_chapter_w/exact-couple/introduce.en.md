An exact couple consists of two objects $D$ and $E$ and three morphisms $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$ forming an exact triangle. Exact couples provide a systematic way to generate spectral sequences: from an exact couple one derives a new exact couple whose $E$-term is the homology of the original $E$-term, producing the successive pages of a spectral sequence. This approach, pioneered by Massey, illuminates the algebraic structure underlying spectral sequences.
