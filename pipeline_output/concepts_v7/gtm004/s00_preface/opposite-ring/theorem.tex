The \textbf{opposite ring} $\Lambda^{\mathrm{opp}}$ of a ring $\Lambda$ has elements $\lambda^{\mathrm{opp}}$ in one-to-one correspondence with the elements $\lambda \in \Lambda$. As abelian groups $\Lambda$ and $\Lambda^{\mathrm{opp}}$ are isomorphic under this correspondence. The product in $\Lambda^{\mathrm{opp}}$ is given by
$$\lambda_1^{\mathrm{opp}} \lambda_2^{\mathrm{opp}} = (\lambda_2 \lambda_1)^{\mathrm{opp}}.$$
The underlying sets of $\Lambda$ and $\Lambda^{\mathrm{opp}}$ are naturally identified.
