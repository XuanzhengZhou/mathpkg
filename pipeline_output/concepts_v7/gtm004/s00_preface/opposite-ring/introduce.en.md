The opposite ring of $\Lambda$ is obtained by reversing the order of multiplication. It is a formal construction that allows the theory of right modules to be subsumed under the theory of left modules: a right $\Lambda$-module is precisely a left $\Lambda^{\mathrm{opp}}$-module. When $\Lambda$ is commutative, $\Lambda^{\mathrm{opp}}$ coincides with $\Lambda$, and the distinction between left and right modules disappears.
