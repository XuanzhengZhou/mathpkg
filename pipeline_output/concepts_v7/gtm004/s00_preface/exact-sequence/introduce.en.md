An exact sequence captures the idea that the image of one map coincides exactly with the kernel of the next. This is the fundamental notion that measures the failure of a sequence of maps to be "exact in the middle." Exactness at a module means the incoming map's image equals the outgoing map's kernel, generalizing the concepts of injectivity (exactness at the first nonzero term) and surjectivity (exactness at the last nonzero term).
