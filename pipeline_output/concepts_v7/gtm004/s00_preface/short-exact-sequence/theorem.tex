A \textbf{short exact sequence} is an exact sequence of the form
$$0 \to A' \xrightarrow{\mu} A \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} A'' \to 0.$$

The sequence is exact at $A'$, $A$, and $A''$ if and only if $\mu$ induces an isomorphism $A' \xrightarrow{\cong} \mu A'$ and $\varepsilon$ induces an isomorphism $A / \ker \varepsilon = A / \mu A' \xrightarrow{\cong} A''$. Thus $A'$ is (isomorphic to) a submodule of $A$ and $A''$ is (isomorphic to) the corresponding quotient module $A / A'$.

A short exact sequence is often written $A' \rightarrowtail A \twoheadrightarrow A''$.
