A \textbf{right module over $\Lambda$}, or \textbf{right $\Lambda$-module}, is a left $\Lambda^{\mathrm{opp}}$-module. That is, a right $\Lambda$-module is an abelian group $A$ together with a ring homomorphism
$$\omega' : \Lambda^{\mathrm{opp}} \to \operatorname{End}(A, A).$$

Equivalently, it is an abelian group $A$ with an operation $A \times \Lambda \to A$, written $(a, \lambda) \mapsto a\lambda$, satisfying for all $a, a_1, a_2 \in A$ and $\lambda, \lambda_1, \lambda_2 \in \Lambda$:

\begin{itemize}
  \item[\textbf{M 1$'$:}] $a(\lambda_1 + \lambda_2) = a\lambda_1 + a\lambda_2$
  \item[\textbf{M 2$'$:}] $a(\lambda_1 \lambda_2) = (a\lambda_1)\lambda_2$
  \item[\textbf{M 3$'$:}] $a 1_{\Lambda} = a$
  \item[\textbf{M 4$'$:}] $(a_1 + a_2)\lambda = a_1\lambda + a_2\lambda$
\end{itemize}

If $\Lambda$ is commutative, the notions of left and right $\Lambda$-module coincide.
