Let $\Lambda$ be a ring with identity $1_{\Lambda}$ and let $A$ be an abelian group. A \textbf{left $\Lambda$-module structure} on $A$ is an operation $\Lambda \times A \to A$, written $(\lambda, a) \mapsto \lambda a$, satisfying for all $a, a_1, a_2 \in A$ and $\lambda, \lambda_1, \lambda_2 \in \Lambda$:

\begin{itemize}
  \item[\textbf{M 1:}] $(\lambda_1 + \lambda_2)a = \lambda_1 a + \lambda_2 a$
  \item[\textbf{M 2:}] $(\lambda_1 \lambda_2)a = \lambda_1(\lambda_2 a)$
  \item[\textbf{M 3:}] $1_{\Lambda} a = a$
  \item[\textbf{M 4:}] $\lambda(a_1 + a_2) = \lambda a_1 + \lambda a_2$
\end{itemize}

Equivalently, a left $\Lambda$-module is an abelian group $A$ together with a ring homomorphism
$$\omega : \Lambda \to \operatorname{End}(A, A), \qquad (\omega(\lambda))(a) = \lambda a.$$
