Left derived functors form the central construction of homological algebra, generalizing the classical functors Tor and Ext. Given an additive covariant functor $T$ from $\Lambda$-modules to abelian groups, its $n$-th left derived functor $L_n T$ is defined by taking a projective resolution $P$ of a module $A$, applying $T$ to obtain the complex $TP$, and taking the $n$-th homology group. The resulting functors $L_n T$ are well-defined, independent of the choice of projective resolution, and are additive. This construction systematically measures the failure of $T$ to be exact.
