Let $T : \mathfrak{M}_A \rightarrow \mathfrak{Ub}$ be an additive covariant functor. For any $\Lambda$-module $A$, take a projective resolution $P$ of $A$, form the chain complex $TP$, and take homology:
$$L_n TA = H_n(TP), \quad n = 0, 1, \ldots$$
The resulting functors $L_n T : \mathfrak{M}_A \rightarrow \mathfrak{Ub}$, $n = 0, 1, \ldots$, are called the \textbf{$n$-th left derived functors} of $T$.

For a homomorphism $\alpha : A \rightarrow A'$ and projective resolutions $P, P'$ of $A, A'$ respectively, there exists a chain map $\alpha : P \rightarrow P'$ inducing $\alpha$, determined up to homotopy. The induced map $T\alpha : TP \rightarrow TP'$ defines
$$\alpha_* = H_n(T\alpha) : L_n TA \rightarrow L_n TA', \quad n = 0, 1, \ldots$$
which is independent of the choice of $\alpha$.
