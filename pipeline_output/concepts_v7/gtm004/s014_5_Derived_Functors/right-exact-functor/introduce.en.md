A covariant functor $T$ is right exact if it preserves the exactness of sequences that terminate in zero on the right. That is, given an exact sequence $A' \rightarrow A \rightarrow A'' \rightarrow 0$, applying $T$ yields an exact sequence $TA' \rightarrow TA \rightarrow TA'' \rightarrow 0$. Right exactness is a minimal requirement for defining left derived functors: the $0$-th left derived functor $L_0 T$ coincides with $T$ precisely when $T$ is right exact. Typical examples include the tensor product functor $-\otimes_A B$.
