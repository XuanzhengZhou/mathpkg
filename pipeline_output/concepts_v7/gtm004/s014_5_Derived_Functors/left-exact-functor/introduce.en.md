A covariant functor $T$ is left exact if it preserves the exactness of sequences that begin with zero on the left. That is, given an exact sequence $0 \rightarrow A' \rightarrow A \rightarrow A''$, applying $T$ yields an exact sequence $0 \rightarrow TA' \rightarrow TA \rightarrow TA''$. Left exactness is the natural prerequisite for defining right derived functors: the $0$-th right derived functor $R^0 T$ coincides with $T$ precisely when $T$ is left exact. The prototypical example is the Hom functor $\operatorname{Hom}_A(B, -)$.
