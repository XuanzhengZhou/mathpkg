Right derived functors are the cohomological counterpart of left derived functors. Given an additive covariant functor $T$, its $n$-th right derived functor $R^n T$ is defined by taking an injective resolution $I$ of a module $A$, applying $T$ to obtain the cochain complex $TI$, and computing the $n$-th cohomology group. The value $R^n TA$ is independent of the chosen injective resolution, and the assignment is functorial in $A$. Right derived functors are the natural tool when $T$ is left exact; in that case $R^0 T \cong T$.
