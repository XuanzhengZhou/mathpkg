The associated graded functor $Gr$ sends a filtered object to the graded object formed by the successive quotients of its filtration. It forgets the extension data connecting the filtration layers and retains only the isomorphism types of the quotients. In the context of filtered differential objects, the $E$-term of the exact couple -- and therefore the starting page of the associated spectral sequence -- admits the elegant factorization $E = H \circ Gr$.
