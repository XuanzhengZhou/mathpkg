The augmentation map $\varepsilon: \mathbb{Z}G \to \mathbb{Z}$ is the ring homomorphism induced by the trivial group homomorphism $G \to \{1\}$. It sums the coefficients of a formal $\mathbb{Z}$-linear combination of group elements. The augmentation plays a central role in group cohomology: its kernel is the augmentation ideal and it endows $\mathbb{Z}$ with the structure of a trivial $G$-module.
