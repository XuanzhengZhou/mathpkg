By definition, $H_0(G, B) = B \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}G} \mathbb{Z}$. Tensoring with $\mathbb{Z}$ over $\mathbb{Z}G$ identifies elements $bx$ with $b$, effectively quotienting out the action of $G$. The resulting group is precisely the coinvariant quotient $B_G = B / B \circ IG$. This gives the first concrete description of a homology group of a group.
