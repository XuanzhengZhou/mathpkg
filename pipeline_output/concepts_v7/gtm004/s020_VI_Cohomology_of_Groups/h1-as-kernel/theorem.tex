Let $G$ be a group and $B$ a right $G$-module. Using the $\mathbb{Z}G$-free presentation $IG \hookrightarrow \mathbb{Z}G \twoheadrightarrow \mathbb{Z}$ of the trivial module, one obtains
$$H_1(G, B) = \operatorname{Tor}_1^{\mathbb{Z}G}(B, \mathbb{Z}) = \ker\!\big( \iota_* : B \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}G} IG \to B \big),$$
where $\iota_*(b \otimes (x-1)) = bx - b$ for $b \in B$, $x \in G$.

In particular, if $B$ is a trivial $G$-module, then $\iota_*$ is the zero homomorphism and
$$H_1(G, B) \cong B \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}G} IG \cong B \otimes G_{\text{ab}}.$$
