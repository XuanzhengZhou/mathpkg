Proposition 4.2 gives an explicit description of the first homology group $H_1(G, B)$ as the kernel of the map $B \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}G} IG \to B$ sending $b \otimes (x-1)$ to $bx - b$. This follows from applying $\operatorname{Tor}_1^{\mathbb{Z}G}(B, -)$ to the standard $\mathbb{Z}G$-free presentation of $\mathbb{Z}$. When $B$ is a trivial module, $H_1(G, B) \cong B \otimes G_{\text{ab}}$, linking group homology to the abelianization.
