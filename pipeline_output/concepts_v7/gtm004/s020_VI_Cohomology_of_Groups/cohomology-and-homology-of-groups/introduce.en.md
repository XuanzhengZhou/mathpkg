Group cohomology $H^n(G, A)$ and group homology $H_n(G, B)$ are defined as the derived functors $\operatorname{Ext}_{\mathbb{Z}G}^n(\mathbb{Z}, -)$ and $\operatorname{Tor}_n^{\mathbb{Z}G}(-, \mathbb{Z})$. Concretely, one takes a projective resolution $P \to \mathbb{Z}$ of the trivial $G$-module and applies $\operatorname{Hom}_G(-, A)$ or $-\otimes_G B$ to obtain a cochain or chain complex whose (co)homology yields the (co)homology groups. These groups are the central objects of study in the cohomology theory of groups.
