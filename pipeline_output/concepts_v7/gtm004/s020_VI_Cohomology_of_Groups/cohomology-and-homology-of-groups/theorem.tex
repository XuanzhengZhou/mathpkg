Let $G$ be a group, $A$ a left $G$-module, and $B$ a right $G$-module. For $n \geq 0$, the \emph{$n$-th cohomology group} of $G$ with coefficients in $A$ and the \emph{$n$-th homology group} of $G$ with coefficients in $B$ are defined respectively by
$$H^n(G, A) = \operatorname{Ext}_{\mathbb{Z}G}^n(\mathbb{Z}, A),$$
$$H_n(G, B) = \operatorname{Tor}_n^{\mathbb{Z}G}(B, \mathbb{Z}),$$
where $\mathbb{Z}$ is regarded as the trivial $G$-module via the augmentation $\varepsilon: \mathbb{Z}G \to \mathbb{Z}$.

Explicitly, let $P \to \mathbb{Z}$ be a projective resolution of the trivial $G$-module $\mathbb{Z}$. Then
$$H^n(G, A) = H^n(\operatorname{Hom}_G(P, A)), \qquad H_n(G, B) = H_n(B \otimes_G P).$$
