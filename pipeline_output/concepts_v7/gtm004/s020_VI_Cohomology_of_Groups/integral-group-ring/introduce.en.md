The integral group ring $\mathbb{Z}G$ is the free abelian group with basis $G$, endowed with multiplication induced by the group operation. It serves as the ground ring for the theory of cohomology of groups: a $G$-module is precisely a module over $\mathbb{Z}G$. The universal property of the group ring provides a natural equivalence between group actions on abelian groups and module structures over $\mathbb{Z}G$.
