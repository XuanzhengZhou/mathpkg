Corollary 1.4 follows from Theorem IV.12.5 and the adjunction between induction and restriction. It guarantees that if a $G$-module is projective (or injective) over $\mathbb{Z}G$, then it remains projective (or injective) when regarded as a module over any subgroup $U \leq G$. This is essential for relating cohomology of a group to cohomology of its subgroups.
