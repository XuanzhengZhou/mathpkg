The augmentation ideal $IG$ consists of all elements in the group ring whose coefficients sum to zero. It is a two-sided ideal of $\mathbb{Z}G$ and a $G$-module in its own right. The augmentation ideal is the key tool for relating group homology in dimension 1 to the abelianization of the group, and it provides the standard $\mathbb{Z}G$-free presentation of the trivial module $\mathbb{Z}$.
