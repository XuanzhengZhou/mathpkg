Let $f: G \to G'$ be a group homomorphism. Then $f$ induces a ring homomorphism $\mathbb{Z}f: \mathbb{Z}G \to \mathbb{Z}G'$ and, via the restriction of scalars functor $Uf: {}_{\mathbb{Z}G'}\mathbf{M} \to {}_{\mathbb{Z}G}\mathbf{M}$, there are natural homomorphisms:

For cohomology (with $A'$ a $G'$-module):
$$\theta: H^n(G', A') \to H^n(G, Uf A'),$$
where $x \in G$ acts on $a' \in Uf A'$ by $x \circ a' = f(x) \circ a'$.

For homology (with $B'$ a right $G'$-module):
$$\theta: H_n(G, Uf B') \to H_n(G', B').$$

The functor $Uf$ is often omitted from the notation when the context is clear.
