A group homomorphism $f: G \to G'$ induces natural maps in both cohomology and homology via restriction of scalars. These maps reflect the functorial nature of (co)homology: $H^n(-, -)$ may be regarded as a functor on the category whose objects are pairs $(G, A)$ consisting of a group and a $G$-module. The induced maps are essential for relating (co)homology of different groups, such as a group and its subgroups or quotients.
