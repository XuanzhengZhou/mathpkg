An additive category is a category enriched over abelian groups with finite products (and hence finite coproducts, which coincide). The morphism sets are abelian groups and composition is bilinear. Key examples include the category of modules over a ring, the category of abelian groups, and the category of graded modules (with morphisms of degree 0). Additive categories are the natural setting for doing homological algebra.
