Let $F, G : \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{D}$ be functors. A \textit{natural transformation} $t : F \to G$ is a rule assigning to each object $X$ in $\mathfrak{C}$ a morphism $t_X : FX \to GX$ in $\mathfrak{D}$ such that for every morphism $f : X \to Y$ in $\mathfrak{C}$, the diagram
$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
FX & \xrightarrow{t_X} & GX \\
{\scriptstyle Ff}\downarrow & & \downarrow{\scriptstyle Gf} \\
FY & \xrightarrow{t_Y} & GY
\end{array}
$$
commutes.
