This theorem formalizes the slogan "right adjoints commute with limits." It states that applying a right adjoint $G$ and then taking a limit (the functor $R$) is naturally equivalent to taking the limit of the diagram in the functor category and then applying $G$. This is a cornerstone result that unifies many preservation properties in homological algebra and will be used heavily in Chapter VIII on spectral sequences.
