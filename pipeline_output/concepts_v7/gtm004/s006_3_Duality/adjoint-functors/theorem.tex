Let $F : \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{D}$ and $G : \mathfrak{D} \to \mathfrak{C}$ be functors. An \textit{adjunction} $F \dashv G$ is a natural equivalence
$$
\eta = \eta_{XY} : \mathfrak{D}(FX, Y) \overset{\simeq}{\longrightarrow} \mathfrak{C}(X, GY)
$$
of functors $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}} \times \mathfrak{D} \to \mathfrak{S}$. We say $F$ is \textit{left adjoint} to $G$ and $G$ is \textit{right adjoint} to $F$. The natural equivalence $\eta$ is called the \textit{adjugant equivalence} or simply the \textit{adjugant}.
