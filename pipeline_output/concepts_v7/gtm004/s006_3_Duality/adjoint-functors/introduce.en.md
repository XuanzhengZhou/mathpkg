Adjoint functors are one of the most fundamental concepts in category theory, introduced by D. M. Kan. An adjunction $F \dashv G$ provides a natural bijection between morphisms $FX \to Y$ in $\mathfrak{D}$ and morphisms $X \to GY$ in $\mathfrak{C}$. Key examples include: free functor left adjoint to forgetful functor, Hom-tensor adjunction, and the product right adjoint to the diagonal functor. Adjoint functors permeate homological algebra.
