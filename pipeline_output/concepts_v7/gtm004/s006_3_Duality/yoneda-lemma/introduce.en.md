The Yoneda Lemma is one of the most fundamental results in category theory. It establishes a bijection between natural transformations from a representable functor $\mathfrak{C}(A, -)$ to any set-valued functor $F$ and the elements of $FA$. This seemingly simple statement has profound consequences: it implies that an object is determined up to isomorphism by its representable functor, and it underlies the Yoneda embedding, which embeds any category into a functor category.
