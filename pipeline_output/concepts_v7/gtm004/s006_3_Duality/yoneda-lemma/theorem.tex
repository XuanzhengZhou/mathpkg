Let $\mathfrak{C}$ be a category and let $F : \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{S}$ be a functor to the category of sets. For any object $A$ in $\mathfrak{C}$, there is a bijection between natural transformations $\tau : \mathfrak{C}(A, -) \to F$ and elements of $FA$, given by
$$
\tau \longmapsto \tau_A(1_A) \in FA.
$$
Moreover, for any $\kappa \in FA$, the rule
$$
\tau_B(\varphi) = (F\varphi)(\kappa), \qquad \varphi \in \mathfrak{C}(A, B)
$$
defines a natural transformation $\tau : \mathfrak{C}(A, -) \to F$.
