Let $\mathfrak{C}$ be a category in which any two objects admit a product. Given objects $X, Y, Z$ in $\mathfrak{C}$, let
$$
p_1 : X \times Y \to X, \quad p_2 : X \times Y \to Y,
$$
$$
q_1 : (X \times Y) \times Z \to X \times Y, \quad q_2 : (X \times Y) \times Z \to Z.
$$
Then $((X \times Y) \times Z;\, p_1 q_1, p_2 q_1, q_2)$ is a product of $X, Y, Z$. Consequently, $(X \times Y) \times Z \cong X \times (Y \times Z)$.
