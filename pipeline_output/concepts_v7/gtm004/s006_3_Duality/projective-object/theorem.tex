An object $P$ of a category $\mathfrak{C}$ is \textit{projective} if for every epimorphism $\varepsilon : A \to B$ and every morphism $\varphi : P \to B$, there exists a morphism $\psi : P \to A$ such that $\varepsilon \psi = \varphi$:
$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
& P & \\
{\scriptstyle\psi}\swarrow & & \searrow{\scriptstyle\varphi} \\
A & \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} & B
\end{array}
$$
