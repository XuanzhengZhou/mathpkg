An injective object is the categorical dual of a projective object. It has the extension property with respect to monomorphisms: any morphism into the injective object from the domain of a monomorphism extends through the monomorphism. In the category of sets, every object is injective. Injective objects are used to construct injective resolutions, which are the dual analogue of projective resolutions in homological algebra.
