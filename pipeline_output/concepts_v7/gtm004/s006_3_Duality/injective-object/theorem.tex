An object $I$ of a category $\mathfrak{C}$ is \textit{injective} if for every monomorphism $\mu : A \to B$ and every morphism $\varphi : A \to I$, there exists a morphism $\psi : B \to I$ such that $\psi \mu = \varphi$:
$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
A & \xrightarrow{\mu} & B \\
{\scriptstyle\varphi}\searrow & & \swarrow{\scriptstyle\psi} \\
& I &
\end{array}
$$
Equivalently, $I$ is injective in $\mathfrak{C}$ if and only if it is projective in $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$.
