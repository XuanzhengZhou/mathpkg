Let $\{X_i\}_{i \in I}$ and $\{Y_i\}_{i \in I}$ be families of objects in $\mathfrak{C}$ admitting products. Given morphisms $f_i : X_i \to Y_i$, there exists a unique morphism $\prod_i f_i : \prod_i X_i \to \prod_i Y_i$ such that
$$
p_i \left( \prod_i f_i \right) = f_i p_i
$$
for all $i \in I$, where $p_i$ denotes the projection onto the $i$-th factor.
