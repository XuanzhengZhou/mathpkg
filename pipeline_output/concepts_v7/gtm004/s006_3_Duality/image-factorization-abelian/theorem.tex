Given $\varphi : A \to B$ in an abelian category $\mathfrak{A}$, there is a unique (up to isomorphism) factorization
$$
\varphi = \nu \eta,
$$
where $\eta : A \to I$ is an epimorphism and $\nu : I \to B$ is a monomorphism. Specifically, let $\mu : K \to A$ be the kernel of $\varphi$ and $\eta : A \to I$ the cokernel of $\mu$; then $\nu : I \to B$ is the unique morphism with $\varphi = \nu \eta$, and $\nu$ is a monomorphism. The object $I$ is called the \textit{image} of $\varphi$.
