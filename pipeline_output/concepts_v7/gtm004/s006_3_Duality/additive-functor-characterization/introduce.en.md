A functor between additive categories is additive precisely when it preserves either finite products or finite coproducts, or equivalently when it induces group homomorphisms on morphism sets. Additive functors are the natural notion of morphism between additive categories and play a crucial role in homological algebra. Most functors of interest (Hom, tensor, derived functors) are additive.
