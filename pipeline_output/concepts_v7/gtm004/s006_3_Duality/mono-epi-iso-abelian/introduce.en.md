A fundamental property of abelian categories: any morphism that is both monic and epic is an isomorphism. This generalizes the familiar fact in module categories that a bijective homomorphism has an inverse. The proof relies on the uniqueness of the image factorization. This property fails in general categories (e.g., in the category of integral domains, the inclusion $\mathbb{Z} \subset \mathbb{Q}$ is both mono and epi but not iso).
