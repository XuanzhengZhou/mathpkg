Adjunctions compose naturally: if $F$ is left adjoint to $G$ and $F'$ is left adjoint to $G'$, then the composition $F'F$ is left adjoint to $GG'$. This means that the composite of left adjoints goes to the composite of right adjoints in reverse order. This property is used repeatedly in homological algebra.
