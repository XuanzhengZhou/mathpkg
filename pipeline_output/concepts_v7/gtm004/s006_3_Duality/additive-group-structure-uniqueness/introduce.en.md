In an additive category, the abelian group structure on the morphism sets is uniquely determined by the existence of products and zero object. This means that "being additive" is a property of a category, not extra structure. Furthermore, any functor between additive categories that preserves products and zero automatically preserves the additive structure, i.e., induces group homomorphisms on morphism sets.
