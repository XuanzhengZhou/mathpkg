Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an additive category. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] The abelian group structure on each $\mathfrak{A}(A, B)$ is uniquely determined by the categorical structure (products, zero object).
\item[(ii)] Any functor $U : \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{B}$ that preserves products and zero lifts uniquely to a functor preserving the additive structure (i.e., lifts to a functor $\mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{B}$ of additive categories).
\end{enumerate}
