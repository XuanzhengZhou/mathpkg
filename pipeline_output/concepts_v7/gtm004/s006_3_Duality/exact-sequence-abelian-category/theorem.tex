In an abelian category $\mathfrak{A}$, a sequence
$$
A \xrightarrow{\varphi} B \xrightarrow{\psi} C
$$
is called \textit{exact} (at $B$) if, writing $\varphi = \mu \eta$ with $\mu$ monomorphic and $\eta$ epimorphic, $\mu$ is the kernel of $\psi$ (equivalently, $\psi$ is the cokernel of $\mu$, or $\operatorname{im} \varphi = \ker \psi$). A sequence $\cdots \to A_{n+1} \xrightarrow{\varphi_n} A_n \xrightarrow{\varphi_{n-1}} A_{n-1} \to \cdots$ is exact if it is exact at each $A_n$.
