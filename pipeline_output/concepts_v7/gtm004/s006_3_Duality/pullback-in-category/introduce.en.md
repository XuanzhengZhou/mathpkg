A pull-back (or fiber product) is a universal commutative square. Given two morphisms with the same codomain, the pull-back provides the "most general" object mapping to both domains in a compatible way. It generalizes the notion of inverse image (fiber) and intersection. In the category of sets, the pull-back of two subset inclusions is the intersection. The dual notion is the push-out.
