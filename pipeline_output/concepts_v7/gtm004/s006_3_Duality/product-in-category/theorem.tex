Let $\{X_i\}_{i \in I}$ be a family of objects in a category $\mathfrak{C}$ indexed by a set $I$. A \textit{product} of the $X_i$ is an object $X$ together with morphisms $p_i : X \to X_i$ (called \textit{projections}) such that for any object $Y$ and morphisms $f_i : Y \to X_i$, there exists a unique morphism $f : Y \to X$ satisfying $p_i f = f_i$ for all $i \in I$. We write $X = \prod_i X_i$ and $f = \{f_i\}$.
