An additive functor is a functor between additive categories that respects the additive structure. Such functors preserve the direct sum (biproduct) and map zero morphisms to zero morphisms. Additive functors are the proper morphisms in the "category of additive categories" and are essential for the development of homological algebra. Hom and tensor functors are additive.
