A long exact sequence is an infinite (or finite) sequence of morphisms in an abelian category that is exact at each object. In a long exact sequence, the image of each morphism equals the kernel of the next. Long exact sequences typically arise from short exact sequences of chain complexes or from the application of derived functors, and are the central organizing principle of homological algebra.
