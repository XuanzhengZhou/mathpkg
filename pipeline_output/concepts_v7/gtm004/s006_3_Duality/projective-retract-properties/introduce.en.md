Projectivity is preserved under retracts: a retract of a projective is projective. In categories where every object has a projective cover (an epimorphism from a projective), an object is projective exactly when it is a retract of any projective mapping onto it. Under mild hypotheses (free functor adjoint to underlying functor preserving epis), every projective is a retract of a free object.
