Two categories are equivalent if there are functors going back and forth whose compositions are naturally isomorphic to the identity functors. This is a weaker notion than isomorphism of categories but captures the essential categorical structure. Isomorphic categories are necessarily equivalent, but equivalent categories need not be isomorphic. Every category is equivalent to any of its skeletons.
