A projective homomorphism is one that factors through a projective module. These homomorphisms form an ideal-like subgroup of the ordinary Hom group and are precisely the morphisms that become zero in the stable category. The concept is analogous to the notion of null-homotopic maps in topology: just as homotopic maps become equal in the homotopy category, projective homomorphisms become zero in the stable category, making it the natural setting for studying modules up to projective summands.
