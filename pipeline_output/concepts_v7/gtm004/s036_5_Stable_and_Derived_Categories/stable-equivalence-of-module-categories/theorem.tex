Two module categories $\mathfrak{M}_R$ and $\mathfrak{M}_S$ are said to be \textbf{stably equivalent} if their stable categories $\mathfrak{StM}_R$ and $\mathfrak{StM}_S$ are equivalent as categories, even though $\mathfrak{M}_R$ and $\mathfrak{M}_S$ may not be equivalent. A functor $F: \mathfrak{StM}_R \to \mathfrak{StM}_S$ establishing such an equivalence is called a stable equivalence.
