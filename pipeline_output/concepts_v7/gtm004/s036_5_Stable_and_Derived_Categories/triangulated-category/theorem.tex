A {\it triangulated category} consists of an additive category $\mathfrak{T}$ together with:
\begin{itemize}
\item an additive invertible endofunctor $T : \mathfrak{T} \to \mathfrak{T}$, called the {\it translation} (or {\it suspension}) functor;
\item a distinguished class of {\it triangles}, i.e., sequences of the form
\[
X \xrightarrow{f} Y \xrightarrow{g} Z \xrightarrow{h} T X,
\]
satisfying a set of axioms (due to J.L. Verdier).
\end{itemize}
In the homotopy category $\mathfrak{K}$ of cochain complexes, the translation functor is given by degree shift: $(T(\mathbf{C}))^n = C^{n+1}$, and distinguished triangles arise from mapping cones.
