Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an abelian category with enough injectives. Let $\mathfrak{C}(I)^+$ be the full subcategory of $\mathfrak{C}^+(\mathfrak{A})$ consisting of cochain complexes of injective objects, and let $\mathfrak{K}(I)^+$ be the corresponding homotopy category. Then the natural functor
\[
\mathfrak{K}(I)^+ \longrightarrow \mathfrak{D}^+(\mathfrak{A})
\]
induced by the localization at quasi-isomorphisms is an equivalence of categories. In particular, every object of $\mathfrak{D}^+(\mathfrak{A})$ is isomorphic to an injective resolution (viewed as a complex in $\mathfrak{K}(I)^+$).
