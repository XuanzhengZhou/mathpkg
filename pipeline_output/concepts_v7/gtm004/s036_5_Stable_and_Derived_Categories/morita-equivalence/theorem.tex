Two rings $R$ and $S$ are said to be {\it Morita equivalent} if their module categories are equivalent:
\[
\mathfrak{M}_R \cong \mathfrak{M}_S.
\]
By Morita theory, this occurs if and only if $S \cong \operatorname{End}_R(G)^{\mathrm{op}}$ for some projective generator $G$ in $\mathfrak{M}_R$. The equivalence can be realized as a tensor product with a suitable bimodule. For example, if $K$ is a commutative ring, then $\mathfrak{M}_K \cong \mathfrak{M}_{M_n(K)}$ via the functor $F(Y) = P \otimes_{M_n(K)} Y$, where $P \cong K^n$ is the free $K$-module of rank $n$, carrying a natural right $M_n(K)$-module structure.
