Theorem 5.1 states that when an abelian category has enough injectives, the bounded-below derived category is equivalent to the homotopy category of bounded-below complexes of injective objects. This result provides the crucial bridge between the modern derived category approach and the classical theory of injective resolutions: every object in the derived category can be represented by an injective complex, and morphisms can be computed via homotopy classes of maps between such resolutions.
