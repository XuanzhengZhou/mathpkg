In the triangulated category $\mathfrak{K}$ of cochain complexes, a \textbf{distinguished triangle} (also called an exact triangle) is a sequence of cochain complexes and cochain maps
$$
\mathbf{C} \xrightarrow{f} \mathbf{D} \xrightarrow{g} \mathbf{E} \xrightarrow{h} T\mathbf{C}
$$
which is isomorphic (in the triangulated sense) to a triangle arising from the mapping cone construction: given a cochain map $f: \mathbf{C} \to \mathbf{D}$, the mapping cone $\mathrm{Cone}(f)$ yields the triangle
$$
\mathbf{C} \xrightarrow{f} \mathbf{D} \xrightarrow{i} \mathrm{Cone}(f) \xrightarrow{p} T\mathbf{C},
$$
where $i$ is the canonical inclusion and $p$ is the canonical projection.
