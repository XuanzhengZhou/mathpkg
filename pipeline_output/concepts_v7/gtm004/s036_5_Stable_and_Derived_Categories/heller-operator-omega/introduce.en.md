The Heller operator $\Omega$ sends a module to the kernel of its projective cover -- the first syzygy in a projective resolution. While $\Omega$ is not a functor on the ordinary module category (the kernel depends on the choice of presentation), it becomes a well-defined functor on the stable category. In the modular representation theory of finite groups (where $p \mid |G|$), $\Omega$ is invertible, establishing a rich self-equivalence structure. The $\Omega$ functor is fundamental to the homological algebra of finite groups and plays a key role in the theory of support varieties.
