There exists a canonical functor
$$
J: \mathfrak{A} \longrightarrow \mathfrak{D}(\mathfrak{A})
$$
from the abelian category $\mathfrak{A}$ to its derived category, defined by sending an object $A \in \mathfrak{A}$ to the cochain complex concentrated in degree zero:
$$
(J(A))^0 = A, \quad (J(A))^n = 0 \text{ for } n \neq 0.
$$
The functor $J$ is full and faithful; thus $\mathfrak{A}$ can be identified with a full subcategory of $\mathfrak{D}(\mathfrak{A})$.
