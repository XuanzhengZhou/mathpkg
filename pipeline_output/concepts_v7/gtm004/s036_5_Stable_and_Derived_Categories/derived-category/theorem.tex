Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an abelian category and let $\mathfrak{C}(\mathfrak{A})$ be the category of cochain complexes over $\mathfrak{A}$. Let $\mathfrak{K}(\mathfrak{A})$ be the homotopy category of $\mathfrak{C}(\mathfrak{A})$ (morphisms are homotopy classes of cochain maps). The {\it derived category} $\mathfrak{D}(\mathfrak{A})$ is the localization of $\mathfrak{K}(\mathfrak{A})$ with respect to the class of quasi-isomorphisms (cochain maps inducing isomorphisms in cohomology). The derived category $\mathfrak{D}(\mathfrak{A})$ inherits a triangulated structure from $\mathfrak{K}(\mathfrak{A})$.

There is a canonical functor $J : \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{D}(\mathfrak{A})$ sending each object $A$ to the complex concentrated in degree zero with $(J(A))^0 = A$. The functor $J$ is full and faithful.
