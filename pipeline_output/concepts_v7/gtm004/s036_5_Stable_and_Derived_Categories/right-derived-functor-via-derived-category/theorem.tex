Let $F: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{B}$ be an additive functor between abelian categories and assume $\mathfrak{A}$ admits enough injectives. The \textbf{right derived functor} of $F$ in the derived category framework is a triangulated functor
$$
RF: \mathfrak{D}^{+}(\mathfrak{A}) \longrightarrow \mathfrak{D}^{+}(\mathfrak{B})
$$
defined by composing the quasi-inverse of the equivalence $\mathfrak{K}(\mathcal{I})^{+} \simeq \mathfrak{D}^{+}(\mathfrak{A})$ with the functor induced by applying $F$ termwise to injective complexes.

If $F$ is left exact, then the $n$-th cohomology of $RF$ recovers the classical $n$-th right derived functor:
$$
H^n(RF(A)) \cong R^n F(A)
$$
for every object $A \in \mathfrak{A}$ (viewed as a complex concentrated in degree zero). More generally, $H^n(RF)$ coincides with the $n$-th right satellite of $F$.
