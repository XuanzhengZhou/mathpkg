Two rings $R$ and $S$ are said to be {\it stably equivalent} if there is an equivalence of categories
\[
\operatorname{StM}_R \cong \operatorname{StM}_S
\]
between their stable module categories. A stable equivalence may exist even when the ordinary module categories $\mathfrak{M}_R$ and $\mathfrak{M}_S$ are not equivalent. A stable equivalence is often induced by a functor $F : \mathfrak{M}_R \to \mathfrak{M}_S$ that preserves projective homomorphisms.
