The derived category provides a unified framework for derived functors. The right derived functor $RF$ is a functor between derived categories satisfying a universal property, and its cohomology objects recover the classical right derived functors when $F$ is left exact. This approach eliminates the need to choose resolutions explicitly and makes the derived functor formalism independent of having enough injectives or projectives. It generalizes classical homological algebra to settings where such resolutions may not exist, and it is the foundation for modern applications in algebraic geometry, representation theory, and beyond.
