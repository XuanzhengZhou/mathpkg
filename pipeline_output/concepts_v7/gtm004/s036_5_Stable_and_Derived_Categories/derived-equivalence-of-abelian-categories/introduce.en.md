A derived equivalence is an equivalence between the derived categories of two abelian categories. This notion generalizes Morita equivalence: while Morita equivalent rings have equivalent module categories, derived equivalent rings have equivalent derived categories. Derived equivalences occur prominently in modular representation theory and sheaf theory. A classical example is the derived equivalence between the principal blocks of a group algebra and the normalizer of a Sylow subgroup, given by the Broué conjecture in certain cases.
