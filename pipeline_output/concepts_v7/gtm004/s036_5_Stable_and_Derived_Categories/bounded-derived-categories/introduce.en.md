The bounded derived categories $\mathfrak{D}^+$, $\mathfrak{D}^-$, and $\mathfrak{D}^b$ restrict attention to complexes that are bounded below, bounded above, or bounded on both sides, respectively. These variants are often more manageable than the full derived category and suffice for many applications. For instance, $\mathfrak{D}^b(\mathfrak{A})$ is the natural setting for studying derived equivalences in representation theory, as it contains all complexes with finite cohomological amplitude while remaining a full subcategory of $\mathfrak{D}(\mathfrak{A})$.
