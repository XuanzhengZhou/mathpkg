Corollary 4.5 is an immediate consequence of Corollary 4.4: if $A'$ is torsion-free, then $\operatorname{Ext}(A', B)$ is divisible for any $B$. Taking $B = \operatorname{Ext}(A, A'')$, it follows that $\operatorname{Ext}(A', \operatorname{Ext}(A, A''))$ is divisible. But more strongly, the vanishing of $\operatorname{Tor}(A', \operatorname{Ext}(A, A''))$ (since $A'$ is torsion-free) combined with the natural isomorphism of Theorem 4.3 forces the group to be zero. This result is useful in iterative computations of Ext groups.
