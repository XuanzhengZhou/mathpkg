Theorem 4.2 establishes associativity-like relations for the Tor functor and tensor product of abelian groups. It shows that $\operatorname{Tor}$ is naturally associative: $\operatorname{Tor}(\operatorname{Tor}(A', A), A'') \cong \operatorname{Tor}(A', \operatorname{Tor}(A, A''))$. There is also a more complicated unnatural isomorphism involving both $\otimes$ and $\operatorname{Tor}$. These relations are derived from the Künneth theorem applied to the associative chain equivalence $(C' \otimes C) \otimes C'' \simeq C' \otimes (C \otimes C'')$ of chain complexes. The asymmetry in the notation (writing Tor to the left) masks what is truly an associativity property of a bifunctor.
