The total complex $\operatorname{Tot} B$ collapses a double complex $B = \{B_{p,q}\}$ into an ordinary chain complex by taking the direct sum along each antidiagonal $p+q=n$. The differential is simply the sum of the horizontal and vertical differentials: $\partial = \partial' + \partial''$. The anticommutativity $\partial''\partial' + \partial'\partial'' = 0$ guarantees that $\partial^2 = 0$, making $\operatorname{Tot} B$ a genuine chain complex.
