Let $C, D$ be free chain complexes over a principal ideal domain $\Lambda$. Let $\alpha : \prod_{q-p=n} \operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(H_p(C), H_q(D))$ be represented by a cycle $f$ of $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(C, D)$ of dimension $n$. Then any lift of $\alpha$ to a cycle of $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(C, D)$ is unique up to boundaries. Equivalently, the map $\zeta$ in the dual Künneth sequence has a canonical right inverse when both $C$ and $D$ are free, constructed via a fixed splitting $D_{p+n} = \bar{Z}_{p+n} \oplus \bar{Y}_{p+n}$ and the lifting $\gamma$ defined by $\gamma_{-p,q+1} = \bar{\partial}^{-1} \circ \alpha_{-p,q}$ on $Z_p$.
