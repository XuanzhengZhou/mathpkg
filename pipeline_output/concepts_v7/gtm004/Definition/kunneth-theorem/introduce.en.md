The Künneth theorem expresses the homology of the tensor product $C \otimes_\Lambda D$ of two chain complexes over a principal ideal domain in terms of the homology of $C$ and $D$. It provides a natural short exact sequence involving the tensor products of homology groups and the Tor functor. The sequence splits (so $H_n(C \otimes_\Lambda D)$ is the direct sum of the two terms) but the splitting is not natural. When $\Lambda$ is a field, the Tor term vanishes and the formula simplifies to $H(C \otimes D) \cong H(C) \otimes H(D)$.
