A \text{double complex of chains} $B$ over $\Lambda$ is an object in $\mathfrak{M}_{\Lambda}^{\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}}$, together with two endomorphisms
$$\partial' : B \rightarrow B, \quad \partial'' : B \rightarrow B$$
of degree $(-1, 0)$ and $(0, -1)$ respectively, called the differentials, such that
$$\partial' \partial' = 0, \quad \partial'' \partial'' = 0, \quad \partial'' \partial' + \partial' \partial'' = 0.$$
Equivalently, a double complex consists of a bigraded family of $\Lambda$-modules $\{B_{p,q}\}$, $p, q \in \mathbb{Z}$, and two families of $\Lambda$-module homomorphisms
$$\{\partial'_{p,q} : B_{p,q} \rightarrow B_{p-1,q}\}, \quad \{\partial''_{p,q} : B_{p,q} \rightarrow B_{p,q-1}\},$$
satisfying the three relations above.
