A double complex of chains is a bigraded module equipped with two differentials: a horizontal differential $\partial'$ of bidegree $(-1,0)$ and a vertical differential $\partial''$ of bidegree $(0,-1)$. These differentials each square to zero and anticommute with each other, i.e., $\partial''\partial' + \partial'\partial'' = 0$. Double complexes provide the algebraic framework for studying spectral sequences and are the natural setting for tensor products and Hom complexes of chain complexes.
