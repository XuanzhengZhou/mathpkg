Corollary 4.4 follows from the natural associativity isomorphism $\operatorname{Ext}(\operatorname{Tor}(A', A), A'') \cong \operatorname{Ext}(A', \operatorname{Ext}(A, A''))$ of Theorem 4.3. When $A$ is torsion-free, $\operatorname{Tor}(A', A) = 0$ for all $A'$, so $\operatorname{Ext}(A', \operatorname{Ext}(A, B)) = 0$ for all $A'$, which means $\operatorname{Ext}(A, B)$ is injective as an abelian group, i.e., divisible. This gives a useful criterion for divisibility of Ext groups in terms of the torsion-freeness of the first argument.
