Corollary 4.6 extracts two consequences from the associativity theorems. Part (i) shows that iterated Ext is symmetric in its first two arguments: $\operatorname{Ext}(A', \operatorname{Ext}(A, A'')) \cong \operatorname{Ext}(A, \operatorname{Ext}(A', A''))$ naturally. Part (ii) provides an unnatural "adjunction" isomorphism between Hom and Ext, showing a duality between these functors analogous to the Hom-Tensor adjunction. These isomorphisms illustrate the deeper structural unity among the fundamental bifunctors of homological algebra.
