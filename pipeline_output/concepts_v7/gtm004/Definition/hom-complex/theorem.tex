Let $D$ be a chain complex and $E$ a chain complex over $\Lambda$. The \text{Hom complex} $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(D, E)$ is the double complex of abelian groups defined by
$$B_{p,q} = \operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(D_{-p}, E_q),$$
with differentials
$$(\partial' f)(d) = (-1)^{p+q+1} f(\partial d), \quad (\partial'' f)(d) = \partial(f(d)), \qquad f \in \operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(D_{-p}, E_q), \; d \in D_{-p+1}.$$
The second total complex $\operatorname{Tot}' B$ is denoted by $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(D, E)$, with differential
$$(\partial^H f)_{p,q} = (-1)^{p+q} f_{p+1,q} \circ \partial + \partial \circ f_{p,q+1},$$
where $f = \{f_{p,q}\}$, $f_{p,q} : D_{-p} \rightarrow E_q$.
