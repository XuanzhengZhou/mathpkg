Let $\mathfrak{C}$ be a category with a zero object $0$. For any objects $X, Y$ in $\mathfrak{C}$, the \textbf{zero morphism} $0_{XY}: X \to Y$ is the composite
$$X \to 0 \to Y,$$
where the maps are the unique morphisms given by the definition of the zero object.

For any $f: W \to X$, $g: Y \to Z$ in $\mathfrak{C}$,
$$0_{XY} f = 0_{WY}, \quad g 0_{XY} = 0_{XZ}.$$
