In a category with a zero object, there is a distinguished zero morphism between any two objects, obtained by factoring through the zero object. The zero morphism absorbs composition on either side: composing any morphism with a zero morphism yields another zero morphism. Zero morphisms are essential in homological algebra for defining kernels, cokernels, and exact sequences.
