A full embedding is a functor that is full, faithful, and injective on objects. Such a functor identifies the source category with a full subcategory of the target category — the image $F(\mathfrak{C})$ contains all morphisms between its objects that exist in $\mathfrak{D}$. Full embeddings are the categorical analog of injective structure-preserving maps, allowing one category to be viewed as living inside another without loss of morphism information.
