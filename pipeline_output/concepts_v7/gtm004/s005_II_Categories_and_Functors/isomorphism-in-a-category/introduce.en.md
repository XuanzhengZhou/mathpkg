An isomorphism in a category is a morphism that has a two-sided inverse. This generalizes the notion of isomorphism across all mathematical structures: bijective functions in sets, group isomorphisms, homeomorphisms in topology, and linear isomorphisms in vector spaces. The inverse morphism $f^{-1}$ is uniquely determined and is itself an isomorphism. The relation of being isomorphic is an equivalence relation on the objects of a category.
