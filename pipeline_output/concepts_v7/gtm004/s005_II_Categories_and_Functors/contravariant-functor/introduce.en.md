A contravariant functor reverses the direction of morphisms: a morphism $f: X \to Y$ is sent to $Ff: FY \to FX$. The composition order is also reversed: $F(gf) = F(f)F(g)$. The canonical example is the contravariant Hom-functor $\mathfrak{C}(-, B)$, which sends $f: A_1 \to A_2$ to the pre-composition map $f^*: \mathfrak{C}(A_2, B) \to \mathfrak{C}(A_1, B)$. Contravariant functors are equivalent to covariant functors from the opposite category.
