A faithful functor is one that is injective on morphism sets: distinct morphisms between $A$ and $B$ remain distinct after applying the functor. Intuitively, a faithful functor does not collapse or identify different morphisms. Many forgetful functors (from groups to sets, from topological spaces to sets) are faithful: they preserve the underlying function but may forget additional structure.
