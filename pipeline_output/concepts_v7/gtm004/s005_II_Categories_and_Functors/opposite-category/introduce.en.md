The opposite category $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$ is formed by reversing all morphisms in $\mathfrak{C}$: every morphism $f: X \to Y$ in $\mathfrak{C}$ becomes a morphism $f: Y \to X$ in $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$. This construction is fundamental for formulating categorical duality: any statement about a category has a dual statement obtained by replacing each category with its opposite. Contravariant functors from $\mathfrak{C}$ to $\mathfrak{D}$ are precisely covariant functors from $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$ to $\mathfrak{D}$.
