A \textbf{category} $\mathfrak{C}$ consists of the following data:

\begin{enumerate}
\item A class of objects $A, B, C, \ldots$
\item To each pair of objects $A, B$ of $\mathfrak{C}$, a set $\mathfrak{C}(A, B)$ of morphisms from $A$ to $B$.
\item To each triple of objects $A, B, C$ of $\mathfrak{C}$, a law of composition
  $$\mathfrak{C}(A, B) \times \mathfrak{C}(B, C) \to \mathfrak{C}(A, C).$$
\end{enumerate}

For $f \in \mathfrak{C}(A, B)$ we write $f: A \to B$; $A$ is the domain, $B$ the codomain. The composition of $f: A \to B$ and $g: B \to C$ is written $g \circ f$ or $gf$.

These data satisfy the following axioms:

\begin{itemize}
\item[\textbf{A1.}] The sets $\mathfrak{C}(A_1, B_1)$ and $\mathfrak{C}(A_2, B_2)$ are disjoint unless $A_1 = A_2$ and $B_1 = B_2$.
\item[\textbf{A2.}] Given $f: A \to B$, $g: B \to C$, $h: C \to D$, then
  $$h(gf) = (hg)f \quad \text{(Associative law of composition)}.$$
\item[\textbf{A3.}] To each object $A$ there is a morphism $1_A: A \to A$ such that, for any $f: A \to B$, $g: C \to A$,
  $$f 1_A = f, \quad 1_A g = g \quad \text{(Identity morphism)}.$$
\end{itemize}
