A category is the fundamental structure in category theory, consisting of objects and morphisms between them, together with an associative composition law and identity morphisms. This concept unifies many mathematical structures: sets with functions, groups with homomorphisms, topological spaces with continuous maps, and modules with linear maps all form categories. The axioms ensure that composition behaves like ordinary function composition while allowing morphisms that are not literally functions.
