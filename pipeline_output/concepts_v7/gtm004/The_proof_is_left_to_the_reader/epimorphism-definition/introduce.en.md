This definition introduces the categorical notion of epimorphism in the context of module theory. An epimorphism is defined purely in terms of the cancellation property with respect to composition, without reference to elements or surjectivity. This formulation in terms of morphisms only is essential for the process of dualization, which will be used to define injective modules. In the category of modules, epimorphisms coincide with surjective homomorphisms, but this is a theorem (Proposition 6.1) rather than part of the definition.
