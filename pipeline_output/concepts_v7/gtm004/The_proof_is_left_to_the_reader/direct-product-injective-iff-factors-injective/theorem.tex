A direct product $\prod_{j \in J} I_j$ of $\Lambda$-modules is injective if and only if each factor $I_j$ is injective.

This is the dual of Proposition 4.5 (which states that a direct sum is projective iff each summand is projective). The proof follows by dualizing the argument for projectives: if each $I_j$ is injective, an extension problem into the product reduces to extension problems into each factor via the projections; conversely, if the product is injective, the injectivity of each factor follows by restricting.
