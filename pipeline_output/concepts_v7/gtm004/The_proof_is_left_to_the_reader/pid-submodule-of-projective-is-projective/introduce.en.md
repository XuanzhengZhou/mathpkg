This corollary establishes that principal ideal domains are hereditary rings: the property of being projective is inherited by submodules. Over a PID, every module has projective dimension at most 1, since any submodule of a free (hence projective) module is itself projective. This dramatically simplifies the computation of derived functors over PIDs, as every module admits a projective resolution of length at most 1.
