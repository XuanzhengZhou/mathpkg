Let $\Lambda$ be a principal ideal domain. Then every submodule of a free $\Lambda$-module is free.

More precisely, let $P = \bigoplus_{i \in J} \Lambda_i$ where $\Lambda_i = \Lambda$ be a free $\Lambda$-module, and let $R$ be a submodule of $P$. Assuming $J$ is well-ordered, define for each $j \in J$:

$$\bar{P}_{(j)} = \bigoplus_{i < j} \Lambda_i, \quad P_{(j)} = \bigoplus_{i \leq j} \Lambda_i.$$

Let $f_j : P_{(j)} \cap R \rightarrow \Lambda$ be the projection onto the $j$-th coordinate. Then $\operatorname{im} f_j$ is an ideal in $\Lambda$, hence principal, generated by some $\lambda_j \in \Lambda$. Choosing $c_j \in P_{(j)} \cap R$ with $f_j(c_j) = \lambda_j$ whenever $\lambda_j \neq 0$, the set $\{c_j : \lambda_j \neq 0\}$ forms a basis of $R$.
