This corollary shows that over a principal ideal domain, the notions of projective and free modules coincide. Every projective is free, and conversely every free module is projective. This greatly simplifies homological algebra over PIDs, because projective resolutions can be replaced by free resolutions. The result follows by combining the theorem that submodules of free modules over PIDs are free with the characterization of projective modules as direct summands of free modules.
