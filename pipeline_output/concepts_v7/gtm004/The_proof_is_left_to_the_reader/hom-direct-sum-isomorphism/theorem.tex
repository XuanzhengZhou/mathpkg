Let $B$ be a $\Lambda$-module and $\{A_j\}, j \in J$ be a family of $\Lambda$-modules. Then there is an isomorphism

$$\eta : \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}\left( \bigoplus_{j \in J} A_j, B \right) \xrightarrow{\sim} \prod_{j \in J} \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A_j, B).$$

For $\psi : \bigoplus_{j \in J} A_j \rightarrow B$, the map is defined by $\eta(\psi) = (\psi \circ i_j : A_j \rightarrow B)_{j \in J}$, where $i_j : A_j \rightarrow \bigoplus_{j \in J} A_j$ are the canonical injections. Conversely, a family $\{\psi_j : A_j \rightarrow B\}, j \in J$, gives rise to a unique map $\psi : \bigoplus_{j \in J} A_j \rightarrow B$ by the universal property of the direct sum, so that $\eta$ is a bijection.
