Let $P$ be a free $\Lambda$-module on the set $S$. For every $\Lambda$-module $M$ and every function $f : S \rightarrow M$ from $S$ into the underlying set of $M$, there exists a unique $\Lambda$-module homomorphism $\varphi : P \rightarrow M$ extending $f$. Explicitly, if $f(s) = m_s$ for each $s \in S$, then

$$\varphi\left( \sum_{s \in S} \lambda_s s \right) = \sum_{s \in S} \lambda_s m_s,$$

where $\lambda_s \in \Lambda$ and only finitely many are non-zero. This homomorphism is uniquely determined by its values on the basis elements.
