Let $P$ be a free $\Lambda$-module on a set $S$. For every surjective $\Lambda$-module homomorphism $\varepsilon : B \rightarrow C$ and every homomorphism $\gamma : P \rightarrow C$, there exists a homomorphism $\beta : P \rightarrow B$ such that $\varepsilon \circ \beta = \gamma$. In other words, the following diagram can be completed:

\[
\begin{CD}
@. P @.\\
@. @V\gamma VV @.\\
B @>\varepsilon>> C @>>> 0
\end{CD}
\]

The lift $\beta$ is obtained by choosing, for each basis element $s \in S$, a preimage in $B$ of $\gamma(s) \in C$ under the surjection $\varepsilon$, and extending by the universal property of the free module.
