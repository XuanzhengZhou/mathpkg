This proposition proves that every free module is projective. Given a free module on a basis, any homomorphism from it into a quotient can be lifted along the quotient map by choosing preimages of the images of basis elements. This is a direct consequence of the universal property of free modules. The lifting property is the defining characteristic of projective modules and makes free modules the prototypical examples of projectives.
