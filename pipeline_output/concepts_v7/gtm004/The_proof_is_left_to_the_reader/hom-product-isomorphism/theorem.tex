Let $A$ be a $\Lambda$-module and $\{B_j\}, j \in J$ be a family of $\Lambda$-modules. Then there is an isomorphism

$$\zeta : \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}\left( A, \prod_{j \in J} B_j \right) \xrightarrow{\sim} \prod_{j \in J} \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, B_j).$$

The isomorphism is given by $\zeta(\varphi) = (\pi_j \circ \varphi)_{j \in J}$, where $\pi_j : \prod_{j \in J} B_j \rightarrow B_j$ are the canonical projections. The inverse map sends a family $\{\varphi_j : A \rightarrow B_j\}_{j \in J}$ to the unique homomorphism $\varphi : A \rightarrow \prod_{j \in J} B_j$ with $\pi_j \circ \varphi = \varphi_j$ for all $j \in J$, whose existence and uniqueness are guaranteed by the universal property of the direct product.
