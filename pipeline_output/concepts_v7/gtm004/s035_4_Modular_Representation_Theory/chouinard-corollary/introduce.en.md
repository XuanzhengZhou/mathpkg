Chouinard's corollary provides a striking detection criterion for projectivity: a $kG$-module is projective precisely when its restriction to every elementary abelian $p$-subgroup is projective. Originally proved independently as a surprising structural result, this corollary follows naturally from the complexity theory developed by Alperin, Evens, and Carlson, since $c_G(M) = 0$ characterizes projectivity.
