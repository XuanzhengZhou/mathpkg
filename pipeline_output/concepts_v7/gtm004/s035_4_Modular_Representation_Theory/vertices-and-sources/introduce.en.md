Green's theory of vertices and sources provides a fundamental structural decomposition of indecomposable modules in modular representation theory. The vertex of a module is the minimal subgroup relative to which the module is projective; it is always a $p$-subgroup. The source is the "building block" from which the module is induced. Together, the vertex and source encode deep information about how a module relates to the $p$-local structure of the group, and they are essential tools for the Green correspondence and the classification of indecomposable modules.
