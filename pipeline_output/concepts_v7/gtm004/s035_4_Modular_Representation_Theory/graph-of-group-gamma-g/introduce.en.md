The extension graph $\Gamma(G)$ provides a homological approach to block theory, originally developed by R. Brauer using character theory. Its vertices correspond to simple $kG$-modules, and edges record the existence of nontrivial extensions between them. The connected components of $\Gamma(G)$ define the blocks of $G$, giving a transparent definition of Brauer's blocks without reference to characters. This graph-theoretic perspective highlights the fundamentally homological nature of block decomposition.
