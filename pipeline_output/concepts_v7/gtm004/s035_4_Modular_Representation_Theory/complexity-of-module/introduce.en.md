Complexity is a numerical invariant that measures how far an indecomposable module is from being projective. Defined through the growth rate of the dimensions in a minimal projective resolution, complexity generalizes the Krull dimension of the cohomology ring (which is the complexity of the trivial module $k$). Complexity $c_G(M) = 0$ characterizes projective modules, providing a clean homological criterion. The complexity partitions indecomposable modules into classes that have been the subject of intensive study in modular representation theory.
