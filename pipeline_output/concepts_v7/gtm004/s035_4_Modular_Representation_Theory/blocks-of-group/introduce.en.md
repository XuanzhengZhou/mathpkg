Blocks partition the set of simple modules (and hence the representation theory of $G$) into connected components determined by the existence of nontrivial extensions. Originally introduced by R. Brauer in the 1940s using character theory, blocks have a natural homological definition via the extension graph $\Gamma(G)$. This decomposition is fundamental to modular representation theory: each block can be studied independently, and the principal block (containing the trivial module) often plays a distinguished role.
