Let $G$ be a finite group and $k$ a field of characteristic $p \mid |G|$. Two simple $kG$-modules $S_i$ and $S_j$ are said to lie in the same {\it block} $\mathcal{B}$ if the corresponding vertices in the extension graph $\Gamma(G)$ lie in the same connected component. The block containing the trivial module $S_0 = k$ is called the {\it principal block}.
