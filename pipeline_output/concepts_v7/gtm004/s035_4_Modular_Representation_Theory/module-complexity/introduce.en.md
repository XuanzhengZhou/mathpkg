The complexity $c_G(M)$ generalizes the Krull dimension invariant from the trivial module $k$ to arbitrary indecomposable $kG$-modules. It measures the polynomial growth rate of the minimal projective resolution of $M$. Complexity $0$ characterizes projective modules, and the complexity of the trivial module recovers the Krull dimension of the cohomology ring. This invariant provides a powerful tool for subdividing the class of indecomposable modules.
