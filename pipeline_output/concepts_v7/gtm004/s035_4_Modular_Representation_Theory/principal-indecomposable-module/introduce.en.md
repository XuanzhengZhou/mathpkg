The principal indecomposable modules (PIMs) are the indecomposable direct summands of the group algebra $kG$ regarded as a module over itself. Over $kG$, projective and injective modules coincide, and the PIMs are precisely the indecomposable projective modules. They correspond bijectively to the simple $kG$-modules via the projective cover construction.
