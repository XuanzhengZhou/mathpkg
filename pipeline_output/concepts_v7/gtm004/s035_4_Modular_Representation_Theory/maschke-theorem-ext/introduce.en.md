Maschke's theorem, reformulated in homological language, states that the category of $kG$-modules is semisimple (has no nontrivial extensions) precisely when the characteristic $p$ of the field does not divide the group order. The existence of a nonzero $\operatorname{Ext}^1$ signals the failure of complete reducibility and marks the entry into modular representation theory, where $p \mid |G|$ and the representation theory becomes significantly more complex.
