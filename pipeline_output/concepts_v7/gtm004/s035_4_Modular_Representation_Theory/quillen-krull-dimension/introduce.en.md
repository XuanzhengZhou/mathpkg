Quillen's theorem (previously conjectured by Atiyah and Swan) was a major breakthrough in the cohomology theory of finite groups. It shows that the Krull dimension of the cohomology ring $H^*(G, k)$ is determined entirely by the $p$-local structure of $G$, specifically by its elementary abelian $p$-subgroups. The result reduces the computation of this global invariant to the elementary abelian case, where the dimension is simply the rank. This theorem has far-reaching consequences, including the theory of varieties for modules and the definition of complexity.
