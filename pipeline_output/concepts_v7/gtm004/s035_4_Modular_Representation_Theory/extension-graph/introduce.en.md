The extension graph $\Gamma(G)$ encodes the nontrivial extensions between simple $kG$-modules. Its vertices are the simple modules, and edges record pairs of simples for which $\operatorname{Ext}^1$ does not vanish. This graph provides a homological foundation for the notion of blocks, which were originally introduced by R. Brauer using character theory in the 1940s.
