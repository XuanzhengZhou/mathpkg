\begin{definition}[Growth of a Sequence]
\label{def:growth-of-sequence}
The \textbf{growth} of a sequence $\{s_n\}_{n \geq 0}$ of nonnegative integers is defined to be the least nonnegative integer $c$ such that there exists a constant $C$ with
$$
s_n \leq C n^{c-1}
$$
for all $n \geq 0$. By convention, $n^{0-1} = n^{-1}$, so growth $c = 0$ means the sequence is uniformly bounded; and $n^{0} = 1$ is interpreted when $c = 1$.
\end{definition}
