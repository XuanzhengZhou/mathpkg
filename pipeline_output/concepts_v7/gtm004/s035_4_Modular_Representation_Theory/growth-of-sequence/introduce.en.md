The growth of an integer sequence provides a measure of its polynomial rate of increase. Growth $c = 0$ corresponds to bounded sequences, while growth $c = 1$ means linear growth. This numerical invariant is used in group cohomology to define the Krull dimension of the cohomology ring $H^*(G,k)$, linking homological algebra to commutative algebra.
