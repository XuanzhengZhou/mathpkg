This theorem, due to Alperin-Evens and Carlson, generalizes Quillen's result from the trivial module to arbitrary modules. It shows that the complexity of any module is determined by its restrictions to elementary abelian $p$-subgroups. Together with Quillen's theorem, this establishes a powerful detection principle: the $p$-local structure of the group, specifically its elementary abelian $p$-subgroups, controls the cohomological and homological behavior of all modules. This result is foundational for the theory of support varieties in modular representation theory.
