Principal indecomposable modules (PIMs) are the basic building blocks of the projective-injective theory of group algebras. In the modular setting, projective and injective modules coincide for $kG$, and the PIMs are exactly the indecomposable direct summands of the regular representation. Their bijective correspondence with simple modules is a fundamental structural fact: each simple module has a unique projective cover, which is the corresponding PIM. Understanding the structure of PIMs is a central problem in modular representation theory.
