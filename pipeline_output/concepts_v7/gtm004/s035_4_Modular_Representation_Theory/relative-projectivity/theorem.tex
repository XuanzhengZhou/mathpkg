\begin{definition}[Projective Relative to a Subgroup]
\label{def:relative-projectivity}
Let $G$ be a finite group, $U$ a subgroup of $G$, and $k$ a field. A $kG$-module $M$ is called \textbf{projective relative to $U$} if every short exact sequence of $kG$-modules
$$
0 \longrightarrow A \longrightarrow B \longrightarrow M \longrightarrow 0
$$
whose restriction to $kU$ splits, also splits over $kG$.

If $S$ is a $p$-Sylow subgroup of $G$, then every $kG$-module is projective relative to $S$.
\end{definition}
