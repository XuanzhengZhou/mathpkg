Let $G$ be a finite group, $k$ a field, and $U \leq G$ a subgroup. A $kG$-module $M$ is called {\it projective relative to $U$} (or {\it $U$-projective}) if every short exact sequence of $kG$-modules
\[
0 \to A \to B \to M \to 0
\]
whose restriction to $kU$ splits, also splits over $kG$. If $S$ is a $p$-Sylow subgroup of $G$, then every $kG$-module is projective relative to $S$.
