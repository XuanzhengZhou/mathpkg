\begin{definition}[Vertex and Source]
\label{def:vertex-and-source}
Let $M$ be an indecomposable $kG$-module, where $G$ is a finite group and $k$ is a field of characteristic $p$. Consider the class of subgroups $U \leq G$ relative to which $M$ is projective. There exists a minimal such subgroup $V$ (unique up to $G$-conjugacy), called the \textbf{vertex} of $M$. Moreover, there exists an indecomposable $kV$-module $N$, unique up to isomorphism and conjugation, such that $M$ is a direct summand of the induced module $kG \otimes_{kV} N$. The $kV$-module $N$ is called the \textbf{source} of $M$.
\end{definition}
