Maschke's theorem in its homological form characterizes when the category of $kG$-modules fails to be semisimple. Over a field $k$ of characteristic $p$, nontrivial extensions between $kG$-modules exist precisely when $p$ divides the group order $|G|$. This equivalence marks the boundary between ordinary and modular representation theory: the modular case begins exactly where $\operatorname{Ext}^1$ becomes nontrivial.
