\begin{definition}[Krull Dimension of $H^*(G, k)$]
\label{def:krull-dimension-cohomology-ring}
Let $G$ be a finite group and $k$ a field. Let $d_n = \dim_k H^n(G, k)$ for $n = 0, 1, 2, \ldots$. The \textbf{Krull dimension} of the cohomology ring $H^*(G, k)$ is defined as the growth $c = c(G)$ of the sequence $\{d_n\}$.

Thus $c(G) = 1$ if and only if the dimensions of the cohomology groups $H^n(G, k)$ are uniformly bounded. This occurs precisely when the $p$-Sylow subgroup of $G$ is cyclic, or when $p = 2$ and the $2$-Sylow subgroup is cyclic or generalized quaternion.
\end{definition}
