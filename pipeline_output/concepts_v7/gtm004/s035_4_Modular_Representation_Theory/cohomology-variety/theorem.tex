\begin{definition}[Cohomology Variety]
\label{def:cohomology-variety}
Let $G$ be a finite group and $k$ a field. Let
$$
H^{\mathrm{ev}}(G, k) = \bigoplus_{n \geq 0} H^{2n}(G, k)
$$
be the even-dimensional part of the cohomology ring (the whole ring if $p = 2$, and the even subring if $p \neq 2$).

The \textbf{cohomology variety} $X_G = X_G(k)$ is the maximal ideal spectrum of $H^{\mathrm{ev}}(G, k)$, a homogeneous affine variety whose dimension equals the Krull dimension $c(G)$ of $H^*(G, k)$.

For any $kG$-module $M$, the \textbf{support variety} $X_G(M)$ is a homogeneous subvariety of $X_G$ whose dimension equals the complexity $c_G(M)$.
\end{definition}
