The Krull dimension of the cohomology ring $H^*(G, k)$ can be characterized purely in terms of the asymptotic growth rate of the dimensions of the cohomology groups. This definition avoids the heavy machinery of commutative algebra and makes the dimension accessible through explicit numerical bounds. The case $c(G) = 1$ corresponds to groups whose Sylow $p$-subgroups are of a very restricted type, highlighting the deep connection between cohomological growth and group structure.
