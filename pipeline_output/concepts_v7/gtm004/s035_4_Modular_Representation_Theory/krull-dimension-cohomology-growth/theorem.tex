Let $G$ be a finite group and $k$ a field of characteristic $p$. The {\it growth} of a sequence $\{s_n\}_{n \geq 0}$ of nonnegative integers is the least nonnegative integer $c$ such that there exists a constant $C$ with
\[
s_n \leq C \, n^{c-1}
\]
for all $n \geq 0$. The {\it Krull dimension} of the cohomology algebra $H^*(G, k)$ is defined as the growth $c(G)$ of the sequence $d_n = \dim_k H^n(G, k)$. One has $c(G) = 1$ if and only if the $p$-Sylow subgroup of $G$ is cyclic, or (when $p = 2$) cyclic or generalized quaternion.
