Proposition 7.3 states that the tensor product functor is right exact: it preserves exactness at the right end of short exact sequences. This follows from the tensor-hom adjunction — a left adjoint preserves cokernels. However, the tensor product is not left exact in general: a monomorphism $\mu$ may not induce a monomorphism $1 \otimes \mu$. The classic counterexample is tensoring the injection $\mathbb{Z} \xrightarrow{2} \mathbb{Z}$ with $\mathbb{Z}_2$, which yields the zero map. This failure of left exactness motivates the definition of the Tor functor as the left derived functor of the tensor product.
