Lemma 5.4 provides a technical construction that lifts a short exact sequence of modules to compatible projective presentations. Given projective presentations of the outer terms $A'$ and $A''$, one can build a projective presentation of the middle term $A$ so that the resulting diagram has exact rows and commutes. The proof constructs $P = P' \oplus P''$ and uses the projectivity of $P''$ to lift $\varepsilon''$ through the epimorphism $\psi$. This lemma is essential for the proof of Theorem 5.3 and is reused in Chapter IV for the two long exact sequences of derived functors.
