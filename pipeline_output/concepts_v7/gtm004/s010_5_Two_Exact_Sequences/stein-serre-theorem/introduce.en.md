Theorem 6.1, due to Stein and Serre, is a partial solution to the Whitehead problem for countable rank abelian groups. For finitely generated groups, the equivalence $\operatorname{Ext}(A, \mathbb{Z}) = 0 \iff A$ free follows easily from the structure theorem. The theorem extends this to groups of countable rank, using Lemma 6.2 to reduce to the finitely generated case. For arbitrary abelian groups, it remains an open question whether $\operatorname{Ext}(A, \mathbb{Z}) = 0$ implies freeness (the Whitehead problem, shown independent of ZFC by Shelah).
