Proposition 7.1 formalizes the tensor product as a bifunctor. Fixing one argument yields a covariant functor from modules to abelian groups, and the tensor product is functorial in both arguments simultaneously. For a homomorphism $\alpha : A \to A'$ of right modules, $\alpha \otimes 1_B : A \otimes_\Lambda B \to A' \otimes_\Lambda B$ sends $a \otimes b$ to $\alpha(a) \otimes b$; similarly for maps in the left argument. This proposition sets up the homological study of Tor as the left derived functor of the tensor product.
