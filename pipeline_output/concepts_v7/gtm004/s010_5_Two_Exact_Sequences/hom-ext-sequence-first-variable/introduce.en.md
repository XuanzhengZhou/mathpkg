Theorem 5.3 is the dual counterpart to Theorem 5.2, establishing a six-term exact sequence linking Hom and Ext when the first argument varies. For a short exact sequence $A' \to A \to A''$, the connecting homomorphism goes from $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(A', B)$ to $\operatorname{Ext}_\Lambda(A'', B)$. The proof uses Lemma 5.4 to construct compatible projective presentations and then applies Lemma 5.1. Together with Theorem 5.2, these two exact sequences are the foundational tools for computing Ext groups via long exact sequences.
