A flat module is defined as a module for which the tensor product functor preserves monomorphisms. Since the tensor product is always right exact, flatness is equivalent to the tensor product being exact (i.e., preserving short exact sequences). Flatness is a weaker condition than projectivity: every projective module is flat (Proposition 7.4), but the converse fails in general. Flat modules are central to homological algebra and algebraic geometry, where flatness is a key notion for families and descent.
