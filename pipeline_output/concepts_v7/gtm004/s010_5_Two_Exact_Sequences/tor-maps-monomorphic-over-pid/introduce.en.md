Corollary 8.4 is the Tor analog of Corollary 5.7. Over a principal ideal domain, submodules of projectives are projective, which forces the induced maps between Tor groups in the exact sequences to be monomorphisms. This provides a useful computational tool: for modules over a PID, the Tor exact sequence gives an injection rather than just exactness, which can be used to compute Tor groups efficiently. As with the Ext case, this result fails for general rings.
