This proposition establishes the naturality of the long exact sequence arising from a short exact sequence of functors (Theorem 6.3) in two independent senses: with respect to morphisms between exact sequences of functors, and with respect to homomorphisms of the module argument. A commutative diagram of functors whose rows are exact on projectives induces a commutative ladder between the corresponding derived functor long exact sequences. Similarly, a module homomorphism $\alpha: A \rightarrow A'$ induces a morphism of long exact sequences via the induced maps on derived functors.
