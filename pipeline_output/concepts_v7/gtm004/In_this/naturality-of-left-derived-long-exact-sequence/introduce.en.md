This proposition establishes the naturality of the long exact sequence of left derived functors in two independent senses. First, a natural transformation $\tau: T \rightarrow T'$ between additive functors induces a commutative ladder between the corresponding long exact sequences. Second, a morphism between short exact sequences of objects induces another commutative ladder relating the derived functor sequences. Together these show that the connecting homomorphisms $\omega_n$ are natural with respect to both arguments.
