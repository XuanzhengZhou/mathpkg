Let $\overline{\operatorname{Ext}}_{\Lambda}^{v}(A, B)$ be defined via an injective presentation $B \rightarrowtail I \twoheadrightarrow S$ of $B$, and let $\operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}^{e}(A, B)$ be defined via a projective presentation $R \rightarrowtail P \twoheadrightarrow A$ of $A$. Then there is a natural isomorphism
$$\overline{\operatorname{Ext}}_{\Lambda}^{v}(A, B) \;\cong\; \operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}^{e}(A, B).$$
Consequently, the two definitions yield the same bifunctor $\operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}(-, -)$, and the subscript/superscript decorations may be dropped.
