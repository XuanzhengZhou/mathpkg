Theorem 2.4 is the central result of Section III.2, establishing that the Yoneda extension functor $E(-, -)$ is naturally equivalent to the homologically-defined $\operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}(-, -)$. The proof constructs explicit maps $\eta : E(A, B) \to \operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}(A, B)$ and $\xi : \operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}(A, B) \to E(A, B)$ using push-outs and pull-backs, and shows they are inverse natural isomorphisms. This equivalence means that the set of equivalence classes of module extensions carries the same information as the Ext group, bridging geometric and algebraic definitions of Ext.
