Given a projective presentation $R \to P \to A$ of a $\Lambda$-module $A$, the Ext group $\operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}^{\varepsilon}(A, B)$ is defined as the cokernel of the induced map $\mu^* : \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(P, B) \to \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(R, B)$. Intuitively, elements of $\operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(R, B)$ represent "relations" that must be satisfied by a homomorphism from $P$ to $B$, and quotienting by those coming from $P$ yields the obstruction group $\operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}^{\varepsilon}(A, B)$. The superscript $\varepsilon$ indicates that this construction a priori depends on the chosen projective presentation; a key result shows that different presentations yield naturally isomorphic groups.
