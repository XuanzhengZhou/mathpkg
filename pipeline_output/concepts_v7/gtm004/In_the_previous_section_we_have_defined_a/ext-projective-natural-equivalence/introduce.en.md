Although the definition of $\operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}^{\varepsilon}(A, B)$ a priori depends on a chosen projective presentation $R \to P \to A$, Lemma 2.1 implies that the identity map on $A$ induces natural isomorphisms between the groups obtained from different presentations. Corollary 2.2 formalizes this: all such constructions yield naturally equivalent abelian groups, so the dependence on the presentation is inessential. This permits the simplification to the notation $\operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}(A, B)$.
