An essential extension $B$ of a module $A$ is one in which $A$ sits as a submodule that intersects every nonzero submodule of $B$ nontrivially. This concept captures the idea that $A$ is "large" or "dense" inside $B$: one cannot find a nonzero submodule of $B$ that avoids $A$ entirely. Essential extensions are transitive, and they form an inductive system under inclusion, which guarantees the existence of maximal essential extensions and ultimately leads to the construction of the injective envelope.
