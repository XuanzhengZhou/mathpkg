Proposition 9.1 gives a useful elementwise reformulation of the essential extension condition: $B$ is an essential extension of $A$ precisely when every nonzero element $b \in B$ can be scaled by some ring element $\lambda \in \Lambda$ to land nontrivially in $A$. The forward direction follows by considering the cyclic submodule generated by $b$, while the converse shows that any nonzero submodule $H$ must contain an element that, after appropriate scaling, belongs to $A \cap H$, forcing the intersection to be nontrivial.
