Given a right $\Lambda$-module $A$ and an abelian group $G$, the abelian group $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, G)$ can be endowed with a left $\Lambda$-module structure by defining the action of $\lambda \in \Lambda$ on a homomorphism $\varphi$ via precomposition with right multiplication by $\lambda$ on $A$. This construction is fundamental to the theory of cofree modules, as it provides the mechanism for converting abelian group data into module-theoretic data. The dual construction for left $\Lambda$-modules yields right $\Lambda$-module structures, exhibiting a contravariant relationship between the module structures on $A$ and on the Hom group.
