A cofree module is the direct product of copies of the character module $\Lambda^{*} = \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\Lambda, \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z})$, where the left $\Lambda$-module structure on $\Lambda^{*}$ comes from the right $\Lambda$-module structure on $\Lambda$. This definition parallels the notion of a free module but serves the dual role in the context of injective modules: just as every module is a quotient of a free module, every module can be embedded into a cofree module. The terminology is justified by the fact that for $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}$, one recovers the description of cofree abelian groups as direct products of $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$.
