Let $A$ be a left $\Lambda$-module and let $G$ be an abelian group. Regard $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\Lambda, G)$ as a left $\Lambda$-module via the right $\Lambda$-module structure of $\Lambda$. Then there is a natural isomorphism of abelian groups
$$\eta_A : \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}\bigl(A, \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\Lambda, G)\bigr) \xrightarrow{\cong} \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, G),$$
given by $\eta_A(\varphi)(a) = \varphi(a)(1)$. Moreover, for every $\Lambda$-module homomorphism $\alpha : A \to B$ the following diagram commutes:
\[
\begin{CD}
\operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}\bigl(B, \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\Lambda, G)\bigr) @>{\eta_B}>{\cong}> \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(B, G) \\
@V{\alpha^{*}}VV @VV{\alpha^{*}}V \\
\operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}\bigl(A, \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\Lambda, G)\bigr) @>{\eta_A}>{\cong}> \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, G)
\end{CD}
\]
