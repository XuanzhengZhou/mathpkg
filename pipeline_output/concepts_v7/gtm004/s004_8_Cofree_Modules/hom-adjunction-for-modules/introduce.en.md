Proposition 8.1 establishes a natural isomorphism $\operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\Lambda, G)) \cong \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, G)$ for any left $\Lambda$-module $A$ and abelian group $G$. This is the module-theoretic analogue of the tensor-hom adjunction, expressing that the functor $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\Lambda, -)$ is right adjoint to the forgetful functor from $\Lambda$-modules to abelian groups. The naturality condition ensures that the isomorphism is functorial in $A$, making it a key technical tool for transferring properties between abelian groups and $\Lambda$-modules.
