Corollary 9.3 asserts that the maximal essential extension constructed in Theorem 9.2 is unique up to isomorphism: any two maximal essential extensions of $A$ contained in injective modules are isomorphic. The proof uses the injectivity of $E_2$ to extend the embedding $A \hookrightarrow E_2$ to a map $\xi : E_1 \to E_2$, which is shown to be an essential monomorphism; maximality then forces $\xi$ to be an isomorphism. This uniqueness justifies the terminology "the" injective envelope of $A$.
