The injective envelope $E(A)$ of a module $A$ is its unique maximal essential extension that is also injective. It is the smallest injective module containing $A$ as a submodule, in the sense that any injective module containing $A$ contains a copy of $E(A)$. The injective envelope plays a role dual to that of the projective cover, providing a canonical minimal injective extension. Its existence for arbitrary modules over any ring is a fundamental result in homological algebra, relying on Baer's criterion and the fact that every module embeds into an injective module.
