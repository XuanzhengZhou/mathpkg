Theorem 8.2 states that for any divisible abelian group $G$, the $\Lambda$-module $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\Lambda, G)$ is injective. The proof uses the adjunction of Proposition 8.1 to transfer the injectivity property from the abelian group $G$ (divisible groups are precisely the injective $\mathbb{Z}$-modules) to the $\Lambda$-module setting. A particularly important special case is $G = \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$, showing that $\Lambda^{*}$ is always an injective $\Lambda$-module, which is the foundation for the cofree module construction.
