The first long exact sequence of derived functors is a fundamental tool in homological algebra that relates the left derived functors $L_n T$ of an additive functor $T$ to short exact sequences in the source category. Given a short exact sequence $A' \rightarrowtail A \twoheadrightarrow A''$, this theorem produces connecting homomorphisms $\omega_n: L_n T A'' \rightarrow L_{n-1} T A'$ that link the derived functors into an infinite exact sequence. The construction proceeds by lifting the short exact sequence to a short exact sequence of projective resolutions and then applying the long exact homology sequence. This result is the homological analogue of the snake lemma and underpins virtually all computations with derived functors.
