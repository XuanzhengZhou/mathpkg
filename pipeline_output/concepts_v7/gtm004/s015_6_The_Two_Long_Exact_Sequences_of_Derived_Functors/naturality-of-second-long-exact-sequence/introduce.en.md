This proposition establishes the naturality of the second long exact sequence of derived functors in both the object argument and the functor argument. A morphism $\alpha: A \rightarrow A'$ induces a commutative ladder between the long exact sequences for $A$ and $A'$ with respect to a fixed sequence of functors $T' \rightarrow T \rightarrow T''$. Similarly, a morphism between short exact sequences of functors (a commutative diagram of natural transformations) induces a commutative ladder between the corresponding long exact sequences evaluated at the same object $A$. This double naturality ensures that the second long exact sequence is a functorial construction and is compatible with all reasonable transformations of its input data.
