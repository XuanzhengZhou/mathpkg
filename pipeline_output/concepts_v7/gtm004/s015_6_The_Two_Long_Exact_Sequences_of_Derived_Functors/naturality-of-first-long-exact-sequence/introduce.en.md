This proposition establishes the naturality of the first long exact sequence of derived functors in both arguments: with respect to natural transformations of the functor $T$ and with respect to morphisms of short exact sequences. Given a natural transformation $\tau: T \rightarrow T'$, the induced maps on derived functors $\tau_A: L_n T A \rightarrow L_n T' A$ commute with all maps in the long exact sequence, including the connecting homomorphisms. Similarly, a morphism between short exact sequences induces a ladder of commutative squares between the corresponding long exact sequences. This naturality is essential for functorial constructions and for comparing derived functors arising from different resolutions.
