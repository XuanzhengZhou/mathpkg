The second long exact sequence of derived functors is the counterpart to Theorem 6.1: here the object $A$ is kept fixed while the functor varies. Given a short exact sequence of additive functors $T' \rightarrow T \rightarrow T''$ that is exact on projective objects, one obtains a long exact sequence relating the left derived functors $L_n T'$, $L_n T$, and $L_n T''$ evaluated at the same object $A$. The construction evaluates the functor sequence on a projective resolution of $A$, yielding a short exact sequence of chain complexes, and then applies the long exact homology sequence. Together with Theorem 6.1, this result provides the two fundamental exact sequences for manipulating and computing derived functors.
