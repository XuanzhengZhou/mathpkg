Let $G$ be a finite group, $U$ a subgroup of $G$, and $k$ a field. A $kG$-module $M$ is called \textbf{projective relative to $U$} if and only if every short exact sequence of $kG$-modules
$$0 \to A \to B \to M \to 0$$
whose restriction to $kU$
$$0 \to A_U \to B_U \to M_U \to 0$$
splits (as a sequence of $kU$-modules), also splits over $kG$.
