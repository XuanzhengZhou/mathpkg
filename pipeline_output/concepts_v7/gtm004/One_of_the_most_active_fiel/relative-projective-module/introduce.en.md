Relative projectivity generalises the notion of a projective module by measuring projectivity with respect to a subgroup rather than absolutely. A module is projective relative to a subgroup $U$ if any extension that splits upon restriction to $U$ already splits over the whole group $G$. This concept is central to J.A. Green's theory of vertices and sources, and underpins the Green correspondence. Every $kG$-module is projective relative to a $p$-Sylow subgroup of $G$.
