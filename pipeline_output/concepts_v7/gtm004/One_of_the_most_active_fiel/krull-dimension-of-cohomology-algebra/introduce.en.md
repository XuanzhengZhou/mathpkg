The Krull dimension of the cohomology algebra $H^*(G, k)$ is a numerical invariant that quantifies the growth of the cohomology groups of a finite group $G$. Defined as the polynomial growth rate of the sequence $d_n = \dim_k H^n(G, k)$, it captures structural information about the $p$-local structure of $G$. The case $c(G) = 1$ corresponds to groups whose cohomology dimensions are uniformly bounded, which happens exactly when the Sylow $p$-subgroup is cyclic (or, for $p = 2$, cyclic or generalized quaternion).
