Let $G$ be a finite group and let $k$ be a field of characteristic $p$ dividing $|G|$. Let $c(G)$ be the Krull dimension of the cohomology algebra $H^*(G, k)$. Then
$$c(G) = \max \{\, c(E) \mid E \text{ is an elementary abelian } p\text{-subgroup of } G \,\}.$$
Equivalently, the Krull dimension $c(G)$ equals the $p$-rank of $G$, i.e., the maximal rank of an elementary abelian $p$-subgroup of $G$.
