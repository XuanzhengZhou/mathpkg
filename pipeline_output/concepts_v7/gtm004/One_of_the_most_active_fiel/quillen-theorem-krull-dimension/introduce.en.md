Quillen's theorem (Theorem 4.2) is a landmark result in the cohomology of finite groups, resolving a conjecture of Atiyah and Swan. It states that the Krull dimension of the cohomology algebra $H^*(G, k)$ is detected on elementary abelian $p$-subgroups, and is equal to the maximal rank of such subgroups. The theorem established a deep connection between the cohomological structure of a group and its $p$-local subgroup structure, and motivated much subsequent work on cohomological varieties and support varieties.
