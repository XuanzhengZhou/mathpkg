Principal indecomposable modules (PIMs) are the building blocks of the regular representation $kG$. They are exactly the indecomposable projective $kG$-modules and correspond bijectively to the simple $kG$-modules. In the group algebra setting, projectivity and injectivity coincide, making PIMs fundamental objects of study. Their structure is of central importance in modular representation theory, and many results are known both in general form and for specific families of finite groups.
