The cohomological variety $X_G(M)$ of a $kG$-module $M$ is a geometric object whose dimension equals the complexity $c_G(M)$. It is a subvariety of the maximal ideal spectrum $X_G$ of the even-dimensional cohomology algebra $H^{\text{ev}}(G, k)$. The theory of these varieties provides a powerful geometric perspective on modular representations, connecting commutative algebra, algebraic geometry, and the representation theory of finite groups. This approach, developed by Carlson and others, has been of great value in both cohomology theory and modular representation theory.
