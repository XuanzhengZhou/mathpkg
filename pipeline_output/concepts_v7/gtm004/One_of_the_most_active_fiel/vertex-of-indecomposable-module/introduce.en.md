The vertex of an indecomposable $kG$-module $M$ is a $p$-subgroup of $G$ that measures the "obstruction" to $M$ being projective. It is defined as a minimal subgroup relative to which $M$ is projective, and is unique up to conjugacy. Vertices were introduced by J.A. Green and form the cornerstone of his celebrated theory, leading to the Green correspondence and providing deep structural insight into modular representations of finite groups.
