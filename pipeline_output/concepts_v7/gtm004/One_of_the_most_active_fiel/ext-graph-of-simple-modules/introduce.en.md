The extension graph $\Gamma(G)$ encodes the first extension groups between simple $kG$-modules as an undirected graph. Its vertices are the isomorphism classes of simple modules, and an edge between two vertices signals a nontrivial extension. This graph provides a simple and transparent homological definition of blocks in modular representation theory, replacing the original character-theoretic approach of R. Brauer.
