Let $G$ be a finite group, $k$ a field of characteristic $p$, and $Q$ a $p$-subgroup of $G$. Let $L = N_G(Q)$ be the normalizer of $Q$ in $G$. Then there exists a bijective correspondence between:
\begin{itemize}
\item the isomorphism classes of indecomposable $kG$-modules with vertex $Q$, and
\item the isomorphism classes of indecomposable $kL$-modules with vertex $Q$.
\end{itemize}
The correspondence is given as follows:
\begin{itemize}
\item If $M$ is an indecomposable $kG$-module with vertex $Q$, its \textbf{Green correspondent} $N$ is the uniquely determined indecomposable direct summand of the restriction $M_L$ having vertex $Q$.
\item Conversely, if $N$ is an indecomposable $kL$-module with vertex $Q$, its \textbf{Green correspondent} $M$ is the uniquely determined indecomposable direct summand of the induced module $kG \otimes_{kL} N$ having vertex $Q$.
\end{itemize}
