A block in modular representation theory is an equivalence class of simple $kG$-modules determined by the connected components of the extension graph $\Gamma(G)$. This homological definition captures the same partition that R. Brauer originally introduced in the 1940s using character theory. Blocks organize the representation theory of a group into smaller, better-behaved pieces, and the block structure plays a fundamental role in the modular theory of finite groups.
