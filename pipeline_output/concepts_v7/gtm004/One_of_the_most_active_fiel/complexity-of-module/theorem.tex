Let $G$ be a finite group, $k$ a field of characteristic $p$ dividing $|G|$, and $M$ an indecomposable $kG$-module. The \textbf{complexity} $c_G(M)$ of $M$ is a numerical invariant generalizing the Krull dimension: it satisfies $c_G(k) = c(G)$, where $c(G)$ is the Krull dimension of $H^*(G, k)$. In particular:
$$c_G(M) = 0 \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad M \text{ is } kG\text{-projective}.$$
