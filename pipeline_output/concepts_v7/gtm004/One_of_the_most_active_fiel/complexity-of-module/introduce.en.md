The complexity $c_G(M)$ of a $kG$-module $M$ is a numerical invariant that generalises the Krull dimension of $H^*(G, k)$ to arbitrary modules. It measures the polynomial rate of growth of a minimal projective resolution of $M$. A module has complexity zero precisely when it is projective, making complexity a natural tool for subdividing the class of indecomposable modules. The complexity classes provide a powerful organising principle in modular representation theory.
