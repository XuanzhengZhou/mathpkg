The growth of a sequence of integers measures its polynomial rate of increase. A growth of $c$ means the sequence is bounded above by a constant times $n^{c-1}$. This notion is used in modular representation theory to define the Krull dimension of the cohomology algebra $H^*(G, k)$: the growth of the sequence of dimensions $d_n = \dim_k H^n(G, k)$ yields a numerical invariant that captures the complexity of the cohomology ring.
