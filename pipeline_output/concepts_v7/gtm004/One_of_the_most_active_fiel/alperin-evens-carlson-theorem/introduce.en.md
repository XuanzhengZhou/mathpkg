The Alperin-Evens-Carlson theorem (Theorem 4.3) extends Quillen's result from the trivial module to arbitrary indecomposable $kG$-modules. It states that the complexity of a module is the maximum of the complexities of its restrictions to elementary abelian $p$-subgroups. This result is a cornerstone of the theory of support varieties and cohomological methods in modular representation theory, and it generalises the idea that cohomological information is controlled by elementary abelian subgroups.
