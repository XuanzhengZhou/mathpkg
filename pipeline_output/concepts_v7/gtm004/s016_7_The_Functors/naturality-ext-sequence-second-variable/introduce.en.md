Proposition 7.6 establishes the naturality of the long exact Ext-sequence in the second variable with respect to morphisms in both arguments simultaneously. Given a homomorphism $\alpha: A \to A'$ in the first variable and a commutative diagram of short exact sequences in the second variable, the induced maps between the corresponding long exact Ext-sequences form a commutative ladder. This means that the connecting homomorphisms $\omega_n$ commute with both the pullback maps $\alpha^*$ induced by $\alpha$ and the pushforward maps $\psi_*$ induced by the vertical morphisms in the second variable. This naturality follows from the full force of Proposition 6.4 and is essential for establishing the bifunctorial nature of Ext.
