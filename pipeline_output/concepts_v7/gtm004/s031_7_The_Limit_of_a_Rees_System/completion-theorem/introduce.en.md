Theorem 8.5 establishes the fundamental properties of the completion construction. It guarantees that completing a filtered object produces a complete filtration while preserving the associated graded object. The bicartesian diagram shows that $X^\infty$ (the left completion) and $X_{-\infty}$ (the right completion) are related by canonical maps through the original object $X$. An important corollary is that the operations of left completion and right completion commute: $(X^\infty)_{-\infty} = (X_{-\infty})^\infty$.
