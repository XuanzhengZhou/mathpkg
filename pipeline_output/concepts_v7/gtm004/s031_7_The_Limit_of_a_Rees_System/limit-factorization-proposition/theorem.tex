\textbf{Proposition 8.2.} Suppose that for all $p$, the inclusion $\nu^p : X^p \to X$ factorizes as $X^p \xrightarrow{\nu^p} Y \xrightarrow{\mu} X$, where $\mu$ is independent of $p$. Set $Y_p = Y / X^p$ and let $\eta'_p : Y \to Y_p$ be the projection. Then $\xi_p : X_p \to X_{p+1}$ induces $\xi'_p : Y_p \to Y_{p+1}$, and we have the commutative diagram

$$
\begin{array}{ccccc}
X^{p-1} \xrightarrow{\xi_p} X^p & Y \xrightarrow{\eta'_p} Y_p & Y_{p+1} \\
\parallel & \downarrow{\scriptstyle \mu} & \downarrow{\scriptstyle \mu_{p+1}} \\
X^{p-1} \xrightarrow{\xi_p} X^p & X \xrightarrow{\eta_p} X_p & X_{p+1}
\end{array}
$$

If $(X; \eta_p) = \varprojlim (X_p, \xi_p)$, then $(Y; \eta'_p) = \varprojlim (Y_p, \xi'_p)$.
