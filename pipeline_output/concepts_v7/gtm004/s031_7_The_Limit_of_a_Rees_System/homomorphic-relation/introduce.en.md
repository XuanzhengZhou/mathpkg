A homomorphic relation generalizes the notion of a morphism in an abelian category. While a morphism $f: X \to Y$ corresponds to its graph as a subobject of $X \oplus Y$, a general homomorphic relation allows for multi-valued or partially defined correspondences. A homomorphic relation can only be an isomorphism if it is actually a morphism, a fact used in Corollary 7.4 to characterize when $\Gamma^+ \cong E_\infty$.
