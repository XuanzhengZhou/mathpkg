Let $C = \{C_n, \partial_n\}$ be a chain complex over $\Lambda$. The condition $\partial \partial = 0$ implies that $\operatorname{im} \partial_{n+1} \subseteq \ker \partial_n$ for all $n \in \mathbb{Z}$. Define the $n$-th homology module of $C$ by
$$
H_n(C) = \ker \partial_n / \operatorname{im} \partial_{n+1}, \quad n \in \mathbb{Z}.
$$
The graded module $H(C) = \{H_n(C)\}$ is called the \textit{homology module} of $C$.
