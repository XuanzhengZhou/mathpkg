Let $A, B$ be $\Lambda$-modules and let $R \xrightarrow{\mu} P \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} B$ be a projective presentation of $B$. Form the chain complex $D$ by
$$
\cdots \longrightarrow 0 \longrightarrow P \otimes_\Lambda A \xrightarrow{\mu \otimes 1} R \otimes_\Lambda A \longrightarrow 0 \longrightarrow \cdots
$$
with $D_1 = P \otimes_\Lambda A$, $D_0 = R \otimes_\Lambda A$, and $D_n = 0$ for $n \neq 0, 1$. Then
$$
H_0(D) \cong B \otimes_\Lambda A, \qquad
H_1(D) \cong \operatorname{Tor}_1^\Lambda(B, A), \qquad
H_n(D) = 0 \ \text{for}\ n \neq 0, 1.
$$

More generally, if $B \to I^\bullet$ is an injective presentation of $B$ and one forms a cochain complex using $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(A, -)$, the cohomology yields $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(A, B)$ and $\operatorname{Ext}_\Lambda^1(A, B)$ as in Example (b). Analogous results hold starting with a projective (or injective) presentation of $B$ and forming a chain complex with $-\otimes_\Lambda A$, yielding $B \otimes_\Lambda A$ and $\operatorname{Tor}_1^\Lambda(B, A)$ as in Examples (c) and (d).
