Taking the obvious free presentation $I\mathfrak{g} \mapsto U\mathfrak{g} \twoheadrightarrow K$ and given a $\mathfrak{g}$-module $A$, one obtains
$$H^1(\mathfrak{g}, A) = \text{coker}\bigl(\text{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(U\mathfrak{g}, A) \rightarrow \text{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(I\mathfrak{g}, A)\bigr).$$
Hence $H^1(\mathfrak{g}, A)$ is isomorphic to the vector space of derivations from $\mathfrak{g}$ into $A$ modulo those derivations that arise from $\mathfrak{g}$-module homomorphisms $f: U\mathfrak{g} \rightarrow A$ (the inner derivations).
