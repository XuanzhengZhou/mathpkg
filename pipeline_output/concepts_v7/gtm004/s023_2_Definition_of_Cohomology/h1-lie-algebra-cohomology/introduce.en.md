The first cohomology group $H^1(\mathfrak{g}, A)$ classifies derivations of $\mathfrak{g}$ into $A$ modulo inner derivations. Using the free presentation $I\mathfrak{g} \to U\mathfrak{g} \to K$, one obtains an explicit description as the cokernel of $\text{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(U\mathfrak{g}, A) \to \text{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(I\mathfrak{g}, A)$. This interpretation generalizes the familiar fact from group cohomology that $H^1$ classifies crossed homomorphisms modulo principal crossed homomorphisms.
