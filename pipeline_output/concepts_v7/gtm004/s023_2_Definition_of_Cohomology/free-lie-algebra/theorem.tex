Given a $K$-vector space $V$, the free $K$-Lie algebra $\mathfrak{f} = \mathfrak{f}(V)$ on $V$ is a Lie algebra over $K$ containing $V$ as a subspace, such that the following universal property holds: for any $K$-linear map $f : V \to \mathfrak{g}$ where $\mathfrak{g}$ is a Lie algebra over $K$, there exists a unique Lie algebra homomorphism $\tilde{f} : \mathfrak{f}(V) \to \mathfrak{g}$ extending $f$.
