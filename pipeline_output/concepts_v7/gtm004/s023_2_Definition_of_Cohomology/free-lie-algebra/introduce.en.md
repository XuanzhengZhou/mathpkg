The free Lie algebra $\mathfrak{f}(V)$ on a $K$-vector space $V$ is the Lie-algebraic analogue of the free group or free associative algebra. It satisfies the universal property that any linear map from $V$ into a Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ extends uniquely to a Lie algebra homomorphism from $\mathfrak{f}(V)$ to $\mathfrak{g}$. This construction is essential for understanding the cohomological dimension of Lie algebras, as free Lie algebras have vanishing cohomology in degrees $n \geq 2$.
