The homology groups $H_n(\mathfrak{g}, B)$ of a Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ with coefficients in a right $\mathfrak{g}$-module $B$ are defined via the Tor functor: $H_n(\mathfrak{g}, B) = \text{Tor}_n^{\mathfrak{g}}(B, K)$. This definition parallels the cohomological one and provides explicit formulas in low degrees: $H_0$ is the module of coinvariants $B/B\mathfrak{g}$, and $H_1$ is the kernel of the canonical map $B \otimes_{\mathfrak{g}} I\mathfrak{g} \to B \otimes_{\mathfrak{g}} U\mathfrak{g}$. For a trivial module $B$, $H_1$ reduces to $B \otimes_K \mathfrak{g}_{\text{ab}}$.
