When a Lie algebra $\mathfrak{h}$ is extended by an abelian Lie algebra $A$, the extension data endows $A$ with a natural $\mathfrak{h}$-module structure. Choosing any $K$-linear section $s : \mathfrak{h} \to \mathfrak{g}$ with $p \circ s = 1_{\mathfrak{h}}$, the action is given by the bracket $x \circ a = [s(x), i(a)]$. Crucially, because $A$ is abelian, this action does not depend on the choice of section. This construction is the Lie-algebraic counterpart of the conjugation action in group extensions.
