Let $A \xrightarrow{i} \mathfrak{g} \xrightarrow{p} \mathfrak{h}$ be an extension of Lie algebras over $K$ with abelian kernel $A$. Given a section $s : \mathfrak{h} \to \mathfrak{g}$ (a $K$-linear map with $p \circ s = 1_{\mathfrak{h}}$), define an $\mathfrak{h}$-module structure on $A$ by
$$x \circ a = [s(x), i(a)]_{\mathfrak{g}}, \quad a \in A,\; x \in \mathfrak{h}.$$
This action is independent of the choice of section $s$ and is called the $\mathfrak{h}$-module structure induced by the extension.
