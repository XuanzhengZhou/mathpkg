Corollary 2.6 asserts that a free Lie algebra $\mathfrak{f}$ has cohomological dimension at most $1$: all cohomology groups $H^n(\mathfrak{f}, A)$ vanish for $n \geq 2$ and for any $\mathfrak{f}$-module $A$. This is the Lie algebra analogue of the group-cohomological fact that free groups have trivial cohomology in dimensions $\geq 2$ (Corollary VI.5.6). The result follows from the universal property of the free Lie algebra and the corresponding statement for the universal enveloping algebra.
