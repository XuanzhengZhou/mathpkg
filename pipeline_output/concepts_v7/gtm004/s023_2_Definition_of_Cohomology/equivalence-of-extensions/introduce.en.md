Two extensions of $\mathfrak{h}$ by the $\mathfrak{h}$-module $A$ are equivalent if there is a Lie algebra homomorphism $f : \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{g}'$ making the diagram commute (with identity maps on $A$ and $\mathfrak{h}$). By the five lemma, such an $f$ is automatically an isomorphism. The set $M(\mathfrak{h}, A)$ of equivalence classes forms the natural domain for the classification theorem relating $H^2(\mathfrak{h}, A)$ to extension classes, exactly as in group cohomology.
