An extension of a Lie algebra $\mathfrak{h}$ by an $\mathfrak{h}$-module $A$ refines the notion of a Lie algebra extension by requiring compatibility between the preassigned module structure on $A$ and the module structure induced by the extension itself. This compatibility condition ensures that $A$ is not merely an abelian ideal but carries the correct $\mathfrak{h}$-action prescribed in advance. The split extension $A \rtimes \mathfrak{h}$ always provides at least one such extension.
