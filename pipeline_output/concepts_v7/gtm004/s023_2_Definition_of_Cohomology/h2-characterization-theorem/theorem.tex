There is a one-to-one correspondence between the second cohomology group $H^2(\mathfrak{h}, A)$ and the set $M(\mathfrak{h}, A)$ of equivalence classes of extensions of the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{h}$ by the $\mathfrak{h}$-module $A$. Under this correspondence, the zero element of $H^2(\mathfrak{h}, A)$ corresponds to the equivalence class of the split extension $A \rtimes \mathfrak{h}$.
