The cohomology groups $H^n(\mathfrak{g}, A)$ of a Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ with coefficients in a $\mathfrak{g}$-module $A$ are defined as the Ext groups $\text{Ext}_{\mathfrak{g}}^n(K, A)$, where $K$ is the trivial $\mathfrak{g}$-module. This definition parallels the group cohomology construction and provides a unified framework for studying Lie algebra extensions and derivations. Since each $H^n(\mathfrak{g}, A)$ is naturally a $K$-vector space, the cohomology theory inherits the rich structure of homological algebra over the universal enveloping algebra $U\mathfrak{g}$.
