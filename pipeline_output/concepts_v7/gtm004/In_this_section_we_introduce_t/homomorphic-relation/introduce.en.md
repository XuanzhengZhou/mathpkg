A homomorphic relation is a generalization of the notion of a morphism in an abelian category. While a morphism $f: X \to Y$ has a graph which is a subobject of $X \oplus Y$, a homomorphic relation may fail to be the graph of any single morphism. The concept is used to compare the limit term $E_\infty$ of a spectral sequence with the target $\Gamma^+$: the relation $\Theta$ from $E_\infty$ to $\Gamma^+$ emerges naturally from the limit diagram of a Rees system, and the question of when it is an actual isomorphism is central to convergence theory.
