Completeness of a filtration is the key condition that allows one to recover a filtered object from its associated graded object. The definition is given in any abelian category using categorical limits and colimits. Left completeness means the filtration exhausts the object from below; right completeness means the object is the projective limit of its quotients. In module categories, a finite filtration is always complete. The concept is introduced to provide hypotheses weaker than homological finiteness under which a morphism inducing an isomorphism on associated graded objects is itself an isomorphism (Theorem 8.4).
