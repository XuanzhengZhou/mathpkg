Proposition 8.1 asserts the naturality of spectral sequences with respect to morphisms. Once a morphism is defined at the $E_k$ page, it propagates uniquely to all later pages, including the $E_\infty$ term. This is an immediate consequence of the definition of the spectral sequence terms $E_n$ as successive homology groups of the $E_k$ page. The result is fundamental because it allows one to compare spectral sequences by checking compatibility at a single finite stage, and then automatically obtaining a morphism at $E_\infty$.
