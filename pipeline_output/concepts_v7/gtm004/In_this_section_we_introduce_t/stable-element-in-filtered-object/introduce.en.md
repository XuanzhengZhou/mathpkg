A stable element in the homology of a filtered differential group is one that survives in all coarser filtrations. That is, its image under the map induced by the inclusion $C^{(p)} \hookrightarrow C^{(r)}$ remains non-zero for all $r \geq p$. Dually, for quotient objects $C/C^{(p)}$, an element is stable if its projection to $H_q(C/C^{(r)})$ remains non-zero for all $r \geq p$. The interplay between elements of filtration $-\infty$ and stable elements provides the concrete meaning of the algebraic conditions (7.8) that guarantee convergence of the spectral sequence.
