Theorem 8.4 is the main result of Section 8. It addresses the problem of inferring an isomorphism $H(C) \cong H(C')$ from an isomorphism $E_\infty \cong E'_\infty$ at the level of spectral sequences. The theorem states that if a morphism of filtered objects induces an isomorphism on the associated graded, then the morphism itself is an isomorphism, provided the filtrations are complete. The proof proceeds by induction on $p - q$ to show that $\psi$ induces an isomorphism on all intermediate subquotients $X_q^p$, and then applies the completeness hypotheses (via Proposition 8.3) to pass to the limits. This generalizes the homological finiteness condition.
