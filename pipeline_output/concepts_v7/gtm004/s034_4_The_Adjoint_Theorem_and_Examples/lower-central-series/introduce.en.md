The lower central series of a group $G$ is a descending chain of normal subgroups defined by iterated commutators: $\gamma^1(G) = G$ and $\gamma^{i+1}(G) = [G, \gamma^i(G)]$. Each successive quotient $\gamma^i(G)/\gamma^{i+1}(G)$ is central in $G/\gamma^{i+1}(G)$ and therefore abelian, so that the lower central series measures how far $G$ is from being abelian. This series is the fundamental tool for defining and studying nilpotent groups, which are precisely those groups for which the series terminates at the trivial subgroup.
