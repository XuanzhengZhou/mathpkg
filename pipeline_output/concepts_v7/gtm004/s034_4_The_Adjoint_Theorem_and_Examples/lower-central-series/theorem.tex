For a group $G$, the \text{lower central series} $\{\gamma^i(G)\}_{i \geq 1}$ is defined inductively by
$$
\gamma^1(G) = G, \quad \gamma^{i+1}(G) = [G, \gamma^i(G)], \quad i \geq 1,
$$
where for two subgroups $U, V$ of $G$ the symbol $[U, V]$ denotes the subgroup of $G$ generated by all commutators $u^{-1}v^{-1}uv$ with $u \in U$ and $v \in V$. Each $\gamma^i(G)$ is a normal subgroup of $G$, and the quotient $\gamma^i(G)/\gamma^{i+1}(G)$ is a central, hence abelian, subgroup of $G/\gamma^{i+1}(G)$.
