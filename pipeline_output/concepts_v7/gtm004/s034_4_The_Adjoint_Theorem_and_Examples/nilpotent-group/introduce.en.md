A group $G$ is nilpotent of class at most $c$ if its lower central series terminates after $c$ steps, i.e., $\gamma^{c+1}(G) = 1$. The nilpotency class is the minimal such $c$. This means a nilpotent group can be built from an abelian group by finitely many successive central extensions, making it amenable to homological analysis. Nilpotent groups generalize abelian groups and play a central role in the homological study of group theory, particularly in localization theory and the computation of homology groups.
