When the functor $J$ along which we take a Kan extension is a full embedding, the left Kan extension simplifies dramatically: its restriction back along $J$ recovers the original functor, so $\tilde{J}T \circ J \cong T$. This is proved by observing that the comma category $\mathfrak{J}_{JU}$ has a terminal object $(U, 1_{JU})$, making the colimit computation trivial. This proposition is a special case of the general principle that colimits are invariant under cofinal functors.
