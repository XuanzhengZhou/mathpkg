This theorem provides a Grothendieck-type spectral sequence relating the $J$-homology, $I$-homology, and $IJ$-homology for a composable pair of functors between small categories. The key step in the proof is showing that the Kan extension $\tilde{J}$ sends projectives in the functor category $[\mathfrak{U}, \mathfrak{A}]$ to $\tilde{I}$-acyclic objects in $[\mathfrak{V}, \mathfrak{A}]$. Applied to the case where $\mathfrak{U} = G$ is a group, $\mathfrak{V} = Q$ is a quotient group, and $\mathfrak{W} = \mathbf{1}$, this yields the Lyndon--Hochschild--Serre spectral sequence for group homology.
