This theorem establishes the existence of $J$-homology as the left derived functors of the Kan extension $\tilde{J}$, provided the target category $\mathfrak{A}$ has enough projectives. The key insight is that Theorem 4.1 (the Adjoint Theorem) can be applied to transfer the projective class of all epimorphisms from the functor category $[\mathfrak{B}_d, \mathfrak{A}]$ to $[\mathfrak{B}, \mathfrak{A}]$. The resulting $\mathcal{E}_1'$-projective functors have an explicit coproduct description, and the $J$-homology $H_n(J, T)$ is computed by taking an $\mathcal{E}_1'$-projective resolution of $T$ and applying $\tilde{J}$.
