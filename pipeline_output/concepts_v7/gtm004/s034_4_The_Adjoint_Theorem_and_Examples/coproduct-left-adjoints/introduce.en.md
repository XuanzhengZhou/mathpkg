This elementary lemma states that a family of adjoint pairs $(F_i \dashv G_i)$ can be combined into a single adjoint pair between product categories and $\mathfrak{D}$, using coproducts. The left adjoint of the product functor $G = \{G_i\}$ sends a family of objects $\{C_i\}$ to the coproduct of their individual images $\coprod_i F_i C_i$. The proof follows from the universal property of coproducts and the individual adjunctions, and this lemma is used to compute Kan extensions along inclusions of discrete categories.
