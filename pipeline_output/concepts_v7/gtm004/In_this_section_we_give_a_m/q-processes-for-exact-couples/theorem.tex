Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an abelian category. Given a diagram

$$A \xrightarrow{\beta} B \xrightarrow{\gamma} C$$

in $\mathfrak{A}$ and a monomorphism $\varrho: C_1 \rightarrowtail C$ such that $\gamma\beta$ factors through $\varrho$, the \textbf{$Q^{\varrho}$-process} produces $(\beta', \gamma') = Q^{\varrho}(\beta, \gamma)$ via the pullback

$$\begin{array}{ccc}
B' & \xrightarrow{\gamma'} & C_1 \\
\varrho' \downarrow & \text{(PB)} & \downarrow \varrho \\
B & \xrightarrow{\gamma} & C
\end{array}$$

where $B' = \gamma^{-1}(C_1)$, so that $\varrho'\beta' = \beta$ and $\gamma'\beta'$ factors through any epimorphism through which $\gamma\beta$ factors.

Dually, given an epimorphism $\sigma: A \twoheadrightarrow A_1$ such that $\gamma\beta$ factors through $\sigma$, the \textbf{$Q_{\sigma}$-process} produces $(\beta_1, \gamma_1) = Q_{\sigma}(\beta, \gamma)$ via the pushout

$$\begin{array}{ccc}
A & \xrightarrow{\beta} & B \\
\sigma \downarrow & \text{(PO)} & \downarrow \sigma_{0,1} \\
A_1 & \xrightarrow{\beta_1} & B_1
\end{array}$$

where $B_1 = B / \beta\sigma^{-1}(0)$, so that $\gamma_1\sigma_{0,1} = \gamma$.
