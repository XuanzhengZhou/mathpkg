Theorem 4.1 states that the two Q-processes commute: applying the epimorphism process $Q_{\sigma}$ followed by the monomorphism process $Q^{\varrho}$ yields the same result as applying them in the reverse order. The common resulting object is $B_1 = \gamma^{-1}(C_1) / \beta\sigma^{-1}(0)$, obtained by first cutting down to the subobject $\gamma^{-1}(C_1)$ and then factoring out $\beta\sigma^{-1}(0)$ (or vice versa). This commutativity is essential for the unambiguous definition of iterated Q-processes in the ladder construction.
