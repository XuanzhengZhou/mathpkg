In the (bi)graded case, the ladder construction is refined to ensure that all vertical morphisms are degree-preserving. Instead of splitting $\alpha$ as a simple epi-mono factorization, one introduces an intermediate isomorphism $\omega$ that carries the degree of $\alpha$, giving $\alpha = \varrho\omega\sigma$. The resulting graded ladder replaces $D_n$ with the pair $(D'_m, D''_n)$ and adjusts the structure maps $\beta_n, \gamma_n$ by composing with $\omega_n^{-1}$ to maintain the correct grading conventions.
