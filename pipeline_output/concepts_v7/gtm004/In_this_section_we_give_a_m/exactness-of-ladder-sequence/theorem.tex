In the ladder of an exact couple, for each $m, n$ the sequence

$$E_{m,n} \xrightarrow{\gamma_{m,n}} D_n \xrightarrow{\alpha_n} D_n \xrightarrow{\beta_{m,n}} E_{m+1,n}$$

is exact. Moreover,

$$E_{m,n} = \gamma^{-1}(D_n) / \beta\eta_m^{-1}(0) = \gamma^{-1}(\alpha^n D) / \beta\alpha^{-m}(0).$$

In particular, when $m = n$, this yields $E_{n,n} = E_n$ and the sequence (4.17) for $m=n$ is precisely the $n^{\text{th}}$ derived couple.
