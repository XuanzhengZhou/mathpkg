Proposition 4.2 states that the square formed by the intermediate objects $B_{0,1}$ and $B_{1,0}$ obtained from the two possible orders of applying the Q-processes is bicartesian — both a pullback and a pushout. Writing $B_{0,1} = \gamma^{-1}(C_1)$ and $B_{1,0} = B/\beta\sigma^{-1}(0)$, the square relates these subobject and quotient constructions in the natural way, with the common object $B_1$ in the corner.
