Just as ordinary derived functors require projective resolutions, relative derived functors require $\mathcal{E}$-projective resolutions. An $\mathcal{E}$-projective complex consists of $\mathcal{E}$-projective objects, and $\mathcal{E}$-acyclicity ensures that the complex is exact in the relative sense with respect to the chosen projective class. These notions allow the construction of $\mathcal{E}$-derived functors $L_n^{\mathcal{E}} T$ that parallel the classical construction but are tailored to the projective class $\mathcal{E}$.
