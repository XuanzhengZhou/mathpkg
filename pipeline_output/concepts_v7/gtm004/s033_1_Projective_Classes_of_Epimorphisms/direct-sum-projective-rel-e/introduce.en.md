The property of being projective relative to a fixed epimorphism is additive:
a direct sum satisfies the lifting condition exactly when each summand does.
This elementary observation follows directly from the universal property of
the direct sum and the additivity of the Hom-functor, and it is used
repeatedly in the construction of $\mathcal{E}$-projective resolutions.
