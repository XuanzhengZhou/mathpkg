Proposition 3.1 verifies that the long sequence associated to an $\mathcal{E}$-connected
sequence of functors is indeed differential (every composite of two consecutive maps
is zero). The proof splits into three checks: at $T_j(A)$ (immediate from the
$\mathcal{E}$-exactness of the original sequence), at $T_j(A'')$ (by naturality
applied to a morphism of $\mathcal{E}$-exact sequences), and at $T_{j-1}(A')$
(again by naturality). This property is a necessary condition for exactness and is
used to define satellites.
