In relative homological algebra, not every exact sequence is suitable for deriving long exact sequences of relative derived functors. The notions of $\mathcal{E}$-admissible morphisms and $\mathcal{E}$-exact sequences restrict attention to those exact sequences whose epimorphic parts belong to the chosen projective class $\mathcal{E}$. This ensures that $\mathcal{E}$-projective resolutions exist and that the relative derived functors enjoy the same formal properties as their absolute counterparts.
