This theorem establishes the long exact sequence for relative ($\mathcal{E}$-)derived functors, generalizing the classical result for absolute derived functors. Given a short exact sequence of additive functors that is exact on $\mathcal{E}$-projectives, the $\mathcal{E}$-derived functors form a long exact sequence connected by natural connecting homomorphisms. This is one of the two fundamental exactness theorems of relative homological algebra, making $\mathcal{E}$-derived functors a practical computational tool.
