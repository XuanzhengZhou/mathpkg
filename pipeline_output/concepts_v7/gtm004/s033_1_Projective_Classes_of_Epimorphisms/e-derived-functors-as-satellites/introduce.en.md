This theorem identifies the $\mathcal{E}$-derived functors with the $\mathcal{E}$-satellites of a right $\mathcal{E}$-exact functor, establishing that the $\mathcal{E}$-derived functors satisfy a universal property. The proof proceeds by induction, constructing the natural transformations $\varphi_j$ step by step using the connecting homomorphisms and the fact that $L_{j+1}^{\mathcal{E}} H(A'') \to L_j^{\mathcal{E}} H(K'')$ is monomorphic. This characterization is fundamental to the theory of relative derived functors, paralleling the classical identification of derived functors with satellites.
