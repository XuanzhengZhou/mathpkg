Let $T = \{T_j\}_{j \in \mathbb{Z}}$ be an $\mathcal{E}$-connected sequence of functors $\mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{B}$ with connecting morphisms $\omega_j : T_j(A'') \to T_{j-1}(A')$. For each short $\mathcal{E}$-exact sequence
\[
0 \to A' \to A \to A'' \to 0
\]
in $\mathfrak{A}$, the induced long sequence
\[
\cdots \to T_j(A') \to T_j(A) \to T_j(A'') \xrightarrow{\omega_j} T_{j-1}(A') \to T_{j-1}(A) \to \cdots
\]
is differential (i.e., the composite of any two consecutive morphisms is zero).
