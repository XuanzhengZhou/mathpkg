Let $\mathfrak{U}$ be an abelian category and let $\mathcal{E}$ be a class of epimorphisms in $\mathfrak{U}$. The {\it closure} $C(\mathcal{E})$ of $\mathcal{E}$ is defined as the class of those epimorphisms $\varepsilon$ in $\mathfrak{U}$ such that every $\mathcal{E}$-projective object of $\mathfrak{U}$ is also projective relative to $\varepsilon$.

The closure satisfies:
\[
\mathcal{E} \subseteq C(\mathcal{E}), \qquad C(C(\mathcal{E})) = C(\mathcal{E}).
\]
The class $\mathcal{E}$ is called {\it closed} if $\mathcal{E} = C(\mathcal{E})$.
