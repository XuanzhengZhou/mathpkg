This lemma generalizes the classical horseshoe lemma to the relative setting. It shows that any short $\mathcal{E}$-exact sequence can be embedded in a $3 \times 3$ diagram whose middle row is a short $\mathcal{E}$-exact sequence of $\mathcal{E}$-projective objects. The construction, analogous to the absolute case, uses the fact that $\mathcal{E}$ is closed to verify that the constructed epimorphisms belong to $\mathcal{E}$. This lemma is the key technical tool for establishing the long exact sequence of $\mathcal{E}$-derived functors.
