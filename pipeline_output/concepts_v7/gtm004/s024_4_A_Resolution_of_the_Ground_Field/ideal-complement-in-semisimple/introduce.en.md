Corollary 5.4 states that every ideal in a semi-simple Lie algebra has a complementary ideal. This follows from the non-degeneracy of the Killing form (Corollary 5.3): the orthogonal complement of $\mathfrak{a}$ with respect to the Killing form is an ideal, and its intersection with $\mathfrak{a}$ is zero because the Killing form restricted to $\mathfrak{a} \cap \mathfrak{a}^\perp$ is both the Killing form of an ideal and identically zero, forcing the intersection to be solvable and hence trivial.
