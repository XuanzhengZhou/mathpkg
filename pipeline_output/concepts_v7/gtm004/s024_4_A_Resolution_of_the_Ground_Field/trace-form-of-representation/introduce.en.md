The trace form $\beta$ associated to a finite-dimensional representation $\varrho$ of a Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ is defined by $\beta(x, y) = \operatorname{Tr}(\varrho x \circ \varrho y)$. It is always symmetric and satisfies the associativity condition $\beta([x, y], z) = \beta(x, [y, z])$ due to the trace identity $\operatorname{Tr}(\varphi \psi) = \operatorname{Tr}(\psi \varphi)$. When $\varrho = \operatorname{ad}$ is the adjoint representation, $\beta$ specializes to the Killing form.
