Theorem 7.4 provides a homological criterion for freeness of graded modules over a polynomial ring: $\operatorname{Tor}_1^P(B, K) = 0$ implies $B$ is a free $P$-module. The proof constructs a free module $F$ mapping to $B$ whose induced map $F \otimes_P K 	o B \otimes_P K$ is an isomorphism. The Tor-vanishing condition implies the kernel $R$ satisfies $R \otimes_P K = 0$, hence $R = 0$ by Proposition 7.3. This theorem is the critical step in Hilbert's syzygy theorem.
