Proposition 5.6 states that for a semi-simple Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$, the only simple $\mathfrak{g}$-module that is also trivial is the one-dimensional module $K$ itself. Equivalently, any non-trivial simple module must have a non-trivial $\mathfrak{g}$-action. This fact is used crucially in the proofs of the Whitehead Lemmas to handle the simple module case in the minimal-dimension induction argument.
