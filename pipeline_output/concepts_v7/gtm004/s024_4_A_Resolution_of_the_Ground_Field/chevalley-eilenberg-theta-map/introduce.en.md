The theta map $\theta(y)$ is a $\mathfrak{g}$-module homomorphism on the Chevalley--Eilenberg chains $C_n = U\mathfrak{g} \otimes_K E_n \mathfrak{g}$. It encodes the adjoint action of $y \in \mathfrak{g}$ twisted by the tensor representation: the first term is the negative of the $\mathfrak{g}$-module action on $C_n$, while the sum applies the bracket with $y$ to each argument of the exterior product with alternating signs. This map generates the algebraic structure needed to define the Chevalley--Eilenberg differential.
