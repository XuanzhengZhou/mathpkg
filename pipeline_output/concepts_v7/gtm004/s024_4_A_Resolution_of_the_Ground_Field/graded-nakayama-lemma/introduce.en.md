Proposition 7.3 is the graded Nakayama lemma for polynomial rings: if $M \otimes_P K = 0$ for a graded $P$-module $M$, then $M = 0$. The proof uses a degree argument: if $m \in M$ is a non-zero homogeneous element of minimal degree, one can express $m \otimes 1$ in terms of the module structure, leading to a contradiction. This lemma is the key to establishing the surjectivity step in Theorem 7.4.
