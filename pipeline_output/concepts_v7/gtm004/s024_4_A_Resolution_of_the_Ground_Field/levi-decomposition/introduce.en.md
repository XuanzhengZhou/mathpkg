Theorem 6.7 (the Levi decomposition) states that every finite-dimensional Lie algebra over a field of characteristic zero splits as a semi-direct product $\mathfrak{g} = \mathfrak{s} \ltimes \mathfrak{r}$, where $\mathfrak{r}$ is the radical and $\mathfrak{s}$ is a semi-simple subalgebra isomorphic to $\mathfrak{g}/\mathfrak{r}$. The proof uses the Second Whitehead Lemma ($H^2(\mathfrak{s}, \mathfrak{r}) = 0$) to show that the extension of $\mathfrak{s}$ by $\mathfrak{r}$ splits. This decomposition reduces the study of general Lie algebras to the study of semi-simple Lie algebras and their representations on solvable Lie algebras.
