The Chevalley--Eilenberg differential $d_n$ is the boundary map of the standard projective resolution for Lie algebra cohomology. It is defined inductively from the theta map and the $\mathfrak{g}$-module structure $\sigma$, and satisfies the key relation $\sigma(y) d_{n-1} + d_n \sigma(y) = -\theta(y)$. The explicit formula expresses $d_n$ as a sum of two terms: the module action on each argument with alternating signs, and the Lie bracket between pairs of arguments.
