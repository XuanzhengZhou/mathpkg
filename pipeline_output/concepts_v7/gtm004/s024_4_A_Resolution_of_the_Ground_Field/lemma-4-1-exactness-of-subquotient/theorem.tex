The complexes $W^p$, defined as the subquotients $F_p C / F_{p-1} C$ of the filtration of the Chevalley--Eilenberg complex $C$, are exact. A $K$-linear contracting homotopy $\Sigma$ is defined by
$$\Sigma_{-1}(1_K) = 1 \langle \rangle,$$
and for $n \geq 0$, on basis elements
$$e_{k_1} \dots e_{k_m} \langle e_{l_1}, \dots, e_{l_n} \rangle,$$
with $k_1 \leq \dots \leq k_m$, $l_1 < \dots < l_n$,
$$\Sigma_n(e_{k_1} \dots e_{k_m} \langle e_{l_1}, \dots, e_{l_n} \rangle) =
\begin{cases}
0, & \text{if } k_m \leq l_n \text{ in } J \text{ (in particular if } m = 0), \\
(-1)^n e_{k_1} \dots e_{k_m} \langle e_{l_1}, \dots, e_{l_n}, e_{k_m} \rangle, & \text{if } k_m > l_n.
\end{cases}$$
One verifies that $\varepsilon \Sigma_{-1} = 1$ and $d_{n+1} \Sigma_n + \Sigma_{n-1} d_n = 1$, so $H_n(W^p) = 0$ for all $p \geq 1$ and all $n$.
