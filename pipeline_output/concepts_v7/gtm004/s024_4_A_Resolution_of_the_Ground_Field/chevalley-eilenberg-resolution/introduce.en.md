Theorem 4.2 (the Chevalley--Eilenberg resolution) provides the standard $\mathfrak{g}$-projective resolution of the trivial $\mathfrak{g}$-module $K$ for Lie algebra cohomology. The chain modules are $C_n = U\mathfrak{g} \otimes_K E_n \mathfrak{g}$, and the differential is the Chevalley--Eilenberg boundary map. Exactness is proved via a filtration argument (Lemma 4.1) together with a PBW basis, conceptually different from the bar resolution approach of general augmented algebras. This resolution is much smaller than the bar resolution and forms the basis for computing Lie algebra cohomology as $H^n(\mathfrak{g}, A) = H^n(\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{g}}(C, A))$.
