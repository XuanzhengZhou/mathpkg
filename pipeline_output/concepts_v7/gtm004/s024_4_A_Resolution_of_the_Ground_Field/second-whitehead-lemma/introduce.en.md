Proposition 6.3 (the Second Whitehead Lemma) asserts the vanishing of $H^2(\mathfrak{g}, A)$ for any finite-dimensional module $A$ over a semi-simple Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ (characteristic zero). By the interpretation of $H^2$ via Theorem 3.3, this is equivalent to the statement that every central extension $K \to \mathfrak{h} \to \mathfrak{g}$ splits. The proof reduces (by minimal dimension induction) to showing $H^2(\mathfrak{g}, K) = 0$, i.e., that the central extension splits, using the non-degenerate trace form and a Casimir-type element $\sum e_i e_i' \in U\mathfrak{g}$ to construct an explicit $\mathfrak{g}$-module splitting.
