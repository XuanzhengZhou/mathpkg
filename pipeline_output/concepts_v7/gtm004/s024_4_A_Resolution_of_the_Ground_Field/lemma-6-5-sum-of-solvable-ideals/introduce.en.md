Lemma 6.5 guarantees that the sum (as ideals) of two solvable ideals is again solvable. Since $\mathfrak{a} + \mathfrak{b} / \mathfrak{a} \cong \mathfrak{b} / (\mathfrak{a} \cap \mathfrak{b})$ and solvability passes to quotients and subalgebras (Lemma 6.4), the result follows. This lemma is the key step in proving that every finite-dimensional Lie algebra has a unique maximal solvable ideal — its radical.
