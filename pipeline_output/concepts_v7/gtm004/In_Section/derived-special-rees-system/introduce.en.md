Proposition 6.5 establishes that the special property of a Rees system is stable under the derivation process. Given a special Rees system with automorphism $\theta : \Gamma \to \Gamma$, the same $\theta$ serves as the automorphism for the derived Rees system. The proof verifies the three defining relations $\theta\varphi_1 = \varphi_1\alpha_1$, $\bar{\varphi}_1\theta = \bar{\alpha}_1\bar{\varphi}$, and $\bar{\varphi}_1\theta^{-1}\varphi_1 = \bar{\gamma}_1\beta_1$ using the factorization relations $\varphi_1\sigma = \varphi$ and $\bar{\varphi}\bar{\varphi}_1 = \bar{\varphi}$ together with the assumption that the original system is special.
