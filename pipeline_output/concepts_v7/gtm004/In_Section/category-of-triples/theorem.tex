Given an abelian category $\mathfrak{A}$, the \textbf{category of triples} $\mathfrak{T}(\mathfrak{A}, d)$ has as objects triples $(G, A, \theta)$ consisting of:
\begin{itemize}
\item a differential object $G$ of the category $(\mathfrak{A}, d)$ of differential objects in $\mathfrak{A}$,
\item a differential subobject $A \subseteq G$,
\item an automorphism $\theta : G \rightsquigarrow G$ such that $\theta A \subseteq A$.
\end{itemize}

Morphisms in $\mathfrak{T}(\mathfrak{A}, d)$ are the evident commutative diagrams respecting the differential, the subobject inclusion, and the automorphism.
