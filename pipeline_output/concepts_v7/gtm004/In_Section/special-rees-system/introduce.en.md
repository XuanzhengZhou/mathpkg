A special Rees system enriches the Rees system structure with an automorphism $\theta$ of the central object $\Gamma$ that is compatible with the morphisms $\varphi, \bar{\varphi}, \alpha, \bar{\alpha}$ and the composite $\bar{\gamma}\beta$. This extra structure is crucial for ensuring that the derived Rees system inherits the special property (Proposition 6.5). In practice, all Rees systems arising from filtered chain complexes via the functor $F$ are special, making the special case the one of principal interest for spectral sequence convergence theory.
