A Rees system is a structured diagram of exact couples that generalizes the data obtained from a filtered chain complex. It packages together two exact couples sharing the same $E$-term together with a third exact triangle that encodes the relationship between them. The concept, introduced by Hilton and Stammbach, provides the natural categorical framework for studying the convergence of spectral sequences beyond the finite convergence criteria. Every exact couple trivially yields a Rees system, and the derived Rees system construction extends the derived couple process to this richer setting, ultimately allowing necessary and sufficient conditions for $E_\infty \cong Gr \circ H(C)$ to be formulated.
