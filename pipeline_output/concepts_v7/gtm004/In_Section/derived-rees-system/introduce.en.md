The derived Rees system extends the operation of passing to the derived exact couple to the full Rees system structure. Given a Rees system, one takes the derived couples of the two constituent exact couples $\textcircled{1}$ and $\textcircled{2}$, and constructs a new exact triangle $\bar{D}_1 \to D_1 \to \Gamma \to \bar{D}_1$ by factoring $(\varphi, \bar{\varphi})$ through the sub-quotient relations between $D, D_1, \bar{D}, \bar{D}_1$. The exactness of the derived triangle follows from Theorem 4.5. Iterating this process yields the $n$-th derived Rees system, which underlies the higher pages of the spectral sequence and is the setting for the convergence analysis in Section 7.
