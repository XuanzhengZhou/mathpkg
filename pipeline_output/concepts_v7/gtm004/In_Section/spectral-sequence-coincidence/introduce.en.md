The spectral sequence coincidence $SS(\textcircled{1}) = SS(\textcircled{2})$ is a fundamental structural property of Rees systems. It asserts that the two exact couples packaged within a Rees system produce identical spectral sequences, even though the couples themselves are generally distinct. This coincidence justifies the use of the Rees system as a unified framework: one may freely work with either exact couple when computing spectral sequence terms, and the derived Rees system construction generalizes the derived couple process while preserving this equality at every stage.
