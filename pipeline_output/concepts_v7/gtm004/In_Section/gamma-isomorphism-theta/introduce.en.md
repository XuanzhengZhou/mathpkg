Proposition 6.7 shows that in a special Rees system, the automorphism $\theta$ provides a canonical isomorphism between the subobject $\Gamma^- = \ker \bar{\varphi}$ and the quotient object $\Gamma^+ = \operatorname{coker} \varphi$. This isomorphism is the key to formulating the convergence criterion $E_\infty \cong Gr \circ H(C)$ entirely within the Rees system as $E_\infty \cong \Gamma^+$. The remark notes that some isomorphism $\Gamma^- \cong \Gamma^+$ exists even without the specialness assumption, but the explicit identification via $\theta$ is what makes the special case useful for studying convergence of spectral sequences arising from filtered complexes.
