A Rees system (6.9) in an abelian category $\mathfrak{A}$ is called \textbf{special} if there exists an automorphism $\theta : \Gamma \to \Gamma$ satisfying the coherence relations (6.11):

$$\theta \varphi = \varphi \alpha, \quad \bar{\varphi} \theta = \bar{\alpha} \bar{\varphi}, \quad \bar{\varphi} \theta^{-1} \varphi = \bar{\gamma} \beta.$$

The categories are denoted $\mathfrak{R}(\mathfrak{A}, d)$ (all Rees systems) and $\mathfrak{S}(\mathfrak{A}, d)$ (special Rees systems), with an underlying functor $U : \mathfrak{S} \to \mathfrak{R}$. Every exact couple $EC$ may be regarded as a special Rees system with $\Gamma = 0$, giving a full embedding $E : \mathfrak{EC}(\mathfrak{A}) \to \mathfrak{S}(\mathfrak{A}, d)$.
