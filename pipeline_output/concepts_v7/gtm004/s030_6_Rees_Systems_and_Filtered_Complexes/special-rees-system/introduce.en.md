A special Rees system is a Rees system equipped with an automorphism $\theta$ of $\Gamma$ that is compatible with the structure morphisms. The automorphism $\theta$ serves as a "rotation" relating the two exact couples in the system, reflecting the shift operator on the underlying filtered complex. Although not every Rees system is special in principle, every Rees system arising from a filtered differential object via the functor $F$ is special, so the distinction is primarily of technical interest for proofs that do not require $\theta$.
