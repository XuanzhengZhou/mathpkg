This proposition states that the operation of passing to the derived Rees system preserves the property of being special. Concretely, if the original Rees system admits an automorphism $\theta$ of $\Gamma$ satisfying the coherence relations $\theta\varphi = \varphi\alpha$, $\bar{\varphi}\theta = \bar{\alpha}\bar{\varphi}$, and $\bar{\varphi}\theta^{-1}\varphi = \bar{\gamma}\beta$, then the same $\theta$ also satisfies the analogous relations for the derived morphisms $\varphi_1$, $\bar{\varphi}_1$, $\alpha_1$, $\bar{\alpha}_1$, $\beta_1$, and $\bar{\gamma}_1$. This ensures that the entire derived tower of a special Rees system remains within the category $\mathfrak{S}(\mathfrak{A}, d)$.
