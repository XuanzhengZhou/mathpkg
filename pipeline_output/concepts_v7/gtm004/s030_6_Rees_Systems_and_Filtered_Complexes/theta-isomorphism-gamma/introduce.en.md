In a special Rees system, the automorphism $\theta : \Gamma \to \Gamma$ restricts to an isomorphism between the subobject $\Gamma^{-}$ (the "lower" part of $\Gamma$) and the quotient $\Gamma^{+}$ (the "upper" part). This isomorphism is important for the convergence analysis of the spectral sequence, because the $E_{\infty}$ term is compared to $\Gamma^{+}$: the convergence criterion $E_{\infty} \cong \Gamma^{+}$ is the key condition studied in the limit theory of Rees systems. The remark notes that the existence of an isomorphism $\Gamma^{-} \cong \Gamma^{+}$ holds for all Rees systems, not only special ones, though $\theta$ gives a canonical such isomorphism in the special case.
