A Rees system is a generalization of an exact couple introduced by Hilton and Stammbach to study the convergence of spectral sequences arising from filtered chain complexes. It comprises two exact couples sharing the same $E$-term, together with a morphism $\xi$ between their $D$-terms and a differential object $\Gamma$ that mediates between them via morphisms $\varphi$ and $\bar{\varphi}$. The key advantage of a Rees system over a single exact couple is that it retains enough information about the original filtered complex to determine whether $E_{\infty} \cong \operatorname{Gr} \circ H(C)$, a question that cannot be answered by the exact couple alone.
