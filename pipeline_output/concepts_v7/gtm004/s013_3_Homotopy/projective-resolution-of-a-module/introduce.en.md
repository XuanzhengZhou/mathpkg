A projective resolution of a module $A$ is a positive chain complex $P$ consisting of projective modules $P_n$ that is acyclic (exact everywhere except at degree zero), together with a specified isomorphism $H_0(P) \cong A$. The complex provides a replacement of $A$ by projective objects, making it possible to apply homological methods. Projective resolutions are the fundamental input for defining left derived functors such as $\operatorname{Tor}$ and $\operatorname{Ext}$.
