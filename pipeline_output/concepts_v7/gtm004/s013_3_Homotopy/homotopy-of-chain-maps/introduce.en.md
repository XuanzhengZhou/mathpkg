A homotopy between two chain maps $\varphi, \psi : C \to D$ is a morphism $\Sigma : C \to D$ of degree $+1$ of graded modules satisfying $\psi - \varphi = \partial\Sigma + \Sigma\partial$. This relation generalizes the idea of continuous deformation from topology to the algebraic setting of chain complexes. When such a $\Sigma$ exists, the maps $\varphi$ and $\psi$ are called homotopic, denoted $\varphi \simeq \psi$, and they induce the same map in homology.
