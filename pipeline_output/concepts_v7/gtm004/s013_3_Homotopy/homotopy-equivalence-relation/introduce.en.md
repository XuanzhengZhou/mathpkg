Lemma 3.2 of Hilton--Stammbach establishes that the homotopy relation $\simeq$ on the set of chain maps $\operatorname{Hom}(C, D)$ is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive. Reflexivity follows from the zero homotopy, symmetry from negating the homotopy $\Sigma$, and transitivity follows from adding the two homotopies. This lemma justifies treating homotopy as a congruence relation and is the essential algebraic prerequisite for forming the homotopy category.
