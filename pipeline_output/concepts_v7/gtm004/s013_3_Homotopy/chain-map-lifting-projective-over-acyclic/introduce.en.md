Theorem 4.1 of Hilton--Stammbach (often called the Comparison Theorem) is the fundamental lifting result of homological algebra. Given a projective positive chain complex $C$ and an acyclic positive chain complex $D$, any homomorphism $\varphi : H_0(C) \to H_0(D)$ can be lifted to a chain map $\varphi : C \to D$, uniquely up to homotopy. The proof constructs $\varphi_n$ recursively using the projectivity of each $C_n$ and the exactness of $D$. This theorem is the basis for the well-definedness of derived functors.
