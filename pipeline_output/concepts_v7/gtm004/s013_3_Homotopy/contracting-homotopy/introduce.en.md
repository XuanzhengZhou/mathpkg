A contracting homotopy for a chain complex $C$ is a homotopy $\Sigma : 0 \Rightarrow 1_C$ from the zero map to the identity map on $C$. It consists of homomorphisms $\Sigma_n : C_n \to C_{n+1}$ satisfying $\partial_{n+1}\Sigma_n + \Sigma_{n-1}\partial_n = 1$ for all $n$. The existence of a contracting homotopy forces $H(C) = 0$, so the complex is exact; this provides one of the most common techniques for proving exactness in homological algebra.
