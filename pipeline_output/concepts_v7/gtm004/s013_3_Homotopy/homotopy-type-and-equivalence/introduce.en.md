Two chain complexes $C$ and $D$ are said to be of the same homotopy type if they are isomorphic in the homotopy category. Concretely, this means there exist chain maps $\varphi : C \to D$ and $\psi : D \to C$ whose compositions are homotopic to the respective identity maps: $\psi\varphi \simeq 1_C$ and $\varphi\psi \simeq 1_D$. The map $\varphi$ (or $\psi$) is then called a homotopy equivalence. This notion is fundamental for the Comparison Theorem: any two projective resolutions of the same module are canonically of the same homotopy type (Proposition 4.3).
