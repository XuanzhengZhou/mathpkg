Proposition 4.3 of Hilton--Stammbach states that any two projective resolutions of the same module $A$ are canonically of the same homotopy type. The proof applies Theorem 4.1 twice: lifting the identity $1_A : H_0(C) \to H_0(D)$ and its inverse yields chain maps $\varphi : C \to D$ and $\psi : D \to C$ whose compositions are homotopic to the identities. This result is the crucial justification that derived functors such as $\operatorname{Tor}$ and $\operatorname{Ext}$ are well-defined, independent of the particular projective resolution chosen.
