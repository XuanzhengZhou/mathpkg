Theorem 9.1, the Yoneda interpretation of $\operatorname{Ext}^n$, establishes a natural isomorphism between $\operatorname{Yext}_A^n(A,B)$ (equivalence classes of $n$-extensions of $A$ by $B$) and the derived-functor $\operatorname{Ext}_A^n(A,B)$. The proof constructs mutual inverse maps: from an $n$-extension, one takes a projective resolution of $A$ and obtains a representing cocycle; conversely, a cocycle is converted to an $n$-extension via pullback/pushout constructions. This theorem gives $\operatorname{Ext}^n$ an explicit geometric meaning and transfers the abelian group structure to Yoneda extension classes.
