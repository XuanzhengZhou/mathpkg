The category $(\mathfrak{A}, d, f)$ collects filtered differential objects as its objects, with morphisms that simultaneously respect the filtration structure and commute with the differential. This category serves as the domain for constructing exact couples: from any filtered differential object one obtains a homology exact triangle and hence an exact couple. The functorial passage from $(\mathfrak{A}, d, f)$ to exact couples is a key step in building spectral sequences.
