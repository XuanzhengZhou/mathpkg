A filtered object of an abelian category $\mathfrak{B}$ is simply an object $B$ together with a nested, doubly-infinite family of subobjects. Morphisms between filtered objects are required to respect the filtration level by level. This general notion of filtration abstracts the concrete idea of a graded or filtered module and provides the domain for the associated graded object functor $Gr$.
