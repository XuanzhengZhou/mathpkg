Let $M = H(C)$ be the homology of a filtered differential object equipped with the induced filtration $\{M^{(p)}\}$. For the subquotients $M^{(p)}/M^{(p-1)}$ to adequately represent $M$, one requires the two conditions

$$\text{(i)} \quad \bigcap_{p} M^{(p)} = 0, \qquad \text{(ii)} \quad \bigcup_{p} M^{(p)} = M.$$

If (i) fails, there exist non-zero elements of $M$ lying in every $M^{(p)}$ and therefore lost in $Gr(M)$. If (ii) fails, there exist non-zero elements of $M$ lying in no $M^{(p)}$ and therefore unrepresented in $Gr(M)$.
