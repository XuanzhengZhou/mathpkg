This theorem shows how a projective class of epimorphisms in an abelian category can be transferred to another abelian category via a pair of adjoint functors. Given a projective class $\mathcal{E}$ in $\mathfrak{A}$ and an adjoint pair $(F, U)$ with $U$ faithful, the preimage $\mathcal{E}' = U^{-1}\mathcal{E}$ forms a projective class in $\mathfrak{A}'$. The $\mathcal{E}'$-projective objects are precisely the direct summands of objects of the form $FP$ for $\mathcal{E}$-projective $P$, and these are sufficient for presenting all objects of $\mathfrak{A}'$.
