Let $\mathfrak{U}$ be a small category, let $\mathfrak{B} = \mathbf{1}$ be the category with one object and one morphism, and let $J: \mathfrak{U} \rightarrow \mathbf{1}$ be the unique functor. Then for a functor $T: \mathfrak{U} \rightarrow \mathfrak{A}$, the homology of the small category $\mathfrak{U}$ with coefficients in $T$ is defined by
$$H_n(\mathfrak{U}, T) = H_n(J, T), \quad n \geq 0,$$
where $H_n(J, T) = (L_n^{\mathcal{E}_1'} \tilde{J}) T$ is the $J$-homology.
