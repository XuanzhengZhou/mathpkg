A $P$-surjective homomorphism is one that is surjective "up to $P'$-powers": every element of the target becomes a $P'$-th power of an element in the image. This means that for each $y \in K$, some $P'$-power $y^q$ lies in the image of $\theta$. Together with $P$-injectivity, this notion characterizes when a homomorphism between nilpotent groups is a $P$-localization map.
