A $P$-injective homomorphism is one whose kernel consists entirely of elements whose orders are $P'$-numbers (integers with prime factors outside $P$). This is a relative notion of injectivity adapted to the $P$-localization theory: a map is $P$-injective if it is injective "up to $P'$-torsion." Together with $P$-surjectivity, it characterizes when a homomorphism is a $P$-localization.
