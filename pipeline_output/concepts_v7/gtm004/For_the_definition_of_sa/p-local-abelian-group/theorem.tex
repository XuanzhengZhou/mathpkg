A commutative group $A$ is $P$-local if and only if it is uniquely divisible by $P'$-numbers. This is equivalent to saying that $A$ is a $\mathbb{Z}_P$-module, where $\mathbb{Z}_P$ is the ring of integers localized at $P$. For any abelian group $A$, the tensor product with $\mathbb{Z}_P$ defines a $P$-localization functor $A_P = \mathbb{Z}_P \otimes A$.
