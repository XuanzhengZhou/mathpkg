Let $\Lambda$ be a principal ideal domain and $P = \bigoplus_{i \in J} \Lambda_i$ a free $\Lambda$-module. Any submodule $R \subseteq P$ is itself a free $\Lambda$-module. The proof uses a well-ordering of the index set $J$ and an inductive construction: for each index $j$, one considers the ideal of coefficients appearing at position $j$ in elements of $R$, which is principal by the PID assumption, and selects a corresponding basis element. This theorem is foundational for the structure theory of modules over PIDs.
