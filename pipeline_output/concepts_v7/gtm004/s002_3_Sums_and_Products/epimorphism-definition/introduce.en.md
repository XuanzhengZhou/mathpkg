An epimorphism in the category of $\Lambda$-modules is defined purely in terms of homomorphisms, without reference to elements: a map $\varepsilon: B \to C$ is epimorphic if it is right-cancellative with respect to composition. That is, for any two parallel maps $\alpha_1, \alpha_2: C \to M$, the equality $\alpha_1 \circ \varepsilon = \alpha_2 \circ \varepsilon$ forces $\alpha_1 = \alpha_2$. This categorical definition will turn out to be equivalent to surjectivity for modules (Proposition 6.1), though this coincidence does not hold in all categories.
