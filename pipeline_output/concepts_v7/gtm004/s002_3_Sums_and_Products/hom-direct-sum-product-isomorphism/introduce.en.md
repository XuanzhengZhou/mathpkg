Let $\{A_j\}_{j \in J}$ be a family of $\Lambda$-modules and $B$ a fixed $\Lambda$-module. This proposition establishes a natural isomorphism between the Hom-module from the direct sum $\bigoplus A_j$ to $B$, and the direct product of the individual Hom-modules $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(A_j, B)$. The map $\eta$ sends a homomorphism $\psi: \bigoplus A_j \to B$ to the family $(\psi i_j)_{j \in J}$, where $i_j: A_j \to \bigoplus A_j$ are the canonical injections. This is a restatement of the universal property of the direct sum in the language of Hom-functors.
