An injective $\Lambda$-module $I$ is defined by the dual property to projectivity: every homomorphism $\alpha: A \to I$ can be extended along any monomorphism $\mu: A \hookrightarrow B$ to a homomorphism $\beta: B \to I$. In diagrammatic terms, one can always complete the triangle with a dashed arrow $\beta$. This definition is obtained by reversing all arrows in the definition of a projective module, and replacing "surjective" (epimorphism) with "injective" (monomorphism). Injective modules play the dual role to projective modules in homological algebra.
