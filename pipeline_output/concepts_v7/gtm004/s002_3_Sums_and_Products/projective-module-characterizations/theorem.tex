For a $\Lambda$-module $P$ the following statements are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $P$ is projective;
\item[(2)] for every short exact sequence $A \xrightarrow{\mu} B \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} C$ of $\Lambda$-modules the induced sequence
$$0 \rightarrow \operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(P, A) \xrightarrow{\mu_*} \operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(P, B) \xrightarrow{\varepsilon_*} \operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(P, C) \rightarrow 0$$
is exact;
\item[(3)] if $\varepsilon : B \rightarrow P$ is surjective, then there exists a homomorphism $\beta : P \rightarrow B$ such that $\varepsilon \beta = 1_P$;
\item[(4)] $P$ is a direct summand in every module of which it is a quotient;
\item[(5)] $P$ is a direct summand in a free module.
\end{enumerate}
