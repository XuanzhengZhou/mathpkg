This central theorem provides five equivalent ways to characterize a projective $\Lambda$-module $P$: (1) the defining lifting property; (2) the Hom-functor $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(P, -)$ preserves exactness of short exact sequences; (3) every surjection onto $P$ splits; (4) $P$ is a direct summand of any module that maps surjectively onto it; (5) $P$ is a direct summand of some free module. The equivalence chain $(1) \Rightarrow (2) \Rightarrow (3) \Rightarrow (4) \Rightarrow (5) \Rightarrow (1)$ is the structural backbone for working with projective modules in homological algebra.
