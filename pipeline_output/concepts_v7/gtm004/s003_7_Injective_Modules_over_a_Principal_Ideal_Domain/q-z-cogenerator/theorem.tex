The abelian group $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ is injective and has the property that for any abelian group $G \neq 0$ there exists a nonzero homomorphism $\psi : G \rightarrow \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$. Consequently, every abelian group $A$ admits a monomorphism $\beta : A \rightarrow \prod_{a \in A} (\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z})_a$ into a product of copies of $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$.
