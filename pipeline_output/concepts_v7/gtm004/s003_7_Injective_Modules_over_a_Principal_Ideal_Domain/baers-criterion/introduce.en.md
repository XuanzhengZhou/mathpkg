Baer's Criterion provides a powerful simplification for verifying injectivity of a module: instead of checking the extension property for every submodule of every module, it suffices to check extension only for left ideals of the base ring. This criterion appears as Exercise 7.1 in Hilton--Stammbach and is a fundamental tool in homological algebra, used throughout the theory of injective modules and in the proof that divisible modules over a PID are injective.
