A direct product of copies of $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ is called \textbf{cofree}. This terminology is chosen because a direct sum of copies of $\mathbb{Z}$ is called free, and $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ possesses the dual properties: it is injective and for any abelian group $G \neq 0$ there exists a nonzero homomorphism $\psi : G \rightarrow \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$.
