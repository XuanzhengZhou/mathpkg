A cofree abelian group is a direct product of copies of the injective cogenerator $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$. The terminology is motivated by duality: just as a free abelian group is a direct sum of copies of the projective generator $\mathbb{Z}$, a cofree abelian group is a direct product of copies of the injective cogenerator $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$. However, "cofree" is not the exact categorical dual of "free" in all respects, only in the sense described here. The generalization of this concept to arbitrary rings is carried out in Section 8.
