Theorem 7.1 of Hilton--Stammbach establishes that over a principal ideal domain, the notions of injectivity and divisibility coincide. The proof that divisible implies injective uses Zorn's Lemma on the set of partial extensions of a homomorphism into the divisible module. The converse direction follows from the observation that an injective module must absorb multiplication by non-zero scalars. This result is the injective analogue of the fact that every projective module over a PID is free (Corollary 5.2).
