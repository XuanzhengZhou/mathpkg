Let $\alpha: A' \to A$ be a homomorphism of $\Lambda$-modules. For an extension $B \xrightarrow{\kappa} E \xrightarrow{\nu} A$ representing a class in $E(A,B)$, form the pullback
$$
\begin{CD}
E^{\alpha} @>{\nu'}>> A' \\
@V{\xi}VV @VV{\alpha}V \\
E @>>{\nu}> A
\end{CD}
$$
Then $B \xrightarrow{\kappa'} E^{\alpha} \xrightarrow{\nu'} A'$ is an extension of $A'$ by $B$, and the induced map
$$
\alpha^*: E(A,B) \to E(A',B)
$$
is defined by assigning to the class of $B \to E \to A$ the class of $B \to E^{\alpha} \to A'$.

Dually, let $\beta: B \to B'$ be a homomorphism. Form the pushout
$$
\begin{CD}
B @>{\kappa}>> E \\
@V{\beta}VV @VV{\xi}V \\
B' @>>{\kappa'}> E_{\beta}
\end{CD}
$$
Then $B' \xrightarrow{\kappa'} E_{\beta} \xrightarrow{\nu'} A$ is an extension of $A$ by $B'$, and the induced map
$$
\beta_*: E(A,B) \to E(A,B')
$$
is defined by assigning to the class of $B \to E \to A$ the class of $B' \to E_{\beta} \to A$.
