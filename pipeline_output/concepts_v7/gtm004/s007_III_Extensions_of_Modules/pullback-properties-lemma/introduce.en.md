Lemma 1.2 records two essential properties of pullback squares in module categories. First, the pullback construction induces a natural map between kernels. Second, and more importantly for extension theory, epimorphisms are stable under pullback: pulling back an epimorphism yields another epimorphism. This ensures that when we construct the induced extension via pullback along $\alpha: A' \to A$, we obtain a genuine extension (i.e., an epimorphism $E^\alpha \twoheadrightarrow A'$).
