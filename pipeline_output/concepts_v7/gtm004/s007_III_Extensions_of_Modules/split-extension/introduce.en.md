A split extension is the trivial kind of extension: it is equivalent to the direct sum $A \oplus B$ with the natural inclusion and projection maps. In a split extension, the middle module $E$ decomposes as a direct sum of the submodule $B$ and a complement isomorphic to $A$. Split extensions correspond to the zero element in the group $E(A,B)$ and, under the isomorphism $E(A,B) \cong \operatorname{Ext}_\Lambda^1(A,B)$, to the zero element of Ext.
