Theorem 1.1 asserts that the derived couple construction preserves exactness: starting from an exact couple $\{D, E, \alpha, \beta, \gamma\}$, the new data $\{D_1, E_1, \alpha_1, \beta_1, \gamma_1\}$ again forms an exact couple. The proof verifies exactness at each of the three vertices by showing: $\alpha_1\gamma_1 = 0$, $\ker\alpha_1 \subseteq \operatorname{im}\gamma_1$, and corresponding relations at the other positions. This is the crucial result that makes the iterative construction of spectral sequences possible, as it guarantees that at each step one obtains a legitimate exact couple from which the next term can be derived.
