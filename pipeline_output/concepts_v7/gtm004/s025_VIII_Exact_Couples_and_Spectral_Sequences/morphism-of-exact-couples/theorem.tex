Let $EC = \{D, E, \alpha, \beta, \gamma\}$ and $EC' = \{D', E', \alpha', \beta', \gamma'\}$ be exact couples in an abelian category $\mathfrak{A}$. A \textbf{morphism} $\Phi: EC \to EC'$ is a pair of morphisms $\kappa: D \to D'$, $\lambda: E \to E'$ such that
$$\alpha' \kappa = \kappa \alpha, \quad \beta' \kappa = \lambda \beta, \quad \gamma' \lambda = \kappa \gamma.$$
