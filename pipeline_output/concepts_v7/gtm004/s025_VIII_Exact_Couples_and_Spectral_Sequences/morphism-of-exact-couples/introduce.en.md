A morphism of exact couples $\Phi: EC \to EC'$ consists of a pair $(\kappa, \lambda)$ of morphisms making the two exact triangles commute in the natural sense. The compatibility conditions $\alpha'\kappa = \kappa\alpha$, $\beta'\kappa = \lambda\beta$, and $\gamma'\lambda = \kappa\gamma$ ensure that $\Phi$ respects the exact couple structure. These morphisms form the category $\mathfrak{EC}(\mathfrak{A})$ of exact couples, which is the domain of the spectral sequence functor.
