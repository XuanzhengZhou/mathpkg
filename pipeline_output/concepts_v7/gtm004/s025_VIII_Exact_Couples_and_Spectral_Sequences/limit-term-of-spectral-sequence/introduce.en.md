The limit term $E_\infty$ of a spectral sequence is the ultimate target of the convergence process. It is constructed by first identifying, for each $n$, the subobject $E_{n,\infty}$ of elements in $E_n$ that survive as cycles through all subsequent differentials $d_r$ ($r \geq n$). The maps between successive $E_{n,\infty}$ form a directed system, and $E_\infty$ is the colimit (direct limit) of this system. An element of $E_\infty$ is represented by a permanent cycle, and it vanishes precisely when it eventually becomes a boundary. In applications, $E_\infty$ is typically related to the homology of the original filtered object.
