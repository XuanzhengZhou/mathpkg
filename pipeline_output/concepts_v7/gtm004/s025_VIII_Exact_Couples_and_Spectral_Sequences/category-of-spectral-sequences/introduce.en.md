The category $\mathfrak{E}(\mathfrak{A})$ of spectral sequences in an abelian category $\mathfrak{A}$ provides the proper setting for studying spectral sequences and their functorial properties. Its objects are spectral sequences $E = \{(E_n, d_n)\}$ and its morphisms are sequences $\varphi_n: E_n \to E'_n$ compatible with the differentials and compatible with taking homology. This categorical framework allows one to define functors into $\mathfrak{E}(\mathfrak{A})$, most notably the spectral sequence functor $SS: \mathfrak{EC} \to \mathfrak{E}$ from the category of exact couples.
