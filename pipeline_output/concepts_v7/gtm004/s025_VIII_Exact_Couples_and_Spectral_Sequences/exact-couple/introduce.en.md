An exact couple is a powerful algebraic device introduced by Massey for generating spectral sequences. It consists of two objects $D$ and $E$ and three morphisms $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$ forming an exact triangle. From an exact couple one constructs a derived couple by setting $D_1 = \alpha D$, $E_1 = H(E, \beta\gamma)$, and defining new morphisms $\alpha_1, \beta_1, \gamma_1$; iterating this process yields a spectral sequence. Exact couples arise naturally in algebraic topology from long exact sequences of pairs and in homological algebra from filtered chain complexes.
