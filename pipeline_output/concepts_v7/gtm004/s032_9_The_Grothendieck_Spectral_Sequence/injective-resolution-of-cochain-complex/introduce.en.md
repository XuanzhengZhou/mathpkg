Lemma 9.4 provides the crucial technical construction needed in the proof of the Grothendieck spectral sequence. Given an injective resolution $I$ of $A$ and the resulting cochain complex $FI$, one must resolve this entire cochain complex by a double complex of injectives in a compatible way. The lemma asserts that one can construct such a resolution where each column is an injective resolution of the corresponding term $F_r$, and the horizontal short sequences $K_{r,s} \to J_{r,s} \to L_{r+1,s}$ are exact. This is an instance of a Cartan--Eilenberg resolution adapted to the cochain complex $FI$.
