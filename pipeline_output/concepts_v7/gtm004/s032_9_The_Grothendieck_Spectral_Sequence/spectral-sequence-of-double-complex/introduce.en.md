A double complex $(B, d', d'')$ admits two natural filtrations of its total complex $\operatorname{Tot} B$: one by rows (filtering on the first index) and one by columns (filtering on the second index). Each filtration gives rise to a spectral sequence, and Proposition 9.1 computes their $E_0$ and $E_1$ pages explicitly. The first spectral sequence takes homology with respect to the vertical differential first, then the horizontal; the second does the opposite. These formulas are the computational backbone for the proof of the Grothendieck spectral sequence.
