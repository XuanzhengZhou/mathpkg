Given a group $G$ and a $G$-module $A$, their \textbf{semi-direct product} $A \rtimes G$ is the group whose underlying set is $A \times G$ with the group operation
$$(a, x) \cdot (a', x') = (a + x a', x x'), \quad a, a' \in A,\; x, x' \in G.$$
The maps $i : A \to A \rtimes G$ given by $i(a) = (a, 1)$ and $p : A \rtimes G \to G$ given by $p(a, x) = x$ form a short exact sequence of groups
$$A \xrightarrow{i} A \rtimes G \xrightarrow{p} G.$$
There is a group homomorphism $s : G \to A \rtimes G$ defined by $s(x) = (0, x)$ satisfying $ps = 1_G$; thus the extension is split.
