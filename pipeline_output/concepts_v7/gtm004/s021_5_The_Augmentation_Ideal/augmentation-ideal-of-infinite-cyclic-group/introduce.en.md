Theorem 5.5 states that for the infinite cyclic group $C = \langle t \rangle$, the augmentation ideal $IC$ is a free $C$-module of rank one, generated by $t - 1$. In fact $IC \cong \mathbb{Z}C$ as $C$-modules. This computation is fundamental: it provides the short free resolution $0 \to IC \to \mathbb{Z}C \to \mathbb{Z} \to 0$ for the trivial $C$-module $\mathbb{Z}$, which serves as the basis for computing the (co)homology of cyclic groups via periodicity.
