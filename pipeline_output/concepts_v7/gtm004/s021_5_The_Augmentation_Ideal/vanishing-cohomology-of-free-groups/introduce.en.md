Corollary 5.6 asserts that free groups have cohomological dimension at most 1: all cohomology and homology groups in degrees $n \geq 2$ vanish for any coefficient modules. The proof follows from the fact that $0 \to IF \to \mathbb{Z}F \to \mathbb{Z} \to 0$ is an $F$-free resolution of the trivial module $\mathbb{Z}$, since the augmentation ideal $IF$ of a free group is itself a free $\mathbb{Z}F$-module. This result is fundamental for applying Theorem 8.1 (the 5-term exact sequence) to free presentations of arbitrary groups.
