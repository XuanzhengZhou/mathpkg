A derivation (also called a crossed homomorphism) is a map from a group $G$ to a $G$-module $A$ satisfying the twisted product rule $d(xy) = dx + x \circ (dy)$. This generalizes the notion of a group homomorphism to the setting where the target carries a non-trivial $G$-action. Derivations form the natural framework for interpreting the first cohomology group $H^1(G, A)$ when $A$ is not necessarily a trivial $G$-module.
