Lemma 1.1 provides the fundamental link between pullback squares and exact sequences: a commutative square is a pullback precisely when the induced map $A \oplus B \to X$ has the pullback object $Y$ as its kernel. This characterisation is the essential technical device that allows one to construct pullbacks of extensions and thereby define the induced maps on $E(A, B)$ studied throughout the chapter.
