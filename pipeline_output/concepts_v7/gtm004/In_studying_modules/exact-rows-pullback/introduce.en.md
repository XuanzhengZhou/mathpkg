Lemma 1.3 is a partial converse to Lemma 1.2(i): given a commutative diagram of extensions (exact rows) where the left-hand map on $B$ is the identity, the right-hand square is automatically a pullback. This structural result guarantees that the pullback construction used to define the induced map $\alpha^*: E(A, B) \to E(A', B)$ is the canonical one, firmly anchoring the functoriality of $E(-, B)$.
