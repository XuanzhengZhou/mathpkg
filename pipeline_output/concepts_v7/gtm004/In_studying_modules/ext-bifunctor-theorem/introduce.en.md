Theorem 1.4 is the structural cornerstone of the extension theory developed in this chapter. It shows that $E(-, -)$ is a bifunctor: contravariant in the first argument (via pullback along maps $A' \to A$) and covariant in the second (via pushout along maps $B \to B'$). The compatibility condition $\beta_* \alpha^* = \alpha^* \beta_*$ — that the two induced maps commute — is verified through a careful three-dimensional diagram chase involving both pullbacks and pushouts. This bifunctoriality is what ultimately allows the identification of $E(A, B)$ with $\operatorname{Ext}^1_A(A, B)$ in the following sections.
