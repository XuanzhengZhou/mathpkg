E(-, -) \text{ is a bifunctor from the category of $\Lambda$-modules to the category of sets. It is contravariant in the first variable and covariant in the second variable.}

\text{For $\alpha: A' \to A$, the induced map $\alpha^*: E(A, B) \to E(A', B)$ is defined by pullback: given a representative $B \xrightarrow{\kappa} E \xrightarrow{\nu} A$, the extension $B \to E^{\alpha} \xrightarrow{\nu'} A'$ is the pullback of $(\alpha, \nu)$.}

\text{For $\beta: B \to B'$, the induced map $\beta_*: E(A, B) \to E(A, B')$ is defined by pushout: given a representative $B \xrightarrow{\kappa} E \xrightarrow{\nu} A$, the extension $B' \to E_{\beta} \xrightarrow{\nu} A$ is the pushout of $(\beta, \kappa)$.}

\text{Moreover, $\beta_* \alpha^* = \alpha^* \beta_*: E(A, B) \to E(A', B')$.}
