Two extensions of $A$ by $B$ are equivalent if there is a homomorphism between the middle modules that commutes with the inclusion of $B$ and the projection onto $A$. The crucial point is that the homomorphism $\xi$ is required to induce the \emph{identity} on both $A$ and $B$, not just an automorphism. This choice — stricter than merely requiring isomorphic middle modules — is essential for the functorial properties developed in Theorem 1.4.
