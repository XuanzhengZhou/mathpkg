A split extension is the trivial case of the extension problem: it corresponds to the direct sum $A \oplus B$ and represents the zero element in the group $E(A, B)$. The existence of splitting maps $\iota_A$ and $\pi_B$ is equivalent to the short exact sequence being split. This concept generalises the familiar idea that not every short exact sequence is split — the non-split extensions are precisely the interesting ones that $E(A, B)$ is designed to classify.
