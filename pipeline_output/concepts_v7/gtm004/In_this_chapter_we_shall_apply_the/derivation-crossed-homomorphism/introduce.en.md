A derivation (or crossed homomorphism) $d: G \to A$ is a map satisfying the twisted product rule $d(xy) = x \circ d(y) + d(x)$. Derivations correspond naturally to $G$-module homomorphisms $\varphi: IG \to A$ via $\varphi(x-1) = d(x)$. This correspondence is the key to interpreting $H^1(G, A)$ in concrete terms: $H^1$ classifies derivations modulo principal derivations (those of the form $x \mapsto xa - a$).
