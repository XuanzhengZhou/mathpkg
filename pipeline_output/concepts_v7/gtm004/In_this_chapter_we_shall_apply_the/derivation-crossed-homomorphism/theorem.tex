Let $A$ be a $G$-module. A map $d: G \to A$ satisfying
$$d(xy) = x \circ d(y) + d(x), \qquad x, y \in G,$$
is called a \emph{derivation} (or \emph{crossed homomorphism}) from $G$ to $A$. The set of all derivations is in natural bijection with $\operatorname{Hom}_G(IG, A)$ via the correspondence $d \mapsto \varphi_d$ where $\varphi_d(x - 1) = d(x)$.
