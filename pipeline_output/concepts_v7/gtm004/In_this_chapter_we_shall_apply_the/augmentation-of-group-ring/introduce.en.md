The augmentation $\varepsilon: \mathbb{Z}G \to \mathbb{Z}$ is the ring homomorphism induced by the trivial group homomorphism $G \to \{1\}$. It sums the integer coefficients of a formal linear combination of group elements. The augmentation and its kernel, the augmentation ideal $IG$, play a central role in the cohomology theory of groups, particularly in identifying $H^0$ and $H_0$.
