Formula (3.4) identifies the zeroth homology group $H_0(G, B)$ with the coinvariant quotient $B_G = B / B \circ IG$. Since $H_0(G, B) = B \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}G} \mathbb{Z}$ and the augmentation ideal $IG$ is the kernel of $\mathbb{Z}G \to \mathbb{Z}$, the tensor product over $\mathbb{Z}G$ with $\mathbb{Z}$ precisely factors out the action of $IG$, yielding the largest trivial quotient of $B$.
