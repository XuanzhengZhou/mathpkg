Formula (3.2) identifies the zeroth cohomology group $H^0(G, A)$ with the subgroup $A^G$ of $G$-invariant elements. A $G$-module homomorphism $\varphi: \mathbb{Z} \to A$ is determined by $\varphi(1)$, which must be fixed by $G$. Thus $H^0$ captures exactly the fixed-point information of the group action, providing a concrete interpretation of the abstract derived functor definition.
