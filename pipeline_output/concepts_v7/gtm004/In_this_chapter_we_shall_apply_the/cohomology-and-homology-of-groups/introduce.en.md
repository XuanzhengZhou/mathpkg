Group cohomology $H^n(G, A)$ and homology $H_n(G, B)$ are defined as the derived functors $\operatorname{Ext}_{\mathbb{Z}G}^n(\mathbb{Z}, -)$ and $\operatorname{Tor}_n^{\mathbb{Z}G}(-, \mathbb{Z})$ respectively. This definition frames group (co)homology within the general machinery of homological algebra: cohomology arises by resolving the trivial module $\mathbb{Z}$, while homology arises by tensoring with $\mathbb{Z}$. The approach unifies algebraic topology and group theory.
