Properties (2.9) and (2.10) describe how group (co)homology behaves functorially with respect to group homomorphisms. A homomorphism $f: G \to G'$ induces natural maps between (co)homology groups, making $H^n$ and $H_n$ bifunctors on the category of pairs $(G, \text{module})$. These induced maps arise from the adjunction between restriction and extension of scalars along $\mathbb{Z}f: \mathbb{Z}G \to \mathbb{Z}G'$.
