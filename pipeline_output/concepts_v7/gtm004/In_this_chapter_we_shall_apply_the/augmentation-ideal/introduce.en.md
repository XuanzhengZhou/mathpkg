The augmentation ideal $IG$ is the kernel of the augmentation map $\varepsilon: \mathbb{Z}G \to \mathbb{Z}$. It consists of all elements of the group ring whose coefficient sum is zero. The ideal $IG$ is fundamental to group cohomology: it provides the standard free resolution of the trivial $G$-module $\mathbb{Z}$, and its structure controls the homology and cohomology groups in low dimensions.
