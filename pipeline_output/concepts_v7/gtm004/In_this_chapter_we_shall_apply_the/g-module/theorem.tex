A (left) $G$-module is an abelian group $A$ together with a group homomorphism $\sigma: G \to \operatorname{Aut} A$. By the universal property of the group ring, $\sigma$ extends uniquely to a ring homomorphism $\sigma': \mathbb{Z}G \to \operatorname{End} A$, making $A$ into a left module over $\mathbb{Z}G$. Conversely, any left $\mathbb{Z}G$-module yields a $G$-module by restricting to the group elements. Thus the categories of $G$-modules and $\mathbb{Z}G$-modules coincide.

A (left) $G$-module is called \emph{trivial} if the structure map $\sigma: G \to \operatorname{Aut} A$ is trivial, i.e., every group element of $G$ acts as the identity on $A$. Every abelian group may be regarded as a trivial left or right $G$-module for any group $G$.
