The coinvariant quotient $B_G$ is the largest quotient of the right $G$-module $B$ on which $G$ acts trivially. It is obtained by factoring out the subgroup generated by all differences $b(x-1)$. Dually to the invariant subgroup $A^G$, the coinvariants $B_G$ identify the zeroth homology group $H_0(G, B)$, providing a concrete algebraic interpretation of the derived functor $\operatorname{Tor}_0^{\mathbb{Z}G}(B, \mathbb{Z})$.
