A functor is called full if it is surjective on hom-sets, and faithful if it is injective on hom-sets. A full embedding is a functor that is simultaneously full, faithful, and injective on objects; its image is then a full subcategory of the target. These notions extend the ideas of full subcategory and embedding to the level of structure-preserving maps between categories.
