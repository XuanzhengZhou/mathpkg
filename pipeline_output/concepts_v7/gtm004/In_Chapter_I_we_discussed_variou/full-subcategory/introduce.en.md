A full subcategory is a subcategory whose morphism sets between its objects are the same as in the ambient category. This means no morphisms are omitted — the subcategory is determined entirely by which objects are included. For example, the category of abelian groups is a full subcategory of the category of groups, while the category of rings with unity is a (non-full) subcategory of the category of rings.
