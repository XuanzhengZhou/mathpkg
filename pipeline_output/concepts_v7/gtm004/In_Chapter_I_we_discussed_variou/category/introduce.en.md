A category is a mathematical structure consisting of a class of objects and, for each ordered pair of objects, a set of morphisms between them, together with an associative composition law and identity morphisms. Categories abstract the notion of sets with structure-preserving functions (groups and homomorphisms, topological spaces and continuous maps, etc.), providing a unified language for describing mathematical systems and their transformations. The formal definition requires three axioms: disjointness of morphism sets across different domain-codomain pairs, associativity of composition, and the existence of identity morphisms that act as neutral elements under composition.
