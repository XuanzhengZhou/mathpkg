A \text{functor} $F: \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{D}$ from a category $\mathfrak{C}$ to a category $\mathfrak{D}$ consists of two assignments:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] To each object $A$ of $\mathfrak{C}$, an object $FA$ of $\mathfrak{D}$.
\item[(ii)] To each morphism $f: A \to B$ in $\mathfrak{C}$, a morphism $Ff: FA \to FB$ in $\mathfrak{D}$.
\end{enumerate}
These assignments must satisfy:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $F(1_A) = 1_{FA}$ for all objects $A$ in $\mathfrak{C}$.
\item[(b)] $F(gf) = (Fg)(Ff)$ for all morphisms $f: A \to B$, $g: B \to C$ in $\mathfrak{C}$.
\end{enumerate}
Such a functor is also called a \text{covariant functor}, to distinguish it from a contravariant functor.
