An isomorphism in a category is a morphism that has a two-sided inverse — there exists a morphism in the opposite direction whose composition on either side yields the identity morphism. The inverse of an isomorphism is itself an isomorphism and is uniquely determined. Isomorphic objects are those connected by an invertible morphism. This definition generalizes the familiar notions of isomorphism across algebraic structures: group isomorphisms, homeomorphisms of spaces, linear isomorphisms, etc.
