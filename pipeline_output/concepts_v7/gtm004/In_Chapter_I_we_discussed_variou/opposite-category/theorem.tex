For any category $\mathfrak{C}$, the \text{opposite category} $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$ is defined by:
\begin{itemize}
\item The objects of $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$ are precisely the objects of $\mathfrak{C}$.
\item $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}(X, Y) = \mathfrak{C}(Y, X)$ for all objects $X, Y$.
\end{itemize}
Composition in $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$ follows naturally: if $f \in \mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}(X, Y) = \mathfrak{C}(Y, X)$ and $g \in \mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}(Y, Z) = \mathfrak{C}(Z, Y)$, then their composite in $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$ is $f \circ g$ as computed in $\mathfrak{C}$.
