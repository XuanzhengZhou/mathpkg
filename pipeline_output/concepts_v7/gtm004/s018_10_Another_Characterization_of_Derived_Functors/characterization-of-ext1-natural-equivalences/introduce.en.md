A homomorphism \(\alpha \colon A' \to A\) induces a natural transformation \(\alpha^* \colon \operatorname{Ext}_\Lambda^1(A, -) \to \operatorname{Ext}_\Lambda^1(A', -)\) by precomposition. This theorem characterizes exactly when \(\alpha^*\) is a natural equivalence: it happens precisely when \(A'\) is isomorphic to \(A \oplus P\) with \(P\) projective, and \(\alpha\) corresponds to the projection map. In other words, the Ext\(^1\) functors are invariant under the addition of projective direct summands. The proof uses the long exact Ext sequence and the criterion that a module is projective if and only if \(\operatorname{Ext}^1\) vanishes in the first variable.
