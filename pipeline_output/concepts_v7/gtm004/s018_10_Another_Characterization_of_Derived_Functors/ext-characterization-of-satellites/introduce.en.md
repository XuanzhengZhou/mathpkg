For any additive covariant functor \(T\) from \(\Lambda\)-modules to abelian groups and any \(\Lambda\)-module \(A\), the set of natural transformations from \(\operatorname{Ext}_\Lambda^q(A, -)\) to \(T\) is naturally isomorphic to the \(q\)-th left satellite \(\tilde{L}_q T\) evaluated at \(A\). This theorem provides a bridge between the Yoneda-style description of Ext via natural transformations and the satellite construction of derived functors. It generalizes the Yoneda lemma by showing that natural transformations out of \(\operatorname{Ext}^q(A, -)\) correspond bijectively to elements of the satellite group. The proof constructs the isomorphism \(\Gamma\) explicitly by evaluating natural transformations on a canonical element in \(\operatorname{Ext}^q(A, R_q)\) arising from a projective resolution.
