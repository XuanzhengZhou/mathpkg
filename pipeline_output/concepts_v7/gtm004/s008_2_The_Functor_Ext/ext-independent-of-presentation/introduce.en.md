Corollary 2.2 of Hilton-Stammbach's treatment of Ext states that the Ext group defined via two different projective presentations of the same module $A$ yields naturally isomorphic abelian groups. This natural equivalence allows one to discard the superscript $\varepsilon$ and speak without ambiguity of $\text{Ext}_\Lambda(A, B)$. The proof follows from Lemma 2.1 by taking $\alpha = 1_A$ and comparing the identity-induced maps between the two presentations.
