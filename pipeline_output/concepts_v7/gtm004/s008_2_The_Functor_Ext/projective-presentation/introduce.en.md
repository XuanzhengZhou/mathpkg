A projective presentation of a $\Lambda$-module $A$ is a short exact sequence $R \to P \to A$ in which the middle term $P$ is projective. This construction provides the foundation for defining the Ext functor: given a projective presentation of $A$, one can associate to any module $B$ the cokernel of the induced map between Hom-groups. Every module possesses a projective presentation, though the choice is not unique — a key fact that makes the well-definedness of Ext a nontrivial theorem.
