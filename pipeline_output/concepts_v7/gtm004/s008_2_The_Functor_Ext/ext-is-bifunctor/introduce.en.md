Theorem 2.3 in Hilton-Stammbach establishes that $\text{Ext}_\Lambda(-, -)$ is a bifunctor from $\Lambda$-modules to abelian groups, contravariant in its first argument and covariant in its second. Given a map $\alpha : A' \to A$, one obtains an induced map $\alpha^* : \text{Ext}_\Lambda(A, B) \to \text{Ext}_\Lambda(A', B)$; given a map $\beta : B \to B'$, one obtains $\beta_* : \text{Ext}_\Lambda(A, B) \to \text{Ext}_\Lambda(A, B')$. The compatibility of these induced maps with composition follows from the naturality established in Lemma 2.1 and Corollary 2.2.
