Theorem 5.1 establishes that limits over a product category $I \times J$ can be computed iteratively, and that the order of iteration does not matter. The proof relies on the canonical identifications of functor categories and the adjunction between diagonal and limit functors. This commutation property is fundamental for handling double limits in spectral sequences and for the results on $E_{\infty,\infty}$ that follow.
