Let $\mathfrak{C}$ be an arbitrary category and $I$ a small connected index category. Let $P : \mathfrak{C} \rightarrow \mathfrak{C}^I$ be the diagonal (constant) functor and suppose that $P$ has a right adjoint $R : \mathfrak{C}^I \rightarrow \mathfrak{C}$. Then, up to natural isomorphism, we may suppose $RP = 1_{\mathfrak{C}}$ and the counit $\delta : PR \rightarrow 1_{\mathfrak{C}^I}$ satisfies $\delta P = 1$, $R\delta = 1$. For any functor $F : I \rightarrow \mathfrak{C}$, the limit of $F$ is defined by
$$
\varprojlim F = R(F),
$$
and the counit $\delta$ yields the canonical morphisms $\delta_i : \varprojlim F \rightarrow F(i)$ for each $i \in I$, completing the description of the limit cone.
