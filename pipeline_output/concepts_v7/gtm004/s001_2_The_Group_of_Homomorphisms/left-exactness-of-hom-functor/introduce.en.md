Theorem 2.1 establishes that the covariant Hom functor $\operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, -)$ is left exact: given an exact sequence $B' \to B \to B''$, applying $\operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, -)$ yields an exact sequence $0 \to \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, B') \to \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, B) \to \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, B'')$. The proof proceeds by showing injectivity of $\mu_{*}$, the inclusion $\operatorname{im} \mu_{*} \subseteq \ker \varepsilon_{*}$, and the reverse inclusion $\ker \varepsilon_{*} \subseteq \operatorname{im} \mu_{*}$. However, $\operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, -)$ is not in general right exact: $\varepsilon_{*}$ need not be surjective even when $\varepsilon$ is.
