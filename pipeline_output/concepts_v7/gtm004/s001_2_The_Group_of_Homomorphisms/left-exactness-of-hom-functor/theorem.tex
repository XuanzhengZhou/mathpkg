Let $B' \xrightarrow{\mu} B \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} B''$ be an exact sequence of $\Lambda$-modules. For every $\Lambda$-module $A$ the induced sequence
$$
0 \longrightarrow \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, B') \xrightarrow{\mu_{*}} \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, B) \xrightarrow{\varepsilon_{*}} \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, B'')
$$
is exact. Moreover, even if $\varepsilon$ is surjective, the induced map $\varepsilon_{*} = \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, \varepsilon)$ is in general not surjective.
