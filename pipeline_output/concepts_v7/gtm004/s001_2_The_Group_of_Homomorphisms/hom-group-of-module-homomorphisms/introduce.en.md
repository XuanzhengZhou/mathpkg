For two modules $A$ and $B$ over a ring $\Lambda$, the set of all $\Lambda$-module homomorphisms from $A$ to $B$ is denoted by $\operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, B)$. This set naturally carries the structure of an abelian group via pointwise addition of homomorphisms. However, it is important to note that $\operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, B)$ is generally not a $\Lambda$-module in any obvious canonical way unless $\Lambda$ is commutative.
