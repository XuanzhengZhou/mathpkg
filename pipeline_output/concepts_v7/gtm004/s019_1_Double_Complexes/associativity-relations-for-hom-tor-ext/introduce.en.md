Theorem 4.3 extends the associativity relations to include the mixed bifunctors $\operatorname{Hom}$ and $\operatorname{Ext}$ alongside $\operatorname{Tor}$. These relations are derived by applying the dual Künneth theorem to the tensor-Hom adjunction, yielding identities that intertwine all four fundamental bifunctors of homological algebra.

The unnatural isomorphism reveals a deep structural relationship: the obstruction to expressing $\operatorname{Hom}(\operatorname{Tor}(A',A), A'')$ purely in terms of simpler pieces is governed by $\operatorname{Ext}$-terms. The natural isomorphism shows that $\operatorname{Ext}$ behaves like an "associative multiplication" on the second argument.
