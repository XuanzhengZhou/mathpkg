Corollary 4.6 extracts two concrete consequences from Theorem 4.3. Part (i) shows that the bifunctor $\operatorname{Ext}$ is symmetric in a precise sense when iterated: swapping the two outer arguments yields a natural isomorphism. This is a remarkable property not obvious from the definition of $\operatorname{Ext}$.

Part (ii) provides an unnatural isomorphism relating $\operatorname{Hom}$ and $\operatorname{Ext}$ in a symmetric fashion. Together, these results reveal the deeper algebraic structure of the standard bifunctors of homological algebra, which the authors note would be more perspicuous in an "infix" notation (writing $A * B$ for $\operatorname{Tor}(A,B)$, etc.).
