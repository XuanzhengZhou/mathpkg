Let $C, D$ be free chain complexes over a principal ideal domain $\Lambda$, and let
$$\alpha_{-p,q} : Z_p(C) \rightarrow \bar{Z}_{p+n}(D)$$
be a family of homomorphisms defined on cycles that vanish on boundaries (hence inducing maps $H_p(C) \rightarrow H_{p+n}(D)$). Define
$$\beta_{-p,q} = \langle \alpha_{-p,q}, (-1)^n \bar{\partial}^{-1} \circ \alpha_{-p+1,q-1} \circ \partial \rangle : C_p = Z_p \oplus Y_p \rightarrow D_{p+n}.$$

Then $\beta = \partial^H(\gamma)$ is a boundary in $\operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(C, D)$, where $\gamma_{-p,q} = \alpha_{-p,q}$ on $Z_p$ and $\gamma_{-p,q} = 0$ on $Y_p$ for $q$ even, with an appropriate extension.
