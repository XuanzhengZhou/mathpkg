The Hom complex $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(D, E)$ is built from a double complex whose $(p,q)$-component consists of homomorphisms from $D_{-p}$ to $E_q$. The grading convention $D_{-p}$ (with a negative sign) ensures that the horizontal differential $\partial'$ has degree $(-1,0)$ as required for a double complex of chains.

This construction is dual to the tensor product of chain complexes and forms the basis for the dual Künneth theorem. The use of $\operatorname{Tot}'$ (direct product) rather than $\operatorname{Tot}$ (direct sum) is essential for the tensor-Hom adjunction to hold at the chain level.
