The Künneth theorem is a fundamental result in homological algebra that expresses the homology of the tensor product of two chain complexes in terms of the homologies of the individual complexes. Over a principal ideal domain, the presence of the $\operatorname{Tor}$ term accounts for torsion phenomena that prevent the homology functor from simply commuting with the tensor product.

The sequence splits (there exists an isomorphism, non-canonical), but the splitting is not natural in $C$ and $D$. This non-naturality means one cannot functorially decompose $H(C \otimes D)$ into the sum of the tensor product term and the Tor term.
