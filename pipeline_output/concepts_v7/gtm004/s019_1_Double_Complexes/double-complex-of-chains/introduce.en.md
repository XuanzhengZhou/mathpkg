A double complex of chains is a bigraded generalization of an ordinary chain complex, equipped with two differentials $\partial'$ (horizontal) and $\partial''$ (vertical) that anticommute: $\partial''\partial' + \partial'\partial'' = 0$. This structure arises naturally when combining two chain complexes, for example via tensor product or Hom functor.

The bigrading means each component $B_{p,q}$ has a bidegree $(p,q)$, and the differentials lower the first and second index respectively. Double complexes are the central object of study in Chapter V, serving as the algebraic foundation for spectral sequences.
