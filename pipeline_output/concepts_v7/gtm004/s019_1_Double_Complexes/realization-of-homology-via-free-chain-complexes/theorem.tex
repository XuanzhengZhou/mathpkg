Let $\Lambda$ be a principal ideal domain and let $\{H_p\}_{p \in \mathbb{Z}}$ be a graded $\Lambda$-module. Then there exists a free chain complex $C$ over $\Lambda$ such that $H_p(C) \cong H_p$ for all $p$.

Explicitly, let $0 \rightarrow R_p \rightarrow F_p \rightarrow H_p \rightarrow 0$ be a free presentation of $H_p$ (with $F_p$ free). Set
$$C_p = F_p \oplus R_{p-1},$$
with differential $\partial(x, y) = (y, 0)$ for $x \in F_p$, $y \in R_{p-1}$. Then $\partial^2 = 0$, $Z_p(C) = F_p$, $B_p(C) = R_p$, and $H_p(C) \cong H_p$.
