Proposition 2.2 shows that every graded module over a principal ideal domain can be realized as the homology of some free chain complex. The construction is explicit: take a free presentation of each $H_p$, form $C_p = F_p \oplus R_{p-1}$, and define the differential to map $(x, y)$ to $(y, 0)$.

This result is used in the proof of the Künneth theorem to handle the general (flat) case by reduction to the free case, and also to construct counterexamples demonstrating the non-naturality of the splitting.
