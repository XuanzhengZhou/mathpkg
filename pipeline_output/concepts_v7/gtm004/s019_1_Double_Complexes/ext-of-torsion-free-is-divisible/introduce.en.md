Corollary 4.4 is an elegant consequence of the associativity relations: since $\operatorname{Tor}(A', A) = 0$ when $A$ is torsion-free, the natural isomorphism $\operatorname{Ext}(\operatorname{Tor}(A', A), A'') \cong \operatorname{Ext}(A', \operatorname{Ext}(A, A''))$ forces $\operatorname{Ext}(A', \operatorname{Ext}(A, B)) = 0$ for all $A', B$, which is exactly the condition that $\operatorname{Ext}(A, B)$ is injective (divisible).

This result generalizes the familiar fact that $\operatorname{Ext}(\mathbb{Q}, B)$ is divisible, and shows that $\operatorname{Ext}(A, -)$ is a functor from abelian groups to divisible groups whenever $A$ is torsion-free.
