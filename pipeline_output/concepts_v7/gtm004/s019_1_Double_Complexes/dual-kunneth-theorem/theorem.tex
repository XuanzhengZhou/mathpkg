Let $\Lambda$ be a principal ideal domain and let $C, D$ be chain complexes over $\Lambda$ with $C$ free. Then there is a natural short exact sequence
$$0 \rightarrow \prod_{q-p=n+1} \operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}^1(H_p(C), H_q(D)) \rightarrow H_n(\operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(C, D)) \xrightarrow{\zeta} \prod_{q-p=n} \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(H_p(C), H_q(D)) \rightarrow 0,$$
where $\zeta$ assigns to a cycle $f \in \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(C, D)$ the induced map $f_*$ on homology.

Moreover, the sequence splits, but \emph{not naturally} in $C$.
