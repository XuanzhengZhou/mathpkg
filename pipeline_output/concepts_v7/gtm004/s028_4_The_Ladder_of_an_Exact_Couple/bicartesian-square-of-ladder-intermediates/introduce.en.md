Proposition 4.2 establishes that the square formed by the four intermediate objects arising from applying the $Q$-processes in both orders — $B$, $B_{0,1}$, $B_1$, and $B_{1,0}$ — is bicartesian, meaning it is both a pullback and a pushout simultaneously. This structural property of the ladder construction reflects the fact that $B_{0,1} \cong \gamma^{-1}(C_1)$ and $B_{1,0} \cong B/\beta\sigma^{-1}(0)$, so the square is essentially the canonical comparison between subobject formation and quotient formation, which is always bicartesian in any abelian category.
