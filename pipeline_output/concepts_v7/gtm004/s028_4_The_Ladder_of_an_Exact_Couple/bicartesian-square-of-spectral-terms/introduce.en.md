Theorem 4.6 states that the square formed by four consecutive $E_{m,n}$ terms in the ladder is bicartesian — both a pullback and a pushout. This structural result provides the precise categorical relationship between the terms on successive rungs and columns, and it is fundamental for describing $E_{\infty}$ as a double limit. The bicartesian nature of this square means one can pass from $E_{m,n}$ to $E_{m+1,n+1}$ by either moving horizontally then vertically, or vertically then horizontally, with the same result.
