To adapt the ladder construction to (bi)graded exact couples — where the differential $\alpha$ carries a nonzero degree — one must factor $\alpha$ as $\varrho\omega\sigma$ with $\varrho$ and $\sigma$ degree-preserving and $\omega$ an isomorphism that absorbs the degree shift. This factorization ensures that all vertical morphisms in the ladder remain degree-preserving, at the cost of introducing an auxiliary pair of objects $D'_n$ and $D''_n$ linked by the degree-carrying isomorphism $\omega_n$. The standard convention is then to identify $D_n$ with $D''_n$ and modify $\beta_n$, $\gamma_n$ accordingly via $\omega_n^{-1}$, yielding the familiar graded exact couple iteration formulas for the spectral sequence.
