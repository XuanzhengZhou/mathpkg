Theorem 4.5 is the main structural result of this section: the ladder sequence $D_m \to E_{m,n} \to D_n \to D_n$ is exact at each node. This generalizes the notion of a derived couple by arranging all the $E$-terms into a two-parameter family $E_{m,n}$ indexed by the rungs $m$ and $n$, each sitting between $D_m$ and $D_n$ in a short exact-like fragment. The explicit description $E_{m,n} = \gamma^{-1}(\alpha^n D)/\beta\alpha^{-m}(0)$ shows how each term is obtained by pulling back the $n$-fold image of $\alpha$ and factoring out the $m$-fold preimage of zero. The diagonal case $m=n$ recovers the $n$th derived couple $E_n$.
