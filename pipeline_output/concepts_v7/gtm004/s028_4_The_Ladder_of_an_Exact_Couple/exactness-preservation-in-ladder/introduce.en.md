Theorem 4.4 states a crucial property of the $Q$-process ladder construction: if the original sequence $A \to B \to C$ is exact at $B$, then applying any combination of $Q^{\varepsilon}$ (pullback) and $Q_{\sigma}$ (pushout) processes preserves exactness in all resulting rows. This is essential for the study of exact couples because it guarantees that the derived couples remain exact after iteration, ensuring that each page of the spectral sequence arises from an exact couple and the associated long exact sequences remain valid.
