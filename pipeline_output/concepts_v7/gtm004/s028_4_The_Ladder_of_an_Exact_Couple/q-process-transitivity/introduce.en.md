Proposition 4.3 establishes the transitivity of the $Q$-processes under composition of monomorphisms (resp. epimorphisms). If a composite $\gamma\beta$ factors through a composition of monomorphisms $\varrho\varrho_1$, then applying the $Q^{\varrho}$-process and then the $Q^{\varrho_1}$-process yields the same result as applying the $Q^{\varrho\varrho_1}$-process directly. The dual statement holds for $Q_{\sigma}$ under composition of epimorphisms. These identities, together with the commutativity in Theorem 4.1, allow iterated $Q$-processes to be composed and rearranged freely, forming the algebraic foundation for constructing the spectral sequence ladder.
