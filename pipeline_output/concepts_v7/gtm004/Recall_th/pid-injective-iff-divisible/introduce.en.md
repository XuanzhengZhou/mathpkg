Over a principal ideal domain, the injective modules are precisely the divisible modules. This is the injective analogue of the fact that projective modules over a PID are free (Corollary 5.2). The proof that divisible implies injective uses Zorn's Lemma: one constructs a maximal extension of a given homomorphism into the divisible module, then shows the domain must be the whole module. The converse — injective implies divisible — follows directly from the definition of injectivity. This characterization gives a complete classification of injective modules over PIDs, paralleling the classification of projectives as free modules.
