A left $\mathfrak{g}$-module $A$ is a $K$-vector space $A$ together with a homomorphism of Lie algebras

$$\varrho: \mathfrak{g} \to L(\operatorname{End}_K A),$$

where $L(\operatorname{End}_K A)$ denotes the Lie algebra associated to the associative $K$-algebra $\operatorname{End}_K A$ via the commutator bracket. Writing $x \circ a = \varrho(x)(a)$ for $x \in \mathfrak{g}$, $a \in A$, the module condition is

$$[x, y] \circ a = x \circ (y \circ a) - y \circ (x \circ a), \quad x, y \in \mathfrak{g}, \; a \in A.$$

By the universal property of $U\mathfrak{g}$, this is equivalent to giving $A$ the structure of a left $U\mathfrak{g}$-module.
