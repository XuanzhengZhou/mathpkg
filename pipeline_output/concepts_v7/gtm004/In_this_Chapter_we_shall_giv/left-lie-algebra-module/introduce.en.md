A left $\mathfrak{g}$-module is a vector space on which a Lie algebra acts by linear transformations, satisfying a compatibility condition with the Lie bracket. This is exactly a Lie algebra representation. By the universal property of the enveloping algebra, $\mathfrak{g}$-modules are equivalent to $U\mathfrak{g}$-modules, allowing the entire homological algebra of associative rings to be applied to Lie algebra representations. Lie algebra cohomology groups $H^n(\mathfrak{g}, A)$ are defined as derived functors on the category of $\mathfrak{g}$-modules.
