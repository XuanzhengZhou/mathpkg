Let $\mathfrak{g}$ be a Lie algebra over a field $K$, and let $U\mathfrak{g}$ be its universal enveloping algebra with canonical map $i: \mathfrak{g} \to U\mathfrak{g}$. Let $\{x_j\}_{j \in J}$ be a totally ordered basis of $\mathfrak{g}$. Then the elements

$$i(x_{j_1})^{e_1} \, i(x_{j_2})^{e_2} \, \cdots \, i(x_{j_k})^{e_k},$$

where $j_1 < j_2 < \dots < j_k$ and $e_i \geq 0$, together with $1$, form a basis of $U\mathfrak{g}$.
