The Poincaré–Birkhoff–Witt (PBW) theorem provides an explicit vector space basis for the universal enveloping algebra of a Lie algebra. It shows that $U\mathfrak{g}$ is isomorphic as a graded vector space to the symmetric algebra $S(\mathfrak{g})$, resolving the apparent complexity of the quotient defining $U\mathfrak{g}$. The theorem is foundational for Lie algebra cohomology and representation theory, as it guarantees that the canonical map from $\mathfrak{g}$ to $U\mathfrak{g}$ is injective.
