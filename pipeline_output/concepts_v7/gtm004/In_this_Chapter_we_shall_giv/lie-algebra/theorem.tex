A Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ over a field $K$ is a vector space over $K$ together with a bilinear operation $[{-},{-}]: \mathfrak{g} \times \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{g}$ satisfying:

$$[x, x] = 0, \quad x \in \mathfrak{g};$$

$$[[x, y], z] + [[y, z], x] + [[z, x], y] = 0, \quad x, y, z \in \mathfrak{g}.$$

The second identity is called the Jacobi identity. Note that $[x, x] = 0$ and bilinearity imply $[x, y] = -[y, x]$ for all $x, y \in \mathfrak{g}$.
