A Lie algebra is a vector space equipped with a bilinear bracket operation that is alternating ($[x, x] = 0$) and satisfies the Jacobi identity. This structure generalizes the commutator bracket of an associative algebra and forms the algebraic foundation for studying continuous symmetries. The Jacobi identity plays a role analogous to the associative law, ensuring the bracket behaves like a derivation. Every associative algebra becomes a Lie algebra under the commutator $[x, y] = xy - yx$.
