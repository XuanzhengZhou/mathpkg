A Lie ideal is a Lie subalgebra closed under bracketing with arbitrary elements from the ambient Lie algebra, analogous to a two-sided ideal in ring theory or a normal subgroup in group theory. Lie ideals are precisely the kernels of Lie algebra homomorphisms, making them essential for constructing quotient Lie algebras and understanding the first isomorphism theorem in the category of Lie algebras.
