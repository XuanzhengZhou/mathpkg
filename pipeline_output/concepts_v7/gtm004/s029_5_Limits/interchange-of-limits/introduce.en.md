The interchange of limits theorem states that the operation of taking limits commutes: a double limit $\lim_I \lim_J$ over two index categories is canonically isomorphic to the limit over the product category $\lim_{I \times J}$. This follows from the exponential law for functor categories, $\mathfrak{C}^{I \times J} \cong (\mathfrak{C}^I)^J$, and the fact that the right adjoints to the diagonal functors compose as shown. The result is essential for manipulating iterated limits in homological algebra and spectral sequence theory.
