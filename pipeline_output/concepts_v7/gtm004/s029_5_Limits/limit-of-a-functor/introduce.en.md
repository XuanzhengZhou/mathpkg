The limit of a functor is a fundamental construction in category theory, defined via the right adjoint to the diagonal functor. Given a small category $I$ and a functor $F: I \to \mathfrak{C}$, the limit $\lim F$ is the object $R(F)$ universal among cones over $F$. This formulation unifies products, equalizers, pullbacks, and inverse limits under a single categorical notion. The counit $\delta$ of the adjunction provides the structure morphisms $\delta_i: \lim F \to F(i)$ that project the limit onto each component.
