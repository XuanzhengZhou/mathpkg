Theorem 5.3 is the fundamental limit theorem for spectral sequences arising from exact couples. It establishes that the limit term $E_{\infty,\infty}$ can be computed as the iterated limit $\lim_m \lim_n E_{m,n}$, and that the two limiting processes commute. This justifies the notation $E_{\infty,\infty}$ and unifies the descriptions of $E_{m,\infty}$, $E_{\infty}$, and $E_{\infty,n}$ given earlier in terms of the $Q$-process. The theorem further provides exact sequences characterizing these limit terms, which replace the original exact couple structure in the limit. Crucially, the proof uses Theorem 5.2 and its dual to handle the limit and colimit processes separately.
