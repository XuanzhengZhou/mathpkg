A chain complex is a sequence of modules connected by boundary homomorphisms of degree $-1$ satisfying $\partial^2 = 0$. This fundamental algebraic structure encodes the condition that the image of each boundary map is contained in the kernel of the next, enabling the definition of homology. Chain complexes form the basic objects of study in homological algebra, arising from projective/injective resolutions, simplicial complexes in topology, and various algebraic constructions.
