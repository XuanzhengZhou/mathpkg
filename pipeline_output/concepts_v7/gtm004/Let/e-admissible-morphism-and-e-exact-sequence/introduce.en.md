In relative homological algebra, the notion of exactness is refined by restricting admissible epimorphisms to a closed class $\mathcal{E}$. An $\mathcal{E}$-exact sequence is one where all morphisms factor appropriately through $\mathcal{E}$. This relativization allows the development of a full theory of derived functors in contexts lacking enough absolute projectives.
