Let $A$ be a left $\Lambda$-module and let $G$ be an abelian group. Regard $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\Lambda, G)$ as a left $\Lambda$-module via the right $\Lambda$-module structure of $\Lambda$. Then there is an isomorphism of abelian groups
$$\eta_A : \operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(A, \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\Lambda, G)) \xrightarrow{\cong} \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, G)$$
natural in $A$. For every $\Lambda$-module homomorphism $\alpha : A \to B$, the diagram
\[
\begin{CD}
\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(B, \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\Lambda, G)) @>{\alpha^*}>> \operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(A, \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\Lambda, G)) \\
@V{\eta_B}VV @V{\eta_A}VV \\
\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(B, G) @>{\alpha^*}>> \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, G)
\end{CD}
\]
is commutative.
