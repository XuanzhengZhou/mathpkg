The injective envelope (or injective hull) of a module $A$ is the unique (up to isomorphism) maximal essential extension of $A$. It is simultaneously an essential extension of $A$ and an injective module. Every module embeds into its injective envelope, and any injective module containing $A$ contains a copy of the injective envelope. The injective envelope generalizes the notion of divisible hull for abelian groups.
