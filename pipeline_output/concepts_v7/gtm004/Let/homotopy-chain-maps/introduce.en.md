A homotopy between two chain maps $\varphi$ and $\psi$ is an algebraic analogue of the topological notion: it provides a family of maps $\Sigma_n$ of degree $+1$ whose boundary with $\partial$ equals the difference $\psi - \varphi$. This relation is fundamental because homotopic chain maps induce the same map in homology. The homotopy category, obtained by identifying homotopic chain maps, is the proper setting for derived functors and homological algebra.
