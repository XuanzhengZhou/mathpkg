Proposition 9.1 gives an element-wise criterion for essential extensions: a module $B$ is an essential extension of its submodule $A$ exactly when every nonzero element of $B$ has a nonzero $\Lambda$-multiple lying in $A$. This practical characterization is extensively used to verify essentiality and to construct maximal essential extensions via Zorn's Lemma.
