This proposition is the direct consequence of Theorem 4.1 and justifies the use of arbitrary projective resolutions in defining derived functors. Any two projective resolutions are not just quasi-isomorphic but homotopy equivalent, and the homotopy equivalence between them is canonical (unique up to homotopy).
