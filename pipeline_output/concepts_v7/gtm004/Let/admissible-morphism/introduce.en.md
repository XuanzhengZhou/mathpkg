An $\mathcal{E}$-admissible morphism is one whose epimorphic factor belongs to the given projective class $\mathcal{E}$. An $\mathcal{E}$-exact sequence is an exact sequence built from $\mathcal{E}$-admissible morphisms. These notions provide the exact structure for relative homological algebra, replacing ordinary exact sequences with $\mathcal{E}$-exact ones throughout the theory.
