A cofree module is defined as a direct product of copies of the module $\Lambda^* = \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\Lambda, \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z})$, where $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ is the divisible abelian group of rationals mod integers. The notion is the dual of a free module: just as every module is a quotient of a free module, every module can be embedded into a cofree module. Cofree modules play the role of injective objects — they are injective by Theorem 8.2.
