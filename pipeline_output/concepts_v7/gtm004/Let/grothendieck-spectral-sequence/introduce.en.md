Theorem 9.3 is one of the most important results in homological algebra. It relates the right derived functors of a composition $G \circ F$ to the derived functors of the individual functors $F$ and $G$, under the acyclicity condition that $F$ sends injectives to $G$-acyclic objects. The $E_1$ page involves $R^p G \circ R^{q-p} F$, and the spectral sequence converges to $R^q(GF)$. Major applications include the Lyndon-Hochschild-Serre spectral sequence in group cohomology and the Hochschild-Serre spectral sequence in Lie algebra cohomology.
