Theorem 9.5 is the Lyndon-Hochschild-Serre spectral sequence, a fundamental tool in group cohomology. For a group extension $N \to K \to Q$, it relates the cohomology of $K$ to the cohomology of the normal subgroup $N$ and the quotient $Q$. It is obtained by applying the Grothendieck spectral sequence to the functors $F(A) = A^N$ ($N$-invariants) and $G(B) = B^Q$ ($Q$-invariants), noting that $A^K = (A^N)^Q$.
