Let $\{A_j\}_{j \in J}$ be a family of $\Lambda$-modules. Let $\iota_k : A_k \to \bigoplus_{j \in J} A_j$ be the injection defined by $\iota_k(a_k) = (b_j)_{j \in J}$ with $b_j = 0$ for $j \neq k$ and $b_k = a_k$. If $M$ is a $\Lambda$-module and $\{\psi_j : A_j \to M\}_{j \in J}$ is a family of $\Lambda$-module homomorphisms, then there exists a unique homomorphism $\psi = \langle \psi_j \rangle : \bigoplus_{j \in J} A_j \to M$ such that $\psi \iota_k = \psi_k$ for every $k \in J$.
