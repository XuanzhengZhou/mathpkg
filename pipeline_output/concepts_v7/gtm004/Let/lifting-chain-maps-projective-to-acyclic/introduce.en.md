This fundamental theorem (the Comparison Theorem for resolutions) states that chain maps lift uniquely up to homotopy from a projective complex to an acyclic complex. It is the cornerstone for proving that derived functors are well-defined independent of the chosen resolution, and that any two projective resolutions are homotopy equivalent.
