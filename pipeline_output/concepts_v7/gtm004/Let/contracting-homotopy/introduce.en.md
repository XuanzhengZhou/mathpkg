A contracting homotopy provides an explicit chain homotopy equivalence between the identity and the zero map on a complex, which forces all homology groups to vanish. The existence of a contracting homotopy is the standard method for proving a complex is exact. The equations $\partial \Sigma + \Sigma \partial = 1$ are a homological version of the familiar formula for the derivative of a homotopy in topology.
