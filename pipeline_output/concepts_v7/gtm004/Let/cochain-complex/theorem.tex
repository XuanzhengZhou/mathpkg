A \emph{cochain complex} $C = \{C^n, \delta^n\}$ over $\Lambda$ is a graded module $\{C^n\}_{n \in \mathbb{Z}}$ together with an endomorphism $\delta : C \to C$ of degree $+1$ (coboundary) satisfying $\delta \delta = 0$. The $n$-th \emph{cohomology module} is
$$H^n(C) = \frac{\ker \delta^n}{\operatorname{im} \delta^{n-1}}.$$
Given a chain complex $\{C_n, \partial_n\}$, setting $D^n = C_{-n}$ and $\delta^n = \partial_{-n}$ yields a cochain complex, and vice versa.
