Corollary 9.3 establishes the uniqueness, up to isomorphism, of the maximal essential extension of a module $A$ inside any injective module. This unique object is called the injective envelope (or injective hull) of $A$. The result is crucial because it provides a minimal injective extension that is well-defined up to canonical isomorphism.
