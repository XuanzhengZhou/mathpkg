Given a double complex, the two natural filtrations (by rows and by columns) give rise to two spectral sequences. The $E_0$ page computes row/column homology, and the $E_1$ page computes the homology with respect to the other differential. These spectral sequences converge to the homology of the total complex, providing a powerful computational tool.
