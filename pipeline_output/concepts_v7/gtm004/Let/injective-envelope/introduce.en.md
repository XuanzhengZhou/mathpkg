The injective envelope of a module $A$ is the unique (up to isomorphism) minimal injective extension of $A$. It is simultaneously an essential extension and an injective module. This concept is fundamental in homological algebra, providing a canonical injective object associated to each module, analogous to the role of the algebraic closure for fields.
