Let $C = \{C_n, \partial_n\}$ be a chain complex. The condition $\partial \partial = 0$ implies that $\operatorname{im} \partial_{n+1} \subseteq \ker \partial_n$ for all $n \in \mathbb{Z}$. The $n$-th \textit{homology module} of $C$ is defined as
$$H_n(C) = \ker \partial_n / \operatorname{im} \partial_{n+1}, \quad n \in \mathbb{Z}.$$
Elements of $\ker \partial_n$ are called $n$-\textit{cycles} ($Z_n(C)$); elements of $\operatorname{im} \partial_{n+1}$ are called $n$-\textit{boundaries} ($B_n(C)$).
