An object $P$ is projective relative to a specific epimorphism $\varepsilon$ if every morphism from $P$ to the target $C$ lifts through $\varepsilon$ to $B$. This relativizes the standard notion of projectivity: instead of requiring lifting against every epimorphism, we only require lifting against $\varepsilon$. The full class of $\mathcal{E}$-projective objects consists of those projective against all $\varepsilon \in \mathcal{E}$.
