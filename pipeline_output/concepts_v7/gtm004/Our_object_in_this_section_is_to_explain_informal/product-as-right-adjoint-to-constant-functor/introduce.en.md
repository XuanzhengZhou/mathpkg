Products and coproducts are special cases of a general pattern: limits are right adjoints to constant functors, and colimits are left adjoints. This insight unifies the theory of universal constructions — every limit (product, pull-back, equalizer, kernel) is a right adjoint, while every colimit (coproduct, push-out, coequalizer, cokernel) is a left adjoint. Theorem 7.7 (right adjoints preserve limits) then explains why limits commute with limits.
