Adjoint functors, introduced by D. M. Kan, are one of the most fundamental notions in category theory. The definition captures the idea of a "free-forgetful" relationship — the free functor is left adjoint to the underlying (forgetful) functor. Many constructions in homological algebra that were originally proved in an ad hoc fashion can be systematically explained via adjoint functors. The adjugant $\eta$ provides a natural bijection between morphism sets $\mathfrak{D}(FX, Y)$ and $\mathfrak{C}(X, GY)$.
