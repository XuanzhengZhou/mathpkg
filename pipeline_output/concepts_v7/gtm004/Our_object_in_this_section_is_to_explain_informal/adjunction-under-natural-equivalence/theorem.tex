Let $\eta: F \dashv G$ and let $\theta: G \to G'$ be a natural equivalence. Define $\eta': F \dashv G'$ by $\eta'(\varphi) = \theta_Y \circ \eta(\varphi)$ for $\varphi: FX \to Y$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] If $\varepsilon, \delta$ are the unit and counit for $\eta$, then the unit and counit for $\eta'$ are given by $\varepsilon' = \theta F \circ \varepsilon$ and $\delta' = \delta \circ F \bar{\theta}$, where $\bar{\theta}$ is the inverse of $\theta$;
\item[(ii)] $\theta_Y \circ \eta(\varphi) = \eta'(\varphi)$ for any $\varphi: FX \to Y$.
\end{enumerate}
Conversely, if $\theta: G \to G'$ is a natural equivalence and $\eta: F \dashv G$, then $\eta'$ defined as above yields $F \dashv G'$, with $\varepsilon'$ and $\delta'$ as in (i).
