Let $\{f_i: X_i \to Y_i\}_{i \in I}$ be a family of morphisms in $\mathfrak{C}$ indexed by the set $I$. There exists a unique morphism $\prod_i f_i: \prod_i X_i \to \prod_i Y_i$ such that
$$p_i \left( \prod_i f_i \right) = f_i p_i$$
for all $i \in I$, where $p_i$ denotes the projection onto the $i$-th factor. Moreover, if $\mathfrak{C}$ admits products for all families indexed by $I$, then $\prod_i$ is a functor
$$\prod_i : \mathfrak{C}^I \to \mathfrak{C}.$$
