When a category has all products indexed by a set $I$, the assignment sending each $I$-indexed family to its product extends functorially: one can take the product of morphisms componentwise. This makes $\prod_i$ a functor from the $I$-fold product category $\mathfrak{C}^I$ to $\mathfrak{C}$. The morphism product is uniquely characterized by the commuting condition with the projections.
