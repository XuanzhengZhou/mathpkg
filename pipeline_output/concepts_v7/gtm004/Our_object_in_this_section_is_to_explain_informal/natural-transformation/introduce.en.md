A natural transformation provides a way to compare two functors by giving a family of morphisms between their images that is compatible with the functors' action on morphisms. This concept was the original motivation for Eilenberg and MacLane to introduce the language of categories and functors. Natural transformations can be composed (both vertically and horizontally) and they serve as the morphisms in functor categories. They capture the intuitive notion of a "canonical map" in many mathematical contexts, such as the Hurewicz homomorphism from homotopy to homology.
