Corollary 4.2 of the Yoneda Lemma shows that the functor $A \mapsto \mathfrak{C}(A,-)$ embeds the opposite category $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$ fully and faithfully into the presheaf category $\mathfrak{S}^{\mathfrak{C}}$. This means every category can be regarded as a full subcategory of a functor category on sets, making the Yoneda embedding a powerful tool for studying arbitrary categories using set-valued functors. It justifies the common technique of "probing" objects by morphisms into them.
