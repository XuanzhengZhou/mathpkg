A long exact sequence is the fundamental algebraic structure of homological algebra. The exactness condition — that the image of each map equals the kernel of the next — can be expressed purely categorically using the canonical epi-mono factorization. The definition in terms of kernel-cokernel relationships ensures that exactness is a self-dual notion. Long exact sequences arise universally from chain complexes and are central to the machinery of derived functors.
