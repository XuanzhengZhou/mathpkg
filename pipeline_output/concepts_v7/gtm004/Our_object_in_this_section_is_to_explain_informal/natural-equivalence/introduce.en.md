A natural equivalence (or natural isomorphism) is the correct notion of "sameness" between functors, stronger than a mere isomorphism of functor categories. When two functors are naturally equivalent, they are indistinguishable from the perspective of category theory. Natural equivalences are closed under composition and inversion, and they form the basis for defining equivalence of categories.
