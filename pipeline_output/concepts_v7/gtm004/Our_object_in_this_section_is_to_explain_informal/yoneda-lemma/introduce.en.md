The Yoneda Lemma is arguably the most fundamental result in category theory. It establishes that every natural transformation from a representable functor $\mathfrak{C}(A,-)$ to any set-valued functor $F$ is completely determined by where it sends the identity morphism $1_A$. This yields a bijection $F(A) \cong \operatorname{Nat}(\mathfrak{C}(A,-), F)$. The lemma underlies the Yoneda embedding, which embeds any category fully and faithfully into its presheaf category, and has far-reaching consequences in algebraic topology, algebraic geometry, and homological algebra.
