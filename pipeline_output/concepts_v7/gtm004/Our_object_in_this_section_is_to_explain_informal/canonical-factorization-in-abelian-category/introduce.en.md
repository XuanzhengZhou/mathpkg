In any abelian category, every morphism factors uniquely (up to unique isomorphism) as an epimorphism followed by a monomorphism — this is the image factorization. The factorization is constructed via the kernel-cokernel sequence: take the cokernel of the kernel to get the epimorphism, then factor through. This result is fundamental to homological algebra, enabling the definition of exact sequences, homology objects, and diagram chases in abstract abelian categories.
