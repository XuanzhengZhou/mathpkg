In categories where the underlying functor preserves epimorphisms (i.e., epimorphisms are surjective on underlying sets), every free object is projective. This applies to abelian groups, modules over a ring, and groups. The result follows from Proposition 10.2 and the fact that sets are projective. However, in categories like integral domains where non-surjective epimorphisms exist, the hypothesis fails and free objects need not be projective.
