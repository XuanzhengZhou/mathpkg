In a category with a zero object, pull-backs interact cleanly with kernels: the kernel of one leg of a pull-back square determines (via composition with the other leg) the kernel of the opposite morphism. This theorem is a very special case of the general result that limits commute with limits, and it is essential for diagram chases in homological algebra. The proof uses the pull-back universal property and the monic nature of the maps $\{\alpha, \beta\}$.
