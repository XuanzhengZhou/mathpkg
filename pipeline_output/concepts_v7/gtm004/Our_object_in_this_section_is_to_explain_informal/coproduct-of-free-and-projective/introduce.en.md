Coproducts respect both freeness and projectivity. That a coproduct of free objects is free follows because left adjoints preserve coproducts. That a coproduct of projective objects is projective can be seen directly from the definition: given a lifting problem for the coproduct, one solves it componentwise using the projectivity of each summand, then assembles the lifts via the universal property of the coproduct. Both results are essential for constructing projective resolutions.
