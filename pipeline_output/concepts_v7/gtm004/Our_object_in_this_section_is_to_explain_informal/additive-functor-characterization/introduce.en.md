A functor between additive categories is additive precisely when it preserves the additive structure — equivalently, it preserves finite biproducts or acts as a group homomorphism on hom-sets. The proof that (i) implies (ii) requires showing $F$ maps zero objects to zero objects, which follows from the fact that a zero object is the sum of itself with any object. Additive functors are the proper notion of "linear" functors and are fundamental in homological algebra.
