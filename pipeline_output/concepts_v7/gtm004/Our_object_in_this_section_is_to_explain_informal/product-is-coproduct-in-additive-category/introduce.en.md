A key structural fact: in an additive category, the product $A_1 \oplus A_2$ is simultaneously the coproduct, with injections $i_1, i_2$ and projections $p_1, p_2$ related by $p_j i_k = \delta_{jk}$ and $i_1 p_1 + i_2 p_2 = 1$. Such an object is called a biproduct (or direct sum). This unification of product and coproduct is what makes linear algebra in additive categories possible and is the defining feature that distinguishes additive categories from general categories.
