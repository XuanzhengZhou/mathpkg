Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an additive category and let $i_1 = \{1, 0\}: A_1 \to A_1 \oplus A_2$ and $i_2 = \{0, 1\}: A_2 \to A_1 \oplus A_2$. Then $(A_1 \oplus A_2; i_1, i_2)$ is the coproduct of $A_1$ and $A_2$ in $\mathfrak{A}$. Given $\varphi_i: A_i \to B$ ($i = 1, 2$), the unique morphism $\langle \varphi_1, \varphi_2 \rangle: A_1 \oplus A_2 \to B$ satisfying $\langle \varphi_1, \varphi_2 \rangle i_j = \varphi_j$ is given by
$$\langle \varphi_1, \varphi_2 \rangle = \varphi_1 p_1 + \varphi_2 p_2,$$
where $p_1, p_2$ are the product projections.
