A projective object satisfies a universal lifting property: any morphism from it to a quotient can be lifted through the quotient map. In module categories, projective objects are precisely the projective modules (direct summands of free modules). The notion generalizes to arbitrary categories and is fundamental to homological algebra — projective objects are used to construct projective resolutions, which in turn define derived functors.
