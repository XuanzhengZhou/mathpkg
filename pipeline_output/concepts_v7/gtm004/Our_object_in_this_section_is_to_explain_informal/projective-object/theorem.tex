An object $P$ in a category $\mathfrak{C}$ is called \text{projective} if, given any diagram
$$\begin{array}{ccc}
& P & \\
& {\scriptstyle\varphi}\downarrow & \\
A & \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} & B
\end{array}$$
in $\mathfrak{C}$ with $\varepsilon$ epimorphic, there exists a morphism $\psi: P \to A$ such that $\varepsilon \psi = \varphi$.
