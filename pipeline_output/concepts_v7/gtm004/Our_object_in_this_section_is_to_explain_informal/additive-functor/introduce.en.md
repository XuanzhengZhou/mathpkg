An additive functor is the categorical analogue of a linear map: it respects the additive (abelian group) structure on hom-sets and preserves finite direct sums. In homological algebra, all important functors (Hom, tensor product, derived functors) are additive. The condition that an additive functor preserves zero objects is a consequence: the zero object is characterized by being the sum of itself with any object.
