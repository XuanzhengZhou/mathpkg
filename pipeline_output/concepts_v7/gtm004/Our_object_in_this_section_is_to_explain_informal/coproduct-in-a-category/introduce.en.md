The coproduct (or categorical sum) is the dual of the product, obtained by reversing all arrows in the definition. In sets it is the disjoint union; in groups it is the free product; in modules it is the direct sum. The morphisms $q_i$ are called injections (or coprojections), and a family of morphisms $\{f_i: X_i \to Y\}$ induces a unique morphism $\langle f_i \rangle$ out of the coproduct. All propositions about products dualize to coproducts.
