Let $\{X_i\}$, $i \in I$, be a family of objects of the category $\mathfrak{C}$ indexed by the set $I$. Then $(X; q_i)$ is a \text{coproduct} of the objects $X_i$ in $\mathfrak{C}$ if and only if it is a product of the objects $X_i$ in $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$. In $\mathfrak{C}$ this means: $q_i: X_i \to X$ (called \text{injections}) and given morphisms $f_i: X_i \to Y$ there exists a unique $f: X \to Y$ with $f q_i = f_i$ for all $i$. We write $X = \coprod_i X_i$, $f = \langle f_i \rangle$.
