A monomorphism is the categorical abstraction of an injective function. In concrete categories like sets, groups, or modules, monomorphisms coincide with injective homomorphisms. The defining left-cancellation property makes monomorphisms the natural notion of "subobject embedding" in any category. Dually, a morphism $\varphi$ is a monomorphism in $\mathfrak{C}$ if and only if it is an epimorphism in the opposite category $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$.
