A fundamental theorem: right adjoints preserve all limits (products, pull-backs, kernels, equalizers) while left adjoints preserve all colimits. This explains why the underlying set of a product of groups is the Cartesian product, and why the free group on a disjoint union is the free product. The result provides a powerful criterion for showing that a functor cannot be a right adjoint (if it fails to preserve some limit) and is essential in homological algebra.
