This proposition provides a powerful criterion for projectivity: if the right adjoint preserves epimorphisms, then the left adjoint preserves projective objects. The proof uses the adjunction to lift the lifting problem from $\mathfrak{D}$ to $\mathfrak{C}$, solve it there using projectivity, and transfer back. Dually, if the left adjoint preserves monomorphisms, then the right adjoint preserves injective objects.
