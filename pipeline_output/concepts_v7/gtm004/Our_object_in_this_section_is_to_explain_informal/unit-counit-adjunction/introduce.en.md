An adjunction $F \dashv G$ can be equivalently described by two natural transformations: the unit $\varepsilon: 1_{\mathfrak{C}} \to GF$ and the counit $\delta: FG \to 1_{\mathfrak{D}}$, satisfying two triangle identities. This formulation is often more convenient for calculations since it works entirely within the categories $\mathfrak{C}$ and $\mathfrak{D}$ without reference to sets. The unit and counit are the universal morphisms associated to the adjunction: $\varepsilon_X$ is the "insertion of generators" and $\delta_Y$ is the "evaluation map."
