In an abelian category, the notions of monomorphism and epimorphism are "balanced": a morphism that is both is necessarily an isomorphism. This fails in general categories (e.g., a continuous bijection need not be a homeomorphism). The result follows from the canonical factorization and the normality axioms of an abelian category, which ensure that a monic epic is its own image.
