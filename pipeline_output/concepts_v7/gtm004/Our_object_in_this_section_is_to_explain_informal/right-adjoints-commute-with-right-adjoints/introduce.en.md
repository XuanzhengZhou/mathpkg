Theorem 8.6 is a general structural result: if two functors commute, so do their (left or right) adjoints. Specializing to the case where the right adjoints are limit functors (right adjoints to constant functors), this yields the well-known principle that "limits commute with limits." This theorem unifies many ad hoc results in category theory and is fundamental for understanding the behavior of limits under functors.
