Let $F \dashv G$ with $F: \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{D}$ and $G: \mathfrak{D} \to \mathfrak{C}$. Let $P \dashv R$ be the adjunction for the constant functor $P$ to the functor category $\mathfrak{C}^{\mathfrak{P}}$ (and similarly for $\mathfrak{D}$). Then there is a natural equivalence $G R \xrightarrow{\cong} R G^{\mathfrak{P}}$, uniquely determined by the given adjugants. In words: right adjoints (limits) commute with right adjoints.
