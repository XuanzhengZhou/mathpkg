The pull-back (or fiber product) is the universal way to complete a pair of morphisms with common codomain into a commutative square. In the category of sets, the pull-back of two subset inclusions is the intersection. The pull-back is dual to the push-out and generalizes several constructions: the kernel (when one morphism is the zero morphism), the inverse image, and the fiber of a morphism. It is unique up to unique isomorphism when it exists.
