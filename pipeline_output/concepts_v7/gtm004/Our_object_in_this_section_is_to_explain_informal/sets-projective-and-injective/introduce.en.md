In the category of sets, the axiom of choice implies that every set is projective (surjective maps have sections), and every set is injective (any map into a set can be extended from a subset). This is a special property of the category of sets not shared by most algebraic categories. However, many algebraic categories have enough projectives (e.g., free objects) and enough injectives (e.g., divisible abelian groups).
