Theorem 2.4 states that smooth maps $C^\infty(U,V)$ form a dense subset of the space of $C^r$ maps $C^r_S(U,V)$ under the strong $C^r$ topology, for any finite $r$. The proof combines convolution approximation (Theorem 2.3) on local charts with a partition of unity argument to achieve global approximation. This density result is a cornerstone of differential topology, allowing one to replace $C^r$ maps with $C^\infty$ maps without loss of generality in transversality and genericity arguments.
