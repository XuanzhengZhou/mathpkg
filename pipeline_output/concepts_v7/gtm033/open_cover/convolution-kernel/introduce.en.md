A convolution kernel is a nonnegative, compactly supported function on $\mathbb{R}^m$ whose total integral equals $1$. It serves as a "weighting function" for the convolution operation: convolving a map $f$ with such a kernel produces a weighted average of the values of $f$, yielding a smooth approximation if the kernel itself is smooth. Convolution kernels of arbitrarily small support radius exist in $C^\infty$, making them fundamental tools in approximation theory and differential topology.
