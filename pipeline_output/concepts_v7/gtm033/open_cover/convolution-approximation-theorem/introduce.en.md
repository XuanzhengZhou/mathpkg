Theorem 2.3 establishes that convolving a continuous map $f$ with a smooth convolution kernel $\theta$ yields a smooth map $\theta*f$ that approximates $f$ arbitrarily closely in the $C^0$ norm on any compact subset, provided the support radius of $\theta$ is chosen small enough. This is the fundamental tool for smoothing maps in differential topology: it implies that $C^r$ maps can always be $C^r$-approximated by $C^\infty$ maps locally, forming the basis for subsequent global approximation theorems via partitions of unity.
