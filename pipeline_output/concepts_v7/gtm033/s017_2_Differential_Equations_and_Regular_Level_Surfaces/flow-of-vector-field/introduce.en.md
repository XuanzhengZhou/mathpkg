The flow of a vector field generalizes the solution of ordinary differential equations to manifolds. Each point follows a maximal integral curve whose velocity equals the vector field at each instant. On a compact boundaryless manifold, the flow exists for all time and forms a one-parameter group of diffeomorphisms. This is the fundamental tool for constructing diffeomorphisms in differential topology, including the regular interval theorem and the collaring theorem.
