Reeb's Sphere Theorem provides a Morse-theoretic characterization of the sphere: any compact boundaryless manifold admitting a Morse function with exactly two critical points (a minimum and a maximum) is homeomorphic to a sphere. The proof decomposes the manifold into two disks (neighborhoods of the critical points) and a product collar (the region between), then glues them together radially.
