The Regular Interval Theorem states that if a smooth function on a compact manifold-with-boundary has no critical points and maps the boundary to the endpoints of an interval, then the manifold is diffeomorphic to a product of a level surface with the interval. This provides the fundamental structural result that between critical values of a Morse function, the level sets do not change topology.
