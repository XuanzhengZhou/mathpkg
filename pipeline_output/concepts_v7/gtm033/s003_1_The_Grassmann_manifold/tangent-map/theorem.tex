Let $f: M \to N$ be a $C^{r+1}$ map, $0 \leqslant r \leqslant \omega$. A $C^r$ map $Tf: TM \to TN$ is defined as follows: a local representation of $Tf$ in natural charts on $TM$ and $TN$ is the derivative of the corresponding local representation of $f$. More explicitly, let $\varphi_i: U_i \to \mathbb{R}^m$, $\psi_j: V_j \to \mathbb{R}^n$ be charts for $M, N$ with $f(U_i) \subset V_j$. The $C^r$ map
$$(Tf)_{ij}: TU_i \to TV_j, \qquad [x,i,a] \mapsto [f(x), j, D(\psi_j f \varphi_i^{-1})(\varphi_i x)a]$$
is independent of $i, j$. Thus there is a well-defined map $Tf: TM \to TN$ which coincides with $(Tf)_{ij}$ on $TU_i$.
