Theorem 3.1 establishes that the local and global characterizations of submanifolds are equivalent: a subset $A \subset N$ is a $C^r$ submanifold if and only if it is the image of a $C^r$ embedding. The forward direction collates submanifold charts into a differential structure on $A$ making the inclusion an embedding. The converse uses the inverse function theorem to pass from the infinitesimal condition (immersion) to the local condition (local embedding), then patches together to get the global submanifold property.
