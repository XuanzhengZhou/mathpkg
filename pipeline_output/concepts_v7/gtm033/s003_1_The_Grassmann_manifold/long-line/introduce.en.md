The long line is a $1$-dimensional manifold constructed by gluing together uncountably many copies of the open interval $(0,1)$ according to the ordering of the first uncountable ordinal. It is Hausdorff and admits a $C^\omega$ differential structure, but it is not paracompact and admits no Riemannian metric. The long line serves as an important counterexample distinguishing paracompactness from the Hausdorff property in manifold theory.
