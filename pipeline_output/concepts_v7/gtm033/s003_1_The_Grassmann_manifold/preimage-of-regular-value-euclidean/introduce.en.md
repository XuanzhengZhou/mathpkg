The preimage of a regular value theorem in Euclidean space states that the inverse image of a regular value under a $C^r$ map from an open subset of $\mathbb{R}^n$ to $\mathbb{R}^q$ is a $C^r$ submanifold of codimension $q$. This follows directly from the implicit function theorem and serves as the Euclidean-space prototype for the general regular value theorem (Theorem 3.2). It provides a powerful method for constructing submanifolds of Euclidean space by defining them as level sets of smooth maps.
