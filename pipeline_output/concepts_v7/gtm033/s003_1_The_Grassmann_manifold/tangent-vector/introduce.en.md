A tangent vector at a point $x$ on a manifold $M$ is defined as an equivalence class of triples $(x, i, a)$, where $i$ indexes a chart containing $x$ and $a$ is a vector in $\mathbb{R}^n$. The equivalence relation identifies representations across different charts via the derivative of the transition map, ensuring the definition is independent of the choice of coordinates. This construction gives the tangent space the structure of a vector space and is the basis for defining the tangent bundle.
