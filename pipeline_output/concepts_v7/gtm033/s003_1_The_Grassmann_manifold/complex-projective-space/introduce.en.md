Complex projective $n$-space $CP^n$ is the space of complex lines through the origin in $\mathbb{C}^{n+1}$. It is constructed as a quotient of $\mathbb{C}^{n+1} \setminus \{0\}$ under scalar multiplication by nonzero complex numbers, yielding a real manifold of dimension $2n$. The standard atlas using nonvanishing homogeneous coordinates makes $CP^n$ a fundamental example of a complex manifold and a compact algebraic variety.
