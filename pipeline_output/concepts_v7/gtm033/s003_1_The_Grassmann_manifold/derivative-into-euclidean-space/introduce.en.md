For a $C^1$ map $f$ from a manifold $M$ into an open subset $U$ of $\mathbb{R}^n$, the derivative $Df_x: M_x \to \mathbb{R}^n$ is defined as the composition of the tangent map $Tf$ with the projection from $TU = U \times \mathbb{R}^n$ onto the second factor. This gives an explicit linear map from the abstract tangent space $M_x$ into Euclidean space, generalizing the Jacobian matrix to the manifold setting.
