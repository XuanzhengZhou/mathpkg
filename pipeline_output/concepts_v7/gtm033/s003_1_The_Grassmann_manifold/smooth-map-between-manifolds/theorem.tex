Let $M$ and $N$ be $C^r$ manifolds and $f: M \to N$ a map. A pair of charts $(\varphi, U)$ for $M$ and $(\psi, V)$ for $N$ is adapted to $f$ if $f(U) \subset V$. The map $f$ is called a $C^r$ map (or $C^r$ differentiable) if for every $x \in M$ there exist adapted charts $(\varphi, U)$ at $x$ and $(\psi, V)$ at $f(x)$ such that the local representation
$$\psi \circ f \circ \varphi^{-1}: \varphi(U) \to \psi(V)$$
is of class $C^r$ as a map between open subsets of Euclidean spaces.
