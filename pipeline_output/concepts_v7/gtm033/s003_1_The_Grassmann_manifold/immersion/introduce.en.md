An immersion is a $C^1$ map between manifolds whose derivative is injective at every point. This "infinitesimal" condition means that locally the map looks like a linear inclusion of $\mathbb{R}^m$ into $\mathbb{R}^n$ with $m \leq n$. Every immersion is locally an embedding by the inverse function theorem, but globally it need not be injective or a homeomorphism onto its image. Immersions are central to the study of submanifolds and the Whitney immersion theorem.
