Let $f: M \to N$ be a $C^1$ map. We call $x \in M$ a regular point if $f$ is submersive at $x$; otherwise $x$ is a critical point and $f(x)$ is a critical value. If $y \in N$ is not a critical value it is called a regular value, even if $y$ is not in $f(M)$. If $y \in f(M)$ is a regular value, $f^{-1}(y)$ is called a regular level surface.
