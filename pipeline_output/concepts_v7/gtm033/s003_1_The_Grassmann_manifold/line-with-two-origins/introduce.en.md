The line with two origins is the standard example of a $1$-dimensional manifold that fails to be Hausdorff. It is constructed by taking two copies of the real line and identifying all corresponding points except the origin, resulting in two distinct points that cannot be separated by disjoint open neighborhoods. Despite the failure of the Hausdorff property, the space admits a $C^\omega$ differential structure.
