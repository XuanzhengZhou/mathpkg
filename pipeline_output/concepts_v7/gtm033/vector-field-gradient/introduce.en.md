The gradient vector field $\text{grad } f$ of a smooth function $f$ on a Riemannian manifold is defined via the duality between tangent and cotangent spaces induced by the metric. On $\mathbb{R}^n$ with the standard metric, this recovers the usual gradient. The gradient is orthogonal to level surfaces and points in the direction of steepest ascent. In Morse theory, gradient-like vector fields are used to study the topology change across critical levels.
