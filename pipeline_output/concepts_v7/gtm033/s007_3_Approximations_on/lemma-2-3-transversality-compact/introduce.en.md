Lemma 2.3 is the local engine of transversality theory. It states that maps from a manifold $U$ into an open set $V \subset \mathbb{R}^n$ that are transverse to a linear subspace $\mathbb{R}^a \cap V$ on a compact set $K$ form a dense open subset of the weak $C^r$ topology. The proof uses that transversality to a linear subspace is an open condition and that generic linear perturbations achieve transversality, making the set dense.
