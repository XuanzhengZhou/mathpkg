Theorem 4.1 states that the space $C_W(X,Y)$ of continuous maps endowed with the weak (compact-open) topology admits a complete metric, provided each component of $X$ is locally compact with a countable base and $Y$ is a complete metric space. The proof embeds $C_W(X,Y)$ as a closed subspace of a countable product of complete metric spaces via restriction maps. This result underpins the Baire category arguments used throughout differential topology.
