Theorem 3.6 establishes that every $C^r$ manifold pair admits an upgraded $C^s$ compatible structure for any $s > r$, meaning the pair is $C^r$-diffeomorphic to a smoother pair. When the submanifold $M_0$ is closed and satisfies a positioning condition (contained in the interior, boundary, or neat), this compatible smooth structure is unique up to $C^r$ diffeomorphism. This theorem is used in invariant manifold theory and analysis, where submanifolds of low differentiability arise naturally.
