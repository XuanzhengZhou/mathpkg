Suppose $M$ and $N$ are $C^\infty$ manifolds. The space $J^\infty(M,N)$ is defined as the inverse limit of the sequence
$$J^0(M,N) \leftarrow J^1(M,N) \leftarrow \cdots$$
and $J^\infty_x(M,N)$ is the inverse limit of
$$J^0_x(M,N) \leftarrow J^1_x(M,N) \leftarrow \cdots$$
An element of $J^\infty_x(M,N)$ is called an $\infty$-jet at $x$.

The maps $j^r$ fit together to define a prolongation map
$$j^\infty: C^\infty(M,N) \rightarrow C^0(M,J^\infty(M,N)).$$
The image of $j^\infty$ is weakly closed, and the weak and strong topologies on $C^\infty(M,N)$ are induced by $j^\infty$ from the corresponding topologies on $C(M,J^\infty(M,N))$.
