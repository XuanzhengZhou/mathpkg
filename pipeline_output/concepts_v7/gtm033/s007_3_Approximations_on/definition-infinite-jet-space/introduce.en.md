The infinite jet space $J^\infty(M,N)$ is defined as the projective (inverse) limit of the tower of finite-order jet spaces. An $\infty$-jet at $x$ encodes the full Taylor series of a smooth map at that point. The infinite jet prolongation $j^\infty$ embeds $C^\infty(M,N)$ into $C^0(M,J^\infty(M,N))$ with weakly closed image, and this embedding induces both the weak and strong $C^\infty$ topologies. This construction is essential for the jet transversality theorem.
