Theorem 4.4 generalizes the complete metric and Baire space results from the $C^0$ setting to $C^r$ function spaces. Part (a) states that the weak $C^r$ topology on $C^r(M,N)$ admits a complete metric. Part (b) asserts that any weakly closed subset of the strong $C^r$ space is a Baire space. The proof uses the jet prolongation map $j^r$ to embed $C^r(M,N)$ into $C^0(M,J^r(M,N))$, transferring the topology from the $C^0$ case.
