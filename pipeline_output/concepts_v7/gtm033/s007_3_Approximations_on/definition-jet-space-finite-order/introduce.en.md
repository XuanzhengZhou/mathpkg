The jet space $J^r(M,N)$ formalizes the idea of "maps up to order $r$ contact." Two maps have the same $r$-jet at $x$ if they agree at $x$ and share the same derivatives up to order $r$ in some (hence any) coordinate chart. The space of all $r$-jets is a finite-dimensional vector bundle over $M$ whose fiber is $P^r(m,n)$, the space of degree-$r$ Taylor polynomials. The jet prolongation map $j^r: C^r(M,N) \to C^0(M,J^r(M,N))$ is a key tool for topologizing $C^r(M,N)$.
