Let $M, N$ be $C^r$ manifolds, $0 \leqslant r < \infty$. Two $C^r$ maps $f, g: M \to N$ have the same $r$-jet at $x \in M$ if $f(x) = g(x)$ and, in local coordinates, $f$ and $g$ have the same Taylor polynomial of order $r$ at $x$. The equivalence class is denoted $j^r_x f$ and called the $r$-jet of $f$ at $x$.

The space of $r$-jets from $\mathbb{R}^m$ to $\mathbb{R}^n$ at $0$ is identified with the vector space
$$P^r(m,n) = \mathbb{R}^n \times \prod_{k=1}^{r} L_{\text{sym}}^k(\mathbb{R}^m,\mathbb{R}^n)$$
where $L_{\text{sym}}^k(\mathbb{R}^m,\mathbb{R}^n)$ denotes the vector space of symmetric $k$-linear maps from $\mathbb{R}^m$ to $\mathbb{R}^n$.

The total jet space is $J^r(m,n) = \mathbb{R}^m \times P^r(m,n)$. For manifolds $M, N$ (without boundary), charts $(\varphi,U)$, $(\psi,V)$ induce a bijection $\theta: J^r(U,V) \to J^r(\varphi U,\psi V)$ by sending $j^r_x f$ to $j^r_{\varphi(x)}(\psi f \varphi^{-1})$, giving $J^r(M,N)$ the structure of a $C^0$ manifold.
