Theorem 2.4 states that any submanifold $B$ can be moved by an arbitrarily small perturbation (isotopy) to intersect a given submanifold $A$ transversely. More precisely, there exist embeddings of $B$ into $N$ arbitrarily close to the inclusion that are transverse to $A$. This is proved by applying the Transversality Theorem 2.1 to the inclusion map and using the openness of the embedding property. The result is fundamental for putting submanifolds in general position.
