Let $f: M \to N$ be a $C^1$ map and $A \subset N$ a $C^1$ submanifold. The map $f$ is transverse to $A$, denoted $f \pitchfork A$, if for every $x \in f^{-1}(A)$,
$$T_{f(x)}N = T_{f(x)}A + T_x f(T_x M).$$
That is, the tangent space of $N$ at $f(x)$ is spanned by the tangent space of $A$ and the image of the derivative of $f$.

If $A, B \subset N$ are submanifolds, they are in general position if the inclusion $B \hookrightarrow N$ is transverse to $A$, equivalently
$$A_x + B_x = N_x \quad \text{whenever } x \in A \cap B.$$
This condition is symmetric; if $A$ and $B$ are in general position then $A \cap B$ is a submanifold of both $A$ and $B$.
