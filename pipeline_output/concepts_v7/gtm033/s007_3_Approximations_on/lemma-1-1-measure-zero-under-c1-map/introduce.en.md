Lemma 1.1 states that a $C^1$ map between open subsets of $\mathbb{R}^n$ sends measure-zero sets to measure-zero sets. The proof relies on the local Lipschitz property of $C^1$ maps: on a ball where $\|Df\|$ is bounded by $\kappa$, the map is $\kappa$-Lipschitz, so an $n$-cube of edge $\lambda$ is mapped into an $n$-cube whose edge is at most $\sqrt{n}\kappa\lambda$. This lemma is a key ingredient in the proof of the Morse-Sard theorem.
