Theorem 2.9 extends the Jet Transversality Theorem to finitely many submanifolds of the jet space simultaneously. The set of maps whose $r$-jet prolongation is transverse to every $A_0, \ldots, A_q$ is residual. As an application, the density of immersions $M \to N$ when $\dim N \geqslant 2\dim M$ is reproved by taking $A_k \subset J^1(M,N)$ to be the submanifolds of 1-jets of rank $k$ for $k = 0, \ldots, m-1$. Transversality to all $A_k$ forces the jet to miss these low-rank sets, hence the map is an immersion.
