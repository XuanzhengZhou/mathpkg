Theorem 5.2 (Whitney) provides an explicit construction for approximating a $C^r$ map $f$ on an open subset of $\mathbb{R}^m$ by an analytic (real-analytic, $C^\omega$) map. The construction multiplies $f$ by a smooth bump function supported near a compact set $K$, then convolves with a scaled Gaussian kernel. For sufficiently large scaling parameter $\kappa$, the resulting analytic map $g$ is $C^r$-close to $f$ on $K$. This theorem is used in Chapter 4 to prove that $C^\infty$ manifolds admit compatible $C^\omega$ structures.
