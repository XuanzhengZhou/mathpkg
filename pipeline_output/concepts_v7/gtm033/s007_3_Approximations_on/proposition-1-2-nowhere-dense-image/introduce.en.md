Proposition 1.2 states that the image of a $C^1$ map from a lower-dimensional manifold into a higher-dimensional one is nowhere dense. The proof reduces to the Euclidean case: any $C^1$ map $g: U \subset \mathbb{R}^m \to \mathbb{R}^n$ with $m < n$ has measure-zero image, by factoring through $\mathbb{R}^n$ and applying Lemma 1.1. Since a measure-zero set is nowhere dense (by Baire category), the result follows.
