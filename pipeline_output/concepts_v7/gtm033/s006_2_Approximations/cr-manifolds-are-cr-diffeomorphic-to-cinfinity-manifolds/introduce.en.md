Theorem 2.10 is a foundational result in differential topology, derived from Theorems 2.7 and 2.9. Part (a) states that every finite-differentiability manifold is $C^r$ diffeomorphic to a $C^\infty$ manifold, so one loses nothing by working exclusively in the smooth category. Part (b) asserts that the diffeomorphism type is independent of differentiability class. Consequently, the $C^\infty$ category is sufficient for most purposes, offering advantages such as closure under derivatives and the Morse-Sard theorem.
