Let $U$ be a $C^r$ manifold, $0 \leqslant r < \infty$, and $W \subset U$ an open set. Let $V \subset \mathbb{R}^n$ be open, $f \in C^r_S(U,V)$, and put $f(W) = V'$. Then there exists a neighborhood $\mathcal{N} \subset C^r(W,V')$ of $f|W$ such that for every $g_0 \in \mathcal{N}$, the map

$$T(g_0) = g \colon U \to V, \qquad g = \begin{cases} g_0 & \text{on } W, \\ f & \text{on } U - W \end{cases}$$

is $C^r$, and $T \colon \mathcal{N} \to C^r_S(U,V)$ is continuous.
