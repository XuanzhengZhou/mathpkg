Theorem 2.7 extends the density result of Theorem 2.6 to maps with special geometric properties. If $M$ has finite dimension, then any $C^r$ diffeomorphism, embedding, closed embedding, immersion, submersion, or proper map can be $C^r$-approximated by a $C^s$ map of the same type. A crucial corollary is that the diffeomorphism type of a manifold is independent of the differentiability class: two $C^s$ manifolds are $C^s$ diffeomorphic if and only if they are $C^r$ diffeomorphic (for $1 \leqslant r < s \leqslant \infty$).
