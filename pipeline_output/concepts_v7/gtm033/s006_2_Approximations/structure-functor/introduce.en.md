A structure functor $(\mathcal{F}, \mathfrak{Y})$ on a set $X$ abstracts the passage from local data to global sections. $\mathfrak{Y}$ is a family of subsets closed under arbitrary unions, and $\mathcal{F}$ assigns a set of "structures" to each $Y \in \mathfrak{Y}$ with contravariant restriction maps. Key properties include nontriviality, continuity (compatibility with direct limits), and local extendability. This formalism captures the globalization pattern used throughout differential topology, including the construction of compatible $C^s$ structures and the density of immersions and embeddings.
