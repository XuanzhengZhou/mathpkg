The Whitney Embedding Theorem (Theorem 2.14) states that every $C^r$ $n$-dimensional manifold can be realized as a closed submanifold of $\mathbb{R}^{2n+1}$. It follows as a corollary of Theorem 2.13 by first constructing a proper map $M \to \mathbb{R}$ (constant if $M$ is compact, or a suitable exhaustion function otherwise) and then approximating by an embedding into $\mathbb{R}^{2n+1}$. This is one of the most celebrated results in differential topology, showing that abstract manifolds can always be concretely realized in Euclidean space.
