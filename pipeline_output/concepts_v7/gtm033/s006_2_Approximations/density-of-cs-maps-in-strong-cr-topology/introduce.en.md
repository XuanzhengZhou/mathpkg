Theorem 2.6 states that every $C^r$ map between $C^s$ manifolds can be approximated arbitrarily closely (in the strong $C^r$ topology) by a $C^s$ map. This is the fundamental approximation result for manifolds without boundary, showing that smoothness can always be improved by an arbitrarily small perturbation. The proof uses locally finite atlases and a countable induction argument, reducing to the Euclidean approximation Theorem 2.4.
