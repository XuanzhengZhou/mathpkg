The Covering Homotopy Theorem for vector bundles (Theorem 2.6) states that a vector bundle morphism can be lifted along a homotopy of its base map. It generalizes the earlier covering homotopy theorem (Theorem 1.5) and is the cornerstone result connecting bundle theory to homotopy theory. Its corollary, the homotopy invariance of pullbacks (Theorem 2.4), is what makes the classification of vector bundles via classifying spaces possible.
