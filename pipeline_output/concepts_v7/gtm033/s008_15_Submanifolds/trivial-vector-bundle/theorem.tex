The \textbf{trivial $n$-dimensional vector bundle} over $B$ is
$$\varepsilon_B^n = (p, B \times \mathbb{R}^n, B, \Phi)$$
where $p: B \times \mathbb{R}^n \to B$ is the natural projection and $\Phi$ is the unique maximal vector bundle atlas containing the identity map of $B \times \mathbb{R}^n$.

More generally, a vector bundle $\xi$ is called \textbf{trivial} if it is isomorphic to $\varepsilon_B^n$ for some $n$. Such an isomorphism is a \textbf{trivialization} of $\xi$.
