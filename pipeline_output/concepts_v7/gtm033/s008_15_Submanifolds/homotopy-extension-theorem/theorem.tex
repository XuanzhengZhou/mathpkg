Let $Z$ be a closed subspace of a normal space $N$. Let $f: N \to P$ be a continuous map and let $g: Z \times I \to P$ be a homotopy of $f|Z$. If $g$ extends to a homotopy of $f|U$, for some neighborhood $U \subset N$ of $Z$, then $g$ extends to a homotopy $h: N \times I \to P$ of $f$. In particular this is the case if $Z$ is a retract of some neighborhood $U \subset N$.
