An exact sequence of vector bundles captures algebraic relationships between bundles fibrewise: the image of each morphism equals the kernel of the next. This translates homological algebra into the geometric setting of vector bundles. Short exact sequences are particularly important, as they appear in constructions like Whitney sums and quotient bundles, and their splitting is guaranteed over paracompact bases.
