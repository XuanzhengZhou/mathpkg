Fix $0 \leqslant r \leqslant \infty$ and let $N, P$ be topological spaces which are $C^r$ manifolds if $r > 0$. If $r = 0$, suppose also that $N$ is a normal space. Let $Z \subset N$ be closed and $V \subset U \subset N$ be open, with $Z \subset V \subset \bar{V} \subset U$. Suppose given a commuting diagram
\[
\begin{CD}
N \times 0 \supset U \times 0 \subset U \times I
\end{CD}
\]
with $C^r$ maps $f: N \times 0 \to P$ and $g: U \times I \to P$. Then there exists a $C^r$ map $h: N \times I \to P$ such that $h|N \times 0 = f$ and $h = g$ on $V \times I$.
