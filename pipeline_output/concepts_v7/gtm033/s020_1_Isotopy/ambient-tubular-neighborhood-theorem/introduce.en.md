Theorem 1.8, the ambient tubular neighborhood theorem, is a corollary of Theorem 1.7 and one of the most practically useful results in the isotopy extension framework. It states that any isotopy of tubular neighborhoods of a closed neat submanifold $A$ can be extended to a global diffeotopy of the ambient manifold, with support contained in any prescribed neighborhood $U$ of $A$.

The key observation in the proof is that an isotopy of tubular neighborhoods leaves the submanifold $A$ pointwise fixed, which guarantees bounded velocity in some neighborhood of $A$. This makes Theorem 1.7 applicable. The theorem is essential for proving that certain constructions (like collars and smoothings) are well-defined up to diffeomorphism.
