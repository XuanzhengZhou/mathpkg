Theorem 1.9, the smoothing homeomorphism theorem, addresses the problem of replacing a homeomorphism between manifolds with a diffeomorphism while preserving the structure of a decomposition into two submanifolds meeting along their common boundary. Given a homeomorphism $h$ that is already a diffeomorphism on each piece $M_0$ and $N_0$, the theorem produces a global diffeomorphism $f$ that agrees with $h$ on the interface $V_0$ and outside a neighborhood of $V_0$.

The proof uses the ambient tubular neighborhood theorem (1.8) to adjust the collars on each side so that they match, isotoping the restrictions $h|_{M_0}$ and $h|_{N_0}$ to diffeomorphisms whose collar germs coincide. This theorem formalizes the geometric intuition shown in Figure 8-2 of Hirsch's text: a homeomorphism that is smooth away from an interface can be smoothed across that interface.
