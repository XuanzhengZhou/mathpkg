Theorem 1.6 is a variant of the isotopy extension theorem that applies to closed (rather than compact) submanifolds. The trade-off is that instead of requiring compactness, one must impose the bounded velocity condition on the isotopy. The conclusion similarly yields a diffeotopy with bounded velocity rather than compact support.

This theorem addresses the case where the submanifold may be non-compact but the isotopy is controlled in its speed. Together with Theorem 1.3, it provides a comprehensive picture of when isotopies on submanifolds can be extended to the ambient manifold.
