Theorem 1.1 from Hirsch's *Differential Topology* establishes the fundamental connection between time-dependent vector fields and diffeotopies. Given a smooth manifold $M$ and a time-dependent vector field $G$ on $M$ with bounded velocity, the theorem guarantees the existence and uniqueness of a diffeotopy $F$ whose time derivative matches the action of $G$.

This result is the cornerstone of the isotopy extension theory developed in Chapter 8. The proof relies on constructing an auxiliary vector field on $M \times I$ and applying completeness arguments via Riemannian metrics. The bounded velocity condition ensures that solution curves lie in compact sets, which guarantees global existence on the full time interval $I = [0,1]$.
