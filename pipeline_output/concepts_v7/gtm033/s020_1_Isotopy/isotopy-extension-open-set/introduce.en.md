Theorem 1.4 provides isotopy extension for the case where the isotopy is defined on an open subset $U$ of the ambient manifold $M$. The key hypothesis is that the graph map $\widehat{F}(U \times I)$ must be open in $M \times I$, which is a technical condition ensuring the constructed vector field can be properly extended.

The proof constructs a time-dependent vector field on $\widehat{F}(U \times I)$ from the tangent vectors to the isotopy curves, then extends it to all of $M \times I$ using a partition of unity. The compactness of $A \times I$ allows the construction of a vector field with compact support that still agrees with the isotopy on a neighborhood of $A$.
