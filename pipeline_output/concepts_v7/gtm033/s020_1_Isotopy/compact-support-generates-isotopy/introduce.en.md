Theorem 1.2 is a direct corollary of Theorem 1.1, stating that compact support of a time-dependent vector field suffices to guarantee the generation of an isotopy. This is because compact support implies bounded velocity, which is the hypothesis required by Theorem 1.1.

The theorem has important practical consequences: on any compact manifold, every time-dependent vector field automatically generates an isotopy without any additional conditions. This makes isotopy theory particularly well-behaved on compact manifolds and provides a powerful tool for constructing deformations in differential topology.
