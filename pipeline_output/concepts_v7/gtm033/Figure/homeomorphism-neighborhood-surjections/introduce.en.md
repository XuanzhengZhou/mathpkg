This theorem states that in the strong $C^0$ topology, any map sufficiently close to a homeomorphism between boundaryless manifolds must be surjective. The proof reduces to showing that a map $h$ on a closed ball that is sufficiently close to the identity must cover the unit ball, using a homotopy argument and a classical topological result: no map of an $(n-1)$-sphere to itself that extends to the $n$-ball can be homotopic to the identity. This property is closely related to degree theory.
