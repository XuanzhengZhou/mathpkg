The strong topology (Whitney or fine topology) on $C^r(M,N)$ uses locally finite atlases to control the closeness of maps and their derivatives over the entire manifold, not just compact subsets. This topology is essential for noncompact domains, where the weak topology does not adequately control behavior at infinity. Important subsets like immersions, embeddings, and proper maps are open in the strong topology.
