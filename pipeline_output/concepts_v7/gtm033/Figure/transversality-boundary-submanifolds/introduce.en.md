A transversality theorem adapted to manifolds with boundary. When a map $f$ and its restriction $f|_{\partial M}$ are both transverse to a submanifold $A$ (which is either neat, in the interior, or in the boundary of $N$), the preimage $f^{-1}(A)$ is a well-behaved submanifold whose boundary is exactly the preimage of $\partial A$. This generalizes the regular value theorem and is essential for constructing submanifolds via pullback.
