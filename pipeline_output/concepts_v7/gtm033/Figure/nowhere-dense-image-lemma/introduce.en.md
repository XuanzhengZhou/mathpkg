This lemma states that a $C^1$ map from a lower-dimensional manifold to a higher-dimensional one cannot have dense image. It provides the dimensional justification for why, when embedding an $n$-manifold into $\mathbb{R}^q$, there exists a projection direction $v$ that avoids secants and tangent directions. The proof is deferred to the Sard theorem chapter.
