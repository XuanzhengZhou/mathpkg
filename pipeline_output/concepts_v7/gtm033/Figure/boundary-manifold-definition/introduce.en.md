The boundary $\partial M$ of a manifold $M$ is the set of points that map to the boundary of a halfspace in any local chart. This is well-defined: being a boundary point is invariant under diffeomorphic changes of coordinates (by invariance of domain). When $\partial M$ is nonempty, $M$ is called a $\partial$-manifold, and $\partial M$ itself inherits a natural $C^r$ manifold structure of one lower dimension. The tangent bundle, immersions, submersions, and embeddings are defined identically for $\partial$-manifolds, but care is needed for submanifolds: neat submanifolds are those whose boundary is exactly their intersection with $\partial M$.
