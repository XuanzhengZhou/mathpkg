The smoothing theorem: given a topological homeomorphism between two manifolds without boundary that is already a diffeomorphism on each of two closed half-submanifolds meeting at a common boundary, the homeomorphism can be deformed (near the common boundary) to a global diffeomorphism. The proof uses collars on the common boundary and the ambient tubular neighborhood theorem to match the collars smoothly while preserving the map away from the boundary. This result is a cornerstone for gluing constructions and for showing that certain naturally defined topological manifolds admit smooth structures.
