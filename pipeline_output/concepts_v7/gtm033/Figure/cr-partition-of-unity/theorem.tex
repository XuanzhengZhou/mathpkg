Let $M$ be a $C^r$ manifold, $0 \leqslant r \leqslant \infty$, and $\mathcal{U} = \{U_i\}_{i \in \Lambda}$ an open cover. A $C^r$ \textbf{partition of unity} subordinate to $\mathcal{U}$ is a family of $C^r$ maps $\lambda_i: M \to [0,1]$, $i \in \Lambda$ such that:

$$\operatorname{Supp} \lambda_i \subset U_i \quad (i \in \Lambda),$$

$$\{\operatorname{Supp} \lambda_i\}_{i \in \Lambda} \text{ is locally finite},$$

$$\sum_{i \in \Lambda} \lambda_i(x) = 1 \quad (x \in M).$$

Local finiteness ensures each point has a neighborhood on which all but finitely many $\lambda_i$ vanish, so the sum is locally finite.
