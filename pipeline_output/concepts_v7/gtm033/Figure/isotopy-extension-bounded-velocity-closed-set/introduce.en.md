The most general isotopy extension theorem in this section: given a closed set $A$ and an isotopy defined on an open neighborhood $U$ of $A$, with bounded velocity and open image condition, the isotopy extends to a global diffeotopy whose support is contained in the image of the original isotopy. The support control — ensuring the diffeotopy is the identity outside $F(U 	imes I)$ — is crucial for applications where one wants to modify the manifold only locally.
