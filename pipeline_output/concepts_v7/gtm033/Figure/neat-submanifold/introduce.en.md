A neat submanifold is a submanifold that meets the boundary of the ambient manifold cleanly — its own boundary equals its intersection with the ambient boundary, and it is not tangent to the ambient boundary. When a submanifold has empty boundary, neatness simply means it does not intersect the ambient boundary. The concept is essential for extending the regular value and transversality theorems to manifolds with boundary.
