The Nowhere Density Lemma states that if a $C^1$ map has domain dimension strictly smaller than codomain dimension, its image is nowhere dense. This result is used in the proof of the Whitney embedding theorem to ensure that a generic projection direction avoids the secant map image. The lemma follows from the Morse-Sard theorem and related measure-theoretic arguments.
