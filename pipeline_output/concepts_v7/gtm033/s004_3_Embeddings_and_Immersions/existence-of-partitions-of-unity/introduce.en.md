The Existence of Partitions of Unity theorem states that for any $C^r$ manifold $M$ ($0 \leq r \leq \infty$), every open cover admits a subordinate $C^r$ partition of unity. The proof first constructs $C^\infty$ bump functions on $\mathbb{R}^n$ using the exponential function $e^{-1/x}$, then uses paracompactness and local finiteness to sum them into a globally defined partition of unity. This result is the essential mechanism for globalizing local differential-topological constructions.
