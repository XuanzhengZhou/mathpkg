Let $M$ be a $C^r$ manifold, $0 \leq r \leq \infty$, and $\mathcal{U} = \{U_i\}_{i \in \Lambda}$ an open cover of $M$. A **$C^r$ partition of unity subordinate to $\mathcal{U}$** is a family of $C^r$ maps $\lambda_i: M \to [0,1]$, $i \in \Lambda$, such that:

1. $\operatorname{Supp} \lambda_i \subset U_i$ for all $i \in \Lambda$,
2. $\{\operatorname{Supp} \lambda_i\}_{i \in \Lambda}$ is locally finite,
3. $\sum_{i \in \Lambda} \lambda_i(x) = 1$ for all $x \in M$.

Local finiteness ensures that each point has a neighborhood on which all but finitely many $\lambda_i$ vanish; thus the sum is locally a finite sum.
