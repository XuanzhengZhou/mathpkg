A map is proper if the inverse image of every compact set is compact. In differential topology, properness is a crucial condition for controlling the behavior of a map at infinity. An embedding $f: M \to N$ is proper if and only if its image $f(M)$ is closed in $N$. Proper maps are used to ensure that constructions such as tubular neighborhoods extend globally.
