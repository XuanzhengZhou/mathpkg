The strong topology (also called the Whitney or fine topology) on $C^r(M,N)$ controls the behavior of maps over the entire manifold $M$, including at infinity. Unlike the weak topology, it uses locally finite atlases and allows different closeness tolerances on different charts. The strong topology is essential in differential topology because many important subsets—such as immersions, embeddings, and proper maps—are open in it.
