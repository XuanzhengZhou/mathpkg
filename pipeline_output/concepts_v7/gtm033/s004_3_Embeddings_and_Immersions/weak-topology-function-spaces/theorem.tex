Let $M$ and $N$ be $C^r$ manifolds, $0 \leq r \leq \infty$. A **weak subbasic neighborhood** $\mathcal{N}^r(f; (\varphi, U), (\psi, V), K, \varepsilon)$ of a map $f \in C^r(M,N)$ is the set of $C^r$ maps $g: M \to N$ such that $g(K) \subset V$ and
$$\| D^k(\psi \circ f \circ \varphi^{-1})(x) - D^k(\psi \circ g \circ \varphi^{-1})(x) \| < \varepsilon$$
for all $x \in \varphi(K)$, $k = 0, \ldots, r$, where $(\varphi, U)$ is a chart on $M$, $(\psi, V)$ is a chart on $N$, $K \subset U$ is compact with $f(K) \subset V$, and $0 < \varepsilon \leq \infty$.

The **weak topology** (or $C^r$ compact-open topology) on $C^r(M,N)$ is generated by these subbasic neighborhoods; the resulting topological space is denoted $C^r_W(M,N)$. When $M$ is compact, the weak and strong topologies coincide.
