Proposition 1.0 states that for a compact $C^r$ manifold $M$ ($r \geq 2$), embeddings form a dense subset of $C^r_W(M, \mathbb{R}^q)$ when $q > 2 \dim M$, and immersions are dense when $q \geq 2 \dim M$. This follows from re-examining the proof of the Easy Whitney Embedding Theorem (Theorem 1.3.5) and shows that not only does an embedding exist, but any map can be approximated by embeddings.
