Theorem 1.7 asserts that any homeomorphism between manifolds without boundary has a neighborhood consisting entirely of surjective maps in the strong $C^0$ topology. The proof reduces to showing that maps $C^0$-close to the identity on $\mathbb{R}^n$ are surjective, which is established by a topological degree argument: a continuous map close enough to the identity on a ball must cover the ball, otherwise a homotopy would contradict the non-retraction theorem for spheres.
