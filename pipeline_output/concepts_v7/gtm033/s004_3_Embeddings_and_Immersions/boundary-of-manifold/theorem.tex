The boundary of a $C^r$ manifold $(M, \Phi)$ is the set
$$\partial M = \{x \in M : x \text{ is a boundary point for some (hence any) chart}\}.$$
A $C^r$ atlas for $\partial M$ is obtained as follows: for each chart $(\varphi, U) \in \Phi$ with $U \cap \partial M \neq \varnothing$, let $H \subset \mathbb{R}^n$ be a halfspace with $\varphi(U) \subset H$ and $U \cap \partial M = \varphi^{-1}(\partial H)$. Let $L: \partial H \to \mathbb{R}^{n-1}$ be any linear isomorphism; then $(L\varphi, U \cap \partial M)$ is a chart on $\partial M$. The set of all such charts is a $C^r$ atlas on $\partial M$, making $\partial M$ a $C^r$ manifold of dimension $n-1$ without boundary.
