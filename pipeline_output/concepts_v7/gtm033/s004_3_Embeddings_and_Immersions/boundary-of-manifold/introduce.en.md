The boundary $\partial M$ of a manifold with boundary is well-defined: the property of being a boundary point is independent of the choice of chart. The boundary inherits a $C^r$ manifold structure of codimension one in $M$ and itself has empty boundary, i.e., $\partial(\partial M) = \varnothing$. The atlas for $\partial M$ is constructed by composing boundary-restricted charts with linear isomorphisms to $\mathbb{R}^{n-1}$.
