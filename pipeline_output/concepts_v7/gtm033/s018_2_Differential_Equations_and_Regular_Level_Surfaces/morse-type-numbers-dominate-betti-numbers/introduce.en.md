Theorem 3.7 is the simplest corollary of the Morse inequalities: each type number $v_k$ is at least the corresponding Betti number $eta_k$. Equivalently, every Morse function on a compact manifold must have at least $eta_k$ critical points of index $k$. A Morse function achieving equality for all $k$ (i.e., $v_k = eta_k$) is called perfect.
