Theorem 3.1 is the fundamental cell-attachment theorem of Morse theory. It states that if an admissible Morse function $f$ on a compact manifold $M$ has exactly one critical point of index $k$, then $M$ deformation retracts onto $f^{-1}(a)$ with a $k$-cell attached along its boundary. This is the key inductive step for building the CW-complex structure of a manifold from a Morse function.
