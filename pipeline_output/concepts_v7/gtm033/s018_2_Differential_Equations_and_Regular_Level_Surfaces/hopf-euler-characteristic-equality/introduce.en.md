Hopf's theorem (Theorem 3.8) proves that the homological Euler characteristic (the alternating sum of Betti numbers) equals the Euler characteristic defined as the sum of indices of zeros of a vector field, for any compact manifold without boundary. The proof uses a Morse function and its gradient vector field, showing each critical point of index $k$ contributes $(-1)^k$ to the index sum.
