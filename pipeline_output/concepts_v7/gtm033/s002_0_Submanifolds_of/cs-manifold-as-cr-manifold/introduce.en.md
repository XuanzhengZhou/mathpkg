A manifold with higher differentiability class $C^s$ can always be regarded as a $C^r$ manifold for any $r < s$, since $C^s$ transition maps are in particular $C^r$. This forgetful operation embeds the category of $C^s$ manifolds into that of $C^r$ manifolds. The converse — whether every $C^r$ manifold admits a $C^s$ structure — is a deeper question addressed later.
