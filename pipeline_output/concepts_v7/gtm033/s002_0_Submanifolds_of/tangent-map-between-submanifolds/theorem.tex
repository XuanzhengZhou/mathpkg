Let $f: M \to N$ be a $C^r$ map between submanifolds with $f(x) = z$. Let $(\varphi, U)$ and $(\psi, V)$ be charts for $M$ and $N$ at $x$ and $z$ respectively, with $\varphi(x) = a$. The \textit{tangent map} (or derivative) of $f$ at $x$ is the linear map

$$Tf_x: M_x \to N_z$$

defined by

$$Tf_x(x, y) = (z, D(\psi \circ f \circ \varphi^{-1})_a \, y).$$

This definition is independent of the choice of charts $(\varphi, U)$ and $(\psi, V)$, by the chain rule.
