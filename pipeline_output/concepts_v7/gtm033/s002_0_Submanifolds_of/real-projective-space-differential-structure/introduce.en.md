Real projective $n$-space $P^n$ is obtained from the $n$-sphere by identifying antipodal points. It inherits a real analytic $C^\omega$ structure from the sphere, making the quotient map $p: S^n \to P^n$ a local diffeomorphism. $P^n$ is a compact $n$-manifold and serves as a fundamental example of a non-orientable manifold when $n$ is even.
