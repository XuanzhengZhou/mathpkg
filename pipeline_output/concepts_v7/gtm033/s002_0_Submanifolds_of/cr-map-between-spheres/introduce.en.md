A map $g: S^n \to S^m$ is declared to be $C^r$ when it is $C^r$ as a map into the ambient Euclidean space $\mathbb{R}^{m+1}$ and its image lies in $S^m$. This extends the chart-based definition of differentiability to maps whose target is also a sphere.
