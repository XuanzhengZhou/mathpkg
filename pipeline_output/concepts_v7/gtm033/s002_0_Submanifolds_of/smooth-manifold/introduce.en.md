A smooth manifold is a $C^r$ manifold with $r \geq 1$, meaning at least continuously differentiable. This is the most commonly studied class of manifolds in differential topology, as it provides enough differentiability for calculus while avoiding the technicalities of real analytic ($C^\omega$) rigidity.
