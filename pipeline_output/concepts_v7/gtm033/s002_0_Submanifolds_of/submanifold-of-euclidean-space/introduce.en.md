An $n$-dimensional submanifold of $\mathbb{R}^{n+k}$ is a subset that locally looks like the zero set of a $C^r$ map of maximal rank $k$. This definition generalizes regular level surfaces and provides the most concrete class of manifolds, serving as the primary object of study in differential topology.
