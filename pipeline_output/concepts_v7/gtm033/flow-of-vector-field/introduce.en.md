The flow of a vector field $X$ on a manifold $M$ is a map $\eta: \Omega \to M$ defined on an open set $\Omega \subset \mathbb{R} \times M$ containing $\{0\} \times M$, such that for each $x \in M$, the map $t \mapsto \eta(t,x)$ is the unique integral curve of $X$ starting at $x$. The flow encodes all integral curves simultaneously. On a compact manifold without boundary, the flow is globally defined and gives a one-parameter group of diffeomorphisms.
