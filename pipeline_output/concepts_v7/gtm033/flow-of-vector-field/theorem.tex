Let $X$ be a $C^{r+1}$ vector field on a $C^{r+1}$ manifold $M$, $0 \leqslant r \leqslant \omega$. For each $x \in M$ there is a maximal open interval $J(x) \subset \mathbb{R}$ about $0$ and a $C^{r+1}$ integral curve

$$\eta^x: (J(x), 0) \to (M, x), \quad \eta^x(t) = \eta(t, x) = \eta_t(x).$$

The set

$$\Omega = \{(t, x) \in \mathbb{R} \times M : t \in J(x)\}$$

is open in $\mathbb{R} \times M$; the flow of $X$ is the $C^r$ map

$$\eta: \Omega \to M, \quad (t, x) \mapsto \eta_t(x).$$

If $M$ is compact and without boundary, then $\Omega = \mathbb{R} \times M$ and each $\eta_t$ is a $C^r$ diffeomorphism of $M$, forming a one-parameter group: $\eta_t \eta_s = \eta_{t+s}$.
