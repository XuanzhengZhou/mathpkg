Let $(f_0,\xi_0)$ and $(f_1,\xi_1)$ be tubular neighborhoods for $(V,M)$, where $\xi_i = (p_i, E_i, M)$. An \text{isotopy of tubular neighborhoods} from $(f_0,\xi_0)$ to $(f_1,\xi_1)$ is an isotopy $F$ from $E_0$ to $V$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F_0 = f_0$,
\item $F_1(E_0) = f_1(E_1)$,
\item $f_1^{-1} \circ F_1: E_0 \rightarrow E_1$ is a vector bundle isomorphism $\xi_0 \rightarrow \xi_1$,
\item $\hat{F}(E_0 \times I)$ is open in $V \times I$.
\end{enumerate}
The last condition is automatic if $\partial M = \varnothing$. Isotopy of tubular neighborhoods is an equivalence relation on the class of tubular neighborhoods for $(V,M)$.
