A partial tubular neighborhood is a slight generalization of a tubular neighborhood in which the domain of the embedding is not required to be the entire total space $E$ of the vector bundle, but only some open neighborhood $U$ of the zero section. This relaxation is technically convenient because many constructions, such as the exponential map or normal bundle constructions in $\mathbb{R}^n$, naturally produce only a partial tubular neighborhood. Every partial tubular neighborhood contains a (full) tubular neighborhood by restriction to a suitable sub-bundle neighborhood.
