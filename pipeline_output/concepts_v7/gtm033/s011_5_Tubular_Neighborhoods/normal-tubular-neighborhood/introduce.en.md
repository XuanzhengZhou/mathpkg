A normal tubular neighborhood is the most geometrically natural tubular neighborhood for a submanifold of Euclidean space. It uses the orthogonal complement of the tangent space at each point as the fibre direction, so that the embedding $f(x,y) = x + y$ maps a normal vector $y$ at $x$ to the point obtained by moving from $x$ in the normal direction. When the neighbourhood is taken sufficiently small, the fibres of the normal tubular neighborhood coincide with the sets of points whose unique nearest point on $M$ is the basepoint $x$.
