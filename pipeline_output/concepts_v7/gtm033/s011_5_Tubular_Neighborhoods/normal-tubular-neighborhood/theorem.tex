Let $M \subset \mathbb{R}^n$ be a submanifold. Choose $v(x) = M_x^\perp$, the orthogonal complement of the tangent space $M_x$ in $\mathbb{R}^n$. Then the tubular neighborhood obtained from the construction
$$\xi = v^*\gamma_{n,k}, \quad E = \{(x,y) \in M \times \mathbb{R}^n : y \in v(x)\}, \quad f(x,y) = x + y$$
is called a \text{normal tubular neighborhood} of $M$ in $\mathbb{R}^n$. In this case $U$ can be chosen small enough so that $f(U \cap v_x)$ is the set of points in $f(U)$ whose nearest point of $M$ is $x$.
