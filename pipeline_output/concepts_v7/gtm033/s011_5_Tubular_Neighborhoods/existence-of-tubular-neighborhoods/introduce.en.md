Theorem 5.2 establishes that every closed submanifold (without boundary) of a closed manifold admits a tubular neighborhood. The proof embeds $V$ in Euclidean space $\mathbb{R}^n$ via Whitney's theorem, constructs a $C^\infty$ retraction $r: W \to V$ from a neighborhood $W \subset \mathbb{R}^n$, and then builds a partial tubular neighborhood using the map $f(x,y) = r(x+y)$ on the normal bundle. This is the foundational existence result that makes tubular neighborhoods a universally available tool in differential topology.
