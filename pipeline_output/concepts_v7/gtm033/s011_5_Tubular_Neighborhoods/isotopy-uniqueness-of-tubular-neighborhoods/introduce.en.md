Theorem 5.3 states that tubular neighborhoods of a closed submanifold are unique up to isotopy. The proof proceeds in two steps. First, when one tubular neighborhood is contained in another, the fibre derivative of the transition map $g = f_1^{-1}f_0$ provides a vector bundle isomorphism $\Phi$, and a canonical homotopy from $\Phi$ to $g$ is constructed using Taylor expansion. Second, the general case is reduced to the containment case by a preliminary isotopy that shrinks one tubular neighborhood into the other using a radial scaling map $h(y) = [\delta(p(y))/(1+|y|^2)]y$.
