An isotopy is a homotopy $F_t$ between embeddings such that the map $(x,t) \mapsto (F_t(x),t)$ is itself an embedding of $P \times I$ into $Q \times I$. This condition ensures that the family of maps $F_t$ does not create self-intersections as $t$ varies — each $F_t$ is an embedding and distinct $t$ values produce disjoint images in the track $\hat{F}$. Isotopies are the natural equivalence relation used to compare tubular neighborhoods: two tubular neighborhoods are isotopic if one can be continuously deformed into the other through a family of tubular neighborhoods.
