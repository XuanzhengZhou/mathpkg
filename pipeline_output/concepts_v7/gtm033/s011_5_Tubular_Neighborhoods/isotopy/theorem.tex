Let $P, Q$ be manifolds. An \text{isotopy} of $P$ in $Q$ is a homotopy
$$F: P \times I \rightarrow Q, \quad F(x,t) = F_t(x)$$
such that the related map
$$\hat{F}: P \times I \rightarrow Q \times I, \quad (x,t) \mapsto (F_t(x),t)$$
is an embedding. We call $\hat{F}$ the \text{track} of $F$. We also say $F$ is an isotopy from $F_0$ to $F_1$. If $A \subset P$ is such that $F_t(x) = F_0(x)$ for all $(x,t) \in A \times I$, then $F$ is a \text{rel } A \text{ isotopy}.
