A tubular neighborhood is a fundamental construction in differential topology that provides a vector bundle neighborhood of a submanifold. Given a submanifold $M$ embedded in a manifold $V$, a tubular neighborhood identifies an open set around $M$ with the total space of a vector bundle over $M$, such that $M$ itself corresponds to the zero section. This structure allows one to treat nearby points of $V$ as vectors in the fibres over $M$, enabling deformation and approximation arguments via linear algebra in the fibres.
