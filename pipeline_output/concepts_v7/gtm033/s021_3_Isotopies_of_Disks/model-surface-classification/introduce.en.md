Model surfaces are classified up to diffeomorphism by three invariants: genus, Euler characteristic (or number of boundary components), and orientability. For an orientable model surface of genus $g$ with $b$ boundary components, the Euler characteristic is $2 - 2g - b$. For a nonorientable model surface, it is $2 - g - b$. These numerical invariants provide a complete and effective classification of all compact connected surfaces.
