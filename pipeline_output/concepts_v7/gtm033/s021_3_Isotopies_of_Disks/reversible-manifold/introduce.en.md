A connected orientable manifold is called reversible if there exists a diffeomorphism from the manifold to itself that reverses orientation. This property plays a role in the classification of surfaces via handle attachment: when a surface is reversible, the result of attaching a handle does not depend on whether the embedding preserves or reverses orientation, as long as it is orientable.
