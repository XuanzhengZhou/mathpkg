The Möbius band is the fundamental nonorientable surface with boundary. Gluing a disk to a Möbius band yields the projective plane. A crosscap is the image of a Möbius band in a surface after a connected sum with the projective plane. A nonorientable surface of genus $p$ is obtained by attaching $p$ crosscaps to the sphere (equivalently, the connected sum of $p$ projective planes). The Klein bottle is the nonorientable surface of genus 2.
