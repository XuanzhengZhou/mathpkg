Let $M$ and $N$ be connected surfaces without boundary. The \textbf{connected sum} $M \# N$ is constructed by taking $M$ and $N$ to be disjoint, choosing an embedding $f: S^0 \times D^2 \hookrightarrow M \cup N$ with one disk in each component, and forming
$$
M \# N = \left[(M \cup N) - \operatorname{Int} f(S^0 \times D^2)\right] \bigcup_f D^1 \times S^1.
$$
Equivalently, remove the interior of a disk from each surface and glue the resulting boundary circles.
