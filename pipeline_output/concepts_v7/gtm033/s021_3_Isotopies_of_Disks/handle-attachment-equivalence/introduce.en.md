This theorem gives sufficient conditions under which attaching handles via two different embeddings yields diffeomorphic surfaces. The three cases cover: nonorientable surfaces (where all handle attachments are equivalent), oriented surfaces with orientation-matched embeddings, and reversible surfaces with orientable embeddings. This result is essential for proving that surfaces of the same genus are diffeomorphic.
