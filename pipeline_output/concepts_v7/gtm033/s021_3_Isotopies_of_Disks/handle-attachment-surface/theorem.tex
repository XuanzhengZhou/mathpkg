Let $M$ be a surface and $f: S^0 \times D^2 \hookrightarrow M - \partial M$ an embedding. The image of $f$ is a pair of disjoint disks in $M$. The surface $M[f]$ obtained by attaching a handle is defined as
$$
M[f] = \left[M - \operatorname{Int} f(S^0 \times D^2)\right] \bigcup_{f} D^1 \times S^1,
$$
where the gluing is via $f|_{S^0 \times S^1}$. The differential structure on $M[f]$ is the unique (up to diffeomorphism) structure inducing the original structures on $M - \operatorname{Int} f(S^0 \times D^2)$ and $D^1 \times S^1$.
