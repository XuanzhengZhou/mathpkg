For a connected nonorientable surface, the diffeomorphism type of the surface obtained by handle attachment does not depend on the choice of the attaching embedding. This follows from the isotopy of disjoint disks in a connected manifold. The result simplifies the classification of nonorientable surfaces by ensuring that handle attachment yields a uniquely defined surface.
