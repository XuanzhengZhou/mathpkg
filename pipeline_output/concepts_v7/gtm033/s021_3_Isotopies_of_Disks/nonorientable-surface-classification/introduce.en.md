Nonorientable surfaces are classified up to diffeomorphism by their genus $p$ (the number of crosscaps). Two nonorientable surfaces are diffeomorphic precisely when they have the same genus. This is the nonorientable counterpart to the classification of orientable surfaces by genus, completing the picture that genus and orientability jointly determine the diffeomorphism type of a compact surface.
