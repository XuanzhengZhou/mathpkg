Attaching a single crosscap to an orientable surface of genus $p$ destroys orientability and produces a nonorientable surface of genus $2p + 1$. The proof for $p > 0$ expresses the orientable surface as a connected sum of $p$ tori, then applies the handle-equals-two-crosscaps theorem. For $p = 0$, the result is the projective plane (genus 1).
