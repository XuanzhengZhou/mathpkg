A \textbf{$\partial$-surface of genus $g$ with $k$ boundary components} is obtained by cutting out the interiors of $k > 0$ disjoint disks from an orientable or nonorientable surface $M$ of genus $g$. By isotopy of disks (Section 8.3), the diffeomorphism class of such a surface depends only on $M$ and the number of disks $k$.

A \textbf{model surface} is either a surface (without boundary) or a $\partial$-surface (with boundary) of genus $p$, orientable or nonorientable.

Every compact connected surface is diffeomorphic to a unique type of model surface.
