Let $M$ be a surface and let $f: S^0 \times D^2 \hookrightarrow M$ be an embedding. We give $S^0 \times D^2$ the product orientation, meaning $1 \times D^2$ has the standard orientation of $D^2$ and $(-1) \times D^2$ has the opposite orientation. The embedding $f$ is called an \textbf{orientable embedding} if $M$ can be oriented so that $f$ preserves orientation. In all other cases, $f$ is called nonorientable.
