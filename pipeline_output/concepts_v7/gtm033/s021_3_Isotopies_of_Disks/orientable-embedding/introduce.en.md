An embedding $f: S^0 \times D^2 \hookrightarrow M$ used for handle attachment is called orientable if the surface $M$ admits an orientation with respect to which $f$ preserves orientation. Here $S^0 \times D^2$ is given the product orientation. This distinction between orientable and nonorientable embeddings is crucial for determining whether the resulting surface $M[f]$ is orientable.
