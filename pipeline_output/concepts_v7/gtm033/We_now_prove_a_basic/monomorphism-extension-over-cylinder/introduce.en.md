This corollary ensures that two monomorphisms defined on the ends of a cylinder $V \times I$ can be extended to a single monomorphism over the entire cylinder, as long as the target dimension $s$ exceeds $k + \dim V$. The proof combines the covering homotopy theorem (to extend to a neighborhood of $V \times \{0,1\}$) with Theorem 3.1. This result is essential for homotopy invariance arguments in the classification of vector bundles.
