This theorem is the cornerstone of vector bundle theory: it establishes a bijection between isomorphism classes of $k$-plane bundles over a manifold $M$ and homotopy classes of maps from $M$ into the Grassmann manifold $G_{s,k}$, provided $s > k + \dim M$. The correspondence is given by pulling back the universal bundle $\gamma_{s,k}$ via a classifying map. Together with the covering homotopy theorem, this converts the study of vector bundles into a problem in homotopy theory, allowing one to apply approximation and obstruction theory to vector bundles.
