Let $V$ be an $n$-dimensional manifold without boundary. Two embeddings $f_0, f_1$ of $E^n$ (either the unit $n$-disk $D^n$ or $\mathbb{R}^n$) into $V$, both sending the origin to the same basepoint $x_0$, are isotopic relative to the origin provided that the derivative at $0$ of the transition map $f_1^{-1} \circ f_0$ has positive determinant. The proof reduces to the linear case via an isotopy of tubular neighborhoods, then uses path-connectedness of the identity component of $GL(n,\mathbb{R})$ to deform the linear map to the identity.
