Let $M$ be a neat submanifold of a manifold $V$. Given any tubular neighborhood $E_0$ of the boundary $\partial M$ inside $\partial V$, there exists a tubular neighborhood of $M$ in $V$ whose intersection with $\partial V$ equals $E_0$. The proof uses collar decompositions and a reduction to the product case $V = W \times I$, $M = N \times I$, combined with an isotopy of tubular neighborhoods to match the two boundary pieces.
