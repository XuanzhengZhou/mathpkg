Reeb's theorem is a classic application of Morse theory: if a compact manifold admits a Morse function with exactly two critical points, it must be homeomorphic to a sphere. The proof decomposes the manifold into two disks (neighborhoods of the critical points) joined by a product region $S^{n-1} \times I$ (the regular interval). This was historically one of the first major successes of Morse-theoretic methods in topology.
