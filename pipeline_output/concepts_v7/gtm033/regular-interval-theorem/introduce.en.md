The Regular Interval Theorem states that if a smooth function on a compact $\partial$-manifold onto $[a,b]$ has no critical points, then the manifold is diffeomorphic to the product of a level surface with the interval. This is a fundamental structural result in Morse theory, showing that the topology of a manifold can only change when passing through critical levels.
