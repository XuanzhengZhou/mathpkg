Let $Q$ be a compact manifold and $B$ a compact manifold without boundary. Then every map $f: Q \to E^*$ (where $E^*$ is the one-point compactification of a vector bundle over $B$) is homotopic to a map $h$ in standard form: there is a submanifold $M \subset Q$ and a tubular neighborhood $U \subset Q$ of $M$ such that $U = h^{-1}(E)$, $M = h^{-1}(B)$, and $h|U$ is a vector bundle map, with $h(Q - U) = \infty$.
