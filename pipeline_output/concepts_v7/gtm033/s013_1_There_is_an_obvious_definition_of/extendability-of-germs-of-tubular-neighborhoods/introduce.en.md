This result states that a tubular neighborhood defined only on a neighborhood $U$ of a closed subset $A \subset M$ can be extended to a full tubular neighborhood of $M$, at least after possibly shrinking the domain $U$ to a smaller neighborhood $W$ of $A$. In other words, the germ of a tubular neighborhood along a closed subset always extends to a global tubular neighborhood. This is a key technical tool for local-to-global arguments involving tubular neighborhoods.
