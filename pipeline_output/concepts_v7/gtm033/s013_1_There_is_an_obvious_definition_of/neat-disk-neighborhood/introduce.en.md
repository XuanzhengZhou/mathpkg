A neat $p$-disk $D$ of codimension $k$ in a manifold $M$ admits a product neighborhood: there exists a neighborhood $E$ of $D$ such that the pair $(E, D)$ is homeomorphic (or diffeomorphic) to the standard pair $(D^p \times \mathbb{R}^k, D^p \times \{0\})$. This is a local triviality result for submanifolds that are disks, analogous to the fact that a point has a Euclidean neighborhood.
