A $C^r$ \textit{isotopy} of a manifold $V$, where $1 \leqslant r < \infty$, is a $C^r$ map $F: V \times I \to V$ such that for each $t \in I$, the map $F_t = F(\cdot, t): V \to V$ is a $C^r$ diffeomorphism, and $F_0 = \mathrm{id}_V$. Two $C^r$ embeddings $f_0, f_1: M \to V$ are $C^r$ \textit{isotopic} if there exists a $C^r$ isotopy $F$ of $V$ such that $F_1 \circ f_0 = f_1$.
