A $C^r$ isotopy is the natural $C^r$-differentiable analogue of an isotopy, where all maps involved are required to be $C^r$ rather than merely continuous or smooth ($C^\infty$). Two tubular neighborhoods are considered equivalent up to $C^r$ isotopy when there is a $C^r$ isotopy of the ambient manifold carrying one to the other. This notion is essential for stating uniqueness results for $C^r$ tubular neighborhoods.
