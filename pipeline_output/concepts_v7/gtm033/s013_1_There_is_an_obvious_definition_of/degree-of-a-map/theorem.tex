Let $M$ and $N$ be compact $n$-manifolds with $N$ connected and $\partial M = \partial N = \varnothing$. For a continuous map $f: M \to N$, the \textit{degree} of $f$, denoted $\deg(f)$, is an integer if $M$ and $N$ are oriented, and an integer modulo $2$ otherwise.

If $f$ is $C^1$ and $y \in N$ is a regular value of $f$, then
$$\deg(f) = \sum_{x \in f^{-1}(y)} \operatorname{sgn}(T_x f),$$
where $\operatorname{sgn}(T_x f) = +1$ if $T_x f$ preserves orientation and $-1$ if $T_x f$ reverses orientation.
