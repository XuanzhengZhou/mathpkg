Theorem 2.1 establishes that the intersection number $\#(f,N)$ depends only on the homotopy class of the map $f$, provided both maps are transverse to $N$. This result parallels the homotopy invariance of the degree of a map and justifies extending the definition of intersection number to arbitrary continuous maps by choosing a smooth transverse approximation.
