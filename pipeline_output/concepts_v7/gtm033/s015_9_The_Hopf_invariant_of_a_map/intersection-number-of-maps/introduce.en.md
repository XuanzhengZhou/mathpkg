The intersection number $\#(f,g)$ of two maps into a common target is defined via the intersection number of their product with the diagonal. It is a homotopy invariant that generalizes the intersection number of a map with a submanifold. The graded-commutativity property $\#(g,f) = (-1)^{\dim M \cdot \dim N} \#(f,g)$ reflects the parity of the dimensions.
