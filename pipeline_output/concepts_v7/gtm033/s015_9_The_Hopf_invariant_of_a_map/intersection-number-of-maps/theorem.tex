Let $M, N, W$ be oriented manifolds without boundaries, with $\dim M + \dim N = \dim W$. The \textbf{intersection number} of maps $f \colon M \to W$ and $g \colon N \to W$ is defined as
$$
\#(f, g) = \#\bigl(f \times g,\; W_{\!\Delta};\; W \times W\bigr),
$$
where $W_{\!\Delta} \subset W \times W$ is the diagonal. This integer depends only on the homotopy classes of $f$ and $g$. If $g$ is an embedding, then
$$
\#(f, g) = \#(f, g(N)).
$$
In general,
$$
\#(g, f) = (-1)^{\dim M \cdot \dim N} \; \#(f, g).
$$
