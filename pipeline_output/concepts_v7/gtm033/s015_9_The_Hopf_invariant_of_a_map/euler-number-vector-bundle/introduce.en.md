The Euler number $\chi(\xi)$ of an oriented $n$-dimensional vector bundle over a compact $n$-manifold is defined as the self-intersection number of the zero section inside the total space. It generalizes the Euler characteristic: when $\xi$ is the tangent bundle $TM$, the Euler number equals the Euler characteristic $\chi(M)$. The Euler number can be computed by counting (with signs) the zeros of a generic section transverse to the zero section.
