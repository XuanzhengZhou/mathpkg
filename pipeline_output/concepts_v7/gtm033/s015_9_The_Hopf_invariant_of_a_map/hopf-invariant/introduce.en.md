The Hopf invariant is an integer-valued homotopy invariant of maps from $S^3$ to $S^2$, defined via the linking number of the preimages of two distinct regular values. It was introduced by Heinz Hopf and provides a fundamental example of a nontrivial homotopy class of maps between spheres of different dimensions. The invariant captures the linking of the fibers of a smooth approximation to the given map.
