Theorem 2.7 states that every compact connected manifold with nonempty boundary admits a nowhere-vanishing vector field. The proof uses the double construction: embed $M$ in its double $M'$, take a vector field on $M'$ with finitely many zeros, and push those zeros outside $M$ via a diffeomorphism, yielding a zero-free field on $M$.
