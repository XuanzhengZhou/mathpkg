Theorem 2.11 is a classical result of Hassler Whitney: every compact oriented $n$-manifold embedded in $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ has a nowhere-vanishing normal vector field. The proof computes the Euler number of the normal bundle $\nu$ and shows $X(\nu) = \#(M,M;\mathbb{R}^{2n}) = 0$, then applies Theorem 2.10. Whitney also showed that orientability is essential: the real projective plane $P^2 \subset \mathbb{R}^4$ provides a counterexample in the nonorientable case.
