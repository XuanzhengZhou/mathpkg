Let $U \subset \mathbb{R}^m$ and $V \subset \mathbb{R}^n$ be open subsets, and let $f: U \to V$ be a $C^r$ map. Let $K \subset U$ be closed and $W \subset U$ open. Assume $f$ is $C^s$ ($s > r \geqslant 1$) on a neighborhood of $K \setminus W$. Then for any neighborhood $\mathcal{N}$ of $f$ in $C^r_S(U, V)$ there exists a map $h \in \mathcal{N}$ such that $h$ is $C^s$ on a neighborhood of $K$, and $h = f$ on $U \setminus W$.
