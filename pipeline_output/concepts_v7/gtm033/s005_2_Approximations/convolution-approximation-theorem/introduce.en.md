Theorem 2.3 states that convolving a continuous map with a convolution kernel of sufficiently small support yields a uniform approximation on any compact set. This is the key analytic result that enables the approximation of $C^r$ maps by $C^\infty$ maps: by convolving locally and using partitions of unity, one can construct smooth global approximations that are arbitrarily close in the strong $C^r$ topology.
