Let $U \subset \mathbb{R}^m$ be open and $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^n$ a continuous map. Let $\theta: \mathbb{R}^m \to \mathbb{R}$ be a convolution kernel. Then for every compact set $K \subset U$ and every $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists $\delta > 0$ such that if the support radius of $\theta$ is less than $\delta$, then
$$\|\theta * f - f\|_{0, K} < \varepsilon.$$
Consequently, if $\{\theta_k\}$ is a sequence of convolution kernels whose support radii tend to $0$, then $\theta_k * f \to f$ uniformly on compact subsets of $U$.
