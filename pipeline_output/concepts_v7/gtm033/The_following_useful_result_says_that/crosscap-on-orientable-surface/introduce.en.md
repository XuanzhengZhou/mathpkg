Adding a single crosscap to an orientable surface of genus $p$ destroys orientability and produces a nonorientable surface whose genus is $2p+1$. For instance, attaching a crosscap to a sphere ($p=0$) yields the projective plane (genus 1), and attaching a crosscap to a torus ($p=1$) yields a nonorientable surface of genus 3.
