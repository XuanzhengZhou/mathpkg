The genus $p$ of an orientable surface has a direct geometric meaning: it is the maximum number of disjoint embedded circles that can be removed without disconnecting the surface. For a sphere ($p=0$), any circle disconnects; for a torus ($p=1$), one meridian does not disconnect but two disjoint circles do.
