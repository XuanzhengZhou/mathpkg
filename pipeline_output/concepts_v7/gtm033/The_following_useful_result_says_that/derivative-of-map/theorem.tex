Let $U \subset \mathbb{R}^m$ be an open set and $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^n$ a map. A linear map $L: \mathbb{R}^m \to \mathbb{R}^n$ is called the \text{derivative} of $f$ at $x \in U$ if

$$\lim_{h \to 0} |h|^{-1} \left( f(x + h) - f(x) - Lh \right) = 0.$$

If such an $L$ exists, it is unique and is denoted by $Df_x$ or $Df(x)$.
