In the orientable case, a connected neighborhood of a single saddle critical point is diffeomorphic to a disk with two holes. The proof caps the two lower boundary components with disks, producing a surface with one saddle and two minima, which by the disk characterization theorem (Theorem 2.1) must be a disk. Removing the capped disks yields the original neighborhood as $H_2$.
