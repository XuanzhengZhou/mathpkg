A real analytic function is locally representable as a convergent power series. Every analytic map is smooth ($C^\infty$), but the converse is false. Analyticity is the strongest differentiability condition and is denoted by $C^\omega$, with the convention $\infty \leqslant \omega$ (smooth is less restrictive than analytic).
