A nonorientable surface must contain an embedded Möbius band. The proof picks an orientation-reversing loop, deforms it to an immersion with clean double points, decomposes it into embedded loops, and observes that at least one embedded loop must reverse orientation. A tubular neighborhood of that loop is a Möbius band.
