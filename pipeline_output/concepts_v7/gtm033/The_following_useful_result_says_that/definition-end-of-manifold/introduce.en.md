An end of a noncompact surface captures the idea of a way to go to infinity. Two such ways are equivalent if they are eventually contained in the same complementary component of large compact subsets. The number of ends is a diffeomorphism invariant; for example, $\mathbb{R}^2$ has exactly one end, while an infinite cylinder has two ends.
