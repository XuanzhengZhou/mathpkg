Any compact surface without boundary that admits a Morse function with exactly two critical points must be diffeomorphic to the 2-sphere. The proof uses the fact that such a manifold is the union of two 2-disks glued along their boundaries, and the gluing map can be taken to be the identity by the classification of circle diffeomorphisms up to isotopy.
