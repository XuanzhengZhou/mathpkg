These are the foundational separation and covering axioms of general topology. Hausdorff spaces separate distinct points, normal spaces separate disjoint closed sets (by Urysohn's lemma), and paracompact spaces admit locally finite refinements of any open cover. Paracompactness is essential for the existence of partitions of unity on manifolds.
