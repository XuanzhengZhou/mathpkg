Theorem 1.6 establishes the two fundamental properties of the degree. Part (a) states that degree is a homotopy invariant, making it well-defined on homotopy classes of maps. Part (b) states that if a map on a boundary extends to the interior, its degree must vanish, providing an obstruction to extension. These properties make the degree a powerful algebraic invariant in differential topology.
