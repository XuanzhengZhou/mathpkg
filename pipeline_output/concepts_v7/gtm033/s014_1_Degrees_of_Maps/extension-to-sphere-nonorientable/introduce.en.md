Theorem 1.9 is the nonorientable analogue of Theorem 1.8. For a compact connected nonorientable manifold with boundary, a map from the boundary to the sphere extends if and only if its mod 2 degree vanishes. The proof for $\dim W \geqslant 3$ uses orientation-reversing loops to adjust the types of preimage endpoints, then proceeds similarly to the orientable case. The 2-dimensional case requires a separate induction argument using standard-form maps.
