Let $M$ be a compact connected $n$-manifold, $n \geqslant 1$. Let $f, g: M \to S^n$ be continuous maps.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] If $M$ is oriented and $\partial M = \varnothing$, then $f \simeq g$ if and only if $\deg f = \deg g$; and there exist maps of every integer degree $m \in \mathbb{Z}$.

\item[(b)] If $M$ is not orientable and $\partial M = \varnothing$, then $f \simeq g$ if and only if $\deg_2 f = \deg_2 g$; and there exist maps of both mod 2 degrees.

\item[(c)] If $\partial M \neq \varnothing$, then every map $M \to S^n$ is null-homotopic.
\end{enumerate}
