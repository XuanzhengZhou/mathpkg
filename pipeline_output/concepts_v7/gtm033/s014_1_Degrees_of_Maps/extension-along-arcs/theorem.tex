Let $W$ be a compact oriented $(n+1)$-manifold, $K \subset W$ an embedded arc, and $V \subset \partial W$ a neighborhood of $\partial K$. Let $f: V \to \mathbb{R}^n$ be a $C^\infty$ map having $0 \in \mathbb{R}^n$ as a regular value, with $f^{-1}(0) = \partial K = \{x_0, x_1\}$. If $\operatorname{sign}_{x_0}(f) = \operatorname{sign}_{x_1}(f)$, then $f$ extends to a $C^\infty$ map $F: U \to \mathbb{R}^n$ defined on a neighborhood $U$ of $K$, having $0$ as a regular value, and such that $F^{-1}(0) = K$.
