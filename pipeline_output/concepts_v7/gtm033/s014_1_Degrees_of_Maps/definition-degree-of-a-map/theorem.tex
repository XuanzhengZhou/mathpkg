Let $(M, \omega)$ and $(N, \theta)$ be compact oriented $n$-manifolds without boundary, with $N$ connected, and let $f: M \to N$ be a continuous map. The degree of $f$, denoted $\deg f$, is the integer $\deg(f, y)$ for any regular value $y \in N$ of a smooth approximation to $f$, where
$$\deg(f, y) = \sum_{x \in f^{-1}(y)} \operatorname{sign} T_x f$$
with $\operatorname{sign} T_x f = +1$ if $T_x f: M_x \to N_y$ preserves orientation and $-1$ otherwise.
