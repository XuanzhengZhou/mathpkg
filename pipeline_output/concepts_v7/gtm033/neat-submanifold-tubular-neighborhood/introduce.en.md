Every neat submanifold $M$ of a manifold $V$ admits a tubular neighborhood --- a vector bundle neighborhood of $M$ in $V$ whose zero section is the inclusion. The boundary case is more delicate than the interior case, requiring compatible collars on $\partial V$ and $\partial M$. The proof embeds $V$ as a neat submanifold of a half-space and uses normal tubular neighborhoods.
