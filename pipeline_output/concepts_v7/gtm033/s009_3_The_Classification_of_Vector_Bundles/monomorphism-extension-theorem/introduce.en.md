The Monomorphism Extension Theorem states that a vector bundle monomorphism defined on a neighborhood of a closed subset can be extended to a global monomorphism into a trivial bundle, provided the target fiber dimension is sufficiently large. The proof reduces to the special case of a trivial bundle and then applies a transversality-based theorem on extending maps into the Stiefel manifold $V_{s,k}$. This result is foundational for the classification of vector bundles, as it guarantees the existence of global monomorphisms into high-dimensional trivial bundles, from which classifying maps into Grassmannians are constructed.
