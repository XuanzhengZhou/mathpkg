The Classification Theorem for Vector Bundles is the central result of this section, establishing a fundamental bridge between vector bundle theory and homotopy theory. It states that isomorphism classes of $k$-plane bundles over an $n$-manifold $M$ correspond bijectively to homotopy classes of maps from $M$ into the Grassmann manifold $G_{s,k}$ of $k$-planes in $\mathbb{R}^s$, whenever $s > k + n$. The forward direction assigns to each bundle its classifying map (obtained from a monomorphism into a trivial bundle via Theorem 3.1), while the inverse direction pulls back the universal bundle. Together with the covering homotopy theorem, this result converts the study of vector bundles into a branch of homotopy theory.
