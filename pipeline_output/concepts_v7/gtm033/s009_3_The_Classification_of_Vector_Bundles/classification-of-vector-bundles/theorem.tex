Let $M$ be an $n$-manifold and let $s > k + n$. Two $C^r$ $k$-plane bundles $\xi, \eta$ over $M$ are $C^r$ isomorphic if and only if their classifying maps
$$f_\xi, f_\eta: M \to G_{s,k}$$
are homotopic. Equivalently, the map
$$\Theta_M: K^k(M) \to [M, G_{s,k}], \quad [\xi] \mapsto [f_\xi]$$
is a bijection, where $K^k(M)$ denotes the set of isomorphism classes of $C^r$ $k$-plane bundles over $M$ and $[M, G_{s,k}]$ denotes the set of homotopy classes of maps from $M$ to the Grassmann manifold $G_{s,k}$. The inverse is given by pulling back the universal $k$-plane bundle $\gamma_{s,k}$ over $G_{s,k}$.
