The Existence of Inverse Bundle theorem guarantees that every $C^r$ $k$-plane bundle over an $n$-manifold admits a complementary $n$-plane bundle whose Whitney sum with the original bundle is trivial. The construction is elegantly simple: use Theorem 3.1 to embed $\xi$ as a subbundle of the trivial bundle $M \times \mathbb{R}^{k+n}$, then take $\eta$ to be the orthogonal complement $F(\xi)^\perp$ with respect to the standard inner product on $\mathbb{R}^{k+n}$. This theorem is essential for defining the stable equivalence class of a vector bundle and underpins the development of topological K-theory.
