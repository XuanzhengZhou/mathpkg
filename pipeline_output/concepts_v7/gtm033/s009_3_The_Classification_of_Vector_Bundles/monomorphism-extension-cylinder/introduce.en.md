This corollary of Theorem 3.1 states that two monomorphisms defined at the two ends of a cylinder $V \times I$ can be extended to a single global monomorphism into Euclidean space, provided the target dimension $s$ is strictly greater than $k + n$. The proof uses the covering homotopy theorem to extend $F_0$ and $F_1$ to a neighborhood of $V \times \{0,1\}$ in $V \times I$, and then applies Theorem 3.1 to extend this local monomorphism globally. This result is a key step in establishing the homotopy invariance of the classifying map.
