Let $M \subset V$ be a submanifold. A tubular neighborhood of $M$ (or for $(V,M)$) is a pair $(f,\xi)$ where $\xi = (p,E,M)$ is a vector bundle over $M$ and $f: E \rightarrow V$ is an embedding such that:

1. $f|M = 1_M$ where $M$ is identified with the zero section of $E$;
2. $f(E)$ is an open neighborhood of $M$ in $V$.

More loosely, the open set $W = f(E)$ is called a tubular neighborhood of $M$, with the understanding that associated to $W$ is a particular retraction $q: W \rightarrow M$ making $(q,W,M)$ a vector bundle whose zero section is the inclusion $M \rightarrow W$.
