A critical point is nondegenerate when its Hessian quadratic form is nondegenerate, meaning the associated symmetric bilinear form has full rank. Equivalently, the section $Df$ of the cotangent bundle meets the zero section transversely at that point. Nondegenerate critical points are isolated and are the building blocks of Morse theory, where the local behavior of the function is completely determined by the index of the Hessian.
