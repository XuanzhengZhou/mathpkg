Let $M$ be a manifold of dimension $n$. The \text{cotangent bundle} $T^*M$ is defined like the tangent bundle $TM$ using the dual vector space $(\mathbb{R}^n)^* = L(\mathbb{R}^n, \mathbb{R})$ instead of $\mathbb{R}^n$. As a set $T^*M = \bigcup_{x \in M} (M_x^*)$ where $M_x^* = L(M_x, \mathbb{R})$. If $(\varphi, U)$ is a chart on $M$, a natural chart on $T^*M$ is the map

$$T^*U \rightarrow \varphi(U) \times (\mathbb{R}^n)^*$$

which sends $\lambda \in M_x^*$ to $(\varphi(x), \lambda \varphi_x^{-1})$. The projection map $p: T^*M \rightarrow M$ sends $M_x^*$ to $x$.

Let $f: M \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ be a $C^{r+1}$ map. For each $x \in M$ the linear map $T_x f: M_x \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ belongs to $M_x^*$, denoted $Df_x$. The map $Df: M \rightarrow T^*M$, $x \mapsto Df_x$ is a $C^r$ section of $T^*M$, generalizing the differential of functions on $\mathbb{R}^n$.
