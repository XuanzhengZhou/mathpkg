The differential $Df$ of a smooth real-valued function on a manifold is a section of the cotangent bundle $T^*M$, assigning to each point $x$ the linear map $T_x f: M_x \to \mathbb{R}$. This intrinsic definition generalizes the usual derivative of a function on $\mathbb{R}^n$ and does not depend on a choice of Riemannian metric. Critical points of $f$ are precisely the zeros of this section, making $Df$ the fundamental object for Morse theory.
