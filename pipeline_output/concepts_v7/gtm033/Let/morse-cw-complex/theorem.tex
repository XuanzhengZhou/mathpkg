\textbf{Theorem 4.1.} Let $M$ be a compact $n$-manifold and $f: M \rightarrow [a, b]$ an admissible Morse function of type $(v_0, \ldots, v_n)$ such that $\partial M = f^{-1}(b)$. Then $M$ has the homotopy type of a finite CW-complex having $v_k$ cells of dimension $k$ for $k = 0, \ldots, n$.

\textbf{Theorem 4.3.} Every (noncompact) manifold has the homotopy type of a countable CW-complex. If the manifold is compact, the CW-complex may be chosen finite.
