A fundamental theorem of Morse theory states that a compact manifold admitting a Morse function has the homotopy type of a finite CW-complex, with one cell of dimension $k$ for each critical point of index $k$. The proof proceeds by building the manifold up through sublevel sets, showing that passing a critical value of index $k$ corresponds to attaching a $k$-cell. This result extends to noncompact manifolds, which are homotopy equivalent to countable CW-complexes.
