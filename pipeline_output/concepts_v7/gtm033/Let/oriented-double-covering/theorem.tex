Let $\xi = (p, E, B)$ be any vector bundle. Its oriented double covering $\tilde{\xi} = (\tilde{p}, \tilde{E}, \tilde{B})$ is defined as follows. Let

$$\tilde{B} = \{(x, \omega) : x \in B, \omega \text{ is an orientation of } \xi_x\}.$$

The topology of $\tilde{B}$ is generated by the subsets $\{x, \theta_x\}_{x \in U}$ where $U \subset B$ is open, $\xi|U$ is orientable, and $\theta$ is an orientation of $\xi|U$. There is a natural map $p: \tilde{B} \rightarrow B$, $p(x, \omega) = x$. Define $\tilde{\xi} = p^*\xi$. The natural orientation of $\tilde{\xi}$ is defined as follows: given $(x, \omega) \in \tilde{B}$, assign to $\tilde{\xi}|(x, \omega)$ the orientation $p^*\omega$.

A section $B \rightarrow \tilde{B}$ is the same as an orientation of $\xi$. Therefore $\xi$ is orientable if $B$ is simply connected.
