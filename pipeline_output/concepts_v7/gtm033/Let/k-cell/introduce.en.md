A $k$-cell in a manifold is simply the image of a smooth embedding of the closed $k$-dimensional unit disk. In Morse theory, each critical point of index $k$ of an admissible Morse function contributes a $k$-cell to the homotopy type of the manifold, attached along the boundary of the cell. This concept bridges the smooth structure of the manifold with the combinatorial structure of CW-complexes.
