Let $V$ be a (real) finite dimensional vector space of dimension $n > 0$. Two bases $(e_1, \ldots, e_n), (f_1, \ldots, f_n)$ of $V$ are equivalent if the automorphism $A: V \rightarrow V$ such that $Ae_i = f_i$ has positive determinant. An orientation of $V$ is an equivalence class $[e_1, \ldots, e_n]$ of bases. If $\dim V > 0$ there are just two orientations. If one of them is denoted by $\omega$, then $-\omega$ denotes the other one.

If $L: V \rightarrow W$ is an isomorphism of vector spaces and $\omega = [e_1, \ldots, e_n]$ is an orientation of $V$ then $L(\omega) = [Le_1, \ldots, Le_n]$ is the induced orientation of $W$.

If $\dim V = 0$ an orientation of $V$ means one of the numbers $\{+1, -1\}$.

Given exact sequences of vector spaces $0 \rightarrow V' \xrightarrow{i} V \xrightarrow{j} V'' \rightarrow 0$ and orientations $\omega', \omega''$ of $V', V''$ respectively, a unique orientation $\omega$ of $V$ is defined by $\omega = i(\omega') \oplus s(\omega'')$ where $s: V'' \rightarrow V$ is any splitting of $j$. Any two of $\omega', \omega, \omega''$ determine the third uniquely. We write $\omega = \omega' \oplus \omega''$, $\omega' = \omega/\omega''$, $\omega'' = \omega/\omega'$.
