Theorem 3.5 is the absolute version of the Morse inequalities, applying when the lower level set is empty. In this case the relative Betti numbers reduce to the absolute Betti numbers of the manifold. The alternating sum of type numbers equals the Euler characteristic, and the partial alternating sums provide lower bounds for the corresponding alternating sums of Betti numbers. This is the most commonly cited form of the Morse inequalities.
