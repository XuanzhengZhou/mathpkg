Theorem 5.3 establishes that tubular neighborhoods of a closed submanifold are unique up to isotopy. Any two tubular neighborhoods can be deformed into each other through a one-parameter family of embeddings, and the composition relating the two vector bundle structures is a vector bundle isomorphism. This isotopy uniqueness is essential for showing that constructions depending on a choice of tubular neighborhood are well-defined up to homotopy.
