Let $M$ be a compact manifold. A Morse function $f: M \rightarrow [a, b]$ is called \text{admissible} if $\partial M = f^{-1}(b)$ (so that $f^{-1}(b)$ is a regular level set), and $f$ has only finitely many critical points, all lying in the interior $M - \partial M$ and having distinct critical values. Equivalently, $a$ and $b$ are regular values unless $f^{-1}(a) = \varnothing$.

If $f: M \rightarrow [a, b]$ is admissible and $p, q \in [a, b]$ are regular values with $p < q$, then the restriction $f|f^{-1}[p, q]: f^{-1}[p, q] \rightarrow [p, q]$ is also admissible.
