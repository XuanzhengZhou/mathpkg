The weak Morse inequalities (Theorem 3.7) state that for any Morse function, the number of critical points of index $k$ is at least the $k$-th Betti number of the manifold. This is a direct consequence of the full Morse inequalities and provides a lower bound on the number of critical points required by the topology of the manifold. In particular, any Morse function must have at least as many critical points as the sum of the Betti numbers.
