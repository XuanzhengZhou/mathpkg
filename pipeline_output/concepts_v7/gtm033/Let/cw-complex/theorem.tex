A \text{CW-complex} is a space $X$ which can be expressed as $X = \bigcup_{n=0}^{\infty} X_n$ where $X_0 \subset X_1 \subset \cdots$, $X_0$ is a discrete subset and $X_{n+1}$ is obtained from $X_n$ by attaching $n$-cells by continuous maps of their boundaries into $X_{n-1}$. It is required that a subset of $X$ is closed provided its intersection with each such cell (image) is closed.

If $X = X_n$, then $X$ is called an $n$-dimensional CW-complex. If the number of cells is finite, $X$ is a \text{finite CW-complex}.

A \text{subcomplex} $A$ of a CW-complex $X$ is a closed subspace $A$ which is the union of cells of $X$. A \text{CW-pair} $(X, A)$ consists of a CW-complex $X$ and a subcomplex $A$.
