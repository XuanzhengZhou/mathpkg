A CW-complex is a topological space built by inductively attaching cells of increasing dimension, starting from a discrete set of points. The $n$-skeleton $X_n$ is obtained from $X_{n-1}$ by attaching $n$-dimensional disks along their boundaries. The topology is the weak topology determined by the closed cells. CW-complexes provide the natural target for Morse theory: a compact manifold with a Morse function is homotopy equivalent to a finite CW-complex with one $k$-cell for each critical point of index $k$.
