Morse's Lemma provides a canonical local coordinate representation for a Morse function near a nondegenerate critical point. In suitable coordinates, the function equals its value at the critical point plus a standard nondegenerate quadratic form: minus the sum of squares of the first $k$ coordinates plus the sum of squares of the remaining coordinates. The index $k$ is uniquely determined by the critical point. This lemma is the cornerstone of Morse theory, allowing the local topology of level sets to be completely understood.
