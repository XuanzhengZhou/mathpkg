Theorem 3.1 is the fundamental cell-attachment result in Morse theory. When a Morse function on a compact manifold has exactly one critical point of index $k$ between two regular levels, the manifold is obtained from the lower level set by attaching a $k$-cell. The proof uses Morse's Lemma to construct explicit deformations along gradient flow lines, patching together motions inside and outside a coordinate chart near the critical point.
