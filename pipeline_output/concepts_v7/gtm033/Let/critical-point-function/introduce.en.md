A critical point of a smooth real-valued function on a manifold is a point where the differential $Df$ vanishes, i.e., the derivative is the zero linear functional. Equivalently, the set of critical points is the preimage of the zero section of the cotangent bundle under the section $Df$. This generalizes the elementary calculus notion where the derivative equals zero.
