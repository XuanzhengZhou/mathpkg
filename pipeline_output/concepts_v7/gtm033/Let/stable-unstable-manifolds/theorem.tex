Let $M$ be a compact manifold and $f: M \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ a Morse function. Let $X = -\text{grad } f$ be the negative gradient vector field with respect to a Riemannian metric on $M$, and let $\varphi_t$ denote its flow. For a critical point $z$ of $f$, define the \text{stable manifold} (or inset) of $z$ by

$$W_s(z) = \{x \in M : \lim_{t \to +\infty} \varphi_t(x) = z\}$$

and the \text{unstable manifold} (or outset) of $z$ by

$$W_u(z) = \{x \in M : \lim_{t \to -\infty} \varphi_t(x) = z\}.$$

Then:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] $W_s(z)$ and $W_u(z)$ are connected $C^r$ submanifolds, of dimensions $n - k$ and $k$ respectively, where $k = \text{Ind}(z)$ and $n = \dim M$.
\item[(b)] $W_s(z)$ and $W_u(z)$ intersect transversely at $z$ and are otherwise disjoint.
\item[(c)] If $\partial M = \varnothing$, then $W_s(z) \approx \mathbb{R}^{n-k}$ and $W_u(z) \approx \mathbb{R}^k$.
\end{itemize}
