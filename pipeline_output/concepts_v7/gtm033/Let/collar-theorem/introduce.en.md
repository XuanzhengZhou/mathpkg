The collar neighborhood theorem asserts that the boundary of any smooth manifold admits an open neighborhood diffeomorphic to $\partial M \times [0, 1)$, with the boundary corresponding to $\partial M \times \{0\}$. This fundamental result ensures that manifolds with boundary are locally well-behaved near their boundaries and is essential for constructing tubular neighborhoods of neat submanifolds.
