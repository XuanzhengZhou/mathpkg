This technical lemma, used in the proof of Morse's Lemma, states that any symmetric matrix sufficiently close to a given invertible symmetric matrix can be smoothly (in fact analytically) transformed to it by a congruence transformation depending smoothly on the matrix. It provides the parametric version of Sylvester's law of inertia.
