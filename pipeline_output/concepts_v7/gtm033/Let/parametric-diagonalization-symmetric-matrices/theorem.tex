Let $A$ be a symmetric invertible $n \times n$ matrix. There exists a neighborhood $\mathcal{N}$ of $A$ in the space of symmetric $n \times n$ matrices and a $C^\omega$ map $P: \mathcal{N} \rightarrow GL(n)$ such that for each $B \in \mathcal{N}$,

$${}^t P(B) \, B \, P(B) = A.$$

If $B$ is near enough to $A$, such a factorization exists with $P$ depending analytically on $B$.
