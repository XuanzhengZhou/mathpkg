The index of a nondegenerate critical point is the dimension of the largest subspace on which the Hessian is negative definite. It counts the number of independent directions in which the function decreases from the critical point. A critical point is a local minimum precisely when its index is zero, and a local maximum precisely when its index equals the dimension of the manifold.
