An orientation of a vector bundle is a coherent family of orientations of its fibres, one for each point of the base space, compatible with a bundle atlas whose charts are orientation preserving. A bundle that admits such an orientation is called orientable; the pair of a bundle and a chosen orientation is an oriented vector bundle. Bundle morphisms and pullbacks transport orientations naturally: a bimorphism from an unoriented bundle to an oriented one induces a unique compatible orientation on the domain, and pullbacks of oriented bundles inherit a canonical orientation.
