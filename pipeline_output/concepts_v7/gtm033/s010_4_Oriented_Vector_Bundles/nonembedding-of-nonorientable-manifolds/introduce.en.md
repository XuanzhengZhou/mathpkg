Theorem 4.7 is a celebrated nonembedding result: a compact nonorientable manifold without boundary cannot be embedded as a hypersurface in any simply connected manifold of one higher dimension. The proof argues by contradiction: if such an embedding existed, the image would separate the ambient space by the Jordan--Brouwer separation theorem, which contradicts Theorem 4.6. The special case states that even-dimensional real projective spaces $P^{2n}$ cannot be embedded in Euclidean space $\mathbb{R}^{2n+1}$.
