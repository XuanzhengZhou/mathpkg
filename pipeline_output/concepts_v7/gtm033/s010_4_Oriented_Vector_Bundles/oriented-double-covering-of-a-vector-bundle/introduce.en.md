Every vector bundle admits an oriented double covering, constructed by taking as the new base space the set of pairs consisting of a point of the original base and an orientation of the fibre above it, topologized locally by orientations over trivializing neighbourhoods. The pulled-back bundle carries a tautological orientation, and a section of the covering projection corresponds precisely to a global orientation of the original bundle. This construction shows that orientability reduces to the existence of a section, and immediately implies that bundles over simply connected base spaces are always orientable.
