Let $\xi = (p, E, B)$ be a vector bundle. Its \text{oriented double covering} $\tilde{\xi} = (\tilde{p}, \tilde{E}, \tilde{B})$ is defined as follows. Let
$$\tilde{B} = \{(x, \omega) : x \in B,\; \omega \text{ is an orientation of the fibre } \xi_x\}.$$
The topology of $\tilde{B}$ is generated by the subsets $\{(x, \theta_x)\}_{x \in U}$ where $U \subset B$ is open, $\xi|_U$ is orientable, and $\theta$ is an orientation of $\xi|_U$. There is a natural projection $p: \tilde{B} \to B$ given by $p(x, \omega) = x$. Define $\tilde{\xi} = p^* \xi$, the pullback of $\xi$ along $p$. The \text{natural orientation} of $\tilde{\xi}$ assigns to the fibre over $(x, \omega) \in \tilde{B}$ the orientation $p^* \omega$.

A section $s: B \to \tilde{B}$ of the projection $p$ is exactly an orientation of $\xi$. Consequently, $\xi$ is orientable if and only if the double covering projection admits a section. In particular, if $B$ is simply connected, then $\xi$ is orientable.
