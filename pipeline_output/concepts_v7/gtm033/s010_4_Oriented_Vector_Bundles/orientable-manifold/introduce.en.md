A manifold is orientable precisely when its tangent bundle is orientable as a vector bundle. Every manifold admits a canonical oriented double covering, constructed by taking the double covering of its tangent bundle, and this covering manifold is always orientable. When the original manifold is orientable, each choice of orientation yields a global section of the double covering projection, and conversely any section determines an orientation.
