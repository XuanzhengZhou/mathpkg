Let $0 \to \xi' \xrightarrow{i} \xi \xrightarrow{j} \xi'' \to 0$ be a short exact sequence of vector bundles over $B$. Given orientations $\omega'$ of $\xi'$ and $\omega''$ of $\xi''$, a coherent orientation $\omega = \{\omega_x\}_{x \in B}$ of $\xi$ is obtained by setting $\omega_x = \omega'_x \oplus \omega''_x$, where the direct sum orientation is defined using the splitting at each fibre. Local trivializations show that $\omega$ is coherent; thus $\omega$ is an orientation of $\xi$.

Any two of the orientations $\omega, \omega', \omega''$ determine the third uniquely. We write $\omega = \omega' \oplus \omega''$, $\omega' = \omega/\omega''$, $\omega'' = \omega/\omega'$.

\textbf{Lemma 4.1.} Two of the bundles $\xi, \xi', \xi''$ are orientable if and only if the third is.
