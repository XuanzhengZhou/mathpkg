Theorem 4.2 states that the algebraic normal bundle of the boundary of a manifold is always trivial, hence always orientable. The proof uses boundary charts mapping to the half-space and the projection onto the normal coordinate to construct a global nonvanishing section. This result is a key ingredient in many arguments involving boundaries and orientability, including the classification of separating hypersurfaces.
