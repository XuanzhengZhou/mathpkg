The paracompactness convention restricts attention to manifolds that are paracompact, Hausdorff, and have a countable base of open sets. This excludes pathological examples such as the long line (non-paracompact) and the line with two origins (non-Hausdorff), ensuring that every manifold admits partitions of unity and Riemannian metrics.
