Theorem 3.8 collects the two most fundamental integral formulas of single-variable complex analysis. The Cauchy integral formula expresses the value of an analytic function at any interior point as a contour integral over the boundary, establishing that analytic functions are determined by their boundary behavior. The argument principle relates the contour integral of the logarithmic derivative $f'/f$ to the number of zeros of $f$ inside the contour, counted with multiplicity. Together these results provide the analytic machinery needed to prove that the zero-set of $p(X,Y)$ locally forms the graph of an analytic function when $p_Y \neq 0$.
