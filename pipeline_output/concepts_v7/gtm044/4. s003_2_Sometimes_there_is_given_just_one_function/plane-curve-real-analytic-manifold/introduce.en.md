Lemma 3.11 connects the algebraic notion of a plane curve defined by a single polynomial equation to the geometric notion of a real-analytic manifold. Writing the complex polynomial in terms of its real and imaginary parts and applying the Cauchy-Riemann equations, one finds that the condition $p_X \neq 0$ or $p_Y \neq 0$ guarantees that the real Jacobian of $(p_1, p_2)\colon \mathbb{R}^4 \to \mathbb{R}^2$ has rank 2, so the real-analytic implicit function theorem yields local manifold coordinates. This lemma is a first step toward showing that irreducible plane curves are Riemann surfaces away from their singular points.
