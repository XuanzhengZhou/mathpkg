A basis of the real plane, when ordered, determines an orientation: the sign of the determinant of the matrix formed by the basis vectors tells whether the orientation is positive or negative. This algebraic definition matches the geometric intuition of counterclockwise versus clockwise rotation from the first vector to the second. It is the starting point for defining orientability of smooth manifolds and ultimately for proving that algebraic curves are orientable.
