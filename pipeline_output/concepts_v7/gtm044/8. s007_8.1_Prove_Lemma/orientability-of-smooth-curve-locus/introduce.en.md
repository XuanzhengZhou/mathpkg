Every projective plane curve has at most finitely many singular points. If these singularities are removed, what remains is a smooth, orientable real 2-dimensional manifold. This lemma is a key step in the topological classification of algebraic curves, showing that the non-singular part of any curve admits a consistent global orientation. The proof uses the implicit mapping theorem and the fact that the Jacobian determinant of the transition functions between local graph coordinates is strictly positive.
