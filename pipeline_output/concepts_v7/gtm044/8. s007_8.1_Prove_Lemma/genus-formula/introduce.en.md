The genus of a nonsingular projective plane curve is a fundamental topological invariant that can be read off directly from the degree $n$ of its defining polynomial: $g = (n-1)(n-2)/2$. For example, lines ($n=1$) and conics ($n=2$) have genus $0$ (they are spheres), while a nonsingular cubic ($n=3$) has genus $1$ (a torus). The proof uses a branched covering of the projective line and Euler's formula, with the key observation that $n(n-1)$ vertices are "lost" above the discriminant points.
