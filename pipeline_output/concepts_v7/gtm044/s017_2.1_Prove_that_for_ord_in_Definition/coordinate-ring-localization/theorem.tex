Let $V \subset \mathbb{C}^n$ be an affine variety with coordinate ring $R = \mathbb{C}[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$, and let $P \in V$ be a point with corresponding maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m} \subset R$. The set of all elements of $\mathbb{C}(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ which are defined and finite at $P$ is
$$\left\{ \frac{p}{q} \;\middle|\; p \in R,\; q \in R \setminus \mathfrak{m} \right\}.$$
This set is called the \text{localization of $R$ at $P$} (or at $\mathfrak{m}$) and is denoted by $R_{\mathfrak{m}}$. It has a unique maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$, which consists of all nonunits of $R_{\mathfrak{m}}$ and corresponds to $P$.
