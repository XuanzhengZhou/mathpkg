Lemma 3.9 asserts that the localization $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ of a Noetherian ring $R$ at a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ remains Noetherian. The proof uses the bijective, inclusion-preserving correspondence between contracted ideals in $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ and certain ideals in $R$: any infinite strictly ascending chain in $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ would pull back to one in $R$, contradicting the Noetherian property of $R$.
