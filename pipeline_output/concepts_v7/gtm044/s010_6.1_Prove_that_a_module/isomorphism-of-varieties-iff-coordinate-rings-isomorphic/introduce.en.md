Theorem 8.7 establishes the fundamental equivalence between the geometric and algebraic viewpoints in affine algebraic geometry: two irreducible affine varieties are isomorphic as varieties precisely when their affine coordinate rings are isomorphic as $\mathbb{C}$-algebras. This result justifies the algebra-geometry dictionary by showing that the coordinate ring is a complete invariant of an affine variety up to isomorphism. The proof constructs polynomial maps between the varieties from the ring isomorphism by expressing the images of generators as polynomials.
