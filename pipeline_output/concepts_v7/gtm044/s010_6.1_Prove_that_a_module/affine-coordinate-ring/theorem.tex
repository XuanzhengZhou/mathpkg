Let $\mathfrak{p}$ be any prime ideal of $\mathbb{C}[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$, and let $V = V(\mathfrak{p}) \subset \mathbb{C}_{X_1, \ldots, X_n}$. Then $\mathbb{C}[X_1, \ldots, X_n]/\mathfrak{p}$ is the \emph{affine coordinate ring} of $V$ (or, commonly, the \emph{affine ring} of $V$ or the \emph{coordinate ring} of $V$); we denote it by $R_V$.

More generally, any domain having the form $\mathbb{C}[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ is called an affine coordinate ring over $\mathbb{C}$, a coordinate ring over $\mathbb{C}$, etc.
