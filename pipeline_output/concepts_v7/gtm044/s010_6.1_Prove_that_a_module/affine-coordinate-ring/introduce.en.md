The affine coordinate ring $R_V = \mathbb{C}[X_1, \ldots, X_n]/\mathfrak{p}$ is the ring of polynomial functions on the irreducible affine variety $V = V(\mathfrak{p})$. Two polynomials define the same function on $V$ precisely when their difference lies in the prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$, making the quotient ring the natural algebraic object encoding the geometry of $V$. This construction provides the foundation for the algebra-geometry dictionary in algebraic geometry.
