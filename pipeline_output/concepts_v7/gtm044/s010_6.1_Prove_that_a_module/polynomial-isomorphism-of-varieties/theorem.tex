Let $V \subset \mathbb{C}^n$ and $W \subset \mathbb{C}^m$ be irreducible affine varieties. A mapping $p: V \to W$ which is 1:1 from $V$ onto $W$ is a \emph{polynomial isomorphism} if there are polynomial maps
$$
p^*: \mathbb{C}^n \to \mathbb{C}^m \quad \text{and} \quad q^*: \mathbb{C}^m \to \mathbb{C}^n
$$
such that
$$
p^*|_V = p \quad \text{and} \quad q^*|_W = p^{-1}.
$$
We then say $V$ and $W$ are \emph{isomorphic}.
