Let $C_1$ and $C_2$ be two algebraic curves in $\mathbb{C}^2$, and let $P$ be an isolated point of intersection of $C_1$ and $C_2$. There exists an integer $n_P$, called the \text{intersection multiplicity of } $C_1$ \text{ and } $C_2$ \text{ at } $P$, such that for ``most'' sufficiently small translates of one curve, there are exactly $n_P$ distinct points of intersection clustered near $P$.
