The Hilbert basis theorem is a cornerstone of commutative algebra and algebraic geometry. It states that if a ring $R$ is Noetherian (every ascending chain of ideals stabilizes, or equivalently every ideal is finitely generated), then the polynomial ring $R[X]$ in one variable over $R$ is also Noetherian. By induction, this implies that polynomial rings in finitely many variables over a Noetherian ring — in particular over a field — are Noetherian. The theorem guarantees that every ideal in a polynomial ring over a field has a finite basis, which is essential for decomposing algebraic varieties into finitely many irreducible components.
