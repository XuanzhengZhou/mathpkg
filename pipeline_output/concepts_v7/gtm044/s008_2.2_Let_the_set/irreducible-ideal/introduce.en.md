An irreducible ideal is one that cannot be expressed as the intersection of two strictly larger ideals. This definition mirrors the notion of irreducibility in a lattice: in the lattice of ideals $(\mathcal{I}, \subset, \cap, +)$, an irreducible ideal is a meet-irreducible element. Irreducible ideals serve as the building blocks for ideal decompositions in Noetherian rings — every ideal can be written as a finite irredundant intersection of irreducible ideals, a result that follows from the Hilbert basis theorem together with the lattice properties of $\mathcal{I}$.
