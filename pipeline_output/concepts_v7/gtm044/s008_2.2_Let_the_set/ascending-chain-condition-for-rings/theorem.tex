Let $R$ be a ring and let $\mathcal{I}$ be the set of all ideals of $R$, partially ordered by inclusion $\subset$. The ring $R$ is said to satisfy the \textbf{ascending chain condition} (a.c.c.) if each strictly ascending chain of ideals of $R$,
$$\mathfrak{a}_1 \subsetneq \mathfrak{a}_2 \subsetneq \mathfrak{a}_3 \subsetneq \ldots,$$
terminates after finitely many steps. Equivalently, the partially ordered set $(\mathcal{I}, \subset)$ satisfies the a.c.c.
