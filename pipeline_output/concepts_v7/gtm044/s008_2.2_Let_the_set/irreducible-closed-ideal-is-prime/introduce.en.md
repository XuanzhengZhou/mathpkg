Lemma 4.8 establishes that in the polynomial ring $\mathbb{C}[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$, every irreducible closed ideal (an ideal that equals its own closure under the ideal-variety correspondence) is necessarily a prime ideal. This is a key structural result: it shows that the algebraic building blocks — irreducible closed ideals — correspond exactly to prime ideals. The reverse direction (that every prime ideal is irreducible and closed) is treated in the subsequent section (Corollary 5.9 and Theorem 5.11), together yielding the fundamental dictionary between irreducible varieties and prime ideals.
