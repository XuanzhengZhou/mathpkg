This lemma states that a plane algebraic curve defined by a nonconstant polynomial $p(X,Y)$ is a real-analytic manifold in a neighborhood of any point where at least one partial derivative does not vanish. The proof reduces to the real-analytic implicit mapping theorem by separating $p$ into its real and imaginary parts. This gives the local smoothness of algebraic curves away from singular points.
