Complex differentiability of a function $f: \mathbb{C} \to \mathbb{C}$ requires that the derivative limit exist independent of the direction of approach in the complex plane. This is a much stronger condition than real differentiability, and implies the Cauchy-Riemann equations. A function complex-differentiable in a neighborhood of a point is actually analytic (holomorphic) there, by a fundamental theorem of Hartogs.
