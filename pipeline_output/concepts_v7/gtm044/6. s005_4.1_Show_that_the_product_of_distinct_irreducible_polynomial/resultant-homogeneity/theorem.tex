Let $p$ and $q$ be homogeneous polynomials in $\mathbb{C}[X_0, \ldots, X_r]$ of positive degree $m$ and $n$, respectively. Write

$$
p(X_0, \ldots, X_r) = p_0 X_r^m + \cdots + p_m \quad (m > 0,\; p_0 \in \mathbb{C} \setminus \{0\})
$$
$$
q(X_0, \ldots, X_r) = q_0 X_r^n + \cdots + q_n \quad (n > 0,\; q_0 \in \mathbb{C} \setminus \{0\})
$$

where for $i \geqslant 1$, each $p_i, q_i \in \mathbb{C}[X_0, \ldots, X_{r-1}]$ is either the zero polynomial or is homogeneous of degree $i$. Then the resultant $\mathcal{R}_{X_r}(p, q)$ is either the zero polynomial or is homogeneous of degree $mn$.
