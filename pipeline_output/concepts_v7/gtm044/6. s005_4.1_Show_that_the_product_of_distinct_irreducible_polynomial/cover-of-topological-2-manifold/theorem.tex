Let $M$ be a topological $2$-manifold and let $A$ be a locally compact topological space. Suppose that there is a continuous map $\pi \colon A \to M$ satisfying these properties:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(5.1.1)] $\pi$ is onto;
\item[(5.1.2)] for each $x \in M$, there exists an open neighborhood $U$ of $x$ in $M$ such that $\pi^{-1}(U)$ is a disjoint union of open subsets $V_{\alpha}$ of $A$, each mapped homeomorphically onto $U$ by $\pi$.
\end{enumerate}

Then the triple $(A, M, \pi)$ is called a \text{cover} and $A$ is called a \text{covering} of $M$. If the number of the $V_{\alpha}$'s associated to each $U$ is a constant $s \in \mathbb{Z}^{+}$, then $(A, M, \pi)$ is called an \text{$s$-sheeted cover}.
