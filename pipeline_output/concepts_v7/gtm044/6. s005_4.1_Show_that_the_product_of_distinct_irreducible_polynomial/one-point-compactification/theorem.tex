Let $T$ be a topological space, and let $P$ be an abstract point not in $T$. The \text{one-point compactification} $T^{*}$ of $T$ is the space described as follows:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(5.9.1)] The underlying set of $T^{*}$ is $T \cup \{P\}$;
\item[(5.9.2)] A basis for the open sets is given by:
  \begin{enumerate}
  \item[(a)] the open sets of $T$;
  \item[(b)] subsets $U$ of $T \cup \{P\}$ such that $(T \cup \{P\}) \setminus U$ is a closed compact set of $T$.
  \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}
