Let $A$, $M$ be as in Definition 5.1, let $P_1, \ldots, P_r$ be finitely many points of $M$, and let $f \colon A \to M$ be a (not necessarily onto) map. Then the triple $(A, M, f)$ is called a \text{near cover} and $A$, a \text{near covering} of $M$ if

$$
\left(A \setminus f^{-1}(\{P_1, \ldots, P_r\}),\; M \setminus \{P_1, \ldots, P_r\},\; f \mid_{A \setminus f^{-1}(\{P_1, \ldots, P_r\})}\right)
$$

is a cover. If this last triple is an $s$-sheeted cover, then $(A, M, f)$ is called a \text{near $s$-sheeted cover}.
