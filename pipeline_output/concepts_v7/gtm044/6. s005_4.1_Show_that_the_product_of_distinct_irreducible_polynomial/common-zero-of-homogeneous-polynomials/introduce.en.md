Any two nonconstant homogeneous polynomials in at least three variables (i.e., $r \geqslant 2$) over $\mathbb{C}$ must share a nontrivial common zero. The proof writes both polynomials in $X_r$ with leading coefficients in the remaining variables, applies a linear change of coordinates to ensure nonvanishing leading coefficients, and uses Theorem 6.2: the resultant $\mathcal{R}_{X_r}$, being homogeneous of positive degree $mn$, necessarily has a nontrivial zero in $\mathbb{C}^r$, which lifts to a common zero of $p$ and $q$. This lemma directly implies the dimension theorem for plane curves (Theorem 6.1): any two curves in $\mathbb{P}^2(\mathbb{C})$ intersect.
