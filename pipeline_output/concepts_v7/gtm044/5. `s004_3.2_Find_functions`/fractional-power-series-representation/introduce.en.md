Theorem 4.13 is a central result in the theory of plane algebraic curves, generalizing the implicit function theorem. It states that near any point $(x_0, y_0)$ on a plane curve defined by a polynomial $p(X,Y)$ with no repeated factors, the curve is locally the union of finitely many branches, each parametrized by a convergent fractional-power series (Puiseux series) in $(X - x_0)^{1/m_j}$. The theorem is proved by analyzing the cyclic permutation of roots as one travels around $x_0$, and it provides the key to understanding the local topological structure described in Lemma 4.1.
