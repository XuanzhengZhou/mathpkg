Let $\mathbb{C}(X)^*$ denote the set of all formal fractional-power series $\sum_{i=i_0}^{\infty} a_i X^{i/n}$ with coefficients $a_i \in \mathbb{C}$, where $n$ is an arbitrary but fixed positive integer and $i_0 \in \mathbb{Z}$. We define $X^{1/1} = X$, and for integers $a, c$ and positive integers $b, d$, we define $X^{a/b} = X^{c/d}$ iff $a/b = c/d$. Two series are equal if they are equal termwise. Addition and multiplication are defined in the natural way, and the quotient $A/B$ ($B \neq 0$) is the unique series $C$ such that $A = BC$. With these definitions, $\mathbb{C}(X)^*$ forms a field.

The field $\mathbb{C}(X)^*$ is algebraically closed.
