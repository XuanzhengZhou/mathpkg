Lemma 4.3 provides a criterion for two polynomials over a unique factorization domain to share a nonconstant common factor. The condition is expressed in terms of the existence of auxiliary polynomials $F$ and $G$ of strictly lower degree satisfying the equation $fG = gF$. This lemma is the algebraic foundation for the definition of the resultant, as the coefficient-comparison of $fG = gF$ leads to a homogeneous linear system whose determinant is exactly the resultant.
