Let $D$ be any unique factorization domain. Let two polynomials in $D[X]$ be
$$f(X) = a_0 X^m + \ldots + a_m,$$
$$g(X) = b_0 X^n + \ldots + b_n.$$
Assume that at least one of $a_0, b_0$ is nonzero. Then $f(X)$ and $g(X)$ have a nonconstant factor in common if and only if there exist polynomials $F(X), G(X) \in D[X]$ such that
$$fG = gF,$$
where $\deg F < m$ and $\deg G < n$.
