Definition 4.5 introduces the resultant and discriminant, two fundamental algebraic tools used throughout algebraic geometry. The resultant $\mathcal{R}(f,g)$ of two polynomials is the determinant of the Sylvester matrix, and it vanishes precisely when $f$ and $g$ share a common factor. The discriminant $\mathcal{D}(f) = \mathcal{R}(f,f')$ detects whether $f$ has repeated factors. These are extended to multivariate polynomials by treating one variable as the main variable, leading to resultants and discriminants with respect to a specific variable, and to the notion of the discriminant variety.
