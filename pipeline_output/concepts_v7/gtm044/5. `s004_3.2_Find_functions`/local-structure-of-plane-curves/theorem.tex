Let $C$ be any complex algebraic curve in $\mathbb{P}^2(\mathbb{C})$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(4.1.1)] $C$ is compact.
\item[(4.1.2)] Let $U_P$ be a neighborhood of $P \in C$. Then at all but finitely many points $P$ of $C$, for a sufficiently small $U_P$, $C \cap U_P$ is topologically an open disk.
\item[(4.1.3)] At each of the remaining points of $C$, for a sufficiently small $U_P$, $C \cap U_P$ is the one-point union of finitely many open disks.
\end{enumerate}
