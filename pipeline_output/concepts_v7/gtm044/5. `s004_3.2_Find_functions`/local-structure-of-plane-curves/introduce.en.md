Lemma 4.1 describes the local topological structure of any complex algebraic curve in the projective plane. Part (4.1.1) states that such a curve is always compact. Parts (4.1.2) and (4.1.3) together describe the local picture near any point: at all but finitely many points, a small neighborhood in the curve is topologically an open disk, while at the finitely many exceptional points, a small neighborhood is a one-point union of finitely many open disks. This lemma is the starting point for the entire section on local structure of plane curves.
