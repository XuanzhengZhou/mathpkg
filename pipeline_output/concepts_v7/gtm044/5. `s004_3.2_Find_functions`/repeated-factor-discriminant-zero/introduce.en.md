Lemma 4.7 establishes the fundamental relationship between repeated factors and the discriminant: over a unique factorization domain of characteristic zero, a polynomial has a repeated nonconstant factor if and only if its discriminant vanishes. The proof uses the formal derivative: if $f$ has no repeated factors, each irreducible factor of $f$ does not divide $f'$, so $f$ and $f'$ are coprime; conversely, a repeated factor $g^s$ ($s \ge 2$) of $f$ also divides $f'$. This lemma is used frequently throughout the study of plane curves to bound the number of singular points.
