Theorem 4.12, the Riemann extension theorem (also known as Riemann's removable singularities theorem), states that if a function is holomorphic on a punctured neighborhood of a point and remains bounded near that point, then the singularity is removable and the function extends holomorphically across the point. This is a fundamental result in complex analysis and is invoked in the study of plane algebraic curves to extend local parametrizations.
