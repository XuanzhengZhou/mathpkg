Let $\Omega$ be a nonempty open subset of $\mathbb{C}$, let $c$ be an arbitrary point of $\Omega$, and let $h(X)$ be single-valued and analytic at each point of $\Omega \setminus \{c\}$. If $h$ is bounded at $c$ (i.e., there exists $M \in \mathbb{R}$ such that $|h(X)| \leq M$ for all $X \in \Omega \setminus \{c\}$), then $h(X)$ can be extended to a single-valued analytic function on all of $\Omega$.
