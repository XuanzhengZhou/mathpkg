Let $p(X, Y) \in \mathbb{C}[X, Y]$ satisfy Assumption 4.2, with $p$ having no repeated irreducible factors and total degree $n$. Then the set of points $(x_0, y_0) \in \mathbb{C}_{XY}$ satisfying
$$p(x_0, y_0) = \frac{\partial p}{\partial Y}(x_0, y_0) = 0$$
is finite. Moreover, $\mathcal{D}_Y(p) \in \mathbb{C}[X]$ is not the zero polynomial, and at any zero $x_0 \in \mathbb{C}_X$ of $\mathcal{D}_Y(p)$, there are at most $n$ solutions $y_0$ to $p(x_0, Y) = (\partial p / \partial Y)(x_0, Y) = 0$.
