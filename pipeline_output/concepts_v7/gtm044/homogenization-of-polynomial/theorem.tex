Let $p \in k[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$ have degree $d$. Write $p = p_0 + p_1 + \ldots + p_d$, where $p_i$ is the homogeneous component of degree $i$. Then

$$H_{X_{n+1}}(p) = p_0 X_{n+1}^d + p_1 X_{n+1}^{d-1} + \ldots + p_d$$

is homogeneous of degree $d$ and is called the homogenization of $p$; we denote it by $H_{X_{n+1}}(p), H_{n+1}(p)$, or by just $H(p)$, depending on context. If $p \in k[X_1, \ldots, \hat{X}_i, \ldots, X_{n+1}]$, the homogenization $H_{X_i}(p) = H_i(p)$ of $p$ at $X_i$ is defined analogously.
