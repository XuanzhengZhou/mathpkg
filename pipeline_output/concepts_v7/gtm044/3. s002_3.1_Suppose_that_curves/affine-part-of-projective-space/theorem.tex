Any $n$-dimensional subspace $W$ of $k^{n+1}$ defines an $(n-1)$-dimensional projective subspace $\mathbb{P}^{n-1}(k)$ of $\mathbb{P}^n(k)$. We may choose this subspace to play the role of the "projective hyperplane at infinity", $\mathbb{P}_{\infty}^{n-1}(k) \subset \mathbb{P}^n(k)$. If we parallel-translate $W$ through a fixed vector $v_0 \in k^{n+1} \setminus W$, obtaining

$$v_0 + W = \{v_0 + w \mid w \in W\},$$

then each $1$-subspace in $k^{n+1} \setminus W$ meets $v_0 + W$ in exactly one point. This sets up a $1$:$1$ correspondence between points of $\mathbb{P}^n(k) \setminus \mathbb{P}_{\infty}^{n-1}(k)$ and points of $k^n$.

We call the set $\mathbb{P}^n(k) \setminus \mathbb{P}_{\infty}^{n-1}(k)$ the affine part of $\mathbb{P}^n(k)$ relative to the hyperplane at infinity $\mathbb{P}_{\infty}^{n-1}(k)$.
