The homogenization of a polynomial $p$ in $n$ variables at a new variable $X_i$ produces a homogeneous polynomial in $n+1$ variables whose dehomogenization recovers $p$. Geometrically, if $p$ defines an affine variety $V \subseteq k^n$, then $H(p)$ defines the affine cone over the projective completion of $V$ in $\mathbb{P}^n(k)$. This operation is fundamental for extending affine varieties to projective varieties, and for studying their behavior at infinity.
