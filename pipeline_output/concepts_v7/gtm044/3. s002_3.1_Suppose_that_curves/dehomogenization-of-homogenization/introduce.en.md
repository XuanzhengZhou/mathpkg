Dehomogenizing the homogenization of a polynomial at the same variable always recovers the original polynomial: $D_i(H_i(p)) = p$. This is clear from the definitions because homogenization introduces the variable $X_i$ in each term to raise the total degree to $\deg(p)$, and setting $X_i = 1$ precisely undoes this process. This lemma confirms that homogenization followed by dehomogenization at the same variable is the identity on $k[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$.
