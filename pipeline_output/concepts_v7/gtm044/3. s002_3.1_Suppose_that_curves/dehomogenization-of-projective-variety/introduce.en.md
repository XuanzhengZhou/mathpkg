This lemma shows that dehomogenization respects the formation of varieties: the affine part of a projective variety defined by homogeneous polynomials is precisely the affine variety defined by their dehomogenizations. The proof is geometric: the dehomogenized variety is the intersection of the projective variety with the affine hyperplane $X_i = 1$. This simple fact justifies the standard technique of homogenizing affine equations to study projective completions and then dehomogenizing to recover affine information.
