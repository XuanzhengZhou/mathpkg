Let $q_1, \ldots, q_r \in k[X_1, \ldots, X_{n+1}]$ be homogeneous polynomials and let $V(q_1, \ldots, q_r) \subset \mathbb{P}^n(k)$ be the projective variety defined by $q_1, \ldots, q_r$. Then

$$D_i(V(q_1, \ldots, q_r)) = V(D_i(q_1), \ldots, D_i(q_r)).$$

Geometrically, this means the dehomogenization of the projective variety at $X_i$ equals the affine variety defined by the dehomogenized polynomials.
