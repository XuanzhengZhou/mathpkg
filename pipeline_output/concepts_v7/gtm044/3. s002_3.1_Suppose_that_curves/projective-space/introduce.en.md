Projective space $\mathbb{P}^n(k)$ over a field $k$ is defined as the set of all $1$-dimensional linear subspaces of the vector space $k^{n+1}$. Each such $1$-subspace is a point, and any $(r+1)$-dimensional subspace yields an $r$-dimensional projective subspace. When $k$ carries a topology (such as $\mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{C}$), the product topology on $k^{n+1}$ induces a natural quotient topology on $\mathbb{P}^n(k)$. This definition unifies the various topological models of projective space and provides a clean framework for defining coordinates and relating affine to projective geometry.
