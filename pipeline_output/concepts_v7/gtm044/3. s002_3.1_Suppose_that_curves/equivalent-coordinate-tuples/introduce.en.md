Two nonzero $(n+1)$-tuples in $k^{n+1}$ are equivalent if one is a nonzero scalar multiple of the other. This equivalence relation captures precisely when two vectors represent the same $1$-subspace, and therefore the same projective point. The equivalence classes are exactly the points of $\mathbb{P}^n(k)$, making this the standard algebraic definition of projective space as $(k^{n+1} \setminus \{0\}) / k^\times$.
