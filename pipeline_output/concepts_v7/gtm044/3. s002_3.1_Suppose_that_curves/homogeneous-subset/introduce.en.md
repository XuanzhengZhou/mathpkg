A subset $S \subseteq k^n$ is homogeneous if it is closed under scalar multiplication by all elements of $k$. Geometrically, such a set is a union of lines through the origin (a cone). Homogeneous subsets of $k^n$ correspond naturally to subsets of projective space $\mathbb{P}^{n-1}(k)$, since each $1$-subspace either is or is not contained in $S$. The empty set, the origin, and unions of $1$-subspaces are the only possibilities.
