A homogeneous polynomial, also called a form, is a nonzero polynomial all of whose monomials have the same total degree. Homogeneous polynomials are essential for defining projective varieties because the condition $q(x) = 0$ is well-defined on projective points: if $q$ is homogeneous of degree $d$, then $q(cx) = c^d q(x)$ for any scalar $c$, so the vanishing of $q$ depends only on the $1$-subspace, not on the choice of representative. The set of all forms of a given degree, together with the zero polynomial, forms a finite-dimensional vector space.
