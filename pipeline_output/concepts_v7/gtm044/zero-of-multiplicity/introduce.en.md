A point $a$ is a zero of multiplicity $n$ for an analytic function $f$ if $f$ can be factored as $(X-a)^n h(X)$ with $h(a) \neq 0$. This means the function vanishes to order $n$ at $a$, and the first $n-1$ derivatives also vanish there. The multiplicity concept is essential for counting zeros correctly in the argument principle and for intersection theory in algebraic geometry.
