Theorem 2.18 provides the purely algebraic definition of dimension: the dimension of the variety defined by an ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ equals the Krull dimension of the quotient ring $\mathbb{C}[X_1,\ldots,X_n]/\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}$. This equivalence between geometric dimension and the length of chains of prime ideals is a cornerstone of modern algebraic geometry, allowing dimension to be studied in arbitrary commutative rings.
