Theorem 3.8 is the strongest form of the dimension theorem proved in this section. While Theorem 3.1 only asserts that the intersection as a whole satisfies the codimension inequality, Theorem 3.8 guarantees that every irreducible component individually respects the bound. This rules out the possibility that some components of the intersection could have anomalously small dimension.
