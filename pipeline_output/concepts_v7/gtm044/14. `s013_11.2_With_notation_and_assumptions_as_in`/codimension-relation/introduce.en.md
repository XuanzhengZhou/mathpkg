Theorem 3.1 is the affine dimension theorem: when two irreducible varieties intersect nontrivially, the codimension of their intersection is bounded above by the sum of their codimensions. Equivalently, the dimension of the intersection is at least the sum of the dimensions minus the ambient dimension. This generalizes the fact that two curves in the plane must intersect, and is a foundational result leading to Bezout's theorem.
