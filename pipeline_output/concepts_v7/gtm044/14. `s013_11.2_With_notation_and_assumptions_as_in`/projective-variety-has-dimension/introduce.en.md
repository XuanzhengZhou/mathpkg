This theorem states the basic fact that every projective variety $V \subset \mathbb{P}^n(\mathbb{C})$ has a well-defined dimension at each of its points, as well as a global dimension. This follows directly from Definition 2.7, since affine representatives always exist and their dimensions are independent of the choice of hyperplane by statement (2.5).
