Theorem 2.24 collects the fundamental properties of product varieties. The set-theoretic product of two affine varieties is again an affine variety. The product of irreducible varieties is irreducible (under a mild condition on their coordinate rings). Most importantly, dimension is additive under products: the dimension of a product variety is the sum of the dimensions of the factors.
