Theorem 2.9 asserts a fundamental uniformity property: every point of an irreducible variety has the same dimension. This means that for irreducible varieties, the local dimension at a point is a global invariant of the variety. In contrast, reducible varieties may have points of different dimensions, and Corollary 2.10 relates these local dimensions to the irreducible components.
