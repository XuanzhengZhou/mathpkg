This definition extends the notion of dimension from affine varieties to projective varieties. The dimension at a point $P$ of a projective variety $V$ is defined as the dimension of any affine representative of $V$ at the corresponding affine representative of $P$. Equivalently, it is one less than the dimension of the associated homogeneous variety $H(V)$ at any nonzero point on the line corresponding to $P$. The dimension of the whole variety is the maximum of the dimensions at its points.
