Lemma 3.7 gives an explicit formula for the resultant of two polynomials that split into linear factors: it is the product of all pairwise differences of their roots. This reveals the resultant as a measure of whether the two polynomials share a common root — the resultant vanishes precisely when some $b_i = c_j$. The sign relation shows the resultant is alternating up to a factor of $(-1)^{rs}$.
