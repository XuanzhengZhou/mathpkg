Notation 11.2 establishes a uniform convention for denoting generic points when studying an embedding $V \subset \mathbb{C}^n_X$ and its extension $V^* \subset \mathbb{C}^{n+m}_{X,\bar{x}}$. The generic point of $V$ is written as $(x) = (x_1, \ldots, x_n)$, and that of $V^*$ as $(x, \bar{x}) = (x_1, \ldots, x_{n+m})$, where $\bar{x} = (x_{n+1}, \ldots, x_{n+m})$. The vector $(x)$ is further decomposed as $(x', x'')$ with $x'$ a transcendence base of the coordinate ring $\mathbb{C}[x]$, which streamlines the proof of Theorem 11.1 on fiber cardinalities.
