Part (11.1.2) of Theorem 11.1 strengthens the fiber cardinality bound when the coordinate ring extension $R \subset R^*$ is integral. In this case, above each point of the dense open subset $W \subset V$, the number of distinct points in $V^*$ lying over it is at least $1$ and at most the field extension degree $D = [\tilde{R}^* : \tilde{R}]$. This ensures that integral extensions induce finite-to-one maps on a Zariski-open subset of the base variety, with the fiber size bounded by the algebraic degree of the extension.
