Lemma 11.5, known as the Normalization Lemma, is the affine version of the Noether Normalization Theorem over the complex numbers. It states that any affine coordinate ring $R = \mathbb{C}[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ of transcendence degree $d$ is integral over a polynomial subring $\mathbb{C}[y_1, \ldots, y_d]$, where the $y_i$ are $\mathbb{C}$-linear combinations of the original generators $x_i$. Geometrically, every irreducible affine variety admits a finite surjective map to $\mathbb{C}^d$, with the linear-combination condition meaning the projection can be realized by a suitable linear change of coordinates in the ambient space.
