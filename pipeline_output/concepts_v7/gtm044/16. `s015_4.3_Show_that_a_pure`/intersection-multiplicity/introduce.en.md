The intersection multiplicity $i(V_1, V_2, L; P)$ is a local numerical invariant that counts, with multiplicity, how the three objects $V_1$, $V_2$, and $L$ meet at $P$. It is defined geometrically as the number of distinct intersection points obtained after a generic small perturbation of all three objects. This definition extends the classical notion of intersection multiplicity for plane curves to varieties of arbitrary dimension in projective or affine space.
