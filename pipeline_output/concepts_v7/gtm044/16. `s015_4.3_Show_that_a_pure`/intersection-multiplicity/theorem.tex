Let $V_1$ and $V_2$ in $\mathbb{P}^n(\mathbb{C})$ or $\mathbb{C}^n$ be of pure dimensions $r$ and $s$, respectively, and let $L^{(n-r)+(n-s)} = L$ be a linear variety of dimension $2n - r - s$ intersecting $V_1$ and $V_2$ properly at a point $P$. The fixed number of distinct points given by Theorem 6.18 is called the \textit{intersection multiplicity} (or \textit{multiplicity of intersection}) of $V_1$, $V_2$, and $L$ at $P$; it is denoted by $i(V_1, V_2, L; P)$.
