Let $V_1$ and $V_2$ in $\mathbb{P}^n(\mathbb{C})$ be pure-dimensional varieties intersecting properly, and let $C_1, \ldots, C_m$ be the irreducible components of $V_1 \cap V_2$, each of dimension $\dim V_1 + \dim V_2 - n$. The \textit{degree of the intersection product} $V_1 \cdot V_2$ is defined as
\[
\deg(V_1 \cdot V_2) = \sum_{j=1}^{m} i(V_1, V_2; C_j) \cdot \deg C_j,
\]
where $i(V_1, V_2; C_j)$ is the intersection multiplicity along the component $C_j$.
