This remark defines the degree of the intersection product $V_1 \cdot V_2$, which is a formal cycle supported on the irreducible components of $V_1 \cap V_2$ with coefficients given by the intersection multiplicities. The notation $\deg(V_1 \cdot V_2)$ is shown to be consistent: it equals the sum over components of the intersection multiplicity times the degree of the component. This definition is essential for the formulation of Bezout's theorem, which relates this degree to the product of the individual degrees.
