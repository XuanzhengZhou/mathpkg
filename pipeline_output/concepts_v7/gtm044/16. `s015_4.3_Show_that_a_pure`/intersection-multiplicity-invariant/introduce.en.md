This theorem shows that the intersection multiplicity of two properly intersecting varieties is independent of the choice of auxiliary linear subspace: for almost every complementary-dimensional linear space through $P$, the same multiplicity is obtained. This is the two-variety analogue of Theorem 6.8 (which concerns the local degree of a single variety). The proof uses a similar incidence-variety argument over the parameter space $\mathbb{C}^{3(n+1)^2}$, reducing to Theorem 6.11.
