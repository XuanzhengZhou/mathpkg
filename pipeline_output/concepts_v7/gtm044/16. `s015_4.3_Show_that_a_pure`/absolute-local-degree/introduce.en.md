The absolute local degree is a fundamental numerical invariant attached to each point of an algebraic variety. It generalizes the multiplicity of a point on a plane curve: for almost every direction through the point, the local intersection multiplicity is the same. At a nonsingular point the absolute local degree is $1$; at a singular point it is strictly greater than $1$. This invariant plays a crucial role in intersection theory and in the definition of the intersection multiplicity of properly intersecting varieties.
