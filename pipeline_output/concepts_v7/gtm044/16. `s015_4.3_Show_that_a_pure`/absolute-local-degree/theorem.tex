Let $V = V^r$ be a pure $r$-dimensional variety in $\mathbb{P}^n(\mathbb{C})$ or $\mathbb{C}^n$ and let $P \in V$. The common fixed value given by Theorem 6.8 (the local degree $i(V, L'; P)$ for almost all $P$-containing transforms $L'$ of an $(n-r)$-dimensional subspace) is called the \textit{absolute local degree} (or \textit{absolute local order}) of $V$ at $P$, denoted by $\mu(P; V)$ or $\operatorname{ord}_P(V)$.
