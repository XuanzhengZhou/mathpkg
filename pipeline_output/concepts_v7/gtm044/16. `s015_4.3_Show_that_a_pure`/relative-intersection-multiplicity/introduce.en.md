This theorem establishes that the local intersection number of a variety $V$ with a complementary-dimensional linear subspace $L$ at a point $P$ is well-defined: by slightly perturbing $L$ (via linear transformations), the intersection near $P$ splits into a fixed number of distinct points. This fixed number depends only on $V$, $P$, and the direction of $L$, and forms the basis for defining the local degree (or order) of $V$ at $P$ in the direction of $L$.
