Let $V_1$ and $V_2$ in $\mathbb{P}^n(\mathbb{C})$ or $\mathbb{C}^n$ be of pure dimensions $r$ and $s$, respectively, and let $L^{(n-r)+(n-s)} = L$ be a linear variety of dimension $2n - r - s$. If $V_1$, $V_2$, and $L$ intersect properly at a point $P$, then for almost every transform $V_1^T$ near $V_1$, $V_2^{T'}$ near $V_2$, and $L^{T''}$ near $L$, there is a common, fixed number of distinct points of $V_1^T \cap V_2^{T'} \cap L^{T''}$ near $P$.
