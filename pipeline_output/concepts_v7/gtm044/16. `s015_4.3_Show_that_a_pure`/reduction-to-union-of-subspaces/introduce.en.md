This lemma is the linchpin in the proof of Bezout's theorem. It shows that any pure-dimensional projective variety can be "degenerated" via a suitable projective transformation (setting certain diagonal entries to zero) into a union of distinct linear subspaces of the same dimension, with the number of subspaces equal to the degree of the original variety. For example, a conic $V(X_1^2 + X_2^2 - X_3^2)$ in $\mathbb{P}^2$ can be transformed into two lines $V((X_2 + X_3)(X_2 - X_3))$ by setting a diagonal coefficient to zero.
