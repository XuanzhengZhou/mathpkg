This lemma supports the proof of the Jacobi criterion by analyzing the behavior of the functions $F_i = Y_i - f_i(X_1, X_2)$ that define the curve as a graph near a smooth point. It states that along any real line in the tangent plane $T$ passing through the origin, the directional derivative of each $F_i$ vanishes. This is a consequence of the differentiability of the local graphing functions $f_i$ and the definition of the tangent plane as the linear approximation to the graph.
