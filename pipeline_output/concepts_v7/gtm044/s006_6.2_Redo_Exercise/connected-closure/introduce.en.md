This is another basic lemma in point-set topology: if a subset $A$ of a topological space is connected, then its closure $\overline{A}$ is also connected. In other words, adding limit points to a connected set cannot disconnect it. This fact is useful in many connectivity arguments, including the proof that complex algebraic curves are connected, where one passes from connectedness of certain open dense subsets to connectedness of their closures.
