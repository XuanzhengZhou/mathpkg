This is a fundamental lemma in point-set topology: if two connected subsets of a topological space share at least one point in common, then their union is also connected. It provides a basic building block for constructing larger connected sets from smaller ones and is used in the proof of Theorem 8.4 to show that the union of intersecting irreducible components of a projective curve is connected.
