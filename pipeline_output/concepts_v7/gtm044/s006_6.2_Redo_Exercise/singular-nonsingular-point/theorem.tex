Let $C$ be any curve in $\mathbb{C}_{XY}$, and let $p \in \mathbb{C}[X, Y]$ be the polynomial having no repeated factors for which $C = V(p)$. Then $V(p)$ is **singular** at $P$ if
$$\frac{\partial p}{\partial X}(P) = \frac{\partial p}{\partial Y}(P) = 0,$$
and is **nonsingular** at $P$ otherwise. We then say $P$ is a *singular* (or *nonsingular*) point of $C$.
