This definition extends the chain concept to define a connectivity relation on a topological space. Two connected open sets are called chain connectible if there exists a finite chain of overlapping connected open sets connecting them. Similarly, two points are chain connectible if they can be joined by such a chain. A space is called chainwise connected when every pair of points is chain connectible. This notion provides a combinatorial connectivity criterion that is particularly useful in the context of analytic continuation arguments used to prove connectedness of complex curves.
