The Jacobi criterion provides a simple algebraic test for determining whether a point $P$ on a plane algebraic curve $V(p) \subset \mathbb{C}^2$ is smooth (nonsingular). It states that $P$ is a smooth point of the curve defined by a square-free polynomial $p(X,Y)$ precisely when at least one of the two partial derivatives $\partial p/\partial X$ or $\partial p/\partial Y$ does not vanish at $P$. This criterion transforms a geometric question about smoothness into an elementary computation with partial derivatives, and it serves as the foundation for the algebraic definition of singular and nonsingular points on affine plane curves.
