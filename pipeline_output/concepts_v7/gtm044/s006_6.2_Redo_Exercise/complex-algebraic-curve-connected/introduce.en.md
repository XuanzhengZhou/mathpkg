This theorem states that every complex algebraic curve in the projective plane $\mathbb{P}^2(\mathbb{C})$ is a connected topological space (in the classical Euclidean topology). The proof proceeds by first reducing to the case of an irreducible curve defined by an irreducible homogeneous polynomial. By dehomogenizing with respect to a suitable line at infinity, the problem is then reduced to proving connectedness of an affine curve $V(p) \subset \mathbb{C}^2$ defined by an irreducible polynomial. The key tool in the proof is the use of analytic continuation along chains in the base of the branched covering $\pi_Y: V(p) \to \mathbb{C}$. This result contrasts with the real case, where real algebraic curves can be disconnected (e.g., a real elliptic curve can consist of two disjoint circles).
