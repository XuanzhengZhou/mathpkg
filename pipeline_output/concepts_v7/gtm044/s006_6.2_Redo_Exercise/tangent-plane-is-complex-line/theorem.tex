Let $V(p) \subset \mathbb{C}_{XY} = \mathbb{R}_{X_1 X_2 Y_1 Y_2} = \mathbb{R}^4$ be smooth at $P = (0) \in \mathbb{R}^4$, with the part of $V(p)$ near $(0)$ being the graph of a smooth function $f = (f_1, f_2)$. Let $T$ be the real tangent plane to $V(p)$ at $(0)$ (the intersection of the two real 3-spaces locally approximating $f_1$ and $f_2$). Then this real 2-space $T$ is actually a complex 1-space.
