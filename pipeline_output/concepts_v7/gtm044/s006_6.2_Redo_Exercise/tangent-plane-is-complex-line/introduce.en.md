This lemma is a key observation used in the proof of the Jacobi criterion (Theorem 7.4). When a complex plane curve $V(p) \subset \mathbb{C}^2$ is viewed as a real surface in $\mathbb{R}^4$, its real tangent plane at a smooth point turns out to carry the structure of a complex line. This follows from equating real and imaginary parts of the defining equations and applying the Cauchy-Riemann equations. The fact that the real 2-dimensional tangent space is a complex 1-dimensional subspace is a manifestation of the holomorphic nature of the curve at smooth points.
