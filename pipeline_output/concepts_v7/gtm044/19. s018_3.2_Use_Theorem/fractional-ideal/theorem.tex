Let $R$ be any integral domain, and let $K$ be its quotient field. A fractional ideal of $R$ is any sub-$R$-module $R'$ of $K$ satisfying these properties:

(5.3.1) There exists a $d_0 \in R$ such that each element of $R'$ can be written in the form $r/d_0$ where $r \in R$.

(5.3.2) $a \in R'$ and $r \in R$ implies $ar \in R'$.

Any $d_0$ satisfying Property 5.3.1 is called a universal denominator of the fractional ideal.
