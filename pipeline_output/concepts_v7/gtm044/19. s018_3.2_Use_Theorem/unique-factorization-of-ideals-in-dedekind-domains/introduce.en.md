This theorem generalizes the fundamental theorem of arithmetic to a broad class of rings called Dedekind domains. A Dedekind domain is a Noetherian integrally closed domain in which every nonzero prime ideal is maximal (Krull dimension one). In such a ring, every nonzero integral ideal factors uniquely as a product of prime ideals, and every fractional ideal is a unique quotient of products of prime ideals. The set of integral ideals forms a semigroup isomorphic to the semigroup of positive point chains (divisors) on the corresponding abstract curve, while the fractional ideals form a group isomorphic to the full divisor group.
