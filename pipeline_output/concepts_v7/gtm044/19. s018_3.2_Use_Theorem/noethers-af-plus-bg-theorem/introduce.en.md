Max Noether's theorem (also called the AF+BG theorem) is a fundamental result about divisors on plane curves. It states that if the divisor of a form $G$ is bounded by the divisor of another form $H$ on a nonsingular plane curve $C = \mathbf{V}(F)$, then $H$ can be expressed as $H = GG^* + FF^*$ for some forms $G^*, F^*$. Geometrically, this means that the difference between two divisors induced by curves can be "filled in" by another curve, modulo multiples of the defining equation of $C$. The proof uses the resultant and a careful cancellation argument.
