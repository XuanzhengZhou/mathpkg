Let $P$ be a point of a nonsingular curve $C$, $z$ any uniformizing variable of $C$'s local ring at $P$, and write, for any $\omega \in \Omega(K_C, \mathbb{C})$, $\omega = f\,dz$ where $f \in K_C$. The order of $\omega$ at $P$ is the order of $f$ at $P$, and is written $\operatorname{ord}_P(\omega)$. If $\operatorname{ord}_P(\omega) \geq 0$, then (in analogy with functions) we say that $\omega$ is holomorphic at $P$.
