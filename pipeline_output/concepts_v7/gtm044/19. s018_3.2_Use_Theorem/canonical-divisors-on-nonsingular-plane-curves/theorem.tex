Let $C \subset \mathbb{P}^2(\mathbb{C})$ be an irreducible nonsingular curve. If $\deg C = d \geqslant 3$ then:

(6.18.1) Every canonical divisor $\operatorname{div}(\omega)$ is of the form $\operatorname{div}(F) + \operatorname{div}(f)$ for some form (that is, curve with multiplicity) $F = F(X, Y, Z)$ of degree $d - 3$ and some $f \in K_C$;

(6.18.2) Conversely, for every curve with multiplicity $F$ in $\mathbb{P}^2(\mathbb{C})$ of degree $d - 3$ and any $f \in K_C$, $\operatorname{div}(F) + \operatorname{div}(f)$ is a canonical divisor.
