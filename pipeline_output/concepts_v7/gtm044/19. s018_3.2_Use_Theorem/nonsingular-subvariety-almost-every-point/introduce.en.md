An irreducible subvariety $W$ of an irreducible variety $V$ is said to be nonsingular in $V$ if $W$ is not contained in the singular locus $S(V)$ of $V$. This theorem shows that this global condition is equivalent to the pointwise condition that almost every point $P \in W$ is a nonsingular point of $V$. The proof uses the Jacobian criterion: a suitable $(n-r) \times (n-r)$ minor of the Jacobian matrix being nonzero at the generic point of $W$ implies it is nonzero at almost all points of $W$, and conversely.
