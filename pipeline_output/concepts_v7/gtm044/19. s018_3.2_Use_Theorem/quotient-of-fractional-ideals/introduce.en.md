This definition introduces the quotient and inverse operations for fractional ideals. The quotient $\mathfrak{a}:\mathfrak{b}$ consists of all elements $x$ of the quotient field such that multiplying any element of $\mathfrak{b}$ by $x$ lands in $\mathfrak{a}$. An invertible fractional ideal is one that has a multiplicative inverse; in a Dedekind domain, every nonzero fractional ideal is invertible. Note that for ordinary ideals, this definition yields a larger set than the ideal quotient restricted to $R$ (Exercise 4.5 of Chapter III), so one must specify which sense is intended.
