This is the algebraic definition of differentials (Kähler differentials) for a field extension $k \subset K$ of characteristic zero. One starts with the free vector space $V_1$ on formal symbols $dx$ for each $x \in K$, then quotients by the subspace $V_2$ enforcing $k$-linearity and the Leibniz rule. For the function field $K_C$ of a curve, one obtains a one-dimensional $K_C$-vector space $\Omega(K_C, \mathbb{C})$. A differential $\omega$ is thus an equivalence class $f\,dx$; the familiar rules $da = 0$ for $a \in \mathbb{C}$ and $d(p/q) = (q\,dp - p\,dq)/q^2$ follow immediately.
