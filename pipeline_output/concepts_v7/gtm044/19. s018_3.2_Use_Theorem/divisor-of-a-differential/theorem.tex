The divisor of a differential $\omega$ on a nonsingular curve $C$, denoted $\operatorname{div}(\omega)$, is

$$\operatorname{div}(\omega) = \sum_{P \in C} (\operatorname{ord}_P \omega) P,$$

where the sum is over all points $P$ of $C$. The divisor of zeros of $\omega$ is $\operatorname{div}_0(\omega) = \sum_{P} (\operatorname{ord}_P \omega) P$ ranging over points where $\operatorname{ord}_P(\omega) > 0$, and the divisor of poles of $\omega$ is $\operatorname{div}_\infty(\omega) = \sum_{P} -(\operatorname{ord}_P \omega) P$ ranging over points where $\operatorname{ord}_P(\omega) < 0$. Thus $\operatorname{div}(\omega) = \operatorname{div}_0(\omega) - \operatorname{div}_\infty(\omega)$. The divisor of any differential is called a canonical divisor (in analogy with principal divisors of functions).
