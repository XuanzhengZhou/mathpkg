A homogeneous polynomial, also called a form, is a polynomial in which every term has the same total degree. Forms are the natural polynomials for defining projective varieties, since the condition $q(x) = 0$ is well-defined on projective space precisely when $q$ is homogeneous. The set of all homogeneous polynomials of a fixed degree forms a finite-dimensional vector space over the base field.
