A module over a ring $R$ is the natural generalization of a vector space, where the field of scalars is replaced by an arbitrary ring. Modules preserve the four axioms of scalar multiplication but lose properties that depend on division (since ring elements may not be invertible). They are fundamental throughout commutative algebra and algebraic geometry, appearing in integral dependence, Noetherian conditions, and homological algebra.
