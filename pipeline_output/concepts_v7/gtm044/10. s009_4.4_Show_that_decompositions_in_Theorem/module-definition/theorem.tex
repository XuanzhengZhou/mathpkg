A module $M$ over a ring $R$ is a commutative group $(M, +)$ together with a map from $R \times M$ to $M$ satisfying, for any $m, n \in M$ and $r, s \in R$:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(1)] $(r + s)m = rm + sm$
\item[(2)] $r(m + n) = rm + rn$
\item[(3)] $r(sm) = (rs)m$
\item[(4)] $1 \cdot m = m$ (if $R$ has a multiplicative identity $1$)
\end{itemize}
