In any ring, every prime ideal is irreducible.

That is, if $\mathfrak{a}$ is a prime ideal and $\mathfrak{a} = \mathfrak{a}_1 \cap \mathfrak{a}_2$ with $\mathfrak{a}_1 \supset \mathfrak{a}$ and $\mathfrak{a}_2 \supset \mathfrak{a}$, then it cannot be that both containments are strict — one must have $\mathfrak{a}_1 = \mathfrak{a}$ or $\mathfrak{a}_2 = \mathfrak{a}$.
