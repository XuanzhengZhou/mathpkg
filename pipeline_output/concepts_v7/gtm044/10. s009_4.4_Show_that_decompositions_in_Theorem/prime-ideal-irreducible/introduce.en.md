This theorem holds in any ring, not just polynomial rings. A prime ideal cannot be expressed as a proper intersection of two strictly larger ideals. The proof is elegant: if a prime ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ were the intersection of two strictly larger ideals, one could pick elements from each difference, and their product would lie in $\mathfrak{a}$ while neither factor does, contradicting primality.
