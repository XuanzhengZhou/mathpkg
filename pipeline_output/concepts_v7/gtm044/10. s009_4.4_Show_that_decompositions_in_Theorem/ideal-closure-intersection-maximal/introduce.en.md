This corollary of the Nullstellensatz gives a concrete description of the closure (radical) of an ideal as the intersection of all maximal ideals containing it. Combined with the correspondence between points and maximal ideals, this means the closure of an ideal corresponds to the set of all points at which every polynomial in the ideal vanishes — the geometric vanishing locus.
