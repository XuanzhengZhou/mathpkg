This corollary follows immediately from Corollary 5.5 and Theorem 5.6 (the strong Nullstellensatz). It states that every prime ideal in the polynomial ring over $\mathbb{C}$ equals its own closure (radical). This fact is essential for establishing the correspondence between irreducible varieties and prime ideals, a cornerstone of the algebra-geometry dictionary.
