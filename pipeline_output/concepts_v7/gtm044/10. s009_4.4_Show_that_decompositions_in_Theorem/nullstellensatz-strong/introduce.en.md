The strong form of the Nullstellensatz states that the intersection of all prime ideals containing a given ideal equals the intersection of all maximal ideals containing it. Combined with Corollary 5.5, this implies that the radical (closure) of an ideal is the intersection of all prime ideals containing it — a fundamental fact used throughout commutative algebra and algebraic geometry.
