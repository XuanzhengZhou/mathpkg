The ideal quotient $\mathfrak{a} : \mathfrak{b}$, also called the colon ideal, is the set of all ring elements that multiply $\mathfrak{b}$ into $\mathfrak{a}$. It is itself an ideal and satisfies $\mathfrak{a} \subset \mathfrak{a} : \mathfrak{b}$. This operation is a fundamental tool in commutative algebra, used in the theory of primary decomposition, ideal arithmetic, and in proving relationships between irreducible and primary ideals.
