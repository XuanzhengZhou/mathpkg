A primary ideal is a generalization of a prime ideal: instead of requiring that $xy \in \mathfrak{a}$ implies either $x \in \mathfrak{a}$ or $y \in \mathfrak{a}$, a primary ideal only requires that if $x \notin \mathfrak{a}$ and $xy \in \mathfrak{a}$, then some power of $y$ lies in $\mathfrak{a}$. Primary ideals play a central role in the Lasker-Noether primary decomposition theorem, which generalizes the fundamental theorem of arithmetic to ideals in Noetherian rings.
