This result connects primary and prime ideals: the radical of any primary ideal is always prime. The prime ideal $\mathfrak{p} = \sqrt{\mathfrak{q}}$ is called the associated prime of the primary ideal $\mathfrak{q}$, and one says $\mathfrak{q}$ is $\mathfrak{p}$-primary. This relationship is fundamental to primary decomposition theory, where each primary component comes with an associated prime.
