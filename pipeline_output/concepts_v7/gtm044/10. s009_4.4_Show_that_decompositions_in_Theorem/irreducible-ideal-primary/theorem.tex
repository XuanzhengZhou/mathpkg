In a Noetherian ring, every irreducible ideal is primary.

That is, if $R$ is Noetherian and $\mathfrak{q} \subset R$ is an irreducible ideal, then $\mathfrak{q}$ is primary:
$$(x \notin \mathfrak{q} \text{ and } xy \in \mathfrak{q}) \implies (y^m \in \mathfrak{q} \text{ for some } m \in \mathbb{Z}^+).$$
