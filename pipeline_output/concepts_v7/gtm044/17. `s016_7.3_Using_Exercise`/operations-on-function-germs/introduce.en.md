This definition lifts the pointwise ring operations on functions to the level of germs. Addition, multiplication, and negation of function germs are defined via the corresponding operations on representative functions, and the definitions are well-defined (independent of the choice of representatives). The reciprocal of a germ is defined when the function is nonzero near $P$. The set of function germs at a point forms a ring, and one may consider various classes (continuous, differentiable, analytic) yielding corresponding subrings.
