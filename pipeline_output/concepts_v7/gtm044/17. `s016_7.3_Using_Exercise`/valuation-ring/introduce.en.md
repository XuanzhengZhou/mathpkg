A valuation ring is a subring $R$ of a field $K$ that totally orders the field via divisibility: for every nonzero $a \in K$, either $a$ or its inverse belongs to $R$. This is the fundamental algebraic object used to formalize evaluation of rational functions on an algebraic variety. In this book, "valuation ring" always means a valuation ring over $\mathbb{C}$ unless stated otherwise (Remark 2.14). Valuation rings capture the idea of assigning a "value" or "order of vanishing" to field elements.
