Pascal's theorem is a classic result in projective geometry concerning a hexagon inscribed in a conic. It states that the three intersection points of opposite sides of the hexagon are collinear. The theorem holds for any irreducible conic in the real projective plane, and the hexagon need not be convex. The theorem can be proved using properties of algebraic curves and the fact that any conic is defined by a homogeneous polynomial of degree 2.
