Theorem 2.32 guarantees that the natural evaluation of regular functions (elements of the coordinate ring) at a point $P$ on a plane curve can be extended to the entire function field via power series. Each rational function can be expanded as a Laurent series in a uniformizing parameter, and its value is the constant term ($c_0$ if the order is 0), $0$ (if positive order), or $\infty$ (if negative order). This construction provides the rigorous foundation for evaluating rational functions at points of indeterminacy by choosing a specific mode of approach (branch).
