Theorem 2.33 provides a canonical power series parametrization for any branch of an irreducible plane curve at a point. After a suitable change of uniformizing parameter, every branch can be represented with $x = T^m$ (a pure power) and $y$ as a convergent power series in $T$. This simple form is crucial for connecting the algebraic theory of valuation rings with the geometric theory of curve branches, and it allows one to relate arbitrary power series representations to the fractional-power series factorization of the defining polynomial of the curve.
