Theorem 2.12 establishes the equivalence between two perspectives on valuation: (i) an assignment of values in $k \cup \{\infty\}$ to field elements satisfying natural axioms (preservation of addition and multiplication, and the property that the value of a sum being $\infty$ implies both summands have value $\infty$), and (ii) a subring $R$ of $K$ with the property that for any $a \in K$, either $a \in R$ or $1/a \in R$. This theorem motivates the algebraic definition of a valuation ring.
