A discrete rank one valuation ring is a valuation ring arising from a discrete rank one valuation, i.e., an order function from $K \setminus \{0\}$ onto $\mathbb{Z}$ satisfying the usual properties. The ring consists of all elements of nonnegative order (those that do not "vanish" at the associated place). These rings are the algebraic counterparts of the power series rings $\mathbb{C}[[T]]$ and are the local rings appearing at nonsingular points of algebraic curves.
