The germ of a set at a point captures the local behavior of the set arbitrarily close to that point. Two subsets that agree on some neighborhood of $P$ belong to the same germ. This concept formalizes the idea of "nearness" and is fundamental for defining modes of approach to a point on an algebraic variety, which in turn is used to evaluate rational functions at points of indeterminacy.
