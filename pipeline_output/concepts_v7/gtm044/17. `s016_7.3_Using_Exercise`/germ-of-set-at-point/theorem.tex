Let $\mathcal{T}$ be a topological space, let $S$ be a subset of $\mathcal{T}$, and let $P$ be a point of $\mathcal{T}$. The germ of $S$ at $P$, denoted $S_P^\sim$, is the set of all subsets $T$ of $\mathcal{T}$ which coincide with $S$ on some $\mathcal{T}$-neighborhood of $P$; that is, $T$ is a member of the germ iff there is some open neighborhood $U$ of $P$ such that $T \cap U = S \cap U$.
