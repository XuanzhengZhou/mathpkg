This corollary shows that every valuation ring is a local ring: it has exactly one maximal ideal, consisting of those elements whose inverses lie outside the ring. This maximal ideal corresponds to the elements that "vanish" under the valuation, i.e., elements assigned value $0$ or $\infty$ by the associated evaluation. The unique maximal ideal structure makes valuation rings particularly well-behaved for algebraic geometry, where the maximal ideal corresponds to a distinguished point (the center of the valuation).
