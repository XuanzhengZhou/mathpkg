\textbf{Theorem 2.7.} If $p \in \mathbb{C}[X, Y] \setminus \mathbb{C}$ is irreducible, then topologically $V(p)$ is obtained by taking a real $2$-dimensional compact, connected, orientable manifold (this turns out to be a sphere with $g < \infty$ handles) and identifying finitely many points to finitely many points; for any $p \in \mathbb{C}[X, Y] \setminus \mathbb{C}$, $V(p)$ is a finite union of such objects, each one furthermore touching every other one in finitely many points.
