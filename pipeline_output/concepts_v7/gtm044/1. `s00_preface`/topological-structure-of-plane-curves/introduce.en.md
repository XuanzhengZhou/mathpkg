This theorem provides a complete topological classification of plane algebraic curves in the complex projective plane $\mathbb{P}^2(\mathbb{C})$. For an irreducible polynomial $p$, the variety $V(p)$ is topologically a compact connected orientable surface of some genus $g$, possibly with finitely many point identifications (singularities). For a general polynomial, $V(p)$ decomposes as a finite union of such irreducible components, with each pair of components intersecting in at most finitely many points. This result links the algebraic notion of irreducibility of the defining polynomial directly to the topological indecomposability of the corresponding curve.
