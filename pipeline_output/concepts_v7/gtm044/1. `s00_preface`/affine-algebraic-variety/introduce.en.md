An affine algebraic variety is the solution set of a system of polynomial equations over a field $k$, viewed inside affine $n$-space $k^n$. When the system consists of a single nonconstant polynomial, the resulting variety is called a hypersurface; in the special case $n = 2$, it is called a plane affine curve. This definition provides the fundamental geometric objects of study in algebraic geometry, generalizing classical shapes such as circles, ellipses, and lines to arbitrary dimensions and arbitrary ground fields.
