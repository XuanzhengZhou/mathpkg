Definition 10.7 gives the algebraic counterpart to the geometric notion of one variety lying over another. For an embedding of coordinate rings $R \subset R^*$, an ideal $\mathfrak{a}^*$ of the larger ring is said to lie over an ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ of the smaller ring precisely when $\mathfrak{a}$ is recovered by intersecting $\mathfrak{a}^*$ with $R$, i.e., when $\mathfrak{a} = \mathfrak{a}^* \cap R$. This is simply the contraction operation from Definition 9.1. The algebraic and geometric formulations of lying over are connected by Theorem 10.8, which shows they are essentially equivalent: an ideal lies over another if and only if the corresponding varieties satisfy the geometric lying-over relation.
