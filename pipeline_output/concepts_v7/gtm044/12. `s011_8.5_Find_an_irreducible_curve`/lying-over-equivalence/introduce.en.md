Theorem 10.8 closes the circle between the algebraic and geometric formulations of the lying-over relation for an embedding of coordinate rings $R \subset R^*$. Part (10.8.1) states that if an ideal $\mathfrak{a}^*$ lies over $\mathfrak{a}$ (in the algebraic sense of Definition 10.7), then the variety $W^*$ defined by $\mathfrak{a}^*$ lies over the variety $W$ defined by $\mathfrak{a}$ (in the geometric sense of Definition 10.6). Part (10.8.2) provides the converse: if a variety $W^*$ lies over $W$, then the defining ideals satisfy the algebraic lying-over relation. Together, these two implications show that the algebra-geometry dictionary extends seamlessly to the notion of projection: contraction of ideals faithfully mirrors the geometric projection of varieties, with Zariski-density exactly corresponding to the contraction operation.
