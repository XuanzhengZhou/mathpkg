Let $R \subset R^*$ be coordinate rings, and let $\mathfrak{a}$ be any ideal of $R$. Then the set of all points in $\mathcal{V}_{R^*}$ lying above $\mathcal{V}(\mathfrak{a}) \subset \mathcal{V}_R$ is precisely the variety $\mathcal{V}(\mathfrak{a}^e)$. Hence
$$\mathcal{V}(\mathfrak{a}^e) = \pi^{-1}(\mathcal{V}(\mathfrak{a})),$$
where $\pi : \mathcal{V}_{R^*} \to \mathcal{V}_R$ is the natural projection $m^* \mapsto m^* \cap R$ of Definition 9.8.
