Definition 9.1 formalizes how a coordinate ring surjection $h_{\mathfrak{p}} : R \to R/\mathfrak{p}$ induces a complete system of maps between the associated ideal and variety lattices of $R$ and $R^* = R/\mathfrak{p}$. The extension $(\cdot)^e$ sends an ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ of $R$ to $\mathfrak{a} + \mathfrak{p}$ in $R^*$, while the contraction $(\cdot)^c$ pulls back ideals along $h_{\mathfrak{p}}^{-1}$. For closed ideals, the closed extension incorporates a radical $\sqrt{\mathfrak{a} + \mathfrak{p}}$ to ensure the image lands in $\mathcal{J}(R^*)$. These induced maps, together with the lattice isomorphisms $V, J, V^*, J^*$, produce a double sequence (Diagram 2) that encodes how much lattice structure and irreducible decomposition is preserved under the homomorphism.
