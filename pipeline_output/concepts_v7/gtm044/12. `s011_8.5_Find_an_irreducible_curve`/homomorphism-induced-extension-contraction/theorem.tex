Let $h_{\mathfrak{p}} : R \to R/\mathfrak{p} = R^*$ be the natural onto homomorphism determined by a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ of the coordinate ring $R$. Then $h_{\mathfrak{p}}$ induces maps between the lattice sequences associated to $R$ and $R^*$ as follows:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $h_{\mathfrak{p}}$ induces the extension map $(\cdot)^e : \mathfrak{a} \mapsto \mathfrak{a} + \mathfrak{p} = \mathfrak{a}^e$ from $\mathcal{I}(R)$ to $\mathcal{I}(R/\mathfrak{p}) = \mathcal{I}(R^*)$; $h_{\mathfrak{p}}^{-1}$ induces the contraction map $(\cdot)^c : \mathfrak{b} \mapsto h_{\mathfrak{p}}^{-1}(\mathfrak{b}) = \mathfrak{b}^c$ from $\mathcal{I}(R^*)$ to $\mathcal{I}(R)$.

\item[(b)] $h_{\mathfrak{p}}$ induces the \textit{closed extension}
$$\mathfrak{a} \mapsto \sqrt{\mathfrak{a} + \mathfrak{p}} = \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}^e}$$
from $\mathcal{I}(R)$ to $\mathcal{J}(R^*)$; $h_{\mathfrak{p}}^{-1}$ defines the contraction
$$\mathfrak{b} \mapsto h_{\mathfrak{p}}^{-1}(\mathfrak{b}) = \mathfrak{b}^c$$
from $\mathcal{J}(R^*)$ to $\mathcal{I}(R)$. This restriction of $(\cdot)^c$ to $\mathcal{J}(R^*)$ is still denoted by $(\cdot)^c$.

\item[(c)] Let $V$ and $J$ be the lattice-reversing isomorphisms between $\mathcal{V}(R)$ and $\mathcal{J}(R)$, and let $V^*$ and $J^*$ be the lattice-reversing isomorphisms between $\mathcal{V}(R^*)$ and $\mathcal{J}(R^*)$. Then $h_{\mathfrak{p}}$ and $h_{\mathfrak{p}}^{-1}$ induce maps
$$V \mapsto V^* \circ (\cdot)^e \circ J, \quad V^* \mapsto V \circ (\cdot)^c \circ J^*$$
between $\mathcal{V}(R)$ and $\mathcal{V}(R^*)$.
\end{enumerate}

In the closed extension the radical is necessary; in the contraction, $h_{\mathfrak{p}}^{-1}(\mathfrak{b})$ is already closed.
