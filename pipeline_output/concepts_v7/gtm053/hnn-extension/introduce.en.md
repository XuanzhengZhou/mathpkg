HNN extensions, introduced by Higman, Neumann, and Neumann in 1949, are a fundamental construction in combinatorial group theory. Given a group G with two isomorphic subgroups, an HNN extension adds a new "stable letter" t that conjugates one subgroup to the other. This construction is essential for embedding countable groups into finitely presented groups (Higman's theorem) and for the study of groups with soluble word problem.
