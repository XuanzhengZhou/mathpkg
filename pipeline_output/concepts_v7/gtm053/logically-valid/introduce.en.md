A logically valid formula is true in every structure of the given language, regardless of interpretation. These are the first-order logical truths — the semantic counterpart of provable formulas (theorems of pure logic). By Godel's completeness theorem, a formula is logically valid if and only if it is provable from the logical axioms alone, without any special (non-logical) axioms.
