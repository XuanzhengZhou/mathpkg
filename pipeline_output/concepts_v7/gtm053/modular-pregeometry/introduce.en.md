Modularity is a strong structural property of pregeometries. In a modular pregeometry, the dimension satisfies the modular law. Zilber's trichotomy principle states that every strongly minimal set is either trivial, locally modular, or interprets an algebraically closed field. This holds for Zariski geometries and structures in DCF_0 and ACFA.
