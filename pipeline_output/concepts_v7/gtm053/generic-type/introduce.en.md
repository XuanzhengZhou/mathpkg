The generic type captures the "typical" behavior of elements in a stable theory. In algebraically closed fields, the generic type corresponds to transcendental elements. In differentially closed fields, the generic type of the solution space of a differential equation encodes its dimension. The study of orthogonality between types (comparison of their generic behavior) is central to the geometry of stable theories.
