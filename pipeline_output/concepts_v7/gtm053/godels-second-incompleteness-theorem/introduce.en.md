Godel's second incompleteness theorem shows that no sufficiently strong, consistent theory can prove its own consistency. This definitively refuted Hilbert's program of proving the consistency of mathematics by finitary means. The proof shows that the Godel sentence G is equivalent to Con(T), so if T could prove Con(T), it would prove G, contradicting the first incompleteness theorem.
