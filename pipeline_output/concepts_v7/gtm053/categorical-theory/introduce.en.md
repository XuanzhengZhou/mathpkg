Categoricity in power is a strong completeness property: an uncountably categorical theory has exactly one model in some uncountable cardinality. This concept, central to Morley's theorem and Shelah's classification theory, forces a rich structure theory on the models. Morley proved that categoricity in one uncountable cardinal implies categoricity in all uncountable cardinals for countable languages.
