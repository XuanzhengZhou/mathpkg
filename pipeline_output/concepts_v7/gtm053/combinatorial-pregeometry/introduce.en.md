A combinatorial pregeometry abstracts the notion of algebraic dependence. The exchange property generalizes the Steinitz exchange lemma. Examples include linear span in vector spaces, algebraic closure in fields, and the model-theoretic algebraic closure in strongly minimal sets. The associated geometry captures dependence up to finite sets, analogous to the passage from a vector space to its projective space.
