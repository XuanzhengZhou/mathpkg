A subset $I \subseteq A$ of an $L$-structure is called an \text{indiscernible set} if for every $n$ and every $L$-formula $P(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$, either $A \vDash P(i_1, \ldots, i_n)$ for all distinct $i_1, \ldots, i_n \in I$ or $A \vDash \neg P(i_1, \ldots, i_n)$ for all distinct $i_1, \ldots, i_n \in I$.
