Let $K$ be a field and $\Gamma$ a subgroup of a commutative algebraic group $A(K)$. $\Gamma$ has the \text{Lang property} if for every algebraic subvariety $V \subseteq A$, the intersection $\Gamma \cap V(K)$ is a finite union of cosets of subgroups of the form $\Gamma \cap B(K)$, where $B$ is an algebraic subgroup of $A$.
