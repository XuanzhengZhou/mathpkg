The Lang property generalizes the Mordell-Lang and Manin-Mumford conjectures. If a strongly minimal group is locally modular (one-based), then any definable subset of its Cartesian powers is a finite union of cosets of definable subgroups, implying the Lang property. Faltings's theorem (Mordell-Lang for finitely generated subgroups of abelian varieties in characteristic zero) is equivalent to one-basedness of the expanded structure.
