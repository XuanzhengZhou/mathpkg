Godel's first incompleteness theorem (1931) states that any consistent, recursively axiomatizable theory containing basic arithmetic is incomplete — there are true statements about natural numbers that cannot be proved within the theory. The proof constructs a self-referential sentence (via Godel numbering) that asserts its own unprovability. This shattered Hilbert's program of finding a complete and consistent axiomatization of mathematics.
