Let $T$ be a recursively axiomatizable consistent theory containing Peano arithmetic (or a sufficient fragment thereof). Then there exists a sentence $G$ in the language of $T$ such that:
\begin{itemize}
\item $T \not\vdash G$ (G is not provable in T),
\item $T \not\vdash \neg G$ (the negation of G is not provable in T).
\end{itemize}
Such a $G$ is called a \text{Gödel sentence} for $T$, and $T$ is said to be \text{incomplete}. Moreover, $G$ is true in the standard model of arithmetic.
