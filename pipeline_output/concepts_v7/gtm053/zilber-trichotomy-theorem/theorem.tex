For any nonlinear Zariski geometry $M$, there exist an algebraically closed field $F$ and a nonconstant continuous map $f : M \to F$ such that the only relations on $F$ induced from $M$ are constructible. Consequently, every strongly minimal Zariski structure is either:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Trivial,
\item Locally modular (interpretable in a vector space over a division ring), or
\item Interprets an algebraically closed field.
\end{enumerate}
