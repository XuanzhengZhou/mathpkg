Finitely satisfiable sets are the key hypothesis of the compactness theorem. A set of sentences may be finitely satisfiable even if the whole set has no model (e.g., the set expressing "there are infinitely many elements" together with a bound on size). The compactness theorem states this cannot happen in first-order logic.
