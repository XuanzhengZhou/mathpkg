For any two sets $M$ and $N$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $M$ and $N$ are comparable: either $\operatorname{card} M \leq \operatorname{card} N$ or $\operatorname{card} N \leq \operatorname{card} M$. If both inequalities hold, then $\operatorname{card} M = \operatorname{card} N$. Thus cardinalities are linearly ordered.
\item[(b)] $\operatorname{card} \mathcal{P}(M) > \operatorname{card} M$. In particular, there is no largest cardinality.
\item[(c)] Cardinalities are well-ordered; in any class of cardinalities there is a least one.
\end{enumerate}
