This fundamental theorem of set theory establishes the basic properties of cardinal arithmetic: comparability (any two sets have comparable cardinalities), Cantor's theorem (the power set is strictly larger), and the well-ordering of cardinals. The proof uses the axiom of choice (via Zermelo's well-ordering theorem) and the Cantor-Schröder-Bernstein argument.
