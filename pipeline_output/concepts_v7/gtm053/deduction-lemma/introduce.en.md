The deduction lemma is the syntactic counterpart of the semantic implication: to prove an implication $P \Rightarrow Q$ from a theory, it suffices to assume $P$ and deduce $Q$. It holds for first-order logic with the standard axiomatization, provided $P$ is a sentence (closed formula). This lemma is essential for formalizing mathematical proofs in first-order logic.
