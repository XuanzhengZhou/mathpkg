The theory of algebraically closed fields is one of the fundamental examples in model theory. It is complete, admits quantifier elimination (Tarski's theorem), and is $\aleph_0$-stable. The Ax-Kochen-Ershov theorem and the Lefschetz principle are deep consequences of the model theory of ACF.
