Let $T$ be an $\aleph_0$-stable theory and $\mathbf{M}$ a universal domain for $T$. Let $\operatorname{Def}_n(\mathbf{M})$ be the collection of nonempty definable (with parameters) subsets of $M^n$. The \text{Morley rank} is the minimal function $\operatorname{rk} : \operatorname{Def}_n(\mathbf{M}) \to \mathrm{Ord}$ satisfying:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\operatorname{rk} S \geq \alpha + 1$ iff there exists a countable family $\{S_i : i \in \mathbf{N}\}$ of pairwise disjoint definable subsets of $S$ with $\operatorname{rk} S_i \geq \alpha$.
\item For a limit ordinal $\delta$, $\operatorname{rk} S \geq \delta$ iff $\operatorname{rk} S \geq \alpha$ for all $\alpha < \delta$.
\end{itemize}
The rank of a complete type is the minimum rank of definable sets in the type.
