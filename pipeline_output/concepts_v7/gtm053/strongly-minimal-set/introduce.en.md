A strongly minimal set is a definable set of Morley rank 1 that cannot be split into two definable subsets of rank 1 in any elementarily equivalent model. In ACF_p, strongly minimal sets are irreducible algebraic curves (up to finitely many points). Strongly minimal sets carry a natural combinatorial pregeometry via algebraic closure, and Zilber's trichotomy conjecture classifies their geometries as trivial, locally modular, or field-like.
