Higman's embedding theorem (1961) establishes a deep connection between group theory and computability theory. A finitely generated group embeds in a finitely presented group exactly when its word problem is recursively enumerable. The proof uses HNN extensions and benign subgroups, and it implies the existence of a universal finitely presented group containing all finitely presented groups.
