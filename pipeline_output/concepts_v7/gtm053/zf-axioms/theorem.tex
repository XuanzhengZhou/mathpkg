The axioms of Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory in $L_1\mathrm{Set}$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] \text{Axiom of the empty set}: $\forall x \neg (x \in \varnothing)$.
\item[(b)] \text{Axiom of extensionality}: $\forall z (z \in x \iff z \in y) \iff x = y$.
\item[(c)] \text{Axiom of pairing}: $\forall u \forall w \exists x \forall z (z \in x \iff z = u \vee z = w)$.
\item[(d)] \text{Axiom of union}: $\forall x \exists y \forall u (\exists z (u \in z \land z \in x) \iff u \in y)$.
\item[(e)] \text{Axiom of power set}: $\forall x \exists y \forall z (z \subset x \iff z \in y)$.
\item[(f)] \text{Axiom of regularity}: $\forall x (\neg x = \varnothing \Rightarrow \exists y (y \in x \land y \cap x = \varnothing))$.
\item[(g)] \text{Axiom of infinity, replacement, and choice} (stated separately).
\end{enumerate}
These axioms are true in the von Neumann universe $V$.
