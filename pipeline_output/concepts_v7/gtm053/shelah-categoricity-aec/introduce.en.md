Shelah's categoricity theorem for abstract elementary classes is a far-reaching generalization of Morley's categoricity theorem to non-first-order contexts. It shows that categoricity in a sufficiently large cardinal propagates upward, provided amalgamation and joint embedding hold. The second statement (for $L_{\omega_1,\omega}$) is a remarkable structural result about infinitary languages.
