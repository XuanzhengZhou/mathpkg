An abstract elementary class is Shelah's framework for non-first-order model theory that replaces syntactic considerations with algebraic axioms. Examples include models of $L_{\omega_1,\omega}$-sentences, classes of models of a first-order theory with the elementary substructure relation, and Hrushovski's fusion classes with strong embeddings. The theory of AECs extends classification theory beyond first-order logic.
