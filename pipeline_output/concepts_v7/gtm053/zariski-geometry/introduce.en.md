A Zariski geometry is a topological structure axiomatizing the behavior of algebraic varieties with the Zariski topology. Hrushovski and Zilber proved that every nonlinear Zariski geometry interprets an algebraically closed field, establishing the trichotomy principle. This framework unifies algebraic geometry, complex analytic geometry, and differential/difference algebraic geometry under a single model-theoretic umbrella.
