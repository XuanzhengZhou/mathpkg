A \text{Zariski structure} (geometry) is a structure $M$ equipped with a Noetherian topology and a dimension notion satisfying natural axioms, including:
\begin{itemize}
\item The topology has a basis of closed sets (like the Zariski topology).
\item Projections are closed maps.
\item Dimension is well-behaved with respect to constructible sets.
\end{itemize}
A key theorem states that the theory of a Zariski structure admits quantifier elimination, is $\omega$-stable, and has finite Morley rank. Examples include:
\begin{itemize}
\item Smooth algebraic varieties over algebraically closed fields in the natural language.
\item Compact complex manifolds in the analytic language.
\item Solution spaces of differential equations in DCF_0.
\item Strongly minimal sets in ACFA.
\end{itemize}
