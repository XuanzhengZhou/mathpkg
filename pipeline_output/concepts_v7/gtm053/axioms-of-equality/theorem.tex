The axioms of equality for a first-order language $L$ with a distinguished binary relation symbol $=$ are:
\begin{align*}
&t = t; \quad t_1 = t_2 \Rightarrow t_2 = t_1; \quad t_1 = t_2 \wedge t_2 = t_3 \Rightarrow t_1 = t_3;\\
&x = t \Rightarrow (P(x, x) \Rightarrow P(x, t)),
\end{align*}
where $P(x, t)$ denotes substitution of $t$ for free occurrences of $x$. These formulas are true in any normal model where $=$ is interpreted as actual equality.
