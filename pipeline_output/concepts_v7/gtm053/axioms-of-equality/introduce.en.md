The axioms of equality capture the standard properties of equality — reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and substitutivity — within a first-order language. They ensure that the equality symbol behaves as genuine identity in all normal models. Any model where equality is not interpreted as true equality can be factored by the equivalence relation defined by the interpretation of $=$ to obtain a normal model.
