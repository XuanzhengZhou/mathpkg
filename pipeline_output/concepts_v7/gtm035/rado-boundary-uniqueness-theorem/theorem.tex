% Rado Boundary Uniqueness Theorem
% From GTM035, Section s010
% Source: Alexander & Wermer, Several Complex Variables and Banach Algebras (GTM 35), 3rd Edition

Let $f \in A(\Omega)$ and assume that $f = 0$ on $\partial \Omega \cap U$ for some neighborhood $U$ of a nonisolated boundary point $z_0$. Then $f \equiv 0$ in $\Omega$.
