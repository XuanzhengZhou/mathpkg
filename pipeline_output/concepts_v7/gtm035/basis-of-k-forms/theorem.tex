% Basis Of K Forms
% From GTM035, Section s006
% Source: Alexander & Wermer, Several Complex Variables and Banach Algebras (GTM 35), 3rd Edition

Fix $k$. The set of elements
$$e_{i_1} \wedge e_{i_2} \wedge \cdots \wedge e_{i_k}, \quad 1 \leq i_1 < i_2 < \cdots < i_k \leq N,$$
forms a basis for $\wedge^k(V)$.
