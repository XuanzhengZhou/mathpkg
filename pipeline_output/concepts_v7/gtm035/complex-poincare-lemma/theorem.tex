% Complex Poincare Lemma
% From GTM035, Section s007
% Source: Alexander & Wermer, Several Complex Variables and Banach Algebras (GTM 35), 3rd Edition

Let $\Omega$ be a neighborhood of $\Delta^n$. Fix $\omega \in \wedge^{p,q}(\Omega)$, $q > 0$, with $\bar{\partial}\omega = 0$. Then there exists a neighborhood $\Omega_1$ of $\Delta^n$ and $\tau \in \wedge^{p,q-1}(\Omega_1)$ such that $\bar{\partial}\tau = \omega$ in $\Omega_1$.
