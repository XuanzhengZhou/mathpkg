This theorem provides sharp two-sided bounds linking the Kakutani--Hellinger distance $\rho(P,\tilde{P})$ and the total variation distance $\|P-\tilde{P}\|$. The left inequality $2\rho^2 \leq \|P-\tilde{P}\|$ follows from the pointwise bound $\frac{1}{2}[\sqrt{z} - \sqrt{2-z}]^2 \leq |z-1|$ for $z \in [0,2]$, while the right inequality $\|P-\tilde{P}\| \leq \sqrt{8}\rho$ is proved via Cauchy--Schwarz and Jensen inequalities. A key application is to hypothesis testing with product measures: the error probability $\mathcal{E}r(P^n,\tilde{P}^n)$ of optimal discrimination between $P$ and $\tilde{P}$ decays exponentially as $e^{-n\rho^2(P,\tilde{P})}$ when $\rho(P,\tilde{P}) > 0$.
