This definition extends the classical notion of tightness of a family of probability measures to a sequence of random variables $(\xi^n)$ relative to a sequence of probability measures $(Q^n)$. The condition $\lim_{N \uparrow \infty} \limsup_n Q^n(|\xi^n| > N) = 0$ ensures that the mass of the distributions does not escape to infinity as $n$ grows. In the Le Cam theory of contiguity, this tightness condition is applied to the likelihood ratios $(Z^n)$ and the log-likelihood ratios $(\log Z^n)$, with the canonical choice $Q^n = (P^n + \tilde{P}^n)/2$, providing the technical framework for the equivalence theorems characterizing contiguity and entire asymptotic separation.
