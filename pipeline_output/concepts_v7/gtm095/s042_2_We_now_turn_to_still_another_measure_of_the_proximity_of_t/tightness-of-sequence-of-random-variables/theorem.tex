Let $(\Omega^n, \mathcal{F}^n)_{n \geq 1}$ be a sequence of measurable spaces, let $Q^n$ be a probability measure on $(\Omega^n, \mathcal{F}^n)$, and let $\xi^n$ be a random variable (possibly extended) on $(\Omega^n, \mathcal{F}^n)$, for $n \geq 1$.

A sequence $(\xi^n)$ of random variables is said to be \textbf{tight} with respect to a sequence of measures $(Q^n)$, denoted $(\xi^n \mid Q^n)$ is tight, if

$$\lim_{N \uparrow \infty} \limsup_n Q^n(|\xi^n| > N) = 0.$$

In the context of contiguity and asymptotic separation, one always sets
$$Q^n = \frac{P^n + \tilde{P}^n}{2}, \quad z^n = \frac{dP^n}{dQ^n}, \quad \tilde{z}^n = \frac{d\tilde{P}^n}{dQ^n},$$
and denotes the Lebesgue derivative of $\tilde{P}^n$ with respect to $P^n$ by
$$Z^n = \frac{\tilde{z}^n}{z^n},$$
with the convention $2/0 = \infty$.
