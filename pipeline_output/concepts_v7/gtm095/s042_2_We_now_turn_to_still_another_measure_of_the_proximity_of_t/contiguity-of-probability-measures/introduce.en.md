Contiguity and entire asymptotic separation are the asymptotic counterparts of absolute continuity and singularity for sequences of probability measures. A sequence $(\tilde{P}^n)$ is contiguous to $(P^n)$ if whenever $P^n(A^n) \to 0$, also $\tilde{P}^n(A^n) \to 0$ -- this is "asymptotic absolute continuity." Entire asymptotic separation means there exist sets that are asymptotically almost sure under one sequence and asymptotically null under the other -- this is "asymptotic singularity." These concepts, introduced by Le Cam, are fundamental to the asymptotic theory of mathematical statistics, especially for studying the limiting behavior of likelihood ratios and statistical tests.
