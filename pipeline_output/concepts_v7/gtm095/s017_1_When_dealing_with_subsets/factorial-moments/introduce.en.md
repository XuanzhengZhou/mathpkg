Factorial moments are an alternative to ordinary moments, defined using falling factorials $\xi(\xi-1)\cdots(\xi-r+1)$ instead of powers $\xi^r$. They are particularly convenient for integer-valued random variables because they can be extracted simply by differentiating the probability generating function and evaluating at $x = 1$. Factorial moments are especially natural when working with discrete distributions such as the binomial, Poisson, and negative binomial.
