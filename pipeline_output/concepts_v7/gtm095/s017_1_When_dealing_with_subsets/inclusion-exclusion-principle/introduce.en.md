The inclusion-exclusion principle systematically corrects for overcounting when computing the size of a union of sets. By alternately adding and subtracting intersections, it yields the exact count. The proof using indicator functions is particularly elegant: expanding $\prod(1 - I_{A_i})$ and taking expectation recovers the formula. The principle extends readily from counting to probability and is indispensable for problems involving "at least one" or "none of" conditions.
