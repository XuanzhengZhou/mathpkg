When probabilities are defined classically as ratios of cardinalities on a finite sample space, the inclusion–exclusion counting formulas translate directly into probability formulas by dividing through by $N(\Omega)$. This probability version is essential for computing the probability of unions of events. The alternating sum structure remains identical: probabilities of single events are added, pairwise intersection probabilities are subtracted, triple intersections are added back, and so forth.
