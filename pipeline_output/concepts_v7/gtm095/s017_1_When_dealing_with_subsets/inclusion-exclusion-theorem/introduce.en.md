The general inclusion–exclusion theorem provides a formula for counting the number of elements in the union (or the complement of the union) of $n$ sets. The formula alternates sums over intersections of increasing order. The elegant proof using indicator functions demonstrates the power of algebraic methods in combinatorics: by expressing the indicator of a union as an alternating sum of products of indicators, one obtains the counting formulas by summing over all outcomes.
