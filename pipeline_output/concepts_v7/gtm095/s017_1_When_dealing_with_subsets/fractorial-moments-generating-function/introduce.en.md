Factorial moments are natural companions to generating functions: differentiating the generating function $r$ times and evaluating at $x=1$ extracts the $r$-th factorial moment. This relationship is the discrete analog of how moment generating functions yield ordinary moments. Factorial moments are particularly convenient for integer-valued random variables because they simplify expressions involving binomial coefficients and are often easier to compute than raw moments.
