The indicator function $I_A$ of a set $A$ is a simple but powerful tool that encodes membership as a binary value. It transforms set-theoretic operations into algebraic ones: for example, the indicator of an intersection is the product of indicators, and the indicator of a complement is one minus the indicator. This algebraic representation provides an elegant proof of the inclusion–exclusion principle by expanding the product $(1 - I_{A_1}) \cdots (1 - I_{A_n})$ and summing over the sample space.
