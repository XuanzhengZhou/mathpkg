Theorem 2 establishes the logical relationship between the classical Lindeberg condition (L) and the nonclassical condition $(\Lambda)$ for triangular arrays of independent random variables. Part (1) shows that Lindeberg's condition is always sufficient for $(\Lambda)$. Part (2) provides the converse: under the additional hypothesis that the maximal second moment tends to zero, condition $(\Lambda)$ implies the Lindeberg condition, making them equivalent in the asymptotically negligible setting.
