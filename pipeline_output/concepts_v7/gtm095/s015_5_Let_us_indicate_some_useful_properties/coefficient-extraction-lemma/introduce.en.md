The coefficient extraction lemma is the fundamental link between generating functions and combinatorial counting. It states that the number of solutions to a linear Diophantine equation with constraints on the variables equals the coefficient of $x^r$ in the product of the generating functions encoding each variable's allowed values. When each variable ranges over all nonnegative integers, the number of solutions is $C_{n+r-1}^r$, recovering the stars-and-bars formula from elementary combinatorics.
