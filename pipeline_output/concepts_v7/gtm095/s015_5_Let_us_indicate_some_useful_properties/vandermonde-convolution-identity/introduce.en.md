Vandermonde's convolution identity is one of the most useful binomial coefficient identities. It states that the coefficient of $x^k$ in $(1+x)^{n+m}$ can be obtained by convolving the coefficients of $(1+x)^n$ and $(1+x)^m$. The generating function proof is remarkably simple: $(1+x)^n(1+x)^m = (1+x)^{n+m}$ immediately yields the identity by equating coefficients. This combinatorial identity has numerous applications in probability theory, particularly in hypergeometric distributions and sampling problems.
