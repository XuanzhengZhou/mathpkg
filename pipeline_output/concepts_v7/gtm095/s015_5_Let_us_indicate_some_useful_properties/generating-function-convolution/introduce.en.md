The convolution property is the most fundamental algebraic property of generating functions: the product of two generating functions corresponds to the convolution (discrete convolution) of their coefficient sequences. This property is the basis for solving combinatorial counting problems, as the number of ways to achieve a total $n$ by combining independent components corresponds to convolutions of their individual counting sequences.
