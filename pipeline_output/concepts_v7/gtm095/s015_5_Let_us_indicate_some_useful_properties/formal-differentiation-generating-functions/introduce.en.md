The formal differentiation operator $D$ acts on generating functions by shifting and scaling coefficients, without any analytic considerations of convergence. It satisfies the same algebraic rules as ordinary differentiation: the product rule and the chain rule. This operator is essential for extracting moments and factorial moments from probability generating functions, since $G_\xi^{(r)}(1)$ yields the $r$th factorial moment of the integer-valued random variable $\xi$.
