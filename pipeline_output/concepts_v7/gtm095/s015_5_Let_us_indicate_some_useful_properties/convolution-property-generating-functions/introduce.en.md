The convolution property is the key algebraic feature of generating functions: it transforms the convolution of two sequences into the ordinary product of their generating functions. In probabilistic terms, if $\xi$ and $\eta$ are independent nonnegative integer-valued random variables, then $G_{\xi+\eta}(x) = G_\xi(x) G_\eta(x)$. This simple multiplicative rule makes generating functions indispensable for analyzing sums of independent random variables.
