An efficient estimator is an unbiased estimator that achieves the minimum possible variance for every value of the parameter. Since the deviation of an estimator from the true parameter value is naturally measured by its variance (or mean squared error for unbiased estimators), efficiency provides a natural optimality criterion. The Cramér–Rao inequality establishes a lower bound on the variance of any unbiased estimator, and an estimator attaining this bound is called efficient.
