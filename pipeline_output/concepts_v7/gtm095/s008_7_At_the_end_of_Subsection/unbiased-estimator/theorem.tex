An estimator $T_n = T_n(\omega)$ is called unbiased if for every $\theta \in \Theta$,

$$E_\theta T_n = \theta,$$

where $E_\theta$ denotes the expectation with respect to the probability measure $P_\theta$. The property of being unbiased expresses that the estimator should, at least on average, yield the correct value of the parameter.
