An unbiased estimator is one whose expected value equals the true parameter value for every possible value of the parameter. This is a natural requirement: a reasonable estimator should be correct "on average." In the Bernoulli scheme, the sample mean $T_n^* = S_n/n$ is unbiased. However, unbiasedness alone does not uniquely determine an estimator; many unbiased estimators may exist, and their quality is further distinguished by their variance.
