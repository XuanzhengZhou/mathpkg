Consistency is a fundamental asymptotic property of estimators: as the sample size grows, a consistent estimator converges in probability to the true value of the parameter being estimated. For the Bernoulli scheme, the sample mean $T_n^* = S_n/n$ is consistent by the law of large numbers. Consistency ensures that with sufficiently many observations, the estimator will be arbitrarily close to the true parameter value with high probability.
