A probabilistic-statistical model formalizes the setting of statistical inference. For the Bernoulli scheme with $n$ independent trials, the model consists of a sample space $\Omega = \{0,1\}^n$ together with a parametric family of probability measures $\{P_\theta\}_{\theta \in \Theta}$ indexed by the unknown parameter $\theta$ representing the success probability. The goal of statistical estimation is to construct functions $T_n : \Omega \to \Theta$ (estimators) that recover $\theta$ from observed data.
