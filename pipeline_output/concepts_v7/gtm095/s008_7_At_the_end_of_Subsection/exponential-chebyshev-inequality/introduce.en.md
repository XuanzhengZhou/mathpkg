The exponential Chebyshev inequality is a powerful refinement of the classical Chebyshev inequality. By exponentiating the random variable before applying Markov's inequality and then optimizing over the exponent parameter $\lambda$, one obtains much sharper tail bounds. This technique, also known as the Chernoff bound method, is the foundation of large deviations theory and yields exponential rather than polynomial decay rates for tail probabilities.
