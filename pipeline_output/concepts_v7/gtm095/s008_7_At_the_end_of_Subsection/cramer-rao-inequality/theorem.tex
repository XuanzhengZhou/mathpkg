Let $T_n = T_n(\omega)$ be an unbiased estimator of the parameter $\theta$ in the Bernoulli scheme. Then

$$E_\theta [T_n - \theta]^2 \geq \frac{1}{I_n(\theta)},$$

where $I_n(\theta) = E_\theta \left[ \frac{\partial L_\theta(\omega)}{\partial \theta} \right]^2$ is the Fisher information. This lower bound on the mean squared error (equivalently, the variance) of any unbiased estimator follows from the Cauchy–Bunyakovskii inequality applied to

$$1 = E_\theta \left[ (T_n - \theta) \frac{\partial L_\theta(\omega)}{\partial \theta} \right].$$
