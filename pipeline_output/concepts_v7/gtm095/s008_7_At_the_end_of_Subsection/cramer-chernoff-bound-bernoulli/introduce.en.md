The Chernoff bound for Bernoulli sums provides exponentially decaying upper bounds on tail probabilities using the moment generating function. By applying the exponential Chebyshev inequality to $S_n/n$ and optimizing over the tilting parameter $\lambda$, one obtains the rate function $H(a)$, which is the binary relative entropy (Kullback-Leibler divergence) between the empirical mean $a$ and the true mean $p$. These bounds are substantially sharper than the polynomial bounds obtained from ordinary Chebyshev inequalities, especially for small deviations.
