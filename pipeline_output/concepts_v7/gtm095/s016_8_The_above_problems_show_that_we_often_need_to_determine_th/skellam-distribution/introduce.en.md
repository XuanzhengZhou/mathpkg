The difference of two independent Poisson random variables follows the Skellam distribution, whose probability mass function involves the modified Bessel function of the first kind. This distribution arises naturally in many settings, including the comparison of two Poisson processes and in sports statistics (modeling score differences). The derivation uses generating functions and the known expansion of $e^{\lambda(t+1/t)}$ in terms of modified Bessel functions.
