Bernoulli numbers are ubiquitous in analysis and number theory, appearing in the Euler-Maclaurin summation formula, the Taylor expansion of $\tan x$, and the values of the Riemann zeta function at even integers. Their exponential generating function $x/(e^x-1)$ is simple but encodes deep arithmetic properties — notably that $b_{2k+1} = 0$ for all $k \geq 1$, as evident from the evenness of the generating function.
