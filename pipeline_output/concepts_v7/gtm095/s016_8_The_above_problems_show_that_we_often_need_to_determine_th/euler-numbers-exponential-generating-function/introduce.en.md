Euler numbers form a sequence of integers appearing in combinatorics and analysis, particularly in the series expansion of the secant function. They are defined by their exponential generating function $2e^x/(e^{2x}+1)$. The odd-indexed Euler numbers $e_{2k+1}$ vanish for all $k \geq 0$. Euler numbers are closely related to alternating permutations (up/down numbers) and to the Maclaurin series of $\sec x$ and related trigonometric functions.
