The Galton-Watson process models the evolution of a population where each individual independently produces a random number of offspring. The extinction probability $q$ is the smallest nonnegative fixed point of the offspring generating function $G$. The critical threshold is $E\eta = 1$: below or at this value extinction is certain, while above it there is a positive probability of indefinite survival. This phase transition is a fundamental result in the theory of branching processes.
