Let $\mathcal{P} = \{P_{\theta}, \theta \in \Theta\}$ be a family of probability measures on $(\Omega, \mathcal{F})$. A $\sigma$-subalgebra $\mathcal{G} \subseteq \mathcal{F}$ is called \text{sufficient} for the family $\mathcal{P}$ if there exist versions of conditional probabilities $P_{\theta}(\cdot \mid \mathcal{G})(\omega), \theta \in \Theta, \omega \in \Omega$, independent of $\theta$, i.e., there is a function $P(A; \omega), A \in \mathcal{F}, \omega \in \Omega$, such that
$$P_{\theta}(A \mid \mathcal{G})(\omega) = P(A; \omega) \quad (P_{\theta}\text{-a.s.})$$
for all $A \in \mathcal{F}$ and $\theta \in \Theta$; in other words, $P(A; \omega)$ is a version of $P_{\theta}(A \mid \mathcal{G})(\omega)$ for all $\theta \in \Theta$.
