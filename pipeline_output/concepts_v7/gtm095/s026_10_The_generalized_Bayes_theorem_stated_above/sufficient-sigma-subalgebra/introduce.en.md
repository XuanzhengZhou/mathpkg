A sufficient sigma-subalgebra captures the intuition that a coarser sigma-algebra contains all the information about the parameter theta that the full sigma-algebra provides. When a sub-sigma-algebra is sufficient, the conditional distribution of the data given that sub-algebra does not depend on theta, so no information about the parameter is lost by restricting to the smaller class of events. This concept is fundamental to the theory of data reduction in mathematical statistics.
