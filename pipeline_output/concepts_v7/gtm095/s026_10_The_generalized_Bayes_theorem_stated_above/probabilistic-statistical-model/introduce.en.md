A probabilistic-statistical model formalizes a statistical experiment as a triple consisting of a sample space, a sigma-algebra of events, and a family of probability measures indexed by a parameter. This framework captures the idea that the true distribution governing observations is unknown but belongs to a specified parametric family. It is the starting point for formal statistical inference, including estimation and hypothesis testing.
