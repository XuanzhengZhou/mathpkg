A necessary and sufficient condition that an $n \times n$ matrix $\mathbb{R}$ is the covariance matrix of a random vector $\xi = (\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_n)$ is that the matrix $\mathbb{R}$ is symmetric and positive semi-definite, or, equivalently, that there exists an $n \times k$ matrix $A$ (with $1 \leq k \leq n$) such that
$$\mathbb{R} = AA^*,$$
where $^*$ denotes the transpose.
