This lemma provides a complete characterization of which matrices can serve as covariance matrices: a matrix is a covariance matrix if and only if it is symmetric and positive semi-definite. The equivalence with the factorization R = AA* connects the probabilistic notion of covariance to matrix factorization, with A serving as a "square root" that can be used to construct a random vector with prescribed covariance from independent standard normal variables.
