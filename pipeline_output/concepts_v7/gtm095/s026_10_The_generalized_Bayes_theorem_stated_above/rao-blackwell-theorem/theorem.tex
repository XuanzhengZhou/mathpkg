Let $\mathcal{G}$ be a sufficient $\sigma$-subalgebra for the family $\mathcal{P} = \{P_{\theta}, \theta \in \Theta\}$ and let $\hat{\theta} = \hat{\theta}(\omega)$ be an estimator.

(a) If $\hat{\theta}$ is an unbiased estimator, i.e., $E_{\theta} \hat{\theta} = \theta$ for all $\theta \in \Theta$, then the estimator
$$T = E_{\theta}(\hat{\theta} \mid \mathcal{G})$$
is also unbiased: $E_{\theta} T = \theta$ for all $\theta \in \Theta$.

(b) The estimator $T$ is better than $\hat{\theta}$ in the sense that
$$E_{\theta}(T - \theta)^2 \leq E_{\theta}(\hat{\theta} - \theta)^2, \quad \theta \in \Theta.$$
