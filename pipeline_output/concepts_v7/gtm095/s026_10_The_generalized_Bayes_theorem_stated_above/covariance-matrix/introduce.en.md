The covariance matrix collects all pairwise covariances between components of a random vector into a single matrix. Every covariance matrix is symmetric and positive semi-definite, reflecting that variances are nonnegative and that covariances satisfy the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality. The covariance matrix is the multivariate generalization of the variance and is central to the study of linear combinations, principal components, and the multivariate normal distribution.
