Let $\xi = (\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_n)$ be a random vector whose components have finite second moments. The \text{covariance matrix} of $\xi$ is the $n \times n$ matrix $\mathbb{R} = \|R_{ij}\|$ where
$$R_{ij} = \operatorname{Cov}(\xi_i, \xi_j).$$
The matrix $\mathbb{R}$ is symmetric and positive semi-definite:
$$\sum_{i,j=1}^{n} R_{ij} \lambda_i \lambda_j \geq 0$$
for all $\lambda_i \in \mathbb{R}$, $i = 1, \ldots, n$.
