Conditional independence of sigma-algebras generalizes ordinary independence by conditioning on a third sigma-algebra. Two families of events are conditionally independent given a third if, once the information in the third sigma-algebra is known, any additional knowledge about one family provides no information about the other. This concept is essential for understanding Markov properties and graphical models in probability theory.
