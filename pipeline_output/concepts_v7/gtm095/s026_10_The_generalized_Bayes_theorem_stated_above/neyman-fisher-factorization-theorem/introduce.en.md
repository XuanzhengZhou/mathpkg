The Neyman-Fisher factorization criterion gives a practical necessary and sufficient condition for sufficiency: a sub-sigma-algebra is sufficient if and only if the likelihood factorizes into a part depending on theta only through the sub-sigma-algebra and a part independent of theta. This makes sufficiency checkable via the functional form of densities, turning an abstract measure-theoretic condition into a concrete factorization equation.
