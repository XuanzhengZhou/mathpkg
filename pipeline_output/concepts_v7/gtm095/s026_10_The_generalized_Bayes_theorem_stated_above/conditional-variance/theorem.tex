Let $X$ and $Y$ be random variables on a probability space $(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \mathsf{P})$. The \text{conditional variance} of $X$ given $Y$ is defined as
$$\operatorname{Var}(X \mid Y) = \mathsf{E}\bigl[(X - \mathsf{E}(X \mid Y))^2 \mid Y\bigr].$$

The variance admits the decomposition (law of total variance):
$$\operatorname{Var} X = \mathsf{E}[\operatorname{Var}(X \mid Y)] + \operatorname{Var}[\mathsf{E}(X \mid Y)].$$
