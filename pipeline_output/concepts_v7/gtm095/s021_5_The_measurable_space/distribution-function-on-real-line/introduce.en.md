A distribution function $F(x)$ on the real line is a nondecreasing, right-continuous function that tends to $0$ as $x 	o -\infty$ and to $1$ as $x 	o +\infty$. Every probability measure $P$ on $(R, \mathcal{B}(R))$ gives rise to a distribution function via $F(x) = P(-\infty, x]$, and conversely (by Theorem 1) every distribution function determines a unique probability measure. This one-to-one correspondence is the foundation for studying probability measures on the real line through the analytically more tractable distribution functions.
