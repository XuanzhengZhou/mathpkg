A discrete probability distribution is a probability measure concentrated entirely on a countable subset of the real line. The distribution is characterized by its probability mass function $(p_k)$, where $p_k = P(\{x_k\})$ gives the probability assigned to each atom $x_k$. The corresponding distribution function $F(x) = \sum_{x_k \leq x} p_k$ is a step function with jumps at the atoms. Important examples include the Bernoulli, binomial, Poisson, and geometric distributions, each arising naturally in the study of independent trials, rare events, and waiting times.
