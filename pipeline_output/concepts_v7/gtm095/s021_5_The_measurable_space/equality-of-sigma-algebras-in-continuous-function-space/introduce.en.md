For the space $C$ of continuous functions on $[0,1]$ with the sup-norm metric, the $\sigma$-algebra generated by cylinder sets coincides with the Borel $\sigma$-algebra generated by the metric topology. One inclusion follows from the observation that cylinder sets $\{x: x_{t_0} < b\}$ are open in the sup-norm topology. The reverse inclusion uses the separability of $C$ under $ho$ to express open balls as countable unions of cylinder sets. This result justifies using either the topological or the cylinder-set approach to define measurability on $C$.
