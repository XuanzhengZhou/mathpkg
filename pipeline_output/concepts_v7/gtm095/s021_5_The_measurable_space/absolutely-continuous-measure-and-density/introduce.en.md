An absolutely continuous probability measure is one whose distribution function can be expressed as the integral of a nonnegative density function $f$. The density $f(x)$ represents the instantaneous probability per unit length at $x$, and the total integral of $f$ over the real line equals $1$. This class of distributions includes the normal (Gaussian), exponential, gamma, beta, and uniform distributions, among others. Absolutely continuous distributions are the continuous counterpart to discrete distributions, and together with singular distributions they exhaust all possibilities via the Lebesgue decomposition.
