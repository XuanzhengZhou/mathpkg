The completion of a probability space adds all subsets of $P$-null sets to the $\sigma$-algebra, extending the measure in a natural way. A set $A$ is included in the completed $\sigma$-algebra $\overline{\mathcal{F}}^P$ if it can be sandwiched between two measurable sets $B_1$ and $B_2$ whose difference has probability zero. The measure of $A$ is then defined as $P(B_1)$. A probability space is called complete if it already contains all such null-set subsets. Completion is a standard technique to avoid technical pathologies in measure-theoretic arguments.
