The generalized version of Theorem 1 extends the one-to-one correspondence from probability measures to all $\sigma$-finite measures that are finite on bounded intervals (Lebesgue-Stieltjes measures). Every such measure $\mu$ corresponds to a generalized distribution function $G$ via $\mu(a, b] = G(b) - G(a)$, where $G$ is determined up to an additive constant. The most important special case is $G(x) = x$, which yields Lebesgue measure -- the unique translation-invariant measure on the real line normalized by $\lambda(0, 1] = 1$.
