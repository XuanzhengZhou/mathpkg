The direct product of an arbitrary family of measurable spaces generalizes the finite product to arbitrary index sets $T$. The points of the product space are functions $\omega = (\omega_t)_{t \in T}$ with each coordinate $\omega_t$ belonging to its respective space $\Omega_t$. The product $\sigma$-algebra $igotimes_{t \in T} \mathcal{F}_t$ is defined as the smallest $\sigma$-algebra making all coordinate projections measurable, which is equivalent to the $\sigma$-algebra generated by finite-dimensional cylinder sets. This construction is foundational for the theory of stochastic processes, where $T$ represents a time index set.
