Three distinct $\sigma$-algebras on $R^T$ are generated by the three types of cylinder sets, with progressively larger generating classes. The $\sigma$-algebra $\mathcal{B}(R^T)$ is generated by cylinder sets whose constraining sets are intervals $(a_k, b_k]$; $\mathcal{B}_1(R^T)$ uses arbitrary Borel sets $B_k$ on the line; and $\mathcal{B}_2(R^T)$ uses a single Borel set $B^n$ in $R^n$. These three $\sigma$-algebras are nested: $\mathcal{B}(R^T) \subseteq \mathcal{B}_1(R^T) \subseteq \mathcal{B}_2(R^T)$.
