An $n$-dimensional distribution function $F_n(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ generalizes the one-dimensional distribution function to $R^n$. The defining properties are the natural multivariate analogues: nonnegativity of mixed differences (ensuring that rectangle probabilities are nonnegative), right-continuity in all variables collectively, normalization to $1$ at $(+\infty, \ldots, +\infty)$, and vanishing when any coordinate tends to $-\infty$. These functions are in one-to-one correspondence with probability measures on $(R^n, \mathcal{B}(R^n))$ via $P(a, b] = \Delta_{a_1 b_1} \cdots \Delta_{a_n b_n} F_n$.
