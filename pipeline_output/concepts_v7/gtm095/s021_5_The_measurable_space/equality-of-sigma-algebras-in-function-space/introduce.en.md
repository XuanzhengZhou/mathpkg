This theorem establishes that the three apparently different $\sigma$-algebras on the function space $R^T$ -- generated respectively by cylinder sets with interval constraints, with Borel-set constraints, and with product-space Borel constraints -- are in fact identical. The proof proceeds by introducing an auxiliary $\sigma$-algebra $\mathcal{E}$ of sets determined by countably many coordinates, showing it contains all cylinder sets of the third type and is itself contained in the $\sigma$-algebra generated by cylinder sets of the first type. This result is fundamental for probability theory on function spaces, as it ensures that the choice among these generating classes does not affect the resulting measurable structure.
