Let $\mathcal{E}$ denote the collection of sets of the form

$$\{x: (x_{t_1}, x_{t_2}, \ldots) \in B\}$$

for various countable aggregates $(t_1, t_2, \ldots) \subseteq T$ and Borel sets $B \in \mathcal{B}(R^\infty)$. Then $\mathcal{E}$ is a $\sigma$-algebra containing all cylinder sets of the form $\mathcal{I}_{t_1, \ldots, t_n}(B^n)$, and

$$\mathcal{B}(R^T) \subseteq \mathcal{B}_1(R^T) \subseteq \mathcal{B}_2(R^T) \subseteq \mathcal{E}.$$

Moreover, every set $A \in \mathcal{E}$ belongs to the $\sigma$-algebra generated by the cylinder sets $\mathcal{I}_{t_1, \ldots, t_n}(I_1 \times \cdots \times I_n)$, which is contained in $\mathcal{B}(R^T)$. Consequently,

$$\mathcal{B}(R^T) = \mathcal{B}_1(R^T) = \mathcal{B}_2(R^T).$$
