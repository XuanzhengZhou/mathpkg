Ionescu Tulcea's theorem generalizes Kolmogorov's extension theorem to arbitrary measurable spaces by constructing a probability measure on an infinite product space from transition probabilities (regular conditional distributions) rather than from a pre-specified consistent family of finite-dimensional distributions. The proof uses Carathéodory's extension theorem applied to an algebra of cylindrical sets, with a crucial argument based on nested sequences of cylindrical sets decreasing to the empty set to establish countable additivity.
