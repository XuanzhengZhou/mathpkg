This corollary provides a simple sufficient condition for almost sure convergence: if $\sum P\{|\xi_k - \xi| \geq \varepsilon\} < \infty$ for every $\varepsilon > 0$, then $\xi_n \to \xi$ a.s. The proof follows from the union bound and the Borel–Cantelli lemma: summability implies the events $\{|\xi_n - \xi| \geq \varepsilon\}$ occur only finitely often with probability 1, which implies convergence. This condition is widely used to establish strong laws of large numbers.
