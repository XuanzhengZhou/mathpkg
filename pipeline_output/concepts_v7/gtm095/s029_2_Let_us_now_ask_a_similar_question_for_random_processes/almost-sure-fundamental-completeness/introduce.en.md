This theorem establishes that the space of random variables is complete under almost sure convergence: every almost surely Cauchy sequence converges almost surely. On the set of full measure where the sequence is fundamental, the ordinary Cauchy criterion guarantees pointwise convergence, and the limit is measurable. This is the probabilistic analogue of the completeness of real numbers and is fundamental for existence proofs via approximation.
