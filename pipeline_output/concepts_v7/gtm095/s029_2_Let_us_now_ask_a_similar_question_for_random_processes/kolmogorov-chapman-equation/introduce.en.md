The Kolmogorov–Chapman equation is the fundamental consistency condition for transition probabilities of a Markov process. It expresses that transitioning from state $x$ at time $s$ to a set $B$ at time $\tau$ can be decomposed by considering the intermediate state at any time $t$ between $s$ and $\tau$, integrating over all possible intermediate states with respect to the transition probability $P(s, x; t, dy)$. This is the continuous analogue of the Chapman–Kolmogorov equation for discrete-time Markov chains, and it generalizes the semigroup property of transition operators.
