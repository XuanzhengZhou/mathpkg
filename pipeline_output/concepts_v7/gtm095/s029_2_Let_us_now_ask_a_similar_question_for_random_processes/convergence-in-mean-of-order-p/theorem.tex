A sequence $\xi_1, \xi_2, \ldots$ of random variables \textbf{converges in the mean of order $p$}, $0 < p < \infty$, to a random variable $\xi$ if

$$E|\xi_n - \xi|^p \to 0, \quad n \to \infty.$$

In analysis this is known as convergence in $L^p$, denoted $\xi_n \xrightarrow{L^p} \xi$. In the special case $p = 2$, it is called \textbf{mean square convergence} and denoted $\xi = \operatorname{l.i.m.} \xi_n$ (``limit in the mean'').
