Convergence in the mean of order $p$ ($L^p$ convergence) requires that the expected $p$-th power of the absolute difference between $\xi_n$ and $\xi$ converges to zero. For $p \geq 1$, $L^p$ convergence implies convergence in probability by Markov's inequality. The case $p = 2$ (mean square convergence) plays a fundamental role in the theory of orthogonal projections. The space $L^p$ of random variables with finite $p$-th moment, equipped with the norm $\|\xi\|_p = (E|\xi|^p)^{1/p}$, is a Banach space for $p \geq 1$.
