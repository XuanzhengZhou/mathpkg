On the canonical probability space $(\mathbb{R}^T, \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R}^T), P)$, the random process $X = (\xi_t)_{t \in T}$ is defined by the \textbf{coordinate method}:

$$\xi_t(\omega) = \omega_t, \quad t \in T,$$

where $\omega = (\omega_t)_{t \in T} \in \mathbb{R}^T$ and $\omega_t$ denotes the $t$-th coordinate of $\omega$.
