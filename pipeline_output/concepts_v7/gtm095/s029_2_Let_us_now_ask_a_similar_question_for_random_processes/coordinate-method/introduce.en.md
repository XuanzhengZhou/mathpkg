The coordinate method is the simplest way to define a random process on a canonical probability space: each random variable $\xi_t$ is simply the coordinate projection $\omega \mapsto \omega_t$. This construction ensures that the finite-dimensional distributions of the process $(\xi_t)_{t \in T}$ coincide with the prescribed family $\{F_{t_1,\ldots,t_n}\}$, since the measure $P$ was constructed precisely to satisfy this property. The coordinate method is universal because any random process can be represented in this form on an appropriate canonical space.
