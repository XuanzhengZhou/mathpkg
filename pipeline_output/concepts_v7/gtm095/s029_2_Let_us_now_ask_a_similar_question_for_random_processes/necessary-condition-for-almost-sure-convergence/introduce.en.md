This theorem provides a necessary and sufficient condition for almost sure convergence in terms of the tail behavior of supremum deviations. The condition $P\{\sup_{k \geq n} |\xi_k - \xi| \geq \varepsilon\} \to 0$ is stronger than convergence in probability, since it controls the maximum deviation over all indices $k \geq n$. The proof uses the characterization $\{\omega : \xi_n \nrightarrow \xi\} = \bigcup_{\varepsilon > 0} \limsup A_n^\varepsilon$ where $A_n^\varepsilon = \{|\xi_n - \xi| \geq \varepsilon\}$, together with the continuity of probability measures. The equivalence of the two fundamental-sequence formulations follows from $\sup_{k \geq 0} |\xi_{n+k} - \xi_n| \leq \sup_{k,l \geq 0} |\xi_{n+k} - \xi_{n+l}| \leq 2 \sup_{k \geq 0} |\xi_{n+k} - \xi_n|$.
