Kolmogorov's existence theorem (also known as Kolmogorov's extension theorem) is the foundational result that guarantees the existence of a random process given a consistent family of finite-dimensional distributions. The theorem constructs a probability measure on the product space $(R^T, \mathcal{B}(R^T))$ using the canonical (coordinate) method, where the random variables are simply the coordinate projections $\xi_t(\omega) = \omega_t$. The key condition is the consistency of the finite-dimensional distributions, which ensures that the marginal distributions obtained by integrating out any coordinate agree with the lower-dimensional distributions in the family. This theorem is the probabilistic analogue of Kolmogorov's theorem for constructing measures on $R^n$ from 1-dimensional distribution functions.
