Let $\{F_{t_1, \ldots, t_n}(x_1, \ldots, x_n)\}$ be a family of finite-dimensional distribution functions defined for all finite sets $t_1, \ldots, t_n$ with $t_1 < t_2 < \cdots < t_n$, $t_i \in T \subseteq \mathbb{R}$, satisfying the consistency condition:

$$F_{t_1, \ldots, t_{k-1}, t_{k+1}, \ldots, t_n}(x_1, \ldots, x_{k-1}, x_{k+1}, \ldots, x_n) = \lim_{x_k \to \infty} F_{t_1, \ldots, t_n}(x_1, \ldots, x_n).$$

Then there exist a probability space $(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, P)$ and a random process $X = (\xi_t)_{t \in T}$ defined on it such that

$$P\{\omega : \xi_{t_1} \leq x_1, \ldots, \xi_{t_n} \leq x_n\} = F_{t_1, \ldots, t_n}(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$$

for all $t_1 < \cdots < t_n$ and all $x_1, \ldots, x_n \in \mathbb{R}$.
