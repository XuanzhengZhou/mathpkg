The canonical probability space $(\mathbb{R}^T, \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R}^T), P)$ is the standard construction obtained from Kolmogorov's existence theorem, where the sample space is the product space $\mathbb{R}^T$ of all functions from the index set $T$ to $\mathbb{R}$, equipped with the product $\sigma$-algebra. The measure $P$ is uniquely determined by the given consistent family of finite-dimensional distributions. This construction provides a universal underlying space on which any random process with the specified finite-dimensional distributions can be realized.
