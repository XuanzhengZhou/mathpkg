This is the Riesz-Fischer theorem in probabilistic language: $L^p$ spaces are complete. The proof extracts a subsequence $(\xi_{n_k})$ such that $\|\xi_{n_{k+1}} - \xi_{n_k}\|_p < 2^{-2k}$, then uses the Borel-Cantelli lemma and Markov's inequality to show almost sure convergence of the subsequence. The full sequence then converges to the same limit in $L^p$. This completeness makes $L^p$ a Banach space, essential for functional analytic methods in probability.
