The Cauchy criterion for convergence in probability states that the space of random variables (modulo almost sure equivalence) is complete with respect to convergence in probability. Necessity follows from $P\{|\xi_n - \xi_m| \geq \varepsilon\} \leq P\{|\xi_n - \xi| \geq \varepsilon/2\} + P\{|\xi_m - \xi| \geq \varepsilon/2\}$. Sufficiency uses Theorem 5 to extract an almost surely convergent subsequence, whose limit then serves as the limit in probability for the full sequence.
