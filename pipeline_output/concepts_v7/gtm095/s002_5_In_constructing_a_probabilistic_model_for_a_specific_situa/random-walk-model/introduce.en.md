The simple random walk is one of the most studied stochastic processes, modeling a particle that moves left or right at each step with fixed probabilities. When symmetric ($p = q = 1/2$), the walk is recurrent in one and two dimensions, and the binomial distribution describes the position after $n$ steps. The random walk serves as a discrete analogue of Brownian motion and is foundational for understanding diffusion, first-passage problems, and the arcsine law.
