The union bound, also known as Boole's inequality, is the simplest and most widely used bound in probability theory. It states that the probability of a union is at most the sum of the individual probabilities. While often loose, it is sufficient for many applications including the Borel-Cantelli lemma, multiple testing corrections, and showing that an event has small probability by controlling a sum of negligible terms.
