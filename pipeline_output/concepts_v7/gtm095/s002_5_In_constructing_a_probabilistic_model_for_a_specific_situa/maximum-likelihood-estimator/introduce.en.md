The maximum likelihood estimator (MLE) chooses the parameter value that makes the observed data most probable. Introduced by Laplace in the capture-recapture problem, it has become the most widely used estimation method in statistics due to its consistency, asymptotic normality, and efficiency. The MLE is invariant under reparameterization and forms the foundation of modern statistical inference.
