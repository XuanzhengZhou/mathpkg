Gumbel inequalities bound the probability of a union using sums of probabilities of unions of subsets, rather than sums of intersection probabilities as in Bonferroni. They offer alternative upper bounds that can be sharper in certain regimes. Together with Bonferroni and Frechet inequalities, they form a family of truncation-based bounds for the inclusion-exclusion formula.
