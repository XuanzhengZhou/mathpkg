The binomial distribution gives the probability of obtaining exactly $k$ successes in $n$ independent Bernoulli trials, each with success probability $p$. It is one of the most important discrete distributions, arising naturally in coin tossing, quality control, and any setting involving repeated independent yes/no experiments. Its name comes from the binomial theorem: the probabilities sum to $(p+q)^n = 1$.
