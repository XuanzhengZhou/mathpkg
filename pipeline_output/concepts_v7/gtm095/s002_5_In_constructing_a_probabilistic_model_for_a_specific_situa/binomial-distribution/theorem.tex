In the Bernoulli scheme with $n$ trials and success probability $p$, let $A_k$ be the event that exactly $k$ successes occur, $k = 0, 1, \ldots, n$. Then
$$\text{P}(A_k) = C_n^k p^k q^{n-k}, \quad q = 1-p.$$
The collection $\{\text{P}(A_0), \text{P}(A_1), \ldots, \text{P}(A_n)\}$ is called the \text{binomial distribution} (with parameters $n$ and $p$).
