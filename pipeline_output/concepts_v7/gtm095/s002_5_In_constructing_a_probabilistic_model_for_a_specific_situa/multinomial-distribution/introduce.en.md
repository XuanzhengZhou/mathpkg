The multinomial distribution generalizes the binomial distribution from two outcomes to $r$ categories. It describes the joint distribution of counts across $r$ types in $n$ independent trials, each trial producing outcome $b_i$ with probability $p_i$. The multinomial coefficient $n!/(n_1! \cdots n_r!)$ counts the number of ordered sequences with those counts, and the distribution is the natural model for categorical data and contingency tables.
