Let $\Omega = \{\omega : \omega = (a_1, \ldots, a_n), \; a_i \in \{b_1, \ldots, b_r\}\}$. Let $\nu_i(\omega)$ be the number of elements of $\omega$ equal to $b_i$, $i = 1, \ldots, r$. Define
$$p(\omega) = p_1^{\nu_1(\omega)} \cdots p_r^{\nu_r(\omega)},$$
where $p_i \geq 0$ and $p_1 + \cdots + p_r = 1$. The \text{multinomial distribution} is given by
$$\text{P}(A_{n_1, \ldots, n_r}) = C_n(n_1, \ldots, n_r) p_1^{n_1} \cdots p_r^{n_r},$$
where $n_i \geq 0$, $n_1 + \cdots + n_r = n$, and
$$C_n(n_1, \ldots, n_r) = \frac{n!}{n_1! \, n_2! \, \cdots \, n_r!}.$$
