A discrete probability space is the fundamental mathematical structure for modeling random experiments with finitely many outcomes. It consists of three ingredients: a finite set $\Omega$ of elementary outcomes, a collection $\mathcal{A}$ of observable events (an algebra of subsets), and a probability measure $\text{P}$ assigning a number between 0 and 1 to each event. In the discrete setting, the probability measure is completely determined by its values on the individual sample points, which sum to 1.
