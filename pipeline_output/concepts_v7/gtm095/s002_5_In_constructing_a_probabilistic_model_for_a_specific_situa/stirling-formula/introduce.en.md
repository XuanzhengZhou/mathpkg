Stirling's formula provides an asymptotic approximation to the factorial function, which is essential in probability theory for approximating binomial coefficients and analyzing the behavior of discrete distributions for large $n$. The leading term $\sqrt{2\pi n} (n/e)^n$ already gives remarkable accuracy, while the exponential factor with $\theta_n$ provides sharp bounds for finite $n$. It is indispensable in deriving the normal approximation to the binomial distribution and in the proof of the de Moivre-Laplace theorem.
