Marcinkiewicz's Theorem classifies which functions of the form $\exp(P(t))$ with polynomial $P$ can be characteristic functions. The theorem states that the polynomial degree must be at most 2, which means the Gaussian distribution (degree 2) and degenerate distributions (degree 1 or 0) are essentially the only examples. A notable consequence is that $e^{-t^4}$ is not a characteristic function. This also implies that the Gaussian distribution is the only distribution whose semi-invariants $s_n$ vanish from some index onward.
