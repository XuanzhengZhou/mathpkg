The characteristic function of a random vector $\xi = (\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_k)^*$ is defined as $\varphi_\xi(t) = E e^{i(t,\xi)}$ for $t \in \mathbb{R}^k$. It generalizes the one-dimensional characteristic function and shares analogous properties: it uniquely determines the joint distribution, is uniformly continuous, and satisfies $\varphi_\xi(0) = 1$ and $|\varphi_\xi(t)| \leq 1$. Its partial derivatives at the origin yield mixed moments when they exist.
