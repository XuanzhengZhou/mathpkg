Theorem 7 provides a sufficient condition (Carleman-type) for the uniqueness of the solution to the Hamburger moment problem. If the absolute moments $\mu_n$ grow at most like $(Cn)^n$ (i.e., $\limsup \mu_n^{1/n}/n < \infty$), then the moments uniquely determine the distribution function. The proof uses the Taylor expansion of the characteristic function to show that the moments determine $\varphi(t)$ in a neighborhood of zero, and then extends uniquely to all $t$ by analytic continuation.
