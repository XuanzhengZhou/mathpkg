A Gaussian (or normal) random variable is defined by its characteristic function $\varphi_\xi(t) = \exp(itm - \sigma^2 t^2/2)$. The unified definition via characteristic functions covers both the nondegenerate case ($\sigma^2 > 0$) with the familiar bell-shaped density, and the degenerate case ($\sigma^2 = 0$) where the distribution is concentrated at a single point $m$. The parameter $m$ is the mean and $\sigma^2$ is the variance.
