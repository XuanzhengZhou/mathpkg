Let $\{m_n\}_{n \geq 1}$ be the moments of a probability distribution.

(a) If

$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{1}{(m_{2n})^{1/2n}} = \infty,$$

then the moments determine the probability distribution uniquely.

(b) If $\{m_n\}_{n \geq 1}$ are the moments of a distribution that is concentrated on $[0, \infty)$, then the solution is unique if

$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{1}{(m_n)^{1/2n}} = \infty.$$
