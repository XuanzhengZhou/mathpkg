Carleman's test provides a sufficient condition for the uniqueness of the solution to the moment problem. If the even moments $m_{2n}$ do not grow too fast — specifically, if $\sum m_{2n}^{-1/2n}$ diverges — then the distribution is uniquely determined by its moments. For distributions supported on $[0,\infty)$, the condition weakens to requiring divergence of $\sum m_n^{-1/2n}$. This test is sharper than Theorem 7 in many cases.
