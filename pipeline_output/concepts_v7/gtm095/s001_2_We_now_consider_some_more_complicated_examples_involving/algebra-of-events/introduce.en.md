An algebra of events is the fundamental structure for describing which subsets of a sample space can be assigned probabilities. It formalizes the idea that events should be closed under the basic logical operations: "or" (union), "and" (intersection), and "not" (complement). In the finite setting, every algebra arises from a decomposition (partition) of the sample space, providing a concrete combinatorial handle on the structure of possible events.
