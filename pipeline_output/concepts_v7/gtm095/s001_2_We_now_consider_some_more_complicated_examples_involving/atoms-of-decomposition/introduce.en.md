The atoms of a decomposition are the indivisible building blocks of the event structure on a finite sample space. Every algebra of events is generated by some decomposition, and the atoms of that decomposition are the minimal nonempty events—they cannot be further refined while staying within the algebra. This connects the combinatorial concept of partitions with the measure-theoretic foundations of probability.
