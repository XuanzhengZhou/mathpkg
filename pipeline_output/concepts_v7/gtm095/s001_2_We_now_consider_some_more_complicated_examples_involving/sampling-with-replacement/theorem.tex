In \text{sampling with replacement}, at each step one ball is drawn at random from an urn containing $M$ distinguishable balls (numbered $1, \ldots, M$) and then returned again. Each sample of $n$ balls can be represented as $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ where $a_i \in \{1, \ldots, M\}$ is the label of the ball drawn at the $i$th step. Two cases are distinguished:
\begin{itemize}
\item \text{Ordered samples}: $N(\Omega) = M^n$,
\item \text{Unordered samples}: $N(M,n) = C_{M+n-1}^{n}$.
\end{itemize}
This model corresponds to Bose--Einstein statistics when particles are indistinguishable and the Pauli exclusion principle is not applied.
