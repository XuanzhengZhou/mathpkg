Sampling with replacement is the fundamental urn model in which each drawn ball is returned before the next draw, making successive draws independent. It is the combinatorial setting for the binomial and multinomial distributions, and it corresponds to the allocation of distinguishable objects among cells without exclusion. In statistical physics, this model describes Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics (for distinguishable particles) and Bose-Einstein statistics (for indistinguishable particles without the exclusion principle).
