Ordered samples with replacement are the simplest sampling scheme in probability theory: each draw is independent and the order of draws matters. This model describes $n$ independent trials each with $M$ equally likely outcomes, making the sample space a Cartesian product of $n$ copies of $\{1, \dots, M\}$ containing $M^n$ equally likely outcomes. It underlies the multinomial distribution and the simplest random walk models.
