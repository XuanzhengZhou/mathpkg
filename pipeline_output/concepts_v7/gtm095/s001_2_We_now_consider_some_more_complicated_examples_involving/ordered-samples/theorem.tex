Consider selecting $n$ balls from an urn containing $M$ distinguishable balls, where at each step one ball is drawn at random and returned. An \text{ordered sample with replacement} is a tuple $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ where $a_i \in \{1, \ldots, M\}$ is the label of the ball drawn at the $i$th step. Samples with the same elements but in different order are considered distinct. The sample space is
$$\Omega = \{\omega : \omega = (a_1, \ldots, a_n), \; a_i = 1, \ldots, M\}$$
and the number of outcomes (called \text{arrangements of $n$ out of $M$ elements with repetitions}) is
$$N(\Omega) = M^n.$$
