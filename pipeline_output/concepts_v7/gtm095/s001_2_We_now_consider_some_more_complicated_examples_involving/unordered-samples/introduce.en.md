Unordered samples with replacement count the number of multisets of size $n$ drawn from $M$ types, where repetitions are allowed but order does not matter. The formula $\binom{M+n-1}{n}$ is a stars-and-bars result: it counts the number of ways to place $n$ indistinguishable balls into $M$ distinguishable boxes. This combinatorial count appears in Bose–Einstein statistics and in the distribution of indistinguishable particles among energy levels without the exclusion principle.
