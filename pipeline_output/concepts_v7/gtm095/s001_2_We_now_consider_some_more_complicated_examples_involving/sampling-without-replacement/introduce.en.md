Sampling without replacement models experiments where each selected item is removed from the pool, so draws are no longer independent. The ordered case uses the falling factorial $(M)_n$ and describes permutations of length $n$ from $M$ elements; the unordered case gives the classical binomial coefficient $\binom{M}{n}$. In statistical physics, indistinguishable particles with the Pauli exclusion principle correspond to Fermi-Dirac statistics, which is mathematically equivalent to unordered sampling without replacement.
