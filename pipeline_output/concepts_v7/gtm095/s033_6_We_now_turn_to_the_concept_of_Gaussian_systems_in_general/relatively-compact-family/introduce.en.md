Definition 1. A family of probability measures $\mathcal{P} = \{P_\alpha; \alpha \in \mathfrak{A}\}$ is relatively compact if every sequence of measures from $\mathcal{P}$ contains a subsequence which converges weakly to a probability measure. This definition introduces a fundamental concept in probability theory.
