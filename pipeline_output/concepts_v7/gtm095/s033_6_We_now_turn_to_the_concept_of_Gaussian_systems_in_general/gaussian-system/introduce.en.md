A Gaussian system generalizes the notion of a Gaussian random vector to collections indexed by an arbitrary set $\mathfrak{A}$. In a Gaussian system, every finite subset of the random variables jointly follows a (possibly degenerate) multivariate normal distribution. This concept is fundamental for the construction and analysis of Gaussian processes such as Brownian motion, where the index set is typically $[0,\infty)$ or $[0,1]$. The closed linear manifold generated by a Gaussian system consists entirely of Gaussian variables, making the class closed under linear operations and limits in probability.
