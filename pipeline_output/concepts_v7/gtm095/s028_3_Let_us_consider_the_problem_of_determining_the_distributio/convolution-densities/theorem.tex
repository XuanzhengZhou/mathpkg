If independent random variables $\xi$ and $\eta$ have densities $f_\xi$ and $f_\eta$, then the density of their sum $\zeta = \xi + \eta$ is given by the convolution of the densities:

$$f_\zeta(z) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_\xi(z - y) \, f_\eta(y) \, dy = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_\eta(z - x) \, f_\xi(x) \, dx.$$
