When independent random variables possess densities, the density of their sum is the convolution of the individual densities. This formula, $f_{\xi+\eta}(z) = \int f_\xi(z-y) f_\eta(y) \, dy$, is one of the most frequently used results in probability theory. It provides a direct way to compute the distribution of sums, as illustrated by the Irwin–Hall distribution (sum of uniform random variables) and the fact that the sum of independent normal random variables is again normal.
