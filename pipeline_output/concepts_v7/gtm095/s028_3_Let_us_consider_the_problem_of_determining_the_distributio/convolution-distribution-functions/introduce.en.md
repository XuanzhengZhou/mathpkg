The distribution function of the sum of two independent random variables is given by the convolution of their individual distribution functions. This operation, defined by $F * G(z) = \int F(z-x)\,dG(x)$, is fundamental to probability theory. It generalizes the idea that the probability of a sum being at most $z$ can be obtained by integrating over all ways the first variable can contribute $x$ while the second contributes at most $z-x$. The convolution is commutative and associative, reflecting the corresponding properties of addition.
