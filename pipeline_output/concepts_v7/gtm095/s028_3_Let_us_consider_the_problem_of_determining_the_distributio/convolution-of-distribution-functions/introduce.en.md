Convolution is the fundamental operation for finding the distribution of a sum of independent random variables. It generalizes the discrete convolution encountered with generating functions. The integral representation $F_\zeta(z) = \int F_\xi(z-y) dF_\eta(y)$ follows from Fubini's theorem applied to the joint distribution. Convolution is commutative and associative, making it a natural addition operation on the space of probability distributions. The convolution of two normal distributions is again normal, a closure property shared by few other families.
