Let $\varphi = \varphi(x)$ be defined on $\bigcup_{k=1}^{n} [a_k, b_k]$, continuously differentiable and either strictly increasing or strictly decreasing on each open interval $I_k = (a_k, b_k)$, with $\varphi'(x) \neq 0$ for $x \in I_k$. Let $h_k = h_k(y)$ be the inverse of $\varphi(x)$ restricted to $I_k$. Let $\eta = \varphi(\xi)$. Then

$$f_\eta(y) = \sum_{k=1}^{n} f_\xi(h_k(y)) \, |h_k'(y)| \cdot I_{D_k}(y),$$

where $D_k$ is the domain of $h_k(y)$ and $I_{D_k}$ is its indicator function.
