When a random variable $\xi$ is transformed by a strictly monotone, continuously differentiable function $\varphi$, the density of the transformed variable $\eta = \varphi(\xi)$ can be obtained by the change-of-variables formula $f_\eta(y) = f_\xi(h(y))|h'(y)|$, where $h = \varphi^{-1}$. The absolute value of the derivative $|h'(y)|$ accounts for the stretching or compression of probability mass under the transformation. This formula generalizes the simple linear case $\eta = a\xi + b$, where $f_\eta(y) = f_\xi((y-b)/a)/|a|$.
