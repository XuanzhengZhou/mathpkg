Let $\xi$ be a random variable with density $f_\xi(x)$ supported on an open interval $I = (a, b)$. Let $\varphi = \varphi(x)$ be continuously differentiable on $(a, b)$ and either strictly increasing or strictly decreasing with $\varphi'(x) \neq 0$. Let $\eta = \varphi(\xi)$ and $h = h(y) = \varphi^{-1}(y)$ be the inverse function. Then the density of $\eta$ is

$$f_\eta(y) = f_\xi(h(y)) \, |h'(y)|.$$
