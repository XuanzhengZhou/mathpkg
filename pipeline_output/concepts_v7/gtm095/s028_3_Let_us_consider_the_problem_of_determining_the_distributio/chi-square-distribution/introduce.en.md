The chi-square distribution with $n$ degrees of freedom is the distribution of the sum of squares of $n$ independent standard normal random variables. It is a special case of the gamma distribution and is one of the most important distributions in mathematical statistics, appearing in hypothesis testing (goodness-of-fit tests, tests of independence), confidence interval construction, and analysis of variance. Its density involves the gamma function $\Gamma(n/2)$ and exhibits different shapes depending on the degrees of freedom.
