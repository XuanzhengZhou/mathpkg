The Rayleigh distribution is a special case of the chi distribution with two degrees of freedom. It describes the magnitude (Euclidean norm) of a two-dimensional vector whose components are independent standard normal random variables. The Rayleigh distribution arises in physics (wave propagation, scattering), engineering (signal processing, wireless communications for fading channel modeling), and other fields where one studies the magnitude of a complex quantity with independent normally distributed real and imaginary parts.
