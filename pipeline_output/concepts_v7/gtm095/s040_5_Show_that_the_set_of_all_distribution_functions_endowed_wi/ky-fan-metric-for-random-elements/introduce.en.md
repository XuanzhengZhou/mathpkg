The Ky Fan metric $d_{\mathbb{P}}$ provides a way to metrize convergence in probability for random elements taking values in a metric space. It measures the distance between two random elements $X$ and $Y$ by the infimum of those $\varepsilon > 0$ for which the probability that the pointwise distance $\rho(X, Y)$ exceeds $\varepsilon$ is itself at most $\varepsilon$. Convergence in probability of $X_n$ to $X$ is equivalent to $d_{\mathbb{P}}(X_n, X) \to 0$.
