Let $X_n$, $n \geq 1$, and $X$ be random elements taking values in a metric space $(E, \mathcal{E}, \rho)$. If $X_n \xrightarrow{\mathcal{D}} X$, then there exists a probability space $(\Omega^*, \mathcal{F}^*, \mathbb{P}^*)$ and random elements $X^*$, $X^*_n$, $n \geq 1$, defined on it such that

$$X^*_n \xrightarrow{\text{a.s.}} X^*$$

and the probability distributions satisfy

$$P^* = P, \quad P^*_n = P_n, \quad n \geq 1,$$

where $P^*$ and $P^*_n$ are the distributions of $X^*$ and $X^*_n$, and $P$ and $P_n$ are the distributions of $X$ and $X_n$.
