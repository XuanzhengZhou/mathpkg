A sequence of random elements $X_n$, $n \geq 1$, taking values in a metric space $(E, \mathcal{E}, \rho)$ is said to \text{converge in distribution}, or \text{in law} (notation: $X_n \xrightarrow{\mathcal{D}} X$, or $X_n \xrightarrow{\mathcal{L}} X$), if their probability distributions $P_n$ converge weakly to $P$:

$$P_n \xrightarrow{w} P,$$

where $P(A) = \mathbb{P}\{\omega : X(\omega) \in A\}$ and $P_n(A) = \mathbb{P}\{\omega : X_n(\omega) \in A\}$ for $A \in \mathcal{E}$.
