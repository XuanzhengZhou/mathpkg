A random variable $T$ (or its distribution function $F$) is called \textit{infinitely divisible} if for each $n \geq 1$ there exist independent identically distributed random variables $\eta_1, \ldots, \eta_n$ such that
$$T \stackrel{d}{=} \eta_1 + \cdots + \eta_n,$$
or equivalently, in terms of distribution functions,
$$F = \underbrace{F_n * \cdots * F_n}_{n\text{ times}},$$
where $F_n$ is the distribution function of $\eta_1$ and $*$ denotes the convolution operation.
