This theorem gives a complete characterization of the class of limit distributions for sums of independent identically distributed random variables arranged in a triangular array. If $T$ can be obtained as the limit in distribution of such row sums $T_n = \sum_{k=1}^n \xi_{n,k}$, then $T$ must be infinitely divisible. Conversely, every infinitely divisible distribution can be realized as such a limit (indeed, as the exact distribution of $T_n$ for every $n$, since $T$ itself decomposes into $n$ i.i.d. summands). The result explains why both the normal distribution (from the classical CLT) and the Poisson distribution (from Poisson's theorem) arise as limits in the triangular array scheme, and it sets the stage for the Levy-Khintchine representation that parametrizes all infinitely divisible laws via their characteristic functions.
