The binary expansion of real numbers provides a bijection between infinite sequences of coin toss outcomes and the interval $[0, 1)$. This correspondence reveals that the sample space for infinitely many independent coin tosses has the cardinality of the continuum, making it fundamentally different from the finite sample spaces studied in Chapter 1. In this uncountable setting, individual outcomes must have probability zero, and probabilities can only be meaningfully assigned to subsets (events) via measure theory, motivating Kolmogorov's axiomatic framework.
