For an experiment consisting of infinitely many independent tosses of a coin with success probability $p$, the natural sample space is
$$\Omega = \{\omega : \omega = (a_1, a_2, \ldots), a_i = 0, 1\},$$
the space of all infinite binary sequences. Via the binary expansion $a = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} a_k 2^{-k}$, $\Omega$ is in one-to-one correspondence with the interval $[0, 1)$ and therefore has the cardinality of the continuum. Since $\Omega$ is uncountable, the elementary formula $P(A) = \sum_{\omega \in A} p(\omega)$ no longer suffices, and a measure-theoretic framework is required.
