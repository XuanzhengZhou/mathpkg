The problem of modeling infinitely many independent coin tosses illustrates why the elementary (finite) probability framework is inadequate for even simple limiting processes. The sample space has the cardinality of the continuum, forcing every individual outcome to have probability zero. A meaningful theory must assign probabilities to interesting subsets (events) like "the limiting frequency of heads is 1/2." This challenge motivated Kolmogorov's measure-theoretic axioms, where probability is defined on a $\sigma$-algebra rather than on individual outcomes.
