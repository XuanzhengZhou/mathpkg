A \text{probabilistic model} in the sense of Kolmogorov consists of a triple $(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, P)$ where:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\Omega$ is the sample space (the set of all possible outcomes).
\item $\mathcal{F}$ is a $\sigma$-algebra of subsets of $\Omega$ (the collection of events).
\item $P$ is a probability measure on $(\Omega, \mathcal{F})$, i.e., a function $P: \mathcal{F} \to [0,1]$ such that $P(\Omega) = 1$ and $P$ is countably additive.
\end{enumerate}
All further exposition must be based exclusively on these axioms, independent of the usual concrete meaning of the elements and their relations.
