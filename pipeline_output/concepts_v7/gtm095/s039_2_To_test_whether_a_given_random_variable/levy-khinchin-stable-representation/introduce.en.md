The Lévy–Khinchin representation for stable distributions gives the complete parametric form of all stable characteristic functions. A stable law is parameterized by four quantities: the index of stability $\alpha \in (0,2)$, a skewness parameter $\theta \in [-1,1]$, a scale parameter $d \geq 0$, and a location parameter $\beta \in \mathbb{R}$. The function $G(t,\alpha)$ distinguishes the cases $\alpha \neq 1$ and $\alpha = 1$, the latter corresponding to the Cauchy-type logarithmic behaviour. The symmetric case reduces to the simple form $\varphi(t) = e^{-d|t|^\alpha}$.
