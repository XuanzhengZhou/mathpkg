A stable random variable is one whose distribution is closed under convolution up to affine transformations. This means the sum of $n$ independent copies of $T$ has the same distribution as $a_n T + b_n$, after appropriate scaling and centering. The three equivalent formulations—in terms of equality in distribution, $n$-fold convolution of distribution functions, and factorisation of the characteristic function—capture the same fundamental stability property.
