This lemma strengthens the portmanteau theorem by showing it is enough to check weak convergence against the smaller class $BL$ of bounded Lipschitz functions, rather than all bounded continuous functions. The proof uses the fact that the approximating functions $f_A^\varepsilon(x) = (1 - \varepsilon^{-1}\rho(x,A))^+$ belong to $BL$ and can approximate indicators of closed sets, thereby linking convergence of integrals of $BL$ functions back to the original definition of weak convergence.
