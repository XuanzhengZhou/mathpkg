This theorem establishes that the topology of weak convergence on the space of probability measures is metrizable via the Lévy–Prokhorov distance. The forward direction ($L \to 0$ implies $P_n \xrightarrow{w} P$) follows directly from the definition of $L$ and the portmanteau characterization of weak convergence. The converse relies on approximating indicator functions by Lipschitz functions of the form $f_A^\varepsilon(x)$ to relate weak convergence back to the metric.
