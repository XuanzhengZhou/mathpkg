Let $\xi_1, \xi_2, \ldots$ be a sequence of independent identically distributed random variables and $S_n = \xi_1 + \cdots + \xi_n$. Suppose there exist constants $b_n$ and $a_n > 0$, and a random variable $T$, such that

$$\frac{S_n - b_n}{a_n} \xrightarrow{d} T.$$

A necessary and sufficient condition for the random variable $T$ to be obtainable as such a limit in distribution is that $T$ is stable.
