This theorem answers the fundamental question: what distributions can appear as limits of suitably centered and normalized sums of i.i.d. random variables? The answer is precisely the class of stable distributions. The forward direction uses the definition of stability to directly construct the limit. The converse direction is more subtle and relies on a convergence-of-types lemma to show that the limit must satisfy the stability equation.
