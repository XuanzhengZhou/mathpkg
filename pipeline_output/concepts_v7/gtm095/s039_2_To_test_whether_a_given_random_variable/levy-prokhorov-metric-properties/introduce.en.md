The Lévy–Prokhorov distance $L(P, \tilde{P})$ is defined via the quantity $\sigma(P, \tilde{P})$, which measures how much probability mass must be shifted to transform one measure into the other, up to an $\varepsilon$-neighborhood tolerance. This lemma establishes that $L$ is indeed a metric: it is symmetric (with the stronger property that $\sigma$ itself is symmetric), satisfies the triangle inequality, and separates points. The key insight is that a probability measure on a metric space is completely determined by its values on closed sets.
