This corollary provides an upper bound for the Levy-Prokhorov distance between two probability distributions in terms of the distance in probability of random elements realizing those distributions on a common probability space. If $X$ and $\tilde{X}$ are defined on the same space $(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \mathbb{P})$, then $L(P_X, P_{\tilde{X}}) \leq d_{\mathbb{P}}(X, \tilde{X})$. The quality of this bound depends on the coupling construction; the trivial product coupling typically does not yield sharp estimates.
