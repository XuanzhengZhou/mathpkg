Let $(E, \mathcal{E}, \rho)$ and $(E', \mathcal{E}', \rho')$ be separable metric spaces, and let $X_n \xrightarrow{\mathcal{D}} X$, where $X_n, X$ are random elements with values in $E$. Let $h = h(x)$, $x \in E$, be a measurable mapping of $(E, \mathcal{E}, \rho)$ into $(E', \mathcal{E}', \rho')$. Denote by $\Delta_h = \{x \in E : h(x) \text{ is not } \rho\text{-continuous at } x\}$ the set of discontinuity points of $h$, and suppose that

$$\mathbb{P}\{\omega : X(\omega) \in \Delta_h\} = 0.$$

Then

$$h(X_n) \xrightarrow{\mathcal{D}} h(X).$$

Equivalently, let $P_n, P$ be probability distributions on $(E, \mathcal{E}, \rho)$ with $P_n \xrightarrow{w} P$, and let $P\{x : x \in \Delta_h\} = 0$. Then $P_n^h \xrightarrow{w} P^h$, where $P_n^h(A) = P_n\{h(x) \in A\}$ and $P^h(A) = P\{h(x) \in A\}$ for $A \in \mathcal{E}'$.
