This lemma provides an equivalent characterization of the distance in variation (total variation distance) between two probability measures. It states that $\|P - \tilde{P}\|$ equals twice the supremum of $|P(A) - \tilde{P}(A)|$ over all measurable sets $A$. The proof uses the symmetry $P(A) - \tilde{P}(A) = \tilde{P}(\overline{A}) - P(\overline{A})$ for the upper bound, and the Hahn decomposition of the signed measure $\mu = P - \tilde{P}$ for the converse inequality. The formula highlights that two distributions close in variation are practically indistinguishable in any specific situation.
