Let $P$ and $\tilde{P}$ be probability measures on a measurable space $(\Omega, \mathcal{F})$. The distance in variation (total variation distance) satisfies

$$\|P - \tilde{P}\| = 2 \sup_{A \in \mathcal{F}} |P(A) - \tilde{P}(A)|.$$

Equivalently,

$$\|P - \tilde{P}\| = \sup_{|\varphi| \leq 1} |\mathbb{E}_P \varphi - \mathbb{E}_{\tilde{P}} \varphi|,$$

where the supremum is taken over all $\mathcal{F}$-measurable functions $\varphi$ with $|\varphi(\omega)| \leq 1$ for all $\omega \in \Omega$.
