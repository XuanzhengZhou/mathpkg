Let $T$ be an arbitrary set of indices and $R_t$ a real line corresponding to each $t \in T$. Let $\{P_\tau\}$ be a family of probability measures on $(R^\tau, \mathcal{B}(R^\tau))$ for all finite unordered subsets $\tau = [t_1, \ldots, t_n] \subseteq T$ with $t_1 < t_2 < \cdots < t_n$, satisfying the consistency condition:

$$P_{(t_1, \ldots, t_n)}(A_{t_1} \times \cdots \times A_{t_n}) = P_{(t_1, \ldots, t_n)}(A_{t_{i_1}} \times \cdots \times A_{t_{i_n}})$$

for any permutation $(i_1, \ldots, i_n)$ of $(1, \ldots, n)$ and $A_{t_i} \in \mathcal{B}(R_{t_i})$, together with the usual consistency condition.

Then there exists a unique probability measure $P$ on $(R^T, \mathcal{B}(R^T))$ such that

$$P(\mathcal{I}_\tau(B)) = P_\tau(B), \quad B \in \mathcal{B}(R^\tau),$$

for all finite unordered subsets $\tau \subseteq T$, where $\mathcal{I}_\tau(B)$ is the cylinder set with base $B$.
