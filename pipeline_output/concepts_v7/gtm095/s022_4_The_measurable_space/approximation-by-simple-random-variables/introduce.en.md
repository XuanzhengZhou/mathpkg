This theorem is the measure-theoretic analogue of approximating Riemann-integrable functions by step functions. For nonnegative random variables, the dyadic construction produces a monotone nondecreasing sequence of simple functions converging pointwise. This result underpins the definition of the Lebesgue integral (expectation) by extending the elementary expectation from simple functions to all integrable random variables via monotone limits.
