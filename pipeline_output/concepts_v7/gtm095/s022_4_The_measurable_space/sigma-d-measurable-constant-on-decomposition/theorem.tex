Let $(\Omega, \mathcal{F})$ be a measurable space and $\mathcal{D} = \{D_1, D_2, \ldots\}$ a finite or countably infinite decomposition of $\Omega$ ($D_i \in \mathcal{F}$, $\sum_i D_i = \Omega$). Let $\sigma(\mathcal{D})$ be the $\sigma$-algebra generated by $\mathcal{D}$, consisting of $\varnothing$ and all countable unions of elements of $\mathcal{D}$.

If $\xi = \xi(\omega)$ is a $\sigma(\mathcal{D})$-measurable random variable, then $\xi$ is representable in the form

$$\xi(\omega) = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} x_k I_{D_k}(\omega),$$

where $x_k \in R$. That is, $\xi(\omega)$ is constant on each element $D_k$ of the decomposition.
