An $n$-dimensional random vector is the natural multivariate generalization of a random variable, representing a random point in Euclidean space $R^n$. The equivalence with random elements in $(R^n, \mathcal{B}(R^n))$ follows from the product structure of the Borel $\sigma$-algebra: $\mathcal{B}(R^n) = \mathcal{B}(R) \otimes \cdots \otimes \mathcal{B}(R)$. Each coordinate projection of a random vector is an ordinary (one-dimensional) random variable.
