A random (or stochastic) process is a family of random variables indexed by time, capturing the evolution of randomness. The definition unifies discrete-time processes (random sequences) and continuous-time processes under a single framework. By the structure of $\mathcal{B}(R^T)$, every random process can be viewed as a random element taking values in the space of all real-valued functions on $T$, linking the theory to Kolmogorov's extension theorem.
