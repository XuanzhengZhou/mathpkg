The distinction between a random process and its realizations is fundamental: the process $X = (\xi_t)_{t \in T}$ is a function of two variables $(\omega, t)$, while a realization (or sample path) is the function $t \mapsto \xi_t(\omega)$ obtained by fixing the random outcome $\omega$. This perspective connects the probabilistic description to the functional-analytic study of path properties such as continuity, differentiability, and integrability.
