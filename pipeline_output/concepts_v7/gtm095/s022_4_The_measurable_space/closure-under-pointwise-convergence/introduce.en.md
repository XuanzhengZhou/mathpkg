This theorem establishes that the space of extended random variables is a vector lattice closed under pointwise limits and algebraic operations. The closure under supremum, infimum, limsup, and liminf follows directly from the countable union/intersection properties of $\sigma$-algebras. Algebraic closure is obtained via approximation by simple functions and passage to the limit, making the set of random variables a robust function space suitable for integration theory.
