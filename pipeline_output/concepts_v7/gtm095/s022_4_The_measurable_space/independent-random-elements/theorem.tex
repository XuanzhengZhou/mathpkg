Let $(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, P)$ be a probability space and $(E_\alpha, \mathcal{E}_\alpha)$ measurable spaces, where $\alpha$ belongs to an arbitrary set $A$.

The $\mathcal{F}/\mathcal{E}_\alpha$-measurable functions $(X_\alpha(\omega))$, $\alpha \in A$, are said to be independent (or mutually independent) if, for every finite set of indices $\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n$ the random elements $X_{\alpha_1}, \ldots, X_{\alpha_n}$ are independent, i.e.,

$$P(X_{\alpha_1} \in B_{\alpha_1}, \ldots, X_{\alpha_n} \in B_{\alpha_n}) = P(X_{\alpha_1} \in B_{\alpha_1}) \cdots P(X_{\alpha_n} \in B_{\alpha_n}),$$

where $B_\alpha \in \mathcal{E}_\alpha$.
