Let $\xi = \xi(\omega)$ be a nonnegative random variable. Construct a sequence of simple nonnegative random variables $\{\xi_n\}_{n \geq 1}$ such that $\xi_n(\omega) \uparrow \xi(\omega)$, $n \to \infty$, for each $\omega \in \Omega$.

Since $E\xi_n \leq E\xi_{n+1}$, the limit $\lim_n E\xi_n$ exists, possibly with the value $+\infty$.

The Lebesgue integral of the nonnegative random variable $\xi = \xi(\omega)$, or its expectation, is

$$E\xi \equiv \lim_n E\xi_n.$$

This definition is consistent: the limit is independent of the choice of the approximating sequence $\{\xi_n\}$.
