A random variable has finite expectation precisely when it is integrable in the Lebesgue sense, i.e., $E|\xi| < \infty$. This absolute integrability property distinguishes the Lebesgue integral from the improper Riemann integral: Lebesgue integrability requires absolute convergence, meaning the integral of the absolute value must be finite. Random variables with finite expectation form the Banach space $L^1(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, P)$, the cornerstone of functional-analytic probability theory.
