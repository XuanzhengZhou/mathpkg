This lemma establishes that the class of random variables is closed under composition with Borel-measurable functions, forming a rich algebraic structure. Since all continuous functions, monotone limits, and elementary algebraic operations produce Borel functions, one immediately obtains that polynomials, absolute values, positive/negative parts, and numerous other transformations of random variables remain random variables.
