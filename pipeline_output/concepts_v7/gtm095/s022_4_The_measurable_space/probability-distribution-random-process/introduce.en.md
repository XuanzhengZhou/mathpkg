The probability distribution $P_X$ of a random process is the push-forward measure on the function space $R^T$, fully characterizing the probabilistic behavior of the process. The finite-dimensional distributions $\{P_{t_1,\ldots,t_n}\}$ are the marginals of $P_X$ and satisfy the Kolmogorov consistency conditions. Conversely, Kolmogorov's extension theorem guarantees that any consistent family of finite-dimensional distributions uniquely determines a probability measure on $(R^T, \mathcal{B}(R^T))$.
