This theorem, sometimes called the Doob-Dynkin lemma, provides a complete characterization of functions measurable with respect to $\sigma(\xi)$: they are precisely those that can be expressed as deterministic Borel functions of $\xi$. This is a factorization result stating that any $\sigma(\xi)$-measurable random variable depends on $\omega$ only through $\xi(\omega)$, formalizing the intuition that $\sigma(\xi)$ encodes precisely the information carried by $\xi$.
