These counting formulas are fundamental in enumerative combinatorics. While functions and injections have simple direct formulas, the number of surjections requires the inclusion-exclusion principle: one counts all functions and subtracts those that miss at least one element of the codomain. The formula for surjections is closely related to Stirling numbers of the second kind: $N(S) = m! \, S(n,m)$. These results also connect to the "occupancy problem" of distributing distinguishable balls into distinguishable boxes.
