The exponential generating function for derangement numbers has the remarkably simple closed form $E(x) = e^{-x}/(1-x)$. Expanding this as the product of the series for $e^{-x}$ and $1/(1-x)$ and equating coefficients of $x^n/n!$ recovers the formula $D_n = n! \sum_{k=0}^n (-1)^k/k!$. The text notes that the inclusion–exclusion method yields this result more directly than the generating function approach in this particular case.
