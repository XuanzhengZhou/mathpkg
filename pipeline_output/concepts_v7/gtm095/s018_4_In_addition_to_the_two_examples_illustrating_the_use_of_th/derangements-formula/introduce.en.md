The derangements problem (also known as the problème des rencontres) asks for the number of permutations of $n$ elements that leave no element in its original position. The solution, $D_n = n! \sum_{k=0}^n (-1)^k/k!$, is a classic application of the inclusion–exclusion principle and yields the elegant asymptotic approximation $D_n \sim n!/e$. This means that for large $n$, the probability that a random permutation is a derangement approaches $1/e \approx 0.3679$.
