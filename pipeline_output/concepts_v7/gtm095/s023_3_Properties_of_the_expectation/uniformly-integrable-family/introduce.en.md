A family of random variables is uniformly integrable if the tail contributions to their expectations vanish uniformly as the truncation threshold tends to infinity. This is a stronger condition than mere integrability of each member. A sufficient condition is the existence of an integrable dominating random variable $\eta$ such that $|\xi_n| \leq \eta$ for all $n$. Uniform integrability plays a key role in extending the dominated convergence theorem.
