Lebesgue's Dominated Convergence Theorem is one of the most important results in integration theory. If a sequence of random variables converges almost surely and is dominated by an integrable random variable, then the expectations converge to the expectation of the limit, and convergence also holds in $L^1$. The proof applies Fatou's Lemma to both $\liminf$ and $\limsup$, yielding equality of the limit inferior and limit superior of expectations.
