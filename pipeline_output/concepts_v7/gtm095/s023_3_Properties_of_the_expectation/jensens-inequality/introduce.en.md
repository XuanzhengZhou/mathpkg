Jensen's Inequality states that for a convex function $g$, the function evaluated at the expectation is bounded above by the expectation of the function. This fundamental inequality generalizes the fact that the square of the mean is less than or equal to the mean of squares. It is a cornerstone result that underpins Lyapunov's inequality and many other important bounds in probability theory.
