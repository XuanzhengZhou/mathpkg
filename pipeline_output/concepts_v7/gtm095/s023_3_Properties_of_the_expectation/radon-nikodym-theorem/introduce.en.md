The Radon--Nikodým theorem is a cornerstone of measure theory. It states that any signed measure $\lambda$ absolutely continuous with respect to a $\sigma$-finite measure $\mu$ can be represented as an integral with a measurable density function $f$, called the Radon--Nikodým derivative. The density is unique up to $\mu$-null sets. This theorem plays a key role in the construction of conditional expectations in probability theory.
