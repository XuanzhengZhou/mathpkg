Hölder's Inequality generalizes the Cauchy–Bunyakovskii inequality to arbitrary conjugate exponents $p$ and $q$ satisfying $1/p + 1/q = 1$. It bounds the $L^1$ norm of a product by the product of $L^p$ and $L^q$ norms. The special case $p = q = 2$ recovers the Cauchy–Bunyakovskii inequality. The proof normalizes the variables and uses Young's inequality for the product of normalized terms.
