Let $(\Omega, \mathcal{F})$ and $(E, \mathcal{E})$ be measurable spaces and $X = X(\omega)$ an $\mathcal{F}/\mathcal{E}$-measurable function with values in $E$. Let $P$ be a probability measure on $(\Omega, \mathcal{F})$ and $P_X$ the probability measure on $(E, \mathcal{E})$ induced by $X = X(\omega)$:
$$P_X(A) = P\{\omega : X(\omega) \in A\}, \quad A \in \mathcal{E}.$$
Then
$$\int_A g(x) \, P_X(dx) = \int_{X^{-1}(A)} g(X(\omega)) \, P(d\omega), \quad A \in \mathcal{E},$$
for every $\mathcal{E}$-measurable function $g = g(x)$, $x \in E$ (in the sense that if one integral exists, the other is well defined, and the two are equal).
