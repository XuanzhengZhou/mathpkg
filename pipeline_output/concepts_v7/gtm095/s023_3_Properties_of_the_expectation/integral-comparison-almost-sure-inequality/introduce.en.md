If the integral of $\xi$ over every measurable set $A$ is bounded above by the integral of $\eta$ over that same set, then $\xi \leq \eta$ almost surely. The proof considers the set $B = \{\omega : \xi(\omega) > \eta(\omega)\}$, shows the two integrals over $B$ are equal, and uses Properties E and H to conclude $P(B) = 0$.
