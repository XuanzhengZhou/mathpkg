The best linear estimator of a random variable $\xi \in L^2$ in terms of an orthonormal system $\eta_1, \ldots, \eta_n$ is denoted by

$$\hat{\mathsf{E}}(\xi \mid \eta_1, \ldots, \eta_n) \equiv \sum_{i=1}^{n} (\xi, \eta_i) \eta_i$$

and called the conditional expectation of $\xi$ with respect to $\eta_1, \ldots, \eta_n$ in the wide sense.

For a countable orthonormal system $\eta_1, \eta_2, \ldots$, the wide-sense conditional expectation is defined as the projection onto the closed linear manifold:

$$\hat{\mathsf{E}}(\xi \mid \eta_1, \eta_2, \ldots) \equiv \hat{\xi} = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^{n} (\xi, \eta_i) \eta_i,$$

where $\hat{\xi}$ is the unique element of $\overline{\mathcal{L}}\{\eta_1, \eta_2, \ldots\}$ satisfying

$$\|\xi - \hat{\xi}\| = \inf\{\|\xi - \zeta\| : \zeta \in \overline{\mathcal{L}}\{\eta_1, \eta_2, \ldots\}\}.$$
