The Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization process constructs an orthonormal sequence from a linearly independent sequence of random variables in $L^2$. At each step, the new variable is projected onto the span of the previously constructed orthonormal elements, and the residual is normalized. Unlike diagonalization of the covariance matrix, Gram-Schmidt is causal: each orthonormal variable depends only on the past of the original sequence, making it applicable to infinite sequences and time-series settings.
