Bessel's inequality bounds the sum of squared Fourier coefficients of a random variable with respect to an orthonormal system by its squared $L^2$ norm. Equality holds precisely when the Fourier series converges to the variable in mean square, which characterizes when an orthonormal system forms a basis. This is the probabilistic formulation of the classical inequality from Hilbert space theory.
