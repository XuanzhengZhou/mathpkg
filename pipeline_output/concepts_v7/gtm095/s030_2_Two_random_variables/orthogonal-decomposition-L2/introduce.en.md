The orthogonal decomposition expresses any square-integrable random variable as the sum of its projection onto a closed linear manifold and a residual component perpendicular to that manifold. This decomposition is unique and generalizes the familiar orthogonal decomposition of a vector in Euclidean space. The projection component is the wide-sense conditional expectation and represents the best linear mean-square estimator. From the estimation viewpoint, the residual is the irreducible error.
