For $\xi \in L^2$ and a closed linear manifold $\overline{\mathcal{L}} = \overline{\mathcal{L}}\{\eta_1, \eta_2, \ldots\}$ spanned by an orthonormal system, the orthogonal decomposition of $\xi$ is

$$\xi = \hat{\xi} + (\xi - \hat{\xi}),$$

where $\hat{\xi} \in \overline{\mathcal{L}}$ is the projection of $\xi$ onto $\overline{\mathcal{L}}$, and $\xi - \hat{\xi} \perp \overline{\mathcal{L}}$, i.e.,

$$(\xi - \hat{\xi}, \zeta) = \mathsf{E}[(\xi - \hat{\xi})\zeta] = 0 \quad \text{for all } \zeta \in \overline{\mathcal{L}}.$$

The projection $\hat{\xi}$ is denoted $\hat{\mathsf{E}}(\xi \mid \eta_1, \eta_2, \ldots)$ and called the conditional expectation of $\xi$ in the wide sense. It is the best linear mean-square estimator of $\xi$ in terms of $\eta_1, \eta_2, \ldots$.
