Two random variables in $L^2$ are orthogonal when their inner product, defined as the expectation of their product, is zero. This notion coincides with uncorrelatedness for random variables having zero mean. Orthogonality is a core concept in the $L^2$-theory of probability, where Hilbert space methods are used to study properties depending on the first two moments of random variables.
