An orthogonal system is a subset of $L^2$ in which every pair of distinct random variables is orthogonal. When additionally each variable has unit norm, the system is called orthonormal. These systems generalize the familiar notions of orthogonal and orthonormal vectors to the Hilbert space of square-integrable random variables, forming the foundation for best linear estimation and spectral decompositions in probability theory.
