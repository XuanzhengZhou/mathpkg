Let $M = \{\eta_1, \ldots, \eta_n\}$ be an orthonormal system in $L^2$ and let $\xi$ be any random variable in $L^2$. Among all linear estimators of the form $\sum_{i=1}^{n} a_i \eta_i$, the best estimator of $\xi$ in the mean-square sense is

$$\hat{\xi} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} (\xi, \eta_i) \eta_i,$$

where $(\xi, \eta_i) = \mathsf{E}[\xi \eta_i]$ denotes the inner product in $L^2$. The corresponding minimal mean-square error is

$$\Delta \equiv \inf \mathsf{E}\left|\xi - \sum_{i=1}^{n} a_i \eta_i\right|^2 = \mathsf{E}|\xi - \hat{\xi}|^2 = \|\xi\|^2 - \sum_{i=1}^{n} |(\xi, \eta_i)|^2.$$
