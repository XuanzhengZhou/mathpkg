This theorem collects the fundamental analytic properties of characteristic functions. The first four properties are elementary: normalization, boundedness, uniform continuity, and conjugate symmetry, with the latter characterizing symmetric distributions. The deeper properties connect differentiability of the characteristic function to existence of moments: finite absolute moments yield derivatives and a Taylor expansion (property 5), while existence of even-order derivatives at zero forces the corresponding moments to be finite (property 6). Under a Cramer-type condition on moment growth, the characteristic function is analytic (property 7).
