This lemma provides a useful criterion for weak convergence of a tight family of probability measures. It states that if a tight sequence of probability measures has the property that all weakly convergent subsequences converge to the same limit measure, then the entire sequence must converge weakly to that measure. The proof proceeds by contradiction: if the whole sequence does not converge to $P$, one can extract a subsequence converging to a different limit $Q \neq P$, contradicting the hypothesis. This result is used as a building block in the proof of the Continuity Theorem (Theorem 1).
