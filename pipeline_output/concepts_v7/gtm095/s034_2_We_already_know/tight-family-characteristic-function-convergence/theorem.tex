Let $\{P_n\}$ be a tight family of probability measures on $(\mathbb{R}, \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R}))$. A necessary and sufficient condition for the sequence $\{P_n\}$ to converge weakly to a probability measure is that for each $t \in \mathbb{R}$ the limit $\lim_n \varphi_n(t)$ exists, where $\varphi_n(t)$ is the characteristic function of $P_n$:
$$\varphi_n(t) = \int_{\mathbb{R}} e^{itx} \, P_n(dx).$$
