Bayes' formula provides a systematic way to update prior beliefs $P(A_i)$ in light of new evidence $B$, yielding posterior probabilities $P(A_i \mid B)$. It combines the prior probabilities with the likelihoods $P(B \mid A_i)$ and normalizes by the total probability of the evidence. This theorem is the mathematical foundation of Bayesian statistics, machine learning, and any reasoning that involves updating uncertainty in response to data.
