Let $\{A_1, \ldots, A_n\}$ be a complete set of disjoint events with $\text{P}(A_i) > 0$, and let $B$ be an event with $\text{P}(B) > 0$. Then \text{Bayes' formula} gives the posterior (a posteriori) probability of $A_i$ given $B$:
$$\text{P}(A_i \mid B) = \frac{\text{P}(A_i) \text{P}(B \mid A_i)}{\sum_{j=1}^{n} \text{P}(A_j) \text{P}(B \mid A_j)}.$$
