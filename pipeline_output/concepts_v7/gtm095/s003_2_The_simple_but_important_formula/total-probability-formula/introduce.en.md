The total probability formula is a fundamental tool for computing probabilities of complex events by conditioning on a partition of the sample space. It decomposes $\text{P}(B)$ into a weighted sum of conditional probabilities $\text{P}(B \mid A_i)$, weighted by the probabilities of the partitioning events $A_i$. This formula underpins Bayesian inference, Markov chain analysis, and any situation where the probability of an event is more naturally expressed conditionally on mutually exclusive scenarios.
