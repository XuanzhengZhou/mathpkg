The first entrance decomposition formula $p_{ij}^{(n)} = \sum_{k=1}^n f_{ij}^{(k)} p_{jj}^{(n-k)}$ is a fundamental identity in Markov chain theory that decomposes the $n$-step transition probability according to the time of first visit to state $j$. It follows from the strong Markov property and expresses the fact that the path from $i$ to $j$ can be split at the first hitting time of $j$. This decomposition is the basis for renewal theory and the analysis of recurrence and transience in Markov chains.
