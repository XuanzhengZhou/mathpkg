The strong Markov property generalizes the ordinary Markov property by allowing the fixed time $k$ to be replaced by a random stopping time $\tau$. It states that, conditional on the state at the stopping time and on the pre-$\tau$ history, the future evolution of the chain is governed by the same transition probabilities and is independent of the past. This property is fundamental to the analysis of Markov chains, enabling the derivation of first-passage decompositions and renewal equations.
