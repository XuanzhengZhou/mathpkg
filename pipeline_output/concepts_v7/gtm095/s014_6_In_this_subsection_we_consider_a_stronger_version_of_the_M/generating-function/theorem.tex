Let $a = (a_0, a_1, a_2, \ldots)$ be a sequence of real numbers. The \text{generating function} $A(x)$ of the sequence $a$ is defined, for $|x| < \lambda$ with some $\lambda > 0$, by the power series
$$A(x) = a_0 + a_1 x + a_2 x^2 + \cdots.$$
The sequence $a$ is uniquely determined by $A(x)$. The \text{exponential generating function} is
$$E(x) = a_0 + a_1 \frac{x}{1!} + a_2 \frac{x^2}{2!} + \cdots.$$
