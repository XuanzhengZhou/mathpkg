Generating functions transform sequences into formal power series, converting combinatorial operations into algebraic ones. If $\xi$ is an integer-valued random variable with distribution $p_k = P(\xi = k)$, its generating function is $G_\xi(x) = E x^\xi = \sum_k p_k x^k$. The method, pioneered by Euler and refined by Laplace, is one of the most powerful algebraic tools in discrete probability theory for solving recurrence relations, counting problems, and analyzing sums of independent random variables.
