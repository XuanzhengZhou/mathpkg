First return and first arrival probabilities are fundamental quantities in the analysis of Markov chains. The first return probability $f_{ii}^{(k)}$ gives the probability that a chain starting at state $i$ returns to $i$ for the first time exactly at step $k$. Similarly, the first arrival probability $f_{ij}^{(k)}$ gives the probability of first visiting state $j$ at time $k$ when starting from $i$. These probabilities are essential for classifying states as recurrent or transient and for computing expected hitting times.
