The Berry–Esseen theorem provides a quantitative bound on the rate of convergence in the Central Limit Theorem. For the special case of independent Bernoulli trials, it states that the uniform distance between the standardized binomial distribution function and the standard normal distribution function is bounded by $(p^2+q^2)/\sqrt{npq}$. This bound reveals that the normal approximation can be poor when $p$ is close to $0$ or $1$, even for large $n$, since the denominator $\sqrt{npq}$ becomes small. The theorem demonstrates that the convergence rate $O(1/\sqrt{n})$ is optimal and cannot be improved.
