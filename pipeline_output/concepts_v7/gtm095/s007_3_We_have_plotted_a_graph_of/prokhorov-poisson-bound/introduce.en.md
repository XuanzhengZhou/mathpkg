Prokhorov's bound quantifies the rate of convergence in Poisson's limit theorem using the total variation distance. When the expected number of successes $np$ is held constant at $\lambda$, the sum of absolute differences between binomial and Poisson probabilities is bounded by $(2\lambda/n) \cdot \min(2, \lambda)$. This provides a precise $O(1/n)$ convergence rate, complementing the qualitative statement of Poisson's theorem with a quantitative estimate.
