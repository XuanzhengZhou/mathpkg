The standard normal (Gaussian) probability density $\varphi(x)$ is the derivative of the normal distribution function $\Phi(x)$. It describes the familiar bell-shaped curve, symmetric about zero, which decreases very rapidly as $|x|$ grows: $\varphi(1) \approx 0.242$, $\varphi(2) \approx 0.054$, $\varphi(3) \approx 0.004$, $\varphi(4) \approx 0.00013$. The density attains its maximum at $x = 0$ with value $(2\pi)^{-1/2} \approx 0.399$. This density arises universally as the limit of normalized histograms of sums of independent random variables.
