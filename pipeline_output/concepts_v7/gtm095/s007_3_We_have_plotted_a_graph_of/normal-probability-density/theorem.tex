The standard normal (Gaussian) probability density is defined by

$$\varphi(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-x^2/2}, \quad x \in \mathbb{R}^1.$$

Its relation to the normal distribution function is $\Phi(x) = \int_{-\infty}^x \varphi(t) \, dt$. The function $\varphi(x)$ is a symmetric bell-shaped curve, decreasing very rapidly with increasing $|x|$, with maximum at $x = 0$ equal to $(2\pi)^{-1/2} \approx 0.399$.
