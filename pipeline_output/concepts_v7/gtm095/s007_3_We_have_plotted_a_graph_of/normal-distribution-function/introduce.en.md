The standard normal (or Gaussian) distribution function $\Phi(x)$ plays an exceptionally important role in probability theory. It first appears in the de Moivre–Laplace integral theorem as the limiting distribution of standardized sums of independent Bernoulli random variables. The function approaches $1$ very rapidly as $x$ increases: $\Phi(1) \approx 0.841$, $\Phi(2) \approx 0.977$, $\Phi(3) \approx 0.999$. Under rather general hypotheses, sums of a large number of independent random variables are closely approximated by the normal distribution.
