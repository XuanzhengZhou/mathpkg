The Poisson distribution is a discrete probability distribution on the nonnegative integers, parameterized by a positive real number $\lambda$ representing the expected number of occurrences. It arises naturally as the limit of binomial distributions when the number of trials grows large and the success probability becomes small while their product stays constant. This is the first example encountered in the text of a discrete distribution concentrated on a countably infinite set of points, in contrast to all previous discrete distributions which were concentrated on finite sets.
