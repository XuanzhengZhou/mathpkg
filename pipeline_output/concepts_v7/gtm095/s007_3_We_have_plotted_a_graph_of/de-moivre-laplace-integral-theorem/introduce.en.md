The De Moivre–Laplace integral theorem is a fundamental result in probability theory stating that the distribution of a standardized sum of independent Bernoulli trials converges to the standard normal distribution as the number of trials grows. It provides the normal approximation to binomial probabilities, forming the earliest version of what is now known as the Central Limit Theorem. The theorem quantifies how the probability that the normalized sum falls within an interval $[a, b]$ is approximated by the integral of the standard Gaussian density over that interval.
