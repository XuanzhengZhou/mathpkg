A normed space is simply a vector space equipped with a norm. The norm induces a translation-invariant metric $d(x,y) = \|x-y\|$ and hence a topology (the norm topology). Every normed space is therefore a metrizable TVS. The distinction between real and complex normed spaces matters for results involving duality, spectral theory, and complex analysis methods. A Banach space is a normed space that is complete with respect to the metric induced by its norm.
