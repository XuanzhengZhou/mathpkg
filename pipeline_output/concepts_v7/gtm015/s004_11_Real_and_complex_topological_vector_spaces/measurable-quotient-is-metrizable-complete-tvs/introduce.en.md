The quotient space $[\mathcal{M}]$ of measurable functions modulo almost-everywhere equality, equipped with the metric $d([f],[g]) = \int |f-g|/(1+|f-g|) d\mu$, is a metrizable complete TVS (an $F$-space). While the additive group structure and metric completeness follow from standard measure-theoretic arguments (Riesz-Weyl theorem), the continuity of scalar multiplication requires a more delicate proof using the properties of convergence in measure. This space is historically important as a counterexample: unlike Banach spaces, it admits no nontrivial continuous linear functionals under certain measure-theoretic hypotheses.
