A mapping $f: E_1 \times \cdots \times E_n \to F$, where $E_1, \ldots, E_n, F$ are vector spaces over $\mathbb{K}$, is said to be \textbf{multilinear} if it is linear in each of its variables separately; this means, precisely, that for each index $k$ and for each selection of $n-1$ vectors $a_j \in E_j$ ($j \neq k$), the mapping $E_k \to F$ defined by
$$x_k \mapsto f(a_1, \ldots, a_{k-1}, x_k, a_{k+1}, \ldots, a_n) \quad (x_k \in E_k)$$
is linear.
