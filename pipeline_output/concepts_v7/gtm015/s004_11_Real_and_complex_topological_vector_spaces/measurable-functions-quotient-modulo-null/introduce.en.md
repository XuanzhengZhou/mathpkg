Given a finite measure space $(X, \mathcal{S}, \mu)$, one considers the real algebra $\mathcal{M}$ of all real-valued $\mathcal{S}$-measurable functions. The subset $\mathcal{N}$ of functions vanishing almost everywhere forms an ideal, and the quotient algebra $[\mathcal{M}] = \mathcal{M}/\mathcal{N}$ identifies functions that agree almost everywhere. This quotient is the underlying vector space for an important example of a metrizable complete TVS that is not locally convex, serving as a source of counterexamples throughout functional analysis.
