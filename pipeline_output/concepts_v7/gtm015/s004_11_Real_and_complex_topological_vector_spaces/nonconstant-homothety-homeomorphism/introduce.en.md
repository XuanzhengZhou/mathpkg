A homothety is an affine map of the form $f(x) = \lambda x + a$ combining scaling by a nonzero scalar $\lambda$ and translation by a vector $a$. In any topological vector space, such maps are homeomorphisms (continuous bijections with continuous inverses), because the inverse $f^{-1}(y) = \lambda^{-1}(y - a)$ is also a homothety. This property means that the topology of a TVS is homogeneous — the local structure near any point is the same as near the origin.
