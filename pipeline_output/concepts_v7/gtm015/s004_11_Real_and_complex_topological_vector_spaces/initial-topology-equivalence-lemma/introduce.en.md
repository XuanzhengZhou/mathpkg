This lemma establishes the equivalence between two descriptions of the initial topology: the coarsest topology making each $f_\iota$ continuous is the same as the coarsest topology making the product map $x \mapsto (f_\iota(x))$ continuous. This is because basic open sets in both descriptions are finite intersections of $f_\iota^{-1}(U_\iota)$, which is precisely $f^{-1}(\prod U_\iota)$. This equivalence allows transferring results about initial topologies for single maps (such as Theorem 19.3 on TVS compatibility) to families of maps, which underlies the definition of projective limits and weak topologies.
