Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$, equipped with a metrizable topology such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] the topology is compatible with the additive group structure,
\item[(ii)] for each $x \in E$, the mapping $\lambda \mapsto \lambda x$ is continuous at $\lambda = 0$,
\item[(iii)] for each $\lambda \in \mathbb{K}$, the mapping $x \mapsto \lambda x$ is continuous at $x = \theta$.
\end{enumerate}
Then the topology is compatible with the vector space structure, i.e., $E$ is a TVS.
