This lemma establishes the fundamental link between the metric topology on $[\mathcal{M}]$ and the classical notion of convergence in measure. The metric $d([f],[g]) = \int |f-g|/(1+|f-g|) d\mu$ is chosen precisely so that $d$-convergence coincides with convergence in measure. This equivalence is the cornerstone for proving that $[\mathcal{M}]$ is a complete metric space, since the Riesz-Weyl theorem guarantees that every sequence fundamental in measure has a subsequence converging almost everywhere, and hence in measure.
