Any linear subspace $M$ of a topological vector space $E$ is itself a topological vector space when given the relative (subspace) topology. Continuity of addition and scalar multiplication on $M$ follows immediately from restricting the continuous operations on $E$ to $M \times M$ and $\mathbb{K} \times M$. This is one of the fundamental constructions for building new TVS from existing ones.
