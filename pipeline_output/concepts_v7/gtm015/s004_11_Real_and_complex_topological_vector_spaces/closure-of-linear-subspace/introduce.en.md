The closure $\overline{M}$ of a linear subspace $M$ in a TVS remains a linear subspace. This follows from the continuity of addition and scalar multiplication: the continuous map $(x,y) \mapsto x+y$ sends $\overline{M} \times \overline{M}$ into $\overline{M}$, and $(\lambda, x) \mapsto \lambda x$ sends $\mathbb{K} \times \overline{M}$ into $\overline{M}$. This property is essential for defining closed subspaces, which play the role of "nice" subspaces in functional analysis.
