For linear maps between TVS, continuity everywhere is equivalent to continuity at zero. This theorem extends the same principle to multilinear maps: a multilinear map $f: E_1 \times \cdots \times E_n \to F$ is globally continuous if and only if it is continuous at the origin $(\theta, \ldots, \theta)$. The proof is more involved than the linear case, using an algebraic expansion of $f(a_1 + z_1, \ldots, a_n + z_n)$ to control the difference $f(a_1 + z_1, \ldots) - f(a_1, \ldots)$ in terms of evaluations near zero. This result is essential for the theory of topological algebras and tensor products.
