Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$. A \textbf{norm} on $E$ is a nonnegative, real-valued function $x \mapsto \|x\|$ ($x \in E$) such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\|\theta\| = 0$,
\item[(ii)] $\|x\| > 0$ when $x \neq \theta$,
\item[(iii)] $\|\lambda x\| = |\lambda| \, \|x\|$ for all $\lambda \in \mathbb{K}$, $x \in E$,
\item[(iv)] $\|x + y\| \leq \|x\| + \|y\|$ for all $x, y \in E$.
\end{enumerate}
In words, a norm is strictly positive, absolutely homogeneous and subadditive.
