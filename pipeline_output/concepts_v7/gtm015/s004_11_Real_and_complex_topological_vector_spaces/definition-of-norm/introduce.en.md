A norm is a function assigning a nonnegative "length" to each vector, generalizing the absolute value on $\mathbb{K}$ and the Euclidean length on $\mathbb{R}^n$. The defining properties are: positivity (only the zero vector has length zero), absolute homogeneity ($\|\lambda x\| = |\lambda| \|x\|$), and the triangle inequality ($\|x+y\| \leq \|x\| + \|y\|$). A norm induces a metric $d(x,y) = \|x-y\|$ and hence a topology. Normed spaces are the most important class of topological vector spaces, and their complete counterparts — Banach spaces — are the central objects of functional analysis.
