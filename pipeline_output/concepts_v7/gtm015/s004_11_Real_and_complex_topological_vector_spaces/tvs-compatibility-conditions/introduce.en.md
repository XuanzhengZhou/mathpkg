This theorem provides an equivalent reformulation of the TVS definition by decomposing the joint continuity of scalar multiplication into four separate conditions. Instead of requiring scalar multiplication to be jointly continuous everywhere, it suffices to check continuity of addition, separate continuity of scalar multiplication in each variable at zero, and joint continuity at the origin. This decomposition is frequently more convenient to verify in practice.
