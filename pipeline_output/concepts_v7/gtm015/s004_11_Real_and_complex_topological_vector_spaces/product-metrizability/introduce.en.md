A countable product of metric spaces can be given an explicit metric whose topology coincides with the product topology. The construction uses weights $2^{-n}$ and the bounded equivalent metric $d_n/(1+d_n)$ for each factor to ensure convergence of the series. The resulting metric preserves completeness: the product is complete if and only if each factor is complete. This is the technical foundation for constructing the sequence space $(s)$ and other Frechet spaces as countable products.
