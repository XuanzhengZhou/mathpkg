Suppose $(X_n, d_n)$ ($n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$) is a sequence of metric spaces and $X = \prod_{n=1}^{\infty} X_n$ is the Cartesian product of the sets $X_n$. For $x = (x_n)$, $y = (y_n)$ in $X$, define
$$d(x, y) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} 2^{-n} \frac{d_n(x_n, y_n)}{1 + d_n(x_n, y_n)}.$$
Then $d$ is a metric on $X$, and the $d$-topology coincides with the product topology. Moreover, $X$ is complete for $d$ if and only if each $X_n$ is complete for $d_n$.
