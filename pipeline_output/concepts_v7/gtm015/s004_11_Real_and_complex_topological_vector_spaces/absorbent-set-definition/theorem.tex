Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$. A subset $A$ of $E$ is said to be \textbf{absorbent} if, for each $x \in E$, there exists an $\varepsilon > 0$ such that $\lambda x \in A$ whenever $|\lambda| \leq \varepsilon$. It follows (from either $\lambda \theta = \theta$ or $0x = \theta$) that $\theta \in A$.
