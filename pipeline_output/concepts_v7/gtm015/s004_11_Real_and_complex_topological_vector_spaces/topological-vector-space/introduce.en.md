A topological vector space (TVS) combines the algebraic structure of a vector space with a topological structure, requiring that vector addition and scalar multiplication be continuous operations. The base field $\mathbb{K}$ is always either the real numbers $\mathbb{R}$ or the complex numbers $\mathbb{C}$, equipped with their usual metric topologies. Every complex TVS is also a real TVS by restricting scalar multiplication to real scalars. This definition is the foundational concept for the entire theory developed in this chapter.
