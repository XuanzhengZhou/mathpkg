A \textbf{topological vector space} (briefly, TVS) over $\mathbb{K}$ is a vector space $E$ over $\mathbb{K}$, equipped with a topology that is compatible with the vector space structure, i.e., such that the mappings
$$(x, y) \mapsto x + y \quad (x, y \in E)$$
and
$$(\lambda, x) \mapsto \lambda x \quad (\lambda \in \mathbb{K}, x \in E)$$
are continuous. (It is assumed that $\mathbb{K}$ bears the usual topology, defined by the metric $|\lambda - \mu|$.) We refer to $E$ as a \textbf{real TVS} or a \textbf{complex TVS} according as $\mathbb{K} = \mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{K} = \mathbb{C}$.
