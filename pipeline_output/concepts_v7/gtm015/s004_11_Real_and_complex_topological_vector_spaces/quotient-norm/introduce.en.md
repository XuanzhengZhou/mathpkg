Given a normed space $E$ and a closed linear subspace $M$, the quotient space $E/M$ can be equipped with a canonical quotient norm: $\|u\| = \inf\{\|x\| : x \in u\}$. This norm measures the distance from the origin to the coset $u$. The closedness of $M$ ensures the quotient norm is strictly positive (otherwise the infimum would be zero for nonzero cosets). The resulting norm topology coincides with the quotient topology, making $E/M$ a normed space.
