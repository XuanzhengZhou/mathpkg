This theorem establishes the equivalence between the abstract notion of a metrizable complete TVS and the concrete notion of an $F$-space (a complete metric vector space with an invariant metric satisfying natural continuity conditions). Every metrizable complete TVS is an $F$-space, and conversely every $F$-space is a metrizable complete TVS. The only nontrivial part of the converse is proving joint continuity of scalar multiplication, which relies on Lemma (13.3). Frechet spaces (locally convex $F$-spaces) are the most important subclass.
