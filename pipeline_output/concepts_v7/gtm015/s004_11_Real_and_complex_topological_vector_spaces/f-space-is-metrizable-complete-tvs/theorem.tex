If $(E, d)$ is a space of type $(F)$ then $E$ is a metrizable complete TVS in the topology derived from $d$. A space of type $(F)$ is defined as a vector space $E$ over $\mathbb{K}$ equipped with a complete, additively invariant metric $d$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $d(x, y) = d(x - y, \theta)$ for all $x, y \in E$,
\item[(ii)] if $\lambda_n \in \mathbb{K}$ and $\lambda_n \to 0$, then $d(\lambda_n x, \theta) \to 0$ for each $x \in E$,
\item[(iii)] if $x_n \in E$ and $d(x_n, \theta) \to 0$, then $d(\lambda x_n, \theta) \to 0$ for each $\lambda \in \mathbb{K}$.
\end{enumerate}
