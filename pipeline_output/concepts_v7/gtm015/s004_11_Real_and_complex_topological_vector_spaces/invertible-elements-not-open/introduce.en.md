In Banach algebras, a fundamental theorem states that the set of invertible elements is always open. This theorem shows that the topological ring $[\mathcal{M}]$ (viewed with pointwise multiplication) does not share this property: when the measure admits arbitrarily small positive values, the invertible elements (functions nonzero almost everywhere) fail to form an open set. This demonstrates that $[\mathcal{M}]$ is fundamentally different from the topological rings arising in Banach algebra theory, highlighting the extreme non-local-convexity of this space.
