If $N$ is a closed linear subspace of a Hilbert space $H$, the subspace $N^\perp$ is called the \emph{orthogonal complement} of $N$. By Theorem~42.9, the orthogonal complement of $N^\perp$ is $N$. The orthogonal projection theorem~42.7 is expressed by writing
$$H = N \oplus N^\perp,$$
where $H = N + N^\perp$ and $N \cap N^\perp = \{\theta\}$. Thus $H$ is the algebraic direct sum of $N$ and $N^\perp$.
