For an arbitrary subset $S$ of a Hilbert space, the double orthogonal complement $S^{\perp\perp}$ coincides with the closed linear span of $S$. This generalizes Theorem~42.9, which covers the special case where $S$ is already a closed linear subspace. The result shows that the operation $S \mapsto S^{\perp\perp}$ acts as a closure operator, giving the smallest closed subspace containing $S$.
