When two vectors are orthogonal, their inner product vanishes, and the squared norm of their sum equals the sum of their squared norms. This is the inner-product-space analog of the Pythagorean theorem, following directly from expanding $\|x+y\|^2 = (x+y \mid x+y)$. It plays an essential role in the proof of the orthogonal projection theorem.
