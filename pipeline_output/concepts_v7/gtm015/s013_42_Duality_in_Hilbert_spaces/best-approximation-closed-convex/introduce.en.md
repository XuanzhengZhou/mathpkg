In a Hilbert space, every nonempty closed convex set admits a unique best approximation to any given point. The proof uses the parallelogram law to establish that a minimizing sequence is Cauchy, and completeness guarantees its limit lies in the closed set. This theorem is the geometric cornerstone that makes orthogonal projections onto closed subspaces possible.
