The orthogonal projection theorem states that every closed linear subspace of a Hilbert space induces a unique orthogonal decomposition of the whole space. Given any point $x$, there is a unique best approximation $y$ in $N$, and the residual $z = x - y$ is orthogonal to $N$. This generalizes the decomposition of Euclidean space into a subspace and its orthogonal complement.
