In an inner product space, every vector $y$ naturally induces a continuous linear form $y'$ by taking the inner product with $y$. The Cauchy-Schwarz inequality guarantees that $y'$ is bounded with norm at most $\|y\|$. This construction is the starting point for establishing that a Hilbert space is self-dual: the map $y \mapsto y'$ embeds the space into its continuous dual.
