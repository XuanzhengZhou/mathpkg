The map $y \mapsto y'$ that sends each vector to its induced linear form preserves addition, turns scalar multiplication into its complex conjugate, and preserves the norm exactly. Thus it is a conjugate-linear isometric embedding of the inner product space into its continuous dual. For Hilbert spaces, the Riesz representation theorem strengthens this to a surjective map, establishing self-duality.
