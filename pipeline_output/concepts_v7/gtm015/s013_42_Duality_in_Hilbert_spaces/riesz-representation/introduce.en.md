The Riesz representation theorem is the central result of Hilbert space duality: every continuous linear form on a Hilbert space is given by taking the inner product with a uniquely determined vector. The proof uses the orthogonal decomposition theorem: the kernel of a nonzero linear form is a closed subspace of codimension one, and a vector in its orthogonal complement provides the representing vector. This establishes that a Hilbert space is self-dual.
