Let $H$ be a Hilbert space. The conjugate-linear isometric mapping $y \mapsto y'$ of $H$ into $H'$ (Theorem~42.2) is surjective: for every continuous linear form $f \in H'$, there exists a unique vector $y \in H$ such that
$$f(x) = (x \mid y) \quad \text{for all } x \in H.$$
Thus $H$ is self-dual: $H'$ is isometrically conjugate-isomorphic to $H$ via the correspondence $y \leftrightarrow y'$.
