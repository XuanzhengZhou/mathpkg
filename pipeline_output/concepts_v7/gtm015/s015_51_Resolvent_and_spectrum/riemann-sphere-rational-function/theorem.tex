Let $\mathfrak{S} = \mathbb{C} \cup \{\infty\}$ be the Riemann sphere. Each rational form $f = p/q$ in reduced form defines a continuous function $f: \mathfrak{S} \to \mathfrak{S}$ by
$$f(\lambda) = p(\lambda)/q(\lambda) \quad (\lambda \in \mathfrak{S}, q(\lambda) \neq 0),$$
with $f(\lambda) = \infty$ when $q(\lambda) = 0$, and $f(\infty) = \lim_{\lambda \to \infty} f(\lambda)$. A function $\mathfrak{S} \to \mathfrak{S}$ arising from some rational form is called a rational function.
