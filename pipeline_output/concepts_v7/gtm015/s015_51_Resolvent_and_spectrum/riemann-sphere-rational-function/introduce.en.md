Every rational form defines a continuous function from the Riemann sphere to itself, with poles mapping to infinity. This geometric interpretation, viewing rational functions as self-maps of $\mathfrak{S}$, is useful for understanding composition of rational functions and the substitution formula $f(g(x)) = (f \circ g)(x)$ in the functional calculus.
