For each $\lambda \in \mathbb{C}$, we write $\varphi_\lambda = \Phi_\lambda^{\mathbb{C}}$. Thus
$$\varphi_\lambda : \mathbb{C}(t; \{\lambda\}) \to \mathbb{C}$$
is the unique algebra homomorphism such that $\varphi_\lambda(1) = 1$ and $\varphi_\lambda(t) = \lambda$. For all $f \in \mathbb{C}(t; \{\lambda\})$, the definition $f(\lambda) = \varphi_\lambda(f)$ coincides with the usual interpretation of $f(\lambda)$.
