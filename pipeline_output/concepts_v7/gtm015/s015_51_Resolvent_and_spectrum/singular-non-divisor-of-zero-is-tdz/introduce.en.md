If an operator is singular but not an algebraic zero divisor (i.e., it is injective with dense range), then it must be a left topological divisor of zero. The proof uses that $T$ being injective with dense but not closed range implies it is not bounded below. This trichotomy -- divisor of zero, TDZ, or invertible -- is fundamental in operator theory.
