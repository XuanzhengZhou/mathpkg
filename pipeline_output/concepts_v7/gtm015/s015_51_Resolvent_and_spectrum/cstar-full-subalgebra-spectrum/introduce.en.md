In a $C^*$-algebra context, if every self-adjoint element of $B$ has real spectrum, then $B$ is full in $A$: invertibility in $A$ implies invertibility in $B$. The proof uses that if $x^*x$ is singular, its real spectrum forces $0$ to be a boundary point, hence a TDZ in both algebras. Thus spectra are permanently preserved.
