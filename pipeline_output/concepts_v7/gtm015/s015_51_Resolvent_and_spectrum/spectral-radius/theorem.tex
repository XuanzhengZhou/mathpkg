For $x \in A$, the spectral radius of $x$ is defined by
$$r(x) = \sup \{|\lambda| : \lambda \in \sigma(x)\}.$$
One has $r(x) \leq \|x\|$. The unbounded component of $\rho(x)$ contains $\{\lambda \in \mathbb{C} : |\lambda| > r(x)\}$; the bounded components of $\rho(x)$ are called the holes of $\sigma(x)$.
