The spectral radius $r(x)$ is the radius of the smallest closed disc centered at $0$ containing the spectrum. It satisfies $r(x) \leq \|x\|$. The complement of the spectrum decomposes into an unbounded component and possibly several bounded components (holes). This geometric picture is fundamental to understanding how spectra behave under changes of algebra.
