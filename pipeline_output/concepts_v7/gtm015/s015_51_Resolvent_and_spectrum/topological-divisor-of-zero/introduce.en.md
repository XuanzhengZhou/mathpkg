A topological divisor of zero (TDZ) generalizes the algebraic notion of zero divisor: instead of $xy = 0$ for some nonzero $y$, one requires $x y_n \to 0$ for a sequence of unit vectors $y_n$. TDZs play a crucial role in understanding the boundary of the spectrum and how spectra change when algebras are enlarged: boundary points of the spectrum correspond to TDZs.
