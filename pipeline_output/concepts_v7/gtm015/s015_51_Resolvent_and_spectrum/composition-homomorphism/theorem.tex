Let $g \in \mathbb{C}(t)$. There exists a unique algebra homomorphism $\Phi_g: \mathbb{C}(t) \to \mathbb{C}(t)$ such that $\Phi_g(1) = 1$ and $\Phi_g(t) = g$. Concretely, for $f = p/q$ in reduced form with $q \neq 0$, one has $\Phi_g(f) = f \circ g$ (composition as rational functions on $\mathfrak{S}$).
