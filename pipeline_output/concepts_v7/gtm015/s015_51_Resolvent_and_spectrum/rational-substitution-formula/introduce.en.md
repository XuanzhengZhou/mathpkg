The substitution formula $f(g(x)) = (f \circ g)(x)$ is the central result of the rational functional calculus. It says that evaluating a rational function $f$ at $g(x)$ is the same as first composing $f$ with $g$ and then evaluating at $x$. This makes the rational calculus compositional, unifying algebraic manipulation with analytic evaluation.
