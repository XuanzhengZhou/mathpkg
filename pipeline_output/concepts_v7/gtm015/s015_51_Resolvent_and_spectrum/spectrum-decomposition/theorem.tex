Let $E$ be a normed space and let $T \in \mathcal{L}(E)$. The \textit{residual spectrum} of $T$, denoted $\sigma_r(T)$, is the set of all $\lambda \in \mathbb{C}$ such that $T - \lambda I$ is injective but its range is not dense in $E$. The \textit{continuous spectrum} of $T$, denoted $\sigma_c(T)$, is the set of all $\lambda \in \mathbb{C}$ such that $T - \lambda I$ is injective, has dense range, but is singular. Thus
$$\sigma(T) = \sigma_p(T) \cup \sigma_r(T) \cup \sigma_c(T) \cup \sigma_{\text{com}}(T)$$
with the four sets mutually disjoint.
