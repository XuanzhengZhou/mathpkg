The residual spectrum $\sigma_r(T)$ consists of $\lambda$ where $T - \lambda I$ is injective but lacks dense range. The continuous spectrum $\sigma_c(T)$ is the remainder: injective, dense range, yet singular. Together with the point spectrum and compression spectrum, this gives the classical partition: $\sigma(T) = \sigma_p(T) \cup \sigma_r(T) \cup \sigma_c(T)$.
