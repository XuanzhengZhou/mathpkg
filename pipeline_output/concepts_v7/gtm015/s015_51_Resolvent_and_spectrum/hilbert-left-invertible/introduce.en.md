In a Hilbert space, an operator is left-invertible precisely when it is bounded below, which in turn is equivalent to $T^*T$ being invertible. The equivalence uses the fact that $(T^*Tx|x) = \|Tx\|^2$, connecting the algebraic property of left-invertibility with the metric property of being bounded below.
