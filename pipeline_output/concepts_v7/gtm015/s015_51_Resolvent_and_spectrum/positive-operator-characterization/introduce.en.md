A bounded operator on a Hilbert space is positive (has nonnegative quadratic form) if and only if it is self-adjoint with spectrum contained in $[0, \infty)$. For a self-adjoint operator $T$, $T \geq 0$ is equivalent to $\sigma(T) \geq 0$, a fundamental link between the order structure and spectral theory.
