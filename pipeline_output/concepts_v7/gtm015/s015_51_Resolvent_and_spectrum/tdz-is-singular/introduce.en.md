If $x$ is a left TDZ, then $x$ cannot be left-invertible: if $yx = 1$, then $\|y_n\| = 1$ implies $1 = \|y_n\| = \|y(x y_n)\| \leq \|y\| \|x y_n\| \to 0$, a contradiction. Thus every TDZ is singular, but not conversely -- some singular elements are not TDZs.
