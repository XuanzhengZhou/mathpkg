The Gel'fand-Mazur theorem states that every complex Banach algebra that is a division ring is isometrically isomorphic to $\mathbb{C}$. Every nonzero element is invertible, and by the nonemptiness of the spectrum, every element $x$ must be a scalar multiple of the identity. This result is the cornerstone of Gel'fand's theory of commutative Banach algebras.
