Let $x \in A$. There exists a unique algebra homomorphism $\varphi: \mathbb{C}[t] \to A$ such that $\varphi(1) = 1$ and $\varphi(t) = x$. If $p = \sum_{k=0}^{n} a_k t^k$, then $p(x) = \sum_{k=0}^{n} a_k x^k$. The range of $\varphi$ is the smallest subalgebra of $A$ containing $x$ and $1$, i.e., the linear span of $\{1, x, x^2, x^3, \ldots\}$.
