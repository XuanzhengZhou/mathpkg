An injective operator with dense but proper range (hence singular, but not a zero divisor) is a two-sided TDZ on a reflexive Banach space. This situation typifies the continuous spectrum: $T$ has no eigenvalues and its range is dense but not closed. The result follows directly from (57.11) and the characterization of zero divisors in (57.1).
