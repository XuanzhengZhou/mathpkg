On a reflexive Banach space, any singular operator that is not an algebraic zero divisor is automatically a two-sided topological divisor of zero. The argument applies (57.9) to both $T$ and its adjoint $T'$, using reflexivity for symmetry. This gives a clean trichotomy: invertible, zero divisor, or two-sided TDZ.
