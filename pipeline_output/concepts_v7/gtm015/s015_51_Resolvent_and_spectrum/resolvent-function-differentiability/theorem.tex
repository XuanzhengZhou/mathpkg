If $x \in A$ and $R = R_x$ is the resolvent function of $x$, then

(i) $\displaystyle\lim_{\lambda \to \infty} R(\lambda) = 0$;

(ii) $R$ is differentiable at every $\lambda \in \rho(x)$, in the sense that the limit
$$\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{1}{h}\bigl[R(\lambda + h) - R(\lambda)\bigr]$$
exists; the limit is $-(\lambda 1 - x)^{-2}$.
