The spectral mapping theorem extends from polynomials to rational functions: if $f = p/q$ with $q$ having no zeros on $\sigma(x)$, then $\sigma(f(x)) = f(\sigma(x))$. The key step is that $f(x) - \mu 1$ is singular precisely when $f(\lambda) - \mu = 0$ for some $\lambda \in \sigma(x)$.
