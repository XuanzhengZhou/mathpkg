The boundary of the spectrum in a subalgebra is contained in the boundary of the spectrum in the larger algebra. The proof uses that boundary points correspond to TDZs, and a TDZ in $B$ is also a TDZ in $A$. As a corollary, if the spectrum in $B$ has empty interior (e.g., it is a subset of $\mathbb{R}$), then the spectra coincide.
