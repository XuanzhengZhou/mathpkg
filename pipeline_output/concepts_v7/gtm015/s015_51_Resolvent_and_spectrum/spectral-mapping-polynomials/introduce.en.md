The spectral mapping theorem for polynomials states that the spectrum of $p(x)$ is exactly the set of values $p(\lambda)$ as $\lambda$ runs over $\sigma(x)$. The proof uses factorization of polynomials into linear factors and the fact that commuting factors are simultaneously invertible. This is the simplest case of the spectral mapping theorem.
