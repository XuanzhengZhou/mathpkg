The Gelfand representation theorem shows that every commutative Banach algebra $A$ is canonically represented as an algebra of continuous functions on its maximal ideal space $\mathcal{M}$ (or equivalently, its character space $\mathcal{X}$). The Gelfand transform $\hat{x}$ sends each $x \in A$ to the continuous function $M \mapsto f_M(x)$. The sup-norm equals the spectral radius, and the kernel is the Jacobson radical.
