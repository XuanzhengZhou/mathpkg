The rational functional calculus extends the polynomial calculus: for any rational function $f = p/q$ whose denominator $q$ has no zeros in $\sigma(x)$, we can define $f(x) = p(x)q(x)^{-1}$. The resulting mapping is a unique algebra homomorphism from the algebra of rational functions regular on $\sigma(x)$ into $A$, sending $t$ to $x$.
