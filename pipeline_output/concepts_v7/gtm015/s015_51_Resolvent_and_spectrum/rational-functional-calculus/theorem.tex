Let $x \in A$. Write $\mathbb{C}(t; \sigma(x))$ for the set of all rational forms $f = p/q$ where $p, q \in \mathbb{C}[t]$ and $q$ has no zeros in $\sigma(x)$. There exists a unique algebra homomorphism $\Phi: \mathbb{C}(t; \sigma(x)) \to A$ such that $\Phi(1) = 1$ and $\Phi(t) = x$. For $f = p/q$,
$$\Phi(f) = p(x)q(x)^{-1}.$$
