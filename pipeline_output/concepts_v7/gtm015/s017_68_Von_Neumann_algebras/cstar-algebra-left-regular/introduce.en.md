The reduced group $C^*$-algebra $\mathcal{A}$ is obtained by taking the norm closure of the left regular representation of $L^1(G)$ (with identity adjoined). As a unital $C^*$-algebra, it provides a setting for applying the Gel'fand-Na\u{i}mark representation theory to the study of group representations, leading ultimately to the Gel'fand-Raikov completeness theorem.
