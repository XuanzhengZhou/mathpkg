An ultraweakly continuous linear form on $\mathcal{L}(H)$ is exactly an infinite sum of matrix coefficient functionals with square-summable coefficient vectors. Such forms constitute the predual of $\mathcal{L}(H)$, and ultraweak continuity is the appropriate notion for the theory of von Neumann algebras, where the ultraweak topology plays a role analogous to the weak$^*$ topology.
