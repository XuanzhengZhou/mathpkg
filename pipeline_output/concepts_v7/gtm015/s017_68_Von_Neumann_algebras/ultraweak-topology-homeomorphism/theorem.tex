\begin{proposition}
Let $K = H \oplus H \oplus \cdots$ be the orthogonal sum of $\aleph_0$ copies of $H$, and define $\tilde{T} \in \mathcal{L}(K)$ by $\tilde{T}x = (Tx_i)$ for $x = (x_i) \in K$. Let $\tau$ be the relative topology on $\mathcal{L}(H)^{\sim} = \{\tilde{T} : T \in \mathcal{L}(H)\}$ induced by the weak operator topology of $\mathcal{L}(K)$. Then the ultraweak topology on $\mathcal{L}(H)$ is the topology that makes the bijection $T \mapsto \tilde{T}$ of $\mathcal{L}(H)$ onto $(\mathcal{L}(H)^{\sim}, \tau)$ a homeomorphism.
\end{proposition}
