\begin{definition}
Let $G$ be a locally compact group and $H$ a Hilbert space. A \text{continuous unitary representation} of $G$ on $H$ is a group homomorphism $t \mapsto U_t$ from $G$ into the unitary group $\mathcal{U}(H)$ that is continuous for the strong operator topology on $\mathcal{U}(H)$. Equivalently, $t \to s$ implies $U_t \to U_s$ strongly. The representation is \text{irreducible} if the $*$-subset $\{U_t : t \in G\}$ of $\mathcal{L}(H)$ is irreducible, i.e., $\{U_t : t \in G\}' = \{\lambda I : \lambda \in \mathbb{C}\}$.
\end{definition}
