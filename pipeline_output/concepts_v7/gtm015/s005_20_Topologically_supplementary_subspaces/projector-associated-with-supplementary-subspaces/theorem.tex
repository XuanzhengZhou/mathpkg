Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$ and let $M, N$ be supplementary linear subspaces of $E$. Define the mapping $p \colon E \to E$ as follows: for $x \in E$, write $x = y + z$ uniquely with $y \in M$, $z \in N$, and set $p(x) = y$. Then $p$ is a linear map satisfying $p^2 = p$ (idempotent), $\operatorname{Im} p = M$, and $\ker p = N$. The map $p$ is called the \textbf{projector} (or \textbf{projection}) of $E$ onto $M$ along $N$.
