Let $E$ be a vector space and let $M, N$ be supplementary linear subspaces of $E$. Let $\pi \colon E \to E/M$ be the canonical quotient mapping, and let $\pi_0$ be the restriction of $\pi$ to $N$. Then $\pi_0 \colon N \to E/M$ is surjective (since $M + N = E$) and injective (since $M \cap N = \{\theta\}$); hence $\pi_0$ is a vector space isomorphism. If $E$ is a TVS, then $\pi_0$ is continuous when $N$ is equipped with the relative topology and $E/M$ with the quotient topology.
