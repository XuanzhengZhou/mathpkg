Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$, and let $M$ be a linear subspace of $E$. A linear subspace $N$ of $E$ is said to be \textbf{supplementary} to $M$ if
$$E = M + N \quad\text{and}\quad M \cap N = \{\theta\}.$$
Such subspaces $N$ always exist: choose a basis of $M$, expand it to a basis of $E$, and let $N$ be the linear span of the basis vectors not in $M$. If $M \neq E$ and $M \neq \{\theta\}$, then there exist infinitely many such subspaces $N$.
