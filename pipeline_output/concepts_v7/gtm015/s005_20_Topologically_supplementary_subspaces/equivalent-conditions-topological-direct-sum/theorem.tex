Let $E$ be a TVS and let $M, N$ be supplementary linear subspaces of $E$; thus every vector $x \in E$ has a unique representation $x = y + z$ with $y \in M$, $z \in N$. The following conditions are equivalent:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item[(a)] $E$ is the topological direct sum of $M$ and $N$;
    \item[(b)] the mapping $y + z \mapsto y$ ($y \in M$, $z \in N$) is continuous;
    \item[(c)] the mapping $y + z \mapsto z$ ($y \in M$, $z \in N$) is continuous.
\end{enumerate}
