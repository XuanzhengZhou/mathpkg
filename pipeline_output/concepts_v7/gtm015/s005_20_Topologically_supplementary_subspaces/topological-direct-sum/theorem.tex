Let $E$ be a TVS and let $M, N$ be supplementary linear subspaces of $E$. Equip $M \times N$ with the product topology (equivalently, with the direct sum topology). The mapping
$$T \colon M \times N \to E, \qquad T(y, z) = y + z$$
is a continuous vector space isomorphism. $E$ is said to be the \textbf{topological direct sum} of $M$ and $N$, and $N$ is called a \textbf{topological supplement} of $M$, if $T$ is a topological isomorphism (i.e., $T^{-1}$ is also continuous). We then write $E = M \oplus_{\text{top}} N$.
