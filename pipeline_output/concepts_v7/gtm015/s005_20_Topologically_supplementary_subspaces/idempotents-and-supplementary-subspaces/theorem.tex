Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$. If $p \colon E \to E$ is an idempotent linear map (i.e., $p^2 = p$), then
$$E = \operatorname{Im} p \oplus \ker p$$
in the algebraic sense; that is, $\operatorname{Im} p$ and $\ker p$ are supplementary linear subspaces of $E$. Conversely, if $M, N$ are supplementary subspaces of $E$, the projector $p$ of $E$ onto $M$ along $N$ satisfies $\operatorname{Im} p = M$ and $\ker p = N$. Thus there is a bijective correspondence between idempotent linear maps on $E$ and pairs of supplementary subspaces of $E$.
