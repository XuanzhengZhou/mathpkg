This theorem extracts the essential properties of the filter of neighborhoods of the neutral element $e$ in a topological group. Properties (1), (2), (6) are general filter properties; (3) follows from continuity of multiplication at $(e,e)$; (4) and (5) follow from inversion and inner automorphisms being homeomorphisms that fix $e$. These properties are characteristic: a filter satisfying them uniquely determines a compatible group topology, making this the cornerstone for constructing topological group structures from local data.
