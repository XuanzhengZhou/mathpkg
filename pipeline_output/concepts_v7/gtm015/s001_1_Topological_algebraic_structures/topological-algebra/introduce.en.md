A topological algebra combines the structures of a topological vector space and a topological ring: it is an algebra over a topological field where addition, scalar multiplication, and algebra multiplication are all continuous. This concept sits at the top of the hierarchy of topological algebraic structures presented in this section, and serves as the foundation for Banach algebras and operator algebras studied later in the book.
