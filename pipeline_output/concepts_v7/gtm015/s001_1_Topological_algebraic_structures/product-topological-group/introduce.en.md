This theorem states that the Cartesian product of finitely many topological groups, equipped with the product topology and componentwise group operation, is again a topological group. This construction generates important examples: the $n$-dimensional vector group $\mathbb{R}^n$ (product of $n$ copies of the additive group $\mathbb{R}$) and the $n$-dimensional torus $\mathbb{T}^n$ (product of $n$ copies of the circle group $\mathbb{T}$).
