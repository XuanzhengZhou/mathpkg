A fundamental system of neighborhoods of a point $x$ (also called a local base or neighborhood base at $x$) is a collection of neighborhoods of $x$ such that every neighborhood of $x$ contains a member of the collection. This concept is essential in topological group theory because, by Theorem (3.3), specifying a suitable fundamental system of neighborhoods at the identity element uniquely determines a compatible group topology.
