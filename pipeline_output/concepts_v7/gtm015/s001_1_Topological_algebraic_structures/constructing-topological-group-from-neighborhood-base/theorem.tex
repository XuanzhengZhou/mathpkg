Let $G$ be an abstract group, with neutral element $e$, and let $\mathcal{B}$ be a nonempty class of subsets of $G$ satisfying the following properties:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $e \in B$ for all $B \in \mathcal{B}$;
\item[(2)] if $B_1, B_2 \in \mathcal{B}$ then there exists $B_3 \in \mathcal{B}$ such that $B_3 \subset B_1 \cap B_2$;
\item[(3)] if $B \in \mathcal{B}$ then there exists $C \in \mathcal{B}$ such that $CC \subset B$;
\item[(4)] if $B \in \mathcal{B}$ then there exists $C \in \mathcal{B}$ such that $C \subset B^{-1}$;
\item[(5)] if $B \in \mathcal{B}$ and $a \in G$ then there exists $C \in \mathcal{B}$ such that $C \subset aBa^{-1}$.
\end{enumerate}
Then, there exists a unique topology on $G$ such that (i) the topology is compatible with the group structure, and (ii) $\mathcal{B}$ is a fundamental system of neighborhoods of $e$ for this topology.
