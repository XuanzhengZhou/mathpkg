This theorem is the converse to the neighborhood properties theorem (3.1): given an abstract group $G$ and a nonempty class $\mathcal{B}$ of subsets satisfying five natural axioms (modeled on the properties of neighborhoods of the identity in a topological group), there exists a unique group topology on $G$ for which $\mathcal{B}$ forms a fundamental system of neighborhoods at $e$. This is the standard method for constructing topological group structures from local data.
