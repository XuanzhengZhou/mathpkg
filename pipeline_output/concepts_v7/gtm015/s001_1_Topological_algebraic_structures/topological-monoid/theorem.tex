Let $X$ be a monoid, i.e., a set $X$ with an internal law of composition $X \times X \to X$, denoted $(x, y) \mapsto xy$, that is associative and admits a neutral element. A topology on $X$ is said to be compatible with the monoid structure if the mapping $(x, y) \mapsto xy$ is continuous, where $X \times X$ bears the product topology. A topological monoid is a pair $(X, \tau)$, where $X$ is a monoid and $\tau$ is a topology on $X$ compatible with the monoid structure.
