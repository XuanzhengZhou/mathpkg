This theorem establishes that inversion, left and right translations, and inner automorphisms are homeomorphisms of a topological group onto itself. A direct consequence is that every topological group is topologically homogeneous: for any two points $a, b \in G$, there exists a homeomorphism sending $a$ to $b$. This means local topological properties (such as having a countable neighborhood base or a compact neighborhood) are uniform across the group.
