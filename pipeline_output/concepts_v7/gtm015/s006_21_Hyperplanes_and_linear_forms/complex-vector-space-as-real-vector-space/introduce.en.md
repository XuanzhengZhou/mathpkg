Every complex vector space carries a natural real vector space structure obtained by restricting scalar multiplication from $\mathbb{C}$ to $\mathbb{R}$. This simple observation is crucial for functional analysis because it allows complex-linear forms to be decomposed into real-linear forms, and complex hyperplanes to be expressed in terms of real hyperplanes. The interplay between the complex and real structures underlies the complex Hahn-Banach theorem and the theory of continuous linear functionals on complex topological vector spaces.
