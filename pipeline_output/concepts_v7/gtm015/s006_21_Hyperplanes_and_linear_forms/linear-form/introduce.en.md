A linear form (or linear functional) is a linear map from a vector space to its scalar field, viewing the field as a one-dimensional vector space over itself. Every nonzero linear form has a maximal linear subspace as its null space, establishing the fundamental link between linear forms and hyperplanes. Linear forms are the algebraic precursors to continuous linear functionals in the theory of topological vector spaces.
