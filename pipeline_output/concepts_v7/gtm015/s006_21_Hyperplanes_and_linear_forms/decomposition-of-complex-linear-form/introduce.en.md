Every complex-linear form $f$ decomposes uniquely as $f = g + i h$ where $g$ and $h$ are real-linear forms, its real and imaginary parts. A key additional relation is $g(ix) = -h(x)$, which follows from complex-linearity $f(ix) = i f(x)$. This implies the important formula $f(x) = g(x) - i g(ix)$, showing that a complex-linear form is completely determined by its real part. This relationship is the algebraic basis for extending the real Hahn-Banach theorem to complex spaces.
