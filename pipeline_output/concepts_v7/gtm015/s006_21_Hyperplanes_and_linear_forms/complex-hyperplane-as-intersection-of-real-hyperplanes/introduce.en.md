This corollary distills the geometric content of Theorem 21.15: every complex hyperplane is the intersection of exactly two real hyperplanes whose underlying maximal real subspaces are $i$-multiples of each other. This is the structural fact that makes the complex Hahn-Banach theorem a direct consequence of the real Hahn-Banach theorem, since a closed complex hyperplane separating two convex sets can be obtained by intersecting two real separating hyperplanes.
