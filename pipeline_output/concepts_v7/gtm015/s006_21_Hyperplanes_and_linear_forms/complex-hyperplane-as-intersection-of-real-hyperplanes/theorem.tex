Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{C}$. Every $\mathbb{C}$-hyperplane in $E$ is the intersection of two $\mathbb{R}$-hyperplanes in $E$. Moreover, the $\mathbb{C}$-hyperplanes in $E$ are precisely the sets of the form $(x_0 + M_0) \cap (y_0 + iM_0)$, where $M_0$ is a maximal $\mathbb{R}$-linear subspace of $E$ and $x_0, y_0 \in E$.
