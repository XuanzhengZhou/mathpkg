A hyperplane is a maximal proper linear variety — an affine subset that cannot be enlarged to a strictly larger proper linear variety. In finite-dimensional geometry over $\mathbb{R}$, hyperplanes correspond to affine subspaces of codimension 1. In the algebraic setting of functional analysis, hyperplanes serve as the geometric counterpart to linear forms: every hyperplane is a level set of a nonzero linear form, and conversely.
