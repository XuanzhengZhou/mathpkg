Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$ and let $H \subset E$. The following conditions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $H$ is a hyperplane in $E$ (a maximal proper linear variety);
\item[(b)] $H = z + M$ where $M$ is a maximal linear subspace of $E$ and $z \in E$;
\item[(c)] $H = \{x \in E : f(x) = \lambda\}$ where $f$ is a nonzero linear form on $E$ and $\lambda \in \mathbb{K}$.
\end{enumerate}
Moreover, if $H = \{x : f(x) = \lambda\} = \{x : g(x) = \mu\}$ with $f, g$ nonzero linear forms, then there exists a nonzero scalar $\rho \in \mathbb{K}$ such that $g = \rho f$ and $\mu = \rho \lambda$.
