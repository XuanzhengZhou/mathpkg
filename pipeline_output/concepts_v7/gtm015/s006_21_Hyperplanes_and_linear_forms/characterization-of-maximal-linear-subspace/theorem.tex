Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$ and let $M$ be a linear subspace of $E$. The following conditions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $M$ is a maximal linear subspace of $E$;
\item[(b)] $M$ is the null space of some nonzero linear form $f: E \to \mathbb{K}$;
\item[(c)] the quotient vector space $E/M$ is one-dimensional;
\item[(d)] there exists a vector $z \notin M$ such that $E = M + \mathbb{K}z$;
\item[(e)] $M \neq E$ and, for each vector $x \notin M$, $E = M + \mathbb{K}x$.
\end{enumerate}
Moreover, if $f$ and $g$ are nonzero linear forms with $\{x : f(x) = 0\} = \{x : g(x) = 0\}$, then there exists a nonzero scalar $\rho \in \mathbb{K}$ such that $g = \rho f$.
