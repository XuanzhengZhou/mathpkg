Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$ and let $V = x + N$ be a linear variety in $E$, where $x \in E$ and $N$ is a linear subspace. Then
$$\bigcap\{H : H \text{ is a hyperplane in } E \text{ and } H \supset V\} = V.$$
If $V = E$, the intersection is taken over the empty family and equals $E$ by convention.
