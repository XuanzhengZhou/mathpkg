Let $$G$$ be a group, $$d$$ a left-invariant metric on $$G$$ such that the topology derived from $$d$$ makes $$G$$ a topological group, and $$N$$ a closed normal subgroup of $$G$$. Let $$G/N$$ be the quotient group and $$\pi: G \to G/N$$ the canonical mapping. Define
$$D(u, v) = \inf \{d(x, y) : x \in u, y \in v\},$$
and $$|x| = d(e, x)$$, $$|u| = \inf \{|x| : x \in u\}$$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $$D$$ is a left-invariant metric on $$G/N$$;
\item the topology derived from $$D$$ coincides with the quotient topology;
\item $$u \mapsto |u|$$ is a value on $$G/N$$;
\item $$D(u, v) = |u^{-1}v|$$.
\end{enumerate}
