Given a left-invariant metric $d$ on a topological group $G$ and a closed normal subgroup $N$, one can define a natural left-invariant metric $D$ on the quotient $G/N$ by taking the infimum of distances between coset representatives. The corresponding value $|u| = \inf_{x \in u} |x|$ on the quotient satisfies $D(u, v) = |u^{-1}v|$, preserving the value-metric correspondence. This construction is essential for proving that quotients of metrizable complete groups remain complete.
