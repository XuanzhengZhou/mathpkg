A topological group is bi-uniform when its left and right uniform structures are identical. Abelian groups are trivially bi-uniform, and every compact group is bi-uniform. For metrizable groups, bi-uniformity is equivalent to the existence of a bi-invariant compatible metric (Klee's theorem). The bi-uniform property is important for the theory of completions and for the construction of invariant metrics.
