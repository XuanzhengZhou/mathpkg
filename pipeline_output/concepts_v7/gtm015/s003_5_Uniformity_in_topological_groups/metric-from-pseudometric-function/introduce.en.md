Given a function $f$ satisfying a relaxed triangle inequality (condition 3 above), one can construct a genuine metric $d$ by taking infima over finite chains. This lemma is the technical core of the Birkhoff-Kakutani metrization theorem, allowing one to build a left-invariant metric from a sequence of symmetric identity neighborhoods. The key estimate $\frac{1}{2}f(x, y) \leq d(x, y) \leq f(x, y)$ shows that $f$ and $d$ are equivalent in a strong sense.
