Let $$G$$ be a topological group, $$\mathcal{V}$$ the class of all neighborhoods of the neutral element $$e$$, and for each $$V \in \mathcal{V}$$ define

$$V_r = \{(x, y) : yx^{-1} \in V\}.$$

The uniform structure $$(G, \mathcal{U}_r)$$, where $$\mathcal{U}_r$$ is the filter on $$G \times G$$ generated by $$\{V_r : V \in \mathcal{V}\}$$, is called the right uniform structure of the topological group $$G$$, and $$\mathcal{U}_r$$ the right uniformity of $$G$$.
