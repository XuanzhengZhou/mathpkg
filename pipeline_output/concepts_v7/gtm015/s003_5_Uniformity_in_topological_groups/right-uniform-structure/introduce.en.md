The right uniform structure of a topological group $G$ is the uniformity generated by the entourages $V_r = \{(x, y) : yx^{-1} \in V\}$ where $V$ ranges over neighborhoods of the identity. In contrast to the left uniformity which uses left-translation, the right uniformity uses right-translation to measure closeness. The left and right uniformities may differ in general, but they induce the same topology on $G$.
