The inversion map $J(x) = x^{-1}$ on a topological group provides a natural isomorphism between the left and right uniform structures. The induced map $(J \times J)(x, y) = (x^{-1}, y^{-1})$ sends entourages of the left uniformity to entourages of the right uniformity and vice versa. This shows that the two uniformities, while possibly distinct, are fundamentally linked by the algebraic structure of the group.
