The left uniform structure of a topological group $G$ is the uniformity generated by the entourages $V_s = \{(x, y) : x^{-1}y \in V\}$ where $V$ ranges over all neighborhoods of the identity $e$. This uniformity encodes the idea that two points are "uniformly close" when their left-translation difference lies in a fixed neighborhood of $e$. The topology derived from this uniform structure coincides with the original topology of $G$.
