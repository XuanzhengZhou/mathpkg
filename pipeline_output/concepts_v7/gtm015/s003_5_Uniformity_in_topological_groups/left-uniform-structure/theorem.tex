Let $$G$$ be a topological group, $$\mathcal{V}$$ the class of all neighborhoods of the neutral element $$e$$, and $$\mathcal{V}_s = \{V_s : V \in \mathcal{V}\}$$ where $$V_s = \{(x, y) : x^{-1}y \in V\}$$. The uniform structure $$(G, \mathcal{U}_s)$$, where $$\mathcal{U}_s$$ is the filter on $$G \times G$$ generated by $$\mathcal{V}_s$$, is called the left uniform structure of the topological group $$G$$, and $$\mathcal{U}_s$$ the left uniformity of $$G$$.
