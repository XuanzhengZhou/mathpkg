Klee's theorem provides a complete characterization: a metrizable topological group has coincident left and right uniformities precisely when it admits a metric that is invariant under both left and right translations. The proof uses the Birkhoff-Kakutani construction with neighborhoods that are invariant under inner automorphisms, yielding a metric $d$ satisfying $d(axa^{-1}, aya^{-1}) = d(x, y)$, which then implies right-invariance as well.
