Let $$G$$ be a topological group and let $$\mathcal{V}$$ be the class of all neighborhoods of the neutral element $$e$$. For each $$V \in \mathcal{V}$$ define a subset $$V_s$$ of $$G \times G$$ by

$$V_s = \{(x, y) : x^{-1}y \in V\}.$$

The class of all such $$V_s$$ is denoted $$\mathcal{V}_s$$. Each $$V_s$$ surrounds the diagonal $$\Delta = \{(x, x) : x \in G\}$$ of $$G \times G$$, in the sense that $$V_s \supset \Delta$$.
