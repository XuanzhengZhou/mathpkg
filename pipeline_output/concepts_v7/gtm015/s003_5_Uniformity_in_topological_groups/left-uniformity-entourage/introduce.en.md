In a topological group, each neighborhood $V$ of the identity $e$ gives rise to a relation $V_s$ on $G \times G$ defined by $(x, y) \in V_s$ iff $x^{-1}y \in V$. These sets $V_s$ are the fundamental "entourages" that generate the left uniform structure on $G$. This construction translates the algebraic notion of nearness (measured by neighborhoods of $e$) into a uniform notion applicable at every point $x \in G$.
