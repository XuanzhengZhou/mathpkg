The transpose (or adjoint) of a continuous linear operator T: E → F is the operator T': F' → E' defined by precomposition: T'(g) = g ∘ T. It maps continuous linear functionals on F to continuous linear functionals on E. The transpose preserves the operator norm and serves as the foundation for duality between operators and their adjoints.
