A normed algebra combines the structures of an associative algebra and a normed space, with the requirement that the norm is submultiplicative: the norm of a product does not exceed the product of the norms. A Banach algebra is a normed algebra that is complete in the norm metric. The prototypical example is L(E), the space of bounded linear operators on a Banach space.
