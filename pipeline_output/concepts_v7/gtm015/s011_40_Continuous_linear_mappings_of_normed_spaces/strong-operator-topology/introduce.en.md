The strong operator topology on L(E,F) is the topology of pointwise convergence, generated by the seminorms T ↦ ||Tx|| for x ∈ E. It is locally convex and separated, coarser than the uniform operator topology. The two topologies coincide precisely when the domain E is finite-dimensional, highlighting a key distinction between finite and infinite dimensions.
