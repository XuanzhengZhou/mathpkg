The weak operator topology on L(E,F) is the coarsest topology making all functionals T ↦ g(Tx) continuous, where x ranges over E and g over the dual F'. It is locally convex and, when F is separated and locally convex, it is separated. It is coarser than the strong operator topology, and the two coincide when F is finite-dimensional.
