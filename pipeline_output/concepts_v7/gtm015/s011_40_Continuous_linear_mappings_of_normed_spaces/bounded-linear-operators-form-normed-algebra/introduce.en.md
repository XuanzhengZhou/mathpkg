The space L(E) of all continuous linear operators from a normed space to itself forms a normed algebra under operator composition, with the identity operator serving as the unit. The operator norm is submultiplicative: ||ST|| ≤ ||S|| ||T||. When E is complete (a Banach space), L(E) becomes a Banach algebra — the fundamental example motivating the general theory.
