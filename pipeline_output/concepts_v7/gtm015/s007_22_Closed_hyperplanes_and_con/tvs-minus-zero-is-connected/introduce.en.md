In a real topological vector space of dimension at least two, the punctured space (the space minus the origin) remains connected. The proof constructs explicit connected sets containing any two nonzero vectors, treating separately the cases where they are linearly independent (using line segments) and linearly dependent (using a third independent vector). This lemma is a key step in the proof of the geometric Hahn-Banach theorem.
