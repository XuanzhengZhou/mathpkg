A Banach generalized limit is a linear form $L$ on the Banach space $(m)$ of bounded sequences satisfying the conditions of a right-invariant mean on the additive semigroup of positive integers. If $L$ is a Banach generalized limit, then $|L(x)| \leq \|x\|_\infty$ for all $x$, and $L$ is a continuous linear form on $(m)$. Moreover, if $x = (\alpha_k)$ and $\alpha_k \geq 0$ for all $k$, then $L(x) \geq 0$.
