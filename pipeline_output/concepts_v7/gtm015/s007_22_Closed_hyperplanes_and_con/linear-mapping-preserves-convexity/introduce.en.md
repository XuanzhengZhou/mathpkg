Convexity is preserved under linear maps in both directions: the direct image and the inverse image of a convex set under a linear map are convex. Moreover, the convex hull of the image equals the image of the convex hull. For inverse images, the convex hull of the preimage is contained in the preimage of the convex hull, with equality when the subset lies in the range.
