In a complex topological vector space, closed complex hyperplanes admit concrete descriptions in terms of closed real hyperplanes. The theorem provides three equivalent formulations: the abstract definition as a closed complex hyperplane, an intersection representation involving two translated real subspaces, and a single-translation representation. These characterizations are obtained by combining the algebraic correspondence with the topological fact that translations are homeomorphisms.
