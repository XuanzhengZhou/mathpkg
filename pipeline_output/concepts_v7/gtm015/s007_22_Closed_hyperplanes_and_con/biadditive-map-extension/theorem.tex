Let $A, B, C$ be complete, abelian, separated topological groups, let $A_0$ and $B_0$ be dense subgroups of $A$ and $B$, and suppose $f: A_0 \times B_0 \rightarrow C$ is bi-additive in the sense that $f(a + a^\prime, b) = f(a, b) + f(a^\prime, b)$ and $f(a, b + b^\prime) = f(a, b) + f(a, b^\prime)$ for all $a, a^\prime \in A_0$ and $b, b^\prime \in B_0$. If $f$ is continuous, then $f$ extends uniquely to a continuous bi-additive map $\bar{f}: A \times B \rightarrow C$.
