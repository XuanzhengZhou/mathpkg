In a topological vector space, if a linear subspace is both closed and maximal (meaning it has codimension 1), then every algebraic supplement automatically becomes a topological supplement. This means the space decomposes as a topological direct sum of the closed maximal subspace and any complementary one-dimensional subspace. The result follows from the fact that the quotient by a closed subspace is separated, and the restriction of the canonical map to the supplement is a bicontinuous isomorphism.
