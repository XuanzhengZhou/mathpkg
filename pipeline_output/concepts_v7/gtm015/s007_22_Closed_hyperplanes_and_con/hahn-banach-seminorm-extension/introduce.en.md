The Hahn-Banach theorem for seminorms: a linear form on a subspace that is bounded by a seminorm extends to a linear form on the whole space with the same seminorm bound. In the real case, this follows from the classical Hahn-Banach theorem. The complex case uses the real case together with a rotation argument. This version is particularly useful in the theory of locally convex spaces and for continuous linear extensions.
