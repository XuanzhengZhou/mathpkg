In a topological vector space, the continuity of a linear form is determined entirely by its null space (kernel). Specifically, a linear form is continuous precisely when its kernel is a closed subset. This equivalence follows from the fact that the null space has codimension 1 (when the form is nonzero), the separatedness of the quotient by a closed subspace, and the automatic continuity of every linear isomorphism from a one-dimensional TVS to the scalar field. The result connects the algebraic notion of a hyperplane being closed with the analytic property of continuity, and it is fundamental for the study of dual spaces and the Hahn-Banach theorem.
