The geometric form of the Hahn-Banach theorem is a separation theorem: given an open convex set and a disjoint linear variety, there exists a closed hyperplane containing the variety and avoiding the open convex set. The proof passes to quotients and uses connectivity arguments to show that the quotient must be one-dimensional. This form is the foundation for all convex separation theorems.
