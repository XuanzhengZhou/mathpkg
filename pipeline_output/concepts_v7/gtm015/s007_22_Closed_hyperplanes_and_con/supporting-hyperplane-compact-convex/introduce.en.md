Every compact convex set in a separated locally convex real TVS admits supporting hyperplanes at each of its boundary points. The proof constructs the point of tangency by intersecting the set with a line segment from an interior point to the boundary point, then applies the separation theorem to obtain the supporting hyperplane. This result underlies the Krein-Milman theorem on extreme points.
