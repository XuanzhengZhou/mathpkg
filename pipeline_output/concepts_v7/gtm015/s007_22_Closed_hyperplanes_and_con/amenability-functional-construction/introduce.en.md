For every abelian semigroup, there exists a subadditive, positively homogeneous functional on the space of bounded functions that lies between infimum and supremum and is translation-invariant in a weakened sense (vanishing on differences of a function and its translates). This functional is constructed via a supremum-infimum technique over finite averages and is the key ingredient for applying the Hahn-Banach theorem to prove amenability.
