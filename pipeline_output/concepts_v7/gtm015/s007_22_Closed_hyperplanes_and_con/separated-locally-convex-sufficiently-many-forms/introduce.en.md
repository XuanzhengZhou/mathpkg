In a separated locally convex TVS, the continuous dual separates points: for every nonzero vector, there is a continuous linear form that does not vanish on it. This follows from the fact that the origin is a closed subspace and the preceding theorem. This property is essential for duality theory and ensures locally convex spaces have a rich supply of continuous linear functionals.
