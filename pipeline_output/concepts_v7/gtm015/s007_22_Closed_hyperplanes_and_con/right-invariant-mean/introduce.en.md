A right-invariant mean is a linear functional on the space of bounded functions that is invariant under right translations and respects the infimum-supremum bounds. Condition (ii) ensures the mean is a genuine averaging operation, assigning to each function a value between its infimum and supremum. The existence of such a mean is the definition of an amenable semigroup.
