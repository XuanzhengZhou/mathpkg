In a locally convex real TVS, a closed convex set and a disjoint compact convex set can be strictly separated by a closed hyperplane. The proof replaces the closed set with a suitable open convex neighborhood: since $B$ is compact and $A$ is closed, there exists a convex open neighborhood $U$ of the origin such that $(A+U) \cap B = \varnothing$, and then the open separation theorem applies. This is one of the most useful separation theorems in functional analysis.
