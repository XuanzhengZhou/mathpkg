The classical Hahn-Banach theorem: a linear form defined on a subspace and dominated by a subadditive, positively homogeneous functional can be extended to a linear form on the whole space while preserving the domination. The proof proceeds by extending one dimension at a time using Zorn's Lemma, with the key step being the verification that a suitable scalar value can be chosen for the extension at each step.
