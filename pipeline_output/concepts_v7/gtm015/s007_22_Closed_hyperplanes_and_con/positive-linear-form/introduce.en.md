A linear form on a preordered vector space is positive if it maps the positive cone into the nonnegative reals. Equivalently, it is monotone with respect to the ordering. The extension of positive linear forms is a central problem in the theory of ordered vector spaces, with applications to integral representation theorems and the Riesz representation theorem.
