A seminorm generalizes the notion of a norm by relaxing the strict positivity condition. Like a norm, it is nonnegative, subadditive, and absolutely homogeneous. The only difference from a norm is that a seminorm may vanish on nonzero vectors. Seminorms are the fundamental building blocks of locally convex topologies via Minkowski functionals.
