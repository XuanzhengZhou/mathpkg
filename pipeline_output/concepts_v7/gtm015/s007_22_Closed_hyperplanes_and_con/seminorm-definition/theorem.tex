Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$. A seminorm on $E$ is a real-valued function $p$ on $E$ such that (1) $p(x) \geq 0$ for all $x \in E$, (2) $p(x + y) \leq p(x) + p(y)$ for all $x, y \in E$, and (3) $p(\lambda x) = |\lambda| p(x)$ for all $\lambda \in \mathbb{K}$, $x \in E$. A seminorm $p$ is a norm if and only if it is strictly positive, i.e., $p(x) > 0$ whenever $x \neq \theta$.
