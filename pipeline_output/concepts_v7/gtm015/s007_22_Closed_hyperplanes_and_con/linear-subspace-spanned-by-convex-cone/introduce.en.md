The linear subspace generated by a nonempty convex cone equals the set of all differences of its elements. The proof shows that $A-A$ is already a linear subspace: it contains zero, is closed under negatives, scalar multiplication, and addition (using the convex cone properties). The minus sign here denotes set-theoretic difference, not set complement.
