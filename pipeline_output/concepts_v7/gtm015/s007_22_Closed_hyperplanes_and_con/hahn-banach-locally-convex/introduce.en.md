In a locally convex TVS, every continuous linear form defined on a subspace can be extended to a continuous linear form on the whole space. This is the topological version of the Hahn-Banach theorem. The proof uses the fact that continuity of $g$ means $|g| \leq p$ for some continuous seminorm $p$ on $M$, then extends $g$ by the Hahn-Banach-Bohnenblust-Sobczyk theorem with the seminorm bound, guaranteeing continuity of the extension.
