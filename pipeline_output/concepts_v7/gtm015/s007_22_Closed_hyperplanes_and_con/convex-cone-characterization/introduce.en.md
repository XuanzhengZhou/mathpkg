A subset of a vector space is a convex cone exactly when it is closed under multiplication by positive scalars (making it a cone) and under addition (making it convex). Condition (ii) follows from convexity and the cone property via the distributive law. Conversely, if both conditions hold, the set is convex because convex combinations reduce to sums after scaling.
