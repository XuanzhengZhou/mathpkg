Let $T$ be a semigroup and let $\mathcal{B} = \mathcal{B}_{\mathbb{R}}(T)$ be the Banach space of all bounded, real-valued functions $x$ on $T$, with pointwise linear operations and the sup norm $\|x\|_\infty$. For $x \in \mathcal{B}$ and $s \in T$, the right translate of $x$ by $s$ is the function $x_s$ defined by $x_s(t) = x(t + s)$ for all $t \in T$. Moreover, $(x + y)_s = x_s + y_s$, $(\alpha x)_s = \alpha x_s$, and $x_{s+t} = (x_t)_s$.
