If $(X, \mathcal{U})$ is any separated uniform structure, there exist a complete uniform structure $(Y, \mathcal{V})$ and a mapping $f: X \rightarrow Y$ such that: (i) $f$ is injective; (ii) $f(X)$ is dense in $Y$; (iii) the mapping $f: X \rightarrow f(X)$ and its inverse are uniformly continuous, where $f(X)$ is equipped with the relative uniform structure induced by $\mathcal{V}$.
