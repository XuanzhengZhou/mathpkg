An ordered vector space is a real vector space equipped with a partial order that respects the vector space operations: translation preserves order, and multiplying by nonnegative scalars preserves order. Preordered vector spaces relax antisymmetry. The compatibility conditions ensure the positive cone $P = \{x : x \geq \theta\}$ is a pointed convex cone, establishing a bijective correspondence between compatible orderings and pointed convex cones.
