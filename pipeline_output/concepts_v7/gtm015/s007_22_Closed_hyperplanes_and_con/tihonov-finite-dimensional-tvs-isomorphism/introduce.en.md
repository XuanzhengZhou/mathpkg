Tihonov's theorem asserts that any finite-dimensional separated topological vector space is canonically homeomorphic to the Euclidean space of the same dimension. The proof proceeds by induction on the dimension, using the fact that closed maximal subspaces yield topological direct sum decompositions. This is one of the fundamental structure theorems for TVS, showing that finite-dimensional separated TVS are topologically indistinguishable from the scalar field product.
