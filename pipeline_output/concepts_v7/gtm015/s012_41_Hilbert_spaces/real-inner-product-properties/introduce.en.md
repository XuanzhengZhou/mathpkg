This theorem verifies that the real inner product defined via the polarization formula indeed possesses all the expected algebraic properties: it recovers the squared norm on the diagonal, is symmetric, and is bilinear (additive and homogeneous in each argument). The proof uses the parallelogram law to establish additivity, and a continuity argument extends homogeneity from rational to real scalars. The result confirms that every real pre-Hilbert space is a real inner product space.
