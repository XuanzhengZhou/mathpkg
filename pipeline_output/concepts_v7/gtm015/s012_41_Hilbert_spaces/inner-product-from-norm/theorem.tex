In a real pre-Hilbert space, the \textbf{inner product} of vectors $x$ and $y$, denoted $(x, y)$, is defined by the formula

\begin{equation}\label{eq:real-inner-product}
(x, y) = \frac{1}{4}\Bigl\{\|x + y\|^2 - \|x - y\|^2\Bigr\}.
\end{equation}

In a complex pre-Hilbert space, the \textbf{inner product} of vectors $x$ and $y$ (in that order), denoted $(x|y)$, is defined by the formula

\begin{equation}\label{eq:complex-inner-product}
(x|y) = \frac{1}{4}\Bigl\{\|x + y\|^2 - \|x - y\|^2 + i\|x + iy\|^2 - i\|x - iy\|^2\Bigr\}.
\end{equation}
