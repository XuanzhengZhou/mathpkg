In a pre-Hilbert space (a normed space whose norm satisfies the parallelogram law), one can recover an inner product directly from the norm through the polarization formulas. In the real case, the inner product is expressed as one quarter of the difference between the squared norms of the sum and difference of two vectors. The complex case adds two additional terms involving multiplication by the imaginary unit $i$, yielding a Hermitian sesquilinear form.
