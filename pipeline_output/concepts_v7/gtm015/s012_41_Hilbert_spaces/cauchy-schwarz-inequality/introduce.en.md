The Cauchy–Schwarz inequality states that the absolute value of the inner product of two vectors is bounded by the product of their norms. The proof in this text uses the polarization formula and the parallelogram law directly. In the real case, it follows from expressing $4(x,y)$ as the difference of two squared norms and applying the parallelogram law. The complex case is reduced to the real case by rotating the inner product to a real value via a unimodular scalar.
