Let $E$ be a pre-Hilbert space.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] \textbf{Real case.} For all $x, y \in E$,
$$|(x, y)| \leq \|x\| \, \|y\|.$$

\item[(ii)] \textbf{Complex case.} For all $x, y \in E$,
$$|(x|y)| \leq \|x\| \, \|y\|.$$
\end{enumerate}

In both cases, equality holds if and only if $x$ and $y$ are linearly dependent.
