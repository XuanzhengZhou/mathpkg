This theorem collects the fundamental algebraic identities for positive sesquilinear forms on complex vector spaces. The polarization identities express the real and imaginary parts of $\varphi(x,y)$ in terms of diagonal values $\varphi(z,z)$. The Cauchy–Schwarz inequality is proved for any positive sesquilinear form (not necessarily Hermitian). The Hermiticity criterion — that $\varphi$ is Hermitian exactly when its diagonal values are real — is a useful test. These results are recorded here for later use (from Section 57 onward) but are fundamental to the theory of Hilbert spaces.
