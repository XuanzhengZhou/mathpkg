This terminological convention fixes the language for the remainder of Berberian's text. Having established the equivalence of pre-Hilbert spaces and inner product spaces, the author adopts the more standard "inner product space" terminology and restricts to the complex case. This avoids duplicating every result for the real and complex cases separately, while allowing the text to exploit properties unique to complex scalars (such as the spectral theorem).
