For the remainder of the text, the following terminological convention is adopted:

\begin{itemize}
\item The term \textbf{inner product space} is used in preference to ``pre-Hilbert space.''
\item The term \textbf{Hilbert space} is used in preference to ``complete inner product space.''
\item Attention is restricted to the \textbf{complex case}, so as to avoid repetition (where the real and complex theories are parallel) and to exploit the peculiar advantages of the complex field (where the theories diverge).
\end{itemize}

Thus, unless otherwise specified, ``inner product space'' and ``Hilbert space'' will mean complex inner product space and complex Hilbert space, respectively.
