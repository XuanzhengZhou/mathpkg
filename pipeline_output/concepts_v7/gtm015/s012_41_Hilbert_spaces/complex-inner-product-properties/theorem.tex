In a complex pre-Hilbert space, the inner product $(x|y)$ defined by

$$(x|y) = \frac{1}{4}\Bigl\{\|x + y\|^2 - \|x - y\|^2 + i\|x + iy\|^2 - i\|x - iy\|^2\Bigr\}$$

satisfies the following conditions for all vectors $x, y, z$ and all complex scalars $\lambda$:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $(x|x) = \|x\|^2$; thus $(x|x) \geq 0$ for all $x$, and $(x|x) = 0$ iff $x = \theta$;
\item[(ii)] $(y|x) = (x|y)^*$;
\item[(iii)] $(x + y|z) = (x|z) + (y|z)$;
\item[(iv)] $(\lambda x|y) = \lambda(x|y)$ for all $\lambda \in \mathbb{C}$.
\end{enumerate}

Consequently,

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(v)] $(x|y + z) = (x|y) + (x|z)$;
\item[(vi)] $(x|\lambda y) = \lambda^*(x|y)$ for all $\lambda \in \mathbb{C}$.
\end{enumerate}

Thus $(\cdot|\cdot)$ is a Hermitian, positive definite, sesquilinear form on $E$ (linear in the first argument, conjugate-linear in the second).
