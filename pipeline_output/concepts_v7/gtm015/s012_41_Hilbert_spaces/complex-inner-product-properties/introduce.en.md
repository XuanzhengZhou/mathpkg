This theorem is the complex analogue of Theorem 41.5. The complex inner product defined by the polarization formula is shown to be a Hermitian sesquilinear form: it is linear in the first argument, conjugate-linear in the second, Hermitian symmetric (conjugate-symmetric), and positive definite. The proof reduces to the real case by treating the complex space as a real pre-Hilbert space and exploiting the identity $(x|y) = (x,y) + i(x,iy)$, where $(\cdot,\cdot)$ is the real inner product.
