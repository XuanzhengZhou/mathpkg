A positive bi-additive form is a function of two variables, additive in each argument separately, whose diagonal values are nonnegative. Such forms provide a straightforward way to construct functionals that satisfy the parallelogram law: if $f$ is a positive bi-additive form, then $N(x) = \sqrt{f(x,x)}$ yields a semi-norm whose square satisfies the parallelogram identity. This construction is the algebraic precursor to defining inner products.
