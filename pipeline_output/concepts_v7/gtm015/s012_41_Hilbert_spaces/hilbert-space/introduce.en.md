A Hilbert space is defined as a Banach space whose norm satisfies the parallelogram law. This definition, due to von Neumann and taken as the primary definition in this text, avoids introducing inner products first. The parallelogram law is the essential geometric identity that distinguishes Hilbert spaces among all Banach spaces, encoding the Euclidean property that the sum of the squares of the diagonals of a parallelogram equals the sum of the squares of its four sides.
