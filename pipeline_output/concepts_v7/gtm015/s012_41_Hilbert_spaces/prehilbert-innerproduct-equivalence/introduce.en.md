This proposition is the synthesis of the entire first half of Section 41. It states the complete equivalence between the two approaches to Hilbert space theory: starting from a norm satisfying the parallelogram law yields an inner product (via polarization), and starting from an inner product yields a norm satisfying the parallelogram law (via $\|x\| = \sqrt{(x|x)}$). Moreover, these two constructions are inverses of each other. This justifies using the terms "pre-Hilbert space" and "inner product space" interchangeably.
