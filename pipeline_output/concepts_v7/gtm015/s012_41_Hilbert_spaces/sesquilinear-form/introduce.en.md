A sesquilinear form on a complex vector space is linear in the first argument and conjugate-linear in the second — a one-and-a-half linearity that generalizes bilinearity to the complex setting. When such a form is also Hermitian (conjugate-symmetric) and strictly positive (positive definite), it is called an inner product. This definition provides the algebraic axiomatization of complex inner product spaces, complementing the norm-based approach of pre-Hilbert spaces; the two perspectives are shown to be equivalent in this section.
