This theorem is the converse to Theorems 41.5 and 41.6: starting from an abstract inner product (defined axiomatically as a positive definite Hermitian sesquilinear form), one obtains a norm via $\|x\| = \sqrt{(x|x)}$ that automatically satisfies the parallelogram law. The triangle inequality follows from the Cauchy–Schwarz inequality. Together with the forward direction (41.5/41.6), this establishes the complete equivalence of the norm-based and inner-product-based approaches to Hilbert space theory.
