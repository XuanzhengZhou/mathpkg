When a topological group G is projected onto the quotient space G/H of left cosets via the canonical mapping π, continuity follows directly from the definition of the quotient topology, while openness relies on the fact that saturation of an open set is a union of translates, which remains open in a topological group. The lemma also characterizes neighborhoods in the quotient: a set is a neighborhood of π(a) precisely when it is the image under π of a translate of a neighborhood of the identity.
