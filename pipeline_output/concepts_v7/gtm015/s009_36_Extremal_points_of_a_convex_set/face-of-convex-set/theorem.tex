Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$ and $A \subset E$ a convex set. A convex subset $F \subset A$ is a **face** of $A$ if
$$\langle u, v \rangle \subset A,\ \langle u, v \rangle \cap F \neq \varnothing \implies \langle u, v \rangle \subset F.$$
In particular, $\{a\}$ is a face of $A$ iff $a$ is an extremal point of $A$.
