Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$ and $A \subset E$ a convex set. A point $a \in A$ is an **extremal point** of $A$ if there exists no nondegenerate open segment $\langle u, v \rangle$ with $a \in \langle u, v \rangle \subset A$. Equivalently,
$$a \in \langle u, v \rangle \subset A \implies u = v.$$
(It is no restriction to suppose $u, v \in A$ in this condition.)
