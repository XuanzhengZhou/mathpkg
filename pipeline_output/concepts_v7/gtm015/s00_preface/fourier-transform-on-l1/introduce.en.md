The Fourier transform maps an element $x \in \ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ to the continuous $2\pi$-periodic function $\hat{x}$ whose Fourier coefficients are precisely the values $x(n)$. This map $\wedge: \ell^1(\mathbb{Z}) \to \mathcal{A}$ is a vector space isomorphism onto the Wiener algebra $\mathcal{A}$, and it converts convolution in $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ into pointwise multiplication in $\mathcal{A}$. The Fourier transform thus provides the fundamental bridge between the sequence-space and function-space perspectives in harmonic analysis.
