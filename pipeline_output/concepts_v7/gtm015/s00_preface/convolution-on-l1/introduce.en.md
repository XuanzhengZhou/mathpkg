Convolution on $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ is defined by the discrete formula $(xy)(m) = \sum_n x(m-n) y(n)$. This operation is well-defined because the series converges absolutely for each $m$ (by Remark 7), and the resulting function $xy$ again belongs to $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ with $\|xy\|_1 \leq \|x\|_1 \|y\|_1$. Convolution generalizes the Cauchy product of power series to bilateral sequences and is the algebraic operation that, under the Fourier transform, corresponds to pointwise multiplication of functions in the Wiener algebra.
