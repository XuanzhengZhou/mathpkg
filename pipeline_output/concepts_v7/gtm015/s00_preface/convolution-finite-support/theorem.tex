If $x, y \in c_{00}(\mathbb{Z})$, then their convolution is given by
$$(xy)(m) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} x(m-n) y(n) \quad (m \in \mathbb{Z}),$$
where for each $m \in \mathbb{Z}$, all but finitely many terms on the right side are zero. In particular, the basis vectors satisfy
$$e_m e_n = e_{m+n} \quad (m, n \in \mathbb{Z}).$$
More generally, for $x = \sum_k x(k) e_k$ and $y = \sum_n y(n) e_n$,
$$xy = \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} x(k) y(n) e_{k+n},$$
and $(xy)(m) = \sum_{k+n=m} x(k) y(n)$.
