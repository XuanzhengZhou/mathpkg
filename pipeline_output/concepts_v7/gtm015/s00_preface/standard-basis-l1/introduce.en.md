The vectors $e_n$ defined by $e_n(m) = \delta_{mn}$ form the standard Schauder basis of $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$. Under the Fourier transform, $e_n$ maps to the trigonometric monomial $u_n(t) = e^{int}$. The $e_n$ satisfy the convolution identity $e_m * e_n = e_{m+n}$, reflecting the additive group structure of $\mathbb{Z}$. Together with the basis expansion theorem (Remark 5), the $e_n$ provide the computational foundation for convolution in $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$.
