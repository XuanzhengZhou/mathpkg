With pointwise addition and scalar multiplication, $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ forms a complex vector space. The $\ell^1$-norm satisfies the triangle inequality $\|x+y\|_1 \leq \|x\|_1 + \|y\|_1$ and absolute homogeneity $\|cx\|_1 = |c| \cdot \|x\|_1$, making $(\ell^1(\mathbb{Z}), \|\cdot\|_1)$ a normed vector space. The proof of the triangle inequality reduces to the finite partial sum inequality and passage to the supremum over all finite subsets of $\mathbb{Z}$.
