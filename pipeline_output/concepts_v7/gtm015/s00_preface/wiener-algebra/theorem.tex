Let $\mathcal{A}$ denote the set of all functions $f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{C}$ of the form
$$f(t) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n e^{int} \quad (t \in \mathbb{R}),$$
where $(a_n)_{n \in \mathbb{Z}}$ is a sequence of complex numbers such that
$$\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} |a_n| < \infty.$$
The elements of $\mathcal{A}$ are continuous functions on $\mathbb{R}$ that are periodic of period $2\pi$. The set $\mathcal{A}$ is closed under pointwise addition and multiplication of functions, forming an algebra of functions called the Wiener algebra.

A function $f \in \mathcal{A}$ is called a trigonometric polynomial if all but finitely many of its coefficients $a_n$ are zero. The set $\mathcal{T}$ of all trigonometric polynomials is a dense linear subspace of $\mathcal{A}$.
