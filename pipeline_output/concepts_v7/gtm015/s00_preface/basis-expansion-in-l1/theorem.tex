If $x \in \ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$, then
$$x = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} x(n) e_n,$$
in the sense that the net of finite partial sums converges to $x$ in the metric of $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$. Equivalently, for any $\varepsilon > 0$ there exists a finite subset $J_0 \subset \mathbb{Z}$ such that for every finite subset $J \supset J_0$,
$$\left\| x - \sum_{n \in J} x(n) e_n \right\|_1 < \varepsilon.$$
In particular, $c_{00}(\mathbb{Z})$ is dense in $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$.
