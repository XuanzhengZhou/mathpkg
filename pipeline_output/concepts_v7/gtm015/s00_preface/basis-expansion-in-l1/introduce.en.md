Every element $x \in \ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ can be expressed as the unconditionally convergent series $x = \sum_{n} x(n) e_n$, where convergence is with respect to the $\ell^1$-norm. This means the net of finite partial sums, indexed by inclusion of finite subsets of $\mathbb{Z}$, converges to $x$. Consequently, $c_{00}(\mathbb{Z})$ is a dense subspace of $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$. This density result is essential for extending algebraic operations (such as convolution) from finitely supported sequences to all of $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$.
