The $\ell^1$-norm $\|x\|_1 = \sum_{n \in \mathbb{Z}} |x(n)|$ measures the total absolute sum of a bilateral sequence. It induces a translation-invariant metric $d(x, y) = \|x - y\|_1$ on $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$, satisfying the standard metric axioms. This norm is fundamental to the Banach space structure of $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ and its completeness (which is a key ingredient in the functional-analytic proof of Wiener's theorem).
