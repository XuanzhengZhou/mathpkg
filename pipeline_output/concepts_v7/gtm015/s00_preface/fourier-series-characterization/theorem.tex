Suppose $f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{C}$ is a continuous, periodic function of period $2\pi$. Define
$$a_n = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} f(t) e^{-int} \, dt \quad (n \in \mathbb{Z}),$$
and suppose that
$$\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} |a_n| < \infty.$$
Then $f$ equals its Fourier series:
$$f(t) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n e^{int} \quad (t \in \mathbb{R}).$$
In other words, $\mathcal{A}$ consists precisely of the continuous, $2\pi$-periodic functions whose Fourier coefficients are absolutely summable.
