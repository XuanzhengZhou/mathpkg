This theorem characterizes the Wiener algebra $\mathcal{A}$ without reference to absolutely summable sequences: a continuous $2\pi$-periodic function belongs to $\mathcal{A}$ if and only if its Fourier coefficients (computed via the standard integral formula) are absolutely summable, and in that case the function equals its Fourier series. The proof uses the Weierstrass approximation theorem by trigonometric polynomials to show that the difference between $f$ and its Fourier series is identically zero.
