If $(a_n)_{n \in \mathbb{Z}}$ is a complex sequence such that
$$\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} |a_n| < \infty,$$
then the formula
$$f(t) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n e^{int}$$
defines a continuous function $f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{C}$ that is periodic of period $2\pi$, the series being uniformly and absolutely convergent on $\mathbb{R}$. Moreover, the coefficients $a_n$ may be recovered from $f$:
$$a_n = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} f(t) e^{-int} \, dt \quad (n \in \mathbb{Z}).$$
