Given an absolutely summable bilateral sequence $(a_n)$, the associated trigonometric series defines a continuous $2\pi$-periodic function via uniform and absolute convergence (by the Weierstrass $M$-test). The coefficients can be uniquely recovered from the function using the standard Fourier coefficient integral formula, thanks to the orthogonality relations $\int_0^{2\pi} e^{int} e^{-imt} dt = 2\pi \delta_{nm}$. This establishes the injectivity of the map from $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ to the Wiener algebra.
