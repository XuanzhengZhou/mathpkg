$\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ is the space of all absolutely summable bilateral sequences of complex numbers, equivalently the space of functions $x: \mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{C}$ with finite $\ell^1$-norm $\|x\|_1 = \sum_n |x(n)|$. It is a Banach space under this norm and serves as the sequence-space counterpart of the Wiener algebra via the Fourier transform. The convolution product makes $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ into a commutative Banach algebra with unity, providing the algebraic framework for Gel'fand's proof of Wiener's theorem.
