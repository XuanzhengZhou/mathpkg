This lemma is the crucial step in the proof of the Open Mapping Theorem. It shows that the closure of the image of any neighborhood of the origin under a surjective continuous linear map has nonempty interior. The proof uses the Baire category theorem: by surjectivity and linearity, $F$ is a countable union of scaled closures of $T(W)$, so one of these closures must have interior — and then a balanced/absorbing argument propagates this to the origin.
