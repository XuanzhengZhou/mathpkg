Let $E$ be a normed space over $\mathbb{K}$ and let $E_0$ be the image of $E$ in $E''$ under the canonical embedding $x \mapsto x''$. Then $E$ is a reflexive Banach space (i.e., $E_0 = E''$) if and only if its closed unit ball $\{x \in E : \|x\| \leq 1\}$ is compact for the weak topology $\sigma(E, E')$.
