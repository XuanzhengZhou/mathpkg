This theorem provides a topological characterization of reflexive Banach spaces. A Banach space is reflexive precisely when its closed unit ball is compact in the weak topology. The proof relies on Goldstine's theorem: the canonical embedding identifies the weak topology on $E$ with the relative weak* topology on $E_0$, so weak compactness of the unit ball of $E$ translates to weak* closedness of its image, which by Goldstine's theorem forces the image to fill the entire bidual unit ball.
