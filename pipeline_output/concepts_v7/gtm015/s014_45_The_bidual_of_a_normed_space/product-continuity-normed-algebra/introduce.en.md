This proposition verifies that the submultiplicative property $\|xy\| \leq \|x\|\|y\|$ guarantees that multiplication is jointly continuous — a fundamental requirement for the algebraic and topological structures to be compatible. The proof uses the identity $xy - x_0 y_0 = (x-x_0)(y-y_0) + x_0(y-y_0) + (x-x_0)y_0$ and the submultiplicativity of the norm.
