Rare sets (also called nowhere dense) and meager sets (also called sets of the first category) are the fundamental building blocks of Baire category theory. A rare set is one whose closure contains no open set, while a meager set is a countable union of rare sets. These notions provide a topological notion of "smallness" that is essential for the formulation of the Baire category theorem and its many applications in functional analysis.
