This theorem provides multiple equivalent ways to recognize a Baire space. A Baire space is one in which meager sets are "small" in a precise topological sense: their complements are dense, nonempty open sets are never meager, and countable unions of closed sets with empty interior still have empty interior. These characterizations are essential for applying Baire category arguments in analysis.
