The Banach-Steinhaus Theorem is the convergence form of the Uniform Boundedness Principle. It guarantees that the pointwise limit of a sequence of continuous linear operators between Banach spaces is itself a continuous linear operator, and that the sequence of operator norms is bounded. This theorem is fundamental for establishing the continuity of operators defined as limits.
