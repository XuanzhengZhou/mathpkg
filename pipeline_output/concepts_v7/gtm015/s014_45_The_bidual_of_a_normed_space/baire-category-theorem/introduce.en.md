The Baire Category Theorem is one of the fundamental results of functional analysis. It states that every complete metric space is a Baire space — in such a space, the complement of any meager (first category) set is dense, and the space itself cannot be expressed as a countable union of closed sets with empty interior. This theorem underpins the Uniform Boundedness Principle, the Open Mapping Theorem, and the Closed Graph Theorem.
