In a Banach algebra, the inversion map $x \mapsto x^{-1}$ is differentiable, with derivative $-(x^{-1})^2$. This is the Banach-algebraic analogue of the elementary calculus formula $d/dx(1/x) = -1/x^2$. The result is fundamental for the analytic functional calculus in Banach algebras.
