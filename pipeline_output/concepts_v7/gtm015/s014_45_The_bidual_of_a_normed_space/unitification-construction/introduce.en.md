The unitification is a standard algebraic construction that adjoins a unit element to an algebra that may lack one. By taking the Cartesian product with $\mathbb{C}$ and defining multiplication appropriately, one obtains a new algebra with unity in which the original algebra sits as a maximal ideal of codimension one. This construction is essential for extending results about algebras with unity to general algebras.
