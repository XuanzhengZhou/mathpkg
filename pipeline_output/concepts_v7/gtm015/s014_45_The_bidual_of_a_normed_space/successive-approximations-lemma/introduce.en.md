The Successive Approximations Lemma is a technical result that bridges the gap between approximate openness (closure of image contains a ball) and exact openness (image contains a ball) for maps between complete metric spaces. It is used in the proof of the Open Mapping Theorem to upgrade the conclusion of the key lemma from "the closure of the image contains a neighborhood" to "the image itself is open."
