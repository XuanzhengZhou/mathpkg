The Closed Graph Theorem is one of the three fundamental principles of functional analysis. It provides a powerful criterion for continuity: a linear map between complete metrizable TVS (e.g., Banach spaces) is continuous precisely when its graph is closed in the product topology. This is particularly useful because verifying that a graph is closed is often much easier than directly proving continuity of the operator.
