The Open Mapping Theorem is one of the three fundamental principles of functional analysis, alongside the Uniform Boundedness Principle and the Hahn-Banach Theorem. It states that a surjective continuous linear map between complete metrizable topological vector spaces (in particular, between Banach spaces) is automatically open. The proof relies on the Baire Category Theorem applied to the images of scaled neighborhoods of the origin.
