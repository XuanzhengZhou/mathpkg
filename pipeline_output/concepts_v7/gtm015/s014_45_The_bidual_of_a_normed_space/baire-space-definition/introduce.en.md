A Baire space is a topological space in which the Baire category theorem holds. The definition formalizes the intuition that meager sets are topologically negligible. Baire spaces form the natural setting for applications of the Baire category theorem, which is one of the cornerstones of functional analysis, underlying the Uniform Boundedness Principle, the Open Mapping Theorem, and the Closed Graph Theorem.
