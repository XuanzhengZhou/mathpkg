In a Banach algebra, the inversion map $x \mapsto x^{-1}$ on the group of invertible elements is continuous (and therefore bicontinuous, since it is its own inverse). This makes the group of invertible elements a topological group, a fundamental fact for the Gel'fand theory of Banach algebras.
