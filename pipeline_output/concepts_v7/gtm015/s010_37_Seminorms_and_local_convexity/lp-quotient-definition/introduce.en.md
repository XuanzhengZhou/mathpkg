The space $L^p$ is defined as the quotient of $\mathcal{L}^p$ (the space of measurable functions with integrable $p$-th power) modulo the subspace of functions that vanish almost everywhere. This quotienting transforms the seminorm $\|\cdot\|_p$ on $\mathcal{L}^p$ into a genuine norm on $L^p$, making it a normed (in fact Banach) space.
