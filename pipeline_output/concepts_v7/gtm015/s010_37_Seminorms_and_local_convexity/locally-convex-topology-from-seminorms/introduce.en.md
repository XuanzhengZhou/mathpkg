This is the main characterization theorem of Section 37: a topological vector space is locally convex (in the sense of having a neighborhood basis of convex sets at zero) if and only if its topology can be generated by a family of seminorms. The proof uses the Minkowski functional theorem: every closed, balanced, convex neighborhood of zero is the unit ball of a continuous seminorm. This result shows that the "neighborhood definition" and the "seminorm definition" of local convexity are completely equivalent.
