A uniformly convex normed space is one where the unit sphere is "strictly round": if the midpoint of two unit vectors is nearly on the unit sphere, then the two vectors must be close together. This geometric property, introduced by Clarkson, has important consequences including the Milman-Pettis theorem that every uniformly convex Banach space is reflexive.
