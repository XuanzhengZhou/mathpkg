This lemma establishes that passing from a family of seminorms $\mathcal{F}$ to its saturation $\mathcal{F}^*$ (all seminorms continuous for $\tau(\mathcal{F})$) does not change the generated locally convex topology. The inclusion $\tau(\mathcal{F}) \subset \tau(\mathcal{F}^*)$ is trivial since $\mathcal{F} \subset \mathcal{F}^*$; the reverse inclusion follows by expressing each $p \in \mathcal{F}^*$ as dominated by a finite sum from $\mathcal{F}$. This result is a key ingredient in establishing the bijection between saturated families of seminorms and locally convex topologies.
