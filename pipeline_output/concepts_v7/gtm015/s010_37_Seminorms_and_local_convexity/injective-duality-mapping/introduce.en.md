This theorem formalizes the idea that a duality allows each of the two spaces to be viewed as a subspace of the algebraic dual of the other. Injectivity of $x \mapsto B_x$ means that if $B(x, y) = 0$ for all $y \in F$, then $x = \theta$; this is exactly condition (i) of nondegeneracy. Similarly, injectivity of $y \mapsto B^y$ corresponds to condition (ii). This is the starting point for the theory of weak topologies.
