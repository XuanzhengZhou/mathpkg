Young's inequality is the fundamental building block for the inequalities of $L^p$ theory. It states that a product $ab$ is bounded by a convex combination of $a^p$ and $b^q$ when $1/p + 1/q = 1$. The proof typically uses the convexity of the exponential function. For $p = q = 2$, it reduces to the elementary inequality $2ab \leq a^2 + b^2$. Young's inequality is the key ingredient in the proof of Hölder's inequality.
