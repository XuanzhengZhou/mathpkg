The $L^2$ space is the canonical example of a Hilbert space. The inner product is defined by integrating the product of one function with the complex conjugate of the other. The $L^2$ norm is the square root of the inner product of a function with itself. Completeness of $L^2$ is the Riesz-Fischer theorem.
