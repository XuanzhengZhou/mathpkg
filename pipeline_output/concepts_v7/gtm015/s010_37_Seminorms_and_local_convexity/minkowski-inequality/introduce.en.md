Minkowski's inequality is the triangle inequality for the $L^p$ (semi)norm, establishing that $\|f + g\|_p \leq \|f\|_p + \|g\|_p$. For $p = 1$, it follows directly from the triangle inequality for absolute values. For $p > 1$, the proof uses Hölder's inequality and the relation $p - p/q = 1$. Together with Hölder's inequality, Minkowski's inequality forms the analytical foundation for the theory of $L^p$ spaces as normed vector spaces.
