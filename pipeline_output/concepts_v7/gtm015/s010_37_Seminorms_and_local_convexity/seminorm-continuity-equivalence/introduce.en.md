This lemma gives three equivalent characterizations for when one seminorm is continuous with respect to the locally convex topology generated by another. Condition (c) — the existence of a dominating constant $M$ — is often the most practical to verify. The equivalence of (a) and (c) generalizes the well-known fact for norms that two norms induce the same topology iff they are equivalent (mutually bounded by constants).
