If $E$ is any locally convex, separated TVS over $\mathbb{K}$, and $F$ is the vector space of all continuous linear forms on $E$ (with the pointwise linear operations), then the formula
$$B(x, f) = f(x) \quad (x \in E, f \in F)$$
defines a duality (called the canonical duality) between $E$ and $F$. Here nondegeneracy rests on the Hahn-Banach theorem.
