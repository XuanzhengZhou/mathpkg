When $E$ is a separated locally convex TVS, the natural evaluation pairing with its continuous dual $E'$ forms a duality. Nondegeneracy in the first variable (separating points of $E$ by continuous linear functionals) is precisely the content of the Hahn-Banach theorem. This is the most important duality in functional analysis: it underlies the weak topology $\sigma(E,E')$, the weak* topology $\sigma(E',E)$, and the entire theory of dual pairs.
