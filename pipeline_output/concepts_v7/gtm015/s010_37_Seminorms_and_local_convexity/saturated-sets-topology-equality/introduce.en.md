This corollary establishes that the mapping $\mathcal{F} \mapsto \tau(\mathcal{F})$ is injective when restricted to saturated families of seminorms. The proof uses the fact that for saturated families, a seminorm is in $\mathcal{F}$ precisely when it is continuous for $\tau(\mathcal{F})$. Together with Lemma 37.16, this yields a bijective correspondence between saturated families of seminorms on $E$ and locally convex (compatible) topologies on $E$.
