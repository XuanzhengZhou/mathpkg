Let $E$ and $F$ be vector spaces over $\mathbb{K}$. A duality between $E$ and $F$ is a bilinear form $B: E \times F \to \mathbb{K}$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] if $x \in E$ and $x \neq \theta$, there exists $y \in F$ such that $B(x, y) \neq 0$;
\item[(ii)] if $y \in F$ and $y \neq \theta$, there exists $x \in E$ such that $B(x, y) \neq 0$.
\end{enumerate}
(That is, the bilinear form $B$ is nondegenerate in each variable.)
