The simplest example of a duality is the evaluation pairing $B(x, f) = f(x)$ between a vector space $E$ and its algebraic dual $F$ (the space of all linear forms on $E$). Nondegeneracy follows from the fact that for any nonzero $x \in E$, one can construct a linear form $f$ with $f(x) \neq 0$ by extending from a basis, and for any nonzero $f$, there exists $x$ with $f(x) \neq 0$ by definition.
