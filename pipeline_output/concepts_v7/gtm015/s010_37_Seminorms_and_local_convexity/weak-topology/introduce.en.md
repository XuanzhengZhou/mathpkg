The weak topology $\sigma(E,F)$ is the coarsest topology on $E$ that makes every linear form $y \mapsto B(x,y)$ (for $x \in E$) continuous. Equivalently, it is the locally convex topology generated by the seminorms $p_y(x) = |B(x,y)|$ for $y \in F$. When $F = E'$ (the continuous dual of a locally convex space), $\sigma(E,E')$ is the standard weak topology. Despite being very coarse, the weak topology shares the same continuous linear functionals as the original topology, a fact of fundamental importance in functional analysis.
