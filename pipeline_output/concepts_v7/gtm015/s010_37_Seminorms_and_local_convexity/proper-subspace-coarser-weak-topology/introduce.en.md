This corollary shows that weak topologies are sensitive to the choice of the dualizing subspace. Even when a proper subspace $F_0 \subsetneq F$ still separates points of $E$ (so the pairing $(E, F_0)$ is a duality), the resulting weak topology $\sigma(E, F_0)$ is strictly coarser than $\sigma(E, F)$. This reflects the fact that different dualities between the same spaces can produce different weak topologies.
