Let $B: E \times F \to \mathbb{K}$ be a duality. If $F_0$ is a linear subspace of $F$ such that the relations $x \in E$, $B(x, y) = 0$ for all $y \in F_0$ imply $x = \theta$ (i.e., $F_0$ still separates points of $E$), and if $F_0 \neq F$, then $\sigma(E, F_0)$ is strictly coarser than $\sigma(E, F)$.
