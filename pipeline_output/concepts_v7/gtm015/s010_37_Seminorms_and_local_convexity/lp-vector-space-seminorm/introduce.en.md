This corollary summarizes the algebraic and geometric structure of $\mathcal{L}^p$: it is a complex vector space under pointwise operations, and the map $f \mapsto \|f\|_p$ satisfies all the axioms of a seminorm (nonnegativity, absolute homogeneity, and the triangle inequality via Minkowski's inequality). The only thing preventing $\|\cdot\|_p$ from being a norm is that $\|f\|_p = 0$ only implies $f = 0$ almost everywhere, not everywhere.
