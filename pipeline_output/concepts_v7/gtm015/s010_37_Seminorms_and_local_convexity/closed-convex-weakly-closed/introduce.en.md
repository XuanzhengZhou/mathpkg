This is a fundamental result sometimes called Mazur's theorem: for convex sets in a locally convex space, closure and weak closure coincide. The proof relies on the geometric form of Hahn-Banach: both $\tau$ and $\sigma(E,E')$ are locally convex separated topologies that determine the same continuous linear functionals, hence the same closed convex sets. This result is heavily used in functional analysis, for instance in proving that norm-closed convex sets in Banach spaces are also weakly closed.
