Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$, $p$ a seminorm on $E$, $M$ a linear subspace of $E$, and $g$ a linear form on $M$ such that $|g(y)| \leq p(y)$ for all $y \in M$. Then there exists a linear form $f$ on $E$ such that $f|_M = g$ and $|f(x)| \leq p(x)$ for all $x \in E$.

\text{(This is the analytical form of Hahn-Banach, reproved here using the locally convex topology $\tau_p$ and the geometric form of Hahn-Banach.)}
