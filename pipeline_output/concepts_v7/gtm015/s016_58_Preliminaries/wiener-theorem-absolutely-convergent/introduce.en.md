Wiener's theorem states that if an absolutely convergent trigonometric series never vanishes, its reciprocal also has an absolutely convergent Fourier series. Gel'fand's celebrated proof uses the theory of Banach algebras: identifying the sequence space $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ with a Banach algebra under convolution, the result follows from the Gel'fand representation theorem characterizing maximal ideals.
