Unital $*$-homomorphisms between $C^*$-algebras intertwine the continuous functional calculus: $\varphi(f(a)) = f(\varphi(a))$ for normal $a$. This naturality property is essential for the proof of the spectral mapping theorem and ensures that the functional calculus transforms correctly under $*$-homomorphisms.
