The Gel'fand transform identifies $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ with a subalgebra of continuous functions on the circle. Every character (multiplicative linear functional) on $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ is evaluation of the Fourier series at a point on the unit circle. This identification is the key step in Gel'fand's proof of Wiener's theorem.
