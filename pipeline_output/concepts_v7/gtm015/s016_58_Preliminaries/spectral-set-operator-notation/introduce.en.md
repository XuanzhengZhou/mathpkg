The notation $f^\sigma(T)$ (or simply $f(T)$) denotes the image of a $\sigma$-analytic function $f$ under the $\sigma$-analytic functional calculus. When $\sigma(T)$ itself is a spectral set, this generalizes the Riesz-Dunford calculus and the continuous functional calculus for normal operators.
