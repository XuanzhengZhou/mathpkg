An operator is unitary if and only if the unit circle is a spectral set. This characterizes unitarity in terms of the rational functional calculus: the norm of any rational function of the operator is bounded by its sup norm on the circle. Combined with the self-adjoint and contraction cases, this shows how spectral properties characterize operator-theoretic classes.
