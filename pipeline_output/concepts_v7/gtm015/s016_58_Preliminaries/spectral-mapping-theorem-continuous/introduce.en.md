The spectral mapping theorem for the continuous functional calculus states that the spectrum of $f(a)$ equals $f$ applied to the spectrum of $a$, for any normal element $a$ and continuous function $f$ on $\sigma(a)$. This generalizes the polynomial spectral mapping theorem and is central to the spectral theory of normal operators.
