The sup norm on subsets of the Riemann sphere $\mathfrak{S} = \mathbb{C} \cup \{\infty\}$ provides the natural norm for function algebras appearing in spectral set theory. For a compact $\sigma$, the algebra $\mathcal{C}(\sigma)$ of continuous functions with this norm is a commutative $C^*$-algebra, isomorphic to the Gel'fand transform of $A(\sigma)$.
