\textbf{Stone-Čech Theorem.} If $T$ is a completely regular space, there exists a compact space $\mathcal{X}$ and a continuous injective mapping $\varphi: T \to \mathcal{X}$ such that $\varphi(T)$ is dense in $\mathcal{X}$, the pair $(\mathcal{X}, \varphi)$ having the universal property: for every compact space $K$ and every continuous $f: T \to K$, there exists a unique continuous $g: \mathcal{X} \to K$ such that $g \circ \varphi = f$.
