Let $\sigma$ be a closed subset of $\mathfrak{S}$ (the Riemann sphere). The closure of $Q(\sigma)$ in $\mathcal{C}(\sigma)$ with respect to the sup norm is denoted $\mathcal{A}(\sigma)$. A function in $\mathcal{A}(\sigma)$ is said to be $\sigma$-\textit{analytic}. Thus $f \in \mathcal{C}(\sigma)$ is $\sigma$-analytic iff there exists a sequence $f_n \in Q(\sigma)$ such that $\|f_n - f\|_\sigma \to 0$.
